What Happens After You Send the Letter

1
Within 15 school days: The district must send you a written response and a Consent to Evaluate form. If they refuse, they must provide a Prior Written Notice explaining why.
2
You sign the consent form. This is the moment the 45-school-day evaluation clock officially starts. Do not sign anything without reading it first.
3
Within 45 school days: The district must complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) and deliver the written report to you before any ARD meeting.
4
Within 30 calendar days of the report: An ARD meeting must be held to determine eligibility. If your child qualifies, the IEP is written at this meeting.
Legal basis for your letter Your request is grounded in IDEA (20 U.S.C. § 1414), 19 TAC Chapter 89, Subchapter AA, and the Texas Education Code Chapter 29. Under Child Find obligations, your district is required to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with suspected disabilities — regardless of whether they suggest RTI first.
