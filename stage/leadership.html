How to Contact Special Education Staff Effectively

Knowing who to contact is only half the battle. Here are the rules that actually get a response:

  • Always create a paper trail. Phone calls are fine for quick updates, but follow every call with an email summarizing what was discussed. If a promise isn't in writing, it doesn't exist.
  • Follow the chain of command. Start with the special education teacher or diagnostician. If they don't act, email the principal and CC the Executive Director of Special Education directly.
  • Be specific, not emotional. State the facts: "I am writing regarding [Child's Name]'s reading goals. I am requesting a formal ARD meeting within 10 days." Vague emails get vague responses.

What to Do If You Can't Reach Anyone

If campus staff is unresponsive on an urgent special education matter, escalate systematically:

  • Attempt 2: Re-send your original email and explicitly note it is your second attempt. Copy the principal.
  • 48 hours, no response: Forward the entire email chain to the Abilene ISD Director of Special Education.
  • Still nothing: File a formal Level 1 Grievance under FNG Local board policy. You typically have 15 days from the incident.
  • Last resort: File a formal complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA will investigate and respond within 60 days.

Your Right to District Records

If staff is stonewalling you, one of your most effective tools is requesting the underlying data. Under FERPA, you have the right to inspect and review your child's complete educational records — including internal emails that mention your child — within 45 days of a written request.

Send the request in writing to the Abilene ISD Special Education Records Clerk or the Executive Director. State clearly that you are requesting a complete copy of all educational records under FERPA, and include your child's name, date of birth, and campus. Keep a copy of everything you send.
