Navigating Special Education in Abilene ISD

Abilene ISD serves over 15,000 students in West Texas under the oversight of TEA Region 14. While the district is required to provide services, parents must understand that the IEP process is a legal negotiation — not just a school meeting.

You are a mandatory, equal member of the ARD committee. The district has up to 45 school days from the date of your written consent to complete evaluations. You are legally entitled to written notice at least five school days before any ARD meeting, and you cannot be pressured to sign anything at the table.

Do not rely on verbal conversations. To trigger Abilene ISD's legal obligations under IDEA, you must put all requests for evaluations or ARD meetings in writing and keep a time-stamped copy.

The RTI Delay Trap

When a parent raises concerns, a campus will often suggest placing the child in RTI (Response to Intervention) instead of testing for special education. RTI cannot be used to delay or deny a special education evaluation. If your child is struggling, you have the right to submit a written request for a Full Individual and Initial Evaluation (FIIE) immediately — regardless of where they are in RTI.

Common Questions from Abilene ISD Families

What special education resources does Abilene ISD offer?

Abilene ISD provides specialized instruction, related services (speech, OT, counseling), dyslexia screening and intervention, ARD/IEP planning, and accommodations in the general education classroom. Services are determined by the ARD committee based on your child's individual evaluation results.

My child's teacher thinks they need special education. What's the first step?

You don't have to wait for the school. Submit a written request for a Full Individual and Initial Evaluation (FIIE) directly to the campus principal and the Abilene ISD Special Education Director. This starts the legal 15-school-day response clock — regardless of what RTI stage your child is in.

How often will my child's ARD committee meet?

At minimum once per year for the annual IEP review. You can request an ARD meeting at any time if you have concerns or believe the IEP isn't being implemented. Put your request in writing and keep a copy.

Is Abilene ISD required to evaluate my child?

Yes, if there is a reasonable suspicion of a disability. Once you submit a written evaluation request, the district must respond within 15 school days with either a consent form or a written refusal (Prior Written Notice). They cannot use RTI as a reason to delay.
