What are my child's dyslexia rights in Texas?
Under the Texas Dyslexia Handbook (2024 update), all districts must screen students in grades K–2 and provide evidence-based intervention programs. If your child is identified, they are entitled to a structured literacy program and — if eligible — a 504 Plan or IEP with accommodations like extended time and audio support.

The Texas Dyslexia Handbook (2024) in Abilene ISD

Abilene ISD is legally required to follow the Texas Dyslexia Handbook, updated in 2024 following the passage of HB 3928. This handbook is the definitive legal roadmap for identifying, evaluating, and providing services to students with dyslexia — and it's binding on every Texas campus.

The 2024 update clarifies something parents often don't know: a formal dyslexia evaluation is now officially classified as a Full Individual and Initial Evaluation (FIIE) under special education law, giving you stronger legal footing when requesting one.

If your child is struggling to read, you don't have to "wait and see." You have the legal right to request a formal dyslexia evaluation in writing at any time.
· · ·

Mandatory Screening Requirements

Texas law requires Abilene ISD to screen all students for dyslexia in kindergarten and first grade. Screening is not a full evaluation — it's designed to flag students who may be at risk by assessing phonological awareness, rapid naming, and letter-sound knowledge.

If your child is flagged, the campus must notify you and begin monitoring. But you don't have to wait for a screener to act. You can request a full evaluation at any grade level, at any time, simply by putting it in writing.

What Is Structured Literacy?

If your child is identified with dyslexia, Abilene ISD is required to provide a Structured Literacy program — an explicit, systematic, cumulative approach to reading instruction that covers phonemic awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension in a sequential order that builds on each prior skill.

The specific program used at your child's campus is determined by the ARD or 504 committee. If you don't know what program your child is currently receiving, ask the campus reading specialist directly — and ask for data showing whether it's working.

· · ·

504 Plan vs. IEP for Dyslexia: Which Does Your Child Need?

The 2024 handbook clarifies that dyslexia is a Specific Learning Disability (SLD). Whether your child receives a 504 Plan or a full IEP depends on the severity of impact on their learning:

  • Section 504 Plan Appropriate when dyslexia affects learning but doesn't require intensive specialized instruction. Provides accommodations — extended time, text-to-speech, oral testing — but not a modified curriculum or specialized teaching approach.
  • IEP (Special Education) Required when dyslexia significantly impairs the ability to learn and the child needs intensive, individualized, specialized instruction to make progress. Comes with more legal protections, measurable goals, and guaranteed service delivery minutes.

If Abilene ISD is offering a 504 when you believe your child needs an IEP, you have the right to request a full FIIE evaluation and push for eligibility review at an ARD meeting.

How to Request a Dyslexia Evaluation in Abilene ISD

Verbal conversations with a teacher don't start the legal clock. Only a written request does.

  1. Write the Request: Draft a letter formally requesting a Full Individual and Initial Evaluation (FIIE) for suspected dyslexia and Specific Learning Disability.
  2. Submit to the Right People: Email the request to your campus principal and copy the Abilene ISD Special Education Director. Keep a time-stamped copy.
  3. Watch the 15-Day Clock: The district has exactly 15 school days from receipt to provide you a written response and a consent form.
  4. Sign Consent to Start Testing: Once you sign, the district has 45 school days to complete the full evaluation and schedule an ARD meeting.

Frequently Asked Questions

What every Abilene ISD parent of a dyslexic child needs to know.

Is my child entitled to dyslexia testing in Texas?

Yes. Texas law requires all districts to screen students for dyslexia in K–1 and conduct a full evaluation at any grade level if a parent formally requests it in writing.

What accommodations can a dyslexic child get in Abilene ISD?

Common accommodations include extended time on tests, text-to-speech software, oral administration of assignments, graphic organizers, and spelling grace. These can be provided through a 504 Plan or an IEP depending on the level of need.

Can Abilene ISD make us wait through RTI before evaluating?

No. Under IDEA and Texas law, RTI (Response to Intervention) cannot be used to delay or deny a parent's written request for a special education evaluation. If the school tells you to "wait and see how RTI goes," put your evaluation request in writing immediately.

What is the Texas Dyslexia Handbook?

The Texas Dyslexia Handbook is the state guidance document — updated in 2024 — that outlines every district's legal responsibilities for identifying, evaluating, and serving students with dyslexia. It is binding on Abilene ISD and all other Texas districts.
