⚡ Quick Answer

How long does Texas have to complete an evaluation?
Once a written request is received, the district must respond within 15 school days with a consent form. Once you sign, they have 45 school days to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) and schedule an ARD meeting. Verbal requests do not start the clock — always submit in writing and keep a copy.

Requesting a Special Education Evaluation

If you have concerns about your child's academic progress, or believe a disability may be affecting their ability to learn, you have the legal right to request a special education evaluation from Abilene ISD at any time.

Under IDEA's "Child Find" mandate, Abilene ISD has a legal obligation to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities within their jurisdiction — regardless of whether teachers have flagged the child or whether the child is passing their classes. Your written request is the trigger that forces the district to act.

Verbal requests to a teacher do not start the legal clock. To trigger Abilene ISD's obligations under IDEA, you must put your evaluation request in writing.
· · ·

How to Submit Your Written Request

A proper evaluation request forces the district into a strict legal timeline. Here's how to do it correctly:

  1. Address It to the Right People: Send the letter to both the campus principal and the Abilene ISD Special Education Director.
  2. Use the Exact Language: State clearly that you are requesting a "Full Individual and Initial Evaluation (FIIE) under IDEA to determine eligibility for special education."
  3. Name Your Concerns Specifically: List suspected disabilities (dyslexia, ADHD, autism, speech delays) and describe what interventions have already been tried and why they haven't worked.
  4. Create a Paper Trail: Send by email for a timestamp, or certified mail with return receipt. Keep a copy of everything.

If the district has refused to evaluate your child, or is telling you to wait through RTI before testing, they may be violating Child Find. A local advocate can force compliance quickly.

The 15 → 45 → 30 Day Timeline

Once Abilene ISD receives your written request, three strict deadlines kick in. Track every date — school days don't include weekends, holidays, or summer breaks.

  • District responds with consent form 15 school days
    After receiving your written request, the district must provide a formal consent form. If they refuse to evaluate, they must give you a written Prior Written Notice explaining exactly why.
  • You sign consent — clock starts Day 0
    The 45-day evaluation window begins the moment the district receives your signed consent form. Keep a copy with the date noted.
  • FIE completed and report delivered 45 school days
    The full evaluation must be complete and a written report delivered to you before this deadline expires.
  • Initial ARD meeting held 30 calendar days
    Following the evaluation report, the district must convene the ARD committee to review results, determine eligibility, and — if eligible — begin developing the IEP.
· · ·

What the Evaluation Must Cover

The FIE is not a single test. It is a comprehensive assessment conducted by a multidisciplinary team — diagnosticians, school psychologists, speech-language pathologists — and must address every area of suspected disability.

  • Academic Achievement Reading decoding and fluency, written expression, and math — with data showing how far behind grade level the child is performing.
  • Cognitive Abilities Intelligence testing, working memory, processing speed, and problem-solving to understand how your child learns.
  • Speech & Language Receptive and expressive language, articulation, and pragmatic (social) communication skills.
  • Social-Emotional & Behavioral Functioning Behavior rating scales, observations, and assessment of emotional regulation and executive function.
  • Motor & Sensory Needs Occupational therapy (fine motor, handwriting, sensory processing) and physical therapy assessments if indicated.

What If Abilene ISD Refuses to Evaluate?

The district can refuse — but only in writing. They must provide a formal Prior Written Notice that explains exactly why they refused and what data they relied on to make that decision. Vague verbal responses don't meet this standard.

If they refuse, you have three paths: challenge the refusal at a meeting with the Special Education Director, file a TEA State Complaint (free, resolved within 60 days), or request a due process hearing to compel the evaluation. You also have the right at any point to obtain an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) — if you later disagree with the district's completed evaluation, you can request an IEE at the district's expense.

Frequently Asked Questions

What every Abilene ISD parent needs to know before submitting a request.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Texas?

Submit a written request to the campus principal and the district's Special Education Director. The written request — not a verbal conversation — starts the mandatory legal timeline. Keep a time-stamped copy for your records.

What is a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in Texas?

An FIE is the comprehensive assessment the school must complete to determine whether your child has a disability and needs special education services. It must cover all areas of suspected disability and be completed within 45 school days of your written consent.

Can Abilene ISD make me wait through RTI before evaluating?

No. Under IDEA and Texas law, RTI (Response to Intervention) cannot be used to delay or deny a parent's written evaluation request. If the school tells you to wait until RTI is finished, put your written request in immediately anyway.

Can Abilene ISD refuse to evaluate my child?

Yes, but only with a formal Prior Written Notice explaining why and what data supports the refusal. You have the right to challenge that refusal through a meeting with the district, a TEA complaint, or a due process hearing.
