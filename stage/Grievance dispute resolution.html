Dispute & Discipline Rights in Abilene ISD

🚨 Know This Now

Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

The 10-Day Suspension Rule in Abilene ISD

This is a crisis guide for parents whose child is facing disciplinary action or alternative placement (DAEP) in Abilene ISD. Time matters. If your child has an IEP or a 504 plan, the standard discipline rules do not fully apply to them.

Abilene ISD can suspend your child for up to 3 school days for certain infractions, and extend that to a maximum of 10 days. But if your child is suspended for MORE THAN 10 cumulative days in a single school year — even across separate short incidents — this triggers significant federal protections under IDEA.

After the 10th day of suspension, Abilene ISD MUST provide educational services to maintain your child's academic progress. Do not let them send your child home with nothing.

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

An MDR is a mandatory meeting that must occur before Abilene ISD can expel your child or place them in a Disciplinary Alternative Education Program (DAEP) for more than 10 days. The meeting must answer two specific questions:

  • Was the conduct caused by, or did it have a direct and substantial relationship to, the child's disability?
  • Was the conduct the direct result of Abilene ISD's failure to implement the IEP?

If the answer to either question is yes, the school cannot discipline your child the same way it would a non-disabled student. It must immediately conduct a Functional Behavioral Assessment (FBA) and return your child to their original placement.

How to File a Level 1 Grievance in Abilene ISD

If a campus administrator has violated your child's rights or ignored their IEP, you must file a formal grievance. Sending an upset email is not enough.

  1. Know the Deadline: Abilene ISD board policy (FNG Local) governs parent complaints. You typically have only 15 days from the incident to file — don't wait.
  2. Get the Form: Request the official Level 1 Grievance form from the campus principal or download it from the district website.
  3. Stick to the Facts: State clearly what happened, which IEP provision or policy was violated, and the exact remedy you want. Remove emotion — stick to dates, names, and specifics.
  4. Submit With a Paper Trail: Send via email and keep a time-stamped copy. This record matters if you escalate later.

If the Grievance Doesn't Work: Escalation Options

If Abilene ISD continues denying your child a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) after a Level 1 grievance, you have formal options under state and federal law:

  • TEA State Complaint — free; a state investigator reviews the district's actions and issues a decision within 60 days. Must be filed within 1 year of the violation.
  • Mediation — free through TEA; a neutral mediator helps both sides reach an agreement without a formal hearing.
  • Due Process Hearing — a formal legal proceeding before an administrative judge. File within 2 years. Legal representation is strongly recommended.
  • Office for Civil Rights (OCR) — if you believe your child was discriminated against based on disability, file with the federal OCR.

You do not lose your child's right to services while pursuing any of these options.

Frequently Asked Questions

What every Abilene ISD parent facing discipline needs to know.

When does a suspension become a Change of Placement?

When a special education student has been suspended for more than 10 cumulative school days in a single school year. This includes short, separate suspensions that add up — not just a single long removal.

Does Abilene ISD have to educate my child during a suspension?

Yes — after the 10th cumulative day of suspension in a school year, the district must continue providing educational services so your child can participate in the general curriculum and make progress toward their IEP goals.

What happens if Abilene ISD skips the MDR?

Skipping or improperly conducting an MDR is a violation of IDEA. You can file a TEA State Complaint or request a due process hearing. Courts have ordered districts to pay compensatory services and attorney's fees for MDR violations.

Do informal "early pickups" count as suspension days?

Yes. If a school removes your child from their educational setting for behavioral reasons — even informally — those hours and days count toward the 10-day threshold. Document every removal in writing.
