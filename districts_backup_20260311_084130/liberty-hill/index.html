Understanding LIBERTY HILL ISD Special Education Services

LIBERTY HILL ISD special education serves students ages 3-22 who have disabilities that significantly impact their educational performance. Under federal law (IDEA) and Texas Education Code Section 29.001, our district is required to provide a free, appropriate public education to all eligible students, regardless of disability type.

LIBERTY HILL ISD maintains special education programs across multiple campuses serving students with the following disability categories:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) – Including early intervention services for preschool-age children
  • Emotional/Behavioral Disorders – With behavior support specialists and counseling services
  • Intellectual Disabilities – Including functional academics and life skills instruction
  • Learning Disabilities – Including dyslexia and specific academic skill deficits
  • Other Health Impairments – Including ADHD, asthma, and medical conditions
  • Physical Disabilities and Orthopedic Impairments – With mobility and accessibility support
  • Speech/Language Impairments – Through licensed speech-language pathologists
  • Visual and Hearing Impairments – Including specialized equipment and accommodations

The range of services available through LIBERTY HILL ISD special education includes classroom instruction, related services, behavioral support, and transition planning to prepare students for adult life.

The ARD Process: Your Child's Path to LIBERTY HILL ISD Special Education Services

The ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting is the foundation of special education in LIBERTY HILL ISD. This is where your child's eligibility is determined, services are planned, and progress is reviewed.

What is an ARD Meeting?

An LIBERTY HILL ISD ARD meeting brings together parents, teachers, administrators, and specialists to discuss your child's needs and create an education plan. Under Texas Education Code Section 29.005, your district must hold an ARD meeting within 30 calendar days after your written request or school-initiated referral.

LIBERTY HILL ISD ARD meetings typically last 1-2 hours and cover your child's current performance, evaluation results, strengths, concerns, and proposed services. You have the right to bring an advocate, special education attorney near LIBERTY HILL, or trusted family member to support you during this process.

How to Request an ARD Meeting for LIBERTY HILL ISD Special Education Evaluation

  1. Submit a written request to your child's campus principal or the LIBERTY HILL ISD special education department expressing your concerns about your child's learning or behavior
  2. Specify what you've observed – Academic struggles, social difficulties, communication challenges, or behavioral concerns
  3. Request evaluation in specific areas if you suspect a disability (reading, math, speech, emotional/behavioral, etc.)
  4. Receive notification from LIBERTY HILL ISD within 15 calendar days regarding evaluation timeline
  5. Attend evaluation ARD meeting to review results and discuss eligibility

LIBERTY HILL ISD parents can also request evaluation if their child is struggling academically or behaviorally, even without a prior diagnosis.

LIBERTY HILL ISD IEP Services and Individualized Education Plans

Once your child qualifies for special education through LIBERTY HILL ISD, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) is developed. The LIBERTY HILL ISD IEP services outlined in this document are legally binding and must be provided regardless of placement (general education classroom, resource room, or special education classroom).

What's Included in LIBERTY HILL ISD IEP Services?

Your child's LIBERTY HILL ISD IEP services may include:

  • Special education instruction – Specialized teaching methods and curriculum modification
  • Related services – Speech therapy, occupational therapy, physical therapy, counseling, or adaptive PE
  • Behavioral support – Positive behavior intervention plans and behavior specialist consultation
  • Assistive technology – Devices or software to support communication, learning, or mobility
  • Accommodations and modifications – Changes to testing, assignments, or classroom environment
  • Supplementary aids and services – One-on-one support, assistive devices, or classroom modifications
  • Extended school year (ESY) services – Summer programming to prevent regression (if eligible)

LIBERTY HILL ISD special education staff will explain each service, its frequency, and how it addresses your child's specific needs. You should understand exactly what services your child will receive and when they will be provided.

Annual IEP Review and Progress Monitoring

LIBERTY HILL ISD special education law requires that your child's IEP be reviewed at least annually. Progress toward IEP goals should be monitored regularly (at least quarterly) and progress reports shared with parents.

Parents in LIBERTY HILL ISD receive progress updates on whether their child is making adequate progress toward IEP goals. If your child is not progressing, you can request a revised ARD meeting to adjust services, goals, or placement.

LIBERTY HILL ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

LIBERTY HILL ISD parent rights are protected under federal law (IDEA) and Texas law. Understanding these rights ensures you can advocate effectively for your child and participate as an equal partner in the special education process.

Your Core Rights as a LIBERTY HILL ISD Special Education Parent

  • Right to notice – Receive written notice in your native language before any evaluation, placement change, or service modification
  • Right to participate – Attend and actively participate in all ARD meetings regarding your child
  • Right to prior written consent – Give or withhold permission before initial evaluation and before initial IEP implementation
  • Right to access records – Review all educational records, test results, and progress reports for your child
  • Right to bring support – Include an advocate, attorney, or knowledgeable family member at meetings
  • Right to dispute – Request mediation or due process hearing if you disagree with LIBERTY HILL ISD decisions
  • Right to independent evaluation – Request (and sometimes obtain at district expense) an independent evaluation if you disagree with district findings
  • Right to compensatory services – Receive makeup services if LIBERTY HILL ISD failed to provide services in your child's IEP

LIBERTY HILL ISD parents should receive a Parents' Rights Notice at the beginning of special education involvement. If you haven't received this document, request it immediately. This document outlines all your rights in detail and includes contact information for resources.

LIBERTY HILL ISD Dyslexia Program and Reading Interventions

Texas Education Code Section 38.003 requires all districts to identify and provide intervention for students with dyslexia. LIBERTY HILL ISD dyslexia program provides scientifically-based intervention for students who struggle with reading despite adequate instruction.

Dyslexia Screening and Evaluation in LIBERTY HILL ISD

LIBERTY HILL ISD screens all kindergarten and first-grade students for dyslexia risk factors. Students showing indicators of dyslexia move into more intensive assessment. The screening process is separate from special education evaluation but may lead to special education eligibility if the student qualifies.

Not all students with dyslexia qualify for special education under LIBERTY HILL ISD. Some students receive intervention through the Response to Intervention (RTI) or dyslexia program without an IEP. Parents in LIBERTY HILL ISD should ask specifically about their child's reading intervention plan if reading struggles are a concern.

What to Expect from LIBERTY HILL ISD Dyslexia Intervention

  • Structured literacy instruction – Explicit, sequential teaching of phonemic awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension
  • Frequency and intensity – Typically 30-45 minutes daily with specialized reading interventionist
  • Progress monitoring – Regular assessment (every 2-4 weeks) to track improvement
  • Accommodation plan – Classroom accommodations while intervention builds reading skills

If your child has been identified with dyslexia in LIBERTY HILL ISD, you have the right to know the specific intervention plan, how progress will be measured, and what happens if your child doesn't respond to intervention.

Special Education Placement Options in LIBERTY HILL ISD

LIBERTY HILL ISD special education operates under the principle of least restrictive environment (LRE). Your child should be educated with non-disabled peers to the maximum extent appropriate, with services provided in the general education setting when possible.

Placement Options for LIBERTY HILL ISD Special Education Students

  • General education classroom with services – Student attends regular classroom with accommodations, modifications, and support services (speech therapy, counseling, etc.)
  • General education classroom with resource room – Student receives specialized instruction in a resource room for specific subjects while remaining in general education for other classes
  • Special education classroom (self-contained) – Student receives most instruction in a special education classroom with specialized curriculum and teaching methods
  • Special education school – LIBERTY HILL ISD or contracted program serving students with significant support needs
  • Homebound/hospital instruction – For students unable to attend school due to medical or behavioral needs
  • Virtual special education – Online instruction with direct services provided based on IEP requirements

The appropriate placement depends on your child's individual needs, not their disability category. LIBERTY HILL ISD special education staff and parents determine placement together at the ARD meeting based on what's needed to achieve IEP goals in the least restrictive setting.

Transition Planning: Preparing for Life After LIBERTY HILL ISD

Beginning at age 14 (or younger if the IEP team decides), LIBERTY HILL ISD special education includes transition planning. This focuses on preparing your student for post-secondary education, employment, independent living, and community participation.

What Transition Planning Includes

  • Academic preparation – Skills for post-secondary education or workforce entry
  • Independent living skills – Money management, self-care, community mobility, social skills
  • Community connections – Links to adult services, colleges, employers, and support agencies
  • Coordination with post-secondary agencies – Vocational Rehabilitation, Social Security, community colleges

LIBERTY HILL ISD parents should expect the transition discussion to begin well before graduation and should advocate for real-world experiences, internships, and job placement support.
