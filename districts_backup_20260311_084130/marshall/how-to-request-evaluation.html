Complete Guide to Requesting Special Education Evaluation in MARSHALL ISD

If you are a parent of a student in MARSHALL ISD and have concerns about your child's academic, social, or emotional development, you have the right to request a **special education evaluation**. This guide explains exactly how to do so and what to expect during the process.

What is a Special Education Evaluation in MARSHALL ISD?

A special education evaluation is a comprehensive process that examines whether your child qualifies for special education services. In MARSHALL ISD, these evaluations are free and mandatory when properly requested.

The special education evaluation in MARSHALL ISD includes multiple areas, such as cognitive skills, academic performance, behavior, language development, and social skills. MARSHALL ISD specialists use standardized tests, classroom observations, and gather information from parents.

This process is fundamental to determining whether your child needs an **Individualized Education Program (IEP)** or a 504 Plan in MARSHALL ISD.

Parent Rights in MARSHALL ISD for Special Education

As a parent in MARSHALL ISD, you have rights protected by federal law under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.

Your Main Rights

  • **Right to request evaluation:** You can request a special education evaluation at any time during the school year in MARSHALL ISD
  • **Free evaluation:** MARSHALL ISD must provide all tests and evaluations at no cost to your family
  • **Participation in decisions:** You must be present at all IEP meetings in MARSHALL ISD
  • **Information in your language:** MARSHALL ISD must provide all important information in Spanish
  • **Informed consent:** MARSHALL ISD cannot evaluate your child without your written permission
  • **Access to records:** You can review all of your child's records in MARSHALL ISD

Steps to Request an Evaluation in MARSHALL ISD

Step 1: Prepare the Request

Begin by gathering information about the specific concerns you have regarding your child. Note concrete examples of academic difficulties, concerning behaviors, or observed skills. This information will be valuable when you speak with MARSHALL ISD.

Consider documenting any private evaluations or pediatrician information that may be relevant. Parents in MARSHALL ISD often find it helpful to keep a record of specific dates and events.

Step 2: Officially Contact MARSHALL ISD

The request for evaluation must be **written and formally submitted** to MARSHALL ISD. You can send your request to:

  • Your child's school principal
  • MARSHALL ISD's special education department
  • The special education coordinator (Special Education Director)

In your request letter to MARSHALL ISD, include: the student's full name, current school, date, a clear description of your concerns, and your contact information.

Step 3: MARSHALL ISD Must Respond

**By law, MARSHALL ISD has 15 days** to respond to your special education evaluation request. This response must state whether MARSHALL ISD accepts or denies the request.

If MARSHALL ISD agrees to evaluate your child, you will receive an informational notice of parent rights in Spanish. MARSHALL ISD must also obtain your written consent before beginning any evaluation.

If MARSHALL ISD denies the request, they must provide a written explanation in Spanish as to why they do not believe a special education evaluation is necessary.

The FIE Evaluation Process in MARSHALL ISD

FIE stands for "Full and Individual Evaluation." An FIE evaluation in MARSHALL ISD is more comprehensive and is what is typically performed when a special education evaluation is requested.

Components of the FIE Evaluation

The FIE evaluation in MARSHALL ISD examines multiple areas of development and learning:

  • **Cognitive skills:** Intelligence, memory, problem-solving
  • **Academic performance:** Reading, writing, mathematics
  • **Language development:** Expression and comprehension of Spanish and English
  • **Motor development:** Gross and fine motor skills
  • **Social and emotional behavior:** Interactions with peers and adults
  • **Educational history:** Previous school records

FIE Evaluation Timeline

After obtaining consent, **MARSHALL ISD has 60 days to complete the FIE evaluation**. This 60-day period is a legal requirement in Texas.

During this time, you can expect MARSHALL ISD to administer formal tests, conduct classroom observations, and collect information from teachers. As a parent in MARSHALL ISD, you will likely complete questionnaires about early development and behavior at home.

Preparation for the Results Meeting

After MARSHALL ISD completes the FIE evaluation, they must meet with you to discuss the results. This is a crucial meeting where MARSHALL ISD presents its findings.

What to Expect at the Meeting

At the results meeting, MARSHALL ISD will explain test scores, observation findings, and evaluator recommendations. Parents in MARSHALL ISD can expect this meeting to last from 30 minutes to an hour.

MARSHALL ISD will determine if your child qualifies for special education under one of these categories: autism, intellectual disability, hearing impairment, visual impairment, deaf-blindness, speech or language impairment, orthopedic impairment, other health impairment, traumatic brain injury, serious emotional disturbance, or other health impairments.

Ask Questions at MARSHALL ISD

Do not hesitate to ask questions during any special education meeting at MARSHALL ISD. Some important questions include:

  • Does my child qualify for special education services?
  • What areas of strength and concern did the evaluation identify?
  • What are MARSHALL ISD's recommendations?
  • What services would be available?
  • How often would progress be reviewed?

If Your Child Qualifies for Special Education in MARSHALL ISD

If MARSHALL ISD determines that your child qualifies, they will proceed to develop an **Individualized Education Program (IEP)**. The IEP is a legal document that describes the specific services MARSHALL ISD will provide.

The IEP includes annual academic goals, specialized services (such as speech therapy, occupational therapy), curriculum modifications, and adjustments to the learning environment. As a parent in MARSHALL ISD, you are an essential part of the team that develops this document.

If Your Child Does Not Qualify in MARSHALL ISD

If MARSHALL ISD determines that your child does not qualify for special education under IDEA, they may still be entitled to protections under Section 504. A 504 plan in MARSHALL ISD provides accommodations and services for students with disabilities who do not qualify for IDEA.

Some examples include students with ADHD, anxiety, diabetes, or other conditions. If you believe your child needs support, ask MARSHALL ISD about a 504 evaluation.
