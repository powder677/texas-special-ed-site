Complete Guide to Requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in AUBREY ISD

Understanding the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in AUBREY ISD

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a thorough assessment conducted by AUBREY ISD to determine whether your child has a disability that affects their educational performance. This evaluation is the gateway to accessing special education services and developing an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

The FIE evaluation goes beyond basic screening. It includes comprehensive testing in multiple areas such as cognitive ability, academic achievement, speech and language, motor skills, social-emotional functioning, and behavioral development. For parents in AUBREY ISD, understanding this process protects your child's educational rights and ensures they receive appropriate support.

Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §29.004, AUBREY ISD must respond to your request within specific timeframes and conduct evaluations in your child's native language when applicable.

When to Request an Evaluation: Recognizing the Signs

AUBREY ISD special education services start with recognizing when your child might benefit from an evaluation. As a parent, you know your child best, and your concerns matter.

Academic Red Flags

  • Significant difficulty keeping up with grade-level academics
  • Consistent struggle with reading, writing, or math despite extra help
  • Gaps between effort and achievement that seem unusual
  • Difficulty understanding or following instructions

Developmental and Behavioral Concerns

  • Speech or language delays compared to same-age peers
  • Difficulty with social interactions or making friends
  • Frequent behavioral outbursts or emotional dysregulation
  • Fine or gross motor skill delays affecting daily activities
  • Sensory sensitivities or unusual reactions to sounds, textures, or lights

Health-Related Factors

  • Recent medical diagnosis that may affect learning
  • Vision or hearing problems
  • Chronic conditions like asthma or seizure disorders
  • Concerns about attention and focus

If you're noticing several of these signs in your child, contacting AUBREY ISD about an evaluation is a prudent next step. Early identification helps ensure your child gets support when they need it most.

AUBREY ISD Child Find: Your Starting Point

AUBREY ISD child find procedures ensure that all children with disabilities in our community are identified and evaluated. This is a federal requirement that protects your family's rights.

The AUBREY ISD child find system works by:

  1. Accepting referrals from parents, teachers, doctors, and community members
  2. Screening students who may have disabilities
  3. Providing parent notification of available services
  4. Responding to evaluation requests within legal timeframes

For families new to the AUBREY area or unsure where to begin, AUBREY ISD publishes information about special education services at your school site and on the district website. School counselors and administrators can explain these services in detail.

How to Request an FIE from AUBREY ISD: Step-by-Step Process

Parents have the right to request an AUBREY ISD FIE evaluation at any time. Here's exactly how to do it:

Step 1: Submit Your Request in Writing

Send a written request to your child's campus or directly to the AUBREY ISD Special Education Department. Your request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current school and grade level
  • Specific areas of concern (academic, behavioral, developmental, etc.)
  • Brief explanation of why you believe an evaluation is needed
  • Your contact information and preferred communication method

While a formal letter isn't required, AUBREY ISD recommends written requests because they create a documented record and ensure compliance with AUBREY ISD evaluation timelines.

Step 2: Understand the 45-Day Timeline

This is critical: Once AUBREY ISD receives your written request, the district has 45 school days to complete the AUBREY ISD 45 day timeline for evaluation. This deadline is protected by federal law.

Important details about this timeline:

  • The clock starts when AUBREY ISD receives your written request
  • 45 school days means instructional days (weekends and holidays don't count)
  • The district must complete all evaluation activities and issue a report within this timeframe
  • Extensions are only allowed with your written consent

Parents in AUBREY ISD should mark this deadline on their calendar and follow up if they don't hear from the district within 10 days of their request.

Step 3: Provide Consent for Evaluation

AUBREY ISD will send you a "Notice of Proposed Evaluation" form explaining what will be evaluated and your parental rights. You must provide written consent before testing begins.

This is your opportunity to:

  • Ask questions about what the AUBREY ISD special education evaluation will include
  • Request specific evaluators or specialists
  • Note any special circumstances (language preferences, cultural considerations, etc.)
  • Understand your right to bring an advocate or attorney

Do not feel pressured to sign immediately. Review the proposed evaluation plan carefully. If you have questions, ask AUBREY ISD to explain any testing areas you don't understand.

Step 4: Participate in the Evaluation Process

Once you consent, AUBREY ISD special education specialists will conduct various assessments. These typically include:

  • Cognitive/intelligence testing to assess thinking and reasoning abilities
  • Academic achievement testing in reading, writing, and math
  • Speech and language evaluation if communication concerns exist
  • Behavioral and emotional assessment using rating scales and observations
  • Classroom observations of your child in learning environments
  • Review of school records and previous assessments

AUBREY ISD may request that you complete rating scales about your child's behavior at home. This information is valuable because it shows how your child functions outside the school setting.

Step 5: Attend the Evaluation Results Meeting

Within the 45 day timeline, AUBREY ISD will schedule a meeting to discuss findings. At this meeting, you'll learn:

  • Whether your child qualifies for special education services under IDEA
  • Which disability category applies (if eligible)
  • Your child's strengths and areas needing support
  • Next steps for developing an IEP if your child qualifies

Bring a notebook to record key points. Ask for a copy of the complete evaluation report before or immediately after the meeting. You have the right to this information.

What If AUBREY ISD Says "No" to Your FIE Request?

If AUBREY ISD denies your evaluation request, they must provide written explanation of their reasons. This doesn't end your options.

You can:

  • Request an independent evaluation at AUBREY ISD expense if you disagree with the district's assessment
  • File a due process complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA)
  • Consult a special education attorney near AUBREY who specializes in IDEA cases
  • Work with a special ed advocate AUBREY area families trust

Parents in AUBREY ISD should not accept a denial without understanding the reasons. If you believe your child needs evaluation, you have legal rights to pursue it.

Your Rights During the AUBREY ISD Evaluation Process

Federal law protects parents throughout the AUBREY ISD FIE process. Understanding these rights empowers you:

Essential Parental Rights

  • Right to request evaluation at any time for any suspected disability
  • Right to prior written notice before the district takes action
  • Right to consent or refuse evaluation (with rare exceptions)
  • Right to copies of all evaluation reports and school records
  • Right to bring support persons to meetings (advocates, family members, attorneys)
  • Right to interpret results and request independent evaluations
  • Right to appeal decisions through due process procedures

AUBREY ISD must communicate with you in a language you understand and provide materials in accessible formats if needed. Never waive these rights, and don't hesitate to advocate for your child.

Preparing for Your AUBREY ISD Evaluation Request Meeting

Before meeting with AUBREY ISD staff to discuss your FIE evaluation request, gather documentation that supports your concerns:

  • Medical records or diagnoses relevant to learning or development
  • Previous evaluations from outside providers (doctors, therapists, etc.)
  • Report cards and teacher comments showing academic struggles
  • Examples of schoolwork demonstrating concerns
  • Documentation of interventions tried and results
  • Notes about your observations at home and in the community

Organize this information clearly so AUBREY ISD evaluators understand the full picture. Thorough documentation strengthens your AUBREY ISD child find referral and helps evaluators conduct a more comprehensive special education evaluation.
