DECATUR ISD Special Education: Complete Resource Guide for Parents

If your child has been identified as needing special education services, you've come to the right place. DECATUR ISD special education programs serve students with diverse learning needs across our district of 3,786 students. This comprehensive guide walks you through everything parents need to know about special education in DECATUR ISD, from initial referrals through ongoing support and advocacy.

As a parent in DECATUR ISD, you have rights, responsibilities, and access to powerful tools that ensure your child receives the free, appropriate public education (FAPE) they deserve under federal and Texas law.

Understanding DECATUR ISD Special Education Services

DECATUR ISD special education operates under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code Chapter 29. Our district serves students ages 3-22 with disabilities that impact educational performance, including autism spectrum disorder, developmental delays, dyslexia, emotional disturbance, hearing impairment, intellectual disabilities, orthopedic impairment, other health impairment, speech/language impairment, traumatic brain injury, and visual impairment.

Every student in DECATUR ISD with a qualifying disability receives individualized services through an IEP (Individualized Education Program). These services are provided at no cost to families and are tailored to your child's unique needs and strengths.

Who Qualifies for DECATUR ISD Special Education?

Students qualify for special education services in DECATUR ISD when they:

  • Have a documented disability under IDEA
  • Need specially designed instruction to access the general education curriculum
  • Require related services like speech therapy, occupational therapy, or counseling
  • Are between ages 3 and 22

If you suspect your child may need evaluation, you can request an assessment at any time. DECATUR ISD special education staff must respond to your request within 15 days.

DECATUR ISD ARD Meeting: Your Gateway to Services

The DECATUR ISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) is where decisions about your child's special education services are made. ARD stands for the committee—composed of parents, teachers, administrators, and specialists—that develops and reviews your child's educational plan.

What Happens at Your DECATUR ISD ARD Meeting?

During an DECATUR ISD ARD meeting, the committee:

  • Reviews evaluation results and current performance data
  • Discusses your child's strengths, needs, and goals
  • Determines eligibility for special education services
  • Develops or revises the IEP
  • Identifies placement and related services
  • Establishes annual goals and progress monitoring plans

Your voice matters in this meeting. You are a required committee member, and your input about your child's needs, strengths, and family priorities directly shapes the services your child receives.

ARD Meeting Timelines in DECATUR ISD

DECATUR ISD special education follows these critical timelines:

  • Initial evaluation: Must be completed within 60 calendar days of your written request (or within the school year if you request near year-end)
  • IEP development: Must occur within 30 calendar days of finding eligibility
  • Annual ARD meeting: Required at least once per school year
  • Reevaluation: Must occur at least every three years (or when suspected disability status changes)

DECATUR ISD families should request ARD meetings in writing and ensure adequate notice is provided.

DECATUR ISD IEP Services Explained

Your child's DECATUR ISD IEP services are documented in an Individualized Education Program—a legally binding contract between you and the school district. This document specifies exactly what services your child will receive, where they'll receive them, and how progress will be measured.

Components of DECATUR ISD IEP Services

An effective IEP in DECATUR ISD includes:

  • Present levels of performance: Accurate description of your child's current academic and functional abilities
  • Measurable annual goals: Specific, realistic targets your child will work toward
  • Special education instruction: Specialized teaching in resource rooms, self-contained classrooms, or general education settings with support
  • Related services: Speech therapy, occupational therapy, physical therapy, counseling, transportation assistance, or other necessary supports
  • Accommodations and modifications: Changes to how your child accesses the curriculum or demonstrates learning
  • Placement decision: Least restrictive environment (LRE) appropriate for your child
  • Progress monitoring: Specific methods and timelines for tracking improvement

DECATUR ISD special education services must be provided in the least restrictive environment possible, meaning your child should be educated alongside non-disabled peers to the maximum extent appropriate.

Related Services Available in DECATUR ISD

If your child needs support beyond classroom instruction, DECATUR ISD offers related services including:

  • Speech-language pathology
  • Occupational therapy
  • Physical therapy
  • School counseling
  • Behavior support services
  • Assistive technology
  • Special transportation
  • Parent training and consultation

DECATUR ISD Dyslexia Program and Reading Support

DECATUR ISD has a dedicated DECATUR ISD dyslexia program for students with dyslexia and related reading disorders. Texas Education Code requires that school districts provide research-based intervention for students with dyslexia beginning in kindergarten.

How DECATUR ISD Dyslexia Program Works

The DECATUR ISD dyslexia program provides:

  • Universal screening in kindergarten and first grade
  • Progress monitoring throughout the school year
  • Intensive, structured literacy instruction based on the science of reading
  • Accommodation strategies and assistive technology when needed
  • Parent communication and involvement
  • Teacher training in evidence-based dyslexia intervention

If your child shows reading difficulties, request evaluation through DECATUR ISD special education. A dyslexia evaluation is part of the comprehensive assessment process and costs nothing to families.

DECATUR ISD Parent Rights: Know Your Power

DECATUR ISD parent rights under IDEA and Texas law are extensive and powerful. Understanding these rights allows you to be an effective advocate for your child's education.

Key DECATUR ISD Parent Rights

As a parent of a student in DECATUR ISD special education, you have the right to:

  • Request evaluation: Initiate assessment for special education at any time
  • Participate in ARD meetings: Attend all meetings regarding your child's special education
  • Access records: Review your child's complete educational file within 10 school days of request
  • Obtain independent evaluation: Get an outside assessment at district expense if you disagree with DECATUR ISD evaluation results
  • Provide input on placement: Influence decisions about where and how your child receives services
  • Request changes to IEP: Propose modifications to goals, services, or placement
  • Due process protection: File a due process complaint if you disagree with DECATUR ISD decisions
  • Receive written notice: Get detailed explanations before any significant changes to special education services

DECATUR ISD families receive a Procedural Safeguards Notice at least once annually, explaining these rights in detail.

Communication Tips with DECATUR ISD Special Education Staff

Building positive relationships with DECATUR ISD educators strengthens advocacy for your child:

  • Request meetings in writing to ensure proper notice and attendance
  • Bring notes about your child's progress and challenges at home
  • Ask specific questions about DECATUR ISD IEP services your child is receiving
  • Request progress reports more frequently than required (monthly or every two weeks is reasonable)
  • Document all communication in writing (email is ideal)
  • Remain collaborative while asserting your rights firmly

Next Steps for DECATUR ISD Parents

If your child hasn't been evaluated yet, request an evaluation in writing to the Special Education Department at DECATUR ISD. Specify your concerns about learning, behavior, speech, or other areas. DECATUR ISD must respond within 15 days.

  • Reviewing current goals and your child's progress data

  • Writing down questions about DECATUR ISD IEP services before the meeting

Remember: DECATUR ISD special education exists to serve your child. You are your child's most important advocate. Trust your instincts, ask questions, document everything, and work collaboratively with your child's educational team to ensure success.
