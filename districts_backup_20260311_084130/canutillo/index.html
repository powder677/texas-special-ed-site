The CANUTILLO ISD IEP Process: Step-by-Step

An CANUTILLO ISD IEP (Individualized Education Program) is a legal document that outlines the special education services your child will receive. Understanding this process is crucial for parents in CANUTILLO ISD who want to actively participate in their child's education planning.

Initial Referral and Evaluation

The referral process in CANUTILLO ISD can begin with parents, teachers, or school personnel who have concerns about a child's learning or development. Once a referral is submitted, CANUTILLO ISD has 15 school days to meet with parents and discuss conducting a comprehensive evaluation.

Key steps include:

  1. Submit a written referral to your child's campus or the CANUTILLO ISD special education department
  2. Receive a written notice of proposed evaluation within 15 days
  3. Give written consent for the evaluation to proceed
  4. Allow 60 calendar days for CANUTILLO ISD to complete all evaluations

CANUTILLO ISD will assess your child in all suspected areas of disability using a variety of assessment tools and methods. This evaluation is conducted at no cost to families.

Eligibility Determination and IEP Development

Following the evaluation, CANUTILLO ISD holds an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to determine eligibility for special education services. This critical meeting brings together parents, teachers, special education staff, and other relevant professionals to review evaluation results.

During the CANUTILLO ISD ARD meeting, the team will:

  • Review all evaluation data collected
  • Determine if your child qualifies for special education under IDEA
  • If eligible, develop the initial IEP document
  • Establish present levels of academic and functional performance
  • Set measurable annual goals
  • Identify specific special education services needed
  • Determine the least restrictive environment (LRE) for your child

You have the right to bring an advocate or attorney to any CANUTILLO ISD ARD meeting. Many parents in CANUTILLO ISD find it helpful to have additional support during these important discussions.

Annual Reviews and IEP Amendments

CANUTILLO ISD is required to review your child's IEP at least once per year. During annual IEP reviews, the team assesses progress toward established goals and makes adjustments as needed. Progress monitoring data from CANUTILLO ISD teachers and service providers helps inform these decisions.

Your child's IEP in CANUTILLO ISD can also be amended at any time if new concerns arise or if your child's needs change significantly. Amendment meetings follow similar procedures to the initial ARD meeting.

CANUTILLO ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

As a parent of a student receiving special education services in CANUTILLO ISD, you have specific rights protected under federal and state law. Understanding CANUTILLO ISD parent rights ensures you can effectively advocate for your child's education.

Your Key Rights in CANUTILLO ISD

  • Right to Prior Written Notice: CANUTILLO ISD must provide written notice before proposing or refusing to make changes to your child's identification, evaluation, educational placement, or provision of FAPE
  • Right to Consent: You must give written consent for initial evaluations and initial placements in special education within CANUTILLO ISD
  • Right to Review Records: You can examine all records CANUTILLO ISD maintains about your child, including evaluations, IEPs, and progress monitoring data
  • Right to Participate in Meetings: You must be invited to and allowed to participate in all ARD meetings regarding your child in CANUTILLO ISD
  • Right to Independent Evaluation: If you disagree with CANUTILLO ISD's evaluation, you may request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at district expense
  • Right to Dispute Resolution: You can request mediation or file a due process complaint if you disagree with CANUTILLO ISD's decisions
  • Right to Advocate: You may bring an advocate, lawyer, or anyone else to support you at CANUTILLO ISD meetings

CANUTILLO ISD parents receive a comprehensive Parent Rights notice at the beginning of each school year and whenever an initial referral for evaluation is made. If you haven't received this document, request it from your campus or the CANUTILLO ISD special education office.

Accommodations and Modifications in CANUTILLO ISD

All students in CANUTILLO ISD who have an IEP receive accommodations and, when appropriate, modifications to help them access the curriculum and participate in school activities.

Understanding the Difference

Accommodations allow students to access curriculum without changing what they're learning. Examples in CANUTILLO ISD include extended time on tests, preferential seating, or assistive technology.

Modifications change the curriculum content or standards. In CANUTILLO ISD, a student might work on grade-level standards with modified expectations or focus on functional life skills instead of grade-level academics.

Your child's CANUTILLO ISD IEP team will determine which accommodations and modifications are necessary based on your child's individual needs. These should be documented clearly in the IEP and communicated to all teachers and staff who work with your child in CANUTILLO ISD.
