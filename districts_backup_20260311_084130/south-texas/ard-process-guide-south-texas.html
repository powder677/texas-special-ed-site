Step 4: The IEP Document—SOUTH TEXAS ISD Goals and Services

The Individualized Education Program (IEP) is the legal document created during your SOUTH TEXAS ISD ARD meeting that outlines everything your child will receive. This document is binding and drives all special education decisions in SOUTH TEXAS ISD.

Essential Components of SOUTH TEXAS ISD IEP goals

Your child's goals should be specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-based (SMART). For example, instead of "improve reading," a SOUTH TEXAS ISD goal might state: "Student will read grade-level texts with 85% accuracy on comprehension questions by May 31, 2024."

SOUTH TEXAS ISD IEP goals must address:

  • Academic needs (reading, math, writing, science, social studies)
  • Functional needs (social skills, behavior, communication, daily living skills)
  • Transition needs (for students ages 14+, including post-secondary goals)

Services and Accommodations in SOUTH TEXAS ISD IEPs

Your SOUTH TEXAS ISD IEP should specify exactly what services your child receives, including frequency and duration. Services might include special education instruction, speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, or behavioral support.

Accommodations are changes to how your child accesses learning—such as extended test time, use of assistive technology, modified assignments, or seating modifications. All accommodations must be written clearly in your SOUTH TEXAS ISD IEP.
