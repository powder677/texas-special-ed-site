Why Request a Special Education Evaluation in MABANK ISD?

A special education evaluation (also called FIE or Full Individual Evaluation) is the first step to determine if your child qualifies for special education services. In MABANK ISD, thousands of families use this process to ensure their children receive the support they need.

If your child has difficulties with learning, behavior, communication, or physical development, a **special evaluation in MABANK ISD** can identify the reasons and open doors to valuable services.

Signs that your child may benefit from an evaluation

  • Persistent difficulties in reading, writing, or mathematics
  • Disruptive behavior or social difficulties
  • Hearing, vision, or motor skill problems
  • Developmental delay compared to age-appropriate peers
  • Difficulties following instructions or completing tasks
  • Frequent absences or sudden decline in academic performance

Your Rights as a Parent in MABANK ISD

Texas and federal law protect your rights to request a **special education evaluation in MABANK ISD**. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, you have specific rights.

  • Right to participate: You must be invited to all meetings regarding evaluation and placement

Parental rights in **MABANK ISD** are protected by the Texas Education Code Section 29.001 et seq. It is important that you know these rights to effectively advocate for your child.

How to Request an Evaluation: Step-by-Step in MABANK ISD

The process for **requesting an evaluation in MABANK ISD** is straightforward, but you must follow the steps correctly to protect your rights. Below you will find clear and detailed instructions.

Step 1: Prepare your request in writing

The request must be **in writing** to create an official record. Use a formal letter addressed to the Special Education Department of **MABANK ISD**. The letter should include:

  • Your full name and phone number
  • Student's full name and date of birth
  • Current grade and school attended
  • Clear description of specific concerns
  • Concrete examples of observed behaviors or difficulties
  • Clear request for "special education evaluation" or "FIE"
  • Your signature and date

Step 2: Submit the request to MABANK ISD

Send your letter by certified mail (with delivery confirmation) to the Special Education Department of **MABANK ISD**. You can also deliver it in person to **MABANK ISD** administrative offices and ask for a signed receipt.

Contact information: Contact your child's school directly or the central offices of **MABANK ISD** to obtain the exact address of the Special Education Department.

Step 3: Await MABANK ISD's response

MABANK ISD has a specific timeframe to respond to your request. Within 15 calendar days after receiving your request, the district must notify you in writing whether it accepts or rejects the evaluation.

If MABANK ISD accepts the request, the district must obtain your written consent before proceeding with testing.

Step 4: Sign the consent for evaluation

Once you have agreed to participate, MABANK ISD will ask you to sign a consent form. This document authorizes the district to conduct the **special education evaluation**. Read it carefully before signing.

Step 5: Participate in the evaluation

MABANK ISD evaluators may use standardized tests, classroom observations, work samples, and teacher input. The process typically takes 2 to 3 months. You may observe the evaluations or request copies of the results.

What Happens After the Evaluation in MABANK ISD

Once MABANK ISD completes the **special education evaluation**, an important meeting called the Placement Team Meeting (IEP meeting) will be held.

The PPT/IEP meeting in MABANK ISD

At this meeting, MABANK ISD will present the results and determine if your child is eligible for special education services. If your child qualifies, the team will develop an **Individualized Education Program (IEP)**.

You have the right to bring a special education attorney or advocate to this meeting if you wish. Some parents in the **MABANK** area find it helpful to have professional support, especially if they have complex questions.

If MABANK ISD determines your child is NOT eligible

If MABANK ISD determines that your child does not qualify for special education under IDEA, they may still have rights under Section 504. Ask the **MABANK ISD** team about a Section 504 evaluation if appropriate.

If MABANK ISD determines your child IS eligible

An **IEP** will be developed detailing:

  • The specific educational needs identified
  • Academic and behavioral goals for the school year
  • Special education services your child will receive
  • Where education will be received (special classroom, regular classroom with support, etc.)
  • How progress will be measured
