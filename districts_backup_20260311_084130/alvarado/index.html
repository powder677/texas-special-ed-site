Understanding Special Education in ALVARADO ISD

ALVARADO ISD special education serves students ages 3-21 who qualify for services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act. Our district serves approximately 3,788 students, with specialized programs designed to meet diverse learning needs across elementary, middle, and high school campuses.

The special education program in ALVARADO ISD is built on the principle that every student deserves access to a free, appropriate public education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment (LRE). This means your child will be educated alongside non-disabled peers to the maximum extent appropriate, with specialized services provided as needed.

ALVARADO ISD maintains multiple service delivery models, including resource rooms, self-contained classrooms, co-teaching arrangements, and inclusive general education settings with support services. The specific model depends entirely on your child's individualized needs as documented in their IEP.

The ARD Process and IEP Development

An ALVARADO ISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal meeting) is where decisions about your child's special education program are made. This is arguably the most important meeting you'll attend as a parent in ALVARADO ISD.

What Happens at an ALVARADO ISD ARD Meeting

During an ALVARADO ISD ARD meeting, a team of educators and parents reviews your child's evaluation results, discusses their strengths and needs, and develops or revises the Individualized Education Program (IEP). The IEP is a legally binding document that outlines:

  • Present levels of academic and functional performance
  • Annual measurable goals and objectives
  • Specific ALVARADO ISD IEP services your child will receive
  • Accommodations and modifications to the curriculum
  • Placement in the least restrictive environment
  • Progress monitoring and reporting procedures

Timeline for ALVARADO ISD ARD Meetings

Initial evaluations must be completed within 60 calendar days of receiving parental consent. Once a child qualifies for services, ALVARADO ISD must hold an ALVARADO ISD ARD meeting at least once per year to review and revise the IEP. Additionally, the district must conduct a triennial (every 3 years) comprehensive reevaluation.

You have the right to request an ALVARADO ISD ARD meeting at any time if you believe your child's IEP needs to be modified based on new information or changes in your child's needs.

ALVARADO ISD IEP Services and Program Options

ALVARADO ISD IEP services are customized based on each student's identified disability and individual needs. Services are designed to address academic, social, emotional, and functional skill development.

Core IEP Services Available in ALVARADO ISD

Families in ALVARADO ISD may access the following special education services:

  • Speech-Language Pathology: For students with communication disorders or language delays
  • Occupational Therapy: For students needing fine motor, self-care, or sensory support
  • Physical Therapy: For students with gross motor or mobility needs
  • Specialized Academic Instruction: Direct teaching in core academic areas
  • Behavioral Support Services: Including positive behavioral interventions and supports (PBIS)
  • Counseling Services: Social-emotional support and mental health services
  • Assistive Technology: Devices and software to enhance communication and learning
  • Related Services: Additional supports needed to benefit from special education

Understanding Your Parent Rights in ALVARADO ISD

ALVARADO ISD parent rights are substantial and legally protected. Understanding these rights empowers you to be an effective advocate for your child and ensures your voice is heard in all decisions affecting their education.

Key ALVARADO ISD Parent Rights

As a parent in ALVARADO ISD, you have the right to:

  • Notice and Consent: Receive written notice before the district initiates, changes, or refuses to initiate special education evaluation or services
  • Participate in IEP Decisions: Attend all ARD meetings and have equal voice in creating your child's IEP
  • Independent Evaluation: Obtain an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at public expense if you disagree with the district's evaluation
  • Access Records: Review and obtain copies of all educational records and assessment results
  • Prior Written Notice: Receive explanations of all proposed actions, alternatives considered, and reasons for decisions
  • Dispute Resolution: File a due process complaint or request mediation if you disagree with ALVARADO ISD's actions

Your ALVARADO ISD parent rights are documented in a detailed Parent Rights notice that ALVARADO ISD must provide at least annually. Make sure you receive and carefully review this document.

Communication and Documentation

Always request that ALVARADO ISD provide important information in writing. Keep organized records of all communications, meeting notes, test scores, and correspondence related to your child's special education services in ALVARADO ISD. This documentation becomes invaluable if disputes arise.

Special Education Evaluation and Eligibility in ALVARADO ISD

When you suspect your child needs special education services in ALVARADO ISD, the first step is requesting a comprehensive evaluation. ALVARADO ISD must evaluate students suspected of having disabilities in all areas of suspected disability.

How to Request an Evaluation for ALVARADO ISD Special Education

To initiate special education services in ALVARADO ISD, submit a written request for evaluation to your child's campus principal or the Special Education Department. The request can be as simple as a letter stating you believe your child has a disability affecting learning and requesting evaluation.

Once ALVARADO ISD receives your written request, the district has 15 calendar days to propose evaluation procedures and obtain your consent. The actual evaluation must be completed within 60 calendar days. During this time, the district's evaluation team will assess your child across multiple domains using standardized tests, classroom observations, rating scales, and other assessment methods.

Qualifying Disabilities Under IDEA

To qualify for special education in ALVARADO ISD, your child must have one of thirteen disability categories under IDEA and require special education services. These categories include:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Hearing Impairment or Deafness
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Specific Learning Disability (including dyslexia)
  • Speech-Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Visual Impairment
  • Blind
  • Deaf-Blind
