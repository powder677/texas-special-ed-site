Understanding Special Education in WAXAHACHIE ISD

WAXAHACHIE ISD special education serves students ages 3-22 who have disabilities that affect their ability to learn. As a parent in WAXAHACHIE ISD, you have the right to a free, appropriate public education (FAPE) for your child, guaranteed under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.

WAXAHACHIE ISD enrolls over 11,000 students across the district, with special education services available at every campus level—from elementary through high school. The district's special education department coordinates identification, evaluation, and service delivery to ensure every child receives individualized support.

Understanding how WAXAHACHIE ISD special education operates is the first step toward getting your child the support they deserve. Whether your child is struggling with reading, mathematics, speech and language, or behavioral challenges, WAXAHACHIE ISD has processes and professionals ready to help.

The Referral and Evaluation Process in WAXAHACHIE ISD

How to Request an Evaluation

Any parent, teacher, or school staff member can request a referral for evaluation in WAXAHACHIE ISD. If you believe your child has a disability, contact your child's campus counselor, teacher, or the special education department directly. Put your request in writing and keep a copy for your records.

Once WAXAHACHIE ISD receives your referral, the district has 15 school days to notify you in writing and request your consent to evaluate. This notification must explain the evaluation process, your parental rights, and the types of assessments that will be conducted.

The Evaluation Timeline

After you sign the consent form, WAXAHACHIE ISD has 60 calendar days to complete the evaluation and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. During this time, certified specialists in WAXAHACHIE ISD assess your child across multiple areas: academics, speech and language, motor skills, social-emotional development, and behavioral functioning.

Evaluations in WAXAHACHIE ISD include:

  • Cognitive assessments measuring reasoning and processing abilities
  • Achievement testing in reading, math, and written expression
  • Speech and language evaluation if communication is a concern
  • Behavioral and social-emotional screening
  • Classroom observations and teacher input
  • Review of educational history and previous assessments

WAXAHACHIE ISD ARD Meetings and IEP Services

What Happens at Your ARD Meeting

Your WAXAHACHIE ISD ARD meeting brings together parents, teachers, special education staff, and your child (when appropriate) to review evaluation results and plan services. This is where WAXAHACHIE ISD IEP services are formally developed and documented in your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP).

At your ARD meeting, the team will determine:

  • Whether your child qualifies for special education under IDEA
  • Your child's specific disability category (if eligible)
  • Present levels of academic and functional performance
  • Annual IEP goals in areas of need
  • Special education services and accommodations
  • Related services like speech therapy, counseling, or occupational therapy
  • Least restrictive environment (LRE) placement

Come prepared to your WAXAHACHIE ISD ARD meeting. Bring any outside evaluations, medical records, or documentation of your child's needs. You have the right to bring an advocate or attorney if desired.

WAXAHACHIE ISD IEP Services and Support Options

WAXAHACHIE ISD IEP services are customized based on your child's disability and individual needs. Services may include:

  • Inclusive education support in general education classrooms with specialized instruction
  • Speech and language therapy for communication disorders
  • Occupational therapy for fine motor and daily living skills
  • Physical therapy for gross motor and mobility needs
  • Counseling and mental health services
  • Behavior support plans and positive behavioral interventions
  • Assistive technology and accommodations
  • Transition services for students 14 and older preparing for post-secondary life

WAXAHACHIE ISD special education staff work collaboratively to ensure your child receives services in the least restrictive environment—meaning your child is educated with non-disabled peers to the maximum extent appropriate.

WAXAHACHIE ISD Parent Rights and Due Process

Understanding Your Rights as a Parent

WAXAHACHIE ISD parent rights are protected by federal and state law. Every parent of a student receiving special education services in WAXAHACHIE ISD has the right to:

  • Notice and consent before evaluation, placement, or service changes
  • Access to records—you can review your child's educational file any time
  • Participate in all ARD meetings as an equal team member
  • Independent evaluation if you disagree with WAXAHACHIE ISD's assessment
  • Prior written notice explaining proposed changes to services
  • Procedural safeguards including due process and mediation
  • Request amendments to your child's records if you believe information is inaccurate
  • Confidentiality of your child's educational and medical information

WAXAHACHIE ISD must provide WAXAHACHIE ISD parent rights documentation annually. If you haven't received this document, request it from your campus special education coordinator or the district's special education department.

Dispute Resolution: Mediation and Due Process

If you disagree with decisions made in WAXAHACHIE ISD regarding your child's identification, evaluation, services, or placement, you have several options. WAXAHACHIE ISD special education disputes can be resolved through:

  • Informal conference with teachers and administrators
  • Mediation—a free, confidential process facilitated by a neutral mediator
  • Due process hearing—a formal legal proceeding before a hearing officer

Many disagreements in WAXAHACHIE ISD can be resolved through open communication and mediation before escalating to a formal due process hearing. However, you have the right to pursue any option that best serves your family.
