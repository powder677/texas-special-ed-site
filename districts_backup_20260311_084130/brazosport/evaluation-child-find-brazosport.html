Complete Guide to Requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in BRAZOSPORT ISD

Understanding What a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) Actually Is

An FIE is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine whether your child has a disability that impacts their education and whether they qualify for special education services under federal law (IDEA – Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) and Texas Education Code.

In BRAZOSPORT ISD, an FIE is not a simple screening or classroom observation. Instead, it's a thorough, multi-faceted evaluation that typically includes:

  • Cognitive (IQ) testing
  • Academic achievement assessments
  • Speech and language evaluations
  • Occupational therapy assessments
  • Psychological evaluations
  • Social, emotional, and behavioral assessments
  • Review of medical and developmental history
  • Classroom observations and teacher input

The evaluation team at BRAZOSPORT ISD gathers this information from multiple sources to create a complete picture of your child's strengths, needs, and educational performance.

Your Right to Request an Evaluation: BRAZOSPORT ISD Child Find

BRAZOSPORT ISD child find procedures ensure that all children with disabilities are identified, located, and evaluated. As a parent, you have the fundamental right to request an evaluation for your child at any time, regardless of grade level or current academic standing.

Who Can Request an Evaluation?

Parents and guardians in BRAZOSPORT ISD can submit a written evaluation request. Additionally, teachers, school counselors, or other school personnel can refer your child for evaluation, but the district cannot proceed without your consent.

When Should You Request an Evaluation?

Consider requesting a BRAZOSPORT special education evaluation if your child shows signs of:

  • Significant difficulties in reading, writing, or math
  • Speech or language delays
  • Behavior that interferes with learning
  • Difficulty paying attention or staying focused
  • Physical or motor skill concerns
  • Emotional or social challenges affecting school performance
  • Suspected hearing or vision problems impacting learning
  • Development significantly delayed compared to peers

You don't need a diagnosis from a doctor to request an evaluation in BRAZOSPORT ISD. Your concerns about your child's learning or development are sufficient grounds for requesting a BRAZOSPORT ISD evaluation request.

Step-by-Step Process for Requesting a BRAZOSPORT ISD FIE Evaluation

Step 1: Submit Your Written Request

Contact your child's school or the BRAZOSPORT ISD Special Education Department with a written request for evaluation. While a verbal request may initiate the process, sending a written request creates an official record and ensures compliance with the BRAZOSPORT ISD 45 day timeline.

Your written request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current grade level and school
  • Specific concerns about your child's learning or development
  • Any relevant medical, developmental, or educational history
  • Your preferred contact information

Address your request to the school principal, special education coordinator, or the BRAZOSPORT ISD Special Education Department. Keep a copy for your records.

Step 2: Consent Conference

Within a reasonable timeframe, BRAZOSPORT ISD staff will contact you to schedule a consent conference. During this meeting, the school will discuss:

  • Your concerns and reasons for requesting evaluation
  • What areas will be evaluated
  • Types of assessments that will be conducted
  • The evaluation timeline
  • Your rights throughout the process

At the conclusion of this meeting, you'll sign consent forms authorizing BRAZOSPORT ISD to proceed with the evaluation.

Step 3: Evaluation Begins

Once you've signed consent, the evaluation clock starts. BRAZOSPORT ISD will assemble a multidisciplinary evaluation team that may include special education teachers, school psychologists, speech pathologists, occupational therapists, and general education teachers.

The team will conduct various assessments, classroom observations, and gather information from you as the parent. Your input is critical—the evaluation team will ask detailed questions about your child's development, medical history, and how they function at home.

Understanding the Critical BRAZOSPORT ISD 45 Day Timeline

Federal law and Texas Education Code require that BRAZOSPORT ISD complete the evaluation within 45 calendar days from the date you provide written consent. This is a hard deadline, and the district must meet it.

What Happens During the 45 Days?

During this period, the BRAZOSPORT ISD evaluation team will:

  1. Administer all necessary assessments and tests
  2. Conduct classroom observations
  3. Review cumulative files and medical records
  4. Compile and analyze all evaluation data
  5. Prepare a comprehensive evaluation report
  6. Schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss findings

The BRAZOSPORT ISD 45 day timeline is non-negotiable. If the district cannot meet this deadline, they must provide written explanation and notify you of the delay.

What If the Deadline Is Missed?

If BRAZOSPORT ISD fails to complete your child's evaluation within 45 days, you have legal recourse. Document the delay and contact the district's special education director. If the issue isn't resolved, you may file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA) or consult with a special education attorney near BRAZOSPORT.

The ARD Meeting: Discussing Your BRAZOSPORT ISD FIE Results

Upon completing the evaluation, BRAZOSPORT ISD will schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting—also called an IEP meeting. This meeting must occur within the 45-day window and is where results are shared and decisions are made.

Who Attends the ARD Meeting?

Your child's ARD meeting in BRAZOSPORT ISD will include:

  • You (the parent/guardian)
  • Your child's general education teacher(s)
  • Special education teacher or coordinator
  • School psychologist or evaluator
  • District representative (usually administrator)
  • Your child (when appropriate)
  • Any specialists who conducted evaluations (speech, OT, etc.)

What to Expect at the Meeting

The evaluation team will present detailed findings from the BRAZOSPORT ISD FIE evaluation, including test scores, observations, and analysis. They'll explain whether your child qualifies for special education services under one of 14 disability categories.

If your child qualifies, the team will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) outlining services, accommodations, goals, and placement. If your child doesn't qualify, the team must explain why and may discuss other support options.

Your Rights During the ARD

Remember that the ARD meeting is a collaborative process. You have the right to:

  • Ask questions about any part of the evaluation or findings
  • Request clarification of technical language or test scores
  • Bring your own evaluations or expert opinions
  • Request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at district expense if you disagree with results
  • Request a specific service or placement for your child
  • Take time to review documents before signing
