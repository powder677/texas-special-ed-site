Understanding What a Full Individual Evaluation Means for DESOTO ISD Families

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment conducted by DESOTO ISD to determine whether your child has a disability and, if so, what special education services they may need. This evaluation goes beyond typical classroom assessments and includes cognitive testing, academic achievement measures, social-emotional evaluations, and developmental history.

Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code Section 29.004, DESOTO ISD is required to conduct a free and comprehensive evaluation at no cost to parents. The evaluation helps identify your child's strengths, challenges, and specific educational needs to develop an appropriate Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Parents in DESOTO ISD should know that requesting an evaluation is a protected right. You don't need to wait for your child's teacher to suggest one—you can initiate this process yourself at any time during the school year.

How to Request a DESOTO ISD Special Education Evaluation

Step 1: Submit Your Written Request

The most important thing to know about requesting a DESOTO ISD special education evaluation is that your request must be in writing. A casual conversation with a teacher or principal won't start the formal evaluation timeline. Here's how to properly submit your request:

  • Write a letter or email addressed to the Special Education Director or your child's school principal
  • Clearly state that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation for your child
  • Include specific concerns about your child's academic performance, behavior, communication, or development
  • Keep a copy for your records
  • Request written confirmation of receipt from DESOTO ISD

When contacting DESOTO ISD directly, you can submit your request to your child's campus or the district's special education department. Email is recommended because it creates a clear paper trail and timestamped evidence of your request.

Step 2: DESOTO ISD Child Find Process

DESOTO ISD operates a Child Find system designed to identify students who may need special education services. The DESOTO ISD child find process means the district must actively search for and identify children with disabilities, regardless of whether parents request an evaluation.

Once you submit your evaluation request, DESOTO ISD staff should acknowledge receipt within a few days. The special education coordinator will typically contact you to discuss your concerns and gather background information about your child's developmental and medical history.

Understanding the DESOTO ISD 45 Day Timeline

One of the most critical aspects of a DESOTO ISD 45 day timeline is knowing exactly what it means for your family. Once DESOTO ISD receives your written evaluation request, the district has 45 calendar days to complete the comprehensive evaluation and provide you with a written report of findings.

What the 45-Day Clock Includes

The 45 day evaluation timeline in DESOTO ISD includes:

  • Parental consent for testing (you must sign permission before testing begins)
  • All comprehensive assessments and testing
  • Review of school records and prior evaluations
  • Teacher input and classroom observations
  • Compilation of all evaluation data into a formal report
  • Scheduling and conducting an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting

At the end of the DESOTO ISD 45 day timeline, you should receive a detailed evaluation report explaining the results and whether your child qualifies for special education services under one of 13 disability categories recognized by Texas and federal law.

What If DESOTO ISD Needs More Time?

There are specific circumstances where DESOTO ISD may request an extension beyond the standard 45 days. These include situations where your child has been absent frequently, specialized testing requires additional time, or obtaining parental consent is delayed. Any extension must be documented in writing and agreed upon by parents.

Key Information About DESOTO ISD Evaluation Request Procedures

Parental Consent is Required

Before any DESOTO ISD evaluation request moves forward, DESOTO ISD must provide you with a Prior Written Notice explaining the evaluation plan. You will receive a Parental Consent for Evaluation form that you must sign and return. Testing cannot legally begin until you provide written consent.

What to Expect During the Evaluation Process

The comprehensive evaluation for DESOTO ISD students typically includes:

  • Cognitive assessment: Testing your child's thinking, reasoning, and learning abilities
  • Academic achievement testing: Measuring reading, math, writing, and other academic skills
  • Social-emotional evaluation: Assessing behavior, feelings, and social interactions
  • Speech and language screening: If speech or language concerns exist
  • Motor skills assessment: Evaluating gross and fine motor development if relevant
  • Classroom observations: DESOTO ISD staff observing your child in natural school settings
  • Teacher and parent input: Questionnaires and interviews about your child's functioning across settings

You Have Rights During the Evaluation

Parents of students in DESOTO ISD have important rights throughout the evaluation process:

  • You may participate in all evaluation meetings
  • You can request that specific people be involved in the evaluation process
  • You can provide independent evaluations or medical reports for DESOTO ISD to consider
  • You can ask questions about testing procedures and results
  • You can request an independent educational evaluation at DESOTO ISD's expense if you disagree with their findings

DESOTO Special Education Eligibility and Next Steps

After the DESOTO special education evaluation is complete, DESOTO ISD will hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to discuss results and determine eligibility. This meeting brings together parents, teachers, administrators, and specialists to review the evaluation data.

In DESOTO ISD, your child may be found eligible for special education services under these 13 disability categories:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Deaf-Blindness
  • Deafness
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Specific Learning Disability
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Visual Impairment

If your child qualifies, DESOTO ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) outlining specific services, accommodations, and goals. If your child does not qualify under IDEA, they may still be eligible for accommodations under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.
