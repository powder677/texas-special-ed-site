Understanding the FIE: Your First Step

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment that determines whether your child qualifies for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). For families in KERRVILLE ISD, this evaluation is the gateway to specialized support and accommodations.

The KERRVILLE ISD special education evaluation process involves multiple assessments across different areas—academic performance, cognitive abilities, social-emotional functioning, and physical development. These evaluations help educators understand your child's strengths and challenges.

Many parents in KERRVILLE ISD don't realize that child find procedures require schools to actively identify students who may need special education. You don't have to wait for the school to suggest an evaluation; you can request one yourself.

When to Request a KERRVILLE ISD FIE Evaluation

Signs Your Child May Need an Evaluation

Parents in KERRVILLE ISD should consider requesting an evaluation if their child shows:

  • Significant difficulty keeping up academically compared to peers
  • Behavior challenges that interfere with learning or social relationships
  • Speech, language, or communication delays
  • Physical or motor skill concerns
  • Difficulty following directions or understanding classroom expectations
  • Social withdrawal or anxiety that impacts school participation
  • Attention or focus problems that persist despite classroom interventions

Early Intervention Attempts

Before requesting a KERRVILLE ISD FIE evaluation, most schools expect teachers to have implemented Response to Intervention (RTI) or other support strategies. This doesn't mean you must wait—you can always request an evaluation—but understanding what interventions have been tried helps document your child's needs.

However, KERRVILLE ISD cannot deny an evaluation request simply because interventions haven't been attempted. Texas Education Code Section 504.301 protects your right to request an evaluation at any time.

Your Legal Rights Under Texas Special Education Law

Understanding IDEA and Texas Requirements

KERRVILLE ISD must follow federal IDEA regulations and Texas Education Code guidelines for all special education evaluations. As a parent in KERRVILLE ISD, you have specific legal protections.

Key rights for KERRVILLE ISD parents include:

  • Right to request an evaluation in writing at any time
  • Right to participate in all evaluation planning and decisions
  • Right to interpret results with qualified professionals
  • Right to independent evaluation at district expense if you disagree with KERRVILLE ISD's assessment
  • Right to written notice of all evaluation procedures and findings
  • Right to access all records related to your child's evaluation

How to Request a KERRVILLE ISD Evaluation Request

Step-by-Step Process

Step 1: Make Your Request in Writing

Contact your child's campus or the KERRVILLE ISD Special Education Department. Submit your evaluation request in writing—this is crucial for creating an official record. A simple letter requesting a KERRVILLE ISD FIE evaluation is sufficient. Include your child's name, grade, campus, and a brief explanation of your concerns.

Step 2: Schedule a Meeting

Within 15 calendar days, KERRVILLE ISD must schedule a meeting with you to discuss your request. This pre-evaluation conference allows the school to gather information about your child's history, medical background, and behaviors at home and school.

Step 3: Obtain Written Consent

Before beginning any assessment, KERRVILLE ISD must provide written notice explaining evaluation procedures and obtain your signed consent. You'll receive information about what will be tested, who will conduct assessments, and timeline expectations.

Step 4: Complete the Comprehensive Evaluation

KERRVILLE ISD must assess your child in all areas of suspected disability. The KERRVILLE ISD special education evaluation typically includes cognitive testing, achievement testing, behavioral observation, and input from teachers and family members.

The KERRVILLE ISD 45-Day Timeline

One of the most important timelines for KERRVILLE ISD parents to understand is the KERRVILLE ISD 45 day timeline. From the date you provide written consent, KERRVILLE ISD has 45 calendar days to complete all evaluations and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss results.

This timeline is mandated by federal law and applies regardless of holidays or school breaks. If KERRVILLE ISD cannot meet this deadline, they must provide written explanation and establish a new timeline with your agreement.

Mark your calendar: 45 days from consent = ARD meeting date. This helps you hold KERRVILLE ISD accountable to the legal timeline.

What Happens During the ARD Meeting

Understanding the Evaluation Results Conference

After completing the KERRVILLE ISD FIE evaluation, your child's ARD committee meets to review findings. This committee includes you, teachers, the school psychologist, and other relevant professionals.

During your ARD meeting with KERRVILLE ISD, you'll:

  • Review all evaluation results and data
  • Discuss whether your child meets eligibility criteria
  • Learn about your child's strengths and areas of need
  • If eligible, develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP)
  • Ask questions and request clarification about findings

Eligibility Determination

KERRVILLE ISD determines eligibility based on 13 disability categories under IDEA. Your child must meet criteria for one category AND require special education services. Common categories include learning disabilities, emotional/behavioral disorders, autism, and speech impairments.

If KERRVILLE ISD determines your child is ineligible, they must explain their decision in writing. You have the right to disagree and can pursue due process options.

If You Disagree with KERRVILLE ISD Evaluation Results

Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE)

Parents in KERRVILLE ISD have the right to obtain an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at district expense if they disagree with KERRVILLE ISD's evaluation. An independent evaluator (not employed by the school) conducts this assessment.

To request an IEE, submit a written request to KERRVILLE ISD within a specific timeframe after receiving evaluation results. The district must either agree to fund the evaluation or initiate due process to show their evaluation was appropriate.

Yes. Even if your child is in a general education setting, you can request a KERRVILLE ISD special education evaluation at any time if you have concerns about their learning or development.

What if KERRVILLE ISD misses the 45-day deadline?

If KERRVILLE ISD cannot meet the 45 day timeline, they must document the reason and establish a new date. Repeated delays may warrant a complaint to the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

Can KERRVILLE ISD refuse my evaluation request?

No. KERRVILLE ISD cannot deny a parent's request for evaluation. They must proceed with assessing your child or provide written documentation of why they believe evaluation is unnecessary—which is rarely acceptable under IDEA.
