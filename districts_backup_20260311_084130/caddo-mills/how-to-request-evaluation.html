Why Request a Special Evaluation in CADDO MILLS ISD?

A special education evaluation is the first step to determine if your child has a disability that affects their learning. Teachers and specialists in CADDO MILLS ISD special evaluation use standardized tests and observations to identify your child's unique needs.

Some signs that your child might benefit from an evaluation include:

  • Persistent difficulties with reading, writing, or mathematics
  • Behavioral problems or difficulty following instructions
  • Speech or language delays
  • Social or emotional difficulties
  • Lack of academic progress despite regular interventions
  • Concerns about hearing or vision

In CADDO MILLS ISD, with a student population of 3,073 students, the district is committed to identifying and supporting all students who need special education.

Parent Rights in CADDO MILLS ISD

Your Fundamental Rights

Parents in CADDO MILLS ISD parent rights have legal protections under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act. These rights include:

  • Right to request evaluation: You can request an evaluation at any time if you believe your child has a disability
  • Right to participation: You must participate in all decisions regarding your child's evaluation and services
  • Right to confidentiality: All information about your child is protected and confidential
  • Right to appeal: If you do not agree with the results, you can appeal CADDO MILLS ISD's decision
  • Right to interpretation: CADDO MILLS ISD must provide you with information in Spanish if it is your preferred language

Legal Protections in Texas

Texas Education Code, Section 29.004, requires that CADDO MILLS ISD identify, evaluate, and provide special education services to all eligible students. The district cannot deny an evaluation based on race, ethnic origin, language, or socioeconomic status.

Steps to Request an Evaluation in CADDO MILLS ISD

Step 1: Contact the School

The first step is to contact your child's school in CADDO MILLS ISD. You can speak with:

  • The school counselor
  • The school principal
  • The special education coordinator for CADDO MILLS ISD

Explain your specific concerns about your child's academic or behavioral performance. It is helpful to provide concrete examples of difficulties you have observed at home or at school.

Step 2: Formal Written Request

To ensure your request is documented, submit a formal request for a special evaluation to CADDO MILLS ISD in writing. You can deliver this request in person or send it via certified email.

Your request should include:

  • Name of the school they attend
  • Clear description of your concerns

Important tip: Keep a copy of your request and note the date you submitted it to CADDO MILLS ISD. If sending by email, request a read receipt.

Step 3: Initial Evaluation by CADDO MILLS ISD

After receiving your request, CADDO MILLS ISD has a timeframe to respond. According to Texas law, the district must make decisions about whether to proceed with the evaluation without undue delay.

The CADDO MILLS ISD special education team will review:

  • Information on interventions already attempted
  • Medical and developmental history information
  • Observations from teachers and other professionals

Step 4: Consent for Evaluation

Once CADDO MILLS ISD special evaluation determines that an evaluation should be conducted, the district will send you a consent form. This form explains exactly what will be evaluated and what tests will be used.

Key points about consent:

The CADDO MILLS ISD ARD meeting is a gathering where the results of the special evaluation are discussed. In this meeting, it is determined if your child is eligible for special education services and what services they will receive.
