Complete Guide to Requesting Special Education Evaluation in BIG SPRING ISD

If your child is experiencing academic or behavioral difficulties, you have the legal right to request a special education evaluation in BIG SPRING ISD. This guide explains how to navigate the process step-by-step, ensuring your child receives the services they need.

Why Request an Evaluation in BIG SPRING ISD?

A special education evaluation is the first step in determining if your child qualifies for services protected under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). In BIG SPRING ISD, with an enrollment of 3,376 students, our district is dedicated to identifying and supporting students who need specialized services.

A child may benefit from an evaluation if they exhibit:

  • Difficulties learning to read, write, or do math
  • Attention or concentration problems in class
  • Speech or language difficulties
  • Behavior that interferes with learning
  • Delays in physical or social development
  • Sensory disabilities (hearing or vision)
  • Brain injuries or medical conditions affecting learning

Parents in BIG SPRING ISD have the fundamental right to request a formal evaluation at any time during their child's educational career.

Understand Your Rights as a Parent in BIG SPRING ISD

Texas law and federal regulations guarantee specific rights to parents of students in BIG SPRING ISD. It is important that you understand these rights before beginning the evaluation process.

Your Key Rights in BIG SPRING ISD

  • Right to request evaluation: You can request in writing that BIG SPRING ISD evaluate your child at no cost
  • Right to notification: BIG SPRING ISD must notify you of all actions related to the special education evaluation
  • Right to participation: You have the right to participate in all meetings about the special education evaluation and services
  • Right to interpreter: BIG SPRING ISD must provide an interpreter in your language during all meetings
  • Right to records: You can review all educational records of your child maintained by BIG SPRING ISD
  • Right to advocate: You can bring a special education attorney or advocate to any meeting in BIG SPRING ISD

These rights are protected under the Texas Education Code (TEC §29.001 et seq.) and the federal Individuals with Disabilities Education Act.

Step 1: Submit a Formal Evaluation Request to BIG SPRING ISD

The first step to obtaining a special education evaluation in BIG SPRING ISD is to submit a formal written request. This request should be addressed to the director of special education or your child's school principal.

How to Submit Your Request to BIG SPRING ISD

Send a formal letter that includes:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current school grade
  • School attended in BIG SPRING ISD
  • Specific description of your academic or behavioral concerns
  • Concrete examples of difficulties you have observed
  • Your name, phone number, and email address
  • Date of the request

Important recommendation: Send your request via certified mail with a return receipt. This documents that BIG SPRING ISD received your request and establishes the start date for legal timelines.

You can also deliver your request in person to BIG SPRING ISD and ask for a signed receipt confirming its reception. Keep a copy of everything you send.

To Whom Should You Address Your Request?

In BIG SPRING ISD, you can address your request to the:

  • Director of Special Education for BIG SPRING ISD
  • Your child's elementary school principal
  • Evaluations Coordinator for BIG SPRING ISD

If you do not know the specific names, call the central office of BIG SPRING ISD and ask for the contact information of the special education department.

Step 2: Understand the BIG SPRING ISD Evaluation Timeline

Once BIG SPRING ISD receives your evaluation request, there are legal time limits that the district must comply with. Understanding these deadlines helps you hold BIG SPRING ISD accountable.

Critical Deadlines in BIG SPRING ISD

  • Within 15 school days: BIG SPRING ISD must respond to your request in writing, explaining whether they accept or reject the evaluation
  • If rejected: BIG SPRING ISD must explain the specific reasons for the rejection in writing
  • Within 60 calendar days: If accepted, BIG SPRING ISD must complete the full evaluation from the date of parental consent
  • Within 30 days: BIG SPRING ISD must schedule a team meeting to discuss the results

If BIG SPRING ISD rejects your request, you have the right to request mediation or an independent hearing to challenge the decision.

Step 3: Provide Informed Consent to BIG SPRING ISD

Before BIG SPRING ISD can evaluate your child, you must sign an informed consent form. This document authorizes the district to conduct the evaluation and explains how the results will be used.

What You Should Know About Consent

BIG SPRING ISD must provide you with a document in Spanish that explains:

  • What tests will be administered to your child
  • Who will conduct the evaluations
  • Where and when the evaluations will occur
  • How the results will be used
  • Your rights during the process
  • The right to obtain an independent evaluation

You may deny consent or withdraw your consent at any time. If you refuse consent, BIG SPRING ISD cannot evaluate your child, but the district may request a hearing to challenge your refusal.

Step 4: Participate in the Evaluations Ordered by BIG SPRING ISD

During the evaluation process, your child will undergo various formal and informal tests. BIG SPRING ISD must use multiple methods to gather comprehensive information about your child.

Types of Evaluations in BIG SPRING ISD

The FIE (Full Individual Evaluation) in BIG SPRING ISD typically includes:

  • Psychological tests: Evaluate cognitive and intellectual functioning
  • Educational evaluations: Measure academic skills in reading, math, and writing
  • Speech/language evaluations: If appropriate for the concern
  • Occupational or physical evaluations: Assess motor skills if necessary
  • Developmental history: BIG SPRING ISD collects medical and educational background information
  • Classroom observation: Evaluators observe your child at school
  • Parent information: Your perspectives on behavior and development at home

You have the right to request that BIG SPRING ISD include additional information or obtain an evaluation in a specific area of concern.

Step 5: Attend the Results Meeting in BIG SPRING ISD

After completing the evaluation, BIG SPRING ISD will schedule a meeting with you to discuss the results. This is a critical meeting where decisions about eligibility for special education are made.

How to Prepare for the Meeting in BIG SPRING ISD

  • Request the evaluation results at least 5 days in advance
  • Review all documents before the meeting
  • Bring a list of your concerns or questions
  • Consider bringing a supportive family member or advocate
  • Ask BIG SPRING ISD to provide an interpreter if necessary
  • Take notes during the meeting

At the meeting, BIG SPRING ISD will explain whether your child qualifies for special education services under one or more disability categories.

Possible Outcomes from BIG SPRING ISD

  • Qualifies for services: BIG SPRING ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP)
  • Does not qualify: BIG SPRING ISD may offer services under Section 504 or general interventions
  • More information needed: BIG SPRING ISD may request additional evaluations
