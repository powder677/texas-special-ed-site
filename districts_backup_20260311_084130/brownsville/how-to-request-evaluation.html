Why Request a Special Education Evaluation?

Children face learning challenges for many reasons. Some may struggle with reading, math, or behavior. Others may need support to process information or develop social skills. If you suspect your child has a disability, a special education evaluation in BROWNSVILLE ISD can determine if they qualify for special education services.

  • Persistent difficulty learning to read or understand math
  • Disruptive behavior or frequent conduct problems

  • Delays in speech or language development
  • Difficulties with social skills or friendships
  • Anxiety or depressive symptoms affecting learning

How to Request an Evaluation in BROWNSVILLE ISD

The first step to obtaining special education services is to make a formal request. In BROWNSVILLE ISD, you can initiate this process in several ways, but all require a clear written request.

Step 1: Make a written request

Write a clear letter requesting a FIE (Full Individual Initial Evaluation) in BROWNSVILLE ISD. The letter should include your child's full name, their school grade, and a brief description of why you believe they need an evaluation.

Step 3: BROWNSVILLE ISD response timeline

The FIE Evaluation Process in BROWNSVILLE ISD

A Full Individual Initial Evaluation (FIE) in BROWNSVILLE ISD is a comprehensive examination of your child's skills and needs. The process usually takes 4 to 6 weeks after you give your consent.

What the evaluation includes

  • Cognitive tests: Measure thinking ability and problem-solving skills
  • Academic evaluation: Test skills in reading, writing, and math
  • Behavioral evaluation: Analyzes behavior patterns at school and home
  • Speech and language evaluation: If necessary, evaluate how your child communicates
  • Occupational or physical evaluation: If there are concerns about motor skills
  • Record review: BROWNSVILLE ISD reviews grades, absences, and teacher notes

Your participation during the evaluation

BROWNSVILLE ISD must include you in the process. Evaluators will want to talk to you about:

  • Your child's developmental history since birth
  • Relevant medical or family background
  • How your child acts at home compared to school
  • Your specific concerns about learning
  • Your child's strengths and interests

The BROWNSVILLE ISD Eligibility Meeting

After completing the evaluation, BROWNSVILLE ISD schedules an eligibility meeting. In this crucial meeting, the district team discusses the results and decides if your child qualifies for special education services.

What to expect at the meeting

BROWNSVILLE ISD assembles a team that typically includes teachers, specialists, their special education director, and you as a parent. The team will present:

  • Detailed results of all tests
  • Analysis of whether the results indicate a disability
  • How the disability (if any) affects learning
  • Recommendations for services

Possible outcomes

  • Eligible for special education: If your child qualifies, BROWNSVILLE ISD develops an IEP (Individualized Education Program)
  • Not eligible but can still help: Some students receive support under Section 504 or general intervention
  • Disagreement with results: You can request an independent evaluation

Annual reviews
