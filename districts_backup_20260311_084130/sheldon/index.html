SHELDON ISD Special Education: Complete Resources and Support Guide for Parents

If your child has been identified as needing special education services, navigating the process can feel overwhelming. SHELDON ISD special education programs serve students with diverse learning needs across our district of nearly 11,000 students. This comprehensive guide walks you through everything parents in SHELDON ISD need to know about evaluations, services, your rights, and the support available for your family.

Understanding Special Education in SHELDON ISD

Special education in SHELDON ISD is designed to provide free, appropriate public education (FAPE) to eligible students with disabilities. Our district identifies and serves students ages 3-22 who qualify under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.

SHELDON ISD works with families to develop individualized plans that address each student's unique learning needs. Whether your child has autism, dyslexia, speech and language delays, emotional or behavioral disabilities, or other qualifying conditions, our special education team is committed to your child's success.

Who Qualifies for SHELDON ISD Special Education?

Students qualify for SHELDON ISD special education services under one of 13 disability categories recognized by Texas Education Code. These include:

  • Autism
  • Deaf-blindness
  • Emotional disturbance
  • Hearing impairment
  • Intellectual disability
  • Multiple disabilities
  • Orthopedic impairment
  • Other health impairment
  • Specific learning disability (including dyslexia)
  • Speech or language impairment
  • Traumatic brain injury
  • Visual impairment
  • Non-categorical early childhood

If you believe your child may qualify, the first step is requesting an evaluation from SHELDON ISD. You do not need a medical diagnosis to request evaluation—your concerns about your child's learning, behavior, or development are sufficient to trigger the evaluation process.

SHELDON ISD Special Education Evaluation Process

The evaluation process in SHELDON ISD is comprehensive and designed to identify your child's strengths and needs across multiple areas. Understanding this process helps you participate meaningfully in your child's assessment.

Requesting an Evaluation

You can request a SHELDON ISD special education evaluation in writing to your campus principal or the SHELDON ISD Special Education Department. Your request should describe the concerns you have about your child's learning, development, or behavior. The district has 15 calendar days to provide you with notices of rights and obtain your written consent to evaluate.

Once you provide written consent, SHELDON ISD has 60 calendar days to complete the comprehensive evaluation. This timeline is your protection—the district cannot delay or extend this period without your agreement.

What the Evaluation Includes

A comprehensive evaluation in SHELDON ISD includes assessments across all areas of suspected disability. Evaluations typically include:

  • Cognitive and intellectual assessment
  • Academic achievement testing
  • Speech and language evaluation (if appropriate)
  • Social, emotional, and behavioral assessment
  • Occupational or physical therapy evaluation (if indicated)
  • Classroom observations
  • Parent interview and input
  • Review of school records and developmental history

SHELDON ISD uses multiple measures and cannot rely on any single test or assessment to determine eligibility. This ensures that evaluations are culturally responsive and identify true disabilities rather than differences in language or background.

ARD Meetings and IEP Development for SHELDON ISD Families

Once evaluation is complete, SHELDON ISD schedules an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. This is where the SHELDON ISD ARD meeting becomes a critical opportunity for you to participate in educational planning for your child.

Your Role in the SHELDON ISD ARD Meeting

You are an equal member of the ARD committee—not just an observer. You bring essential information about your child's strengths, challenges, and needs outside of school. SHELDON ISD staff will present evaluation results and recommendations, but your voice and perspective are equally important in the decision-making process.

Before the ARD meeting, request copies of all evaluation reports from SHELDON ISD. Take time to review these documents and write down questions or concerns. If you don't understand something, ask for clarification during the meeting—professional jargon should not prevent you from fully understanding your child's evaluation.

What Happens in an SHELDON ISD ARD Meeting

The ARD committee at SHELDON ISD will:

  1. Review evaluation results and determine eligibility
  2. If eligible, identify your child's present levels of academic and functional performance
  3. Establish annual goals that are measurable and meaningful
  4. Determine what services and supports your child needs
  5. Decide on the appropriate educational setting (least restrictive environment)
  6. Address accommodations and modifications needed
  7. Create the Individualized Education Program (IEP)

You have the right to bring a support person to your SHELDON ISD ARD meeting—a family member, advocate, or anyone you choose. This person can help you understand information, take notes, and support you in advocating for your child's needs.

Understanding SHELDON ISD IEP Services

SHELDON ISD IEP services are individualized and based on your child's identified needs. These may include special education instruction, speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, behavior support, assistive technology, or other services.

The key question in developing your child's IEP is: "What does your child need to make progress in the general curriculum?" Services should be based on this question, not on what is convenient or budgeted. If your child needs a particular service, that need should be documented and addressed.

SHELDON ISD Parent Rights and Procedural Safeguards

Texas and federal law provide important protections for families in SHELDON ISD. Understanding your rights ensures that you can effectively advocate for your child's education.

Core SHELDON ISD Parent Rights

As a parent of a student in SHELDON ISD special education, you have the right to:

  • Request an evaluation of your child at any time
  • Receive a copy of evaluation results before the ARD meeting
  • Participate fully in ARD meetings and IEP development
  • Request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at district expense if you disagree with SHELDON ISD's evaluation
  • Receive notices of meetings and changes to services in your primary language
  • Request a manifestation determination if your child is disciplined
  • Access your child's educational records within 10 business days
  • Request mediation or a due process hearing if you disagree with SHELDON ISD

SHELDON ISD is required to provide you with a document outlining your procedural safeguards at evaluation, ARD, and whenever requested. Read this document carefully and ask questions about rights you don't understand.

What to Do If You Disagree with SHELDON ISD Decisions

Disagreements happen, and parents in SHELDON ISD have clear processes to resolve them. Start with informal conversation with your child's teacher or special education coordinator. Many concerns are resolved through open communication.

If informal discussion doesn't resolve the issue, you can request mediation through SHELDON ISD. Mediation is confidential, free, and involves a neutral third party helping you and the district reach agreement. This is often faster and less adversarial than due process.

You also have the right to file a due process complaint with the Texas Education Agency if you believe SHELDON ISD has violated IDEA. Consider consulting with a special education attorney near SHELDON before filing, as the process has specific requirements and deadlines.

Dyslexia Identification and Support in SHELDON ISD

Texas law requires districts to provide comprehensive support for students with dyslexia. SHELDON ISD dyslexia program services are available for students who struggle with reading despite adequate instruction and intelligence.

How SHELDON ISD Identifies Dyslexia

SHELDON ISD screens all students for dyslexia using validated assessments. If screening indicates potential dyslexia, the district will conduct a comprehensive evaluation to assess decoding, fluency, phonological processing, and reading comprehension.

Important: Your child does not need to fail or fall significantly behind to be evaluated for dyslexia. Early identification through screening is more effective than waiting for achievement gaps to develop. If you suspect dyslexia, request evaluation even if your child is currently performing at grade level.

SHELDON ISD Dyslexia Services

Students identified with dyslexia in SHELDON ISD receive:

  • Evidence-based, structured literacy instruction
  • Explicit, systematic phonics teaching
  • Intervention programs designed for dyslexic learners
  • Accommodations such as extended time on reading tasks
  • Assistive technology tools for reading support
  • Monitoring of progress toward reading goals

Dyslexia support may be provided through special education or Section 504 accommodations, depending on your child's overall profile. SHELDON ISD is committed to ensuring students with dyslexia receive specialized instruction beginning as early as possible.

Parent Involvement and Communication in SHELDON ISD

Your partnership with SHELDON ISD special education staff is essential to your child's success. Effective communication and collaboration between home and school support better outcomes for students.

Regular Progress Monitoring

SHELDON ISD provides progress reports on IEP goals at regular intervals—at minimum, as frequently as report cards are issued. Review these reports carefully and ask questions about progress. If your child is not making adequate progress toward annual goals, the IEP may need adjustment.

Don't wait for formal progress reports to communicate. Contact your child's special education teacher or service provider to discuss how things are going. Quick check-ins about progress, challenges, and celebrations build strong partnerships.

Annual IEP Reviews

SHELDON ISD must hold an ARD meeting at least annually to review your child's IEP. This meeting ensures that goals remain appropriate and services continue to meet your child's needs. You can also request an ARD meeting at any time if you have concerns or want to make changes.
