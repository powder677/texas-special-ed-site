Complete Guide to Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in LINDALE ISD

If your child is facing academic or developmental difficulties in LINDALE ISD, you have the legal right to request a special education evaluation. This guide explains step-by-step how to navigate the LINDALE ISD special evaluation process and protect your family's rights.

Why Request an Evaluation in LINDALE ISD?

Students in LINDALE ISD who have learning, behavioral, communication, or developmental difficulties may be eligible for special education services. A formal evaluation determines if your child qualifies for support under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA).

Many parents in LINDALE ISD wait too long before requesting an evaluation. The sooner you start the process, the sooner your child can receive the necessary support to achieve academic success.

Signs Your Child Might Need an Evaluation

  • Difficulty following instructions or completing tasks
  • Delays in speech, language, or motor skills
  • Frequent disruptive or aggressive behavior
  • Problems interacting with other students
  • Academic performance significantly below peers
  • Difficulty with reading, writing, or mathematics
  • Attention or concentration problems

Understand Parental Rights in LINDALE ISD

Under Texas special education law and LINDALE ISD policies, you have fundamental rights. Knowing these rights empowers you to effectively advocate for your child.

Your Basic Rights in LINDALE ISD

  • Right to request an evaluation: You can request a LINDALE ISD special evaluation in writing at any time
  • Participation in decisions: Your participation is mandatory in all aspects of the process
  • Access to records: You can review all of your child's educational files
  • Due process: You have the right to a hearing if you disagree with LINDALE ISD's decisions
  • Communication in your language: LINDALE ISD must provide Spanish interpretation
  • Legal waiting period: LINDALE ISD must begin the evaluation within 60 calendar days

LINDALE ISD parental rights are protected by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.

Step 1: How to Request an Evaluation in LINDALE ISD

The first step is to make a formal request for evaluation to LINDALE ISD. This request must be in writing to create an official record.

Where to Send Your Request in LINDALE ISD

You should submit your evaluation request to the LINDALE ISD Special Education Director or your child's school principal. You can:

  • Deliver the request in person to the LINDALE ISD special education office
  • Send it by certified mail (recommended to maintain proof of delivery)
  • Send it by email to the LINDALE ISD special education department

What to Include in Your Request

Your request to LINDALE ISD to request an evaluation should include:

  • Full name and date of birth of the student
  • Current grade and school name in LINDALE ISD
  • Clear description of areas of concern (academic, behavioral, physical, language)
  • Specific examples of difficulties observed at home and/or at school
  • Your contact information (phone and email)
  • Your signature and date

Tip: Keep a copy of your request. If LINDALE ISD rejects it, you will have proof that you submitted it.

Step 2: The Evaluation Process in LINDALE ISD

Once LINDALE ISD receives your request, the formal evaluation process begins. This process is designed to be comprehensive and examine multiple areas of your child's development.

Evaluations Included in LINDALE ISD

For the LINDALE ISD FIE Spanish (Full Individual Evaluation, or FIE), LINDALE ISD evaluators will examine:

  • Cognitive aptitude: Intelligence and thinking tests
  • Academic achievement: Evaluation of reading, writing, and mathematics
  • Language development: Evaluation of receptive/expressive speech and language
  • Motor development: Fine and gross motor skills
  • Social and emotional functioning: Behavior and social adjustment
  • Educational and medical history: Comprehensive student history

LINDALE ISD will use multiple evaluation methods to obtain a complete picture of your child.

Process Timeline in LINDALE ISD

  1. Day 1-5: LINDALE ISD acknowledges your written request
  2. Day 5-15: A parent consent meeting is scheduled
  3. Day 15-45: LINDALE ISD completes all evaluations
  4. Day 45-60: The ARD (Admission, Review, Dismissal) meeting is held to discuss results
  5. Day 60: Legal deadline to complete the entire process in LINDALE ISD

Important note: LINDALE ISD must obtain your written consent before evaluating your child. You have the right to ask questions about any proposed test.

Step 3: The ARD Meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal Committee)

After completing the evaluations, LINDALE ISD will hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal Committee) meeting. This is the most important meeting in the special education process.

Who Attends the ARD Meeting in LINDALE ISD

The LINDALE ISD ARD meeting will include:

  • You (parent/guardian) - your participation is mandatory
  • A LINDALE ISD representative with decision-making authority
  • The student's teacher (or teachers if secondary)
  • A LINDALE ISD evaluation specialist
  • Other professionals as needed (speech pathologist, social worker, etc.)
  • The student, if appropriate

What to Expect at the Meeting

During the LINDALE ISD ARD meeting:

  • All evaluation results will be presented and discussed
  • It will be determined if the student qualifies for special education services
  • The student's strengths, concerns, and educational history will be discussed
  • An Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be developed if the student qualifies
  • Specific academic and behavioral goals will be defined
  • The services and supports the student will receive will be outlined

Step 4: The Individualized Education Program (IEP) in LINDALE ISD

If your child qualifies for special education services in LINDALE ISD, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be created. This document is the foundation of all your child's special education.

Key Components of the LINDALE ISD IEP

  • Present levels of performance: Detailed description of the student's academic and behavioral functioning
  • Measurable annual goals: What your child will achieve during the school year
  • Short-term objectives or success criteria: Steps to achieve annual goals
  • Special education services: Type, duration, and frequency of services (speech therapy, tutoring, etc.)
  • Participation in regular education: How much time your child will spend in regular classes
  • Accommodations and modifications: Changes to help your child succeed
  • Progress evaluation: How LINDALE ISD will measure if goals are being met

Step 5: Ongoing Support and Subsequent Rights in LINDALE ISD

Once your child is receiving special education services in LINDALE ISD, you have ongoing rights and responsibilities.

Required Follow-up Meetings in LINDALE ISD

  • Annual IEP meetings: LINDALE ISD must meet with you at least once a year to review progress
  • Regular progress meetings: It is recommended to meet every 6-8 weeks to discuss progress
  • Re-evaluation every three years: LINDALE ISD will re-evaluate your child every three years or more frequently if requested

Your Rights During Special Education Services

  • Right to change the IEP at any time (through an IEP meeting)
  • Right to request an independent evaluation if you disagree with LINDALE ISD
  • Right to regular access to educational records
  • Right to file a due process complaint if you are dissatisfied
  • Right to mediation before a formal hearing
