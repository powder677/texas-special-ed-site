Understanding WIMBERLEY ISD Special Education Services

WIMBERLEY ISD special education serves students ages 3-21 who have been identified as eligible under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). Our district provides comprehensive services across multiple disability categories, including autism spectrum disorder, dyslexia, speech-language impairments, intellectual disabilities, and emotional/behavioral disorders.

With an enrollment of 2,508 students, WIMBERLEY ISD maintains dedicated special education teams at each campus committed to creating inclusive learning environments. Whether your child is in elementary, middle, or high school, WIMBERLEY ISD provides evidence-based interventions and specialized instruction tailored to individual needs.

Who Qualifies for Services

Students in WIMBERLEY ISD may qualify for special education services if they have a documented disability that adversely affects educational performance. Qualifying disabilities include:

  • Autism spectrum disorder
  • Dyslexia and specific learning disabilities
  • Speech-language impairments
  • Intellectual disabilities
  • Emotional/behavioral disorders
  • Orthopedic and physical impairments
  • Visual and hearing impairments
  • Traumatic brain injury
  • Other health impairments

The WIMBERLEY ISD ARD Meeting Process

The WIMBERLEY ISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) is the cornerstone of special education planning. This collaborative meeting brings together parents, teachers, administrators, and specialists to evaluate your child's needs and develop or review educational plans.

What to Expect at Your WIMBERLEY ISD ARD Meeting

Your WIMBERLEY ISD ARD meeting typically includes presentation of evaluation results, discussion of your child's strengths and needs, and collaborative goal-setting. The ARD committee determines eligibility, develops the IEP or 504 plan, and establishes timelines for service delivery. Meetings usually last 45 minutes to two hours depending on complexity.

WIMBERLEY ISD schedules ARD meetings at times that work for families, and you have the right to bring a support person or advocate. If you cannot attend in person, the district can arrange video conferencing or other accommodations to ensure your participation.

Your Rights During WIMBERLEY ISD ARD Meetings

As a parent of a WIMBERLEY ISD student in special education, you have critical rights protected by federal law:

  • Prior written notice: WIMBERLEY ISD must notify you in writing before proposing changes to special education services
  • Meaningful participation: Your input is essential; WIMBERLEY ISD must consider your concerns and preferences
  • Request for independent evaluation: If you disagree with WIMBERLEY ISD assessments, you can request an independent educational evaluation at district expense under certain circumstances
  • Interpreter services: WIMBERLEY ISD provides interpreters if your home language is not English
  • Documentation: You receive a copy of all documents discussed at the meeting

WIMBERLEY ISD IEP Services and Individualized Education Plans

WIMBERLEY ISD IEP services are personalized educational plans developed specifically for your child. The IEP includes present levels of academic and functional performance, annual measurable goals, specific services to be provided, accommodations, and progress monitoring plans.

Key Components of WIMBERLEY ISD IEP Services

Your child's WIMBERLEY ISD IEP services include:

  1. Specialized instruction: Direct teaching in areas of disability, such as dyslexia intervention or speech-language therapy
  2. Related services: Occupational therapy, physical therapy, counseling, or other therapeutic support
  3. Classroom accommodations: Modifications like extended time on tests, preferential seating, or assistive technology
  4. Transition services: For students age 16+, WIMBERLEY ISD includes post-secondary planning for employment or higher education
  5. Progress monitoring: Regular communication about how your child is progressing toward IEP goals

WIMBERLEY ISD Dyslexia Program

The WIMBERLEY ISD dyslexia program provides intensive, evidence-based intervention for students with dyslexia and related reading difficulties. Texas Education Code §38.003 requires districts like WIMBERLEY ISD to provide dyslexia screening and specialized instruction.

If your child is identified with dyslexia in WIMBERLEY ISD, they receive structured literacy instruction using multisensory, phonics-based approaches. The WIMBERLEY ISD dyslexia program includes progress monitoring every 2-4 weeks to ensure intervention effectiveness. Many WIMBERLEY ISD elementary and secondary students benefit from this specialized support within their regular classroom or resource setting.

WIMBERLEY ISD Parent Rights and Procedural Safeguards

WIMBERLEY ISD parent rights ensure you have a voice in your child's special education. These protections, grounded in IDEA and Texas Education Code, are essential for advocating effectively for your child.

Critical WIMBERLEY ISD Parent Rights You Should Know

Understanding WIMBERLEY ISD parent rights empowers you to be an effective advocate:

  • Right to evaluation: You can request that WIMBERLEY ISD evaluate your child for special education eligibility at no cost
  • Right to due process: If you disagree with WIMBERLEY ISD decisions, you can file for a due process hearing with the Texas Education Agency
  • Right to records access: WIMBERLEY ISD must provide you access to all educational records within 10 school days
  • Right to refuse services: You can decline specific services, though WIMBERLEY ISD may file for due process in certain circumstances
  • Right to interpretation: WIMBERLEY ISD provides communication in your native language
  • Manifestation determination: Before disciplining a student in special education, WIMBERLEY ISD must determine if behavior is related to disability

Procedural Safeguards Notice

WIMBERLEY ISD provides a detailed Procedural Safeguards Notice explaining your rights under IDEA. This document must be given to you at least once per school year, or when you request it. The notice is available in English and other languages; contact your campus special education coordinator to receive your copy.

Getting Started: Referral and Evaluation in WIMBERLEY ISD

If you believe your child may need special education services, here's how to begin the process in WIMBERLEY ISD:

Step-by-Step Process for WIMBERLEY ISD Referral

  1. Request evaluation: Contact your child's campus principal or special education coordinator to request a full and individual initial evaluation (FIIEP)
  2. Complete referral form: WIMBERLEY ISD provides a referral form; you can request evaluation verbally or in writing
  3. Consent for evaluation: WIMBERLEY ISD sends a consent form; you must sign before testing begins
  4. Evaluation period: WIMBERLEY ISD completes comprehensive evaluation within 60 school days
  5. Initial ARD meeting: WIMBERLEY ISD convenes an ARD meeting to discuss results and determine eligibility
  6. Eligibility determination: WIMBERLEY ISD notifies you in writing of eligibility decision

This timeline is critical—WIMBERLEY ISD families should know that delays in evaluation can impact your child's access to services. If WIMBERLEY ISD extends the evaluation beyond 60 days, they must provide written explanation.
