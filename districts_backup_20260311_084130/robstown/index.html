Understanding Special Education in ROBSTOWN ISD

ROBSTOWN ISD serves approximately 2,411 students across our community, and we are dedicated to identifying and supporting children with disabilities. ROBSTOWN ISD special education programs are designed to meet students' unique needs through individualized education plans and specialized instruction.

Special education in ROBSTOWN ISD is guided by federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act or IDEA) and Texas state regulations. Our district evaluates students who may qualify for services in categories including learning disabilities, speech/language impairments, autism spectrum disorder, intellectual disabilities, emotional/behavioral disorders, and other health impairments.

Parents in ROBSTOWN ISD who suspect their child may need special education services should know that evaluation and eligibility determination is available at no cost to families. Early identification is critical—the sooner your child receives appropriate support, the better positioned they are for academic and social success.

ROBSTOWN ISD ARD Meetings: Your Child's Educational Planning

An ROBSTOWN ISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) is where your child's special education program is developed and reviewed. This is your opportunity to participate in meaningful decisions about your child's education, and your involvement is not just welcomed—it's required by law.

What to Expect at Your ARD Meeting

ROBSTOWN ISD ARD meetings typically include:

  • School administrators or representatives with decision-making authority
  • General education teachers (for initial evaluations)
  • Special education teachers or service providers
  • Parents or guardians
  • Other specialists as needed (speech therapists, counselors, etc.)

During an ROBSTOWN ISD ARD meeting, the team reviews your child's evaluation results, discusses their strengths and needs, and develops or updates the Individualized Education Program (IEP). You'll discuss eligibility, goals, services, placement, and accommodations.

ARD Meeting Timelines

ROBSTOWN ISD follows these important timelines:

  • Initial Evaluation: Within 60 calendar days of receiving written consent (may be extended by 30 days under certain circumstances)
  • Eligibility ARD: Held within 30 calendar days after evaluation is complete
  • Annual Review: At least once per school year
  • Triennial Reevaluation: Every three years (can be waived with parental agreement)

Parents in ROBSTOWN ISD have the right to request an ARD meeting at any time if concerns arise about your child's program or progress.

ROBSTOWN ISD IEP Services: Individualized Education Plans Explained

ROBSTOWN ISD IEP services are the foundation of special education. An IEP is a legally binding document that outlines your child's educational program, including goals, services, accommodations, and how progress will be measured.

Components of Your Child's IEP

Every IEP in ROBSTOWN ISD includes:

  • Present levels of academic and functional performance
  • Annual goals (measurable objectives)
  • Specific special education services and supports
  • Accommodations and modifications
  • Transition planning (for students age 16 and older)
  • Progress monitoring and reporting methods
  • Placement and least restrictive environment (LRE) determination

ROBSTOWN ISD IEP services may include resource room instruction, self-contained classroom placement, speech/language therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, assistive technology, and behavioral support services. The specific services your child receives depend on their individual needs as determined by the ARD committee.

Progress Monitoring in ROBSTOWN ISD

ROBSTOWN ISD parents receive regular reports on how their child is progressing toward IEP goals. This typically happens at least as frequently as progress reports for non-special education students. Understanding your child's progress is essential for determining whether the current program is working.

ROBSTOWN ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

Understanding your rights as a parent is essential when navigating ROBSTOWN ISD parent rights in special education. These rights are protected under IDEA and Texas Education Code and ensure you have a voice in your child's education.

Core Parent Rights in ROBSTOWN ISD

ROBSTOWN ISD parents have the right to:

  • Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE): Your child is entitled to special education services at no cost
  • Notice and Consent: Written notice before evaluation, ARD meetings, and significant changes to services
  • Meaningful Participation: Active involvement in all decisions about identification, evaluation, placement, and IEP development
  • Access to Records: Review and obtain copies of all educational records within 10 business days
  • Independent Evaluation: Request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at district expense if you disagree with ROBSTOWN ISD evaluation results
  • Due Process Hearing: File a complaint or request a due process hearing if you believe ROBSTOWN ISD is not providing FAPE
  • Representation: Bring an advocate or attorney to ARD meetings (see information below about special education attorneys)

Communication in Your Home Language

If your home language is not English, ROBSTOWN ISD must provide documents and interpretation services in your native language so you can participate meaningfully in all meetings and decisions regarding your child's special education.

ROBSTOWN ISD Dyslexia Program & Literacy Support

A ROBSTOWN ISD dyslexia program specifically addresses students with dyslexia and related reading difficulties. Dyslexia is a specific learning disability that affects how the brain processes written language, and early intervention is critical.

Dyslexia Screening and Identification

Texas Education Code requires that all students be screened for dyslexia by the end of first grade. ROBSTOWN ISD conducts these screenings using research-based assessments. If your child shows indicators of dyslexia, ROBSTOWN ISD will notify you and discuss evaluation options.

The ROBSTOWN ISD dyslexia program includes:

  • Universal screening for all early elementary students
  • Research-based dyslexia interventions
  • Specialized reading instruction using evidence-based approaches
  • Progress monitoring specific to reading skill development
  • Accommodations such as extra time, text-to-speech technology, and audiobooks

Getting Your Child Assessed for Dyslexia

If you suspect your child has dyslexia, contact your campus principal or the ROBSTOWN ISD special education office. ROBSTOWN ISD can provide dyslexia screening and comprehensive evaluation to determine if your child qualifies for services under the dyslexia program or through special education eligibility.

How to Request Special Education Services in ROBSTOWN ISD

If you believe your child may benefit from ROBSTOWN ISD special education services, here's the step-by-step process:

Step 1: Express Concern in Writing

Contact your child's teacher, campus principal, or the ROBSTOWN ISD special education director with a written request for evaluation. Be specific about your concerns (academic, behavioral, speech, etc.). Keep a copy for your records.

Step 2: ROBSTOWN ISD Must Respond

Within a reasonable timeframe, ROBSTOWN ISD must respond to your request and either agree to evaluate or explain why they believe evaluation is not necessary. If ROBSTOWN ISD denies your request, they must provide written notice of your right to request a hearing.

Step 3: Consent and Evaluation

Once ROBSTOWN ISD agrees to evaluate, you'll sign a consent form. The evaluation will assess your child's academic, developmental, and functional abilities across all areas of concern.

Step 4: ARD Meeting and Eligibility Determination

ROBSTOWN ISD will hold an ROBSTOWN ISD ARD meeting to discuss evaluation results and determine eligibility. If your child qualifies, the IEP will be developed at this same meeting.
