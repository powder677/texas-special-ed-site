Understanding PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD Special Education Services

PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD special education serves students ages 3-21 with disabilities under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act. The district provides a continuum of services designed to meet individual student needs in the least restrictive environment.

The special education program in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD includes:

  • Speech-language pathology services for students with communication disorders
  • Occupational and physical therapy for motor skill development
  • Specialized instruction in resource rooms and self-contained classrooms
  • Behavioral support services for students with emotional and behavioral disabilities
  • Assistive technology and adaptive equipment
  • Related services including counseling and social work support
  • Transition services for students ages 16 and older preparing for adult life

Parents of students in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD can request a comprehensive evaluation at any time if they suspect their child may have a disability affecting educational performance.

The ARD Meeting Process in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD

An PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) is where your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP) is developed, reviewed, and updated. Understanding this process empowers you to participate as an equal team member.

What to Expect at Your PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD ARD Meeting

The PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD ARD meeting brings together teachers, special education staff, parents, and other specialists to discuss your child's needs and progress. You'll receive written notice at least 10 days before the meeting, allowing time to prepare.

Required participants at PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD ARD meetings include:

  • Parent or guardian
  • Special education teacher
  • General education teacher (if child attends general education classes)
  • District representative with authority to commit resources
  • Person who can interpret evaluation results
  • School counselor or social worker (when appropriate)
  • Your child (especially for transition planning)

Preparing for Your PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD ARD Meeting

Come prepared to make your PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD ARD meeting productive. Bring any outside evaluations, medical records, or documentation of concerns you've observed at home. Write down specific questions about your child's progress, placement, and services.

Pro tip: Bring a support person to your PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD ARD meeting—whether a family member, advocate, or attorney. Having someone to take notes and help you navigate the discussion ensures your voice is heard and accurate records are kept.

PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD IEP Services Explained

The Individualized Education Program (IEP) is the legal document that outlines all PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD IEP services your child will receive. This document is critical—it protects your child's right to appropriate education and holds the district accountable for delivery of services.

Key Components of PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD IEP Services

Every IEP developed through PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD must include measurable annual goals, present levels of academic and functional performance, and specific PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD IEP services to be provided. The IEP specifies:

  • Frequency and duration of each service (minutes per week)
  • Service providers (special education teacher, speech therapist, etc.)
  • Location of services (general classroom, resource room, off-campus facility)
  • Accommodations and modifications for curriculum and testing
  • Progress monitoring procedures and report frequency
  • Assistive technology needs
  • Extended school year (ESY) eligibility, if applicable

Parents in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD have the right to question whether recommended services are appropriate and sufficient for their child's needs. If you disagree with the IEP, you can request a revision before signing.

Monitoring IEP Progress in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD

Your child's progress on PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD IEP services must be monitored regularly. You should receive progress reports at least as frequently as non-special education students receive report cards—typically every four to six weeks.

Review these progress reports carefully. If your child is not making progress toward IEP goals, request an ARD meeting to discuss changes. The district must adjust services if they're not proving effective.

Parent Rights Under Texas Special Education Law

Understanding your PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD parent rights is essential. Texas Education Code and federal IDEA regulations guarantee specific protections and opportunities for parent participation.

Core PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD Parent Rights

As a parent in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD, you have the legal right to:

  • Request evaluation of your child at any time if you suspect disability
  • Participate in all meetings related to identification, evaluation, and placement
  • Review all educational records and request copies
  • Obtain an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at district expense under certain conditions
  • Receive written notice in your native language before any changes in services
  • Request mediation or due process hearing if you disagree with the district
  • Bring an advocate or attorney to meetings
  • Provide prior written consent before special education services begin

Filing a Complaint with PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD

If you believe PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD has violated your child's special education rights, you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA) or request a due process hearing. The complaint must be filed within one year of the alleged violation.

Before escalating to state agencies, consider requesting a meeting with the Director of Special Education for PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD to resolve the issue informally. Many concerns can be addressed through direct communication.

PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD Dyslexia Program

Texas law requires all districts, including PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD, to provide reading intervention and support for students with dyslexia and related reading disorders. The PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD dyslexia program includes screening, evaluation, and evidence-based intervention.

Dyslexia Screening and Identification in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD

All students in kindergarten and first grade in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD are screened for dyslexia and related reading disabilities using approved assessment tools. Students in grades 2-9 may also be screened if teachers or parents request it.

If your child is identified through the PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD dyslexia program, the district will provide:

  • Explicit, systematic phonics instruction using research-based methods
  • Small group intervention provided by trained dyslexia specialists
  • Progress monitoring to ensure improvement in reading skills
  • Accommodations such as extended time on reading-based assignments
  • Parent education about dyslexia and how to support learning at home

Parents in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD can request dyslexia evaluation at any time if they notice their child struggles with reading despite adequate instruction. Early identification and intervention significantly improve outcomes.

Section 504 Plans in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD

Not all students with disabilities qualify for special education IEPs. Some students in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD may be eligible for protections and accommodations under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.

A Section 504 plan in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD protects students whose disability substantially limits a major life activity but who don't need special education services. Common examples include ADHD, asthma, diabetes, and anxiety disorders.

If your child has a disability diagnosis but hasn't been evaluated for special education, ask your PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD campus administrator about 504 eligibility. Section 504 plans provide important accommodations such as preferential seating, extended time, and medication administration at school.
