Understanding the FIE Evaluation Process in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD

A Full Individual Evaluation, or FIE, is a thorough assessment designed to determine whether your child qualifies for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). For families in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD, understanding this process is the first step toward getting your child the support they need.

The PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD special education evaluation process is governed by both federal law and Texas Education Code requirements. The district serves 2,919 students and maintains a special education department dedicated to identifying students who need additional support. Whether your child is struggling academically, behaviorally, or socially, you have the legal right to request an evaluation.

Unlike a simple screening or progress monitoring, an FIE is a comprehensive, multidisciplinary assessment that examines your child's cognitive abilities, academic performance, social-emotional functioning, and other relevant areas. This detailed evaluation helps determine eligibility for services like resource classes, self-contained classrooms, or related services such as speech therapy or counseling.

Child Find Obligations in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD

PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD has a legal obligation under IDEA to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities within the district. This responsibility is known as the PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD child find obligation, and it applies to all children ages 3-21, regardless of whether they attend public school.

Key aspects of child find include:

  • The district must actively search for children who may have unidentified disabilities
  • PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD staff must monitor students who are struggling, even without a formal referral
  • Teachers and administrators are trained to recognize warning signs of disabilities
  • The district must provide evaluation services at no cost to families
  • Services apply to children in public school, charter schools, and private schools within the district's jurisdiction

If you believe your child isn't being identified despite clear signs of struggle, you can file a formal request with PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD's special education department. The district cannot refuse an evaluation based on limited English proficiency, homeschooling status, or prior classroom performance alone.

How to Request an FIE from PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD

You don't need a lawyer, diagnosis, or permission from school staff to request a PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD evaluation request. As a parent, you have the unilateral right to request an FIE at any time during your child's enrollment in the district.

Step 1: Prepare Your Written Request

Submit your request in writing to PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD's special education department. Your request should be clear and specific, stating that you want a Full Individual Evaluation or comprehensive evaluation under IDEA. Include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current school and grade level
  • Specific concerns about your child's learning, behavior, or development
  • Examples of difficulties you've observed at home or school
  • Any prior evaluations or testing your child has undergone
  • Your contact information and preferred method of communication

While you can make a verbal request, written documentation is essential. A written request ensures PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD cannot claim they didn't receive it and creates a clear timeline for the evaluation process.

Step 2: Submit to the Right Department

Contact PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD's special education office or the campus where your child attends school. You can email, mail, hand-deliver, or use the district's formal referral form. Request written confirmation that your evaluation request has been received and document the date you submitted it.

Keep copies of everything you send to PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD. This documentation protects your rights and creates a clear record if disputes arise later.

Understanding the 45-Day Timeline for PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD Evaluations

One of the most critical aspects of the evaluation process is the PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD 45 day timeline. Once you submit your evaluation request, the district has exactly 45 calendar days to complete the FIE and hold an Individualized Education Program (IEP) meeting to discuss results.

What the 45-Day Timeline Includes

The 45-day clock starts the day PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD receives your written request. During this period, the district must:

  • Notify you in writing about the evaluation plan within 10 school days
  • Obtain your written consent before beginning any testing
  • Conduct comprehensive evaluations in all areas of suspected disability
  • Review existing data and records
  • Complete all testing and assessments
  • Compile findings into an evaluation report
  • Schedule and conduct an IEP meeting to discuss results

Important: The 45 days can be extended only in limited circumstances, such as if your child is absent from school for extended periods or you request a delay. PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD cannot delay the evaluation simply because staff are busy.

What Happens at the 45-Day Mark

At the end of the evaluation, PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD must hold a meeting with you and their IEP team. During this meeting, they present the evaluation findings and determine eligibility for special education services. You'll receive a comprehensive evaluation report explaining:

  • Assessment tools and methods used
  • All test results and scores
  • Interpretation of findings
  • Whether your child meets IDEA disability criteria
  • Recommendations for services or placement

If your child is found eligible, PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD will develop an IEP specifying the special education and related services your child will receive. If ineligible, you have the right to request an independent educational evaluation at district expense under certain circumstances.

Areas Evaluated in a PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD FIE

A comprehensive PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD special education evaluation examines multiple areas of functioning. The district's evaluation team assesses any area where your child shows signs of difficulty or disability.

Typical Evaluation Areas

Cognitive and Intellectual Functioning: Tests assess reasoning, processing speed, working memory, and overall intellectual ability. These evaluations help identify intellectual disabilities or gifted abilities.

Academic Achievement: Reading, mathematics, writing, and spelling are tested to identify specific learning disabilities. Results show whether your child is performing significantly below grade level.

Speech and Language: A speech-language pathologist evaluates expressive and receptive language, articulation, and communication skills. This assessment identifies speech/language disorders.

Social-Emotional and Behavioral Functioning: Rating scales and observations assess your child's emotional regulation, social skills, behavior, and mental health. This helps identify emotional disturbances or behavioral disorders.

Adaptive Functioning: Self-care, daily living, and independence skills are evaluated, particularly important for students with intellectual or developmental disabilities.

Fine and Gross Motor Skills: Coordination, strength, and physical development are assessed, relevant for children with physical or developmental disabilities.

PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD's evaluation team determines which specific areas to assess based on your concerns and observations. You can request that additional areas be evaluated if you believe they're relevant to your child's needs.

Your Rights During the Evaluation Process

Parents in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD have significant rights throughout the evaluation process. Understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Essential Parent Rights

  • Right to Request Evaluation: You can request a PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD FIE evaluation at any time without a diagnosis or school recommendation
  • Right to Written Notice: PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD must provide written notice before conducting any evaluation, explaining the purpose, scope, and timeline
  • Right to Informed Consent: You must give written permission before the district begins testing. Withholding consent delays the evaluation
  • Right to Participate: You can attend all evaluation planning meetings and discuss findings with the IEP team
  • Right to Interpreters: If you don't speak English, PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD must provide an interpreter at no cost
  • Right to Independent Evaluation: If you disagree with PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD's evaluation, you can request an independent evaluation funded by the district under certain conditions
  • Right to a Copy of Records: You can request all evaluation reports, test results, and educational records

If PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD denies your evaluation request or violates your rights, you have recourse through due process complaint procedures and mediation. Don't hesitate to advocate strongly for your child.

Common Concerns and Questions About PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD Evaluations

Parents in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD often have concerns about the evaluation process. Here are answers to frequently asked questions.

Will My Child Be Labeled?

An evaluation determines whether your child qualifies for services under specific IDEA disability categories. While your child receives a classification, this information is confidential and protected by federal privacy laws. The purpose is to ensure your child gets appropriate support, not to stigmatize.

What If PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD Says My Child Doesn't Qualify?

If the district finds your child ineligible for special education but you disagree, you have several options. You can request an independent educational evaluation at PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD's expense, file a due process complaint, or request mediation. Many students who don't qualify for special education may still be eligible for accommodations under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.

Can I Request Specific Types of Evaluations?

Yes. If you believe your child needs specific testing (such as neuropsychological evaluation or occupational therapy assessment), communicate this clearly in your evaluation request to PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD. While the district determines the comprehensive evaluation plan, they must consider parent input.

What If My Child Already Has an Outside Diagnosis?

A medical diagnosis (like ADHD or autism) doesn't automatically qualify your child for special education through PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD. The district must conduct its own evaluation to determine whether the disability affects educational performance and whether your child needs special education services. Outside evaluations can be helpful supporting documents for the IEP team to consider.
