Understanding NAVASOTA ISD Special Education Programs

NAVASOTA ISD special education serves students ages 3-21 with disabilities through a range of evidence-based programs and support services. Our district enrolls over 3,100 students and maintains a dedicated special education department committed to meeting each child's unique needs.

The NAVASOTA ISD special education department provides services in multiple disability categories, including:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
  • Developmental Delay (ages 3-8)
  • Emotional/Behavioral Disorders
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment (OHI)
  • Specific Learning Disability (including dyslexia)
  • Speech/Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Visual Impairment

Each student in NAVASOTA ISD receives a personalized education plan designed to address their specific strengths, challenges, and educational goals.

The IEP Process: NAVASOTA ISD IEP Services Explained

An Individualized Education Program (IEP) is the foundation of special education services in NAVASOTA ISD. This legally binding document outlines your child's educational goals, the services they'll receive, and how progress will be measured.

Getting Started with NAVASOTA ISD IEP Services

The process begins when your child is referred for evaluation. Parents, teachers, or school staff can request an evaluation if they suspect a disability affecting educational performance. Once NAVASOTA ISD receives a referral, the district has 15 calendar days to decide whether to evaluate your child.

If NAVASOTA ISD agrees to evaluate, you'll receive a notice explaining:

  • Why the evaluation is being proposed
  • What tests and assessments will be used
  • Your rights as a parent
  • The timeline for completing the evaluation

The evaluation period typically takes 60 calendar days. During this time, NAVASOTA ISD will use multiple assessment tools to understand your child's strengths, weaknesses, and educational needs.

The NAVASOTA ISD ARD Meeting

After evaluation, NAVASOTA ISD convenes an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting—also called an IEP meeting. This is where your child's IEP is developed, reviewed, or updated.

Your NAVASOTA ISD ARD meeting will include:

  • Parent(s) or guardian(s)
  • Special education teacher
  • General education teacher(s)
  • District representative (usually administrator or special education coordinator)
  • Evaluation specialist or diagnostician
  • Your child (when appropriate)
  • Other specialists as needed (speech therapist, occupational therapist, etc.)

During the NAVASOTA ISD ARD meeting, the team will discuss evaluation results, determine eligibility for special education, identify specific needs, and create goals targeting those needs. You have the right to bring an advocate or attorney to your NAVASOTA ISD ARD meeting.

What Happens After Your NAVASOTA ISD ARD Meeting

Once your IEP is finalized at the NAVASOTA ISD ARD meeting, services begin. The district must provide written copies of the IEP within a reasonable timeframe. NAVASOTA ISD special education staff will implement the services specified in the IEP, and progress will be monitored regularly.

The IEP is reviewed at least annually, and parents in NAVASOTA ISD can request additional NAVASOTA ISD ARD meetings if they believe changes are needed. You're also entitled to progress reports on your child's goals at the same frequency as report cards for non-disabled students.

NAVASOTA ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

Understanding your rights is essential to advocating effectively for your child. NAVASOTA ISD parent rights are protected under federal law (IDEA—Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) and Texas Education Code.

Your Core Rights as a NAVASOTA ISD Parent

Parents in NAVASOTA ISD have the right to:

  • Be informed and involved — NAVASOTA ISD must notify you before any actions affecting your child's special education
  • Have your child evaluated — NAVASOTA ISD must conduct a comprehensive, unbiased evaluation at no cost to you
  • Participate in every decision — You're a full member of the ARD team at every NAVASOTA ISD ARD meeting
  • Access your child's records — You can review all educational records NAVASOTA ISD maintains about your child
  • Request independent evaluations — If you disagree with NAVASOTA ISD's evaluation, you can obtain an independent evaluation at district expense under certain circumstances
  • Receive services in your native language — NAVASOTA ISD must communicate with you in a language you understand
  • Due process protections — If you disagree with NAVASOTA ISD's decisions, you have the right to formal dispute resolution

Protecting Your Rights During NAVASOTA ISD Interactions

Document everything. Keep copies of all communications with NAVASOTA ISD, including emails, letters, and notes from meetings. If you're uncertain about anything discussed during a NAVASOTA ISD ARD meeting, ask for clarification in writing.

You have the right to an independent educational evaluation (IEE) if you disagree with NAVASOTA ISD's evaluation. You may also request that NAVASOTA ISD pay for the IEE if you can demonstrate that the district's evaluation was inappropriate.

If NAVASOTA ISD denies your requests or you believe your NAVASOTA ISD parent rights have been violated, you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA) or pursue mediation and due process hearings.

NAVASOTA ISD Dyslexia Program and Intervention Services

Dyslexia is a specific learning disability affecting reading, spelling, and language processing. NAVASOTA ISD dyslexia program provides specialized identification and intervention for students with dyslexia and related disorders.

How NAVASOTA ISD Identifies Dyslexia

NAVASOTA ISD uses a comprehensive evaluation process to identify students with dyslexia. Teachers administer universal screening assessments to all students, and those showing signs of reading difficulty receive additional diagnostic testing.

If your child is identified with dyslexia through NAVASOTA ISD screening, the district will:

  • Notify you of the identification
  • Explain the NAVASOTA ISD dyslexia program options available
  • Offer intervention services through either special education (if your child qualifies) or the dyslexia intervention program
  • Implement evidence-based reading interventions focusing on phonics, phonemic awareness, and fluency

Dyslexia Intervention in NAVASOTA ISD

Students with dyslexia receive intensive, specialized instruction through NAVASOTA ISD's dyslexia program. These interventions are typically delivered in small groups or individually, using structured, multisensory approaches proven effective for dyslexic learners.

The NAVASOTA ISD dyslexia program focuses on:

  • Explicit phonics instruction
  • Phonemic awareness development
  • Fluency building
  • Comprehension strategies
  • Spelling and written expression support

Progress in the NAVASOTA ISD dyslexia program is monitored regularly, and the intervention plan is adjusted based on your child's response to instruction. Parents receive updates on their child's progress and can request modifications to the intervention approach.

Frequently Asked Questions About NAVASOTA ISD Special Education

How do I request an evaluation from NAVASOTA ISD?

You can request an evaluation by contacting the NAVASOTA ISD special education department directly. Submit your request in writing to ensure it's documented. NAVASOTA ISD has 15 days to respond and 60 days to complete the evaluation.

What if I disagree with NAVASOTA ISD's evaluation results?

You have the right to request an independent educational evaluation (IEE). If NAVASOTA ISD refuses to pay for it, you can request a due process hearing to challenge the district's position. Bring documentation showing why you believe NAVASOTA ISD's evaluation was incomplete or biased.

Can I bring an advocate to my NAVASOTA ISD ARD meeting?

Yes. You can bring a special ed advocate NAVASOTA ISD parents trust, a teacher, counselor, or other supportive person. If you're considering legal action, you may also bring a special education attorney near NAVASOTA to protect your rights.

What accommodations can my child receive in NAVASOTA ISD?

NAVASOTA ISD provides accommodations listed in your child's IEP or 504 plan. These might include extended time on tests, modified assignments, assistive technology, preferential seating, or specialized instruction. The specific accommodations depend on your child's needs and are determined during the NAVASOTA ISD ARD meeting.
