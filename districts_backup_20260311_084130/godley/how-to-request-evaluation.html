Complete Guide to Requesting Special Education Evaluation in GODLEY ISD

If your child is experiencing academic or behavioral difficulties in GODLEY ISD, you have the right to request a special education evaluation at no cost. This guide will help you understand the GODLEY ISD special education evaluation process and provide you with the necessary steps to protect your child's rights.

Why Request an Evaluation in GODLEY ISD?

The special education evaluation in GODLEY ISD is the first step to determine if your child qualifies for special services. A child who is not learning at the expected pace, who has difficulty concentrating, or who presents social and emotional challenges can benefit from this comprehensive evaluation.

Parents in GODLEY ISD often notice signs such as reading difficulties, behavioral problems, speech delays, or motor skill difficulties. If your child exhibits any of these signs, it's time to consider requesting a GODLEY ISD evaluation.

Common Signs Indicating Need for Evaluation

  • Difficulties following instructions or understanding academic concepts
  • Disruptive or aggressive behavior at school
  • Delays in language or speech development
  • Difficulties socializing with peers
  • Attention problems or hyperactivity
  • Significant difficulties in reading, writing, or mathematics

Your Rights as a Parent in GODLEY ISD

The Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and the Vocational Rehabilitation Act ensure that GODLEY ISD parents' rights are protected. These rights are fundamental and cannot be violated under any circumstances.

As a parent or guardian in GODLEY ISD, you have the right to:

  • Request free evaluation: GODLEY ISD must evaluate your child at no cost
  • Participate in all decisions: Your voice is crucial in the evaluation process
  • Receive information in Spanish: All important documents must be available in your language
  • Access to records: You can review all of your child's files in GODLEY ISD
  • Obtain an independent evaluation: If you disagree with GODLEY ISD, you can seek another evaluation
  • Legal representation: You can bring a special education attorney or advocate

Step-by-Step Process for Requesting an Evaluation in GODLEY ISD

Step 1: Communicate with Your Child's School

The first step is to contact your child's teacher, counselor, or principal in GODLEY ISD. Express your specific concerns about your child's academic performance or behavior. This initial conversation is important to establish a record of your concern.

We recommend that this communication be in writing. Send an email or letter to the school principal in GODLEY ISD documenting your concern. This creates evidence that you have requested attention to these issues.

Step 2: Formally Request the Evaluation

Send a formal written request for an educational evaluation to the GODLEY ISD Special Education Department. The request must be clear and include the specific reasons why you believe your child needs evaluation.

You can use a simple letter or the GODLEY ISD form if available. The request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current grade in GODLEY ISD school
  • Specific description of academic or behavioral concerns
  • Concrete examples of difficulties you have observed
  • Your signature and date
  • Your contact information (phone and email)

Deliver this request in person or send it by certified mail to the special education director of GODLEY ISD. Keep a copy for your records.

Step 3: Await GODLEY ISD's Response

According to Texas and federal law, GODLEY ISD has 15 calendar days to respond to your request for a GODLEY ISD special education evaluation. In this response, the school must indicate whether it agrees to conduct the evaluation or rejects it with justification.

If GODLEY ISD rejects the evaluation, they must provide a detailed explanation of why they believe your child does not need to be evaluated. In this case, you can challenge the decision.

Step 4: Provide Written Consent

If GODLEY ISD agrees to conduct the evaluation, the school will send you a consent form. This is a critical step. You must give written consent before any evaluation begins.

Read this form carefully. It specifies exactly what evaluations will be conducted, who will conduct them, and how the results will be used. Do not hesitate to ask questions if anything is unclear.

Step 5: Participate in the Evaluations

During the GODLEY ISD special education evaluation process, several tests will be conducted. These may include psychological, educational, speech and language evaluations, and assessments of social skills and behavior.

You have the right to be present during these evaluations or to receive detailed reports about what was found. GODLEY ISD must provide you with copies of all evaluation reports.

The FIE (Full Individual Evaluation) Meeting in GODLEY ISD

After completing all evaluations, GODLEY ISD must convene an FIE (Full Individual Evaluation) or Multidisciplinary Comprehensive Evaluation meeting. This meeting is extremely important for your child.

What to Expect at the Meeting

At the GODLEY ISD FIE meeting, a multidisciplinary team will discuss the results of all evaluations. This team generally includes:

  • A school psychologist
  • Special education teachers
  • A diagnostic specialist
  • The special education director or coordinator
  • You (parent or guardian)
  • An interpreter if necessary

The GODLEY ISD team will present each evaluation, explain the findings, and answer all your questions. This is your time to ask questions, express concerns, and actively participate in decision-making.

Eligibility Determination

After reviewing all information, the GODLEY ISD team will determine if your child is eligible for special education services. To qualify, your child must have a disability that adversely affects their educational performance.

Eligibility categories in Texas include: autism, auditory impairment, intellectual disability, multiple disabilities, orthopedic impairments, other health impairment, emotional disturbance, specific learning disability, traumatic brain injury, and visual impairment.

If GODLEY ISD Determines Your Child is Eligible

If the GODLEY ISD team determines your child is eligible, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be developed. This document is the master plan for your child's education.

The Individualized Education Program (IEP)

The GODLEY ISD IEP is much more than a document: it is the contract between the school and your family. The IEP must include:

  • Current levels of academic and functional performance
  • Specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound (SMART) annual goals
  • Special services your child will receive
  • Changes to the regular educational program if necessary
  • Evaluations to measure progress
  • Review date (minimum annually)

In GODLEY ISD, you must be an active participant in the development of the IEP. Your contributions, observations, and priorities are valuable and legally required in this process.

If You Disagree with GODLEY ISD

If you are not satisfied with GODLEY ISD's evaluation, or if you believe the recommendations are not appropriate for your child, you have options. Parents in GODLEY ISD are not obliged to passively accept the school's decisions.

Obtain an Independent Evaluation

You have the right to a publicly funded independent educational evaluation (IEE) if you disagree with GODLEY ISD's evaluation. GODLEY ISD must provide information on where to obtain an independent evaluation at no cost.

This evaluation can be conducted by a professional outside of GODLEY ISD and can provide additional perspectives on your child's needs.
