What is a Special Education Evaluation in MARBLE FALLS ISD?

A special education evaluation is a comprehensive process where MARBLE FALLS ISD specialists assess your child's strengths and needs in various areas. This includes academic, behavioral, developmental, and medical evaluations.

The main goal is to determine if your child qualifies for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) or Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act. For families in MARBLE FALLS ISD, this evaluation is completely free.

In MARBLE FALLS ISD, evaluation staff includes school psychologists, special education teachers, speech and language specialists, and other professionals according to your child's specific needs.

MARBLE FALLS ISD Special Evaluation: Steps to Request

Step 1: Express Concern to the School

The first step is to contact your child's school in MARBLE FALLS ISD. You can speak with the regular classroom teacher, the school counselor, or the main office.

Describe your specific concerns about your child's academic performance, behavior, or development. Providing concrete examples helps MARBLE FALLS ISD better understand the situation.

The school in MARBLE FALLS ISD may offer general interventions first, such as additional help in reading or math. This is appropriate and does not delay your right to request a formal evaluation.

Step 2: Written Request for Evaluation

This is the most important legal request. It must be in writing so that MARBLE FALLS ISD has clear documentation of your request.

You can submit your request in several ways:

  • Deliver a letter to the school principal
  • Send an email to the special education director of MARBLE FALLS ISD
  • Use the evaluation request form available at MARBLE FALLS ISD
  • Submit the request in person at the MARBLE FALLS ISD central office

In your request, include your child's full name, school, age or grade, and a clear description of your concerns. Keep a copy for your records.

Step 3: Informed Consent

Within 15 school days, MARBLE FALLS ISD must contact you with an informed consent request. This document, in your language, explains what will be evaluated and why.

Read the consent document carefully. You have the right to ask questions about the evaluation process in MARBLE FALLS ISD. Do not sign anything you do not fully understand.

Once you sign the consent, MARBLE FALLS ISD has 60 calendar days (approximately 10-12 school weeks) to complete the evaluation and meet with you to discuss the results.

Step 4: Complete the Evaluation

During this period, MARBLE FALLS ISD specialists will evaluate your child in multiple areas. This may include:

  • Academic tests in reading, writing, and math
  • Behavioral and emotional evaluation
  • Language development evaluation
  • Review of academic and medical records
  • Classroom observations
  • Interviews with teachers and parents

MARBLE FALLS ISD evaluators may contact you to ask about your child's development and academic history. Your participation is important for an accurate evaluation.

Step 5: ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) Committee Meeting

MARBLE FALLS ISD must schedule an ARD committee meeting within 60 days to share the evaluation results. In Texas, these meetings are regulated by Texas Education Code §26.008.

In this meeting, you will learn:

  • If your child qualifies for special education
  • The specific areas where they need services
  • The proposed Individualized Education Program (IEP), if applicable
  • Educational placement options

MARBLE FALLS ISD must provide an interpreter if you speak Spanish, and you can bring someone you trust to support you in this important meeting.

Disability Classifications in MARBLE FALLS ISD

If your child qualifies, MARBLE FALLS ISD will determine the disability category. Classifications include:

  • Orthopedic Impairment: Physical limitations

Understanding the category MARBLE FALLS ISD assigns helps determine what services your child will receive.

The IEP: Individualized Education Program

What is an IEP?

If your child qualifies for special education in MARBLE FALLS ISD, the team will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). This is a legal document that describes the services and supports your child will receive.

A MARBLE FALLS ISD IEP includes:

  • Current level of academic and behavioral performance
  • Measurable annual educational goals
  • Specialized services (speech therapy, psychology, etc.)
  • Accommodations and modifications
  • Educational placement (regular class, special education classroom, etc.)
  • Transition to adult services (starting at age 14)

Your Role in the IEP

In MARBLE FALLS ISD, you are an equal member of the IEP team. Your voice and perspective are valuable. It is not enough to simply attend; you must actively participate in decisions.

If you do not agree with the IEP proposed by MARBLE FALLS ISD, you have options. You can request an additional meeting, bring a special education attorney, or pursue dispute resolution.
