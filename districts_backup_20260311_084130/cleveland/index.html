Understanding Special Education in CLEVELAND ISD

CLEVELAND ISD special education services are designed to meet the unique learning needs of students with disabilities in grades Pre-K through 12. Our district serves students with a wide range of disabilities and learning differences, from autism spectrum disorder and speech impairments to intellectual disabilities and orthopedic impairments.

Special education in CLEVELAND ISD is guided by federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas Education Code requirements. Every student in our district who qualifies receives a free, appropriate public education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment (LRE) possible.

Our special education programs are tailored to individual student needs rather than one-size-fits-all approaches. Whether your child attends school in downtown CLEVELAND or another area of our district, we maintain consistent, high-quality services across all campuses.

The ARD Process: Getting Started with CLEVELAND ISD Special Education

The CLEVELAND ISD ARD meeting is your gateway to special education services. ARD stands for "Admission, Review, and Dismissal," and it's the formal process where parents, teachers, and specialists come together to discuss your child's educational needs.

What Happens at Your First ARD Meeting

Your initial CLEVELAND ISD ARD meeting typically includes evaluation results, teacher observations, and parent input. The committee reviews assessment data to determine if your child qualifies for special education under one of 13 disability categories recognized by Texas law.

  • Review of comprehensive evaluations in academics, speech, occupational therapy, and other areas
  • Discussion of your child's strengths and challenges
  • Determination of eligibility for special education services
  • Initial development of an Individualized Education Program (IEP)

Important timeline: From initial referral to completion of evaluation and eligibility determination, CLEVELAND ISD has 60 calendar days to complete this process. Parents in CLEVELAND ISD receive written notice of all decisions and have the right to request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) if you disagree with district findings.

Annual and Triennial ARD Meetings

Once your child qualifies for special education in CLEVELAND ISD, annual ARD meetings review progress and update services. Every three years, a comprehensive reevaluation occurs to determine if your child continues to qualify for special education services.

Between scheduled meetings, you can request an ARD meeting anytime if you believe your child's needs have changed or if current services aren't meeting educational goals.

IEP Services: Customized Education Plans in CLEVELAND ISD

CLEVELAND ISD IEP services form the foundation of your child's special education program. An IEP is a legally binding document that outlines your child's present levels of performance, annual goals, specialized instruction, and related services.

Components of Your Child's CLEVELAND ISD IEP

Every IEP developed by CLEVELAND ISD includes several critical components that work together to support your child's learning:

  1. Present Levels of Performance: Clear descriptions of how your child's disability affects learning in academic and functional areas
  2. Measurable Annual Goals: Specific, realistic objectives your child will work toward during the school year
  3. Specialized Instruction: The specific services and modifications your child receives, including frequency and duration
  4. Related Services: Speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, or other services needed for educational success
  5. Accommodations and Modifications: Changes to how your child accesses curriculum or demonstrates knowledge
  6. Progress Monitoring: How CLEVELAND ISD will track your child's advancement toward IEP goals
  7. Placement and LRE: The setting where your child receives services (general education classroom, resource room, or specialized setting)

CLEVELAND ISD families receive progress reports on IEP goals at least as frequently as grades are reported to non-disabled peers, typically every nine weeks.

CLEVELAND ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

CLEVELAND ISD parent rights are protected under IDEA and Texas Education Code §29.001-29.017. Understanding these rights empowers you to be an effective advocate for your child throughout the special education process.

Your Key Rights as a Parent in CLEVELAND ISD

Parents of students in CLEVELAND ISD special education have the right to:

  • Prior written notice of any changes to your child's identification, evaluation, or placement
  • Access to educational records and request amendments if information is inaccurate
  • Participate meaningfully in all ARD meetings and decision-making processes
  • Request an Independent Educational Evaluation at district expense if you disagree with CLEVELAND ISD evaluations
  • Obtain interpreters or translated documents if English is not your primary language
  • File a due process complaint or request mediation if you believe CLEVELAND ISD is not providing a free, appropriate public education
  • Access your child's records without unnecessary delay and at reasonable cost

CLEVELAND ISD provides a Procedural Safeguards Notice to every parent explaining these rights in plain language. Request this document at your child's school or the district's special education department.

Specialized Programs: Dyslexia Services and More

The CLEVELAND ISD Dyslexia Program

The CLEVELAND ISD dyslexia program serves students with dyslexia and related disorders through specialized instruction grounded in evidence-based reading science. Dyslexia is a specific learning disability affecting reading, spelling, and written language that requires targeted intervention.

Students identified through the dyslexia screening process in CLEVELAND ISD receive specialized reading instruction that emphasizes:

  • Phonemic awareness and phonics
  • Fluency development
  • Vocabulary instruction
  • Reading comprehension strategies
  • Written expression support

The CLEVELAND ISD dyslexia program employs certified dyslexia specialists and uses intensive, multi-sensory instructional approaches. Instruction typically occurs in addition to general education reading instruction and is based on your child's specific pattern of strengths and weaknesses.

Other Specialized Programs in CLEVELAND ISD

Beyond dyslexia services, CLEVELAND ISD offers specialized programs including:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) services: Behavioral support, social skills instruction, and sensory-friendly environments
  • Speech-Language Pathology: Services for students with speech, language, and communication disorders
  • Occupational and Physical Therapy: Support for students with motor and functional needs
  • Emotional/Behavioral Support: Counseling and behavioral interventions for students with emotional disturbances
  • Deaf/Hard of Hearing services: Interpreters, auditory support, and specialized instruction
  • Visually Impaired services: Braille instruction and mobility training when appropriate
