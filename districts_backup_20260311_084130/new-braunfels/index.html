Understanding NEW BRAUNFELS ISD Special Education Services

NEW BRAUNFELS ISD special education serves students ages 3-22 who qualify under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). The district provides a continuum of services ranging from inclusive classroom support to specialized programs for students with significant support needs.

Special education in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD is built on the foundation of individualized education, ensuring each child receives customized instruction and support. The district's special education department works collaboratively with families to identify needs, set meaningful goals, and monitor progress throughout the school year.

Parents of students in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD have legal protections under both federal and Texas state law. Understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child's educational success.

The ARD Meeting Process for NEW BRAUNFELS ISD Parents

The Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting is the cornerstone of special education planning in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD. This is where your child's IEP gets created, reviewed, and updated annually. The ARD team includes parents, teachers, administrators, and specialists who work together to develop your child's individualized education plan.

What Happens During a NEW BRAUNFELS ISD ARD Meeting

  • Initial Referral Review: If your child is being evaluated for the first time, the ARD team discusses assessment results and determines eligibility for special education services
  • IEP Development: Team members collaborate to write goals, identify services, and determine placement in the least restrictive environment
  • Annual Reviews: Every 12 months, your child's progress is reviewed and the IEP is updated as needed
  • Progress Monitoring: Quarterly reviews may occur to assess whether goals are being met

Timeline for NEW BRAUNFELS ISD ARD meetings: After your initial referral, the district has 60 school days to complete evaluation and hold an ARD meeting. Annual ARD meetings must occur within a 12-month window from the previous meeting date.

Your Rights During NEW BRAUNFELS ISD ARD Meetings

Parents in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD have specific rights during the ARD process. You have the right to participate fully, bring an advocate or attorney, request independent evaluations, and take time to review documents before signing. You do not have to agree with the team's recommendations—you can request additional time, request a second opinion, or pursue dispute resolution.

NEW BRAUNFELS ISD IEP Services and Supports

The Individualized Education Program (IEP) is the legal document that guides your child's special education in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD. Your child's IEP specifies services, accommodations, modifications, and goals tailored to their unique needs.

Types of Services Available Through NEW BRAUNFELS ISD

Related Services: NEW BRAUNFELS ISD provides related services including speech-language pathology, occupational therapy, physical therapy, counseling, social work services, and other supports as needed. These services are provided when they enable your student to benefit from special education.

Instructional Services: Depending on your child's disability category and severity of need, services range from consultative support in general education classrooms to full-time specialized instruction. NEW BRAUNFELS ISD offers resource room programs, self-contained classrooms, and inclusion models.

Assistive Technology: Students who need assistive technology devices or software to access curriculum receive these supports through their IEP. NEW BRAUNFELS ISD special education evaluates and provides appropriate technology solutions.

Behavioral Support: For students whose behavior impacts learning, NEW BRAUNFELS ISD develops Behavior Intervention Plans (BIPs) and provides behavioral support services. Positive behavior strategies and classroom modifications are written into the IEP when needed.

Specialized Programs in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD

NEW BRAUNFELS ISD operates specialized programs for students with autism spectrum disorder, emotional and behavioral disorders, intellectual disabilities, physical disabilities, and other health impairments. The district also provides dyslexia services through its intervention programs, recognizing that dyslexia is a specific learning disability requiring targeted instruction.

NEW BRAUNFELS ISD Dyslexia Program and Intervention Services

The NEW BRAUNFELS ISD dyslexia program serves students identified with dyslexia and other reading disabilities. Under Texas Education Code §38.003, districts must provide dyslexia intervention services using research-based practices, and NEW BRAUNFELS ISD maintains a specialized dyslexia program to meet this requirement.

Dyslexia Screening and Identification in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD

Students in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD are screened for dyslexia in kindergarten and first grade. Additional students may be referred for evaluation if they show signs of reading difficulty. Dyslexia assessment includes comprehensive reading evaluation, cognitive processing, and phonological awareness testing.

When your child is identified with dyslexia in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD: The district develops a dyslexia intervention plan, often incorporated into the child's IEP if they also qualify for special education. Intervention typically includes structured, multisensory reading instruction delivered by specially trained teachers.

What to Expect From NEW BRAUNFELS ISD Dyslexia Services

  • Evidence-based reading intervention using programs like Orton-Gillingham, Wilson Reading System, or similar structured approaches
  • Frequency and duration based on severity of reading difficulty
  • Progress monitoring every 4-6 weeks to measure intervention effectiveness
  • Parent updates and home strategies to support reading development
  • Accommodations such as extended time on reading tasks and assistive technology

Parent Rights and Procedural Safeguards in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD

NEW BRAUNFELS ISD is legally obligated to inform parents of their rights in writing. These procedural safeguards ensure fair treatment and protect your family's interests throughout the special education process.

Key Parent Rights in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD Special Education

  • Right to Involvement: You have the right to participate in all ARD meetings and decisions about your child's special education
  • Right to Independent Evaluation: If you disagree with NEW BRAUNFELS ISD's evaluation, you can request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at no cost in certain circumstances
  • Right to Prior Written Notice: The district must provide written notice before proposing or refusing to change your child's special education program
  • Right to Confidentiality: Your child's special education records are protected; NEW BRAUNFELS ISD must obtain your permission before sharing information
  • Right to Dispute Resolution: If you disagree with NEW BRAUNFELS ISD's decisions, you can request mediation or file a due process complaint
  • Right to Specialized Services: Your child has the right to related services, accommodations, and modifications as needed to access education

Parents in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD should receive a copy of procedural safeguards in their native language. This document explains your rights in detail and includes contact information for dispute resolution options.

Getting Started: How to Request Special Education Evaluation in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD

If you believe your child may need special education services through NEW BRAUNFELS ISD, requesting an evaluation is the first step. You can request evaluation at any time during the school year.

Steps to Request Special Education Evaluation

  1. Contact your child's school: Reach out to the campus principal, special education coordinator, or classroom teacher at your child's NEW BRAUNFELS ISD school
  2. Submit written request: Send a letter requesting evaluation for special education. Include specific concerns about learning, behavior, communication, or other areas
  3. Participate in problem-solving: NEW BRAUNFELS ISD may offer interventions before formal evaluation. Participate in meetings to document your child's response to support
  4. Provide consent for evaluation: Once you formally request evaluation, NEW BRAUNFELS ISD will send consent paperwork outlining the evaluation plan
  5. Attend ARD meeting: Within 60 school days, NEW BRAUNFELS ISD conducts evaluations and holds an ARD meeting to discuss results and determine eligibility

Keep records: Document all conversations, save emails, and keep copies of your child's work samples and teacher feedback. This information becomes invaluable during the special education process with NEW BRAUNFELS ISD.
