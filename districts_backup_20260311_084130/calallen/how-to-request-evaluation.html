Complete Guide to Requesting Special Education Evaluation in CALALLEN ISD

If your child is facing academic or developmental difficulties in CALALLEN ISD, you have the right to request a special education evaluation. This guide provides you with all the necessary steps to navigate the evaluation process in CALALLEN ISD and protect your child's rights.

Why Request an Evaluation in CALALLEN ISD?

Parents in CALALLEN ISD should consider requesting a special evaluation if their child shows persistent difficulties in learning, behavior, communication, or physical development. A comprehensive evaluation can identify disabilities that qualify students for specialized services and academic accommodations.

In CALALLEN ISD, evaluation is the first step in determining if your child needs an Individualized Education Program (IEP). Without a formal evaluation, your child cannot receive special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA).

Signs that your child may need evaluation

  • Significant difficulty in reading, math, or language
  • Delays in speech or language development
  • Behavioral problems that interfere with learning
  • Attention or focus difficulties in class
  • Motor coordination problems or physical skills
  • Anxiety, depression, or other mental health issues

Parent Rights in CALALLEN ISD

As a parent or guardian in CALALLEN ISD, you have rights protected by law during the evaluation process. These rights are guaranteed under IDEA and the Rehabilitation Act (Section 504).

Your main rights include:

  • Request evaluation: You can request a free evaluation at any time if you believe your child has a disability
  • Receive prior written notice: CALALLEN ISD must inform you in Spanish about any proposed evaluation
  • Participate in decisions: You have the right to participate in all meetings related to evaluation and services
  • Access to records: You can review all evaluations and educational records of your child
  • Spanish interpreter: CALALLEN ISD must provide Spanish interpreters during all important meetings
  • Legal representation: You can bring an attorney or special education advocate to any meeting

How to Request an Evaluation in CALALLEN ISD

The process of requesting a special evaluation in CALALLEN ISD is accessible and should be initiated promptly. Here are the specific steps for families in CALALLEN.

Step 1: Contact your child's teacher or counselor

Start by contacting the classroom teacher, school counselor, or special education coordinator in CALALLEN ISD. Express your concerns about your child's academic performance or behavior. These professionals can provide information about interventions that may have already been attempted.

Step 2: Send a written request to CALALLEN ISD

To protect your rights, send a formal written request for evaluation to the Director of Special Education of CALALLEN ISD. You can send this request by email, certified mail, or in person. Keep a copy for your records.

What to include in your request:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • School your child attends in CALALLEN ISD
  • Specific concerns about performance or behavior
  • Relevant disabilities or medical conditions
  • Clear request for a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE)
  • Your name, phone number, and email address

Step 3: CALALLEN ISD responds to your request

CALALLEN ISD has 15 days to respond to your evaluation request. During this time, the district must contact you to discuss concerns and schedule a consent conference for the evaluation (also called an FIE conference).

The Evaluation Process in CALALLEN ISD

Once you have requested an evaluation in CALALLEN ISD, the district will complete a comprehensive evaluation of your child. This process is free and does not require parents to pay anything.

Evaluation Consent Conference

Before the evaluation begins, CALALLEN ISD must obtain your written consent. In this conference, they will explain what evaluations will be conducted and why they are necessary. You can ask questions, suggest additional evaluations, and establish a timeline.

Important points: If you request specific evaluations (such as hearing tests, vision tests, or psychological evaluation), mention this during the consent conference. CALALLEN ISD must consider your requests.

Types of evaluations in CALALLEN ISD

During a special education evaluation in CALALLEN ISD, specialists may evaluate multiple areas:

  • Academic: Reading, math, writing, and other school skills
  • Cognitive: Intellectual ability and thinking skills
  • Language: Comprehension and expression of oral and written language
  • Behavioral: Social, emotional, and conduct patterns
  • Medical: Vision, hearing, and physical health exams
  • Neuropsychological: Brain functions and information processing

Evaluation Timeline

CALALLEN ISD must complete all evaluations within 60 calendar days from when you sign consent (unless there is an extended school break period). Evaluators may include school psychologists, speech-language pathologists, learning specialists, and other CALALLEN ISD professionals.

Evaluation Results Meeting in CALALLEN ISD

After completing all evaluations, CALALLEN ISD will schedule a meeting to review the results with you. This is a crucial meeting where important decisions about your child's services are made.

What to expect at the meeting

CALALLEN ISD evaluators will present findings in clear, non-technical language. They will explain what strengths your child has and in what areas they face difficulties. If your child qualifies for special education services, the CALALLEN ISD team will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE)

You can request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) in CALALLEN ISD at the district's expense. An IEE is an evaluation conducted by a specialist external to CALALLEN ISD. The district must provide a list of approved evaluators in the CALALLEN area.

Next Steps After the Evaluation in CALALLEN ISD

If your child qualifies for special education services in CALALLEN ISD, the next step is to develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). This document describes exactly what services and accommodations your child will receive in CALALLEN ISD.

The IEP in CALALLEN ISD will include: Measurable academic goals, special services (such as speech therapy), classroom accommodations, service schedule, and transition plans for older students.

Families in the CALALLEN area and parents of CALALLEN ISD should regularly review their child's IEP progress and request review meetings annually or when necessary. Your active participation is crucial for your child's academic success in CALALLEN ISD.
