Complete Guide: How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in MERCEDES ISD

If your child is experiencing academic or behavioral difficulties at school, you have the legal right to request a special education evaluation through MERCEDES ISD. This guide will explain exactly how to navigate the process, what to expect, and how to protect your family's rights in the MERCEDES area.

Why Request a Special Education Evaluation in MERCEDES ISD?

Students who qualify for special education services in MERCEDES ISD receive specialized support, curricular accommodations, and additional services specifically designed for their needs. If your child shows signs of learning difficulties, physical disabilities, emotional problems, or developmental delays, a formal evaluation is the first step.

In MERCEDES ISD, the evaluation determines if your child is eligible to receive services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) or Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.

Signs your child might need an evaluation

  • Difficulty reading, writing, or doing math compared to peers their age
  • Frequent behavioral problems or extreme emotional changes
  • Difficulty following instructions or completing tasks
  • Delay in speech or language development
  • Attention problems or hyperactivity
  • Social difficulties or interaction with other students

Your Rights as a Parent in MERCEDES ISD

Texas and federal law protect the rights of MERCEDES ISD parents at every step of the special education process. It is important that you know these rights before starting.

Fundamental rights in MERCEDES ISD

  • Right to request evaluation: Any parent can formally request an evaluation without undue delay
  • Right to participate: You must be present at all planning and decision-making meetings
  • Right to complete information: MERCEDES ISD must provide you with all information about evaluations and results in Spanish
  • Right to free services: MERCEDES ISD evaluations and services are free of charge for parents
  • Right to a second opinion: If you disagree with MERCEDES ISD's results, you can request an independent evaluation
  • Right to mediation and due process: If there is disagreement, you can request mediation or a due process hearing

Step 1: Submitting the Formal Evaluation Request in MERCEDES ISD

The first step to initiate the MERCEDES ISD special evaluation process is to make a formal, written request. Although the law states that the request can be verbal, always request in writing to create a documented record.

How to submit your request

Option 1: Written letter

Write a letter addressed to the Director of Special Education of MERCEDES ISD. Include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current school grade
  • Specific school in MERCEDES ISD where they attend
  • Clear description of your academic, behavioral, or developmental concerns
  • Specific examples of difficulties you have observed
  • The phrase: "I formally request a comprehensive evaluation to determine if my child is eligible for special education services"
  • Your name, phone number, and email address
  • Date of the request

Option 2: In person

Visit the MERCEDES ISD Special Education office. Bring your identification and your child's information. Ask staff to document your verbal request in writing and provide you with a dated copy.

Where to send your request in MERCEDES ISD

Contact MERCEDES ISD directly for the specific mailing address or email of the Special Education Department. Be sure to send the letter by certified mail or deliver it in person to have proof of receipt.

Step 2: MERCEDES ISD Timeline to Begin the Evaluation

Once MERCEDES ISD receives your request, Texas law establishes specific and mandatory deadlines.

Legal timeline in MERCEDES ISD

  • Within 15 days: MERCEDES ISD must acknowledge your request in writing and explain the next steps
  • Within an additional 15 days: MERCEDES ISD must obtain your signed written consent (total of 30 days from the request)
  • Within 60 days: MERCEDES ISD must complete the comprehensive evaluation from the day you sign the consent

If MERCEDES ISD does not meet these deadlines, you can file a formal complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

Step 3: Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in MERCEDES ISD

The MERCEDES ISD FIE (Full Individual Evaluation) assesses your child in multiple areas to create a complete picture of their strengths and needs.

Areas MERCEDES ISD evaluates

  • Academic skills: Reading, writing, math, science
  • Language processing: Comprehension, expression, vocabulary
  • Cognitive functioning: General intelligence, reasoning, memory
  • Motor development: Gross and fine motor skills
  • Social and emotional status: Behavior, social interactions, emotional state
  • Hearing and vision: Basic sensory evaluations

Who participates in the MERCEDES ISD evaluation

MERCEDES ISD uses a multidisciplinary team that may include:

  • School psychologist
  • Special education specialist
  • Speech/language pathologist (if necessary)
  • Regular classroom teacher
  • You as a parent

As a parent in MERCEDES ISD, you have the right to be present during any evaluation you wish to witness.

Step 4: Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) Committee Meeting in MERCEDES ISD

After completing the evaluation, MERCEDES ISD will schedule an ARD committee meeting to discuss the results and determine eligibility.

What to expect at the MERCEDES ISD ARD meeting

The meeting will include:

  • Detailed review of evaluation results
  • Clear explanation of what the results mean in understandable language
  • Determination of whether your child is eligible for services
  • If eligible, development of the Individualized Education Program (IEP)
  • Discussion of educational placement and support services

Your rights during the ARD meeting in MERCEDES ISD

  • Bring a special education attorney or parent advocate
  • Request information before the meeting
  • Ask questions about results you don't understand
  • Disagree and request time to consider the decision
  • Request an independent evaluation if you disagree

Step 5: Individualized Education Program (IEP) in MERCEDES ISD

If your child is found eligible, MERCEDES ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) that details all services and accommodations.

Components of the IEP in MERCEDES ISD

  • Current levels of performance: Description of strengths and needs
  • Annual goals: What your child is expected to achieve in one year
  • Required services: Therapy, tutoring, assistant help, etc.
  • Accommodations: Changes in how material is taught or tested
  • Educational placement: Where services will be received in MERCEDES ISD
  • Transition: For students aged 14 or older, planning for after school

The IEP is a legally binding document. MERCEDES ISD must comply exactly with what is written.

Frequently Asked Questions: MERCEDES ISD Special Education

Can my request be rejected by MERCEDES ISD?

MERCEDES ISD cannot reject your initial request for evaluation. However, after the evaluation, they may determine that your child is not eligible. If you disagree, you can request an independent evaluation or mediation.

Cost of the MERCEDES ISD evaluation?

No. All MERCEDES ISD evaluations and services are completely free for parents. If you believe MERCEDES ISD is not adequately evaluating, you can request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at MERCEDES ISD's expense.

Can I change schools within MERCEDES ISD after the evaluation?

Possibly. The MERCEDES ISD ARD committee will consider the least restrictive educational options. If you wish to change placement, discuss it during the meeting.

How often does MERCEDES ISD review the IEP?

MERCEDES ISD must review and update the IEP at least once a year. Parents can request more frequent meetings if necessary.
