Understanding the FIE: Your First Step

A Full Individual Evaluation is a thorough assessment designed to determine whether your child has a disability and if they qualify for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). For BROWNSBORO ISD families, requesting an FIE is often the gateway to getting your child the support they need.

The evaluation process in BROWNSBORO ISD is part of the district's broader child find responsibility. This means BROWNSBORO ISD is legally required to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities, regardless of the severity of their condition.

An FIE typically includes assessments in multiple areas such as cognitive ability, academic achievement, social-emotional functioning, motor skills, and adaptive behavior. The results help determine if your child qualifies for services and what those services should include.

BROWNSBORO ISD Child Find: Know Your Rights

Every parent in BROWNSBORO ISD should understand child find, which is a federal requirement under IDEA. BROWNSBORO ISD must actively search for and identify children who may need special education evaluation, even if parents haven't formally requested one.

How BROWNSBORO ISD Identifies Children for Evaluation

  • Teacher referrals based on academic or behavioral concerns
  • Screening assessments given to all students
  • Parent requests for evaluation
  • School counselor or administrator recommendations
  • Results from state-mandated assessments

If your child attends school in BROWNSBORO ISD and you've noticed delays, learning difficulties, behavioral challenges, or any area of concern, you have the right to request a BROWNSBORO ISD special education evaluation at any time.

How to Request a Full Individual Evaluation in BROWNSBORO ISD

Step 1: Submit Your Written Request

Your request for an BROWNSBORO ISD FIE evaluation must be in writing. This is critical—verbal requests, while they may be acknowledged, don't trigger the same legal timeline protections.

Send your written request to the Director of Special Education at BROWNSBORO ISD. You can typically find this person's name and contact information on the district website or by calling the main district office. Your letter should clearly state that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation for your child.

What to include in your request:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current school and grade level
  • Specific areas of concern (academic, behavioral, physical, social, etc.)
  • Brief description of why you believe evaluation is needed
  • Your contact information and preferred communication method
  • Request for evaluation in specific areas if applicable

Step 2: Keep Documentation of Your Request

Make a copy of your written request and send it via email or certified mail. Email is often the safest option because you receive confirmation of delivery. Keep this documentation—it serves as your proof of the date BROWNSBORO ISD received your request, which is crucial for the 45-day timeline.

Step 3: Expect Contact Within 5-10 School Days

BROWNSBORO ISD should contact you within a few school days to acknowledge receipt of your request. This initial contact may be a phone call or email. During this conversation, the special education team will begin gathering information about your child's history and concerns.

Understanding the BROWNSBORO ISD 45-Day Timeline

One of the most important protections for parents in BROWNSBORO ISD is the 45-day evaluation timeline. This requirement comes from the Texas Education Code and federal IDEA regulations.

What the 45-Day Timeline Means

BROWNSBORO ISD must complete a Full Individual Evaluation within 45 calendar days from the date you submit your written request. This deadline is non-negotiable unless you agree in writing to extend it.

The 45-day clock starts when BROWNSBORO ISD receives your written request. This includes time for:

  • Obtaining parental permission to evaluate
  • Administering all assessment tools
  • Scoring and analyzing results
  • Writing the evaluation report
  • Scheduling and conducting the Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting

Extensions to the 45-Day Timeline

BROWNSBORO ISD can only extend the 45-day timeline if you agree to it in writing. Reasons for extension might include language barriers, inability to complete testing, or need for additional assessments. Never agree to an extension without understanding why it's necessary.

The BROWNSBORO ISD Evaluation Process: What to Expect

Phase 1: Permission and Initial Information Gathering (Days 1-5)

BROWNSBORO ISD will send you a Permission to Evaluate form. You must sign and return this before any testing occurs. During this phase, the special education team gathers information about your child's developmental history, previous assessments, and current concerns.

Phase 2: Comprehensive Assessment (Days 5-25)

Licensed evaluators administer multiple assessments depending on areas of concern. In BROWNSBORO ISD, evaluations typically include:

  • Cognitive or intellectual assessments
  • Academic achievement tests
  • Behavioral or social-emotional scales
  • Speech/language evaluations (if applicable)
  • Occupational or physical therapy assessments (if applicable)
  • Classroom observations and teacher input

Phase 3: Report Development and ARD Meeting (Days 25-45)

After testing is complete, BROWNSBORO ISD compiles results into a comprehensive Evaluation Report. This report includes scores, observations, and recommendations. The district then schedules an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting where results are discussed and eligibility is determined.

At this BROWNSBORO ISD ARD meeting, the team determines whether your child qualifies for special education services under one or more disability categories recognized by IDEA.

BROWNSBORO ISD Special Education Eligibility Categories

For your child to qualify for services in BROWNSBORO ISD, they must meet criteria for at least one of these disability categories:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Deaf-Blindness
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Specific Learning Disability
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Visual Impairment

Additionally, the child must need special education services as a result of the disability. It's not enough to have a diagnosis; BROWNSBORO ISD must determine that special education is required.

Your Rights During the BROWNSBORO ISD Evaluation Process

You are a critical member of the BROWNSBORO ISD evaluation team. You have the right to participate in every meeting, ask questions, and request clarification about assessment results and recommendations.

Right to Request Independent Evaluation

If you disagree with BROWNSBORO ISD's evaluation results, you have the right to request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at public expense. You must make this request in writing within specific timeframes.

Right to Prior Written Notice

BROWNSBORO ISD must provide you with Prior Written Notice explaining all decisions, the reasons for decisions, and your procedural rights. This notice must be in your native language if applicable.

Right to Interpreter Services

If English is not your primary language, BROWNSBORO ISD must provide an interpreter during meetings and evaluations at no cost to your family.

Common Concerns Parents in BROWNSBORO ISD Ask

Will Getting an Evaluation Label My Child?

A BROWNSBORO ISD special education evaluation does not automatically label your child. The evaluation simply determines whether your child qualifies for services. Many children are evaluated but don't qualify for special education—they may qualify for a 504 Plan instead.

What if BROWNSBORO ISD Says My Child Doesn't Qualify?

If BROWNSBORO ISD determines your child doesn't qualify for special education, you can request an Independent Educational Evaluation, file a due process complaint, or explore 504 Plan eligibility. You also retain the right to request re-evaluation at any time with new concerns.

Can BROWNSBORO ISD Refuse to Evaluate My Child?

No. BROWNSBORO ISD cannot refuse a reasonable request for evaluation. If you believe your child may have a disability and needs special education services, BROWNSBORO ISD must evaluate. Refusal violates federal law.

Moving Forward: Next Steps After BROWNSBORO ISD Evaluation

The IEP outlines specific services, goals, accommodations, and support your child will receive.

If your child doesn't qualify for special education but has a disability, explore whether a 504 Plan is appropriate. 504 Plans provide accommodations and modifications to help your child access education.
