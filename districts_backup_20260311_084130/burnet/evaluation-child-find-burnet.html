Complete Guide to Requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in BURNET CISD

If you're a parent in BURNET CISD concerned about your child's learning or development, understanding how to request a BURNET CISD FIE evaluation is one of the most important steps you can take. This comprehensive guide walks you through the entire process, timelines, and your rights as a BURNET CISD family.

Understanding the FIE Process in BURNET CISD

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine whether your child has a disability and needs special education services. In BURNET CISD, the FIE evaluation process is governed by both federal law (IDEA) and Texas Education Code requirements.

BURNET CISD special education evaluations are thorough and multifaceted, examining your child's cognitive abilities, academic performance, social-emotional functioning, and any other areas of concern. The goal is to gather complete information to make informed decisions about your child's educational needs.

Parents in BURNET CISD should know that requesting an FIE is your legal right, and the district must respond appropriately to your request. The district cannot refuse an evaluation without good reason, and you have the right to disagree with results and request independent evaluations.

How to Request a BURNET CISD Child Find Evaluation

The first step in accessing a BURNET CISD child find evaluation is making a formal request. Child Find is the district's responsibility to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities who need special education services.

Step 1: Submit Your Request in Writing

The most important thing to remember is that your request for a BURNET CISD evaluation request should be in writing. This creates an official record and starts the legal timeline.

  • Email the request to your child's campus special education department or the BURNET CISD special education office
  • Include a letter or email stating that you're requesting a full individual evaluation
  • Explain why you believe your child may need special education services
  • Keep a copy for your records with the date sent
  • Consider sending via email with read receipt or certified mail for verification

Step 2: Communicate Your Concerns

In your written request, be specific about your concerns. Share concrete examples of difficulties your child is experiencing—whether academic, behavioral, social, or developmental.

Examples of concerns that warrant evaluation include:

  • Significant difficulty keeping up academically compared to peers
  • Trouble with reading, math, or writing skills
  • Difficulty paying attention or staying organized
  • Social or behavioral challenges affecting learning
  • Speech, language, or communication concerns
  • Physical or motor skill difficulties
  • Suspected autism spectrum disorder or ADHD

Understanding the BURNET CISD 45 Day Timeline

One of the most critical pieces of information for BURNET CISD families is the evaluation timeline. Texas law and IDEA require that once you submit a written request for special education evaluation, the district has specific deadlines to follow.

The BURNET CISD 45 Day Timeline Explained

After you submit a written request for a BURNET CISD special education evaluation, BURNET CISD has 45 calendar days to complete the evaluation and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committee meeting. This timeline is non-negotiable under Texas Education Code.

Important dates to track:

  1. Day 0: You submit your written request
  2. Days 1-5: BURNET CISD sends permission to evaluate form home
  3. Days 6-40: BURNET CISD conducts the evaluation (tests, observations, questionnaires)
  4. Days 41-45: ARD committee meeting held and Individualized Education Program (IEP) developed if eligible

The district can extend the timeline only in very limited circumstances—typically only if your family is unavailable or requests additional time. You should receive updates on progress before the 45 days concludes.

What Happens If BURNET CISD Misses the Deadline?

If BURNET CISD fails to complete the evaluation within 45 days without your agreement, this is a procedural violation. You may have grounds to file a due process complaint or request compensatory services to make up for the delay.

The BURNET CISD Special Education Evaluation Request Process

Understanding each component of the BURNET CISD special education evaluation process helps you know what to expect and how to support your child.

Permission to Evaluate

After you submit your request, BURNET CISD must send home a Permission to Evaluate form. This form explains what evaluations will be conducted and asks for your written permission to proceed.

  • You must sign and return this form before testing begins
  • You can request additional evaluations or limit specific assessments
  • BURNET CISD cannot begin evaluation without your written permission
  • Keep a copy of this signed form for your records

Comprehensive Evaluation Components

A complete BURNET CISD FIE evaluation typically includes multiple assessment areas:

  • Cognitive/IQ Testing: Measures thinking and reasoning abilities
  • Academic Assessment: Evaluates reading, math, writing, and other academic skills
  • Speech/Language Evaluation: If communication concerns exist
  • Social-Emotional/Behavioral Assessment: Examines behavior and emotional functioning
  • Classroom Observations: Teachers and specialists observe your child in natural settings
  • Parent and Teacher Input: Questionnaires and interviews about your child's development
  • Review of School Records: Examination of grades, attendance, and previous assessments

What You Can Do During Evaluation

Parents in BURNET CISD have active roles during the evaluation process. Don't just sit on the sidelines—actively participate in your child's evaluation.

  • Complete rating scales and questionnaires honestly and thoroughly
  • Provide detailed developmental and medical history
  • Share examples of concerns you've noticed at home
  • Ask questions about specific tests being used
  • Request that evaluators explain results in plain language
  • Bring in outside evaluations or medical reports from doctors

Your Rights During BURNET CISD Special Education Evaluation

It's crucial to understand that parents in BURNET CISD have significant legal rights throughout the special education process. These rights are protected under federal and state law.

Key Parent Rights in BURNET CISD

  • Right to Request Evaluation: You can request FIE at any time if you believe your child needs evaluation
  • Right to Notice: BURNET CISD must explain everything in your native language
  • Right to Participate: You must be invited to and can participate in all ARD meetings
  • Right to Records: You can review all evaluation reports and educational records
  • Right to Independent Evaluation: If you disagree with BURNET CISD results, you can obtain an independent FIE at district expense
  • Right to Due Process: You can file a complaint if BURNET CISD violates your child's rights

When BURNET CISD Denies an Evaluation Request

In rare cases, BURNET CISD may decline to evaluate, but they must provide written explanation with supporting evidence. If you believe the denial is inappropriate, you can:

  • Request a meeting to discuss why evaluation was denied
  • File a special education complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA)
  • Consult with a special education attorney near BURNET for guidance
  • Request a due process hearing through TEA

ARD Committee Meeting and Results in BURNET CISD

The Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committee meeting is where BURNET CISD families receive evaluation results and make decisions about special education services.

What Happens at the ARD Meeting

Within the BURNET CISD 45 day timeline, the ARD committee meets to review evaluation findings. This committee includes:

  • Parents (you are required to be invited)
  • Special education teacher
  • General education teacher
  • School administrator
  • Evaluators/specialists who conducted assessments
  • Other professionals as needed

At this meeting, BURNET CISD presents evaluation results and discusses whether your child qualifies for special education. If eligible, the team develops an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Understanding Eligibility Categories in BURNET CISD

BURNET CISD special education covers 13 disability categories under IDEA. Your child must qualify under at least one category and need special education services:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment (including ADHD)
  • Specific Learning Disability
  • Speech-Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Visual Impairment
