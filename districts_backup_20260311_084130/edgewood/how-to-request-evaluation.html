Complete Guide to Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in EDGEWOOD ISD

If your child is experiencing academic or behavioral difficulties at school, you have the right to request a special education evaluation in EDGEWOOD ISD. This guide will explain step-by-step how to navigate the request process and what to expect during the evaluation.

Why Is It Important to Request a Special Education Evaluation?

Special education in EDGEWOOD ISD is designed to provide additional support to students who have disabilities that affect their learning. A FIE (Full and Individual Evaluation) is the first step in determining if your child qualifies for special services.

Qualifying students may receive accommodations, curriculum modifications, and support services specifically designed for their needs. In EDGEWOOD ISD, these services are provided under an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Do not wait for problems to worsen. Parents in EDGEWOOD ISD who act early generally see better academic and behavioral outcomes for their children.

Parent Rights in EDGEWOOD ISD

What Texas Law Says

According to the Texas Education Code and the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), parents in EDGEWOOD ISD have specific rights. These rights include the right to request an evaluation, participate in all IEP meetings, and decline services if desired.

Parent rights in EDGEWOOD ISD also include access to all of your child's information, the right to bring an attorney or special education advocate to meetings, and the right to appeal decisions with which you disagree.

Your Right to Request an Evaluation

As a parent in EDGEWOOD ISD, you can request an evaluation at any time during the school year. You do not need permission from a teacher or administrator. The request should be in writing, although a verbal request must also be honored.

EDGEWOOD ISD has the legal responsibility to respond to your request within 15 school days. During this time, the school may accept the request or propose an alternative diagnostic evaluation.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in EDGEWOOD ISD

Step 1: Prepare Your Written Request

The best way to document your request is to do so in writing. This documentation is important if you need to file a formal complaint or work with a special education attorney or special education advocate near EDGEWOOD.

Your request should include:

  • Your child's full name
  • Your name and contact information (parent/guardian)
  • Current grade and school in EDGEWOOD ISD
  • Specific description of academic or behavioral concerns
  • Any previous evaluations or diagnoses (if applicable)
  • Your signature and date

Step 2: Submit the Request to the Special Education Department

In EDGEWOOD ISD, you should submit your request to the Special Education Department or directly to the campus principal. You can deliver the request in person, send it by certified mail, or send it by email (always request confirmation of receipt).

Keep a copy of your request and any confirmation of receipt. This documentation will be valuable if any questions arise regarding timelines or if you need to work with a special education attorney in the EDGEWOOD area.

Step 3: Await Response from EDGEWOOD ISD

EDGEWOOD ISD has 15 school days to respond to your request. The school may:

  • Accept the request and proceed with the FIE evaluation
  • Propose an alternative diagnostic evaluation (such as RTI - Response to Intervention)
  • Refuse the request (though rarely happens)

If EDGEWOOD ISD proposes an alternative to formal evaluation, you may agree or insist on a full special education evaluation.

Step 4: Participate in the Eligibility Determination Meeting

Once the evaluation is complete, EDGEWOOD ISD will schedule a meeting within 60 days from when the request was accepted. In this meeting, the school team will present the results and determine if your child is eligible for special education services.

You have the right to bring someone to support you, such as a family member, a special education attorney, or a special education advocate near EDGEWOOD. This person can help you understand the information and advocate for your child's rights.

Areas of Evaluation in EDGEWOOD ISD

When EDGEWOOD ISD conducts a special education evaluation, it must assess multiple areas to get a complete picture of your child's functioning. These areas include:

Academic Evaluation

Specialists in EDGEWOOD ISD will assess your child's reading, math, and writing skills. Standardized tests, classroom work samples, and teacher observations will be used.

Behavioral Evaluation

Your child's behavioral patterns, social skills, and ability to follow instructions will be examined. This may include interviews with teachers, parents, and the student themselves.

Language Development Evaluation

A speech-language pathologist can assess your child's language comprehension, verbal expression, and communication skills. This is especially important for Spanish-speaking students in EDGEWOOD ISD.

Psychological Evaluation

A school psychologist can conduct cognitive tests to assess intellectual ability, memory, attention, and other brain functions that affect learning.

Next Steps If Your Child Is Eligible

IEP Development (Individualized Education Program)

If your child is declared eligible for special education in EDGEWOOD ISD, the team will develop an IEP. This document details educational goals, the services your child will receive, and how progress will be measured.

As a parent, you have the right to fully participate in the creation of the IEP. Your opinions and concerns are important and should be incorporated into the plan.

Available Support Services

Depending on your child's identified needs, EDGEWOOD ISD may offer:

  • Special education in an inclusive classroom
  • Special education services in a resource classroom
  • Speech and language pathology services
  • School health services
  • Occupational or physical therapy services
  • Behavioral and emotional support

Practical Tips for Parents in EDGEWOOD ISD

Documentation is Key

Keep detailed records of all communications with EDGEWOOD ISD. Include dates, names of school personnel, topics discussed, and any agreed-upon actions. These records are valuable if you need to work with a special education attorney or advocate.

When communicating academic or behavioral concerns, be as specific as possible. Instead of saying "my child struggles with reading," say "my child cannot decode multi-syllable words and their reading speed is two years below grade level."

Actively Participate in EDGEWOOD ISD

Attend all scheduled meetings, ask questions, and provide information about your child's functioning at home. Your participation is a key factor in the success of the special education process in EDGEWOOD ISD.

Know Your Rights

EDGEWOOD ISD must provide you with a document explaining your parent rights in EDGEWOOD ISD. Read it carefully. If you have questions, ask the special education coordinator or consult with a special education advocate in the EDGEWOOD area.
