Why Request a Special Education Evaluation in LOCKHART ISD?

The initial evaluation is the first step to determine if your child qualifies for special education services. LOCKHART ISD parents should know that a professional evaluation can identify learning disabilities, hearing or vision problems, emotional difficulties, or developmental needs requiring specialized support.

Many families in the LOCKHART area wait too long before requesting an evaluation. The sooner special needs are identified, the sooner your child can receive the necessary services and support to achieve academic success.

A formal evaluation in LOCKHART ISD is different from informal classroom assessments. This professional evaluation examines multiple areas of development and provides a diagnosis that opens access to services protected by federal law.

Your Rights as a Parent in LOCKHART ISD

As a parent in LOCKHART ISD, federal law (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act - IDEA) guarantees you specific rights. You have the right to request an evaluation at no cost to your family. LOCKHART ISD must evaluate your child thoroughly and without bias.

Parental rights in LOCKHART ISD include the right to:

  • Request an evaluation at any time during the school year
  • Receive information about the process in your language (Spanish)
  • Participate in all meetings and decisions about your child
  • Obtain a copy of all evaluation results
  • Have an interpreter during meetings in LOCKHART ISD
  • Challenge decisions you disagree with

Legal Protections in LOCKHART ISD

LOCKHART ISD must comply with the deadlines established by Texas law. They cannot deny your evaluation request without legitimate reason. If they have concerns about whether your child truly needs evaluation, they must document those concerns in writing.

How to Request a FIE Evaluation in LOCKHART ISD

Step 1: Submit the Formal Request

The first step is to formally request a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation) in LOCKHART ISD. Although you can verbally mention your concerns, it's important to submit a written request to establish a legal start date.

You can request the evaluation in several ways:

  • Send a letter to your school's principal in LOCKHART ISD
  • Visit the LOCKHART ISD Special Education office directly
  • Contact your campus's evaluation coordinator
  • Ask your child's teacher to initiate a referral

Your request should clearly state that you are asking for a full evaluation to determine eligibility for special education. You don't need to use specific words, just express your concerns about your child's learning or development.

Step 2: Understand the Deadlines in LOCKHART ISD

Once LOCKHART ISD receives your written request, they have 15 calendar days to respond. During this time, an evaluation team will determine which tests to administer and who will conduct them.

The complete evaluation must be completed within 60 calendar days from the request. After completing the evaluation, LOCKHART ISD must meet with you within 30 days to discuss the results. These deadlines are legal and mandatory.

Mark these milestones on your calendar:

  1. Day 15: LOCKHART ISD responds and obtains your consent to evaluate
  2. Day 60: The evaluation must be completed
  3. Day 90: The meeting to discuss results and determine eligibility takes place

Step 3: The Evaluation Process in LOCKHART ISD

The special education evaluation in LOCKHART ISD examines multiple areas of development. A multidisciplinary team, which may include school psychologists, speech-language pathologists, special education teachers, and other professionals, will administer different tests to your child.

Areas typically evaluated include:

  • Cognitive and intellectual abilities
  • Language and communication development
  • Academic skills (reading, math, writing)
  • Social and emotional development
  • Motor skills (gross and fine)
  • Hearing and vision
  • Developmental history and parent information

During this process, LOCKHART ISD will also gather important information from you as a parent. They will be interested in medical history, early development, home behavior, and any specific concerns you may have.

Important Information to Provide to LOCKHART ISD

For the evaluation in LOCKHART ISD to be as complete and accurate as possible, prepare the following information:

  • Medical history: Pregnancy, birth, illnesses, current medications
  • Developmental history: When they started talking, walking, and other milestones
  • Home behavior: How they behave outside of school, relationships with siblings
  • Specific academic concerns: Difficulties with reading, math, writing
  • Previous records: Prior evaluations, therapies, diagnoses
  • Contact information for other providers: Doctor, therapist, specialist

Although not all of these items will be necessary, having them available speeds up the evaluation process in LOCKHART ISD and provides a complete picture of your child's development.

What Happens After the Evaluation in LOCKHART ISD

The Eligibility Meeting

After completing the evaluation, LOCKHART ISD will schedule a meeting (called an Admission, Review, and Dismissal or ARD Meeting) to discuss the results. In this crucial meeting, the LOCKHART ISD team will present its findings and determine if your child qualifies for special education services.

Your child qualifies if:

  • They have a recognized disability under IDEA, AND
  • As a result of that disability, they need special education

If Your Child Qualifies

If LOCKHART ISD determines that your child qualifies, they will create an Individualized Education Program (IEP). This document specifies the services your child will receive, academic goals, and how progress will be measured. As a parent in LOCKHART ISD, you are an equal member of the IEP team.

If Your Child Does Not Qualify

If LOCKHART ISD determines that your child does not qualify for special education under IDEA, but has learning difficulties, they may be eligible for services under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act. Ask specifically about this option at LOCKHART ISD.
