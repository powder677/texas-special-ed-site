Signs Your Child Might Need an Evaluation

Many parents in BRIDGE CITY ISD wonder if their child truly needs an evaluation. You do not need a prior diagnosis to request a BRIDGE CITY ISD special evaluation. If you observe any of the following indicators, it is appropriate to make a request.

Academic Indicators

  • Your child has significant difficulties with reading, writing, or mathematics
  • They are not progressing at the same rate as their classmates
  • They need constant accommodations or help to complete tasks
  • They have difficulty understanding instructions or following directions

Behavioral and Developmental Indicators

  • Problems socializing or interacting with other children
  • Delays in speech or language development

Health and Sensory Indicators

  • Uncorrected vision or hearing problems
  • Health conditions that affect attendance or learning
  • Unusual movement or coordination

If your child in BRIDGE CITY ISD presents any of these indicators, a special education evaluation is an appropriate step to identify needs and develop a support plan.

How to Formally Request a FIE Evaluation in BRIDGE CITY ISD

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is the formal process by which BRIDGE CITY ISD determines if your child qualifies for special education services. Here's how to initiate the process.

Step 1: Contact Your Child's Campus

Contact your child's elementary, middle, or high school directly in the BRIDGE CITY area. Ask to speak with the principal, counselor, your child's teacher, or the BRIDGE CITY ISD special education coordinator.

During this initial conversation, briefly explain your concerns about your child's learning or development. School staff in BRIDGE CITY ISD must take them seriously and provide information on how to proceed.

Step 2: Submit a Written Request

Although a verbal request is valid, it is best to send a written evaluation request to BRIDGE CITY ISD. This creates an official record and starts the legal evaluation timeline.

What to include in your letter:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • School name and current grade
  • Specific description of your academic, behavioral, or developmental concerns
  • Concrete examples of difficulties you have observed
  • Any relevant previous evaluations or medical diagnoses
  • Your name, phone number, and email address
  • A clear request: "I formally request that BRIDGE CITY ISD conduct a Full Individual Evaluation"

Send this letter to the campus principal and mark it for certified mail receipt. Keep a copy for your records.

Step 3: BRIDGE CITY ISD Responds within 15 Days

By law, BRIDGE CITY ISD has 15 school days to respond to your evaluation request. This response must include an evaluation proposal explaining what will be evaluated, how it will be evaluated, and where it will take place.

BRIDGE CITY ISD must also provide you with a "Notice of Parents' Rights" document in Spanish that explains your rights during the special evaluation process.

The Evaluation Process in BRIDGE CITY ISD

In BRIDGE CITY ISD, evaluators may include school psychologists, special education teachers, speech pathologists, and other specialists as per your child's needs.

The Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) Meeting

What Happens at the BRIDGE CITY ISD ARD Meeting

The ARD meeting in BRIDGE CITY ISD will include you as a parent, teachers, specialists who evaluated your child, and administrators. Together, they will review the evaluation results and decide if your child is eligible for services under IDEA or Section 504.

Possible outcomes in BRIDGE CITY ISD:

  • Eligible for Section 504: Your child will receive accommodations in the general classroom but not a formal IEP

If your child is found eligible, BRIDGE CITY ISD will develop a detailed plan at that same meeting (or at a later meeting) that specifies services, accommodations, and educational goals.
