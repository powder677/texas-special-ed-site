Understanding the FIE Evaluation Process in CALHOUN COUNTY ISD

A Full Individual Evaluation, commonly called an FIE, is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine whether a child has a disability and qualifies for special education services under IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) or Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.

For families in CALHOUN COUNTY ISD, the CALHOUN COUNTY ISD special education evaluation process begins when school personnel or a parent suspects a child may have a disability. The evaluation looks at all areas of suspected disability, including academic performance, cognitive abilities, social-emotional functioning, and adaptive behaviors.

Understanding this process empowers CALHOUN COUNTY ISD parents to advocate effectively for their children. The district is required by law to conduct evaluations that are fair, comprehensive, and conducted in the child's native language.

Your Child Find Rights in CALHOUN COUNTY ISD

Child find is a critical responsibility of every school district. CALHOUN COUNTY ISD child find obligations mean the district must actively search for and identify children who may have disabilities, from birth through age 21, regardless of the severity of their disability.

Who Can Request an Evaluation?

  • Parents or guardians of students enrolled in CALHOUN COUNTY ISD
  • Teachers and school staff who suspect a disability
  • Doctors and healthcare providers who have evaluated your child
  • The school district itself through its child find process

In CALHOUN COUNTY, you don't need permission from anyone to request an evaluation. As a parent, you have the fundamental right to request a CALHOUN COUNTY ISD evaluation request at any time during the school year.

Reasons to Request an Evaluation

Parents in CALHOUN COUNTY ISD should consider requesting an evaluation if their child shows signs of:

  • Difficulty keeping up academically despite effort and support
  • Significant behavioral or emotional challenges
  • Speech, language, or hearing concerns
  • Attention or focus difficulties
  • Social interaction struggles
  • Motor skill delays or coordination problems
  • Suspected learning disabilities

Step-by-Step Process for Requesting a CALHOUN COUNTY ISD FIE Evaluation

Step 1: Request in Writing

To initiate a CALHOUN COUNTY ISD FIE evaluation, you must submit a written request to your child's school or the Special Education Department. In CALHOUN COUNTY ISD, written requests ensure there's documentation of your request and the date received.

Your written request should include:

  • Your child's full name and grade level
  • Specific areas of concern (academic, behavioral, social, etc.)
  • Examples of difficulties you've observed at home
  • Any previous evaluations or diagnoses
  • Your contact information and preferred communication method

Email the request to your campus principal or the CALHOUN COUNTY ISD Special Education Department. Keep a copy for your records and request a receipt confirming the date your request was received.

Step 2: Initial Consultation Meeting

Within a reasonable timeframe after your CALHOUN COUNTY ISD evaluation request, the school will schedule an initial meeting to discuss your concerns. This meeting, sometimes called a pre-evaluation conference, is an opportunity for school personnel and parents to discuss the child's strengths and weaknesses.

During this meeting, the district must explain:

  • What evaluation procedures will be used
  • The purpose of each assessment
  • The CALHOUN COUNTY ISD 45 day timeline for completing the evaluation
  • Your rights as a parent
  • How results will be used to determine eligibility

Come prepared with observations, previous school records, medical history, and any outside evaluations you've obtained. This information helps CALHOUN COUNTY ISD special education professionals understand your child's complete picture.

Step 3: Obtain Parental Consent

Before conducting any evaluation, CALHOUN COUNTY ISD must obtain your written consent. You have the right to:

  • Ask questions about proposed evaluations
  • Request additional assessments
  • Refuse to allow certain evaluations
  • Take time to review consent documents

Do not sign anything until you fully understand what you're authorizing. If you have questions about the evaluation plan, ask for clarification before signing the consent form.

The 45-Day Timeline: What You Need to Know

The 45 days ends with: The completion of all assessments and the scheduling of an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss results and eligibility determination.

Exceptions to the timeline: In limited circumstances, CALHOUN COUNTY ISD may request an extension, but this requires written justification and your agreement. The extension cannot exceed an additional 30 days.

  • Review academic records and previous evaluations

What to Expect at the ARD Meeting

Once the CALHOUN COUNTY ISD FIE evaluation is complete, the school will schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting within the 45 day timeline. This meeting is where results are discussed and eligibility is determined.
