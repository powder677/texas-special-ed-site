Understanding SOUTHSIDE ISD Special Education Services

Parents in SOUTHSIDE ISD should know that special education is not a place—it's a service. Our goal is to provide the least restrictive environment where your child can access a quality education alongside their peers while receiving specialized instruction tailored to their needs.

The IEP Process: What Parents in SOUTHSIDE ISD Need to Know

SOUTHSIDE ISD IEP services begin with the development of an Individualized Education Program, a legally binding document that outlines your child's educational goals, accommodations, and services. Understanding this process is essential for parents navigating special education in SOUTHSIDE ISD.

Referral and Evaluation

The process starts with a referral—either from a parent, teacher, or SOUTHSIDE ISD staff member. Once referred, SOUTHSIDE ISD has a specific timeline to evaluate your child. Parents in SOUTHSIDE ISD should expect the evaluation process to take approximately 60 calendar days from the date of referral.

During evaluation, SOUTHSIDE ISD special education professionals will conduct comprehensive assessments in all areas of suspected disability. You'll receive detailed evaluation reports explaining the results and recommendations.

ARD Meeting and IEP Development

The SOUTHSIDE ISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) is where your child's IEP is created or updated. ARD stands for the committee that includes parents, teachers, special education staff, and school administrators who work together to develop your child's educational program.

At your SOUTHSIDE ISD ARD meeting, the team will:

  • Review evaluation results and your child's current performance
  • Determine your child's eligibility for special education services
  • Set annual goals and short-term objectives
  • Decide on accommodations, modifications, and related services
  • Determine the appropriate educational setting
  • Establish a transition plan (for students age 14 and older)

SOUTHSIDE ISD families should prepare for ARD meetings by gathering information about your child's strengths, challenges, and learning style. Bring any outside evaluations, medical records, or documentation that might be relevant to the discussion.

SOUTHSIDE ISD Dyslexia Program and Support

Dyslexia is one of the most common learning disabilities, and SOUTHSIDE ISD recognizes the importance of early identification and intervention. Our SOUTHSIDE ISD dyslexia program is designed to provide specialized instruction based on current research.

Dyslexia Screening and Identification

Texas Education Code requires that all students in grades K-2 be screened for dyslexia. The SOUTHSIDE ISD dyslexia program includes universal screening to identify students who may have dyslexia or related disorders. If screening indicates a need for further evaluation, parents will be notified and offered the opportunity for comprehensive assessment.

Intervention and Instruction

Students identified with dyslexia in SOUTHSIDE ISD receive specialized instruction using evidence-based, multisensory approaches. The SOUTHSIDE ISD dyslexia program emphasizes phonics, phonemic awareness, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension strategies specifically designed for students with dyslexia.

Parents in SOUTHSIDE ISD can expect:

  • Targeted, intensive reading instruction
  • Regular progress monitoring and data collection
  • Accommodations such as extended time on reading assignments
  • Technology supports and assistive devices when appropriate
  • Regular communication with parents about progress

Parent Rights and Advocacy in SOUTHSIDE ISD

Understanding your rights is crucial when navigating special education. SOUTHSIDE ISD parent rights are protected under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code. Families in SOUTHSIDE ISD should be aware of these protections from the moment you enter the special education process.

Key Parent Rights in SOUTHSIDE ISD

SOUTHSIDE ISD parent rights include:

  • Prior written notice: You must receive written notice before SOUTHSIDE ISD proposes or refuses any action regarding your child's identification, evaluation, or placement
  • Informed consent: SOUTHSIDE ISD must obtain your written consent before conducting an initial evaluation or providing services
  • Participation in ARD meetings: Parents have the right to participate in all ARD meetings regarding their child's special education
  • Access to records: You have the right to review and obtain copies of all educational records at no cost
  • Independent evaluation: If you disagree with SOUTHSIDE ISD's evaluation, you can request an independent evaluation at district expense under certain conditions
  • Due process: You have the right to file a due process complaint if you believe SOUTHSIDE ISD is violating your child's rights

Parent Involvement and Advocacy

Active parent participation is essential for success in SOUTHSIDE ISD special education. Come prepared to ARD meetings with observations about your child's learning, questions about instruction, and ideas for supporting progress at home.

Consider working with special ed advocate SOUTHSIDE or parent liaisons if you need support navigating the process. SOUTHSIDE ISD provides information about parent training resources and support groups for families in our district.

Parents in SOUTHSIDE ISD should expect transition planning to include:

The transition process in SOUTHSIDE ISD helps ensure that graduating students have the skills and supports needed to transition successfully into the community and workplace.
