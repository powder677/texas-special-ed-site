Why Request a Special Education Evaluation in LAMPASAS ISD?

The Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is the first step to determine if your child qualifies for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). Parents in LAMPASAS ISD should understand that this evaluation is free and can open doors to valuable resources and supports for their child's academic success.

Signs that your child might benefit from an evaluation include:

  • Difficulty keeping up with peers academically
  • Recurrent or challenging behavioral problems
  • Delays in language, speech, or motor skills
  • Significant difficulties with reading, writing, or mathematics
  • Attention or concentration problems
  • Anxiety, depression, or other emotional challenges

In LAMPASAS ISD, with a student population of 3,640 students, our district is committed to identifying all students who require special services.

Your Rights as a Parent in LAMPASAS ISD

As a parent of a student in LAMPASAS ISD, you have specific special education rights under federal and Texas state laws. Knowing these rights is essential to effectively advocate for your child.

Fundamental Rights in LAMPASAS ISD

Parents in LAMPASAS ISD have the right to:

  • Request evaluation: You can formally request a full evaluation at no cost
  • Participate in meetings: You must be invited to all meetings concerning evaluation and placement
  • Access records: You can review all academic and evaluation records of your child
  • Obtain reports in Spanish: LAMPASAS ISD must provide key documents in Spanish
  • Prior consent: The school needs your written consent before conducting evaluations
  • Due process: You have the right to resolve disputes through mediation or a due process hearing

Steps to Request an FIE Evaluation in LAMPASAS ISD

The process of requesting a LAMPASAS ISD special evaluation is straightforward and designed for families. Follow these specific steps:

Step 1: Contact Your Child's School

Contact your child's principal, teacher, or counselor at their LAMPASAS ISD school. Clearly express your concerns about your child's academic performance or development. You can do this in person, by phone, or by email.

Step 2: Formal Written Request

To formally initiate the process of requesting an evaluation in LAMPASAS ISD, you must send a written request to the special education department. This request should include:

  • Student's full name and date of birth
  • Current grade and school
  • Specific concerns about learning, behavior, or development
  • Any previous evaluations or interventions conducted
  • Your name, phone number, and email address

Keep a copy of your request. In LAMPASAS ISD, it is advisable to send the request by registered email or certified mail to have a record of the submission date.

Step 3: Informed Consent Meeting

LAMPASAS ISD must schedule a meeting within five business days of receiving your request. In this meeting, the school will explain:

  • The purpose of the evaluation
  • What areas will be evaluated
  • Who will conduct the evaluation
  • The estimated time to complete the evaluation
  • How the results will be shared

You will be asked to sign a consent form authorizing the evaluation. It is not mandatory to sign immediately; you may take time to review the documentation.

Step 4: Full Evaluation

Once LAMPASAS ISD receives your signed consent, the multidisciplinary evaluation team will conduct a comprehensive evaluation. This evaluation typically includes:

  • Cognitive and psychological tests
  • Academic evaluations (reading, writing, mathematics)
  • Speech and language evaluation
  • Physical and motor evaluation
  • Socio-emotional evaluation
  • Student's medical and educational history

The team in LAMPASAS ISD must complete this evaluation within 60 calendar days of receiving your consent.

Step 5: Findings Meeting

LAMPASAS ISD will schedule a meeting to review the evaluation results. This meeting must occur before any placement decisions are made. You have the right to:

  • Receive a complete evaluation report at least 5 days in advance
  • Ask questions about the findings
  • Request clarification on terminology or results
  • Bring a support person to the meeting

Understanding Evaluation Results in LAMPASAS ISD

After LAMPASAS ISD completes the evaluation, you will receive a detailed report. This report is crucial and should include:

Components of the Evaluation Report

  • Findings for each evaluated area: The results of each test and evaluation
  • Interpretation of findings: What the results mean in simple terms
  • Recommendations: What type of services or support is recommended
  • Eligibility determination: Whether your child qualifies for special education under a specific category

It is important to review this report carefully. If you don't understand something, do not hesitate to contact LAMPASAS ISD for clarification. You can request that any document be translated into Spanish.

After the Evaluation: The Next Step in LAMPASAS ISD

If LAMPASAS ISD determines your child is eligible, the next step is to develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). The IEP team in LAMPASAS ISD includes:

  • You (parent)
  • A special education teacher
  • A general education teacher
  • An administrative representative from LAMPASAS ISD
  • An evaluation specialist
  • Other specialists as needed (speech pathologist, psychologist, etc.)

The IEP will establish your child's annual goals, the services they will receive, and how progress will be measured in LAMPASAS ISD.

Frequently Asked Questions about Special Education in LAMPASAS ISD

How much does the evaluation cost in LAMPASAS ISD?

The special education evaluation in LAMPASAS ISD is completely free for parents. You should not pay anything for the evaluation, meetings, or related services.

Yes. If you disagree with LAMPASAS ISD's results, you can request an independent re-evaluation at LAMPASAS ISD's expense. This is called an "Independent Educational Evaluation" (IEE).
