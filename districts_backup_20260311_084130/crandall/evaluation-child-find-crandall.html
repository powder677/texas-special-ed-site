Complete Guide to Requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in CRANDALL ISD

If you're a parent in CRANDALL ISD concerned about your child's academic progress or developmental delays, understanding how to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is one of the most important steps you can take. This comprehensive guide walks you through the entire process, timelines, and your rights as a parent in CRANDALL ISD.

Understanding the CRANDALL ISD Evaluation Process

A CRANDALL ISD child find evaluation is the formal gateway to special education services. Under federal law and Texas Education Code, CRANDALL ISD is required to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities who may need special education support.

The CRANDALL ISD FIE evaluation is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine if your child has a disability and whether they qualify for special education services. This isn't a simple screening—it's an in-depth examination involving multiple assessments, observations, and professional evaluations.

Many parents in CRANDALL ISD don't realize they can request an evaluation at any time during the school year. You don't need a referral from a teacher or administrator to begin this process. Your request triggers specific legal timelines that CRANDALL ISD must follow.

Who Can Request an Evaluation in CRANDALL ISD

Parents Have Strong Rights

As a parent of a student in CRANDALL ISD, you have the right to request a CRANDALL ISD special education evaluation for your child. This is your right under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §29.004.

You don't need to prove your child has a disability before requesting an evaluation. You simply need to have concerns about your child's development, learning, behavior, or health that suggest a disability might be present.

School Personnel Can Also Request

Teachers, counselors, and other school staff in CRANDALL ISD can also initiate the CRANDALL ISD child find process if they observe potential signs of disability. However, your request as a parent carries equal weight and cannot be ignored or delayed.

Healthcare Providers and Others

Outside professionals, including pediatricians, therapists, and psychologists, can recommend that CRANDALL ISD conduct an evaluation. However, your written request as a parent is the most legally binding way to initiate the process.

How to Request a CRANDALL ISD Evaluation

Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing

The most important action you can take is to submit your evaluation request in writing. Don't rely on verbal requests or casual conversations with teachers. A written request creates a documented record and triggers the legal timelines CRANDALL ISD must follow.

Your request should be addressed to the CRANDALL ISD Director of Special Education or Special Education Department. Include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • The school your child currently attends
  • Your contact information
  • A brief description of your concerns (academic struggles, behavior concerns, developmental delays, etc.)
  • Your request for a full CRANDALL ISD FIE evaluation

Step 2: Send to the Right Department

Contact CRANDALL ISD's Special Education Department to obtain the current mailing address or email for evaluation requests. You can reach the district office to verify the proper submission method for your CRANDALL ISD evaluation request.

Consider sending your request via certified mail or email with a read receipt. This creates proof that CRANDALL ISD received your request on a specific date—important for verifying compliance with the CRANDALL ISD 45 day timeline.

Step 3: Document Everything

Keep copies of your written request and all communications with CRANDALL ISD. Include dates, names of staff members you speak with, and summaries of conversations. This documentation becomes valuable if you need to advocate for your child later.

The CRANDALL ISD 45 Day Timeline: What You Need to Know

The Legal Deadline

Once CRANDALL ISD receives your written request for a CRANDALL ISD special education evaluation, the district has 45 calendar days to complete the evaluation and provide you with results. This timeline is non-negotiable under Texas Education Code.

This CRANDALL ISD 45 day timeline begins on the date the district receives your written request, not when you verbally mention concerns to a teacher. This is why written documentation is critical for parents in CRANDALL ISD.

Important Exceptions to the Timeline

The timeline may be extended if:

  • You and CRANDALL ISD mutually agree in writing to extend the timeline
  • Your child is absent for 10 or more school days during the evaluation period
  • Circumstances prevent CRANDALL ISD from completing evaluations (though this is rare)

Any extension must be documented in writing. Don't allow CRANDALL ISD to exceed 45 days without your agreement and written documentation.

What Happens at Day 45

By day 45, CRANDALL ISD must schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting with you. At this meeting, the evaluation results are presented, and the team determines whether your child qualifies for special education services under IDEA.

What CRANDALL ISD Must Evaluate

Comprehensive Assessment Areas

A complete CRANDALL ISD FIE evaluation must assess your child in all areas of suspected disability. This typically includes:

  • Cognitive ability—intellectual functioning and reasoning
  • Academic performance—reading, math, writing, and overall achievement
  • Speech and language—communication skills and language development
  • Social-emotional functioning—behavior, emotional regulation, and mental health
  • Motor skills—gross and fine motor development
  • Adaptive behavior—daily living and self-care skills
  • Medical/health factors—vision, hearing, and other health considerations

Multiple Methods of Assessment

CRANDALL ISD cannot rely on a single test or assessment. A comprehensive CRANDALL ISD evaluation includes standardized testing, classroom observations, teacher interviews, parent interviews, and review of existing records and work samples.

You have the right to participate in the evaluation process. Share information about your child's development, health history, family background, and strengths at home. This information helps evaluators in CRANDALL ISD gain a complete picture of your child's abilities and needs.

Your Rights During the Evaluation Process

You're a Full Team Member

As a parent in CRANDALL ISD, you have significant rights during the evaluation process. You must be:

  • Informed about all evaluation procedures CRANDALL ISD will use
  • Invited to participate in the evaluation meetings
  • Provided with a copy of the evaluation report
  • Included in all decisions about your child's assessment

Understanding Evaluation Results

CRANDALL ISD must explain evaluation results to you in clear, understandable language—not educational jargon. If you don't understand the findings, ask questions. You can also request an independent evaluation if you disagree with CRANDALL ISD's assessment results.

Prior Written Notice

You must receive Prior Written Notice from CRANDALL ISD before any evaluation begins. This notice explains why the evaluation is recommended, what procedures will be used, and your rights as a parent. Keep this document for your records.

Common Concerns Parents in CRANDALL ISD Face

"What If CRANDALL ISD Refuses to Evaluate?"

CRANDALL ISD cannot refuse your request for a CRANDALL ISD child find evaluation simply because school staff disagree with your concerns. If CRANDALL ISD denies your request, they must provide you with written explanation and information about dispute resolution options.

If you believe CRANDALL ISD wrongfully denied your evaluation request, you have the right to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA) or pursue due process.

"What If My Child Is Progressing But I Still Have Concerns?"

Good grades or academic progress don't disqualify your child from evaluation. A CRANDALL ISD special education evaluation can identify disabilities that aren't yet significantly affecting academic performance but may impact your child's learning potential.

"What If the Evaluation Takes Longer Than 45 Days?"

The CRANDALL ISD 45 day timeline is a legal requirement. If the district exceeds this timeline without your written agreement, document this violation. You may be entitled to compensatory education services or have grounds for a due process complaint.

Moving Forward: After the Evaluation

Understanding the ARD Meeting

After CRANDALL ISD completes the evaluation, you'll attend an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. At this meeting, the evaluation team discusses results and determines:

  • Whether your child qualifies for special education under IDEA
  • What disability category applies (if any)
  • What services your child needs
  • Where services will be provided

If Your Child Qualifies

CRANDALL ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) outlining specific services, goals, and accommodations. You're a full member of this team and must agree to the IEP before services begin.

If Your Child Doesn't Qualify

You can request an independent evaluation, appeal the decision, or request a due process hearing. You also maintain the right to request another evaluation from CRANDALL ISD in the future if circumstances change.
