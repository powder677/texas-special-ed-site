CORSICANA ISD Special Education: Complete Resource Guide for Parents

If you're a parent navigating special education in CORSICANA ISD, you're not alone. With nearly 6,000 students enrolled across our district, CORSICANA ISD serves hundreds of children with diverse learning needs. This comprehensive guide walks you through every aspect of CORSICANA ISD special education services, your rights, and the resources available to support your child's success.

Understanding Special Education Services in CORSICANA ISD

CORSICANA ISD special education programs are designed to provide individualized support for students with disabilities ages 3-22. Our district follows federal guidelines under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code requirements to ensure every eligible student receives a free appropriate public education (FAPE).

CORSICANA ISD families have access to a full continuum of services, from specialized instruction in general education classrooms to self-contained programs for students with significant support needs. Whether your child has autism, dyslexia, emotional/behavioral disabilities, intellectual disabilities, or physical disabilities, CORSICANA ISD maintains dedicated programs and trained specialists.

Who Qualifies for Special Education in CORSICANA ISD

Your child may qualify for services if they have a disability in one or more of these categories: autism spectrum disorder, emotional disturbance, hearing impairment, intellectual disability, orthopedic impairment, other health impairment, specific learning disability, speech/language impairment, traumatic brain injury, or visual impairment.

Simply having a diagnosis doesn't automatically qualify your child. The disability must impact educational performance and require specialized instruction. CORSICANA ISD conducts comprehensive evaluations at no cost to families to determine eligibility.

The ARD Meeting Process: Your Child's Education Plan

The ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting is the cornerstone of special education in CORSICANA ISD. This is where your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP) is created, reviewed, and updated annually.

What Happens in a CORSICANA ISD ARD Meeting

During a CORSICANA ISD ARD meeting, a team of educators, specialists, and parents collaborate to discuss your child's strengths, challenges, and learning goals. The ARD committee includes your child's teacher, special education director, general education teacher (if applicable), and you—the parent.

Your team will:

  • Review current assessment data and your child's performance
  • Discuss present levels of academic and functional performance
  • Set measurable annual goals aligned with grade-level standards
  • Determine the appropriate placement and services needed
  • Document accommodations and modifications to curriculum
  • Plan transition services (for students age 14+)

Timeline for CORSICANA ISD ARD Meetings

Initial ARD meeting: Within 30 calendar days of determining eligibility through evaluation. CORSICANA ISD will schedule this meeting and provide written notice with at least 5 business days' advance notice.

Annual ARD meetings: Required once per school year. Most CORSICANA ISD families schedule these in late spring to review progress and plan for the following year.

Interim ARD meetings: CORSICANA ISD can hold additional meetings if your child isn't making adequate progress toward goals, if you request changes to services, or if significant concerns arise.

CORSICANA ISD IEP Services and Specialized Instruction

The Individualized Education Program (IEP) developed at your CORSICANA ISD ARD meeting is your legal document guaranteeing specific services and support. CORSICANA ISD IEP services are tailored to your child's unique needs and might include general education classroom support, resource room instruction, specialized therapies, or self-contained programming.

Types of Services Available in CORSICANA ISD

Direct Special Education Services: Specialized instruction in reading, math, written expression, and other academic areas from certified special education teachers.

Related Services: Speech-language pathology, occupational therapy, physical therapy, counseling, and transportation assistance as documented needs in the IEP.

Behavioral Support Services: Functional behavior assessments, behavior intervention plans, and positive behavior supports for students with emotional or behavioral disabilities.

Assistive Technology: CORSICANA ISD provides assistive technology assessments and devices to support communication, mobility, and academic access.

Dyslexia Intervention in CORSICANA ISD

CORSICANA ISD recognizes dyslexia as a significant learning difference affecting many students. Our CORSICANA ISD dyslexia program includes screening, specialized intervention, and multisensory reading instruction based on the science of reading.

If your child struggles with reading, CORSICANA ISD provides dyslexia screening for students showing reading difficulties. Results help determine if your child needs dyslexia intervention services as part of their IEP or through Response to Intervention (RTI) support in general education.

CORSICANA ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

Texas law and federal IDEA regulations guarantee CORSICANA ISD parent rights in the special education process. Understanding these rights empowers you to be an effective advocate for your child.

Your Core Parent Rights in CORSICANA ISD

Right to Notice: CORSICANA ISD must provide written notice before evaluating your child, before changing services, or before discussing IEP changes. This notice must be in your native language and explain your rights.

Right to Participate: You have the right to attend all CORSICANA ISD ARD meetings, ask questions, request additional evaluations, and contribute your perspective about your child's needs and progress.

Right to an Independent Evaluation: If you disagree with CORSICANA ISD's evaluation, you can request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at district expense. CORSICANA ISD must consider this evaluation in IEP decisions.

Right to Dispute Resolution: CORSICANA ISD parents can request mediation or file a due process complaint if disagreements arise about identification, evaluation, placement, or services. These processes are free under state and federal law.

Right to Records Access: You can review your child's special education records, request copies, and request amendment of inaccurate information within CORSICANA ISD's files.

Protecting Your Rights: What Parents Should Know

Bring a trusted support person—spouse, family member, or advocate—to CORSICANA ISD ARD meetings. They can help you remember information and ensure your voice is heard. Consider requesting meetings in writing so you have documented notice.

Keep copies of all IEPs, progress reports, correspondence, and evaluation results. Document your child's progress at home and share specific examples at CORSICANA ISD ARD meetings. This creates a complete picture of your child's needs.

Advocates help you understand evaluations, prepare for meetings, ask questions, and ensure your child's needs are addressed.

Special Education Attorneys Near CORSICANA

If significant disputes arise with CORSICANA ISD—such as disagreements about appropriate placement, denial of necessary services, or disciplinary issues—a special education attorney near CORSICANA can protect your child's rights. Attorneys can represent you in due process hearings or court proceedings.

Transition Planning and Post-Secondary Services

Beginning at age 14 (or younger if CORSICANA ISD deems it appropriate), transition planning becomes part of your CORSICANA ISD ARD meeting agenda. This planning prepares your child for life after high school—whether that means college, vocational training, employment, or supported community living.

What Transition Planning Includes in CORSICANA ISD

CORSICANA ISD develops transition plans addressing post-secondary education, employment, independent living skills, and community participation. Your child will take vocational assessments, explore career options, and participate in work experiences or internships during high school.

CORSICANA ISD coordinates with adult service agencies—including Division of Rehabilitation Services (DARS) and Texas Department of Assistive and Rehabilitative Services—to ensure continuity of services after graduation.

How to Request an Evaluation or Initiate Special Education in CORSICANA ISD

If you believe your child needs special education evaluation, contact CORSICANA ISD's special education department directly. You have the right to request an evaluation in writing—CORSICANA ISD must respond within 15 school days.

Provide specific examples of learning or behavioral concerns: "My child struggles to decode words during reading instruction" or "My child has difficulty organizing assignments and completing tasks." CORSICANA ISD uses these descriptions to plan comprehensive evaluations.

The evaluation typically takes 2-4 weeks and includes academic testing, behavioral observations, teacher input, parent questionnaires, and assessments by special education specialists. CORSICANA ISD will schedule an ARD meeting to discuss results and determine eligibility.

Taking Action: Your Next Steps with CORSICANA ISD

  5. Seek support: Connect with parent advocates or attorneys if you have concerns about CORSICANA ISD's services or decisions
  6. Follow up: Request progress reports from CORSICANA ISD on your child's IEP goal achievement and service delivery
