Understanding the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in MEDINA VALLEY ISD

A Full Individual Evaluation is a comprehensive assessment conducted by MEDINA VALLEY ISD to determine whether your child has a disability and qualifies for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). The evaluation examines your child's academic performance, cognitive abilities, social-emotional functioning, and physical health to create a complete picture of their strengths and needs.

For MEDINA VALLEY ISD families, the FIE process is free and federally mandated. The district cannot charge you any fees for evaluations, meetings, or services related to special education. This evaluation is different from routine school testing—it's a detailed assessment designed specifically to identify disabilities and determine eligibility for special education support.

Why Your Child Might Need an FIE

Parents in MEDINA VALLEY ISD often pursue evaluations when they notice:

  • Significant gaps between your child's abilities and grade-level expectations
  • Difficulty with reading, writing, math, or processing information
  • Behavioral challenges or difficulty managing emotions
  • Speech or language concerns
  • Suspected autism, ADHD, or other developmental disorders
  • Physical disabilities affecting school participation
  • Lack of progress despite intervention attempts

How to Request a MEDINA VALLEY ISD Special Education Evaluation

Your request for a MEDINA VALLEY ISD special education evaluation must be made in writing to begin the formal process. This creates an official record and starts the legal timeline that protects your rights.

Step 1: Submit Your Written Request

Send a written request to the Director of Special Education at MEDINA VALLEY ISD. Your letter should include:

  • Your child's name, age, and grade level
  • Specific concerns about your child's learning or development
  • Examples of behaviors or skills you've noticed at home
  • Any previous evaluations or outside testing
  • Your contact information and preferred communication method

Keep your request simple and direct. You don't need legal language—honest descriptions of your concerns are most effective. Send your request via email, certified mail, or hand-delivery so you have proof of submission.

Step 2: Request Acknowledgment

MEDINA VALLEY ISD should acknowledge receipt of your evaluation request within 5 school days. If you don't hear from the district, follow up immediately. Documentation of your request is crucial for protecting your legal timeline.

Step 3: The 15-Day Meeting Timeline

Within 15 school days of receiving your written request, MEDINA VALLEY ISD must meet with you to discuss your concerns and explain the evaluation process. This meeting doesn't require formal paperwork yet—it's a conversation to understand your perspective and outline next steps.

At this meeting, ask about:

  • What specific areas will be evaluated
  • Which professionals will conduct the evaluation
  • Timeline for completing the assessment
  • How results will be shared with you
  • Your right to bring an advocate or attorney

The MEDINA VALLEY ISD 45-Day Timeline for Evaluation

The most important deadline in the special education process is the MEDINA VALLEY ISD 45 day timeline. Federal law requires that once MEDINA VALLEY ISD receives your written evaluation request, they must complete the entire FIE and hold an eligibility meeting within 45 calendar days.

This 45-day clock is your legal protection. Parents in MEDINA VALLEY ISD should understand that this deadline is firm—the district cannot extend it without your written consent. Mark this date on your calendar and follow up if you don't hear updates.

What Happens During the 45 Days

During this critical window, MEDINA VALLEY ISD will:

  1. Obtain consent: Get your written permission before conducting any formal assessments
  2. Conduct comprehensive testing: Administer evaluations in all suspected areas of disability
  3. Gather information: Review school records, teacher input, and developmental history
  4. Document findings: Create the Evaluation Report (also called the ER)
  5. Hold Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting: Meet with you to discuss results and determine eligibility

Extensions to the 45-Day Timeline

MEDINA VALLEY ISD may extend the 45-day timeline only if:

  • You request an extension in writing
  • Exceptional circumstances prevent completion (defined very narrowly in IDEA)
  • You agree to extended timelines in writing

Do not agree to informal delays. If MEDINA VALLEY ISD cannot meet the 45-day deadline, insist on written documentation of why and a specific new completion date.
