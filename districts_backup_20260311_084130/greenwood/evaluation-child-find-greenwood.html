Understanding the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in GREENWOOD ISD

A Full Individual Evaluation, or FIE, is a comprehensive assessment designed to identify whether your child has a disability that affects their ability to learn. For GREENWOOD ISD families, this evaluation is the gateway to accessing special education services and accommodations your child may need.

The FIE goes beyond classroom observations and teacher reports. It includes standardized testing, cognitive assessments, behavioral evaluations, and educational performance reviews. GREENWOOD ISD special education professionals conduct these evaluations to gather detailed information about your child's strengths, challenges, and learning needs.

Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code Section 29.004, GREENWOOD ISD must evaluate any child suspected of having a disability at no cost to families. This is a critical protection for GREENWOOD ISD parents.

GREENWOOD ISD Child Find: Your Right to Request Evaluation

GREENWOOD ISD has a legal obligation under "Child Find" requirements to identify, locate, and evaluate all children who may need special education services. This means the district actively looks for students who might benefit from evaluation, but parents can also initiate the process themselves.

You don't need a teacher referral or permission from school administrators to request an evaluation. As a parent in GREENWOOD ISD, you have the right to request a GREENWOOD ISD special education evaluation at any time, regardless of your child's current academic performance or behavior.

Common reasons GREENWOOD ISD parents request evaluations include:

  • Difficulty with reading, writing, or math skills
  • Challenges with attention and focus
  • Speech or language concerns
  • Social and emotional difficulties
  • Physical or motor skill delays
  • Suspected autism spectrum disorder
  • Behavioral concerns affecting learning

Step-by-Step: How to Request a GREENWOOD ISD Evaluation

Step 1: Contact GREENWOOD ISD Special Education Department

Start by reaching out to GREENWOOD ISD's special education department directly. You can request the contact information for your child's school or the district office. Ask specifically for the special education coordinator or child find coordinator for GREENWOOD ISD.

A simple phone call or email expressing that you'd like to request a GREENWOOD ISD FIE evaluation is the first step. You don't need formal language or legal documentation at this stage—just be clear about your concerns regarding your child's learning or development.

Step 2: Submit Your Evaluation Request in Writing

While a verbal request starts the process, GREENWOOD ISD requires written documentation to officially begin the evaluation timeline. Send a written request to the special education department at your child's school or the GREENWOOD ISD central office.

Your letter should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Your name and contact information
  • Specific concerns about your child's learning or development
  • A clear statement requesting a GREENWOOD ISD FIE evaluation
  • The date of your request

Pro tip: Send your request via email with read receipt or certified mail. GREENWOOD ISD special education timelines begin when they receive written notification, so having proof of delivery protects your family's rights.

Step 3: Expect Initial Contact from GREENWOOD ISD

Within a few business days, GREENWOOD ISD should contact you to acknowledge receipt of your evaluation request. A special education staff member will likely call or email to discuss your concerns and answer questions about the GREENWOOD ISD special education evaluation process.

This is your opportunity to share detailed information about what you've observed. Mention specific examples of challenges, when difficulties started, and any medical or developmental history that's relevant. The more information you provide to GREENWOOD ISD at this stage, the better prepared the evaluation team will be.

The GREENWOOD ISD 45-Day Timeline Explained

One of the most important protections for GREENWOOD ISD families is the mandatory 45-day evaluation timeline. Once GREENWOOD ISD receives your written request for a GREENWOOD ISD FIE evaluation, the district has exactly 45 calendar days to complete all testing and provide you with results and recommendations.

This timeline is protected under IDEA and Texas law. GREENWOOD ISD cannot delay or extend this deadline without your written permission. The 45 days includes:

  • All assessment and testing activities
  • Gathering information from teachers and parents
  • Analyzing results and preparing reports
  • Scheduling and conducting the evaluation meeting

Mark your calendar: If you submit your written request on a specific date, count forward 45 days. GREENWOOD ISD must complete the evaluation meeting by that deadline. If your request arrives near a major holiday or district break, the timeline still applies—plan accordingly.

If GREENWOOD ISD cannot meet the 45-day timeline, they must obtain your written consent to extend it. Never agree to an extension unless you have a specific reason and have confirmed the extension in writing.

What Happens During the GREENWOOD ISD Special Education Evaluation Process

Comprehensive Assessment Components

A complete GREENWOOD ISD FIE evaluation includes multiple areas of assessment. The evaluation team will gather information about your child's academic performance, cognitive abilities, social-emotional functioning, and physical health.

Your child may participate in:

  • Cognitive testing: Measures thinking skills, problem-solving, and reasoning
  • Achievement testing: Assesses reading, writing, and math skills
  • Speech and language screening: Evaluates communication abilities
  • Behavioral and social-emotional assessment: Examines behavior patterns and emotional functioning
  • Vision and hearing screening: Ensures sensory abilities aren't affecting learning
  • Classroom observation: GREENWOOD ISD staff observe your child in natural school settings

Parent Input During GREENWOOD ISD Evaluation

Your perspective as a parent is essential to the GREENWOOD ISD FIE evaluation. The evaluation team will ask you about your child's developmental history, health background, behavior at home, and learning concerns you've observed outside of school.

Prepare for your parent interview by writing down specific examples and observations. Think about milestones your child reached, any medical conditions or diagnoses, family history of learning or developmental differences, and how your child learns best. This information helps GREENWOOD ISD special education professionals understand your child's complete picture.

Teacher Input for the GREENWOOD ISD FIE

Current and previous teachers provide critical information to the GREENWOOD ISD evaluation team. They observe your child daily in academic and social settings and can describe specific learning patterns and challenges. GREENWOOD ISD special education staff will gather this input formally as part of the comprehensive evaluation process.

Your Rights During GREENWOOD ISD Special Education Evaluation

As a parent in GREENWOOD ISD, you have important legal protections during the evaluation process. Understanding these rights ensures your child's evaluation is thorough, fair, and properly documented.

Right to Participate in the Evaluation Meeting

You have the right to attend the meeting where GREENWOOD ISD discusses evaluation results and recommendations. This is called the Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. You can bring support people, ask questions, and voice concerns about your child's evaluation at this meeting.

Right to an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE)

If you disagree with GREENWOOD ISD's FIE evaluation results, you can request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) conducted by professionals outside the district. Under IDEA, GREENWOOD ISD must either fund the IEE or request a hearing to defend their evaluation.

Right to Special Education Attorney or Advocate Support

After the GREENWOOD ISD FIE Evaluation: What's Next?

Understanding Evaluation Results

At the conclusion of the 45-day GREENWOOD ISD evaluation timeline, you'll receive a comprehensive evaluation report. This document explains the testing conducted, results from each assessment area, and recommendations from the evaluation team.

The report should clearly state whether GREENWOOD ISD found your child eligible for special education services. If eligible, the report identifies your child's disability category under IDEA and explains how this disability affects their educational performance.

The ARD Meeting and IEP Development

If GREENWOOD ISD determines your child is eligible for special education, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) meeting will be scheduled. During this meeting, the GREENWOOD ISD team and your family develop the IEP—a legal document outlining your child's goals, services, and accommodations.

Come prepared with your own observations and ideas about what your child needs to succeed. This is a collaborative process where GREENWOOD ISD staff and parents work together to create an appropriate educational plan.

If Your Child Is Found Ineligible

Not every child who is evaluated qualifies for special education services under IDEA. However, your child might still be eligible for accommodations under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act or Texas Education Code provisions for struggling learners.

If GREENWOOD ISD finds your child ineligible, ask for specific reasons and request clarification on other support options available. This is when consulting with a special ed advocate GREENWOOD can be helpful—they can review the evaluation results and advise whether you should pursue an independent evaluation or appeal.
