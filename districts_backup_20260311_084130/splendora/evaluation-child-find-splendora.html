Complete Guide to Requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in SPLENDORA ISD

Understanding the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in SPLENDORA ISD

A Full Individual Evaluation is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine whether your child has a disability and needs special education services. In SPLENDORA ISD, this evaluation examines your child's cognitive abilities, academic performance, social-emotional functioning, and adaptive behaviors. The evaluation process is free for all parents and students in SPLENDORA ISD.

Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §29.004, SPLENDORA ISD is required to conduct a thorough and individualized evaluation before determining special education eligibility. This isn't a quick screening—it's a detailed assessment that provides the foundation for your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Why Your Child Might Need an FIE

Parents in SPLENDORA ISD should consider requesting an evaluation if their child shows signs of:

  • Difficulty keeping up academically compared to peers
  • Behavioral challenges at school or home
  • Speech, language, or hearing concerns
  • Motor skill delays or coordination problems
  • Attention or focus difficulties
  • Social interaction challenges
  • Suspected learning disabilities like dyslexia
  • Autism spectrum disorder characteristics

Trust your instincts as a parent. If you believe something isn't quite right, requesting a SPLENDORA ISD special education evaluation is your right and responsibility.

The Child Find Obligation in SPLENDORA ISD

SPLENDORA ISD has a "child find" responsibility, which means the district must actively locate, identify, and evaluate all children with disabilities, including those attending private schools in the SPLENDORA area. This is required under federal law, regardless of whether a parent has formally requested an evaluation.

As a parent, you should know that SPLENDORA ISD's SPLENDORA ISD child find process means teachers and staff are trained to recognize potential developmental or learning delays. However, you don't need to wait for the school to notice—you can take action yourself by requesting an evaluation directly.

What Triggers Child Find in SPLENDORA ISD

Schools in SPLENDORA ISD may initiate evaluation discussions if they observe:

  • Academic performance significantly below grade level
  • Behavioral incidents or disciplinary concerns
  • Teacher referrals based on classroom observations
  • Results from universal screening assessments
  • Parent concerns shared with school staff

Step-by-Step: How to Request an Evaluation in SPLENDORA ISD

Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing

The most important first step is to submit your request in writing. While verbal requests to teachers or counselors may be noted, a written request creates an official record and triggers the legal timeline. Send a letter to the Special Education Department at SPLENDORA ISD stating clearly that you are requesting a SPLENDORA ISD evaluation request for your child.

Your letter should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Your contact information (phone and email)
  • A brief description of your concerns
  • The date of your request
  • Your signature

Example: "I am requesting a Full Individual Evaluation for my child, [Child's Name], born [Date]. I have concerns about [specific areas: reading, behavior, social skills, etc.]. Please contact me to discuss this evaluation request."

Step 2: Submit Your Request to the Correct Department

Contact SPLENDORA ISD's Special Education Department directly. You can:

  • Hand-deliver your letter to the Special Education office
  • Email your request (and request a read receipt for documentation)
  • Mail your letter via certified mail
  • Give it directly to your child's school principal

For SPLENDORA ISD, keeping documentation of when you submitted your request is critical. Take photos of letters, save email confirmations, and note dates and names of staff who received your request.

Step 3: Understand the SPLENDORA ISD 45 Day Timeline

Once SPLENDORA ISD receives your written request, the district has 45 calendar days to complete the SPLENDORA ISD 45 day timeline for evaluation. This is a legal deadline established by federal law under IDEA.

Important timeline markers:

  • Days 1-5: School should acknowledge your request and gather background information
  • Days 5-20: District conducts testing and gathers assessment data
  • Days 20-40: Evaluators analyze results and prepare comprehensive reports
  • Day 45: SPLENDORA ISD must complete the evaluation and schedule an eligibility meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal or ARD meeting)

Mark day 45 on your calendar. If SPLENDORA ISD misses this deadline without a documented extension agreement, this is a serious concern to address.

Step 4: Participate in Pre-Evaluation Planning

SPLENDORA ISD staff will likely contact you before formal testing begins. During this conversation, the district should:

  • Explain what areas will be evaluated
  • Describe the specific tests and assessments
  • Answer questions about the evaluation process
  • Get your permission to evaluate (you'll sign consent forms)

Parents in SPLENDORA ISD have the right to ask questions, request clarification, and understand exactly what will be assessed. Don't hesitate to ask about specific tests or request explanations in plain language.

Step 5: Provide Relevant Background Information

SPLENDORA ISD evaluators will ask for developmental and medical history. Have ready:

  • Birth and developmental milestones
  • Medical history and current medications
  • Previous evaluations or assessments
  • Vision and hearing screening results
  • Behavioral concerns at home and school
  • Academic performance history
  • Family history of learning disabilities

This information helps SPLENDORA ISD create a complete picture of your child's needs.

What to Expect During the SPLENDORA ISD Evaluation Process

Types of Assessments in a SPLENDORA ISD FIE

A comprehensive SPLENDORA ISD special education evaluation typically includes:

  • Cognitive Testing: Measures thinking, reasoning, and problem-solving abilities
  • Academic Achievement Testing: Assesses reading, math, writing, and spelling skills
  • Speech and Language Evaluation: If applicable, evaluates communication abilities
  • Behavioral and Emotional Assessment: Explores social-emotional functioning
  • Classroom Observations: Teachers observe your child in natural school settings
  • Vision and Hearing Screening: Ensures sensory issues aren't misidentified as disabilities
  • Adaptive Behavior Assessment: Evaluates life and daily living skills

The specific assessments your child receives depend on SPLENDORA ISD's concerns and your child's needs. Multiple measures are required—the district cannot rely on a single test.

Your Child's Evaluation Experience

Many parents worry about how their child will experience testing. In SPLENDORA ISD, evaluations are designed to be low-stress and child-friendly. A trained evaluator (often a school psychologist) will work with your child in a quiet setting, typically during one or more sessions.

Your child won't "fail" an evaluation—there are no passing or failing grades. The evaluator is gathering information about how your child learns, thinks, and behaves compared to age-appropriate expectations.

The Eligibility Meeting: Understanding Your Results

What Happens at the ARD Meeting

After SPLENDORA ISD completes the evaluation, within the SPLENDORA ISD 45 day timeline, the district must schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. This meeting brings together:

  • You (the parent)
  • Your child's teacher(s)
  • A school administrator
  • The evaluator(s)
  • Your child (may be present, depending on age and IEP considerations)

At this meeting, SPLENDORA ISD presents evaluation findings and determines eligibility for special education services.

Understanding Eligibility Categories in Texas

If your child is found eligible, SPLENDORA ISD will identify a disability category. Under Texas Education Code §29.004, eligible categories include:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Deaf-Blindness
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Specific Learning Disability
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Visual Impairment

Not all concerns result in an eligible category. If SPLENDORA ISD determines your child doesn't meet criteria for special education, you have the right to request an independent evaluation or pursue other interventions.

Your Rights as a Parent in SPLENDORA ISD

Special Education Protections and Safeguards

Parents of students in SPLENDORA ISD have important rights throughout the evaluation process:

  • Prior Written Notice: SPLENDORA ISD must notify you in writing about evaluation decisions
  • Informed Consent: You must agree in writing before the district evaluates your child
  • Right to Participate: You have the right to attend all ARD meetings
  • Right to Records: You can access all evaluation reports and educational records
  • Right to an Advocate: You can bring a support person, advocate, or attorney to meetings
  • Right to Disagree: You can dispute evaluation findings and request an independent evaluation
  • Right to Translation: SPLENDORA ISD must provide services in your home language

Bringing Support to Meetings

You are absolutely entitled to bring someone to your SPLENDORA ISD evaluation meetings. This might be:

  • A family member or trusted friend
  • A special education advocate
  • A special ed advocate SPLENDORA families recommend from advocacy organizations
  • An attorney specializing in special education
  • A medical provider familiar with your child

SPLENDORA ISD staff should welcome your support person. Having an extra set of ears and an advocate on your side can be invaluable when discussing your child's evaluation results.

After the Evaluation: Moving Forward in SPLENDORA ISD

What Happens if Your Child is Found Eligible

If SPLENDORA ISD determines your child is eligible for special education, the next step is developing an Individualized Education Program (IEP). Your IEP meeting will address:

  • Your child's current performance levels
  • Measurable annual goals
  • Special education services and supports
  • Classroom placement and accommodations
  • Related services (speech, occupational therapy, counseling, etc.)
  • Progress monitoring and reassessment procedures

The IEP is legally binding, and SPLENDORA ISD must implement it as written. Regular communication with your child's IEP team ensures the plan continues meeting your child's needs.

What Happens if Your Child is Not Found Eligible

If SPLENDORA ISD determines your child doesn't meet special education eligibility criteria, alternative supports may still be available. Ask about:

  • Section 504 Plans (accommodations for students with disabilities not eligible for special education)
  • Response to Intervention (RTI) programs
  • General education accommodations
