Complete Guide to Requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in EVERMAN ISD

If you're a parent in EVERMAN ISD concerned about your child's learning or development, understanding how to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is one of the most important steps you can take. This comprehensive guide walks you through the entire process, timeline, and your rights under special education law.

Understanding Child Find and Your Rights in EVERMAN ISD

EVERMAN ISD has a legal obligation under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with suspected disabilities. This responsibility is known as "child find." As a parent in EVERMAN ISD, you have the right to request an evaluation for your child at any time if you believe they may have a disability affecting their education.

The EVERMAN ISD child find process ensures that no child goes unidentified or unevaluated. Whether your child attends public school, private school, or is homeschooled in the EVERMAN area, EVERMAN ISD must consider them for special education services if there's reason to believe they may qualify.

Your request doesn't require a diagnosis or medical documentation. You simply need to express concern about your child's learning, behavior, or development to trigger the evaluation process.

What is a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE)?

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in EVERMAN ISD is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine whether your child has a disability and whether they qualify for special education services under IDEA. This evaluation goes far beyond a simple academic test.

Components of an EVERMAN ISD FIE

An EVERMAN ISD special education evaluation typically includes:

  • Cognitive assessments – Tests measuring thinking, reasoning, and problem-solving abilities
  • Academic achievement testing – Evaluations of reading, math, writing, and language skills
  • Behavioral observations – Systematic observation in classroom and other settings
  • Social-emotional assessments – Evaluation of emotional well-being and social skills
  • Speech and language evaluation – Assessment of communication abilities (if applicable)
  • Occupational or physical therapy assessment – Evaluation of motor skills and sensory processing (if applicable)
  • Vision and hearing screening – Basic sensory checks to rule out sensory issues
  • Review of educational records – Examination of grades, attendance, and previous assessments

The evaluation team in EVERMAN ISD uses multiple sources of information to get a complete picture of your child's abilities and needs.

How to Request an EVERMAN ISD Evaluation

Requesting an EVERMAN ISD evaluation request is straightforward and your request must be taken seriously by the district. Here's how to proceed.

Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing

While verbal requests can start the process, sending a written request creates a formal record. Address your letter to the EVERMAN ISD Special Education Department or your child's campus principal. Include the date, your child's name, grade level, and a brief description of your concerns.

A simple letter stating "I am requesting a full individual evaluation for my child due to concerns about [specific areas: reading, attention, behavior, speech, etc.]" is sufficient. You don't need legal language or attorney involvement to make a valid request.

Step 2: Submit to the Right Department

Contact EVERMAN ISD directly to identify the correct office for EVERMAN ISD child find and evaluation requests. Ask for:

  • The special education department contact information
  • The name of the director of special education
  • The required forms or procedures specific to EVERMAN ISD
  • Confirmation that your request has been received

Keep copies of everything you submit and request written confirmation of receipt.

Step 3: Understand the EVERMAN ISD 45 Day Timeline

This is critical: EVERMAN ISD has a legal obligation to complete the evaluation within 45 calendar days of receiving your written request. This is known as the EVERMAN ISD 45 day timeline, and it's a hard deadline established by Texas Education Code §29.004.

The only exceptions to this 45 day timeline are if:

  • You request extensions in writing
  • You fail to provide consent for the evaluation
  • Your child is absent for extended periods during the evaluation window

After the initial EVERMAN ISD 45 day timeline expires, the district must provide you with an evaluation report and schedule a meeting to discuss results.

The EVERMAN ISD Evaluation Timeline and What to Expect

Week 1-2: Initial Response and Consent

Within 2-3 days of your EVERMAN ISD evaluation request, you should receive written acknowledgment. The district will send a Notice of Recommended Evaluation that explains:

  • What tests and assessments will be conducted
  • Why each assessment is being used
  • Your right to participate in the process
  • Your right to obtain independent evaluations

You must provide written consent before EVERMAN ISD special education evaluation can begin. This consent form is not a waiver of your rights—you're simply authorizing the evaluation to proceed.

Week 2-4: Testing and Assessment

Once you've signed consent, the EVERMAN ISD evaluation team will conduct various assessments. This may involve:

  • One-on-one testing sessions with your child
  • Classroom observations by special education staff
  • Teacher questionnaires and behavior rating scales
  • Parent interviews about developmental history and concerns

You have the right to be present during many of these assessments and to ask questions. Don't hesitate to contact EVERMAN ISD to schedule convenient times.

Week 4-6: Report Completion and ARD Meeting

Before the EVERMAN ISD 45 day timeline expires, the district must complete a comprehensive Evaluation Report and schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. This is your opportunity to discuss findings and determine eligibility for services.

Understanding EVERMAN ISD Special Education Categories

If EVERMAN ISD determines that your child qualifies for special education, they will be identified under one of these disability categories under IDEA:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder – Persistent deficits in social communication and restricted, repetitive behaviors
  • Deaf-Blindness – Simultaneous hearing and visual impairments
  • Emotional Disturbance – Behavioral or emotional difficulties affecting education
  • Hearing Impairment – Permanent or fluctuating hearing loss
  • Intellectual Disability – Significant limitations in intellectual functioning and adaptive behavior
  • Multiple Disabilities – Combination of disabilities requiring special education
  • Orthopedic Impairment – Physical disabilities affecting mobility or function
  • Other Health Impairment – Chronic health conditions affecting learning (ADHD, asthma, etc.)
  • Specific Learning Disability – Significant discrepancy between ability and academic achievement
  • Speech or Language Impairment – Communication disorders affecting education
  • Traumatic Brain Injury – Acquired brain injuries affecting learning
  • Visual Impairment – Partial or complete vision loss

Not every concern results in eligibility, but EVERMAN ISD must conduct a thorough evaluation regardless.

Your Rights During the EVERMAN ISD FIE Process

Parents in EVERMAN ISD have significant rights throughout the evaluation process. Understanding these protections ensures you can advocate effectively for your child.

Key Parent Rights in EVERMAN ISD

  • Right to Prior Written Notice – You must receive written explanation of any evaluation or service proposal before it happens
  • Right to Informed Consent – You must understand and agree to evaluations; consent cannot be forced or coerced
  • Right to Participate – You must be included in all ARD meetings and evaluation discussions
  • Right to Records – You can request and review all evaluation records and test results
  • Right to Independent Evaluation – If you disagree with EVERMAN ISD findings, you can obtain an independent evaluation at district expense under certain conditions
  • Right to Dispute Resolution – You can pursue mediation or due process if you disagree with evaluation or placement decisions
  • Right to an Interpreter – If English isn't your primary language, EVERMAN ISD must provide interpretation

What Happens After Your EVERMAN ISD Evaluation

If Your Child Qualifies for Special Education

If EVERMAN ISD determines your child qualifies for special education, the district will:

  1. Develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) at your ARD meeting
  2. Explain special education services, placement options, and supports
  3. Begin services within 30 days of your written agreement
  4. Schedule regular ARD meetings at least annually to review progress

If Your Child Doesn't Qualify

If EVERMAN ISD determines your child doesn't meet criteria for special education, you have options:

  • Request an Independent Evaluation – You can disagree with findings and seek evaluation at district expense
  • Pursue Section 504 Plan – Your child may still qualify for accommodations under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act
  • Request Re-evaluation – You can request another evaluation after additional time has passed
  • File a Due Process Complaint – You can pursue formal dispute resolution if you believe EVERMAN ISD violated IDEA

Common Questions About EVERMAN ISD Evaluations

Can I Request an Evaluation if My Child Attends Private School?

Yes. EVERMAN ISD's child find obligation extends to privately-educated children in the EVERMAN area. The district must evaluate eligible children regardless of school enrollment.

What if EVERMAN ISD Refuses My Evaluation Request?

EVERMAN ISD cannot legally refuse a reasonable EVERMAN ISD evaluation request from a parent. If the district denies your request, ask for written explanation and contact the Texas Education Agency Special Education division.

Can I Request Specific Evaluators for My EVERMAN ISD Assessment?

While EVERMAN ISD selects qualified evaluators, you can request that specific professionals conduct assessments if you have legitimate concerns about bias or conflict of interest.

What if I Disagree with the EVERMAN ISD 45 Day Timeline?

You can request a written extension, but EVERMAN ISD cannot unilaterally extend the 45 day timeline without your permission. Mark this deadline on your calendar and follow up if it approaches without contact from the district.
