Complete Guide to Requesting Special Education Evaluation in PARIS ISD

If your child is experiencing academic or developmental difficulties in PARIS ISD, you have the fundamental right to request a special education evaluation. This guide will help you understand the step-by-step process and protect your family's rights in PARIS ISD.

Why Is It Important to Request an Evaluation in PARIS ISD?

Children who qualify for special education services in PARIS ISD receive personalized support specifically designed for their unique needs. A formal evaluation is the first step to access these services which can transform your child's academic future.

In PARIS ISD, students with recognized disabilities are entitled to accommodations, curricular modifications, and free support services. Without a formal evaluation, your child would not qualify for these legal protections.

The evaluation also provides valuable information about your child's strengths and areas for improvement, information you can use to support them at home and at school.

Signs Your Child Could Benefit from an Evaluation in PARIS ISD

Academic Difficulties

  • Significant difficulty reading, writing, or understanding math
  • Academic performance significantly below their classmates
  • Consistent need for repeated instructions or extra help
  • Difficulty completing homework or school projects

Behavioral and Communication Challenges

  • Difficulty following instructions in PARIS ISD or at home
  • Repeated disruptive behavior in the classroom
  • Difficulty communicating or expressing needs
  • Difficulty interacting with classmates

Developmental Concerns

  • Delays in fine or gross motor skills
  • Difficulty with coordination or balance
  • Speech or language developmental delays
  • Signs of autism or other developmental disorders

Your Rights: Parent Rights in PARIS ISD

As a parent in PARIS ISD, you have parent rights protected by federal law (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act - IDEA) and by Texas state law. These rights include:

  • Right to request evaluation: You can ask for a special education evaluation at any time if you believe your child has a disability
  • Right to participate: You must participate in all decisions regarding your child's evaluation and placement in PARIS ISD
  • Right to obtain information: You have the right to all information about the evaluation process and results
  • Right to an interpreter: PARIS ISD must provide interpreters for Spanish-speaking families at all meetings
  • Right to a copy of results: You will receive copies of all evaluation reports and special education documents

Step by Step: How to Request Special Education Evaluation in PARIS ISD

Step 1: Submit Your Request in Writing

The first step to request an evaluation in PARIS ISD is to send a written request to the PARIS ISD Special Education Department. This request should be clear and specific.

You can submit your request in several ways:

  • Deliver it in person to your child's school
  • Send it by certified mail to the district
  • Send it by email to the special education office
  • Complete an application form available at PARIS ISD

In your request, clearly explain your concerns about your child and provide specific examples of behaviors or academic difficulties you have observed.

Step 2: PARIS ISD Reviews Your Request

After receiving your request, PARIS ISD has 15 school days to respond. During this time, the district will assess if there is evidence of a possible disability.

It is important to know that PARIS ISD cannot automatically deny an evaluation. If you believe your child needs an evaluation, you have the right to one, especially if you provide medical or professional information supporting your concerns.

Step 3: Written Consent for the FIE

If PARIS ISD accepts your request, they will send you a consent form called "FIE" (Full Individual Evaluation). In PARIS ISD, this form must be in Spanish if it is your preferred language.

Do not sign this document until you fully understand:

  • What tests will be administered
  • Who will conduct the evaluation
  • How long the process will take
  • How the results will be used

You can ask questions before signing. PARIS ISD must answer all your questions clearly.

Step 4: The Full Individual Evaluation (FIE)

A comprehensive PARIS ISD FIE includes evaluations in multiple areas:

  • Cognitive tests (intelligence, reasoning)
  • Academic evaluation (reading, math, writing)
  • Speech and language evaluation
  • Social and emotional behavior evaluation
  • Developmental and medical history
  • Classroom observations

The complete process usually takes 30 to 60 days from the time you sign consent. PARIS ISD must complete the entire evaluation within this timeframe.

Step 5: Eligibility Meeting (IEP Meeting)

After the evaluation is completed, PARIS ISD will schedule an eligibility meeting within 30 days. In this crucial meeting:

  • The evaluation results will be presented
  • It will be determined if your child qualifies for special education
  • Your child's special needs will be discussed
  • A decision will be made regarding special education services

This meeting is very important. You can bring someone to support you, such as a special education attorney or advocate.

Special Education in PARIS ISD: Eligibility Categories

In PARIS ISD, special education covers 13 different disability categories:

  • Autism
  • Deafness
  • Deaf-blindness
  • Hearing impairment
  • Intellectual disability
  • Speech or language impairment
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Orthopedic impairment
  • Other health impairment
  • Specific learning disability
  • Traumatic brain injury
  • Visual impairment
  • Developmental delay (for children aged 3-9)

What Happens After Diagnosis in PARIS ISD

IEP Plan Development

If your child qualifies for special education in PARIS ISD, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be developed. This document is your legal agreement with PARIS ISD regarding:

  • Specific educational goals
  • Services your child will receive
  • How many hours per week special services will be received
  • How progress will be measured
  • Accommodations in the regular classroom

Available Support Services

  • Personalized special instruction
  • Speech and language therapy
  • Occupational therapy services
  • School psychology services
  • Behavior support
  • Curricular adaptations
  • Assistive technology

Important Tips for Families in PARIS ISD

Prepare Information Before Your Meeting

Before any important meeting in PARIS ISD, gather:

  • Relevant medical histories
  • Previous evaluations from external professionals
  • Specific examples of academic or behavioral difficulties
  • Notes on what your child has said about their challenges
  • Photographs or documents demonstrating concerns

Bring Support to Meetings

You are not alone. You can bring to PARIS ISD meetings:

  • A family member
  • A special education attorney
  • A special education advocate (special ed advocate PARIS)
  • A trusted friend who understands your child's needs

Document Everything

Keep copies of all PARIS ISD documents, including:

  • Evaluation reports
  • Your child's IEP
  • Correspondence from PARIS ISD
  • Meeting notes
  • Teacher emails

If PARIS ISD Denies the Evaluation or Disagrees with Your Request

If PARIS ISD rejects your evaluation request, you have options. According to Texas law, you can:

  • Request a problem-solving meeting with PARIS ISD
  • Contact a special education attorney near PARIS for legal advice
  • File a state complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA)
  • Request mediation
  • Request a due process hearing

Do not accept "no" as a final answer. If you genuinely believe your child needs an evaluation, you have the right to pursue this right.

Your voice matters. Your concerns are valid. And your child deserves access to the services and supports they need to succeed in PARIS ISD.
