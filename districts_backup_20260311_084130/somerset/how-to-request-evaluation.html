Complete Guide: How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in SOMERSET ISD

If your child is experiencing academic or behavioral difficulties, you have every right to request a special education evaluation in SOMERSET ISD. This guide will explain step-by-step how to navigate the request process, your rights as a parent, and what to expect during the evaluation.

Why Request an Evaluation in SOMERSET ISD?

Students in SOMERSET ISD who have disabilities that affect their learning are entitled to receive free special education services. A SOMERSET ISD special education evaluation is the first step to determine if your child qualifies for these legally protected services.

If you notice your child is falling behind academically, has difficulty following instructions, exhibits behavioral challenges, or has problems with social skills, an evaluation can help identify the causes and solutions.

Signs that your child might need an evaluation

  • Significant difficulty with reading, writing, or math
  • Hearing, vision, or speech problems
  • Disruptive or aggressive behavior at school
  • Lack of concentration or difficulty following instructions
  • Limited social interaction with peers
  • Delay in physical or motor development
  • Anxiety or depression affecting learning

Your Parental Rights in SOMERSET ISD

Federal law (IDEA - Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) and Texas laws guarantee specific parental rights in SOMERSET ISD. It is crucial that you understand these rights before starting the process.

Fundamental Rights in SOMERSET ISD

  • Right to request an evaluation: You can request an evaluation anytime you believe your child needs special education evaluation
  • Right to participation: You must be part of all steps of the evaluation and planning process
  • Right to interpretation: SOMERSET ISD must provide Spanish interpreters at all meetings
  • Right to know results: You have the right to fully understand the results and recommendations
  • Right to appeal: If you disagree with SOMERSET ISD's decisions, you can request an impartial hearing
  • Right to confidentiality: All information about your child is protected

Step 1: Submitting a Formal Evaluation Request in SOMERSET ISD

The first step to obtaining a FIE evaluation in SOMERSET ISD (Full Individual Evaluation) is to submit a written request. This request initiates the legal process and creates a documented record of your petition.

How to submit your request in SOMERSET ISD

You can request an evaluation in several ways:

  1. In writing: Send a letter to your child's school principal or to the SOMERSET ISD special education department
  2. In person: Visit the SOMERSET ISD special education office and request the official form
  3. By email: Many schools in SOMERSET ISD accept requests by email (save confirmation)
  4. Verbally: You can make the request verbally, but follow up with a written letter

What to include in your request to SOMERSET ISD

Your request should be clear and professional. Include:

  • Your child's full name and student ID number
  • Current grade level in SOMERSET ISD
  • Specific description of academic or behavioral concerns
  • Concrete examples of what you have observed at home
  • Any prior evaluations or treatments (medical, psychological, etc.)
  • Your name, phone number, and email address
  • Date of the request

Important tip: Keep a copy of everything you send to SOMERSET ISD. This is crucial to document that you formally submitted your request.

Step 2: Understand the FIE Evaluation in SOMERSET ISD

A comprehensive special education evaluation in SOMERSET ISD is much more than a simple test. It is a comprehensive process that examines multiple areas of your child's development to obtain a complete picture of their strengths and needs.

Areas typically evaluated in SOMERSET ISD

  • Academic: Reading, writing, math, and study skills
  • Cognitive: Reasoning, memory, and processing skills
  • Language: Comprehension and expression in Spanish and English
  • Social and Emotional: Peer relationships, behavior, and mental health
  • Physical: Gross and fine motor skills, coordination
  • Vision and Hearing: Screening to detect sensory problems
  • Adaptive: Independence in daily activities

Professionals involved in the SOMERSET ISD evaluation

SOMERSET ISD will use various specialists to comprehensively evaluate your child:

  • School or educational psychologist
  • Special education teacher
  • Speech-language pathologist (if necessary)
  • Hearing or vision specialist (if necessary)
  • General education teacher
  • Parents and interpreters (if necessary)

Step 3: Legal Timelines that SOMERSET ISD Must Comply With

Texas law and federal law establish specific timelines that SOMERSET ISD must follow during the evaluation process. Knowing these deadlines helps you ensure that the district is complying with its legal obligations.

Legal Timeline for SOMERSET ISD

  • Within 15 days of your request: SOMERSET ISD must contact you to obtain consent to evaluate
  • Within 60 days of consent: SOMERSET ISD must complete the evaluation and convene an ARD meeting (planning meeting)
  • At the ARD meeting: SOMERSET ISD will present the results and determine eligibility
  • If eligible: SOMERSET ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP)

Important note: If SOMERSET ISD does not meet these deadlines, that is a violation of your rights. Consider contacting a special education advocate near SOMERSET if this occurs.

Step 4: The ARD Meeting in SOMERSET ISD

After the evaluation is completed, SOMERSET ISD will convene an ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal). This meeting is where the results are discussed and your child's eligibility for services is determined.

What to expect at the SOMERSET ISD ARD meeting

The meeting will include:

  • The evaluators who completed the special education evaluation
  • The general education teacher
  • A SOMERSET ISD administrator
  • You as a parent or legal guardian
  • Your child (if appropriate)
  • A Spanish interpreter if it is your native language

Your rights at the SOMERSET ISD ARD meeting

  • Ask questions about the special education evaluation results
  • Request explanations in Spanish for any technical terms
  • Bring a special education attorney near SOMERSET if you wish
  • Request an independent evaluation if you disagree
  • Receive copies of all evaluation documents
  • Take time to review the information before signing

Step 5: Next Steps if Your Child Qualifies

If your child qualifies as a special education student in SOMERSET ISD, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be developed. This is your most important legal document.

Components of the SOMERSET ISD IEP

  • Present level of performance: Where your child is now academically and socially
  • Annual goals: What your child is expected to achieve in one year
  • Special services: Therapy, classroom assistance, or other services
  • Placement: Where services will be received (general education classroom, special education classroom, etc.)
  • Accommodations and modifications: Changes in how instruction or evaluation is provided
  • Transition: Plans for future educational or employment (after age 14)

Frequently Asked Questions about Evaluation in SOMERSET ISD

How much does a special education evaluation cost in SOMERSET ISD?

The evaluation is completely free. SOMERSET ISD is legally obligated to provide evaluations at no cost to families.

What if I disagree with the SOMERSET ISD evaluation results?

You have the right to request an independent evaluation in SOMERSET ISD. You can also request an impartial hearing to challenge the district's decisions.

Can SOMERSET ISD refuse my evaluation request?

No. If you formally request a special education evaluation in SOMERSET ISD, the district must proceed. They can only deny if an evaluation was recently completed without a disability finding.

Do I need an attorney to request an evaluation in SOMERSET ISD?

It is not required, but many parents find it beneficial to have a special education advocate near SOMERSET to ensure all rights are respected.
