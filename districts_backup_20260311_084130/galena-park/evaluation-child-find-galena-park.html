Understanding the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in GALENA PARK ISD

How to Request a GALENA PARK ISD Evaluation: Step-by-Step Process

Step 1: Submit Your Written Request

The most important action you can take is putting your GALENA PARK ISD evaluation request in writing. While you can speak with teachers or counselors informally, a written request creates a documented timeline and legally obligates the district to respond.

Your written request should:

  • Include your child's full name, date of birth, and current grade level
  • Clearly state "I am requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE)"
  • Describe specific concerns (academic struggles, behavioral issues, developmental delays, speech concerns)
  • Include relevant background information (previous evaluations, medical diagnoses, family history)
  • Be addressed to the Director of Special Education or Student Support Services

Best practice for GALENA PARK ISD parents: Send your request via email with read receipt and certified mail. Keep copies of everything you send to the district. This creates an official paper trail for your records.

Step 2: Understand the 45-Day Timeline

Once GALENA PARK ISD receives your written GALENA PARK ISD evaluation request, the district has a critical deadline. Under Texas Education Code and IDEA regulations, GALENA PARK ISD must complete the evaluation within 45 days of receiving your request, not counting school days during summer break.

The GALENA PARK ISD 45 day timeline includes:

  1. Days 1-3: District acknowledges receipt and schedules parent meeting
  2. Days 4-15: District obtains parental consent for evaluation
  3. Days 16-40: Multidisciplinary team completes all assessments
  4. Days 41-45: Eligibility meeting held and Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) conference scheduled if eligible

If GALENA PARK ISD cannot meet the 45-day deadline, they must provide you with a written explanation and revised timeline. You have the right to know why the evaluation is delayed.

Step 3: Participate in the Consent Process

Before GALENA PARK ISD can conduct any GALENA PARK ISD special education evaluation, the district must obtain your written, informed consent. This means you must understand what's being tested, who will test your child, and how the results will be used.

During this stage, GALENA PARK ISD should explain:

  • What assessment tools will be used
  • Why each assessment is necessary
  • Who will conduct the evaluations
  • How long the evaluation will take
  • How results will be reported
  • Your right to bring an advocate or attorney

Important: You can request additional assessments or areas of evaluation that you believe are relevant. For example, if your child has hearing concerns, you can request an audiological evaluation as part of GALENA PARK ISD's comprehensive assessment.
