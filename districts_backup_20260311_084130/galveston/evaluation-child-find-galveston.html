Complete Guide to Requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in GALVESTON ISD

If you're a parent in GALVESTON ISD and you believe your child may have a disability affecting their education, requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is often the critical first step toward getting your child the support they need. This comprehensive guide walks you through the process, timelines, and your rights under federal and Texas law.

Understanding Special Education Evaluation in GALVESTON ISD

GALVESTON ISD serves approximately 5,982 students across the Galveston area, and the district is required by law to identify and evaluate children who may have disabilities. A GALVESTON ISD special education evaluation is a thorough assessment designed to determine if your child qualifies for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA).

The GALVESTON ISD FIE evaluation is more comprehensive than initial screening or short-cycle assessments. It examines your child's academic performance, cognitive abilities, social-emotional functioning, physical development, and other areas that may be affected by a potential disability.

Understanding this process empowers you to advocate effectively for your child's needs within GALVESTON ISD and ensures your child receives timely, appropriate evaluations and services.

Child Find: How GALVESTON ISD Identifies Children Who May Need Evaluation

Before requesting an evaluation, it's helpful to understand GALVESTON ISD child find procedures. Child find is a federal requirement that obligates GALVESTON ISD to actively search for and identify children who may have disabilities, even if parents haven't requested evaluations.

How Child Find Works in GALVESTON ISD

  • Universal Screening: GALVESTON ISD may conduct universal screenings in early childhood or at key grade transitions to identify students who might benefit from evaluation.
  • Teacher Referrals: Educators in GALVESTON ISD who observe academic or developmental concerns can refer students for evaluation.
  • Parent Concerns: When parents express concerns to GALVESTON ISD staff, the district must take these seriously and consider evaluation.
  • Previous Records: GALVESTON ISD reviews records from previous schools, doctors, or early intervention services.

If you have concerns about your child's development or school performance, communicating with your child's teacher or the school principal in GALVESTON ISD initiates important conversations that may lead to evaluation.

Step-by-Step Process for Requesting a Full Individual Evaluation in GALVESTON ISD

Step 1: Submit Your Formal Request in Writing

The most effective way to request a GALVESTON ISD evaluation request is in writing. Send a letter to your child's school principal or the Director of Special Education at GALVESTON ISD stating that you want your child evaluated for possible special education eligibility.

Your written request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • The specific areas of concern (academic performance, behavior, social skills, speech, etc.)
  • Examples of difficulties you've observed at home or school
  • Any previous evaluations or diagnoses
  • Your contact information

Pro Tip: Send your request via email with a read receipt or hand-deliver it in person and ask for a dated receipt. This creates documentation that GALVESTON ISD received your request on a specific date—important for the evaluation timeline.

Step 2: Understand the GALVESTON ISD 45 Day Timeline

Once GALVESTON ISD receives your written evaluation request, the district has 45 calendar days to complete the GALVESTON ISD FIE evaluation (with limited exceptions). This timeline is established by the Texas Education Code and federal IDEA regulations.

The GALVESTON ISD 45 day timeline begins on the day the district receives your written request, so documentation of submission is critical. The 45 days includes:

  • Completing all assessments and testing
  • Reviewing records and previous evaluations
  • Observing your child in classroom and other settings
  • Gathering input from parents and teachers
  • Scheduling and conducting your initial ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting

Mark your calendar: if you submit your request on January 15th, GALVESTON ISD must complete evaluation and hold an ARD meeting by March 1st at the latest.

Step 3: GALVESTON ISD Contacts You About Consent

Within a few days of receiving your request, GALVESTON ISD special education staff will contact you. They'll explain the evaluation process and provide forms requesting your informed consent to proceed with assessment.

Your consent is legally required before GALVESTON ISD can conduct evaluations. You have the right to:

  • Ask questions about what will be evaluated
  • Know which professionals will conduct assessments
  • Understand what tests and procedures will be used
  • Request independent evaluations if you disagree with GALVESTON ISD findings

Don't feel pressured to rush. Take time to read all materials and ask questions before signing consent forms.

Step 4: Evaluation Assessments Are Conducted

Once you provide consent, GALVESTON ISD begins the comprehensive GALVESTON ISD special education evaluation. Depending on your child's age and specific concerns, assessments may include:

  • Cognitive Testing: Measures intelligence, reasoning, and learning abilities
  • Achievement Testing: Assesses reading, math, writing, and other academic skills
  • Speech and Language Evaluation: Examines communication abilities (if needed)
  • Occupational/Physical Therapy Assessment: Reviews motor skills and daily functioning (if needed)
  • Behavioral and Emotional Assessment: Explores social-emotional functioning and behavior patterns
  • Vision and Hearing Screening: Rules out sensory issues affecting learning
  • Classroom Observations: GALVESTON ISD staff observe your child in typical school environments

You may be asked to complete rating scales, questionnaires, or participate in interviews. Your perspective as a parent is valuable and legally required to be included in the evaluation.

Step 5: ARD Meeting and Eligibility Determination

Before the 45 day GALVESTON ISD timeline expires, the district must schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. This meeting includes:

  • You and your family members
  • GALVESTON ISD general education teacher(s)
  • Special education teacher or specialist
  • School administrator or district representative
  • The evaluator(s) who conducted assessments
  • Other professionals as needed

At this meeting, GALVESTON ISD presents evaluation results and determines whether your child qualifies for special education services under one of 13 disability categories under IDEA.

Disability Categories Under GALVESTON ISD Special Education Services

To qualify for special education in GALVESTON ISD, your child must meet criteria in at least one of these categories:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Deaf-Blindness
  • Deafness
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Specific Learning Disability
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Visual Impairment

GALVESTON ISD must determine not only that your child has a disability but that the disability requires special education services to benefit from education.

Your Rights During the FIE Process in GALVESTON ISD

Parents in GALVESTON ISD have important legal protections throughout the evaluation process under IDEA and Texas law:

Prior Written Notice

GALVESTON ISD must provide prior written notice in your native language before proposing or refusing to evaluate your child. This notice explains what GALVESTON ISD proposes to do and why.

Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE)

If you disagree with GALVESTON ISD's evaluation results, you have the right to request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at district expense. GALVESTON ISD cannot automatically deny this request.

Procedural Safeguards Notice

GALVESTON ISD must provide you with a complete Procedural Safeguards Notice explaining your rights in special education. Request this document in writing if you haven't received it.

Interpreter or Translator Services

If English is not your primary language, GALVESTON ISD must provide interpretation services during meetings and in written communications.

Preparing for Your Child's ARD Meeting in GALVESTON ISD

The ARD meeting where GALVESTON ISD discusses evaluation results is critical. Here's how to prepare:

Gather Documentation

  • Bring medical records, previous evaluations, and developmental history
  • Compile examples of your concerns (report cards, work samples, behavioral incidents)
  • Document observations about your child's strengths and challenges

Prepare Questions

  • "How do the test results explain my child's difficulties?"
  • "What does this mean for my child's education going forward?"
  • "What services does my child need?"
  • "How will you measure progress?"

Bring Support

You can bring a trusted family member, advocate, or attorney to the ARD meeting. Some GALVESTON ISD families benefit from having a special ed advocate GALVESTON or special education professional present to help them understand evaluation results and advocate for their child's needs.

Request Materials in Advance

Contact GALVESTON ISD at least one week before the ARD meeting and request evaluation reports and test results in advance so you can review them carefully.

What Happens If Your Child Is Found Eligible in GALVESTON ISD

If your child is found eligible for special education services through the GALVESTON ISD FIE evaluation, the ARD committee will immediately begin developing an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Your IEP with GALVESTON ISD will include:

  • Present levels of academic and functional performance
  • Measurable annual goals based on your child's needs
  • Special education services and accommodations
  • Placement decisions (general education with supports, resource room, specialized services, etc.)
  • Frequency and duration of services
  • Progress monitoring and evaluation procedures

Your family will meet with the ARD committee at least annually to review your child's progress and make any necessary adjustments.

What Happens If Your Child Is Not Found Eligible in GALVESTON ISD

If GALVESTON ISD determines your child doesn't meet eligibility criteria despite the GALVESTON ISD special education evaluation, you have options:

Request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE)

You can request that GALVESTON ISD pay for an independent evaluator to assess your child. This evaluator is not employed by the district and provides an objective second opinion.

Explore Section 504 Plans

Even without special education eligibility, your child might qualify for accommodations under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act. Ask GALVESTON ISD about a 504 plan evaluation.

Request Mediation or Due Process

If you believe GALVESTON ISD improperly denied your child's evaluation or eligibility, you can request mediation or due process hearings. A special education attorney near GALVESTON can explain whether these options are appropriate for your situation.
