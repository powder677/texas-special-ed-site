Understanding the FIE Process in GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a thorough assessment designed to determine whether your child qualifies for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). The GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD special education evaluation process examines multiple areas of development, including cognitive abilities, academic performance, social-emotional functioning, speech and language skills, and motor development. Warning Signs That Your Child May Need an Evaluation

  • Academic struggles: Persistent difficulty with reading, math, writing, or other core subjects despite quality instruction
  • Speech and language delays: Difficulty expressing thoughts, understanding instructions, or articulating words clearly
  • Social-emotional challenges: Difficulty making friends, managing emotions, or following behavioral expectations
  • Motor skill delays: Clumsiness, difficulty with fine motor tasks like writing, or gross motor challenges like running

What the GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD Evaluation Will Include

A comprehensive GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD special education evaluation must assess all areas of suspected disability and should never rely on a single test or measure. For GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD families, understanding what to expect helps you ask informed questions during the IEP meeting.

Required Assessment Areas

The GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD FIE evaluation team will likely assess your child in the following areas:
