Understanding Special Education in NAVARRO ISD

NAVARRO ISD special education services are designed to provide students with disabilities the support they need to access the general education curriculum and achieve academic success. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, NAVARRO ISD is required to identify, evaluate, and serve eligible students.

The special education process in NAVARRO ISD begins with referral and evaluation. Any parent, teacher, or school administrator can refer a student for evaluation if there are concerns about the child's learning, behavior, or development. NAVARRO ISD must respond to all referrals and conduct a comprehensive evaluation within 45 school days to determine eligibility for services.

Who Qualifies for Special Education?

NAVARRO ISD serves students ages 3-21 who have a disability that impacts their educational performance. Federal law recognizes 13 disability categories, including:

  • Autism spectrum disorder
  • Emotional disturbance
  • Hearing impairment
  • Intellectual disability
  • Orthopedic impairment
  • Other health impairment
  • Specific learning disability (including dyslexia)
  • Speech or language impairment
  • Traumatic brain injury
  • Visual impairment

Not every student who struggles in school will qualify for special education. The student must have a documented disability and require specially designed instruction to benefit from education.

NAVARRO ISD IEP Services and Your Child's Individualized Education Program

An Individualized Education Program (IEP) is the legal document that outlines the special education services your child will receive. When your child is determined eligible for special education, NAVARRO ISD IEP services are developed collaboratively with your child's teachers, specialists, and your family.

Your child's IEP includes present levels of academic and functional performance, annual goals, the specific services your child will receive, and the extent to which your child will participate in the general education classroom. In NAVARRO ISD, we believe in maximizing inclusion whenever possible while ensuring your child receives the specialized support needed.

What Happens at an IEP Meeting?

NAVARRO ISD holds annual IEP meetings to review your child's progress and make any necessary updates to services. The IEP team typically includes:

  • Parents or guardians
  • Special education teacher
  • General education teacher (if student is in general education classes)
  • School administrator or designee
  • Other specialists (speech therapist, counselor, etc. as needed)

NAVARRO ISD IEP services meetings are scheduled at a time convenient for parents. If you cannot attend in person, NAVARRO ISD will arrange for your participation via phone or videoconference. You have the right to bring an advocate, family member, or attorney to support you during the meeting.

IEP Goals and Progress Monitoring

Annual goals developed for NAVARRO ISD special education students must be measurable, specific, and focused on addressing your child's unique needs. Teachers in NAVARRO ISD monitor progress toward these goals regularly, typically providing progress reports every 9 weeks or marking period.

Parents should ask for progress data regularly and discuss how their child is progressing toward annual goals. If your child is not making adequate progress, this may trigger a meeting to revise the IEP or explore additional support strategies.

NAVARRO ISD ARD Meeting: Your Guide to Admission, Review, and Dismissal

In Texas, special education IEP meetings are called NAVARRO ISD ARD meetings (Admission, Review, and Dismissal). The ARD process is how NAVARRO ISD admits students to special education, reviews their progress, and determines dismissal from services.

The NAVARRO ISD ARD meeting is more than just a routine check-in—it's an opportunity to ensure your child is receiving appropriate services and making meaningful progress. Parents in NAVARRO ISD should treat ARD meetings as important planning sessions where they have equal voice in decision-making.

Before Your NAVARRO ISD ARD Meeting

Preparation helps you get the most from your ARD meeting. Before your next NAVARRO ISD ARD meeting, consider these steps:

  1. Gather information: Collect recent progress reports, work samples, and notes about what you observe at home
  2. Write questions: Make a list of concerns or questions you want to address during the meeting
  3. Review the current IEP: Understand what services your child is currently receiving
  4. Request prior notice: Ensure you received written notice at least 10 days before the NAVARRO ISD ARD meeting
  5. Bring support: Consider inviting an advocate or family member familiar with special education

What to Expect During Your ARD Meeting

Your NAVARRO ISD ARD meeting will typically follow this agenda: review of present levels of performance, discussion of progress toward goals, review of services and placement, and development of new goals if needed. The meeting should feel collaborative—NAVARRO ISD staff should listen to your concerns and incorporate your input into decisions.

Take notes during your NAVARRO ISD ARD meeting about what was discussed and decided. After the meeting, NAVARRO ISD will provide you with a copy of the IEP or ARD document. Review it carefully to ensure all discussions are accurately reflected.

NAVARRO ISD Dyslexia Program and Reading Support

Dyslexia is a specific learning disability that affects how the brain processes written language. If you suspect your child has dyslexia, NAVARRO ISD dyslexia program services can provide specialized instruction and support.

Texas Education Code requires districts to identify and serve students with dyslexia. NAVARRO ISD uses screening and evaluation tools to identify students at risk for dyslexia and provides evidence-based interventions. If your child is struggling with reading, request that they be screened for dyslexia during your conference with NAVARRO ISD staff.

How to Request Dyslexia Evaluation in NAVARRO ISD

If you believe your child has dyslexia, you can request an evaluation through NAVARRO ISD's special education referral process. A comprehensive dyslexia evaluation examines phonological processing, rapid naming, sound-symbol association, and reading fluency.

Once identified with dyslexia, students in NAVARRO ISD may receive specialized reading instruction, assistive technology, extended time on tests, or other accommodations. The NAVARRO ISD dyslexia program emphasizes structured literacy approaches that have strong evidence of effectiveness for students with dyslexia.

NAVARRO ISD Parent Rights and Legal Protections

NAVARRO ISD parent rights are protected under federal law (IDEA), Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, and the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA). Understanding your rights is essential to advocating effectively for your child.

Key Parent Rights in NAVARRO ISD

Parents of students in NAVARRO ISD special education have the right to:

  • Prior written notice: Receive written notice before NAVARRO ISD proposes or refuses to make a change to your child's education program
  • Participate in decisions: Have equal voice in all decisions about evaluation, eligibility, services, and placement
  • Free evaluation: Obtain a comprehensive evaluation at no cost to determine if your child is eligible for special education
  • Confidentiality: Have all records kept confidential and disclosed only with your consent
  • Independent evaluation: Obtain an independent evaluation if you disagree with NAVARRO ISD's evaluation at public expense under certain circumstances
  • Due process hearing: Request a due process hearing to resolve disputes with NAVARRO ISD regarding your child's special education services
  • Surrogate parent: Have a surrogate parent appointed if you are unavailable to participate in decisions

NAVARRO ISD must provide you with a Parent Rights Notice each year. This document explains your rights under federal and state law. If you don't have a copy, contact the NAVARRO ISD Special Education Department to request one.

What to Do If You Disagree with NAVARRO ISD Decisions

If you disagree with NAVARRO ISD's decisions regarding special education services, you have several options. Start by requesting a meeting with the ARD team to discuss your concerns. Often, misunderstandings can be resolved through open communication.

If concerns persist, you can file a due process complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA) or request a due process hearing. Some parents in NAVARRO ISD choose to consult with a special education attorney or advocate to help resolve disputes. Many special education attorneys near NAVARRO offer free initial consultations.

NAVARRO ISD Special Education Services and Programs

NAVARRO ISD special education encompasses multiple service delivery models to meet diverse student needs. Depending on your child's disability and needs, services in NAVARRO ISD may include:

Service Delivery Options in NAVARRO ISD

  • Inclusion programs: Special education students receiving services within the general education classroom
  • Resource classes: Students spending part of their day in specialized instruction and part in general education classes
  • Self-contained classes: Full-time special education placement for students with significant support needs
  • Related services: Speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, and other specialized services
  • Early childhood special education: Services for children ages 3-5 in NAVARRO ISD
  • Transition services: Programs helping students ages 14-22 prepare for life after secondary school

The goal of NAVARRO ISD special education is to provide the least restrictive environment where students can succeed. Parents in NAVARRO ISD should advocate for maximum inclusion while ensuring their child's individualized needs are met.
