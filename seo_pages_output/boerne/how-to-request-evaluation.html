Complete Guide to Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in BOERNE ISD

If your child is experiencing academic or behavioral difficulties at school, they may be entitled to a special education evaluation in BOERNE ISD. This guide will explain exactly how the process works, what your rights are as a parent, and what steps you should take to ensure your child receives the support they need in BOERNE ISD schools.

Why Request a Special Education Evaluation in BOERNE ISD?

Students in BOERNE ISD who have disabilities affecting their learning are entitled to special education services. If your child struggles with reading, math, behavior, or social interactions, a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation) in BOERNE ISD can identify if they need special services.

Families in BOERNE ISD often notice signs such as:

  • Difficulty concentrating in class
  • Problems with reading or writing compared to peers of their age
  • Frequent disruptive or aggressive behavior
  • Difficulty understanding and following instructions
  • Social isolation or difficulty making friends
  • Low academic performance despite consistent effort

If you recognize some of these signs in your child, it's time to contact BOERNE ISD schools to initiate the evaluation process.

Your Rights as a Parent in BOERNE ISD

Federal law (IDEA - Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) and Texas laws protect the rights of parents in BOERNE ISD. You have the right to:

  • Request a free evaluation of your child at any time during the school year
  • Receive written notification of all steps in the process in BOERNE ISD
  • Participate in all meetings related to the evaluation and educational decisions
  • Obtain copies of all evaluation documents in BOERNE ISD
  • Challenge the results if you disagree with the conclusions
  • Bring a special education attorney or advocate to BOERNE ISD meetings
  • Receive all information in Spanish, including notifications and evaluation documents

Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act and the IDEA Act guarantee these parental rights in BOERNE ISD. If you feel your child is not receiving fair treatment, consider contacting a special education attorney near BOERNE or a local special education advocate.

Steps to Request a FIE Evaluation in BOERNE ISD

Step 1: Contact Your BOERNE ISD School

The first step to obtaining a special evaluation in BOERNE ISD is to contact your child's teacher or school principal. You can do this in person, by phone, or by email. Clearly tell them that you wish to request a special education evaluation in BOERNE ISD because you have concerns about your child's learning.

If possible, specifically explain what difficulties you are observing. For example: "My child has trouble reading at grade level" or "They exhibit aggressive behaviors in the classroom that interfere with their learning." BOERNE ISD must take your request seriously.

Step 2: Formal Written Request

While a verbal request is a good start, it's stronger to make a written request for evaluation in BOERNE ISD. This creates an official record. Send a letter or email to the principal, special education coordinator, or the BOERNE ISD special education services department.

Your letter should include:

  • Your child's full name and grade
  • Your names and contact information
  • A clear description of academic or behavioral concerns
  • An explicit request for a special education evaluation
  • Your signature and date

Save a copy for your records. If you send an email to BOERNE ISD, the email counts as a written request.

Step 3: BOERNE ISD Must Respond Within 30 Days

Once BOERNE ISD receives your written request, the district has 30 days to respond. BOERNE ISD special education staff must contact you to discuss the potential evaluation. During this call, they may ask questions about your child's history and why you believe they need an evaluation.

In this initial meeting, BOERNE ISD may:

  • Agree to proceed with a full evaluation
  • Offer an intervention plan first (response to intervention)
  • Ask for more information from you or the teacher

Step 4: Signed Consent for Evaluation

If BOERNE ISD agrees to conduct a FIE evaluation in BOERNE ISD, they must provide you with a written consent form. They cannot evaluate your child without your written permission. This is an important protection of your parental rights.

Read this form carefully before signing. If you have questions, ask BOERNE ISD staff to explain what evaluations they will conduct and why. You can bring a special ed advocate in BOERNE to help you understand the process.

Step 5: The Full Evaluation

Once you have signed consent, BOERNE ISD has 60 calendar days to complete the evaluation (according to Texas law). During this time, BOERNE ISD evaluators will conduct tests on:

  • Academic skills (reading, writing, math)
  • Intellectual functioning
  • Language development
  • Behavior in the classroom and at home
  • Social skills
  • Hearing and vision (if relevant)

BOERNE ISD will also review previous school records, medical evaluations, and examples of your child's work. It is important that you share any information you believe is relevant with the BOERNE ISD evaluation team.

Step 6: Evaluation Results Meeting

After completing the special education evaluation in BOERNE ISD, the evaluation team will meet with you to present the results. This is called the "Eligibility Determination Meeting." BOERNE ISD must provide you with a written report of the complete findings.

In this meeting, BOERNE ISD will determine if your child is eligible for special education services under one of the disability categories, such as:

  • Intellectual disability
  • Emotional disturbance
  • Autism
  • Hearing or visual impairment
  • Speech or language impairment
  • Traumatic brain injury
  • Orthopedic impairment
  • Other health impairment
  • Dyslexia or other specific learning disability

Step 7: Individualized Education Program (IEP)

If BOERNE ISD determines your child is eligible, the special education team will create an Individualized Education Program (IEP). This is a legal document that outlines:

  • Your child's special needs
  • Learning goals for the upcoming year
  • Specific services they will receive (speech therapy, special education, etc.)
  • How many hours per week they will receive special services
  • Accommodations in the regular classroom

As a parent of a student in BOERNE ISD, you participate in the creation of the IEP. Your ideas and concerns must be included.

Expected Timeline for the Process in BOERNE ISD

Here's what you can expect in terms of timing when requesting a special evaluation in BOERNE ISD:

  1. Day 1: Written request to BOERNE ISD
  2. Days 2-30: BOERNE ISD responds and discusses the proposed evaluation
  3. Day 30: Sign consent for evaluation
  4. Days 31-90: Full evaluation (up to 60 school days)
  5. Day 90: Results meeting and eligibility determination in BOERNE ISD
  6. Day 90 (if eligible): IEP meeting to create the service plan

Important note: If BOERNE ISD refuses to conduct an evaluation, you can challenge this decision. Contact a special education attorney near BOERNE or a special ed advocate in BOERNE if this happens.

Preparing for BOERNE ISD Meetings

Evaluation meetings in BOERNE ISD can be overwhelming, especially if English is not your primary language. Here are tips for preparing:

  • Bring an interpreter. BOERNE ISD must provide interpreters free of charge if you request it in advance
  • Bring an advocate or attorney. A special ed advocate in BOERNE can help you understand the process
  • Make a list of questions. Write down anything you don't understand about the evaluation or services
  • Bring documentation. Bring medical reports, private evaluations, or examples of school work
  • Ask for copies of everything. You should receive copies of all BOERNE ISD evaluation documents

BOERNE ISD has a district-level special education coordinator. This person oversees all special education services in BOERNE ISD and can answer questions about the evaluation process.

Special Education Advocate

A special ed advocate in BOERNE is a trained professional who helps you navigate the education system. Many attorneys who work with special education offer free initial consultations and can attend BOERNE ISD meetings with you.

State Advocacy Organizations

In Texas, there are state organizations dedicated to protecting the rights of individuals with disabilities. They can provide you with information about your rights as a parent of a student with a disability in BOERNE ISD or any Texas school.

What To Do If You Disagree with BOERNE ISD Decisions

Sometimes, parents in BOERNE ISD disagree with the evaluation results or the proposed IEP. You have options.

Resolution Meeting: You can request a resolution meeting with BOERNE ISD to discuss your concerns before proceeding with a formal dispute.

Independent Evaluation: If you disagree with BOERNE ISD's evaluation, you can request an independent educational evaluation conducted by an evaluator outside of BOERNE ISD. In certain cases, BOERNE ISD must pay for this.

Mediación: Mediation is a process where a neutral third party helps BOERNE ISD and your family reach an agreement regarding the IEP.

Due Process Hearing: If you still cannot resolve the issue with BOERNE ISD, you can request a formal hearing before an administrative officer. A special education attorney near BOERNE can represent you at this hearing.
