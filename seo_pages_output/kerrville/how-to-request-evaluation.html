Why Request a Special Education Evaluation in KERRVILLE ISD?

Parents in KERRVILLE ISD should consider requesting a special education evaluation (FIE - Formal Individual Evaluation) when they notice their child is experiencing difficulties that affect their learning. These difficulties may include reading problems, dyscalculia, speech disorders, autism, ADHD, emotional, or behavioral issues.

KERRVILLE ISD is obligated by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act to provide comprehensive and free evaluations to all students who need them.

  • A professional evaluation can identify disabilities that are not evident
  • Diagnosis allows for the creation of personalized educational plans (IEP or 504 Plan)
  • Your child will have access to specialized services and supports within KERRVILLE ISD
  • The evaluation is completely free for families

Knowing Your Rights as Parents in KERRVILLE ISD

Before requesting an evaluation, it is crucial for parents in KERRVILLE ISD to know their fundamental special education rights. These rights are protected by federal and Texas state laws.

  • Right to participate in all decisions: KERRVILLE ISD must include you in all meetings and decisions
  • Right to a comprehensive evaluation: KERRVILLE ISD must evaluate all aspects of your child's development and learning
  • Right to obtain copies of results: You have the right to copies of all documentation and evaluations
  • Right to an attorney or advocate: You can bring a special education attorney or advocate to any KERRVILLE ISD meeting

Steps to Request a Special Education Evaluation in KERRVILLE ISD

Step 1: Contact Your Child's School

The first step is to contact your child's teacher or the school counselor in KERRVILLE ISD. Express your specific concerns about your child's academic performance or behavior.

Many schools in KERRVILLE ISD offer early interventions (RTI - Response to Intervention) before a formal evaluation. These are additional teaching strategies to help your child without the need for special education.

Tip: Document all your communications with the school. Save emails and note dates and names of people you spoke with.

Step 2: Submit a Formal Written Request

If, after initial interventions, your child continues to experience difficulties, you must make a formal written request for evaluation to KERRVILLE ISD. This request is critical because it starts the 30-day legal clock.

Send your letter to:

  • Your child's school principal
  • KERRVILLE ISD Special Education Department
  • Preferably by certified mail to have confirmation of receipt

What to include in your request letter for KERRVILLE ISD:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Name of the school they attend
  • Specific description of the difficulties you have observed
  • Concrete examples (difficulty reading, problems concentrating, disruptive behavior, etc.)
  • Duration of these difficulties
  • Explicit request for a complete evaluation
  • Your signature and the date

Step 3: KERRVILLE ISD Reviews Your Request

Once KERRVILLE ISD receives your written request, the district has 15 calendar days to notify parents of its intent to evaluate. This means KERRVILLE ISD will confirm in writing that it will proceed with the evaluation.

The district will also provide you with a "Consent for Evaluation" document that explains the process, the types of tests that will be used, and parental rights.

Step 4: Provide Informed Consent

It is essential that you carefully read the consent form KERRVILLE ISD sends you. You must sign and return this document for the evaluation to officially begin.

The consent must be in Spanish or English, according to your preference. KERRVILLE ISD is obligated to provide documents in your preferred language.

Step 5: The Formal Evaluation in KERRVILLE ISD

After KERRVILLE ISD receives your consent, the district's evaluators will proceed with the evaluation. The process typically includes:

  • Cognitive tests: Evaluate intelligence and learning ability
  • Academic tests: Measure reading, math, and writing skills
  • Speech and language evaluation: If necessary
  • Occupational or physical evaluation: If motor disabilities are suspected
  • Classroom observations: Professionals observe your child's behavior in class
  • Information from teachers and parents: Educational and developmental history is collected

Step 6: Eligibility Meeting in KERRVILLE ISD

KERRVILLE ISD must complete the evaluation within 45 calendar days from receiving the signed consent. Within this time, the district will schedule an eligibility meeting.

In this meeting, a team of professionals will discuss the results of the KERRVILLE ISD evaluation and determine if your child qualifies for special education services under one or more disability categories.

Possible categories in KERRVILLE ISD and Texas:

  • Autism
  • Auditory Impairment
  • Deaf-Blindness
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment (ADHD, asthma, diabetes, etc.)
  • Specific Learning Disability (dyslexia, dyscalculia)
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Visual Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury

What Happens After the Evaluation in KERRVILLE ISD?

If Your Child Qualifies for Special Education in KERRVILLE ISD

If KERRVILLE ISD determines that your child has a disability and needs special services, they will create an Individualized Education Program (IEP). This document details:

  • Your child's current level of functioning
  • Specific and measurable annual educational goals
  • Special services that KERRVILLE ISD will provide (speech therapy, special education, etc.)
  • The least restrictive learning environment for your child
  • Necessary accommodations and modifications
  • Transition plan (for students aged 16 or older)

KERRVILLE ISD is required to review and update your child's IEP at least once a year. You have the right to request additional meetings if you notice that the IEP is not working.

If Your Child Does NOT Qualify in KERRVILLE ISD

If KERRVILLE ISD determines that your child does not qualify for special education under IDEA, they may still qualify under Section 504. A 504 plan provides accommodations and support even if the student does not receive special education.

You can also request a second opinion or independent evaluation if you do not agree with the results from KERRVILLE ISD. In this case, contact a special ed advocate KERRVILLE for advice.
