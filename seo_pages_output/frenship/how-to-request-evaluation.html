Complete Guide: How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in FRENSHIP ISD

If your child has learning or behavioral difficulties at school, you have legal rights to request a special education evaluation in FRENSHIP ISD. This guide will help you understand the step-by-step process and advocate for your child's rights.

Why Request a Special Education Evaluation in FRENSHIP ISD?

A special evaluation in FRENSHIP ISD can identify if your child has a disability that affects their learning. If the evaluation determines that your child qualifies, FRENSHIP ISD must provide free special education services.

Common reasons to request an evaluation include:

  • Difficulties with reading, writing, or math
  • Attention or concentration problems
  • Delays in speech or language development
  • Social or emotional difficulties
  • Challenging or disruptive behaviors
  • Suspicions of autism, dyslexia, or ADHD

In FRENSHIP ISD, with an enrollment of 11,770 students, many families use the evaluation process to obtain the support their children need.

Your Rights as a Parent in FRENSHIP ISD

Fundamental Parental Rights

Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and the Texas Rehabilitation Act, parents in FRENSHIP ISD have rights protected by law. These rights include:

  • Request an initial evaluation: You can ask for an evaluation at no cost
  • Participate in meetings: You must be included in all decisions regarding evaluation and special education
  • Access to records: You can review all of your child's educational records
  • Due process: You have the right to resolve disagreements through legal procedures
  • Services in your language: FRENSHIP ISD must communicate with you in Spanish if necessary

Important Information about FRENSHIP ISD Parental Rights

FRENSHIP ISD must provide all parents with a notice of their special education rights. If you have not received this document, request it from the FRENSHIP ISD special education department.

Step 1: Express Your Concern to the Teacher or School

Before formally requesting an evaluation from FRENSHIP ISD, it is helpful to communicate with your child's teacher. Many issues can be addressed with classroom adjustments.

How to Communicate Effectively

Schedule a meeting with your child's teacher to:

  • Describe the specific difficulties you have observed at home
  • Share concrete examples of behavior or performance
  • Ask what the teacher is observing at school
  • Ask if FRENSHIP ISD has considered accommodations or interventions

Document this conversation by email. Write: "I appreciate our conversation on [date] about [topic]. I look forward to working together to support my child."

Step 2: Formally Request an FIE Evaluation in FRENSHIP ISD

What is an FIE Evaluation?

FIE stands for "Full Individual Evaluation." This is a comprehensive evaluation that FRENSHIP ISD conducts to determine if your child qualifies for special education.

How to Request a FRENSHIP ISD FIE Evaluation

You can request an FIE evaluation from FRENSHIP ISD in two ways:

  1. Written request: Send a letter or email to the FRENSHIP ISD special education director requesting an evaluation
  2. Verbal request: Inform FRENSHIP ISD school staff verbally that you want an evaluation (although doing so in writing is recommended)

What to Include in Your Request

Your request should be clear and specific. Include:

  • Student's full name and FRENSHIP ISD identification number
  • Current school grade
  • Specific description of concerns (e.g., "My child has difficulty reading aloud")
  • When these difficulties began
  • What previous attempts have been made to help the student
  • Your name, phone number, and email address
  • Explicit request for "an initial special education evaluation"

Sample letter: "I formally request that FRENSHIP ISD conduct a full special education evaluation for my child, [name], a [grade] student at [school name]. I have observed [describe specific difficulties]. I understand that FRENSHIP ISD has [timeframe] days to respond."

Step 3: Understand the FRENSHIP ISD Timeline and Process

Legal Deadlines FRENSHIP ISD Must Meet

Under Texas law and IDEA, FRENSHIP ISD has specific deadlines it must meet:

  • 15 school days: FRENSHIP ISD must respond to your evaluation request
  • 60 school days: FRENSHIP ISD must complete the evaluation (from when you sign consent)
  • Within the timeframe: FRENSHIP ISD must conduct the ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting

It is important to note these dates. If FRENSHIP ISD does not meet these deadlines, request a meeting with the special education director.

What Happens After Your Request

After you request an evaluation in FRENSHIP ISD:

  1. FRENSHIP ISD will send you a consent for evaluation notice
  2. You must sign and return this document
  3. FRENSHIP ISD will gather information: medical history, school records, classroom observations
  4. Certified FRENSHIP ISD evaluators will administer formal tests
  5. An ARD meeting will be held to review the results

Step 4: Preparing for the FRENSHIP ISD ARD Meeting

What is an ARD Meeting?

An ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting is a meeting where the FRENSHIP ISD team, including you as a parent, discusses the evaluation results and determines if your child qualifies for special education.

How to Prepare for the FRENSHIP ISD ARD Meeting

To make the FRENSHIP ISD ARD meeting more productive:

  • Gather information: Bring medical records, previous reports, examples of schoolwork
  • Write down your concerns: List specific behaviors or difficulties you have observed
  • Prepare questions: Does my child qualify? What services do they need? How can I help at home?
  • Bring support: Consider bringing another person who knows your child well
  • Request documents in advance: Ask FRENSHIP ISD to send you the evaluation report before the meeting

During the ARD Meeting

At the FRENSHIP ISD ARD meeting, the team will explain the evaluation results. If your child qualifies for special education, they will create an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Remember: You are an equal member of the FRENSHIP ISD ARD team. Your observations and concerns are important. You can ask questions at any time.

Step 5: After the FRENSHIP ISD Decision

If Your Child Qualifies for Special Education

If the FRENSHIP ISD evaluation determines that your child qualifies, they will receive:

  • A detailed Individualized Education Program (IEP)
  • Specialized services (special education, speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, etc.)
  • Curriculum accommodations and modifications
  • Annual reviews of their progress

If Your Child Does Not Qualify According to FRENSHIP ISD

If FRENSHIP ISD determines that your child does not qualify for special education under IDEA, you still have options:

  • 504 Plan: If your child's condition affects a major life activity, they may qualify for a plan under Section 504
  • Response to Intervention (RTI) interventions: FRENSHIP ISD may provide intensive support before a special education evaluation
  • Request an independent evaluation: You can pay for a private evaluation and present it to FRENSHIP ISD
  • Request a due process review: If you believe FRENSHIP ISD made an error

Important Legal Information about FRENSHIP ISD Special Education

Laws Protecting Students in FRENSHIP ISD

FRENSHIP ISD must comply with several federal and state laws:

  • IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act): Guarantees free and appropriate special education
  • Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act: Prohibits discrimination based on disability
  • Texas Education Code §29.001 et seq.: Regulates special education in Texas
  • ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act): Requires access and reasonable accommodations

As a parent in FRENSHIP ISD, these rights protect you and your child.
