Complete Guide: How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in SEALY ISD

If your child is experiencing academic or behavioral difficulties, you have rights guaranteed by federal law to request a special education evaluation in SEALY ISD. This guide will help you understand the step-by-step process, your rights as a parent, and how to navigate the special education system in SEALY, Texas.

Why Seek a Special Education Evaluation in SEALY ISD?

A special education evaluation is the first step in determining if your child qualifies for special services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). If your child in SEALY ISD shows signs of learning difficulties, emotional problems, developmental delays, or physical disabilities, an evaluation can open the door to crucial support.

Many families in SEALY ISD discover that their children have disabilities that explain their academic struggles. With the correct diagnosis and appropriate services, students can thrive in the classroom and reach their full potential.

Your Rights as a Parent in SEALY ISD

Fundamental Rights in the Evaluation Process

As a parent of a student in SEALY ISD, you have rights protected by federal law. These rights include being notified in your preferred language, participating in all decisions regarding your child's evaluation, and receiving a copy of all reports.

  • Right to request evaluation: You can request that your child be evaluated at any time if you have concerns
  • Right to refuse: SEALY ISD must obtain your written consent before conducting the initial evaluation
  • Right to interpreters: All documents and meetings can be in Spanish if that is your preferred language
  • Right to representation: You can bring a special education attorney or advocate to any meeting in SEALY ISD
  • Right to records: You can request and review all of your child's educational records

Protections under Texas Law

The state of Texas and SEALY ISD must comply with the Texas Education Code, which specifies the procedures for requesting special education evaluations. Parents in SEALY ISD have specific protections under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act and under IDEA.

Steps to Request an Evaluation in SEALY ISD

Step 1: Express Your Concerns in Writing

The first step to request a special evaluation in SEALY ISD is to make a formal written request. Although you can speak verbally with your child's teachers, a written request creates an official record that initiates the legal timeline.

Send a letter or email to your school principal and the SEALY ISD special education department. Include:

  • Your child's full name and student ID number
  • Specific academic or behavioral concerns
  • How these concerns affect your child's learning
  • Your clear request: "I formally request that my child be evaluated for special education"
  • Your contact information (phone and email)

Keep a copy of everything you send and submit your request in a way that allows you to confirm delivery (certified mail, email with read receipt, or deliver in person and ask for a receipt).

Step 2: Quick Response Meeting

SEALY ISD must respond to your evaluation request within a specific timeframe. By law, the district must hold a meeting with you to discuss your concerns.

In this SEALY ISD meeting, the school team may:

  • Ask questions about your child's history
  • Review current academic performance data
  • Discuss interventions that have already been tried
  • Agree to or decline the formal evaluation

If SEALY ISD declines the evaluation, they must explain why and provide you with information on how to dispute the decision. You can always request mediation or a due process hearing.

Step 3: Written Consent for Evaluation

If SEALY ISD accepts your request, the district will send you a parental consent form that you must sign. This form should be in Spanish if that is your preferred language.

The consent explains:

  • What areas your child will be evaluated in (academic, behavioral, language development, etc.)
  • What tests will be used
  • When the evaluation will begin
  • Your rights during the process

Do not sign anything you do not fully understand. Ask SEALY ISD to explain each part, or seek assistance from a special education advocate.

Step 4: The Full Individual Evaluation (FIE)

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is the formal examination that determines if your child qualifies for special education. SEALY ISD must complete the SEALY ISD FIE within 60 days of receiving your consent.

The evaluation typically includes:

  • Intelligence and academic aptitude tests
  • Behavioral and emotional evaluations
  • Language and communication assessment
  • Medical examination or motor development (if necessary)
  • Review of previous school records

During the special evaluation in SEALY ISD, specialists may work with your child at school or in other appropriate locations.

Step 5: ARD Committee Meeting (Admission, Review, Dismissal)

After completing the evaluation, SEALY ISD will hold an ARD committee meeting within 30 days. ARD stands for "Admission, Review, Dismissal" and this team reviews the evaluation results.

At the SEALY ISD ARD committee meeting, the following will be discussed:

  • The evaluation findings
  • Whether your child qualifies for special education
  • The disability category (if applicable)
  • The services your child will receive
  • The Individualized Education Program (IEP)

You must be invited to this meeting. SEALY ISD must send you the invitation at least 10 days before the date. If you cannot attend, request an alternative date.

Understanding the Individualized Education Program (IEP)

What is an IEP?

If your child qualifies for special education in SEALY ISD, they will receive an Individualized Education Program (IEP). The IEP is a legal document that describes exactly what special services your child will receive.

The SEALY ISD IEP includes:

  • Your child's current levels of performance
  • Specific and measurable annual education goals
  • Special services your child will receive (therapy, special instruction, etc.)
  • Classroom accommodations and modifications
  • How progress will be measured
  • The amount of time your child will spend in general education versus special education

Your Role in IEP Development

As a parent, your voice is critical in the development of your child's IEP in SEALY ISD. You know your child better than anyone, and your perspectives on their strengths and needs are valuable.

During the IEP meeting at SEALY ISD, you can:

  • Share information about how your child functions at home
  • Express your concerns and priorities
  • Ask questions about any aspect of the plan
  • Request specific services or changes
  • Refuse to sign if you do not agree with the plan

Key Timelines to Remember

Federal law establishes specific timelines that SEALY ISD must follow. Knowing these deadlines helps you monitor the process and ensure the district is fulfilling its legal obligations.

Process Stage SEALY ISD Timeline
Response to evaluation request 15-30 days
Complete evaluation (FIE) 60 days from consent
ARD committee meeting 30 days after evaluation completion

What to Do if You Disagree with SEALY ISD?

Dispute Process

If you believe SEALY ISD is not correctly following the evaluation process, or if you disagree with the results, you have options.

You can:

  • Request a second opinion: Ask for an independent evaluator to assess your child (SEALY ISD may have to pay)
  • Request mediation: A neutral mediator can help resolve disagreements between you and SEALY ISD
  • File a formal complaint: You can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA)
  • Request a due process hearing: This is a more formal legal process where a judge reviews the case

When to Consider Legal Help

If SEALY ISD denies an evaluation without adequate justification, or if you believe your child is not receiving necessary services, it may be time to consult with a special education attorney near SEALY or a special education advocate in SEALY.
