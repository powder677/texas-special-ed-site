Complete Guide to Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in AZLE ISD

If your child shows learning or developmental difficulties, you have protected legal rights to request a special education evaluation in AZLE ISD. This guide explains step-by-step how to navigate the AZLE ISD special evaluation process and what to expect as a parent.

Why is it Important to Request an Evaluation in AZLE ISD?

Children in AZLE ISD who qualify for special education receive free services and support specifically designed for their needs. Without a formal evaluation, your child could miss out on the resources they need to succeed academically.

A comprehensive evaluation in AZLE ISD can identify disabilities such as:

  • Specific learning difficulties (dyslexia, dyscalculia)
  • Attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD)
  • Autism or autism spectrum disorder
  • Intellectual disabilities
  • Speech and language disorders
  • Physical or orthopedic disabilities
  • Health problems affecting learning
  • Significant emotional or behavioral problems

For families in the AZLE area, understanding their AZLE ISD parent rights is the first step towards getting the support their child deserves.

Know Your Rights: AZLE ISD Parent Rights

Federal and state law ensures that all AZLE ISD parents have fundamental rights in the special education process. These rights are protected under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.

Your AZLE ISD parent rights include:

  • Right to request an evaluation: You can request an evaluation at any time if you believe your child has a disability
  • Right to a complete and free evaluation: AZLE ISD must conduct free evaluations in all home languages
  • Right to be informed: AZLE ISD must notify you in Spanish about all steps of the process
  • Right to participate: You participate in all important decisions about your child's education
  • Right to confidentiality: Your information is protected under privacy laws
  • Right to resolve conflicts: If you disagree with AZLE ISD, you can request mediation or due process

It is important for Spanish-speaking families to know that they can request that all communication with AZLE ISD be in Spanish, including documents, meetings, and evaluations.

Step 1: Recognize Warning Signs in AZLE ISD

Before requesting an AZLE ISD special education evaluation, many parents notice changes or difficulties in their children's behavior or learning. These signs may include:

Academic Signs

  • Difficulty learning to read, write, or do math at the same pace as peers
  • Problems following instructions or completing tasks
  • Persistent underperformance despite tutoring or additional help
  • Memory or concentration problems

Behavioral or Developmental Signs

  • Difficulty interacting socially with other children
  • Repetitive or unusual behavior
  • Significant behavioral problems at school or home
  • Delays in speech, language, or motor skills
  • Extreme anxiety or depression

If you observe these signs in your child, do not wait. The first step is to request an evaluation from AZLE ISD.

Step 2: Formally Request an Evaluation in AZLE ISD

To initiate an AZLE ISD special evaluation, you must make a formal written request. This is the most important way to ensure AZLE ISD responds in accordance with the law.

How to Submit Your Request

Address your written request to the Director of Special Education for AZLE ISD. You can submit the request:

  • In person at the AZLE ISD central office
  • By certified mail (recommended for proof of delivery)
  • By email (request confirmation of receipt)
  • By fax (request confirmation)

What to Include in Your Request

Your written request to AZLE ISD should contain:

  1. Your child's full name and age
  2. Name of the school your child attends in AZLE ISD
  3. Clear description of your academic, behavioral, or developmental concerns
  4. Specific examples of the difficulties you have observed
  5. Clear request: "I formally request a comprehensive evaluation to determine if my child qualifies for special education services"
  6. Your name, phone number, and email address
  7. Date and your signature

Example: "I formally request that AZLE ISD evaluate my son Juan for special education. He has had significant difficulties with reading since second grade. Despite help from teachers in AZLE ISD, he remains two years behind in reading fluency..."

Legal Timeline for AZLE ISD

By Texas law, AZLE ISD has 15 school days to respond to your evaluation request. The response must be in writing and state:

  • If AZLE ISD will conduct the evaluation
  • If AZLE ISD denies the evaluation and the reason (uncommon)
  • Information about your parent rights

Step 3: The Evaluation Process in AZLE ISD

Once AZLE ISD accepts your request, the AZLE ISD FIE (Full Individual Evaluation) process begins. This is a comprehensive process that examines multiple aspects of your child's learning.

Evaluation Components

AZLE ISD will evaluate:

  • Academic skills: Reading, writing, math
  • Cognition: Intellectual ability and processing
  • Language and communication: Expression and comprehension
  • Behavioral and emotional: Mental health and conduct
  • Vision and hearing: Sensory function
  • Motor development: Physical coordination

Duration of the Evaluation in AZLE ISD

AZLE ISD has a maximum of 60 calendar days from when it accepts your request to complete the evaluation. However, if you request an extension or miss an appointment, the deadline may be extended.

Evaluation in Your Language

AZLE ISD must provide evaluations in Spanish if that is the home language. Evaluators must be qualified to administer tests in Spanish and consider cultural and linguistic factors.

Step 4: The Eligibility Meeting in AZLE ISD

After completing the evaluation, AZLE ISD convenes an Eligibility Committee meeting to review the results and decide if your child qualifies for special education.

Who Participates in AZLE ISD

The following must be present at this meeting:

  • You (parent) - **mandatory participant**
  • An AZLE ISD representative authorized to make decisions
  • Evaluation specialist who conducted the tests
  • A general education teacher or relevant specialist
  • Other professionals as needed (speech pathologist, psychologist, etc.)

Possible Outcomes in AZLE ISD

Option 1: Qualifies for Special Education

If AZLE ISD determines that your child has a disability affecting education, it will proceed to create an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Option 2: Does Not Qualify, but May Qualify for Section 504

Sometimes a student does not qualify under IDEA but does qualify under Section 504, which is a broader protection. AZLE ISD must offer you this option.

Option 3: Does Not Qualify for Special Services

If AZLE ISD determines there is no disability, you can request an independent second opinion at AZLE ISD's expense (at no cost to you).

Step 5: Development of the Individualized Education Program (IEP) in AZLE ISD

If your child qualifies, AZLE ISD must develop an IEP (Individualized Education Program) within 30 days. This document specifies the special services your child will receive.

What an AZLE ISD IEP Includes

  • Current level of academic and functional performance
  • Measurable annual educational goals
  • Special services your child will receive (therapy, tutoring, accommodations)
  • Least restrictive learning environment possible
  • How progress will be measured
  • Review and reevaluation dates

As an AZLE ISD parent, you fully participate in this process and must agree before the IEP is implemented.

Frequently Asked Questions about AZLE ISD Special Education

How much does it cost to request an evaluation in AZLE ISD?

Nothing. AZLE ISD must provide evaluations completely free of charge. It cannot charge AZLE ISD parents for evaluations or special education services.

Can I request an evaluation during the school year in AZLE ISD?

Yes, at any time. AZLE ISD must process your request regardless of the time of year.

What happens if AZLE ISD refuses to evaluate my child?

AZLE ISD must document the reason in writing. If you disagree, you can request mediation or due process. Consider contacting a special education attorney near AZLE.

Do I need an attorney to request an evaluation in AZLE ISD?

It is not mandatory, but many AZLE ISD parents work with a special ed advocate AZLE to ensure their rights are respected.
