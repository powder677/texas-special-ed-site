What is a Special Education Evaluation in FORT STOCKTON ISD?

A special education evaluation, also known as a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation) in FORT STOCKTON ISD, is a series of tests and observations that determine if your child qualifies for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA).

This evaluation is completely free for parents in FORT STOCKTON ISD. There are no costs associated with requesting or completing a FIE in our district.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in FORT STOCKTON ISD

The process for requesting a FORT STOCKTON ISD special evaluation is relatively straightforward. Below are the exact steps you should follow.

Step 1: Contact Your Child's School

Your first step is to contact the school your child attends. In FORT STOCKTON ISD, you can speak with the classroom teacher, school counselor, or school administrator. Clearly express your concerns about your child's learning or behavior.

It is advisable that this communication be in writing (email or letter). Keep a copy for your records. Clearly state that you are requesting a special education evaluation.

Step 2: Submit a Formal Evaluation Request

Send a formal written request to the special education department of FORT STOCKTON ISD. The request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Name of the school your child attends
  • Your specific concerns about learning or behavior
  • Your contact information (phone and email)
  • Your signature and date

In FORT STOCKTON ISD, you can obtain a request form at your school's office or at the central district office. You can also send a simple letter with this information.

Step 3: FORT STOCKTON ISD Will Respond Within the Legal Timeline

Once FORT STOCKTON ISD receives your formal request, the district has 15 school days to respond. During this time, FORT STOCKTON ISD must:

  • Acknowledge receipt of your request
  • Provide information about your rights as FORT STOCKTON ISD parents' rights
  • Explain the evaluation process
  • Request your written consent to proceed with the evaluation

Step 4: Grant Informed Consent

Before FORT STOCKTON ISD can evaluate your child, you must sign an informed consent form. This document explains exactly what will be evaluated, who will conduct the evaluation, and how the results will be used.

Read it carefully. If anything is unclear, ask questions. FORT STOCKTON ISD is obligated to answer all your questions before you sign the consent.

The FORT STOCKTON ISD FIE Evaluation Process

What Areas Does FORT STOCKTON ISD Evaluate?

A full evaluation in FORT STOCKTON ISD generally includes:

  • Academic evaluation: Tests of reading, writing, math, and other academic skills
  • Cognitive evaluation: Tests of intelligence and thinking skills
  • Behavioral and emotional evaluation: Observations and questionnaires about the child's behavior
  • Speech and language evaluation: If there are concerns about how your child communicates
  • Motor evaluation: Gross and fine motor skills if necessary
  • Social/adaptive evaluation: How your child interacts with others and performs daily activities

Evaluation Timeline in FORT STOCKTON ISD

FORT STOCKTON ISD has 60 calendar days to complete the evaluation after you give consent. This timeline is important and guaranteed by Texas law.

During these 60 days, FORT STOCKTON ISD evaluators will conduct tests, observe your child in the classroom, and gather information from teachers and other professionals who know your child.

After the Evaluation: Results Meeting in FORT STOCKTON ISD

Who Participates in the FORT STOCKTON ISD Meeting

Possible Results of the FORT STOCKTON ISD Evaluation

  • Needs additional evaluation: In some cases, FORT STOCKTON ISD may recommend additional specialized evaluations
