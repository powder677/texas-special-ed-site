Complete Guide to Requesting Special Education Evaluation in CHAPEL HILL ISD

If your child is struggling in school, you have the right to request a special education evaluation in CHAPEL HILL ISD. This guide will explain step-by-step how to navigate the process and protect your family's rights.

Why Request a Special Education Evaluation?

Parents in CHAPEL HILL ISD can request an evaluation when their children show signs of learning difficulties, emotional disabilities, hearing or vision problems, or any other condition that affects learning. A CHAPEL HILL ISD special education evaluation is the first step to obtaining appropriate special education services and supports.

The evaluation is free for all CHAPEL HILL ISD students. There is no cost to families. The district is obligated to evaluate your child within certain timelines established by Texas law.

Signs your child might benefit from an evaluation

  • Difficulty following instructions or maintaining attention
  • Language or reading delays
  • Frequent or intense behavioral problems
  • Difficulty interacting with other students
  • Low academic performance despite efforts
  • Problems with gross or fine motor skills

Parent Rights in CHAPEL HILL ISD

Federal and state law protects the rights of parents in CHAPEL HILL ISD throughout the special education evaluation and services process. Understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Your main rights include:

  • Right to request: You can request a special education evaluation at any time during the school year
  • Right to notice: CHAPEL HILL ISD must notify you in writing before evaluating your child
  • Right to consent: You must give written consent before any FIE (Full Individual Evaluation) begins
  • Right to privacy: Information about your child is confidential
  • Right to participate: You can attend all meetings regarding your child's evaluation and services
  • Right to interpreter: You can receive information in Spanish about CHAPEL HILL ISD special education services

Step 1: How to Request an Evaluation in CHAPEL HILL ISD

The process begins with a formal request. While it can be verbal, it is better to do it in writing for documentation. Parents in CHAPEL HILL ISD can request an evaluation in several ways.

Ways to request the evaluation

  1. Contact your child's teacher or counselor to express your concerns
  2. Submit a written request to the school principal or the CHAPEL HILL ISD special education coordinator
  3. Send a certified letter if you wish for official documentation of your request
  4. Speak directly with the CHAPEL HILL ISD special education department

In your request, explain specifically why you believe your child needs an evaluation. Mention the behaviors or academic difficulties you have observed. The more detailed your CHAPEL HILL ISD evaluation request, the better prepared the evaluation team will be.

Step 2: The Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) Process in CHAPEL HILL ISD

Once CHAPEL HILL ISD receives your request, the district will initiate what is called a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation). This is a comprehensive process designed to identify if your child has a disability requiring special education services.

FIE process timeline in CHAPEL HILL ISD

  • Within 15 days: CHAPEL HILL ISD must notify you that they received your request and provide you with information about your parental rights
  • Within 60 days: The district must complete the evaluation (or 45 instructional days if less)
  • Within 60 days: An ARD (IEP) meeting must be held to discuss the results

It is important to understand that CHAPEL HILL ISD cannot begin the evaluation without your written consent. The district will provide you with documents you must sign authorizing the evaluation.

What is evaluated in CHAPEL HILL ISD

The special education evaluation in CHAPEL HILL ISD is comprehensive and includes multiple areas:

  • Academic skills (reading, writing, math)
  • Language and communication development
  • Social and emotional skills
  • Motor development (gross and fine motor skills)
  • Medical and developmental history
  • Home information and behavior

Step 3: The ARD (IEP) Meeting

After completing the evaluation, CHAPEL HILL ISD will schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to discuss the results. This is a crucial meeting where important decisions about your child's services are made.

Who attends the ARD meeting

The law requires the following individuals to attend the CHAPEL HILL ISD ARD meeting:

  • Parents or guardians
  • The regular education teacher
  • A special education specialist
  • A CHAPEL HILL ISD administrator
  • The person who conducted the evaluation (or someone who can interpret the results)
  • Your child (if age-appropriate)

As a parent, you have the full right to bring someone to support you to the ARD meeting in CHAPEL HILL ISD. This could be a special education attorney, a parent advocate, a family member, or any trusted person.

What happens at the meeting

  1. The CHAPEL HILL ISD team presents the evaluation results
  2. It is discussed whether the student qualifies for special education
  3. If qualified, an IEP (Individualized Education Program) is developed
  4. Necessary services and supports are determined
  5. It is established where your child will receive services

Understanding the CHAPEL HILL ISD Evaluation Results

The results of the special education evaluation in CHAPEL HILL ISD may include standardized tests, behavioral assessments, and classroom observations. It's normal to feel overwhelmed by technical information.

How to prepare to understand the results

  • Ask for clear explanations: CHAPEL HILL ISD team members should explain the results in language you understand
  • Request copies: Ask for copies of the entire evaluation before the ARD meeting
  • Bring an interpreter: If needed, CHAPEL HILL ISD must provide a Spanish interpreter at no cost
  • Take notes: Write down what is said during the meeting
  • Ask questions: Never hesitate to ask what something means

Disability Categories Recognized by CHAPEL HILL ISD

Texas and CHAPEL HILL ISD recognize several disability categories that qualify for special education services. Your child must fall into one of these categories to receive formal services.

Main categories

  • Intellectual Disability: Significant limitations in intellectual functioning
  • Emotional Disability: Severe emotional or behavioral problems
  • Specific Learning Disability: Difficulties in reading, writing, or math
  • Autism: Autism spectrum disorder
  • Speech or Language Impairment: Communication difficulties
  • Hearing or Visual Impairment: Significant hearing or vision loss
  • Orthopedic Impairment: Physical or mobility disabilities
  • Other Health Impairment: Medical conditions affecting education
  • Traumatic Brain Injury: Brain damage due to trauma
  • Multiple Disabilities: More than one disability

  3. Seek legal advice: Consider consulting with a special education attorney near CHAPEL HILL to understand your options
  4. Request mediation: CHAPEL HILL ISD can facilitate mediation between you and the district to resolve disputes
