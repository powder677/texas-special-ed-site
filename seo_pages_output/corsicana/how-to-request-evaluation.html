Complete Guide to Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in CORSICANA ISD

If your child is struggling in school, you have fundamental rights under federal and state law to request a special education evaluation. This guide will help you navigate the process in CORSICANA ISD and understand your options as a parent or guardian.

Who Qualifies for a Special Education Evaluation in CORSICANA ISD?

In CORSICANA ISD, any student showing signs of a disability may be referred for evaluation. This includes students with learning difficulties, behavioral problems, language development delays, or special health needs.

The special education evaluation in CORSICANA ISD is the first step in determining if your child is eligible to receive special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA).

Signs Your Child Might Need Evaluation

  • Difficulty learning to read or do math despite quality instruction
  • Frequent behavioral problems or difficulty following instructions
  • Speech or language delays compared to same-aged peers
  • Difficulty socializing or making friends
  • Frequent absences or resistance to attending school
  • Health needs that affect academic performance

Your Rights as a Parent in CORSICANA ISD

As a parent of a student in CORSICANA ISD, you have fundamental rights protected by law. These rights include the right to request an evaluation, participate in all decision-making meetings, and receive information in your preferred language.

Key Parent Rights in CORSICANA ISD

  • Right to request evaluation: You can request in writing that CORSICANA ISD evaluate your child at no cost
  • Right to comprehensive evaluation: The evaluation must examine all areas related to the suspected disability
  • Right to participate: You have the right to participate in all special education meetings and decisions
  • Right to information in your language: CORSICANA ISD must provide documents and meetings in Spanish if that is your preferred language
  • Right to challenge decisions: If you disagree with the evaluation results, you have appeal options
  • Right to a second opinion: You can request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at the district's expense

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in CORSICANA ISD

The process begins when you formally request that CORSICANA ISD evaluate your child. This request must be in writing and addressed to the district's special education department.

Step 1: Prepare Your Written Request

Your request does not need to be complicated. Write a simple letter addressed to the special education director of CORSICANA ISD that includes:

  • Your child's full name
  • Date of birth and student ID number
  • Name of the school your child attends
  • Specific reasons why you believe your child needs evaluation
  • Your concerns about academic or behavioral performance
  • Your name, phone number, and email address

Sample letter: "I formally request that CORSICANA ISD conduct a comprehensive special education evaluation for my child [name]. I have noticed that he/she is having difficulties with [describe specific difficulties]. I would like all areas to be evaluated to determine if he/she is eligible for special education services."

Step 2: Submit the Formal Request

Send your request by one of these methods to CORSICANA ISD:

  • Hand-delivery to your child's school principal
  • Hand-delivery to the CORSICANA ISD special education department
  • Certified mail with return receipt
  • Email (request confirmation of receipt)

Important tip: Keep a copy of your request and the confirmation of receipt. This is critical for documenting that CORSICANA ISD received your request on time.

Step 3: Await Response from CORSICANA ISD

By Texas state and federal law (IDEA), CORSICANA ISD has a very specific timeline for responding. The district must:

  • Acknowledge your request within 5 school days
  • Complete the evaluation within 60 calendar days from when you give your written consent
  • Schedule an IEP (Individualized Education Program) meeting if your child is found eligible

The Evaluation Process in CORSICANA ISD

Once CORSICANA ISD accepts your request, the district will conduct a comprehensive evaluation. This evaluation is much more than a simple test: it examines multiple aspects of your child's development and performance.

Areas Evaluated in CORSICANA ISD

The comprehensive evaluation in CORSICANA ISD must include:

  • Academic evaluation: Performance in reading, math, writing, and other content areas
  • Cognitive evaluation: Thinking, reasoning, and learning skills
  • Behavioral evaluation: Conduct at school and home, behavioral patterns
  • Language evaluation: Receptive and expressive language skills
  • Motor development evaluation: Gross and fine motor skills when appropriate
  • Health evaluation: Relevant medical information and developmental history

Your Participation in the CORSICANA ISD Evaluation

As a parent, your participation is essential in the CORSICANA ISD evaluation process. You know your child better than anyone and can provide valuable information that evaluators need.

  • You can participate in the evaluations alongside CORSICANA ISD professionals
  • You will be asked to complete questionnaires about your child's behavior and development
  • You must give your written informed consent before the evaluation begins
  • You have the right to see all test results before the IEP meeting

Understanding the Results: Special Education Evaluation in CORSICANA ISD

After completing the evaluation in CORSICANA ISD, the team of professionals will meet with you to discuss the results. It is important to understand what these results mean.

Possible Outcomes of the CORSICANA ISD Evaluation

  • Eligible for special education: If CORSICANA ISD finds that your child has a disability and needs specialized services, they will be eligible. An Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be developed.
  • Not eligible for special education: If CORSICANA ISD determines that your child does not qualify, you can request an independent evaluation or explore other support options.
  • Eligible under Section 504: Your child might not qualify for special education but may qualify for protections under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.

If You Disagree with the Results in CORSICANA ISD

If you disagree with the evaluation conducted by CORSICANA ISD, you have important rights:

  • You can request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at CORSICANA ISD's expense
  • You can request a mediated conference to resolve disagreements
  • You can file a formal complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA)
  • You can consider consulting with a special education attorney or advocate
