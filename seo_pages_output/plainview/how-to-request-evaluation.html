Complete Guide to Requesting Special Education Evaluation in PLAINVIEW ISD

If your child needs additional academic support, you have the right to request a special education evaluation in PLAINVIEW ISD. This guide will explain the step-by-step process so you can advocate for your child's rights.

Why is it Important to Request an Evaluation in PLAINVIEW ISD?

Many parents in PLAINVIEW ISD do not know that they can request an evaluation if they suspect their child has a disability. A professional evaluation can identify learning difficulties, behavioral problems, or special needs that are not evident at home.

If your child qualifies for special education services, PLAINVIEW ISD must provide an Individualized Education Program (IEP) at no additional cost to your family. This plan ensures that your child receives the necessary support to achieve academic success.

Some signs that your child might need an evaluation include:

  • Difficulty following instructions
  • Speech or language delays
  • Frequent behavioral problems
  • Difficulty reading or doing math
  • Attention or concentration problems
  • Physical or visual disabilities
  • Difficulty interacting with other children

Parents' Rights in PLAINVIEW ISD

As a parent, you have important rights under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and the Texans with Disabilities Act. PLAINVIEW ISD must respect these rights at all times.

Your Main Rights

Under federal and state laws, you have the right to:

  • Request an evaluation at any time if you believe your child has a disability
  • Receive complete information about the evaluation process before it begins
  • Participate in all decisions regarding your child's special education
  • Obtain a copy of all your child's educational records
  • Challenge any decision you disagree with
  • Have an interpreter if Spanish is your primary language
  • Receive communication in your preferred language

PLAINVIEW ISD must ensure that all these rights are respected for families in the PLAINVIEW community.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in PLAINVIEW ISD

The process for requesting a special education evaluation in PLAINVIEW ISD is relatively simple, but it is important to follow the correct steps. Here's what you need to do:

Step 1: Submit a Written Request

Send a written request to the PLAINVIEW ISD special education director or directly to your child's school. You can send it by mail, email, or in person. The request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • The school they attend
  • A brief description of your concerns
  • Your contact information (phone and email)

Example request: "I hereby request a full special education evaluation for my child [name]. I observe difficulties in [reading/behavior/attention] that concern me."

Step 2: PLAINVIEW ISD Must Respond

Under the Texas Education Code, PLAINVIEW ISD must respond to your request within 15 school days. They may:

  • Agree to conduct the evaluation
  • Request more information
  • Deny the request (but they must provide written reasons)

If PLAINVIEW ISD denies your request, you have the right to appeal it. Consider contacting a special education attorney or parent advocate if this happens.

Step 3: Informed Consent

Before any evaluation begins, PLAINVIEW ISD must obtain your written consent. They will provide you with a form explaining:

  • What types of tests will be conducted
  • Who will conduct the evaluations
  • Where and when they will occur
  • Your rights as a parent

Read it carefully and ask questions if anything is unclear. You can request that the document be read to you in Spanish.

The Evaluation Process in PLAINVIEW ISD

Once you have given your consent, PLAINVIEW ISD must complete the evaluation within 60 calendar days. The evaluation team will include specialists such as school psychologists, special education teachers, and other professionals.

Types of Evaluations

Evaluations may include:

  • Academic achievement tests to assess reading and math skills
  • Psychoeducational evaluations to measure cognitive functioning
  • Behavioral evaluations to understand behavioral problems
  • Speech and language evaluations if there are communication concerns
  • Motor skills evaluations if necessary
  • Review of previous medical and academic records

The PLAINVIEW ISD team may conduct additional tests based on your child's specific needs.

Your Role During the Evaluation

As a parent, you are a critical part of the process. It is important that you:

  • Share information about your child's behavior and development
  • Describe any medical or family concerns
  • Provide specific examples of difficulties you have observed
  • Talk about your child's strengths as well
  • Ask questions if you don't understand something

After the Evaluation: FIE Meeting in PLAINVIEW ISD

Once the evaluation is complete, PLAINVIEW ISD must convene a Findings and Implementation of Evaluation (FIE) meeting within 60 days. In this critical meeting, the team will discuss the results.

What Happens at the FIE Meeting

During the FIE meeting in PLAINVIEW ISD:

  • All test results will be presented
  • The team will determine if your child is eligible for services
  • Disability categories under IDEA will be explained
  • Special educational needs will be discussed
  • Your questions will be answered

If your child is eligible, PLAINVIEW ISD will proceed to develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

If Your Child Does Not Qualify

If PLAINVIEW ISD evaluators determine that your child does not have a disability that makes them eligible for IDEA, you still have options:

  • You can request an independent second opinion
  • You can request services under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act
  • You can seek legal representation if you believe the decision is incorrect
  • PLAINVIEW ISD must provide information about available resources

The Individualized Education Program (IEP)

If your child qualifies for special education, PLAINVIEW ISD must create an IEP within 30 days after the eligibility determination. The IEP is a legal document that outlines how the school will support your child.

Important Components of the IEP

Your PLAINVIEW ISD IEP must include:

  • Current level of performance: Where your child is now academically and behaviorally
  • Annual goals: What your child will achieve in the coming year
  • Special services: Speech therapy, special education, support services
  • Modifications and accommodations: Necessary changes for success
  • Participation in the general curriculum: How they will be included in regular class when possible
  • Progress evaluation: How progress will be measured
