Complete Guide to Requesting Special Education Evaluation in BROWNWOOD ISD

If your child is experiencing academic or behavioral difficulties at school, they may need a special education evaluation. This guide will help you understand the process for requesting an evaluation in BROWNWOOD ISD and your rights as a parent.

Who Can Request an Evaluation in BROWNWOOD ISD?

Anyone can request a special evaluation in BROWNWOOD ISD, including:

  • Parents or legal guardians
  • School teachers
  • BROWNWOOD ISD administrative staff
  • Doctors or medical specialists
  • The student themselves (if of legal age)

However, as a parent in BROWNWOOD ISD, you have the legal right to initiate this process on your own, without relying on the school to suggest it first.

Steps to Request a FIE Evaluation (Full Individual Evaluation) in BROWNWOOD ISD

Step 1: Formal Written Request

The first step is to submit a formal written request to the BROWNWOOD ISD Special Education Department. This request must be clear and include:

  • Student's full name and date of birth
  • Name of current school in BROWNWOOD ISD
  • Specific reason for requesting the evaluation
  • Areas of concern (academic, behavioral, physical, etc.)
  • Your contact information (phone and email)
  • Your signature and date

Send this request to your school's principal or special education coordinator. It is advisable to send it via certified mail to have a record of delivery.

Step 2: Eligibility and Consent Conference

Once BROWNWOOD ISD receives your request, they must schedule a conference within 15 school days. In this meeting:

  • They will explain the evaluation process
  • They will discuss the specific areas to be evaluated
  • They will provide you with a consent form for the evaluation
  • They will answer your questions about your child's rights

You must sign the written consent before BROWNWOOD ISD begins any evaluation. Without your permission, the school cannot legally proceed.

Step 3: The Evaluation Process

BROWNWOOD ISD staff will evaluate your child in areas such as:

  • Academic skills (reading, writing, math)
  • Cognitive development (intelligence and reasoning)
  • Social and emotional behavior
  • Communication and language skills
  • Motor development (coordination and movement)

Evaluations may include standardized tests, classroom observations, record reviews, and interviews with teachers and parents.

Step 4: IEP (Individualized Education Program) Meeting

After completing the evaluation, BROWNWOOD ISD must meet with you within 45 school days to discuss the results. In this IEP meeting:

  • They will share the evaluation findings
  • They will determine if your child is eligible for special education services
  • If eligible, they will develop an individualized education plan (IEP)
  • They will establish specific educational goals for the school year

Parent Rights in BROWNWOOD ISD During the Evaluation

Texas special education law grants parents in BROWNWOOD ISD several important rights:

Notification and Consent Rights

BROWNWOOD ISD must notify you in writing about any proposed evaluation. You also have the right to:

  • Receive all information in your language (Spanish)
  • Ask for clarification on terms you do not understand
  • Request a copy of all evaluations performed
  • Withdraw your consent at any time

Participation Rights

You have the right to:

  • Actively participate in all BROWNWOOD ISD meetings related to the evaluation and the IEP
  • Bring a special education advocate or attorney to meetings
  • Ask questions and express your concerns
  • Request an independent evaluation if you disagree with BROWNWOOD ISD's results

Confidentiality and Privacy

BROWNWOOD ISD must protect your child's privacy. This means:

  • Only necessary personnel access records
  • Records are kept confidential
  • You can review and request copies at any time
  • Written consent is required to share information with others

Timeline and Important Deadlines in BROWNWOOD ISD

It is important to understand the legal deadlines to ensure BROWNWOOD ISD complies with its obligations:

Milestone Deadline
BROWNWOOD ISD receives your request Day 1
Consent and eligibility conference 15 school days
Completion of all evaluations 45 school days
IEP meeting to discuss results 45 school days

Note these dates carefully. If BROWNWOOD ISD fails to meet these deadlines, this is a violation of your rights under federal law.

Preparation for BROWNWOOD ISD Meetings

To make the most of your meetings with BROWNWOOD ISD, prepare the following:

Documentation and Records

  • Gather student's school work from the past 6-12 months
  • Obtain copies of grades and progress reports
  • Collect any previous medical or psychological evaluations
  • Document specific behaviors or learning difficulties

List of Questions

Write down questions before the meeting, such as:

  • What are the specific findings of the BROWNWOOD ISD evaluation?
  • Does my child qualify for special education services?
  • What specific services will be provided?
  • How often will services be received?
  • How will progress be measured?

Bring Support

Consider bringing someone with you to the BROWNWOOD ISD meeting:

  • A trained special education advocate
  • An attorney specializing in special education
  • A family member or friend for support

If You Disagree with BROWNWOOD ISD's Results

If you believe the evaluation performed by BROWNWOOD ISD is not complete or accurate, you have options:

Independent Evaluation (IEE)

You can request an Independent Evaluation (IEE) at the district's expense. BROWNWOOD ISD must:

  • Provide a list of approved evaluators in the BROWNWOOD area
  • Fully pay for the independent evaluation
  • Consider the results of the IEE in special education decisions

Dispute Resolution

If you cannot resolve disagreements directly with BROWNWOOD ISD, you can request:

  • Mediation: A neutral third party helps resolve the conflict
  • Due Process Hearing: An impartial officer hears both sides
  • State Complaint: File a formal complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA)

Resources for Parents in BROWNWOOD ISD and the BROWNWOOD Area

You are not alone in this process. Resources are available for parents in BROWNWOOD ISD:

  • BROWNWOOD ISD Special Education Coordinator: Contact the central office for contact information
  • Regional Education Service Center (ESC): TEA Region 8 (South Central Education) serves the BROWNWOOD area
  • Special Education Advocates: Organizations that assist parents for free or low cost
  • Parents as Teachers: Support programs for BROWNWOOD ISD families

Conclusion: Your Rights in BROWNWOOD ISD

Requesting a special education evaluation in BROWNWOOD ISD is your right as a parent. The process may seem complicated, but with this information and proper support, you can navigate it confidently.

Remember that BROWNWOOD ISD has the legal responsibility to provide a free appropriate public education to all students. If your child needs special education services, BROWNWOOD ISD must provide them.

Do not hesitate to ask questions, seek clarification, and advocate for your child's rights. You are your child's most important advocate in the BROWNWOOD ISD school system.
