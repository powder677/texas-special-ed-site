Complete Guide to Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in DECATUR ISD

If you are a parent of a student in DECATUR ISD and suspect your child may have a disability affecting their learning, you have the legal right to request a special evaluation. What is a Special Education Evaluation in DECATUR ISD?

A special education evaluation, also known as a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation), is a comprehensive process of testing and observations to determine if your child qualifies for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). In DECATUR ISD, this evaluation is an essential step to identify your student's needs.

The DECATUR ISD special evaluation examines multiple areas of a child's development, including cognitive, academic, communication, motor, and socio-emotional skills. DECATUR ISD professionals use a variety of assessment tools to obtain a complete picture of your child's strengths and needs.

It is important to understand that requesting an evaluation does not automatically mean your child will receive special education services. However, it is the necessary first step to get the answers your family needs.

Parent Rights in DECATUR ISD

As a parent in DECATUR ISD, you have specific rights under federal and Texas state law. Understanding these DECATUR ISD parent rights is fundamental to effectively advocate for your child.

Your Main Rights

  • Right to request evaluation: You can request an evaluation at any time if you believe your child has a disability affecting their education
  • Right to be informed: DECATUR ISD must notify you in writing about any proposed evaluation and obtain your consent
  • Right to participate: You have the right to be present and participate in all meetings related to the evaluation and placement in special education
  • Right to obtain records: You can request and review all of your child's evaluation records
  • Right to a second opinion: If you disagree with the results, you can request an independent evaluation paid for by DECATUR ISD
  • Right to an interpreter: If necessary, DECATUR ISD must provide an interpreter at meetings related to special education

Under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act and the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), in addition to IDEA, students in the DECATUR area have additional protections against discrimination.

How to Request a DECATUR ISD Special Evaluation: Key Steps

The process for DECATUR ISD evaluation request is relatively straightforward, but it is important to follow the correct steps to ensure your request is processed appropriately.

Step 1: Express Your Concerns to the Teacher or Counselor

The first step is usually to contact your child's teacher or school counselor at DECATUR ISD. Explain your specific concerns about your child's academic performance or behavior. Many teachers in DECATUR ISD can offer initial interventions or provide helpful information about what they are observing in the classroom.

While it is not mandatory to contact the school first, this informal step can expedite the process and help everyone better understand the concerns.

Step 2: Formally Request the Evaluation in Writing

To ensure an official record, you must formally request the evaluation in writing from DECATUR ISD. Address your request to your child's elementary school principal or the special education department of DECATUR ISD.

Your written request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current school grade
  • A clear description of your specific concerns
  • Any previous relevant medical evaluations or information
  • Your name, phone number, and email address

Send this request via certified mail or email to have a record of its delivery. DECATUR ISD must respond within a specific timeframe after receiving your request.

Step 3: Informed Consent

Within 15 business days, DECATUR ISD must contact you to obtain your informed consent to proceed with the evaluation. You will receive documentation explaining what will be evaluated, what methods will be used, and where the evaluation will take place.

Do not sign any consent document until you fully understand what you are authorizing. If you have questions, ask DECATUR ISD staff for clarification before signing.

Step 4: The Evaluation

Once you grant your consent, DECATUR ISD has 60 business days (approximately three school months) to complete the evaluation. During this period, DECATUR ISD specialists will evaluate your child using multiple tools and methods.

The evaluation may include:

  • Psychological and cognitive tests
  • Educational and academic evaluations
  • Language and speech evaluations
  • Motor evaluations
  • Classroom observations
  • Review of previous school records
  • Parent and teacher input

Step 5: Results Meeting and Eligibility Determination

After completing the evaluation, DECATUR ISD will schedule a meeting to review the results. In this meeting, the evaluation team will explain the findings and determine if your child qualifies for special education services.

If your child qualifies, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be developed. If they do not qualify for IDEA, they may be eligible for protections under Section 504 or a 504 Plan.

DECATUR ISD Special Education: Eligibility Categories

Under IDEA and DECATUR ISD special education, students may qualify for services if they have a disability in one or more of the following categories:

  • Intellectual disability
  • Hearing impairment
  • Deafness
  • Speech or language impairment
  • Visual impairment
  • Emotional disturbance
  • Orthopedic impairment
  • Other health impairment
  • Deaf-blindness
  • Traumatic brain injury
  • Specific learning disability (including dyslexia)
  • Autism spectrum disorder

The disability must adversely affect the student's educational performance for them to be eligible for services in DECATUR ISD.

DECATUR ISD FIE Spanish: Evaluation in Your Language

If you speak Spanish at home, you have the right for the DECATUR ISD FIE Spanish to be administered considering your family's language. DECATUR ISD must ensure that evaluations are culturally and linguistically appropriate.

This means:

  • Tests can be administered in Spanish, English, or both languages, as appropriate
  • Careful consideration must be given as to whether difficulties are due to a disability or normal linguistic differences
  • Bilingual interpreters and evaluators must be involved when necessary
  • All documents must be provided in Spanish

Be sure to inform DECATUR ISD staff about your language preference when requesting the evaluation.

Preparing for the DECATUR ISD Meeting: Practical Tips

When meeting with the DECATUR ISD team to discuss evaluation results, being prepared will help you effectively advocate for your child.

Before the Meeting

  • Gather specific examples of behaviors or academic challenges you have observed at home
  • Prepare a list of questions for the DECATUR ISD team
  • Review your child's school records if possible
  • Consider bringing another person for support (partner, family member, or advocate)
  • If you are not fluent in English, request an interpreter from DECATUR ISD in advance

During the Meeting

  • Take notes on the key points discussed
  • Ask for clarification if you don't understand something
  • Ask questions about how the results will impact your child's education
  • Ask about the next steps if your child qualifies or does not qualify for services

If You Disagree with DECATUR ISD's Results

If you believe the evaluation conducted by DECATUR ISD is incomplete, inaccurate, or unfair, you have options available.

Requesting an Independent Evaluation

You can request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at DECATUR ISD's expense. This evaluation must be performed by an evaluator who does not work for DECATUR ISD.

To request an IEE, send a written request to the special education director of DECATUR ISD expressing your disagreement with the original evaluation.

Dispute Resolution

If you cannot resolve disagreements with DECATUR ISD, you have the right to:

  • Mediation: An informal process facilitated by a neutral third party
  • Due Process Hearing: A formal hearing before an administrative law judge
  • File a complaint with TEA: The Texas Education Agency can investigate IDEA violations
