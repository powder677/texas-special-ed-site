Understanding Parents' Rights in UVALDE CISD

As a parent in UVALDE CISD, you have rights protected under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act. These rights ensure that your child receives a free and appropriate public education in UVALDE CISD.

Your Main Rights in UVALDE CISD

  • Request evaluation: You can formally request a special education evaluation from UVALDE CISD at any time if you believe your child has a disability.
  • Receive notification: UVALDE CISD must inform you about the evaluation process before beginning any testing.
  • Participate in decisions: You have the right to participate in all UVALDE CISD meetings regarding your child's evaluation and services.
  • Obtain an interpreter: UVALDE CISD must provide Spanish interpretation services during all proceedings.
  • Access records: You can review all educational records of your child maintained by UVALDE CISD.

UVALDE CISD parents' rights are codified in the Texas Education Code and federal regulations. Knowing these rights is the first step to obtaining the services your child needs.

When Should You Request an Evaluation in UVALDE CISD?

Parents in UVALDE CISD should consider requesting an evaluation if they notice signs of academic, behavioral, or developmental difficulties. Do not wait for problems to worsen—early intervention can make a significant difference in your child's success.

Warning Signs in UVALDE CISD

  • Significant difficulty learning to read, write, or do math
  • Persistent behavioral problems or aggression without clear cause
  • Difficulty following instructions or staying on task
  • Social isolation or difficulty making friends
  • Speech or language delays compared to age-matched peers
  • Motor coordination problems or unusual physical development
  • Frequent absences related to mental or physical health issues

If you observe any of these signs, consider contacting your child's school in UVALDE CISD to discuss your concerns.

Steps to Request a UVALDE CISD Evaluation

The process for requesting an evaluation from UVALDE CISD is structured but accessible. These steps will guide you through the complete procedure for UVALDE CISD special education.

Step 1: Initial Communication with UVALDE CISD

Begin by contacting your child's teacher or school counselor in UVALDE CISD. Explain your specific concerns about your child's learning or behavior. This informal conversation often provides valuable insights into how your child is performing at school.

If the UVALDE CISD school suggests an evaluation, request this recommendation in writing. If the school does not recommend an evaluation but you still have concerns, proceed to the next step.

Step 2: Submit a Formal Written Request

To formally request an evaluation from UVALDE CISD, you must send a written request to the UVALDE CISD Special Education Department. This request is critical because it creates an official record of when you requested the evaluation.

What to include in your letter:

  • Your child's full name and age
  • Name of current school within UVALDE CISD
  • Clear description of your academic or behavioral concerns
  • Specific examples of difficulties you have observed
  • Explicit request for a comprehensive evaluation from UVALDE CISD (Full Individual Evaluation)
  • Your contact information and signature

Send the request by:

  • Certified mail to the UVALDE CISD Special Education Department
  • Email with read receipt
  • In-person delivery at UVALDE CISD offices (obtain a receipt)

Step 3: UVALDE CISD Responds to Your Request

UVALDE CISD has 15 calendar days to respond to your evaluation request. This response must include:

  • Confirmation that they received your request
  • A detailed proposed evaluation plan
  • Explanation of the tests to be administered
  • Notice of rights (in Spanish)

If UVALDE CISD denies your request, they must provide a detailed written reason. You have the right to disagree with this decision and can request mediation or a due process hearing.

Step 4: Informed Parental Consent

UVALDE CISD must obtain your written informed consent before beginning any evaluation. This means you must fully understand what evaluations will be conducted and how the results will be used.

Carefully review the evaluation plan. If you have questions, ask the UVALDE CISD special education team to clarify any aspects. Do not feel pressured to sign immediately—you have the right to take time to reflect.

Step 5: Comprehensive Evaluation in UVALDE CISD

Once you have given consent, the UVALDE CISD (Full Individual Evaluation) team will complete a comprehensive evaluation. This evaluation typically includes:

  • Cognitive tests: Assessment of thinking and reasoning
  • Academic evaluation: Tests in reading, writing, and mathematics
  • Behavioral assessment: Classroom observations and questionnaires
  • Language development evaluation: Tests of receptive/expressive speech and language
  • Developmental history: Review of medical and educational background

UVALDE CISD must complete the evaluation within 60 calendar days from receiving consent. In exceptional cases, this timeline may be extended.

The Evaluation Meeting in UVALDE CISD

After completing all tests, UVALDE CISD will schedule a meeting to discuss the results. This is a crucial meeting where your child's special education services will be determined.

Preparation for the Meeting in UVALDE CISD

Before the UVALDE CISD special education evaluation meeting:

  • Review all evaluation results provided by UVALDE CISD
  • Note any questions or concerns
  • Prepare a list of your child's strengths and difficulties
  • Consider bringing a special education attorney or advocate if desired
  • Request a Spanish interpreter if you need one for the UVALDE CISD meeting

During the Meeting in UVALDE CISD

At the UVALDE CISD evaluation meeting:

  • The team will explain the findings of each evaluation
  • They will discuss if your child qualifies for special education services under IDEA or Section 504
  • If qualified, the UVALDE CISD team will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP)
  • You will participate in all decisions regarding services
  • You can question or challenge the results if you believe they are inaccurate

Remember: your voice matters in this UVALDE CISD meeting. It is a collaboration between you and the school team.

After the Evaluation: Next Steps in UVALDE CISD

After UVALDE CISD completes the evaluation, there are several possible paths depending on the results.

If Your Child Qualifies for Services in UVALDE CISD

If the UVALDE CISD special education team determines that your child has a disability affecting their education, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be developed. The IEP will include:

  • Specific and measurable educational goals
  • Necessary specialized services
  • Classroom accommodations and modifications
  • Timeline for review and progress evaluation

If Your Child Does Not Qualify in UVALDE CISD

If UVALDE CISD determines that your child does not qualify for special education services under IDEA, they may still be eligible under Section 504. Section 504 covers students with disabilities who do not meet IDEA criteria but require accommodations.

If you disagree with UVALDE CISD's results, you have options including mediation, an independent second evaluation, or a due process hearing.
