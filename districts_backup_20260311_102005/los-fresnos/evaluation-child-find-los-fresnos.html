Understanding Child Find and Your Rights in LOS FRESNOS CISD

LOS FRESNOS CISD is legally required under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities, regardless of the severity of their condition. This responsibility is known as "child find" and is a foundational principle in special education law.

Every parent in LOS FRESNOS CISD has the right to request an evaluation if they suspect their child has a disability that may affect their educational performance. You do not need a teacher's referral, medical diagnosis, or formal documentation to make this request. Your concern alone is sufficient to trigger the evaluation process.

LOS FRESNOS CISD special education services begin with a LOS FRESNOS CISD child find process that includes:

  • Universal screening and monitoring of all students
  • Referral procedures for concerned parents and educators
  • Response to intervention (RTI) data review before formal evaluation
  • Formal evaluation upon parent request

What is a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE)?

A Full Individual Evaluation, or FIE, is a comprehensive assessment of your child conducted by LOS FRESNOS CISD special education professionals. The evaluation examines multiple areas of your child's functioning to determine whether they have a disability and, if so, what special education services they need.

A complete LOS FRESNOS CISD FIE evaluation typically includes:

  • Cognitive assessment – testing intellectual functioning and reasoning abilities
  • Academic achievement testing – measuring reading, math, and writing skills
  • Speech and language evaluation – assessing communication abilities (if needed)
  • Social-emotional assessment – evaluating behavior and social-emotional development
  • Motor skills evaluation – assessing fine and gross motor development (if needed)
  • Vision and hearing screening – ruling out sensory concerns
  • Classroom observation – observing your child in educational settings
  • Parent and teacher input – gathering information about your child's development and behavior across settings

Step 1: Submit Your Written Request

To begin the LOS FRESNOS CISD evaluation request process, you must submit a written request to your child's campus principal or the LOS FRESNOS CISD special education department. While a verbal request can initiate the process, providing written documentation creates an official record.

Your request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Your contact information
  • Specific areas of concern (academic, behavioral, speech, motor skills, etc.)
  • Examples of how the concern affects your child at home and school
  • Any relevant medical or developmental history

Send this request to your child's campus administrator or email it to the LOS FRESNOS CISD special education department. Request a written acknowledgment confirming receipt of your request.

Step 2: Understand the LOS FRESNOS CISD 45-Day Timeline

Once LOS FRESNOS CISD receives your written evaluation request, the district has 45 calendar days to complete the LOS FRESNOS CISD FIE evaluation and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. This timeline is mandated by Texas Education Code and IDEA.

The LOS FRESNOS CISD 45-day timeline includes:

  1. Days 1-5: Parent notification and consent process
  2. Days 6-35: Completion of all evaluation components
  3. Days 36-45: Compilation of results and ARD meeting

If LOS FRESNOS CISD cannot complete the evaluation within 45 days, the district must provide written explanation and schedule a makeup appointment within 10 days after the original deadline.

Step 3: Provide Informed Consent

Within five days of receiving your request, LOS FRESNOS CISD special education personnel will contact you with a Prior Written Notice (PWN) and request your informed consent to evaluate. This document explains:

  • Why the evaluation is being proposed
  • What assessments will be conducted
  • Your parental rights and protections
  • How the results will be used

You must sign and return this consent form for the evaluation to proceed. Your consent applies only to the evaluation process—it does not mean your child will automatically receive special education services.

Step 4: Participate in the Evaluation Process

During the evaluation window, LOS FRESNOS CISD evaluators will conduct various assessments. You may be asked to:

  • Complete developmental history questionnaires
  • Participate in parent interviews
  • Provide school records and medical documentation
  • Observe your child's evaluation (you may request to observe)

Stay engaged and responsive during this period. If you have questions about what's being assessed or when results will be ready, contact your child's campus special education coordinator.

Step 5: Attend the ARD Meeting

The Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting is where LOS FRESNOS CISD discusses evaluation results and determines eligibility for special education services. This meeting must occur within the 45-day timeline and must include:

  • Special education teacher or specialist
  • General education teacher
  • School administrator
  • Parent(s) or guardian(s)
  • Other relevant personnel

At the ARD meeting, you will receive a copy of the complete LOS FRESNOS CISD FIE evaluation report with all assessment results explained in understandable language.

Understanding LOS FRESNOS CISD Evaluation Results

After your LOS FRESNOS CISD FIE evaluation is complete, the evaluation team will determine whether your child qualifies for special education services under one of 13 eligibility categories under IDEA:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Deaf-Blindness
  • Deafness
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Specific Learning Disability
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Visual Impairment

If your child qualifies: LOS FRESNOS CISD will develop an Individualized Education Plan (IEP) detailing specialized instruction, related services, and accommodations your child will receive.

If your child does not qualify: You have the right to request an independent evaluation at district expense or file for due process hearing if you disagree with LOS FRESNOS CISD's determination.

Important Parental Rights in LOS FRESNOS CISD Special Education

Parents in LOS FRESNOS CISD have specific protections under IDEA and Texas Education Code. Understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child's needs.

Right to Independent Evaluation

If you disagree with LOS FRESNOS CISD special education evaluation results, you may request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at no cost to you. LOS FRESNOS CISD must either agree to pay for the independent evaluation or file for due process to defend its evaluation.

Right to Due Process Hearing

If you disagree with LOS FRESNOS CISD's decisions regarding identification, evaluation, or placement, you may request a due process hearing through the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This formal process allows you to present evidence and arguments before an impartial hearing officer.

Right to Prior Written Notice

LOS FRESNOS CISD must provide written notice before proposing or refusing to propose changes to your child's identification, evaluation, or placement. You have the right to understand these decisions and respond.

Right to Parent Participation

You have the right to participate in all decisions regarding your child's special education in LOS FRESNOS CISD, including evaluation, IEP development, and placement decisions.

What to Do If LOS FRESNOS CISD Misses the 45-Day Timeline

If LOS FRESNOS CISD does not complete the LOS FRESNOS CISD FIE evaluation and hold an ARD meeting within 45 calendar days, this is a procedural violation. While a missed timeline doesn't automatically mean your child qualifies for services, it demonstrates the district failed to follow required procedures.

If the deadline is missed, you should:

  1. Request written explanation from LOS FRESNOS CISD for the delay
  2. Request a makeup ARD meeting within 10 days
  3. Document all communication with the district
  4. Contact a special ed advocate LOS FRESNOS or attorney if the delay continues
