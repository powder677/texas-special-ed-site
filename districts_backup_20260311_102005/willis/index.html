Understanding Special Education in WILLIS ISD

WILLIS ISD special education serves students ages 3-22 who have disabilities affecting their educational performance. Our district operates under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §29.001, ensuring every eligible student receives a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment.

Special education in WILLIS ISD isn't a separate school—it's a range of services and supports delivered in regular classrooms, resource rooms, and specialized settings based on your child's needs. Whether your child needs speech therapy, occupational therapy, behavioral support, or modified curriculum, WILLIS ISD provides these services as part of special education.

Who Qualifies for WILLIS ISD Special Education

Students in WILLIS ISD qualify for special education if they have one of thirteen disability categories under IDEA and need specialized instruction because of their disability:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment (ADHD, diabetes, etc.)
  • Specific Learning Disability (including dyslexia)
  • Speech/Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Visual Impairment
  • Deaf-Blindness
  • Developmental Delay (ages 3-9)

Not all learning struggles qualify for special education. WILLIS ISD first considers whether a child needs Response to Intervention (RTI) support or a 504 Plan before pursuing special education eligibility.

The Evaluation and Eligibility Process for WILLIS ISD Special Education

Getting your child evaluated is the first step toward accessing WILLIS ISD special education services. The process is detailed, thorough, and requires your written permission at each stage.

Step 1: Request for Evaluation

You can request an evaluation for WILLIS ISD special education in writing to your child's campus principal or the Special Education Department. Teachers or other professionals can also request an evaluation with parental consent. WILLIS ISD must acknowledge your request within five school days and provide a timeline for evaluation completion.

Step 2: Comprehensive Evaluation

WILLIS ISD will conduct a full evaluation examining your child's academic performance, social-emotional functioning, health history, and specific areas of concern. This evaluation typically includes:

  • Cognitive and achievement testing
  • Classroom observations
  • Parent and teacher interviews
  • Hearing and vision screenings
  • Review of medical and educational records
  • Specialized assessments (speech, occupational therapy, psychological evaluation, etc.)

The evaluation must be completed within 60 school days of your written request, unless you agree to an extension. WILLIS ISD will use multiple measures in your child's native language to ensure a fair and accurate assessment.

Step 3: Eligibility Conference

After evaluation is complete, WILLIS ISD will hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss findings and determine eligibility. You'll receive a detailed evaluation report at least two school days before the meeting, giving you time to review results.

WILLIS ISD ARD Meetings: Your Child's Special Education Plan

An ARD meeting is where you, WILLIS ISD staff, and your child's teachers develop the special education plan. Understanding how WILLIS ISD ARD meeting procedures work empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

What Happens in a WILLIS ISD ARD Meeting

The ARD committee includes the parent(s), special education teacher, general education teacher, school counselor, administrator, and any related service providers. The team reviews evaluation results and determines your child's eligibility and, if eligible, develops an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

You'll discuss your child's strengths, challenges, and how the disability affects learning. The team identifies specific, measurable annual goals and the special education services needed to achieve them. This isn't a rubber-stamp meeting—your input shapes your child's educational path.

Your Rights in a WILLIS ISD ARD Meeting

Texas Education Code §29.004 guarantees you specific rights during WILLIS ISD ARD meeting processes:

  • Participate fully in all decisions about your child's special education
  • Request an interpreter if English isn't your primary language
  • Bring advocates or attorneys to the meeting
  • Request an independent evaluation if you disagree with WILLIS ISD's assessment
  • Have meeting notes and decisions provided in writing within a reasonable time
  • Request prior written notice 10 school days before WILLIS ISD implements changes

WILLIS ISD IEP Services and Supports

Once eligible, your child receives an Individualized Education Program (IEP). The WILLIS ISD IEP services section of the plan details exactly what support your child will receive, where, and how often.

Components of WILLIS ISD IEP Services

Your child's IEP includes:

  • Present Levels of Performance - Current academic and functional abilities
  • Annual Goals - Specific, measurable targets for the school year
  • Benchmark Progress Monitoring - How WILLIS ISD will track progress toward goals
  • Special Education Services - Which services (speech therapy, behavioral support, resource room instruction, etc.)
  • Frequency and Duration - How often and how long services occur (30 minutes twice weekly, for example)
  • Least Restrictive Environment - What percentage of time in regular classes versus specialized settings
  • Accommodations and Modifications - Changes to how your child learns without changing what they learn
  • Transition Services - For students age 16+, plans for post-secondary education, employment, and independent living

WILLIS ISD provides progress reports on IEP goals at least quarterly. You should receive updates on whether your child is meeting, approaching, or not meeting annual goals.

Common WILLIS ISD Special Education Services

  • Speech-Language Pathology Services

  • Physical Therapy

  • Behavioral Support Services including Functional Behavioral Assessments (FBA) and Behavior Intervention Plans (BIP)
  • Counseling Services

  • Extended School Year (ESY) Services - Summer programs for students who need them

WILLIS ISD Dyslexia Program

Dyslexia is a specific learning disability that affects reading and language processing. If your child struggles with reading, spelling, or written expression, ask about the WILLIS ISD dyslexia program.

Dyslexia Identification in WILLIS ISD

Texas law requires WILLIS ISD to screen all students for dyslexia by the end of kindergarten and first grade. If screening suggests dyslexia, WILLIS ISD will conduct comprehensive assessment. Students identified with dyslexia in WILLIS ISD have specific rights under Texas Education Code §38.003.

Dyslexia Services Through WILLIS ISD

If your child qualifies, the WILLIS ISD dyslexia program provides evidence-based, specialized reading instruction. This typically includes:

  • Structured literacy instruction using multi-sensory, phonics-based approaches
  • Progress monitoring to ensure intervention effectiveness
  • Assistive technology and accommodations (extended time, text-to-speech, etc.)
  • Parent training on how to support reading at home

Dyslexia services in WILLIS ISD can be delivered through special education, a 504 Plan, or general education intervention programs depending on your child's needs.

Understanding WILLIS ISD Parent Rights

WILLIS ISD parent rights in special education are extensive and legally protected. Knowing these rights ensures you can advocate effectively for your child.

Core WILLIS ISD Parent Rights

You have the right to:

  • Receive Notice in Your Native Language - All notifications about WILLIS ISD special education decisions must be in a language you understand
  • Participate in All Decisions - WILLIS ISD cannot make special education decisions without your input and consent
  • Access Records - Review and obtain copies of all evaluation reports, IEPs, and educational records
  • Request Independent Evaluation - If you disagree with WILLIS ISD's assessment, you can request an independent evaluation at no cost under certain circumstances
  • Prior Written Notice - Receive 10 school days notice before WILLIS ISD changes your child's special education placement or services
  • Dispute Resolution - File a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA) or request due process hearing if you disagree with WILLIS ISD decisions
  • Confidentiality Protections - WILLIS ISD must keep your child's special education information confidential
  • Transition Planning - For students 16 and older, participate in planning for life after high school

WILLIS ISD Parent Rights at a Glance

WILLIS ISD must provide a document outlining WILLIS ISD parent rights at evaluation time, each annual IEP meeting, and upon request. This document explains your procedural safeguards under IDEA. Request a copy in your language if you don't have one.
