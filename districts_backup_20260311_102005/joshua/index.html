Understanding JOSHUA ISD Special Education Services

JOSHUA ISD special education serves students ages 3-22 who have disabilities that impact their ability to learn. Our district provides a full continuum of services ranging from inclusive classroom support to specialized programs for students with significant disabilities. Whether your child attends school in Joshua or nearby areas, JOSHUA ISD maintains the same commitment to quality special education for all qualifying students.

Special education in JOSHUA ISD is not a place—it's a service. Your child may receive services while spending most of the day in general education classrooms alongside non-disabled peers, or may require more specialized settings depending on their individual needs. The amount and type of services provided are determined through a collaborative process involving parents, teachers, and specialists.

Who Qualifies for JOSHUA ISD Special Education?

Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code, students may qualify for special education if they have one of 13 disability categories and require specially designed instruction. Categories served in JOSHUA ISD include:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Deaf-Blindness
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Specific Learning Disability (including dyslexia)
  • Speech/Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Visual Impairment
  • Developmental Delay (ages 3-9)

If you suspect your child may have a disability, you can request an evaluation from JOSHUA ISD at any time. The district must respond to evaluation requests within specific timelines under Texas law.

The ARD Meeting Process in JOSHUA ISD

The JOSHUA ISD ARD meeting is where special education decisions are made. ARD stands for "Admission, Review, and Dismissal," and it's the legal meeting where a team of educators, specialists, and parents come together to determine if your child qualifies for special education and what services they'll receive.

What Happens During a JOSHUA ISD ARD Meeting?

During an ARD meeting in JOSHUA ISD, the team reviews evaluation results, discusses your child's strengths and needs, and develops an Individualized Education Program (IEP). The meeting typically includes:

  • JOSHUA ISD special education staff and general education teachers
  • School psychologist or evaluation specialist
  • Parents or guardians
  • Your child (when appropriate)
  • Other specialists who have evaluated or worked with your child

As a parent in JOSHUA ISD, you have the right to bring an advocate or attorney to your ARD meeting, ask questions, and request additional time if you need it. JOSHUA ISD staff will explain assessment results, discuss your child's progress, and work with you to set measurable goals.

Timeline for JOSHUA ISD ARD Meetings

JOSHUA ISD must conduct an initial ARD meeting within 60 calendar days of receiving a written evaluation request. For students already in special education, JOSHUA ISD is required to hold an ARD meeting at least once per year to review progress and update the IEP.

Parents in JOSHUA ISD can request an ARD meeting at any time if they believe their child's services need to change or if concerns arise during the school year. JOSHUA ISD must schedule these meetings within 10 school days of the parent's written request.

JOSHUA ISD IEP Services and Goals

An Individualized Education Program (IEP) is the written document that outlines all JOSHUA ISD IEP services your child will receive. This legally binding document is created during your ARD meeting and guides all special education instruction and support throughout the school year.

What's Included in Your Child's JOSHUA ISD IEP?

Your child's IEP in JOSHUA ISD will include:

  • Present Levels of Performance: Your child's current academic, social, and functional abilities
  • Measurable Annual Goals: Specific, achievable targets your child will work toward in reading, math, behavior, social skills, or other areas
  • Special Education Services: The specific services JOSHUA ISD will provide, including resource room instruction, speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, or other support
  • Related Services: Transportation, assistive technology, or other services needed for your child to benefit from special education
  • Accommodations and Modifications: Changes to how your child learns (accommodations) or what they learn (modifications)
  • Transition Planning: For students age 16+, plans for preparing your child for life after high school

JOSHUA ISD IEP services are provided at no cost to parents. Whether your child receives services in a school building or through in-home support, families never pay for the special education services outlined in the IEP.

How JOSHUA ISD Measures Progress

JOSHUA ISD is required to measure your child's progress toward IEP goals regularly. You'll receive progress reports at least as often as general education students receive report cards—typically every 9 weeks. If your child is not making adequate progress toward goals, JOSHUA ISD must convene an ARD meeting to review and revise the IEP.

JOSHUA ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

JOSHUA ISD parent rights are protected under federal law (IDEA) and Texas Education Code. Understanding these rights empowers you to be a strong advocate for your child and ensures your child receives an appropriate education.

Key Parent Rights in JOSHUA ISD Special Education

As a parent in JOSHUA ISD, you have the right to:

  • Request an evaluation: You can ask JOSHUA ISD to evaluate your child for special education at any time if you suspect a disability
  • Participate in all decisions: You must be invited to and included in every ARD meeting and major special education decision
  • Review records: You can inspect your child's educational records, evaluation reports, and IEP at any time
  • Obtain independent evaluations: If you disagree with JOSHUA ISD's evaluation, you can request a district-funded independent evaluation
  • Prior written notice: JOSHUA ISD must notify you in writing before making any changes to your child's services or placement
  • Due process hearing: If you disagree with JOSHUA ISD's decisions, you can request a hearing before an independent hearing officer
  • Bring an advocate or attorney: You can bring a special education advocate or attorney to any meeting in JOSHUA ISD

JOSHUA ISD provides parents with a document explaining all special education rights at no cost. Ask your special education coordinator for a copy of this important information.
