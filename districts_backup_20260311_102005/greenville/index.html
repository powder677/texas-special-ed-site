Understanding GREENVILLE ISD Special Education Services

Under federal law (IDEA) and Texas Education Code, GREENVILLE ISD is required to provide a free, appropriate public education (FAPE) to all eligible students with disabilities, from age 3 through age 21.

GREENVILLE ISD special education encompasses multiple service categories, including students with intellectual disabilities, autism spectrum disorder, emotional and behavioral disorders, specific learning disabilities (including dyslexia), speech and language impairments, hearing impairments, visual impairments, orthopedic impairments, other health impairments, traumatic brain injuries, and multiple disabilities.

Each student receiving special education in GREENVILLE ISD receives services based on their individual needs, not on a one-size-fits-all approach. Services can be delivered in various settings, from general education classrooms with support to specialized resource rooms or separate campuses, depending on what's most appropriate for your child.

How to Request an Evaluation

If you believe your child may need special education services, you can request an evaluation at any time. As a parent in GREENVILLE ISD, you have the right to request a comprehensive evaluation to determine if your child qualifies for special education.

To request an evaluation:

  1. Contact your child's campus and speak with the special education coordinator or campus administrator
  2. Submit a written request for evaluation (you can do this verbally, but written is best documented)
  3. GREENVILLE ISD has 15 school days to determine if evaluation is warranted
  4. If approved, the evaluation must be completed within 60 calendar days
  5. You'll receive a written report of evaluation results

You don't need a doctor's diagnosis or outside evaluation to request assessment. GREENVILLE ISD special education staff will conduct appropriate testing at no cost to your family.

The ARD Meeting: Your Voice in GREENVILLE ISD Special Education

The ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting is the most important component of GREENVILLE ISD special education. This is where your child's educational program is designed, reviewed, and adjusted based on progress.

ARD meetings in GREENVILLE ISD bring together parents, teachers, specialists, and administrators to create your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP). You are a critical member of this team—your input about your child's strengths, needs, and goals carries equal weight with school staff.

What Happens at Your GREENVILLE ISD ARD Meeting

During your GREENVILLE ISD ARD meeting, the team will:

  • Review your child's current performance in academics, behavior, and social/emotional development
  • Discuss evaluation results (if this is an initial eligibility meeting)
  • Identify your child's strengths and areas of need
  • Establish annual goals and benchmarks for progress
  • Determine appropriate special education services and placement
  • Discuss accommodations and modifications to the curriculum
  • Plan for transition services (if your child is age 14 or older)
  • Document all decisions in the IEP

GREENVILLE ISD IEP Services: What Your Child Will Receive

The Individualized Education Program (IEP) is the legal document that specifies exactly what special education and related services your child will receive in GREENVILLE ISD. This is far more detailed than a general education plan—it's customized to your individual child's needs.

GREENVILLE ISD IEP services may include:

  • Special education instruction in core academics (reading, math, language arts, science, social studies)
  • Speech and language services to address communication disorders
  • Occupational therapy for fine motor skills and daily living activities
  • Physical therapy for gross motor skills and mobility
  • Counseling services for emotional and behavioral support
  • Assistive technology devices and training
  • Behavioral support services and Behavior Intervention Plans (BIPs)
  • Accommodations and modifications to curriculum and testing
  • Extended school year services for students who need year-round support
  • Transition services beginning at age 14 to prepare for post-secondary life

Progress Monitoring in GREENVILLE ISD Special Education

GREENVILLE ISD is required to monitor your child's progress toward IEP goals regularly. You should receive progress reports at least as often as general education students receive report cards—typically every 4-6 weeks.

Progress data helps determine if your child's current services are effective. If your child isn't making adequate progress toward goals, the ARD team can adjust the program. Conversely, if your child exceeds goals, services can be reduced or your child can be considered for general education placement.
