Understanding Child Find Obligations in SOUTHSIDE ISD

SOUTHSIDE ISD has a legal responsibility under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities, regardless of the severity of their disability. This is known as Child Find. For SOUTHSIDE ISD families, this means the district must actively search for children who may need special education services.

How SOUTHSIDE ISD Implements Child Find

SOUTHSIDE ISD conducts child find activities through several methods:

  • Teacher referrals based on classroom observation
  • Universal screening for all students at specified grade levels
  • Community outreach and awareness programs
  • Monitoring of students in general education who may be at risk

If your child attends school in SOUTHSIDE ISD and shows signs of struggling academically or developmentally, the district's child find system should identify them. However, you don't need to wait for the district to initiate this process—you can request an evaluation yourself.

Requesting a SOUTHSIDE ISD special education evaluation begins with a formal written request to your child's school or the SOUTHSIDE ISD special education department. This formal request is crucial because it triggers the district's legal obligations and timelines.

Step-by-Step Request Process

  1. Contact your child's school: Call the main office or special education department at your SOUTHSIDE ISD campus and ask to speak with someone about requesting an evaluation.
  2. Submit a written request: Put your request in writing and deliver it to the school. Include your child's name, grade, specific concerns, and areas you believe warrant evaluation. Keep a copy for your records.
  3. Document the date: Note the exact date you submitted your request. This is important for tracking the SOUTHSIDE ISD 45 day timeline that the district must follow.
  4. Request written confirmation: Ask for written confirmation that the district received your request. This protects you legally if there are any disputes about timelines.
  5. Provide supporting information: Include any relevant medical history, previous evaluations, or documentation of concerns you've observed at home.

What Information to Include in Your Request

Your SOUTHSIDE ISD evaluation request should be clear and specific. Instead of general concerns, provide concrete examples of behaviors or academic struggles. For instance, rather than saying "my child has trouble reading," explain that "my third-grader is reading at a kindergarten level and struggles to identify sight words."

The SOUTHSIDE ISD 45-Day Evaluation Timeline Explained

  • Days 1-5: SOUTHSIDE ISD reviews your request and determines evaluation areas needed
