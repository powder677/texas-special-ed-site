Understanding TERRELL ISD Special Education Services

TERRELL ISD special education serves approximately 600-700 students with disabilities across our 4,932-student district. Our programs address a wide range of learning differences, developmental delays, and physical disabilities, ensuring every child has access to quality education in the least restrictive environment.

The special education process in TERRELL ISD begins with identification and evaluation. If you notice your child is struggling academically, behaviorally, or developmentally, you have the right to request an evaluation at any time. TERRELL ISD must respond to evaluation requests within 15 school days, per Texas Education Code §29.004.

Age Groups Served by TERRELL ISD

  • Birth to Age 3: Part C early intervention services through partnerships with regional centers
  • Ages 3-5: Preschool special education programs
  • Ages 6-21: School-age special education services across all campuses
  • Transition Ages 14-22: Vocational training and transition to adult services

TERRELL ISD recognizes that early identification and intervention make a measurable difference in long-term outcomes. Families in TERRELL ISD can access developmental screenings at any campus to determine if their child needs further evaluation.

The TERRELL ISD ARD Meeting Process

The TERRELL ISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) is the cornerstone of special education planning. This is where educators, parents, specialists, and your child (when appropriate) meet to discuss evaluation results, determine eligibility, and develop your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP).

TERRELL ISD schedules initial eligibility ARD meetings within 60 calendar days of receiving your written evaluation request. Annual ARD meetings occur every 12 months, with more frequent reviews available if you or the school requests them.

What to Expect at Your TERRELL ISD ARD Meeting

  1. Introduction and Purpose: School personnel explain the meeting agenda and your rights as a parent
  2. Evaluation Presentation: Teachers, diagnosticians, and specialists share assessment results and observations
  3. Eligibility Determination: The team determines if your child meets criteria under 13 federal disability categories
  4. IEP Development: If eligible, the team creates goals, services, and placement recommendations
  5. Parent Input and Agreement: You share your perspective and sign consent forms

Parents in TERRELL ISD have the right to bring advocates, family members, or attorneys to ARD meetings. You can also request the meeting be held at a time and location that works for your family.

Preparing for Your TERRELL ISD ARD Meeting

  • Request all evaluation reports and previous IEPs at least one week before the meeting
  • Write down questions and concerns you want addressed
  • Document your child's strengths, challenges, and daily functioning at home
  • Bring examples of work samples or behavioral documentation
  • Consider bringing a support person or advocate if you prefer

TERRELL ISD IEP Services and Support Options

Once your child is found eligible, TERRELL ISD IEP services are customized based on individual needs. The Individualized Education Program is a legal document that outlines measurable annual goals, specific services your child will receive, frequency and duration of services, and how progress will be monitored.

TERRELL ISD offers multiple service delivery models to meet diverse needs. Some students receive services in general education classrooms with support (inclusion), while others benefit from resource rooms, self-contained classes, or specialized programs depending on their disability category and severity.

Types of TERRELL ISD IEP Services

  • Special Education Instruction: Direct teaching in a resource or self-contained setting
  • Speech-Language Pathology: Services for communication disorders
  • Occupational Therapy (OT): Support with daily living and motor skills
  • Physical Therapy (PT): Services addressing mobility and physical functioning
  • Counseling Services: Social-emotional and behavioral support
  • Assistive Technology: Tools and devices supporting learning access
  • Specialized Transportation: When required for access to services
  • Behavior Support Services: Positive behavior intervention plans (PBIS)

TERRELL ISD families should know that the number of service minutes in an IEP is less important than whether services directly address your child's needs. A child receiving 30 minutes weekly of focused, appropriate instruction may benefit more than a child receiving two hours of unfocused support.

Progress Monitoring in TERRELL ISD

TERRELL ISD teachers monitor progress toward IEP goals continuously throughout the school year. You'll receive progress reports at the same frequency as report cards (typically every 4-6 weeks). If your child isn't making adequate progress, you can request an IEP revision meeting before the annual ARD date.

TERRELL ISD Parent Rights & Procedural Safeguards

Texas law and federal IDEA regulations guarantee specific rights to parents of children with disabilities. TERRELL ISD parent rights include the right to participate fully in special education decisions, the right to see all educational records, the right to request an independent educational evaluation, and the right to due process if you disagree with school decisions.

Every parent of a child receiving special education in TERRELL ISD must receive a Procedural Safeguards Notice in their primary language. This document, provided at the initial ARD meeting and annually thereafter, details your legal protections under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA).

Critical Parent Rights in TERRELL ISD

  • Right to Notice: Receive written notice 10 days before any change in placement or services
  • Right to Consent: Written parental consent required before initial evaluation and initial IEP implementation
  • Right to Records: Access all educational and health records within 10 business days
  • Right to Independent Evaluation: Request at public expense if you disagree with TERRELL ISD evaluation
  • Right to Dispute Resolution: Access mediation or due process hearings if disagreements arise
  • Right to Compensatory Education: Services to make up for prior lost special education

Parents in TERRELL ISD who feel their child's rights are violated or who strongly disagree with school decisions have multiple recourse options. You can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA), request mediation, or pursue a due process hearing.

TERRELL ISD Dyslexia Program & Reading Support

Dyslexia is one of the most common learning differences, and TERRELL ISD dyslexia program provides evidence-based interventions for students with this reading disability. Texas Education Code §38.003 requires every school district to establish dyslexia programs that include screening, evaluation, and intervention.

TERRELL ISD screens all first-grade students for dyslexia risk factors using research-validated tools. If your child shows indicators of dyslexia, TERRELL ISD will provide a comprehensive evaluation to confirm the diagnosis and recommend appropriate services.

Dyslexia Intervention Options in TERRELL ISD

  • Tier 1 Interventions: Enhanced classroom reading instruction using structured literacy approaches
  • Tier 2 Interventions: Small group specialized reading instruction 3-5 times weekly
  • Tier 3/Special Education: Intensive intervention through IEP services if child qualifies as a student with a disability

Many students in TERRELL ISD with dyslexia receive help through Response to Intervention (RTI) programs before becoming eligible for special education services. This isn't a "wait and fail" approach—it's evidence-based early intervention.

Parent Request for Dyslexia Evaluation

You don't need to wait for school screening. If you suspect your child has dyslexia, contact your campus principal or the TERRELL ISD Special Education Department to request a dyslexia evaluation. Parent request evaluations must be completed within 60 days.

Transition Planning & Future Planning

As your child approaches age 14, TERRELL ISD transitions focus from academics to post-secondary outcomes. Transition planning addresses employment, further education, independent living skills, and community participation. These discussions begin in the IEP when your child is age 14 (or younger if appropriate).

TERRELL ISD families should understand that transition services aren't one-time planning—they're ongoing preparation for adult life. Your child's IEP goals from ages 14-22 should specifically address career interests, work skills, and community integration.

Transition Services in TERRELL ISD

  • Vocational assessments and career exploration
  • Life skills instruction and independent living training
  • Coordination with adult service agencies (vocational rehabilitation, DARS, etc.)
  • Secondary education completion pathways aligned with post-secondary goals
