SPLENDORA ISD ARD Meeting: Your Child's Education Plan

The SPLENDORA ISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) is the cornerstone of special education in our district. This is where your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP) is developed, reviewed, and updated. SPLENDORA ISD ARD meetings bring together parents, teachers, special educators, and administrators to create a roadmap for your student's success.

What Happens During an ARD Meeting

During your SPLENDORA ISD ARD meeting, the team will:

  1. Review your child's current performance and progress
  2. Discuss assessment results and evaluation data
  3. Identify your child's strengths and areas needing support
  4. Establish measurable annual goals
  5. Determine placement and special education services
  6. Discuss accommodations and modifications
  7. Plan for transition services (for students age 14+)

Your participation in the SPLENDORA ISD ARD meeting is essential. Come prepared with observations about how your child learns best, what challenges they face at home, and what goals matter most to your family.

Preparing for Your ARD Meeting

SPLENDORA ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

SPLENDORA ISD parent rights are guaranteed by federal law (IDEA) and Texas Education Code. Understanding your SPLENDORA ISD parent rights empowers you to be an effective advocate for your child. You are not a guest at the ARD table—you are a critical, equal member of the team.

Your Key SPLENDORA ISD Parent Rights

As a parent in SPLENDORA ISD, you have the right to:

  • Prior Written Notice: Written notification before SPLENDORA ISD proposes or refuses any action regarding your child's education
  • Participate in Decision-Making: Full participation in all meetings about identification, evaluation, placement, and IEP development
  • Consent for Initial Evaluation: Right to consent before SPLENDORA ISD conducts initial special education evaluations
  • Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE): Request an independent evaluation at district expense if you disagree with SPLENDORA ISD's evaluation
  • Access to Records: Inspect and review all educational records within 10 school days of request
  • Due Process Hearing: Request a due process hearing to resolve disputes with SPLENDORA ISD
  • Dispute Resolution: Mediation or other resolution options before escalating to formal hearings

SPLENDORA ISD provides a Parent Rights notice at the beginning of each school year and whenever a special education referral is made. If you need a copy, contact your campus or the special education office.

Understanding Due Process in SPLENDORA ISD

If you and SPLENDORA ISD cannot reach agreement on your child's evaluation, placement, or services, you have the right to request a due process hearing. This formal process allows an impartial hearing officer to review both sides and make a binding decision. Before proceeding to a hearing, SPLENDORA ISD may offer mediation or resolution sessions to resolve disputes cooperatively.

Special Education Evaluation and Assessment in SPLENDORA ISD

The SPLENDORA ISD Evaluation Process

Once you request evaluation for SPLENDORA ISD special education services:

  1. Referral and Consent: Submit referral to your school. SPLENDORA ISD obtains your written consent before proceeding (within 10 days of request)
  2. Comprehensive Assessment: SPLENDORA ISD conducts assessments across all areas of suspected disability (cognitive, academic, social-emotional, motor, communication)
