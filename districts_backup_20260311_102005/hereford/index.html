Understanding HEREFORD ISD Special Education Services

HEREFORD ISD special education provides individualized support for students with disabilities under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act. Our district is committed to providing free, appropriate public education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment (LRE) for all eligible students.

Special education in HEREFORD ISD includes services for students with:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Intellectual disabilities
  • Specific learning disabilities, including dyslexia
  • Speech and language impairments
  • Emotional and behavioral disorders
  • Orthopedic and physical impairments
  • Visual and hearing impairments
  • Other health impairments
  • Traumatic brain injury

HEREFORD ISD families receive personalized support through our dedicated special education staff who work collaboratively to develop programs matching each student's unique needs.

The ARD Process: Your Child's Individualized Education Plan

The HEREFORD ISD ARD meeting is where your child's education program is developed and reviewed. ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal, and these meetings are central to special education in HEREFORD ISD.

Initial Evaluation and Referral

Your child may be referred for evaluation through several pathways. Teachers, parents, or other professionals can request an evaluation if they suspect a disability affecting educational performance. Once HEREFORD ISD receives a referral, the district has 15 school days to notify you and obtain consent for evaluation.

The evaluation process in HEREFORD ISD includes:

  1. Comprehensive assessments in all areas related to the suspected disability
  2. Review of existing educational and medical records
  3. Input from parents, teachers, and specialists
  4. Assessment of academic, social, emotional, and behavioral functioning

Initial ARD Meeting

If your child qualifies for services, HEREFORD ISD will hold an initial ARD meeting within 30 days of completing the evaluation. This is where your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP) is created. As a parent, you are an essential team member at this meeting.

At your initial HEREFORD ISD ARD meeting, the team will determine:

  • Your child's present levels of academic and functional performance
  • Annual goals and short-term objectives
  • Special education and related services needed
  • The extent to which your child participates in general education
  • How progress toward goals will be measured

Annual ARD Reviews

HEREFORD ISD requires an ARD meeting at least once annually to review your child's progress, update goals, and adjust services as needed. You can request additional meetings if circumstances change or your child's needs evolve.

Parent Rights and Procedural Safeguards in HEREFORD ISD

Federal and state law grant parents substantial rights in special education. Understanding these HEREFORD ISD parent rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Your Core Rights

As a parent in HEREFORD ISD, you have the right to:

  • Notice and consent—receive written notice before evaluation, eligibility determination, or placement changes
  • Participation—be a full member of all ARD meetings affecting your child's education
  • Access to records—review all educational records within 10 school days
  • Independent evaluation—obtain an independent evaluation at public expense if you disagree with HEREFORD ISD's assessment
  • Due process—file a complaint or request due process hearing if disputes arise
  • Interpretation—receive all notices and participate in meetings in your native language

If You Disagree with HEREFORD ISD Decisions

Parents of students in HEREFORD ISD who disagree with eligibility, evaluation, or placement decisions have several options. You can request a due process hearing, file a state complaint, or seek mediation through the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

Many parents consult with a special education attorney near HEREFORD or a special ed advocate in HEREFORD when navigating disputes. These professionals understand both HEREFORD ISD procedures and Texas special education law and can provide valuable guidance during challenging situations.
