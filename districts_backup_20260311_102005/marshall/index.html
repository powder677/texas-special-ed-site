Understanding MARSHALL ISD Special Education Services

MARSHALL ISD special education provides specialized instruction and related services for students ages 3-22 who have disabilities that impact their educational performance. Our district serves approximately 4,681 students, with dedicated teams committed to ensuring every child receives an appropriate education in the least restrictive environment.

Special education in MARSHALL ISD covers thirteen disability categories under federal law (IDEA). These include autism spectrum disorder, emotional disturbance, intellectual disability, learning disabilities, orthopedic impairment, other health impairment, speech or language impairment, traumatic brain injury, visual impairment, and more.

How MARSHALL ISD Identifies Students for Special Education

The referral process for MARSHALL ISD special education begins when a teacher, parent, or other professional suspects a child may have a disability. You can request an evaluation at any time by contacting your campus principal or the Special Education Department directly.

  • Step 1: Submit a written referral (verbal requests are also accepted)
  • Step 2: MARSHALL ISD conducts comprehensive evaluation within 60 calendar days
  • Step 3: Evaluation team meets to determine eligibility
  • Step 4: If eligible, an ARD committee develops an IEP

MARSHALL ISD ARD Meetings: What Parents Need to Know

An MARSHALL ISD ARD meeting brings together the student's parent(s), teachers, special education specialists, and administrators to develop or review the student's education plan. ARD stands for "Admission, Review, and Dismissal," and these meetings are required at least once annually.

Parents in MARSHALL ISD have significant rights in the ARD process. You are equal participants in all decisions about your child's special education, not passive observers. Your input shapes your child's educational path.

Preparing for Your MARSHALL ISD ARD Meeting

Successful preparation leads to better outcomes for your child. Consider these steps before your MARSHALL ISD ARD meeting:

  • Gather recent assessment results, report cards, and work samples
  • Write down specific concerns and observations about your child's learning
  • List questions you want addressed during the meeting
  • Bring a trusted family member or friend for support
  • Consider bringing a special education advocate or attorney if you anticipate disagreements

MARSHALL ISD schedules ARD meetings at times convenient for parents whenever possible. If the proposed time doesn't work, contact your child's campus to arrange an alternative date. Schools cannot proceed with ARD meetings without parental participation.

What Happens During a MARSHALL ISD ARD Meeting

Your MARSHALL ISD ARD meeting typically covers present levels of performance, annual goals, special education and related services, placement decisions, and how progress will be measured. The team reviews evaluation results and discusses your child's strengths, needs, and interests.

You'll discuss accommodations and modifications needed for your child to access the general curriculum. For MARSHALL ISD families with students receiving services, this might include extended time on tests, modified assignments, assistive technology, or specialized instruction in specific areas.

MARSHALL ISD IEP Services Explained

An MARSHALL ISD IEP (Individualized Education Program) is a legally binding document that outlines your child's special education and related services. This detailed plan ensures your child receives the specific support needed to make meaningful progress.

The MARSHALL ISD IEP services section specifies exactly what your child will receive. This might include speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling services, specialized academic instruction, behavior support services, or assistive technology.

Key Components of MARSHALL ISD IEP Services

  • Present Levels of Academic and Functional Performance: Detailed description of how disability affects learning
  • Measurable Annual Goals: Specific, realistic objectives your child will work toward
  • Special Education Services: Amount of time receiving specialized instruction
  • Related Services: Speech, occupational therapy, counseling, or other supports
  • Accommodations and Modifications: Changes to how your child accesses curriculum
  • Progress Monitoring: How often and how the school will measure progress
  • Least Restrictive Environment: How much time your child spends in general education classes

MARSHALL ISD IEP services are based on your child's individual needs, not a one-size-fits-all approach. Your input during the ARD meeting directly shapes which services are included in your child's plan.

MARSHALL ISD Dyslexia Program and Services

Texas law requires all districts, including MARSHALL ISD, to provide dyslexia services for students with this specific learning disability. The MARSHALL ISD dyslexia program uses evidence-based interventions to help students who struggle with reading despite adequate instruction.

If you suspect your child has dyslexia, request a dyslexia evaluation from MARSHALL ISD. This specialized assessment goes beyond standard reading assessments to identify specific dyslexic characteristics. Many students qualify for services through the MARSHALL ISD dyslexia program even if they don't qualify for special education in other categories.

What MARSHALL ISD Dyslexia Program Services Include

  • Systematic, explicit phonics instruction
  • Structured literacy tutoring with certified dyslexia specialists
  • Classroom accommodations (extended time, text-to-speech technology, alternative assignments)
  • Progress monitoring and adjustment of instruction as needed
  • Family education and support resources

Students in the MARSHALL ISD dyslexia program typically receive small-group or individual instruction in addition to their regular classroom instruction. These specialized services continue as long as your child needs them to develop reading proficiency.

MARSHALL ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

MARSHALL ISD parent rights are protected by federal law (IDEA) and Texas Education Code. Understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child. As a parent in MARSHALL ISD, you have the right to:

Fundamental MARSHALL ISD Parent Rights

  • Right to participate: Fully participate in all decisions about identification, evaluation, and IEP development
  • Right to information: Receive clear explanations in your native language about all evaluations and services
  • Right to records: Review and obtain copies of all special education records without delay
  • Right to notice: Receive written notice in advance of any significant changes to your child's program
  • Right to due process: File a due process complaint if you disagree with MARSHALL ISD decisions
  • Right to independent evaluation: Obtain an independent evaluation at MARSHALL ISD expense if you disagree with the district's evaluation
  • Right to surrogate parent appointment: Have someone appointed to represent your child's interests if you're unavailable

MARSHALL ISD provides a detailed Parent Rights Notice each year. Request this document if you haven't received it, as it explains all your rights and procedures for resolving disagreements with the school.
