Understanding ANGLETON ISD Special Education Services

Our special education department is dedicated to identifying, evaluating, and serving students with a wide range of learning differences and developmental needs.

ANGLETON ISD IEP services are designed to meet each child's unique needs through personalized educational plans. Whether your child needs support for autism spectrum disorder, dyslexia, speech and language delays, intellectual disabilities, or other conditions, our team works collaboratively with families to ensure appropriate services and accommodations.

The special education process in ANGLETON ISD begins with early identification and evaluation. If you suspect your child may have a disability affecting their education, our district provides free comprehensive evaluations to determine eligibility for services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.

The ARD Process and IEP Development

An ANGLETON ISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) is where critical decisions about your child's special education services are made. This meeting brings together parents, teachers, administrators, and specialists to create and review your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP).

What to Expect at Your ARD Meeting

During an ANGLETON ISD ARD meeting, the team will review your child's evaluation results, discuss current performance, identify goals, and determine what services your child needs. You'll discuss placement options, accommodations, and modifications that will support your child's learning in both academic and behavioral areas.

  • Initial evaluation results: Assessment data showing your child's strengths and areas needing support
  • Current performance: How your child is doing academically and socially in the classroom
  • Goals and objectives: Specific measurable targets for growth over the next year
  • Services and placement: The amount of special education instruction, related services, and classroom setting
  • Accommodations and modifications: Changes to how your child accesses the curriculum

Preparing for Your ARD Meeting

Come prepared to your ANGLETON ISD IEP services meeting by gathering information about your child's progress at home and school. Bring any outside evaluations, medical reports, or documentation from specialists. Write down questions and concerns ahead of time, and don't hesitate to ask for clarification on any recommendations.

In ANGLETON ISD, parents have the right to bring a support person to the ARD meeting. Many families choose to bring an advocate, family member, or friend to help take notes and ensure their voice is heard during discussions about their child's education.

ANGLETON ISD Dyslexia Programs and Services

Dyslexia is a common learning difference affecting reading and language processing. ANGLETON ISD has established specialized ANGLETON ISD dyslexia program services to help students who struggle with reading despite adequate instruction and intelligence.

Dyslexia Screening and Evaluation

Under Texas Education Code §38.003, all students in ANGLETON ISD are screened for dyslexia risk factors at specified grade levels. If your child shows indicators of dyslexia, the district will conduct a comprehensive evaluation to determine the specific nature of their reading difficulty.

The ANGLETON ISD dyslexia program includes evidence-based interventions using structured literacy approaches. Students receive explicit, systematic instruction in phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension.

Support for Dyslexic Learners

  • Specialized reading intervention delivered by trained educators
  • Assistive technology tools like text-to-speech and reading software
  • Individualized accommodations such as extended time on tests
  • Progress monitoring to track improvement and adjust instruction
  • Parent education about dyslexia and how to support learning at home

Understanding Your Rights in ANGLETON ISD Special Education

ANGLETON ISD parent rights are protected under federal and Texas laws. As a parent in ANGLETON ISD, you have fundamental rights in the special education process, and understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Key Parent Rights in ANGLETON ISD

Parents in ANGLETON ISD special education have the right to be fully informed about decisions affecting their child's education. This includes the right to:

  • Request an evaluation: Ask ANGLETON ISD to evaluate your child for special education eligibility at any time
  • Participate in meetings: Be present and participate in all ARD meetings and decisions about your child's IEP
  • Access records: Review all educational records, evaluations, and progress reports
  • Receive notice: Get written notice before any significant changes to your child's placement or services
  • Dispute decisions: Request mediation or due process hearings if you disagree with district recommendations
  • Obtain independent evaluations: Request independent educational evaluations at district expense under certain circumstances

Prior Written Notice Requirements

ANGLETON ISD must provide prior written notice before making any changes to your child's identification, evaluation, placement, or the provision of a free appropriate public education (FAPE). This notice must be in your native language and explain the action being proposed, why it's being proposed, and what other options were considered.

The Referral and Evaluation Process

If you believe your child has a disability affecting education, the referral process in ANGLETON ISD is your first step toward getting help. Referrals can come from parents, teachers, school counselors, or other professionals who work with your child.

Steps in the ANGLETON ISD Evaluation Process

  1. Submit referral: Contact your child's campus or the ANGLETON ISD special education department to request an evaluation
  2. Parent consent: Sign permission for evaluation; ANGLETON ISD has 15 school days to obtain your written consent
  3. Comprehensive evaluation: District conducts testing in all areas related to suspected disability (typically 45-60 days)
  4. ARD meeting: Team reviews results and determines eligibility for services
  5. IEP development: If eligible, the ARD team develops your child's Individualized Education Program
  6. Service begins: Special education services start according to the timeline specified in the IEP

What Gets Evaluated

ANGLETON ISD evaluation teams conduct thorough assessments in multiple areas to understand your child's complete picture. This includes academic achievement, cognitive ability, social-emotional functioning, speech and language skills, motor skills, and adaptive behavior depending on the suspected disability.

Accommodations and Modifications for ANGLETON ISD Students

Many students in ANGLETON ISD benefit from accommodations—changes in how instruction is delivered without changing what is taught. Others need modifications, which involve changing the actual curriculum content and expectations.

Common Accommodations in ANGLETON ISD

  • Extended time on tests and assignments
  • Alternative testing formats (oral tests, written responses instead of multiple choice)
  • Preferential seating for better focus or access to materials
  • Copies of class notes or study guides
  • Use of assistive technology like text-to-speech or speech-to-text
  • Breaks during testing or instruction
  • Modified homework or assignment loads

When Modifications Become Necessary

Modifications are appropriate when a student cannot access the grade-level curriculum even with accommodations. ANGLETON ISD uses modifications carefully, ensuring they don't limit your child's access to challenging, meaningful learning while providing the support they need to succeed.
