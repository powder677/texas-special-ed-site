Understanding Your Rights as a LANCASTER ISD Parent

These rights include the right to participate in all decisions about your child's identification, evaluation, and educational placement.

LANCASTER ISD must provide you with written notice before making, refusing to make, or changing any decision related to your child's free appropriate public education (FAPE). Understanding these foundational rights helps you recognize when something may warrant a formal complaint. The district should have provided you with a procedural safeguards notice explaining your rights in detail—if you haven't received this document, request it immediately from the special education department.

LANCASTER ISD Special Education Complaint Process

If informal resolution doesn't address your concerns, you can file a formal LANCASTER ISD special education complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This process is separate from due process and has specific timelines and requirements.

What Qualifies as a Complaint for TEA

A complaint to TEA must allege that LANCASTER ISD has violated a requirement of IDEA or state special education law. Examples include:

  • Failure to provide services listed in the IEP
  • Inappropriate disciplinary procedures for a child with disabilities
  • Failure to conduct required evaluations
  • Denial of FAPE
  • Violation of procedural safeguards

How to File a TEA Complaint

You must file a LANCASTER ISD complaint with TEA within one year of the alleged violation. Submit your complaint in writing to the Texas Education Agency Special Education Compliance and Monitoring Division. Include:

  1. Your name, address, and phone number
  2. Your child's name and date of birth
  3. The specific violation or problem
  4. Dates when the problem occurred
  5. The resolution you're requesting
  6. Copies of relevant documents (keep originals)

TEA will investigate your complaint within 30 days and send you a written determination. If LANCASTER ISD violated special education law, TEA will order corrective action. Note that complaints to TEA don't resolve disputes about IEP content or appropriateness—that's where due process comes in.

Due Process Hearings: Formal Dispute Resolution for LANCASTER ISD Families

When you believe LANCASTER ISD due process protections have been violated or you disagree about your child's IEP, placement, or evaluation, you can request a due process hearing. This is the primary mechanism for resolving disputes about special education services.

Grounds for a Due Process Complaint

Due process allows you to challenge decisions about whether your child is receiving FAPE. Common disputes include disagreements about:

  • Proposed IEP goals or services
  • Placement decisions (general education versus specialized settings)
  • Evaluation results and eligibility determinations
  • Graduation and diploma options
  • Behavior intervention plans and discipline

Filing a Due Process Complaint with LANCASTER ISD

To initiate due process, submit a written complaint to LANCASTER ISD's special education director. You have two years from when you knew or should have known about the problem to file. Your complaint must include:

  1. Your child's name and address
  2. LANCASTER ISD's name and address
  3. A description of the problem and proposed solution
  4. Facts relating to the problem
  5. The relief (remedy) you're requesting

Send your complaint via certified mail so you have proof of receipt. LANCASTER ISD must provide you with notice of your procedural rights within 15 days, and you can expect a response regarding resolution options.

The Due Process Timeline

Understanding the timeline helps you plan ahead. Within 15 days of receiving your complaint, LANCASTER ISD must hold a resolution meeting with you (unless you agree in writing to skip it). At this meeting, the district explains their perspective and you have an opportunity to resolve the dispute without a formal hearing.

If the matter isn't resolved, you can request an impartial due process hearing. A hearing officer who has no connection to LANCASTER ISD will hear evidence and arguments from both sides. The hearing officer typically issues a decision within 45 days.

LANCASTER ISD Mediation: Resolving Disputes Without Hearing

LANCASTER ISD mediation offers parents and the district an alternative to formal hearings. This voluntary, confidential process involves a neutral mediator helping both parties reach agreement.

Benefits of Mediation for LANCASTER ISD Parents

Mediation can be faster and less adversarial than due process hearings. Since the process is confidential, candid discussion is possible without statements becoming part of a legal record. Mediation also preserves relationships, which matters when your child continues attending LANCASTER ISD schools.

You can request mediation at any time—even before filing a formal due process complaint. LANCASTER ISD must offer mediation free of charge. If mediation succeeds, you and the district sign a binding agreement outlining the resolution.

Mediation Process Steps

Once requested, LANCASTER ISD arranges a mediation session within a reasonable timeframe. You and district representatives meet with the mediator, who helps identify common ground and work toward solutions. You can bring an advocate or attorney to mediation for support.

If mediation doesn't resolve all issues, you can still pursue due process. Nothing said in mediation can be used as evidence in a hearing, making mediation a low-risk option worth considering.

Specific Issues and Solutions for LANCASTER ISD Families

LANCASTER ISD must educate your child in the least restrictive environment (LRE) appropriate for them. If you believe your child is placed in an overly restrictive setting—separate from non-disabled peers—you can challenge this placement through due process.

Conversely, if you want your child placed in a more inclusive setting and LANCASTER ISD refuses, you can also file. Document your child's abilities, obtain evaluations from outside professionals if helpful, and explain why inclusion would be appropriate.

Evaluations and Eligibility Issues

If you obtain an outside evaluation showing LANCASTER ISD missed something significant, this strengthens your position in any dispute.
