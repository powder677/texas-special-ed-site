Understanding ARD Meetings in MABANK ISD

An MABANK ISD ARD meeting is a formal gathering where school personnel, parents, and sometimes the student meet to discuss special education services. The ARD committee determines whether a child qualifies for special education, develops an Individualized Education Program (IEP), and reviews progress regularly.

For families in MABANK ISD, the ARD process is governed by both Texas Education Code and the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). These meetings typically occur at least once per school year, though MABANK ISD families may request additional meetings when needed.

The committee usually includes your child's teacher, special education director, school nurse or counselor, you as the parent, and any evaluation specialists who have assessed your child. Understanding who will be present and their roles helps parents in MABANK ISD prepare more effectively.

The MABANK ISD IEP Process: Step by Step

The MABANK ISD IEP process consists of several phases, beginning with referral and evaluation, moving through ARD meetings, and continuing with implementation and monitoring.

Phase 1: Referral and Evaluation

The special education process begins when a child is referred for evaluation. In MABANK ISD, referrals may come from teachers, parents, or other school personnel who have concerns about a child's learning or development.

Once referred, MABANK ISD has 15 calendar days to determine if evaluation is warranted. If the district agrees an evaluation is needed, you'll receive a notice of proposed evaluation. Parents must give written consent before MABANK ISD can conduct any formal assessments.

Evaluations typically include academic testing, cognitive assessments, behavioral observations, and input from teachers and parents. MABANK ISD completes comprehensive evaluations within 60 calendar days of receiving your consent.

Phase 2: Initial ARD Meeting and IEP Development

After evaluation, MABANK ISD schedules an ARD meeting to discuss results and determine eligibility. During this initial meeting, the ARD committee reviews evaluation data and decides whether your child qualifies for special education under one of the eligibility categories.

If your child qualifies, the committee develops an Individualized Education Program (IEP). This legally binding document outlines your child's educational plan for the next year.

Phase 3: Writing MABANK ISD IEP Goals

One of the most important parts of the MABANK ISD IEP process is developing appropriate MABANK ISD IEP goals. These goals should address your child's academic and functional needs based on evaluation results and parent input.

Effective IEP goals should be:

  • Specific: Clearly describe what skill or behavior the goal targets
  • Measurable: Include specific criteria for determining if the goal has been met (e.g., "80% accuracy," "3 out of 5 trials")
  • Achievable: Realistic for your child to accomplish within one school year
  • Relevant: Directly related to your child's disability and educational needs
  • Time-bound: Include a specific timeline for achievement

During your MABANK ISD ARD meeting, request clarification if any MABANK ISD IEP goals seem unclear or unattainable. You have the right to suggest modifications and discuss how progress will be monitored.

Phase 4: Annual Review and Triennial Evaluation

MABANK ISD is required to hold an ARD meeting at least once every 12 months to review your child's progress on MABANK ISD IEP goals and discuss any necessary changes. Additionally, your child must be re-evaluated every three years to continue eligibility under special education in MABANK ISD.

These regular meetings ensure that your child's services remain appropriate and that the MABANK ISD IEP process continues to address evolving needs.
