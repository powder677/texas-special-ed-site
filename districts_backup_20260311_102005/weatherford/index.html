Understanding WEATHERFORD ISD Special Education Programs

WEATHERFORD ISD special education serves students ages 3-21 with disabilities that significantly impact their learning. Who Qualifies for Services?

Students in WEATHERFORD ISD qualify for special education if they meet two criteria: they have a disability in one of 13 protected categories under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), AND they require specially designed instruction to benefit from their education.

The 13 disability categories served by WEATHERFORD ISD include:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Deaf-Blindness
  • Deafness
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Specific Learning Disability (including dyslexia)
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Visual Impairment

The Evaluation and IEP Process

If you believe your child needs special education services in WEATHERFORD ISD, the evaluation process begins with a referral. You can request an evaluation from your child's teacher, school counselor, or directly through the WEATHERFORD ISD special education department. The district must respond to evaluation requests in writing within specific timeframes.

Step-by-Step: WEATHERFORD ISD Evaluation Timeline

  1. Referral Stage: You submit a request for evaluation. WEATHERFORD ISD acknowledges your request and explains the evaluation process.
  2. Permission to Evaluate: The district provides a Permission to Evaluate form. You have 15 calendar days to sign and return it. This is a critical step—your written permission is required before testing begins.
  3. Evaluation Period: WEATHERFORD ISD has 60 school days to complete comprehensive evaluations in all areas of suspected disability.
  4. Eligibility ARD Meeting: An WEATHERFORD ISD ARD meeting is held to review evaluation results and determine eligibility. ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal.
  5. IEP Development: If eligible, your child's IEP is developed at this same meeting, outlining goals, services, and accommodations.

Many parents find the evaluation process overwhelming. Remember that throughout this timeline, you have rights—and WEATHERFORD ISD families are encouraged to bring an advocate or attorney to any meeting.

WEATHERFORD ISD IEP Services and Supports

WEATHERFORD ISD IEP services are individualized based on your child's unique needs. An IEP is a legal document that specifies the specialized instruction and related services your child will receive. Services are delivered in the least restrictive environment, meaning your child learns alongside non-disabled peers whenever appropriate.

Types of Services Available in WEATHERFORD ISD

Special Education Instruction: Direct teaching from certified special education teachers who modify curriculum, pacing, and instruction methods to match your child's learning style.

Related Services: WEATHERFORD ISD provides supporting services such as speech-language pathology, occupational therapy, physical therapy, counseling, and behavior support services.

Accommodations and Modifications: These adjust how your child accesses the curriculum. Accommodations might include extended test time, large print materials, or use of assistive technology. Modifications change what is being taught.

Assistive Technology: WEATHERFORD ISD evaluates whether your child needs specialized equipment or software to access learning, from text-to-speech programs to specialized communication devices.

Transition Services for Students Ages 16+

WEATHERFORD ISD provides transition planning services to prepare older students for life after graduation. Your child's IEP will include transition goals related to post-secondary education, employment, and independent living skills. These services begin at age 16 (or younger if appropriate) and continue through graduation or age 22.

WEATHERFORD ISD Dyslexia Program

Dyslexia is a specific learning disability affecting reading and language processing. If your child has been identified with WEATHERFORD ISD dyslexia or suspected dyslexia, specialized support is available. Texas law requires that students with dyslexia receive evidence-based interventions.

Dyslexia Identification and Support

The WEATHERFORD ISD dyslexia program includes universal screening in early grades to identify students at risk. If your child shows signs of dyslexia—such as difficulty with phonics, slow reading speed, or spelling challenges—the district provides targeted interventions and progress monitoring.

Students identified with dyslexia in WEATHERFORD ISD may qualify for:

  • Specialized reading instruction using structured literacy approaches
  • Extended time on reading-based assignments and assessments
  • Use of text-to-speech and other assistive technology
  • Small group instruction focused on phonological awareness, decoding, and fluency
  • Progress monitoring to ensure interventions are working

If your child isn't responding adequately to these general interventions, WEATHERFORD ISD will conduct a comprehensive evaluation for specific learning disability eligibility, which may result in special education placement.

Understanding Your WEATHERFORD ISD Parent Rights

WEATHERFORD ISD parent rights are protected under federal law (IDEA) and Texas Education Code. Understanding these rights is essential for advocating effectively for your child. Every parent of a child receiving special education services in WEATHERFORD ISD is entitled to detailed information about their rights and procedural safeguards.

Key Parent Rights in WEATHERFORD ISD

Right to Notice and Participation: You must receive written notice before WEATHERFORD ISD proposes or refuses to initiate any change to your child's special education services. You have the right to fully participate in all WEATHERFORD ISD ARD meeting decisions.

Right to Evaluation: You can request an independent evaluation if you disagree with WEATHERFORD ISD's assessment results. The district must either pay for the evaluation or defend their assessment through a hearing.

Right to Due Process: If you disagree with WEATHERFORD ISD's decisions regarding evaluation, eligibility, IEP services, or placement, you can request mediation or file for a due process hearing.

Right to Records: You can review and obtain copies of all records related to your child's evaluation, eligibility, IEP, and placement. WEATHERFORD ISD must provide these within 10 school days of your request.

Right to Prior Written Notice: WEATHERFORD ISD must give you detailed written notice in your native language before taking any action regarding your child's special education services.

The ARD Meeting Process

The WEATHERFORD ISD ARD meeting is where critical decisions about your child's special education are made. ARD meetings occur annually to review and revise your child's IEP, but can be called more frequently if needed. You have the right to bring anyone to this meeting who has knowledge about your child, including relatives, advocates, or attorneys.

Before your next WEATHERFORD ISD ARD meeting, gather information about your child's progress, any concerns you have, and questions about services. Come prepared to discuss goals, progress toward current goals, and any new needs.
