Understanding Special Education Services in VIDOR ISD

VIDOR ISD special education begins with identification. If you suspect your child needs support—whether they struggle with reading, mathematics, speech, emotional regulation, or physical disabilities—VIDOR ISD has a legal responsibility to evaluate your child at no cost to your family.

Special education isn't a place; it's a service. Your child might receive services in the general classroom, a resource room, or a specialized setting, depending on their needs and what's best for their learning.

Who Qualifies for Special Education?

Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §89.1004, children ages 3-21 qualify for special education if they have one of 13 disability categories and need specialized instruction. VIDOR ISD evaluates children in these areas:

  • Speech or language impairment
  • Autism spectrum disorder
  • Emotional disturbance
  • Other health impairment (including ADHD, asthma, diabetes)
  • Specific learning disability (including dyslexia)
  • Intellectual disability
  • Visual or hearing impairment
  • Orthopedic impairment
  • Traumatic brain injury
  • Multiple disabilities

Just because your child struggles doesn't automatically qualify them. The VIDOR ISD evaluation team must determine that the disability affects educational performance and that your child needs specialized instruction.

How to Request an Evaluation

Parents can request an evaluation for special education at any time. Contact your child's campus counselor, teacher, or the VIDOR ISD Special Education Department directly. Your request should be in writing, but even a verbal request starts the clock on the 60-day evaluation timeline.

Once VIDOR ISD receives your request, the district must complete evaluation and hold an ARD meeting within 60 calendar days. You'll be notified of the ARD meeting date at least 5 days in advance.

VIDOR ISD ARD Meetings: What to Expect

An VIDOR ISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) is where your child's special education plan is developed, reviewed, and adjusted. The ARD committee includes you, teachers, a school administrator, and sometimes a counselor or special education specialist.

What Happens During the ARD Meeting

The ARD committee reviews evaluation results and discusses your child's strengths, challenges, and needs. Together, you'll make decisions about:

  • Whether your child qualifies for special education
  • What services your child will receive
  • How much time your child spends in general education classrooms
  • Academic and functional goals for the year
  • How progress will be measured
  • Accommodations and modifications your child needs

You are a full voting member of the ARD committee. Your perspective on your child's needs, strengths, and home behavior is invaluable. Many VIDOR ISD parents find it helpful to bring notes, samples of their child's work, or a trusted advocate to their ARD meeting.

ARD Meeting Timeline and Your Rights

VIDOR ISD must hold ARD meetings at least once per school year. However, meetings can happen anytime if there's a significant change in your child's needs, progress isn't sufficient, or you request a meeting.

You have the right to:

  • Receive notice of the meeting at least 5 days in advance
  • Bring an advocate, attorney, or support person to the meeting
  • Review your child's records before the meeting
  • Request an independent evaluation if you disagree with VIDOR ISD's findings
  • Request a different meeting time if you can't attend
  • Ask for a postponement if you need time to gather information

If you and VIDOR ISD disagree about your child's IEP, you have options: ask for a resolution session, file for mediation, or request a due process hearing.

VIDOR ISD IEP Services and How They Work

An VIDOR ISD IEP (Individualized Education Program) is the written plan that guarantees your child receives the services and supports they need. This isn't just a document—it's a legal contract that VIDOR ISD must follow.

What's Included in an IEP?

Your child's IEP includes:

  1. Present levels of academic and functional performance – what your child can currently do
  2. Measurable annual goals – what your child will accomplish in the next year
  3. Special education services – the hours and types of instruction your child receives
  4. Related services – speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, etc.
  5. Accommodations and modifications – changes to how your child learns or is tested
  6. LRE statement – how much time your child spends in general education
  7. Transition services – for students age 14+, planning for post-secondary life

Types of Services Available in VIDOR ISD

VIDOR ISD special education services may include:

  • Special education instruction (pullout resource, collaborative teaching, self-contained classroom)
  • Speech-language pathology
  • Occupational and physical therapy
  • Counseling and mental health support
  • Assistive technology
  • Behavior support services
  • Dyslexia intervention (discussed in detail below)
  • Social-emotional learning support

VIDOR ISD Dyslexia Program and Reading Intervention

Texas Education Code §38.003 requires districts to identify and serve students with dyslexia. VIDOR ISD dyslexia program provides screening, diagnosis, and evidence-based intervention for students who struggle with reading and spelling despite adequate instruction.

Dyslexia is a neurological condition that affects how the brain processes written language. It's not laziness, poor vision, or low intelligence. With proper intervention, children with dyslexia can become strong readers.

Dyslexia Screening and Identification

VIDOR ISD screens all kindergarten and first-grade students for dyslexia risk. If your child shows signs of dyslexia at any grade level, you can request a dyslexia evaluation. Warning signs include:

  • Difficulty learning letter sounds and names
  • Trouble rhyming or blending sounds
  • Slow reading rate or poor comprehension
  • Spelling errors that look phonetically incorrect
  • Difficulty with sequencing or directions
  • Reversals of letters or numbers (past age 7)

If dyslexia is identified, VIDOR ISD must develop a dyslexia intervention plan within 30 days. This plan describes the specific, intensive reading instruction your child will receive.

Dyslexia Intervention Methods

Effective dyslexia intervention in VIDOR ISD focuses on phonics, phonological awareness, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension. Interventions are intensive, systematic, and evidence-based—delivered by trained specialists.

Your child's dyslexia intervention might happen in addition to special education services, or it might be part of their IEP. Either way, progress is monitored frequently, and the plan is adjusted if your child isn't responding.

Understanding Your Rights as a VIDOR ISD Parent

VIDOR ISD parent rights are guaranteed under federal and Texas law. Knowing these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child and catch problems early.

Core Parent Rights in Special Education

As a parent of a child receiving special education in VIDOR ISD, you have the right to:

  • Prior written notice – VIDOR ISD must inform you before evaluating your child, changing services, or changing placement
  • Informed consent – you approve or deny evaluations and initial IEP services
  • Records access – review your child's files and evaluations at any time
  • Independent evaluation – get an outside evaluation at VIDOR ISD's expense if you disagree with their findings
  • Participation in meetings – attend and participate in all ARD and special education decisions
  • Dispute resolution – use mediation or due process if you disagree with VIDOR ISD

Procedural Safeguards and Due Process

If you and VIDOR ISD reach an impasse, you're not stuck. Texas Education Code provides multiple pathways to resolve disagreements:

  1. Informal conversation – talk with your child's teacher or special education director
  2. Resolution session – a mandatory meeting to resolve concerns before formal hearing
  3. Mediation – a neutral third party helps you and VIDOR ISD reach agreement
  4. Due process complaint – a formal hearing before an impartial hearing officer

You can file a due process complaint about any aspect of VIDOR ISD's identification, evaluation, placement, or provision of FAPE (Free Appropriate Public Education) for your child.
