Understanding LUMBERTON ISD Special Education Services

LUMBERTON ISD special education provides specialized instruction and support services for students with disabilities under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act. These services begin as early as age three and continue through age 21.

Special education in LUMBERTON ISD is not a place—it's a service. Your child may receive services in general education classrooms, resource rooms, or specialized settings depending on their needs and the least restrictive environment (LRE) determination made by the Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committee.

The ARD Meeting Process in LUMBERTON ISD

The LUMBERTON ISD ARD meeting is where critical decisions about your child's education are made. ARD stands for "Admission, Review, and Dismissal," and this committee meeting is required by Texas Education Code §29.001 and federal IDEA regulations.

Your child's ARD committee in LUMBERTON ISD includes you (the parent), special education teachers, general education teachers, a school administrator, and other professionals as needed. This team works together to develop, review, and update your child's educational plan.

During your LUMBERTON ISD ARD meeting, the committee will:

  1. Review your child's current performance and progress
  2. Discuss evaluation results and assessment data
  3. Determine eligibility or continued eligibility for special education
  4. Develop or revise the Individualized Education Program (IEP)
  5. Discuss placement in the least restrictive environment

LUMBERTON ISD IEP Services Explained

The Individualized Education Program (IEP) is the legal document that outlines all special education services your child will receive. LUMBERTON ISD IEP services are individualized based on your child's unique needs, abilities, and goals.

Your child's IEP includes present levels of academic and functional performance, annual goals, the specific services to be provided, how progress will be measured, and placement recommendations. LUMBERTON ISD IEP services may include resource support, speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, or behavior intervention services.
