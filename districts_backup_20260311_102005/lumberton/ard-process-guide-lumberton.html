A comprehensive IEP for LUMBERTON ISD students includes several critical components:

  • Measurable Annual Goals: Specific LUMBERTON ISD IEP goals addressing identified needs in academics, speech, behavior, or motor skills
  • Special Education Services: The type and amount of services your child will receive (resource room, speech therapy, occupational therapy, etc.)
  • Accommodations and Modifications: Changes to how your child accesses curriculum or demonstrates knowledge
  • Progress Monitoring: How the school will measure your child's progress toward IEP goals

Writing Effective LUMBERTON ISD IEP Goals

During your LUMBERTON ISD IEP process, goals should be SMART: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. Quality goals directly address your child's identified needs and include clear success criteria.

For example, instead of "improve reading skills," an effective goal states: "The student will read grade-level text with 80% accuracy at the end of the school year, as measured by oral reading fluency assessments given monthly."
