Overview of TAYLOR ISD Special Education Services

TAYLOR ISD special education provides a full continuum of services designed to meet students with disabilities across all grade levels, from pre-K through high school. Our program is built on the principle that every child deserves access to quality education in the least restrictive environment possible.

The special education department in TAYLOR ISD employs certified special education teachers, speech-language pathologists, school psychologists, occupational therapists, and support staff. We serve students with disabilities including autism spectrum disorder, intellectual disabilities, emotional/behavioral disorders, specific learning disabilities, speech/language impairments, visual and hearing impairments, orthopedic impairments, and other health impairments.

Our TAYLOR ISD special education services align with federal requirements under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, while following Texas Education Code requirements. We maintain high standards for evidence-based practices and continuous professional development for all staff members.

Understanding ARD Meetings in TAYLOR ISD

The TAYLOR ISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) is the foundation of special education planning. This is where your child's individualized education program (IEP) is developed, reviewed, and revised annually. As a parent, you are a critical member of the ARD committee—your input shapes your child's educational experience.

What Happens at a TAYLOR ISD ARD Meeting

  • Initial evaluation discussion: If your child is being evaluated for the first time, the ARD committee reviews assessment results and determines eligibility
  • IEP development: Goals, services, accommodations, and placement are decided collaboratively
  • Annual review: Progress is evaluated, and goals may be adjusted based on your child's current performance
  • Triennial evaluation: Every three years, comprehensive testing occurs to reassess needs

Your Rights During TAYLOR ISD ARD Meetings

Parents in TAYLOR ISD have specific rights before, during, and after ARD meetings. You have the right to bring an advocate or attorney, receive all documents in advance, request an independent evaluation, request the meeting be recorded, and take time to review proposals before signing. You can also request a reconvened meeting if you disagree with decisions.

TAYLOR ISD must provide notice at least 10 days before the ARD meeting and must schedule meetings at a mutually convenient time. If you cannot attend in person, participation by phone or videoconference is available. Documents must be translated if English is not your primary language.

TAYLOR ISD IEP Services and Supports

An TAYLOR ISD IEP services plan is a legally binding document that outlines exactly what services your child will receive. Understanding each component helps you ensure your child gets appropriate support.

Types of IEP Services in TAYLOR ISD

Direct services include special education instruction, speech-language pathology, occupational therapy, physical therapy, and counseling. These services are delivered by licensed specialists who work directly with your child multiple times per week.

Accommodations are changes to how your child accesses the curriculum—such as extended time on tests, preferential seating, use of assistive technology, or modified assignments. Accommodations do not change what is being taught, but rather how your child accesses the material.

Modifications are changes to the content itself. If your child has significant intellectual disabilities, the curriculum may be modified so grade-level standards are adjusted to match your child's learning level.

Related services support your child's ability to benefit from special education. In TAYLOR ISD, these may include transportation, assistive technology, transition services, and behavioral support.

IEP Goals and Progress Monitoring

TAYLOR ISD IEP services include measurable annual goals in areas such as academics, social-emotional skills, communication, motor skills, and behavior. Each goal must be specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound. Progress toward goals is monitored regularly—at minimum, you'll receive progress reports alongside regular report cards.

You have the right to request more frequent progress updates. If your child is not making adequate progress toward IEP goals, the ARD committee must meet to revise the IEP and consider whether additional services are needed.

TAYLOR ISD Parent Rights and Procedural Safeguards

TAYLOR ISD parent rights are extensive and are designed to ensure families have a voice in every decision made about their child's education. Understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child with special needs.

Core Parent Rights in Special Education

  • Prior written notice: TAYLOR ISD must notify you before proposing or refusing any change to your child's special education program
  • Consent: Your written consent is required before initial evaluation, initial IEP implementation, and any major service changes
  • Access to records: You can review all records related to your child's education within 10 school days of request
  • Independent evaluation: If you disagree with TAYLOR ISD's evaluation, you can request an independent evaluation at district expense under certain circumstances
  • Due process hearing: You can request a hearing if you dispute TAYLOR ISD decisions regarding identification, evaluation, placement, or FAPE (free appropriate public education)
  • Representation: You can bring an attorney or special ed advocate TAYLOR families recommend to any meeting

Dispute Resolution Options in TAYLOR ISD

Before pursuing formal due process, TAYLOR ISD offers mediation as a free, confidential way to resolve disagreements. Mediation involves a neutral third party helping both sides reach agreement. This process is often faster and less adversarial than due process hearings.

If mediation doesn't resolve the issue, you have the right to file for due process. A hearing officer will review evidence and make a binding decision. Many families in the TAYLOR area also consult with special education attorney near TAYLOR to understand their options.

TAYLOR ISD Dyslexia Program and Language-Based Learning Disabilities

Dyslexia is a specific learning disability that affects reading, spelling, and language processing. TAYLOR ISD dyslexia program provides specialized intervention using structured literacy approaches based on the science of reading.

Identifying Dyslexia in TAYLOR ISD

Students suspected of having dyslexia undergo comprehensive evaluation including phonological awareness assessment, letter naming fluency, decoding ability, spelling, and oral language skills. If your child struggles with reading despite adequate instruction, request an evaluation to determine if dyslexia or other language-based learning disabilities are present.

Intervention and Support

TAYLOR ISD dyslexia program includes small-group or individual instruction in phonics, phonemic awareness, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension strategies. Instruction follows structured literacy methods and typically occurs 3-5 times per week. Progress is monitored frequently using curriculum-based measurement tools.

Many students with dyslexia also benefit from accommodations such as audiobooks, extended time on reading assignments, and assistive technology like text-to-speech software. These supports should be included in an IEP or 504 plan.

Transition Planning for High School and Beyond

Beginning at age 14 (or younger if the IEP team determines it's appropriate), TAYLOR ISD focuses on transition planning to prepare students for life after high school. This includes career exploration, vocational training, post-secondary education options, and independent living skills.

Key Transition Components

Transition planning in TAYLOR ISD includes vocational assessments, job shadowing, work-based learning experiences, and connections with community resources. By age 16, the IEP must include a statement of transition services needed.

Students may participate in supported work experiences, apprenticeships, or dual-enrollment at local community colleges. TAYLOR ISD coordinates with Williamson County workforce development and adult service agencies to ensure continuity after graduation.

How to Access TAYLOR ISD Special Education Services

Step 1: Referral for Evaluation

If you suspect your child has a disability, request a special education evaluation in writing to the TAYLOR ISD special education department. Teachers, doctors, or other professionals can also refer students. TAYLOR ISD must respond within 10 days and obtain your written consent before evaluation begins.

Step 2: Comprehensive Evaluation

TAYLOR ISD conducts a comprehensive evaluation using multiple assessment methods across all suspected areas of disability. This typically takes 4-6 weeks. You'll receive a copy of the evaluation report at least 5 days before the ARD meeting where eligibility is determined.

Step 3: ARD Meeting and IEP Development

TAYLOR ISD schedules the initial ARD meeting within a reasonable timeframe after evaluation is complete. The committee reviews evaluation results and develops an IEP if your child is eligible. You must give written consent before services begin.

Step 4: Service Implementation and Progress Monitoring

Once the IEP is in place, services begin and progress is monitored regularly. Communicate frequently with your child's special education team. Request updates anytime, not just at scheduled progress report times.

Supporting Your Child's Learning at Home

Families in TAYLOR ISD who actively participate in their child's education see better outcomes. Ask your child's special education teacher for strategies you can use at home to reinforce skills being taught at school.

Attend IEP meetings prepared with questions and observations about your child's strengths and challenges. Keep organized records of evaluations, IEPs, progress reports, and correspondence with the school. This documentation is valuable if you need to pursue dispute resolution.
