Understanding Special Education in ANDREWS ISD

ANDREWS ISD special education services are designed to meet the unique learning needs of students with disabilities. Our district serves over 4,200 students across the ANDREWS area, with a dedicated special education department committed to inclusive, evidence-based practices that help every child succeed.

Special education in ANDREWS ISD is built on the foundation of the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code (TEC). These laws guarantee that students with disabilities receive a free, appropriate public education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment (LRE).

Who Qualifies for Special Education Services?

Students in ANDREWS ISD may qualify for special education if they have a disability in one of 13 categories recognized under IDEA, including:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Hearing Impairment or Deafness
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Specific Learning Disability (including dyslexia)
  • Speech-Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Visual Impairment or Blindness

If you suspect your child may have a disability, contact the ANDREWS ISD special education office to request an evaluation.

ANDREWS ISD ARD Meetings: What Parents Need to Know

The ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting is the cornerstone of special education in ANDREWS ISD. This is where your child's educational plan is developed, reviewed, and adjusted based on progress and changing needs.

What is an ARD Meeting?

An ANDREWS ISD ARD meeting brings together parents, teachers, special education staff, and sometimes the student to discuss your child's educational needs and goals. This collaborative process ensures that decisions about your child's education are made with input from everyone who knows them best.

Your Role in the ANDREWS ISD ARD Process

As a parent, you are an equal member of the ARD team. Your input is not optional—it's essential. During ANDREWS ISD ARD meetings, you have the right to:

  • Participate in all decisions about your child's special education
  • Ask questions and request clarification
  • Request additional evaluations or assessments
  • Bring an advocate or attorney to the meeting
  • Request a copy of the meeting notes and IEP document
  • Ask for additional time if you need to process information

ANDREWS ISD ARD Meeting Timeline and Frequency

ARD meetings in ANDREWS ISD must occur at least once per year to review your child's progress. However, meetings can be called more frequently if needed—when your child's needs change, after an evaluation is completed, or at your request.

Initial evaluations typically take 60 days from the date of your written consent. Once your child is found eligible for services, the initial ARD meeting to develop an IEP should occur within 30 days.

ANDREWS ISD IEP Services: Creating Your Child's Educational Plan

The Individualized Education Program (IEP) is your child's legal educational roadmap. ANDREWS ISD IEP services are tailored to your child's specific strengths, needs, and goals, with accommodations and modifications designed to help them access the general education curriculum.

What's Included in an ANDREWS ISD IEP?

A comprehensive ANDREWS ISD IEP includes:

  • Present Levels of Performance: How your child is currently functioning academically, socially, and behaviorally
  • Measurable Annual Goals: Specific, achievable objectives in areas of need
  • Special Education Services: The specific services your child will receive (speech therapy, resource room, counseling, etc.)
  • Related Services: Occupational therapy, physical therapy, or other support services
  • Accommodations and Modifications: Changes to curriculum, instruction, or testing to support success
  • LRE Statement: Explanation of why your child is or isn't in the general education classroom
  • Transition Services: For students 16 and older, plans for post-secondary education, employment, and independent living

Types of Special Education Services Available in ANDREWS ISD

ANDREWS ISD offers a continuum of special education services, including:

  • General education classroom with consultation
  • Pullout resource room instruction
  • Separate special education classrooms
  • Specialized services (speech-language pathology, occupational therapy, counseling)
  • Behavior support services and positive behavior intervention plans
  • Assistive technology and adapted materials

ANDREWS ISD Dyslexia Program: Specialized Support for Reading Disabilities

The ANDREWS ISD dyslexia program provides targeted intervention for students with dyslexia and related reading disabilities. Texas Education Code (TEC 38.003) requires that all districts identify and serve students with dyslexia.

How ANDREWS ISD Identifies Dyslexia

Students in the ANDREWS area who show signs of dyslexia—such as difficulty with reading fluency, decoding, or spelling—are screened using research-based assessment tools. If your child shows characteristics of dyslexia, the ANDREWS ISD dyslexia program team will recommend additional evaluation.

ANDREWS ISD Dyslexia Program Services

Once identified, students in the ANDREWS ISD dyslexia program receive:

  • Intensive, evidence-based reading intervention using structured literacy approaches
  • Specialized instruction from dyslexia-trained teachers
  • Progress monitoring and regular reassessment
  • Accommodations during instruction and testing
  • Parent education and home support strategies

If you believe your child may have dyslexia, request a referral to the ANDREWS ISD dyslexia program through your campus principal or the special education office.

ANDREWS ISD Parent Rights and Legal Protections

ANDREWS ISD parent rights are protected by federal law (IDEA) and Texas state law. Understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Your Core ANDREWS ISD Parent Rights

Parents of students with disabilities in ANDREWS ISD have the right to:

  • Notice: Receive written notice before the district evaluates, changes placement, or refuses your request
  • Consent: Give informed consent before initial evaluation and any significant change in services
  • Confidentiality: Protect your child's educational records; only authorized personnel can access them
  • Independent Evaluation: Request an independent evaluation if you disagree with the district's findings
  • Due Process: File a formal complaint or due process hearing if you disagree with special education decisions
  • Representation: Bring a special education attorney or advocate to meetings
  • Participation: Be a full member of the ARD team

Understanding Your ANDREWS ISD Parent Rights Documentation

The district must provide you with a document explaining your rights under IDEA. This should be given at your first ARD meeting and whenever you request it. If you don't have this information, ask the ANDREWS ISD special education office for the Texas Procedural Safeguards Notice.

Special Education Advocacy and Legal Support for ANDREWS Families

Advocating for your child is an important parental responsibility. Whether you're navigating ANDREWS ISD special education processes or preparing for an ANDREWS ISD ARD meeting, having support can make a significant difference.

Types of Advocates Available to ANDREWS ISD Parents

Parents in ANDREWS and surrounding areas have several options for professional support:

  • Parent Advocates: Trained professionals who help parents understand special education processes and attend meetings with you
  • Educational Consultants: Specialists in specific disabilities or educational strategies who can advise on your child's needs
  • Special Education Attorneys: Lawyers specializing in IDEA who can represent you in formal disputes

When to Consider Legal Support

A special education attorney near ANDREWS may be necessary if:

  • The district denies your child a service or program you believe they need
  • Progress toward IEP goals is not being made despite documented intervention
  • You disagree with the district's evaluation results
  • Your child's placement or services are being changed without your agreement
  • You're preparing to file a due process complaint

How to Get Started with ANDREWS ISD Special Education

Step 1: Request an Evaluation

Contact your child's campus principal or the ANDREWS ISD special education office and submit a written request for a special education evaluation. The request should describe your concerns and provide specific examples of how your child's learning is affected.

Step 2: Evaluation and Eligibility Determination

The ANDREWS ISD special education team will conduct a comprehensive evaluation within 60 days of receiving your consent. This typically includes academic testing, behavioral assessment, classroom observation, and parental input.

Step 3: Attend Your First ARD Meeting

If your child is found eligible, an initial ANDREWS ISD ARD meeting will be scheduled to develop an IEP. Come prepared to discuss your child's strengths, concerns, and goals.

Step 4: Implement and Monitor Progress

Once the IEP is in place, monitor your child's progress regularly. Request progress reports and ask questions about how interventions are working.

Contacting ANDREWS ISD Special Education

For more information about ANDREWS ISD special education, your rights as a parent, or to begin the evaluation process, reach out to your child's campus or the district special education office.
