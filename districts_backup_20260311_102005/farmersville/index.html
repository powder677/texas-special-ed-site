The Evaluation Process: Getting Your Child Assessed

If you believe your child needs special education support, the evaluation process is the first critical step. Parents of students in FARMERSVILLE ISD can request a comprehensive evaluation at any time by submitting a written request to the campus principal or the Special Education Department.

Steps in the FARMERSVILLE ISD Evaluation Process

  1. Submit a written referral: Contact your child's school or the FARMERSVILLE ISD Special Education Department. You can request an evaluation if you suspect your child has a disability.
  2. Receive notice within 5 school days: The district must notify you that a referral has been received and explain the evaluation process and your parent rights.
  3. Provide consent: You'll receive and sign the Permission to Evaluate form. The district cannot proceed without your written consent.
  4. Complete assessments within 60 days: FARMERSVILLE ISD evaluators will conduct testing in all areas of suspected disability, using multiple measures and culturally responsive tools.
  5. Receive results and recommendations: You'll receive a comprehensive evaluation report explaining findings and initial recommendations for eligibility.

The evaluation should assess cognitive ability, academic achievement, social/emotional functioning, language development, motor skills, and adaptive behavior—whatever domains are relevant to your child's suspected disability. Parents in FARMERSVILLE ISD have the right to request independent evaluations at district expense if you disagree with results.

The ARD Meeting and IEP Development

Once your child is found eligible for services, the ARD (Admission, Review, Dismissal) Committee will meet to develop your child's FARMERSVILLE ISD IEP (Individualized Education Program). This document is the legal agreement detailing your child's services, goals, placement, and accommodations.

What to Expect at Your FARMERSVILLE ISD ARD Meeting

An FARMERSVILLE ISD ARD meeting brings together parents, teachers, special education specialists, and administrators to discuss your child's needs and create their IEP. You are an equal member of this team—not a guest.

Before the meeting: Request copies of all evaluations and reports at least 5 days before the meeting. Ask questions about anything you don't understand. You can bring a support person, advocate, or attorney to any ARD meeting.

During the meeting: The team will review evaluation data, discuss your child's strengths and needs, set measurable annual goals, determine the services your child requires, and identify the appropriate educational setting. You have the right to ask for breaks, request additional meetings, or table decisions if you need time to consider recommendations.

After the meeting: You'll receive a copy of the completed IEP. Special education services in FARMERSVILLE ISD must begin within a reasonable timeframe after the ARD meeting, typically 10-15 school days.

Key IEP Components Parents Should Understand

  • Present Levels of Academic and Functional Performance (PLAAFP): Describes your child's current strengths and challenges
  • Annual Goals: Specific, measurable objectives addressing priority needs, usually 3-5 goals aligned with grade-level standards
  • Special Education Services: How often, where, and for how long your child receives specialized instruction (resource, self-contained, itinerant services)
  • Related Services: Speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, or other support your child needs
  • Accommodations and Modifications: Changes to how your child accesses curriculum (extended time, small group instruction, assistive technology)
  • Placement: Where services are delivered—general education class with support, resource room, or specialized classroom

Parents of FARMERSVILLE ISD students must receive prior written notice before any changes to placement or services. You have the right to disagree with ARD decisions and pursue resolution through mediation or due process hearings.

Dyslexia and reading disabilities are among the most common reasons students are referred for FARMERSVILLE ISD special education evaluation. Texas Education Code Section 38.003 requires all districts to identify and provide services to students with dyslexia.

Dyslexia Screening and Intervention in FARMERSVILLE

All students in grades K-3 in FARMERSVILLE ISD are screened for dyslexia using evidence-based screening tools. If your child is identified as at-risk, the district must provide intensive, structured literacy interventions using research-based approaches like Orton-Gillingham or Structured Literacy methods.

Students who do not respond adequately to high-quality interventions may be referred for a comprehensive dyslexia evaluation. If eligible, your child will receive specialized instruction targeting phonological processing, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension. The FARMERSVILLE ISD dyslexia program uses multisensory, systematic approaches proven effective for students with reading disabilities.

Parents in FARMERSVILLE ISD should know that dyslexia is a neurobiological condition—not laziness or lack of effort. Early intervention is critical, and students with dyslexia often respond remarkably well to specialized instruction.

Understanding Your Rights: FARMERSVILLE ISD Parent Rights and Procedural Safeguards

Federal law grants you significant rights and protections under IDEA. All parents in FARMERSVILLE ISD must receive a copy of parent rights and procedural safeguards before the first ARD meeting and upon request thereafter.

Critical Parent Rights in FARMERSVILLE ISD

  • Right to notice: FARMERSVILLE ISD must notify you before evaluating, placing, or changing services for your child
  • Right to consent: You must provide written permission before initial evaluations or IEP implementation
  • Right to participate: You are a voting member of the ARD Committee with equal authority in decisions
  • Right to records: You can inspect all educational and medical records related to your child at no cost
  • Right to independent evaluation: If you disagree with FARMERSVILLE ISD evaluations, you can obtain an independent evaluation at district expense
  • Right to dispute resolution: You can pursue mediation or due process hearings to resolve disagreements with the district
  • Right to representation: You can bring an advocate, attorney, or support person to all meetings

If you believe FARMERSVILLE ISD has violated your rights or your child's rights, you have options: request a meeting with special education administrators, pursue mediation (free through the Texas Education Agency), file a formal complaint with TEA, or request a due process hearing.
