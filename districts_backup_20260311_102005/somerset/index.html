Understanding SOMERSET ISD Special Education Services

SOMERSET ISD special education serves students ages 3-22 who qualify under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). Our district identifies and supports students with various disabilities, including autism spectrum disorder, dyslexia, speech impairments, developmental delays, and emotional/behavioral needs.

With an enrollment of over 4,259 students, SOMERSET ISD maintains specialized programs and trained staff across multiple campuses. We believe every child in SOMERSET deserves access to quality education tailored to their unique needs.

Who Qualifies for Services?

Your child may qualify for SOMERSET ISD special education services if they have a documented disability affecting educational performance. Qualifying disabilities under Texas law include:

  • Autism spectrum disorder
  • Emotional disturbance
  • Hearing impairment
  • Intellectual disability
  • Orthopedic impairment
  • Other health impairment
  • Specific learning disability (including dyslexia)
  • Speech or language impairment
  • Traumatic brain injury
  • Visual impairment

How to Request an Evaluation

Parents can request a special education evaluation at any time. Submit your written request to your campus principal or the SOMERSET ISD special education office. The district must respond within 15 days and complete the comprehensive evaluation within 60 days of receiving your consent.

The ARD Meeting: Your Voice Matters

The SOMERSET ISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) is where your child's special education plan is developed and reviewed. This is your opportunity to collaborate with educators and ensure your child's needs are met.

What Happens at a SOMERSET ISD ARD Meeting?

ARD meetings typically include parents, special education teachers, general education teachers, administrators, and sometimes your child. Together, the team discusses your child's strengths, challenges, and educational goals. This is where placement decisions, services, and accommodations are determined.

Parents in SOMERSET ISD should come prepared to share observations about their child's behavior and learning at home. Bring any medical records, outside evaluations, or documentation that might be relevant to your child's needs.

Your Rights During the SOMERSET ISD ARD Process

As a parent in SOMERSET ISD, you have important rights during ARD meetings:

  • Equal participation: Your input carries equal weight with school personnel
  • Prior notice: The district must notify you at least five business days before any ARD meeting
  • Interpreters: You can request interpreters or translation services
  • Representation: You may bring an advocate, attorney, or trusted advisor
  • Documentation: Request copies of all evaluations and meeting minutes
  • Decision-making time: You don't need to agree immediately; ask for time to review proposals

Individualized Education Plans (IEPs) in SOMERSET ISD

Your child's SOMERSET ISD IEP services are outlined in their Individualized Education Program document. This legally binding plan specifies what services your child receives, where they receive them, and how progress is measured. IEPs are reviewed annually, though parents in SOMERSET ISD can request reviews more frequently if needed.

Key Components of a SOMERSET ISD IEP

An effective IEP includes measurable annual goals, specific services and supports, accommodations and modifications, and transition planning for students age 16 and older. Your SOMERSET ISD IEP should address your child's present levels of performance, ensuring goals are challenging yet achievable.

Accommodations help your child access learning without changing expectations (like extended time on tests). Modifications change what your child is expected to learn (like simplified curriculum standards). Both may be appropriate for your SOMERSET ISD student.

Progress Monitoring and Communication

Throughout the school year, your child's progress toward IEP goals should be monitored regularly. SOMERSET ISD provides progress reports at least as often as grades are distributed to general education students. Ask your special education teacher about specific progress data during regular conversations and at review meetings.

SOMERSET ISD Parent Rights and Procedural Safeguards

SOMERSET ISD parent rights are protected under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code. Understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Essential Parent Rights in SOMERSET ISD

  • Free appropriate education: Your child has the right to a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) tailored to their needs
  • Least restrictive environment: Your child should be educated with non-disabled peers to the maximum extent possible
  • Due process: You can request mediation or a due process hearing if you disagree with the district
  • Records access: You can review all education records within 10 business days
  • Independent evaluations: You can obtain independent evaluations at district expense if you disagree with official evaluations
  • Notice and consent: The district must provide written notice in your native language before taking action

The SOMERSET ISD special education department provides a detailed Parent Rights Notice annually. Request a copy if you haven't received one, or access it online through the district website.
