Understanding ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD's Child Find Obligation

ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD, like all Texas school districts, is legally required under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) to identify, locate, and evaluate children who may have disabilities. This requirement is known as ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD child find, and it applies to all students from birth through age 21.

The district must actively search for children who may need special education services, even if parents haven't formally requested an evaluation. If your child is struggling academically or socially, ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD has an obligation to consider whether an evaluation is needed.

Your Child Find Rights in ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD

  • The district must evaluate your child at no cost to your family
  • ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD must conduct evaluations in your child's native language or through an interpreter
  • Evaluations must be comprehensive and address all areas of suspected disability
  • You have the right to participate in all decisions about evaluation and placement

When to Request an ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD Special Education Evaluation

Parents should consider requesting an ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD special education evaluation when their child shows persistent signs of struggling. These signs might include difficulty keeping up with peers academically, behavioral challenges, social difficulties, or unexplained gaps between ability and performance.

It's important to note that requesting an evaluation doesn't guarantee your child will be found eligible for services. However, if you suspect a disability, an evaluation is the only way to determine whether your child qualifies for special education support in ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD.

Common Reasons Parents Request FIE Evaluations in ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD

  • Suspected learning disabilities affecting reading, writing, or math
  • Speech and language delays or disorders
  • Autism spectrum disorder concerns
  • Attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) or attention difficulties
  • Emotional or behavioral difficulties
  • Physical disabilities or health impairments
  • Intellectual disabilities
  • Vision or hearing concerns

How to Formally Request an ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD Evaluation Request

To initiate the process, you must submit a formal ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD evaluation request to the district. This request should be in writing and clearly state that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for your child.

Step-by-Step Process for Submitting Your Request

  1. Put Your Request in Writing: Send a letter to the Special Education Director or your child's campus principal stating that you request an FIE evaluation. Email or hand-delivered letters both work, but sending certified mail provides proof of receipt.
  2. Be Specific About Your Concerns: Explain what areas of concern prompted your request. For example: "I'm requesting an evaluation because my child struggles with reading comprehension despite extra tutoring" or "My child has difficulty with social interactions and following directions."
  3. Include Your Contact Information: Provide your phone number, email, and mailing address so ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD can contact you promptly.
  4. Keep a Copy for Your Records: Always maintain documentation of your written request and any responses from the district.

Pro Tip for ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD Parents: Consider requesting a meeting with your child's teacher or the school counselor before submitting a formal evaluation request. They may provide valuable information about your child's performance and can help you understand whether an evaluation is appropriate.

The ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD 45 Day Timeline and FIE Process

Once ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD receives your written evaluation request, the district has a specific timeline it must follow. Understanding the ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD 45 day timeline helps you know what to expect and when.

Critical Deadlines in the ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD 45 Day Evaluation Timeline

Day 1-5: Initial Review and Notice

ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD must acknowledge your evaluation request and provide you with a written Notice of Proposed Evaluation. This notice explains the evaluation plan, the areas the district will assess, and your parental rights.

Day 5-30: Obtaining Parental Consent

You must sign a form giving ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD permission to conduct the evaluation. The district cannot proceed without your written consent. If you disagree with the evaluation plan, you can request changes before signing.

Day 30-45: Conducting Assessments

ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD evaluators will conduct various tests and assessments. These may include cognitive testing, academic achievement tests, social-emotional evaluations, speech and language assessments, and classroom observations. The district must complete all evaluations within the 45-day window.

Day 45: IEP Meeting and Eligibility Decision

ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD must convene an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting by the 45th day. At this meeting, the evaluation team will present findings and determine whether your child qualifies for special education services under IDEA or Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.

Important Considerations About the ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD Timeline

  • The 45-day clock starts when ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD receives your written request, not when you first mention concerns to a teacher
  • If you and the district mutually agree in writing to extend the timeline, it can be extended beyond 45 days
  • Holidays and school breaks may affect when meetings can be scheduled, but they don't stop the 45-day clock
  • ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD cannot delay the evaluation process while pursuing interventions like tutoring or Response to Intervention (RTI)

What the ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD Full Individual Evaluation Includes

A comprehensive ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD FIE evaluation should be thorough and examine all areas related to your child's suspected disability. Texas Education Code Section 29.004 requires that evaluations include multiple measures and cannot rely on a single assessment.

Components Typically Included in an ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD FIE

  • Cognitive Assessment: IQ testing to measure reasoning, problem-solving, and processing abilities
  • Academic Achievement Testing: Assessment of reading, writing, math, and other academic skills
  • Social-Emotional Evaluation: Assessment of behavior, emotional functioning, and social skills
  • Classroom Observations: Direct observation of your child in academic and social settings
  • Review of Records: Analysis of grades, previous evaluations, and medical history
  • Teacher and Parent Input: Rating scales and questionnaires completed by those who know your child
  • Specialized Assessments: Speech-language, occupational therapy, vision, or hearing evaluations if indicated

ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD parents have the right to request specific assessments or areas of evaluation. If you believe your child needs an evaluation in a particular area, communicate this clearly to the special education team.

Preparing for Your Child's ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD Evaluation

As a parent in ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD, you play an essential role in the evaluation process. Taking time to prepare ensures that evaluators have complete information about your child.

How to Prepare for Your ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD Special Education Evaluation

  • Gather Medical History: Compile information about birth, developmental milestones, health conditions, medications, and any previous evaluations
  • Document Concerns: Write down specific examples of behaviors or academic struggles you've observed at home and school
  • Review Report Cards: Bring grades, progress reports, and any written feedback from teachers
  • Make a List of Questions: Note any concerns or questions you want addressed during the evaluation process
  • Prepare Your Child: Explain that your child will be taking some tests to help the school understand how to support them better

Understanding ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD Evaluation Results and Next Steps

At the end of your child's ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD FIE evaluation, you'll receive a detailed report explaining the findings. This report is crucial for understanding whether your child qualifies for special education services.

Possible Outcomes from Your ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD Evaluation

Eligible for Special Education Services: If your child is found eligible, ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) outlining the services and supports your child will receive.

Eligible for Section 504 Plan: If your child doesn't qualify for special education but has a disability that impacts learning, ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD may develop a 504 plan instead.

Not Eligible for Special Education: If ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD determines your child isn't eligible, the district must provide you with written notice explaining why and information about your appeal rights.

Your Rights if You Disagree with ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD Results

  • You can request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at district expense
  • You can request a due process hearing to challenge the evaluation or eligibility decision
  • You have the right to bring an advocate or attorney to meetings
  • You can request mediation as an alternative to a due process hearing
