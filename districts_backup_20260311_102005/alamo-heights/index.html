  • Orthopedic impairments

ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD ARD Meetings and IEP Development

ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD ARD meetings (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) are where the magic happens. This is where your child's individualized education program is created, reviewed, and adjusted based on progress and changing needs.

What to Expect at Your ARD Meeting

The ARD committee includes you (the parent), your child (when appropriate), a special education teacher, a general education teacher, a school administrator, and other specialists as needed. ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD ARD meetings typically last 1-2 hours and cover:

  • Your child's current performance in academics and behavior
  • Strengths and areas of concern
  • Annual goals and short-term objectives
  • Placement and service delivery options
  • Progress monitoring and assessment methods
  • Transition planning (for students 14 and older)

Preparing for Your ARD Meeting

Come prepared with observations, concerns, and questions. Bring any outside evaluations or medical documentation. Review your child's current progress reports beforehand. Request an interpreter if English isn't your home language—ALAMO HEIGHTS ISD must provide one at no cost. You have the right to bring a support person, advocate, or attorney to your ARD meeting.

Know your timeline: Initial ARD meetings must occur within 30 calendar days of evaluation completion. Annual reviews happen every 12 months. Reevaluations occur every 3 years or more frequently if requested.
