The KINGSVILLE ISD ARD Meeting: What to Expect

Understanding the structure and typical flow of a KINGSVILLE ISD ARD meeting reduces anxiety and helps you participate more effectively.

Federal law requires specific participants at your KINGSVILLE ISD ARD meeting:

  • At least one special education teacher or specialist
  • A school administrator or designee with authority to commit resources

  • Additional specialists as needed (speech therapist, counselor, etc.)

For KINGSVILLE ISD families, you can always request additional participants who have knowledge of your child, such as therapists, tutors, or other service providers.

Meeting Agenda and Timeline

Your KINGSVILLE ISD ARD meeting typically follows this sequence:

  1. Introductions: Team members introduce themselves and explain their roles
  2. Purpose statement: The facilitator explains why the meeting is being held
  3. Evaluation results: If applicable, evaluation data is presented and explained
  4. Current performance: Progress data from current classroom is discussed
  5. Eligibility determination: Team discusses whether child qualifies for special education under federal/state categories
  6. Present levels of performance: Detailed description of current academic, behavioral, and functional skills
  7. Goals and objectives: KINGSVILLE ISD IEP goals are developed to address identified needs
  8. Services and placement: Specific special education services and classroom placement are determined
  9. Accommodations and modifications: Supports needed for success in general education are listed
  10. Transition services: For students age 16+, post-secondary planning is discussed
  11. Signature: Parents and team members sign the IEP document

Most KINGSVILLE ISD ARD meeting sessions last 1-2 hours, though complex cases may take longer. KINGSVILLE ISD must complete the IEP within 30 calendar days of initial eligibility determination.

Developing Effective KINGSVILLE ISD IEP Goals

The KINGSVILLE ISD IEP goals are the heart of the IEP document—they define what your child will work toward throughout the school year. Effective goals directly address your child's specific needs and are measurable.

What Makes a Strong Goal?

Effective KINGSVILLE ISD IEP goals follow the SMART criteria:

  • Specific: Clearly describes exactly what skill will be addressed, not vague statements like "improve reading"
  • Measurable: Includes specific numbers, percentages, or observable behaviors so progress can be tracked objectively
  • Achievable: Realistic for your child given their current performance and available supports
  • Relevant: Directly tied to your child's academic and functional needs
  • Time-bound: Includes a specific timeline (usually one school year) for achieving the goal

For example, instead of "improve math skills," a strong goal might read: "Marcus will solve two-step word problems with 80% accuracy on curriculum-based measurements administered bi-weekly by June 2024."

Questions to Ask About KINGSVILLE ISD IEP Goals

  • How was this goal chosen? How does it address my child's specific needs?
  • How will progress be measured and how often?
  • What instruction or support will help my child reach this goal?
  • Is this goal appropriate for my child's age and grade level?
  • How realistic is this goal given my child's current abilities and available resources?

Don't hesitate to suggest revisions to KINGSVILLE ISD IEP goals if you believe they don't adequately address your child's needs or are unrealistic.

KINGSVILLE ISD must provide services in the "least restrictive environment"—meaning your child spends as much time as possible with non-disabled peers while receiving necessary support.

  • Resource/pull-out services: Child spends part of the day in special education setting and part in general education
