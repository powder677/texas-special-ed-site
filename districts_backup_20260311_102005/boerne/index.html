The Referral and Evaluation Process for BOERNE ISD Students

The journey toward BOERNE ISD IEP services begins with either a teacher referral or a parent request for evaluation. Parents concerned about their child's academic progress, behavioral challenges, or developmental delays have the right to request a comprehensive evaluation at any time.

How to Request an Evaluation

BOERNE ISD parents can request an evaluation by contacting their school's campus administrator or the district's Special Education Department directly. The request should be submitted in writing, though verbal requests are also honored. Once BOERNE ISD receives your request, the district must provide written notice within reasonable timeframes outlining the evaluation process and parent rights.

What Happens During Evaluation

BOERNE ISD special education staff conduct comprehensive evaluations that may include:

  • Academic and cognitive assessments
  • Speech and language evaluations
  • Classroom observations
  • Review of educational and developmental history
  • Input from parents regarding home behaviors and skills
  • Standardized testing administered by qualified professionals

The complete evaluation typically takes 4-8 weeks. BOERNE ISD must complete all evaluations and hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 60 calendar days of receiving your written consent.

Understanding the ARD Meeting

The BOERNE ISD ARD meeting is where your child's eligibility is determined and educational plans are developed. ARD meetings bring together parents, teachers, administrators, and diagnosticians to discuss evaluation findings and determine whether your child qualifies for special education services under IDEA.

Parents should understand that BOERNE ISD ARD meetings operate collaboratively, with all participants having equal voice in decisions. You have the right to bring advocates, family members, or attorneys to your ARD meeting to support you in advocating for your child's needs.

IEP Development and BOERNE ISD Special Education Services

If your child qualifies for services, BOERNE ISD special education staff will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) during your ARD meeting. This legally binding document outlines your child's current performance, annual goals, specific services, accommodations, and modifications needed for educational success.

Key Components of Your Child's IEP

An effective IEP includes:

  • Present Levels of Performance: Detailed descriptions of your child's current academic, behavioral, and developmental functioning
  • Annual Goals: Measurable, achievable objectives across all areas of need
  • Special Education Services: Specific instruction and support your child receives, including frequency and duration
  • Related Services: Speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, or other services addressing identified needs
  • Accommodations and Modifications: Classroom adjustments and curriculum changes ensuring your child accesses instruction
  • Placement Decision: Where services are delivered (general education classroom, resource room, or specialized setting)
  • Progress Monitoring Plan: How BOERNE ISD will measure whether your child is meeting annual goals

BOERNE ISD parents receive copies of all IEP documents and progress reports on your child's achievement toward goals. You should review progress data regularly and request additional meetings if you have concerns.
