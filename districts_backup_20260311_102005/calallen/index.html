Understanding Special Education in CALALLEN ISD

CALALLEN ISD special education serves students ages 3-21 with disabilities that impact their educational performance. Our district follows federal IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) requirements and Texas Education Code mandates to ensure every child receives a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment.

With an enrollment of 3,745 students across CALALLEN, we maintain dedicated special education teams in each school. These professionals—special education teachers, speech therapists, occupational therapists, and counselors—collaborate to create individualized support plans.

Who Qualifies for Services

Children in CALALLEN ISD may qualify for special education under 13 disability categories recognized by Texas and federal law:

  • Autism spectrum disorder
  • Emotional disturbance
  • Hearing impairment
  • Intellectual disability
  • Multiple disabilities
  • Orthopedic impairment
  • Other health impairment
  • Speech or language impairment
  • Traumatic brain injury
  • Visual impairment
  • Specific learning disability (including dyslexia)
  • Deaf-blindness
  • Developmental delay (ages 3-9 only)

Simply having a diagnosis does not guarantee special education eligibility. The disability must adversely affect educational performance, and the child must need specially designed instruction. CALALLEN ISD special education specialists evaluate each student individually.

The ARD Meeting Process in CALALLEN ISD

An ARD (Admission, Review, Dismissal) meeting is the cornerstone of CALALLEN ISD special education. This is where you, as a parent, meet with school professionals to discuss your child's needs, design services, and set goals.

What to Expect at a CALALLEN ISD ARD Meeting

The CALALLEN ISD ARD meeting typically includes your child's general education teacher, special education teacher, school counselor, administrator, and assessment specialist. You know your child best—your input shapes every decision made during this meeting.

During the meeting, the team will:

  1. Review assessment results and current performance data
  2. Discuss your child's strengths and areas of need
  3. Establish annual goals aligned with grade-level standards
  4. Determine which special education services your child needs
  5. Decide the appropriate classroom setting (mainstream, resource, self-contained)
  6. Plan accommodations and modifications
  7. Set a timeline for progress monitoring

Parents in CALALLEN ISD have the right to bring advocates, family members, or attorneys to ARD meetings. You can request an interpreter if English isn't your first language. Never feel pressured to sign documents immediately—you can take them home to review.

ARD Meeting Timelines

CALALLEN ISD special education follows these critical timelines:

  • Initial evaluation: Must begin within 30 calendar days of referral
  • Evaluation completion: Must be finished within 60 calendar days
  • First ARD meeting: Must occur within 30 days of evaluation completion
  • Annual ARD meetings: Must occur at least once yearly
  • Reevaluation: Must occur at least every three years, or more often if requested

IEP Services and Individualized Education Plans

Your child's CALALLEN ISD IEP services are detailed in the Individualized Education Program (IEP) document. This legal plan specifies exactly what your child will receive, how progress will be measured, and who will provide services.

CALALLEN ISD offers diverse IEP services to meet individual student needs:

  • Occupational therapy: For fine motor, self-care, and sensory needs
  • Physical therapy: For gross motor and mobility skills
  • Counseling services: For social-emotional and behavioral support
  • Visual impairment services: Specialized instruction in Braille and orientation/mobility

CALALLEN ISD special education teams recommend services based on assessment data and documented needs—not availability or convenience. Your child's IEP will specify frequency (how often) and duration (how long) of each service.

Measuring Progress on IEP Goals

Schools in CALALLEN ISD must measure and report your child's progress toward IEP goals. You'll receive progress reports at least as often as general education students receive report cards—typically every 4-6 weeks.

Ask your special education teacher specific questions: Is my child on track to meet this goal? What can I do at home to support progress? Should we adjust the goal or services? These conversations matter and influence future decisions.
