Understanding MANOR ISD Special Education Services

MANOR ISD special education provides a full continuum of services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §29.001. Our district identifies and serves students with disabilities from birth through age 21, ensuring each child receives a free appropriate public education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment.

The special education program in MANOR ISD includes services for students with:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Specific Learning Disabilities (including dyslexia)
  • Speech and Language Impairments
  • Emotional and Behavioral Disorders
  • Intellectual Disabilities
  • Orthopedic and Physical Disabilities
  • Visual and Hearing Impairments
  • Other Health Impairments
  • Multiple Disabilities

With comprehensive evaluation and placement procedures, MANOR ISD ensures that each student's needs are carefully assessed before services begin. Our experienced special education staff collaborates with parents, teachers, and specialists to create meaningful educational plans.

The ARD Meeting Process in MANOR ISD

The MANOR ISD ARD meeting is the cornerstone of special education planning. ARD stands for "Admission, Review, and Dismissal," and it's where your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP) is developed, reviewed, and modified. Understanding this process empowers you as a parent to actively participate in decisions about your child's education.

What Happens During an ARD Meeting?

During your MANOR ISD ARD meeting, a committee including parents, special education teachers, general education teachers, a campus administrator, and other specialists (as needed) will:

  • Review evaluation results and current performance data
  • Discuss your child's strengths and areas needing support
  • Set measurable annual goals aligned with grade-level standards
  • Determine special education services and placement
  • Address accommodations, assistive technology, and behavioral support needs
  • Plan transition services (for students age 14 and older)

MANOR ISD ARD Meeting Timeline

MANOR ISD follows Texas timelines for ARD meetings:

  1. Initial Evaluation: Completed within 60 days of written consent
  2. Initial IEP Meeting: Held within 30 days of eligibility determination
  3. Annual Review: At least once per school year
  4. Reevaluation: At least every three years, or more frequently if requested

You have the right to request an ARD meeting whenever you have concerns about your child's progress or placement. MANOR ISD will schedule meetings within a reasonable timeframe of your request.

Decoding MANOR ISD IEP Services and Support

MANOR ISD IEP services are customized for each student based on individual needs determined during the ARD process. Your child's IEP is a legally binding document that outlines exactly what services and support your child will receive.

Types of IEP Services Available in MANOR ISD

Special education and related services available through MANOR ISD IEP services include:

  • Direct Special Education Instruction: Delivered by certified special education teachers in resource, self-contained, or inclusive settings
  • Speech-Language Pathology: For students with communication disorders
  • Occupational Therapy: Supporting fine motor skills, sensory processing, and independence
  • Physical Therapy: Addressing gross motor skills and mobility
  • Counseling and Mental Health Services: Supporting emotional and behavioral needs
  • Assistive Technology: Specialized equipment and software to enhance learning
  • Behavior Support Services: Positive Behavior Intervention and Support (PBIS) and functional behavior assessments
  • Transition Services: Job training, independent living skills, and post-secondary planning

How to Advocate for Appropriate Services

During MANOR ISD ARD meetings, you can advocate for services your child needs by bringing documentation of challenges, requesting specific support services, and asking questions about how services will be delivered and monitored. If you believe your child needs services not offered by MANOR ISD, you can request that the ARD committee consider contracting with outside providers or developing a plan to meet those needs.

Know Your Rights: MANOR ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

MANOR ISD parent rights are protected by federal law (IDEA) and Texas Education Code. Understanding these rights ensures you can effectively advocate for your child's education and respond appropriately if disagreements arise with school personnel.

Core MANOR ISD Parent Rights

Parents in MANOR ISD have the right to:

  • Prior Written Notice: Receive written explanation before MANOR ISD proposes or refuses any action regarding evaluation, eligibility, placement, or services
  • Informed Consent: Consent before initial evaluation, initial placement, and reevaluation—this is not automatic
  • Confidentiality: Maintain privacy of all records and information about your child
  • Access to Records: Request and review all records within 10 school days; request amendments if information is inaccurate or misleading
  • Participate in Meetings: Attend and fully participate in all ARD meetings; request interpreters or other accommodations as needed
  • Bring Advocates: Bring a friend, family member, special education advocate, or attorney to meetings
  • Independent Evaluation: Request an independent evaluation at district expense if you disagree with MANOR ISD's evaluation
  • Dispute Resolution: File a complaint with Texas Education Agency (TEA) or request due process hearing if you disagree with decisions

Requesting Evaluations in MANOR ISD

If you suspect your child has a disability, you can request evaluation in writing to your school's special education director. MANOR ISD must evaluate your child within 60 days of receiving your written consent. This is true even if teachers have not flagged concerns—parent referrals carry equal weight to school referrals in MANOR ISD.

MANOR ISD Dyslexia Program and Intervention

Dyslexia is a common specific learning disability affecting reading, spelling, and sometimes writing. The MANOR ISD dyslexia program provides specialized screening, intervention, and support under Texas Education Code §38.003.

Dyslexia Screening in MANOR ISD

MANOR ISD screens all kindergarten and first-grade students for dyslexia risk factors. If your child shows indicators, the school will notify you and may conduct further assessment. Parents can also request dyslexia evaluation at any grade level if they have concerns about their child's reading development.

Dyslexia Intervention Services

Students identified with dyslexia through MANOR ISD's program receive:

  • Evidence-Based Reading Instruction: Explicit, multisensory intervention using programs like Orton-Gillingham or similar approaches
  • Specialized Teacher Training: Instruction from teachers with dyslexia endorsement or specialized training
  • Progress Monitoring: Regular assessment of reading improvement to adjust instruction
  • Related Services: Speech-language pathology if language processing impacts reading

The MANOR ISD dyslexia program is delivered through the special education system when dyslexia qualifies as a Specific Learning Disability, or through general education interventions for students not eligible for special education but demonstrating dyslexia characteristics.
