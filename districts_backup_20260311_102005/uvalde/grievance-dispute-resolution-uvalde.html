Understanding Your Rights in UVALDE CISD Special Education

As a parent in UVALDE CISD, you have protected rights under federal and Texas law to ensure your child receives appropriate special education services. The UVALDE CISD special education department should provide you with procedural safeguards documentation when your child is first identified for special education services. This document explains your rights in detail and is often your first reference point when concerns arise.

The Three-Step Dispute Resolution Process for UVLADE CISD

You can file a formal special education complaint with UVALDE CISD's special education director at any time. This complaint must be filed within one year of the incident and should include specific dates, actions, and how the district violated IDEA or state special education rules.

If you're dissatisfied with UVALDE CISD's response or prefer to bypass the district investigation, you can file a complaint directly with the Texas Education Agency (TEA) Special Education Dispute Resolution Division. This is often called a UVALDE CISD TEA complaint and triggers an independent investigation.

TEA will appoint an investigator who reviews all evidence, interviews relevant parties, and makes findings about whether UVALDE CISD violated special education law.

The TEA complaint process is typically faster than due process hearings and costs nothing to file. Many parents in the UVALDE area find this option appealing because it provides independent review without the expense and time commitment of legal proceedings.
