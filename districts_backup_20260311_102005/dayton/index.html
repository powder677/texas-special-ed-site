Understanding Special Education in DAYTON ISD

Special education in DAYTON ISD is designed to provide free appropriate public education (FAPE) to students with disabilities ages 3-22. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code, DAYTON ISD is required to identify, evaluate, and serve eligible students.

DAYTON ISD special education services include:

  • Academic instruction tailored to individual learning needs
  • Speech and language therapy
  • Occupational and physical therapy
  • Counseling and behavioral support services
  • Assistive technology and adaptive equipment
  • Specialized transportation when needed
  • Transition planning for students ages 14 and older

All DAYTON ISD special education services are documented in a legally binding Individualized Education Program (IEP) that is reviewed and updated annually.

The Referral and Evaluation Process

If you believe your child may need special education services in DAYTON ISD, the first step is requesting an evaluation. Parents, teachers, or other school staff can initiate this process.

How to Request an Evaluation in DAYTON ISD

Submit a written request to your child's campus principal or the DAYTON ISD special education department. Your request must be in writing to start the formal timeline. You can also request an evaluation during a parent-teacher conference or through email to the school's special education coordinator.

Once DAYTON ISD receives your request, the district has 15 calendar days to:

  • Acknowledge receipt of your request
  • Explain the evaluation process
  • Obtain your written consent for testing
  • Provide information about your parent rights

The Evaluation Timeline

After you sign the evaluation consent form, DAYTON ISD has 60 calendar days to complete all testing and assessments. During this time, your child may be evaluated in multiple areas including cognitive ability, academic achievement, social-emotional functioning, and adaptive behaviors.

The evaluation team—which may include school psychologists, special education teachers, speech therapists, and other specialists employed by DAYTON ISD—will conduct assessments in all suspected areas of disability.

DAYTON ISD IEP Services and the ARD Meeting Process

DAYTON ISD IEP services are determined during an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. This is where parents, teachers, and school administrators collaborate to design your child's special education program.

What Happens During a DAYTON ISD ARD Meeting

Your DAYTON ISD ARD meeting is your opportunity to discuss your child's evaluation results, educational needs, and appropriate services. The team will review:

  • Whether your child qualifies for special education under IDEA and Texas law
  • Your child's strengths, interests, and areas of need
  • Specific special education services and supports your child will receive
  • Placement in the least restrictive environment
  • Measurable annual goals and short-term objectives
  • Progress monitoring methods and reporting timelines

DAYTON ISD must schedule the ARD meeting within 30 calendar days of completing the evaluation. You'll receive written notice at least 10 days in advance with the meeting date, time, location, and attendees.

Your Rights During DAYTON ISD ARD Meetings

As a parent participating in a DAYTON ISD ARD meeting, you have the right to:

  • Bring an advocate or attorney to represent your interests
  • Request an interpreter if English is not your primary language
  • Request a meeting at a convenient time and location
  • Review all evaluation and assessment data before the meeting
  • Ask questions and request clarification about services
  • Request independent educational evaluations if you disagree with DAYTON ISD's assessment
  • Accept or reject the proposed IEP

DAYTON ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

Texas law and federal IDEA regulations provide specific protections for families navigating special education in DAYTON ISD. Understanding your DAYTON ISD parent rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Key Parent Rights in DAYTON ISD Special Education

Right to Notice and Participation: DAYTON ISD must provide written notice before taking any special education action. You must be invited to all meetings where decisions about your child are made, and your input must be considered.

Right to Due Process: If you disagree with DAYTON ISD's decisions about identification, evaluation, placement, or services, you have the right to file a due process complaint. You can request mediation or a hearing before an impartial hearing officer.

Right to Independent Evaluation: If you believe DAYTON ISD's evaluation is incomplete or inaccurate, you may request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at DAYTON ISD's expense. The district must either agree or file for a due process hearing to defend their evaluation.

Right to Records Access: You can review your child's special education records, obtain copies, and request amendments to any information you believe is inaccurate or misleading.

Right to Confidentiality: DAYTON ISD must maintain strict confidentiality of all special education records and information. Personally identifiable information is only shared with authorized personnel and with your consent.

DAYTON ISD Dyslexia Program and Reading Interventions

Dyslexia and reading difficulties are among the most common reasons students receive special education referrals. DAYTON ISD dyslexia program services are available for students identified with dyslexia, dysgraphia, dyscalculia, or other reading-based learning disabilities.

Dyslexia Screening in DAYTON ISD

DAYTON ISD screens all kindergarten and first-grade students for dyslexia risk factors using research-based assessment tools. If your child shows characteristics of dyslexia, DAYTON ISD dyslexia program staff will conduct a comprehensive evaluation.

Characteristics of dyslexia include:

  • Difficulty with phonemic awareness and phonics
  • Slow reading fluency or accuracy
  • Spelling and written expression difficulties
  • Trouble with sight word recognition
  • Challenges with rapid naming of letters or numbers

DAYTON ISD Dyslexia Intervention Services

Students identified with dyslexia in DAYTON ISD receive instruction in a specialized, structured literacy program. These evidence-based programs include systematic, explicit instruction in phonemics, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension.

DAYTON ISD dyslexia program services may be delivered through:

  • Small group instruction with a dyslexia-trained specialist
  • One-on-one tutoring sessions
  • Classroom accommodations and modifications
  • Assistive technology tools and text-to-speech software
  • Extended time on reading-based assignments and assessments
