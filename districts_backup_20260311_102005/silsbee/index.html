The SILSBEE ISD ARD Meeting & IEP Process

The SILSBEE ISD ARD meeting is where your child's special education program is planned, developed, and reviewed. ARD stands for "Admission, Review, and Dismissal," and these meetings are required at least annually under Texas Education Code §89.1011.

What to Expect at Your SILSBEE ISD ARD Meeting

Your SILSBEE ISD ARD meeting brings together school staff, parents, and specialists to discuss your child's progress and plan services. Expect the meeting to cover:

  1. Evaluation Results: Assessment data showing your child's current level of performance
  2. Present Levels of Performance (PLOP): A detailed description of how your child functions academically, socially, and behaviorally
  3. Annual Goals: Measurable objectives your child will work toward during the school year
  4. SILSBEE ISD IEP services: Specific supports, accommodations, and modifications your child receives
  5. Placement Decisions: Where and how your child receives instruction
  6. Progress Monitoring: How the school will track your child's achievement of goals

The Individualized Education Program (IEP) document created at your SILSBEE ISD ARD meeting is legally binding and guides all special education decisions.

Timeline for SILSBEE ISD ARD Meetings

Knowing the timeline helps you prepare and plan. Here's what to expect:

  • Initial Referral to Evaluation: Within 15 days of written referral, SILSBEE ISD must provide written notice and obtain consent
  • Evaluation Completion: Within 60 calendar days from consent, SILSBEE ISD must complete comprehensive evaluation
  • Initial ARD Meeting: Within 30 days of completed evaluation, SILSBEE ISD must hold ARD meeting

SILSBEE ISD Dyslexia Program & Literacy Support

The SILSBEE ISD dyslexia program provides targeted intervention for students with dyslexia and other reading disabilities. Texas Education Code §38.003 requires all school districts to identify and serve students with dyslexia.

Dyslexia Screening in SILSBEE ISD

SILSBEE ISD screens all kindergarten and first-grade students for dyslexia using research-based tools. If your child shows indicators of dyslexia, the school will notify you and may recommend further evaluation.

Indicators of dyslexia include difficulty with phonemic awareness, decoding, fluency, spelling, and reading comprehension—even when instruction is strong and the child is intelligent.

The SILSBEE ISD Dyslexia Program Intervention Plan

  • Small group or individual instruction at least 30 minutes daily
  • Progress monitoring every 2-4 weeks
  • Accommodations such as extended time on tests, text-to-speech technology, and modified assignments
  • Regular communication with parents about progress
