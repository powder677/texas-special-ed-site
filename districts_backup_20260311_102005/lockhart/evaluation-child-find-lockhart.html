Understanding Your Rights: Child Find in LOCKHART ISD

LOCKHART ISD is legally required to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities who may need special education services. This responsibility is called "child find," and it applies to all students in LOCKHART ISD, regardless of how well they're performing academically.

As a parent of a LOCKHART ISD student, you have the right to refer your child for evaluation at any time. You don't need a teacher's recommendation, a formal diagnosis, or permission from school administrators to request testing. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code, LOCKHART ISD must respond to your evaluation request within a specific timeframe.

Many parents in LOCKHART ISD wait too long, hoping their child will "catch up" or "outgrow" concerns. However, early identification is critical. The sooner your child receives a LOCKHART ISD special education evaluation, the sooner they can access support services if they qualify.

Step 1: Submit Your Written Request

The first step in the LOCKHART ISD evaluation process is submitting a written request for evaluation. While LOCKHART ISD may accept verbal requests, sending a written request creates documentation and starts the official timeline.

Your written request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Your contact information (phone and email)
  • A description of your concerns about your child's learning, behavior, or development
  • Specific examples of difficulties you've observed at home or school
  • A clear request for a Full Individual Evaluation

Send this request to your child's school principal or the LOCKHART ISD special education department. Consider sending it via email or certified mail so you have proof of delivery. Keep a copy for your records.

Step 2: Understand the LOCKHART ISD 45-Day Timeline

Once LOCKHART ISD receives your written evaluation request, the district has 45 calendar days to complete the Full Individual Evaluation and provide you with results. This LOCKHART ISD 45 day timeline is a critical deadline established under Texas law.

Important timeline details:

  • The 45 days begins when LOCKHART ISD officially receives your request
  • This timeline applies to the completion of testing, not just the start
  • LOCKHART ISD must provide you with an evaluation report by day 45
  • You should receive written notice before any evaluation begins

If LOCKHART ISD cannot complete the evaluation within 45 days, they must document why and provide you with a revised completion date. Extensions are rare and require legitimate reasons, such as incomplete medical records or absences.

Step 3: Receive Prior Written Notice

Before LOCKHART ISD begins any evaluation, you must receive Prior Written Notice describing the evaluation plan. This notice is a legal requirement under IDEA and Texas Education Code.

The Prior Written Notice must explain:

  • What evaluation LOCKHART ISD proposes to conduct
  • Why the evaluation is being recommended
  • What assessment instruments will be used
  • Your parental consent requirements
  • Your right to request independent evaluations

Review this notice carefully. If you have questions about any proposed assessment, ask LOCKHART ISD special education staff to clarify before consenting.

Step 4: Provide Informed Consent

You must provide written informed consent before LOCKHART ISD begins evaluating your child. Informed consent means you understand what will be tested and why. You can consent to the full evaluation or request to limit certain assessments.

LOCKHART ISD cannot proceed with a Full Individual Evaluation without your written permission. You have the right to ask questions and request additional information before signing consent forms. Don't feel rushed—take time to review everything LOCKHART ISD provides.

What Does a LOCKHART ISD Special Education Evaluation Include?

A comprehensive LOCKHART ISD special education evaluation assesses multiple areas of your child's functioning. The evaluation is designed to determine whether your child has a disability and whether they need special education services.

Common Assessment Areas

LOCKHART ISD evaluations typically include assessments in these areas:

  • Cognitive ability: Intelligence and reasoning skills
  • Academic achievement: Reading, math, writing, and other academic subjects
  • Speech and language: Communication and language processing abilities
  • Social-emotional functioning: Behavior, emotional regulation, and social skills
  • Motor skills: Gross motor (large movement) and fine motor (small movement) development
  • Adaptive functioning: Self-care and daily living skills
  • Vision and hearing screening: Basic sensory screening

LOCKHART ISD may use standardized tests, classroom observations, teacher and parent interviews, and review of school records. The evaluation team will determine which specific assessments your child needs.

Who Participates in LOCKHART ISD Evaluations?

A team of professionals conducts the LOCKHART ISD evaluation:

  • Special education teacher or diagnostician
  • School psychologist (for cognitive and behavioral assessments)
  • Speech-language pathologist (if communication is a concern)
  • Occupational or physical therapist (if motor skills are a concern)
  • General education teacher
  • You, as the parent

You are a vital member of the evaluation team. Share your observations and concerns about how your child functions at home and in different environments.

Understanding LOCKHART ISD Evaluation Results

After completing the assessment, LOCKHART ISD must schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss results. This meeting must occur within the LOCKHART ISD 45 day timeline.

The ARD Meeting Explained

At the ARD meeting, the LOCKHART ISD evaluation team presents findings and determines whether your child qualifies for special education services. The team will discuss:

  • Test results and what they mean
  • Whether your child meets criteria for a disability category
  • Your child's strengths and areas of need
  • Eligibility for special education services
  • If eligible, what services and support your child will receive

Bring notes to the ARD meeting. Ask questions about anything you don't understand. LOCKHART ISD must provide clear explanations of results in language you comprehend.

Possible Outcomes from LOCKHART ISD Evaluation

After the evaluation, LOCKHART ISD will determine one of three outcomes:

1. Eligible for Special Education
Your child qualifies for special education services under one of 13 disability categories. LOCKHART ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) outlining services and accommodations.

2. Ineligible, But Needs Support
Your child doesn't qualify for special education but may need accommodations under Section 504 Plan or Response to Intervention (RTI) services.

3. Ineligible
Your child doesn't have a qualifying disability. However, you can request another evaluation in the future if concerns persist.

Your Rights During the LOCKHART ISD Evaluation Process

As a parent in LOCKHART ISD, you have important protections and rights:

  • Right to request evaluation: You can initiate a LOCKHART ISD special education evaluation request at any time
  • Right to written notice: LOCKHART ISD must provide Prior Written Notice before evaluation begins
  • Right to consent: LOCKHART ISD cannot evaluate without your informed written consent
  • Right to copies: You must receive a copy of the evaluation report and all assessment results
  • Right to participate: You are a full member of the evaluation and ARD team
  • Right to independent evaluation: If you disagree with LOCKHART ISD results, you can request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at district expense
  • Right to due process: You can file a due process complaint if you believe LOCKHART ISD violated your child's rights

Tips for LOCKHART ISD Parents During Evaluation

Requesting a LOCKHART ISD FIE evaluation can feel overwhelming. These practical tips help you navigate the process:

Before the Evaluation

  • Gather records showing your concerns (report cards, notes from teachers, work samples)
  • Document specific examples of difficulties at home and school
  • List questions you want answered during the ARD meeting
  • Request a parent-friendly copy of special education evaluation procedures

During the Evaluation

  • Stay involved and respond promptly to LOCKHART ISD requests for information
  • Share observations about how your child functions in different settings
  • Ask the evaluation team to clarify anything you don't understand
  • Request copies of all assessment results before the ARD meeting

At the ARD Meeting

  • Bring a notebook and take detailed notes on all discussions
  • Ask for explanations of technical terms and jargon
  • Don't feel pressured to sign IEP documents at the meeting—ask to take them home and review
  • Request a copy of everything LOCKHART ISD provides

Common Questions About LOCKHART ISD Evaluation Request

How long does a LOCKHART ISD FIE evaluation take?

LOCKHART ISD must complete the evaluation within 45 calendar days of receiving your written request. Testing itself may occur over 2-4 weeks, with the remaining time used for scoring and report writing.

What if LOCKHART ISD denies my evaluation request?

LOCKHART ISD cannot deny your request for evaluation. If the district refuses, contact the Texas Education Agency (TEA) or consult a special education attorney near LOCKHART.

Can I request specific evaluations?

Yes. You can request that LOCKHART ISD evaluate specific areas of concern. LOCKHART ISD will consider your input and may adjust the evaluation plan accordingly.

What if I disagree with LOCKHART ISD evaluation results?

You can request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at LOCKHART ISD's expense. An IEE provides an objective second opinion on your child's needs.

Moving Forward After LOCKHART ISD Evaluation

After receiving LOCKHART ISD evaluation results, whether your child qualifies for special education or not, you have clear next steps. If your child is eligible, work with LOCKHART ISD to develop a comprehensive Individualized Education Program. If ineligible, discuss other support options available through LOCKHART ISD.
