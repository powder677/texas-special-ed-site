Understanding ARD Meetings in BIG SPRING ISD

An ARD meeting is a formal conference where BIG SPRING ISD special education professionals, parents, and school staff determine whether your child qualifies for special education services and what support they'll receive. The ARD committee makes critical decisions about your child's educational future during these structured meetings.

Under Texas Education Code §414.201, every student receiving special education in BIG SPRING ISD must have an ARD meeting at least annually. These meetings ensure your child's individualized education program (IEP) remains appropriate and effective throughout their school year.

For parents in BIG SPRING ISD, the ARD process represents your opportunity to partner with educators in planning specialized instruction tailored to your child's unique learning needs.

What ARD Stands For

  • Admission: Determining if your child qualifies for special education services
  • Review: Examining current IEP goals and services effectiveness
  • Dismissal: Deciding whether your child should continue receiving special education or exit services

The BIG SPRING ISD IEP Process: Step-by-Step Timeline

Understanding the complete BIG SPRING ISD IEP process helps parents know what to expect at each stage and ensures their child receives timely services.

Step 1: Referral for Evaluation (Days 1-5)

The process begins when someone—a parent, teacher, or BIG SPRING ISD staff member—requests a special education evaluation. BIG SPRING ISD must acknowledge your referral in writing and explain the evaluation process. Parents in BIG SPRING ISD have the right to request evaluation at any time, even if the school hasn't suggested it.

Send your referral request via email to the Special Education Department at BIG SPRING ISD to create a written record. Include specific concerns about your child's learning, behavior, or development.

Step 2: Written Notice and Parent Consent (Days 6-15)

Within 15 calendar days of your referral, BIG SPRING ISD must provide written notice in your home language explaining the proposed evaluation, the purpose, and your parental rights. You must sign consent forms before the district can conduct any assessments.

This notice period gives parents time to understand the evaluation scope and ask questions before assessment begins. Review the notice carefully and ask BIG SPRING ISD staff to clarify any procedures you don't understand.

Step 3: Comprehensive Evaluation (Days 16-60)

BIG SPRING ISD has up to 60 calendar days to complete a comprehensive evaluation of your child. The evaluation must assess all areas where the child has suspected disabilities, including cognitive ability, academic achievement, social-emotional functioning, and physical development.

During this period, you may request progress updates on the evaluation. The special education staff at BIG SPRING ISD may contact you with questions or request observations from home.

Step 4: ARD Meeting and Eligibility Determination (Days 55-70)

When evaluation is complete, BIG SPRING ISD must schedule an ARD meeting within 60 days of evaluation consent. At this crucial meeting, the committee determines whether your child qualifies for special education under one of thirteen eligible disability categories under IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act).

In the BIG SPRING area, eligible categories include autism, emotional disturbance, hearing impairment, intellectual disability, multiple disabilities, orthopedic impairment, other health impairment, specific learning disability, speech-language impairment, traumatic brain injury, visual impairment, and more.

Understanding BIG SPRING ISD ARD Rights and Procedural Safeguards

Texas and federal law provide parents with specific BIG SPRING ISD ARD rights designed to ensure fair and appropriate special education decisions. Understanding these protections empowers you to advocate effectively.

  • Right to attend and participate: You must be invited to every ARD meeting, and your input must be considered in all decisions
  • Right to bring support: You may bring a lawyer, advocate, or knowledgeable family member to your BIG SPRING ISD ARD meeting
  • Right to independent evaluation: If you disagree with BIG SPRING ISD evaluations, you can obtain independent assessments at district expense under certain conditions
  • Right to written notice: BIG SPRING ISD must provide written documentation of all ARD decisions and your procedural rights
  • Right to interpreter services: If you need an interpreter, BIG SPRING ISD must provide one at no cost
  • Right to private records access: You may request and review all education records BIG SPRING ISD maintains about your child

BIG SPRING ISD must provide prior written notice before proposing or refusing to change your child's identification, evaluation, IEP, or placement. This notice must include the reason for the proposal or refusal, the evaluation procedures used, and an explanation in understandable language.

You have the right to disagree with ARD committee decisions. If you don't agree with decisions made at your BIG SPRING ISD ARD meeting, request documentation of your disagreement in writing.

Developing Meaningful IEP Goals at Your BIG SPRING ISD ARD Meeting

The IEP itself—the document created during ARD meetings—is a legally binding contract describing special education services your child receives. Effective BIG SPRING ISD IEP goals are specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound (SMART goals).

Characteristics of Strong BIG SPRING ISD IEP Goals

Measurable goals specify exactly what your child will accomplish. Rather than "improve reading skills," a strong goal states: "Student will read grade-level text at 90 words per minute with 95% accuracy by May 2025."

Each BIG SPRING ISD IEP goals should address priority academic and functional needs identified during evaluation. Goals should relate to accessing the general education curriculum and preparing for post-secondary education or employment.

Ask BIG SPRING ISD staff to explain how they'll measure progress on each goal. Progress monitoring should occur regularly—typically monthly or quarterly—with data shared with parents.

Questions to Ask About BIG SPRING ISD IEP Goals

  • How will this goal help my child access general education curriculum?
  • How will progress be measured and monitored?
  • How often will I receive progress reports?
  • What specific services and supports will help my child achieve this goal?
  • Is this goal ambitious yet achievable within one school year?

Special Education Services and Placement Decisions in BIG SPRING ISD

During your BIG SPRING ISD ARD meeting, the committee determines what services your child needs and in what setting they'll receive them. BIG SPRING ISD must provide services in the "least restrictive environment" (LRE)—the setting most similar to general education that appropriately addresses your child's needs.

Service Options Available Through BIG SPRING ISD

  • General education classroom with consultation: Your child remains in typical classes while special education staff collaborates with the teacher
  • General education with supplementary aids and services: Your child attends general classes with accommodations, assistive technology, or specialized materials
  • Resource room (pull-out services): Your child spends part of the day in specialized instruction for targeted skill development
  • Self-contained classroom: Your child receives instruction primarily outside general education for intensive, specialized teaching
  • Related services: Speech-language pathology, occupational therapy, counseling, or other specialized support
