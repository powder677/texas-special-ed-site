Understanding What a Full Individual Evaluation Is

A BIG SPRING ISD FIE evaluation is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine whether your child has a disability and qualifies for special education services. This evaluation goes far beyond a simple screening—it's an in-depth examination of your child's academic, behavioral, social, and developmental functioning.

The evaluation includes assessments in all areas of suspected disability, such as speech and language, cognitive abilities, academic performance, emotional and behavioral functioning, and physical/motor skills. In BIG SPRING ISD, these assessments are conducted by qualified professionals including special education teachers, school psychologists, speech-language pathologists, and other specialists.

BIG SPRING special education evaluations are conducted at no cost to families and are protected by federal laws including the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.

Child Find: How BIG SPRING ISD Identifies Students Who May Need Evaluation

BIG SPRING ISD child find responsibilities require the district to actively identify all children who may have disabilities, from birth through age 21. This means the district is legally obligated to locate, evaluate, and identify children who need special education services.

Child find activities in BIG SPRING ISD include:

  • Reviewing classroom performance and teacher concerns
  • Screening all students for potential disabilities
  • Responding to parent concerns and referrals
  • Monitoring students who are struggling academically or behaviorally
  • Publicizing the availability of special education services to parents in the BIG SPRING area

Even if your child hasn't been referred by a teacher, you have every right as a parent to request a BIG SPRING ISD evaluation request directly. The district cannot refuse your request without providing valid reasons in writing.

Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing

While you can initially speak with your child's teacher or school counselor, BIG SPRING ISD special education evaluation requests must be submitted in writing to trigger the official timeline. Send your request to the Special Education Department at BIG SPRING ISD or directly to your campus principal.

Your written request should include:

  • Your child's full name, grade level, and current school
  • Specific concerns about your child's learning, behavior, or development
  • Examples of how these concerns affect school performance
  • Any evaluations done outside school (if applicable)
  • Request for a full individual evaluation

Keep a copy for your records and note the date you submitted it—this is important for tracking the BIG SPRING ISD 45 day timeline.

Step 2: Consent for Evaluation

Within a reasonable timeframe after receiving your written request, BIG SPRING ISD will contact you to schedule a meeting to discuss the evaluation. At this meeting or shortly after, the district will ask for your written consent to conduct the evaluation.

Important: The evaluation cannot begin until you sign the consent form. BIG SPRING ISD must provide you with a copy of your procedural rights in writing before asking for consent. Take time to read these documents carefully—they explain your rights throughout the special education process.

If you have questions or concerns about the proposed evaluation, don't hesitate to ask. You can also request that a special ed advocate BIG SPRING or educational advocate attend this meeting with you.

Understanding the 45-Day Evaluation Timeline

Once you provide written consent, BIG SPRING ISD has a critical deadline: the BIG SPRING ISD 45 day timeline to complete the evaluation and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss results.

Here's how the timeline works:

  1. Day 1: You submit written consent for evaluation
  2. Days 2-40: BIG SPRING ISD completes all evaluation components, including classroom observations, standardized testing, parent interviews, and review of educational records
  3. Day 45 or before: District holds ARD meeting to present findings and determine eligibility for special education services

The 45-day timeline is legally mandated under the Texas Education Code. If BIG SPRING ISD special education evaluation cannot be completed within this window due to extenuating circumstances, the district must notify you in writing and explain the reason for the delay.

Common reasons for delays include incomplete evaluations from outside providers, extended absences, or need for additional specialized testing. However, the district must still work diligently to complete the evaluation as quickly as possible.

What Happens During the Evaluation Process

Types of Assessments Included in a BIG SPRING ISD FIE Evaluation

A comprehensive BIG SPRING ISD FIE evaluation may include:

  • Cognitive Assessment: Measures thinking skills, reasoning, and learning ability
  • Academic Achievement Testing: Evaluates reading, writing, and math skills
  • Speech and Language Evaluation: Assesses communication abilities
  • Behavioral Assessment: Reviews social and emotional functioning
  • Classroom Observation: Specialists observe your child in actual learning environments
  • Parent and Teacher Input: Detailed interviews about your child's functioning at home and school
  • Review of Records: Examination of grades, attendance, discipline history, and previous evaluations
  • Vision and Hearing Screening: Rules out sensory issues affecting learning

Your Role During the Evaluation

As a parent, your input is crucial to the accuracy of your child's BIG SPRING ISD special education evaluation. You'll be asked to participate in:

  • A parent interview discussing your child's developmental history
  • Completion of behavioral rating scales
  • Review of medical and family history
  • Discussion of your child's strengths, interests, and areas of concern

Be honest and thorough in your responses. Share specific examples of challenges your child faces and how they affect daily life at home and school. This information helps professionals in BIG SPRING ISD paint a complete picture of your child's needs.

The ARD Meeting: Understanding Your Child's Evaluation Results

After the BIG SPRING ISD 45 day timeline is completed, the district will invite you to an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. This is where you'll learn the results of your child's BIG SPRING ISD FIE evaluation and determine next steps.

What to Expect at the ARD Meeting

The ARD team at BIG SPRING ISD typically includes:

  • Your child's parents/guardians (you)
  • Special education teacher or coordinator
  • Regular education teacher
  • School administrator
  • Staff members who conducted evaluations
  • Your advocates or representatives (if you bring them)

During the meeting, evaluators will present their findings, explain what the results mean, and answer your questions. If your child qualifies for special education services under IDEA or Section 504, the team will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) or 504 Plan.

If your child does not qualify, BIG SPRING ISD must explain why and discuss alternative supports or interventions available at school.

Your Rights During the ARD Meeting

Remember these important rights when attending your child's ARD meeting for BIG SPRING ISD special education evaluation results:

  • You have the right to bring an advocate, attorney, or support person
  • You can request an interpreter if you speak a language other than English
  • You can ask for copies of all evaluation reports before or during the meeting
  • You can request additional time to review results before making decisions
  • You don't have to agree with the team's conclusions—you can request an independent evaluation
  • You can ask for the ARD meeting to be rescheduled if you need more time to prepare

Next Steps After Your BIG SPRING ISD FIE Evaluation

If Your Child Qualifies for Special Education

If your child is found eligible through the BIG SPRING ISD child find process and comprehensive evaluation, the ARD team will develop an IEP tailored to your child's specific needs. Your child will receive special education services beginning the following school day.

You'll participate in regular ARD meetings (at least annually) to review progress and adjust the IEP as needed. BIG SPRING ISD is required to provide special education services in the "least restrictive environment," meaning your child should spend as much time as possible with non-disabled peers.

If Your Child Does Not Qualify

If BIG SPRING ISD special education evaluation results show your child doesn't qualify for special education but struggles academically or behaviorally, discuss alternative support options:

  • Section 504 Plan for students with disabilities who don't qualify for IDEA
  • Response to Intervention (RTI) programs
  • Counseling or behavioral support services
  • English Language Learner (ELL) support if applicable
  • General education accommodations or modifications

You can also request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at district expense if you disagree with BIG SPRING ISD's findings.
