Understanding GREENWOOD ISD Special Education Services

GREENWOOD ISD special education services are provided under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code Chapter 29. These services ensure that students with disabilities receive a free, appropriate public education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment possible.

Our district serves students with various disability categories including autism, intellectual disabilities, speech/language impairments, learning disabilities, emotional disturbances, other health impairments, orthopedic impairments, visual impairments, and hearing impairments. GREENWOOD ISD special education programs are designed to meet each student's unique needs through individualized planning and evidence-based interventions.

Who Qualifies for Services?

A student may qualify for GREENWOOD ISD special education if they have a disability that adversely affects educational performance and require specialized instruction. Students don't need to wait until they fall significantly behind. Early identification and intervention through GREENWOOD ISD's Response to Intervention (RTI) process can prevent learning gaps from widening.

The ARD Process: Your Child's Special Education Plan

The GREENWOOD ISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) is the foundation of your child's special education program. This meeting brings together parents, teachers, counselors, and specialists to develop your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP).

What to Expect at Your First ARD Meeting

During your initial GREENWOOD ISD ARD meeting, the team will review evaluation results, discuss your child's strengths and needs, and collaboratively develop an IEP. You'll receive documentation of:

  • Your child's present levels of academic and functional performance
  • Specific, measurable annual goals
  • The special education and related services your child will receive
  • How your child will participate in general education
  • Your rights as a parent
  • Timeline for reviewing progress (typically annually)

GREENWOOD ISD families should know that you have the right to bring anyone to the GREENWOOD ISD ARD meeting who knows your child well and can contribute to the discussion—this might include a therapist, advocate, or family member.

Annual Review and IEP Updates

GREENWOOD ISD requires annual ARD meetings to review your child's IEP services and progress toward goals. If your child isn't making expected progress, you can request an interim ARD meeting. Changes in services, placement, or goals require parent notification and agreement in most cases.

GREENWOOD ISD IEP Services and Support Options

GREENWOOD ISD IEP services are individualized based on your child's needs and may include resource room support, self-contained special education classes, speech therapy, occupational therapy, physical therapy, counseling, and behavioral support services.

Service Delivery Models in GREENWOOD ISD

GREENWOOD ISD provides IEP services through various models to ensure students benefit from the least restrictive environment:

  • Inclusion with Support: Your child attends general education classes with special education support and accommodations
  • Resource Room: Students receive specialized instruction in a separate setting for specific subjects, typically for one to three periods daily
  • Self-Contained Classes: For students with more significant support needs, GREENWOOD ISD offers specialized classrooms with intensive instruction
  • Related Services: Speech-language pathology, occupational therapy, physical therapy, and counseling services
  • Behavioral Support: Functional behavioral assessments and positive behavioral intervention plans (PBIS)

Every decision about GREENWOOD ISD IEP services placement should be made by the ARD team with parent input, always considering how your child can access the general curriculum.
