GRANBURY ISD Due Process: Formal Dispute Resolution

When informal resolution and mediation fail, GRANBURY ISD due process hearing offers your final remedy before litigation. This is a formal, legal proceeding where a neutral hearing officer reviews evidence and makes a binding decision.

Situations Requiring Due Process Hearings

Parents may file for GRANBURY ISD due process when:

  • GRANBURY ISD refuses to evaluate your child
  • You disagree with the IEP or evaluation results
  • Your child isn't receiving agreed-upon services
  • You believe GRANBURY ISD is violating IDEA or Section 504
  • The district wants to suspend or expel your child for disability-related behavior
  • You request placement in a specific program and GRANBURY ISD denies it

Important Timelines for GRANBURY ISD Due Process

Statute of Limitations: You have two years from the date GRANBURY ISD violated your child's rights to request a hearing. However, this timeline is tolled if the district misrepresented that it resolved the issue or concealed the violation.

Notice Requirements: You must provide GRANBURY ISD written notice of your intent to request a hearing. This notice should include:

  • Your child's name and address
  • The date of birth
  • The name of the school where your child attends
  • A description of the problem and facts relating to it
  • Your proposed solution

Response Timeline: GRANBURY ISD has 10 days to respond to your due process complaint.

What to Expect in a GRANBURY ISD Due Process Hearing

Due process hearings are serious legal proceedings. You'll present evidence and testimony, GRANBURY ISD will present their case, and a hearing officer will decide who prevails. Hearings often take several days and can extend over weeks or months.

Many parents in GRANBURY ISD choose to hire special education attorneys for due process hearings because the legal and procedural complexity requires expertise. However, you're not required to have an attorney—you can represent yourself or have an advocate accompany you.

How to File a GRANBURY ISD TEA Complaint

Submit a written complaint directly to the Texas Education Agency. The complaint must:

  • Identify your child and GRANBURY ISD
  • Describe the alleged violation in detail
  • Explain how GRANBURY ISD's actions negatively affected your child
  • State what you believe GRANBURY ISD should do to resolve the issue

TEA typically investigates complaints within 15-30 days, making this faster than due process. If GRANBURY ISD is found to have violated regulations, the agency can require corrective action.

Advantages of GRANBURY ISD TEA Complaints
