Understanding KAUFMAN ISD Special Education Services

KAUFMAN ISD special education serves students ages 3-21 who have disabilities or developmental delays that impact their educational performance. The district provides a continuum of services ranging from inclusive classroom support to specialized day programs, depending on each student's individual needs as documented in their Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Special education in KAUFMAN ISD is built on the principle of Least Restrictive Environment (LRE), meaning your child will be educated with non-disabled peers to the maximum extent appropriate. The district's special education team works collaboratively with families to determine the right placement and services for each student.

The KAUFMAN ISD ARD Meeting Process

The KAUFMAN ISD ARD meeting is your primary opportunity to collaborate with school staff about your child's education. ARD stands for "Admission, Review, and Dismissal," and this meeting is where your child's IEP is created, reviewed, and revised annually (or more frequently if needed).

What Happens During Your KAUFMAN ISD ARD Meeting

The ARD committee includes you (the parent), your child's teachers, a special education representative from KAUFMAN ISD, and other professionals who know your child. Together, you'll discuss your child's current performance, strengths, needs, and goals for the coming year.

During the KAUFMAN ISD ARD meeting, the committee will:

  1. Review evaluation results and current performance data
  2. Discuss your child's strengths and areas needing support
  3. Establish annual goals in academic and functional areas
  4. Determine special education services and placement
  5. Address accommodations and modifications needed
  6. Discuss transition planning (for students age 14+)
  7. Plan for any related services (speech, occupational therapy, counseling, etc.)

Your Rights During the ARD Process

As a parent in KAUFMAN ISD, you have significant protections under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §29.001. You have the right to bring advocates, request independent evaluations, ask for changes to the IEP, and request additional meetings at any time during the school year.

If you disagree with KAUFMAN ISD's ARD decisions, you can request mediation or due process, which are free dispute resolution options available to KAUFMAN ISD families. Many parents in the KAUFMAN area find it helpful to bring an advocate or attorney to understand their options.

KAUFMAN ISD IEP Services and Supports

Your child's KAUFMAN ISD IEP services are personalized based on their specific needs. The IEP is a legal document that describes the special education and related services your child will receive to help them access the general education curriculum and make meaningful progress.

Types of Services Available in KAUFMAN ISD

KAUFMAN ISD provides a full array of services to meet diverse student needs:

  • Direct Special Education Services: Instruction by a certified special education teacher in resource, self-contained, or co-taught classrooms
  • Related Services: Speech-language pathology, occupational therapy, physical therapy, school psychology services, counseling, and audiology services
  • Assistive Technology: Devices and software to help students with disabilities access the curriculum
  • Behavior Support Services: Positive behavior interventions and supports (PBIS) tailored to individual student needs
  • Accommodations and Modifications: Changes to how material is presented, how students respond, and testing conditions

Your KAUFMAN ISD IEP services package is determined entirely by your child's needs, not by disability category or program availability. If your child needs a service, KAUFMAN ISD must provide it or contract with another provider.

KAUFMAN ISD Dyslexia Program

The KAUFMAN ISD dyslexia program provides specialized intervention for students with dyslexia and related reading disabilities. Dyslexia is a specific learning disability that affects how the brain processes written language, and it requires explicit, systematic, multisensory instruction.

Dyslexia Identification and Services

KAUFMAN ISD uses research-based screening and assessment to identify students who may have dyslexia. If your child struggles with reading despite adequate instruction, or if dyslexia runs in your family, request a dyslexia evaluation for your child in KAUFMAN ISD.

The KAUFMAN ISD dyslexia program includes:

  • Universal dyslexia screening for grades K-2
  • Comprehensive dyslexia evaluation for students who don't respond to standard instruction
  • Specialized intervention using evidence-based methods (Orton-Gillingham, Structured Literacy, etc.)
  • Progress monitoring to ensure intervention effectiveness
  • IEP goals and services for students who qualify under Specific Learning Disability
  • Dyslexia-informed classroom instruction in general education

Texas Education Code §38.003 requires that students with dyslexia receive evidence-based reading interventions. KAUFMAN ISD families can expect their children to receive scientifically-supported instruction in decoding, fluency, comprehension, and written expression.

Special Education Evaluation and Assessment

The evaluation process is your first step toward understanding your child's needs and accessing KAUFMAN ISD special education services. KAUFMAN ISD must complete a comprehensive, nondiscriminatory evaluation to determine eligibility.

KAUFMAN ISD Evaluation Timeline

Once you request evaluation for your child in KAUFMAN ISD:

  1. Within 5 school days: KAUFMAN ISD must send you written notice of evaluation plan
  2. Within 15 calendar days: You have time to provide consent for evaluation

The evaluation will assess your child's academic achievement, cognitive abilities, social-emotional development, and functional skills in all areas of suspected disability.
