GODLEY ISD ARD Meetings and IEP Development

The GODLEY ISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) is the cornerstone of special education planning. This meeting brings together parents, teachers, administrators, and specialists to develop or review your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP).

GODLEY ISD IEP services are custom-tailored to address your child's unique needs. Your IEP is a legally binding document that outlines your child's goals, services, accommodations, and placement decisions.

What to Expect in Your ARD Meeting

When you attend a GODLEY ISD ARD meeting, you'll participate in a collaborative discussion about:

  • Your child's current academic and functional performance
  • Evaluation results and diagnostic information
  • Present levels of performance and learning goals
  • Services needed (speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, etc.)
  • Placement and environment decisions
  • Transition planning (for students age 14 and older)
  • Progress monitoring and annual review timelines

Your Rights During the IEP Process

Texas law guarantees parents specific rights throughout the GODLEY ISD IEP services process. You have the right to:
