Understanding What a Full Individual Evaluation Is

A Full Individual Evaluation, commonly called an FIE, is a comprehensive assessment process designed to determine whether your child has a disability that affects their ability to learn. For families in GODLEY ISD, this evaluation is the critical first step in accessing special education services and individualized support.

The GODLEY ISD special education evaluation examines multiple areas of your child's functioning, including cognitive abilities, academic achievement, social-emotional development, motor skills, and speech/language abilities. The evaluation team uses multiple assessment methods to gather a complete picture of your child's strengths and needs.

This process is protected under federal law through the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §29.004. Parents in GODLEY ISD have the right to request an evaluation at any time if they suspect their child has a disability.

Who Can Request a GODLEY ISD Child Find Evaluation

Understanding who has the authority to initiate the GODLEY ISD child find process helps you take action quickly. Multiple people can request an evaluation for your child.

Parents and Guardians

You, as a parent or legal guardian, have the absolute right to request an evaluation for your child at any time. You do not need the school's permission to request an assessment. This is a fundamental right under IDEA and Texas special education law.

School Staff Members

Teachers, counselors, nurses, and other school personnel working with your child can also request a GODLEY ISD evaluation. They may notice developmental delays, academic struggles, behavioral concerns, or other indicators that suggest your child needs assessment.

Other Professionals

Outside professionals, including pediatricians, speech therapists, psychologists, and other medical providers, can recommend that GODLEY ISD conduct an evaluation, though the school must determine whether to proceed.

Steps to Request a GODLEY ISD FIE Evaluation

The process for initiating a GODLEY ISD evaluation request is straightforward but requires attention to detail. Following these steps ensures your request is documented and acted upon promptly.

Step 1: Submit Your Request in Writing

Contact your child's school and request an evaluation in writing. While verbal requests can be made, putting your request in writing creates an important paper trail. Address your request to the campus principal or special education department at your child's school.

  • Include your child's full name, grade level, and campus
  • State that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation
  • Briefly explain your concerns (academic struggles, developmental delays, behavioral issues, etc.)
  • Keep a copy for your records
  • Consider sending via email to have a timestamped record

Step 2: Understand the GODLEY ISD 45 Day Timeline

Once you submit your evaluation request to GODLEY ISD, the district has a specific legal timeline to follow. Under Texas Education Code and IDEA regulations, GODLEY ISD has 45 calendar days to complete the Full Individual Evaluation from the date you provide written consent for testing.

This 45-day timeline is critical to understand because it protects your rights. The school cannot indefinitely delay the evaluation process. If GODLEY ISD does not meet this deadline without documented justification, you may have grounds to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

Mark this date on your calendar and follow up with the school if you haven't received results within this window.

Step 3: Review the Evaluation Plan

After you request an evaluation, GODLEY ISD must provide you with an Evaluation Plan that outlines:

  • Specific tests and assessment tools that will be used
  • Areas your child will be evaluated in (academics, cognition, communication, motor skills, etc.)
  • Who will conduct each part of the evaluation
  • Timeline for completing the evaluation
  • Your rights as a parent throughout the process

You have the right to ask questions about the evaluation plan before giving your consent. If you disagree with any part of the proposed evaluation, communicate your concerns to GODLEY ISD in writing.

Step 4: Provide Informed Consent

Before GODLEY ISD can proceed with the Full Individual Evaluation, you must provide written informed consent. This means you understand what will be tested, who will do the testing, and how the results will be used.

You have the right to refuse certain evaluations or request additional ones. Be clear about your expectations and any concerns you have about specific assessment methods, especially if your child has test anxiety or other factors that might affect performance.

Step 5: Participate in the Evaluation Process

As a parent, you play an important role in the GODLEY ISD evaluation. You will likely be asked to complete questionnaires about your child's developmental history, medical background, and behaviors at home.

Provide detailed, honest information about:

  • Early developmental milestones
  • Speech and language development
  • Medical history and medications
  • Behavioral and emotional concerns
  • Family history of learning disabilities or special needs
  • Strengths and talents your child demonstrates

What to Expect During the GODLEY ISD Special Education Evaluation

Understanding what happens during the GODLEY ISD special education evaluation process helps reduce anxiety for both you and your child. Different professionals may conduct different portions of the assessment.

Cognitive and Achievement Testing

A school psychologist will likely administer standardized tests to measure your child's intellectual abilities and academic achievement. These tests help determine if your child has a significant discrepancy between their intelligence and academic performance.

Speech and Language Evaluation

If communication concerns exist, a speech-language pathologist will assess your child's receptive language (understanding), expressive language (speaking), and other communication skills relevant to your child's needs.

Classroom Observations

GODLEY ISD staff will observe your child in classroom settings to understand how they function in educational environments and interact with peers and teachers.

Review of Records

The evaluation team will examine your child's educational records, previous test results, report cards, and teacher feedback to gather comprehensive information about academic progress and behavior patterns.

Understanding the Evaluation Results and Eligibility Determination

After the GODLEY ISD FIE evaluation is complete, the evaluation team meets to review findings and determine if your child qualifies for special education services under IDEA. This determination meeting is critical for your family's next steps.

Eligibility Categories in GODLEY ISD

Your child may qualify for special education if they are identified as having one of these disabilities:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
  • Deaf-Blindness
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Specific Learning Disability (SLD)
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI)
  • Visual Impairment

If Your Child Qualifies

If the evaluation team determines your child qualifies for special education, GODLEY ISD must develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) within 30 days of the eligibility determination. The IEP outlines the specific services, accommodations, and supports your child will receive.

If Your Child Does Not Qualify

Even if your child is not found eligible under IDEA, they may still qualify for a Section 504 Plan if they have a physical or mental impairment that substantially limits a major life activity. Parents in GODLEY ISD can request a 504 evaluation if standard special education eligibility is not met.

Your Rights Throughout the GODLEY ISD Evaluation Process

Understanding your parental rights during the GODLEY ISD special education evaluation process empowers you to advocate effectively for your child. Texas and federal law provide substantial protections.

Right to Prior Written Notice

GODLEY ISD must provide you with Prior Written Notice before taking any action regarding evaluation, eligibility, or special education services. This notice must be in your native language and explain your rights.

Right to an Independent Educational Evaluation

If you disagree with the GODLEY ISD evaluation results, you have the right to request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at public expense. This means you can have an outside professional conduct an evaluation at no cost to your family.

To request an IEE, submit a written request to GODLEY ISD. The district must either agree to fund the independent evaluation or file for due process to defend their evaluation as appropriate.

Right to Bring Support to Meetings

You have the right to bring a friend, family member, advocate, or attorney to any meeting with GODLEY ISD regarding evaluation, eligibility, or special education services. This support person can help you understand information and ask questions.

Right to Request Evaluation in Other Languages

If you speak a language other than English, GODLEY ISD must provide evaluation information and materials in your language, and may need to conduct evaluations in your child's native language to ensure accurate assessment.

Common Challenges Parents Face with GODLEY ISD Evaluations

Understanding common obstacles helps you navigate them effectively when requesting a GODLEY ISD FIE evaluation.

Slow Response Times

Some parents report delays in GODLEY ISD responding to evaluation requests or taking extended periods beyond the 45-day timeline. If this happens, send a follow-up letter referencing the legal deadline and request a specific completion date.

Incomplete Evaluations

The evaluation may not assess all areas of concern you identified. If you believe additional testing is necessary, request that GODLEY ISD add specific evaluations to the plan before consent is given.

Disagreement About Results

You may disagree with the evaluation team's interpretation of results or eligibility determination. Document your concerns in writing and request a copy of the complete evaluation report. Consider requesting an Independent Educational Evaluation if significant concerns exist.

Moving Forward After the GODLEY ISD Evaluation

Once your child's evaluation is complete, your journey with GODLEY ISD special education services continues. Understanding what comes next helps you stay engaged and ensure your child receives appropriate support.

If your child qualifies for special education, work closely with GODLEY ISD to develop a comprehensive IEP that addresses your child's unique needs. Stay involved in annual reviews and maintain open communication with the special education team.

If your child does not qualify, explore other support options including Response to Intervention (RTI) programs, 504 plans, gifted services, or counseling support available through GODLEY ISD.

Remember, advocating for your child's needs is not adversarial—it's your responsibility as a parent. GODLEY ISD families deserve quality special education services, and understanding the evaluation process empowers you to ensure your child receives them.
