DRIPPING SPRINGS ISD IEP Services and Your Child's Education Plan

The Individualized Education Program (IEP) is the legal document that guides your child's special education services. Every student receiving special education in DRIPPING SPRINGS ISD must have an IEP developed collaboratively with parents, teachers, and specialists.

What's Included in Your Child's IEP

Your child's DRIPPING SPRINGS ISD IEP services document includes:

  • Present levels of academic and functional performance
  • Measurable annual goals aligned with grade-level standards
  • Specific special education services and accommodations
  • Frequency and duration of services
  • Progress monitoring and reporting methods
  • Transition plans (for students 14 and older)
  • Behavioral intervention plans if needed

The IEP Development Timeline

Understanding the timeline helps DRIPPING SPRINGS ISD parents stay informed:

  1. Referral: Parent or teacher requests evaluation (immediate documentation begins)
  2. Notice and Consent: District provides notice; parents must consent to evaluation (within 5 school days)
  3. Evaluation: District completes comprehensive assessment (within 60 calendar days)
  4. Eligibility Meeting: DRIPPING SPRINGS ISD determines if child qualifies (within 60 days)
  5. IEP Development: Team creates the program (at eligibility meeting if eligible)
  6. Implementation: Services begin as soon as possible
  7. Annual Review: IEP is reviewed and updated yearly

Parents in DRIPPING SPRINGS ISD have the right to request an IEP meeting at any time if they believe their child's program needs adjustment.

DRIPPING SPRINGS ISD Dyslexia Program and Reading Support

Dyslexia is one of the most common learning disabilities. If you suspect your child has dyslexia, the DRIPPING SPRINGS ISD dyslexia program provides specialized screening and intervention.

Dyslexia Screening in DRIPPING SPRINGS ISD

Texas Education Code Section 38.003 requires schools to screen all students for dyslexia. In DRIPPING SPRINGS ISD, this screening happens:

  • Universally for all kindergarten and first-grade students
  • Upon request from parents
  • When teachers observe reading difficulties
  • During special education evaluation

The DRIPPING SPRINGS ISD Dyslexia Program

If your child is identified with dyslexia, DRIPPING SPRINGS ISD special education services include:

  • Structured literacy instruction using evidence-based methods
  • Multisensory approaches to teach phonics and decoding
  • Small group or individual intervention
  • Progress monitoring every 1-2 weeks
  • IEP accommodation such as extended time, text-to-speech, or audiobooks

Parents in DRIPPING SPRINGS ISD should request evaluation if they notice persistent reading struggles, difficulty with phonics, or slow reading speed.
