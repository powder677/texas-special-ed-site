Understanding LEVELLAND ISD ARD Meetings and Your IEP

An ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting is where special education decisions happen for LEVELLAND ISD students. This is your opportunity to discuss your child's needs, review assessment results, and make important decisions about their education plan. LEVELLAND ISD special education staff facilitate these meetings to ensure parents have a voice in every decision.

What Happens During a LEVELLAND ISD ARD Meeting?

  • Review of Assessment Data: LEVELLAND ISD evaluations and classroom performance are discussed
  • IEP Development: Goals, services, and accommodations are determined for your child
  • Placement Decision: LEVELLAND ISD determines the least restrictive environment for your child's learning
  • Parent Input: Your concerns, observations, and preferences are documented
  • Agreement or Disagreement: Parents sign indicating consent or concerns about the IEP

LEVELLAND ISD IEP Services and Support Options

LEVELLAND ISD special education offers a continuum of services designed to meet each student's unique needs. Your child's IEP may include any combination of these services:

  • Resource Room Services: Pull-out instruction in reading, math, or other academic areas
  • Inclusive Classroom Support: In-class assistance with curriculum modifications
  • Speech and Language Therapy: Services for communication disorders
  • Occupational Therapy: Support for fine motor skills and daily living activities
  • Physical Therapy: Assistance with gross motor development and mobility
  • Counseling Services: Mental health and behavioral support through LEVELLAND ISD
  • Special Education Teacher Consultation: Specialized support for general education teachers

LEVELLAND ISD special education services are individualized based on your child's assessment results and educational needs, not based on disability category alone.

Dyslexia Support Within LEVELLAND ISD

If your child has dyslexia or a reading disability, LEVELLAND ISD is required to provide specialized interventions. Dyslexia is a specific learning disability that affects reading, spelling, and sometimes written expression. Many parents in LEVELLAND ISD discover their child has dyslexia during the evaluation process.

LEVELLAND ISD Dyslexia Program Components

Texas Education Code requires districts to identify and serve students with dyslexia, and LEVELLAND ISD takes this responsibility seriously. Our approach includes:

  • Screening: Universal screening in grades K-2 to identify at-risk readers
  • Evaluation: Comprehensive assessment if dyslexia is suspected
  • Intervention: Structured literacy instruction based on evidence-based practices
  • Progress Monitoring: Regular assessment to ensure interventions are working
  • ARD Planning: Your LEVELLAND ISD ARD meeting will address dyslexia-specific goals and services

If you believe your child may have dyslexia, request an evaluation from LEVELLAND ISD. You have the right to request dyslexia intervention services, and our staff will work with you to develop an appropriate plan.

Understanding Your LEVELLAND ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

LEVELLAND ISD parent rights are fundamental protections under federal and Texas law. As a parent in LEVELLAND ISD, you have specific rights throughout the special education process, and understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Core LEVELLAND ISD Parent Rights

  1. Right to Notice: LEVELLAND ISD must inform you of all proposed actions in writing, including evaluations, ARD meetings, and service changes
  2. Right to Participate: You must be invited to and allowed to participate in all LEVELLAND ISD meetings regarding your child's identification, evaluation, and placement
  3. Right to Consent: LEVELLAND ISD must obtain your written consent before conducting initial evaluations and before initially providing special education services
  4. Right to Review Records: You can access all of your child's educational records maintained by LEVELLAND ISD
  5. Right to Independent Evaluation: If you disagree with LEVELLAND ISD's evaluation, you can request an independent evaluation at no cost to you
  6. Right to Due Process: If you disagree with LEVELLAND ISD special education decisions, you can file a due process complaint
  7. Right to an Interpreter: LEVELLAND ISD will provide an interpreter if you need one to participate in meetings

LEVELLAND ISD provides a document outlining all parent rights in special education at the beginning of each school year. Request a copy if you don't have one, and don't hesitate to ask questions during any ARD meeting.

Getting Started: Referral and Evaluation in LEVELLAND ISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability or needs special education support, LEVELLAND ISD has a specific process for referral and evaluation. Understanding this timeline helps you know what to expect.

The LEVELLAND ISD Referral Process

Step 1: Submit a Referral — Contact your child's school or LEVELLAND ISD special education department to request an evaluation. Referrals can come from parents, teachers, or other school staff.

Step 2: LEVELLAND ISD Review Meeting — Within 10 school days, LEVELLAND ISD staff will meet to discuss concerns and determine if evaluation is warranted.

Step 3: Evaluation Plan — If evaluation is recommended, LEVELLAND ISD will send you an evaluation plan outlining what will be assessed and why. You must provide written consent before evaluation begins.

Step 4: LEVELLAND ISD Evaluation — Testing and assessment occurs over the next 4-8 weeks, depending on the areas being evaluated.

Step 5: Initial ARD Meeting — LEVELLAND ISD will convene an ARD meeting to discuss evaluation results and determine eligibility. You'll receive the evaluation report at least two days before this meeting.
