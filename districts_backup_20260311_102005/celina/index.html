Understanding Special Education in CELINA ISD

CELINA ISD special education services are designed to provide students with disabilities the support they need to access quality education alongside their peers. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §29.001 et seq., CELINA ISD is committed to identifying, evaluating, and serving eligible students with disabilities.

Our district recognizes 13 disability categories under federal law, including autism spectrum disorder, dyslexia, speech/language impairments, learning disabilities, intellectual disabilities, emotional/behavioral disorders, and orthopedic impairments. Whether your child in CELINA ISD requires specialized instruction, related services, or assistive technology, our special education team works collaboratively with families to create individualized plans.

The special education process in CELINA ISD begins with a referral—either from a parent, teacher, or school administrator—and follows a structured timeline to ensure your child receives appropriate evaluation and services within 60 days of written consent.

The ARD Process: Your Child's Right to Special Education

At the heart of CELINA ISD special education is the ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. This is your opportunity to work directly with teachers, administrators, and specialists to develop and review your child's educational plan.

What Happens in a CELINA ISD ARD Meeting

During a CELINA ISD ARD meeting, your team will discuss:

  • Your child's current academic and functional performance
  • Results from comprehensive evaluations and assessments
  • Present levels of performance and areas of need
  • Measurable annual goals and short-term objectives
  • Special education services and classroom placement
  • Related services (speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, etc.)
  • Accommodations, modifications, and assistive technology
  • Participation in state and district assessments

Parents in CELINA ISD have the right to bring advocates, family members, or legal representatives to any CELINA ISD ARD meeting. You are an equal partner in this process, and your input about your child's strengths, needs, and goals is invaluable.

ARD Meeting Timeline and Frequency

CELINA ISD conducts ARD meetings at least annually to review your child's progress toward annual goals. However, parents can request an additional CELINA ISD ARD meeting at any time if concerns arise or if your child's needs change. The district must schedule meetings at mutually convenient times and provide materials in your home language when requested.

IEP Services: Creating Your Child's Individualized Education Plan

The CELINA ISD IEP (Individualized Education Program) is a legally binding document that outlines all special education services and supports your child will receive. This is not a general curriculum—it's a customized plan designed specifically for your child's unique learning profile.

Components of CELINA ISD IEP Services

CELINA ISD IEP services typically include:

  1. Special Education Instruction: Pull-out or co-taught classes with modified curriculum and differentiated instruction
  2. Related Services: Speech-language pathology, occupational therapy, physical therapy, counseling, and audiological services
  3. Accommodations: Changes to how your child accesses instruction (extended time, smaller group setting, preferential seating)
  4. Modifications: Changes to what your child is expected to learn (alternative grade-level standards, reduced assignments)
  5. Assistive Technology: Tools or devices that help your child access curriculum and communicate
  6. Transition Services: For students age 16 and older, planning for post-secondary education, employment, and independent living

Every CELINA ISD IEP must include measurable annual goals with progress monitoring data reviewed at least quarterly. Parents receive progress reports alongside report cards, giving you clear visibility into how your child is progressing toward their IEP goals.

CELINA ISD Dyslexia Program and Language-Based Learning Disabilities

CELINA ISD recognizes dyslexia and related language-based learning disabilities as significant barriers to literacy development. Under Texas Education Code §38.003, all CELINA ISD students are screened for dyslexia risk factors in kindergarten and first grade. If your child shows indicators of dyslexia, CELINA ISD will:

  • Conduct a comprehensive dyslexia evaluation by a trained specialist
  • Provide feedback about assessment results and recommendations
  • Develop an intervention plan or special education CELINA ISD IEP as appropriate

The CELINA ISD dyslexia program emphasizes structured literacy instruction with proven techniques including explicit phonics, multisensory methods, and systematic decoding practice. Accessing Special Education Services: From Referral Through Implementation

Understanding the timeline for accessing CELINA ISD special education services helps you plan and anticipate next steps in your child's educational journey.

Timeline for CELINA ISD Special Education Evaluation

  1. Referral: Parent, teacher, or administrator submits referral to CELINA ISD special education department
  2. Notice of Evaluation: CELINA ISD sends written notice of intent to evaluate (must include CELINA ISD parent rights)
  3. Consent: You sign written consent form within reasonable time
  4. Evaluation: CELINA ISD conducts comprehensive evaluation within 60 calendar days
  5. ARD Meeting: CELINA ISD convenes ARD meeting to review results and determine eligibility
  6. IEP Implementation: If eligible, CELINA ISD IEP services begin within 10 school days of ARD meeting

This 60-day timeline is critical. If CELINA ISD cannot complete evaluation within this window, they must document why and adjust timelines accordingly. You have the right to request a written explanation if timelines are extended.
