Understanding Your Rights in ROBINSON ISD Special Education

Parents of students receiving special education services in ROBINSON ISD have significant rights under federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas Education Code. These rights protect your ability to participate in decisions about your child's education and to challenge decisions you believe are inappropriate.

Key rights for ROBINSON ISD parents include:

  • The right to participate meaningfully in all IEP meetings and special education decisions
  • The right to obtain independent educational evaluations at district expense under certain conditions
  • The right to access all of your child's educational records
  • The right to prior written notice before the district makes changes to your child's program
  • The right to dispute decisions through mediation or due process hearings
  • The right to request compensatory education services if the district fails to provide appropriate services
  • The right to have an advocate or attorney present at meetings

Understanding these foundational rights empowers you to address concerns promptly and effectively within ROBINSON ISD.
