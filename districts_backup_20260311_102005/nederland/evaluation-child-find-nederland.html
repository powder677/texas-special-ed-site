Understanding What a Full Individual Evaluation Is

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment of your child's educational, developmental, and behavioral needs. In NEDERLAND ISD, this evaluation determines whether your child qualifies for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA).

The evaluation goes beyond a simple classroom assessment. It includes cognitive testing, academic achievement measures, behavioral observations, and input from parents and teachers. For NEDERLAND ISD students, this thorough process helps identify specific disabilities and guides the development of an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Understanding the difference between a screening and a full evaluation is crucial. A screening might identify concerns, but only a complete NEDERLAND ISD special education evaluation can determine eligibility for services.

Child Find Requirements in NEDERLAND ISD

Texas law and federal IDEA regulations require NEDERLAND ISD to actively identify children who may need special education services. This process is called child find, and it's a fundamental responsibility of every school district.

What NEDERLAND ISD Child Find Means for Your Family

The NEDERLAND ISD child find obligation means the district must:

  • Actively search for children who might have disabilities
  • Screen all students for potential learning or developmental concerns
  • Respond to parent concerns and referrals promptly
  • Ensure students are not discriminated against in evaluation processes
  • Evaluate children from birth to age 21

If you believe your child in NEDERLAND ISD is struggling and might need special education services, you have the right to request an evaluation at any time. The district cannot refuse an evaluation request without valid reasons.

The process of requesting a NEDERLAND ISD evaluation request is straightforward, but documentation matters. Here's exactly what you need to do:

Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing

Contact your child's school and request a meeting with the school counselor, special education coordinator, or your child's teacher. While verbal requests count, it's critical to follow up with written documentation. Send an email or letter to the special education department at NEDERLAND ISD stating your concern and formally requesting an evaluation.

Your written request should include:

  • Your child's full name and grade level
  • Specific concerns about learning, behavior, or development
  • Examples of how these concerns affect your child at home or school
  • A clear statement: "I am requesting a Full Individual Evaluation"
  • Your contact information

Step 2: Know Who to Contact

In NEDERLAND ISD, you can submit your evaluation request to:

  • Your child's campus principal
  • The NEDERLAND ISD special education department
  • Your child's classroom teacher
  • The district's special education coordinator

Keep copies of everything you submit. This documentation protects your rights throughout the process.

Step 3: Request a Meeting

After submitting your written request for a NEDERLAND ISD evaluation request, the district must contact you within a reasonable timeframe to schedule a meeting. This meeting gives NEDERLAND ISD the opportunity to discuss your concerns and explain the evaluation process.

What to Expect at the ARD Meeting

At the end of the NEDERLAND ISD 45 day timeline, NEDERLAND ISD convenes an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to present evaluation findings. This is a critical moment for your family.

During the ARD meeting, the team discusses whether your child meets eligibility criteria for special education in one or more disability categories recognized by IDEA. These categories include:

  • Specific Learning Disability
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Hearing Impairment or Deafness
  • Visual Impairment
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Deaf-Blindness

If NEDERLAND ISD determines your child qualifies, they develop an IEP. If they determine your child doesn't qualify, they must explain why and discuss other support options.
