Understanding ARD Meetings in NEDERLAND ISD

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal. It's the formal meeting process that NEDERLAND ISD uses to discuss your child's special education needs, create an Individualized Education Program (IEP), and make decisions about their educational placement. For parents in NEDERLAND ISD families, the ARD meeting is where you work together with school professionals to support your child's success.

Under Texas Education Code, every child with a disability in NEDERLAND ISD special education must have an ARD committee. This committee includes you as a parent, special education teachers, regular education teachers, a school administrator, and other specialists as needed. Your involvement is not optional—it's required by state and federal law.

The NEDERLAND ISD ARD meeting happens at least once every school year. However, additional meetings can be called if your child's needs change, if you request one, or if concerns arise about your child's progress.

The NEDERLAND ISD IEP Process: Step-by-Step

Step 2: Evaluation and Assessment

Once a referral is made, NEDERLAND ISD will conduct a comprehensive evaluation. This isn't just one test—it includes multiple assessments of your child's academic performance, social-emotional development, physical abilities, and other relevant areas. The evaluation team at NEDERLAND ISD will use various tools and observations to get a complete picture of your child's needs.

During this meeting, the ARD committee reviews all evaluation results and decides whether your child qualifies for special education services.

If your child qualifies, the committee develops an IEP. If your child doesn't qualify, NEDERLAND ISD will explain why and discuss alternative support options through general education.

What Happens During a NEDERLAND ISD ARD Meeting

Key Components of Your IEP

During a NEDERLAND ISD ARD meeting, your team creates or reviews your child's IEP, which is a legal document. The IEP includes:

  • Present Levels of Academic and Functional Performance (PLAAFP) – How your child is currently performing in school and life skills
  • NEDERLAND ISD IEP goals – Specific, measurable objectives your child will work toward over the school year
  • Special Education Services – What specialized instruction or services your child will receive
  • Related Services – Speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, or other support services
  • Accommodations and Modifications – Changes to how your child learns (like extended time on tests)
  • Placement Decision – Where your child will receive services (general education classroom, resource room, or specialized setting)
  • Transition Planning – For students age 16+, planning for post-secondary education and employment

Your Role as a Parent in NEDERLAND ISD ARD Meetings

You are an equal member of the ARD committee. Your input about your child's strengths, needs, and family priorities is essential. In NEDERLAND ISD, parents should:

  • Ask questions about any part of the NEDERLAND ISD ARD process you don't understand
  • Discuss your concerns about your child's education
  • Contribute ideas for NEDERLAND ISD IEP goals
