In HIGHLAND PARK ISD, this evaluation is the gateway to accessing specialized support services, accommodations, and an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Parents in HIGHLAND PARK ISD should understand that you don't need to wait for the district to identify potential concerns. HIGHLAND PARK ISD teachers, counselors, and administrators are also trained to recognize signs that a student may need evaluation. Common indicators include:

Step 1: Submit Your Written Request

Begin your HIGHLAND PARK ISD FIE evaluation request by putting your request in writing. While a verbal request initiates the process, submit a formal written request to document the date and ensure compliance with the HIGHLAND PARK ISD 45 day timeline.

Your request should be sent to:

  • Your child's school principal
  • The HIGHLAND PARK ISD special education department
  • The campus counselor or special education coordinator

Keep a copy for your records and request written confirmation of receipt from HIGHLAND PARK ISD. Your written request should clearly state that you're requesting a HIGHLAND PARK ISD special education evaluation and ask for clarification about the timeline and next steps.

Step 2: Understand the HIGHLAND PARK ISD 45 Day Timeline

Once HIGHLAND PARK ISD receives your written evaluation request, the district has 45 calendar days to obtain parental consent, conduct the evaluation, and provide you with a comprehensive evaluation report. This timeline is mandated by Texas Education Code §29.004.

During this period, parents in HIGHLAND PARK ISD should expect:

  1. Days 1-3: HIGHLAND PARK ISD sends home parental consent forms
  2. Days 4-10: You review, sign, and return consent to HIGHLAND PARK ISD
  3. Days 11-40: HIGHLAND PARK ISD conducts various assessments and evaluations
  4. Days 41-45: HIGHLAND PARK ISD compiles reports and schedules an ARD meeting

It's important to note that the HIGHLAND PARK ISD 45 day timeline begins when the district receives your written request, not when you first mention concerns verbally. This is why documentation is crucial for all HIGHLAND PARK ISD families.

Step 3: Provide Informed Consent

HIGHLAND PARK ISD must obtain your informed written consent before conducting any evaluation. You'll receive a Prior Notice of Evaluation that explains:

  • Why HIGHLAND PARK ISD is proposing the evaluation
  • What specific tests and assessments will be used
  • How results will be used in HIGHLAND PARK ISD decision-making
  • Your procedural safeguards and rights as a parent

Review this document carefully. You have the right to ask questions and request clarification before signing. Don't hesitate to contact HIGHLAND PARK ISD special education staff if anything is unclear.

Step 4: Participate in the HIGHLAND PARK ISD Evaluation

While HIGHLAND PARK ISD conducts formal testing, your input is valuable. Evaluators may ask you about your child's developmental history, behaviors at home, medical conditions, and family background. Be honest and detailed in your responses to HIGHLAND PARK ISD evaluators.

You may also request that HIGHLAND PARK ISD include specific assessments or consider information from outside evaluators, doctors, or therapists. If you've had your child evaluated privately, share those results with HIGHLAND PARK ISD.

What HIGHLAND PARK ISD Will Evaluate

A comprehensive HIGHLAND PARK ISD FIE evaluation covers multiple domains to ensure no area of concern is missed. Here's what parents in HIGHLAND PARK ISD should expect:

Academic Assessment

HIGHLAND PARK ISD evaluators will administer standardized tests measuring reading, writing, and math skills. These assessments help identify specific learning disabilities and compare your child's performance to same-age peers.

Cognitive and Intellectual Assessment

HIGHLAND PARK ISD may administer IQ tests to measure overall intellectual functioning. This helps evaluators understand your child's learning potential and reasoning abilities.

Speech-Language Evaluation

If concerns exist, HIGHLAND PARK ISD's speech-language pathologist will assess expressive and receptive language, articulation, and fluency.

Social-Emotional and Behavioral Assessment

HIGHLAND PARK ISD evaluators will review behavioral observations, rating scales, and interviews to assess emotional functioning and social skills.

Adaptive Behavior Assessment

HIGHLAND PARK ISD may evaluate how well your child functions independently in daily living and social situations.

After the HIGHLAND PARK ISD Evaluation is Complete

The ARD Meeting

Within the HIGHLAND PARK ISD 45 day timeline, the district will schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. This meeting brings together parents, teachers, HIGHLAND PARK ISD evaluators, and administrators to discuss evaluation findings.

At your ARD meeting with HIGHLAND PARK ISD:

  • You'll receive a comprehensive evaluation report
  • The team will discuss whether your child qualifies for special education
  • If eligible, HIGHLAND PARK ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP)
  • You'll discuss placement, services, and accommodations

If Your Child Qualifies

If the HIGHLAND PARK ISD evaluation determines your child has a disability that adversely affects educational performance, your child becomes eligible for special education services. HIGHLAND PARK ISD will develop a detailed IEP outlining specific goals, accommodations, and services.

If Your Child Does Not Qualify

If HIGHLAND PARK ISD evaluators determine your child doesn't meet criteria for special education, you have options. You can request a due process hearing, seek an independent evaluation, or ask HIGHLAND PARK ISD about Section 504 accommodations, which have different eligibility standards.

Common Questions from HIGHLAND PARK ISD Parents

Can I Request an Evaluation Before Kindergarten?

Yes. HIGHLAND PARK ISD has child find obligations for children as young as three years old. If your preschooler shows developmental delays, you can request a HIGHLAND PARK ISD evaluation.

What If HIGHLAND PARK ISD Misses the 45-Day Timeline?

The HIGHLAND PARK ISD 45 day timeline is a legal requirement. If the district exceeds this deadline without your written consent to an extension, you may file a due process complaint and potentially recover compensatory services for your child.

Can I Request Specific Evaluators?

While you can't always request specific evaluators, you can communicate preferences to HIGHLAND PARK ISD. You can also request that HIGHLAND PARK ISD consider assessments from private evaluators you've hired.

What Happens if My Child Has a Medical Diagnosis?

A medical diagnosis doesn't automatically qualify your child for special education within HIGHLAND PARK ISD. The evaluation must show that the condition adversely affects educational performance and that your child needs specially designed instruction.
