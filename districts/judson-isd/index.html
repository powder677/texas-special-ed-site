What can I do right now if Judson ISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Judson ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Judson ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the special education system can feel overwhelming, but you are not alone. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas law, your child has the right to a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) designed to meet their unique needs. Special education encompasses specially designed instruction and related services provided to children with disabilities. This may include academic supports, therapies, counseling, and other services tailored to help your child succeed in school.

Judson ISD, like all school districts in Texas, operates under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA provides guidance, funding, and support to ensure that districts are meeting their obligations to students with disabilities. Additionally, Judson ISD benefits from the resources and expertise of TEA Region 20 Education Service Center, which offers professional development, technical assistance, and other services to educators in the region. This collaborative network is intended to ensure your child receives the best possible support within Judson ISD.

Crucially, as a parent, you have legally enforceable rights throughout the entire special education process. These rights are designed to ensure your voice is heard, your concerns are addressed, and your child receives the services they need. From the initial evaluation to the development and implementation of an Individualized Education Program (IEP), you are an integral member of the team. Remember, you are your child's strongest advocate, and understanding your rights is the first step toward ensuring their success within Judson ISD. Take the time to familiarize yourself with your rights and never hesitate to ask questions.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Judson ISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability that requires special education services, the first step is to formally request an evaluation from Judson ISD. It is crucial to understand that this request must be in writing. A verbal request, while you might discuss your concerns with a teacher or administrator, does not officially start the timeline for evaluation. Once Judson ISD receives your written consent to evaluate your child, the district has 45 school days to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). This evaluation will assess your child's strengths and weaknesses and determine if they meet the criteria for special education services.

Remember, Judson ISD also has a responsibility under Child Find laws to proactively identify students who may need special education services, even if you haven't explicitly requested an evaluation. Schools are required to have systems in place to screen and identify students who may be struggling. However, if you have concerns about your child's progress, don't hesitate to take the initiative. Write a letter to the school principal and special education director requesting a Full Individual Evaluation for your child. Keep a copy of the letter for your records, and send it via certified mail with return receipt requested to ensure proof of delivery.

ARD Meetings in Judson ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal, and it refers to the committee that makes decisions about your child's special education program. The ARD committee is composed of you, as the parent; school administrators; teachers; and other professionals who have knowledge or expertise about your child's needs. Together, this team develops your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP), which outlines their goals, services, and accommodations.

Judson ISD is legally required to provide you with at least five calendar days' written notice before any ARD meeting. This gives you time to prepare and gather any information you want to share. During the meeting, remember that you have the right to request a 10-day recess (or postponement) at any time. This can be helpful if you feel overwhelmed, need more information, or want to consult with an advocate. Additionally, you are not required to sign the IEP on the same day as the ARD meeting. Take the time you need to review the document carefully and ensure that you agree with everything it contains.

Your voice is critical in the ARD process. Never hesitate to ask questions, express your concerns, and advocate for your child's needs. Remember, you are the expert on your child, and your input is invaluable to the team. It is essential to document every step of the ARD process within Judson ISD. Dyslexia Services in Judson ISD

Texas law mandates that Judson ISD screen all students for dyslexia. Dyslexia is a learning disorder that affects reading and spelling skills. Early identification and intervention are crucial for students with dyslexia to succeed. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook sets the standards for screening, identification, and intervention that Judson ISD must follow. This handbook provides detailed guidance on evidence-based practices and interventions for students with dyslexia.

If your child is identified as having dyslexia in Judson ISD, they are entitled to receive structured literacy intervention. This is a specific type of instruction that focuses on phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension. Depending on the severity of your child's dyslexia and their individual needs, they may also qualify for a 504 Plan or an Individualized Education Program (IEP). If your child has not been screened for dyslexia, or if you have concerns about their reading skills, request a screening in writing from Judson ISD.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Judson ISD

Disciplinary actions, particularly suspensions, can have a significant impact on students with disabilities. If your child, who receives special education services, is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year within Judson ISD, it triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This review is a critical process that determines whether your child's misconduct was a manifestation of their disability or a result of the school's failure to appropriately implement their IEP. Suspension beyond this threshold also constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA and necessitates an ARD meeting to review the situation and make necessary adjustments to the IEP.

This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a special education student. You must act quickly to protect your child's rights. Request the MDR immediately and ensure that the ARD committee meets to discuss the suspension and its relationship to your child's disability. Time is of the essence. If your child has been suspended from Judson ISD, use the Discipline Defense Kit right now to understand your rights and take immediate action.

Filing a Complaint Against Judson ISD

If you believe that Judson ISD has violated your child's rights under IDEA or other special education laws, you have several options for filing a complaint. These escalation paths exist regardless of what Judson ISD staff may tell you.

First, you can file a State Complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This is a free process, and TEA is required to resolve the complaint within 60 days. Second, you can request mediation through TEA. Mediation is a voluntary process where a neutral third party helps you and the school district reach an agreement. This is also a free service. Third, you have the right to request a Due Process Hearing. This is a more formal process, similar to a court hearing, where an impartial hearing officer makes a decision about your complaint. Finally, you can file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR) if you believe the district has discriminated against your child based on their disability. If you are considering these options, contact a special education attorney or advocate for guidance. File a TEA complaint today to start holding Judson ISD accountable.

Frequently Asked Questions About Judson ISD Special Education

The questions every Judson ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Judson ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Judson ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Judson ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Judson ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Judson ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Judson ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Judson ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Judson ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
