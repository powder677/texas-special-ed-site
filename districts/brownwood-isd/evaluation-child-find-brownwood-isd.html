BROWNWOOD ISD has a legal obligation under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) to identify, locate, and evaluate all children who may have disabilities and need special education services. This process is called Child Find, and it applies to every student in the district, regardless of their grades or attendance record.

As a parent in BROWNWOOD ISD, you have the fundamental right to request that your child receive a comprehensive evaluation at any time if you suspect a disability. You don't need to wait for your child's teacher to suggest it, and you don't need permission from school administrators to make this request. The BROWNWOOD ISD Child Find process ensures that no child falls through the cracks.

Understanding these rights empowers BROWNWOOD ISD parents to advocate effectively for their children. Your concerns about your child's learning, behavior, communication, or social-emotional development are valid reasons to initiate the evaluation process in BROWNWOOD ISD.

A Full Individual Evaluation is a comprehensive assessment of your child across multiple areas of functioning. BROWNWOOD ISD conducts FIEs to determine whether your child has a disability under IDEA and, if so, what special education services your child needs.

The BROWNWOOD ISD special education evaluation process includes assessments in these key areas:

  • Cognitive abilities – intellectual functioning and learning potential
  • Academic achievement – reading, math, writing, and other school subjects
  • Speech and language skills – communication abilities and disorders
  • Social-emotional functioning – behavior, emotional regulation, and mental health
  • Motor skills – fine and gross motor development and coordination
  • Adaptive functioning – daily living and self-care skills
  • Vision and hearing screening – sensory abilities

BROWNWOOD ISD evaluation teams use multiple sources of information, including standardized tests, classroom observations, teacher input, parent reports, and review of school records. This comprehensive approach ensures that BROWNWOOD ISD teams get an accurate picture of your child's strengths and needs.

The BROWNWOOD ISD Special Education Evaluation Request Process

Step 1: Making Your Formal Request

To begin the process, you must submit a written request for an BROWNWOOD ISD special education evaluation to your school. Put your request in writing—email, letter, or formal request form—so there's a clear record of when you made the request.

Address your letter to the campus principal or director of special education for BROWNWOOD ISD. You can find contact information on the BROWNWOOD ISD website or by calling your child's school directly.

Your request should include:

  • Your child's name, grade, and school
  • Specific concerns you have about your child's learning or development
  • Examples of behaviors or struggles you've observed at home and school
  • Any relevant family or medical history
  • Your contact information and preferred method of communication

Step 2: Understanding the BROWNWOOD ISD 45-Day Timeline

Once BROWNWOOD ISD receives your written request, the district has 45 calendar days to complete the evaluation. This BROWNWOOD ISD 45 day timeline is required by Texas Education Code and federal law.

Here's how the timeline works for BROWNWOOD ISD families:

  1. Day 1 – BROWNWOOD ISD receives your written request
  2. Days 1-5 – BROWNWOOD ISD sends you a Permission to Evaluate form and explains evaluation procedures
  3. Days 6-44 – BROWNWOOD ISD conducts assessments and gathers information
  4. Day 45 – BROWNWOOD ISD must complete all evaluations and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting

The BROWNWOOD ISD 45 day timeline is non-negotiable. If BROWNWOOD ISD needs an extension, they must request your written permission and provide a specific reason.

Step 3: Permission to Evaluate

Within five days of receiving your request, BROWNWOOD ISD will send you a Permission to Evaluate form. You must sign and return this form before any testing can begin. Keep a copy for your records.

The permission form explains what areas BROWNWOOD ISD will evaluate, who will conduct the assessments, and your procedural safeguards as a parent. Take time to read this carefully and ask questions if anything is unclear.

What Happens During the BROWNWOOD ISD Evaluation

Assessment Activities

Once you sign the Permission to Evaluate form, BROWNWOOD ISD evaluators will begin comprehensive testing. Depending on your child's age and concerns, this may include:

  • One-on-one cognitive and achievement testing
  • Classroom observations by special education staff
  • Speech-language pathology assessment
  • Occupational or physical therapy evaluation
  • Behavioral rating scales and questionnaires
  • Review of school records, attendance, and grades

Parent Participation in BROWNWOOD ISD Evaluations

You are an important part of the BROWNWOOD ISD child find and evaluation process. BROWNWOOD ISD is required to consider your observations, concerns, and input about your child. You may be asked to:

  • Complete questionnaires about your child's development and behavior
  • Provide information about your child's medical and family history
  • Discuss your concerns with evaluators
  • Share examples of how your child performs at home

Don't hesitate to share detailed examples with BROWNWOOD ISD evaluators. Information about your child's behavior, social interactions, and skills outside of school helps create a complete picture.

The ARD Meeting and Evaluation Results

What to Expect at Your BROWNWOOD ISD ARD Meeting

At the end of the BROWNWOOD ISD 45 day timeline, you'll attend an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. This meeting must happen before the 45 days are up, even if all testing isn't completely finished.

At your ARD meeting, the BROWNWOOD ISD evaluation team will:

  • Present evaluation results and findings
  • Discuss whether your child meets the criteria for a disability category
  • Determine your child's eligibility for special education services
  • If eligible, develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP)

Understanding Evaluation Outcomes

After reviewing all evaluation data, BROWNWOOD ISD must make a determination: either your child is eligible for special education services, or your child is not eligible.

If eligible: BROWNWOOD ISD will develop an IEP that outlines specific services, accommodations, and support your child will receive. The IEP becomes a legal document that BROWNWOOD ISD must implement.

If not eligible: BROWNWOOD ISD must explain why your child doesn't meet eligibility criteria. You have the right to disagree and pursue additional options, including independent evaluation or due process hearings.

Your Procedural Safeguards as a BROWNWOOD ISD Parent

Throughout the BROWNWOOD ISD special education evaluation process, you have important legal rights called procedural safeguards. These protections ensure that BROWNWOOD ISD follows proper procedures and respects your role as your child's advocate.

Key Safeguards in BROWNWOOD ISD

  • Right to notice: BROWNWOOD ISD must inform you of all evaluation activities and meetings in advance
  • Right to consent: You must give written permission before BROWNWOOD ISD can evaluate your child
  • Right to participation: You can attend all BROWNWOOD ISD evaluation and planning meetings
  • Right to records: You can request and review all evaluation reports and school records
  • Right to interpretation: BROWNWOOD ISD must provide interpreters if you need them at meetings
  • Right to due process: If you disagree with BROWNWOOD ISD's decisions, you can file a complaint or request a hearing

BROWNWOOD ISD must provide you with a written document explaining these safeguards. Ask for it if you don't receive one.

Common Questions from BROWNWOOD ISD Parents

Can BROWNWOOD ISD refuse to evaluate my child?

BROWNWOOD ISD cannot refuse to evaluate your child if you make a written request and there's reason to suspect a disability. If BROWNWOOD ISD refuses, you can file a special education complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

What if I disagree with BROWNWOOD ISD's evaluation results?

You have the right to request an independent evaluation at BROWNWOOD ISD's expense if you believe the evaluation was inaccurate or incomplete. You can also request a due process hearing to resolve disagreements with BROWNWOOD ISD.

How long will the BROWNWOOD ISD evaluation request process take?

BROWNWOOD ISD must complete the entire evaluation process within 45 calendar days of your written request, including the ARD meeting. The actual timeline may be shorter if testing is completed early.
