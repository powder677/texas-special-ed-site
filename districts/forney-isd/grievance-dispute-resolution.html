Dispute & Discipline Rights in Forney ISD

Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

The 10-Day Suspension Rule: Know Your Rights!

Forney ISD can suspend your child for up to three school days for certain infractions. However, any suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days in a school year triggers significant procedural protections, especially if your child has an IEP (Individualized Education Program) or 504 plan! Keep meticulous records of all suspensions. If your child is approaching the 10-day mark, be vigilant. Request a meeting with school administrators to discuss preventative strategies and alternative disciplinary measures.

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

If your child with an IEP is facing a suspension exceeding 10 days or a Change of Placement (see below), the school MUST conduct a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This meeting determines whether the behavior leading to the disciplinary action was a manifestation of your child's disability, or a direct result of the school's failure to implement the IEP. This is CRUCIAL!

At the MDR, you have the right to present evidence, including medical records, evaluations, and personal observations. Prepare thoroughly! If the MDR team determines the behavior WAS a manifestation, the school MUST take immediate steps to address the root cause of the behavior. They cannot simply punish your child. Demand a functional behavior assessment (FBA) and a behavior intervention plan (BIP) if one is not already in place, or if the existing BIP is ineffective.

Change of Placement: A Serious Matter

A Change of Placement occurs when your child is removed from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days, or when a pattern of removals creates a cumulative impact equivalent to a change of placement (even if no single removal exceeds 10 days). This includes suspensions, alternative educational settings (DAEP), or expulsion.

If a Change of Placement is proposed, ensure the MDR is conducted properly and that your child's rights are protected. If the behavior is deemed a manifestation of the disability, the school MUST return your child to their previous placement (unless you and the school agree otherwise).

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe Forney ISD has violated your child's rights or followed improper disciplinary procedures, you can file a grievance. The first step is a Level 1 Grievance. This involves submitting a written complaint to the principal or appropriate administrator within a specified timeframe (check the Forney ISD policy manual, often found online, for exact deadlines). Be clear, concise, and provide specific details of the incident, the alleged violation, and the remedy you are seeking. Keep a copy of your grievance and any supporting documentation.

The school is required to respond to your Level 1 Grievance within a certain timeframe (again, check the policy manual). If you are not satisfied with the response, you can escalate the grievance to the next level. Adhere strictly to the deadlines and procedures outlined in the Forney ISD policy.

TEA State Complaint

If you believe Forney ISD has violated state or federal special education laws (particularly the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act - IDEA), you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This is a more formal process with specific requirements. Contact TEA directly or consult with a special education attorney to ensure your complaint is properly filed.

Due Process: Your Ultimate Protection

If you disagree with the school's decisions regarding your child's discipline, eligibility for special education services, or IEP implementation, you have the right to request a Due Process Hearing. This is a formal legal proceeding where you can present evidence and witnesses to challenge the school's actions. Due process is a complex process, and it is strongly recommended that you seek legal representation. This is your ultimate protection!

Remember: Document everything! Keep all emails, letters, and notes from meetings. Don't hesitate to contact an attorney or advocate specializing in special education law to protect your child's rights. ACT IMMEDIATELY!

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
