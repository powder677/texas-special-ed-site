What can I do right now if Hays CISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Hays CISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Hays CISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the special education system can feel overwhelming, but you are not alone. Special education, as defined by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas state law, ensures that children with disabilities receive a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) designed to meet their unique needs. This includes specially designed instruction, related services (like speech therapy or counseling), and accommodations to help your child succeed in the classroom. Hays CISD, like all school districts in Texas, is overseen by the Texas Education Agency (TEA) and receives support from TEA Region 13 Education Service Center to ensure compliance with these federal and state mandates.

It's crucial to remember that as a parent of a child with a disability, you have enforceable legal rights throughout the entire special education process in Hays CISD. These rights are designed to protect your child's access to appropriate educational services and ensure your voice is heard. From requesting evaluations to participating in ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meetings, you have a say in every decision concerning your child's education. Hays CISD must adhere to specific timelines and procedures, and you have the right to challenge decisions you disagree with.

Knowing your rights and understanding the special education process is the first step toward advocating effectively for your child. Hays CISD is committed to providing a quality education for all students, including those with disabilities, but it is ultimately your responsibility to ensure your child receives the services they need. Take the time to learn about the process and don't hesitate to ask questions. Now, familiarize yourself with the process for requesting an evaluation, which is outlined in the next section.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Hays CISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability that is impacting their academic progress, you have the right to request a special education evaluation from Hays CISD. It's crucial to understand that this request must be made in writing. A verbal request to a teacher or administrator, while helpful for initiating the conversation, does not officially start the timeline for evaluation under federal and state law. Once Hays CISD receives your written consent to evaluate, the district has 45 school days to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). This evaluation will assess your child's strengths and weaknesses and determine if they meet the eligibility criteria for special education services.

Remember, Hays CISD also has a legal obligation under "Child Find" laws to proactively identify students within the district who may have disabilities and require special education services. However, as a parent, you don't have to wait for the district to identify your child. If you have concerns, it's always best to be proactive. Now is the time to draft that written request for an evaluation. Include your child's name, date of birth, school, and specific concerns about their academic, behavioral, or developmental progress. State clearly that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation for special education services. Keep a copy for your records and send the original via certified mail with a return receipt requested, so you have proof that Hays CISD received it.

ARD Meetings in Hays CISD: Your Rights at the Table

The ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee meeting is the cornerstone of the special education process. It's where decisions are made about your child's eligibility for special education, their Individualized Education Program (IEP), and their placement. Hays CISD is required to provide you with at least five calendar days' written notice before any ARD meeting. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, the proposed agenda, and who will be attending. Review this notice carefully and contact the school if you have any questions or need clarification.

During the ARD meeting, you are an equal member of the committee, and your input is valued. You have the right to ask questions, express concerns, and advocate for your child's needs. Remember, you also have the right to request a 10-day recess of the ARD meeting at any time if you need more time to consider information presented or seek outside consultation. This is a valuable tool if you feel pressured to make a decision you're not comfortable with. Furthermore, you are not obligated to sign the IEP the same day as the ARD meeting. Take the time to review the document thoroughly at home before signing.

Your active participation in ARD meetings ensures that your child's IEP is tailored to their unique needs and helps them achieve their full potential. Hays CISD is obligated to consider your input and make decisions that are in your child's best interest. Before your next ARD meeting in Hays CISD, download our free ARD preparation checklist. This checklist will guide you through the process, help you organize your thoughts, and ensure you are prepared to advocate effectively for your child.

Dyslexia Services in Hays CISD

Texas law mandates that Hays CISD screen all students for dyslexia. This screening typically occurs in kindergarten and first grade, but can happen at any grade level if concerns arise. The identification and support of students with dyslexia is guided by the Texas Dyslexia Handbook, a comprehensive resource published by the TEA. The current version is the 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook, which sets the standards that Hays CISD must follow in terms of screening, identification, and intervention.

Students identified with dyslexia are entitled to structured literacy intervention, which is a research-based approach to reading instruction that focuses on phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and reading comprehension. Depending on the severity of their dyslexia and the impact on their academic progress, students may also qualify for accommodations through a 504 Plan or more intensive services through an IEP. If your child has not been screened for dyslexia and you have concerns about their reading skills, request a screening in writing from Hays CISD immediately.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Hays CISD

Disciplinary actions, especially suspensions, can have a significant impact on students with disabilities. It's crucial to understand your child's rights in these situations. If a special education student in Hays CISD is suspended for more than 10 cumulative school days in a school year, this triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This is a critical ARD meeting where the committee determines if the student's behavior was a manifestation of their disability or a result of the district's failure to properly implement the IEP. This also constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA, requiring careful consideration of the student's educational needs.

This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a special education student. You must act quickly and decisively. The MDR process requires an ARD meeting to be convened to make the manifestation determination. If the behavior is determined to be a manifestation of the disability, the IEP team must develop or revise the IEP to address the behavior. It is essential to understand your rights and advocate for your child's needs throughout this process. If your child has been suspended, use the Discipline Defense Kit right now to understand your rights and take immediate action.

Filing a Complaint Against Hays CISD

If you disagree with decisions made by Hays CISD regarding your child's special education services, you have several avenues for recourse. It's important to know that these rights exist regardless of what any staff member at Hays CISD may tell you. The first, and often simplest, option is to file a State Complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This is a free process, and the TEA is required to investigate and resolve the complaint within 60 days. Another free option is to request Mediation through the TEA, where a neutral third party helps you and the district reach a mutually agreeable resolution.

If those options are not successful, you can pursue a Due Process Hearing, a more formal legal proceeding where an administrative law judge hears evidence and makes a binding decision. Finally, you can also file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR) if you believe Hays CISD has discriminated against your child based on their disability. Remember, you have the right to advocate for your child's educational needs and ensure they receive a free and appropriate public education. Start by documenting every interaction and decision that has led to your frustration, and then choose the escalation path that best aligns with your needs.

Frequently Asked Questions About Hays CISD Special Education

The questions every Hays CISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Hays CISD? +

Submit a written request to the Hays CISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Hays CISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Hays CISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Hays CISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Hays CISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Hays CISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Hays CISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
