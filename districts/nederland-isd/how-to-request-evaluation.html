As a parent in NEDERLAND ISD, you have specific parental rights protected by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act. These rights are fundamental to ensuring your child receives an appropriate education.

Your Key Rights

  • Right to request evaluation: You can formally ask NEDERLAND ISD to evaluate your child at any time
  • Free evaluation: The NEDERLAND ISD special education evaluation is free of charge for families
  • Timely notification: NEDERLAND ISD must notify you before evaluating your child and obtain your written consent
  • Participation in decisions: You are an equal member of the decision-making team
  • Information in your language: All documents and communications must be available in Spanish
  • Confidentiality: NEDERLAND ISD must protect the privacy of your child's information
  • Access to records: You can review all educational and evaluation records

Steps to Request an Evaluation in NEDERLAND ISD

The process for requesting an evaluation in NEDERLAND ISD is straightforward and designed to be accessible to all parents. Follow these steps carefully to protect your child's rights.

Step 1: Contact the School

Begin by contacting your child's teacher or the school principal. Explain your specific concerns about your child's learning or behavior. Often, the school can offer interventions before a formal evaluation.

If your concerns persist after 30 days, proceed to make a formal written request. Address it to the Special Education Coordinator or the Special Education Department of NEDERLAND ISD.

Step 2: Formal Written Request

Submit a formal evaluation request in writing. This request should include:

  • Student's full name and date of birth
  • School name and phone number
  • Specific description of your concerns regarding learning or behavior
  • Your name, relationship to the student, and contact information
  • Clear request for special education evaluation

Send this request by certified mail, email, or deliver it in person. Keep a copy for your records. NEDERLAND ISD must receive and document your request.

Step 3: NEDERLAND ISD Notification and Consent

Within 15 days of receiving your request, NEDERLAND ISD must contact you by phone, email, or letter. A representative will explain the evaluation process and ask you to sign a consent form in Spanish.

They should not proceed with the evaluation without your signed consent. You have the right to ask questions and fully understand what will be evaluated and how the results will be used.

Step 4: Completion of the NEDERLAND ISD Special Education Evaluation

Once you have given consent, NEDERLAND ISD will complete the NEDERLAND ISD FIE in Spanish (Full Individual Evaluation). This typically occurs within 60 school days from your consent.

The evaluation may include cognitive tests, academic assessments, classroom behavior observations, and language and communication evaluations. Evaluators may include school psychologists, special education specialists, speech-language pathologists, or other professionals.

Step 5: Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) Meeting

After the evaluation is completed, NEDERLAND ISD will schedule an ARD meeting. This is your opportunity to review the results and jointly decide if your child qualifies for special education.

Evaluators, teachers, NEDERLAND ISD administrators, and you as a parent attend this meeting. If your child qualifies, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be developed. If your child does not qualify for special education but has another disability, they might qualify under Section 504.

Important Information on Timelines in NEDERLAND ISD

It is crucial to understand the timelines for NEDERLAND ISD special education. The Texas Education Code establishes specific deadlines that NEDERLAND ISD must meet:

  • 15 days: Initial notification of receipt of your request
  • 60 school days: Maximum time to complete the evaluation from your consent
  • 30 days: Deadline to convene the ARD meeting after completing the evaluation
  • 5 business days: Prior notification before any meeting

If NEDERLAND ISD does not adhere to these timelines, it is a violation of your rights. Document everything with dates.

What to Do if NEDERLAND ISD Denies the Request?

Occasionally, NEDERLAND ISD may argue that the evaluation request is not appropriate. However, under IDEA and Texas law, you have the right to a NEDERLAND ISD special education evaluation if you suspect a disability.

If NEDERLAND ISD formally denies your request in writing, you have options:

  • Request free mediation through the Texas Education Agency (TEA)
  • File a formal compliance complaint
  • Consult with a special education attorney near NEDERLAND or a special ed advocate NEDERLAND

Frequently Asked Questions about NEDERLAND ISD Special Education Evaluation

How much does an evaluation cost in NEDERLAND ISD?

The evaluation is completely free. NEDERLAND ISD pays all costs. Parents cannot be charged for evaluation, testing, or special education services.

What happens after the evaluation?

If your child qualifies, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be developed. This document specifies services, accommodations, and learning goals. If they do not qualify for special education but have another condition, you might choose a Section 504 Plan.

Can I have an attorney at the ARD meeting?

Yes. You can bring a special education attorney near NEDERLAND or an advocate to any meeting with NEDERLAND ISD. It is your right to have professional support.

What if I disagree with the results?

You can request an independent evaluation at public expense. You can also initiate a mediation process or file a compliance complaint with the Texas Education Agency.

Next Steps After the Evaluation

Once the NEDERLAND ISD special education evaluation is completed, the process continues. If your child qualifies, you will work with the NEDERLAND ISD team to create an IEP that addresses their specific needs.

Remember that special education is an ongoing process. IEPs will be reviewed annually, and you can request additional evaluations if your child's needs change.
