Parents in CELINA ISD should consider requesting a special education evaluation when they notice their child has persistent difficulties in learning, behavior, or development. A CELINA ISD special education evaluation can identify if your child qualifies for services protected under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA).

Signs Your Child Might Need Evaluation

  • Significant difficulties in reading, writing, or mathematics
  • Speech or language delays
  • Attention difficulties or hyperactivity
  • Disruptive behaviors or social challenges
  • Low academic performance despite receiving general support
  • Suspicions of autism or other developmental conditions

At CELINA ISD, we recognize that every child learns differently. If you have concerns about your child's progress, do not hesitate to contact your child's school in the CELINA area.

State and federal law, including the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, protect parental rights in CELINA ISD. As a parent in CELINA ISD, you have specific rights that you should know.

Fundamental Rights Under the Law

  • Right to request evaluation: You can request a CELINA ISD FIE (Full Individual Evaluation) at any time
  • Right to be informed: CELINA ISD must notify you in your language about all aspects of the process
  • Right to participate: You must be an integral part of all evaluation meetings and educational decisions
  • Right to consent: CELINA ISD needs your written consent before conducting any evaluation
  • Right to due process: You have the right to challenge CELINA ISD's decisions if you disagree
  • Right to counsel: You can request legal representation in CELINA ISD proceedings

Steps to Request an Evaluation in CELINA ISD

The process of requesting a CELINA ISD evaluation is straightforward and accessible for all parents. Here's how to proceed step-by-step.

Step 1: Contact Your Child's School

Contact the principal, your child's teacher, or the special education coordinator at your child's school within CELINA ISD. Express your specific concerns about your child's development or academic performance. Request a meeting to discuss your concerns and ask for information about the evaluation process.

Step 2: Submit a Written Evaluation Request

Although a verbal request is valid, it is advisable to send a written request to your school's principal in CELINA ISD. This letter should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Name of the school your child attends in CELINA ISD
  • Clear description of your specific concerns
  • Areas where you observe difficulties (academic, behavioral, social, etc.)
  • Explicit request for a CELINA ISD special education evaluation
  • Your name, phone number, and email address
  • Date of the request

Keep a copy of your request and consider sending it via certified mail to confirm its receipt by CELINA ISD.

Step 3: Await CELINA ISD's Response

By Texas state law, CELINA ISD has 15 school days to respond to your request. The response should indicate whether CELINA ISD accepts or denies your evaluation request. If CELINA ISD denies the request, they must provide a clear explanation of the reasons.

Step 4: Provide Written Consent

If CELINA ISD accepts your request, they will provide you with a consent-to-evaluate form. This form must be in your preferred language. Your consent is mandatory before CELINA ISD can proceed with the CELINA ISD FIE evaluation.

Step 5: Participate in the Evaluation

CELINA ISD will conduct a comprehensive and integral evaluation that may include academic assessments, behavioral evaluations, language evaluations, and other types of specialized assessments. The process generally takes 4 to 6 weeks within CELINA ISD.

Step 6: Results Meeting and Eligibility Decision

CELINA ISD will schedule a meeting to share the evaluation results. At this meeting, CELINA ISD's multidisciplinary team will discuss whether your child qualifies for special education and what services would be appropriate.

Understanding the CELINA ISD FIE Evaluation

The CELINA ISD Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is an exhaustive analysis of your child's capabilities, needs, and strengths in all areas related to the suspected disability.

Components of a Comprehensive Evaluation in CELINA ISD

  • Academic evaluation: Measures skills in reading, math, and writing
  • Cognitive evaluation: Assesses thinking and learning ability
  • Behavioral evaluation: Examines behavior patterns in different environments
  • Language evaluation: Assesses communication and language skills
  • Developmental evaluation: For younger students in CELINA ISD
  • Other specialized evaluators: As needed (occupational therapists, speech pathologists, etc.)

All CELINA ISD evaluators must be certified professionals in their respective fields.

Important Information on CELINA ISD Parental Rights

It is fundamental that all parents in CELINA ISD fully understand their CELINA ISD parental rights under federal and state special education law.

Right to Obtain Records

You can request to view all CELINA ISD evaluation records, tests, scores, and recommendations. You have the right to a copy within 10 school days of your request to CELINA ISD.

Right to an IEP Meeting

If your child is eligible, CELINA ISD must schedule an Individualized Education Program (IEP) meeting within 30 days of the eligibility start. You must be present as a full participant in CELINA ISD.

Right to a Special Education Attorney

If you encounter difficulties with CELINA ISD's decisions, you may seek legal representation. There are specialized special education attorneys and special education advocates available for families in the CELINA area.

When to Seek Legal Assistance?

If CELINA ISD denies your evaluation request, denies services, or if you disagree with the evaluation results, it may be time to seek professional help. A special education attorney near CELINA can help you understand your options.

Signs You Might Need an Advocate or Attorney

  • CELINA ISD denies your evaluation request without clear justification
  • You believe CELINA ISD's evaluation was inadequate or biased
  • You disagree with the Individualized Education Program proposed by CELINA ISD
  • Your child is not making progress with current CELINA ISD services
  • CELINA ISD does not honor agreed-upon accommodations

A special ed advocate CELINA can help you navigate the system, prepare for meetings, and advocate for your child's rights within CELINA ISD.
