A special evaluation (FIE - Full Individual Evaluation) is a comprehensive process that examines whether your child has a disability that affects their learning. In NAVASOTA ISD, these evaluations include academic, psychological, speech, and other specialized tests.

The goal is to determine if your child qualifies for special education services in NAVASOTA ISD under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) or under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.

If NAVASOTA ISD determines that your child qualifies, they will receive an Individualized Education Program (IEP) that sets specific goals and tailored services.

Parental rights in NAVASOTA ISD are protected by federal and Texas state laws. These rights are fundamental throughout the special evaluation process.

Main Rights You Have

  • Request an evaluation: You can request that NAVASOTA ISD evaluate your child at any time if you believe they have a disability.
  • Informed consent: NAVASOTA ISD must obtain your written permission before conducting any evaluation.
  • Free evaluation: NAVASOTA ISD must provide all evaluations at no cost to families.
  • Clear information: You must receive explanations in English about the evaluation results in NAVASOTA ISD.
  • Participate in decisions: You have the right to actively participate in all planning meetings in NAVASOTA ISD.
  • Access to records: You can review all school records for your child maintained by NAVASOTA ISD.
  • Due process: If you disagree with NAVASOTA ISD's decisions, you have the right to a due process hearing.

As a parent in NAVASOTA ISD, these rights ensure that your voice is heard and that your child receives the fair evaluation they deserve.

Steps to Request a Special Evaluation in NAVASOTA ISD

The process for requesting an evaluation in NAVASOTA ISD is systematic and clear. Follow these steps carefully.

Step 1: Contact the School

First, speak with your child's teacher or the school principal in NAVASOTA ISD. Explain your specific concerns about your child's learning or behavior.

It's helpful to be prepared with concrete examples: "My child has difficulty reading," "They cannot concentrate in class," or "They have trouble interacting with other children."

Step 2: Formal Written Request

Send a written request to NAVASOTA ISD formally asking for a special education evaluation. This request should be addressed to the Director of Special Education for NAVASOTA ISD.

Include in your letter:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • School name and current grade level in NAVASOTA ISD
  • Clear description of your educational concerns
  • Specific behaviors or difficulties you have observed
  • Your contact information (phone and email)

Keep a copy of this request for your records. It's best to send it via certified mail or deliver it in person to the NAVASOTA ISD offices.

Step 3: Informed Consent

NAVASOTA ISD must send you a consent form in English within days of receiving your request. This document explains:

  • What tests will be performed
  • How the information will be used
  • Your rights as a parent in NAVASOTA ISD
  • The estimated timeline

Do not sign anything without fully understanding it. You can ask NAVASOTA ISD for clarifications in English before consenting.

Step 4: Complete Evaluations

Once you sign the consent, NAVASOTA ISD has 60 school days to complete the special evaluation. During this time, your child will be evaluated by different specialists:

  • School psychologist: Evaluates cognitive and emotional abilities
  • Reading specialist: Examines basic academic skills
  • Speech and language pathologist: If there are communication concerns
  • Behavior specialist: If there are behavioral issues

Step 5: Eligibility Determination Meeting

NAVASOTA ISD will schedule a meeting with you to discuss the results. At this meeting, it will be determined if your child qualifies for special education services in NAVASOTA ISD.

You can bring another person to support you, such as an educational rights attorney or a special education advocate near NAVASOTA, if you wish.

What to Do if NAVASOTA ISD Says There Is No Qualification?

If NAVASOTA ISD determines that your child does not qualify for special education services, you have options. This does not mean your child doesn't need support; it simply means they don't meet IDEA criteria.

Available Options

  • 504 Plan: You can request a Section 504 plan which provides accommodations without formal special education.
  • Request review: You can ask NAVASOTA ISD to review their decision if you believe it was incorrect.
  • Independent evaluation: You can request an independent evaluation at NAVASOTA ISD's expense if you disagree.
  • Mediation: The state of Texas offers free mediation to resolve disagreements with NAVASOTA ISD.
  • Due process hearing: You can request a formal hearing if the disagreement is serious.

Although many parents in NAVASOTA ISD navigate the process alone, there are situations where a professional can significantly help.

  • The proposed IEP does not address your child's actual needs
  • NAVASOTA ISD refuses to make changes you believe are necessary
  • There have been multiple disagreements over services in NAVASOTA ISD

A special education attorney near NAVASOTA or a special education advocate in NAVASOTA ISD can review your child's documents, attend meetings, and advocate for your rights.

Important Timelines and Deadlines in NAVASOTA ISD

Understanding timelines is crucial when working with NAVASOTA ISD. Here are the key times to remember:

  • Within 1-2 days: NAVASOTA ISD must acknowledge receipt of your request
  • Within 5 days: You should receive the informed consent form
  • 60 school days: NAVASOTA ISD must complete all special evaluations
  • Once evaluated: NAVASOTA ISD will schedule the eligibility meeting
  • If qualified: An IEP meeting within 30 days of eligibility

These timelines are established by law in Texas. If NAVASOTA ISD does not comply, you have the right to compensatory education.

Preparing for NAVASOTA ISD Meetings

Meetings with NAVASOTA ISD are important. Coming prepared ensures your voice is heard and that the best decisions are made for your child.

Before the Meeting:

  • Gather specific home observations about your child's performance
  • Write down questions you want to ask NAVASOTA ISD
  • Review all previous documentation sent by NAVASOTA ISD
  • Bring copies of schoolwork, grades, and previous reports

During the Meeting:

  • Ask questions if you don't understand something NAVASOTA ISD says
  • Request explanations in English if necessary
  • Take notes on what is discussed
  • Do not feel pressured to sign documents the same day
  • Ask for copies of everything you sign
