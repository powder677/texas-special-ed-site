A Full Individual Evaluation is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine whether your child has a disability and if they qualify for special education services under IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act). For families in NAVASOTA ISD, this evaluation is the gateway to accessing specialized instruction and support services.

The NAVASOTA ISD special education evaluation process is conducted by a multidisciplinary team that examines your child's cognitive abilities, academic performance, social-emotional functioning, and physical health. This thorough assessment goes beyond simple screening and provides detailed information about your child's strengths and areas of need.

Texas Education Code §29.004 requires that all school districts, including NAVASOTA ISD, implement comprehensive child find procedures. This means NAVASOTA ISD is actively looking to identify students who may need special education services, even if parents haven't formally requested an evaluation.

Under federal and Texas law, any parent or guardian of a school-age child in NAVASOTA ISD can request a NAVASOTA ISD child find evaluation at no cost. You don't need a doctor's referral, teacher recommendation, or permission from anyone else—you have this right simply by being the parent of a student in NAVASOTA ISD.

Who Can Request an Evaluation?

  • Parent or legal guardian
  • Foster parent or caregiver authorized to act on the child's behalf
  • School personnel (teachers, counselors, administrators) within NAVASOTA ISD
  • Court-appointed surrogate parent

If you're a parent in the NAVASOTA area and your child attends a NAVASOTA ISD campus, you absolutely have the authority to request an evaluation without needing anyone's approval.

Step-by-Step Process for Requesting an FIE in NAVASOTA ISD

Step 1: Contact Your Campus Special Education Coordinator

Your first action should be to contact the special education coordinator at your child's NAVASOTA ISD campus. This person is responsible for managing special education referrals and can answer preliminary questions about the NAVASOTA ISD evaluation request process.

You can request this contact information from the campus main office. Most NAVASOTA ISD campuses have a special education department with dedicated staff to assist families.

Step 2: Submit Your Written Request

While a verbal request is technically sufficient, always submit your request in writing. This creates an official record and protects your rights. Your written request should:

  • Include your child's full name, date of birth, and grade level
  • State that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation
  • Briefly describe your concerns about your child's learning or development
  • Include your contact information and signature
  • Be dated

Address your request to the Special Education Director at NAVASOTA ISD. Keep a copy for your records and consider sending it via certified mail or email with a read receipt to document when NAVASOTA ISD received it.

Step 3: Understand the Important 45-Day Timeline

This is critical: Once NAVASOTA ISD receives your written evaluation request, the district has 45 calendar days to complete the entire evaluation. This deadline is non-negotiable under Texas Education Code §29.004 and federal IDEA regulations.

The NAVASOTA ISD 45 day timeline runs from the date they receive your written request, not from when you first mentioned it to a teacher. This is why written documentation is essential—it establishes the official start date.

Step 4: Consent and Permission Forms

Within a few days of receiving your request, NAVASOTA ISD must provide you with a Permission to Evaluate form. You must sign this form granting permission before any evaluation activities begin. NAVASOTA ISD cannot proceed without your written consent.

Review this form carefully. It should describe the types of assessments that will be conducted. You have the right to ask questions about any proposed evaluation procedure before signing.

Step 5: Evaluation Components

The NAVASOTA ISD special education evaluation typically includes:

  • Cognitive/Intellectual Assessment: Tests measuring reasoning, problem-solving, and processing abilities
  • Academic Achievement Testing: Assessments in reading, math, writing, and other academic areas
  • Speech and Language Evaluation: If speech/language concerns exist
  • Occupational/Physical Therapy Evaluation: If motor skills are a concern
  • Behavioral/Social-Emotional Assessment: Rating scales and observations related to behavior and emotions
  • Classroom Observations: Direct observation of your child in the learning environment
  • Vision and Hearing Screening: To rule out sensory issues
  • Review of Educational Records: Grades, previous testing, disciplinary records
  • Parent Interview: Detailed discussion of your child's developmental history and current functioning

What to Expect During the NAVASOTA ISD FIE Process

Communication and Timeline Expectations

NAVASOTA ISD staff should contact you within the first week of your evaluation request to schedule the parent interview. This meeting typically occurs at your child's school and usually lasts 30-60 minutes.

During this time, you'll discuss your child's developmental history, any medical or developmental delays, family history, and current concerns. Bring any documentation you have, such as medical records, previous evaluations, or notes about specific struggles your child is experiencing.

Preparing for Your Parent Interview

Before meeting with NAVASOTA ISD evaluators, prepare by:

  • Writing down specific examples of concerns (delays, behavioral issues, academic struggles)
  • Gathering medical records or outside evaluations if available
  • Creating a timeline of developmental milestones
  • Noting whether other family members have similar conditions
  • Bringing a list of questions for the NAVASOTA ISD team

It's entirely appropriate to bring another adult to this meeting for support, whether a family member, friend, or advocate.

Testing and Assessment Activities

Most testing occurs during the school day, though some assessments may be scheduled at alternative times based on family needs. NAVASOTA ISD will coordinate with you about specific dates and times.

Your child will work with licensed evaluators (school psychologists, educational diagnosticians, speech pathologists) who conduct assessments in separate sessions. The entire testing process usually spans 2-4 weeks within the 45-day window.

The 45-Day Deadline and IEP Meeting: What Happens Next

The NAVASOTA ISD 45 day timeline includes completion of all evaluations AND holding an Individualized Education Program (IEP) meeting to discuss results. You cannot be responsible for delays in scheduling this meeting if they're caused by NAVASOTA ISD or your unavailability.

However, if the evaluation cannot be completed within 45 days due to circumstances, NAVASOTA ISD must provide written notification explaining why and giving you an estimated completion date.

The Eligibility Determination Meeting

At the conclusion of the NAVASOTA ISD special education evaluation, NAVASOTA ISD will hold a meeting with you and evaluation team members. During this meeting, you'll learn:

  • Whether your child qualifies for special education services
  • The specific disability category (if eligible)
  • A summary of evaluation findings
  • Recommendations for services and placement

The team must decide eligibility by consensus. If they determine your child doesn't qualify, they must provide you with an explanation and notice of your procedural rights.

If Your Child Qualifies: The IEP

If evaluation results indicate your child has a disability affecting education, NAVASOTA ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). This legal document outlines your child's:

  • Current levels of academic and functional performance
  • Annual goals addressing identified needs
  • Services and accommodations your child will receive
  • How progress will be measured
  • Placement and environment where services will be delivered

Your Rights During the NAVASOTA ISD Evaluation Process

Essential Procedural Safeguards

NAVASOTA ISD must provide you with a document explaining your procedural rights under IDEA. These rights include:

  • Right to be fully informed: NAVASOTA ISD must communicate with you in your native language or preferred communication method
  • Right to participate: You must be included in all meetings regarding your child's evaluation and placement
  • Right to independent evaluation: If you disagree with NAVASOTA ISD's evaluation, you can request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at district expense
  • Right to records: You can access all evaluation reports and educational records
  • Right to due process: If you disagree with NAVASOTA ISD's decisions, you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency or request mediation/due process hearing

Bringing Support to Meetings

You have the absolute right to bring anyone you choose to any meeting with NAVASOTA ISD, including a special education attorney near NAVASOTA or a special ed advocate NAVASOTA families trust. This person can help you understand information, take notes, and advocate for your child's needs.

If you think you need an advocate, consider reaching out to your local parent training and information center or contacting a special education attorney near NAVASOTA who can provide guidance.

What If NAVASOTA ISD Denies Your Evaluation Request?

NAVASOTA ISD cannot legally deny a parent's request for evaluation. If the district refuses to evaluate or delays unreasonably beyond 45 days, you have several options:

Taking Action Against NAVASOTA ISD

  • Document everything in writing: Keep records of all communications, dates, and attempts to move the process forward
  • Send a formal written demand: Address a letter to the NAVASOTA ISD Superintendent restating your evaluation request and referencing the 45-day deadline
  • File a State Complaint: Contact the Texas Education Agency (TEA) Division of Special Education to file a complaint against NAVASOTA ISD for failure to evaluate
  • Request Due Process: You can request a due process hearing with the TEA
  • Consult a special ed advocate NAVASOTA: Contact a local advocate or special education attorney near NAVASOTA to discuss your options

Most NAVASOTA ISD staff follow the law and timelines appropriately. However, knowing your rights ensures you can advocate effectively if issues arise.

Special Considerations and Common Scenarios

Evaluation Requests for Secondary Students

The process for evaluating high school students in NAVASOTA ISD is the same, but evaluations should include assessments of transition needs and post-secondary goals if the student is age 16 or older.

Evaluation Requests for English Language Learners

If your child is an English language learner in NAVASOTA ISD, evaluations must be conducted in a manner that distinguishes between language learning needs and disability. Testing should include assessment in the child's native language when possible.

Re-evaluations

NAVASOTA ISD must re-evaluate students with IEPs at least every three years. You can also request a re-evaluation if you believe your child's needs have changed.

Getting Additional Support

Local Resources for NAVASOTA ISD Families

Parents in NAVASOTA ISD don't have to navigate this process alone. Several resources can help:

  • NAVASOTA ISD Special Education Department: Your first stop for questions about the evaluation process
  • Parent Training and Information Centers in Texas: Free resources and training for special education parents
  • Special education attorney near NAVASOTA: Consider consulting with an attorney experienced in Texas special education law if you face disputes
  • Special ed advocate NAVASOTA: Local advocates can attend IEP meetings and help ensure your child's needs are met

Preparing for Your Role as Advocate

You are your child's best advocate. As a parent in NAVASOTA ISD, preparing yourself with knowledge about special education law and your child's specific needs positions you to effectively collaborate with NAVASOTA ISD staff toward solutions that benefit your child.

Read all documents NAVASOTA ISD provides. Ask questions when you don't understand. Request clarification about evaluation results. Don't hesitate to express concerns about recommendations.

Key Takeaways for NAVASOTA ISD Parents

Requesting a NAVASOTA ISD FIE evaluation is your right as a parent. Remember these essential points:

  • Put your request in writing and keep documentation of when NAVASOTA ISD receives it
  • The 45-day timeline is firm—NAVASOTA ISD must complete evaluation and hold an IEP meeting within this window
  • You must give written permission before NAVASOTA ISD can begin any evaluation
  • Participate fully in the parent interview and meetings
  • Bring support if you want—a family member, advocate, or attorney
  • Request a copy of all reports and take time to review them before the IEP meeting
  • Know your rights including the right to independent evaluation if you disagree with findings
  • Seek help if needed from a special ed advocate NAVASOTA or special education attorney near NAVASOTA

The evaluation process exists because every child deserves an appropriate, free, public education. NAVASOTA ISD has a legal obligation to fairly and thoroughly assess your child if you request it. By understanding the process and advocating confidently for your child, you ensure that NAVASOTA ISD provides the services your child needs to succeed.
