A Full Individual Evaluation is a thorough assessment of your child's cognitive abilities, academic performance, social-emotional functioning, and physical health. Parents and Guardians

As a parent or legal guardian, you have the right to request a NAVARRO ISD child find evaluation at any time. Your request doesn't need to be approved by teachers, administrators, or anyone else in NAVARRO ISD. You simply need to submit it in writing to the appropriate department.

School District Staff

Teachers, counselors, school nurses, and administrators in NAVARRO ISD can also initiate the referral process if they observe concerning patterns in a student's learning or behavior. However, even if the school hasn't referred your child, you retain the right to request an evaluation independently.

Other Professionals

Doctors, therapists, and other professionals outside NAVARRO ISD can recommend an evaluation, though the formal request typically comes from parents or school personnel.

Step 1: Document Your Concerns

Before contacting NAVARRO ISD, write down specific examples of your concerns. Include dates, behaviors, academic struggles, or developmental delays you've noticed. Examples might include: "My child reads two grade levels below peers," or "Teachers report my child has difficulty focusing and completing assignments."

This documentation strengthens your position and helps NAVARRO ISD staff understand the scope of your concerns when you submit your NAVARRO ISD evaluation request.

Step 2: Submit Your Written Request

Contact your child's school principal or the Special Education Department in NAVARRO ISD and request a meeting or submit a written evaluation request. While a phone call starts the conversation, always follow up with written documentation. Send your request via email or hand-deliver it to the school with a dated receipt.

Your written request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Your child's current school and grade level
  • Specific concerns about learning, behavior, or development
  • Examples of how these concerns affect your child's education
  • Your contact information (phone and email)
  • A clear statement: "I am requesting a Full Individual Evaluation"

Step 3: Attend the Pre-Evaluation Conference

Within a specific timeframe, NAVARRO ISD staff will schedule a pre-evaluation conference with you. This meeting allows school personnel to discuss your concerns, gather background information, and explain the evaluation process.

Bring relevant documents to this meeting, such as:

  • Medical records or diagnoses
  • Previous evaluations from outside providers
  • Report cards and work samples
  • Records from previous schools
  • Documentation of family history of learning disabilities

Step 4: Give Written Consent

NAVARRO ISD must obtain your written consent before conducting the evaluation. You have the right to know exactly what assessments will be administered, who will conduct them, and how the results will be used. Don't sign anything you don't understand—ask questions until everything is clear.

Understanding the NAVARRO ISD 45 Day Timeline

The NAVARRO ISD 45 day timeline is a critical deadline you must know about. Under Texas Education Code and IDEA, NAVARRO ISD has 45 calendar days from the date you provide written consent to complete the FIE and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting.

What Happens During the 45 Days?

During this period, NAVARRO ISD will conduct all necessary assessments, gather information from teachers and family, and compile results into a comprehensive evaluation report. The district must schedule your ARD meeting before the 45 days expire.

The ARD Meeting at the End of 45 Days

At the ARD meeting, the NAVARRO ISD team will present evaluation findings and determine eligibility for special education services. You'll receive a copy of the evaluation report and have the opportunity to discuss results and ask questions.

If NAVARRO ISD determines your child qualifies, they'll develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). If they determine your child doesn't qualify, you have the right to dispute this decision.

What If NAVARRO ISD Exceeds the 45 Days?

If NAVARRO ISD doesn't complete the evaluation within 45 days without your permission, this is a procedural violation. Document everything and contact the district's special education director to ask about the delay. If delays continue, you may want to contact a special education attorney near NAVARRO or a special ed advocate NAVARRO parents trust.

What to Expect During the FIE Process in NAVARRO ISD

Assessments Your Child Might Receive

NAVARRO ISD FIE evaluations typically include multiple types of assessments depending on your child's needs:

  • Cognitive/Psychological Testing: Measures intelligence, reasoning, and processing abilities
  • Educational Assessments: Evaluates reading, math, writing, and other academic skills
  • Speech-Language Evaluation: If communication concerns exist
  • Occupational/Physical Therapy Screening: Assesses motor skills and sensory processing
  • Behavioral Rating Scales: Documents behavior patterns across settings
  • Vision and Hearing Screening: Rules out sensory factors affecting learning

Who Participates in the Evaluation?

NAVARRO ISD brings together a multidisciplinary team to conduct the FIE. This team typically includes:

  • School psychologist or licensed evaluator
  • Special education teacher
  • General education teacher
  • School nurse or medical professional
  • You, the parent (your input is essential)
  • Your child (when age-appropriate)

Your Rights and Protections During a NAVARRO ISD Special Education Evaluation

Federal law provides significant protections for parents pursuing special education services in NAVARRO ISD. Understanding these rights helps you advocate effectively.

Right to Prior Written Notice

NAVARRO ISD must provide written notice of any proposed or refused evaluation before taking action. This notice must be in your native language and explain your procedural safeguards.

Right to Independent Evaluation

If you disagree with NAVARRO ISD's evaluation results, you can request an independent evaluation at district expense. NAVARRO ISD cannot simply refuse your request—they must either pay for an independent evaluation or pursue due process.

Right to an Advocate or Attorney

You have the absolute right to bring a special ed advocate NAVARRO families recommend or a special education attorney near NAVARRO to any meeting. NAVARRO ISD cannot discourage you from having representation, and they cannot charge you for this advocacy.

Right to Records Access

You can request and review all educational records related to your child at any time. NAVARRO ISD must provide copies within 10 school days of your request.

Common Reasons Why NAVARRO ISD Parents Request Evaluations

Understanding typical scenarios helps you recognize if your child might benefit from a NAVARRO ISD special education evaluation:

  • Reading difficulties: Your child struggles significantly with reading despite quality instruction
  • Math challenges: Consistent difficulty with math concepts despite practice and support
  • Behavior concerns: Frequent disciplinary issues, aggression, or defiance at school
  • Attention problems: Difficulty focusing, completing tasks, or following directions
  • Speech/language delays: Speech is unclear, vocabulary is limited, or language development lags peers
  • Fine/gross motor delays: Struggles with handwriting, coordination, or physical activities
  • Social difficulties: Trouble making or maintaining friendships, understanding social cues
  • Anxiety or emotional concerns: Excessive worrying, withdrawal, or emotional reactivity

What Happens After the NAVARRO ISD Evaluation Is Complete

If Your Child Qualifies for Special Education

NAVARRO ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) outlining specific services, modifications, and accommodations. Your child becomes eligible for special education protections and services.

If Your Child Doesn't Qualify

If NAVARRO ISD determines your child doesn't meet eligibility criteria, you have several options. You can request an independent evaluation, request a due process hearing, or explore a 504 Plan as an alternative accommodation plan.

Don't accept a "no" without understanding the reasoning. Ask NAVARRO ISD staff to explain specifically why your child doesn't meet eligibility criteria for the disability category you believe applies.
