Understanding ARD Meetings in NAVARRO ISD

An ARD meeting is a formal gathering where school staff, parents, and sometimes the student meet to discuss special education eligibility, services, and goals. In NAVARRO ISD, these meetings determine whether your child qualifies for special education under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §89.1010.

The ARD committee typically includes the student's teacher, special education director, school psychologist, parent(s), and the student (when appropriate). For NAVARRO ISD families, understanding who will attend and what to expect helps you prepare effectively for this important meeting.

The NAVARRO ISD ARD process ensures that every child with a disability receives a free, appropriate public education (FAPE) tailored to their individual needs. This meeting is your opportunity to advocate for your child and ensure their educational plan supports their success.

The IEP Document and NAVARRO ISD ARD Goals

What is an IEP?

The Individualized Education Program (IEP) is the legal document created during your NAVARRO ISD ARD meeting that outlines your child's special education services. For NAVARRO ISD students, the IEP is a binding agreement between the school district and parents specifying services, modifications, and goals.

The IEP is not a curriculum document—it's a plan for how the school will support your child's access to the general education curriculum while addressing disability-related needs.

Developing NAVARRO ISD IEP Goals

One of the most important aspects of the NAVARRO ISD IEP process is developing appropriate NAVARRO ISD IEP goals. These goals should be specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound (SMART).

NAVARRO ISD IEP goals must address:

  • The child's present levels of academic and functional performance
  • How the disability affects educational performance
  • Annual measurable goals with specific progress monitoring
  • Short-term objectives or benchmarks (for younger students or those with significant disabilities)
  • Services needed to meet the goals and access the curriculum

During your NAVARRO ISD ARD meeting, advocate for goals that are meaningful to your family and address your child's actual needs, not just school-selected priorities. Realistic, achievable NAVARRO ISD IEP goals help track your child's genuine progress.

Understanding Accommodations and Modifications

NAVARRO ISD must specify all accommodations (changes to how your child accesses instruction) and modifications (changes to what your child is taught). Common accommodations include extended time on tests, modified assignments, or assistive technology. Modifications might include different grade-level standards or curriculum.

Ensure your NAVARRO ISD IEP process clearly documents all accommodations and modifications in writing so all teachers at NAVARRO ISD who work with your child understand and implement them consistently.
