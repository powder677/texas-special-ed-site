About Special Education in Laredo ISD

Laredo ISD serves the Texas area and is committed to providing a quality education for all students, including those with disabilities. The district operates under the oversight of TEA Region unknown and adheres to all state and federal regulations regarding special education services. We understand that navigating the special education process can be complex, and we are here to support you every step of the way.

In Laredo ISD, the process from referral to completion of the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is generally completed within 45 school days. Once the FIE is complete, parents will receive a written notice at least 5 school days prior to the ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. This meeting is where the IEP (Individualized Education Program) is developed and implemented.

Common Questions from Laredo ISD Families

What if I disagree with the ARD committee's decisions?

You have the right to disagree with the decisions made during the ARD meeting. You can request a recess of the meeting to gather more information or seek outside consultation. Document your concerns in writing and submit them to the ARD committee. You also have the right to pursue formal dispute resolution options such as mediation or a due process hearing.

How often will my child's IEP be reviewed?

Your child's IEP will be reviewed at least annually, but you can request a review meeting more frequently if you feel your child's needs are not being adequately met. This could be due to a change in your child's academic progress or behaviors. Don't hesitate to reach out to your child's special education teacher to discuss your concerns and schedule a meeting.

What types of related services are available in Laredo ISD?

Laredo ISD offers a variety of related services to support students with disabilities, depending on their individual needs as determined by the ARD committee. These services may include speech therapy, occupational therapy, physical therapy, counseling, transportation assistance, and assistive technology. The ARD committee will determine which related services are appropriate for your child.

A Practical Tip for Laredo ISD Parents

Laredo ISD, with approximately 10,000 students, is a smaller district. Often, smaller districts have one special education director handling many responsibilities. For important communication, it's often more effective to communicate via email rather than phone. This creates a written record of your concerns and requests, ensuring clear documentation and follow-up.
