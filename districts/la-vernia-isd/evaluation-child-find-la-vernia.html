Child Find is a fundamental requirement under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), and LA VERNIA ISD is legally obligated to identify, locate, and evaluate all children who may have disabilities. As a parent in LA VERNIA ISD, you should know that this responsibility begins at birth and continues through age 21.

LA VERNIA ISD must actively search for children who may need special education services, including those who are:

  • Struggling academically despite receiving quality classroom instruction
  • Showing developmental delays in speech, language, or motor skills
  • Displaying behavioral or emotional concerns
  • At risk for dropping out of school
  • Potentially gifted but underachieving due to learning disabilities

The LA VERNIA ISD child find process means the district cannot simply wait for parents to request evaluations—they must actively monitor all students and refer those showing signs of potential disabilities for evaluation consideration.

You have the right to request a LA VERNIA ISD FIE evaluation for your child at any time, regardless of whether the school has already referred your child. Here's how to make your request official:

Step 1: Submit a Written Request

Contact your child's campus principal, special education coordinator, or teacher and request a special education evaluation in writing. Your written request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • The specific areas of concern (academics, behavior, speech, motor skills, etc.)
  • A brief description of how these concerns affect your child's learning
  • The date you're submitting the request
  • Your contact information

While LA VERNIA ISD must respond to verbal requests, a written request creates a documented timeline that protects your rights and ensures accountability. Keep a copy for your records.

Step 2: The District's Response Window

Once LA VERNIA ISD receives your written request for a LA VERNIA ISD special education evaluation, the district has 15 calendar days to respond. During this time, they will:

  • Review your concerns and your child's educational history
  • Consult with your child's teacher and other relevant staff
  • Decide whether an FIE is warranted
  • Contact you to discuss next steps

The district may determine that a full evaluation is appropriate, or they may propose alternative interventions. However, you have the right to request an FIE even if the school recommends trying other approaches first.

The LA VERNIA ISD 45-Day Evaluation Timeline

One of the most critical aspects of LA VERNIA ISD FIE evaluation requests is understanding the 45-day timeline. Once the district determines that an evaluation is necessary and you provide written permission, the clock starts.

What Is the 45-Day Timeline?

The LA VERNIA ISD 45 day timeline is the legal requirement under IDEA and Texas Education Code §29.004. From the date you sign a Permission to Evaluate form, LA VERNIA ISD has 45 calendar days to:

  • Complete all necessary testing and assessments
  • Gather information from parents, teachers, and other professionals
  • Analyze evaluation results
  • Schedule and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting
  • Provide you with the evaluation report before or at the ARD meeting

This timeline is non-negotiable. LA VERNIA ISD cannot extend this period without your written consent, and delays can impact your ability to receive services for your child.

Protecting Your Rights During the Timeline

As a parent in LA VERNIA ISD, ensure that:

  • You receive written notification of the evaluation plan before testing begins
  • You understand what areas will be evaluated (academic, speech, occupational therapy, behavioral, etc.)
  • You know who will conduct each evaluation component
  • You're informed if any evaluations will occur outside the typical school day

Request written updates from LA VERNIA ISD if you haven't heard about progress on your child's evaluation by day 30 of the 45-day period.

Understanding Different Types of Evaluations in LA VERNIA ISD

When you request a LA VERNIA ISD special education evaluation, the district may conduct various assessments depending on your child's needs:

Academic Achievement Testing

These assessments measure your child's performance in reading, mathematics, writing, and other academic areas compared to grade-level standards. LA VERNIA ISD will use standardized tests to determine if your child has significant gaps in learning.

Cognitive (IQ) Evaluation

This testing measures reasoning, problem-solving, processing speed, and other cognitive abilities. The results help determine if your child has intellectual disability or specific learning disabilities.

Speech and Language Assessment

If there are concerns about communication, a speech-language pathologist will evaluate your child's ability to understand language, express themselves, and use language appropriately in social situations.

Behavioral and Emotional Assessment

For children with behavior or emotional concerns, LA VERNIA ISD may use rating scales, observations, and interviews to assess anxiety, depression, aggression, or other behavioral patterns affecting school performance.

Occupational and Physical Therapy Evaluations

If motor skills, sensory processing, or fine/gross motor development are concerns, occupational and physical therapists may be involved in your child's LA VERNIA ISD FIE evaluation.

What Happens After the 45-Day Evaluation Period in LA VERNIA ISD

When LA VERNIA ISD completes the LA VERNIA ISD 45 day timeline, the district must hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to discuss the evaluation results with you.

The ARD Meeting

At this critical meeting, LA VERNIA ISD will present:

  • Complete evaluation results in understandable language
  • Whether your child meets criteria for special education eligibility
  • Your child's strengths and areas needing support
  • Recommendations for services if your child qualifies

You have the right to bring a support person, advocate, or attorney to this meeting. If LA VERNIA ISD determines your child is eligible for special education, they must develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) at this meeting.

If Your Child Qualifies for Special Education

Eligibility categories under Texas law include:

  • Specific Learning Disability
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Visual or Hearing Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Multiple Disabilities

If your child meets criteria in any category, LA VERNIA ISD must provide special education services tailored to his or her specific needs.

If Your Child Does Not Qualify

If LA VERNIA ISD determines your child does not meet eligibility criteria, you have the right to:

  • Request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at district expense
  • Ask for a Due Process hearing to challenge the eligibility decision
  • Request intervention services under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act
  • Seek consultation from a special education attorney near LA VERNIA

As a parent in LA VERNIA ISD, the law provides you with specific rights throughout the LA VERNIA ISD evaluation request process:

Right to Prior Written Notice

LA VERNIA ISD must provide you with written notice before evaluating your child, explaining what they want to assess and why. You must give written permission before evaluation can begin.

Right to Understand Evaluation Results

The district cannot use confusing jargon or technical language that prevents you from understanding your child's LA VERNIA ISD special education evaluation results. Request explanations in your native language if English is not your first language.

Right to Participate in Decision-Making

You are a full member of the ARD team and your input is essential. LA VERNIA ISD cannot make eligibility decisions or design IEPs without your participation and input.

Right to Independent Evaluations

If you disagree with LA VERNIA ISD's evaluation results, you may obtain an independent evaluation at the district's expense. This is particularly valuable if the results seem inconsistent with what you observe at home.

Documentation and Preparation Tips for LA VERNIA ISD Parents

To make your LA VERNIA ISD evaluation request as strong as possible, prepare documentation before contacting the district:

Gather Supporting Information

  • Report cards and progress reports from LA VERNIA ISD or previous schools
  • Examples of your child's work showing academic struggles
  • Records from pediatrician visits noting developmental concerns
  • Outside evaluations (speech therapy, occupational therapy, psychological evaluations)
  • Documentation of behavioral incidents or disciplinary actions
  • Teacher feedback about classroom performance and behavior

Create a Timeline

Document when concerns first appeared, what specific incidents occurred, and how these concerns have progressed over time. This timeline helps LA VERNIA ISD understand the full scope of your child's needs.

Write Clear Descriptions of Concerns

Instead of vague statements like "my child struggles in school," provide specific examples: "My child cannot decode multi-syllabic words and falls significantly behind peers in reading fluency," or "My child has difficulty managing anger and has had three aggressive incidents in the past month."

Moving Forward: Next Steps After Requesting Evaluation

Once you submit your LA VERNIA ISD FIE evaluation request, stay actively involved by:

  • Following up within 15 days if LA VERNIA ISD hasn't contacted you
  • Providing any additional information the district requests promptly
  • Attending all evaluation sessions and appointments scheduled by LA VERNIA ISD
  • Preparing questions for the ARD meeting
  • Bringing documentation and examples to the ARD meeting
  • Taking notes during meetings and requesting copies of all evaluation reports

Remember that you are your child's most important advocate. The evaluation process is designed to help identify your child's needs and ensure LA VERNIA ISD provides appropriate services.
