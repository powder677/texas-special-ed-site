Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

Discipline Rights & Grievances in Lubbock ISD: A Parent's Urgent Guide

This is a crisis-mode guide for parents navigating disciplinary actions in Lubbock ISD. Don't panic, but act quickly! Understand your rights and the rights of your child, especially if your child has an IEP or 504 plan. This guide focuses on suspensions, MDRs (Manifestation Determination Reviews), Change of Placement, grievances, and due process. Time is of the essence.

The 10-Day Suspension Rule: Know Your Limits!

Lubbock ISD, like all school districts in Texas, has rules regarding suspensions. A key concept is the 10-day rule. This rule matters significantly, especially if your child has a disability.

  • Isolated Incidents: Lubbock ISD can generally suspend a student for up to three school days for disciplinary reasons without needing formal documentation like a formal notice beyond standard procedure.
  • Cumulative Suspensions: Things change when suspensions start adding up. If your child is suspended for more than 10 school days in a single school year, it triggers specific rights and protections under federal law (IDEA) if your child has an IEP or 504 plan. Keep meticulous records of every suspension, no matter how short. Note the date, reason, and length of each suspension.
  • Counting Days: Suspension days count cumulatively. Three days here, five days there...it all adds up. Be vigilant!
  • What Happens After 10 Days?: Once your child reaches 10 cumulative days of suspension in a school year, Lubbock ISD must determine if the series of short term suspensions constitutes a pattern of removal. If the series of suspensions are for the same misconduct, or create a pattern of removal and create a change in placement, a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) might be required.

What to Do IMMEDIATELY:

  1. Track Suspension Days: Create a spreadsheet or log to meticulously track all suspensions, including the date, reason, and length of each suspension.
  2. Request Documentation: Demand written documentation from the school detailing the reason for each suspension. Don't accept verbal explanations alone.
  3. Consult an Advocate: If your child is nearing 10 cumulative suspension days, contact a special education advocate or attorney immediately. Time is critical.

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)? Understanding Your Child's Rights Under IDEA

An MDR is a crucial process under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). It determines if a student's behavior that led to disciplinary action (like a suspension or expulsion) was caused by, or directly and substantially related to, their disability. It also considers if the behavior was a direct result of the school district's failure to implement the student's IEP (Individualized Education Program) correctly.

When is an MDR Required?

  • Cumulative Suspensions: As mentioned above, exceeding 10 cumulative suspension days in a school year.
  • Change of Placement: Any disciplinary change of placement (explained below).
  • Expulsion Recommendations: If the school is considering expelling your child.

The MDR Process: A Parent's Role

  • Notice: Lubbock ISD must notify you of the MDR meeting. This notice should be in writing and provide sufficient time for you to prepare and attend. If the district makes it difficult to schedule, document that.
  • Participants: The MDR team typically includes:
    • You (the parent/guardian)
    • Relevant members of your child's IEP team (teachers, special education staff, administrators)
    • School staff who know your child and can provide information about the incident.
  • Review: The team will review:
    • Your child's IEP
    • Teacher observations
    • Any relevant medical or psychological information
    • The specific incident leading to the disciplinary action
  • Determination: The team must determine:
    • Was the behavior caused by or directly and substantially related to the child's disability?
    • Was the behavior a direct result of the school's failure to implement the IEP?

Crucial Outcomes of the MDR:

  • Manifestation: If the team determines the behavior was a manifestation of the disability, the school cannot discipline your child in the same way they would a student without a disability. The IEP team must conduct a Functional Behavior Assessment (FBA), implement a Behavior Intervention Plan (BIP), or revise an existing plan to address the behavior. Your child typically returns to their original placement.
  • No Manifestation: If the team determines the behavior was not a manifestation of the disability, the school can apply the same disciplinary consequences as they would to any other student. However, they must still provide educational services so your child can continue to participate in the general education curriculum, although in another setting.
  • IEP Implementation Failure: If the team determines the school failed to implement the IEP correctly, the school must take immediate steps to correct the problem.

What to Do IMMEDIATELY:

  1. Document Everything: Keep detailed notes of all communication with the school, including dates, times, who you spoke with, and what was discussed.
  2. Prepare for the MDR: Gather all relevant information, including your child's IEP, medical records, and any documentation related to the incident. Write down your concerns and questions.
  3. Advocate Fiercely: At the MDR meeting, advocate strongly for your child. Clearly explain why you believe the behavior is related to their disability or the school's failure to implement the IEP. Don't be intimidated.
  4. Record the Meeting (If Allowed): Check Lubbock ISD policy on recording meetings. If allowed, record the MDR meeting. If not, bring a trusted friend or advocate to take detailed notes.
  5. Obtain the MDR Determination in Writing: Demand a written copy of the MDR determination, including the team's findings and the rationale behind their decision.

Change of Placement: Protect Your Child's Educational Environment

A Change of Placement occurs when a student is removed from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days, or when a series of removals constitutes a pattern of removal, even if each individual removal is less than 10 days.

What Constitutes a Change of Placement?

  • Long-Term Suspension: Suspension exceeding 10 consecutive school days.
  • Expulsion: Removal from school.
  • Alternative Educational Setting (AES): Assignment to a different school or program for disciplinary reasons.
  • Pattern of Removals: A series of suspensions or short-term removals that, when considered together, create a significant disruption to the student's educational experience. The District must consider a series of short-term suspensions as a change of placement if they total more than 10 days and create a pattern of removal.

Why Change of Placement Matters:

  • Disruption to Learning: A change of placement can significantly disrupt a student's learning and progress.
  • Loss of Services: The student may lose access to specialized services and supports outlined in their IEP or 504 plan.
  • Due Process Rights: A change of placement triggers specific due process rights for students with disabilities, including the right to an MDR and the right to challenge the decision.

Your Rights During a Change of Placement:

  • Notice: You must receive written notice of the proposed change of placement, including the reasons for the change and your right to challenge the decision.
  • MDR: If your child has an IEP or 504 plan, an MDR must be conducted to determine if the behavior leading to the change of placement was a manifestation of their disability.
  • Stay Put: The "stay put" provision of IDEA allows your child to remain in their current placement while you challenge the change of placement decision, unless the District can prove the student is a danger to him/herself or others. You must formally request "stay put" in writing. This is CRITICAL.
  • Due Process Hearing: You have the right to request a due process hearing to challenge the change of placement decision. This is a formal legal proceeding where you can present evidence and arguments to an impartial hearing officer.

What to Do IMMEDIATELY:

  1. Request Stay Put: If you disagree with the proposed change of placement, IMMEDIATELY send a written request to the school district invoking the "stay put" provision of IDEA. This will keep your child in their current placement while you pursue your legal options. Use certified mail with return receipt requested to prove you sent the letter.
  2. Request a Due Process Hearing: Consider requesting a due process hearing to challenge the change of placement decision. Contact a special education attorney or advocate for assistance.
  3. Continue to Advocate: Even if you request stay put and a due process hearing, continue to advocate for your child. Communicate with the school, attend meetings, and document everything.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance: Internal Resolution Attempts

Lubbock ISD has a grievance policy for addressing complaints. The first step is often filing a Level 1 grievance. This is an internal process within the district, offering an opportunity to resolve the issue before escalating further.

When to File a Level 1 Grievance:

  • Violation of Rights: When you believe Lubbock ISD has violated your child's rights, including their rights under IDEA, Section 504, or district policy.
  • Unfair Treatment: When you believe your child has been treated unfairly due to a disciplinary action, lack of accommodations, or other issue.
  • Failure to Implement IEP/504: When you believe the school is not properly implementing your child's IEP or 504 plan.

The Level 1 Grievance Process:

  • Filing the Grievance: Obtain the grievance form from Lubbock ISD. Complete the form clearly and concisely, outlining the specific issue, the dates and times of relevant events, the individuals involved, and the specific relief you are seeking. Attach any supporting documentation. Submit the completed form to the designated person (usually the principal or supervisor). Keep a copy for your records!
  • Timeline: Lubbock ISD policy will specify the timeline for responding to the grievance. Adhere to the timeline and document all communication.
  • Investigation: The school or district will investigate the grievance. They may interview you, your child, and other relevant individuals.
  • Response: The school or district will provide a written response to your grievance, outlining their findings and any actions they will take.

Important Considerations:

  • Exhaustion of Remedies: In some cases, you may need to exhaust the internal grievance process before pursuing external remedies, such as filing a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).
  • Documentation: Keep meticulous records of all communication, documents, and actions related to the grievance.
  • Advocate: Consider consulting with a special education advocate or attorney for assistance in filing and pursuing the grievance.

What to Do IMMEDIATELY:

  1. Obtain the Grievance Form: Get the official grievance form from Lubbock ISD's website or the school.
  2. Draft the Grievance: Write a clear and concise statement of your complaint, including all relevant details.
  3. Submit the Grievance: Submit the completed grievance form to the appropriate person within the specified timeframe.

TEA State Complaint: An External Option

If you are dissatisfied with the outcome of the Level 1 grievance or believe that Lubbock ISD has violated IDEA regulations, you can file a formal complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

When to File a TEA Complaint:

  • Violation of IDEA: When you believe Lubbock ISD has violated federal IDEA regulations.
  • Failure to Provide FAPE: When you believe Lubbock ISD has failed to provide your child with a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE).
  • Unresolved Grievance: After exhausting the internal grievance process within Lubbock ISD without a satisfactory resolution.

The TEA Complaint Process:

  • Filing the Complaint: Obtain the complaint form from the TEA website. Complete the form accurately and thoroughly, providing specific details about the alleged violation, the dates and times of relevant events, and the individuals involved. Attach all supporting documentation, including copies of your child's IEP, school records, and any communication with Lubbock ISD.
  • Investigation: TEA will investigate the complaint. They may request information from Lubbock ISD, interview you, your child, and other relevant individuals.
  • Findings: TEA will issue a written report of their findings, including whether Lubbock ISD violated IDEA regulations.
  • Corrective Action: If TEA finds that Lubbock ISD violated IDEA regulations, they will order the district to take corrective action to remedy the violation.

Important Considerations:

  • Timeline: There are strict timelines for filing a TEA complaint. Ensure you file the complaint within the specified timeframe.
  • Specificity: Be as specific as possible in your complaint, providing detailed information and evidence to support your allegations.
  • Documentation: Maintain meticulous records of all communication, documents, and actions related to the TEA complaint.
  • Legal Advice: Consider consulting with a special education attorney or advocate for assistance in preparing and filing the TEA complaint.

What to Do IMMEDIATELY:

  1. Obtain the Complaint Form: Download the official complaint form from the TEA website.
  2. Draft the Complaint: Write a detailed and well-supported complaint, including all relevant evidence.
  3. Submit the Complaint: Submit the completed complaint form and supporting documentation to TEA within the specified timeframe.

Due Process: Your Ultimate Legal Recourse

Due process is the ultimate legal recourse available to parents of children with disabilities when they disagree with the school district's decisions regarding their child's education. It is a formal legal proceeding that provides parents with the opportunity to present evidence and arguments to an impartial hearing officer.

When to Request Due Process:

  • Disagreement with IEP: When you disagree with the content of your child's IEP.
  • Failure to Provide FAPE: When you believe Lubbock ISD has failed to provide your child with FAPE.
  • Change of Placement: When you disagree with a proposed change of placement.
  • Disciplinary Action: When you disagree with a disciplinary action taken against your child.

The Due Process Hearing:

  • Request for Hearing: You must file a formal request for a due process hearing with the TEA.
  • Mediation: TEA will offer mediation to attempt to resolve the dispute informally before the hearing. Mediation is voluntary.
  • Hearing: If mediation is unsuccessful, a due process hearing will be scheduled.
  • Hearing Officer: An impartial hearing officer will preside over the hearing.
  • Evidence: You and Lubbock ISD will have the opportunity to present evidence, call witnesses, and cross-examine opposing witnesses.
  • Decision: The hearing officer will issue a written decision based on the evidence presented at the hearing.

Important Considerations:

  • Legal Representation: Due process hearings are complex legal proceedings. It is highly recommended that you seek legal representation from a special education attorney.
  • Timeline: There are strict timelines for requesting and conducting due process hearings.
  • Burden of Proof: The burden of proof in a due process hearing typically rests with the party challenging the school district's decision.
  • Appeals: You have the right to appeal the hearing officer's decision to state or federal court.

What to Do IMMEDIATELY:

  1. Consult an Attorney: Contact a special education attorney immediately. Due process is a complex legal process, and you need expert legal guidance.
  2. Gather Documentation: Begin gathering all relevant documentation, including your child's IEP, school records, and any communication with Lubbock ISD.
  3. File the Request: Work with your attorney to file a formal request for a due process hearing with the TEA.

This guide is for informational purposes only and does not constitute legal advice. Consult with a qualified special education attorney or advocate for personalized advice regarding your specific situation. Act quickly to protect your child's rights!

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
