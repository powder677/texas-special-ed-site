What can I do right now if Lubbock ISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Lubbock ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Lubbock ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the special education system can feel overwhelming, but you're not alone. The Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) is a federal law that guarantees eligible children with disabilities the right to a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) designed to meet their unique needs. Texas law mirrors and expands upon IDEA, ensuring that your child receives the support and services necessary to thrive in school. This includes specially designed instruction, related services like speech therapy or occupational therapy, and modifications to the general education curriculum.

Lubbock ISD operates under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA) and benefits from the resources and support offered by TEA Region 17 Education Service Center. This regional service center provides training, technical assistance, and other support to educators and families in Lubbock ISD and surrounding districts, helping to ensure that your child has access to the best possible special education services. It is important to understand that you, as a parent, have legally enforceable rights throughout the entire special education process, from initial evaluation to the development and implementation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP). These rights are designed to empower you as an active participant in your child's education.

These rights are not just suggestions; they are legal guarantees designed to protect your child's access to a quality education within Lubbock ISD. You have the right to be involved in every decision regarding your child's special education program, to disagree with the school district if you believe they are not meeting your child's needs, and to seek outside support and advocacy. Knowing your rights is the first step in ensuring that your child receives the appropriate services and support they deserve. Remember, your voice matters and you have the power to advocate for your child's success within the Lubbock ISD system.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Lubbock ISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability that is impacting their educational performance, you have the right to request a special education evaluation from Lubbock ISD. It is absolutely critical that this request is made in WRITING. A verbal request to a teacher or administrator, while a good first step, does NOT formally trigger the special education evaluation timeline. Only a written request initiates the process and sets the legal clock ticking.

Once Lubbock ISD receives your written request and obtains your written consent to evaluate, the district has 45 school days to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). This evaluation is a comprehensive assessment of your child's strengths and weaknesses across various areas, such as academic skills, cognitive abilities, communication skills, and social-emotional development. Remember also that Child Find laws require Lubbock ISD to proactively identify students within the district who may have a disability and need special education services. Even if you haven't formally requested an evaluation, Lubbock ISD has a responsibility to identify and assess students who may be struggling. If you have any concerns, it is always best to be proactive.

Your next step is to draft a letter to the Lubbock ISD special education department requesting a Full Individual Evaluation for your child. Be sure to include your child's full name, date of birth, school, grade level, and a brief description of your concerns. Keep a copy of the letter for your records and send the original via certified mail with return receipt requested. This will provide proof that the school received your request and start the 45-day timeline. Do this today to begin advocating for your child immediately.

ARD Meetings in Lubbock ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal. It is the committee responsible for making decisions about your child's special education program. The ARD committee is comprised of you, the parent; school administrators; teachers; evaluation personnel; and other relevant professionals who have knowledge about your child and their educational needs. These meetings are critical opportunities to collaborate with the school and ensure your child is receiving appropriate support. Lubbock ISD is required to give you at least five calendar days written notice before any ARD meeting, allowing you time to prepare and gather any necessary information.

During the ARD meeting, the committee will review your child's evaluation data, discuss their strengths and weaknesses, and develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) tailored to their unique needs. The IEP outlines specific goals, objectives, and services that will be provided to your child to help them succeed. It is important to remember that you have the right to request a 10-day recess during any ARD meeting. This means you can pause the meeting for 10 calendar days if you need more time to consider the information presented or if you feel overwhelmed by the process. Also, you are never obligated to sign the IEP the same day as the ARD meeting. Take the IEP home, review it carefully, and seek outside input if needed before making a decision.

Your role as a parent is invaluable in the ARD process. You know your child best and your input is essential to developing an effective IEP. Do not be afraid to ask questions, express your concerns, and advocate for your child's needs. Remember that you are an equal member of the ARD committee and your voice matters. Your participation ensures the IEP reflects your child's individual needs and allows them to achieve their full potential within Lubbock ISD.

Dyslexia Services in Lubbock ISD

Texas law mandates that Lubbock ISD screen all students for dyslexia. Early identification and intervention are crucial for students with dyslexia, as timely support can significantly improve their reading skills and academic outcomes. This screening process is designed to identify students who may be at risk for dyslexia and ensure they receive appropriate interventions. The district should be using a TEA-approved screener and following all legal procedures.

The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook sets the standards that Lubbock ISD must follow in identifying, evaluating, and providing services to students with dyslexia. Students identified with dyslexia are entitled to structured literacy intervention, a research-based approach that focuses on explicit and systematic instruction in phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and reading comprehension. In some cases, students with dyslexia may also qualify for a 504 Plan or an IEP, which would provide additional accommodations and support to address their specific learning needs. These accommodations could include extended time on tests, assistive technology, or modifications to assignments.

If your child has not been screened for dyslexia by Lubbock ISD, request a screening in writing to the special education director immediately. It is your right and will provide you with much-needed information about your child's literacy development.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Lubbock ISD

Disciplinary actions, particularly suspensions, can have a significant impact on students with disabilities. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year within Lubbock ISD triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This review is conducted by the ARD committee to determine whether the student's misconduct was a manifestation of their disability. If the ARD committee determines that the misconduct was a manifestation of the disability, the student cannot be suspended for more than 10 days and the ARD committee must develop or revise the student's IEP to address the behavior.

Furthermore, any suspension exceeding 10 cumulative days constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA, requiring an ARD meeting to determine the appropriate next steps for your child's education. This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a special education student. You must act quickly to protect your child's rights and ensure they continue to receive a FAPE. The school must also follow all rules and regulations in the Student Code of Conduct. It is vital to document everything and to act fast.

Filing a Complaint Against Lubbock ISD

If you believe that Lubbock ISD is not meeting your child's special education needs, or is violating their rights under IDEA or Texas law, you have several avenues for escalating your concerns. It is important to remember these options are available regardless of what you are told by district personnel. The first option is to file a TEA State Complaint. This is a formal written complaint to the Texas Education Agency alleging specific violations of special education law. The TEA will investigate your complaint and issue a decision within 60 days. This is a free option that requires you to gather your evidence and write a detailed account of the issues.

A second option is to request mediation through the TEA. Mediation is a voluntary process in which you and Lubbock ISD meet with a neutral mediator to attempt to resolve your dispute. The mediator facilitates communication and helps you and the district explore potential solutions. TEA provides mediators for free. Third, you have the right to request a Due Process Hearing. This is a more formal legal proceeding in which you present evidence and arguments to an impartial hearing officer, who will make a binding decision on your case. Finally, you may file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR) if you believe Lubbock ISD has discriminated against your child based on their disability. OCR investigations can take a very long time to be resolved, however.

Frequently Asked Questions About Lubbock ISD Special Education

The questions every Lubbock ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Lubbock ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Lubbock ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Lubbock ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Lubbock ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Lubbock ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Lubbock ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Lubbock ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Lubbock ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
