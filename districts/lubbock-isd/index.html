About Special Education in Lubbock ISD

Lubbock ISD serves the West Texas area, providing a comprehensive range of special education services to students with disabilities. The district is committed to ensuring that all students have the opportunity to reach their full potential in a supportive and inclusive environment. Lubbock ISD operates under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA) Region 17, adhering to all state and federal guidelines for special education.

The Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) process in Lubbock ISD is designed to be collaborative and student-centered. Parents are integral members of the ARD committee. After a referral, the district has approximately 45 school days to complete the Full and Individual Evaluation (FIE) to determine eligibility for special education services. Parents will receive at least a five-day advance written notice of any ARD committee meeting.

Common Questions from Lubbock ISD Families

What if I disagree with the ARD committee's decision?

You have the right to disagree with the ARD committee's decisions. You can request another ARD meeting to further discuss your concerns. You also have the right to pursue other options, such as mediation or a formal complaint with the Texas Education Agency.

Where can I find information about specific special education programs offered within Lubbock ISD?

Your child's campus should be your first point of contact. You can also visit the Lubbock ISD website and search for "Special Education" to find information about district-wide programs and services. Contacting the district special education department directly is also an option.

My child needs assistive technology. How does Lubbock ISD determine what is appropriate?

The ARD committee will consider your child's individual needs and goals when determining if assistive technology is necessary. An evaluation may be conducted to determine the most appropriate technology to support your child's learning. The district provides a range of assistive technology options.

Practical Tip for Lubbock ISD Parents

Lubbock ISD is a large district with over 27,000 students. If you have questions or concerns about your child's special education services, start by contacting the special education campus coordinator at your child's school. These coordinators are the primary point of contact and can often resolve issues quickly and efficiently. Escalating directly to the district office may delay resolution, as they will typically direct you back to the campus level first.
