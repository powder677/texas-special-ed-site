BULLARD ISD child find obligations mean the district must actively search for and identify all children with disabilities, birth through age 21. This includes children in public schools, private schools, and homeschooled children in the BULLARD area. You don't have to wait for your child to fail in school to request an evaluation.

Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §29.001, BULLARD ISD must provide a free and appropriate public education to all eligible students with disabilities. If you suspect your child has a disability affecting their educational performance, BULLARD ISD must evaluate your child upon request.

The BULLARD ISD child find process ensures no child falls through the cracks. Whether your child attends a BULLARD ISD campus or is educated at home, you have the right to request an evaluation.

Parents often wonder whether their child truly needs an evaluation. Consider requesting a BULLARD ISD special education evaluation if your child demonstrates any of the following:

  • Significant difficulty keeping up academically compared to peers
  • Speech, language, or communication delays
  • Motor skill delays (fine or gross motor)
  • Behavioral or emotional concerns affecting learning
  • Difficulty with attention, focus, or impulse control
  • Social interaction or peer relationship challenges
  • Vision or hearing concerns impacting learning
  • Suspected autism spectrum disorder (ASD) or developmental delays

You know your child best. If you're concerned, BULLARD ISD staff cannot refuse your request for evaluation based on the child's current grades or perceived abilities.

Common Reasons Parents Request Evaluations in BULLARD ISD

Parents in BULLARD ISD request evaluations for various reasons. Some children show clear developmental delays, while others struggle in traditional classroom settings. Some have received outside diagnoses and want BULLARD ISD to recognize these needs for school services.

Step-by-Step: How to Request a BULLARD ISD FIE Evaluation

The process for requesting a BULLARD ISD FIE evaluation is straightforward. Here are the essential steps:

Step 1: Make Your Request in Writing

Submit a written request to your child's school principal or the BULLARD ISD Special Education Department. While verbal requests may start the process, always follow up with written documentation. A simple letter stating you request a Full Individual Evaluation for your child is sufficient.

Include your child's name, date of birth, current grade, and campus. Briefly describe your concerns—for example, "My child struggles with reading comprehension and needs to be evaluated for possible learning disabilities." Keep it concise and factual.

Address your letter to:

  • Your child's campus principal, AND
  • BULLARD ISD Special Education Director

Hand-deliver it if possible and request a dated receipt. Email is acceptable, but request a confirmation of receipt to have proof of your request date.

Step 2: Understand the BULLARD ISD 45 Day Timeline

Once BULLARD ISD receives your written request for evaluation, the district has a critical deadline: 45 calendar days to complete the BULLARD ISD FIE evaluation and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committee meeting to discuss results.

The BULLARD ISD 45 day timeline begins the day your written request arrives. This period includes:

  • Completing all assessments and evaluations
  • Gathering information from teachers and parents
  • Analyzing results and writing reports
  • Scheduling and conducting the ARD meeting

The 45 days may be extended by up to 20 additional days only if your child is absent for a significant portion of the evaluation period or if a licensed specialist requires more time for specific assessments.

Step 3: Provide Written Consent

BULLARD ISD will send you a Notice of Proposed Evaluation or Norwood Notice explaining the types of assessments your child will receive. You must provide written consent before evaluations begin.

Review this notice carefully. It describes:

  • Specific tests and assessments planned
  • Areas of concern being evaluated
  • Your rights and procedural safeguards
  • Timelines for completion

You can request clarification about any proposed assessment. If you disagree with the proposed evaluation, you can refuse to consent, but BULLARD ISD can pursue due process if they believe the evaluation is necessary.

Step 4: Participate in the Evaluation Process

Once you consent, BULLARD ISD special education staff will complete assessments. Many evaluations include:

  • Cognitive testing - Intelligence and reasoning abilities
  • Achievement testing - Academic skills in reading, math, writing
  • Language assessment - Speech and language development
  • Behavioral rating scales - Emotional and social functioning
  • Classroom observations - How your child performs in academic settings
  • Parent and teacher questionnaires - Information from people who know your child well

You may be asked to complete questionnaires or participate in interviews. Your input is valuable to the evaluation process at BULLARD ISD.

Understanding BULLARD ISD Special Education Categories

The BULLARD ISD special education evaluation process determines whether your child qualifies for services under IDEA. Texas recognizes 13 eligibility categories under the special education framework:

  • Speech or Language Impairment (SLI)
  • Intellectual Disability (ID)
  • Specific Learning Disability (SLD)
  • Emotional Disturbance (ED)
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
  • Other Health Impairment (OHI)
  • Orthopedic Impairment (OI)
  • Visual Impairment (VI)
  • Hearing Impairment (HI)
  • Deaf-Blindness
  • Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI)
  • Multiple Disabilities (MD)
  • Non-Categorical Early Childhood (NCEC) - ages 3-5 only

BULLARD ISD must evaluate across all suspected areas of disability, not just one category. The ARD committee determines eligibility based on evaluation results.

The ARD Meeting: What to Expect in BULLARD ISD

Within the BULLARD ISD 45 day timeline, the district will schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee meeting to discuss evaluation results. This is your opportunity to hear findings and plan your child's special education services if they qualify.

Who Attends BULLARD ISD ARD Meetings?

BULLARD ISD requires specific team members to attend:

  • Parent(s) or guardian(s)
  • General education teacher(s)
  • Special education teacher or BULLARD ISD special education director
  • District representative with authority to commit resources
  • Someone who can interpret evaluation results
  • The student (when appropriate)

You may bring an advocate, attorney, or support person to your BULLARD ISD ARD meeting. Having another set of ears helps you understand complex information and advocate effectively for your child.

What Happens During the ARD Meeting

The BULLARD ISD ARD committee will:

  1. Review all evaluation data collected during the BULLARD ISD special education evaluation
  2. Discuss whether your child meets eligibility criteria under IDEA
  3. If eligible, develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) with specific goals and services
  4. Determine the appropriate educational setting (least restrictive environment)
  5. Establish how progress will be measured

If your child is found eligible, you'll leave the meeting with a completed IEP. If ineligible, BULLARD ISD must explain why and discuss other options like a 504 Plan or Response to Intervention (RTI) services.

Your Rights During the BULLARD ISD FIE Evaluation Process

Federal law grants parents specific rights throughout the BULLARD ISD evaluation request process. Understanding these protections empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Key Parent Rights in BULLARD ISD Special Education

  • Right to request independent evaluations - If you disagree with BULLARD ISD's evaluation, you can obtain an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at the district's expense
  • Right to due process - You can file a due process complaint if you disagree with BULLARD ISD's decisions
  • Right to prior written notice - BULLARD ISD must notify you in writing of any changes to identification, evaluation, or placement
  • Right to participation - You must be meaningfully involved in all decisions about your child in BULLARD ISD
  • Right to interpreter services - If your home language isn't English, BULLARD ISD must provide interpretation
  • Right to records access - You can inspect and copy all records related to your child

What If BULLARD ISD Denies Your Evaluation Request?

BULLARD ISD cannot legally deny a parent's request for a Full Individual Evaluation. If the district claims your child doesn't need evaluation, this violates federal law. However, BULLARD ISD can refuse to evaluate if:

  • You previously received an evaluation and sufficient time hasn't passed since the last evaluation (typically one year)
  • You've already agreed your child doesn't need further evaluation

If you face resistance from BULLARD ISD, request the denial in writing and document all communication. Consider reaching out to a special education attorney near BULLARD or a special ed advocate BULLARD for guidance.

Resources and Support for BULLARD ISD Families

Parents in BULLARD ISD don't have to navigate special education alone. Several resources can support you through the evaluation process:

BULLARD ISD Special Education Department

Contact your BULLARD ISD Special Education Director directly with questions about the BULLARD ISD FIE evaluation process or your child's evaluation status. The special education office can provide copies of evaluation timelines and explain procedures specific to BULLARD ISD.

Independent Advocates and Attorneys

A special ed advocate BULLARD can represent your interests at ARD meetings and help you understand evaluation results without the cost of legal fees. If you need stronger representation, a special education attorney near BULLARD provides legal counsel and can represent you in disputes with BULLARD ISD.

Parent Training and Information Centers

Texas Parent to Parent and other statewide organizations offer free training on special education rights and the evaluation process. These resources help parents in BULLARD ISD understand complex procedures and advocacy strategies.

External Evaluations

You can obtain evaluations from outside providers (pediatricians, developmental pediatricians, psychologists, speech-language pathologists) and bring results to BULLARD ISD. While BULLARD ISD isn't bound by outside evaluations, they must consider this information when determining eligibility.

Common Questions About BULLARD ISD Special Education Evaluation

How long does a BULLARD ISD FIE evaluation actually take?

The BULLARD ISD 45 day timeline is the legal requirement, but actual evaluation time depends on complexity. Simple cases might be completed faster, while comprehensive evaluations touching multiple areas may use the full 45 days.

Can I request reevaluation while my child is already receiving BULLARD ISD special education services?

Yes. You can request BULLARD ISD special education evaluation updates every three years automatically, or more frequently if needed. If you believe your child's needs have changed significantly, request a reevaluation sooner.

What if my child has a diagnosis from an outside doctor?

Medical diagnoses don't automatically qualify a child for BULLARD ISD special education. However, BULLARD ISD must conduct a Full Individual Evaluation to determine if the diagnosis affects educational performance. Bring any outside diagnoses and medical records to support your request.

Can I bring someone to help me during the ARD meeting?

Absolutely. You have the right to bring an advocate, attorney, family member, or friend to your BULLARD ISD ARD meeting. Additional support helps you understand complex information and ensures your voice is heard.

Moving Forward: Taking Action in BULLARD ISD

If you believe your child needs evaluation, don't delay. Document your concerns, send a written request for a BULLARD ISD FIE evaluation to your campus and the special education office, and keep copies of everything.

Remember: The BULLARD ISD 45 day timeline protects you by ensuring timely evaluation. BULLARD ISD cannot stall or postpone indefinitely. Your child's educational needs are important, and the evaluation process is designed to identify and address them.

Stay engaged throughout the process. Ask questions, request clarification, and participate actively in your child's evaluation and ARD meetings. Parents in BULLARD ISD who take an active role in special education advocacy see better outcomes for their children.

Your child deserves an appropriate education tailored to their unique needs. A BULLARD ISD special education evaluation is the first step toward ensuring they receive the support necessary to learn and succeed.
