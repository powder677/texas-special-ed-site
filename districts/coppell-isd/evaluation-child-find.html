How long does Texas have to complete an evaluation?
Once a written evaluation request is received, the district must complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) and hold an ARD meeting within 45 school days. This timeline is strictly enforced by the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Verbal requests do not start the clock — always submit in writing and keep a copy.

Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in Coppell ISD

This guide outlines the process for requesting a special education evaluation for your child within Coppell ISD, adhering to Texas state law and the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). If you suspect your child may have a disability that impacts their ability to learn, you have the right to request an evaluation to determine eligibility for special education services.

How to Submit a Written Request

The first step is to submit a formal, written request for an evaluation to Coppell ISD. This request should be addressed to the school principal or the Special Education Director. While a formal form may exist (check the CISD website), a detailed letter is perfectly acceptable. The letter should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth.
  • Your child's school and grade level.
  • A clear statement requesting a Full and Individual Evaluation (FIE) to determine eligibility for special education services under IDEA.
  • A detailed description of your concerns regarding your child's academic, behavioral, social-emotional, or physical development. Include specific examples of difficulties your child is experiencing. For instance, "My child is significantly behind in reading compared to their classmates and struggles with phonics despite repeated interventions at home."
  • Any previous interventions attempted (e.g., tutoring, Response to Intervention (RtI) strategies).
  • Copies of relevant documentation, such as report cards, previous evaluations (even if not special education related), medical records (if applicable), and work samples.
  • Your contact information, including your phone number and email address.

Keep a copy of your request for your records. Sending the request via certified mail with a return receipt requested is highly recommended, as it provides proof of delivery.

The 45 School Day Timeline

Upon receiving your written request, Coppell ISD has a specific timeline they must adhere to under Texas law. Within 15 school days of receiving your request, the school must provide you with written notice regarding their decision to either proceed with the evaluation or deny the request. If they choose to evaluate, they must also provide you with an "Assessment Plan" that outlines the specific areas of assessment to be conducted.

You must provide your consent to the Assessment Plan before the evaluation can begin. Once you provide consent, the school has 45 school days to complete the Full and Individual Evaluation (FIE) and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committee meeting to discuss the evaluation results and determine your child's eligibility for special education services. "School days" exclude weekends, holidays, and days that school is closed.

What the FIE Must Cover

The Full and Individual Evaluation (FIE) must assess all areas related to the suspected disability. This may include, but is not limited to:

  • Academic achievement (reading, writing, math)
  • Cognitive abilities (intellectual functioning)
  • Speech and language skills
  • Motor skills (fine and gross motor)
  • Social-emotional functioning
  • Behavioral functioning
  • Adaptive behavior (daily living skills)
  • Vision and hearing screening

The evaluations must be conducted by qualified professionals. You have the right to request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at public expense if you disagree with the school's evaluation. However, this process has specific guidelines and procedures that must be followed; it's best to consult with a special education advocate or attorney if you choose to pursue this option.

Child Find Obligations

Coppell ISD has a legal obligation under "Child Find" to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities residing within its boundaries who are in need of special education and related services, regardless of whether they are enrolled in public school. This includes children who are homeschooled or attend private schools. Your request triggers this obligation, ensuring your child is considered for evaluation and potential services.

What If They Refuse

If Coppell ISD refuses to conduct an evaluation, they must provide you with written notice of their refusal, including the reasons for their decision. You have the right to challenge this decision through various dispute resolution processes, including mediation, filing a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA), or requesting a due process hearing. Consulting with a special education advocate or attorney is highly recommended if your request is denied.

After the Evaluation

After the FIE is complete, the ARD committee will meet to review the results. The ARD committee includes you (the parent), school administrators, teachers, and evaluation specialists. The ARD committee will determine if your child meets the eligibility criteria for special education services under one of the 13 disability categories defined by IDEA. If eligible, the ARD committee will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) tailored to your child's specific needs. The IEP will outline specific goals, accommodations, and services your child will receive. You are an integral part of the ARD committee, and your input is crucial in developing an appropriate IEP for your child.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Texas? +

Submit a written request directly to the school's principal or special education director. The written request starts the mandatory 45 school-day clock. Keep a dated copy for your records.

What is a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in Texas? +

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment the school must complete to determine if your child has a disability and needs special education services. It must cover all areas of suspected disability and be completed within 45 school days of your written consent.

Can a Texas school refuse to evaluate my child? +

Yes, but only if the district provides written notice (Prior Written Notice) explaining why they are refusing and what data they relied on. You have the right to challenge that refusal through mediation or a due process hearing.
