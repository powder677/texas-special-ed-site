Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

Discipline Rights & Grievances in Coppell ISD: An Urgent Guide

Discipline Rights & Grievances in Coppell ISD: An Urgent Guide

If your child is facing disciplinary action in Coppell ISD, act NOW. This is NOT the time to be passive. Understanding your child's rights and the grievance process is crucial to protecting their educational future. This guide is designed for parents in crisis mode.

The 10-Day Suspension Rule

Coppell ISD, like all Texas schools, has limitations on suspensions. A school district CANNOT unilaterally suspend a student for more than 10 school days in a school year without triggering additional protections, especially if your child has an Individualized Education Program (IEP) or a Section 504 plan. Keep meticulous track of suspension days. Each day counts.

If your child has already been suspended for close to 10 days, be extremely vigilant. Any further suspension, even for a single day, should raise immediate red flags. Consult an attorney or special education advocate immediately.

What Is an MDR (Mandatory Disciplinary Removal)?

An MDR, or Mandatory Disciplinary Removal, is a serious consequence in Coppell ISD. This means your child is removed from their regular classroom setting and placed in an alternative educational setting (like DAEP - Disciplinary Alternative Education Program) or even expelled. MDRs are typically triggered by specific violations, often involving drugs, weapons, or serious physical harm. These are serious charges, and the consequences can be devastating.

If your child is facing an MDR, DO NOT WAIT. Secure legal representation IMMEDIATELY. The school district will likely have its legal team prepared. You need to level the playing field.

Change of Placement (Especially for Students with IEPs or 504 Plans)

For students with disabilities covered by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) or Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, any disciplinary action that constitutes a "Change of Placement" triggers significant protections. A Change of Placement occurs when a student is:

  • Suspended for more than 10 consecutive school days.
  • Subjected to a series of suspensions that create a pattern totaling more than 10 school days in a school year.
  • Placed in an alternative educational setting for more than 45 school days.

IMPORTANT: If a Change of Placement is being considered, the school MUST conduct a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This meeting determines whether the misconduct was a manifestation of your child's disability. If the misconduct IS a manifestation of the disability, the school MUST take steps to address the underlying issues and cannot simply punish your child. They must revisit the IEP or 504 plan.

Demand a Manifestation Determination Review in WRITING, immediately, if your child is being disciplined and has an IEP or 504 plan. Do not rely on verbal assurances.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe Coppell ISD has violated your child's rights (regarding discipline, special education services, or any other issue), you have the right to file a grievance. Start with a Level 1 Grievance. This typically involves submitting a written complaint to the school principal or the administrator directly involved in the decision you're disputing.

Be clear, concise, and factual. State the specific policy or law you believe was violated. Include dates, times, and names of individuals involved. Keep a copy of everything you submit. Time is of the essence. Adhere strictly to any deadlines outlined in the Coppell ISD policy manual (usually found on the district website).

TEA State Complaint

If you are unsatisfied with the outcome of the Level 1 grievance (or if you believe the situation warrants immediate attention), you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). The TEA has a process for investigating complaints of alleged violations of state or federal laws and regulations related to education. Research the TEA complaint process on their website. There are strict timelines involved and the TEA requires specific evidence to begin an investigation.

Due Process

Regardless of whether your child has an IEP or a 504 plan, they are entitled to due process. This means they have the right to a fair hearing, the right to present evidence, and the right to challenge the school district's actions. Fight for your child's right to due process at every stage. Do not be intimidated. Document everything. Seek professional assistance if needed. Your child's future depends on it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
