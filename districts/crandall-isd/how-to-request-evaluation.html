Parents of students in CRANDALL ISD can request an evaluation (FIE - Full Individual Evaluation) when they suspect their child has a disability affecting their academic performance. A formal evaluation is the first step in determining if your student qualifies for special education services.

Common reasons to request a CRANDALL ISD special education evaluation include:

  • Language or reading developmental delay
  • Difficulties with math or processing
  • Behavioral or attention problems
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
  • Emotional or social difficulties
  • Low performance despite classroom interventions

Federal law (IDEA - Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) and Texas state laws guarantee specific rights to parents. These CRANDALL ISD parental rights include the right to participate in all decisions regarding your child's education.

Key Rights in CRANDALL ISD

Right to request an evaluation: Any parent or teacher in CRANDALL ISD can formally request a special education evaluation at any time during the school year.

Right to timely evaluation: Once CRANDALL ISD receives your written request, the district has 15 school days to obtain your informed consent and 60 calendar days to complete the evaluation.

Right to information in your language: CRANDALL ISD must provide all documents and notifications in Spanish if English is not your primary language.

Right to an independent evaluator: If you disagree with the results of the CRANDALL ISD evaluation, you may request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at public expense.

Step-by-Step Process for Requesting an Evaluation in CRANDALL ISD

Step 1: Prepare Your Written Request

The first step is to send a formal written request to CRANDALL ISD. This request must be specific and clear. Do not rely on verbal conversations; the law requires written documentation to activate legal evaluation timelines.

In your request, include:

  • Student's full name and age
  • Parent/guardian's name and phone number
  • Specific description of academic or behavioral concerns
  • Concrete examples of how these difficulties affect learning
  • Any relevant private evaluations or diagnoses
  • Explicit request for a CRANDALL ISD special education evaluation (FIE)

Step 2: Deliver the Request to the Special Education Coordinator

Go to your child's school in CRANDALL ISD and ask for the name of the special education coordinator or director of special services. Deliver your request in person and ask for a receipt confirming the date of reception. You can also send it by certified mail or email with a read receipt.

Maintaining documented records is crucial. Keep copies of everything you send to CRANDALL ISD regarding special education.

Step 3: Await the Informed Consent Notification

Within 15 school days after your request, CRANDALL ISD must send you a Notice of Informed Consent (Consent to Evaluate) in Spanish. This document explains:

  • What areas will be evaluated
  • What evaluation methods will be used
  • Your rights as a parent during the process
  • The proposed timeline

Read this document carefully. You must sign and return this consent for CRANDALL ISD to proceed with the evaluation.

Step 4: The Evaluation is Conducted

Once the signed consent is returned, CRANDALL ISD has 60 calendar days to complete the evaluation. This 60-day window does not include extended school closure days.

The evaluation in CRANDALL ISD typically includes:

  • Psychoeducational tests of cognitive abilities
  • Reading and math assessments
  • Language evaluation (if appropriate)
  • Social-emotional behavior assessment
  • Review of school records and medical history
  • Classroom observations

You can request to be kept informed of the progress and may ask to see preliminary results before the meeting.

Step 5: Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) Committee Meeting

After the evaluation is completed, CRANDALL ISD will schedule an ARD meeting with you. In this meeting, the multidisciplinary team from CRANDALL ISD will discuss the results and determine if your child qualifies for special education services under IDEA law.

You have the right to bring someone else to this meeting, such as a private specialist, special education advocate, or special education attorney near CRANDALL.

Key Information about CRANDALL ISD and Special Education

CRANDALL ISD, located in the CRANDALL, Texas area, serves 6,922 students and is committed to providing quality CRANDALL ISD special education services. The district has established procedures for handling evaluation requests and complies with Texas state special education law.

According to the Texas Education Code (Texas Education Code § 29.001), all students with disabilities have the right to a free and appropriate public education. Parents in CRANDALL ISD can request an evaluation at any time if they have concerns.

What Happens After the ARD Meeting

If Your Child Qualifies for Services

If the CRANDALL ISD team determines your child is eligible, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be developed. This CRANDALL ISD special education document specifies:

  • The services your child will receive
  • The time spent in special education vs. general education
  • Annual academic goals
  • Accommodations and modifications
  • How progress will be measured

If Your Child Does Not Qualify

If CRANDALL ISD determines your child does not qualify under IDEA law, they may still qualify under Section 504 or you can request an Independent Educational Evaluation. You have the right to a copy of all evaluation results, regardless of the decision.
