Child Find is a federal requirement under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) that obligates LEVELLAND ISD to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with suspected disabilities. This means LEVELLAND ISD must actively look for students who may need special education services, even if parents haven't formally requested an evaluation.

As a parent in LEVELLAND ISD, you have the fundamental right to request that your child be evaluated for special education eligibility at any time. This right is protected under both federal law and Texas Education Code §29.001. You don't need to wait for your child to fail or fall significantly behind before requesting help.

What This Means for LEVELLAND ISD Families

LEVELLAND ISD families should know that requesting a LEVELLAND ISD evaluation request doesn't automatically place your child in special education. Instead, it begins a process to determine whether your child qualifies for services under one or more disability categories recognized by IDEA.

Several parties can initiate a LEVELLAND ISD child find evaluation:

  • Parents or guardians – You can request an evaluation for your child at any time during the school year
  • LEVELLAND ISD teachers – Classroom teachers observing learning difficulties may recommend evaluation
  • School counselors and administrators – School staff can formally refer your child for assessment
  • Doctors and outside service providers – Medical professionals can recommend evaluation to LEVELLAND ISD
  • You, as a parent – This is your most direct option and gives you immediate control over the process

If you believe your child needs assessment, don't wait for school staff to initiate the process. Taking action yourself ensures LEVELLAND ISD begins the LEVELLAND ISD FIE evaluation timeline immediately.

The LEVELLAND ISD 45-Day Evaluation Timeline Explained

One of the most critical aspects of requesting a LEVELLAND ISD evaluation is understanding the legal timeline. Under IDEA and Texas Education Code, LEVELLAND ISD must complete the full evaluation within 45 calendar days from when they receive your written request.

What the 45-Day Clock Includes

The LEVELLAND ISD 45 day timeline encompasses everything needed to complete the LEVELLAND ISD special education evaluation:

  • Obtaining your written consent to evaluate
  • Conducting all required assessments and observations
  • Gathering information from teachers, parents, and other service providers
  • Compiling results and preparing an Evaluation Report
  • Holding an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss findings

The clock does not stop during school breaks if those breaks are fewer than 10 school days. However, LEVELLAND ISD may count only school days rather than calendar days in certain circumstances, so clarify this when you submit your request.

What Happens if LEVELLAND ISD Misses the Deadline

If LEVELLAND ISD doesn't complete your child's evaluation within 45 days, this is a procedural violation. You have the right to file a due process complaint or request compensatory services. Document all delays in writing and keep copies of all correspondence with LEVELLAND ISD about the evaluation timeline.

The process of submitting a LEVELLAND ISD evaluation request is straightforward, but following proper procedures protects your rights and ensures LEVELLAND ISD takes your request seriously.

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Put Your Request in Writing – Email, hand-deliver, or send certified mail to your child's school principal or the LEVELLAND ISD Special Education Director. A written request is essential; verbal requests may not trigger the legal timeline.
  2. Be Specific About Your Concerns – Explain why you believe your child may have a disability. Mention specific behaviors, learning difficulties, or developmental concerns you've observed.
  3. Request All Necessary Evaluations – Ask LEVELLAND ISD to assess all areas of suspected disability (academic achievement, cognitive abilities, speech/language, motor skills, social-emotional functioning, etc.).
  4. Keep Copies – Retain a copy of your written request and any receipt or confirmation of delivery. This documents when LEVELLAND ISD received your request and when the 45-day clock started.
  5. Wait for Consent Documentation – LEVELLAND ISD must provide you with a Notice of Proposed Evaluation and request your written consent. You must sign this before testing begins.

Important Contact Information for LEVELLAND ISD

When requesting a LEVELLAND ISD special education evaluation, you'll want to contact the right department. Start with your school's principal or counselor, then reach out directly to the LEVELLAND ISD Special Education Department. Having the direct contact ensures your request is properly logged and tracked.

For parents in LEVELLAND ISD, keeping detailed records of all communications is crucial. Create a folder (physical or digital) containing copies of every email, letter, and note related to your LEVELLAND ISD evaluation request.

What to Expect During the Evaluation Process

Once you've submitted your LEVELLAND ISD child find request and LEVELLAND ISD has obtained your consent, your child will undergo various assessments based on their suspected disability areas.

Typical Components of a LEVELLAND ISD FIE Evaluation

  • Cognitive Assessment – Tests measuring overall intelligence and thinking skills
  • Academic Achievement Testing – Evaluations in reading, math, writing, and other subjects
  • Speech and Language Evaluation – Assessment of communication skills if needed
  • Social-Emotional and Behavioral Assessment – Screening for emotional or behavioral disorders
  • Motor Skills Evaluation – Testing fine and gross motor development if appropriate
  • Classroom Observations – Special education staff observing your child in educational settings
  • Parent and Teacher Input – Questionnaires and interviews with adults who know your child well

LEVELLAND ISD evaluators must use multiple measures and cannot rely on a single test to determine disability. This multi-faceted approach ensures a comprehensive picture of your child's strengths and needs.

Understanding Eligibility Categories in LEVELLAND ISD Special Education

After LEVELLAND ISD completes the LEVELLAND ISD FIE evaluation, your child's results must be compared against specific disability categories. Texas recognizes 13 disability categories under which students may qualify for special education services.

Common Eligibility Categories

LEVELLAND ISD can identify students with:

  • Specific Learning Disability (SLD) – Significant discrepancy between ability and achievement in specific academic areas
  • Speech or Language Impairment – Difficulties with communication that affect education
  • Intellectual Disability – Significantly below-average intellectual functioning affecting adaptive behavior
  • Emotional Disturbance – Behavioral or emotional patterns significantly affecting school performance
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder – Challenges in social communication and repetitive behaviors
  • Other Health Impairment – Conditions like ADHD affecting educational performance
  • Orthopedic Impairment – Physical disabilities affecting school participation

Not every child who struggles in school qualifies for special education. LEVELLAND ISD must determine that your child has a disability that adversely affects educational performance and requires specially designed instruction.

The ARD Meeting: Understanding LEVELLAND ISD's Next Steps

Once LEVELLAND ISD completes the evaluation, they must hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting within the 45-day timeline. This meeting is critical for parents in LEVELLAND ISD, as it determines eligibility and plans your child's special education services.

What Happens at the ARD Meeting

The ARD committee includes you (as parent), your child's teacher, a special education specialist, a school administrator, and sometimes your child. During this meeting, the team reviews evaluation results and decides whether your child qualifies for special education.

If LEVELLAND ISD determines your child is eligible, you'll develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) outlining services, goals, and accommodations. If LEVELLAND ISD determines ineligibility, you have the right to request an independent evaluation at district expense.

Your Rights During the ARD Meeting

  • Request an independent evaluator or advocate to attend
  • Ask questions about evaluation findings and recommendations
  • Take time to review documents before making decisions
  • Request a second ARD meeting if you need more time or information
  • Disagree with LEVELLAND ISD's conclusions and request due process

Common Concerns Parents in LEVELLAND ISD Face

Parents seeking a LEVELLAND ISD special education evaluation often have similar questions and concerns. Addressing these helps you navigate the process confidently.

What If LEVELLAND ISD Says My Child Doesn't Need Evaluation?

LEVELLAND ISD cannot refuse a parent-requested evaluation based on academic performance alone. If you believe your child may have a disability, you can insist on the LEVELLAND ISD FIE evaluation. Put your request in writing and reference Texas Education Code §29.004, which protects your right to evaluation.

Will Evaluation Label or Stigmatize My Child?

A LEVELLAND ISD child find evaluation identifies whether your child qualifies for services—it doesn't assign judgment. Many children benefit tremendously from special education support. The goal is understanding your child's needs and providing appropriate instruction.

What If My Child Qualifies But I Don't Want Services?

You can decline special education services after LEVELLAND ISD finds your child eligible. However, this decision should be made carefully with full understanding of available support. Consult with LEVELLAND ISD staff and advocates before declining needed services.

Taking Action: Your Next Steps

If you believe your child may benefit from a LEVELLAND ISD evaluation, don't delay. Early identification and intervention lead to better outcomes for children with disabilities.

  1. Document Your Concerns – Write down specific behaviors, learning difficulties, or developmental delays you've observed
  2. Request the Evaluation in Writing – Email or mail your LEVELLAND ISD evaluation request to your school principal and special education department
  3. Keep Records – Save copies of all correspondence related to the LEVELLAND ISD special education evaluation
  4. Get Support – Connect with a special ed advocate LEVELLAND parents recommend or contact advocacy organizations
  5. Prepare for the ARD Meeting – Review evaluation results before the meeting and write down questions or concerns

For parents in LEVELLAND ISD navigating special education, remember that you are your child's strongest advocate. Understanding the evaluation process, knowing your rights, and taking proactive steps ensures your child receives the support they need to succeed.
