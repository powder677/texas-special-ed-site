Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

Discipline Rights & Grievances in Tyler ISD: Urgent Guide

If your child is facing disciplinary action in Tyler ISD, especially a suspension or potential change of placement, you need to act NOW. This guide outlines your rights and how to navigate the grievance process. Time is of the essence!

The 10-Day Suspension Rule

Tyler ISD can suspend your child for up to 3 days without holding a formal hearing. However, if your child accumulates more than 10 days of suspension in a school year (even for seemingly minor offenses), this triggers significant procedural safeguards, especially if your child has an IEP or 504 plan. Track the number of suspension days your child has received! Anything approaching or exceeding 10 days demands immediate and proactive measures on your part.

What Is an MDR?

MDR stands for Manifestation Determination Review. This is CRITICAL if your child has an IEP (Individualized Education Program) or 504 plan AND is facing a suspension that exceeds 10 cumulative school days OR any disciplinary action that constitutes a Change of Placement.

The MDR meeting MUST determine if the behavior leading to the discipline was:

  • Caused by or directly related to the child's disability, OR
  • A result of the school's failure to implement the IEP.

If either of these is TRUE, the school CANNOT proceed with the disciplinary action in the same way as a student without an IEP/504. The IEP team MUST review the IEP and make necessary adjustments. Demand this meeting IMMEDIATELY if a long-term suspension or expulsion is threatened. Don't let them skip this step!

Change of Placement

A Change of Placement occurs when a student is removed from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days, or when a series of removals creates a pattern that exceeds 10 days and constitutes a significant change in the student's education program. Examples include expulsion, alternative education placement (DAEP), or any removal that significantly disrupts your child's ability to access their education. A Change of Placement triggers automatic protections under IDEA, even if your child has less than 10 cumulative suspension days.

If a Change of Placement is proposed, the school MUST conduct an MDR. This is NOT optional. If they try to bypass this, contact an attorney or disability rights advocate immediately!

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe Tyler ISD has violated your child's rights or failed to follow proper procedures, you need to file a grievance. Start at Level 1, which is typically with the Principal.

IMMEDIATELY put your grievance in writing. State the specific policy or procedure that you believe was violated, the facts of the situation, and the remedy you are seeking. Keep a copy of everything you submit, and document the date and time you submitted it. Include dates, names, and specific details of the incident and any witnesses.

Don't delay! Grievance procedures have strict timelines. Find the Tyler ISD grievance policy on their website or request a copy from the school. Typically, you have a very short window (often just a few days) to file your Level 1 grievance.

TEA State Complaint

If you are unsatisfied with the outcome of the Level 1 grievance, or if you believe the school is violating federal or state law, you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This is a more formal process, and requires documenting everything and presenting a clear case of the violation. You will need concrete evidence.

The TEA complaint form is available on the TEA website. Be prepared to provide detailed information and documentation to support your complaint.

Due Process

All students, including those with disabilities, are entitled to due process. This means the school must follow fair procedures before suspending or expelling a student. You have the right to be notified of the charges against your child, to present evidence on their behalf, and to appeal the school's decision.

If your child is facing disciplinary action, DO NOT hesitate to seek legal advice. An attorney specializing in special education law can help you understand your rights and navigate the complex disciplinary process. Organizations like Disability Rights Texas can also provide valuable assistance and advocacy.

Remember: YOU are your child's best advocate. Be proactive, document everything, and don't be afraid to fight for their rights. This is about their future.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
