How long does Texas have to complete an evaluation?
Once a written evaluation request is received, the district must complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) and hold an ARD meeting within 45 school days. This timeline is strictly enforced by the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Verbal requests do not start the clock — always submit in writing and keep a copy.

Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in Tyler ISD

If you have concerns about your child's academic, behavioral, or developmental progress in Tyler ISD, and you suspect they may have a disability that requires special education services, you have the right to request a special education evaluation. Understanding the process and your rights under Texas law is crucial. This guide outlines the steps to take.

How to Submit a Written Request

The most important step is to submit your request in writing. While you can initially express concerns verbally to your child's teacher or school administration, a written request officially starts the process and ensures a record of your request. The request should be addressed to the school principal, the special education director, or a designated special education administrator at your child's school. Be sure to keep a copy of the request for your records. Your letter should clearly state that you are requesting a Full and Individual Evaluation (FIE) for special education services. Clearly and specifically describe your child's difficulties, including specific examples of challenges at school and at home. Highlight any interventions or accommodations that have already been tried and their effectiveness (or lack thereof). Include your child's full name, date of birth, grade level, and the school they attend. Provide your contact information (phone number and email address) so the school can easily reach you. While not strictly required, it can be helpful to include any medical or psychological evaluations your child has already undergone.

The 45 School Day Timeline

Once the school receives your written request, they have a specific timeline to follow under Texas Education Code. First, within 15 school days, the school must decide whether to proceed with the evaluation or not. They will notify you in writing of their decision. If the school decides to evaluate, they must obtain your written consent to proceed. Once you provide consent for the evaluation, the school has 45 school days to complete the evaluation and determine your child's eligibility for special education services. This 45-day timeline is crucial, so keep track of the dates. School days are defined as days when students are in attendance, excluding holidays and weekends. If the school doesn't meet the timeline, you have the right to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

What the FIE Must Cover

The Full and Individual Evaluation (FIE) must assess all areas related to your child's suspected disability. This includes academic achievement, intellectual ability, adaptive behavior, communication skills, and any other areas where your child is experiencing difficulties. The evaluation must be conducted by qualified professionals, such as educational diagnosticians, speech-language pathologists, psychologists, and other specialists as needed. The evaluation process should use multiple assessment tools and methods, including standardized tests, observations, and interviews with parents, teachers, and the child. The FIE report must clearly document the assessment results, conclusions, and recommendations regarding eligibility for special education services. It should also detail the specific areas where your child needs support and the types of services that might be beneficial.

Child Find Obligations

Tyler ISD, like all Texas school districts, has a "Child Find" obligation under federal and state law. This means they have a proactive responsibility to identify, locate, and evaluate children with disabilities who may be in need of special education services, regardless of whether the child is enrolled in public school. This includes children who are homeschooled or attending private schools within the district. If you suspect a child (even if not your own) may have a disability and is not receiving appropriate services, you can report your concerns to the school district. The district is then obligated to investigate and determine if an evaluation is warranted.

What If They Refuse

If Tyler ISD refuses to conduct an evaluation, they must provide you with written notice explaining the reasons for their refusal. This notice must include information about your rights to disagree with the decision and how to pursue options such as mediation or due process. You have the right to request a meeting to discuss the decision. If you disagree with the school's decision, you can request mediation through the TEA. Mediation is a voluntary process where a neutral third party helps you and the school district reach an agreement. You can also file a formal complaint with the TEA. Finally, you have the right to request a due process hearing, which is a more formal legal proceeding where an impartial hearing officer will make a decision about whether the school is required to conduct an evaluation.

After the Evaluation

After the evaluation is complete, an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committee meeting will be scheduled. This committee includes you, school administrators, teachers, evaluators, and other relevant professionals. At the ARD meeting, the evaluation results will be discussed, and a determination will be made about whether your child is eligible for special education services. If your child is found eligible, the ARD committee will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) tailored to your child's specific needs. The IEP will outline the specific goals, services, and accommodations your child will receive. You have the right to actively participate in the development of the IEP and to disagree with any part of it. Remember to advocate for your child's needs and ensure the IEP is designed to help them succeed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Texas? +

Submit a written request directly to the school's principal or special education director. The written request starts the mandatory 45 school-day clock. Keep a dated copy for your records.

What is a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in Texas? +

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment the school must complete to determine if your child has a disability and needs special education services. It must cover all areas of suspected disability and be completed within 45 school days of your written consent.

Can a Texas school refuse to evaluate my child? +

Yes, but only if the district provides written notice (Prior Written Notice) explaining why they are refusing and what data they relied on. You have the right to challenge that refusal through mediation or a due process hearing.
