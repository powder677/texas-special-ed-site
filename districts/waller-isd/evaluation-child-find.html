Requesting an Evaluation in Waller ISD

⚡ Quick Answer

How long does Texas have to complete an evaluation?
Once a written evaluation request is received, the district must complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) and hold an ARD meeting within 45 school days. This timeline is strictly enforced by the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Verbal requests do not start the clock — always submit in writing and keep a copy.

Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in Waller ISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability that is impacting their academic performance and requires specialized instruction, you have the right to request a special education evaluation from Waller ISD. This guide outlines the process, timelines, and your rights as a parent under Texas law, specifically the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) as interpreted and implemented in Texas.

How to Submit a Written Request

The first crucial step is to submit a written request for a full and individual initial evaluation (FIIE) to the school. This request should be directed to the principal of your child's school, or to the district's Special Education Director. Keep a copy of the request for your records, preferably with proof of delivery (e.g., certified mail receipt or confirmation email if sent electronically).

Your written request should include the following information:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Your child's current grade level and teacher
  • Your child's school
  • A clear statement that you are requesting a full and individual initial evaluation for special education services. For example, "I am writing to request a full and individual initial evaluation to determine if my child, [Child's Name], is eligible for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA)."
  • A brief explanation of your concerns about your child's academic performance and/or behavior, outlining the specific challenges they face. Include examples of these challenges if possible. For example, "My child is struggling with reading comprehension, frequently fails spelling tests, and has difficulty focusing in class."
  • Any supporting documentation you have, such as medical diagnoses, previous evaluations, or work samples that demonstrate your concerns.
  • Your contact information, including phone number and email address.

While not legally required, including the above information strengthens your request and provides the school with a clear understanding of your concerns.

The 45 School Day Timeline

Under Texas law and IDEA, Waller ISD has a specific timeline to respond to your request. Upon receiving your written request, the school has 15 school days to provide you with written notice of whether they will conduct an evaluation. This notice is often referred to as an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committee meeting invitation.

If the ARD committee decides to evaluate, they then have 45 school days from the date you provided consent for the evaluation to complete the FIE and hold an ARD meeting to discuss the results and determine if your child is eligible for special education services. This timeline can be extended if the parent agrees in writing. It's important to understand that these are school days, meaning weekends, holidays, and school breaks are not included in the calculation.

What the FIE Must Cover

The Full and Individual Initial Evaluation (FIE) must be comprehensive and assess all areas related to the suspected disability. This may include assessments in areas such as:

  • Cognitive ability (IQ testing)
  • Academic achievement (reading, writing, math)
  • Language skills (receptive and expressive)
  • Social and emotional development
  • Adaptive behavior
  • Motor skills (fine and gross motor)
  • Speech and language
  • Hearing and vision screening

The FIE should be conducted by qualified professionals, such as educational diagnosticians, psychologists, speech-language pathologists, and occupational therapists. You have the right to review all evaluation data and reports prior to the ARD meeting.

Child Find Obligations

Waller ISD has a "Child Find" obligation under IDEA. This means they have a legal responsibility to actively identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities residing within the district, regardless of whether they are enrolled in public school. This obligation extends to children who are homeschooled or attending private schools within the district's boundaries. If you believe your child may have a disability, you can contact the district's special education department to inquire about Child Find services.

What If They Refuse

If Waller ISD refuses to conduct an evaluation, they must provide you with a written explanation of their reasons for refusal. You have the right to challenge this decision through various dispute resolution options, including mediation, a state complaint, or a due process hearing. Information on these options should be provided to you by the district. Consider seeking legal advice from a special education attorney or advocate to understand your rights and options.

After the Evaluation

Once the FIE is completed, an ARD committee meeting will be held to review the results. The committee, which includes you as the parent, will determine if your child meets the eligibility criteria for special education services under one of the disability categories recognized by IDEA. If your child is deemed eligible, the ARD committee will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) tailored to your child's specific needs. The IEP will outline goals, accommodations, modifications, and specialized services that will be provided to help your child succeed. Remember that the IEP is a living document and can be reviewed and revised as needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Texas? +

Submit a written request directly to the school's principal or special education director. The written request starts the mandatory 45 school-day clock. Keep a dated copy for your records.

What is a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in Texas? +

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment the school must complete to determine if your child has a disability and needs special education services. It must cover all areas of suspected disability and be completed within 45 school days of your written consent.

Can a Texas school refuse to evaluate my child? +

Yes, but only if the district provides written notice (Prior Written Notice) explaining why they are refusing and what data they relied on. You have the right to challenge that refusal through mediation or a due process hearing.
