How to Effectively Contact Waller ISD Special Education Staff

Communicating effectively with Waller ISD special education staff is crucial for your child's success. Here are some tips:

  • Best Times: Typically, before or after school hours (7:30-8:00 AM or 3:30-4:00 PM) are ideal for phone calls, as teachers are more available. Email is effective any time.
  • Email vs. Phone: For complex issues or detailed information, email allows for clear documentation. Use phone calls for urgent matters or quick questions.
  • Your First Message: Clearly state your child's name, grade, school, and the specific reason for your contact. Be polite and concise. For example: "Good morning, I'm [Your Name], parent of [Child's Name] in [Grade] at [School]. I'm contacting you regarding [Specific Issue]."

Important Tip: Always follow up any phone conversation with an email summarizing the discussion and any agreed-upon actions. This creates a written record of your interactions.

What to Do If You Can't Reach Anyone

If you are having trouble reaching someone at Waller ISD, follow these steps:

  1. Start with the Teacher: The classroom teacher is your primary contact.
  2. Campus Special Education Coordinator: If the teacher doesn't respond within a reasonable timeframe (2-3 business days), contact the campus special education coordinator.
  3. District Special Education Director: If you still don't receive a response, escalate your concern to the Waller ISD Special Education Director. Their contact information should be available on the Waller ISD website.
  4. Texas Education Agency (TEA): As a last resort, you can file a complaint with the TEA. See tea.texas.gov for information on filing a complaint.

Remember, Texas Education Code Chapter 29 outlines the rights and responsibilities regarding special education services. Familiarize yourself with this code.

Your Right to District Records

You have the right to access your child's educational records and district information.

  • FERPA: Under the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA), Waller ISD must provide you with access to your child's educational records within 45 days of your written request.
  • Public Information Act: The Texas Public Information Act (Texas Government Code, Chapter 552) allows you to request information from Waller ISD, including staff contact information not readily available.

To submit a records request, send a written request to the Waller ISD Special Education Department. Clearly state the specific records you are requesting and include your child's name, date of birth, and school. Include your contact information. Keep a copy of your request for your records.
