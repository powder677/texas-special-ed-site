Parents in TAYLOR ISD can request a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation) when they suspect their child has a disability that affects their learning. A comprehensive evaluation is the first step to accessing special education services.

Some signs that might indicate the need for an evaluation include:

  • Persistent difficulties with reading, writing, or math
  • Attention or behavior problems in class
  • Delays in language development or social skills
  • Difficulty following instructions or completing tasks
  • Low academic performance despite additional efforts

In TAYLOR ISD, with an enrollment of 2,930 students, the district is committed to identifying and supporting all students who need special education.

As a parent in TAYLOR ISD, you have fundamental rights under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and the Rehabilitation Act. These rights are guaranteed by federal and Texas state law.

Key Parental Rights

TAYLOR ISD parental rights include:

  • Right to request an evaluation: You can make a written request for TAYLOR ISD to evaluate your child at any time
  • Right to participation: You must participate in all meetings related to your child's evaluation and placement
  • Right to information: TAYLOR ISD must provide all information about evaluations in your language
  • Right to confidentiality: Your child's special education records are protected
  • Right to a second opinion: You can request an independent evaluation if you do not agree with TAYLOR ISD's results

Knowing these rights empowers you to effectively advocate for your child in TAYLOR ISD.

Steps to Request a Special Education Evaluation in TAYLOR ISD

Step 1: Contact Your Child's School

The first step is to speak with your child's teacher, school counselor, or administrator at their TAYLOR ISD school. Explain your concerns about your child's learning or behavior. Many schools in TAYLOR ISD offer general interventions before a formal referral for special education evaluation.

Ask for the name and phone number of the TAYLOR ISD special education coordinator at your campus. This is your main point of contact for the evaluation process.

Step 2: Submit a Formal Written Request

To officially initiate the evaluation process in TAYLOR ISD, you must submit a formal written request. This request can be sent to the school principal, the special education coordinator, or the TAYLOR ISD central office.

Your request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current grade and school in TAYLOR ISD
  • Your specific concerns about your child's learning or development
  • Behaviors or academic issues you have observed
  • Your contact information (phone and email)

Important recommendation: Send your request via certified mail or deliver it in person. Keep a copy for your records. This creates official documentation of your request for TAYLOR ISD.

Step 3: Await TAYLOR ISD's Response

By Texas law, TAYLOR ISD has 15 calendar days to respond to your special education evaluation request. During this time, the district must:

  • Acknowledge receipt of your request
  • Explain the evaluation process
  • Request your informed written consent
  • Provide information about your parental rights

If you do not receive a response from TAYLOR ISD within 15 days, contact the district's special education office directly.

Step 4: Provide Informed Consent

Before TAYLOR ISD can begin the evaluation, you must sign an informed consent form. This document should explain:

  • What evaluations will be conducted
  • How the results will be used
  • Your rights during the process
  • The process for requesting an independent evaluation

Take time to carefully read this document. If you have questions, ask the TAYLOR ISD special education coordinator for clarification.

Step 5: The Comprehensive Evaluation in TAYLOR ISD

TAYLOR ISD must complete the evaluation within 60 calendar days of receiving your consent. The comprehensive evaluation includes:

  • Cognitive and academic achievement evaluations
  • Language evaluations (if necessary)
  • Behavioral and social evaluations
  • Review of previous school records
  • Parental input on development and behavior at home
  • Physical or vision/hearing examination if appropriate

During this period, TAYLOR ISD may contact you for more information about your child. Cooperate fully and share important observations of behavior at home.

The Special Education Evaluation Process in TAYLOR ISD

The FIE evaluation in TAYLOR ISD is a comprehensive process designed to fully understand your child's needs.

Who Conducts the Evaluation?

TAYLOR ISD uses a multidisciplinary team that may include:

  • Educational psychologist or testing specialist
  • Special education teachers
  • Speech and language pathologist (if necessary)
  • Behavior specialist
  • Other specialists as needed

Areas of Evaluation

TAYLOR ISD will evaluate several key areas:

  • Cognitive development: Thinking and reasoning skills
  • Academic achievement: Reading, writing, math
  • Physical development: Motor skills and hearing/vision
  • Social and emotional development: Relationships, behavior, self-esteem
  • Language development: Comprehension and expression
  • General educational needs: How your child learns best

The Evaluation Results Meeting in TAYLOR ISD

After completing the evaluation, TAYLOR ISD will schedule a results meeting within the next 30 days. In this important meeting, you will learn about the findings and discuss the next steps.

What Happens at the Results Meeting

During this meeting, the TAYLOR ISD team will:

  • Present the evaluation results in understandable language
  • Explain what each result means for your child
  • Determine if your child qualifies for special education under a specific category
  • If they qualify, develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP)
  • If they do not qualify, discuss alternative support options

You have the right to bring a special education attorney, a special education advocate near TAYLOR, or a trusted friend to this meeting.

Eligibility Categories in Texas

If your child qualifies for special education in TAYLOR ISD, it will be under one of these categories:

  • Autism
  • Deaf-blindness
  • Emotional disturbance
  • Deafness
  • Hearing impairment
  • Intellectual disability
  • Other health impairment
  • Orthopedic impairment
  • Speech or language impairment
  • Visual impairment
  • Traumatic brain injury
  • Specific learning disability

Frequently Asked Questions about Special Education in TAYLOR ISD

How much does it cost to request an evaluation in TAYLOR ISD? The evaluation is completely free. TAYLOR ISD, as a public school district, must evaluate your child at no cost.

Can my child remain in their regular class during the evaluation? Yes. Unless there are extreme safety behaviors, your child will remain in their regular class during the evaluation process in TAYLOR ISD.

What if I disagree with TAYLOR ISD's results? You can request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at the district's expense. TAYLOR ISD will pay for an independent evaluation if you disagree with the results of their evaluation.

Can I get all information in Spanish? Yes. By law, TAYLOR ISD must provide all special education information in your preferred language.

Next Steps After the Evaluation in TAYLOR ISD

If your child qualifies for special education in TAYLOR ISD, the team will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). This document outlines:

  • The special education services your child will receive
  • Their annual educational goals
  • How progress will be measured
  • When the IEP will be reviewed (at least annually)

If your child does not qualify, TAYLOR ISD must discuss other support options, such as classroom interventions or Section 504 plans.

Remember: you are the expert on your child. Your active participation in the TAYLOR ISD evaluation process is essential to ensure your child receives the appropriate services. Do not hesitate to ask questions, seek clarification, and advocate for what your child needs in TAYLOR ISD.

For parents in TAYLOR ISD, navigating the special education process can seem overwhelming, but with correct information and support, you can ensure your child receives the evaluation and services they deserve.
