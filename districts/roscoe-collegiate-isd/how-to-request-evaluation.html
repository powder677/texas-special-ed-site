Parents in ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD can request an evaluation when they have concerns about their child's learning, behavior, or development. A formal evaluation determines if the student qualifies for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) or Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.

Common signs indicating the need for an evaluation include:

  • Difficulty keeping up academically with classmates
  • Persistent or challenging behavioral issues
  • Communication or language difficulties
  • Attention or concentration problems
  • Delays in motor or self-care skills
  • School anxiety or social problems

Federal and state law guarantees specific rights to parents in ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and the Texas Education Code, you have the right to:

  • Request a free evaluation at any time during the school year
  • Fully participate in all meetings related to evaluation and services
  • Receive prior written notice in your preferred language before any evaluation
  • Access to your child's school records
  • An independent second opinion (independent evaluation) if you disagree
  • Legal or advocate representation at ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD meetings

Language and Communication Rights

Bilingual families in the ROSCOE COLLEGIATE area have the right for all special education communication to be in Spanish. This includes parent rights notices, evaluation reports, and special education documents.

Step 1: Submit a Formal Evaluation Request to ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD

The first step to request a special education evaluation in ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD is to submit a written request to your campus's special education department or the district's central office.

How to Submit the Request?

Contact your school's ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD special education coordinator. You can submit your request by:

  • Postal mail to the ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD address
  • Email (request a delivery confirmation)
  • In person, at the campus special education office
  • Certified mail to have an official record of the request

What to Include in Your Request

Your request should be clear and specific. Include:

  1. Your child's full name and ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD student identification number
  2. Current school grade and campus
  3. A clear description of your specific concerns
  4. Concrete examples of difficulties you have observed at home or at school
  5. Any previous evaluations or diagnoses you may have
  6. Your name, phone number, and email address

Example: "I am requesting a special education evaluation for my son, Juan Garcia, a 3rd-grade student at ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD Elementary School. Juan has significant difficulty reading compared to his peers. He cannot identify basic letters or letter sounds after direct instruction. He also has behavioral problems when asked to read in class."

Step 2: Deadlines ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD Must Meet

Once ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD receives your evaluation request, the district has specific legal deadlines it must meet:

Key Deadlines in the Process

  • 15 school days: ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD must send you a written notice acknowledging the request and explaining your rights
  • 60 school days: ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD must complete all evaluations and present you with the results
  • 30 days after evaluation: Meeting to discuss results and determine eligibility for services

It is important that you document all dates and communications with ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD. Keep copies of emails, letters, and notes about phone conversations.

Step 3: The ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD Evaluation Process

During the 60 school days, ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD will complete a comprehensive evaluation of your child. This evaluation is completely free for your family.

Areas ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD Must Evaluate

According to the Texas Education Code and IDEA, the evaluation must examine:

  • Academic achievement: Reading, math, writing, and study skills
  • Cognitive development: Memory, reasoning, and processing
  • Health and vision/hearing: Medical and sensory examinations
  • Motor development: Gross and fine motor skills
  • Social and emotional development: Behavior and relationships
  • Communication and language: Expression and comprehension in both languages if applicable

Your Role in the Evaluation

As a parent, your input is critical. ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD must request:

  • A developmental history of your child
  • Your perspective on academic or behavioral difficulties
  • Information about any previous evaluations or services
  • Your written consent before any formal evaluation

Step 4: Interpretation of Results and Eligibility Meeting

After ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD completes the evaluation, the district will provide you with a written report in Spanish (if that is your preferred language). This report explains the findings in clear language.

The Eligibility (ARD) Meeting

ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD will organize a formal meeting called an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) Committee meeting or IEP Meeting. In this meeting:

  1. The ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD team presents the evaluation results
  2. It is determined if your child meets the definition of a disability category
  3. If eligible, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) is developed
  4. If not eligible for IDEA, other options such as 504 services are discussed

You have the right to bring someone to this ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD meeting — a friend, family member, special education attorney, or parent advocate who can support you.

Step 5: Special Education Services in ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD

If your child is eligible, ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD must develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) that describes:

  • Your child's current performance levels
  • Measurable annual educational goals
  • Special services your child will receive (speech therapy, special education, etc.)
  • Time in regular classroom vs. specialized programs
  • Curriculum accommodations and modifications
  • How progress will be measured

ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD will review the IEP annually and adjust it as needed. You participate in all these meetings.

Next Steps: Taking Action Today

If your concerns about your child's learning or behavior have not been addressed by the school, do not wait any longer. Parents of students in ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD have the power to request a special education evaluation at any time.

Immediate actions:

  1. Document specific difficulties observed at home and at school
  2. Meet informally with the ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD teacher or counselor
  3. Prepare a written request to the ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD special education coordinator
  4. Request a copy of the Parent Rights Notification in Spanish
  5. Keep copies of all communication with ROSCOE COLLEGIATE ISD
