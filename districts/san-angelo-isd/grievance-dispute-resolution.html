Dispute & Discipline Rights in San Angelo ISD

🚨 Know This Now

Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

Discipline Rights & Grievances in San Angelo ISD

URGENT GUIDE: Discipline Rights & Grievances in San Angelo ISD

If your child is facing disciplinary action in San Angelo ISD, especially if they have an IEP or 504 plan, ACT NOW! Time is critical. This guide outlines your rights and how to protect your child. Know your rights regarding suspensions, Manifestation Determination Reviews (MDRs), Changes of Placement, and grievance procedures. Do not delay! Document everything.

The 10-Day Suspension Rule

San Angelo ISD can suspend your child for up to 10 school days for disciplinary reasons without triggering certain IDEA protections. However, even one day of suspension can impact your child's education. Pay close attention to the reasons for the suspension. Ensure the school is following its own disciplinary policies and the Student Code of Conduct. Ask for written notification of the suspension, including the specific reason(s) for the disciplinary action and the length of the suspension.

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

If your child with an IEP faces suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days in a school year (or a pattern of shorter suspensions that constitute a change of placement - see below), a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) MUST BE HELD. This meeting determines if the behavior leading to the disciplinary action was:

  • Caused by or directly and substantially related to the child's disability; OR
  • The direct result of the school's failure to implement the IEP.

FIGHT FOR THIS! If the behavior is a manifestation of the disability or the result of IEP implementation failure, the school CANNOT suspend your child for more than 10 days (with some exceptions for dangerous behavior, detailed below). Insist on a complete and thorough review of the IEP, relevant evaluations, and observations of your child's behavior. Bring your own documentation and evidence. Have an advocate present. If the school denies the MDR, immediately consult with an attorney.

Change of Placement

A "Change of Placement" occurs if the disciplinary action is:

  • A removal of more than 10 consecutive school days;
  • A series of removals that constitute a pattern (e.g., frequent short suspensions adding up to more than 10 days) and are based on similar behaviors; OR
  • A long-term suspension or expulsion.

If a Change of Placement occurs, the school MUST conduct an MDR, regardless of the child's disability status. The school MUST also provide your child with continued educational services to enable them to participate in the general education curriculum and progress toward meeting their IEP goals, even during suspension. If the school fails to provide these services, it is a violation of IDEA!

IMPORTANT: For serious infractions (weapons, drugs, serious bodily injury), schools can remove a student with an IEP to an Interim Alternative Educational Setting (IAES) for up to 45 school days, regardless of the MDR outcome. However, you STILL have the right to challenge this placement and demand a due process hearing.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe San Angelo ISD has violated your child's rights or failed to follow proper procedures, you can file a grievance. Typically, the first step is a Level 1 grievance. This usually involves submitting a written complaint to the school principal or other designated administrator. The grievance should clearly state:

  • The specific issue or violation
  • The date(s) of the incident(s)
  • The names of involved personnel
  • The specific relief you are seeking (e.g., reinstatement, MDR review, change of disciplinary action)

Document everything! Keep copies of your grievance, any supporting documentation, and any responses from the school. Adhere to the timelines outlined in the San Angelo ISD's grievance policy. If the Level 1 grievance is not resolved satisfactorily, you can escalate to Level 2 and potentially higher levels.

TEA State Complaint

You can also file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA) if you believe San Angelo ISD has violated state or federal laws related to special education. This is a more formal process, and the TEA will investigate your complaint. Information on filing a TEA complaint can be found on the TEA website. Filing a TEA complaint can be time-consuming, but it's an important avenue for ensuring compliance.

Due Process

If you disagree with the outcome of the MDR or any other disciplinary decision related to your child's IEP, you have the right to request a Due Process hearing. This is a formal legal proceeding where you can present evidence and challenge the school district's actions. Due process is a complex process, and it's strongly recommended that you seek legal representation from an attorney specializing in special education law. Tight deadlines apply for requesting a due process hearing. Missing these deadlines can jeopardize your child's rights.

This is a stressful time. Remember to document EVERYTHING, seek professional help (legal and advocacy), and never give up fighting for your child's rights.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
