Requesting an Evaluation in San Angelo ISD

⚡ Quick Answer

How long does Texas have to complete an evaluation?
Once a written evaluation request is received, the district must complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) and hold an ARD meeting within 45 school days. This timeline is strictly enforced by the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Verbal requests do not start the clock — always submit in writing and keep a copy.

Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in San Angelo ISD

If you suspect your child in San Angelo ISD (SISD) may have a disability that is impacting their learning, you have the right to request a full and individual evaluation to determine if they are eligible for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas state law. This guide outlines the process for requesting an evaluation, the timelines involved, and your rights throughout the process.

How to Submit a Written Request

The most important first step is to submit a written request for a special education evaluation to SISD. This request should be clearly worded and include specific details. Address the letter to the school principal, the special education director, or your child's teacher. In the request, state clearly that you are requesting a full and individual evaluation for special education services. Include the following information:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth.
  • Your child's grade level and current school.
  • A brief description of your concerns regarding your child's academic, behavioral, or developmental challenges. Be as specific as possible and include examples.
  • Any relevant background information, such as medical diagnoses, previous evaluations, or interventions.
  • Your contact information (phone number, email address, and mailing address).
  • A statement that you are requesting a full and individual evaluation to determine eligibility for special education services.

Keep a copy of the request for your records. It is recommended to send the request via certified mail with return receipt requested, or hand-deliver it to the school and obtain a signed receipt, to ensure that the school acknowledges receipt of your request.

The 45 School Day Timeline

Texas law requires SISD to follow a specific timeline after receiving your written request. Within 15 school days, the school must decide whether to conduct an evaluation. They will typically hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee meeting, which you are entitled to attend, to discuss the request. If the school decides to evaluate, they must obtain your written consent for the evaluation. From the date the school receives your consent, they have 45 school days to complete the evaluation and hold another ARD meeting to discuss the evaluation results and determine your child's eligibility for special education services. If the school refuses to evaluate, they must provide you with written notice of the refusal and the reasons for the refusal. This notice must also inform you of your rights to appeal the decision.

What the FIE Must Cover

The Full and Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment of your child's strengths and needs. It must be conducted by qualified professionals and must assess all areas of suspected disability. This may include academic achievement, cognitive abilities, communication skills, social-emotional functioning, motor skills, and adaptive behavior. The evaluation must use a variety of assessment tools and strategies, including observations, interviews, and standardized tests. The FIE should provide specific recommendations for interventions and services to address your child's needs.

Child Find Obligations

Under IDEA, school districts have a "Child Find" obligation to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities who reside within their jurisdiction, regardless of whether they are enrolled in public schools. This means that SISD has a proactive responsibility to identify students who may need special education services, even if their parents haven't explicitly requested an evaluation. If you suspect your child has a disability, SISD is obligated to investigate and determine if an evaluation is warranted.

What If They Refuse

If SISD refuses to conduct an evaluation, they must provide you with written notice of the refusal, including the reasons for the refusal and a description of other options considered. You have the right to disagree with the school's decision. You can request mediation, file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA), or request a due process hearing. These are formal dispute resolution processes that allow you to challenge the school's decision. You can find more information about these options on the TEA website. Additionally, you are entitled to request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at public expense if you disagree with the school's evaluation. There are specific procedures and criteria for obtaining an IEE.

After the Evaluation

After the evaluation is complete, the ARD committee, which includes you as a parent, will meet to discuss the results. If your child is found eligible for special education services, the ARD committee will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). The IEP outlines your child's specific educational needs, goals, and the services and supports they will receive to achieve those goals. You are a critical member of the ARD committee and have the right to participate in all decisions regarding your child's education. Be sure to ask questions and express your concerns throughout the process. If you disagree with the IEP, you have the right to pursue dispute resolution options.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Texas? +

Submit a written request directly to the school's principal or special education director. The written request starts the mandatory 45 school-day clock. Keep a dated copy for your records.

What is a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in Texas? +

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment the school must complete to determine if your child has a disability and needs special education services. It must cover all areas of suspected disability and be completed within 45 school days of your written consent.

Can a Texas school refuse to evaluate my child? +

Yes, but only if the district provides written notice (Prior Written Notice) explaining why they are refusing and what data they relied on. You have the right to challenge that refusal through mediation or a due process hearing.
