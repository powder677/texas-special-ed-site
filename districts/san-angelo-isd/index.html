Special Education in San Angelo ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the world of special education can feel overwhelming, but you're not alone. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), both federal and Texas law ensure that children with disabilities have access to a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) designed to meet their unique needs. This includes specialized instruction and related services. San Angelo ISD is committed to providing these services to eligible students within its boundaries. The Texas Education Agency (TEA) oversees San Angelo ISD, ensuring compliance with state and federal laws. Furthermore, San Angelo ISD receives vital support and resources from TEA Region 15 Education Service Center, located right here in our region.

It's essential to remember that as a parent, you have enforceable legal rights throughout the entire special education process. From the initial evaluation to the development and implementation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP), you are a crucial member of the team. The decisions made at ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meetings profoundly impact your child's education. Understanding your rights and actively participating in these meetings are paramount to advocating for your child's needs. The law is on your side, and San Angelo ISD is obligated to uphold these protections.

Your voice matters. Don't hesitate to ask questions, seek clarification, and express your concerns. Remember, you are your child's best advocate. To ensure your child receives the appropriate support and services in San Angelo ISD, familiarize yourself with the special education process and know your rights. Right now, consider bookmarking this page for easy access to information as you navigate your child's special education journey.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in San Angelo ISD

If you suspect that your child may have a disability that requires special education services, the first step is to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) from San Angelo ISD. It is critically important to understand that this request must be made in writing. A verbal request, while helpful for initiating the conversation, does not officially start the timeline for the evaluation process. Documenting your request is key. Once San Angelo ISD receives your written consent to evaluate, the district has 45 school days to complete the FIE and determine your child's eligibility for special education services.

It's also important to know about Child Find. Child Find is a legal mandate requiring San Angelo ISD to proactively identify, locate, and evaluate all children within its jurisdiction who may have a disability and be in need of special education and related services. This means that even if you haven't requested an evaluation, if school personnel have reason to believe your child may have a disability, they are required to initiate the evaluation process. However, as a parent, you have the right to initiate the process yourself. Write a letter to the principal of your child's school in San Angelo ISD, clearly stating that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation to determine eligibility for special education services. Keep a copy of the letter for your records, and send it via certified mail with return receipt requested to ensure proof of delivery.

ARD Meetings in San Angelo ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal, and ARD meetings are the cornerstone of the special education process. These meetings bring together parents, educators, administrators, and other relevant professionals to make decisions about your child's educational program. San Angelo ISD is required to provide you with written notice at least five calendar days before any ARD meeting. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, the date, time, location, and a list of the individuals who will be attending. Reviewing this notice carefully is important to ensure you're prepared for the discussion.

You have significant rights at the ARD table. You can request a 10-day recess at any point during the ARD meeting if you need more time to consider the proposed IEP or discuss it with outside experts. This is your right, and San Angelo ISD must honor it. Furthermore, you are not obligated to sign the IEP on the same day as the ARD meeting. Take the time you need to review the document thoroughly and seek clarification on any points you don't understand. Your signature indicates your agreement with the IEP, so it is vital to ensure it accurately reflects your child's needs and your expectations.

Remember, you are an equal partner in the ARD process. Your input and perspective are invaluable. Dyslexia Services in San Angelo ISD

Texas law mandates that San Angelo ISD screen all students for dyslexia. Early identification is critical, as timely intervention can significantly improve outcomes for students with dyslexia. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook sets the standards that San Angelo ISD must follow in screening, identifying, and providing services to students with dyslexia. This handbook outlines the specific procedures and methodologies that must be used.

If your child is identified as having dyslexia, they are entitled to structured literacy intervention. This specialized instruction focuses on phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and reading comprehension. Depending on the severity of your child's dyslexia and their individual needs, they may qualify for a 504 Plan or an IEP. A 504 Plan provides accommodations to support your child's learning in the general education classroom, while an IEP provides more intensive, individualized support. If your child has not yet been screened for dyslexia and you have concerns, request a dyslexia screening in writing from your child's school in San Angelo ISD.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in San Angelo ISD

Discipline procedures can be particularly complex for students with disabilities. It is crucial to understand your child's rights if they face disciplinary action. If a special education student in San Angelo ISD is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year, it triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This review is designed to determine whether the student's misconduct was a manifestation of their disability. The MDR is an ARD meeting in which the team reviews the student's IEP, evaluations, and other relevant information to determine if the behavior was caused by or directly related to the student's disability, or if it was the result of the school's failure to implement the IEP.

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative days constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA, requiring an ARD meeting to address the situation. This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a special education student. You must act quickly. If your child has been suspended, request a Manifestation Determination Review immediately. If you believe the school is not following proper procedures, document everything carefully. To help you navigate this challenging situation, use our Discipline Defense Kit if your child has been suspended from San Angelo ISD. It provides valuable information and resources to protect your child's rights.

Filing a Complaint Against San Angelo ISD

If you believe that San Angelo ISD has violated your child's rights under IDEA or other special education laws, you have several options for filing a complaint. It's important to understand that these rights exist regardless of what any staff member at San Angelo ISD may tell you.

Your first escalation path is a TEA State Complaint. This is a free process where you file a formal complaint with the Texas Education Agency. TEA will investigate your complaint and issue a decision within 60 days. A second option is Mediation through TEA, also free. Mediation involves a neutral third party facilitating a discussion between you and San Angelo ISD in an attempt to resolve the dispute. A third, more formal option is a Due Process Hearing. This is a legal proceeding where an impartial hearing officer hears evidence and makes a decision. This process can be more complex and may require legal representation. Finally, you can file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR), which investigates allegations of discrimination based on disability. Choose the path that best suits your needs and remember that documenting all interactions with San Angelo ISD is crucial. As your next step, research the TEA State Complaint process on the TEA website to understand the requirements and procedures involved.

Frequently Asked Questions About San Angelo ISD Special Education

The questions every San Angelo ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in San Angelo ISD? +

Submit a written request to the San Angelo ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in San Angelo ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does San Angelo ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires San Angelo ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how San Angelo ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if San Angelo ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. San Angelo ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
