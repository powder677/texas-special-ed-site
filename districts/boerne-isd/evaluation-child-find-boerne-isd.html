A Full Individual Evaluation is a complete assessment of your child's cognitive abilities, academic performance, social-emotional functioning, and physical development. For parents in BOERNE ISD, an FIE is the formal gateway to determining if your child has a disability that impacts their education and whether they need special education services.

The BOERNE ISD special education evaluation process is governed by both federal law (IDEA) and Texas Education Code. This evaluation goes far deeper than a simple classroom screening—it includes standardized tests, observations, work samples, and input from multiple professionals.

Understanding this distinction matters because many BOERNE ISD families confuse classroom assessments with formal special education evaluations. Your child may score below grade level in reading without automatically qualifying for special education services. The FIE determines the actual presence of a disability and the extent to which it affects their education.

As a parent in BOERNE ISD, you know your child best. Consider requesting an evaluation if you've noticed:

  • Academic struggles that don't improve despite classroom interventions or tutoring
  • Significant gaps between your child's ability and classroom performance
  • Speech or language delays compared to peers the same age
  • Behavioral challenges that seem out of proportion to situations
  • Difficulty with social skills or making/maintaining friendships
  • Fine or gross motor delays affecting writing, sports, or coordination
  • Chronic attendance issues or school avoidance
  • Extreme anxiety or emotional dysregulation impacting learning

BOERNE ISD has a legal obligation to identify all students who may need special education services through a process called BOERNE ISD child find. This means the district must actively look for students who might have disabilities, even if parents haven't formally requested an evaluation.

Your Legal Right to Request a BOERNE ISD Evaluation Request

Under IDEA and Texas Education Code Section 29.004, you have an absolute right to request a BOERNE ISD evaluation request in writing. The school cannot deny your request simply because teachers think your child is fine or because your child hasn't failed a class.

Key legal protections for BOERNE ISD families include:

  • You don't need teacher permission or recommendation to request an evaluation
  • The district must respond to your written request within specific timeframes
  • Your child is protected under IDEA before, during, and after the evaluation process
  • You have the right to independent evaluations if you disagree with BOERNE ISD's findings
  • All evaluations must be conducted in your child's native language if English isn't their first language

This is not a suggestion or favor—it's your child's legal right. BOERNE ISD must take your request seriously and follow established procedures.

Step-by-Step Process for Requesting an FIE in BOERNE ISD

Step 1: Submit Your Written Request

Begin your BOERNE ISD FIE evaluation request by putting your request in writing. While a verbal request to a teacher or counselor creates a record, submitting a formal written request establishes a clear timeline and creates documentation.

Your request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Your contact information (phone and email)
  • Specific areas of concern (academic, behavioral, speech, motor skills, etc.)
  • Brief description of how these concerns impact learning
  • Any relevant medical, developmental, or family history
  • Current interventions or supports already tried

Where to send your request in BOERNE ISD:

  • Special Education Department at your child's school campus
  • BOERNE ISD Central Office Special Education Department
  • Email to the special education coordinator if available
  • Hand-deliver and request a signed receipt

Keep a copy for your records and note the date submitted. This documentation proves when your formal request was made.

Step 2: Understand the BOERNE ISD 45 Day Timeline

Once BOERNE ISD receives your written evaluation request, the district has 45 calendar days to complete the Full Individual Evaluation and hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. This BOERNE ISD 45 day timeline is a critical deadline established by IDEA.

Important timeline details:

  • The 45 days starts when BOERNE ISD receives your written request
  • Weekends and holidays are included in the count
  • The evaluation and ARD meeting must be completed within these 45 days
  • BOERNE ISD can request a 15-day extension with written parental consent
  • The district cannot delay simply because they're busy or understaffed

Mark your calendar 45 days from your request date. If you haven't received written notice of the ARD meeting and evaluation results by then, follow up immediately with BOERNE ISD special education staff.

Step 3: Participate in Pre-Evaluation Planning

Before formal testing begins, BOERNE ISD staff will contact you to discuss the evaluation plan. This is your opportunity to share information about your child and understand what assessments will be used.

During this planning phase, ask BOERNE ISD about:

  • Which specific tests will be administered and why
  • How results will be interpreted and reported
  • Who will conduct the evaluations (school psychologist, speech therapist, etc.)
  • Whether your child's native language will be used in testing
  • Your role in providing input about your child's development and health history

You'll typically complete a developmental history form for BOERNE ISD with information about pregnancy, birth, developmental milestones, medical conditions, medications, and family background. This information is crucial for the evaluation team to understand your child's full picture.

Step 4: The Evaluation Process

A comprehensive BOERNE ISD special education evaluation includes multiple components conducted by qualified professionals. This isn't a single test but rather a collection of assessments designed to examine different areas of functioning.

Components typically included in BOERNE ISD evaluations:

  • Cognitive assessment: Tests of thinking, reasoning, and problem-solving abilities
  • Academic achievement testing: Standardized measures of reading, math, and writing
  • Social-emotional evaluation: Assessment of behavior, emotional functioning, and social skills
  • Speech-language evaluation: If speech or language concerns exist
  • Motor skills assessment: Fine and gross motor development screening
  • Classroom observations: How your child functions in educational settings
  • Review of records: BOERNE ISD examines report cards, previous test scores, and medical records

During this time, BOERNE ISD staff may conduct classroom observations and request information from teachers about your child's academic and behavioral performance. You'll be kept informed of progress toward the BOERNE ISD 45 day timeline.

The ARD Meeting and Eligibility Decision

The ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting is where BOERNE ISD presents evaluation findings and determines eligibility for special education services. This meeting must occur within the BOERNE ISD 45 day timeline and includes you, special education staff, and relevant specialists.

What to expect at the BOERNE ISD ARD meeting:

  • Presentation of evaluation results in clear, understandable language
  • Discussion of your child's strengths and areas of concern
  • Determination of whether your child has a qualifying disability
  • If eligible, development of an Individualized Education Program (IEP)
  • Your opportunity to ask questions and provide input

Possible outcomes from your BOERNE ISD evaluation:

  • Eligible for special education: Your child qualifies under a disability category and will receive services under an IEP
  • Ineligible for special education: Your child doesn't meet criteria but may be eligible for a 504 Plan or general education supports
  • Need additional assessment: BOERNE ISD may request more time or additional evaluations in specific areas

If You Disagree with BOERNE ISD's Evaluation Results

Not every parent agrees with BOERNE ISD's conclusions, and that's okay. You have several options if you believe the evaluation was incomplete, biased, or inaccurate.

Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE)

You have the right to request an Independent Educational Evaluation at BOERNE ISD's expense if you disagree with the district's evaluation. An IEE provides an objective second opinion from a professional outside the school system.

Important details about IEEs in BOERNE ISD:

  • BOERNE ISD must provide information about obtaining an IEE
  • The district may either fund the evaluation or request a due process hearing to defend their evaluation
  • Most parents choose to use the BOERNE ISD-funded IEE for objectivity
  • The IEE provider must be qualified and independent from the school

Moving Forward After Your BOERNE ISD FIE Evaluation

Once your child has been evaluated by BOERNE ISD, the special education journey continues. If your child qualifies, you'll work with BOERNE ISD to develop an IEP that addresses their specific needs.

Next steps for BOERNE ISD families:

  • If eligible: Work with BOERNE ISD to develop annual IEP goals and services
  • Schedule regular progress monitoring to ensure services are effective
  • Request IEP meetings if needs change or concerns arise
  • Keep organized records of all communications with BOERNE ISD
  • Stay informed about your rights under IDEA and Texas law

Remember, your involvement doesn't end with the evaluation. Successful special education depends on ongoing collaboration between BOERNE ISD staff and parents. Regular communication and advocacy ensure your child receives the services they need to succeed.

Key Takeaways for BOERNE ISD Parents Requesting an FIE

  • You have the legal right to request a BOERNE ISD evaluation request without teacher permission
  • Submit your request in writing and keep documentation of the date
  • BOERNE ISD must complete evaluation and ARD meeting within the BOERNE ISD 45 day timeline
  • A comprehensive BOERNE ISD special education evaluation examines multiple areas of functioning
  • The ARD meeting determines eligibility and next steps for your child
  • You can request an independent evaluation if you disagree with BOERNE ISD's findings
  • Consider working with a special ed advocate BOERNE area or special education attorney near BOERNE if issues arise
  • Active parental participation is key to your child's success in the special education process

Understanding the BOERNE ISD FIE evaluation process empowers you to advocate effectively for your child. Don't hesitate to ask questions, request clarification, and push for comprehensive assessment if you believe your child needs it. Your advocacy can make a significant difference in your child's educational outcomes and overall development.
