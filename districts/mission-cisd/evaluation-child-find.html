Requesting an Evaluation in Mission CISD

⚡ Quick Answer

How long does Texas have to complete an evaluation?
Once a written evaluation request is received, the district must complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) and hold an ARD meeting within 45 school days. This timeline is strictly enforced by the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Verbal requests do not start the clock — always submit in writing and keep a copy.

Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in Mission CISD

If you believe your child, who attends a school within the Mission Consolidated Independent School District (CISD), may have a disability that affects their ability to learn, you have the right to request a full and individual initial evaluation for special education services. This guide outlines the process, timelines, and key considerations under Texas law.

How to Submit a Written Request

The most crucial step is submitting a written request for an evaluation. This request should be addressed to the school principal or the Special Education Director of Mission CISD. The request should clearly state that you are requesting a full and individual initial evaluation for special education services. Be specific about your concerns regarding your child's academic, behavioral, or developmental difficulties. Provide concrete examples, such as struggling with reading comprehension, difficulty focusing in class, or delays in speech development. Include your child's full name, date of birth, grade level, and the name of the school they attend. Your contact information, including phone number and email address, is also essential. Retain a copy of the request for your records; sending it via certified mail with return receipt requested provides proof of delivery.

The 45 School Day Timeline

Texas law mandates a strict timeline for the evaluation process. Once Mission CISD receives your written request, the school district has 15 school days to provide you with written notice of whether they agree to evaluate your child. If they agree, they must also provide you with an "Assessment Plan" which will detail what areas will be assessed. If the school district does not respond within 15 school days, or refuses to provide the Assessment Plan, contact an advocate or attorney.

If the Assessment Plan is provided and you consent, the school district then has 45 school days (not calendar days) from the date you provided your written consent for the evaluation to complete the Full and Individual Evaluation (FIE). This timeline is legally binding, but extensions can be granted with your written agreement.

What the FIE Must Cover

The Full and Individual Evaluation (FIE) must be comprehensive and tailored to your child's specific needs. It should assess all areas related to the suspected disability. This might include academic achievement (reading, writing, math), intellectual ability, adaptive behavior, communication skills (speech and language), motor skills, social-emotional functioning, and sensory processing. Under Texas law, the FIE must be conducted by qualified professionals. For example, psychological testing should be administered by a Licensed Specialist in School Psychology (LSSP) or a licensed psychologist. The FIE should use a variety of assessment tools and methods, including observations, interviews with parents and teachers, and standardized tests. The goal is to gain a complete understanding of your child's strengths and weaknesses and determine if they meet the eligibility criteria for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas state law.

Child Find Obligations

Mission CISD has a legal obligation under IDEA to actively seek out and identify children with disabilities within its boundaries, regardless of whether the child attends public school. This is known as "Child Find." This obligation extends to children who are suspected of having a disability, even if they are performing adequately in school. If you suspect your child has a disability, even if their grades are passing, you still have the right to request an evaluation, and the school district must consider your request.

What If They Refuse

If Mission CISD refuses to conduct an evaluation, they must provide you with written notice explaining their reasons for refusal. This is called Prior Written Notice. You have the right to challenge this decision. Options include requesting mediation, filing a formal complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA), or requesting a due process hearing. Due process hearings are formal legal proceedings where an impartial hearing officer makes a determination about whether the district complied with IDEA. Contacting a special education advocate or attorney is strongly recommended if the school district refuses to evaluate your child.

After the Evaluation

Once the FIE is complete, an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committee meeting will be scheduled. The ARD committee includes you, the parent, as well as school personnel, such as teachers, administrators, and evaluators. At the ARD meeting, the evaluation results will be reviewed, and a determination will be made about whether your child is eligible for special education services. If your child is determined eligible, the ARD committee will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) that outlines your child's specific educational needs and the services and supports that will be provided to meet those needs. You have the right to participate fully in the ARD process and to advocate for your child's needs. Remember you are an equal part of this committee and must provide consent to the IEP before it can be implemented. If you disagree with the IEP, you have the right to request changes, or if changes cannot be agreed upon, you have the right to challenge the IEP using the dispute resolution options mentioned above.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Texas? +

Submit a written request directly to the school's principal or special education director. The written request starts the mandatory 45 school-day clock. Keep a dated copy for your records.

What is a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in Texas? +

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment the school must complete to determine if your child has a disability and needs special education services. It must cover all areas of suspected disability and be completed within 45 school days of your written consent.

Can a Texas school refuse to evaluate my child? +

Yes, but only if the district provides written notice (Prior Written Notice) explaining why they are refusing and what data they relied on. You have the right to challenge that refusal through mediation or a due process hearing.
