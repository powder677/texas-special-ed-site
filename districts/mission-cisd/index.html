About Special Education in Mission CISD

Mission CISD serves the Texas area, providing educational services to a diverse student population. The district is committed to supporting students with disabilities through a comprehensive special education program. Mission CISD operates under the oversight of TEA Region unknown, adhering to all state and federal regulations regarding special education services.

The Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) process in Mission CISD follows a specific timeline. Once parental consent for a Full and Individual Evaluation (FIE) is received, the district has 45 school days to complete the evaluation. Parents will receive a written notice at least five school days before the ARD committee meeting to discuss the evaluation results and develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP), if appropriate.

Common Questions from Mission CISD Families

My child is struggling in reading. How do I request an evaluation for dyslexia services?

Contact your child’s teacher or the school principal to express your concerns. The school will likely conduct some interventions within the general education setting first. If those interventions are not successful, you can formally request a full and individual evaluation in writing, specifying your concerns about dyslexia. The school is then required to respond.

What if I disagree with the ARD committee's decisions?

You have several options if you disagree with the ARD committee’s decisions. You can request another ARD meeting to further discuss your concerns. You also have the right to pursue mediation or file a formal complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). The district is required to provide you with information about your rights and dispute resolution options.

My child's IEP isn't being followed. What can I do?

First, communicate directly with your child's teacher and case manager to address your concerns. Document instances where the IEP isn't being followed and present this information to the school. If the issue persists, contact the Special Education Director for Mission CISD for further assistance and resolution.

Practical Tip for Mission CISD Parents

Mission CISD, with approximately 10,000 students, may have a more centralized special education administration. In smaller districts, it's common for one Special Education Director to oversee many aspects of the program. To ensure clear communication and maintain a record of your interactions, prioritize email communication over phone calls when contacting the Special Education Director or other district personnel. This creates a paper trail that can be helpful if you need to refer back to previous conversations or decisions.
