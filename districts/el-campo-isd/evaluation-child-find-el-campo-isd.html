A Full Individual Evaluation is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine whether your child has a disability that affects their educational performance and qualifies them for special education services. For parents in EL CAMPO ISD, requesting an FIE is often the gateway to accessing accommodations, modifications, and specialized instruction that can transform your child's educational experience.

The EL CAMPO ISD special education evaluation process is governed by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code Section 29.004. This thorough assessment examines your child's cognitive abilities, academic performance, social-emotional development, and adaptive functioning across multiple domains.

Parents often have questions about why an FIE is necessary. If your child is struggling academically, experiencing behavioral challenges, or you suspect a learning disability, language disorder, or developmental delay, an EL CAMPO ISD evaluation request can provide clarity and open doors to support services.

Before requesting a formal FIE, it's important to understand how EL CAMPO ISD child find procedures work. Child find is the district's responsibility to identify all children with disabilities, ages birth through 21, who may need special education services.

How Child Find Works in EL CAMPO ISD

ELCAMP ISD uses multiple strategies to identify students who may have disabilities:

  • Classroom teacher observations and referrals
  • Universal screening and benchmark assessments
  • Parental concerns and requests for evaluation
  • School nurse and counselor referrals
  • Documentation of struggling learners in intervention programs
  • Medical or developmental history information

As a parent in EL CAMPO ISD, you don't need to wait for the district to identify your child. You have the right to request an evaluation at any time if you believe your child may have a disability affecting their education.

The Role of Response to Intervention (RTI)

Many EL CAMPO ISD schools use Response to Intervention programs to support struggling students before a formal FIE is conducted. However, RTI is not a prerequisite for requesting an evaluation. If you believe your child needs an EL CAMPO special education evaluation, you can request one directly, even if your child hasn't participated in RTI.

The process for requesting a Full Individual Evaluation in EL CAMPO ISD is straightforward, but knowing the correct steps ensures your request is properly documented and processed.

Step 1: Submit Your Written Evaluation Request

Contact your child's school and request to speak with the special education coordinator or campus administrator. Provide a written request for evaluation that clearly states your concern and why you believe your child may have a disability.

Your request should include:

  • Your child's name, grade, and school
  • Specific concerns about learning, development, or behavior
  • Examples of difficulties you've observed at home
  • Your contact information and preferred communication method
  • A request for a comprehensive Full Individual Evaluation

Keep a copy of your written request. Email is an excellent way to document your request, as it creates a timestamped record that EL CAMPO ISD must acknowledge.

Step 2: Understand the 45-Day Timeline

One of the most important aspects of the EL CAMPO ISD 45 day timeline is knowing exactly when it begins and what it means for your family. Once EL CAMPO ISD receives your written request for evaluation, the district has 45 school days to complete the Full Individual Evaluation and hold an Individualized Education Program (IEP) meeting to discuss the results.

The EL CAMPO ISD 45 day timeline clock starts on the date the district receives your written request. This means:

  • Day 1-5: District sends you a Notice of Proposed Evaluation and requests consent
  • Day 6-40: The evaluation team conducts assessments in all areas of suspected disability
  • Day 41-45: The evaluation report is completed, and the IEP meeting is scheduled

These 45 days are school days only—weekends, holidays, and summer breaks do not count toward this timeline. If your request falls near a long holiday break, the timeline will extend accordingly.

Step 3: Provide Informed Consent

Before EL CAMPO ISD can begin testing, you must receive and sign a Notice of Proposed Evaluation. This document explains the evaluation process, the types of assessments your child will participate in, and your rights as a parent.

You have the right to:

  • Ask questions about any assessment before it's administered
  • Request that certain evaluations not be conducted
  • Request independent evaluations at district expense if you disagree with findings
  • Have an interpreter present if English is not your primary language

Sign the consent form and return it to EL CAMPO ISD promptly. Your child cannot be evaluated without your written consent.

Step 4: Participate in the Evaluation Process

Once consent is obtained, the EL CAMPO ISD special education evaluation team will conduct comprehensive assessments. Your child will participate in various tests and observations, which typically include:

  • Cognitive and intellectual assessments
  • Academic achievement testing in reading, math, and written expression
  • Speech and language evaluations
  • Social-emotional and behavioral assessments
  • Adaptive behavior ratings
  • Vision and hearing screenings
  • Teacher rating scales and classroom observations

As a parent, you'll also complete rating forms and provide information about your child's development, medical history, and functioning at home. Your input is a critical part of the evaluation process.

What Happens After the Full Individual Evaluation

The Evaluation Report and IEP Meeting

Within the EL CAMPO ISD 45 day timeline, you'll receive the comprehensive evaluation report and attend an IEP meeting. This meeting brings together you, the evaluation team, and school personnel to review findings and determine eligibility for special education services.

During the IEP meeting, the team will discuss:

  • Whether your child qualifies for special education under one of 13 disability categories
  • Your child's strengths and areas of need
  • Present levels of academic and functional performance
  • Recommended special education services and placement
  • Goals and objectives for the upcoming school year

If your child qualifies for services, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) is developed. If your child does not qualify, you have the right to request an independent evaluation at EL CAMPO ISD's expense and can file a due process complaint if you disagree with the findings.

Understanding Eligibility Categories in EL CAMPO ISD

Students in EL CAMPO ISD may qualify for special education under these disability categories:

  • Specific Learning Disability (SLD)
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Visual Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Deaf-Blindness
  • Noncategorical Early Childhood (ages 3-5 only)

Your child must have a disability that adversely affects educational performance and require specially designed instruction to qualify under Texas and federal law.

Your Rights as a Parent in EL CAMPO ISD

Parents in EL CAMPO ISD have significant rights throughout the evaluation and special education process. Understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Key Parental Rights

  • Right to Request Evaluation: You can request an EL CAMPO ISD evaluation request at any time, even without teacher referral
  • Right to Participate: You must be invited to all IEP meetings and evaluation conferences
  • Right to Independent Evaluation: If you disagree with district findings, you can request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at district expense
  • Right to Interpreter Services: If you need an interpreter for meetings, EL CAMPO ISD must provide one
  • Right to Due Process: You can file a due process complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA) if you dispute the evaluation or IEP decisions
  • Right to Records: You can access and obtain copies of your child's educational records
  • Right to Confidentiality: Your child's special education information is protected under FERPA and IDEA

Common Questions Parents Ask About EL CAMPO ISD Evaluations

How Long Does the Evaluation Process Take?

The EL CAMPO ISD 45 day timeline is the legal requirement from request to completion of evaluation and IEP meeting. However, if you request the evaluation near a holiday break, the timeline extends because only school days count. Most evaluations are completed within 6-8 weeks from request.

What if EL CAMPO ISD Refuses My Evaluation Request?

By law, EL CAMPO ISD cannot refuse to evaluate your child if you make a written request and suspect a disability. If the district denies your request, you have the right to file a due process complaint with the Texas Education Agency. Document your request in writing and keep copies of all correspondence.

Can I Have Someone Attend the IEP Meeting with Me?

Absolutely. You can bring a family member, friend, advocate, or attorney to any EL CAMPO ISD IEP meeting. Having support helps you process information and ask important questions. Let the school know in advance that you're bringing someone.

What If I Disagree with the Evaluation Results?

You have the right to request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at EL CAMPO ISD's expense if you disagree with the district's findings. You can also file a due process complaint, which initiates a formal dispute resolution process.
