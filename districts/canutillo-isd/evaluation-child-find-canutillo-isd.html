CANUTILLO ISD's child find system is designed to identify children who may need special education services. This is not optional—it's a legal requirement under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §29.001.

As a parent, you have the right to request a CANUTILLO ISD special education evaluation at any time. You don't need permission from your child's teacher or principal. You don't need to wait for the school to suggest it first. You have the authority to initiate this process yourself, and CANUTILLO ISD must respond to your request in writing within a specific timeframe.

What is a Full Individual Evaluation?

An FIE is a comprehensive assessment conducted by CANUTILLO ISD professionals to determine whether your child has a disability and, if so, what special education services your child needs. The evaluation examines multiple areas including cognitive ability, academic performance, social-emotional functioning, speech and language development, and physical abilities.

The evaluation must be:

  • Comprehensive and address all areas of concern
  • Conducted by qualified examiners in CANUTILLO ISD
  • Completed in your child's native language when possible
  • Free of cultural and racial bias
  • Completed within the CANUTILLO ISD 45 day timeline

The process of requesting an evaluation request for CANUTILLO ISD is straightforward, though the terminology can vary. You may hear it called a "request for evaluation," "referral for evaluation," or "request for FIE." They all mean the same thing.

Step 1: Prepare Your Request

Your CANUTILLO ISD evaluation request should be in writing. Email is acceptable, though you may also deliver it in person or by mail. Keep your request clear and specific about your concerns. Instead of saying "my child struggles in school," explain what specifically you've observed: "My child reverses letters, reads significantly below grade level, and has difficulty following multi-step directions."

Include information about:

  • Specific academic or behavioral concerns
  • When you first noticed these concerns
  • How these concerns affect your child at home and school
  • Any evaluations or assessments already completed outside of CANUTILLO ISD
  • Your preferred contact information and best times to reach you

Step 2: Submit to the Right CANUTILLO ISD Contact

Contact your child's campus principal, the special education coordinator, or the CANUTILLO ISD special education department directly. Request a copy of the district's referral form and keep documentation that you submitted your request. If submitting by email, request a read receipt. If delivering in person, ask for a stamped copy showing the date received.

CANUTILLO ISD families should know that the district cannot deny your request. They may ask clarifying questions or request additional information, but they cannot refuse to evaluate your child if you request it.

Step 3: Receive Written Notice

Within a reasonable timeframe (typically 3-5 days), CANUTILLO ISD must provide you with written notice that your CANUTILLO ISD special education evaluation request has been received. This notice should outline:

  • The evaluation areas that will be assessed
  • The assessment methods and tools that will be used
  • Your right to request specific evaluations or assessments
  • Your right to have an independent evaluation at district expense under certain circumstances
  • The timeline for completing the evaluation

The CANUTILLO ISD 45 Day Timeline Explained

One of the most important things parents in CANUTILLO ISD need to understand is the CANUTILLO ISD 45 day timeline. This is not a suggestion—it's a legal requirement under IDEA and Texas law.

When Does the Clock Start?

The 45-day timeline begins the day CANUTILLO ISD receives your written evaluation request. This is why documentation of your request submission is critical. If you submitted your request on September 15th, the 45-day clock starts on September 15th, not when the principal reads it or the special education coordinator receives it.

What Happens During the 45 Days?

During this period, CANUTILLO ISD must:

  • Conduct all necessary assessments and evaluations
  • Gather information from parents, teachers, and other relevant sources
  • Compile results and complete a written evaluation report
  • Schedule and hold an IEP eligibility meeting (called an "ARD meeting" in Texas)
  • Provide you with a copy of the evaluation report

The evaluation must be completed within this CAnutillo ISD 45 day timeline unless you and the district agree in writing to extend it, or your child has multiple absences that make timely completion impossible.

What If CANUTILLO ISD Misses the Deadline?

If CANUTILLO ISD fails to meet the 45-day deadline without your written agreement to extend it, this is a procedural violation of special education law. Document the missed deadline carefully, as this becomes important if you need to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency or pursue due process hearings.

What to Expect During a CANUTILLO ISD Evaluation

Understanding what happens during the CANUTILLO ISD FIE evaluation helps you prepare your child and ask informed questions about the results.

Areas of Assessment

The evaluation will examine different areas depending on your child's age and your concerns. Typical areas include:

  • Cognitive/Intellectual Ability – Intelligence testing and reasoning skills
  • Academic Achievement – Reading, math, writing, and spelling performance
  • Speech and Language – Communication, language development, and articulation
  • Social-Emotional/Behavioral – Emotional regulation, social skills, and behavior patterns
  • Motor Skills – Gross and fine motor development and coordination
  • Adaptive Behavior – Self-care and independence skills

Who Conducts the Evaluation?

CANUTILLO ISD employs or contracts with qualified professionals to conduct evaluations, including special education teachers, school psychologists, speech-language pathologists, occupational therapists, and social workers. These professionals must be licensed and trained in the assessment tools they use.

Can You Be Involved?

Absolutely. CANUTILLO ISD must provide you the opportunity to observe evaluations and must actively solicit your input about your child's development, health history, behavior at home, and your concerns. Attend any meetings during the evaluation process and share information that teachers and other school staff may not see.

After the CANUTILLO ISD Evaluation: What Comes Next

After the CANUTILLO ISD special education evaluation is complete, your child's evaluation report will be shared with you, and CANUTILLO ISD will schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to discuss results.

The ARD Meeting

This critical meeting happens within the CANUTILLO ISD 45 day timeline and brings together you, special education staff, general education teachers, and a campus administrator. The team reviews the evaluation results and determines:

  • Whether your child qualifies for special education services under IDEA or Texas special education law
  • What disability category applies (if eligible)
  • What special education and related services your child needs
  • The child's IEP goals and placement

If Your Child Qualifies

CANUTILLO ISD will develop an Individualized Education Plan (IEP) outlining special education services, accommodations, and goals. You'll receive a copy and have an opportunity to discuss or request changes before the IEP takes effect.

If Your Child Does Not Qualify

This doesn't mean your child won't receive support. CANUTILLO ISD may offer interventions through RTI (Response to Intervention) programs, Section 504 plans for students with disabilities who don't qualify for special education, or other general education supports.

Your Rights as a Parent in CANUTILLO ISD Special Education

Parents of students in CANUTILLO ISD have significant rights throughout the evaluation and special education process, protected by federal and state law.

Key Parent Rights

  • Right to Request Evaluation – You can request a CANUTILLO ISD FIE evaluation at any time without permission from school staff
  • Right to Participate – You must be invited to and can attend all meetings regarding your child's evaluation and special education
  • Right to Notice – CANUTILLO ISD must inform you in writing about all proposed evaluations and services
  • Right to Records – You can review and request copies of your child's evaluation report and school records
  • Right to Independent Evaluation – If you disagree with CANUTILLO ISD's evaluation results, you can request an independent evaluation at district expense
  • Right to Due Process – If you disagree with CANUTILLO ISD's decisions, you can file a due process complaint

Navigating special education in CANUTILLO ISD can be complex, and many parents benefit from professional guidance. If you're feeling overwhelmed or facing disagreement with CANUTILLO ISD, consider seeking support from an experienced special education attorney near CANUTILLO or a trained special ed advocate CANUTILLO.

When to Consider Professional Help

You might want to contact a special education attorney near CANUTILLO if:

  • CANUTILLO ISD refuses to evaluate your child despite your request
  • CANUTILLO ISD misses the CANUTILLO ISD 45 day timeline without your agreement
  • You disagree significantly with the evaluation results
  • The proposed IEP doesn't adequately address your child's needs
  • You believe CANUTILLO ISD discriminated against your child based on disability

Common Questions Parents in CANUTILLO ISD Ask

How Long Does a CANUTILLO ISD FIE Evaluation Actually Take?

The legal requirement is the CANUTILLO ISD 45 day timeline, but the actual assessment process varies. Your child might have 5-10 hours of testing across multiple days, depending on the areas being evaluated. You'll likely have at least one parent conference before or after the evaluation.

Can CANUTILLO ISD Test My Child Without My Permission?

No. CANUTILLO ISD must obtain your written consent before conducting an initial evaluation. Consent is required before the evaluation begins, and you can withdraw consent at any time, though CANUTILLO ISD can pursue due process if they believe evaluation is necessary.

What if CANUTILLO ISD Wants to Evaluate My Child But I'm Not Sure?

Take time to ask questions. Understand what areas will be assessed and why. Request information about the specific assessment tools. You can also ask CANUTILLO ISD to gather additional data through classroom observations or teacher input before conducting a full evaluation. However, if you're genuinely concerned, getting the CANUTILLO ISD special education evaluation started is usually the right decision.

Does the Evaluation Cost Anything?

No. CANUTILLO ISD must provide the evaluation free of charge. You should not be asked to pay for any assessments, testing, or professional time related to the CANUTILLO ISD FIE evaluation.

Taking Action: Your Next Steps

If you believe your child may benefit from a CANUTILLO ISD special education evaluation, don't wait. Early identification and intervention make a significant difference in outcomes.

This week: Document your concerns about your child's learning or development. Write down specific examples of struggles or areas where your child differs from peers.

Next week: Contact your child's campus principal or CANUTILLO ISD special education department. Request a referral form and submit your written evaluation request. Keep documentation of submission.

Within 45 days: CANUTILLO ISD must complete the evaluation and hold the ARD meeting. Participate fully, ask questions, and advocate for your child's needs.

CANUTILLO ISD families deserve to know that special education exists because of you—because parents fought for the right to a free, appropriate public education for children with disabilities. Your advocacy matters, and seeking an evaluation is one of the most powerful steps you can take for your child's educational future.
