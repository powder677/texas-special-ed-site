Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. McKinney ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. Special Education in McKinney ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the special education system can feel overwhelming, but understanding the basics is the first step toward ensuring your child receives the support they need. Special education, as defined by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas law, encompasses specially designed instruction and related services provided to eligible students with disabilities. These services are tailored to meet your child's unique needs and allow them to access the general education curriculum to the greatest extent possible. Your child's education is governed by the law, meaning you have rights that McKinney ISD must uphold.

McKinney ISD, like all school districts in Texas, operates under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA Region 10 Education Service Center provides support and resources to McKinney ISD, helping to ensure that the district complies with state and federal regulations. However, ultimately, the responsibility for providing a free appropriate public education (FAPE) to your child rests with McKinney ISD. The important thing for parents to remember is that you have enforceable legal rights throughout the entire special education process, from initial evaluation to the development and implementation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Remember that you are your child's strongest advocate. Understanding your rights, documenting your concerns, and actively participating in the ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) process are crucial to ensuring your child receives the individualized support they deserve within McKinney ISD. Special education services are not a privilege, but a right, and it's our goal to help you understand and assert those rights effectively.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in McKinney ISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability that requires special education services, the first step is to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) from McKinney ISD. It's crucial to understand that this request MUST be submitted in writing. A verbal request to a teacher or principal, while helpful for raising your initial concerns, does not officially start the timeline for the evaluation process. The written request should clearly state your concerns about your child's academic, behavioral, or developmental progress and explain why you believe an evaluation is warranted. Include any supporting documentation you have, such as report cards, medical records, or previous evaluations from private professionals. It is also advisable to send this request certified to guarantee receipt. Remember, a delay in making a written request will delay the process.

Once McKinney ISD receives your written request and obtains your written consent to evaluate, the district has 45 school days to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). This evaluation will assess your child's strengths and weaknesses across various areas, such as cognitive ability, academic achievement, speech and language skills, and social-emotional functioning. Keep in mind that Child Find laws mandate that McKinney ISD proactively identify students who may need special education services, even if a parent hasn't specifically requested an evaluation. However, if you have concerns, don't wait for the district to initiate the process - be proactive and make the request yourself. Your insights as a parent are invaluable in determining your child's needs.

The next step is to write that formal request for a special education evaluation. Keep a copy for your records, and send it to the principal of your child's school in McKinney ISD via certified mail with return receipt requested. This provides proof that the school received your request and officially starts the 45-day timeline.

ARD Meetings in McKinney ISD: Your Rights at the Table

The ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee meeting is where decisions about your child's special education program are made. This is a crucial forum where you, as a parent, play a vital role in advocating for your child's needs and ensuring that the IEP (Individualized Education Program) is tailored to their specific strengths and challenges. McKinney ISD is required to provide you with written notice of any ARD meeting at least five calendar days in advance. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, the date, time, and location, and a list of individuals who will be attending. It's imperative that you attend these meetings and actively participate in the discussions. If the scheduled time is impossible, contact the school to request an alternative.

You have several important rights during the ARD meeting. One key right is the ability to request a 10-day recess at any time during the meeting if you feel overwhelmed or need more time to consider the proposed IEP. This gives you the opportunity to consult with advocates, review documentation, or simply gather your thoughts before making any decisions. Another crucial right is that you are not required to sign the IEP on the same day as the ARD meeting. You can take the proposed IEP home to review it thoroughly before making a final decision. The team at McKinney ISD is there to help provide your child with the best education and are there to help you through the process.

Remember, you are an equal member of the ARD committee, and your input is essential. Do not hesitate to ask questions, express your concerns, and advocate for the services and accommodations that you believe your child needs. The ARD process is collaborative, and your active participation is vital to ensuring that your child receives a Free and Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) in McKinney ISD.

Dyslexia Services in McKinney ISD

Texas law mandates that McKinney ISD screen all students for dyslexia. Early identification is critical because the earlier a child receives appropriate intervention, the better their chances of success. These screenings typically occur in kindergarten and first grade, but can happen later if concerns arise. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook sets the standards that McKinney ISD must follow for screening, identification, and intervention for students with dyslexia. This handbook provides a comprehensive framework for best practices in dyslexia education, and McKinney ISD is expected to adhere to these guidelines.

If your child is identified as having dyslexia, they are entitled to structured literacy intervention, which is a research-based approach that focuses on systematic and explicit instruction in phonological awareness, phonics, reading fluency, vocabulary, and reading comprehension. Depending on the severity of your child's dyslexia and their individual needs, they may qualify for a 504 Plan, which provides accommodations and modifications to help them access the general education curriculum, or they may qualify for an IEP under special education, which provides more intensive and individualized support. Remember that parental involvement is crucial in your student's success.

If your child has not been screened for dyslexia, or if you have concerns about their reading skills, request a dyslexia screening in writing from the principal of your child's school within McKinney ISD. Do not delay in getting your student tested.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in McKinney ISD

Disciplinary actions against students with disabilities can be particularly complex, and it's important to understand your child's rights in these situations. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), if a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year in McKinney ISD, it triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This review is an ARD meeting where the team determines whether the student's behavior was a manifestation of their disability or a result of the school's failure to appropriately implement the IEP. This also constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA. The failure of the team at McKinney ISD to follow these guidelines is a violation of law.

This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a child with a disability. If your child has been suspended or is facing disciplinary action, you must act quickly. Contact the school immediately to request an ARD meeting to discuss the situation and ensure that your child's rights are protected. Time is of the essence in these situations, and you must act within days, not weeks, to ensure that your child's rights are not violated and that appropriate supports are in place.

If your child has been suspended in McKinney ISD, immediately use our Discipline Defense Kit. This kit provides step-by-step guidance on how to protect your child's rights and advocate for appropriate disciplinary procedures.

Filing a Complaint Against McKinney ISD

If you feel that McKinney ISD is not fulfilling its legal obligations to your child, you have several avenues for filing a complaint. These rights are protected by law, regardless of what any staff member in McKinney ISD might tell you. The first is filing a TEA State Complaint. This is a free process where you submit a written complaint to the Texas Education Agency alleging specific violations of special education law. TEA has 60 days to investigate and issue a decision. Secondly, you can request mediation through TEA. Mediation is a free, voluntary process where a neutral third party helps you and McKinney ISD reach a mutually agreeable resolution.

Two other escalation paths are available. You also have the right to request a Due Process Hearing. This is a more formal, adversarial process where an impartial hearing officer hears evidence and makes a legally binding decision. This path requires legal representation and expertise. Lastly, you can file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR). This is appropriate if you believe that McKinney ISD has discriminated against your child based on their disability. Remember that each of these options has its own specific procedures and timelines. These escalation paths are available to all parents and you do not have to exhaust these other avenues before you file a civil rights complaint.

Frequently Asked Questions About McKinney ISD Special Education

The questions every McKinney ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in McKinney ISD? +

Submit a written request to the McKinney ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in McKinney ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does McKinney ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires McKinney ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how McKinney ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if McKinney ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. McKinney ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
