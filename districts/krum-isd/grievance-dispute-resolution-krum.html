A due process hearing is a formal legal proceeding before an impartial hearing officer when KRUM ISD and parents cannot reach agreement on special education matters. This process allows both sides to present evidence and arguments.

When to Consider a KRUM ISD Due Process Hearing

Due process is appropriate when disputes involve significant issues such as:

  • Disagreement about whether your child has a disability
  • Disputes over IEP goals, services, or placement
  • Concerns that KRUM ISD denied a free, appropriate public education
  • Disagreements about evaluation or assessment practices
  • Concerns about manifestation determination or disciplinary placement

Filing for KRUM ISD Due Process

To request a due process hearing with KRUM ISD, you must submit a written due process complaint notice to the district and the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Your notice should describe the problem, what happened, and what you want to happen. Meet with a special education attorney before filing—they'll ensure your notice contains everything required and strengthens your case.

The Due Process Timeline

Once KRUM ISD receives your due process complaint, the district has 10 days to respond in writing. You and KRUM ISD then have 15 days to participate in a resolution meeting to try to settle before a hearing occurs. If resolved, you'll sign an agreement. If not, you proceed to a hearing before an impartial hearing officer.

KRUM ISD Mediation: A Collaborative Alternative

Mediation offers a middle ground between informal resolution and due process. A trained mediator helps KRUM ISD and parents communicate and reach voluntary agreement without the formality and cost of a hearing.

Benefits of KRUM ISD Mediation

Mediation preserves relationships because both parties work toward a mutually acceptable solution rather than litigating. It's also faster and less expensive than due process. Many families in KRUM ISD successfully resolve disputes through mediation that might otherwise require hearing officers to decide.

Requesting Mediation with KRUM ISD

You can request mediation even if you've already filed a due process complaint. Contact KRUM ISD's special education department and ask about mediation availability. The district must provide information about free mediation services. You may also have a special education advocate or attorney participate in mediation to support you.

Mediation sessions are confidential, meaning anything discussed cannot be used in a later due process hearing. This confidentiality encourages honest communication and creative problem-solving.

Filing a Complaint with Texas Education Agency (TEA)

Beyond local KRUM ISD complaint processes, you can file a complaint directly with the Texas Education Agency, which oversees special education compliance statewide.

When to File a KRUM ISD TEA Complaint

A KRUM ISD TEA complaint is appropriate when you believe the district has violated state or federal special education law. Unlike due process (which focuses on whether your child received FAPE), TEA complaints examine whether KRUM ISD followed proper procedures and complied with legal requirements.

The TEA Complaint Process

To file a complaint with TEA regarding KRUM ISD, submit the State Complaint Form available on TEA's website. Your complaint must be filed within one year of when you knew or should have known about the alleged violation. TEA investigators will contact KRUM ISD, review documentation, interview staff and family, and issue a written determination within 60 days.

TEA can require KRUM ISD to take corrective actions, provide compensatory education services, and implement monitoring to ensure future compliance.
