A Full Individual Evaluation, often called an FIE, is a comprehensive assessment that determines whether your child has a disability and qualifies for special education services under IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) and Texas Education Code. For parents in KRUM ISD, this evaluation is the foundation of special education support.

The KRUM ISD FIE evaluation process examines your child's cognitive abilities, academic performance, social-emotional functioning, and adaptive behaviors. It's designed to provide a complete picture of your child's strengths and needs across multiple areas of development.

An FIE can identify disabilities including autism, learning disabilities, intellectual disabilities, speech/language impairments, emotional disturbances, and other health impairments. For KRUM ISD students, obtaining an FIE often leads to an Individualized Education Program (IEP) if the child qualifies for services.

Why Parents Request FIEs

  • Your child is struggling academically despite intervention efforts
  • You've noticed developmental delays compared to peers
  • A teacher or school counselor has recommended evaluation
  • Your child has been recently diagnosed with a condition
  • Previous evaluations are outdated and need updating

KRUM ISD has a legal obligation under IDEA to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities, including those in private schools and homeschooled students in the area. This is called the "KRUM ISD child find" process, and it's an important protection for families in the KRUM area.

The district must conduct KRUM ISD child find activities including screening programs and awareness campaigns. However, as a parent, you don't need to wait for the district to identify your child—you can initiate the process yourself.

If your child attends a private school in or around KRUM, you still have the right to request a KRUM ISD FIE evaluation from the public school district. KRUM ISD must respond to your request even if your child doesn't attend their schools.

Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing

The most important thing to understand about requesting a KRUM ISD special education evaluation is that your request must be in writing. While verbal requests may be acknowledged, a written request creates a paper trail and starts the official timeline.

Your written request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current school and grade level
  • Specific areas of concern (academics, behavior, speech, motor skills, etc.)
  • Examples of difficulties you've observed at home
  • Any previous evaluations or testing
  • Your contact information and preferred method of communication

Keep your letter straightforward and factual. You don't need to use special terminology—simply describe what you're observing that concerns you.

Step 2: Submit to the Right Contact

For a KRUM ISD evaluation request, you should send your written request to multiple people to ensure it's documented:

  • Special Education Director – KRUM ISD Administration Office
  • Your child's campus principal – if your child is enrolled in KRUM ISD
  • Your child's classroom teacher

Send your request via email (request a read receipt), certified mail, or hand-deliver it to the KRUM ISD administration office. This creates documentation that your request was received on a specific date.

KRUM ISD parents should keep copies of everything for their records. This documentation becomes important if disputes arise later.

Step 3: Know the KRUM ISD 45 Day Timeline

Once KRUM ISD receives your written KRUM ISD evaluation request, the district has a critical timeline to follow. The KRUM ISD 45 day timeline is a legal requirement under Texas Education Code and IDEA.

Specifically, KRUM ISD must complete the entire FIE evaluation and hold an IEP eligibility meeting within 45 school days of receiving your written consent. This is a non-negotiable deadline for KRUM ISD special education evaluations.

However, this timeline begins only after KRUM ISD obtains your written consent for the evaluation. The district must give you an evaluation plan to review and sign before assessment can begin.

Step 4: Review and Sign the Evaluation Plan

Within a reasonable time after your KRUM ISD child find request, you'll receive a detailed Evaluation Plan from KRUM ISD. This document outlines:

  • Tests and assessments that will be administered
  • Timeline for completing the evaluation
  • Your rights as a parent
  • The KRUM ISD 45 day timeline explanation

You have the right to ask questions about any assessment KRUM ISD proposes. If you disagree with the proposed assessment methods, discuss your concerns with the school before signing.

Your signature gives KRUM ISD permission to begin testing. Without your signature, KRUM ISD cannot legally proceed with the KRUM ISD FIE evaluation.

What to Expect During the KRUM ISD Evaluation Process

Types of Assessments in a KRUM ISD FIE

A comprehensive KRUM ISD FIE evaluation typically includes multiple assessment components:

  • Cognitive/IQ testing – Measures reasoning, memory, and processing abilities
  • Achievement testing – Assesses reading, math, writing, and other academic skills
  • Speech/language evaluation – If speech or language concerns exist
  • Social-emotional assessment – Evaluates behavior, emotional functioning, and social skills
  • Parent and teacher rating scales – Gathers input from adults who know your child
  • Classroom observation – KRUM ISD evaluators watch your child in the school environment
  • Developmental and medical history review – Examines background information

KRUM ISD special education evaluation teams are trained to use multiple methods and data sources. This ensures the evaluation is fair, comprehensive, and not biased.

Parent Participation During the Evaluation

As a parent in KRUM ISD, you have important rights during the KRUM ISD special education evaluation process. You should:

  • Provide background information about your child's development and medical history
  • Share concerns you've observed at home
  • Attend meetings and conferences related to the evaluation
  • Ask for clarification about any assessment or procedure
  • Request copies of evaluation reports before the IEP meeting
  • Bring a support person or advocate to meetings if desired

KRUM ISD must involve you as a meaningful participant in the evaluation process, not merely as someone to sign paperwork.

Understanding the KRUM ISD 45 Day Timeline Requirements

The KRUM ISD 45 day timeline is one of the most important protections for parents. However, understanding exactly how it works prevents confusion and disappointment.

When the Clock Starts

The KRUM ISD 45 day timeline begins the day KRUM ISD receives your written consent for evaluation. This is why documenting that KRUM ISD received your request in writing is crucial.

The 45 days counts school days only, not calendar days or weekends. This means the timeline extends through typical school breaks and holidays.

What Must Happen Within the Timeline

Within the KRUM ISD 45 day timeline, the district must:

  1. Complete all testing and assessment activities
  2. Score and interpret the results
  3. Write a comprehensive evaluation report
  4. Schedule and hold an IEP eligibility meeting
  5. Determine whether your child qualifies for special education
  6. If eligible, develop an IEP at that same meeting

The entire process—from testing through IEP development—must occur within the KRUM ISD 45 day timeline. This is why KRUM ISD families need to stay engaged and keep the process moving.

Exceptions to the Timeline

There are limited circumstances where KRUM ISD may extend the KRUM ISD 45 day timeline:

  • Parent-initiated delays or inability to attend meetings
  • Medical or other emergencies affecting evaluation
  • Need for outside medical evaluation or specialist input

Even with these exceptions, KRUM ISD cannot indefinitely delay your child's evaluation. The district must document any extension and keep you informed.

At the end of the KRUM ISD FIE evaluation process, KRUM ISD will schedule an IEP eligibility meeting. This meeting determines whether your child qualifies for special education services.

Before the Meeting

Request the evaluation report from KRUM ISD at least 3-5 days before the meeting. This gives you time to review findings and prepare questions. You have the right to this report before the meeting.

Write down your observations, concerns, and questions. If you have medical records or previous evaluations, bring copies to the meeting.

Consider bringing a trusted support person, family member, or special ed advocate KRUM residents can contact for assistance. Having support helps you participate more effectively.

During the Eligibility Meeting

The KRUM ISD IEP team will present evaluation findings and discuss whether your child has a disability and needs special education. This meeting is collaborative—your input matters.

Ask questions about anything you don't understand. Request clarification on test scores, conclusions, or recommendations. This is your opportunity to ensure KRUM ISD has an accurate, complete picture of your child.

If the team determines your child qualifies, you'll develop an IEP at this same meeting or schedule a follow-up meeting. If your child doesn't qualify, ask about alternative supports and services available through KRUM ISD.

Your Rights as a Parent in the KRUM ISD FIE Process

KRUM ISD families have important legal protections throughout the KRUM ISD special education evaluation process. Understanding these rights empowers you to be an effective advocate.

Core Parent Rights

  • Prior written notice – KRUM ISD must explain evaluation plans and timelines before proceeding
  • Informed consent – KRUM ISD cannot evaluate your child without your written permission
  • Access to records – You can request and review all evaluation materials and reports
  • Independent evaluation – You can obtain a private evaluation at public expense under certain circumstances
  • Due process hearing – You can request a hearing if you disagree with KRUM ISD's evaluation or decisions
  • Parent participation – You must be involved meaningfully in all evaluation decisions

Confidentiality Protections

KRUM ISD must keep all evaluation information confidential. Your child's evaluation results and any special education records cannot be shared without your permission (except with school staff who have a legitimate educational need).

You can request information about who has accessed your child's records. KRUM ISD must provide this information upon request.

What If You Disagree With KRUM ISD's Evaluation?

If you believe KRUM ISD's KRUM ISD FIE evaluation is incomplete, inaccurate, or biased, you have options.

Requesting an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE)

You can request an Independent Educational Evaluation at public expense if you disagree with KRUM ISD's evaluation. KRUM ISD must either fund the independent evaluation or request a due process hearing to defend their evaluation.

An independent evaluation provides an objective assessment and can be particularly valuable if you believe KRUM ISD missed something or evaluated unfairly.

After the FIE: What Comes Next for KRUM ISD Families

If your child qualifies for special education through the KRUM ISD FIE evaluation, KRUM ISD will develop an IEP. If your child doesn't qualify, explore alternative supports available through KRUM ISD.

If Your Child Qualifies

KRUM ISD will develop a detailed IEP specifying:

  • Special education and related services your child will receive
  • How often services are provided and in what setting
  • Annual goals addressing your child's needs
  • How progress will be measured and reported
  • Accommodations and modifications to help your child access the curriculum

You'll review and approve the IEP before KRUM ISD begins services. You have the right to request changes and participate in all IEP decisions.

If Your Child Doesn't Qualify

If the KRUM ISD evaluation team determines your child doesn't meet eligibility criteria, ask about:

  • RTI (Response to Intervention) programs KRUM ISD offers
  • 504 Plan eligibility if your child has a disability affecting major life activities
  • Counseling or behavioral support services available
  • Tutoring or academic intervention programs
  • Requesting a re-evaluation after additional data is gathered
