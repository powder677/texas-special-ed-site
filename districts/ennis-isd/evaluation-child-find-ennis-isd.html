A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine whether your child has a disability and qualifies for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). For families in ENNIS ISD, this evaluation is the gateway to accessing specialized support and services for students who need them.

The ENNIS ISD evaluation process includes testing in multiple areas, such as cognitive ability, academic achievement, social-emotional functioning, and adaptive behavior. The goal is to create a complete picture of your child's strengths, challenges, and educational needs.

Parents in ENNIS ISD should know that an FIE is different from routine screening or classroom assessments. It's a formal, multi-faceted assessment conducted by trained professionals that forms the basis for special education eligibility decisions.

ENNIS ISD, like all Texas school districts, has a legal obligation under the ENNIS ISD child find mandate to identify, locate, and evaluate all children suspected of having a disability. This responsibility applies to all children ages 3-21, including those in private schools within the ENNIS area.

What ENNIS ISD Child Find Includes

  • Actively searching for children with disabilities in the community
  • Screening students who may have disabilities
  • Evaluating students referred by parents, teachers, or other professionals
  • Providing services to children with disabilities living within the ENNIS ISD boundaries

If you believe your child may have a disability, ENNIS ISD cannot ignore your concerns. The district's child find obligations mean they must respond to your evaluation request in a timely manner.

Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing

The most important action you can take is to submit a written request for an evaluation to ENNIS ISD. While verbal requests may be made, having a written document protects your rights and creates an official record. Address your letter to the Director of Special Education or your child's campus principal.

Your request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • The school and grade level
  • Specific concerns about your child's learning or development
  • Areas where you've noticed difficulties (academics, behavior, social skills, communication, etc.)
  • Any relevant medical, developmental, or family history
  • A request for a full individual evaluation

Step 2: Send Your Request to the Right Contact

In ENNIS ISD, you should send your evaluation request to:

  • Your child's school principal
  • The ENNIS ISD Director of Special Education
  • The special education department at the ENNIS ISD central office

Keep a copy for your records and consider sending it via certified mail or email with a read receipt. This creates documentation that ENNIS ISD received your request.

Step 3: Know Your Rights During the Request Process

Once you submit your written request for an ENNIS ISD evaluation, ENNIS ISD must respond. You have the right to:

  • Receive a response to your evaluation request within specific timeframes
  • Understand all evaluation procedures before they begin
  • Have the evaluation conducted in your child's primary language if applicable
  • Ask questions about assessment methods and instruments
  • Be part of the evaluation team that determines eligibility

The ENNIS ISD 45 Day Timeline Explained

One of the most critical timelines for parents in ENNIS ISD to understand is the ENNIS ISD 45 day timeline for completing an evaluation. After ENNIS ISD receives your written request for an ENNIS ISD FIE evaluation, the district must complete all testing and provide you with results and eligibility determination within 45 calendar days.

What Happens During This 45-Day Window

The 45 day timeline in ENNIS ISD includes:

  1. Days 1-5: ENNIS ISD reviews your request and may gather existing data about your child
  2. Days 6-35: Evaluators conduct assessments in required areas (cognitive, academic, behavioral, etc.)
  3. Days 36-45: The evaluation team reviews results and holds an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss findings

Important Exceptions to the 45-Day Rule

The ENNIS ISD 45 day timeline can be extended only in specific circumstances:

  • With your written consent to extend the timeline
  • If your child has significant absences during the evaluation period
  • If you request additional assessments beyond the standard evaluation

ENNIS ISD cannot simply delay your evaluation without documentation of these exceptions.

What to Expect During the ENNIS ISD Evaluation Process

Areas Assessed in an ENNIS ISD Special Education Evaluation

A comprehensive ENNIS ISD special education evaluation typically includes assessment in these areas:

  • Cognitive abilities: IQ and reasoning skills
  • Academic achievement: Reading, math, writing, and other school subjects
  • Communication: Speech and language development
  • Social-emotional functioning: Behavior, emotions, and social skills
  • Motor skills: Fine and gross motor development
  • Adaptive behavior: Daily living and self-care skills

Who Conducts the ENNIS ISD Evaluation

Your child's evaluation in ENNIS ISD will be conducted by a team that may include:

  • Special education teachers
  • School psychologists
  • Speech-language pathologists
  • Occupational or physical therapists
  • The child's current teacher(s)
  • Your child's parent or guardian (you)

Understanding Your Rights During ENNIS ISD Special Education Evaluation

Parent Participation in ENNIS ISD FIE Evaluation

Texas Education Code and IDEA regulations guarantee that parents in ENNIS ISD are active participants in the evaluation process. You have the right to:

  • Be present during all evaluation meetings
  • Ask evaluators to explain assessment results
  • Share your observations about your child's behavior at home
  • Request additional evaluations or second opinions
  • Obtain copies of all evaluation reports

Understanding Evaluation Results

After ENNIS ISD completes the evaluation, you'll receive a detailed report. Don't hesitate to ask questions. Evaluators must explain:

  • What each test measures and what the scores mean
  • How results relate to your child's educational performance
  • How findings support or don't support special education eligibility
  • Recommendations for services if your child qualifies

Next Steps After Your ENNIS ISD Evaluation Request

The ARD Meeting

Upon completion of your child's evaluation, ENNIS ISD must schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. At this meeting, the ENNIS ISD evaluation team will discuss whether your child qualifies for special education services.

During the ARD meeting, ENNIS ISD will determine eligibility based on whether your child:

  • Has a disability recognized under IDEA
  • Requires special education services as a result of that disability
  • Would benefit from an Individualized Education Program (IEP)

If Your Child Qualifies

If the ENNIS ISD evaluation team determines your child qualifies for special education, they will immediately begin developing an Individualized Education Program (IEP). This document outlines the specific services and support your child will receive.

If Your Child Does Not Qualify

If ENNIS ISD determines your child does not qualify under IDEA, you have the right to:

  • Request a due process hearing
  • Request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at district expense
  • Explore other support options, such as Section 504 plans
  • Consult with a special education attorney near ENNIS
