A Full Individual Evaluation is a thorough assessment conducted by MARBLE FALLS ISD to determine whether your child has a disability and qualifies for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). The evaluation examines your child's academic performance, developmental history, and any suspected areas of concern.

Parents in MARBLE FALLS ISD should understand that this evaluation is free and is one of the most important tools available to you. The MARBLE FALLS ISD special education evaluation process is designed to be comprehensive and fair, examining multiple areas of development and functioning.

The evaluation can lead to identification of disabilities including learning disabilities, speech/language impairments, autism spectrum disorder, emotional disturbance, intellectual disability, and other health impairments. For families in the MARBLE FALLS area, understanding this process empowers you to advocate effectively for your child's educational needs.

Step 1: Know Your Right to Request

Under Texas Education Code Section 29.001, any parent or guardian can request a MARBLE FALLS ISD child find evaluation at any time during your child's school years. You don't need permission from anyone, and you don't need to have documentation of prior concerns.

Simply being a parent in MARBLE FALLS ISD gives you the legal right to request evaluation. Your child doesn't need to have already failed a class or be significantly behind to qualify for assessment. Early intervention can make a tremendous difference.

Step 2: Submit Your Written Request

Contact MARBLE FALLS ISD Special Education Department in writing. Your request should be clear and include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • The school your child attends within MARBLE FALLS ISD
  • Specific areas of concern (academic struggles, behavior, speech, social skills, etc.)
  • Your contact information
  • Your signature and date

You can deliver your request in person, mail it, email it, or submit it through the school office. Get written confirmation that MARBLE FALLS ISD received your request—this is important for the timeline.

Step 3: Understand the MARBLE FALLS ISD 45 Day Timeline

Once MARBLE FALLS ISD receives your written request for a special education evaluation, the district has 45 calendar days to complete the evaluation and provide you with results. This timeline is mandated by IDEA and Texas law.

Parents in MARBLE FALLS ISD should mark their calendar 45 days from the date the district received your request. The MARBLE FALLS ISD 45 day timeline is a critical protection ensuring your child doesn't wait excessive time for evaluation.

Note: This timeline can be extended if you and the district agree in writing, or if your child is absent during much of the evaluation period. However, MARBLE FALLS ISD must still provide evaluation results within a reasonable timeframe.

Step 4: Consent for Evaluation

Before MARBLE FALLS ISD can conduct the evaluation, the district must provide you with written notice and obtain your informed consent. You'll receive a Prior Written Notice (PWN) explaining:

  • Why the evaluation is being considered
  • What evaluations will be conducted
  • Your rights as a parent
  • Procedural safeguards

Read this document carefully. You have the right to ask questions and request clarification before signing consent for the MARBLE FALLS ISD special education evaluation.

What Happens During the MARBLE FALLS ISD Evaluation

Comprehensive Assessment Areas

A Full Individual Evaluation in MARBLE FALLS ISD typically includes assessments in multiple areas:

  • Cognitive/Intellectual Assessment: Tests of reasoning, problem-solving, and processing ability
  • Academic Achievement: Reading, math, writing, and other academic skills
  • Speech and Language: Communication skills (if applicable)
  • Social-Emotional/Behavioral: Social skills, emotional functioning, and behavior patterns
  • Motor Skills: Fine and gross motor development (if applicable)
  • Vision and Hearing Screening: Basic sensory assessments

MARBLE FALLS ISD evaluators use standardized tests, classroom observations, teacher input, parent information, and medical records to create a complete picture of your child's abilities and challenges.

Your Role During the Evaluation

As a parent in MARBLE FALLS ISD, your input is vital. You'll likely participate by:

  • Completing a developmental and health history questionnaire
  • Providing background information about your child's early development
  • Sharing information about behaviors and skills at home
  • Attending parent interviews or meetings
  • Providing medical or prior evaluation records

Don't hesitate to share concerns or observations. MARBLE FALLS ISD evaluators need this information to accurately assess your child's needs.

Understanding Your Rights and Protections

MARBLE FALLS ISD Must Use Culturally and Linguistically Appropriate Assessments

The district must ensure evaluations are not discriminatory. If English is not your primary language, MARBLE FALLS ISD must provide interpreters and materials in your home language as needed. This protects families in the MARBLE FALLS area from biased evaluations.

You Can Bring Support to Your Meetings

During any meeting with MARBLE FALLS ISD regarding evaluation, you have the right to bring someone with you—a spouse, family member, friend, advocate, or attorney. Many parents in MARBLE FALLS ISD find having a support person helpful for taking notes and asking questions.

Right to Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE)

If you disagree with the MARBLE FALLS ISD evaluation results, you have the right to request an Independent Educational Evaluation at the district's expense. You can file this request within 15 days of receiving the evaluation report from MARBLE FALLS ISD.

After the Evaluation: What Happens Next

The Evaluation Report

Within the MARBLE FALLS ISD 45 day timeline, you'll receive a comprehensive written evaluation report. This document should include:

  • Test scores and assessment results
  • Interpretation of findings
  • Whether your child meets disability criteria
  • Recommendations for services or supports
  • Notice of your rights and next steps

Take time to review this carefully. If you don't understand something, ask the evaluators to explain it.

IEP Meeting or IAES Meeting

If your child qualifies for special education, MARBLE FALLS ISD will schedule an Individualized Education Program (IEP) meeting within 30 days. If your child doesn't qualify, you'll receive notice of that determination and information about other supports available through MARBLE FALLS ISD.

The IEP meeting is where parents in MARBLE FALLS ISD work with educators to develop the specific services and supports your child will receive.

Key Timeline Reference for MARBLE FALLS ISD Families

Here's a quick reference for important dates:

  • Day 1: You submit written evaluation request to MARBLE FALLS ISD
  • Within 5-10 days: MARBLE FALLS ISD provides Prior Written Notice and requests consent
  • Day 45: Evaluation must be complete and results provided to you
  • Within 15 days: Deadline to request Independent Educational Evaluation if you disagree
  • Within 30 days: IEP meeting (if eligible) or notice of ineligibility

Common Questions from MARBLE FALLS ISD Parents

Will an evaluation hurt my child's records?

No. An evaluation is a diagnostic tool. If your child doesn't qualify for special education, the evaluation simply provides information. If your child does qualify, special education services provide support, not punishment.

What if I'm worried about stigma?

Parents in MARBLE FALLS ISD sometimes worry about labeling. However, early identification and appropriate support—which may result from a MARBLE FALLS ISD special education evaluation—actually improves outcomes and builds confidence. Without proper evaluation, children often struggle unnecessarily.

Can MARBLE FALLS ISD refuse my evaluation request?

MARBLE FALLS ISD cannot refuse a reasonable request for evaluation. If the district denies your request, they must provide written explanation. You can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency if you believe MARBLE FALLS ISD violated your rights.
