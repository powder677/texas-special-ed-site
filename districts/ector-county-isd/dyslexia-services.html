Dyslexia Services in Ector County ISD

Dyslexia Services in Ector County ISD

This guide provides information regarding dyslexia services available to students within Ector County ISD, in accordance with Texas state law and guidelines. It outlines screening procedures, intervention programs, support systems, and evaluation processes. This information is intended for parents, educators, and community members seeking a better understanding of how Ector County ISD supports students with dyslexia.

Texas Dyslexia Handbook 2024

Ector County ISD adheres to the guidelines outlined in the most current version of the Texas Dyslexia Handbook. This handbook, developed by the Texas Education Agency (TEA), serves as the primary resource for understanding dyslexia, its identification, and effective intervention strategies within Texas public schools. The handbook is updated periodically to reflect current research and best practices in the field. Understanding the Texas Dyslexia Handbook is crucial for both parents and educators.

Key aspects covered in the Texas Dyslexia Handbook include:

  • Definition of Dyslexia: The handbook provides a clear and consistent definition of dyslexia as a specific learning disability that is neurological in origin. It is characterized by difficulties with accurate and/or fluent word recognition and by poor spelling and decoding abilities.
  • Characteristics of Dyslexia: The handbook describes the various characteristics associated with dyslexia, including difficulties with phonological awareness, phonological memory, rapid naming, and orthographic processing. These characteristics can manifest differently in individuals, and the handbook offers detailed explanations for recognizing these variations.
  • Screening Procedures: The handbook outlines recommended screening procedures for identifying students at risk for dyslexia in kindergarten and first grade, and procedures for older students who may be experiencing reading difficulties.
  • Instructional Practices: The handbook emphasizes the importance of structured literacy instruction for students with dyslexia. This includes specific components such as phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension.
  • Evaluation and Diagnosis: The handbook describes the process for evaluating students to determine if they have dyslexia, including the assessments that should be used and the criteria for diagnosis.
  • Legal Requirements: The handbook details the legal requirements for providing services to students with dyslexia under state and federal law, including Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act and the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA).

Parents and educators are strongly encouraged to familiarize themselves with the Texas Dyslexia Handbook, which can be found on the Texas Education Agency website. A direct link will be provided on the Ector County ISD website as well.

Mandatory Screening

Ector County ISD implements mandatory dyslexia screening for all students in kindergarten and first grade, as mandated by Texas law. This screening is designed to identify students who may be at risk for dyslexia early in their academic careers. Early identification and intervention are crucial for improving reading outcomes for students with dyslexia.

The screening process typically involves the following components:

  • Phonological Awareness: Assessing a student's ability to hear and manipulate the sounds in spoken words (e.g., identifying rhyming words, blending sounds to form words, segmenting words into individual sounds).
  • Rapid Automatized Naming (RAN): Assessing a student's ability to quickly name a series of familiar objects, colors, or numbers. This is often assessed using a standardized RAN test.
  • Letter Sound Knowledge: Assessing a student's knowledge of the sounds associated with individual letters.

Students who demonstrate difficulty on the initial screening may be provided with targeted interventions and progress monitoring. If a student continues to struggle despite these interventions, further evaluation may be recommended to determine if they have dyslexia. Parents are notified of the screening results and any recommended follow-up actions. The exact screening instruments used are aligned with the guidelines in the Texas Dyslexia Handbook and selected based on their validity and reliability.

For students in grades 2 and above who are exhibiting reading difficulties, teachers may refer them for dyslexia screening or evaluation at any time. Parents can also request an evaluation if they have concerns about their child's reading progress.

Structured Literacy Programs

Ector County ISD utilizes structured literacy programs that align with the principles outlined in the Texas Dyslexia Handbook. Structured literacy is an explicit, systematic, and cumulative approach to teaching reading and spelling. It is particularly effective for students with dyslexia because it directly addresses the underlying language skills that are often affected by the condition.

Key characteristics of structured literacy programs include:

  • Explicit Instruction: Concepts are taught directly and clearly, with no assumptions made about prior knowledge.
  • Systematic Instruction: Skills are taught in a logical and sequential order, building upon previously learned concepts.
  • Cumulative Instruction: New skills are integrated with previously learned skills, ensuring mastery and retention.
  • Multisensory Instruction: Instruction incorporates visual, auditory, and kinesthetic modalities to engage multiple senses and enhance learning.
  • Diagnostic Teaching: Teachers continuously monitor student progress and adjust instruction based on individual needs.

Specific structured literacy programs implemented in Ector County ISD may include (but are not limited to):

  • Neuhaus Education Center Programs: Neuhaus offers a variety of structured literacy programs that focus on foundational reading skills. Specific programs like Basic Language Skills or Reading Readiness are often used.
  • Orton-Gillingham Based Approaches: These programs are rooted in the Orton-Gillingham methodology, which is a multisensory approach to teaching reading and spelling. Several different commercial programs are available that align with Orton-Gillingham principles.
  • Other Research-Based Programs: Ector County ISD continuously evaluates and adopts research-based programs that meet the needs of our students. Specific program choices may vary by campus and grade level.

The specific program used for a student will be determined by a team of educators, including the student's teacher, a dyslexia specialist, and other relevant personnel, based on the student's individual needs and learning profile.

504 vs. IEP for Dyslexia

Students with dyslexia may receive support through either a Section 504 plan or an Individualized Education Program (IEP), depending on the severity of their needs and the impact of dyslexia on their access to the general education curriculum.

Section 504 Plan:

  • Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 is a civil rights law that prohibits discrimination based on disability.
  • A 504 plan provides accommodations and modifications to ensure that students with disabilities have equal access to the general education curriculum.
  • Students with dyslexia may be eligible for a 504 plan if their dyslexia substantially limits one or more major life activities, such as reading, learning, or thinking.
  • 504 plans typically include accommodations such as extended time on tests, preferential seating, assistive technology, and modified assignments.
  • A 504 plan does NOT provide specialized instruction.

Individualized Education Program (IEP):

  • An IEP is a legally binding document developed under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA).
  • An IEP provides specialized instruction and related services to students with disabilities who require significant support to access the general education curriculum.
  • Students with dyslexia may be eligible for an IEP if their dyslexia meets the criteria for a Specific Learning Disability (SLD) and requires specialized instruction to make progress in reading and other academic areas.
  • IEPs include goals, objectives, and accommodations, as well as specialized instruction that is tailored to the student's individual needs.
  • IEPs may also include related services such as speech-language therapy or occupational therapy.

Key Differences:

  • Eligibility: 504 eligibility is based on substantial limitation of a major life activity, while IEP eligibility is based on meeting specific disability criteria and requiring specialized instruction.
  • Instruction: 504 plans provide accommodations, while IEPs provide specialized instruction.
  • Legal Requirements: IEPs are governed by IDEA, while 504 plans are governed by Section 504.

The decision of whether a student requires a 504 plan or an IEP is made by a team of professionals, including the student's teacher, a dyslexia specialist, and other relevant personnel, in collaboration with the parents.

Accommodations List

Accommodations are changes to the way a student learns or is assessed that do not change the content of what is being taught. Accommodations are designed to provide students with equal access to the curriculum and to demonstrate their knowledge and skills. The specific accommodations offered to a student will be determined by the 504 or IEP team based on the student's individual needs.

Examples of common accommodations for students with dyslexia include:

  • Extended Time: Providing additional time to complete assignments and tests.
  • Preferential Seating: Placing the student in a location in the classroom that minimizes distractions.
  • Assistive Technology: Using tools such as text-to-speech software, speech-to-text software, or graphic organizers.
  • Modified Assignments: Adjusting the length, complexity, or format of assignments.
  • Oral Administration of Tests: Reading test questions aloud to the student.
  • Use of a Calculator: Allowing the student to use a calculator for math assignments and tests.
  • Quiet Testing Environment: Providing a quiet space for the student to complete tests.
  • Breaks: Allowing the student to take short breaks during assignments and tests.
  • Highlighting Key Information: Highlighting important information in textbooks and other materials.
  • Use of Graphic Organizers: Providing graphic organizers to help the student organize their thoughts and ideas.
  • Multi-Sensory Strategies: Using tactile or kinesthetic activities to enhance learning.
  • Recorded Lectures: Providing access to recorded lectures for review.
  • Access to Notes: Providing copies of teacher notes or allowing the student to take pictures of the board.
  • Alternative Assessments: Providing alternative ways for the student to demonstrate their knowledge and skills, such as projects or presentations.

This is not an exhaustive list, and other accommodations may be appropriate depending on the student's individual needs. The goal of accommodations is to level the playing field and provide students with dyslexia the opportunity to succeed in school.

How to Request Evaluation

Parents who have concerns about their child's reading progress can request a dyslexia evaluation. The process for requesting an evaluation in Ector County ISD is as follows:

  1. Contact the School: The first step is to contact your child's teacher, school counselor, or principal to express your concerns. They can provide you with information about the evaluation process and answer any questions you may have.
  2. Submit a Written Request: Submit a written request for a dyslexia evaluation to the school principal or special education director. The request should include your child's name, date of birth, grade level, and a brief description of your concerns.
  3. Evaluation Process: Once the school receives your written request, the evaluation process will begin. The school will gather information about your child's academic history, including their reading performance, classroom observations, and any previous interventions.
  4. Assessments: A team of professionals, including a dyslexia specialist and educational diagnostician, will conduct a comprehensive evaluation to determine if your child has dyslexia. The evaluation will typically include assessments of phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension.
  5. Evaluation Report: After the evaluation is completed, the school will provide you with a written report outlining the results of the evaluation. The report will indicate whether or not your child meets the criteria for dyslexia and will recommend appropriate interventions and support services.
  6. ARD Meeting (if applicable): If your child is found to have dyslexia and requires specialized instruction, an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committee meeting will be held to develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). The ARD committee will include you, your child's teacher, a dyslexia specialist, and other relevant personnel.

Ector County ISD is committed to providing timely and appropriate evaluations for students who are suspected of having dyslexia. We encourage parents to reach out to their child's school if they have any concerns about their child's reading development. We also encourage parents to maintain open communication with school staff to ensure their child receives the necessary support and services.

Important Note: This guide is intended to provide general information about dyslexia services in Ector County ISD. Specific policies and procedures may vary by campus and grade level. Please contact your child's school for more detailed information.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

Is my child entitled to dyslexia testing in Texas? +

Yes. Texas law requires all districts to screen students for dyslexia and related disorders in Kindergarten and First Grade, and at any grade level if a parent or teacher requests it.

What accommodations can a dyslexic child get in Texas? +

Accommodations may include extended time on tests, text-to-speech software, oral administration of tests, reduced-answer-choice formats, and use of a calculator. These can be provided through a 504 Plan or an IEP.

What is the Texas Dyslexia Handbook? +

The Texas Dyslexia Handbook is a state guidance document updated in 2024 that outlines districts' responsibilities for identifying, evaluating, and serving students with dyslexia using structured literacy methods.
