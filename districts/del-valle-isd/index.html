⚡ Quick Answer

What can I do right now if Del Valle ISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Del Valle ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Del Valle ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the special education system can feel overwhelming, but understanding your rights and your child's entitlements is the first step. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), both federal and Texas law guarantee children with disabilities access to a Free and Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) designed to meet their unique needs. This includes specially designed instruction and related services tailored to help your child succeed. Your voice is vital in ensuring Del Valle ISD meets its obligations to your child.

Del Valle ISD is subject to the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA Region 13 Education Service Center also provides crucial support and resources to Del Valle ISD, assisting the district in implementing effective special education programs and practices. This means Del Valle ISD has access to expertise and guidance to ensure they are meeting the needs of students with disabilities.

Crucially, remember that as a parent, you have legally enforceable rights throughout the entire special education process. From initial evaluation to the development and implementation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP), you are a key member of the team, and your input is essential. Understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child within Del Valle ISD. Remember that even if a Del Valle ISD staff member tells you something contrary to your legal rights, those rights still exist.

Action Step: Familiarize yourself with the TEA's Parent's Guide to Special Education Rights. You can find a link on texasspecialed.com.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Del Valle ISD

If you suspect your child has a disability that is impacting their educational performance, you have the right to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) from Del Valle ISD. This evaluation is crucial for determining if your child qualifies for special education services. It's important to know that to initiate the evaluation process, your request must be in WRITING. A verbal request, while helpful to get the conversation started, does not officially start the timeline for evaluation. Do not rely on a meeting or verbal discussion to initiate the evaluation process. Del Valle ISD is legally required to conduct this evaluation at no cost to you.

Once you provide written consent for the evaluation, Del Valle ISD has 45 school days to complete the FIE and hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to discuss the results. The evaluation will assess your child's strengths and needs in various areas, such as academic skills, communication, and behavior. Furthermore, remember that Child Find laws mandate that Del Valle ISD actively seek out and identify students within the district who may have disabilities and require special education services. This means that if the school suspects your child needs services, they should initiate the evaluation process even without a parent request.

Action Step: Right now, draft a written request for a Full Individual Evaluation. Clearly state your concerns and reasons for requesting the evaluation. Keep a copy for your records, and send the original to the Del Valle ISD principal and special education director via certified mail with return receipt requested. This provides proof of delivery.

ARD Meetings in Del Valle ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal, and these meetings are the cornerstone of the special education process. It's where decisions are made about your child's eligibility for services, their IEP, and their educational placement within Del Valle ISD. Under Texas law, Del Valle ISD must provide you with at least five calendar days' written notice before any ARD meeting. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, who will be attending, and your rights as a parent.

You have the right to actively participate in every ARD meeting. You are an equal member of the ARD committee, and your input is valued. You have the right to request a 10-day recess, or break, at any point during the ARD meeting to review information, seek advice, or simply take time to process the discussion. This is your right under Texas law. Additionally, you are never obligated to sign the IEP the same day as the ARD meeting. Take the time you need to carefully review the document and ensure it accurately reflects your child's needs and the agreed-upon services within Del Valle ISD.

During the ARD meeting, do not be afraid to ask questions, voice your concerns, and advocate for your child's needs. The ARD committee must consider your input when making decisions. Remember that the goal is to create an IEP that is tailored to your child's individual needs and helps them make progress in the general education curriculum within the Del Valle ISD setting.

Action Step: Before your next ARD meeting in Del Valle ISD, download our free ARD preparation checklist from texasspecialed.com. This checklist will help you organize your thoughts, gather important information, and feel confident advocating for your child.

Dyslexia Services in Del Valle ISD

Texas law mandates that Del Valle ISD screen all students for dyslexia. This screening is intended to identify students who may be at risk for reading difficulties and ensure they receive appropriate interventions. If your child is identified as having characteristics of dyslexia, they are entitled to receive structured literacy interventions designed to address their specific needs. These interventions are research-based and focus on building foundational reading skills, such as phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension.

The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook provides the standards and guidelines that Del Valle ISD must follow when screening for dyslexia, providing interventions, and supporting students with dyslexia. Depending on the severity of your child's dyslexia and their individual needs, they may qualify for a 504 Plan or an Individualized Education Program (IEP) to provide additional support and accommodations. Remember that early identification and intervention are critical for students with dyslexia.

Action Step: If your child has not been screened for dyslexia in Del Valle ISD, submit a written request to the school principal and special education director asking for a dyslexia screening.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Del Valle ISD

Discipline issues can be especially challenging for students with disabilities. It's crucial to understand your child's rights regarding discipline and suspensions within Del Valle ISD. If a student with a disability is suspended for more than 10 cumulative school days in a school year, this triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This review is an ARD meeting that must be held to determine if the behavior leading to the suspension was a manifestation of the student's disability. This also constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA, requiring careful consideration of the student's educational needs.

The MDR is a critical safeguard to ensure that students with disabilities are not unfairly punished for behaviors related to their disability. If the ARD committee determines that the behavior was a manifestation of the disability, the committee must then develop or revise the student's IEP to address the behavior and prevent future incidents. This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face within Del Valle ISD. Do not delay; you only have days to act. A school district may try to push this off or delay it, but you must insist that the ARD meeting be held promptly.

Action Step: If your child with a disability has been suspended from Del Valle ISD, immediately access and begin using the Discipline Defense Kit available on texasspecialed.com. This kit provides essential information and resources to help you navigate the discipline process and protect your child's rights.

Filing a Complaint Against Del Valle ISD

If you believe that Del Valle ISD is not meeting its legal obligations to your child, you have several options for filing a complaint. There are four main escalation paths available to you, and you can pursue these options regardless of what you are told by anyone at Del Valle ISD. These paths are your right under the law.

The first option is to file a TEA State Complaint. This is a free process, and TEA is required to investigate and resolve the complaint within 60 days. Another free option is to request mediation through TEA. Mediation is a voluntary process where a neutral third party helps you and Del Valle ISD reach a mutually agreeable resolution. If mediation is unsuccessful or you prefer a more formal process, you can request a Due Process Hearing. This is a more formal legal proceeding where an impartial hearing officer hears evidence and makes a decision. Finally, you can file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR) if you believe Del Valle ISD has discriminated against your child based on their disability. Each of these paths has different timelines and procedures, and it's important to understand your options before proceeding.

Action Step: Visit texasspecialed.com to learn more about each of these complaint options and determine which is the best fit for your situation. Knowing your rights and options is crucial for effective advocacy.

Frequently Asked Questions About Del Valle ISD Special Education

The questions every Del Valle ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Del Valle ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Del Valle ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Del Valle ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Del Valle ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Del Valle ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Del Valle ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Del Valle ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Del Valle ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
