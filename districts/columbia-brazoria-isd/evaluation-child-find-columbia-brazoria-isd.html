Child find is a legal requirement that obligates COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD to actively search for, identify, and evaluate all children with disabilities, regardless of the severity of their disability. Warning Signs That Your Child May Need Evaluation

  • Significant gaps between your child's performance and that of peers
  • Difficulty following instructions or understanding concepts taught in class
  • Speech or language delays compared to age-matched peers
  • Behavioral challenges that interfere with learning or social relationships
  • Difficulty with motor skills, coordination, or balance
  • Persistent anxiety, withdrawal, or emotional regulation problems
  • Poor social skills or difficulty making and maintaining friendships

The process of requesting a COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD evaluation request is straightforward, though it's essential to follow proper procedures to protect your rights. There are several ways you can formally initiate the evaluation process.

Step 1: Submit a Written Request

While you can request an evaluation verbally, always follow up with a written request to COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD. Address your letter to the Director of Special Education and send it via email or hand-deliver it with a signed receipt. Keep a copy for your records.

Your written request should clearly state that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation or FIE. You don't need to use special terminology—simply stating "I request that my child be evaluated for special education eligibility" is sufficient. Include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • The school your child attends
  • Specific areas of concern (learning, behavior, speech, motor skills, etc.)
  • A brief description of how these concerns affect your child's education
  • Your contact information and preferred method of communication

Step 2: Know Your Rights Under IDEA

Federal law gives parents in COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD specific rights regarding evaluation. You have the right to:

  • Request a COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD FIE evaluation at any time during your child's school career
  • Receive evaluation at no cost to your family
  • Have the evaluation conducted in your child's native language
  • Request independent evaluations if you disagree with the district's findings
  • Participate in all decisions about your child's evaluation and services

Understanding the COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD 45 Day Timeline

One of the most important protections in special education law is the COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD 45 day timeline. After you submit a written request for evaluation, COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD has exactly 45 calendar days to complete the FIE and conduct an eligibility meeting to determine whether your child qualifies for special education services.

This timeline is critical. Parents in COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD should mark their calendars when they submit their written request. The 45 day timeline is not a suggestion—it's a legal requirement under Texas Education Code §22.304 and federal IDEA regulations.

What Happens During the 45-Day Window

  1. Days 1-5: COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD acknowledges receipt of your request and obtains consent for evaluation
  2. Days 6-30: District conducts comprehensive assessments in all areas related to your child's suspected disability
  3. Days 31-44: Evaluators analyze results and prepare the evaluation report
  4. Day 45: COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD holds an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss findings and determine eligibility

The COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD 45 day timeline can be extended only if you agree in writing or if your child is absent from school for more than 10 days during the evaluation period. Don't allow the district to delay without your consent.

If COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD Misses the Deadline

If COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD fails to meet the 45-day timeline, this is a violation of your child's rights. You have grounds to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA) and may be entitled to compensatory services. Keep detailed records of all communication with the district regarding your evaluation request.

What to Expect in the COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD Special Education Evaluation Process

A COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD special education evaluation is comprehensive and multidisciplinary, meaning it involves professionals from different disciplines assessing different aspects of your child's functioning. Understanding what happens during this process reduces anxiety and helps you prepare your child.

Components of a Comprehensive FIE

Cognitive/Intellectual Assessment: A school psychologist administers standardized tests to measure your child's reasoning, problem-solving, and overall intellectual functioning. This is not an IQ test in the traditional sense but rather a measure of cognitive abilities relevant to learning.

Academic Achievement Testing: Your child's performance in reading, mathematics, writing, and other academic subjects is measured using standardized assessments. COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD compares your child's performance to grade-level expectations.

Speech and Language Evaluation: If speech or language concerns exist, a speech-language pathologist assesses your child's ability to understand language, express ideas, and use language for communication.

Social-Emotional and Behavioral Assessment: Evaluators gather information about your child's behavior, emotional regulation, and social interactions through classroom observation, rating scales, and interviews with teachers and parents.

Motor Skills Assessment: For younger children or those with suspected motor difficulties, an occupational or physical therapist may assess fine and gross motor development.

Classroom Observation: A specialist observes your child in the classroom setting to see how they function in an actual learning environment compared to peers.

Parent Participation in COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD Evaluation

Your involvement in the COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD FIE evaluation process is not optional—it's essential and required by law. Parents in COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD should actively participate in all stages of evaluation.

Before the Evaluation

Before testing begins, COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD will ask you to sign a consent form. Read this form carefully before signing. You have the right to ask questions about what will be evaluated, who will conduct the evaluation, and how results will be used. Don't feel rushed to sign immediately—take time to understand what you're consenting to.

Provide COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD with detailed information about your child's developmental history, health conditions, previous evaluations, and your specific concerns. This background information helps evaluators understand your child's full context.

During the Evaluation

You may be asked to complete rating scales or questionnaires about your child's behavior at home. Be honest and thorough in your responses. COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD evaluators rely on parent input to understand how your child functions outside of school.

The ARD Meeting: Discussing Results

At the end of the 45-day period, COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD will invite you to an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. This is where the evaluation team discusses findings and determines whether your child qualifies for special education services.

Come prepared with your own observations and documentation. If you disagree with any findings, voice your concerns at the meeting. You have the right to request an independent evaluation at district expense if you believe the COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD evaluation was inadequate or inaccurate.

Special Education Services in COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD After FIE

If your child qualifies for special education following the COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD special education evaluation, the district will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). This legally binding document outlines the services your child will receive.

COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD offers various service options, including resource classes, self-contained classrooms, speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, and assistive technology. Your child's specific needs determine which services are appropriate.

Parents in COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD should understand that receiving an FIE evaluation and qualifying for services means your child is protected under federal special education law. COLUMBIA-BRAZORIA ISD must provide free, appropriate public education in the least restrictive environment.
