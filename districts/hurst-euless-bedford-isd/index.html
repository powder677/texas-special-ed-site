Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the world of special education can feel overwhelming, but you are not alone. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas state law, special education is specially designed instruction and related services provided to eligible students with disabilities. The goal is to ensure your child has access to a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) designed to meet their unique needs. Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD, like all school districts in Texas, is overseen by the Texas Education Agency (TEA), which sets the standards for special education services. The district also receives support from TEA Region 11 Education Service Center, a valuable resource for both educators and parents. Understanding the process and your rights is the first step in ensuring your child receives the support they deserve within Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD.

Throughout the special education process, remember that you have enforceable legal rights. These rights, guaranteed by federal and state law, ensure that you have a voice in your child's education. From the initial evaluation to the development and implementation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP), your participation is crucial. Don't hesitate to ask questions, express your concerns, and advocate for your child's needs. Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD is legally obligated to respect your rights and work collaboratively with you to create an educational plan that truly benefits your child.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD

The first step in accessing special education services is a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). To initiate this process, you must submit a written request to Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD. A verbal request, while important, does not officially begin the timeline for evaluation. Your written request should clearly state that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation to determine if your child is eligible for special education services. Be specific about your concerns and any areas where you believe your child may be struggling. Remember, the burden is on Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD to evaluate your child and determine eligibility.

Once Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD receives your written consent to evaluate, they have 45 school days to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). It's also important to understand Child Find laws. These laws mandate that Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD proactively identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities who reside within the district, regardless of whether they are enrolled in public school. If you suspect your child may need special education services, don't wait for the school to come to you. Write a letter requesting an evaluation today. Keep a copy for your records, and send it via certified mail to ensure proof of delivery.

ARD Meetings in Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal, and an ARD meeting is the cornerstone of the special education process. This is where decisions are made about your child's eligibility for special education, their IEP goals, and the services they will receive within Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD. As a parent, you are a crucial member of the ARD committee, and your input is vital to creating a successful educational plan for your child. The ARD committee includes you, teachers, administrators, and other professionals who work with your child. Your participation ensures that your child's unique needs and perspectives are considered.

Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD is required to provide you with at least five calendar days of written notice before any ARD meeting. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, the proposed agenda, and who will be attending. You have the right to request a reschedule if the meeting time is inconvenient or if you need more time to prepare. Furthermore, you have the right to request a 10-day recess during the ARD meeting. This means you can pause the meeting for up to 10 calendar days to gather more information, consult with experts, or simply take time to process the information presented. It's also important to remember that you are not obligated to sign the IEP on the same day as the ARD meeting. You have the right to take the IEP home to review it thoroughly and make an informed decision.

Remember, you are an equal member of the ARD committee. You have the right to ask questions, express your concerns, and advocate for your child's needs. Do not hesitate to challenge the school's recommendations if you believe they are not in your child's best interest. Dyslexia Services in Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD

Texas law mandates that Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD screen every student for dyslexia. This screening is designed to identify students who may be at risk for reading difficulties and ensure they receive appropriate intervention. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook outlines the specific screening procedures and instructional methods that Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD must adhere to. Familiarize yourself with this handbook to understand the standards that the district is held to regarding dyslexia identification and intervention.

If your child is identified as having dyslexia, they are entitled to structured literacy intervention, a research-based approach that focuses on explicit and systematic instruction in phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and reading comprehension. Depending on the severity of your child's dyslexia and their individual needs, they may qualify for a 504 Plan or an Individualized Education Program (IEP). A 504 Plan provides accommodations and modifications to help students with disabilities access the general education curriculum, while an IEP provides specialized instruction and related services to meet the unique needs of students with disabilities. If your child has not been screened for dyslexia, request screening in writing from Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD today.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD

Disciplinary actions, particularly suspensions, can have a significant impact on students with disabilities. If your child, who receives special education services in Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD, is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year, this triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This review is a crucial step to determine if your child's misconduct was a manifestation of their disability or a result of the district's failure to properly implement their IEP. A suspension of this length also constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA, requiring an ARD meeting to address the disciplinary action and its impact on your child's educational program.

This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a special education student. You must act quickly. If your child has been suspended or is facing disciplinary action, contact the school immediately to request an ARD meeting. Document all communications with the school, and be prepared to advocate for your child's rights. Remember, Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD has specific obligations under IDEA to ensure that students with disabilities are not unfairly disciplined. If your child has been suspended, use our Discipline Defense Kit now to understand your rights and how to protect your child's educational opportunities.

Filing a Complaint Against Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD

If you feel that Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD is not meeting its obligations to your child, you have several options for recourse. These options are available to you regardless of what you may be told by district staff. The first option is to file a State Complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This is a free process, and TEA is required to investigate and resolve the complaint within 60 days. A second option is to pursue mediation through TEA. This is also a free service that allows you and the district to work together with a neutral mediator to resolve your concerns.

If mediation is unsuccessful or you prefer a more formal process, you can request a Due Process Hearing. This is an adversarial legal proceeding where an impartial hearing officer will hear evidence and make a binding decision. Finally, you can file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR) if you believe the district has discriminated against your child based on their disability. Regardless of which path you choose, remember to document all interactions with Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD and keep copies of all relevant documents. If you believe your child's rights have been violated, explore your options and consider filing a complaint to hold Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD accountable.

Frequently Asked Questions About Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD Special Education

The questions every Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Hurst-Euless-Bedford ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
