Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

This is critical if your child has an IEP! An MDR is a meeting required under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) when a school proposes a disciplinary action that results in a Change of Placement (more than 10 consecutive school days suspension, or a pattern of suspensions totaling more than 10 days and creating a pattern of removal). The purpose of the MDR is to determine if the student's behavior was caused by, or directly and substantially related to, the student's disability OR if the behavior was the direct result of the school's failure to implement the IEP.

DEMAND AN MDR IMMEDIATELY if your child faces a Change of Placement! Don't let the school dictate the narrative. Bring documentation, observations, and any evidence supporting your belief that the behavior is disability-related or due to IEP failures. The MDR team (including YOU, as a parent) will review the student's IEP, relevant information, and observations. If the behavior IS determined to be a manifestation of the disability or related to IEP implementation failure, the school MUST take immediate steps to address the root cause, including returning the student to their previous placement or modifying the IEP.

Change of Placement: Fight for Your Child's Education!

A Change of Placement occurs when a student is:

  • Removed from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days; OR
  • Subject to a series of removals that constitute a pattern (e.g., multiple suspensions totaling more than 10 days), creating a pattern of removal. Factors like the length of each removal, the total amount of time the student is removed, and the proximity of the removals to one another are considered.

A Change of Placement triggers significant rights under IDEA, including the right to an MDR (as discussed above). Longview ISD cannot simply unilaterally change your child's placement without proper process. They MUST hold an MDR if applicable, and they MUST provide written notice of the proposed change and your right to appeal.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance: Your First Line of Defense

If you believe Longview ISD has violated your child's rights or acted unfairly in a disciplinary matter, file a Level 1 Grievance IMMEDIATELY. Obtain a grievance form from the school or district website (search "Longview ISD Grievance Policy"). Complete the form thoroughly, clearly stating:

  • The specific policy or procedure violated.
  • The facts of the situation, including dates, times, and witnesses.
  • The relief you are seeking (e.g., retraction of the suspension, modification of the disciplinary record, reinstatement to the original placement).

Submit the grievance to the designated school official (usually the principal). The school has a limited time (check the district policy) to respond in writing. If you are not satisfied with the Level 1 response, you can escalate to Level 2 and beyond, following the procedures outlined in the Longview ISD Grievance Policy.

TEA State Complaint: An Additional Avenue for Justice

If you believe Longview ISD has violated IDEA, you can file a formal complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). The TEA investigates complaints alleging noncompliance with federal special education laws. Gather all documentation (IEP, suspension notices, grievance filings, etc.) and submit a detailed complaint to TEA following their specific procedures. TEA's website (tea.texas.gov) has information and complaint forms.

Due Process: Your Ultimate Protection

IDEA guarantees students with disabilities "due process" rights. This includes the right to notice, the right to be heard, and the right to appeal disciplinary decisions. If you disagree with the outcome of an MDR or a Change of Placement decision, you have the right to request a due process hearing. This is a formal legal proceeding before an impartial hearing officer. Consider seeking legal representation for a due process hearing.

DO NOT WAIT! Contact disability rights organizations, legal aid services, or a special education attorney immediately if you feel your child's rights are being violated. Time is critical in these situations! Document everything, keep copies of all correspondence, and advocate fiercely for your child's education and well-being.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
