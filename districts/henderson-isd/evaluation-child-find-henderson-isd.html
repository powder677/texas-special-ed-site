A Full Individual Evaluation is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine whether your child has a disability and qualifies for special education services in HENDERSON ISD. This evaluation goes far beyond classroom observations—it includes standardized testing, educational assessments, medical information, and developmental history.

The HENDERSON ISD FIE evaluation process is governed by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code. Parents in HENDERSON ISD have the right to request an evaluation at no cost, regardless of whether their child is currently enrolled in the district or in private school.

Understanding this process empowers you to advocate effectively for your child's educational needs. Many parents in the HENDERSON area don't realize they can initiate this process independently, without waiting for teacher recommendations.

HENDERSON ISD has a legal obligation under Child Find provisions to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities from birth through age 21. This includes students who are homeschooled or enrolled in private schools within the HENDERSON ISD service area.

The HENDERSON ISD child find process means the district must actively search for children who may need special education services. However, as a parent, you don't need to wait for the district to find your child—you can request an evaluation yourself at any time during the school year.

If you believe your child has a disability affecting their education, the responsibility falls on you to initiate the request. HENDERSON ISD families should understand that early identification and evaluation can lead to earlier intervention and better outcomes.

Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing

The most important step is to submit your evaluation request in writing. While verbal requests can be made to teachers or administrators, written requests create an official record and trigger the legal timeline.

Your written request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current school and grade level (if applicable)
  • Specific areas of concern (academic, behavioral, social, developmental)
  • Your contact information
  • A statement requesting a Full Individual Evaluation

Send this letter to the Director of Special Education or campus principal. Keep a copy for your records and consider sending it via certified mail for proof of receipt.

Step 2: Know the HENDERSON ISD 45 Day Timeline

Once HENDERSON ISD receives your written request, the district has specific timeframes to follow. The HENDERSON ISD 45 day timeline is critical—this is the maximum number of school days the district has to complete the evaluation and provide you with results.

Here's the timeline breakdown:

  1. Days 1-5: HENDERSON ISD acknowledges receipt of your request and provides written notice of evaluation procedures
  2. Days 6-35: District completes all testing and assessments for the FIE
  3. Days 36-45: HENDERSON ISD holds an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss results and determine eligibility

These are school days, not calendar days. The HENDERSON special education department must adhere to this timeline strictly. If delays occur, ask HENDERSON ISD in writing why the timeline is being extended.

Step 3: Consent to Evaluation

Before HENDERSON ISD can begin any testing, the district must obtain your written consent. You'll receive a notice of evaluation procedures explaining what will be assessed and who will conduct the evaluation.

Review this notice carefully. You have the right to:

  • Ask questions about the proposed evaluation
  • Request specific types of assessments
  • Refuse consent (though this prevents the evaluation from proceeding)
  • Request independent evaluations at district expense under certain circumstances

Sign and return the consent form promptly to keep the HENDERSON ISD FIE evaluation process on schedule.

What Gets Assessed During a HENDERSON ISD FIE?

A comprehensive HENDERSON ISD special education evaluation typically includes multiple components:

Academic Assessment

Testing covers reading, mathematics, written expression, and overall academic performance. HENDERSON ISD will use standardized tests appropriate for your child's age and grade level.

Cognitive and Psychological Evaluation

This measures intellectual functioning, processing skills, and cognitive strengths and weaknesses. A school psychologist typically administers these tests.

Speech and Language Assessment

If communication is a concern, HENDERSON ISD will assess expressive and receptive language skills, articulation, and fluency.

Physical and Occupational Therapy Evaluation

These assess motor skills, fine motor coordination, and functional abilities if developmental delays are suspected.

Social-Emotional and Behavioral Assessment

HENDERSON ISD evaluates behavioral patterns, emotional functioning, and social skills through observations and rating scales.

Parent and Teacher Input

Your observations and your child's teacher's observations are documented through questionnaires and interviews. This information is crucial to the special education evaluation request process.

Your Rights During the HENDERSON ISD Evaluation Process

Parents in HENDERSON ISD have significant rights protected by federal and state law. Understanding these rights helps you advocate effectively for your child.

Right to Prior Written Notice

HENDERSON ISD must provide written explanation of any evaluation procedures, including what will be tested, who will test, and when testing will occur.

Right to Participate in Meetings

You have the right to attend all meetings related to your child's evaluation. HENDERSON ISD must schedule the ARD meeting at a time convenient for you or offer alternative meeting arrangements.

Right to Bring Support

You can bring an advocate, attorney, or knowledgeable family member to any meetings. If you need a special ed advocate HENDERSON area families recommend, contact local parent training and information centers or advocacy organizations.

Right to Independent Evaluation

If you disagree with HENDERSON ISD's evaluation results, you have the right to obtain an independent evaluation at the district's expense under certain conditions.

Right to Records

You can request and review all evaluation results and records. HENDERSON ISD must provide these within a reasonable timeframe, typically 10 business days.

The ARD Meeting: Understanding Your HENDERSON ISD Results

The Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting is where HENDERSON ISD presents evaluation results and determines special education eligibility. This meeting must occur within the HENDERSON ISD 45 day timeline.

What Happens at the ARD Meeting

HENDERSON ISD will present findings from each evaluation component. The team discusses whether your child meets criteria for a disability category under IDEA, such as:

  • Specific Learning Disability
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities

You'll hear recommendations for services, placement, and support. This is your opportunity to ask questions and ensure you fully understand the results.

If HENDERSON ISD Finds Your Child Eligible

If your child qualifies for special education, HENDERSON ISD will develop an Individualized Education Plan (IEP). You'll approve goals, services, and placement during this meeting.

If HENDERSON ISD Determines Ineligibility

If HENDERSON ISD concludes your child doesn't qualify for special education, you have the right to disagree with the evaluation request decision. You can request an independent evaluation or pursue dispute resolution options.

When to Consider a Special Education Attorney for HENDERSON ISD

While many families navigate the special education process independently, complex situations sometimes require professional guidance. Consider consulting a special education attorney near HENDERSON if:

  • HENDERSON ISD misses the 45-day evaluation timeline
  • You disagree significantly with evaluation results
  • Your child has complex medical or behavioral needs
  • HENDERSON ISD refuses to evaluate your child
  • Previous special education services weren't effective
  • Disputes arise regarding appropriate placement or services

Many attorneys offer free initial consultations. If cost is a concern, contact your local advocacy organization for low-cost or free legal assistance.

Preparing for Success: What HENDERSON ISD Parents Should Do Now

If you're considering a HENDERSON ISD special education evaluation for your child, take these steps:

Gather Documentation

Compile medical records, previous educational assessments, developmental history, and documentation of concerns. HENDERSON ISD will review this during the evaluation.

Document Your Observations

Keep notes about specific behaviors, academic struggles, or developmental concerns. Include dates and examples. This strengthens your evaluation request and helps evaluators understand your child's functioning.

Request Records

If your child has attended other schools, request records from HENDERSON ISD or previous districts. This provides evaluators important context.

Communicate With Teachers

Share your concerns with your child's current teacher. Their observations are valuable for the HENDERSON ISD FIE evaluation process.

Understanding Texas Education Code Requirements

HENDERSON ISD operates under Texas Education Code Chapter 29, which governs special education. Key provisions include the requirement that evaluations be comprehensive, non-discriminatory, and culturally appropriate.

The code also requires that evaluations assess your child's functioning in all areas related to the suspected disability. This ensures the HENDERSON special education evaluation captures a complete picture of your child's needs.

Next Steps After Evaluation in HENDERSON ISD

Whether your child is found eligible or ineligible, the evaluation is just the beginning. HENDERSON ISD families should understand what comes next:

If Eligible: You'll work with HENDERSON ISD to develop an IEP, arrange services, and ensure appropriate placement. Annual reviews will assess progress.

If Ineligible: You can request an independent evaluation, pursue dispute resolution, or consult with a specialist about alternative supports available through general education or Section 504 plans.

Conclusion: Advocating for Your Child in HENDERSON ISD

Requesting a Full Individual Evaluation is a crucial step in ensuring your child receives the support they need. Parents in HENDERSON ISD who understand the process, know their rights, and stay involved significantly improve outcomes for their children.

The HENDERSON ISD evaluation request process is designed to be parent-friendly, but it requires you to take action. Don't hesitate to ask questions, request clarification, or seek additional support from advocates or attorneys as needed.

Your child deserves an education that meets their unique needs. By becoming informed about the HENDERSON ISD FIE evaluation process, you're taking the most important step toward making that happen.
