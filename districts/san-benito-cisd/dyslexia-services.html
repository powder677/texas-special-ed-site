Dyslexia Services in San Benito CISD

Dyslexia Services in San Benito CISD

This guide provides information on dyslexia services offered within the San Benito Consolidated Independent School District (CISD), aligning with Texas state law and guidelines.

Texas Dyslexia Handbook 2024

San Benito CISD adheres to the guidelines and procedures outlined in the most current version of the Texas Dyslexia Handbook. This handbook, published by the Texas Education Agency (TEA), serves as the primary resource for identifying, assessing, and providing support for students with dyslexia. The 2024 version includes updated research, best practices, and legal requirements. Parents and educators are encouraged to familiarize themselves with this document. The handbook is accessible on the TEA website and provides detailed information on screening, evaluation, instruction, and accommodations for students with dyslexia. Referencing the handbook ensures consistent and effective dyslexia services across the district.

Mandatory Screening

Texas law mandates dyslexia screening for all students in kindergarten and first grade. San Benito CISD implements universal screening procedures aligned with the Texas Dyslexia Handbook. These screenings are designed to identify students who may be at risk for dyslexia. Screening typically involves assessing phonological awareness, phonological memory, rapid naming, and letter-sound knowledge. Students flagged during the initial screening may receive further assessment to determine the need for intervention. Parents will be notified if their child is identified as potentially at risk for dyslexia and informed about next steps. Screening results are used to inform instruction and provide targeted support to students in need.

Structured Literacy Programs

San Benito CISD utilizes structured literacy programs that are research-based and explicitly teach the essential components of reading. These programs are designed to address the specific needs of students with dyslexia. Structured literacy instruction focuses on phonology (the sound structure of language), orthography (spelling patterns), morphology (meaningful parts of words), syntax (sentence structure), and semantics (word meanings). These programs are delivered in a systematic and sequential manner, providing students with explicit and cumulative instruction. The specific structured literacy programs used in San Benito CISD may vary depending on the grade level and individual student needs, but all programs align with the principles of structured literacy.

504 vs IEP for Dyslexia

Students with dyslexia may receive support through either a Section 504 plan or an Individualized Education Program (IEP) under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA).

  • Section 504 Plan: A 504 plan provides accommodations to students with disabilities that substantially limit one or more major life activities, such as learning. A student with dyslexia may qualify for a 504 plan if their dyslexia significantly impacts their ability to learn. 504 plans focus on providing accommodations and modifications to the general education setting to ensure equal access to learning.
  • IEP (Individualized Education Program): An IEP is developed for students with a disability that requires specialized instruction. If a student's dyslexia significantly impacts their educational performance and requires specially designed instruction, they may be eligible for an IEP under the category of Specific Learning Disability (SLD). IEPs include specific goals, objectives, and services tailored to the student's individual needs.
The determination of whether a student requires a 504 plan or an IEP is made on an individual basis by a team of professionals, including parents.

Accommodations List

The following are examples of accommodations that may be provided to students with dyslexia through a 504 plan or an IEP. This list is not exhaustive, and accommodations are individualized based on student needs:

  • Extended time on tests and assignments
  • Preferential seating
  • Audiobooks and text-to-speech software
  • Use of graphic organizers
  • Modified assignments
  • Access to notes or outlines
  • Alternative testing formats
  • Multi-sensory instruction
  • Use of assistive technology

How to Request Evaluation

Parents who suspect their child may have dyslexia should contact their child's teacher or school principal to initiate the evaluation process. A written request for an evaluation is recommended. The school will then follow the procedures outlined in the Texas Dyslexia Handbook to determine if a full evaluation is warranted. This process involves gathering information about the student's academic history, classroom performance, and any previous interventions. Parents are integral members of the evaluation team and will be involved in all aspects of the process. San Benito CISD is committed to providing timely and appropriate evaluations to identify students with dyslexia and provide them with the support they need to succeed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

Is my child entitled to dyslexia testing in Texas? +

Yes. Texas law requires all districts to screen students for dyslexia and related disorders in Kindergarten and First Grade, and at any grade level if a parent or teacher requests it.

What accommodations can a dyslexic child get in Texas? +

Accommodations may include extended time on tests, text-to-speech software, oral administration of tests, reduced-answer-choice formats, and use of a calculator. These can be provided through a 504 Plan or an IEP.

What is the Texas Dyslexia Handbook? +

The Texas Dyslexia Handbook is a state guidance document updated in 2024 that outlines districts' responsibilities for identifying, evaluating, and serving students with dyslexia using structured literacy methods.
