A DUMAS ISD FIE evaluation is a thorough assessment designed to determine whether your child has a disability and needs special education services. This evaluation examines your child's cognitive abilities, academic performance, social-emotional development, and physical health—providing the foundation for appropriate educational support.

Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §29.001, DUMAS ISD must evaluate any child suspected of having a disability at no cost to families. The evaluation process is confidential and involves multiple assessment methods rather than a single test.

Many parents in DUMAS ISD families don't realize they can initiate this process themselves. You don't need to wait for a teacher or administrator to suggest an evaluation—you have the legal right to request one directly.

DUMAS ISD child find procedures ensure that all children with disabilities are identified and evaluated. This process applies to students ages 3-21, including preschoolers and those in private schools within the district boundaries.

The DUMAS ISD child find system is designed to catch children who may be struggling academically, behaviorally, or socially. If you suspect your child has learning difficulties, attention issues, speech delays, autism spectrum disorder, or emotional/behavioral challenges, you can initiate the evaluation process immediately.

Who Should Request an Evaluation?

  • Parents concerned about academic progress or learning challenges
  • Parents noticing developmental delays in speech, motor skills, or social interaction
  • Parents observing behavioral or emotional difficulties at home
  • Parents with family history of learning disabilities or special needs
  • Parents whose children aren't progressing despite intervention efforts

Submitting a formal request for a DUMAS ISD special education evaluation starts with written notification to your child's school or the special education department. While verbal requests may prompt action, written requests establish a clear timeline and create important documentation.

Step-by-Step Request Process

  1. Contact Your School's Special Education Department: Reach out to your child's campus principal or the DUMAS ISD special education coordinator. Ask specifically how to request an evaluation in writing.
  2. Submit a Written Request: Write a simple letter stating you're requesting a DUMAS ISD FIE evaluation for your child. Include your child's name, date of birth, grade, and campus. Describe your concerns briefly (academic, behavioral, speech, developmental, etc.).
  3. Keep Documentation: Email your request or deliver it in person with a signature receipt. Keep copies for your records—this starts the official 45-day timeline.
  4. Expect Initial Contact: DUMAS ISD staff should contact you within a few days to discuss your request and answer questions.
  5. Provide Permission in Writing: Before testing begins, DUMAS ISD will give you a Parent Consent for Evaluation form. Read this carefully and sign it—testing cannot begin without your written permission.

What to Include in Your Evaluation Request

Make your request clear and specific. Here's what should be in your written request:

  • Child's full name and date of birth
  • Current grade and campus name
  • Specific concerns (academics, behavior, speech, motor skills, social interaction)
  • Examples of difficulties you've observed
  • Any family history relevant to the concerns
  • Request for a full individual evaluation
  • Your contact information

The DUMAS ISD 45-Day Timeline Explained

Once you submit a written request for a DUMAS ISD evaluation request, the district has 45 school days to complete the evaluation and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. This timeline is critical—it's a legal requirement under Texas special education law.

The DUMAS ISD 45 day timeline doesn't include days when school is not in session. Understanding this timeline helps you track progress and know when to expect updates.

What Happens During the 45-Day Window

  • Days 1-5: Parent contact, discussion of evaluation plan, signing consent forms
  • Days 6-30: Assessments, classroom observations, teacher interviews, parent interviews conducted
  • Days 31-40: Results compiled, evaluation report written, meetings scheduled
  • Days 41-45: ARD meeting held to discuss findings and determine special education eligibility

Important: If DUMAS ISD needs additional time, they must notify you in writing before day 45. They cannot simply extend the timeline without your permission.

What Gets Assessed in a DUMAS ISD FIE Evaluation

A comprehensive DUMAS ISD FIE evaluation covers multiple areas of your child's development and functioning. The specific tests used depend on your child's age, grade, and the concerns you've identified.

Common Assessment Areas

  • Cognitive/Intellectual Ability: Problem-solving, reasoning, verbal and nonverbal skills
  • Academic Achievement: Reading, math, writing, spelling performance
  • Speech and Language: Receptive and expressive language, pronunciation, fluency
  • Motor Skills: Fine motor (handwriting, coordination) and gross motor development
  • Social-Emotional Functioning: Behavior, emotional regulation, social skills, peer relationships
  • Classroom Observation: How your child behaves and learns in the school setting
  • Vision and Hearing Screening: Basic sensory assessments

DUMAS special education evaluators use standardized tests, classroom observations, teacher input, parent interviews, and review of school records. This multi-method approach ensures a complete picture of your child's abilities and needs.

Your Rights During the Evaluation Process in DUMAS ISD

As a parent in DUMAS ISD, you have specific legal rights throughout the evaluation and special education process. Understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Key Parental Rights in DUMAS ISD

  • Right to Request Evaluation: You can request a DUMAS ISD special education evaluation without teacher or administrative approval
  • Right to Written Notice: DUMAS ISD must provide written notice of evaluation plans, timelines, and assessment methods
  • Right to Informed Consent: You must understand and sign permission before testing begins
  • Right to Review Records: You can access all evaluation reports and school records related to your child
  • Right to Participate in Meetings: You must be included in all ARD meetings discussing evaluation results and services
  • Right to Interpreter Services: DUMAS ISD must provide interpretation if English isn't your primary language
  • Right to Independent Evaluation: If you disagree with DUMAS ISD findings, you can request an independent evaluation at district expense under certain circumstances

What Happens After the Evaluation

Once testing is complete, DUMAS ISD schedules an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to discuss results. This meeting is where the district determines whether your child qualifies for special education services under IDEA.

The ARD Meeting

At the ARD meeting, the evaluation team presents findings in clear language. They'll explain what the tests show, discuss whether your child meets eligibility criteria for special education, and if eligible, develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

You have the right to bring an advocate or attorney to this meeting. Many parents in the DUMAS area find it helpful to have additional support, particularly if they have concerns about the evaluation or recommendations.

If Your Child Qualifies for Special Education

If DUMAS ISD determines your child is eligible, they'll develop an IEP outlining:

  • Specific special education and related services
  • Frequency and duration of services
  • Annual measurable goals
  • Placement and accommodations
  • How progress will be monitored

If Your Child Doesn't Qualify

DUMAS ISD must explain in writing why your child doesn't meet special education eligibility. You can request a meeting to discuss results, seek an independent evaluation, or file a complaint if you believe the evaluation was incomplete or inaccurate.

To make the evaluation process smoother and more effective, follow these recommendations:

  • Communicate Clearly: Provide DUMAS ISD with specific examples of your concerns, not general observations

Frequently Asked Questions About DUMAS ISD Evaluation Request Process

Can I request a DUMAS ISD special education evaluation without a teacher's recommendation?

Yes. You have the right to request a DUMAS ISD special education evaluation independently. You don't need teacher or administrative approval or agreement.

What if DUMAS ISD says my child doesn't need evaluation?

If you submit a written request for a DUMAS ISD FIE evaluation, the district must either evaluate your child or provide written explanation of why they believe evaluation isn't necessary. You can challenge this decision.

How long does the full evaluation take?

The DUMAS ISD 45 day timeline covers the entire process from request to ARD meeting. However, actual testing might span 2-3 weeks, with remaining time for report writing and meeting scheduling.

Can I be present during testing?

Generally, parents aren't present during formal testing, as this can affect results. However, you can request information about assessment methods and review results afterward.

What if I disagree with the evaluation results?

You can request an independent evaluation at DUMAS ISD expense under certain circumstances. You can also request a second opinion or consult a special education attorney near DUMAS about your options.
