Child Find is a critical component of federal special education law. TERRELL ISD has a legal obligation to identify, locate, and evaluate all children suspected of having disabilities, regardless of whether they attend public school.

How TERRELL ISD Child Find Works

TERRELL ISD child find activities include screening programs, teacher referrals, parent referrals, and community awareness efforts. School staff monitor students for signs that may indicate a disability, including:

  • Difficulty keeping up with peers academically
  • Behavioral concerns or social interaction challenges
  • Speech or language delays
  • Fine or gross motor skill difficulties
  • Attention or organizational problems
  • Emotional or mental health concerns

If your child shows any of these signs, you can initiate the TERRELL ISD evaluation request process at any time, regardless of whether the school has already identified concerns.

Parents in TERRELL ISD can formally request an evaluation in several ways. The most effective approach is to submit a written evaluation request to your child's school or to the Special Education Department.

Step-by-Step Evaluation Request Process

Step 1: Submit Your Written Request

Contact your child's campus principal, special education coordinator, or the TERRELL ISD Special Education Department directly. Your request should be submitted in writing—email or hand-delivered letters both create documentation of your request date.

In your letter, include your child's name, grade level, and specific concerns you've observed. Be clear and concise about why you believe your child needs evaluation. Example: "I've noticed my child struggles with reading comprehension and has difficulty finishing assignments compared to classmates."

Step 2: Understand the TERRELL ISD 45 Day Timeline

Once TERRELL ISD receives your written evaluation request, the district has 45 calendar days to complete the full evaluation and provide you with results. This is a critical deadline established under Texas Education Code §22.301.

The TERRELL ISD 45 day timeline is the legal requirement, though some circumstances may extend this period. Keep records of when you submitted your request to monitor this deadline closely.

Step 3: Participate in Pre-Evaluation Meeting

Before TERRELL ISD staff can conduct a full evaluation, they must obtain your written consent. This typically happens during a brief meeting where they explain what will be evaluated, which specialists will be involved, and what to expect from the process.

Use this meeting to ask questions and clarify any concerns. This is your opportunity to provide background information about your child's development, medical history, and any previous evaluations or interventions.

What TERRELL ISD Special Education Evaluation Includes

A comprehensive TERRELL ISD special education evaluation examines multiple areas of your child's functioning. The specific tests and assessments depend on your child's age and presenting concerns, but typically include:

Core Evaluation Components

  • Cognitive Assessment: Tests of thinking, reasoning, problem-solving, and learning ability
  • Academic Assessment: Evaluation of reading, math, writing, and spelling skills
  • Speech and Language Assessment: Review of communication abilities if concerns exist
  • Social-Emotional and Behavioral Assessment: Evaluation of emotional development and behavioral patterns
  • Physical and Motor Assessment: Review of fine and gross motor skills when relevant
  • Classroom Observation: Direct observation of your child in the learning environment
  • Teacher and Parent Input: Questionnaires and rating scales from educators and family members

TERRELL ISD special education evaluators will tailor the assessment to address your specific concerns while ensuring comprehensive coverage. They may also review educational records, past report cards, and attendance information.

Your Rights During TERRELL ISD Evaluation Request

Parents in TERRELL ISD have significant legal protections during the special education evaluation process. Knowing your rights ensures you can effectively advocate for your child.

Key Parent Rights in TERRELL ISD

  • Right to Written Notice: TERRELL ISD must provide written notice of the evaluation plan and your procedural safeguards
  • Right to Consent: You must give written permission before evaluation begins
  • Right to Access Records: You can review all evaluation materials and educational records
  • Right to an Independent Evaluation: If you disagree with TERRELL ISD evaluation results, you may obtain an independent evaluation at district expense under certain circumstances
  • Right to Interpretation: TERRELL ISD must provide translated materials and interpreters if English is not your primary language
  • Right to Due Process: You can request a due process hearing if disputes arise with TERRELL ISD

Many parents in TERRELL ISD benefit from having a special education advocate or attorney review their rights before beginning the evaluation process. Understanding these protections puts you in a stronger position to ensure your child receives appropriate assessment and services.

Understanding TERRELL ISD Evaluation Results

After TERRELL ISD completes the full evaluation within the 45-day timeline, you'll receive an Evaluation Report detailing findings, scores, and recommendations. This meeting, called the Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) Conference, is where you'll learn the results.

What Happens at Your ARD Conference

The ARD committee includes you, your child's teachers, TERRELL ISD special education staff, and other relevant professionals. Together, you'll review evaluation results and determine whether your child qualifies for special education services.

If your child qualifies under IDEA, TERRELL ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) detailing specific services, goals, and accommodations. If your child doesn't qualify, TERRELL ISD must explain why and discuss alternative support options that may be available.

Request copies of all evaluation reports before the meeting so you can review results carefully. If you don't understand the findings, ask TERRELL ISD staff to explain results in clear, non-technical language.

If You Disagree with TERRELL ISD Evaluation Results

Sometimes parents in TERRELL ISD disagree with evaluation findings or believe the assessment didn't adequately address their child's needs. You have options.

Addressing Disagreement with TERRELL ISD

Request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE): If you disagree with TERRELL ISD's evaluation, you can request an independent evaluation at no cost to your family. TERRELL ISD must either agree to fund the IEE or request a due process hearing to defend their evaluation.

Seek Mediation: Before formal litigation, you may request mediation through the Texas Education Agency. A neutral mediator helps you and TERRELL ISD reach agreement on evaluation findings and services.

File a Due Process Complaint: If disagreements persist, you can file a formal due process hearing. A hearing officer will review evidence and make a binding decision. Many families in TERRELL ISD work with a special education attorney near TERRELL for due process proceedings.

Timeline and Next Steps After TERRELL ISD Evaluation

Understanding what happens after the 45-day TERRELL ISD evaluation timeline helps you plan for your child's educational support. If your child qualifies for services, action moves quickly.

After Evaluation Approval

Within 30 days of your ARD meeting, TERRELL ISD must implement your child's IEP if they qualified for special education. Services cannot be delayed. Your child immediately begins receiving the accommodations, modifications, and specialized instruction outlined in the IEP.

Schedule follow-up meetings with your child's case manager to ensure services are delivered as documented. Monitor your child's progress toward IEP goals and communicate regularly with TERRELL ISD staff about how your child is responding to intervention.

Annual Review and Updates

TERRELL ISD will conduct an annual ARD meeting to review your child's progress and update the IEP. Every three years, TERRELL ISD must complete a re-evaluation to determine if your child continues to qualify for services.

Come prepared to these meetings with progress data, observations, and feedback about how well the current IEP is meeting your child's needs. Active participation ensures the IEP remains aligned with your child's evolving capabilities and needs.
