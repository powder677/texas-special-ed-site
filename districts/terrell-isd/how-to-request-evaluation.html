Parents in TERRELL ISD can request a special education evaluation when their children face academic, behavioral, or developmental challenges. A formal evaluation can identify if your child has a disability that requires special services.

Some indicators that suggest your child could benefit from an evaluation include:

  • Persistent difficulties learning to read, write, or do math
  • Behavioral or attention problems in class
  • Speech or language delays
  • Social or emotional difficulties
  • Low academic performance despite additional efforts
  • Mobility or physical accessibility needs

In TERRELL ISD, the special education evaluation team will work with you to determine if your child qualifies for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA).

Federal and state law grants specific rights to parents in TERRELL ISD when it comes to special education. Knowing these rights is essential to protect your child's interests.

Your Main Rights

According to Texas law and TERRELL ISD, you have the right to:

  • Request evaluation: You can request a special education evaluation at any time during the school year
  • Receive complete information: TERRELL ISD must provide you with information about the evaluation process in your language
  • Informed consent: You must give your written consent before any evaluation begins
  • Access to records: You can review all information collected about your child
  • Participate in decisions: You must be included in all IEP (Individualized Education Program) meetings
  • Obtain a second opinion: You can request independent evaluations if you disagree with TERRELL ISD's results
  • Legal representation: You can bring a special education attorney to TERRELL ISD meetings

Steps to Request a Special Education Evaluation in TERRELL ISD

The process of requesting a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation) in TERRELL ISD is straightforward and accessible for all parents. Follow these steps carefully.

Step 1: Contact Your Child's School

Contact your child's teacher or the school principal in TERRELL ISD. Clearly express your concerns about your child's learning or behavior. Do this in writing (email or letter) to maintain a record.

Step 2: Formally Request an Evaluation in Writing

Send a written request for evaluation to the school principal or TERRELL ISD's special education department. Use these keywords: "I am requesting a special education evaluation (FIE) for my child under the IDEA Act."

Your request should include:

  • Your child's full name
  • Current grade level
  • TERRELL ISD school your child attends
  • Clear description of difficulties or concerns
  • Your name, phone number, and email address
  • Date of the request

Step 3: Maintain Documentation

Keep copies of:

  • Your written request to TERRELL ISD
  • Emails with teachers and administrators
  • Low grades or behavior reports
  • Any correspondence from the district
  • Notes from important conversations (with date and time)

Legal Timelines in TERRELL ISD

TERRELL ISD must follow specific timelines established by federal law. It is important for you to know these dates to ensure your child receives a timely evaluation.

  • 15 days: TERRELL ISD must notify you in writing that they have received your request and obtain your consent
  • 60 calendar days: From obtaining your consent, TERRELL ISD will complete the evaluation (may extend to 90 days in exceptional circumstances)
  • 30 days after completing the evaluation: TERRELL ISD must schedule an IEP meeting to discuss the results

If TERRELL ISD does not meet these timelines, you have the right to file a due process complaint.

The Evaluation Process in TERRELL ISD

Once TERRELL ISD approves your request, the evaluation team will complete a comprehensive special education evaluation. This evaluation is more in-depth than regular school assessments.

What Does the Evaluation Include?

The TERRELL ISD evaluation typically includes:

  • Cognitive ability tests: Evaluate thinking and reasoning skills
  • Academic evaluation: Tests in reading, writing, and mathematics
  • Behavioral evaluation: Observation of behavior in different environments
  • Language evaluation: Communication and language skills (important in Spanish for bilingual families)
  • Developmental evaluation: Motor and physical development skills
  • Parent interviews: TERRELL ISD will ask you about your child's medical and developmental history
  • Review of school records: Analysis of past grades and behavior

Participation During the Evaluation

You play an important role in the TERRELL ISD evaluation process. Provide honest and complete information about:

  • Your child's medical history
  • Developmental milestones (when they learned to walk, talk, etc.)
  • Behavior at home
  • Any previous evaluations or treatments
  • Family history of disabilities

Results Meeting and IEP in TERRELL ISD

After TERRELL ISD completes the special education evaluation, a meeting to discuss the results will be scheduled. This meeting is crucial to determine your child's educational future.

What Happens at the Meeting?

During the TERRELL ISD meeting, the evaluation team will present:

  • Results of all tests
  • Eligibility determination (if your child qualifies for special education)
  • Disability category if applicable
  • Your child's strengths and needs
  • Recommendations for services and interventions

If Your Child is Eligible for Special Education

If TERRELL ISD determines that your child qualifies, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be developed. The IEP is a legal document that describes:

  • Your child's specific needs
  • Services your child will receive (speech therapy, special resources, etc.)
  • Annual educational goals
  • Accommodations and modifications in the classroom
  • Least restrictive learning environment

You must give your consent before any special education services begin in TERRELL ISD.

If You Disagree with the Results

If you believe TERRELL ISD's evaluation was not accurate or complete, you have rights. You can:

  • Request an independent evaluation: TERRELL ISD may be required to pay for an external evaluation
  • File a due process complaint: Contact the Texas Education Agency (TEA)
  • Consult with a special education advocate: Search "special ed advocate TERRELL" for local representation
  • Obtain legal advice: A "special education attorney near TERRELL" can help you protect your child's rights
