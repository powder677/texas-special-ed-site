An ARD meeting is a formal gathering where a team of educators, specialists, and parents develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) for a student with disabilities. In CORSICANA ISD, the ARD committee is responsible for determining your child's eligibility for special education services and creating a detailed plan for their education.

The CORSICANA ISD IEP process begins with evaluation and continues through annual reviews. Your IEP is a legally binding document that outlines your child's educational goals, accommodations, modifications, and services. This plan is tailored specifically to your child's strengths, weaknesses, and individual learning needs.

Key Participants in CORSICANA ISD ARD Meetings

  • Parent or guardian – You have equal decision-making authority
  • General education teacher – Provides classroom performance insights
  • Special education teacher – Explains special education services and accommodations
  • School administrator or designee – Represents the district and ensures compliance
  • Diagnostician or evaluation specialist – Shares assessment results and recommendations
  • Related services personnel – Speech therapists, counselors, occupational therapists, as needed
  • Student – May attend, especially for secondary students (age 14+)

Step-by-Step CORSICANA ISD ARD Meeting Process

Step 1: Initial Referral and Evaluation Request

The first phase of the CORSICANA ISD ARD process begins when a teacher, parent, or other professional expresses concern about a student's learning or development. If you suspect your child needs special education services, you can request an evaluation in writing to your school's special education department.

CORSICANA ISD must acknowledge your request within five school days and explain the evaluation process. The district will provide information about your CORSICANA ISD ARD rights, including your right to refuse evaluation and your right to independent evaluation at no cost under certain circumstances.

Step 2: Comprehensive Evaluation

Once the evaluation is authorized, CORSICANA ISD special education staff will conduct a comprehensive assessment using multiple evaluation methods. This may include cognitive testing, achievement testing, classroom observations, teacher input, parent interviews, and medical or developmental history reviews.

The evaluation period must be completed within 60 calendar days from the date your written request was received. CORSICANA ISD will gather information about your child's academic performance, social-emotional functioning, and any physical or developmental concerns relevant to educational performance.

Step 3: ARD Meeting Scheduling and Notice

After evaluation is complete, CORSICANA ISD will schedule an ARD meeting to review findings and discuss eligibility. You must receive written notice at least five school days before the meeting. This notice will include the meeting date, time, location, and purpose, along with information about your legal rights.

Under Texas Education Code § 414.001, parents have the right to bring additional participants to the CORSICANA ISD ARD meeting, including advocates, relatives, or outside experts. CORSICANA ISD ARD preparation is crucial to your success. Parents who arrive prepared advocate more effectively for their children. Consider these preparation steps:

  • Review evaluation reports: Read all assessment results before the meeting and note questions
  • List your concerns: Write down specific areas where your child struggles academically or socially
  • Document your child's strengths: Note talents, interests, and areas where your child excels

Step 5: The ARD Meeting Discussion

During the CORSICANA ISD ARD meeting, the team will review evaluation results and determine if your child qualifies for special education. The committee will discuss your child's present levels of performance, addressing academic achievement, functional ability, and how disability affects educational participation.

If your child qualifies, the team will identify the disability category (such as autism, dyslexia, other health impairment, emotional disturbance, or speech-language impairment) under which your child receives services. The ARD committee will then develop an IEP addressing your child's specific educational needs.

Step 6: IEP Development and Goal Setting

Creating measurable CORSICANA ISD IEP goals is the heart of your child's IEP. Goals should be SMART: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. Each goal must address areas where your child has demonstrated need based on the comprehensive evaluation.

Components of CORSICANA ISD IEP Goals

  • Present level of performance (PLOP): Describes your child's current academic and functional abilities
  • Annual goal: What your child should accomplish in one school year
  • Benchmarks or short-term objectives: Measurable steps toward achieving the annual goal
  • Service delivery: Frequency, duration, and location of special education services
  • Progress monitoring: How progress toward goals will be measured and reported
  • Accommodations and modifications: Changes to how material is presented or how student demonstrates learning

Step 7: Placement and Service Decisions

CORSICANA ISD must determine the appropriate placement for your child in the least restrictive environment (LRE). This principle ensures your child receives education with non-disabled peers to the maximum extent appropriate. Possible placements range from mainstream general education classrooms with support services to more specialized settings.

Your CORSICANA ISD ARD rights include the right to request specific placements and to challenge placement decisions you believe are inappropriate. The team will specify whether your child receives services in a regular classroom, resource room, self-contained classroom, or other setting.

Your Legal CORSICANA ISD ARD Rights

Parents of students in CORSICANA ISD special education programs have substantial legal protections under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.

Key Parent Rights in CORSICANA ISD

  • Right to be informed: Receive notice and procedural safeguards in your native language
  • Right to participate: Have equal voice in all ARD decisions for your child
  • Right to records: Access your child's educational and medical records
  • Right to prior written notice: Receive 5-day advance notice before any change in services
  • Right to consent: Initial evaluation, initial placement, and major service changes require your written consent
  • Right to disagreement: Request mediation or due process hearing if you disagree with district decisions
  • Right to independent evaluation: Obtain outside evaluation at district expense under specific circumstances
  • Right to advocates: Bring representatives, special ed advocate CORSICANA, or special education attorney near CORSICANA to meetings

Understanding Your IEP Document

Your IEP is a complex legal document. Understanding each section empowers you to advocate for your child's needs in CORSICANA ISD special education programs.

Essential IEP Sections

  • Student information: Demographics, disability category, and service eligibility
  • Present levels: How disability affects your child's education and daily functioning
  • Measurable annual goals: What your child will achieve in one school year
  • Special education services: Type, frequency, duration, and location of services provided
  • Related services: Speech, occupational therapy, counseling, or other support services
  • Supplementary aids and services: Modifications to curriculum, materials, or environment
  • Accommodations: Changes to testing, assignments, or classroom procedures
  • Progress reporting: How often you'll receive updates on goal progress
  • Transition services: For students age 16+, plans for post-secondary education or employment
