The CORSICANA ISD special education evaluation process begins with understanding what an FIE actually is. A Full Individual Evaluation is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine whether your child has a disability that qualifies them for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §89.1011.

Parents in CORSICANA ISD should know that the district is required to evaluate children suspected of having disabilities at no cost to families. This evaluation goes far beyond a simple academic test—it examines your child's cognitive abilities, academic performance, social-emotional development, and physical health.

Understanding this process empowers you to make informed decisions about your child's educational future. Many CORSICANA ISD families find that a timely FIE evaluation is the first step toward meaningful academic support and success.

Step 1: Submit Your Written Request

Begin by requesting an evaluation in writing to CORSICANA ISD. While verbal requests are legally binding, written documentation creates an important paper trail for your records. Address your request to the Director of Special Education or the campus principal.

Your letter should clearly state that you are requesting a CORSICANA ISD FIE evaluation and briefly describe your concerns. You might mention specific areas of concern such as reading difficulties, behavior challenges, social skills concerns, or developmental delays.

Keep your request simple and straightforward. You don't need legal language or formal documentation—a simple letter will suffice to initiate the CORSICANA ISD evaluation request process.

Step 2: Understand the 45-Day Timeline

One of the most important facts for parents in CORSICANA ISD is the 45-day evaluation timeline. Under Texas Education Code, CORSICANA ISD has 45 days from the date of your written request to complete the FIE and provide you with results.

  • Day 1: Your written request is received by CORSICANA ISD
  • Days 1-10: You should receive notice of your rights and proposed evaluation plan
  • Days 15-40: Evaluators conduct assessments and observations
  • Day 45: CORSICANA ISD must complete the evaluation and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting

The CORSICANA ISD 45-day timeline is a legal requirement, not a suggestion. Understanding this timeline helps you track progress and know when to follow up if the district falls behind.

Step 3: Receive the Evaluation Plan

Within 10 days of receiving your request, CORSICANA ISD must provide you with a written evaluation plan. This document explains which areas the district will assess and which specific tests and procedures they will use.

Your rights notice will include information about:

  • Types of evaluations planned
  • Your right to independent evaluations
  • Your right to have an advocate or attorney present
  • Confidentiality protections for your child's information

Review this plan carefully. If you disagree with the proposed evaluation, you can request changes before the district begins testing. This is your opportunity to ensure the CORSICANA ISD special education evaluation covers all areas of concern.

Step 4: Provide Consent and Participate in Evaluations

CORSICANA ISD requires your written consent before beginning the evaluation. This is a crucial protection—the district cannot evaluate your child without your explicit approval.

You may participate in the evaluation process by providing background information, attending observation sessions, and sharing your perspectives on your child's strengths and challenges. Many parents in CORSICANA ISD find it helpful to write a detailed summary of their child's history to share with evaluators.

Evaluations typically include:

  • Cognitive/IQ testing
  • Academic achievement assessments
  • Social-emotional or behavioral evaluations
  • Speech and language screening
  • Classroom observations
  • Parent and teacher interviews

Step 5: Attend the ARD Meeting and Receive Results

Within 45 days, CORSICANA ISD must schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. This meeting brings together school staff, evaluators, and you to review the CORSICANA ISD FIE evaluation results.

At this meeting, you'll learn whether your child qualifies for special education services and, if eligible, what services CORSICANA ISD will provide. You'll also receive a comprehensive evaluation report explaining the findings in detail.

Come prepared with questions and bring an advocate or special education attorney if desired. This meeting is your opportunity to discuss your child's needs and ensure the proposed Individualized Education Plan (IEP) meets those needs.

Key Legal Protections for CORSICANA ISD Parents

Texas law and federal IDEA regulations provide significant protections for families requesting evaluations in CORSICANA ISD. Understanding these rights empowers you throughout the process.

Right to Independent Evaluations

If you disagree with the CORSICANA ISD special education evaluation, you have the right to obtain an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at public expense. CORSICANA ISD must consider your independent evaluation in making eligibility and placement decisions.

Right to Due Process

If you and CORSICANA ISD disagree about your child's evaluation, eligibility, or services, you can request a due process hearing. A neutral hearing officer will review your case and make a binding decision.

Protection Against Discrimination

The district cannot deny your CORSICANA ISD evaluation request based on race, ethnicity, language spoken at home, or socioeconomic status. If you believe discrimination has occurred, you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency.

Common Concerns CORSICANA ISD Parents Ask About

Will the Evaluation Label My Child?

Many parents worry about labeling, but an evaluation is simply fact-finding. Information from the CORSICANA ISD FIE evaluation helps identify your child's strengths and the specific support needed for success. The evaluation itself doesn't create a "label"—only a formal eligibility determination does, and that happens only with your consent.

What if CORSICANA ISD Says My Child Doesn't Qualify?

If CORSICANA ISD determines your child doesn't meet eligibility criteria, you still have options. You can request an independent evaluation, provide additional documentation, or request a reevaluation after new information emerges. You may also qualify for a 504 Plan or interventions through Response to Intervention (RTI) programs.

Can I Request an Evaluation for a Preschooler?

Yes. CORSICANA ISD child find obligations extend to children ages 3-5. If your preschooler in the CORSICANA area has developmental concerns, you can request evaluation immediately—even before school entry.
