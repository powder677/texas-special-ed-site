Special Education in Tomball ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the special education system can feel overwhelming, but understanding your rights and the available resources is crucial for your child's success. Special education, as defined by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas state law, is specially designed instruction provided to children with disabilities to meet their unique needs. This can include a wide range of supports and services, from academic accommodations to therapies and assistive technology.

Tomball ISD is responsible for providing a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) to all eligible students with disabilities within its boundaries. The Texas Education Agency (TEA) oversees all school districts in the state, including Tomball ISD, ensuring compliance with state and federal laws. Tomball ISD also receives valuable support and resources from TEA Region 4 Education Service Center, which provides professional development, technical assistance, and other services to improve student outcomes.

Remember, as a parent, you are an equal and essential member of your child's educational team. You have legally enforceable rights throughout the entire special education process, from initial evaluation to the development and implementation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP). Tomball ISD must respect these rights and ensure your active participation in all decisions regarding your child's education. Understand that you are not alone. Many organizations and advocates are available to help you navigate this complex system. Educate yourself on your rights, and never hesitate to ask questions.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Tomball ISD

If you believe your child may have a disability that requires special education services, the first step is to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) from Tomball ISD. This evaluation will help determine if your child meets the eligibility criteria for special education and related services. It's important to understand that a verbal request is not sufficient to initiate the evaluation process. You must submit your request in writing.

Once Tomball ISD receives your written request and obtains your written consent to evaluate, they have 45 school days to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). This is a critical timeline, so ensure you keep a record of the date you submitted your request. Remember that Tomball ISD also has a legal obligation under Child Find laws to proactively identify students who may have disabilities and require special education services, even if you haven't specifically requested an evaluation. If you have concerns about your child's learning, development, or behavior, do not hesitate to voice those concerns. To initiate the evaluation process, immediately write a letter to the Tomball ISD special education director formally requesting a Full Individual Evaluation for your child. Keep a copy of the letter for your records and send it via certified mail with return receipt requested to ensure proof of delivery.

ARD Meetings in Tomball ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal. The ARD committee is responsible for determining your child's eligibility for special education, developing their Individualized Education Program (IEP), and making decisions about their educational placement. The ARD committee is comprised of you as the parent, special education teachers, general education teachers, and relevant Tomball ISD administrative staff. Your input as a parent is extremely important.

Tomball ISD is required to provide you with at least five calendar days written notice before any ARD meeting. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, the date, time, and location, and the names of the individuals who will be attending. During the ARD meeting, you have the right to participate fully in all discussions and decisions. You can request a 10-day recess (a temporary pause) at any point during the meeting if you need more time to consider the information presented or consult with outside experts. It's important to remember that you are not obligated to sign the IEP on the same day as the ARD meeting. Take your time, review the document carefully, and seek legal advice if needed.

As an active participant, understand that the meeting is your opportunity to advocate for your child's needs, discuss their strengths and weaknesses, and collaborate with the team to develop an IEP that will help them succeed. You have the right to bring anyone to an ARD meeting, including advocates, attorneys, or educational specialists who can offer support and guidance. Dyslexia Services in Tomball ISD

Texas law mandates that Tomball ISD screen all students for dyslexia. This screening is designed to identify students who may be at risk for reading difficulties. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook sets the standards that Tomball ISD must adhere to when identifying and serving students with dyslexia. This handbook outlines the recommended screening procedures, instructional strategies, and accommodations for students with dyslexia.

If your child is identified as having dyslexia, they are entitled to receive structured literacy intervention, which is a research-based approach to reading instruction that focuses on phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension. Depending on the severity of your child's dyslexia and their individual needs, they may also qualify for a 504 Plan or an IEP, which can provide additional accommodations and supports. If your child has not yet been screened for dyslexia in Tomball ISD, or if you have concerns about their reading progress, request a dyslexia screening in writing from your child's school immediately.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Tomball ISD

Discipline can be a particularly challenging area for students with disabilities. If your special education student in Tomball ISD is suspended for more than 10 cumulative school days in a school year, this triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) under IDEA. This is considered a Change of Placement and requires an ARD meeting to determine if the student's behavior was a manifestation of their disability or a result of the school's failure to appropriately implement the IEP.

This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a special education student. If the behavior is determined to be a manifestation of the disability, the ARD committee must conduct a Functional Behavioral Assessment (FBA), implement or review a Behavior Intervention Plan (BIP), and return the student to their original placement unless the parents and Tomball ISD agree to a change of placement as part of the modification of the behavioral intervention plan. Time is of the essence. If your child has been suspended, act within days, not weeks. Use the Discipline Defense Kit immediately to protect your child's rights and ensure appropriate supports are in place.

Filing a Complaint Against Tomball ISD

If you believe that Tomball ISD is not fulfilling its legal obligations to your child, or that your child's rights are being violated, you have several options for filing a complaint. These avenues for escalation are in place regardless of what any staff member at Tomball ISD might tell you.

First, you can file a TEA State Complaint with the Texas Education Agency. This is a free process, and TEA is required to investigate and resolve the complaint within 60 days. Second, you can request mediation through TEA, which is also a free service that brings you and Tomball ISD together with a neutral third-party mediator to attempt to resolve the dispute. Third, you can request a Due Process Hearing, which is a more formal legal proceeding where an administrative law judge will hear evidence and make a decision. Finally, you can file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR), which investigates allegations of discrimination based on disability. Remember, you have the right to advocate for your child's needs and hold Tomball ISD accountable for providing a Free Appropriate Public Education. Contact a special education advocate or attorney to discuss your specific situation and determine the best course of action.

Frequently Asked Questions About Tomball ISD Special Education

The questions every Tomball ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Tomball ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Tomball ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Tomball ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Tomball ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Tomball ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Tomball ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Tomball ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Tomball ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
