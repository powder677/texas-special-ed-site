A special education evaluation is a comprehensive process that examines whether your child has a disability that affects their learning. In SANGER ISD, this evaluation is conducted by certified professionals such as school psychologists, special education teachers, and other specialists.

The evaluation includes academic tests, behavioral assessments, classroom observations, and interviews with teachers and parents. The goal is to create a comprehensive profile of your child's needs to determine what special services they might require.

Under federal laws IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) and Section 504, SANGER ISD must provide free evaluations for any student suspected of having a disability.

Fundamental Parental Rights

SANGER ISD must respect your rights as a parent. These include the right to request a special education evaluation, the right to participate in all meetings, and the right to challenge evaluation results.

Key rights in SANGER ISD:

  • Request a special education evaluation at any time
  • Receive written notice before any evaluation
  • Informed consent before the evaluation begins
  • Review all of your child's records
  • Participate in the IEP (Individualized Education Program) meeting
  • Bring an attorney or advocate to any meeting
  • Request an independent evaluation at SANGER ISD's expense if you disagree with the results
  • Receive all information in Spanish

Legal Protections for Families in SANGER ISD

Texas Education Code Section 29.001 and IDEA protect SANGER ISD students who may have disabilities. SANGER ISD must follow specific procedures and respect your rights at every step of the process.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in SANGER ISD

Step 1: Make a Written Request to SANGER ISD

The first step is to formally request a special education evaluation from SANGER ISD. You can do so in several ways: in person, by mail, by email, or by phone. However, you should always make a written request to create an official record.

What to include in your request:

  • Your full name and contact information
  • Your child's full name and grade level
  • The school your child attends within SANGER ISD
  • A clear description of your specific concerns
  • Any previous evaluations or relevant medical history
  • A clear request for a special education evaluation

Send this request to your school's principal in SANGER ISD or directly to the special education department. Keep a copy for your records.

Step 2: Await SANGER ISD's Response

By law, SANGER ISD must respond to your request within 15 school days. The school must notify you if they agree to proceed with the evaluation or if they believe it is not necessary.

If SANGER ISD states that an evaluation is not necessary, they may still suggest regular classroom interventions. If you disagree with this decision, you have options. You can request a meeting with the special education director or seek legal advice.

Step 3: Provide Written Consent to SANGER ISD

If SANGER ISD decides to proceed with the evaluation, they must obtain your informed written consent before beginning any evaluation. This is a federal requirement and a key parental right in SANGER ISD.

The consent form should explain what will be evaluated, who will conduct the evaluation, and your rights as a parent. You have the right to ask questions and to take time to review before signing.

The Evaluation Process in SANGER ISD

Components of the Special Education Evaluation

A complete special education evaluation in SANGER ISD includes multiple components to get a comprehensive picture of your child:

  1. Psychoeducational Evaluation: Tests of IQ, academic skills, and cognitive function
  2. Speech and Language Evaluation: If communication problems are suspected
  3. Occupational and Physical Evaluation: If there are concerns about motor skills
  4. Vision and Hearing Evaluation: To rule out sensory issues
  5. Social and Developmental History: Family and medical background
  6. Classroom Observations: How your child behaves in school
  7. Teacher Interview: Educators' perspectives on performance

Timeline for SANGER ISD Evaluation

The complete evaluation process in SANGER ISD generally takes 60 school days from the time you provide consent. During this time, SANGER ISD specialists will gather information from multiple sources.

You can expect teachers to begin observing your child and to ask you to complete questionnaires about your child's behavior and development at home.

The IEP (Individualized Education Program) Meeting in SANGER ISD

What Happens at the IEP Meeting

Once the special education evaluation in SANGER ISD is complete, there must be a meeting to discuss the results. This is the IEP (Individualized Education Program) meeting, where it is determined if your child qualifies for special education services.

At this meeting with SANGER ISD, the evaluation team will present their findings. If your child qualifies, the specific services they will receive, educational goals, and how progress will be measured will be discussed.

Your Rights During the IEP Meeting in SANGER ISD

Remember these rights during your IEP meeting in SANGER ISD:

  • Right to receive all information in Spanish
  • Right to bring an attorney, advocate, or support person
  • Right to review all records before the meeting
  • Right to ask questions about any aspect of the evaluation
  • Right to agree or disagree with the results
  • Right to request changes to the proposed IEP
  • Right to ask for more time to decide

When SANGER ISD Says "No" – Your Options

If SANGER ISD Denies the Evaluation

If SANGER ISD initially denies your request for a special education evaluation, you still have options. You can:

  • Request an informal resolution meeting with SANGER ISD
  • Formally request a special education evaluation (FIE) again in writing
  • Request an independent evaluation on your own
  • Contact a disability advocacy agency
  • Consult with a special education attorney near SANGER

If You Disagree with the Results

If you complete the special education evaluation in SANGER ISD but disagree with the results, you can request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE). SANGER ISD must pay for this evaluation if you can demonstrate that their evaluation was inadequate.

An independent evaluation can provide a different perspective and is often very valuable when there is disagreement about the services your child needs.
