What can I do right now if Montgomery ISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Montgomery ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Montgomery ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating special education can feel overwhelming, but you're not alone. The Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) is a federal law that ensures children with disabilities have access to a free appropriate public education (FAPE) designed to meet their unique needs. Texas law echoes these protections, and Montgomery ISD is obligated to follow both. Special education isn't just a place or a program; it's a system of individualized supports and services designed to help your child succeed in school.

Montgomery ISD is under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA), the state agency responsible for public education in Texas. TEA provides resources and guidance to school districts like Montgomery ISD, and the TEA Region 4 Education Service Center in Houston offers additional support to schools and families in the area. Knowing that these support systems exist can be reassuring as you navigate the special education process with Montgomery ISD. Remember, the goal is to ensure your child receives the individualized education they deserve.

Crucially, as a parent, you have enforceable legal rights throughout the entire special education process. These rights are designed to protect your child and ensure you have a meaningful voice in their education. From the initial evaluation to the development of an Individualized Education Program (IEP) and beyond, you have the right to be informed, to participate, and to advocate for your child's needs within Montgomery ISD.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Montgomery ISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability that requires special education services, the first step is to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) from Montgomery ISD. It's vital to understand that this request MUST be in writing. A verbal request, while it may seem like a start, does NOT begin the timeline for the school district to act. This written request should clearly state your concerns about your child's academic or behavioral performance and why you believe they may need special education services. Be as specific as possible, and include any relevant documentation you have, such as previous evaluations, medical records, or teacher observations.

Once Montgomery ISD receives your written request and you provide written consent, the district has 45 school days to complete the FIE. This evaluation will assess your child's strengths and weaknesses in all areas of suspected disability. Additionally, remember that Child Find is a critical component of IDEA. It mandates that Montgomery ISD proactively identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities who reside within their boundaries, regardless of whether they are enrolled in public school. If you believe your child may need services, don't hesitate to reach out, even if you haven't received a formal recommendation. Your proactive involvement is crucial.

Your next step should be to draft a letter requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for your child. Keep a copy of this letter for your records, and send it to the Montgomery ISD Special Education Director via certified mail with return receipt requested. This will provide you with proof that the district received your request and when.

ARD Meetings in Montgomery ISD: Your Rights at the Table

The ARD committee meeting, where ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal, is the cornerstone of the special education process. It's where decisions are made about your child's eligibility for services, their IEP, and their placement. Montgomery ISD must provide you with written notice at least five calendar days before any ARD meeting. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, the date, time, location, and a list of who will be attending. Review this notice carefully and make sure you can attend or reschedule if needed.

You are an equal member of the ARD committee, and your input is invaluable. Don't hesitate to ask questions, share your concerns, and advocate for your child's needs. Remember, you can request a ten-day recess (postponement) of the ARD meeting at any time. This is a critical right if you feel overwhelmed, need more information, or want to consult with an advocate or attorney. This recess allows you time to process information and prepare further input. Also, you are under no obligation to sign the IEP on the same day as the ARD meeting. Take the time you need to review it thoroughly and seek outside advice if you wish.

Your role at the ARD table is to advocate for your child. Don't feel pressured to make immediate decisions. Take your time, ask questions, and remember your rights. Before your next ARD meeting in Montgomery ISD, download our free ARD preparation checklist to help you stay organized and informed.

Dyslexia Services in Montgomery ISD

Texas law mandates that Montgomery ISD screen all students for dyslexia. This screening is a critical first step in identifying students who may struggle with reading and spelling due to dyslexia. If your child's screening indicates a risk for dyslexia, they are entitled to further evaluation and, if identified as dyslexic, structured literacy intervention. This intervention should be evidence-based and tailored to meet your child's specific needs.

The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook sets the standards for how Montgomery ISD identifies and supports students with dyslexia. Be sure to familiarize yourself with this handbook, as it outlines the district's responsibilities. Students identified with dyslexia may qualify for a 504 Plan, which provides accommodations to help them succeed in the classroom, or they may require more intensive support through an IEP. Remember, early identification and intervention are crucial for students with dyslexia.

If your child has not been screened for dyslexia, or if you have concerns about their reading skills, request a dyslexia screening in writing from your child's school in Montgomery ISD.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Montgomery ISD

Disciplinary actions, especially suspensions, can have a significant impact on students with disabilities. If your child with an IEP or 504 Plan is suspended for more than ten cumulative days in a school year in Montgomery ISD, this triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This review is a critical process to determine if the student's misconduct was caused by, or directly and substantially related to, their disability. It also considers whether the conduct was a direct result of Montgomery ISD's failure to implement the IEP.

Suspensions exceeding ten cumulative days also constitute a Change of Placement under IDEA, requiring an ARD meeting to review the situation and determine if the current placement is appropriate. This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a special education student. You must act quickly to protect your child's rights. If the MDR determines the behavior was a manifestation of the disability or due to a failure to implement the IEP, the student cannot be disciplined in the same way as a student without a disability. Do not delay seeking help, as these situations can quickly escalate.

If your child has been suspended from Montgomery ISD, immediately use the Discipline Defense Kit to understand your rights and take appropriate action to protect your child's education.

Filing a Complaint Against Montgomery ISD

If you believe Montgomery ISD is not meeting its obligations under IDEA, or is violating your child's rights, you have several avenues for recourse. These options exist regardless of what school staff may tell you. The first is a TEA State Complaint, which is a free process where you file a formal complaint with the TEA. The TEA will investigate the complaint and issue a decision, typically within 60 days. Another free option is Mediation through TEA, where a neutral mediator facilitates a discussion between you and Montgomery ISD to try to resolve the dispute.

You also have the right to request a Due Process Hearing, a more formal legal proceeding where an impartial hearing officer makes a decision on the matter. This option may require legal representation. Finally, you can file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR) if you believe Montgomery ISD has discriminated against your child based on their disability. These escalation paths are in place to protect your child's rights and ensure they receive the education they are entitled to. Choose the path that best suits your needs and comfort level, but remember that you have options.

Your next step is to carefully document all instances where you believe Montgomery ISD has failed to meet its obligations. Having detailed records will be essential if you choose to file a complaint.

Frequently Asked Questions About Montgomery ISD Special Education

The questions every Montgomery ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Montgomery ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Montgomery ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Montgomery ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Montgomery ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Montgomery ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Montgomery ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Montgomery ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Montgomery ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
