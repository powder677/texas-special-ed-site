Dispute & Discipline Rights in Montgomery ISD

🚨 Know This Now

Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

Discipline Rights & Grievances in Montgomery ISD (URGENT!)

URGENT GUIDE: Discipline Rights & Grievances in Montgomery ISD

If your child in Montgomery ISD is facing disciplinary action, especially suspension or potential expulsion, ACT IMMEDIATELY. This guide provides crucial information. Don't delay, every day counts.

The 10-Day Suspension Rule

Montgomery ISD, like other Texas districts, has rules regarding out-of-school suspension. Know this: a student CANNOT be suspended for more than 3 school days at a time without proper procedures. If the total suspensions throughout the year exceed 10 days, it triggers specific rights, ESPECIALLY if your child has an IEP (Individualized Education Program) or 504 plan under IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act). TRACK every suspension day!

After exceeding 10 cumulative suspension days in a school year, the school MUST determine if the behavior leading to the suspensions is a manifestation of your child's disability. This is CRITICAL!

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

An MDR is a meeting held to determine if the behavior that led to the suspension or potential expulsion is a direct result of your child's disability, OR if the school failed to properly implement the IEP or 504 plan. YOU MUST DEMAND AN MDR IF YOUR CHILD IS SUBJECT TO DISCIPLINE EXCEEDING 10 DAYS!

Prepare for the MDR! Bring documentation: IEP, 504 plan, medical records, any reports from therapists or doctors. Be prepared to argue that the behavior is related to the disability or that the school failed to provide appropriate supports. Don't go it alone! Consider bringing an advocate.

Change of Placement

A Change of Placement occurs when a student is removed from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days OR when the student is subjected to a pattern of removals that constitute a change of placement (even if no single removal is longer than 10 days). This is a BIG DEAL.

If the school is proposing a change of placement (e.g., expulsion to a DAEP - Disciplinary Alternative Education Program), IMMEDIATELY invoke your rights! You have the right to challenge the placement. The school must provide evidence supporting their decision.

Crucially, IDEA protects students with disabilities from being punished for behaviors related to their disability. A change of placement requires a manifestation determination. Fight for your child's right to stay in their current educational environment, especially if it's serving their needs.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe Montgomery ISD has violated your child's rights, you can file a formal grievance. The first step is usually a Level 1 grievance, typically filed with the school principal. DOCUMENT EVERYTHING! Keep copies of all communication.

Your grievance should clearly state the violation, the date of the incident, the names of individuals involved, and the remedy you seek. Be specific. Time is of the essence. Check Montgomery ISD's policy for specific grievance deadlines. Missing the deadline can jeopardize your case.

TEA State Complaint

If the grievance process doesn't resolve the issue, or if you believe the district is fundamentally violating IDEA, you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA complaints have strict timelines. Research the TEA's complaint process online and gather all supporting documentation. A TEA complaint requires substantial evidence of the violation.

Due Process

Your child has due process rights! This means they are entitled to fair treatment, including the right to notice of the charges against them, the right to a hearing, the right to present evidence, and the right to cross-examine witnesses.

Don't be intimidated by the school district. Advocate fiercely for your child's rights. Seek legal advice if needed. Your child's future depends on it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
