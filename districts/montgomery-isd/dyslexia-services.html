Dyslexia Services in Montgomery ISD

Dyslexia Services in Montgomery ISD

Texas Dyslexia Handbook 2024

Montgomery ISD follows the guidelines outlined in the Texas Dyslexia Handbook 2024 (link opens in a new tab). This handbook provides comprehensive information on dyslexia identification, intervention, and support services for students in Texas public schools. It includes updated information on screening procedures, evidence-based interventions, and legal requirements. Parents and educators are encouraged to review the handbook thoroughly to understand their rights and responsibilities regarding dyslexia services. Montgomery ISD uses the handbook as a primary resource for ensuring all students with dyslexia receive appropriate and effective support.

Mandatory Screening

Texas law requires all students in kindergarten and first grade to be screened for dyslexia. Montgomery ISD adheres to this mandate and utilizes approved screening instruments to identify students at risk for dyslexia. The district also uses ongoing progress monitoring to identify students who may develop characteristics of dyslexia in later grades. Screenings typically assess phonological awareness, phonological memory, rapid naming, and letter-sound knowledge. Parents will be notified if their child's screening results indicate a need for further evaluation.

Structured Literacy Programs

Montgomery ISD utilizes structured literacy programs for students identified with dyslexia or at risk for dyslexia. These programs are research-based and focus on explicit and systematic instruction in phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension. Structured literacy emphasizes the connections between letters and sounds, teaching students to decode and encode words in a sequential and logical manner. These programs are implemented by trained teachers and specialists. Specific program names used may vary across campuses, but all align with the principles of structured literacy as defined by the International Dyslexia Association. Parents can inquire with their child's teacher or the campus dyslexia specialist to learn more about the specific program used at their school.

504 vs IEP for Dyslexia

Students with dyslexia may be eligible for support through either a 504 plan or an Individualized Education Program (IEP) under special education. A 504 plan provides accommodations and modifications to ensure students with disabilities have equal access to the general education curriculum. An IEP is for students who require specialized instruction and related services due to a disability that significantly impacts their academic performance. The determination of whether a student qualifies for a 504 plan or an IEP is made by a team of professionals, including teachers, administrators, and parents, based on comprehensive evaluation data. Generally, students needing significant, intensive, and specially designed instruction would qualify for an IEP, while those needing primarily accommodations would be served through a 504 plan. The team will determine the most appropriate plan based on the student's individual needs and the impact of dyslexia on their learning.

Accommodations List

The following are examples of accommodations that may be provided to students with dyslexia in Montgomery ISD. The specific accommodations provided will be determined by the 504 or ARD (IEP) committee based on the student's individual needs.

  • Extended time on tests and assignments
  • Preferential seating
  • Use of assistive technology (e.g., text-to-speech software, audiobooks)
  • Modified assignments
  • Shorter assignments
  • Oral administration of tests
  • Use of graphic organizers
  • Multi-sensory instruction
  • Frequent breaks
This is not an exhaustive list, and other accommodations may be provided as deemed necessary.

How to Request Evaluation

If you suspect your child may have dyslexia, contact your child's teacher, school counselor, or campus principal to express your concerns. You can also reach out to the district's Special Education Department. A written request for an evaluation is recommended. The school will then initiate a process to gather information about your child's academic performance, including classroom observations, work samples, and standardized test scores. If, after reviewing this information, the school team determines that further evaluation is warranted, they will obtain your consent and proceed with a comprehensive evaluation to determine if your child meets the criteria for dyslexia. The evaluation results will be shared with you, and a meeting will be scheduled to discuss the findings and determine appropriate support services, if needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

Is my child entitled to dyslexia testing in Texas? +

Yes. Texas law requires all districts to screen students for dyslexia and related disorders in Kindergarten and First Grade, and at any grade level if a parent or teacher requests it.

What accommodations can a dyslexic child get in Texas? +

Accommodations may include extended time on tests, text-to-speech software, oral administration of tests, reduced-answer-choice formats, and use of a calculator. These can be provided through a 504 Plan or an IEP.

What is the Texas Dyslexia Handbook? +

The Texas Dyslexia Handbook is a state guidance document updated in 2024 that outlines districts' responsibilities for identifying, evaluating, and serving students with dyslexia using structured literacy methods.
