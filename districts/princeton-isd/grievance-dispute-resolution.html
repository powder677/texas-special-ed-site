Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

Discipline Rights & Grievances in Princeton ISD: An Urgent Guide

If your child in Princeton ISD is facing disciplinary action, especially a suspension or expulsion, ACT IMMEDIATELY. Time is NOT on your side. This guide provides essential information to protect your child's rights. This information is for general guidance only and should not be considered legal advice. Consult with an attorney or advocate specializing in special education law as soon as possible.

The 10-Day Suspension Rule

Princeton ISD can suspend a student for up to 3 school days for any disciplinary infraction. However, cumulative suspensions totaling more than 10 school days in a single school year trigger significant protections, especially if your child has an IEP or is suspected of having a disability. Tracking the number of suspension days is CRUCIAL. Once the 10-day threshold is met, specific rules under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) kick in.

What Is an MDR? (Manifestation Determination Review)

An MDR, or Manifestation Determination Review, is REQUIRED under IDEA when a student with an IEP (or suspected of having a disability) faces suspension exceeding 10 cumulative days in a school year, or any proposed change of placement (see below). The purpose of the MDR is to determine if the student's behavior that led to the disciplinary action was caused by or directly and substantially related to the student's disability, OR was a direct result of the school district's failure to implement the IEP.

IMPORTANT: The MDR meeting MUST include relevant members of the IEP team, the parent, and relevant school personnel. Preparation is KEY. Gather all relevant documentation: IEP, behavior plans, progress reports, disciplinary referrals, teacher emails, and any medical or psychological evaluations. Argue forcefully that the behavior is linked to the disability and/or IEP non-compliance. If the MDR determines the behavior was a manifestation of the disability OR IEP failure, the student CANNOT be disciplined in the same manner as a non-disabled student. The IEP team MUST conduct a Functional Behavioral Assessment (FBA), implement or revise the Behavior Intervention Plan (BIP), and return the student to their original placement (unless the parents and school agree to a change of placement as part of the IEP team decisions).

Change of Placement

A "Change of Placement" occurs when a student is removed from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days OR when the removal constitutes a pattern of removals (even if less than 10 days at a time) that totals more than 10 school days in a school year AND demonstrates a change in the student's educational placement (e.g., a significant change in location, curriculum, or services). Suspension or expulsion to an alternative educational setting (like DAEP) can be considered a Change of Placement. Any Change of Placement triggers the MDR requirements explained above. Be vigilant about tracking cumulative removals and advocating for your child's right to remain in their current placement if appropriate.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

Princeton ISD has a formal grievance policy. If you believe your child's rights have been violated (e.g., improper suspension, failure to provide services outlined in the IEP, failure to conduct a timely MDR), you should file a Level 1 grievance IMMEDIATELY. Obtain a copy of the Princeton ISD grievance policy from the district website or school administration. The Level 1 grievance is typically filed with the campus principal. Your grievance should be specific, detailing the dates, times, individuals involved, and the specific policy/law that was violated. Gather all supporting documentation. Keep a copy of everything you submit. The principal has a limited time to respond (usually 10-15 days, check the policy!). If the principal's response is unsatisfactory, you can proceed to Level 2.

TEA State Complaint

If you believe Princeton ISD has violated IDEA or state special education law, you can file a formal complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This is a more formal process with specific requirements. TEA will investigate your complaint. Information on filing a TEA complaint can be found on the TEA website. Filing a TEA complaint does NOT preclude you from pursuing other legal options.

Due Process

If you disagree with the outcome of the MDR, a proposed Change of Placement, or other decisions related to your child's special education, you have the right to request a Due Process Hearing. This is a legal proceeding where you can present evidence and argue your case before an impartial hearing officer. Due Process is a complex legal process, and you should absolutely consult with an attorney before pursuing this option. Filing a Due Process complaint triggers specific timelines and requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
