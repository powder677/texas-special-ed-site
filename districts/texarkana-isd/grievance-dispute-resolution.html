Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

Keep in mind, if your child is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year, especially if they have an IEP, it triggers important procedural protections. Pay close attention to the total number of days your child has been suspended. Each day counts!

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

An MDR (Manifestation Determination Review) is crucial if your child with an IEP or 504 plan faces a suspension exceeding 10 school days or a potential Change of Placement. It's a meeting where the IEP team (including you!) decides if the misconduct was a direct result of the child's disability OR if the school failed to implement the IEP. This is the most important meeting you can attend. Insist on having this meeting scheduled IMMEDIATELY.

Crucial questions to ask during the MDR:

  • Does the IEP address the behavior that led to the disciplinary action?
  • Was the IEP being implemented with fidelity (exactly as written)?
  • Was there a failure on the school's part to provide appropriate accommodations or supports?
  • Was the incident caused by the disability?

If the answer to ANY of these questions is YES, the behavior is considered a manifestation of the disability, and your child CANNOT be disciplined in the same way as a general education student. The school MUST return your child to their previous placement (unless you agree otherwise) and conduct a Functional Behavioral Assessment (FBA) or revise the existing one, and implement a Behavior Intervention Plan (BIP). If they say the behavior is NOT related to the disability, demand detailed justification and get it in writing. Prepare for this meeting by gathering all documentation related to your child's IEP, behavior incidents, and communication with the school.

Change of Placement

A Change of Placement occurs when a student is removed from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days or when a series of removals constitutes a pattern. Suspensions exceeding 10 days often trigger a Change of Placement analysis. The school cannot unilaterally decide to change your child's placement without following proper procedures, which include notification and an opportunity to challenge the decision. Understand this is a significant legal trigger and requires careful attention to detail to ensure IDEA rights are protected. A Change of Placement can involve moving your child to a different classroom, campus, or even a disciplinary alternative education program (DAEP). Fight this if the action is a violation of IDEA requirements.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe Texarkana ISD violated your child's rights or followed an improper procedure, you have the right to file a grievance. Typically, the first step is a Level 1 Grievance. This involves submitting a written complaint to the principal or administrator directly involved. Be specific. State clearly the policy that was violated, the facts of the incident, and the remedy you are seeking. Keep a copy of your grievance and track the date it was submitted. Texarkana ISD is required to respond within a specific timeframe outlined in their policies. Failure to respond adequately or within the given timeframe can allow you to escalate the grievance to the next level.

TEA State Complaint

If you believe Texarkana ISD has violated the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), you have the right to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). A TEA complaint is a more formal process than a local grievance. There are specific timelines and requirements for filing a TEA complaint. The complaint must describe the violation of IDEA and provide supporting documentation. Contact TEA directly for information on the complaint process and deadlines.

Due Process

If you disagree with the outcome of an MDR or a disciplinary action that constitutes a Change of Placement, and you believe the school is violating IDEA, you have the right to request a due process hearing. This is a formal legal proceeding where you can present evidence and arguments to an impartial hearing officer. Due process hearings can be complex and require significant preparation. Consult with an attorney or advocate specializing in special education law immediately if you are considering requesting a due process hearing. The deadline to request a hearing is tight, so act quickly!

Remember: Document everything! Keep records of all communication with the school, disciplinary actions, and meetings. Your child's future depends on you being informed and proactive.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
