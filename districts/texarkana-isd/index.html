What can I do right now if Texarkana ISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Texarkana ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Texarkana ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the world of special education can feel overwhelming, but understanding your rights and the resources available to you is the first step. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), both federal and Texas law guarantee that your child, if eligible, receives a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) designed to meet their unique needs. This means individualized instruction and related services, such as speech therapy or occupational therapy, are provided at no cost to you. Texarkana ISD is committed to fulfilling this obligation to all students within its boundaries.

Texarkana ISD operates under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA), which sets the standards for special education programs throughout the state. TEA Region 8 Education Service Center, located right here in Northeast Texas, provides support and resources to Texarkana ISD to help ensure students receive the best possible services. This support includes training for teachers, access to specialized expertise, and assistance with implementing best practices in special education.

As a parent, you are a crucial member of your child's educational team, and you have legally enforceable rights throughout the entire special education process within Texarkana ISD. From the initial evaluation to the development and implementation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP), your voice matters, and your consent is required at key decision points. Understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child's needs and ensure they receive the support they deserve. Start familiarizing yourself with the IDEA law, specifically the portions covering parental rights.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Texarkana ISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability that is impacting their educational performance, you have the right to request a special education evaluation from Texarkana ISD. It is crucial to understand that this request must be submitted in writing. A verbal request, while helpful for starting the conversation, does not officially initiate the evaluation process and does not begin the timeline required by law.

Once Texarkana ISD receives your written consent to evaluate your child, the district has 45 school days to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). This comprehensive evaluation assesses your child's strengths and weaknesses across various areas, such as academic skills, cognitive abilities, and social-emotional functioning. Remember, Child Find laws require Texarkana ISD to proactively identify students who may need special education services, even if you haven't requested an evaluation. If you believe your child is struggling, do not hesitate to act.

Right now, write a letter to your child's principal at their Texarkana ISD school formally requesting a special education evaluation. Be clear and concise, stating your concerns and the reasons you believe your child may need special education services. Keep a copy of the letter for your records and send the original via certified mail with return receipt requested. This provides proof that Texarkana ISD received your request and officially starts the 45-day timeline.

ARD Meetings in Texarkana ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal, and an ARD meeting is where crucial decisions about your child's special education program are made. This is where you, along with teachers, administrators, and other specialists, collaborate to develop your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP). Your active participation is essential to ensure the IEP is tailored to your child's unique needs and goals within Texarkana ISD.

Texarkana ISD is required to provide you with at least five calendar days written notice before any ARD meeting. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, the date, time, location, and a list of the individuals who will be attending. Remember, you have the right to request a 10-day recess during the ARD meeting if you need more time to process information or consult with outside experts. Don't feel pressured to make immediate decisions. You also do not have to sign the IEP document the same day of the ARD meeting. Take it home, review it carefully, and seek advice if needed.

It is important to remember that you are an equal member of the ARD committee and your input is valued. Do not let the school steamroll you. Before your next ARD meeting in Texarkana ISD, download our free ARD preparation checklist. This checklist will help you organize your thoughts, prepare questions, and ensure you are advocating effectively for your child's needs.

Dyslexia Services in Texarkana ISD

Texas law mandates that Texarkana ISD screen all students for dyslexia. This screening is typically conducted in the early grades to identify students who may be at risk for reading difficulties. Early identification and intervention are crucial for students with dyslexia, as it can significantly impact their academic success and overall well-being. The school must follow state guidelines.

The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook sets the standards that Texarkana ISD must follow in identifying and supporting students with dyslexia. If your child is identified as having dyslexia, they are entitled to structured literacy intervention, which is a research-based approach that focuses on teaching phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and reading comprehension. Depending on the severity of your child's dyslexia and their individual needs, they may qualify for a 504 Plan or an IEP to receive additional support and accommodations within Texarkana ISD.

If your child has not been screened for dyslexia, or if you have concerns about their reading development, request a dyslexia screening in writing from your child's school in Texarkana ISD. Don't delay; the sooner your child receives the support they need, the better.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Texarkana ISD

Disciplinary actions, such as suspensions, can have a significant impact on students with disabilities. It's important to know that special education students have specific protections under IDEA regarding discipline. If your child is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year within Texarkana ISD, it triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This review is designed to determine if the student's misconduct was a manifestation of their disability or a result of the school's failure to implement the IEP correctly.

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative days also constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA, which requires an ARD meeting to determine if the change is appropriate. This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a special education student. Failure to act quickly can result in your child being unfairly disciplined and missing valuable instructional time in Texarkana ISD.

If your child has been suspended from Texarkana ISD, use the Discipline Defense Kit immediately. It provides you with the essential information and steps you need to take to protect your child's rights and ensure they receive a fair and appropriate disciplinary process.

Filing a Complaint Against Texarkana ISD

If you have exhausted all other avenues to resolve a disagreement with Texarkana ISD regarding your child's special education services, you have several options for filing a formal complaint. It is important to know these rights exist regardless of what a Texarkana ISD employee tells you.

The first option is to file a TEA State Complaint. This is a free process that is resolved within 60 days. You can also request Mediation through TEA, which is also free. A neutral third-party mediator will help you and Texarkana ISD try to reach a mutually agreeable solution. If mediation is unsuccessful, you can pursue a Due Process Hearing, which is a more formal legal proceeding. Finally, you can file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR) if you believe Texarkana ISD has discriminated against your child based on their disability. Research all four pathways now to prepare yourself should you need them in the future.

Frequently Asked Questions About Texarkana ISD Special Education

The questions every Texarkana ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Texarkana ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Texarkana ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Texarkana ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Texarkana ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Texarkana ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Texarkana ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Texarkana ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Texarkana ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
