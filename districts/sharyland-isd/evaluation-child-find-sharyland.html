Step 1: Document Your Concerns

Before making a formal request, gather specific examples of your concerns. Note dates, incidents, and how your child's difficulties compare to peers. Are they struggling with reading? Do they have trouble following instructions? Are there social conflicts? Concrete examples are invaluable when discussing evaluation with SHARYLAND ISD staff.

Keep records of report cards, teacher communications, work samples, and any outside evaluations (medical, psychological, or developmental assessments). This documentation becomes important evidence during the SHARYLAND ISD FIE evaluation process.

Step 2: Request an Evaluation Meeting

Contact your child's campus principal, school counselor, or special education coordinator to request a meeting to discuss evaluation for special education. You can make this request verbally, but it's best to follow up in writing to create a paper trail. Email works perfectly for this purpose.

Your request should briefly explain your concerns and request that SHARYLAND ISD consider a SHARYLAND ISD child find evaluation. Include specific areas of concern—academic, behavioral, social, or developmental.

Step 3: Attend the Pre-Evaluation Meeting

SHARYLAND ISD will schedule a meeting before the formal evaluation begins. During this meeting, school staff will discuss your concerns, review your child's history, and explain what the SHARYLAND ISD FIE evaluation process involves.

This is an excellent opportunity to ask questions about the evaluation's scope, what areas will be assessed, who will conduct the evaluation, and how results will be shared. Come prepared with your documentation and specific questions about your child's needs.

Step 4: Complete Parental Consent Forms

Before SHARYLAND ISD can conduct a formal SHARYLAND ISD special education evaluation, you must provide written consent. The district will provide consent forms explaining what will be evaluated and how the results will be used.

Read these forms carefully. You have the right to ask questions about any aspect of the evaluation before signing. Once you consent, the 45-day evaluation timeline begins.

Understanding the SHARYLAND ISD 45-Day Timeline

Texas Education Code §26.305 requires that SHARYLAND ISD complete the SHARYLAND ISD FIE evaluation within 45 calendar days from the date you provide written consent. This is a critical deadline that parents in SHARYLAND ISD should monitor closely.

What Happens During These 45 Days

During the evaluation period, school psychologists, special education specialists, and other professionals will conduct comprehensive assessments. This might include cognitive testing, academic achievement testing, classroom observations, and teacher/parent rating scales.

The evaluators will also review your child's school records, health history, and any previous evaluations. This thorough approach ensures the SHARYLAND ISD special education evaluation captures a complete picture of your child's abilities and needs.

What Happens After 45 Days

SHARYLAND ISD must complete the evaluation and schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting within the 45-day timeline. At this meeting, the evaluation results are presented, and a decision is made about special education eligibility.

If you don't hear from SHARYLAND ISD about scheduling the ARD meeting with about a week remaining in the 45-day window, contact the special education department immediately. They should never let this critical deadline pass.

What to Expect During the SHARYLAND ISD FIE Evaluation

Types of Assessments Included

A comprehensive SHARYLAND ISD evaluation request typically includes several types of assessments:

  • Cognitive/Intelligence Testing: Measures reasoning, problem-solving, and thinking skills
  • Academic Achievement Testing: Evaluates reading, math, writing, and other academic skills
  • Speech-Language Evaluation: Assesses communication abilities if language concerns exist
  • Occupational/Physical Therapy Evaluations: Addresses fine and gross motor skills if relevant
  • Classroom Observations: Documents your child's behavior and performance in academic settings
  • Parent and Teacher Rating Scales: Captures perspectives on social-emotional and behavioral functioning

The Role of the School Psychologist

The school psychologist plays a central role in SHARYLAND ISD special education evaluation. They typically coordinate the evaluation, conduct cognitive and behavioral assessments, and synthesize results from all evaluators into a comprehensive report.

The school psychologist will likely contact you to discuss your child's developmental history and current concerns. Be prepared to discuss family background, developmental milestones, and any behaviors or health issues relevant to the evaluation.

Preparing for Your Child's SHARYLAND ISD FIE Evaluation

Talk to Your Child

In age-appropriate terms, explain to your child that they'll be working with school specialists who will ask questions and give them activities to complete. Emphasize that this helps the school understand how to best help them learn.

Avoid creating anxiety by saying "you're taking a test." Instead, frame it as "learning activities" that help adults understand how you learn best.

Ensure Adequate Rest and Health

Evaluation results are most accurate when your child is well-rested and healthy. Try to schedule evaluations when your child is well and avoid scheduling them immediately after illness or significant stress.

If your child takes medication for ADHD or other conditions, ensure they're taking it as prescribed during evaluation days. Consistency in medication timing helps evaluators see your child's typical functioning.

Communicate With SHARYLAND ISD Staff

Share any information about your child that might affect testing. If your child was up all night with anxiety, if there's stress at home, or if your child has hearing or vision concerns, tell the evaluators. This context helps them interpret results accurately.

The ARD Meeting and Evaluation Results

Understanding Your Rights at the ARD Meeting

The Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting is where SHARYLAND ISD presents SHARYLAND ISD FIE evaluation results and discusses your child's eligibility for special education. You have important rights during this meeting:

  • Right to bring an advocate or attorney to support you
  • Right to ask questions about any aspect of the evaluation or results
  • Right to request clarification of technical or unfamiliar terms
  • Right to disagree with evaluation conclusions
  • Right to request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at school expense if you disagree

What Eligibility Categories Mean

If your child qualifies for special education through the SHARYLAND ISD special education evaluation, they'll be identified under one of several disability categories, such as Specific Learning Disability, Other Health Impairment, Autism Spectrum Disorder, Intellectual Disability, Emotional Disturbance, or Speech-Language Impairment.

Understanding your child's disability category matters because it influences the types of services and support available in SHARYLAND ISD.

If Your Child Doesn't Qualify

Not all children evaluated through SHARYLAND ISD child find qualify for special education. This doesn't mean your child doesn't need support—it means they don't meet disability criteria under IDEA.

If your child doesn't qualify, discuss with SHARYLAND ISD other support options, such as Response to Intervention (RTI), 504 plans, or general education accommodations. You also have the right to request an Independent Educational Evaluation at district expense if you disagree with the SHARYLAND ISD evaluation results.
