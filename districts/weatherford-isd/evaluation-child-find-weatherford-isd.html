Child Find is a federal requirement that obligates WEATHERFORD ISD to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities who may need special education services. As a parent in WEATHERFORD ISD, you don't have to wait for your child's teacher to suggest an evaluation—you can request one at any time.

Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and the Texas Education Code, WEATHERFORD ISD must evaluate your child if there's reason to believe they have a disability. This includes physical disabilities, speech/language impairments, autism, emotional disturbances, learning disabilities, and other health impairments.

Your child doesn't need to be failing classes to qualify for an evaluation. WEATHERFORD ISD must consider any factor that suggests your child may need special education services, regardless of current grades.

A WEATHERFORD ISD FIE evaluation is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine whether your child has a disability and, if so, what services they need. It goes far beyond a simple screening or classroom observation.

Components of a WEATHERFORD ISD Special Education Evaluation

A complete WEATHERFORD ISD special education evaluation typically includes:

  • Cognitive/intellectual testing – Assessment of thinking, learning, and reasoning abilities
  • Academic achievement testing – Evaluation of reading, math, writing, and other academic skills
  • Speech and language evaluation – Assessment if communication concerns exist
  • Occupational/physical therapy evaluation – If motor skills or sensory processing are concerns
  • Behavioral/emotional assessment – Evaluation of social, emotional, and behavioral functioning
  • Vision and hearing screening – To rule out sensory issues
  • Classroom observations – How your child functions in actual school settings
  • Parent/teacher interviews and rating scales – Information from multiple perspectives
  • Review of medical and educational records – Consideration of your child's complete history

Step 1: Submit Your Formal Request in Writing

To request a WEATHERFORD ISD evaluation request, submit a written request to your child's campus or the WEATHERFORD ISD Special Education Department. While verbal requests can begin the process, always follow up with a written request to create an official record.

Your written request should include:

  • Your child's name and date of birth
  • Current campus/grade level
  • Specific concerns you have about your child's learning or development
  • Any previous evaluations or interventions your child has received
  • Your contact information

Email your request to the campus principal or the WEATHERFORD ISD Special Education Department. Keep a copy for your records and document the date sent.

Step 2: Understand the Timeline for WEATHERFORD ISD Evaluations

One of the most important details for WEATHERFORD ISD parents to understand is the WEATHERFORD ISD 45 day timeline. Once WEATHERFORD ISD receives your written evaluation request, the district has 45 calendar days to complete the full evaluation and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting.

This timeline is protected by federal law. WEATHERFORD ISD cannot delay evaluation without your written permission. If your child is absent during the evaluation window, the clock may pause, but WEATHERFORD ISD must resume promptly.

Here's what the timeline looks like:

  • Days 1-3: WEATHERFORD ISD acknowledges your request and sends permission forms
  • Days 4-35: WEATHERFORD ISD conducts evaluations in all suspected areas of disability
  • Days 36-45: WEATHERFORD ISD completes report and schedules ARD meeting

Step 3: Provide Written Consent

Within three days of receiving your evaluation request, WEATHERFORD ISD must provide you with a Notice of Proposed Evaluation and request your written consent. This document explains what will be tested, who will conduct the evaluation, and your rights.

Do not sign consent until you understand what's being evaluated. Ask questions about:

  • Which evaluators will test your child
  • What specific areas will be assessed
  • How long the evaluation will take
  • Whether your child will miss regular classes
  • Your right to bring an advocate or attorney

You have the right to request an independent evaluation at district expense if you disagree with the WEATHERFORD ISD special education evaluation results.

Important Protections and Timelines for WEATHERFORD ISD Families

The 45-Day Window Cannot Be Extended Without Permission

The WEATHERFORD ISD 45 day timeline is binding. WEATHERFORD ISD cannot arbitrarily extend this deadline. Exceptions exist only if:

  • You request an extension in writing
  • Your child is repeatedly absent during evaluation windows
  • You request an independent evaluation (which pauses the clock)

If WEATHERFORD ISD misses the deadline without valid reason, this is a procedural violation that may be addressed through due process.

Staying Informed Throughout the WEATHERFORD ISD Evaluation Process

WEATHERFORD ISD must keep you informed throughout the evaluation. You have the right to:

  • Ask questions about evaluation methods and results
  • Request progress updates
  • Participate in the ARD meeting where results are discussed
  • Bring a trusted advocate, family member, or attorney
  • Ask for the evaluation report in advance of the ARD meeting

Don't wait for the ARD meeting to understand the evaluation results. Ask WEATHERFORD ISD to share the complete report at least 24 hours before your meeting.

The ARD Meeting: What Happens Next in WEATHERFORD ISD

After the WEATHERFORD ISD special education evaluation is complete, WEATHERFORD ISD will schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. This meeting must occur within the 45-day timeline.

Who Attends the WEATHERFORD ISD ARD Meeting?

Required participants for WEATHERFORD ISD ARD meetings include:

  • Parent(s) or guardian
  • Special education teacher
  • General education teacher (if your child is in regular classrooms)
  • WEATHERFORD ISD representative who can commit resources
  • Person who conducted or is knowledgeable about the evaluation
  • Your invited advocates, attorneys, or family members

You can bring anyone you trust to your WEATHERFORD ISD ARD meeting. Many parents in WEATHERFORD ISD bring a special education advocate or attorney, particularly if this is your first evaluation.

What Gets Decided at the WEATHERFORD ISD ARD Meeting?

During your ARD meeting in WEATHERFORD ISD, the team will determine:

  • Eligibility: Does your child have a qualifying disability under IDEA?
  • Services needed: What special education and related services will your child receive?
  • Placement: Where will services be provided (general classroom, resource room, special education classroom)?
  • IEP goals: What specific academic and behavioral goals will guide instruction?
  • Accommodations and modifications: What adjustments help your child access the curriculum?

If your child is found eligible for special education, WEATHERFORD ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) at this same meeting.

Common Reasons for Referral in WEATHERFORD ISD

Parents in WEATHERFORD ISD often request evaluations for these reasons:

  • Reading difficulties – Struggling to decode words or understand text despite instruction
  • Math struggles – Difficulty with number concepts, computation, or problem-solving
  • Speech/language concerns – Difficulty pronouncing words, understanding directions, or expressing ideas
  • Autism spectrum characteristics – Social difficulties, repetitive behaviors, sensory sensitivities
  • Attention/focus issues – Difficulty paying attention or following multi-step directions
  • Behavior challenges – Aggression, defiance, or emotional dysregulation
  • Fine/gross motor delays – Poor handwriting, clumsiness, or coordination difficulties
  • Social difficulties – Trouble making friends or understanding social rules

If any of these concerns apply to your child, you can request a WEATHERFORD ISD child find evaluation regardless of whether the teacher has suggested one.

What to Prepare Before Your WEATHERFORD ISD Evaluation

Help your child succeed during the evaluation process by preparing:

  • Medical records: Any diagnoses, medications, or health conditions affecting learning
  • Previous evaluations: Preschool assessments, private evaluations, or outside testing
  • Work samples: Portfolios showing your child's academic progress or struggles
  • Behavioral history: Examples of behavioral or social concerns
  • Questions list: What you want to know about your child's learning and development
  • Family history: Information about learning disabilities or other conditions in your family

Bring this information to your ARD meeting. WEATHERFORD ISD evaluators need context from parents to interpret results accurately.

Your Rights Under Texas Law and IDEA

As a parent in WEATHERFORD ISD, you have federally protected rights under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code Section 29.001 et seq.

Key rights include:

  • Right to request evaluation: You can request a WEATHERFORD ISD special education evaluation at any time without teacher referral
  • Right to timely evaluation: WEATHERFORD ISD must complete evaluation within the 45 day timeline
  • Right to independent evaluation: If you disagree with WEATHERFORD ISD results, you can request an independent evaluation at district expense
  • Right to due process: You can file a formal complaint if WEATHERFORD ISD violates your rights
  • Right to meaningful participation: You're a full team member in ARD meetings, not just an observer
  • Right to prior notice: WEATHERFORD ISD must notify you before any significant changes to evaluation, placement, or services
