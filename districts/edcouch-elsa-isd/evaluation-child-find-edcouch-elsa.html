Texas Education Code and the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) guarantee that all children with disabilities are identified and evaluated. This is called "child find," and EDCOUCH-ELSA ISD child find procedures require the district to actively search for students who may need special education services.

As a parent in EDCOUCH-ELSA ISD, you don't need to wait for a teacher to suggest evaluation. You can request an EDCOUCH-ELSA ISD evaluation request at any time if you suspect your child has a disability or learning difference.

What Qualifies Your Child for Evaluation?

Your child may be eligible for a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) if they show signs of:

  • Speech or language delays or disorders
  • Intellectual disabilities or developmental delays
  • Autism spectrum disorder
  • Emotional or behavioral difficulties that impact learning
  • Learning disabilities in reading, writing, or math
  • Orthopedic, visual, or hearing impairments
  • Other health impairments affecting school performance
  • Traumatic brain injury

In EDCOUCH-ELSA ISD, evaluations are designed to determine if your child qualifies for special education services and, if so, what services they need.

Step 1: Gather Documentation and Concerns

Before formally requesting an evaluation, document your concerns about your child's development or academic performance. Keep records of:

  • Specific behaviors or academic struggles you've noticed
  • When these concerns began
  • Previous teacher feedback or report cards
  • Medical evaluations or diagnoses from outside professionals
  • Dates of conversations with school staff

This documentation strengthens your request and provides valuable information to the EDCOUCH-ELSA ISD evaluation team.

Step 2: Submit Your Written Request

Send a written request for an EDCOUCH-ELSA ISD evaluation request to your child's school principal or the Special Education Director. While a verbal request begins the process, always follow up with written documentation to start the official timeline.

Your written request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current school and grade level
  • Specific areas of concern (academic, behavioral, speech, etc.)
  • Examples of how these concerns affect your child's learning
  • Any previous evaluations or diagnoses
  • Your preferred contact information

Address your request to:

EDCOUCH-ELSA ISD Special Education Department
Contact your child's campus principal or the district's special education office for the specific mailing address and contact person.

Step 3: Know the EDCOUCH-ELSA ISD 45 Day Timeline

Once EDCOUCH-ELSA ISD receives your written evaluation request, the district has 45 school days to complete the Full Individual Evaluation. This is a critical deadline established by Texas Education Code §29.004.

The EDCOUCH-ELSA ISD 45 day timeline includes:

  • Days 1-5: District acknowledges your request and explains evaluation procedures
  • Days 6-40: EDCOUCH-ELSA ISD completes comprehensive testing and assessment
  • Days 41-45: ARD (Admission, Review, Dismissal) committee meeting to review results

The district may extend this timeline by up to 2 weeks with your written consent. Without your permission, EDCOUCH-ELSA ISD must complete the evaluation within 45 school days.

What to Expect During the EDCOUCH-ELSA ISD Special Education Evaluation

Types of Assessments

When you request an EDCOUCH-ELSA ISD special education evaluation, the district will conduct comprehensive testing across multiple areas. The evaluation typically includes:

  • Achievement testing: Measures academic skills in reading, writing, and math
  • Speech-language evaluation: Assesses communication abilities if speech concerns exist
  • Behavioral/emotional assessment: Evaluates social-emotional functioning and behavior
  • Adaptive behavior assessment: Measures daily living and self-care skills
  • Classroom observations: EDCOUCH-ELSA ISD staff observe your child in natural school settings
  • Parent input: You'll complete questionnaires about your child's development and home behaviors
  • Teacher input: Current teachers provide information about academic and behavioral performance
