Understanding ARD Meetings and IEPs in CASTLEBERRY ISD

An ARD meeting is a formal gathering of school personnel, parents, and sometimes the student to discuss special education services. In CASTLEBERRY ISD, the ARD committee is responsible for determining whether a child qualifies for special education under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and developing an IEP if eligible.

The CASTLEBERRY ISD IEP (Individualized Education Program) is a legally binding document that outlines your child's educational goals, the services they'll receive, and how progress will be measured. For families in CASTLEBERRY ISD, this document becomes the foundation for all special education decisions.

If your child qualifies for special education, the ARD committee will develop CASTLEBERRY ISD IEP goals. These goals should:
