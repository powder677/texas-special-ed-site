What can I do right now if Friendswood ISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Friendswood ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Friendswood ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the special education system can feel overwhelming, but remember that you are not alone. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas law, special education provides tailored instruction and related services to eligible children with disabilities. These services are designed to meet your child's unique needs and help them succeed in school. Friendswood ISD is committed to providing a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) to all students with disabilities within its boundaries.

Friendswood ISD operates under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA) and receives support from the TEA Region 4 Education Service Center. These entities provide guidance and resources to ensure that Friendswood ISD complies with state and federal regulations regarding special education. Region 4, located in Houston, offers training and technical assistance to district staff and parents. This ensures your child receives the support they deserve within Friendswood ISD.

Throughout this process, remember that you, as a parent, have enforceable legal rights. These rights include the right to participate in all decisions regarding your child's education, the right to access your child's educational records, and the right to challenge decisions made by Friendswood ISD if you believe they are not in your child's best interest. If something feels off or wrong, trust your instincts. Your voice is powerful and essential. To learn more about your rights as a parent in Friendswood ISD, you can visit the TEA website or contact Disability Rights Texas.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Friendswood ISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability that requires special education services, the first step is to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) from Friendswood ISD. It is crucial to understand that this request must be submitted in writing. A verbal request does not initiate the timeline for evaluation under IDEA and Texas law. The written request should clearly state your concerns and the reasons why you believe your child may need special education services.

Once Friendswood ISD receives your written consent to evaluate, the district has 45 school days to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). This evaluation will assess your child's strengths and weaknesses in various areas, such as academic performance, cognitive abilities, and social-emotional functioning. It is also important to know that "Child Find" laws require Friendswood ISD to proactively identify and evaluate students who may need special education services, regardless of whether a parent makes a formal request. However, initiating the process yourself ensures that your concerns are addressed directly. If you haven't already, write a letter today requesting a Full Individual Evaluation for your child at Friendswood ISD. Keep a copy of the letter for your records and send it to the school principal via certified mail with return receipt requested to document its delivery.

ARD Meetings in Friendswood ISD: Your Rights at the Table

The ARD meeting, which stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal, is a cornerstone of the special education process. This is where decisions about your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP) are made. Friendswood ISD is required to provide you with written notice at least five calendar days before any ARD meeting. This notice will include the purpose of the meeting, the date, time, and location, and a list of individuals who will be attending.

It is vital to remember that you have the right to actively participate in the ARD meeting and express your opinions and concerns. You also have the right to request a ten-day recess of the ARD meeting at any time if you need more time to consider the proposed IEP or gather additional information. This can be particularly helpful if you feel pressured or overwhelmed during the meeting. Take your time, gather your thoughts, and advocate for your child's needs in Friendswood ISD. Importantly, you are not obligated to sign the IEP on the same day as the ARD meeting. You have the right to take the IEP home, review it carefully, and sign it later if you agree with its contents. This gives you time to fully consider the proposed plan and seek legal advice if needed.

Remember, you are an equal member of the ARD committee, and your input is essential. You know your child best, and your insights are invaluable in developing an IEP that meets their unique needs within Friendswood ISD. Dyslexia Services in Friendswood ISD

Texas law mandates that Friendswood ISD screen all students for dyslexia, typically in kindergarten and first grade. This screening is designed to identify students who may be at risk for reading difficulties. The standards for dyslexia screening, identification, and intervention are outlined in the 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook, which Friendswood ISD must follow. This handbook provides guidance on best practices for supporting students with dyslexia.

If your child is identified as having dyslexia, they are entitled to structured literacy intervention, which is a research-based approach to teaching reading that focuses on phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension. Depending on the severity of your child's dyslexia and their individual needs, they may qualify for a 504 Plan or an Individualized Education Program (IEP) to receive specialized support and accommodations within Friendswood ISD. If your child has not been screened for dyslexia, request this screening in writing from the school principal today, to ensure Friendswood ISD is following the state mandates.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Friendswood ISD

Disciplinary actions against students with disabilities require careful consideration to ensure that their rights are protected. If a special education student in Friendswood ISD is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year, this triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This review is an ARD committee meeting where the team determines whether the student's behavior was a manifestation of their disability. It is also considered a Change of Placement under IDEA and requires an ARD meeting to discuss next steps.

This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a special education student. You must act within days to protect your child's rights. If the behavior is determined to be a manifestation of their disability, the ARD committee must conduct a Functional Behavior Assessment (FBA) and implement a Behavior Intervention Plan (BIP), or review the current one. It is important to remember that a child cannot be punished for behaviors that are a direct result of their disability within Friendswood ISD. If your child has been suspended, use the Discipline Defense Kit immediately to understand your rights and protect your child's access to education.

Filing a Complaint Against Friendswood ISD

If you believe that Friendswood ISD has violated your child's rights under IDEA or other special education laws, you have several avenues for filing a complaint. Understanding these paths can empower you to advocate effectively for your child. It is important to know these rights exist, regardless of what school staff may tell you.

You have four primary options: (1) Filing a TEA State Complaint is a free process and the TEA is required to resolve it within 60 days. (2) Mediation through the TEA is also free, offering a neutral third party to help resolve disputes between you and Friendswood ISD. (3) A Due Process Hearing is a more formal legal process involving an administrative law judge, requiring legal representation is often advised. (4) You also have the right to file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR), which investigates allegations of discrimination based on disability. Choose the option that best fits your needs and circumstances, remembering that you have the right to advocate for your child's education regardless of any resistance from Friendswood ISD. To take the next step, research the specific requirements for filing each type of complaint and gather all relevant documentation to support your case.

Frequently Asked Questions About Friendswood ISD Special Education

The questions every Friendswood ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Friendswood ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Friendswood ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Friendswood ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Friendswood ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Friendswood ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Friendswood ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Friendswood ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Friendswood ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
