Dispute & Discipline Rights in Friendswood ISD

🚨 Know This Now

Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

Urgent Guide: Discipline Rights & Grievances in Friendswood ISD

If your child is facing disciplinary action in Friendswood ISD, especially suspension or a Disciplinary Alternative Education Program (DAEP) placement, act immediately. Time is of the essence. This guide provides a crash course on your rights. Don't delay! Consult with an advocate or attorney ASAP!

The 10-Day Suspension Rule

Friendswood ISD can suspend your child for up to 3 school days without a formal hearing. However, any suspension lasting longer than 3 days requires a more formalized process. Be aware that cumulative suspensions adding up to 10 days in a school year trigger additional rights, especially if your child has an IEP or 504 plan. Pay close attention to the reasons given for the suspension; inconsistency or vague accusations are red flags.

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

THIS IS CRUCIAL IF YOUR CHILD HAS AN IEP OR 504 PLAN. An MDR is required when a disciplinary action (suspension, DAEP placement, or expulsion) constitutes a "Change of Placement" (see below). The MDR team (including you!) must determine if the misconduct was:

  • Caused by or directly and substantially related to the child's disability; OR
  • The direct result of the district's failure to implement the IEP.

If either of these is true, the district CANNOT discipline your child in the same way as a non-disabled student. Instead, the IEP team must conduct a functional behavioral assessment (FBA) and implement (or revise) a behavior intervention plan (BIP) to address the behavior. Demand an MDR meeting immediately if your child with an IEP or 504 is facing significant disciplinary action. Document everything!

Change of Placement

A "Change of Placement" occurs if the suspension is for more than 10 consecutive school days OR if the student is subjected to a series of removals that constitute a pattern because they cumulate to more than 10 school days in a school year AND because of factors such as the length of each removal, the total amount of time the student is removed, and the proximity of the removals to one another. Placement in a DAEP can also be considered a Change of Placement. If a Change of Placement occurs for a student with an IEP or 504, an MDR is MANDATORY. Don't let the school tell you otherwise.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe the school has violated your child's rights or acted unfairly in disciplinary matters, you have the right to file a grievance. The first step is the Level 1 Grievance. This is typically filed with the school principal. It is vital to document everything in writing and keep copies. Include specific dates, times, names of involved personnel, and a clear explanation of why you believe the district's actions were wrong. State the specific relief you are seeking. Deadlines are tight - usually within 10-15 days of the incident. Find the Friendswood ISD grievance policy online or request it from the school. Follow the outlined procedures meticulously. If you do not receive a satisfactory response at Level 1, you can appeal to higher levels (Superintendent, School Board) following the district's policy. Each level has a strict deadline.

TEA State Complaint

If you believe Friendswood ISD has violated special education laws (IDEA), you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This is a separate process from the district grievance procedure. The complaint must be filed within one year of the alleged violation and must contain specific allegations and supporting documentation. Filing a TEA complaint can trigger an investigation by the state and may lead to corrective action by the district. Look for "Special Education Complaints" on the TEA website for detailed instructions and forms.

Due Process

Ultimately, you have the right to due process in disciplinary matters. This means you have the right to be notified of the charges against your child, the right to present evidence on your child's behalf, and the right to a fair hearing. If your child is facing expulsion or a significant disciplinary action, strongly consider seeking legal representation. Expulsion hearings are serious matters, and navigating the legal complexities without an attorney is extremely difficult. Your child's future is at stake. Do not hesitate to seek help.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
