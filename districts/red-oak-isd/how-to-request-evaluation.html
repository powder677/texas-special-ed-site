Your Fundamental Rights

Under federal law and the Texas Education Code, parents in RED OAK ISD have specific special education rights. These rights include:

  • Request a special education evaluation at any time if you suspect a disability
  • Receive written notice in Spanish about all steps of the process
  • Fully participate in all evaluation and planning meetings in RED OAK ISD
  • Access to your child's educational records
  • Informed consent before any evaluation
  • Right to an independent second opinion
  • Access to advocacy resources in RED OAK ISD

Protections Against Discrimination

RED OAK ISD must provide services without discrimination based on race, color, national origin, gender, or disability. If you believe your child has been discriminated against, you can file a formal complaint.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in RED OAK ISD

Step 1: Submit a Written Request

The first step is to contact the special education department of RED OAK ISD and request a special education evaluation in writing. While you can speak verbally with the teacher or principal, you must submit your request in writing to create an official record.

Your request should include:

  • Full name of the student and date of birth
  • Name of parent/guardian
  • Contact information (phone and email)
  • Description of academic or behavioral concerns
  • Specific request for a special education evaluation

Send this request via email, certified mail, or in person to the director of special services of RED OAK ISD. Keep a copy for your records.

Step 2: Understand the Timelines

Under Texas law, RED OAK ISD has 15 school days from the receipt of your written request to:

  • Respond in writing in Spanish
  • Explain if they will proceed with the evaluation
  • Request formal consent to evaluate

Once you provide consent, RED OAK ISD has 60 calendar days to complete the full special education evaluation.

Step 3: Provide Informed Consent

Before RED OAK ISD begins any special education evaluation, they must obtain your written consent as a parent. The form must explain:

  • What evaluations they will conduct
  • Why these evaluations are needed
  • Your rights as a parent in the process
  • Options for an independent evaluation

Read this form carefully. If you have questions, ask. Do not feel pressured to sign if you do not understand.

The Special Education Evaluation Process in RED OAK ISD

What Types of Evaluations Does RED OAK ISD Conduct?

The special education evaluation in RED OAK ISD is comprensive and multidisciplinary. This means that multiple professionals evaluate the student from different perspectives:

  • Psychoeducational evaluation: Intelligence and academic skills tests
  • Behavioral evaluation: Classroom and home observation
  • Speech and language evaluation: If necessary
  • Physical/occupational evaluation: For motor issues
  • Vision and hearing evaluation: To ensure these senses do not affect learning
  • Review of previous school records

Parent Participation During the Evaluation

As a parent in RED OAK ISD, you can:

  • Observe some evaluations (when appropriate)
  • Provide information about your child's behavior at home
  • Share medical or developmental concerns
  • Ask questions about the tests and methods
  • Request additional evaluations if you deem them necessary

The Results Meeting and Evaluation Team in RED OAK ISD

What Happens After the Evaluation?

After completing all tests, RED OAK ISD will schedule a meeting with a multidisciplinary team. This team includes:

  • Special education teachers
  • School psychologists
  • Speech therapy staff (if applicable)
  • You as a parent
  • An administrator from RED OAK ISD

In this meeting, the team will present the results in terms you understand. If the team determines your child qualifies for special education, they will proceed to create an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Understanding the Results

It's normal to feel overwhelmed by technical terms. Ask for any results you don't understand to be explained in Spanish. Don't be afraid to ask questions in the meeting. You have the right to have it clearly explained how the results affect your child.

RED OAK ISD FIE and Other Evaluation Services

What is an FIE?

A FIE (Full Individual Evaluation) is the standard evaluation that RED OAK ISD conducts when a parent requests a special education evaluation. This is the most comprehensive evaluation available.

The RED OAK ISD FIE in Spanish ensures that all materials and explanations are provided in your language. This includes:

  • Written notifications in Spanish
  • Tests adapted to the Spanish language when available
  • Explanation of results in Spanish
  • IEP documents in Spanish

Reevaluations in RED OAK ISD

If your child already receives special education services in RED OAK ISD, they must be reevaluated every three years. You can request a more frequent reevaluation if you deem it necessary.

Frequently Asked Questions About Special Education in RED OAK ISD

Does it cost money to request an evaluation?

No. RED OAK ISD must provide special education evaluation at no cost to the family. This is required by law.

What happens if I don't agree with the results?

You have the right to an independent evaluation at public expense in RED OAK ISD. You can also request a dispute resolution meeting or a due process hearing.

Does my child have to leave their current school?

Not necessarily. Many students in RED OAK ISD receive special education services in their regular neighborhood schools through general classroom placement with support, or through special education classrooms within the same school.

Next Steps After the Evaluation in RED OAK ISD

If your child qualifies for special education, the next step is to develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). This plan will be specifically designed for your child's needs in RED OAK ISD.

If your child does not qualify, RED OAK ISD may offer a 504 plan or Response to Intervention (RTI) services. You always have the right to request a reevaluation if new concerns arise.
