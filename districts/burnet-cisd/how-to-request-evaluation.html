Parents in BURNET CISD often notice that their children face unique learning challenges. A formal evaluation is the first step to identify if your child qualifies for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) or Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.

BURNET CISD is required by state and federal law to evaluate any student who may have an educational disability. This evaluation is free for all families in BURNET CISD and requires no cost.

Signs that your child might need an evaluation

  • Difficulty reading, writing, or doing math at their grade level
  • Problems with attention, concentration, or behavior in class
  • Delays in language or communication development
  • Persistent social or emotional difficulties
  • Inconsistent performance despite effort and quality instruction

The rights of parents in BURNET CISD are protected by federal law. You have the right to be informed at every stage of the evaluation process and to actively participate in decisions about your child's services.

Fundamental rights according to Texas Education Code

  • Request an evaluation at any time during the school year
  • Receive a written response to your request within 15 calendar days
  • Participate in all meetings related to evaluation and educational services
  • Obtain a copy of the full evaluation and results
  • Bring a special education attorney or advocate to any meeting
  • Request an independent evaluation if you disagree with BURNET CISD's results
  • Receive all information in your preferred language (Spanish)

Steps to Request a Special Evaluation in BURNET CISD

Step 1: Identify BURNET CISD's special education coordinator

The first step is to contact the BURNET CISD special education office. Ask for the coordinator responsible for initial evaluation or the director of special services. This person will coordinate the entire evaluation process for your child.

If you don't know who to contact in BURNET CISD, call your child's school's main office. They will direct you to the appropriate special education staff.

Step 2: Submit your request in writing

Although you can request an evaluation verbally, it is important to do so in writing to create a record. Send a letter or email to the BURNET CISD special education coordinator that includes:

  • Your child's full name and age
  • The school they attend in BURNET CISD
  • The specific reasons you believe they need an evaluation
  • The symptoms or behaviors you have observed
  • Your contact name, phone number, and email
  • The best way to communicate with you

Keep a copy of this request for your records.

Step 3: Await BURNET CISD's initial response

BURNET CISD must respond to your request within 15 calendar days. You will receive a letter informing you whether the evaluation will proceed or if the request is denied. If denied, BURNET CISD must explain the reasons for the denial in writing.

If BURNET CISD denies the evaluation, you have the right to dispute that decision. You can request mediation or a due process hearing.

Step 4: Participate in BURNET CISD's evaluation

If BURNET CISD approves your request, the evaluation team will meet with you. This team may include a school psychologist, special education specialist, general education teacher, and other professionals as needed.

The team will explain the tests and evaluations that will be administered. You have the right to ask questions and understand what will be evaluated. The evaluation in BURNET CISD may include:

  • Cognitive and intelligence tests
  • Reading, writing, and math assessments
  • Language and speech evaluation
  • Behavioral and social-emotional evaluation
  • Review of medical and educational history
  • Classroom observations

Step 5: Attend the eligibility conference meeting

Once the evaluation is complete, BURNET CISD will schedule a meeting to discuss the results. This meeting is called an "Eligibility Conference" or "ARD Meeting" (Admissions, Review, and Dismissal).

At this meeting, the BURNET CISD team will present the evaluation results and determine if your child is eligible for special education services. You have the right to bring an advocate or special education attorney to this meeting.

Understanding the BURNET CISD Evaluation

What is a "Full Individual Evaluation" (FIE)?

A BURNET CISD FIE (Full Individual Evaluation) is a comprehensive examination of your child's skills, strengths, and needs. The FIE process in BURNET CISD is multifaceted and includes multiple assessment methods.

The BURNET CISD FIE is not just a test. It is a process that gathers information from various sources to obtain a complete picture of the student.

Duration of the process

The entire process from request to eligibility meeting in BURNET CISD typically takes 30 to 45 days. BURNET CISD must complete the evaluation before the conference.

After the Evaluation: Your Options

If your child qualifies for special education in BURNET CISD

If the BURNET CISD team determines that your child has a disability and needs special education, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be developed. This plan details:

  • Your child's specific needs
  • Educational goals for the school year
  • Special services they will receive (speech therapy, occupational therapy, etc.)
  • How progress will be measured
  • The least restrictive environment where they will receive education

The IEP is reviewed annually in BURNET CISD. You have the right to request changes at any time if you feel the plan is not meeting your child's needs.

If your child does not qualify for special education in BURNET CISD

If BURNET CISD determines that your child does not qualify for special education under IDEA, they may still have rights under Section 504. This law protects students with disabilities that affect major life activities.

You also have the right to request an independent evaluation at BURNET CISD's expense if you disagree with the results.

Getting Help: Special Education Advocates and Attorneys

If you feel that your child's rights are not being respected by BURNET CISD, or if you have questions about the process, you can seek professional help.

Advocacy services available for families in BURNET CISD

  • Special education advocates: Professionals who help parents understand the system, often at no cost
  • Special education attorneys near BURNET: Legal professionals specializing in educational rights
  • Free mediation from BURNET CISD: A neutral process to resolve disputes
  • Due process hearings: A formal process if you cannot resolve differences with BURNET CISD

Organizations like Texas Project FIRST and The Arc of Texas offer free resources for parents in BURNET CISD and the surrounding area.

Important Tips for Parents in BURNET CISD

How to ensure success during the evaluation process in BURNET CISD

  1. Document everything: Keep a record of dates, conversations, and concerns about your child. This documentation will be valuable if BURNET CISD denies the evaluation.
  2. Gather medical information: If your child has seen doctors, psychologists, or other professionals outside BURNET CISD, obtain copies of those reports to share.
  3. Prepare for meetings: Before any meeting in BURNET CISD, write down your main questions and concerns.
  4. Bring support: Attend BURNET CISD meetings with a spouse, advocate, or supportive friend who can help you understand the information.
  5. Request clear explanations: If BURNET CISD uses terminology you don't understand, ask for clarification. You have the right to fully comprehend.
  6. Check deadlines: Make sure BURNET CISD is following legal deadlines. If not, make it known in writing.

Frequently Asked Questions about Evaluation in BURNET CISD

Can I request an evaluation if my child is very young?

Yes. Preschool students in BURNET CISD can be evaluated if there are concerns about their development. BURNET CISD can also refer children under school age to Early Intervention Services.

What if my child is being evaluated by another district?

If your family moves to BURNET CISD, request that all evaluation records from your previous district be transferred. BURNET CISD may use those results as part of their evaluation.

Can I withdraw the evaluation request in BURNET CISD?

Yes, you can withdraw your evaluation request in BURNET CISD at any time. However, it is important to carefully consider this decision, as evaluation information could be important for your child's future.
