Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

The 10-Day Suspension Rule

Melissa ISD can suspend your child for up to 10 school days for disciplinary reasons. BUT, before the 10th day, you need to be absolutely sure the school has followed all proper procedures. Has due process been followed? Have they provided you with written notification? Is the suspension warranted? Document everything and keep records of every communication.

Crucially, if your child has an IEP or 504 plan and has been suspended for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year, special rules apply! This triggers potential IDEA protections and could lead to a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). Don't let the school brush this aside.

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

An MDR is a meeting to determine if your child's behavior that led to the suspension was caused by, or directly and substantially related to, their disability. If the behavior is determined to be a manifestation of their disability, the school cannot discipline them in the same way as a student without a disability!

Demand an MDR immediately if your child with an IEP or 504 plan faces a suspension that brings them over the 10-day cumulative limit. Prepare to argue that the behavior is linked to their disability. Gather any evidence you can: doctor's reports, therapist notes, examples of how their disability impacts their behavior in the classroom. This is your chance to protect your child. Don't let them treat your child's disability as an excuse to unfairly discipline them.

Change of Placement

A "Change of Placement" occurs when the school wants to remove your child from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days or a series of removals that constitute a pattern. This could involve moving them to a DAEP (Disciplinary Alternative Education Program) or even expulsion.

A Change of Placement also triggers IDEA protections and an MDR for students with IEPs. The school must consider the impact of your child's disability on their behavior before permanently removing them. Fight any proposed Change of Placement if you believe it is based on disability-related behavior.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe Melissa ISD has violated your child's rights or has unfairly disciplined them, you have the right to file a grievance. Level 1 typically involves submitting a written complaint to the school principal or administrator involved.

Be specific in your grievance. Outline the facts, cite specific policies that you believe have been violated, and state clearly what outcome you are seeking. Keep a copy of everything you submit. Demand a written response within a reasonable timeframe (check the Melissa ISD policy for specific deadlines). Don't back down! Document everything.

TEA State Complaint

If the Level 1 grievance does not resolve the issue, and you believe Melissa ISD has violated state or federal laws related to special education, you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

The TEA complaint process can be complex, so consider seeking legal advice or advocacy support. Gather all relevant documentation: IEPs, 504 plans, suspension notices, correspondence with the school, etc. Make sure your complaint clearly states the alleged violations and the impact on your child.

Due Process

"Due process" means that you have the right to be heard and challenge the school's actions. This includes the right to:

  • Receive written notice of disciplinary actions.
  • Review relevant records and documents.
  • Present evidence and witnesses.
  • Be represented by an attorney or advocate.

Never waive your right to due process! If you feel overwhelmed, contact a special education attorney or advocacy organization. They can help you navigate the complex legal landscape and ensure your child's rights are protected. This is your child's future - fight for it!

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
