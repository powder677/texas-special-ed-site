A Full Individual Evaluation is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine whether your child has a disability that qualifies them for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). In SAN ELIZARIO ISD, the SAN ELIZARIO ISD FIE evaluation process is your formal pathway to accessing specialized educational support and accommodations your child may need.

The evaluation examines multiple areas including cognitive abilities, academic performance, speech and language development, social-emotional functioning, and motor skills. For families in the SAN ELIZARIO area, this evaluation is completely free and is federally mandated when properly requested.

SAN ELIZARIO ISD must conduct an evaluation if there is reasonable suspicion that your child has a disability affecting their education. This is part of the district's obligation under what's called "child find"—the legal requirement to identify all children who may need special education services.

The SAN ELIZARIO ISD child find obligation means that the district is actively searching for students who may have disabilities, even if parents haven't formally requested an evaluation. This applies to all children ages 3-21, whether enrolled in public school or homeschooled in the SAN ELIZARIO community.

How Child Find Works

  • Teachers, counselors, and staff in SAN ELIZARIO ISD monitor student progress and recommend evaluations when concerns arise
  • The district screens all students in kindergarten and first grade
  • Parent observations and concerns trigger the evaluation process
  • SAN ELIZARIO ISD cannot delay or deny an evaluation based on lack of previous intervention attempts

If school staff has expressed concerns about your child, or if you've noticed developmental delays, behavioral issues, or academic struggles, you have every right to request an evaluation from SAN ELIZARIO ISD.

The process of requesting a SAN ELIZARIO ISD special education evaluation is straightforward, though it's important to follow the correct procedures to ensure your request is documented and honored.

Step 1: Submit Your Written Request

Send a written request for evaluation to the Special Education Department at SAN ELIZARIO ISD. While verbal requests are acknowledged, written requests create an official record and protect your rights. Address your letter to the Director of Special Education or your campus principal.

Your request should include:

  • Your child's full name, date of birth, and current grade
  • Specific areas of concern (academic, behavioral, social, developmental)
  • Examples of behaviors or struggles you've observed at home
  • Any relevant family history of disabilities or learning differences
  • Request for evaluation in English and/or Spanish if needed

Keep a copy for your records and consider sending your request via certified mail to document the delivery date—this becomes important for the 45-day timeline that SAN ELIZARIO ISD must follow.

Step 2: Understand the SAN ELIZARIO ISD 45-Day Timeline

Once SAN ELIZARIO ISD receives your written request for evaluation, the district has 45 calendar days to complete the evaluation. This SAN ELIZARIO ISD 45 day timeline is a federal requirement under IDEA and Texas Education Code §29.004.

Important timeline facts:

  • The 45 days begins when SAN ELIZARIO ISD receives your written request
  • The district must provide written notice of whether they will conduct the evaluation
  • If SAN ELIZARIO ISD refuses evaluation, they must explain their reasons in writing
  • Extensions are only allowed with written parent consent
  • You should receive evaluation results and an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting invitation before day 45

Step 3: Work with the SAN ELIZARIO ISD Evaluation Team

SAN ELIZARIO ISD will assemble a multidisciplinary team to conduct your child's evaluation. This team typically includes a special education teacher, school psychologist, classroom teacher, and other specialists as needed based on your child's area of concern.

The evaluation team will:

  • Conduct classroom observations
  • Administer standardized assessments
  • Review school records and academic history
  • Interview teachers, parents, and sometimes the student
  • Consider cultural and linguistic factors

Cooperate fully with SAN ELIZARIO ISD evaluators and provide requested information promptly. However, you also have the right to request specific evaluations or additional assessments if you believe SAN ELIZARIO ISD is not adequately addressing your concerns.

Your Rights During the SAN ELIZARIO ISD Evaluation Request Process

As a parent in SAN ELIZARIO ISD, you have specific legal protections throughout the SAN ELIZARIO ISD special education evaluation process:

Right to Prior Written Notice

SAN ELIZARIO ISD must provide you with prior written notice in your native language explaining their decision regarding your evaluation request, the basis for their decision, other options the district considered, and your procedural safeguards.

Right to an Independent Educational Evaluation

If you disagree with SAN ELIZARIO ISD's evaluation results, you have the right to request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at district expense. This is particularly important if SAN ELIZARIO ISD determines your child is not eligible for services.

Right to Interpreter Services

If your family speaks Spanish or another language, SAN ELIZARIO ISD must provide interpreters during all meetings and evaluations at no cost to you.

Right to Participate in Meetings

You must be invited to and can participate in all meetings regarding your child's SAN ELIZARIO ISD special education evaluation. You may bring an advocate, family member, or attorney to these meetings.

What Happens After the SAN ELIZARIO ISD Evaluation is Complete?

Once SAN ELIZARIO ISD completes the FIE evaluation, the district must schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committee meeting. This meeting determines whether your child qualifies for special education services under IDEA.

Eligibility Categories in SAN ELIZARIO ISD

Your child may qualify under one of these disability categories:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Deaf-Blindness
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Specific Learning Disability
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Visual Impairment

If SAN ELIZARIO ISD determines your child qualifies, the ARD committee will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) detailing specific services and accommodations.

If SAN ELIZARIO ISD Says Your Child is Not Eligible

You have the right to challenge SAN ELIZARIO ISD's eligibility determination. You can request an independent evaluation, file a due process complaint, or request mediation. Many parents in the SAN ELIZARIO area find it helpful to consult with a special ed advocate SAN ELIZARIO or special education attorney near SAN ELIZARIO when they disagree with district determinations.

Common Concerns Parents Raise About SAN ELIZARIO ISD Evaluations

Parents in the SAN ELIZARIO ISD community frequently ask important questions about the evaluation process:

Will the evaluation label my child?

An evaluation by SAN ELIZARIO ISD does not automatically result in a disability label. The evaluation simply provides information. Only if your child qualifies under IDEA and the ARD committee agrees will services be provided. Many parents find that early identification leads to better outcomes.

What if I disagree with SAN ELIZARIO ISD's evaluation results?

You can request an independent evaluation at SAN ELIZARIO ISD's expense if you believe the district's FIE evaluation is inaccurate. You can also request additional evaluations in specific areas of concern.

Can SAN ELIZARIO ISD refuse to evaluate my child?

SAN ELIZARIO ISD can only refuse evaluation if they provide a written explanation of why they believe evaluation is not necessary. This decision can be challenged through due process.
