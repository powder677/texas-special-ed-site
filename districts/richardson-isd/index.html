What can I do right now if Richardson ISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Richardson ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Richardson ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the special education system can feel overwhelming, but you're not alone. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas law, special education is designed to provide individualized instruction and related services to children with disabilities who need help to access their education. This means that if your child has a disability that affects their ability to learn, they are entitled to a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) designed to meet their unique needs. Richardson ISD, like all public school districts in Texas, is overseen by the Texas Education Agency (TEA). The TEA Region 10 Education Service Center provides additional support and resources to Richardson ISD to ensure students with disabilities receive the services they deserve.

It's crucial to remember that as a parent, you have enforceable legal rights throughout the entire special education process. From the initial evaluation to the development of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP) and beyond, you are a vital member of the team. Understanding your rights and responsibilities is the first step towards ensuring your child receives the support they need to thrive in Richardson ISD. You have the right to participate in all meetings, review your child's records, and disagree with the school district's decisions. Don't hesitate to assert your rights and advocate for your child.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Richardson ISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability that requires special education services, the first step is to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) from Richardson ISD. This evaluation is essential to determine if your child meets the criteria for special education eligibility. It's important to understand that a verbal request for an evaluation is not enough. To officially start the process, you must submit your request in WRITING to the school principal or special education director.

Once Richardson ISD receives your written consent to evaluate, the district has 45 school days to complete the FIE. This evaluation will assess your child's strengths and weaknesses in various areas, such as academic skills, cognitive abilities, communication skills, and social-emotional functioning. Keep in mind that Child Find laws require Richardson ISD to proactively identify students who may need special education services, even if you haven't specifically requested an evaluation. However, if you have concerns, don't wait - take the initiative. Write a letter to the principal or special education director requesting a special education evaluation for your child. Keep a copy of the letter for your records and send it via certified mail to ensure proof of delivery.

ARD Meetings in Richardson ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal, and ARD meetings are central to the special education process. These meetings bring together parents, educators, and other relevant professionals to make decisions about your child's special education program. Richardson ISD is required to provide you with at least five calendar days written notice before any ARD meeting. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, the date, time, and location, and who will be attending. It's your right to be fully informed and prepared for these important discussions.

During the ARD meeting, the team will review your child's evaluation results, discuss their present levels of performance, and develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). The IEP outlines specific goals and objectives, as well as the services and supports your child will receive to achieve those goals. Remember that you are an equal member of the ARD committee and your input is highly valued. You can request a 10-day recess at any time if you need more time to consider the information presented or consult with other professionals. Furthermore, you are not obligated to sign the IEP on the same day as the meeting. You have the right to take it home, review it thoroughly, and seek advice before making a decision. Never feel pressured to make a quick decision that you're not comfortable with.

Dyslexia Services in Richardson ISD

Texas law mandates that Richardson ISD screen all students for dyslexia. Early identification is crucial for providing timely intervention and support. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook sets the standards that Richardson ISD must follow when screening, identifying, and providing services for students with dyslexia. This handbook outlines the research-based practices and interventions that should be implemented to address the specific needs of students with dyslexia. If your child is identified as having dyslexia, they are entitled to structured literacy intervention designed to address the core deficits associated with dyslexia. Depending on the severity of their needs, students with dyslexia may qualify for a 504 Plan or an Individualized Education Program (IEP) to provide additional accommodations and support.

If your child has not yet been screened for dyslexia in Richardson ISD, and you have concerns about their reading skills, it's your right to request a screening in writing from the school principal or special education director. Early intervention is key for students with dyslexia, so don't hesitate to advocate for your child's needs.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Richardson ISD

Discipline can be a complex issue for students with disabilities. It's essential to understand your child's rights when it comes to suspensions and other disciplinary actions in Richardson ISD. If a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year, it triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This review is designed to determine if the student's behavior was a manifestation of their disability or a result of the school's failure to properly implement their IEP. A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative days also constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA, requiring an ARD meeting to discuss the situation and determine if the student's educational placement needs to be adjusted.

This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a special education student. You must act quickly to protect your child's rights. If your child has been suspended or is facing disciplinary action, immediately request an ARD meeting to discuss the situation and ensure that their rights are protected. Time is of the essence. Use our Discipline Defense Kit if your child has been suspended from Richardson ISD to help you navigate this challenging process.

Filing a Complaint Against Richardson ISD

If you believe that Richardson ISD is not meeting its legal obligations to your child, or if you disagree with the district's decisions regarding their special education program, you have several options for filing a complaint. The first is to file a State Complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This is a free process, and TEA is required to investigate and resolve the complaint within 60 days. Another option is to request Mediation through TEA, which is also a free service. Mediation brings you and representatives from Richardson ISD together with a trained mediator to try and reach a mutually agreeable resolution.

If mediation is unsuccessful or you prefer a more formal process, you can request a Due Process Hearing. This is a more adversarial process that involves presenting evidence and arguments before an impartial hearing officer. Finally, you can also file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR) if you believe that Richardson ISD has discriminated against your child based on their disability. Remember, these rights exist regardless of what any Richardson ISD staff member may tell you. You have the right to advocate for your child and seek redress if you believe their rights have been violated. Take the first step and file that complaint today.

Frequently Asked Questions About Richardson ISD Special Education

The questions every Richardson ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Richardson ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Richardson ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Richardson ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Richardson ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Richardson ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Richardson ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Richardson ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Richardson ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
