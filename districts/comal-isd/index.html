What can I do right now if Comal ISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Comal ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Comal ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the world of special education can feel overwhelming, but you are not alone. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), both federal and Texas law guarantee specific rights and protections for students with disabilities. Special education encompasses specially designed instruction and related services tailored to meet the unique needs of your child. These services are provided at no cost to you and are designed to help your child succeed in school.

Comal ISD, like all school districts in Texas, operates under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA). The TEA provides guidance and resources to ensure that districts like Comal ISD comply with special education laws and regulations. Furthermore, Comal ISD benefits from the support of TEA Region 20 Education Service Center, which offers professional development, technical assistance, and other services to educators in the region, ultimately benefiting your child. Understanding this framework can empower you to be a more effective advocate for your child's needs within Comal ISD.

It's crucial to remember that you, as a parent, have legally enforceable rights throughout the entire special education process. These rights include the right to participate in all decisions regarding your child's education, the right to access your child's educational records, and the right to challenge decisions made by the school district if you disagree. These rights are your foundation for advocating for your child. Remember, knowledge is power. Start educating yourself about these rights today. You can begin by reviewing the TEA's Parent's Guide to Special Education, available on the TEA website.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Comal ISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability that requires special education services, the first step is to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) from Comal ISD. It is critically important to understand that this request MUST be made in writing. A verbal request, while perhaps initiating a conversation, does not officially begin the timeline for evaluation under the law.

Once Comal ISD receives your written request and obtains your written consent to evaluate, the district has 45 school days to complete the FIE. This evaluation will assess your child's strengths and needs in various areas, such as academic performance, communication skills, and social-emotional development. Remember that Comal ISD also has a legal obligation under Child Find laws to proactively identify students within their boundaries who may be in need of special education services. If you believe your child needs an evaluation, don't wait. Draft that letter today!

Take action now: Draft a letter to the Comal ISD special education director requesting a Full Individual Evaluation for your child. Be sure to include your child's name, date of birth, school, grade level, and a brief explanation of your concerns. Keep a copy of the letter for your records and send the original via certified mail with return receipt requested. This provides proof that the district received your request and establishes the start date for the 45-day timeline.

ARD Meetings in Comal ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal committee. This committee is responsible for developing, reviewing, and revising your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP). ARD meetings are where critical decisions about your child's special education services are made, so it's vital to be prepared and informed. Comal ISD must provide you with at least five calendar days written notice before any ARD meeting, allowing you time to review the agenda and prepare your questions and concerns.

During the ARD meeting, you are an equal member of the team. You have the right to share your perspectives, ask questions, and advocate for your child's needs. Remember that you can request a 10-day recess of the ARD meeting at any time if you feel overwhelmed or need more time to consider the proposed IEP. This is your right and is designed to ensure you have sufficient time to make informed decisions. It can be invaluable to use this recess to consult with an advocate or attorney.

Finally, you are not required to sign the IEP on the same day as the ARD meeting. Take the IEP home, review it carefully, and consult with other professionals if needed. Signing the IEP signifies your agreement with the plan, so it's essential to be confident that it meets your child's needs. Your active participation and informed decision-making are essential to ensure your child receives the best possible education in Comal ISD. Dyslexia Services in Comal ISD

Texas law mandates that Comal ISD screen all students for dyslexia. This screening is designed to identify students who may be at risk for reading difficulties and ensure they receive timely intervention. If concerns arise from the screening, Comal ISD is required to conduct further evaluations to determine if a student meets the criteria for dyslexia.

The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook sets the standards that Comal ISD must follow in identifying and serving students with dyslexia. Students identified with dyslexia are entitled to structured literacy intervention, a research-based approach that focuses on explicit and systematic instruction in phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and reading comprehension. Depending on the severity of the dyslexia and the student's individual needs, they may qualify for a 504 Plan or an IEP to provide additional support and accommodations. If your child has not been screened for dyslexia, request screening in writing today.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Comal ISD

Discipline policies in schools can be complex, particularly for students with disabilities. It's important to be aware of your child's rights when it comes to suspensions and other disciplinary actions in Comal ISD. If a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year, this triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) under IDEA. This is a very important safeguard.

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative days also constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA, requiring an ARD meeting to determine if the behavior leading to the suspension was a manifestation of the child's disability. This meeting is critical to ensure that your child's disability is considered when addressing disciplinary issues. Time is of the essence in these situations. Acting swiftly is crucial. If your child has been suspended, utilize the Discipline Defense Kit right away.

Filing a Complaint Against Comal ISD

If you disagree with decisions made by Comal ISD regarding your child's special education services, you have several options for resolving the issue. These options, or escalation paths, exist to protect your child's rights. These rights exist no matter what Comal ISD staff may tell you. The first option is to file a State Complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This is a free process, and the TEA is required to investigate and resolve the complaint within 60 days. A second option is to request Mediation through the TEA. This is also free and involves a neutral third party who helps you and the district reach a mutually agreeable resolution.

If mediation is unsuccessful or you prefer a more formal process, you can request a Due Process Hearing. This is a more adversarial process that involves presenting evidence and arguments to a hearing officer, who will make a binding decision. Finally, you can file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR) if you believe that Comal ISD has discriminated against your child based on their disability. Remember, pursuing these options can feel daunting, but it is essential to assert your child's rights and ensure they receive the appropriate special education services. Consider talking with an advocate about which option is right for you.

Frequently Asked Questions About Comal ISD Special Education

The questions every Comal ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Comal ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Comal ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Comal ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Comal ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Comal ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Comal ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Comal ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Comal ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
