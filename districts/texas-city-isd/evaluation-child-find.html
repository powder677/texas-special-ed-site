How long does Texas have to complete an evaluation?
Once a written evaluation request is received, the district must complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) and hold an ARD meeting within 45 school days. This timeline is strictly enforced by the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Verbal requests do not start the clock — always submit in writing and keep a copy.

Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in Texas City ISD

Requesting a special education evaluation for your child in Texas City ISD (TCISD) can be a crucial step in ensuring they receive the support they need to succeed academically and developmentally. This guide outlines the process based on Texas state law. Remember to document everything and keep copies of all correspondence.

How to Submit a Written Request

The first, and perhaps most important, step is to submit a written request for an evaluation. This request must be in writing and addressed to the school principal, the special education director, or the school district's designated contact person for special education referrals. The request should clearly state that you are requesting a full and individual initial evaluation for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas state law. Your written request should include: * Your child's full name, date of birth, and address. * Your name and contact information (phone number and email address). * A brief description of your child's academic or behavioral difficulties that are causing you concern. Be specific and provide examples. Mention any interventions or accommodations that have already been tried. * If applicable, any relevant medical or psychological diagnoses or information that supports your request. * A clear statement requesting a full and individual initial evaluation to determine if your child is eligible for special education services. It is highly recommended to send your request via certified mail with a return receipt requested. This provides proof that the school district received your request. Keep a copy of the request and the return receipt for your records.

The 45 School Day Timeline

Upon receiving your written request, TCISD has specific timelines they must adhere to under Texas state law. Generally, the school district has 15 school days to provide you with written notice regarding their decision to evaluate or not to evaluate. If they decide to evaluate, they must obtain your written consent for the evaluation. The evaluation must then be completed, and an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committee meeting held to discuss the results within 45 *school* days of the date they received your written consent for the evaluation. Note that summer break and other school holidays do not count towards the 45 school day timeline.

What the FIE Must Cover

The Full and Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment conducted by qualified professionals to determine if your child has a disability and, if so, the nature and extent of their educational needs. The FIE *must* assess all areas related to the suspected disability. This may include, but is not limited to: * Academic achievement (reading, writing, math) * Cognitive abilities (intelligence, problem-solving) * Communication skills (speech, language) * Motor skills (gross motor, fine motor) * Social-emotional functioning * Adaptive behavior (daily living skills) The FIE must be conducted in your child's native language or other mode of communication, unless it is clearly not feasible to do so. You have the right to request that specific assessments be included in the FIE if you believe they are necessary to accurately assess your child's needs.

Child Find Obligations

Texas City ISD, like all school districts in Texas, has a "Child Find" obligation under IDEA. This means they have a legal responsibility to actively seek out and identify children with disabilities who may be in need of special education services, regardless of whether the child is enrolled in public school. If you suspect your child has a disability, you can and should request an evaluation, even if they are not showing overt signs of struggle in the classroom. The Child Find mandate applies to all children residing within the district boundaries.

What If They Refuse

If TCISD refuses to conduct an evaluation, they must provide you with written notice explaining the reasons for their refusal. You have the right to challenge this decision. You can request a due process hearing, file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA), or pursue mediation. It is highly recommended that you seek legal advice from an attorney specializing in special education law if the district refuses to evaluate and you disagree with their decision. You also have the right to obtain an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at public expense if you disagree with the results of the district's evaluation, under certain circumstances.

After the Evaluation

Once the FIE is complete, an ARD committee meeting will be held to discuss the results and determine if your child meets the eligibility criteria for special education services. The ARD committee includes you, the parent, as well as school personnel such as teachers, administrators, and evaluation specialists. If your child is found eligible, the ARD committee will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) tailored to your child's specific needs. The IEP will outline the special education services and supports your child will receive. Remember that you are an integral part of the ARD committee, and your input is crucial in developing an appropriate IEP for your child.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Texas? +

Submit a written request directly to the school's principal or special education director. The written request starts the mandatory 45 school-day clock. Keep a dated copy for your records.

What is a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in Texas? +

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment the school must complete to determine if your child has a disability and needs special education services. It must cover all areas of suspected disability and be completed within 45 school days of your written consent.

Can a Texas school refuse to evaluate my child? +

Yes, but only if the district provides written notice (Prior Written Notice) explaining why they are refusing and what data they relied on. You have the right to challenge that refusal through mediation or a due process hearing.
