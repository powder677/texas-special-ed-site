Dispute & Discipline Rights in Texas City ISD

🚨 Know This Now

Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

Discipline Rights & Grievances in Texas City ISD: An Urgent Guide

Discipline Rights & Grievances in Texas City ISD: An Urgent Guide

This guide is for parents facing disciplinary actions against their child in Texas City ISD. Time is of the essence. Do NOT delay. These are your child's rights, and you must act quickly to protect them.

The 10-Day Suspension Rule

In Texas, a student can be suspended for up to 3 school days (short-term suspension) without a formal hearing. However, if the suspension will exceed 3 days, but is under 10 school days, the student has the right to be informed of the reason for the suspension. The student also has the right to explain their version of the events leading to the suspension.

IMPORTANT: If your child faces more than 10 cumulative days of suspension in a school year, additional safeguards kick in, especially if your child has an IEP (Individualized Education Program) or 504 plan. Pay close attention to any suspension notice and track the total number of suspension days your child has received this academic year.

What Is an MDR? (Manifestation Determination Review)

An MDR (Manifestation Determination Review) is critical if your child with an IEP or 504 plan is facing a suspension that will result in a change of placement (see below). An MDR determines whether the behavior leading to the suspension was:

  1. Caused by or directly and substantially related to the child's disability, OR
  2. The direct result of the school district's failure to implement the IEP or 504 plan.

If the behavior IS determined to be a manifestation of the disability or a failure to implement the IEP/504, your child CANNOT be suspended or expelled in the same way as a student without disabilities. The school must conduct a Functional Behavioral Assessment (FBA) if one has not been done, and implement or revise a Behavior Intervention Plan (BIP).

DEMAND an MDR meeting IMMEDIATELY if a change of placement is being considered for your child with an IEP or 504 plan. Don't wait for the school to schedule it; advocate proactively!

Change of Placement

A "Change of Placement" occurs when a student is:

  • Removed from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days.
  • Subjected to a series of removals that constitute a pattern because they total more than 10 school days in a school year; because the child's behavior is substantially similar to the child's behavior in previous incidents that resulted in the series of removals; and because of such additional factors as the length of each removal, the total amount of time the child is removed, and the proximity of the removals to one another.

Any change of placement triggers IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) protections if your child has an IEP or 504 plan. This includes the right to an MDR. Don't let the school try to circumvent this process!

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

Texas City ISD has a grievance policy. If you believe your child's rights have been violated (e.g., improper suspension, failure to implement the IEP/504, discrimination), you can file a grievance. Start at Level 1, which typically involves submitting a written complaint to the school principal.

Keep a copy of EVERYTHING. Document dates, times, names, and specifics of the incident. Be clear, concise, and focused on the facts. State the specific violation you believe occurred and the remedy you are seeking (e.g., rescinding the suspension, revising the IEP/504).

TEA State Complaint

If the Level 1 grievance doesn't resolve the issue, or if you believe the school district is systemically violating your child's rights, you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). The TEA investigates complaints of noncompliance with special education laws (IDEA) and other state and federal regulations.

TEA complaints have strict deadlines, so act quickly. Gather all relevant documentation: IEP, 504 plan, suspension notices, grievance filings, and any correspondence with the school district.

Due Process

Due Process is your ultimate weapon in protecting your child's rights under IDEA. If you disagree with the school district's decisions regarding your child's IEP, placement, or disciplinary actions, you have the right to request a due process hearing. This is a formal legal process where an independent hearing officer will review the evidence and make a binding decision.

Due process hearings are complex and often require legal representation. Consult with an attorney specializing in special education law as soon as possible if you are considering this option. The ARC of Texas and Disability Rights Texas are potential resources.

IMPORTANT: This is a crisis situation. Advocate fiercely for your child. Don't be afraid to ask questions, demand documentation, and seek legal help if necessary. Your child's future is at stake.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
