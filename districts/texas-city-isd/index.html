Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Texas City ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. Special Education in Texas City ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

If you're a parent navigating the special education system in Texas City ISD, it's important to understand your rights and how the process works. Special education, as defined by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas law, is designed to provide tailored instruction and support services to children with disabilities who need it to access the general education curriculum. This ensures your child receives a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) that meets their unique needs. In short, special education is NOT a place, but a system of support and services that come to your child wherever they are educated.

Texas City ISD, like all school districts in Texas, is overseen by the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA ensures compliance with state and federal laws regarding special education. Additionally, Texas City ISD receives support from TEA Region 4 Education Service Center, which provides training, resources, and technical assistance to help the district effectively serve students with disabilities. It's important to understand that this is a multi-layered oversight, and the goal is always to ensure your child receives the best possible educational experience.

Crucially, remember that as a parent, you have enforceable legal rights throughout the entire special education process in Texas City ISD. From the initial evaluation to the development and implementation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP), you have the right to be actively involved, informed, and heard. You are your child's strongest advocate, and understanding your rights will empower you to effectively navigate the system and ensure your child's needs are met. Don't let anyone in Texas City ISD tell you otherwise; your rights are protected by law.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Texas City ISD

The first step in accessing special education services for your child in Texas City ISD is requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). It's important to remember that this request must be in WRITING. A verbal request does not start the legally mandated timeline. Once Texas City ISD receives your written consent to evaluate, they have 45 school days to complete the FIE. This evaluation will help determine if your child has a disability and, if so, what types of services and supports they need.

It's also important to know about Child Find, which is a legal requirement that mandates Texas City ISD to proactively identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities within their jurisdiction who may be in need of special education and related services. This means Texas City ISD has a responsibility to look for students who may need help, even if you haven't requested an evaluation. However, if you suspect your child needs special education services and they haven't been identified, it's always best to be proactive and request an evaluation yourself. Write a letter to the school principal and special education director today, stating that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for your child due to concerns about [mention specific concerns, e.g., reading difficulties, behavioral issues]. Keep a copy of the letter for your records, and send it via certified mail with return receipt requested. This will provide proof that Texas City ISD received your request and started the clock on the 45-day timeline.

ARD Meetings in Texas City ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal. An ARD meeting is where important decisions about your child's special education program are made. These meetings are crucial for developing, reviewing, and revising your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP). Texas City ISD is required to provide you with at least five calendar days written notice before any ARD meeting. This gives you time to prepare and gather any information you need to effectively participate.

You have several important rights during ARD meetings in Texas City ISD. You can request a 10-day recess at any time during the meeting if you need more time to consider information or consult with outside professionals. Don't feel pressured to make decisions on the spot. You also do not have to sign the IEP the same day as the meeting. You can take it home to review it thoroughly and seek advice. Remember, the ARD committee must consider your input and perspectives. You are an equal member of the team, and your voice matters.

The ARD committee is obligated to create a plan tailored to your child's needs. If you disagree with the proposed IEP, you have the right to file a formal complaint. You also have the right to bring anyone you wish to the ARD meetings, including an advocate. Before your next ARD meeting in Texas City ISD, download our free ARD preparation checklist. This checklist will help you organize your thoughts, gather relevant information, and ensure you are prepared to advocate effectively for your child.

Dyslexia Services in Texas City ISD

Texas law mandates that Texas City ISD screen every student for dyslexia. Early identification is critical for providing timely and effective intervention. This screening is intended to catch potential reading difficulties early on, giving your child the best chance for success. If the screening indicates your child may be at risk for dyslexia, further evaluation is required.

The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook sets the standards that Texas City ISD must follow in identifying and supporting students with dyslexia. This handbook provides detailed guidance on screening, evaluation, and intervention. If your child is identified as having dyslexia, they are entitled to structured literacy intervention. This intervention focuses on explicit and systematic instruction in phonological awareness, phonics, reading fluency, vocabulary, and reading comprehension. Students with dyslexia may also qualify for a 504 Plan or an IEP, depending on the severity of their needs and the impact of dyslexia on their educational performance. If your child has not been screened for dyslexia, request a screening in writing from the school principal and special education director today. Don't assume they will do it; be proactive.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Texas City ISD

Discipline can be a particularly challenging area for special education students. If your child with an IEP is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year in Texas City ISD, it triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This is a crucial process to determine if the behavior leading to the suspension was caused by or directly related to your child's disability. Suspending a special education student for more than 10 days also constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA, requiring an ARD meeting to determine if the suspension is appropriate.

This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a special education student. The timelines are very short, and you must act within days to protect your child's rights. The ARD meeting must be convened quickly to conduct the MDR. If the ARD committee determines that the behavior was a manifestation of your child's disability, the school cannot suspend them in the same way as a general education student. If your child has been suspended, use the Discipline Defense Kit immediately. This kit will help you understand your rights, gather evidence, and prepare for the ARD meeting.

Filing a Complaint Against Texas City ISD

If you believe Texas City ISD is violating your child's special education rights, you have several options for escalating your concerns. It's important to know that these options exist regardless of what Texas City ISD staff tell you. One option is to file a TEA State Complaint, which is a free process and must be resolved within 60 days. Another free option is Mediation through TEA, where a neutral third party helps you and the district reach an agreement. You can request either of these directly through the TEA website.

You also have the option of requesting a Due Process Hearing, which is a more formal legal proceeding where an impartial hearing officer makes a decision. Finally, you can file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR) if you believe Texas City ISD has discriminated against your child based on their disability. Don't hesitate to explore these options if you feel your child's needs are not being met. File a TEA State Complaint against Texas City ISD today if you believe they have violated your child's rights. You can find the complaint form on the TEA website.

Frequently Asked Questions About Texas City ISD Special Education

The questions every Texas City ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Texas City ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Texas City ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Texas City ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Texas City ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Texas City ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Texas City ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Texas City ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Texas City ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
