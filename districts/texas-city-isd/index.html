About Special Education in Texas City ISD

Texas City ISD serves the Texas area, providing a comprehensive special education program for eligible students. The district operates under the oversight of TEA Region unknown, ensuring adherence to state and federal regulations regarding students with disabilities.

Texas City ISD is committed to identifying and serving students with disabilities through a comprehensive evaluation process. Following a referral for special education services, the district has 45 school days to complete a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). If a student is found eligible for special education services, an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committee meeting will be scheduled. Parents will receive at least five (5) school days' notice prior to the ARD meeting, allowing ample time to prepare and participate.

Common Questions from Texas City ISD Families

My child is struggling in class. How do I request an evaluation for special education services?

The first step is to contact your child's teacher or the school principal to discuss your concerns. You can then submit a written request for a special education evaluation to the school. The school is then required to begin the evaluation process.

What if I disagree with the ARD committee's decisions?

You have several options if you disagree with the ARD committee’s decisions. You can request another ARD meeting to further discuss your concerns. You also have the right to pursue mediation or file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

How can I be an active participant in my child's ARD meeting?

Come prepared with a list of your child's strengths and weaknesses, as well as any concerns you have about their educational progress. Don't hesitate to ask questions and share your insights about your child's needs. Remember you are a vital member of the ARD committee.

Practical Tip for Parents

Texas City ISD, with approximately 10,000 students, is considered a smaller district. In smaller districts, the special education director often manages many responsibilities. For important requests or concerns, communicating via email to the special education director is highly recommended. This creates a written record of your communication, ensuring accountability and providing a clear paper trail for future reference.
