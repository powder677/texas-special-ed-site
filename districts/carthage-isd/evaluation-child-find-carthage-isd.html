CARTHAGE ISD is legally required to identify and evaluate all children who may have disabilities affecting their education. The CARTHAGE ISD child find process begins the moment you or a school professional suspects your child may need special education services.

In CARTHAGE ISD, the child find process applies to all students enrolled in the district, from preschool through high school. CARTHAGE ISD special education evaluation may be appropriate if your child exhibits concerns in several areas. Understanding what qualifies can help you determine if requesting an FIE is the right step for your family.

Academic Performance Concerns

  • Chronic health conditions impacting school attendance or performance
  • Concerns related to physical development or motor skills
  • Recent trauma, loss, or significant life changes

The process for requesting a Full Individual Evaluation in CARTHAGE ISD follows specific procedures outlined in the Texas Education Code. Here's exactly what you need to do.

Step 1: Submit Your Written Request

Put your request in writing and submit it to your child's campus principal or the CARTHAGE ISD Special Education department. A verbal request won't start the official timeline, so written documentation is critical. You can request the evaluation through:

  • A formal letter addressed to the campus principal
  • An email to your child's school
  • CARTHAGE ISD's official evaluation request form (available from the Special Education office)

Keep a copy of your request and note the date you submitted it. This documentation proves when CARTHAGE ISD received your request and starts the legal 45-day timeline.

Step 2: Participate in the Pre-Evaluation Conference

Within 10 school days of your written request, CARTHAGE ISD must schedule a pre-evaluation conference with you. This meeting allows the school to discuss your concerns and explain the evaluation process. During this conference, the CARTHAGE ISD team will:

  • Review your child's current academic and behavioral information
  • Discuss your observations and concerns as a parent
  • Explain what a Full Individual Evaluation involves
  • Provide you with prior written notice describing the proposed evaluation

You have the right to bring support to this meeting. Many parents in CARTHAGE ISD bring a trusted family member, friend, or special education advocate to help them navigate the discussion and ask important questions.

Step 3: Give Formal Consent

CARTHAGE ISD must obtain your written consent before beginning the evaluation. You'll receive a form explaining the evaluation procedures, areas being assessed, and your rights. Review this carefully and ask questions if anything is unclear.

Important: Your consent is required for each evaluation. If you disagree with parts of the proposed evaluation, you can specify what you're consenting to and note your concerns in writing.

Understanding the CARTHAGE ISD 45-Day Timeline

Once you submit a written request for special education evaluation, the CARTHAGE ISD 45-day timeline begins. This is a critical deadline established by Texas law and the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA).

What the 45-Day Window Means

CARTHAGE ISD must complete the entire evaluation process—administering tests, gathering information, analyzing results, and documenting findings—within 45 school days from the date of your written request. This timeline ensures families don't wait months to learn whether their child qualifies for special education services.

The CARTHAGE ISD 45-day timeline includes:

  1. Pre-evaluation conference (within 10 school days)
  2. Obtaining parental consent (same meeting or shortly after)
  3. Conducting all assessment activities
  4. Completing the Evaluation Report
  5. Scheduling the Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting

What Happens if CARTHAGE ISD Misses the Deadline

If CARTHAGE ISD fails to complete the evaluation within 45 school days, the district is in violation of special education law. This can be documented and reported to the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Missing the deadline doesn't automatically mean your child qualifies for services, but it demonstrates the district's failure to follow procedures.

Parents in CARTHAGE ISD should mark the 45-day deadline on their calendar and follow up if they haven't heard from the school. Document any delays in writing.

What CARTHAGE ISD Special Education Evaluation Includes

A comprehensive CARTHAGE ISD FIE evaluation assesses multiple areas of your child's functioning. The evaluation team uses various tools and methods to gather complete information.

Assessment Areas

  • Cognitive abilities: Intelligence, reasoning, and thinking skills
  • Academic performance: Reading, mathematics, and written expression
  • Language: Speech, language development, and communication
  • Social-emotional functioning: Behavior, emotional regulation, and social skills
  • Motor skills: Gross and fine motor development
  • Adaptive functioning: Self-care, independence, and life skills
  • Classroom behavior and performance: Teacher observations and records

Evaluation Methods Used by CARTHAGE ISD

CARTHAGE ISD special education evaluation combines multiple assessment methods to ensure accuracy and fairness:

  • Standardized tests administered by qualified professionals
  • Classroom observations by special education staff
  • Review of school records and previous evaluations
  • Parent interviews and input
  • Rating scales and behavior checklists
  • Work samples and portfolio review
  • Medical or developmental history (when relevant)

CARTHAGE ISD is required to use multiple sources of information rather than relying on a single test score. This comprehensive approach provides a complete picture of your child's strengths and needs.

Your Rights During CARTHAGE ISD Special Education Evaluation

As a parent requesting a CARTHAGE ISD FIE evaluation, you have important legal rights that protect your child's interests.

Key Parent Rights in the Evaluation Process

  • Right to participate: You must be included in all pre-evaluation conferences and planning meetings
  • Right to prior written notice: CARTHAGE ISD must inform you in writing of all proposed evaluations before they begin
  • Right to review records: You can see all evaluation materials, test results, and reports before the ARD meeting
  • Right to independent evaluation: If you disagree with CARTHAGE ISD's findings, you can request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at district expense
  • Right to interpretation: CARTHAGE ISD must communicate with you in your native language
  • Right to bring support: You can bring an advocate, attorney, or trusted person to all meetings

Many parents in CARTHAGE ISD find it helpful to bring an advocate or special education attorney to evaluation meetings. These professionals understand the process and can ensure your child's rights are protected and your voice is heard.

After CARTHAGE ISD Completes the Evaluation

Once CARTHAGE ISD finishes the Full Individual Evaluation, the process moves toward determining whether your child qualifies for special education services.

The Evaluation Report

CARTHAGE ISD must provide you with a written Evaluation Report explaining the findings. This document describes:

  • All evaluation results and test scores
  • Information considered during the evaluation
  • Whether your child has a disability under IDEA
  • Whether your child is eligible for special education services
  • Recommendations for next steps

Take time to read the entire report carefully. If you don't understand any part, ask for clarification. Some parents in CARTHAGE ISD choose to have an advocate review the report before the ARD meeting.

The ARD Meeting

CARTHAGE ISD will schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss the evaluation results. This meeting includes you, school personnel, and specialists who conducted the evaluation. At the ARD meeting, the team will:

  • Review all evaluation findings
  • Determine if your child qualifies for special education
  • If eligible, develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP)
  • Discuss placement and services

This is a critical meeting for parents in CARTHAGE ISD. Come prepared with questions, document the discussion, and remember that you have equal voice in decisions about your child's services.
