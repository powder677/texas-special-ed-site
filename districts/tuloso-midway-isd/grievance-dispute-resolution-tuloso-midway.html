Filing a TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD Special Education Complaint

When informal resolution fails, you can file a formal complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). A TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD special education complaint triggers investigation by the state into whether the district violated special education law.

What Issues Qualify for Complaint Filing

You can file a TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD TEA complaint if the district violated IDEA, Section 504, or Texas Education Code provisions. Common complaint issues include:

  • Failure to provide services in the IEP
  • Inappropriate disciplinary actions against your child
  • Denial of evaluation or special education eligibility
  • Inadequate compensatory education services
  • Failure to hold required IEP meetings
  • Placement in excessively restrictive environments
  • Denial of parent participation in decision-making

The violation must have occurred within two years of filing. This two-year window is important—don't wait too long to file your TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD special education complaint .

How to File Your Complaint

Contact the Texas Education Agency Special Education Complaint Resolution Team. You can file online through the TEA website or submit a written complaint by mail. Include your child's name, TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD campus, specific facts supporting each allegation, and relief you're seeking.

Your complaint must clearly explain what TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD did (or failed to do) that violated the law. Vague accusations won't suffice—provide dates, names of staff involved, and concrete examples of how your child was harmed.

TEA will acknowledge your TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD special education complaint within 5 days and conduct an investigation. The process typically takes 60 days. You'll receive a written determination explaining whether TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD violated the law and what corrective actions must occur.
