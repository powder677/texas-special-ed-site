In PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD, anyone interested in a child's educational well-being can initiate the evaluation process. This includes parents, teachers, school counselors, pediatricians, and other health professionals.

If you believe your child has a disability that affects their ability to learn, you have the right to request a special education evaluation in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD at no cost. The district cannot charge you for this process.

Signs Your Child Might Need Evaluation

  • Persistent difficulties in reading, writing, or math
  • Recurring behavioral or emotional problems
  • Difficulties following instructions or processing information
  • Speech or language development delay
  • Hearing or vision problems that affect learning
  • Attention or concentration difficulties
  • Social withdrawal or difficulties interacting with peers

Federal and state special education law in Texas protects your rights as parents. PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD must comply with the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas laws.

Fundamental Parental Rights

  • Right to request evaluation: You can ask PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD to evaluate your child at any time
  • Right to free evaluation: PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD cannot charge for special education evaluations
  • Right to participate: You must be present at all special education meetings
  • Right to an interpreter: PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD must provide a Spanish interpreter if you need one
  • Right to written information in Spanish: All special education-related documents must be available in your language
  • Right to advocacy: You can bring an attorney or special education advocate to meetings
  • Right to appeal: If you disagree with PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD's decisions, you can appeal

Steps to Request Special Education Evaluation in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD

Step 1: Initial Communication with the School

Begin by contacting your child's teacher or the school principal. Explain the specific concerns you have about your child's learning or development. Document this conversation in writing via email or a dated letter.

Many schools in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD offer early interventions or support programs before a formal evaluation. Ask about these options.

Step 2: Formal Request for Evaluation

To initiate the formal process in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD, you must submit a written request for evaluation. You can do this in several ways:

  • Formal letter addressed to the school principal
  • Email to the special education department of PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD
  • Official district evaluation request form (available at the school or online)
  • In-person submission at the special education office

The request must be clear and specific. Include your child's full name, grade, school, and the specific reasons you are requesting the evaluation. Mention specific areas of concern: academic, behavioral, motor, or social.

Step 3: Acceptance of the Request

PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD has 15 days after receiving your request to respond. The district must inform you in writing whether they will accept or deny the evaluation request.

If PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD denies the request, they must provide the reasons in writing. If you disagree, you have the right to request a due process hearing.

Step 4: Informed Consent

Once PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD accepts the request, they will provide you with an informed consent form in Spanish. You must fully understand what evaluations will be conducted before signing.

Ask all the questions you have. PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD staff must clearly explain the process to you, what will be evaluated, who will conduct the tests, and how the results will be used.

Step 5: Conducting Evaluations

Evaluations begin after receiving your signed consent. PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD must complete all evaluations within 60 calendar days. Evaluations may include:

  • Psychoeducational and intelligence tests
  • Speech and language evaluation
  • Academic evaluation
  • Social and emotional behavior evaluation
  • Medical or physical evaluation (if necessary)
  • Classroom observation
  • Review of previous academic records

Step 6: Eligibility Meeting (FIE - Full Initial Evaluation)

After completing the evaluations, PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD will schedule a meeting to discuss the results. This meeting is called the "Full Initial Evaluation" or FIE in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD.

At this meeting, a multidisciplinary team of professionals will present the evaluation findings. You will receive copies of all reports in Spanish. The team will determine if your child is eligible to receive special education services.

Step 7: Eligibility Decision

PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD must determine if your child has a disability under one of the categories allowed by federal law (such as autism, intellectual disability, emotional disturbance, visual, hearing, physical disability, or others).

If your child is eligible: PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) within 30 days. This document specifies the services and supports your child will receive.

If your child is not eligible: PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD must clearly explain why. You may request an independent second opinion at PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD's expense.

Understanding the Special Education Evaluation Process in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD

How Long Does the Process Take?

The total time from request to eligibility meeting in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD generally takes between 60 to 90 days. The specific deadlines are:

  • 15 days: District response to your request
  • 60 days: Complete all evaluations
  • 30 days: Develop the IEP if your child is eligible

If PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD needs more time for justified reasons, they may request an extension. You may agree or disagree with the extension.

Who Conducts the Evaluations?

Evaluations in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD are conducted by certified professionals, including:

  • School psychologists
  • Special education specialists
  • Speech and language pathologists
  • Resource teachers
  • School doctors or nurses
  • School social workers

Where Are Evaluations Conducted?

Evaluations are generally conducted in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD schools during school hours. However, if your child needs additional evaluation outside of school, the district may provide referrals to independent evaluators.

Important Documentation to Bring to Meetings

When you meet with the PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD team, bring any documentation that supports your concerns:

  • Medical reports or previous evaluations
  • Your child's grades and schoolwork
  • Letters from teachers or counselors
  • Notes about specific difficulties at home
  • Records of relevant medical appointments
  • Any private evaluations previously conducted
  • Examples of behaviors or academic problems you observed

Parent Rights and Education

PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD must provide all parents with a copy of the "Parent Rights and Responsibilities in Special Education" in Spanish. If you have not received it, request it formally.

Advocacy and Professional Support

For families in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD who need additional support, options include:

  • Special education advocates: Professionals who help parents understand the process
  • Special education attorneys: Available in the PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH area for complex cases
  • Local parent organizations: Support groups for families in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD
  • Texas Parent Resource Center: Free advocacy services for parents in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD and other areas

If You Disagree with PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD's Decisions

Complaint Procedure

If you believe PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD has not followed correct procedures, you can file a formal complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

Due Process

If you disagree with decisions regarding evaluation or special education services in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD, you can request a due process hearing. An independent hearing officer will hear both sides and make a decision.

Mediation

Before going to a formal hearing, PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD may offer you mediation. A neutral mediator helps both parties resolve disagreements. Mediation is free.

Frequently Asked Questions About Special Education Evaluation in PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD

Do I need to get permission from the school to have an independent private evaluation? No. You can seek private evaluations at any time. However, if PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD denies a district evaluation request, you can request that the district pay for an independent private evaluation.

What if my child primarily speaks Spanish at home? PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD must evaluate your child in their primary language. All evaluations, consents, and documents must be available in Spanish.

Can I bring someone to the evaluation meeting? Yes. You have the right to bring an attorney, advocate, family member, or friend to support you during meetings at PECOS-BARSTOW-TOYAH ISD.
