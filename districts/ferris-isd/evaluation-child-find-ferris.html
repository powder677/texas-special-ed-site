Every parent of a student in FERRIS ISD has the legal right to request a Full Individual Evaluation if they suspect their child has a disability that affects their education. This right is protected under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code § 29.004.

The FERRIS ISD child find system is designed to identify, locate, and refer students who may need special education services. Whether your child attends one of FERRIS ISD's elementary, middle, or high schools, the district is legally obligated to respond to your evaluation request in a timely manner.

You don't need to wait for the school to suggest an evaluation. If you believe your child needs an assessment, you have every right to initiate the process yourself. FERRIS ISD cannot refuse your request without providing specific reasons documented in writing.

Parents should consider requesting a FERRIS ISD special education evaluation if they notice persistent concerns about their child's academic performance, social behavior, communication, or physical abilities. Early intervention often leads to better outcomes.

Warning Signs That May Warrant an Evaluation

  • Difficulty keeping up academically compared to grade-level peers
  • Persistent behavior problems or emotional outbursts
  • Speech or language delays
  • Difficulty with attention, focus, or organization
  • Physical coordination challenges or fine/gross motor delays
  • Social interaction difficulties or isolation from peers
  • Significant gaps between ability and achievement
  • Concerns that started or worsened at a specific time

If your child in FERRIS ISD displays any of these signs, documenting specific examples with dates will strengthen your evaluation request.

Step-by-Step Process for FERRIS ISD FIE Evaluation Requests

Step 1: Submit Your Written Request

To initiate a FERRIS ISD evaluation request, you must submit a written request to your child's campus special education coordinator or to the FERRIS ISD Special Education Department. A written request ensures there's a clear record of when you made your request, which is critical for the FERRIS ISD 45 day timeline that follows.

Your written request should include:

  • Your child's full name and student ID number
  • The specific areas of concern (academic, behavioral, physical, social-emotional, etc.)
  • Examples of how these concerns affect your child's learning
  • Your contact information (phone and email)
  • The preferred method of contact

Email requests to FERRIS ISD are acceptable, but requesting written confirmation of receipt is a best practice.

Step 2: Attend the Pre-Evaluation Conference

Within 45 days of your written request, FERRIS ISD must schedule a pre-evaluation conference. This meeting brings together parents and school staff to discuss the evaluation process before testing begins.

During this conference for your FERRIS ISD child's evaluation, the school will:

  • Explain what assessments will be given and why
  • Discuss the timeline for completion
  • Answer questions about the evaluation process
  • Provide you with written notice of parental rights
  • Obtain your written consent before evaluation begins

Do not sign any consent forms until you fully understand the evaluation plan. You can request that additional specialists be included in the evaluation, and FERRIS ISD must consider your input.

Step 3: Evaluation Takes Place

Once you've given consent, FERRIS ISD's evaluation team will conduct comprehensive testing in all areas related to your child's suspected disability. The FERRIS ISD 45 day timeline includes this entire evaluation period, from your initial request through completion of the evaluation report.

Evaluations for students in FERRIS ISD may include:

  • Cognitive and intellectual assessments
  • Academic achievement testing
  • Speech and language evaluations
  • Occupational and physical therapy assessments
  • Behavioral and emotional assessments
  • Review of existing school records and medical history
  • Teacher and parent input questionnaires
  • Classroom observations

School personnel from FERRIS ISD will conduct most testing, though the district may refer your child to outside specialists for specific evaluations when necessary.

Step 4: Review the Evaluation Report

FERRIS ISD must complete the evaluation within the 45 day timeline and provide you with a detailed report. You'll receive a comprehensive written report explaining the results, what they mean, and what they recommend regarding special education eligibility.

Take time to carefully review this report. You have the right to request clarification on any findings. If you disagree with any portion of the evaluation, you can request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at FERRIS ISD's expense.

Step 5: Attend the Eligibility Determination Meeting

FERRIS ISD will schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss the evaluation results and determine if your child qualifies for special education services under one of the thirteen disability categories defined by IDEA and Texas law.

At this ARD meeting for families in FERRIS ISD, the team will decide whether your child:

  • Qualifies for special education services
  • Requires a specific disability category for services
  • Needs accommodations under Section 504
  • Does not qualify for special education at this time

You are a critical member of this team, and your input matters tremendously in determining the best path forward for your child.

Understanding the FERRIS ISD 45 Day Timeline

The 45 day timeline is one of the most important aspects of the FERRIS ISD FIE evaluation process. This deadline is mandated by Texas Education Code and ensures that families don't wait excessively long for answers.

The clock starts the moment you submit your written request to FERRIS ISD. This 45-day period includes:

  • Scheduling and conducting the pre-evaluation conference
  • Obtaining your written consent
  • Completing all assessments and testing
  • Writing the comprehensive evaluation report
  • Scheduling the ARD meeting for eligibility determination

FERRIS ISD cannot extend this timeline without your written permission. If the district needs additional time, they must request it in writing, explaining why, and you have the right to refuse the extension.

Mark the 45th day on your calendar. If FERRIS ISD hasn't completed the evaluation process by that date without your agreement, follow up immediately with the special education department.

Understanding your rights when requesting a FERRIS ISD special education evaluation empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

  • Right to written notice: FERRIS ISD must provide written notice in your native language of all procedural safeguards before evaluation.
  • Right to participate: You must be invited to all meetings regarding your child's evaluation and eligibility in FERRIS ISD.
  • Right to bring support: You may bring a spouse, advocate, or special education attorney near FERRIS to support you in meetings.

  • Right to due process: If you and FERRIS ISD disagree about evaluation or services, you can file a due process complaint.

What to Do If FERRIS ISD Denies Your Evaluation Request

While FERRIS ISD must respond to legitimate evaluation requests, the district cannot automatically deny your request. However, if FERRIS ISD refuses to evaluate your child, they must provide written documentation explaining their reasons.

If this happens:

  1. Request the written denial in writing. Call FERRIS ISD's Special Education Department and follow up with an email asking for their documented reasons.
  2. Review the reasons carefully. FERRIS ISD must have documented evidence that your child does not have a suspected disability.
  3. Request a meeting to discuss. Ask to meet with the special education director at FERRIS ISD to discuss your concerns.
  4. Consider an independent evaluation. You can obtain a private evaluation to document your child's needs.
  5. Contact a special education attorney near FERRIS or a special ed advocate FERRIS. If FERRIS ISD continues to refuse evaluation, professional advocacy becomes essential.

Parents in FERRIS ISD should never accept a refusal without understanding the specific reasons and exploring their options.

After the Evaluation: What Happens Next

Once your FERRIS ISD child's evaluation is complete and eligibility is determined, the next step depends on the results.

If your child does not qualify under IDEA: FERRIS ISD may still offer services under Section 504, or you can request a follow-up evaluation at any time if your concerns continue.

If you disagree with findings: You can request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at FERRIS ISD's expense and present those results at a future ARD meeting.
