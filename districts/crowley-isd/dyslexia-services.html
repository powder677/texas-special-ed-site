Dyslexia Services in Crowley ISD

This guide provides information about dyslexia services available to students within the Crowley Independent School District (ISD) in Texas. This information is current as of October 26, 2023, but please refer to the official Crowley ISD website and the Texas Education Agency (TEA) for the most up-to-date policies and procedures.

Texas Dyslexia Handbook 2024

Crowley ISD adheres to the guidelines outlined in the Texas Dyslexia Handbook. The 2024 update provides comprehensive information regarding dyslexia identification, evaluation, and intervention in Texas schools. It is essential for parents, educators, and students to be familiar with this handbook. Key areas covered include screening procedures, evidence-based interventions, parent rights, and the legal framework surrounding dyslexia services. The handbook is available on the TEA website. Significant updates in 2024 may pertain to enhanced screening methods and more specific guidance on implementing structured literacy programs.

Mandatory Screening

Texas law mandates that all kindergarten and first-grade students be screened for dyslexia using TEA-approved screeners. Crowley ISD follows this mandate and implements the screenings as outlined in the Texas Dyslexia Handbook. Students identified as being at risk for dyslexia will receive appropriate intervention services. Parents will be notified of the screening results and any subsequent interventions recommended. Screenings are designed to identify indicators of dyslexia early on, enabling timely support and intervention to improve reading outcomes.

Structured Literacy Programs

Crowley ISD implements Structured Literacy programs for students identified with dyslexia or at risk for dyslexia. These programs are research-based and provide explicit, systematic, and multi-sensory instruction in the foundational skills of reading, including phonological awareness, phonics, vocabulary, fluency, and reading comprehension. The specific Structured Literacy program(s) used by Crowley ISD may vary by campus, but all programs align with the principles outlined in the Texas Dyslexia Handbook. These programs aim to address the underlying difficulties associated with dyslexia and build strong reading skills.

504 vs IEP for Dyslexia

Students with dyslexia may be eligible for accommodations and services through either a Section 504 plan or an Individualized Education Program (IEP) under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). The determination of which plan is appropriate depends on the severity of the student's needs and the impact of dyslexia on their overall academic performance.

  • 504 Plan: A 504 plan provides accommodations and modifications to ensure that a student with a disability has equal access to the educational environment. A student may qualify for a 504 plan if their dyslexia substantially limits one or more major life activities, such as learning. 504 plans focus on removing barriers to learning.
  • IEP (Individualized Education Program): An IEP is a legally binding document that outlines specialized instruction and related services for students with disabilities who require more intensive support. To qualify for an IEP under IDEA, a student's dyslexia must significantly impact their academic progress and require specially designed instruction. An IEP provides individualized goals, benchmarks, and progress monitoring.

Accommodations List

Accommodations provide students with dyslexia the opportunity to demonstrate their knowledge and skills without being hindered by their reading difficulties. Common accommodations for students with dyslexia include:

  • Extended time on tests and assignments
  • Use of assistive technology (e.g., text-to-speech software, audiobooks)
  • Preferential seating
  • Reduced workload
  • Modified testing formats
  • Oral administration of tests
  • Note-taking assistance
  • Use of graphic organizers

This list is not exhaustive, and the specific accommodations provided will be determined based on the individual needs of the student.

How to Request Evaluation

If you suspect your child may have dyslexia, it is crucial to contact their teacher or the school principal to express your concerns. You can formally request a dyslexia evaluation in writing. This request should be directed to the campus principal or the Special Education Director. Crowley ISD will follow established procedures for evaluating students suspected of having dyslexia, as outlined in the Texas Dyslexia Handbook. The evaluation process may involve a variety of assessments to determine the presence and severity of dyslexia. The results of the evaluation will be shared with parents, and if dyslexia is identified, a plan for appropriate intervention and support will be developed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

Is my child entitled to dyslexia testing in Texas? +

Yes. Texas law requires all districts to screen students for dyslexia and related disorders in Kindergarten and First Grade, and at any grade level if a parent or teacher requests it.

What accommodations can a dyslexic child get in Texas? +

Accommodations may include extended time on tests, text-to-speech software, oral administration of tests, reduced-answer-choice formats, and use of a calculator. These can be provided through a 504 Plan or an IEP.

What is the Texas Dyslexia Handbook? +

The Texas Dyslexia Handbook is a state guidance document updated in 2024 that outlines districts' responsibilities for identifying, evaluating, and serving students with dyslexia using structured literacy methods.
