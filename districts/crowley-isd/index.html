Special Education in Crowley ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the world of special education can feel overwhelming, but you're not alone. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), both federal and Texas law guarantee that students with disabilities receive a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) designed to meet their unique needs. This means that your child is entitled to specialized instruction and related services, provided at no cost to you. This is a fundamental right, and Crowley ISD is responsible for upholding it.

Crowley ISD operates under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA) and receives support from TEA Region 11 Education Service Center. These agencies provide guidance and resources to help Crowley ISD implement special education programs effectively. However, ultimately, the responsibility for ensuring your child's rights are protected rests with Crowley ISD. While district personnel work to provide the best possible education to all students, misunderstandings can occur. It's important to remember that as a parent, you have legally enforceable rights throughout the entire special education process.

These rights include the right to participate in all meetings concerning your child's education, the right to access your child's educational records, the right to request evaluations, and the right to disagree with the school's decisions. This resource aims to empower you with the knowledge and tools you need to advocate effectively for your child within Crowley ISD. Don't hesitate to exercise your rights and seek support when needed. Your active participation is crucial to ensuring your child receives the appropriate education they deserve within Crowley ISD.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Crowley ISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability that is affecting their academic performance, you have the right to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) to determine if they are eligible for special education services. It's crucial to understand that this request must be made in WRITING. A verbal request, even to multiple teachers or administrators, does not trigger the legally mandated timeline for evaluation. Once Crowley ISD receives your written request, and after obtaining your written consent, they have 45 school days to complete the FIE. This evaluation will assess your child's strengths and weaknesses and help determine if they meet the eligibility criteria for special education under IDEA.

Furthermore, remember that Child Find laws require Crowley ISD to proactively identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities residing within their boundaries who are in need of special education and related services, regardless of whether they are enrolled in public school, private school, or are homeschooled. If you believe Crowley ISD should have identified your child earlier but did not, document your concerns and bring this up during the ARD process. Your written documentation is critical. Your next step should be to draft a written request for a special education evaluation. Be sure to keep a copy of the letter for your records and send it to the school principal and special education director via certified mail with return receipt requested. This provides proof that Crowley ISD received your request and starts the 45-day evaluation timeline.

ARD Meetings in Crowley ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal, and ARD meetings are the cornerstone of the special education process. These meetings bring together parents, educators, administrators, and other relevant professionals to make important decisions about your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP). Crowley ISD is required to provide you with at least five calendar days' written notice before any ARD meeting, giving you time to prepare and gather any necessary information. This notice must include the purpose of the meeting, who will be attending, and your rights as a parent.

Remember that you are an equal member of the ARD committee, and your input is invaluable. You have the right to ask questions, share your concerns, and participate fully in the decision-making process. You also have the right to request a 10-day recess of the ARD meeting at any time if you need more time to consider the proposed IEP or gather additional information. This allows you to seek outside opinions, consult with advocates, or simply process the information presented. Importantly, you are not obligated to sign the IEP on the same day as the ARD meeting. Take your time to review the document carefully and ensure that it accurately reflects your child's needs and goals. Your informed consent is required before the IEP can be implemented.

The ARD process is designed to be collaborative and focused on creating an IEP that will help your child succeed. If you ever feel that your voice is not being heard or that your concerns are not being adequately addressed, don't hesitate to speak up and advocate for your child's rights. You are the most important advocate your child has, and your participation is essential. Before your next ARD meeting in Crowley ISD, download our free ARD preparation checklist to ensure you are fully prepared and ready to advocate effectively for your child.

Dyslexia Services in Crowley ISD

Texas law mandates that Crowley ISD screen every student for dyslexia at specific grade levels. The purpose of this screening is to identify students who may be at risk for reading difficulties and provide them with appropriate interventions. The standards that Crowley ISD must follow are outlined in the 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook, which provides detailed guidance on screening procedures, identification criteria, and evidence-based interventions.

If your child is identified as having dyslexia, they are entitled to receive structured literacy intervention designed to address their specific reading needs. This intervention should be provided by trained professionals and should be aligned with the principles of structured literacy. In some cases, students with dyslexia may also qualify for a 504 Plan or an IEP, depending on the severity of their needs and the impact of dyslexia on their academic performance. A 504 Plan can provide accommodations and modifications to help your child access the general education curriculum, while an IEP can provide specialized instruction and related services tailored to their individual needs. If your child has not been screened for dyslexia, and you have concerns about their reading development, request a screening in writing from Crowley ISD immediately.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Crowley ISD

Discipline can be a challenging issue for students with disabilities, and it's crucial to understand your child's rights in this area. If a student with a disability is suspended for more than 10 cumulative school days in a school year within Crowley ISD, this triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). The purpose of the MDR is to determine whether the student's behavior was a manifestation of their disability. This suspension also constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA, requiring an ARD meeting to be convened.

This ARD meeting must take place within a specific timeframe following the suspension and must include a review of the student's IEP and the circumstances surrounding the disciplinary incident. The MDR is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a special education student. You must act quickly to protect your child's rights. If the ARD committee determines that the behavior was a manifestation of the student's disability, the school cannot suspend the student and must instead develop or revise the IEP to address the underlying behavioral issues. If your child has been suspended from Crowley ISD, use our Discipline Defense Kit immediately to understand your rights and take the necessary steps to protect your child's educational opportunities.

Filing a Complaint Against Crowley ISD

If you believe that Crowley ISD is not fulfilling its legal obligations to your child, you have several options for filing a complaint. These options provide different avenues for resolving disputes and ensuring that your child receives the appropriate special education services. It is important to understand these options and choose the one that best suits your individual circumstances. Regardless of what Crowley ISD staff may tell you, these rights are protected by law, and you have the right to pursue them.

The first option is to file a State Complaint with the TEA. This is a free process, and the TEA is required to investigate your complaint and issue a decision within 60 days. Another option is to request Mediation through the TEA, also free of charge. Mediation brings you and Crowley ISD representatives together with a neutral mediator to attempt to resolve the dispute through a collaborative process. If mediation is unsuccessful, or if you prefer, you can request a Due Process Hearing, a more formal legal proceeding where an independent hearing officer will make a binding decision. Finally, you can file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR) if you believe that Crowley ISD has discriminated against your child based on their disability. Take action today by reviewing the specific steps and timelines for each of these complaint processes and determine which path is right for you and your child.

Frequently Asked Questions About Crowley ISD Special Education

The questions every Crowley ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Crowley ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Crowley ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Crowley ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Crowley ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Crowley ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Crowley ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Crowley ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Crowley ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
