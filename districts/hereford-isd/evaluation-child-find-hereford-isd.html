A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment that examines whether your child has a disability that impacts their ability to learn. For families in HEREFORD ISD, an FIE evaluation is the formal process that determines eligibility for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.

The evaluation looks at your child's cognitive abilities, academic performance, social-emotional functioning, and physical health. This detailed assessment helps HEREFORD ISD create an appropriate Individualized Education Program (IEP) if your child qualifies for services.

Many parents in HEREFORD ISD worry that requesting an evaluation might label their child negatively. The truth is that an HEREFORD ISD special education evaluation provides answers, identifies strengths, and opens doors to support your child truly needs to succeed.

HEREFORD ISD child find procedures require the district to actively search for and identify all children with disabilities, ages 3-21, who may need special education services. This is not optional—it's a federal requirement that protects your child's right to a free appropriate public education (FAPE).

Under Texas Education Code Section 29.001, HEREFORD ISD must implement child find activities including:

  • Universal screening in pre-K through second grade
  • Review of grades and attendance patterns
  • Teacher observations and referrals
  • Parent inquiries and concerns
  • Community outreach to identify underserved populations

If your child attends school in HEREFORD ISD and shows signs of learning difficulties, the district has an obligation to evaluate. You have the right to request an evaluation at any time, and HEREFORD ISD must respond to your request within a specific timeline.

Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing

The most important step in requesting a HEREFORD ISD evaluation request is making it official and documented. While verbal requests to teachers may be noted, always submit a written request to protect your rights and create a paper trail.

Send your request to:

  • Your child's school principal
  • The Director of Special Education for HEREFORD ISD
  • Send via email with a read receipt and follow up with certified mail

Your letter should include:

  • Your child's full name, date of birth, and grade level
  • Specific concerns about learning, behavior, or development
  • Examples of difficulties you've observed at home
  • Request for a Full Individual Evaluation
  • Your contact information and preferred method of communication

Step 2: Know the 45-Day Timeline

Texas law, specifically the HEREFORD ISD 45 day timeline, governs how quickly the district must respond. Once HEREFORD ISD receives your written request for evaluation, the district has 45 calendar days to complete the FIE and hold an initial IEP meeting if your child qualifies for services.

This timeline is critical. Mark your calendar 45 days from the date you submit your request. If HEREFORD ISD doesn't complete the evaluation by this deadline, you have grounds to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

The 45-day clock includes:

  • Obtaining parental consent for evaluation
  • Conducting all assessments and tests
  • Compiling the evaluation report
  • Holding the IEP eligibility meeting

Step 3: Parental Consent

HEREFORD ISD must obtain your written consent before conducting a Full Individual Evaluation. The district will send you a Prior Notice that explains:

  • What they propose to evaluate
  • Why they believe evaluation is needed
  • What procedures will be used
  • Your right to decline the evaluation
  • Your right to request an independent evaluation if you disagree

Read this notice carefully. You have the right to ask questions and request clarification. For families in HEREFORD ISD, this is also an appropriate time to request that the district explain their child find procedures and why they identified your child for evaluation.

Step 4: The Comprehensive Evaluation Process

Once you consent, HEREFORD ISD will conduct a comprehensive HEREFORD ISD special education evaluation that typically includes:

  • Cognitive assessment: Measures reasoning, memory, and processing abilities
  • Academic achievement testing: Evaluates reading, math, and writing skills
  • Social-emotional assessment: Examines behavior and emotional functioning
  • Speech and language evaluation: If communication concerns exist
  • Occupational or physical therapy assessment: If motor skills are questioned
  • Classroom observations: How your child functions in school settings
  • Parent interviews: Your perspective on your child's development and behavior
  • Review of school records: Grades, attendance, discipline history

HEREFORD ISD will assign an evaluator who will communicate with you about scheduling tests and gathering information. Cooperate fully with this process by providing requested information and ensuring your child gets adequate sleep before testing dates.

What Happens After the HEREFORD ISD FIE Evaluation

The Evaluation Report

HEREFORD ISD must provide you with a written Comprehensive Evaluation Report within the 45-day timeline. This report summarizes findings, explains the evaluator's interpretation, and makes recommendations for services.

Review this report thoroughly. You have the right to request clarification on any findings. If you disagree with HEREFORD ISD's evaluation, you can request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at district expense.

The Eligibility Meeting

HEREFORD ISD will hold a meeting to determine if your child has a disability under IDEA and qualifies for special education. This meeting must occur within the 45-day timeline. The meeting includes:

  • Special education director or representative
  • Your child's teachers
  • The evaluator or someone familiar with evaluation results
  • You and any support person you bring

At this meeting, HEREFORD ISD will explain whether your child qualifies for special education. If eligible, they will discuss disability category (such as Specific Learning Disability, Other Health Impairment, Autism Spectrum Disorder, Emotional Disturbance, etc.) and schedule an IEP development meeting.

Your Rights as a Parent in HEREFORD ISD Special Education

Procedural Safeguards

HEREFORD ISD must provide you with Procedural Safeguards—a document explaining your rights under IDEA. These include:

  • Right to independent evaluation: If you disagree with HEREFORD ISD's evaluation, you can request an IEE at district cost
  • Right to due process: You can file a due process complaint if you believe HEREFORD ISD violated your child's rights
  • Right to prior notice: HEREFORD ISD must notify you before proposing or refusing any special education action
  • Right to participation: You must be included in all meetings regarding evaluation, placement, and services
  • Right to confidentiality: Your child's evaluation records are protected under FERPA

Bringing Support to Your Meetings

You have every right to bring a support person to meetings with HEREFORD ISD. This might be a family member, friend, or advocate. For families in HEREFORD ISD who need professional guidance, consider bringing a special ed advocate HEREFORD who understands special education law and can help ensure your child's rights are protected.

If you believe HEREFORD ISD is not evaluating appropriately or denying your child needed services, you may also consult with a special education attorney near HEREFORD who can review your case and advise you on next steps.

Common Reasons HEREFORD ISD Refers Children for FIE Evaluation

Understanding why HEREFORD ISD might recommend evaluation helps parents recognize when their child might benefit from assessment:

  • Academic struggles: Difficulty learning to read, write, or do math despite instruction
  • Attention and focus issues: Cannot stay on task, frequently distracted, impulsive behavior
  • Social difficulties: Problems making friends, understanding social cues, or managing frustration
  • Speech and language delays: Limited vocabulary, difficulty expressing ideas, or understanding instructions
  • Fine and gross motor concerns: Awkward movements, poor handwriting, or coordination problems
  • Behavioral challenges: Frequent disciplinary referrals, aggression, or defiance
  • Health conditions affecting learning: Asthma, seizures, or other chronic health issues
  • Developmental delays: Slower progress than peers in multiple areas

If HEREFORD ISD Denies Your Evaluation Request

In rare cases, HEREFORD ISD might refuse your request for evaluation. The district must provide written explanation of why they declined. However, if your child shows signs of disability, HEREFORD ISD has child find obligations.

If your request is denied, you can:

  • Request a meeting with the special education director to discuss concerns
  • File a due process complaint with the Texas Education Agency
  • Request an independent evaluation at your own cost (though HEREFORD ISD may be liable for the cost)
  • Consult with a special education attorney near HEREFORD to understand your legal options

Moving Forward After Your Child's Evaluation

If your child qualifies for special education through the HEREFORD ISD FIE evaluation, HEREFORD ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) tailored to your child's needs. This document specifies services, accommodations, and goals that will help your child access the curriculum and succeed academically.

If your child is evaluated but does not qualify for special education under IDEA, they may still qualify for accommodations under Section 504. Ask HEREFORD ISD about this option.
