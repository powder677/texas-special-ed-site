A Full Individual Evaluation is a thorough assessment designed to determine whether your child has a disability that affects their ability to learn and benefit from general education. For parents in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD, an FIE is the gateway to special education services and accommodations that can transform your child's educational experience.

The NEW BRAUNFELS ISD special education evaluation process is governed by federal law (IDEA) and Texas Education Code, ensuring that every child receives a fair, comprehensive assessment. Unlike classroom grades or teacher observations, an FIE includes standardized testing, developmental history, and input from multiple professionals.

Parents should understand that requesting an evaluation does not automatically qualify a child for services. Instead, it provides the data needed to make informed decisions about your child's educational future and what support they may need.

Parents in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD often wonder if their concerns warrant a formal evaluation request. Several key indicators suggest it may be time to take action:

Academic Struggles Despite Effort

  • Your child works hard but still falls behind grade-level expectations
  • They struggle with reading, writing, or math significantly more than peers
  • Progress reports consistently show difficulty in specific academic areas
  • Homework battles are frequent and frustrating for your family

Behavioral or Social Concerns

  • Difficulty following directions or managing emotions at school
  • Trouble making or keeping friendships
  • Frequent disciplinary referrals or behavior reports
  • Anxiety, hyperactivity, or inattention that interferes with learning

Developmental Red Flags

  • Speech or language delays compared to same-age peers
  • Fine or gross motor skill difficulties
  • Sensory sensitivities that affect school participation
  • Significant gaps in developmental milestones

If you've noticed any of these patterns, discussing your concerns with a teacher, school counselor, or the special education director at your NEW BRAUNFELS ISD campus is an appropriate first step.

Child Find: How NEW BRAUNFELS ISD Must Respond to Your Concerns

NEW BRAUNFELS ISD is required to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities through a process called "Child Find." This is a federal mandate under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), and it protects your right to request evaluation.

The NEW BRAUNFELS ISD child find obligation means that the district must respond to parent-initiated requests for evaluation, even if teachers believe evaluation isn't necessary. You do not need teacher approval or agreement to request an NEW BRAUNFELS ISD FIE evaluation.

This is crucial for parents to understand: your request for evaluation carries legal weight. The district cannot simply ignore or delay your request without cause, and any delay requires documented justification.

Step 1: Make Your Request in Writing

The most important action you can take is to request the NEW BRAUNFELS ISD special education evaluation in writing. Email is acceptable, but sending a letter via certified mail creates the strongest documentation.

Your written request should include:

  • Your child's full name, date of birth, and current grade level
  • Specific concerns about your child's learning, behavior, or development
  • Examples of struggles you've observed at home and school
  • Any previous testing, medical diagnoses, or evaluations outside the school
  • A clear statement requesting a Full Individual Evaluation

Keep copies of everything you submit. You'll need this documentation if disputes arise.

Step 2: Submit to the Right Contact

Send your evaluation request to your child's campus special education coordinator, the district special education director, or the campus principal. For NEW BRAUNFELS ISD, you can typically find contact information through the district website or your child's school office. When in doubt, submit to multiple contacts to ensure receipt.

NEW BRAUNFELS ISD families should not feel pressured to request evaluation through informal channels. Written submission ensures the district's legal obligations are properly triggered.

Step 3: Document Everything

From the moment you submit your request, keep detailed records of:

  • Dates and times of all communications with school staff
  • Names of the people you spoke with or emailed
  • What was discussed or decided in each communication
  • Copies of all written correspondence
  • Any behavior or academic documentation from home

The 45-Day Timeline: What Parents in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD Should Expect

One of the most important things parents need to know about a NEW BRAUNFELS ISD 45 day timeline is that it's a legal requirement, not a suggestion.

After receiving your written request for a NEW BRAUNFELS ISD evaluation request, the district has 45 calendar days to complete the Full Individual Evaluation and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss results. This timeline is mandated by Texas Education Code and IDEA.

The 45-day countdown begins on the day the district receives your written request, so dating and timestamping your submission matters.

What Happens During the 45 Days

Within this timeframe, NEW BRAUNFELS ISD must:

  1. Send you a notice of evaluation and request permission (typically within 5-10 days)
  2. Conduct assessments in all areas of suspected disability
  3. Review existing records and classroom performance data
  4. Gather input from parents, teachers, and other relevant professionals
  5. Compile findings into a formal evaluation report
  6. Schedule and conduct an ARD meeting to discuss results

Parents in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD should know that this timeline protects them. If the district fails to meet the 45-day deadline without justifiable cause, it may constitute a procedural violation of special education law.

Exceptions to the 45-Day Rule

The only legitimate reasons for exceeding the NEW BRAUNFELS ISD 45 day timeline include:

  • Parent request for additional time
  • Parent unavailability for required meetings
  • Exceptional circumstances documented by the district (rare)

If the district requests an extension, you can agree or insist on the original timeline. Your permission is legally required for any delay beyond 45 days.

What the Full Individual Evaluation Includes

A comprehensive NEW BRAUNFELS ISD FIE evaluation should assess your child across multiple domains:

Cognitive and Academic Assessment

These tests measure intelligence, reasoning, and academic skills in reading, writing, and mathematics. Results help identify learning disabilities and processing deficits.

Social-Emotional and Behavioral Assessment

Evaluators gather information about your child's emotional functioning, social skills, and behavioral patterns through rating scales, observations, and interviews.

Speech and Language Evaluation

If concerns exist, a speech-language pathologist assesses communication skills, language development, and any articulation or fluency issues.

Occupational or Physical Therapy Evaluation

These assessments examine motor skills, sensory processing, and self-care abilities if relevant to your child's concerns.

Review of Existing Data

The evaluation team reviews report cards, attendance records, previous test scores, medical history, and any outside evaluations you provide.

Parent and Teacher Input

Your observations and your child's teacher's observations are critical components. The special education evaluation request process ensures your voice is heard.

Your Rights During the Evaluation Process

Parents in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD have specific legal protections throughout the NEW BRAUNFELS ISD special education evaluation process:

Right to Written Notice

The district must provide written notice of the evaluation plan in your native language, explaining what will be tested and why.

Right to Informed Consent

You must give written permission before testing begins. Your consent is required—the district cannot evaluate without it.

Right to Participate

You have the right to be present during evaluations and the ARD meeting. Your input is valued and legally required.

Right to Independent Evaluation

If you disagree with NEW BRAUNFELS ISD's evaluation results, you can request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at district expense. This is a powerful tool for parents who have concerns about the findings.

Right to an Interpreter

If English is not your primary language, NEW BRAUNFELS ISD must provide interpretation during all meetings and written materials in your native language.

What Happens After the Evaluation: The ARD Meeting

The Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting is where results are discussed and decisions are made. For NEW BRAUNFELS ISD families, this meeting is crucial to your child's educational future.

At the ARD meeting, the team will determine whether your child qualifies for special education services under one of these disability categories:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Deaf-Blindness
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Specific Learning Disability
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Visual Impairment

If your child qualifies, the team develops an Individualized Education Program (IEP) detailing services, goals, and accommodations.

Common Questions Parents Ask About NEW BRAUNFELS ISD Evaluations

Can the Teacher Refuse My Evaluation Request?

No. While teachers may share their perspective, they cannot prevent you from requesting evaluation. The NEW BRAUNFELS ISD child find obligation ensures your request must be honored.

What if I Disagree with the Evaluation Results?

You can request an Independent Educational Evaluation at district expense. You can also request a re-evaluation after a minimum of one year, or sooner if circumstances warrant.

Does Evaluation Guarantee Special Education Services?

No. Evaluation must show that your child has a disability that adversely affects education and requires special services. Not all struggling students qualify for special education, though many may benefit from accommodations under Section 504 plans.

Can I Bring Someone to the ARD Meeting?

Absolutely. You can bring an advocate, attorney, family member, or trusted friend to support you during NEW BRAUNFELS ISD ARD meetings and ensure your voice is heard.
