A special education evaluation is a comprehensive process in which trained specialists in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD assess whether your child has a disability that affects their learning. This evaluation includes academic, developmental, behavioral, and medical tests conducted by certified professionals.

Before requesting an evaluation, it is important to know your NEW BRAUNFELS ISD parental rights under special education laws. These rights protect your child and allow you to be an active participant in all educational decisions.

Your Main Rights

  • Right to request evaluation: You can request an evaluation at any time if you have concerns about your child's development or academic performance.
  • Right to a comprehensive and free evaluation: NEW BRAUNFELS ISD must provide evaluation at no cost to families, within a reasonable timeframe (generally within 60 calendar days).
  • Right to participate in meetings: You must be invited to all meetings where evaluation results and any proposed services are discussed.
  • Right to communication in your language: NEW BRAUNFELS ISD must provide you with all information in Spanish, your preferred language.
  • Right to an attorney or advocate: You may bring a special education attorney or advocate to accompany you to any meeting.
  • Right to obtain records: You can request and review all of your child's educational records.
  • Right to due process: If you disagree with NEW BRAUNFELS ISD's decisions, you have the right to challenge them through a formal process.

When to Request a Special Education Evaluation

Parents in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD should consider requesting an evaluation when they notice signs that their child is falling behind academically or experiencing difficulties that affect their ability to learn.

Signs Your Child May Need an Evaluation

  • Difficulty learning to read, write, or do math at the pace of their peers
  • Frequent behavioral problems, aggression, or difficulty following instructions
  • Difficulty socializing or significant problems with friends
  • Concerns about hearing or vision
  • Delays in speech or language development
  • Consistent lack of attention or hyperactivity
  • Frequent absences or resistance to going to school
  • Underperformance that does not improve with general interventions

If you notice any of these signs in your child, it's time to explore your options for requesting an evaluation in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation

The process for requesting an evaluation in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD is relatively simple, but it is important to do so formally and with documentation. Here is the step-by-step process.

Step 1: Contact Your Child's School

Your first step is to contact your child's school. You can speak with the classroom teacher, school counselor, or principal. Clearly and thoroughly explain your specific concerns about your child's learning or behavior.

While this initial conversation is important, it is not sufficient. You must follow up with a formal written request.

Step 2: Submit a Formal Written Request

Send a letter or email to the school principal and the NEW BRAUNFELS ISD special education department, formally requesting a comprehensive special education evaluation. This written request is legal and mandatory, and it starts the 60-day clock for the evaluation to be completed.

In your request, include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current grade and school attended
  • Specific description of areas of concern (academic, behavioral, social, etc.)
  • Concrete examples of difficulties you have observed
  • A clear request for comprehensive evaluation in all relevant areas
  • Your name, relationship to the child, and contact information (phone and email)
  • The date of the request

Send this request by certified mail or deliver it in person to have proof of delivery. Keep a copy for your records.

Step 3: Participate in the Consent for Evaluation Meeting

Within a reasonable time after your request, NEW BRAUNFELS ISD will contact you to schedule a consent meeting. In this meeting, school staff will discuss:

  • The specific areas to be evaluated
  • The types of tests that will be administered
  • The timeline for completing the evaluation
  • Your rights as a parent

You must authorize in writing for NEW BRAUNFELS ISD to proceed with the evaluation. This is an important protection for you.

Step 4: The Evaluation is Conducted

During the following period (typically 30-45 days), NEW BRAUNFELS ISD specialists, which may include educational psychologists, special education teachers, speech-language pathologists, and other professionals, will evaluate your child.

Evaluations may include:

  • Cognitive and aptitude tests
  • Academic evaluations in reading, writing, and mathematics
  • Speech and language evaluations
  • Social, emotional, and behavioral evaluations
  • Motor development evaluations
  • Medical history and review of previous educational records

The NEW BRAUNFELS ISD FIE Results Meeting

After all tests are completed, NEW BRAUNFELS ISD must schedule a meeting to share the results. This meeting is called the "FIE" (Full Individual Evaluation), and it is extremely important.

What to Expect at the FIE Meeting

At the NEW BRAUNFELS ISD FIE meeting (or in your preferred language), the evaluation team will present to you:

  • A written summary of all evaluation findings
  • Test scores in language you can understand
  • A recommendation on whether your child qualifies for special education services under IDEA
  • If they qualify, an initial proposal of services in an IEP document

You have the right to a copy of all these documents and may bring someone with you to this meeting for support, including a special education attorney.

Questions to Ask at the FIE Meeting

  • What were the main findings of the evaluation?
  • Does my child qualify under IDEA in any disability category?
  • What are my child's strengths and areas of need?
  • What special education services are recommended?
  • How many hours per week will they receive special education?
  • Will they spend time in the general classroom with other students?
  • How often will their progress be reviewed?

Special Education in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD: Service Options

If your child qualifies for NEW BRAUNFELS ISD special education services, several options are available. The type and amount of services will depend on your child's specific needs.

Placement Options

  • Full-time special education classroom: Your child primarily attends special education classes with other students with disabilities.
  • Part-time special education classroom: Your child spends part of the day in special education and part in general education classes.
  • Consultation services in general education classroom: Your child remains in general education classes but receives support from a special education specialist who works with the teacher.
  • Itinerant services: A specialist regularly goes to the school to work with your child in specific areas such as speech/language or occupational therapy.
  • Separate school or resource center: In rare cases, NEW BRAUNFELS ISD may place a student in a specialized alternative setting.

Next Steps After the Evaluation

Once NEW BRAUNFELS ISD completes the evaluation and determines that your child qualifies, the real work begins with the development of the Individualized Education Program (IEP).

The IEP is a legal document that describes the specific services your child will receive, educational goals, and how progress will be measured. As a parent in NEW BRAUNFELS ISD, you are an equal member of the IEP team, and your voice is important.

Actively participate in IEP meetings, ask questions, and do not sign anything you disagree with. If at any point you feel that NEW BRAUNFELS ISD is not adequately providing services or if your concerns are not being addressed, you have the right to request an additional meeting or seek dispute resolution.
