A Full Individual Evaluation is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine whether your child has a disability and whether they qualify for special education services. In SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD, the FIE process follows federal IDEA regulations and Texas Education Code requirements to ensure fair, unbiased evaluation of every student.

The SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD special education evaluation process includes assessments in all areas of suspected disability, including cognitive ability, academic performance, social-emotional functioning, motor skills, and adaptive behavior. This thorough approach helps educators understand your child's unique strengths and challenges.

Understanding this process empowers you as a parent in SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD to advocate effectively for your child's educational needs and ensure they receive appropriate support and services.

Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing

Submit a written request for an SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD FIE evaluation to your child's school principal or the Special Education Department. While verbal requests are acknowledged, having a written record protects your legal rights. You can request the evaluation through email, a formal letter, or by using the district's official referral form.

In your request, clearly state that you are requesting a SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD special education evaluation and mention any specific concerns you have about your child's learning, behavior, or development. Include relevant background information, such as previous evaluations, medical diagnoses, or documented concerns.

Step 2: Await the District's Response

Within 15 calendar days of receiving your written request, SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD must respond to you in writing. The district will either agree to evaluate your child or explain why they believe an evaluation is not necessary. This response is critical—keep it for your records.

If SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD refuses to evaluate your child, they must provide you with procedural safeguards documentation. You have the right to request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at the district's expense if you disagree with their decision.

Step 3: Understand the 45-Day Timeline

Once SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD agrees to evaluate your child, the district has 45 calendar days to complete the SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD 45 day timeline for assessment and provide you with an evaluation report. This 45 day timeline is a critical legal deadline—mark it on your calendar.

The clock starts from the date you signed consent for evaluation. During this period, the district will conduct various assessments and gather information about your child's academic performance, social-emotional functioning, and any other areas of suspected disability.

Step 4: Participate in Pre-Evaluation Activities

Before formal testing begins, you should receive information about what the SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD child find evaluation will include. The district may ask you to complete developmental or behavioral rating scales. Provide honest, detailed information—your insights are valuable to the evaluation process.

School staff in SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD may also review your child's academic records, previous assessments, and attendance. Request copies of any materials the district is reviewing so you understand what information is being considered.

What Happens During the SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD Special Education Evaluation

Areas of Assessment

A comprehensive SOUTH SAN ANTHONY ISD special education evaluation examines multiple areas, depending on your child's suspected disability:

  • Cognitive/Intellectual Ability: Tests to measure reasoning, problem-solving, and learning capacity
  • Academic Achievement: Assessments in reading, mathematics, written expression, and other academic subjects
  • Social-Emotional Functioning: Evaluation of behavior, emotional regulation, and social skills
  • Motor Skills: Fine and gross motor development assessments
  • Adaptive Behavior: Daily living skills and independence in self-care and community participation
  • Speech and Language: Communication abilities, if applicable
  • Vision and Hearing: Screening or formal testing if needed

Who Participates in the Evaluation

The evaluation team in SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD includes certified special education staff, classroom teachers, and any specialists needed (speech therapist, psychologist, occupational therapist). As a parent, you are a valuable team member and can request to participate in or observe assessments.

The SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD Evaluation Report and Eligibility Determination

Within the SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD 45 day timeline, you will receive a detailed evaluation report. This document summarizes assessment results, the evaluation team's findings, and a determination of whether your child meets eligibility criteria for special education services under one of thirteen disability categories recognized in Texas.

Eligibility Categories in SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD

SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD evaluates students for eligibility in these categories:

  1. Autism Spectrum Disorder
  2. Deaf-Blind
  3. Emotional Disturbance
  4. Hearing Impairment
  5. Intellectual Disability
  6. Multiple Disabilities
  7. Orthopedic Impairment
  8. Other Health Impairment
  9. Specific Learning Disability
  10. Speech/Language Impairment
  11. Traumatic Brain Injury
  12. Visual Impairment
  13. Noncategorical Early Childhood

If Your Child is Found Eligible

If SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD determines your child is eligible for special education, the district will schedule an Individualized Education Program (IEP) meeting within 30 days. During this meeting, the team will develop a comprehensive IEP that specifies services, accommodations, and educational goals for your child.

Parents in SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD have the right to request specific services, dispute the evaluation findings, or request an independent evaluation at the district's expense if they disagree with the results.

If Your Child is Found Not Eligible

If SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD concludes your child is not eligible for special education but may benefit from classroom accommodations, the district may develop a 504 Plan under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act. You have the right to appeal this determination and request an independent evaluation.

Your Rights as a Parent in SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD

Federal law and Texas Education Code guarantee specific rights for parents throughout the SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD evaluation request process:

  • Right to Written Notice: Receive notice in your native language before and after evaluation
  • Right to Informed Consent: Give written permission before evaluation begins
  • Right to Participate: Be involved in all evaluation and IEP meetings
  • Right to Independent Evaluation: Obtain an outside evaluation at district expense if you disagree with SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD results
  • Right to Records Access: Review all evaluation results and educational records
  • Right to an Advocate: Bring a special education advocate or attorney to meetings
  • Right to Due Process: File a due process complaint if you disagree with decisions
