The 10-Day Suspension Rule

Bastrop ISD can suspend a student for up to 3 school days at a time. However, a cumulative suspension exceeding 10 school days in a single school year triggers significant rights, especially for students with disabilities protected under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA).

Key Point: Track the number of days your child is suspended. Once it hits 10 cumulative days, demand a meeting to discuss your child's rights, especially if you suspect a disability is playing a role in the behavior leading to the suspension.

What Is an MDR? (Manifestation Determination Review)

If your child has an IEP (Individualized Education Program) or a 504 plan and faces a suspension that will exceed 10 cumulative days, the school MUST conduct a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This is crucial.

What it is: The MDR team (including you, the parent) determines if the misconduct was a manifestation of the student's disability. In other words, did the disability cause, or have a direct and substantial relationship to, the behavior?

Your Role: Be prepared to advocate fiercely. Bring documentation, observations, and any information that supports your argument that the disability influenced the behavior. This includes doctor's reports, therapist notes, and your own detailed records.

Crucial Outcome: If the MDR team determines the behavior WAS a manifestation of the disability, the school CANNOT suspend your child beyond 10 days for that incident. They must implement or revise the IEP/504 plan to address the behavior.

Change of Placement

A "Change of Placement" occurs when your child is removed from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days OR when there is a series of removals that constitute a pattern. This is often related to disciplinary action.

Important Note: Even if the suspension *appears* to be for less than 10 days at a time, if the suspensions create a pattern of removal, it could be considered a Change of Placement. This triggers additional rights and protections under IDEA.

Protect your child: Challenge any disciplinary action that leads to a significant change in your child's educational placement. Demand documentation and understand your rights to appeal.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

Bastrop ISD has a grievance procedure. If you disagree with disciplinary action, you can file a Level 1 grievance with the principal or appropriate administrator.

Steps to take:

  1. Obtain the grievance form from the school or district website.
  2. Complete the form clearly and concisely, stating the specific disciplinary action you are grieving and the reasons for your disagreement. Include dates, names, and any supporting documentation.
  3. Submit the grievance within the timeframe specified in the Bastrop ISD policy (usually within 10 days of the incident).
  4. Keep a copy of your grievance and all related documentation.

TEA State Complaint

If you believe Bastrop ISD is violating IDEA or other special education laws, you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This is a more formal process and requires specific documentation. Contact Disability Rights Texas or a special education attorney for assistance with this process.

Due Process

You have the right to request a due process hearing if you disagree with the school's decision regarding your child's special education services, including disciplinary actions. This is a formal legal proceeding with strict timelines. Immediately seek legal counsel if you are considering requesting a due process hearing.

Remember: You are your child's best advocate. Be proactive, document everything, and don't be afraid to ask questions and demand your child's rights are protected. This is a stressful situation, but swift action is crucial.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
