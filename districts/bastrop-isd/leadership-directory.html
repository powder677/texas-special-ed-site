How to Effectively Contact Bastrop ISD Special Education Staff

Building strong communication with your child's special education team in Bastrop ISD is crucial for their success. Consider these tips:

Best Times to Contact: Many teachers and specialists are most accessible before or after school hours. Check the Bastrop ISD website or contact the campus directly for specific staff availability. Email is often a more efficient initial point of contact.

Email vs. Phone: For initial inquiries or complex issues, email allows for a clear record and allows staff time to formulate a thoughtful response. For urgent matters or quick questions, a phone call might be more appropriate. Always leave a detailed message if you reach voicemail.

What to Say in Your First Message: Be clear and concise. Include your child's name, grade, school, and a brief summary of your concern or question. For example: "Dear [Teacher's Name], I am writing to you regarding [Child's Name]'s IEP goals in reading. I would like to schedule a time to discuss progress monitoring. Thank you for your time."

Important Tip: Always follow up any phone conversations with an email summarizing what was discussed and any agreed-upon actions. This creates a written record of the conversation.

What to Do If You Can't Reach Anyone

If you're having trouble getting in touch with someone at Bastrop ISD regarding your child's special education, follow these escalation steps:

  • Step 1: Contact your child's teacher directly.
  • Step 2: If you don't receive a response within a reasonable timeframe (e.g., 2 business days), contact the campus Special Education Coordinator.
  • Step 3: If you still need assistance, contact the Bastrop ISD Director of Special Education. Contact information should be available on the district website.
  • Step 4: As a last resort, you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Be sure to document all previous attempts to resolve the issue.

Remember that Texas Education Code Chapter 29 outlines rights of students with disabilities and the responsibilities of school districts. Familiarize yourself with these provisions.

Your Right to District Records

As a parent of a student with a disability in Bastrop ISD, you have the right to access your child's educational records.

FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act): You have the right to inspect and review your child's education records within 45 days of submitting a written request. This includes the IEP, evaluations, and progress reports.

Public Information Act (Texas Government Code Chapter 552): You can request information about Bastrop ISD employees, including their contact information. This Act ensures transparency in government operations.

Submitting a Records Request: All records requests should be submitted in writing to the Bastrop ISD Special Education Department. Clearly state what specific records you are requesting. Keep a copy of your request for your records. You may need to specify if you want to inspect the records in person or receive copies.
