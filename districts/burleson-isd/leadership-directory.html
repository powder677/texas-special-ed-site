How to Effectively Contact Burleson ISD Special Education Staff

Building a strong partnership with your child's special education team in Burleson ISD is crucial. Here are some tips for effective communication:

  • Best Times to Contact: Early mornings (before classes begin) or late afternoons (after dismissal) are generally the best times to reach teachers by phone. Check your teacher's preferred contact hours, usually listed in their syllabus or on the school website.
  • Email vs. Phone: Email is often the most efficient way to initiate contact. It allows staff to respond when they have time to gather information. For urgent matters or complex discussions, a phone call might be more appropriate.
  • What to Say in Your First Message: Be clear and concise. State your child's name, grade, teacher, and the specific reason for your contact. For example: "I'm writing to you about [child's name]'s IEP goals in reading and would like to schedule a time to discuss progress monitoring."

Tip: Always follow up any phone conversations with an email summarizing the discussion and any agreed-upon actions. This creates a written record.

What to Do If You Can't Reach Anyone

If you're having trouble getting a response from Burleson ISD special education staff, here's a suggested escalation path:

  1. Teacher: Attempt to contact the teacher multiple times through different methods (email, phone).
  2. Campus Special Education Coordinator: If you still haven't received a response, contact the campus special education coordinator. Their contact information should be available on the school's website or by calling the front office.
  3. District Special Education Director: If the campus coordinator is unable to resolve your issue, contact the Burleson ISD Director of Special Education. Their information can be found on the Burleson ISD website.
  4. Texas Education Agency (TEA): As a last resort, you can file a complaint with the TEA. Be sure to exhaust all other avenues first.

Remember that Texas Education Code Chapter 29 outlines the rights and responsibilities related to special education services. Familiarize yourself with these regulations.

Your Right to District Records

As a parent of a child receiving special education services in Burleson ISD, you have the right to access your child's educational records.

  • FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act): You have the right to inspect and review your child's education records within 45 days of submitting a written request.
  • Public Information Act (Texas Government Code Chapter 552): You can request access to public information, including staff contact information and other relevant documents.

To submit a records request, send a written request to the Burleson ISD Special Education Department. The request should clearly state the specific records you are seeking. Include your child's name, date of birth, and school. Keep a copy of your request for your records.
