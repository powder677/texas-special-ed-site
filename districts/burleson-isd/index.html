Special Education in Burleson ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the special education system can feel overwhelming, but you're not alone. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas law, your child is entitled to a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) designed to meet their unique needs. This means Burleson ISD is legally obligated to identify, evaluate, and provide services for students with disabilities. Special education encompasses a wide range of supports, including specialized instruction, related services like speech therapy or occupational therapy, and accommodations designed to help your child succeed.

Burleson ISD, like all school districts in Texas, operates under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA provides guidance and monitors compliance with state and federal special education laws. Furthermore, Burleson ISD receives support from TEA Region 11 Education Service Center, which offers professional development and resources to educators in the area. However, it's crucial to remember that ultimate responsibility for your child's education lies with Burleson ISD itself.

Importantly, you, as a parent, have enforceable legal rights throughout the entire special education process. From the initial request for evaluation to the development and implementation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP), you have a voice and a right to participate meaningfully. Burleson ISD must adhere to specific procedures and timelines to ensure your child receives the services they need. Don't hesitate to advocate for your child - your involvement is critical to their success within Burleson ISD's special education programs.

Action you can take right now: Bookmark this page for future reference as you navigate your child's special education journey within Burleson ISD.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Burleson ISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability that requires special education services, the first step is to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) from Burleson ISD. It's absolutely crucial that this request be made in WRITING. A verbal request does NOT start the timeline for the evaluation. This written request should clearly state your concerns about your child's academic, behavioral, or developmental progress and why you believe they may need special education services. Be as specific as possible, citing examples of challenges your child faces.

Once Burleson ISD receives your written request and you provide consent for the evaluation, the district has a maximum of 45 school days to complete the FIE. This evaluation will assess your child's strengths and weaknesses in various areas and determine if they meet the criteria for special education eligibility under IDEA. It is important to note that Child Find laws mandate that Burleson ISD proactively identify students who may need special education services, even if parents haven't explicitly requested an evaluation. However, if you have concerns, don't wait for the district to initiate the process - your proactive request is essential. Burleson ISD should never discourage you from pursuing an evaluation for your child.

Action you can take right now: Draft a written request for a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE), keep a copy for your records, and send it to the Burleson ISD special education director via certified mail, return receipt requested, so you have proof of delivery.

ARD Meetings in Burleson ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal. An ARD meeting is a crucial gathering where you, school professionals, and other relevant individuals come together to discuss your child's special education needs and develop their Individualized Education Program (IEP). These meetings are the cornerstone of the special education process in Burleson ISD, and your active participation is vital to ensuring your child receives appropriate support. The ARD committee is responsible for determining eligibility, developing IEP goals and objectives, and deciding on the placement and services your child will receive.

Burleson ISD is required to provide you with at least five calendar days written notice before any ARD meeting. This notice should clearly state the purpose of the meeting, the individuals who will be attending, and your rights as a parent. Remember, you have the right to request a 10-day recess during any ARD meeting. This allows you time to consider the information presented, seek outside opinions, or simply process the discussions without feeling pressured to make immediate decisions. Don't hesitate to use this right if you need it. Your involvement in the ARD process ensures your child receives the best support Burleson ISD can provide.

It's also important to remember that you are not obligated to sign the IEP on the same day as the ARD meeting. Take the time you need to review the document carefully, discuss it with family members or advocates, and ensure that you fully understand and agree with the proposed plan before signing. Your signature indicates your consent to the IEP, so it's crucial to make an informed decision. Your voice matters within Burleson ISD's ARD process.

Dyslexia Services in Burleson ISD

Texas law mandates that Burleson ISD screen all students for dyslexia and related disorders. This screening process is designed to identify students who may be at risk for reading difficulties. Early identification is key to providing effective interventions and preventing long-term academic struggles. If your child is identified as having characteristics of dyslexia, Burleson ISD is responsible for providing appropriate interventions.

The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook sets the standards that Burleson ISD must follow in identifying and serving students with dyslexia. This handbook outlines the evidence-based practices and interventions that are required in Texas schools. Students identified with dyslexia are entitled to structured literacy intervention, which focuses on phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and reading comprehension. Depending on the severity of their needs, students with dyslexia may qualify for a 504 Plan, which provides accommodations to help them access the general education curriculum, or an IEP if their needs are more significant.

Action you can take right now: If your child has not been screened for dyslexia and you have concerns, request a dyslexia screening in writing from Burleson ISD without delay. Retain a copy of this written request for your records.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Burleson ISD

Disciplinary actions, especially suspensions, can have a significant impact on students with disabilities. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year within Burleson ISD triggers specific protections. This threshold triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR), which is an ARD meeting convened to determine if the student's behavior was a manifestation of their disability. This process is critically important for protecting your child's rights and ensuring that disciplinary actions are fair and appropriate.

Furthermore, a suspension exceeding 10 cumulative days constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA. This means that an ARD meeting must be held to determine if the suspension is appropriate and if any changes need to be made to the student's IEP or placement. This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a special education student. You must act quickly and decisively to protect your child's rights and ensure they receive the appropriate support and services. The district must justify the suspension and demonstrate that it aligns with the student's IEP and overall educational needs. You will only have days, not weeks, to respond to the district's action.

Filing a Complaint Against Burleson ISD

If you believe Burleson ISD is not fulfilling its legal obligations to your child, you have several options for filing a complaint. It's important to understand these escalation paths and to know that you have the right to pursue them, regardless of what Burleson ISD staff may tell you. The first option is a TEA State Complaint. This is a free process where you file a formal complaint with the Texas Education Agency, which will investigate your concerns and issue a decision within 60 days. The second option is Mediation through TEA, which is also free. A trained mediator will facilitate a discussion between you and Burleson ISD representatives to try to resolve the dispute amicably.

The third option is a Due Process Hearing, which is a more formal legal proceeding where an impartial hearing officer hears evidence and makes a decision. This option typically involves legal representation and can be more complex and costly. Finally, you can file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR), which investigates allegations of discrimination based on disability. Each of these avenues exist to protect your child. You are allowed to pursue any, all, or none of them. Your rights are not diminished by district employees suggesting otherwise.

Frequently Asked Questions About Burleson ISD Special Education

The questions every Burleson ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Burleson ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Burleson ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Burleson ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Burleson ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Burleson ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Burleson ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Burleson ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Burleson ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
