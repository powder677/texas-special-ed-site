Understanding Special Education in LOS FRESNOS CISD

LOS FRESNOS CISD special education programs are designed to provide Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) to students with disabilities ages 3-21. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, LOS FRESNOS CISD is required to identify, evaluate, and serve all eligible students regardless of the severity of their disability.

The district offers a continuum of services ranging from inclusion in general education classrooms with support to specialized placements. Every student receiving special education services in LOS FRESNOS CISD has an Individualized Education Program (IEP) that outlines their specific needs, goals, and the services they will receive.

Who Qualifies for Special Education in LOS FRESNOS CISD?

Students may qualify for LOS FRESNOS CISD special education services under 13 disability categories recognized by Texas Education Code and IDEA:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Deaf-Blindness
  • Deafness
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Specific Learning Disability (including dyslexia)
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Visual Impairment

If you believe your child may qualify, contact your campus administrator or LOS FRESNOS CISD Special Education Department to request an evaluation.

The ARD Meeting: Your Role in Your Child's Education

The Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting is the cornerstone of LOS FRESNOS CISD special education services. A LOS FRESNOS CISD ARD meeting brings together parents, teachers, administrators, and specialists to develop or review your child's educational program.

What to Expect During Your LOS FRESNOS CISD ARD Meeting

Your first LOS FRESNOS CISD ARD meeting occurs after your child has been evaluated and found eligible for services. The committee will review evaluation results and determine which disability category best describes your child's needs. This meeting typically lasts 1-2 hours and covers:

  • Summary of evaluation findings
  • Determination of eligibility
  • Identification of your child's strengths and needs
  • Annual goals and short-term objectives
  • Specific special education and related services
  • Placement and amount of time in general education
  • Accommodations and modifications

LOS FRESNOS CISD must provide you with meeting notice at least 10 school days in advance. You have the right to bring an advocate, attorney, or supportive family member to any LOS FRESNOS CISD ARD meeting.

Preparing for Your ARD Meeting in LOS FRESNOS CISD

Before your LOS FRESNOS CISD ARD meeting, gather information about your child's strengths, challenges, and interests. Write down questions you want answered and bring recent work samples or progress reports. Request all evaluation reports and assessment data at least 5 days before the meeting so you can review them thoroughly.

Families in LOS FRESNOS CISD often benefit from having written notes prepared about their child's performance at home, medical or behavioral concerns, and specific goals they want included in the IEP.

IEP Services and Educational Planning

Your child's Individualized Education Program is the legal document that outlines all LOS FRESNOS CISD IEP services they will receive. This document is developed collaboratively during your LOS FRESNOS CISD ARD meeting and reviewed annually, with the option for additional reviews when needed.

Components of Your Child's LOS FRESNOS CISD IEP

LOS FRESNOS CISD IEP services are individualized based on your child's specific needs. A comprehensive IEP includes:

  1. Present Levels of Performance: Current academic, social, behavioral, and functional abilities
  2. Annual Goals: Measurable objectives your child will work toward during the school year
  3. Special Education Services: Specialized instruction, frequency, location, and duration
  4. Related Services: Speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, physical therapy, or other support
  5. Accommodations and Modifications: Changes to curriculum, instruction, or testing to support access
  6. Assistive Technology: Devices or software needed to support learning
  7. Participation in General Education: Time spent with non-disabled peers and any supports needed
  8. Progress Monitoring: How LOS FRESNOS CISD will measure your child's progress toward goals

All families in LOS FRESNOS CISD receive progress reports on IEP goals at least as frequently as grades are issued to general education students.

Dyslexia Services in LOS FRESNOS CISD

Texas Education Code requires all school districts to provide dyslexia intervention services. LOS FRESNOS CISD dyslexia program includes screening, assessment, and intervention for students identified with dyslexia or suspected dyslexia.

Dyslexia Screening and Assessment in LOS FRESNOS CISD

LOS FRESNOS CISD screens all kindergarten and first-grade students for characteristics of dyslexia. Students in grades 2-12 are screened if parents request it or if teachers refer them. The LOS FRESNOS CISD dyslexia program uses evidence-based assessments to determine if your child needs specialized instruction.

If your child shows indicators of dyslexia, they may qualify for specialized reading intervention within LOS FRESNOS CISD either through the general education reading program or through special education services if they meet eligibility criteria for Specific Learning Disability.

Intervention and Support Through LOS FRESNOS CISD Dyslexia Services

The LOS FRESNOS CISD dyslexia program emphasizes structured literacy instruction using evidence-based methods. Interventions are intensive and targeted, addressing phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension. Progress is monitored frequently, and instruction is adjusted based on your child's response.

If your child receives dyslexia services in LOS FRESNOS CISD, you'll receive regular updates on their progress and recommendations for support at home.

Understanding Your Rights as a LOS FRESNOS CISD Parent

Federal and state law grant you specific rights regarding your child's special education. LOS FRESNOS CISD parent rights include the right to participate in all decisions about your child's identification, evaluation, placement, and services.

Key LOS FRESNOS CISD Parent Rights Under IDEA

  • Right to Evaluation: Request a full evaluation at any time if you suspect a disability
  • Right to Consent: Provide written consent before initial evaluation and before initial IEP implementation
  • Right to Participate: Attend all ARD meetings and have meaningful input on all decisions
  • Right to Information: Receive copies of all evaluations, reports, and assessment data
  • Right to Challenge: Disagree with district determinations and request independent evaluations
  • Right to Due Process: Request a due process hearing if disputes cannot be resolved
  • Right to Records: Access and review all educational records at least once yearly at no cost
  • Right to Confidentiality: Protect your child's privacy in all special education matters

All parents in LOS FRESNOS CISD receive a document explaining their procedural safeguards at the time of initial referral for evaluation, and annually thereafter.

Special LOS FRESNOS CISD Parent Rights for Specific Situations

If LOS FRESNOS CISD proposes to change your child's placement or discontinue special education services, you have the right to prior written notice explaining the reasons, the alternatives considered, and your procedural safeguards. If you disagree, you may request mediation or a due process hearing.

Parents in LOS FRESNOS CISD also have the right to request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at district expense if they disagree with the district's evaluation results.

Getting Started with LOS FRESNOS CISD Special Education

If you believe your child may need special education services, here are the steps to get started with LOS FRESNOS CISD special education:

  1. Request an Evaluation: Contact your child's principal or the LOS FRESNOS CISD Special Education office in writing
  2. Provide Consent: LOS FRESNOS CISD will send you a Parent Consent for Evaluation form to sign
  3. Participate in Evaluation: Share information about your child's strengths, challenges, and medical history
  4. Attend the ARD Meeting: Meet with the committee to review results and determine eligibility
  5. Approve the IEP: Provide written consent before services begin
  6. Monitor Progress: Review progress reports, attend annual reviews, and communicate with teachers

The entire evaluation process in LOS FRESNOS CISD typically takes 60 days from the date you request an evaluation to the ARD meeting where eligibility is determined.
