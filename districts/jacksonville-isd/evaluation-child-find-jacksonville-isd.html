Every parent of a child in JACKSONVILLE ISD has the right to request a JACKSONVILLE ISD child find evaluation. Child Find is a federal requirement that ensures all children with suspected disabilities are identified, evaluated, and provided with appropriate special education services. This right exists regardless of your child's current grade level or academic performance.

Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code Section 29.004, JACKSONVILLE ISD must actively seek out children who may have disabilities. As a parent, you don't need to wait for a teacher to suggest evaluation—you can initiate the process yourself at any time during the school year.

Your child may be eligible for a JACKSONVILLE ISD FIE evaluation if they struggle with learning, communication, behavior, motor skills, or social-emotional development. The evaluation process is free, and your family will not be charged any fees for assessment, diagnosis, or eligibility determination.

The first step in requesting a JACKSONVILLE ISD evaluation request is to submit a written request to your child's school or to the JACKSONVILLE ISD special education department. While Texas law does not strictly require a written request, documentation is crucial for establishing timelines and protecting your rights.

How to Submit Your Request

  • Email: Contact your child's principal or the special education coordinator at your school with a clear subject line: "Request for Full Individual Evaluation"
  • Hand-delivered letter: Bring a written request directly to the school office and ask for a dated receipt
  • Certified mail: Send your request to JACKSONVILLE ISD administration with delivery confirmation
  • In-person meeting: Request a meeting with the campus administrator or special education staff to discuss your concerns verbally, then ask them to document your request

What to Include in Your Written Request

Your letter should be clear and specific. Include your child's full name, date of birth, current grade, and campus. Describe the specific areas of concern—such as "difficulty with reading," "trouble following directions," or "challenges with social interactions." The more specific you are, the better JACKSONVILLE ISD can tailor the evaluation process.

Mention any evaluations or assessments conducted outside of school, such as evaluations by a pediatrician, speech therapist, or psychologist. Include relevant medical history or family history of learning disabilities. Keep your tone professional and factual; avoid emotional language while being honest about your concerns.

Step 2: Know the JACKSONVILLE ISD 45 Day Timeline

One of the most important aspects of special education law is the JACKSONVILLE ISD 45 day timeline. Once JACKSONVILLE ISD receives your written request for a JACKSONVILLE ISD FIE evaluation, the district has 45 calendar days to complete the evaluation and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss the results.

Timeline Breakdown

Days 1-5: JACKSONVILLE ISD should acknowledge receipt of your request and contact you to obtain written permission to evaluate. You will receive a Permission to Evaluate form that explains the evaluation process, your rights, and the types of assessments your child will complete.

Days 6-40: Once you sign and return the Permission to Evaluate form, JACKSONVILLE ISD staff will conduct comprehensive evaluations in all suspected areas of disability. This may include cognitive assessments, academic achievement tests, speech-language evaluations, social-emotional assessments, and classroom observations.

Days 41-45: JACKSONVILLE ISD must complete all evaluation activities and hold the ARD meeting to review results with you. At this meeting, the team will determine whether your child meets the eligibility criteria for special education services under IDEA or Texas Education Code Section 29.004.

What If JACKSONVILLE ISD Needs More Time?

In rare circumstances, JACKSONVILLE ISD may request an extension of 10 additional days if your child requires specialized assessments or if there are unusual circumstances. This extension must be requested in writing and approved by you. Even with an extension, the evaluation must be completed within 55 calendar days total.

Understanding the JACKSONVILLE ISD Special Education Evaluation Process

A JACKSONVILLE ISD special education evaluation is comprehensive and multidisciplinary. Rather than relying on a single test, JACKSONVILLE ISD uses multiple evaluation methods to gain a complete picture of your child's strengths and needs.

Components of the Evaluation

Cognitive/Intellectual Assessment: Measures reasoning, problem-solving, processing speed, and overall cognitive ability. This helps determine if your child has an intellectual disability or gifted abilities that have been masked by other challenges.

Academic Achievement Testing: Assesses performance in reading, math, writing, and spelling. This identifies specific academic areas where your child is struggling compared to grade-level peers.

Speech-Language Evaluation: Evaluates receptive and expressive language, articulation, and pragmatic communication skills. This is particularly important if your child has delayed speech, difficulty understanding directions, or social communication challenges.

Social-Emotional/Behavioral Assessment: May include rating scales, observations, and interviews to understand your child's emotional development, behavioral patterns, and social skills.

Classroom Observations: JACKSONVILLE ISD staff observe your child in typical school environments to see how they function academically and socially with peers.

Parent and Teacher Input: You will complete rating scales and questionnaires, as will your child's teachers. Your insights about your child's development at home are invaluable to the evaluation team.

Preparing for Your Child's Evaluation

Parents in JACKSONVILLE ISD can take several steps to support their child during the evaluation process. Ensure your child gets adequate sleep the night before testing, eats a healthy breakfast, and arrives at school on time. Let your child know they will be working with some new people but reassure them that these activities are not tests they can fail.

Information to Provide JACKSONVILLE ISD

  • Medical history, including vision and hearing test results
  • Developmental milestones (when child sat up, walked, said first words)
  • Prior evaluations or assessments from outside providers
  • Medication history and current medications
  • Family history of learning disabilities, mental health conditions, or developmental delays
  • Your observations of specific behaviors or skills of concern

The ARD Meeting: Understanding Your Results

After the evaluation is complete, JACKSONVILLE ISD will schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. At this meeting, you'll receive detailed results from the JACKSONVILLE ISD FIE evaluation and learn whether your child qualifies for special education services.

What to Expect at the ARD Meeting

The ARD team typically includes the principal or designee, the special education teacher, a general education teacher, school nurse, your child's current teachers, and you. Each team member may present evaluation results from their area. You have the right to bring an advocate, attorney, or additional family members to support you during this meeting.

The team will discuss whether your child meets eligibility criteria for one of 13 disability categories recognized under IDEA, including Specific Learning Disability, Speech or Language Impairment, Autism Spectrum Disorder, Emotional Disturbance, Other Health Impairment, and others. JACKSONVILLE ISD families should know that not all children with challenges qualify for special education—the disability must significantly impact educational performance.

If Your Child Qualifies

If JACKSONVILLE ISD determines your child is eligible, the team will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). This document outlines your child's present levels of performance, annual goals, the special education services they'll receive, and how progress will be measured.

If Your Child Does Not Qualify

If your child does not meet special education eligibility, JACKSONVILLE ISD may offer interventions through general education (Response to Intervention/RTI programs). You also have the right to request a due process hearing if you disagree with the evaluation results. Parents in JACKSONVILLE ISD who have concerns about the evaluation should ask for detailed explanations of the team's reasoning.

Your Rights During the JACKSONVILLE ISD Evaluation Process

Texas Education Code and IDEA provide specific rights to parents during special education evaluation. Understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child in JACKSONVILLE ISD.

  • Right to Consent: JACKSONVILLE ISD must obtain your written permission before conducting a comprehensive evaluation
  • Right to Records: You can access all evaluation records, test protocols, and assessment results
  • Right to Independent Evaluation: If you disagree with JACKSONVILLE ISD's evaluation, you may obtain an independent evaluation at no cost to your family in certain circumstances
  • Right to Interpreter Services: JACKSONVILLE ISD must provide interpreters if you speak a language other than English
  • Right to Participate: You are a required member of all ARD meetings and evaluation team decisions
  • Right to Dispute Resolution: You may request mediation or due process hearing if you disagree with JACKSONVILLE ISD's decisions

Common Questions Parents in JACKSONVILLE ISD Ask

How do I know if my child needs an evaluation?

Consider requesting a JACKSONVILLE ISD child find evaluation if your child struggles with academics significantly behind peers, has difficulty following directions or communicating, displays concerning behavior patterns, or if you've observed developmental delays. You know your child best—if you're concerned, trust your instinct and request an evaluation.

Can JACKSONVILLE ISD refuse to evaluate my child?

JACKSONVILLE ISD must evaluate your child if you request it in writing and there is reason to believe your child has a disability affecting educational performance. A school cannot refuse evaluation simply because they believe your child will not qualify.

What if I disagree with JACKSONVILLE ISD's evaluation results?

You have the right to request an independent evaluation. You can also request that JACKSONVILLE ISD reconsider the evaluation, ask for a second opinion from a specialist outside the school, or request a due process hearing.

Will my child be labeled or stigmatized?

JACKSONVILLE ISD must maintain confidentiality of all evaluation records. Special education services are individualized and designed to help your child succeed. Many successful people received special education services—the goal is getting your child the support they need to learn.
