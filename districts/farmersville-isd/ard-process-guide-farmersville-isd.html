An ARD meeting is a formal gathering where the FARMERSVILLE ISD special education team, school personnel, and parents come together to discuss a student's educational needs, develop or review their Individualized Education Program (IEP), and make decisions about special education services. The term "ARD" stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal, reflecting the three primary purposes of these meetings.

For families in FARMERSVILLE ISD, ARD meetings are the cornerstone of special education decision-making. These meetings occur at least once per year for students already receiving services, though parents and educators can request additional meetings as needed. Understanding how the FARMERSVILLE ISD ARD meeting process works empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Texas Education Code §89.1001 requires every student receiving special education services to have an IEP developed and reviewed through the ARD process. This legal requirement ensures that students throughout FARMERSVILLE ISD and across Texas receive individualized, appropriate educational services.

Understanding the IEP and Its Connection to FARMERSVILLE ISD ARD Meetings

An Individualized Education Program (IEP) is a legally binding document that outlines your child's special education services, accommodations, modifications, and FARMERSVILLE ISD IEP goals. The IEP is created and updated during ARD meetings and serves as the roadmap for your child's education throughout the school year.

The FARMERSVILLE ISD IEP process begins with a comprehensive evaluation of your child's strengths, weaknesses, and educational needs. This evaluation informs all decisions made during the ARD meeting about what services and supports your child requires.

Key components of every IEP include:

  • Current levels of academic and functional performance
  • Measurable annual goals aligned with grade-level standards
  • Description of special education services and modifications
  • Accommodations for classroom and standardized testing
  • Transition services (for students age 14 and older)
  • Behavioral support plans, if needed
  • Progress monitoring and reporting procedures

If you cannot attend in person, parents in FARMERSVILLE ISD can request to participate by telephone or video conference. You may also bring an advocate, family member, or attorney to the meeting to support you.

Parents in FARMERSVILLE ISD who disagree with ARD decisions can request mediation or file a due process complaint. These procedures, outlined in the Procedural Safeguards document FARMERSVILLE ISD provides at the beginning of special education services, protect your child's rights.

  3. Present Evaluation Data: FARMERSVILLE ISD staff review assessment results and current performance

Developing Meaningful IEP Goals for FARMERSVILLE ISD Students

One of the most critical parts of the FARMERSVILLE ISD ARD meeting is developing FARMERSVILLE ISD IEP goals that truly address your child's needs and lead to meaningful progress. Effective goals are SMART: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound.

During the ARD meeting, advocate for goals that:

  • Connect to grade-level academic standards when appropriate
  • Address areas where your child struggles most
  • Build on your child's strengths and interests
  • Are realistic and achievable within one school year
  • Include regular progress monitoring and reporting to parents
  • Prepare your child for post-secondary success

For example, instead of a vague goal like "improve reading," a meaningful FARMERSVILLE ISD IEP goal might state: "Given grade-level text with teacher support, the student will read with 80% accuracy and answer comprehension questions with 75% accuracy, as measured by weekly reading assessments."

Services available in FARMERSVILLE ISD may include:

  • Specialized Services: Services for students with severe disabilities or specific needs
