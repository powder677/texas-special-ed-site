Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

Discipline Rights & Grievances in Victoria ISD: Urgent Guide

URGENT GUIDE: Discipline Rights & Grievances in Victoria ISD

If your child in Victoria ISD is facing disciplinary action, especially suspension or possible expulsion, ACT IMMEDIATELY! This guide provides critical information to protect your child's rights, particularly if they have an IEP or are suspected of having a disability. Time is of the essence.

The 10-Day Suspension Rule

Victoria ISD can suspend a student for up to 3 consecutive school days for certain infractions without significant formal processes. However, if your child faces suspensions adding up to more than 10 cumulative school days within a single academic year, it triggers special protections, especially under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) if your child has an IEP or suspected disability. This 10-day threshold requires further investigation and potential interventions.

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

If your child with an IEP or suspected disability faces a suspension exceeding 10 cumulative days, the school must conduct a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This meeting determines if the student's behavior was a manifestation of their disability, or a direct result of Victoria ISD's failure to properly implement their IEP. This is CRITICAL.

  • Before the MDR: Demand to see all records related to the incident and your child's IEP. Thoroughly review everything!
  • During the MDR: Actively participate! Advocate for your child. Present evidence that connects the behavior to their disability or a lack of proper support. Bring supporting documentation and any relevant medical or psychological evaluations.
  • After the MDR: If the MDR determines the behavior WAS a manifestation of their disability, the school MUST take immediate steps to address the underlying issues, including implementing or modifying the IEP. If the MDR incorrectly determines the behavior was NOT a manifestation, you have the right to challenge it immediately.

Change of Placement

A "Change of Placement" occurs when a student is removed from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days, or when there is a pattern of removals that constitute a change. This can include suspension, expulsion, or placement in a different setting like an alternative education program (AEP). A Change of Placement triggers significant procedural safeguards under IDEA, ensuring your child's rights are protected.

Remember: Any placement change must be appropriate to your child's needs and documented in their IEP. Demand this documentation in writing.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe Victoria ISD has violated your child's rights or the law (e.g., improper suspension, failure to conduct an MDR, inappropriate IEP implementation), you have the right to file a grievance. Level 1 is the first step. Get the grievance policy from the Victoria ISD website or the school. It details timelines and procedures. Document everything in writing. Keep copies of all correspondence.

Key Actions:

  • Act Quickly: Grievance policies have strict deadlines. Missed deadlines can void your right to appeal.
  • Be Specific: Clearly state the violation, the date it occurred, and the specific relief you are seeking.
  • Gather Evidence: Include copies of relevant documents (IEP, suspension notices, emails, etc.).

TEA State Complaint

You can file a formal complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA) if you believe Victoria ISD has violated IDEA or other special education laws. TEA investigations can be lengthy, but they can result in corrective actions for the district. Information on filing a complaint can be found on the TEA website.

Due Process

As a parent, you have the right to request a due process hearing if you disagree with the school's decisions regarding your child's education, including disciplinary actions and IEP implementation. A due process hearing is a formal legal proceeding where you can present evidence and argue your case before an impartial hearing officer. This is a complex process, and you should strongly consider seeking legal representation.

This information is for guidance only and should not be considered legal advice. Contact a special education attorney or advocate immediately if your child's rights are being violated. Don't delay!

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
