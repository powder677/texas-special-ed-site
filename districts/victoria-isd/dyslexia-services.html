Dyslexia Services in Victoria ISD

This guide provides information on dyslexia services available to students within Victoria ISD, in accordance with Texas state law and guidelines.

Texas Dyslexia Handbook 2024

The Texas Dyslexia Handbook, updated in 2024, serves as the cornerstone for identifying and supporting students with dyslexia in Texas public schools. Victoria ISD adheres to the principles and practices outlined in this handbook. This handbook provides definitions of dyslexia and related disorders, screening procedures, intervention strategies, parent rights and responsibilities, and the legal requirements for addressing dyslexia in Texas. It is crucial that parents, educators, and administrators familiarize themselves with this document. Key updates in the 2024 version should be reviewed upon release, but will likely include changes regarding early literacy, screening practices, and updated research on effective interventions.

Mandatory Screening

Victoria ISD implements mandatory dyslexia screening for all kindergarten and first-grade students. This screening typically occurs early in the school year. The purpose of the screening is to identify students who may be at risk for dyslexia. The screening tools used are aligned with the requirements outlined in the Texas Dyslexia Handbook. These tools assess areas such as phonological awareness, rapid naming, letter knowledge, and decoding skills. Students who demonstrate indicators of dyslexia during this initial screening will be referred for further evaluation to determine if they meet the criteria for a diagnosis of dyslexia and require specialized intervention.

Structured Literacy Programs

Victoria ISD utilizes Structured Literacy programs for students identified with dyslexia. Structured Literacy is a research-based approach to reading instruction that emphasizes explicit, systematic, and sequential teaching of foundational reading skills. These programs focus on the key components of reading: phonology, sound-symbol association (phonics), syllable instruction, morphology, syntax, and semantics. The specific Structured Literacy programs used within Victoria ISD may vary by campus and grade level, but all must adhere to the principles of Structured Literacy and be approved for use with students with dyslexia. Parents are encouraged to inquire about the specific program being implemented for their child and how its effectiveness is being monitored.

504 vs IEP for Dyslexia

Students with dyslexia may receive support through either a Section 504 plan or an Individualized Education Program (IEP), depending on the severity of their needs and eligibility criteria. A 504 plan provides accommodations and modifications to ensure that students with disabilities have equal access to the general education curriculum. For students with dyslexia, this may include extended time on assignments, preferential seating, or access to assistive technology. An IEP, on the other hand, is a more comprehensive plan developed for students with disabilities who require specialized instruction and related services. To qualify for an IEP under the category of Specific Learning Disability (SLD), which can include dyslexia, a student must demonstrate a significant discrepancy between their intellectual ability and their academic achievement in reading. An IEP outlines specific goals, objectives, and services designed to address the student's individual needs. The ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee, which includes parents, determines whether a student qualifies for an IEP.

Accommodations List

Common accommodations for students with dyslexia in Victoria ISD may include, but are not limited to:

  • Extended time on tests and assignments
  • Preferential seating
  • Use of assistive technology (e.g., text-to-speech software, audiobooks)
  • Provision of notes or outlines
  • Modified assignments
  • Oral administration of tests
  • Reduced workload
  • Frequent breaks
  • Access to a quiet testing environment

The specific accommodations provided will be determined based on the individual needs of the student and documented in their 504 plan or IEP.

How to Request Evaluation

If you suspect your child may have dyslexia, you should contact your child's teacher or the school principal to request an evaluation. The request should be made in writing and should include specific observations of your child's difficulties with reading and spelling. The school will then initiate the referral process, which may include gathering additional information from teachers and parents, conducting standardized assessments, and observing the student in the classroom. You have the right to participate in all stages of the evaluation process and to receive a copy of the evaluation report. Victoria ISD is committed to providing timely and appropriate evaluations to identify students with dyslexia and provide them with the necessary support to succeed academically.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

Is my child entitled to dyslexia testing in Texas? +

Yes. Texas law requires all districts to screen students for dyslexia and related disorders in Kindergarten and First Grade, and at any grade level if a parent or teacher requests it.

What accommodations can a dyslexic child get in Texas? +

Accommodations may include extended time on tests, text-to-speech software, oral administration of tests, reduced-answer-choice formats, and use of a calculator. These can be provided through a 504 Plan or an IEP.

What is the Texas Dyslexia Handbook? +

The Texas Dyslexia Handbook is a state guidance document updated in 2024 that outlines districts' responsibilities for identifying, evaluating, and serving students with dyslexia using structured literacy methods.
