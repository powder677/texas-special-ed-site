How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD

The first step in accessing special education services for your child in Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD is requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). This evaluation is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine if your child has a disability and, if so, what specific educational needs they have. It's crucial to understand that this request must be in writing. A verbal request to a teacher or administrator, while helpful for initial conversations, does not officially start the evaluation timeline. Only a written request formally triggers the school district's obligation to initiate the evaluation process.

Once Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD receives your written consent to evaluate, the district has 45 school days to complete the FIE. This timeline is a key legal protection, ensuring that evaluations are conducted in a timely manner. Remember that "school days" exclude weekends, holidays, and school breaks. Furthermore, it's important to be aware of Child Find, a legal mandate that requires Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD to proactively identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities residing within their jurisdiction, regardless of whether they are enrolled in public school. This means the district has a responsibility to seek out and assess students who may need special education services, even if you haven't yet made a formal request.

Your next step is to draft and submit a written request for a Full Individual Evaluation to the Special Education Director for Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD. Be sure to keep a copy of the letter for your records, and send it via certified mail with return receipt requested to confirm delivery. This provides proof that your request was received and starts the 45-school-day timeline.

ARD Meetings in Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal, and the ARD committee is the group responsible for making decisions about your child's special education program in Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD. These meetings are where your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP) is developed, reviewed, and revised. As a parent, you are a vital member of the ARD committee, and your input is crucial to creating an IEP that meets your child's unique needs. Remember that the ARD meeting is a collaborative process, and you have the right to actively participate and advocate for your child's best interests within Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD.

Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD is legally required to provide you with at least five calendar days' written notice before any ARD meeting. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, the time and location, and the names of the individuals who will be attending. This allows you time to prepare and gather any relevant information. Furthermore, you have the right to request a 10-day recess (pause) during the ARD meeting at any time. This allows you time to process information, consult with experts, or simply take a break to ensure you're making informed decisions. Use this option strategically if you feel overwhelmed or need more time to consider the proposed IEP.

Finally, remember that you are not required to sign the IEP on the same day it is presented. Take your time to review the document carefully and ensure that you understand all of its components and agree with the proposed goals and services. You have the right to take the IEP home, consult with trusted advisors, and return it to Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD with your signature at a later date. Don't feel pressured to make an immediate decision. Your thoughtful consideration is paramount to ensuring your child's educational success.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD

Discipline issues can be particularly challenging for students with disabilities. It's important to understand your child's rights if they face disciplinary action within Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD. A critical threshold is reached when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year. This triggers significant procedural safeguards under IDEA.

Suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative days constitutes a "change of placement" under IDEA, requiring a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This review, conducted by the ARD committee, determines whether the student's behavior was a manifestation of their disability or a result of the school's failure to properly implement the IEP. The ARD committee must convene within days of the suspension exceeding 10 days. This situation is extremely time-sensitive, and you must act quickly to protect your child's rights within Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD. Failure to respond promptly could result in your child facing further disciplinary action without the appropriate safeguards in place. This is also a good time to consider whether the BIP is adequate to address your student's behaviors.

Filing a Complaint Against Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD

Sometimes, despite your best efforts, disagreements with Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD cannot be resolved through informal communication and ARD meetings. In such cases, you have several options for escalating your concerns. Remember that these rights exist regardless of what you may be told by district staff. You always have the right to seek additional assistance.

You have four main escalation paths: (1) a TEA State Complaint, which is a formal written complaint filed with the Texas Education Agency, is a free process that TEA must resolve within 60 days. (2) Mediation through TEA offers a neutral third party to facilitate communication and help you and Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD reach a mutually agreeable solution. This is also a free service. (3) A Due Process Hearing is a more formal, adversarial process involving an administrative law judge who will hear evidence and make a binding decision. This is a significantly more complex and costly option, often requiring legal representation. (4) You can also file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights if you believe Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD has discriminated against your child based on their disability. It is up to you to determine which route is most appropriate for your specific situation.

Research each of these options carefully to decide which is best for you. Then, begin gathering all relevant documentation and prepare to file your complaint. You can find more information on each of these processes, including complaint forms and contact information, on the TEA website or the website for the Office of Civil Rights. Don't be afraid to stand up for your child's rights within Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD.

Frequently Asked Questions About Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD Special Education

The questions every Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. Does Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. What if Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
