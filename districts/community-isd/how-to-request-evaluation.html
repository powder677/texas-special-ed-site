How to Request an FIE Evaluation in COMMUNITY ISD

A FIE (Full Individual Evaluation) is a comprehensive assessment that examines all areas of your child's development. Here is the process for requesting an FIE evaluation in COMMUNITY ISD:

Step 1: Make a Formal Request in Writing

You must submit a written request for evaluation to your school principal or COMMUNITY ISD's special education department. Informal communication is not sufficient; the request must be documented.

Your letter should include:

  • Student's full name and grade
  • Student's date of birth
  • Specific description of academic or behavioral concerns
  • Concrete examples of observed behaviors or difficulties
  • Your name, phone number, and email address
  • Signature and date

Send the request by certified mail or email to have proof that COMMUNITY ISD received it. Keep a copy for your records.

Step 2: COMMUNITY ISD Responds within 15 Days

By law, COMMUNITY ISD has 15 school days to respond to your evaluation request. You will receive a letter explaining:

  • Whether COMMUNITY ISD accepts or rejects the evaluation
  • The next steps in the process
  • Your rights as a parent
  • A meeting date if applicable

If COMMUNITY ISD rejects the evaluation, they must provide their reasons in writing. You have the right to appeal this decision.

Step 3: Consent for Evaluation

Before COMMUNITY ISD can evaluate your child, you must sign an evaluation consent form. This document explains what will be evaluated, who will conduct it, and how the results will be used. Read it carefully before signing.

Step 4: Comprehensive Evaluation

Once you have given consent, COMMUNITY ISD specialists will evaluate your child in all suspected areas of disability. This special education evaluation in COMMUNITY ISD may include:

  • Intelligence and cognitive skills tests
  • Academic evaluations (reading, writing, math)
  • Language and communication evaluations
  • Physical and motor examinations
  • Social, emotional, and behavioral evaluations
  • Review of previous school records
  • Interviews with teachers and parents

COMMUNITY ISD must complete the evaluation within 60 school days from the date you gave consent.

Step 5: Results Meeting (ARD/IEP Meeting)

Within 60 days, COMMUNITY ISD will hold a meeting to discuss the evaluation results. In this meeting, it will be determined if your child qualifies for special education services.

At the COMMUNITY ISD meeting, you will learn:

  • The evaluation findings
  • If your child has a disability that qualifies them for services
  • The Individualized Education Program (IEP) if applicable
  • The available services and supports
  • Placement options (regular classroom, resource room, etc.)

Difference Between COMMUNITY Special Education and General Education

It is important to understand how special education works compared to general education in COMMUNITY ISD.

Students in special education in COMMUNITY ISD receive an Individualized Education Program (IEP) that is specific to their needs. The IEP sets goals, services, accommodations, and the percentage of time the student spends in general versus special education classrooms.

In contrast, students in general education in COMMUNITY ISD follow the standard curriculum with limited accommodations. If your child is struggling in general education, a special education evaluation in COMMUNITY ISD can provide the necessary support.
