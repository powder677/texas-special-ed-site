A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine whether your child has a disability and qualifies for special education services in COMMUNITY ISD. The evaluation examines your child's cognitive abilities, academic performance, social-emotional development, and physical health.

Child Find is COMMUNITY ISD's legal obligation to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities, regardless of the severity of their disability. This means COMMUNITY ISD special education services are proactively searching for children who may need support, even if parents haven't formally requested an evaluation.

Understanding these concepts is crucial because they establish your rights as a parent in the COMMUNITY area. Texas Education Code §29.001 requires all school districts, including COMMUNITY ISD, to implement comprehensive child find activities.

Any parent or guardian of a student attending COMMUNITY ISD can request an evaluation. Additionally, teachers, school counselors, and other school personnel may recommend an evaluation to parents. If your child is not yet in school, you can still request a COMMUNITY ISD child find evaluation for children ages 3-5 through the district's preschool special education program.

When to Consider Requesting an Evaluation

Parents in COMMUNITY ISD should consider requesting an evaluation if their child demonstrates:

  • Difficulty with reading, writing, or math compared to peers
  • Delayed speech or language development
  • Behavioral concerns affecting academic or social performance
  • Difficulty following directions or staying focused
  • Social interaction challenges with other children
  • Gross or fine motor skill delays
  • Significant changes in school performance or behavior

You don't need to have a diagnosis from a doctor to request a COMMUNITY ISD special education evaluation. The district will conduct the assessment at no cost to your family.

Step 1: Submit Your Written Request

The first action is to submit a written request for a COMMUNITY ISD FIE evaluation to your child's school or the special education department. While verbal requests can be made to teachers or principals, always follow up with written documentation. This creates a paper trail and ensures the request is officially logged.

Your written request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Your contact information (phone and email)
  • Specific concerns about your child's learning or behavior
  • Any previous evaluations or assessments
  • A request for the evaluation timeline

Send this request via email or hand-deliver it to the campus principal or COMMUNITY ISD special education coordinator, keeping a copy for your records.

Step 2: Receive Notice of Evaluation Plan

Within 5 school days of receiving your written request for a COMMUNITY ISD evaluation request, the district must provide you with written notice of their intention to evaluate your child. This notice, called a Notice of Proposed Evaluation (NPE), includes:

  • A description of the proposed evaluation areas
  • Your parental rights and procedural safeguards
  • Information about consent requirements
  • A timeline for completing the evaluation

COMMUNITY ISD must provide this notice in your home language if English is not your primary language, ensuring all parents in the COMMUNITY area understand the process.

Step 3: Provide Written Consent

You must provide written consent before COMMUNITY ISD can proceed with the COMMUNITY ISD child find evaluation. This is a critical protection under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). Your consent must be informed and voluntary, and you may withdraw consent at any time, though any evaluations already completed remain valid.

The consent form will detail exactly what types of assessments your child will undergo. Take time to review this document carefully and ask questions if anything is unclear.

The COMMUNITY ISD 45 Day Timeline for Evaluation

One of the most important deadlines for COMMUNITY ISD special education evaluation is the 45-day timeline. Once you provide written consent, COMMUNITY ISD has 45 calendar days to complete the entire evaluation and hold an eligibility meeting with your family.

What Happens During the 45-Day Window

During this critical COMMUNITY ISD 45 day timeline, the special education team will:

  • Administer cognitive and achievement tests
  • Conduct classroom observations
  • Review school records and medical history
  • Gather teacher input through rating scales
  • Assess social-emotional and behavioral functioning
  • Complete speech/language, occupational, or physical therapy evaluations if needed
  • Compile findings into a comprehensive report

This COMMUNITY ISD FIE evaluation is thorough and multi-faceted, providing a complete picture of your child's strengths and challenges.

Important Notes About the 45-Day Timeline

The COMMUNITY ISD 45 day timeline begins when you sign consent and can be extended by 15 additional days if the district requests a second consent for additional evaluation areas. If your child is absent for extended periods during this window, the timeline may be adjusted. Always ask COMMUNITY ISD staff for updates on the evaluation progress if you don't hear back within 3-4 weeks.

Understanding the Evaluation Components

Academic Achievement Testing

Your child will take standardized tests assessing reading, math, writing, and overall academic performance. These tests compare your child's skills to same-age peers to identify discrepancies.

Cognitive Assessment

Cognitive testing measures intellectual abilities including reasoning, processing speed, working memory, and verbal comprehension. This helps determine if intellectual disability is a factor.

Behavioral and Social-Emotional Evaluation

COMMUNITY ISD will gather information about your child's behavior at school and home through teacher and parent rating scales, and may conduct a functional behavioral assessment if behavioral concerns are primary.

Speech and Language Evaluation

If language concerns are noted, a speech-language pathologist will evaluate your child's expressive and receptive language skills.

Additional Specialized Assessments

Depending on your child's needs, COMMUNITY ISD special education may include occupational therapy, physical therapy, or vision/hearing screenings.

What Happens After the COMMUNITY ISD FIE Evaluation

The Eligibility Meeting

At the end of the COMMUNITY ISD 45 day timeline, you'll attend an eligibility meeting (also called an IEP meeting if your child qualifies). The evaluation team will present findings and determine if your child meets eligibility criteria under IDEA.

COMMUNITY ISD must determine that your child has a disability in one of 13 categories and that special education services are needed. Possible eligibility categories include:

  • Specific Learning Disability (SLD)
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Emotional/Behavioral Disorder
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Speech/Language Impairment
  • Visual Impairment
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Physical Disability
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Deaf-Blindness

If Your Child Qualifies

When your child qualifies, the COMMUNITY ISD team will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). This legal document outlines your child's goals, the services they'll receive, and how progress will be measured.

If Your Child Does Not Qualify

If COMMUNITY ISD determines your child doesn't meet eligibility criteria, you have the right to challenge this decision. You can request an independent evaluation at district expense or pursue due process options.

Your Rights as a Parent in COMMUNITY ISD

Parents of students in COMMUNITY ISD have significant rights throughout the COMMUNITY ISD FIE evaluation process:

  • Right to Notice: You must receive clear, understandable information at every stage
  • Right to Participate: You're a full team member in all evaluation and IEP decisions
  • Right to Access Records: You can review all evaluation materials and school records
  • Right to an Interpreter: COMMUNITY ISD must provide translation services if needed
  • Right to Disagreement: You can request independent evaluations or due process hearings
  • Right to Procedural Safeguards: You receive detailed information about your legal protections

Make sure you understand these rights before the eligibility meeting. If anything is unclear during the COMMUNITY ISD special education evaluation process, ask questions.

When to Consider Getting a Special Education Advocate or Attorney

Many parents benefit from professional support during the COMMUNITY ISD FIE evaluation process. If you're concerned about the evaluation's adequacy, disagree with the results, or feel your child's needs aren't being met, a special ed advocate COMMUNITY or special education attorney near COMMUNITY can help.

When to Seek Professional Support

Consider consulting with a special ed advocate COMMUNITY if:

  • The evaluation feels incomplete or rushed
  • The district disagrees with obtaining an evaluation
  • Your child is significantly struggling but the district says they don't qualify
  • You receive the evaluation report and don't understand the findings
  • You disagree with the eligibility decision

A special education attorney near COMMUNITY can provide legal guidance on your rights and help with due process hearings or settlement negotiations if disputes arise.

Common Questions About COMMUNITY ISD FIE Evaluations

How long does a COMMUNITY ISD FIE evaluation take?

The evaluation itself may take 3-6 weeks, with results due within the COMMUNITY ISD 45 day timeline. Your child might have several testing appointments ranging from 1-3 hours each.

Can I attend the testing sessions?

COMMUNITY ISD will determine when parents can observe. Typically, parents observe some assessments but not all, as presence can affect test validity.

What if I disagree with the COMMUNITY ISD evaluation results?

You have the right to request an independent evaluation at COMMUNITY ISD's expense if you disagree with the district's evaluation. You can also request a due process hearing.

Does COMMUNITY ISD evaluate preschool children?

Yes, COMMUNITY ISD has a child find program for children ages 3-5. Contact the district's preschool special education coordinator to request an evaluation.

Taking Action: Next Steps for COMMUNITY ISD Parents

If you believe your child needs evaluation in COMMUNITY ISD, take these immediate actions:

  1. Document specific concerns about your child's learning and behavior
  2. Contact your child's teacher or school principal to discuss your concerns
  3. Request the COMMUNITY ISD special education department's contact information
  4. Submit a written COMMUNITY ISD evaluation request
  5. Keep copies of all written communication with the district
  6. Mark your calendar with the COMMUNITY ISD 45 day timeline end date
  7. Prepare questions to ask at the eligibility meeting

The evaluation process can feel overwhelming, but remember: COMMUNITY ISD is legally required to evaluate your child if you request it, and the goal is finding out how to best support your child's success.

You know your child better than anyone. Trust your instincts, stay organized, and advocate confidently for your child's educational needs in COMMUNITY ISD.
