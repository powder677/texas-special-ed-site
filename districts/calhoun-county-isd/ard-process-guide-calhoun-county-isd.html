Understanding ARD Meetings and IEP at CALHOUN COUNTY ISD

An ARD meeting is a formal gathering where parents, teachers, administrators, and specialists meet to discuss your child's special education needs and create or review an Individualized Education Program (IEP). At CALHOUN COUNTY ISD, these meetings are governed by federal law (IDEA) and state regulations under the Texas Education Code.

The purpose of a CALHOUN COUNTY ISD ARD meeting is to ensure your child receives a free, appropriate public education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment (LRE). Whether this is your first meeting or a scheduled review, understanding your rights and responsibilities will help you participate fully in the process.

Key Terms You'll Encounter

  • IEP (Individualized Education Program): The legal document detailing your child's special education services and goals
  • ARD Committee: The team that meets to develop, review, and revise the IEP
  • FAPE: Free Appropriate Public Education guaranteed to eligible students
  • LRE: Least Restrictive Environment—placing your child in regular classrooms as much as possible
  • Special Education: Specialized instruction for students with disabilities under CALHOUN COUNTY special education services

Developing Strong IEP Goals for CALHOUN COUNTY ISD Students

One of the most critical outcomes of a CALHOUN COUNTY ISD ARD meeting is the development of measurable CALHOUN COUNTY ISD IEP goals. These goals drive your child's special education instruction and progress monitoring throughout the year.

Characteristics of Effective IEP Goals

Quality CALHOUN COUNTY ISD IEP goals should be:

  • Specific: Clearly state what skill or behavior will be targeted
  • Measurable: Include concrete criteria for success (e.g., "80% accuracy" or "4 out of 5 attempts")
  • Achievable: Realistic given your child's current functioning level and the school year timeline
  • Relevant: Address your child's documented disability and educational needs
  • Time-bound: Include a review date, typically one school year

Tips for Goal Development at CALHOUN COUNTY ISD ARD Meetings

During your CALHOUN COUNTY ISD ARD meeting, ask questions about each proposed goal. Don't accept vague language like "improve reading skills." Instead, ensure goals specify grade level, percentage of accuracy, and how progress will be measured.

Request that the ARD committee explain how each goal addresses your child's disability and supports access to the general education curriculum. This ensures alignment between special education services and classroom expectations.

CALHOUN COUNTY ISD Special Education Services and Accommodations

A critical component of the CALHOUN COUNTY ISD IEP process is determining what special education services and accommodations your child needs. CALHOUN COUNTY special education services might include resource instruction, specialized curriculum, speech therapy, occupational therapy, or counseling services.

Questions to Ask About Services

  • How many hours per week will my child receive each service?
  • Will services be provided in the general education classroom, resource room, or elsewhere?
  • Who will provide services (certified special educator, therapist, paraprofessional)?
  • How will progress be monitored and communicated to me?
  • Are services based on research-based interventions?

Accommodations allow your child to access curriculum without changing expectations. Examples include extended test time, preferential seating, or use of assistive technology. Modifications actually change what is being taught and are more significant than accommodations.
