Understanding CALHOUN COUNTY ISD Due Process

CALHOUN COUNTY ISD due process involves a formal hearing before an independent hearing officer who makes a binding decision about your dispute. This is the most formal and adversarial option, appropriate when other methods haven't resolved your concerns and you need a decision about your child's special education services.

Due process is governed by IDEA and can address issues including:

  • Whether your child was appropriately identified as needing special education
  • Whether the IEP is appropriate and reasonably calculated to provide benefit
  • Whether the district implemented the IEP as written
  • Appropriate placement for your child
  • Compensatory services for services not provided

Filing a Due Process Complaint with CALHOUN COUNTY ISD

You must file your CALHOUN COUNTY ISD special education complaint in writing with the district and TEA. The filing must include sufficient detail that the district understands your specific concerns and requested relief.

File within two years of when you knew or should have known about the alleged violation. Your written complaint should include:

  • Child's name and CALHOUN COUNTY ISD school location
  • Description of the problem and facts relevant to the issue
  • Proposed resolution
  • Whether you're requesting compensatory services

Send your complaint to both CALHOUN COUNTY ISD special education director and the TEA due process unit. Keep copies for your records.

The Due Process Timeline

Once you file your CALHOUN COUNTY ISD dispute resolution complaint, specific timelines apply:

  • Mediation Opportunity: CALHOUN COUNTY ISD must offer mediation within 15 days
  • Pre-Hearing Conference: A preliminary meeting occurs to narrow issues
  • Hearing Date: Typically scheduled within 30-45 days of filing
  • Hearing Officer's Decision: Usually issued within 45 days of the hearing

These timelines can be extended if both parties agree or if the hearing officer finds good cause for delays.

What Happens During the Hearing

Both you and CALHOUN COUNTY ISD will present evidence and arguments to an independent hearing officer. You can present witness testimony, documents, evaluations, and expert opinions. The district will do the same.

Having professional representation during a due process hearing is important. Consider hiring a special education attorney near CALHOUN COUNTY or a special ed advocate CALHOUN COUNTY to help present your case effectively.

Due Process Remedies

If the hearing officer decides in your favor, CALHOUN COUNTY ISD may be ordered to:

  • Change your child's placement
  • Provide compensatory education services
  • Reimburse private school or private services costs

Hearing officer decisions are binding, though either party can appeal to court.
