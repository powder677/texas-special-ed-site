A Full Individual Evaluation is a thorough assessment designed to determine whether your child has a disability and, if so, what special education services they need. In FLORESVILLE ISD, the FIE evaluation process is governed by federal law (IDEA), state law (Texas Education Code), and FLORESVILLE ISD's own policies.

The evaluation examines your child's cognitive abilities, academic performance, social-emotional development, and physical health. FLORESVILLE ISD special education professionals use multiple assessment tools to gather comprehensive information about your child's strengths and challenges.

Many parents in FLORESVILLE ISD don't realize they have the right to request an evaluation at any time during the school year. You don't need permission from school staff or a teacher referral—your request as a parent carries legal weight in FLORESVILLE ISD.

Signs Your Child May Need FLORESVILLE ISD Evaluation

Parents in FLORESVILLE ISD should consider requesting an evaluation if their child:

  • Struggles significantly with reading, writing, or math compared to peers
  • Has difficulty paying attention or staying organized
  • Experiences behavioral challenges at school or home
  • Has delayed speech, language, or motor skills
  • Shows signs of autism spectrum disorder
  • Has hearing or vision problems affecting learning
  • Experiences emotional or social difficulties
  • Is not progressing despite classroom support or tutoring

FLORESVILLE ISD Child Find Responsibility

FLORESVILLE ISD has a child find obligation to identify all children with disabilities in the district. However, don't wait for the school to reach out—parents can and should take the initiative to request a FLORESVILLE ISD special education evaluation.

If your child attends a private school in the FLORESVILLE area or is homeschooled, FLORESVILLE ISD still has responsibilities under child find. Contact your local FLORESVILLE ISD special education office to learn about your options.

Step-by-Step: How to Request an Evaluation from FLORESVILLE ISD

Step 1: Submit Your Request in Writing

The most important rule when requesting a FLORESVILLE ISD FIE evaluation is to put your request in writing. A verbal request to a teacher or principal may not start the legal timeline, so always use written communication.

Your written request to FLORESVILLE ISD should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • The school your child attends in FLORESVILLE ISD
  • A clear statement that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation
  • Specific concerns about your child's learning, behavior, or development
  • Your preferred contact information
  • Your signature and date

Send your request via email (request a read receipt), certified mail, hand delivery, or fax to the Special Education Department at your child's FLORESVILLE ISD campus or the district office. Keep a copy for your records.

Step 2: Understand the 15-Day Response Requirement

Once FLORESVILLE ISD receives your written request for a FIE evaluation, the district has 15 calendar days to respond. During this time, FLORESVILLE ISD must contact you to discuss your concerns and provide information about the evaluation process.

FLORESVILLE ISD may propose an evaluation plan, ask clarifying questions, or request additional information about your child's history. This is a two-way conversation—the district needs your input as a parent.

If FLORESVILLE ISD disagrees that an evaluation is necessary, they must provide written notice explaining why they believe evaluation is not needed and inform you of your right to request mediation or due process.

Step 3: Review and Consent to the Evaluation Plan

FLORESVILLE ISD will prepare an Evaluation Plan detailing what assessments will be conducted, which areas of disability will be evaluated, and who will conduct the tests. You must carefully review this plan.

The FLORESVILLE ISD evaluation plan should address all areas of suspected disability. If you believe important areas are missing, request that they be added. For example, if your child has speech concerns, the plan should include speech-language evaluation.

You must provide written consent before FLORESVILLE ISD can begin the evaluation. Do not sign blank forms. Make sure you understand exactly what will be tested and when.

The FLORESVILLE ISD 45-Day Evaluation Timeline

Countdown Starts with Your Consent

Once you sign the evaluation consent form, FLORESVILLE ISD has 45 calendar days to complete the FIE evaluation and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss results. This is a critical deadline—mark it on your calendar.

The 45-day FLORESVILLE ISD 45 day timeline is required by Texas Education Code and federal law. If FLORESVILLE ISD misses this deadline without your written agreement for an extension, it violates your rights.

Note that the timeline may be extended if:

  • You and FLORESVILLE ISD mutually agree to extend it
  • Your child is absent for extended periods
  • Unusual circumstances make completion impossible

Any extension must be agreed to in writing before the original 45 days expires.

What Happens During the FLORESVILLE ISD Evaluation Period

During these 45 days, FLORESVILLE ISD will conduct comprehensive assessments. Your child may participate in:

  • Cognitive/IQ testing
  • Academic achievement testing
  • Speech and language evaluation
  • Occupational or physical therapy assessment
  • Behavioral and emotional screening
  • Vision and hearing screening
  • Classroom observations
  • Review of school records and teacher input

FLORESVILLE ISD special education evaluators should keep you informed about the process and may ask you to complete rating scales or questionnaires about your child's behavior and development at home.

Understanding FLORESVILLE ISD Evaluation Results

The ARD Meeting and Eligibility Decision

At the end of the FLORESVILLE ISD FIE evaluation period, the district will schedule an ARD meeting with you and the evaluation team. This is where you'll learn the results and whether your child qualifies for special education services under IDEA.

To qualify for special education in FLORESVILLE ISD, your child must have one of 13 eligible disability categories AND require special education services. Simply having a diagnosis isn't enough—FLORESVILLE ISD must find that the disability adversely affects educational performance.

The eligibility categories in FLORESVILLE ISD include:

  • Autism
  • Deaf-blindness
  • Emotional disturbance
  • Hearing impairment
  • Intellectual disability
  • Multiple disabilities
  • Orthopedic impairment
  • Other health impairment
  • Specific learning disability
  • Speech or language impairment
  • Traumatic brain injury
  • Visual impairment
  • Developmental delay (ages 3-9)

If Your Child Qualifies for FLORESVILLE ISD Special Education

Congratulations—if FLORESVILLE ISD determines your child is eligible, the ARD team will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). This legal document specifies the special education services, accommodations, and goals your child will receive.

You are an equal member of the FLORESVILLE ISD ARD team. Your input about your child's strengths, needs, and goals is essential. Don't hesitate to ask questions or request changes during the IEP development process.

If Your Child Does Not Qualify in FLORESVILLE ISD

If FLORESVILLE ISD determines your child is not eligible for special education, you have the right to disagree. FLORESVILLE ISD must provide written notice explaining the decision and your procedural rights, including:

  • Requesting mediation
  • Filing a due process complaint
  • Obtaining an independent educational evaluation at FLORESVILLE ISD's expense under certain conditions
  • Appealing through the State Education Agency

Even if your child doesn't qualify for an IEP, they may be eligible for a 504 Plan under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, which provides accommodations for disabilities that substantially limit major life activities.

Your Rights During the FLORESVILLE ISD FIE Process

Procedural Safeguards in FLORESVILLE ISD Special Education

FLORESVILLE ISD must provide you with written notice of your procedural safeguards at key points in the special education evaluation process. These legal protections include:

  • Right to informed consent: FLORESVILLE ISD must explain evaluations in language you understand before getting your permission
  • Right to participate: You have the right to be present and participate fully in all ARD meetings
  • Right to records: You can request and review all evaluation results and educational records
  • Right to an independent evaluation: If you disagree with FLORESVILLE ISD's evaluation, you may obtain an independent evaluation
  • Right to dispute resolution: You can pursue mediation or due process hearings if you disagree with FLORESVILLE ISD's decisions

FLORESVILLE ISD must provide these notices in your native language and in a format you can understand.

Bringing Support to FLORESVILLE ISD Meetings

You don't have to navigate the FLORESVILLE ISD special education process alone. You have the right to bring anyone you choose to ARD meetings, including:

  • A spouse or family member
  • A special education advocate
  • A special education attorney near FLORESVILLE
  • A medical professional familiar with your child
  • A trusted friend or mentor

Consider having a special ed advocate FLORESVILLE or special education attorney near FLORESVILLE attend if the process feels confusing or if you anticipate disagreement with FLORESVILLE ISD.

When to Consider a Special Education Attorney Near FLORESVILLE

If FLORESVILLE ISD denies your evaluation request, misses the 45-day deadline without justification, or disagrees with your assessment of your child's needs, consulting a special education attorney near FLORESVILLE may protect your child's rights.

A special ed advocate FLORESVILLE or attorney can help you:

  • Request a formal due process hearing
  • Demand an independent educational evaluation
  • File state complaints with the Texas Education Agency
  • Appeal FLORESVILLE ISD decisions
  • Recover compensatory education services for missed opportunities

Next Steps After Your FLORESVILLE ISD FIE Evaluation Request

Taking action today to request a FLORESVILLE ISD FIE evaluation is one of the most powerful steps you can take for your child's education. Remember:

  • Submit your request in writing and keep copies
  • Expect FLORESVILLE ISD to respond within 15 days
  • Mark the 45-day deadline on your calendar
  • Come prepared to the ARD meeting with information about your child
  • Don't hesitate to ask questions or request clarification from FLORESVILLE ISD staff
  • Reach out to parent advocates and special education professionals if you need support

Parents in FLORESVILLE ISD shouldn't feel intimidated by the process. FLORESVILLE ISD special education exists to serve your child, and you have legal rights throughout the FLORESVILLE ISD evaluation and special education process. Trust your instincts about your child's needs, document everything, and advocate firmly for the services your child deserves.
