Informal Resolution: Your First Step

Steps for Informal Problem-Solving

  1. Document the issue: Write down what happened, when it occurred, and how it affects your child
  2. Contact the appropriate staff member: Start with the special education teacher or campus special education coordinator
  3. Schedule a meeting: Request an in-person conversation rather than relying on emails alone
  4. Come prepared: Bring documentation and be specific about what you want changed
  5. Follow up in writing: Send an email summarizing the discussion and any agreements made
  6. Set a timeline: Agree on when the GALVESTON ISD staff will implement changes and how you'll measure improvement

GALVESTON ISD Mediation Process

GALVESTON ISD mediation offers a neutral approach to resolving disputes without the formality or expense of a due process hearing. A trained mediator helps both you and the district communicate and reach mutually agreeable solutions. This process is confidential and often faster than other options.

Parents in GALVESTON ISD can request mediation at any time, even if you've already filed for GALVESTON ISD due process . Mediation costs nothing to families and doesn't prevent you from pursuing other remedies if it's unsuccessful.

How GALVESTON ISD Mediation Works

Contact GALVESTON ISD Special Education Department to request mediation. The district has 10 calendar days to respond. The mediator, assigned by the region's Education Service Center, contacts both parties to schedule a session within 30 days of your request.

During the mediation session, you'll meet with the mediator, your child's special education team, and possibly district administrators. The mediator helps everyone understand different perspectives and guides you toward compromise. If you reach agreement, you'll sign a binding mediation agreement.

Tips for Successful GALVESTON ISD Mediation

GALVESTON ISD due process hearings provide a formal legal process for resolving special education disputes. You can request a hearing when you believe the district has violated your child's rights under IDEA, failed to provide appropriate services, or made incorrect placement decisions.

To initiate GALVESTON ISD due process , you must submit a written due process complaint to the district. This formal notice must include specific details about your dispute and proposed resolutions. Contact the GALVESTON ISD Special Education Department for the required complaint form.

Due Process Complaint Requirements

Your complaint must include:

  • Your child's name and address
  • The school attended or school the child is seeking admission to
  • A description of the problem and facts related to the complaint
  • How the problem affects your child's right to a free appropriate public education (FAPE)
  • Proposed solutions you've discussed with GALVESTON ISD staff
  • The date you became aware of the alleged violation

Your complaint must be filed with the GALVESTON ISD Special Education Department and a copy sent to the Texas Education Agency. GALVESTON ISD has 10 calendar days to respond to your complaint.

GALVESTON ISD Due Process Timeline

  1. You file the complaint with GALVESTON ISD
  2. GALVESTON ISD sends response within 10 days
  3. Parties can request mediation (30 days to complete)
  4. Resolution period: 30 days for GALVESTON ISD to resolve the complaint
  5. If unresolved, hearing is scheduled (typically within 45 days)
  6. Hearing officer issues decision within 45 days of hearing conclusion

TEA Complaints Against GALVESTON ISD

If you believe GALVESTON ISD has violated state or federal special education law, you can file a GALVESTON ISD TEA complaint with the Texas Education Agency. This process is separate from GALVESTON ISD due process and doesn't require you to be involved in a dispute about your individual child's services.

GALVESTON ISD TEA complaints are appropriate when the district has systematic problems, fails to implement state or federal requirements, or denies services to students with disabilities. TEA complaints can address issues affecting your child or broader concerns about GALVESTON ISD special education services.

Filing a TEA Complaint Against GALVESTON ISD

Complete the TEA complaint form available at the Texas Education Agency website. Include detailed information about the violation, dates it occurred, students affected, and actions you've already taken with GALVESTON ISD . Send the completed form to TEA's Department of Special Education Compliance.

TEA investigates GALVESTON ISD complaints and issues findings. If violations are confirmed, the agency requires GALVESTON ISD to develop a corrective action plan. This process typically takes 60-90 days.
