Understanding ARD Meetings in BARBERS HILL ISD

An ARD meeting stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal meeting—the formal process that BARBERS HILL ISD uses to discuss your child's special education eligibility, services, and goals. During a BARBERS HILL ISD ARD meeting, a team of educators, specialists, and parents comes together to create an Individualized Education Program (IEP) tailored to your child's unique needs.

The ARD process is governed by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code Section 29.001, ensuring that every student in BARBERS HILL ISD receives a free, appropriate public education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment. This meeting occurs when your child is first being evaluated for special education eligibility in BARBERS HILL ISD. The team reviews evaluation results and determines whether your child qualifies for services under one of the 13 disability categories recognized by IDEA.
