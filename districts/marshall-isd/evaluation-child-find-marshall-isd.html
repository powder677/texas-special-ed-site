MARSHALL ISD child find obligations require the district to locate, identify, and evaluate all children with suspected disabilities. As a parent in MARSHALL ISD families, you have the right to request an evaluation for your child at any time if you suspect a disability affecting education.

The MARSHALL special education team must respond to your request within specific timeframes. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code § 29.004, MARSHALL ISD cannot delay or deny your request based on:

  • Your child's current grades or test scores
  • Whether your child attends general education or special education classes
  • Whether the school thinks an evaluation is necessary
  • Your child's age or grade level
  • Prior evaluations or lack thereof

Your right to request a MARSHALL ISD evaluation request is protected by federal and state law, and MARSHALL ISD must take your concerns seriously.

Parents in MARSHALL ISD should consider requesting a MARSHALL ISD special education evaluation if their child demonstrates persistent academic, behavioral, or developmental concerns. Common reasons include:

Academic Concerns

  • Significant gaps between ability and achievement
  • Difficulty keeping up with grade-level peers despite intervention efforts
  • Reading, writing, or math skills lagging behind peers
  • Inconsistent academic performance across subjects

Behavioral and Social Concerns

  • Frequent disciplinary issues or aggression
  • Difficulty following directions or staying focused
  • Poor social skills or difficulty making friends
  • Emotional outbursts or anxiety affecting school performance

Developmental Concerns

  • Speech or language delays
  • Gross or fine motor skill delays
  • Sensory sensitivities (sounds, textures, lights)
  • Attention or memory concerns

If your child in MARSHALL ISD shows any of these signs, requesting an evaluation is appropriate and encouraged.

Step-by-Step: How to Request a MARSHALL ISD Evaluation

Step 1: Contact Your Child's Campus

Start by reaching out to your child's campus counselor, special education coordinator, or administrator. Request to speak about your concerns and your desire to pursue a MARSHALL ISD special education evaluation. You can make this request verbally, but it's best to follow up in writing.

Step 2: Submit Your Written Request

Send a written MARSHALL ISD evaluation request to the Special Education Director. Include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current grade and campus
  • Specific areas of concern (academic, behavioral, developmental)
  • Examples of how these concerns affect your child's learning
  • Any interventions already tried
  • Your contact information

Keep a copy for your records. Consider sending your request via email or certified mail to create documentation of your submission date—this is crucial for the MARSHALL ISD 45 day timeline.

Step 3: Understand the MARSHALL ISD 45 Day Timeline

Once MARSHALL ISD receives your written request, the district has 45 calendar days to complete the evaluation, obtain your consent, and provide you with results. This is the MARSHALL ISD 45 day timeline mandated by Texas Education Code § 29.004.

The clock starts the day MARSHALL ISD receives your written request. The district must:

  1. Acknowledge receipt of your request within 2 days
  2. Invite you to an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting
  3. Explain evaluation plans and obtain written consent
  4. Conduct all necessary evaluations
  5. Analyze results and hold an ARD meeting to discuss findings

Step 4: Attend the Initial ARD Meeting

MARSHALL ISD will schedule an ARD meeting to discuss the MARSHALL ISD FIE evaluation plan. At this meeting, the district explains what areas will be evaluated, which specialists will participate, and timelines. You'll receive consent forms to sign authorizing the evaluation.

This is your opportunity to:

  • Ask questions about the evaluation process
  • Request specific areas be assessed
  • Discuss any concerns or family history relevant to MARSHALL special education evaluation
  • Understand what happens next

Step 5: Evaluation Takes Place

MARSHALL ISD evaluation specialists—which may include special educators, speech pathologists, occupational therapists, psychologists, or other professionals—conduct assessments. These may include:

  • Cognitive testing
  • Academic achievement assessments
  • Behavioral observations
  • Speech and language evaluations
  • Classroom performance reviews
  • Social-emotional assessments

Step 6: Review Results at the Eligibility ARD Meeting

Within the 45 day timeline, MARSHALL ISD holds an ARD meeting to present evaluation results. The team reviews data and determines whether your child qualifies for special education services under IDEA disability categories.

If your child is found eligible, the team develops an Individualized Education Program (IEP) at the same meeting. If not found eligible, you have the right to request additional evaluation or pursue independent evaluation at MARSHALL ISD's expense in some circumstances.

Understanding the MARSHALL ISD FIE Evaluation Process

A comprehensive MARSHALL ISD FIE evaluation must be multidisciplinary, meaning multiple specialists evaluate different areas. The evaluation should be thorough, objective, and tailored to your child's specific concerns.

Key Components of MARSHALL ISD Child Find Evaluations

MARSHALL ISD special education evaluations must include assessments in all areas of suspected disability:

  • Cognitive ability – intellectual functioning
  • Academic achievement – reading, math, writing performance
  • Speech and language – communication skills
  • Physical development – gross and fine motor skills
  • Social and emotional functioning – behavior, relationships, emotional health
  • Classroom behavior and performance – how your child functions in school settings

MARSHALL ISD cannot limit the evaluation to just one area. If you believe your child has multiple areas of concern, the complete MARSHALL ISD FIE evaluation must address all suspected disabilities.

Your Rights During the MARSHALL ISD Special Education Evaluation Process

Texas law and IDEA protect your rights as a parent throughout the MARSHALL ISD special education evaluation process. Understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Right to Information and Explanation

MARSHALL ISD must explain:

  • What will be evaluated and why
  • What tests and assessments will be used
  • Who will conduct the evaluation
  • When results will be available
  • How results will be used
  • What eligibility means for your child

Right to Consent

You must provide written, informed consent before MARSHALL ISD begins any evaluation. The district cannot evaluate your child without your permission. You have the right to:

  • Revoke consent at any time
  • Request independent evaluation at district expense if you disagree with results
  • Have an independent evaluator present during MARSHALL ISD assessments

Right to Prior Written Notice

MARSHALL ISD must provide prior written notice in your native language explaining:

  • What action the district proposes
  • Why they're taking this action
  • What evaluation procedures, tests, and records they'll use
  • Your right to refuse or request modifications

Right to an Interpreter or Advocate

You can bring a friend, family member, or special ed advocate MARSHALL to any ARD meeting. If English isn't your primary language, MARSHALL ISD must provide an interpreter at district expense. If you're in the MARSHALL area and want professional advocacy support, consider hiring a special education attorney near MARSHALL or certified education advocate.

Common Questions About MARSHALL ISD FIE Evaluation

How Long Does a MARSHALL ISD Evaluation Take?

The MARSHALL ISD 45 day timeline includes everything from request receipt through final ARD meeting. However, individual assessments may take place over several weeks. Your child won't miss excessive class time, as MARSHALL ISD special education evaluations typically occur during school hours in short sessions.

What If MARSHALL ISD Says My Child Doesn't Qualify?

If the evaluation team determines your child doesn't have a disability affecting education, they're not eligible for special education services under IDEA. However, your child might qualify for:

  • 504 Plan – accommodations for conditions affecting major life activities
  • Response to Intervention (RTI) – supplemental classroom support
  • Dyslexia support – if your child has reading difficulties

You can request these alternative services from MARSHALL ISD, or request an independent evaluation.

Can I Request a MARSHALL ISD Reevaluation?

Yes. If your child is already receiving special education services, you can request reevaluation annually or whenever you believe your child's needs have changed. MARSHALL ISD must reevaluate every three years at minimum.

What If I Disagree with MARSHALL ISD Evaluation Results?

You have the right to an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at MARSHALL ISD's expense if you disagree with the district's findings. Contact MARSHALL ISD's Special Education Director to request the IEE process and details about qualified evaluators in the MARSHALL area.

Moving Forward: After Your Child's MARSHALL ISD FIE Evaluation

Once your child completes the MARSHALL ISD FIE evaluation, the results guide everything moving forward. If eligible, MARSHALL ISD develops an IEP tailored to your child's unique needs, addressing goals, services, classroom placement, and accommodations.

Remember that evaluation is not a one-time event. Your child's needs may change, and you always have the right to request updated MARSHALL ISD special education evaluation as needed. Stay involved in all ARD meetings, ask questions, and advocate for your child's success within MARSHALL ISD.
