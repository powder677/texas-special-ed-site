The ARD Meeting: Your Child's Educational Planning

The LITTLE CYPRESS-MAURICEVILLE CISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) is the cornerstone of special education planning. This meeting brings together parents, educators, and specialists to develop your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP). Understanding this process empowers you to be an active advocate for your child.

What to Expect at Your LITTLE CYPRESS-MAURICEVILLE CISD ARD Meeting

  • Evaluation Results: School personnel will present findings from assessments conducted in LITTLE CYPRESS-MAURICEVILLE CISD
  • Eligibility Discussion: The team determines if your child qualifies for special education services under federal and state law
  • IEP Development: Goals, services, and accommodations are created collaboratively
  • Placement Decisions: Your team determines the least restrictive environment for your child
  • Parent Input: Your perspective and concerns are documented and considered

Parents have the right to bring additional support to any LITTLE CYPRESS-MAURICEVILLE CISD ARD meeting. This may include an advocate, attorney, or family friend. The district cannot proceed without your consent to special education services.

Preparing for Your ARD Meeting in LITTLE CYPRESS-MAURICEVILLE CISD

Come prepared with questions, documentation of your child's progress, and clear ideas about what support your child needs. Request all evaluation reports at least five days before your meeting. Bring copies of any outside evaluations or medical records relevant to your child's educational needs. Document any concerns you have about your child's current performance in writing before the meeting.

Understanding Your Parent Rights in LITTLE CYPRESS-MAURICEVILLE CISD

LITTLE CYPRESS-MAURICEVILLE CISD parent rights are protected under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §29.001-29.017. Knowing these rights is essential to effectively advocating for your child's education.

Key Parent Rights in LITTLE CYPRESS-MAURICEVILLE CISD

  • Right to Notice: You must receive prior written notice before the district proposes or refuses to change your child's identification, evaluation, or placement
  • Right to Participate: You are a required member of all IEP and ARD teams for LITTLE CYPRESS-MAURICEVILLE CISD students
  • Right to Evaluate: You can request comprehensive evaluations at no cost to your family
  • Right to Access Records: You may review all educational records within 10 school days of request
  • Right to Independent Evaluation: If you disagree with district findings, you may obtain an independent evaluation at no cost under certain circumstances
  • Right to Due Process: You can file a formal complaint or due process hearing if you disagree with decisions made in LITTLE CYPRESS-MAURICEVILLE CISD
  • Right to Mediation: Free mediation services are available to resolve disputes without litigation

LITTLE CYPRESS-MAURICEVILLE CISD provides a Parent's Rights Notice in your preferred language at the beginning of each school year. Request a copy immediately if you don't have one.
