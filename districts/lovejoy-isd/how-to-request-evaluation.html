A special education evaluation, also called a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation), is a comprehensive assessment that determines if your child has a disability affecting their learning. In LOVEJOY ISD, this evaluation is the first step to accessing special education services and academic support.

Your child might benefit from an evaluation if:

  • They have persistent difficulties in reading, writing, or mathematics
  • They exhibit behavioral problems or difficulty following instructions
  • They have a delay in language or communication development
  • They show difficulties with attention or concentration
  • They have a history of developmental delays
  • They face significant barriers to accessing the general curriculum

Parents and educators in LOVEJOY ISD can request this evaluation at any time during the school year.

  • Right to participation: You must participate in all decisions regarding your child
  • Right to complete information: LOVEJOY ISD must explain the process to you in your language

  • Right to representation: You can bring an attorney or special education advocate to meetings

In LOVEJOY ISD, these rights are protected under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act and the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA).

Step-by-Step Process: How to Request an Evaluation in LOVEJOY ISD

Step 1: Prepare Your Written Request

The first step to requesting a LOVEJOY ISD special evaluation is to make a written request. Although teachers can refer a student, as a parent, you have the right to directly request an evaluation in LOVEJOY ISD.

Your letter should include:

  • Student's full name and age
  • Name of the school in LOVEJOY ISD they attend
  • Specific description of concerns (academic, behavioral, communication)
  • Concrete examples of how these difficulties affect learning
  • Any previous evaluations or diagnoses
  • Current medications or treatments
  • Your contact information (phone and email)

Step 2: Submit the Request to LOVEJOY ISD

Send your written request to the Director of Special Education for LOVEJOY ISD or to the specific campus your child attends. It is advisable to:

  • Send by email or deliver in person (keep a copy)
  • Mark it as "Request for Special Education Evaluation"
  • Keep documentation of the submission date
  • Consider certified mail if possible

LOVEJOY ISD must acknowledge receipt of your request within 5 business days.

Step 3: Attend the Consent Meeting

Within 15 business days of receiving your request, LOVEJOY ISD will contact you to schedule a meeting. In this meeting:

  • A LOVEJOY ISD special education team will explain the evaluation process
  • They will discuss which areas will be evaluated (academic, cognitive, behavioral, language)
  • They will ask you to sign a consent form for the evaluation
  • They will answer all your questions about the next steps

Important: Do not sign anything without fully understanding the process. You may bring a special education advocate or attorney to this meeting.

Step 4: The Evaluation

Once LOVEJOY ISD has your written consent, the evaluation team will proceed to evaluate your child. This process generally includes:

  • Cognitive tests: Evaluation of thinking and memory skills
  • Academic evaluations: Reading, writing, and math tests
  • Behavioral evaluations: Classroom observations and questionnaires
  • Language evaluations: If appropriate for LOVEJOY ISD
  • Records review: Medical and educational history

The complete evaluation in LOVEJOY ISD must be completed within 60 business days from the date consent was obtained.

Step 5: Eligibility Determination Meeting

After completing all evaluations, LOVEJOY ISD will schedule a meeting to discuss the results. In this meeting of the LOVEJOY ISD special education team:

  • All evaluation results will be presented
  • The team will determine if your child is eligible for special education services
  • If eligible, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be developed
  • Placement and service options will be discussed

You must receive a copy of the entire evaluation report at least 5 days before this meeting.
