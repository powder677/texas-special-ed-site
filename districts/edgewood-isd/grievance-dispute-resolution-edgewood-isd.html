When disagreements about your child's special education services cannot be resolved informally, EDGEWOOD ISD due process procedures provide more formal resolution mechanisms. These options are particularly important when disputes involve IEP decisions, placement, or services.

Understanding Due Process Hearings

A due process hearing is a formal legal proceeding where an impartial hearing officer listens to evidence from both the parent and EDGEWOOD ISD, then makes a binding decision. Due process rights are protected under federal law and apply to all parents of students receiving special education in EDGEWOOD ISD.

To request a due process hearing, you must submit a detailed written complaint to EDGEWOOD ISD Special Education Department and the Texas Education Agency. This complaint must describe the dispute, alleged violations, and remedies you're seeking.

Mediation as an EDGEWOOD ISD Dispute Resolution Option

Mediation offers a less formal alternative to due process for EDGEWOOD ISD families. A neutral third party (mediator) helps parents and EDGEWOOD ISD discuss concerns and reach mutually acceptable agreements. Mediation is usually faster, less expensive, and less adversarial than due process hearings.

EDGEWOOD ISD must offer mediation as an option when disputes arise. Mediation is voluntary—both parents and the district must agree to participate. However, most EDGEWOOD ISD families find mediation helpful for resolving misunderstandings and finding creative solutions.

The Mediation Process with EDGEWOOD ISD

  • You request mediation through EDGEWOOD ISD Special Education Department or TEA
  • The district provides a list of qualified mediators trained in special education law
  • You and EDGEWOOD ISD select a mutually acceptable mediator
  • A mediation session is scheduled (typically within 10-15 days)
  • Both you and district representatives meet with the mediator to discuss the dispute
  • If agreement is reached, you sign a binding mediation agreement

Many disputes with EDGEWOOD ISD are successfully resolved through mediation, avoiding the time and expense of due process hearings. The mediation agreement becomes legally binding on both parents and the district.

Filing a Complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA)

Parents can also file special education complaints directly with the Texas Education Agency, which investigates allegations that EDGEWOOD ISD violates special education laws. TEA maintains a separate complaint process from EDGEWOOD ISD's internal procedures.

When to File an EDGEWOOD ISD TEA Complaint

Consider filing an EDGEWOOD ISD TEA complaint when:

  • You believe EDGEWOOD ISD violated your procedural rights under special education law
  • The district failed to implement your child's IEP as written
  • EDGEWOOD ISD denied you meaningful participation in IEP decisions
  • The district failed to provide required evaluations or assessments
  • EDGEWOOD ISD complaint procedures didn't adequately address your concerns

Filing Requirements for EDGEWOOD ISD TEA Complaints

TEA complaints must be filed within one year of the alleged violation. You must submit your complaint in writing, include specific facts and dates, and explain how EDGEWOOD ISD violated state or federal law. TEA provides a standard complaint form available online.

Send your complaint to the Texas Education Agency's Special Education Division. Include your contact information, your child's name and EDGEWOOD ISD student ID, and a clear description of the dispute with EDGEWOOD ISD.

TEA Investigation Timeline

After receiving your complaint, TEA investigates whether EDGEWOOD ISD violated special education regulations. The investigation typically takes 30-60 days. TEA interviews relevant staff, reviews documents, and provides findings to both you and EDGEWOOD ISD.

If TEA determines EDGEWOOD ISD violated regulations, the agency issues a report identifying the violation and requiring corrective action. EDGEWOOD ISD must implement TEA's findings. While TEA cannot award compensatory services directly, TEA findings often prompt EDGEWOOD ISD to provide remedies.
