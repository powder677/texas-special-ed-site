What can I do right now if Dickinson ISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Dickinson ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Dickinson ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the special education system can feel overwhelming, but understanding the basics is crucial for advocating for your child. Special education, as defined by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas law, is specially designed instruction provided to children with disabilities who require it to succeed in school. This means that if your child has a disability that impacts their ability to learn, they are entitled to receive personalized support and services designed to meet their unique needs.

Dickinson ISD, like all school districts in Texas, is overseen by the Texas Education Agency (TEA). The TEA sets the standards and guidelines for special education programs across the state. Additionally, Dickinson ISD receives support and resources from TEA Region 4 Education Service Center, one of twenty regional centers that provide training and technical assistance to schools. This means that Dickinson ISD operates within a larger framework dedicated to ensuring that children with disabilities receive a free and appropriate public education (FAPE). Understanding this structure can help you better understand the responsibilities and resources available to your child within Dickinson ISD.

Most importantly, remember that you, as a parent, have legally protected rights throughout the entire special education process in Dickinson ISD. From the initial evaluation to the development and implementation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP), you have a voice and the right to participate in all decisions concerning your child's education. These rights are enforceable, and understanding them is your first step towards becoming an effective advocate. Begin familiarizing yourself with the TEA's Parent's Guide to the Admission, Review, and Dismissal Process. You can find it on the TEA website.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Dickinson ISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability that requires special education services, your first step is to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in writing. This is a critical step because verbal requests do not trigger the legal timelines that Dickinson ISD is required to follow. The FIE is a comprehensive assessment of your child's strengths and weaknesses in various areas, such as academic skills, communication, and social-emotional development. It helps determine if your child has a disability and, if so, what specific educational needs they have.

Once Dickinson ISD receives your written consent to conduct the FIE, the district has 45 school days to complete the evaluation. It is important to note that Child Find laws require Dickinson ISD to proactively identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities residing within the district's boundaries, regardless of whether they are enrolled in public school. Even if you haven't formally requested an evaluation, Dickinson ISD has a responsibility to identify students who may need special education services. If you have any concerns about your child's development or academic progress, don't hesitate to reach out to their teacher, counselor, or the special education department at their school. To start the process, draft a letter stating that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation for your child, [Child's Name], at [Child's School]. Keep a copy of the letter for your records, and send it to the school principal and special education director via certified mail, return receipt requested, to ensure proof of delivery.

ARD Meetings in Dickinson ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal, and an ARD meeting is the cornerstone of the special education process. This is where decisions are made about your child's eligibility for special education services, their IEP, and their placement. The ARD committee includes you, the parent, as a key member, along with school personnel such as teachers, administrators, and specialists. The ARD meeting is a collaborative process, and your input and perspective are essential.

Dickinson ISD is required to provide you with written notice at least five calendar days before any ARD meeting. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, the date, time, and location, and a list of who will be attending. During the ARD meeting, you have the right to ask questions, share your concerns, and advocate for your child's needs. Texas law allows you to request a 10-day recess of the ARD meeting at any time if you need more time to consider the information presented or seek outside advice. Remember, you are not required to sign the IEP at the meeting. You have the right to take it home, review it carefully, and seek input from others before making a decision.

Your active participation and informed advocacy are crucial for ensuring that your child receives the appropriate support and services. Dyslexia Services in Dickinson ISD

Texas law mandates that Dickinson ISD screen all students for dyslexia. Early identification is critical, as dyslexia, a language-based learning disability, can significantly impact a child's reading and writing skills. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook outlines the procedures and interventions that Dickinson ISD must follow when identifying and supporting students with dyslexia. This handbook serves as a guide for best practices in dyslexia instruction and support.

If your child is identified as having dyslexia, Dickinson ISD is responsible for providing them with structured literacy interventions. Structured literacy is a research-based approach to teaching reading and writing that focuses on systematic and explicit instruction in phonological awareness, phonics, morphology, syntax, and semantics. Depending on the severity of your child's dyslexia and their individual needs, they may qualify for a 504 Plan or an IEP. If your child has not been screened for dyslexia and you have concerns about their reading skills, request a dyslexia screening in writing from their teacher and school principal immediately.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Dickinson ISD

Discipline can be a sensitive issue, especially when it involves students with disabilities. Suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year triggers specific protections under IDEA. When this threshold is reached in Dickinson ISD, a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) must be conducted. This review determines whether the student's behavior was a manifestation of their disability or a result of the school's failure to properly implement the IEP.

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative days also constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA, requiring an ARD meeting to determine the appropriate disciplinary actions and educational services. This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a child with a disability. You must act quickly and assert your rights immediately. This kit provides essential information and resources to help you navigate the disciplinary process and protect your child's rights.

Filing a Complaint Against Dickinson ISD

If you believe that Dickinson ISD has violated your child's rights under IDEA or other special education laws, you have several options for resolving the issue. The first is filing a TEA State Complaint. This is a free process that involves submitting a written complaint to the TEA outlining the specific violations. The TEA will investigate the complaint and issue a decision within 60 days. Another option is mediation through TEA, which is also free. Mediation brings you and representatives from Dickinson ISD together with a neutral third-party mediator to try to reach a mutually agreeable resolution. You can also request a Due Process Hearing, which is a more formal legal proceeding where an administrative law judge hears evidence and makes a binding decision.

Finally, you have the option to file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR). These rights exist regardless of what Dickinson ISD staff may tell you. Remember that you are your child's best advocate, and you have the right to pursue any of these options if you believe it is necessary. If you are considering filing a complaint, the first step should be to document every incident in detail, including dates, times, locations, and names of individuals involved. This documentation will be crucial in supporting your case. Research TEA complaint form instructions to begin this process.

Frequently Asked Questions About Dickinson ISD Special Education

The questions every Dickinson ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Dickinson ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Dickinson ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Dickinson ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Dickinson ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Dickinson ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Dickinson ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Dickinson ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Dickinson ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
