Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

Discipline Rights & Grievances in Dickinson ISD: An Urgent Guide

URGENT GUIDE: Discipline Rights & Grievances in Dickinson ISD

If your child is facing disciplinary action in Dickinson ISD, particularly a suspension or potential expulsion, time is of the essence. This guide provides crucial information to protect your child's rights and navigate the grievance process. Do not delay seeking legal counsel if you feel overwhelmed or your child's rights are being violated.

The 10-Day Suspension Rule: Know Your Limits

Dickinson ISD, like all Texas school districts, operates under certain rules regarding suspensions. Generally, a school can suspend a student for up to three school days for certain offenses. However, the key issue is the *cumulative* effect. If your child is suspended for more than 10 school days in a single school year (whether consecutive or not), it triggers significant rights and potentially a Change of Placement review, particularly if your child has an IEP or 504 plan.

Track all suspensions carefully. Document the dates, reasons, and the length of each suspension. If you are approaching or have exceeded 10 days total, the school MUST consider whether the suspensions constitute a "pattern of removals." This is CRITICAL!

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

An MDR, or Manifestation Determination Review, is a legal requirement under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). It is triggered when a student with an IEP is facing a suspension that would result in a Change of Placement (see below). The purpose of the MDR is to determine if the student's misconduct was caused by, or directly and substantially related to, the student's disability, or if the misconduct was the direct result of the school's failure to implement the student's IEP.

If your child has an IEP and is facing significant disciplinary action, DEMAND an MDR immediately. The meeting must include you, relevant school personnel, and your child (when appropriate). Prepare to present evidence that links your child's behavior to their disability. If the team determines the behavior was a manifestation of the disability, the school cannot discipline the child in the same way as a general education student. The school must review and revise the IEP to address the problematic behavior.

Change of Placement: A Game Changer

A "Change of Placement" occurs when a student is removed from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days, or when there is a "pattern of removals" that total more than 10 school days in a school year, AND the removals constitute a significant change in the student's educational program. This triggers additional rights under IDEA for students with IEPs.

A Change of Placement can include being sent to an alternative educational setting (DAEP), being suspended for an extended period, or being recommended for expulsion. Protect your child! Challenge any disciplinary action that you believe constitutes an illegal Change of Placement. If your child is approaching or has exceeded 10 days of suspension, and is facing further disciplinary action, you MUST actively fight to ensure their rights are protected under IDEA.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you disagree with a disciplinary decision, you have the right to file a grievance. The first step is usually a Level 1 grievance. This typically involves submitting a written complaint to the principal or relevant school administrator.

Document everything! Your grievance should be clear, concise, and factual. State the specific disciplinary action you are challenging, the reasons you believe it is unfair or violates your child's rights (citing specific policies or laws), and the outcome you desire. Include copies of any relevant documentation, such as suspension notices, IEPs, and emails. Keep a copy of your grievance for your records.

TEA (Texas Education Agency) State Complaint

If you are not satisfied with the outcome of the grievance process within the school district, you may have the option of filing a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA investigates allegations of violations of special education laws. There are strict timelines for filing a TEA complaint, so act quickly. Review the TEA website for specific complaint procedures and requirements.

Due Process: Your Fundamental Right

Regardless of whether your child has an IEP, they are entitled to due process. This means they have the right to be notified of the charges against them, the opportunity to present their side of the story, and the right to appeal a disciplinary decision. Do not let the school railroad you or your child. Ensure all procedures are followed fairly and transparently.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
