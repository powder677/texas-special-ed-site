Special Education in McAllen ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the special education system can feel overwhelming, but understanding your rights and the processes involved is crucial for advocating for your child in McAllen ISD. Special education, as defined by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas law, is specially designed instruction and related services provided to children with disabilities who require additional support to succeed in school. These services are designed to meet your child's unique needs and help them access the general education curriculum to the greatest extent possible.

McAllen ISD operates under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA) and receives support from TEA Region 1 Education Service Center, located right here in Edinburg. These entities ensure that McAllen ISD adheres to state and federal regulations regarding special education. Remember, throughout the entire special education process, from initial evaluation to the development and implementation of an Individualized Education Program (IEP), you, as a parent, have legally enforceable rights. Don't hesitate to assert those rights and actively participate in every decision made regarding your child's education within McAllen ISD.

Understanding these rights is the first step toward ensuring your child receives the appropriate education and support they need. We will walk you through these rights and what you can do to make sure that McAllen ISD is providing what your child needs.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in McAllen ISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability that requires special education services, the first step is to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) from McAllen ISD. It is extremely important to know that this request must be submitted in writing. A verbal request, even if acknowledged by school staff, does not officially start the timeline for evaluation. Once McAllen ISD receives your written consent to evaluate, they have 45 school days to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE).

It's also important to understand Child Find. These laws require McAllen ISD to proactively identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities who reside within their boundaries, regardless of whether they are enrolled in public school. If you believe your child needs special education services, don't wait for the school to reach out; take the initiative and request an evaluation. Your written request should clearly state your concerns and the reasons why you believe your child may have a disability. For example, you can explain any challenges that your child is experiencing in school such as reading difficulties, problems with math, social or emotional challenges, or any other observations you have noticed at home or in the community. Action to take right now: Draft a written request for a special education evaluation. Keep a copy for your records, and send the original to the principal of your child's school in McAllen ISD via certified mail with return receipt requested. This provides you with proof that the school received your request.

ARD Meetings in McAllen ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal, and ARD meetings are at the heart of the special education process. These meetings bring together parents, teachers, administrators, and other relevant professionals to discuss your child's needs, develop their Individualized Education Program (IEP), and make decisions about their educational placement within McAllen ISD. The ARD committee as a whole is responsible for determining what is in your child's best educational interests. The IEP is a legally binding document that outlines the specific services, supports, and accommodations your child will receive to access their education.

McAllen ISD is required to provide you with at least five calendar days' written notice before any ARD meeting, giving you time to prepare and gather any information you wish to share. During the ARD meeting, you have the right to actively participate, ask questions, and voice your concerns. You also have the right to request a 10-day recess, or postponement, of the meeting at any time if you need more time to consider the information presented or consult with outside experts. This can be invaluable to give you a chance to process the details and consider the IEP as a whole. Remember, you are not obligated to sign the IEP on the same day as the meeting; you have the right to take it home, review it carefully, and sign it later. Your signature indicates your participation and agreement with the plan and services.

It is crucial to be prepared for your ARD meeting and to advocate for your child's needs effectively. You and the school are supposed to be on the same team. Your advocacy and expertise of your child are critical in the ARD meeting. Action to take right now: Before your next ARD meeting in McAllen ISD, download our free ARD preparation checklist. This checklist will help you organize your thoughts, gather relevant documentation, and prepare questions to ask the ARD committee.

Dyslexia Services in McAllen ISD

Texas law mandates that McAllen ISD screen all students for dyslexia. Early identification and intervention are crucial for students with dyslexia to achieve academic success. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook sets the standards and guidelines that McAllen ISD must follow when screening for and addressing dyslexia. This handbook provides comprehensive information on identifying students with dyslexia, implementing effective interventions, and providing appropriate accommodations.

If your child is identified as having dyslexia, they are entitled to receive structured literacy intervention, which is a research-based approach that focuses on explicit and systematic instruction in phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension. Depending on the severity of their needs, students with dyslexia may qualify for a 504 Plan or an IEP, which will outline the specific accommodations and services they will receive. A 504 plan may be available to students that have a disability impacting their ability to learn, but do not require intensive special education services. Action to take right now: If your child has not been screened for dyslexia, request a written screening in writing from McAllen ISD.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in McAllen ISD

When a student with a disability in McAllen ISD is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year, it triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). A Manifestation Determination Review is an ARD meeting that determines if your child's misconduct was caused by or directly related to their disability, or was a result of the school's failure to implement your child's IEP. If the ARD committee determines that the behavior was a manifestation of the disability or due to the school's failure to implement the IEP, the school cannot suspend your child and must take steps to address the underlying causes of the behavior.

Suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative days also constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA, and requires an ARD meeting to determine what steps can be taken to best support your child. This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent, and it is imperative that you act quickly. The school must follow strict timelines and procedures, and you have the right to challenge any disciplinary actions that you believe are unfair or discriminatory. This kit provides you with the information and resources you need to understand your child's rights and advocate for them effectively.

Filing a Complaint Against McAllen ISD

If you believe that McAllen ISD has violated your child's rights under IDEA or other special education laws, you have several options for filing a complaint. It is important to understand these options and choose the one that is most appropriate for your situation. Regardless of what staff at McAllen ISD may say, you have these rights to escalate your concerns. Your first option is to file a TEA State Complaint, which is a free process that is resolved within 60 days. This complaint should detail the specific violations you believe have occurred and provide supporting documentation.

Your other options for escalating a dispute are to request mediation through TEA, which is also free and provides a neutral third party to help you and McAllen ISD reach a resolution; pursue a Due Process Hearing, which is a more formal legal process where an administrative law judge hears evidence and makes a binding decision; or file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR), which investigates allegations of discrimination based on disability. Action to take right now: Familiarize yourself with the TEA Complaint process by visiting the TEA website. Understanding the process will empower you to take action if you believe your child's rights have been violated by McAllen ISD.

Frequently Asked Questions About McAllen ISD Special Education

The questions every McAllen ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in McAllen ISD? +

Submit a written request to the McAllen ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in McAllen ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does McAllen ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires McAllen ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how McAllen ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if McAllen ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. McAllen ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
