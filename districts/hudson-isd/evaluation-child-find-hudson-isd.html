The HUDSON ISD special education evaluation process is designed to identify whether your child has a disability that impacts their educational performance. Under federal law (IDEA) and Texas Education Code, HUDSON ISD is required to conduct evaluations at no cost to parents.

A Full Individual Evaluation is a comprehensive assessment using multiple evaluation methods and materials. It examines your child's academic performance, cognitive abilities, social-emotional functioning, and any other areas of concern. For families in HUDSON ISD, this evaluation is the gateway to special education services and accommodations.

HUDSON ISD's child find procedures ensure that all students with disabilities are identified and evaluated. Whether your child attends Hudson Elementary, Hudson Intermediate, or Hudson High School, the same evaluation protections apply across the entire district.

Recognizing Signs Your Child May Need Evaluation

Parents in HUDSON ISD should consider requesting an evaluation if their child experiences:

  • Significant gaps between academic performance and peers
  • Difficulty understanding or following directions
  • Speech, language, or communication challenges
  • Behavioral concerns that interfere with learning
  • Motor skill delays or coordination difficulties
  • Social interaction challenges or isolation
  • Attention or focus problems in classroom settings

Early Intervention and HUDSON ISD Child Find

The HUDSON ISD child find system is responsible for identifying students who may need special education services. However, you don't need to wait for the school to identify concerns. As a parent, you have the right to request an HUDSON ISD evaluation request at any time, regardless of your child's current performance level.

If your child previously received early intervention services (ages 0-3), those records should be shared with HUDSON ISD when your child enters school. This information can support your evaluation request.

How to Formally Request an FIE in HUDSON ISD

Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing

To initiate an HUDSON ISD FIE evaluation, submit a written request to your child's campus principal or the HUDSON ISD Special Education Department. While verbal requests can begin the process, a written request creates an official record that triggers the legal timeline.

Your written request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current campus and grade level
  • Specific areas of concern (academic, behavioral, social-emotional, physical)
  • Examples of how these concerns affect your child's learning
  • Any relevant medical, developmental, or family history
  • Date of the request
  • Your contact information

Keep a copy for your records and consider sending the request via email with a read receipt or delivering it in person with a signature confirmation.

Step 2: Understand the Timeline

Once HUDSON ISD receives your written HUDSON ISD evaluation request, the district has specific legal timelines that must be followed. The HUDSON ISD 45 day timeline is critical to understand.

Within 15 calendar days of receiving your written request, HUDSON ISD must:

  • Acknowledge receipt of your request in writing
  • Explain the evaluation procedures they will use
  • Obtain your written consent to proceed with the evaluation
  • Provide information about parent rights in special education

Within 45 calendar days of obtaining your consent, HUDSON ISD must complete the HUDSON ISD special education evaluation and conduct an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss results.

Step 3: Provide Consent

HUDSON ISD will send you a "Permission to Evaluate" form. Your written consent is required before the evaluation can begin. Review this document carefully. You have the right to ask questions about the evaluation process, the specific tests that will be used, and how results will be used.

You may revoke consent at any time before the evaluation begins. Once you sign, the HUDSON ISD 45 day timeline officially starts, and the district must complete all assessment activities within this period.

What to Expect During the HUDSON ISD Evaluation Process

Types of Assessments Included in an FIE

A comprehensive HUDSON ISD FIE evaluation typically includes assessments in these areas:

  • Cognitive/Intellectual Assessment: IQ testing and reasoning abilities
  • Academic Assessment: Reading, math, writing, and language arts achievement
  • Speech and Language Evaluation: Communication and language processing skills
  • Behavioral/Social-Emotional Assessment: Behavior rating scales and observations
  • Motor Skills Evaluation: Fine and gross motor coordination
  • Vision and Hearing Screenings: To rule out sensory issues
  • Classroom Observations: Teacher observations of your child in learning settings

The specific evaluations included depend on your child's areas of concern and the evaluation team's professional judgment.

Preparing Your Child for Evaluation

Parents in HUDSON ISD should prepare their children by explaining the evaluation in age-appropriate terms. Assure your child that these are not "tests" to pass or fail, but ways for adults to understand how they learn best.

Maintain normal sleep schedules and routines before evaluation days. On evaluation day, arrive early and ensure your child has eaten and used the restroom. A calm, positive attitude from parents helps children approach the evaluation with confidence.

Your Role During the HUDSON ISD Evaluation

Evaluators may ask to interview you as part of the HUDSON ISD special education evaluation process. Be prepared to discuss:

  • Your child's developmental history and early milestones
  • Family medical and educational history
  • Your observations of your child's strengths and challenges
  • Behaviors you notice at home versus school
  • Impact of concerns on daily functioning and family life
  • Previous evaluations or interventions

Your input is valuable and legally required to be considered as part of the comprehensive evaluation.

The ARD Meeting and Evaluation Results

Understanding Your ARD Meeting

Within the HUDSON ISD 45 day timeline, HUDSON ISD will schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss evaluation results. This meeting must include:

  • You (the parent)
  • Special education staff from HUDSON ISD
  • General education teacher(s)
  • Evaluators or someone who can explain results
  • District representative with authority to commit resources
  • Your child (when appropriate)

HUDSON ISD must provide you with evaluation results and supporting documents before or at the ARD meeting. Request this information in advance if possible so you can review it thoroughly.

Possible Outcomes of the HUDSON ISD FIE Evaluation

After reviewing evaluation results, HUDSON ISD may determine:

  • Your child qualifies for special education services: An Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be developed at your ARD meeting, outlining services and accommodations.
  • Your child does not qualify under IDEA but may qualify for Section 504 accommodations: A 504 Plan provides accommodations without special education eligibility.
  • Your child does not qualify for special education or 504: HUDSON ISD may recommend Response to Intervention (RTI) or other general education supports.

You have the right to disagree with HUDSON ISD's findings and pursue independent evaluation or dispute resolution procedures.

Your Rights as a Parent in HUDSON ISD Special Education

Key Parent Rights During Evaluation

Texas Education Code and IDEA guarantee these rights to parents in HUDSON ISD:

  • Right to Request Evaluation: You can request an HUDSON ISD FIE evaluation at any time
  • Right to Timely Evaluation: HUDSON ISD must follow the 45 day timeline for completion
  • Right to Informed Consent: You must understand and approve the evaluation process
  • Right to Participate: You have a voice in meetings and decisions
  • Right to Records: You can access your child's educational and evaluation records
  • Right to Independent Evaluation: If you disagree with HUDSON ISD results, you can request an independent evaluation at no cost
  • Right to Due Process: You can file complaints if HUDSON ISD violates your rights

Dispute Resolution Options

If you disagree with HUDSON ISD's evaluation or decisions, you have several options:

  • Request Mediation: A neutral third party helps you and HUDSON ISD reach agreement
  • File a Due Process Complaint: A hearing officer reviews your case
  • File a State Complaint: Texas Education Agency (TEA) investigates if HUDSON ISD violated special education law

These procedures are free, but many parents in HUDSON ISD area communities benefit from consulting with special education advocates or attorneys.

Preparing for Success: Tips for HUDSON ISD Parents

Documentation and Records

Before requesting an HUDSON ISD special education evaluation, gather and organize:

  • Report cards and progress reports from HUDSON ISD
  • Previous evaluations or assessments
  • Medical records or doctor's notes
  • Examples of work samples showing concerns
  • Teachers' written observations or emails
  • Any prior intervention plans or accommodations

This documentation supports your concerns and helps evaluators understand your child's history.

Advocating for Your Child

Parents in HUDSON ISD can strengthen their position by:

  • Keeping detailed records of your child's challenges and progress
  • Requesting regular updates from teachers
  • Asking specific questions about evaluation procedures
  • Bringing a trusted support person to ARD meetings
  • Taking notes during all meetings and discussions
  • Following up all conversations in writing via email

When to Seek Additional Support

Consider consulting a special ed advocate HUDSON or special education attorney near HUDSON if:

  • HUDSON ISD denies your evaluation request unreasonably
  • Results don't align with your observations or concerns
  • The district refuses to provide adequate services
  • You feel your child's needs aren't being met despite an IEP
  • Communication with HUDSON ISD becomes difficult or adversarial

Advocates and attorneys specializing in special education can help navigate complex situations and ensure HUDSON ISD follows legal requirements.

Next Steps for HUDSON ISD Families

If you believe your child needs a HUDSON ISD FIE evaluation, don't delay. Early identification and intervention make a significant difference in your child's educational outcomes. Contact your child's campus principal or HUDSON ISD's Special Education Department to request an evaluation.

Remember: you know your child best. Trust your instincts, advocate confidently, and work collaboratively with HUDSON ISD to ensure your child receives the support and services needed to succeed.

For families in HUDSON ISD seeking additional resources or guidance, organizations like the Texas Council of Administrators of Special Education (TCASE) and parent training and information centers can provide ongoing support throughout your special education journey.
