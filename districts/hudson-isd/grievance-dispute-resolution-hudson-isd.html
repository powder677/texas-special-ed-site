Understanding Your Rights as a HUDSON ISD Parent

Foundational Rights in HUDSON ISD Special Education

Before filing any complaint, understand that HUDSON ISD parents have fundamental rights under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code:

  • Informed Consent: HUDSON ISD must obtain your written consent before evaluating, placing, or significantly changing services for your child
  • IEP Participation: You have the right to participate in all IEP meetings and decisions affecting your child in HUDSON ISD
  • Independent Evaluations: When you disagree with HUDSON ISD evaluations, you can request an independent educational evaluation at district expense
  • Due Process: You can request a formal hearing before an impartial hearing officer to resolve disagreements with HUDSON ISD
  • Mediation: HUDSON ISD must offer free mediation to help resolve conflicts without formal proceedings
  • Records Access: You can inspect and copy all educational records HUDSON ISD maintains about your child

When You Might Consider Filing a Complaint

Parents in HUDSON ISD typically consider formal complaints when informal communication hasn't resolved concerns. HUDSON ISD Mediation: Free, Confidential Dispute Resolution

HUDSON ISD mediation offers parents a powerful middle option between informal discussions and formal due process hearings. Mediation is free, confidential, and often resolves disputes that informal conversations couldn't address.

During mediation, a trained neutral third party helps you and HUDSON ISD staff find common ground. The mediator doesn't make decisions but facilitates productive conversation. Mediation sessions typically last 1-2 hours. You can bring an advocate, attorney, or support person. HUDSON ISD brings relevant staff members with authority to make decisions. Many HUDSON ISD mediation sessions result in written agreements that resolve the dispute completely.

Filing a HUDSON ISD Special Education Complaint

If informal resolution and mediation don't work, you can file a formal special education complaint with HUDSON ISD or the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Understanding which type of complaint to file depends on your specific situation.

HUDSON ISD Internal Special Education Complaint

You can file a formal complaint directly with HUDSON ISD's special education department . This process is faster than filing with TEA but less formal than due process. HUDSON ISD must investigate your complaint and provide a written response within 30 calendar days.

What to include in your HUDSON ISD complaint:

  • Your child's name, school, and grade level
  • Clear description of the problem
  • Specific dates and incidents
  • How HUDSON ISD's actions violated special education law or your child's IEP
  • What resolution you're seeking
  • Copies of relevant documents (IEP pages, emails, progress reports)

Submit your complaint in writing to HUDSON ISD's special education director . Request written confirmation that the district received your complaint. Keep detailed copies of everything you submit.

Texas Education Agency (TEA) Special Education Complaint

You can also file a HUDSON ISD special education complaint with the Texas Education Agency . The TEA investigates complaints about violations of special education law and can order HUDSON ISD to take corrective action.

File a TEA complaint when:

  • HUDSON ISD has violated special education law (not just IEP implementation)
  • The problem has been ongoing despite your previous attempts to resolve it
  • HUDSON ISD has failed to follow legally mandated procedures
  • Your child may have missed critical educational time due to HUDSON ISD's failure

How to File a TEA Complaint About HUDSON ISD

Visit the Texas Education Agency Special Education Complaint Resolution website . Download the complaint form and complete it thoroughly. You must file within one year of when you discovered the HUDSON ISD violation , though earlier filing is always better.

Email or mail your completed complaint form to the Texas Education Agency. The TEA will notify HUDSON ISD that a complaint has been filed. The district then has 10 business days to respond. You'll receive a copy of HUDSON ISD's response and have an opportunity to provide additional information.

The TEA investigation typically takes 30-60 days for HUDSON ISD complaints. You'll receive a written determination of findings and any corrective actions HUDSON ISD must take. TEA complaints are public record, unlike mediation agreements.

When other methods haven't resolved your dispute with HUDSON ISD, a due process hearing before an impartial hearing officer is your most powerful option. This formal legal proceeding allows a neutral decision-maker to determine who is right.

When to Consider Due Process Against HUDSON ISD

Due process is appropriate when your dispute with HUDSON ISD involves serious matters with significant educational impact: disagreement about whether your child qualifies for special education, disputes about appropriate placement or services, or when HUDSON ISD's actions have caused meaningful educational harm.

Due process is also your option when HUDSON ISD proposes to change your child's placement and you disagree . In these situations, federal law requires that your child remains in the current placement while the dispute is resolved—a powerful protection for HUDSON ISD families.

How to Request a Due Process Hearing

Send a written due process hearing request to HUDSON ISD and the Regional Education Service Center. Your request must include:

  • Your child's name and address
  • HUDSON ISD school name
  • A description of the problem with sufficient detail that HUDSON ISD understands the issue
  • Your proposed solution
  • Whether you're requesting a hearing decision or resolution session
  • Your contact information

HUDSON ISD has 15 calendar days to schedule a resolution session with you unless you both agree to skip this step. The resolution session gives you and HUDSON ISD one final chance to resolve the dispute before the formal hearing.

The Due Process Timeline

Understanding the due process timeline helps you plan your approach. From filing your due process request with HUDSON ISD to receiving a final hearing decision typically takes 3-6 months . Complex cases may take longer.

Here's the typical HUDSON ISD due process timeline:

  1. Day 0: You file your due process request with HUDSON ISD and the service center
  2. Days 1-15: HUDSON ISD's resolution session with you (if not waived)
  3. Days 1-45: Pre-hearing phase including document exchange and preliminary conferences
  4. Days 45-120: Hearing officer conducts the hearing (may take multiple sessions)
  5. Days 120-135: Hearing officer issues written decision

What Happens During the Due Process Hearing

The hearing before an impartial hearing officer resembles a legal trial but is less formal. Both you and HUDSON ISD present evidence, call witnesses, and make arguments. You can bring an attorney or advocate to represent you. The hearing officer is neutral and has no connection to HUDSON ISD.

The hearing officer can only address issues in your original due process request . New concerns that emerge during the hearing cannot be addressed unless HUDSON ISD agrees to add them. Be thorough and specific when filing your due process request with HUDSON ISD.

The hearing officer has authority to order HUDSON ISD to provide compensatory education services if the district failed to provide a free appropriate public education. The officer cannot award money damages but can order HUDSON ISD to pay for outside evaluations, tutoring, or other services needed to remedy the violation.
