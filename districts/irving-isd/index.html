Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Irving ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Irving ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating special education can feel overwhelming, but you're not alone. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas law, special education is designed to provide individualized instruction and related services to eligible students with disabilities. This ensures your child receives the support they need to succeed academically, socially, and emotionally. This might include specialized teaching methods, therapies, assistive technology, and more, all tailored to your child's unique needs as documented in their Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Irving ISD operates under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA). The TEA provides guidance and ensures compliance with federal and state special education laws. Additionally, Irving ISD receives support from TEA Region 10 Education Service Center, which offers resources, training, and technical assistance to educators and families. Remember that as a parent, you are a critical member of your child's educational team, and Irving ISD is obligated to partner with you every step of the way.

Crucially, you have enforceable legal rights throughout the entire special education process. From initial evaluation to IEP development and implementation, you have the right to participate fully, access information, and advocate for your child's needs. It's essential to understand these rights so you can ensure your child receives the appropriate services in Irving ISD. These rights are designed to protect your child and ensure they receive a free and appropriate public education (FAPE). Don't hesitate to assert these rights; they are there to help you and your child.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Irving ISD

The first step in accessing special education services is usually an evaluation to determine if your child has a disability that impacts their educational performance. To formally request an evaluation in Irving ISD, you must submit a written request to the school principal or special education director. A verbal request does NOT start the evaluation timeline. The letter should clearly state that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) to determine eligibility for special education services. Be specific about your concerns and any areas where you suspect your child may be struggling. For example, you might mention difficulties with reading, writing, math, or social skills. Include any relevant documentation, such as previous evaluations or medical records.

Once Irving ISD receives your written consent to evaluate, the district has 45 school days to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Child Find laws also require Irving ISD to proactively identify students who may need special education services. This means that even if you haven't requested an evaluation, the school has a responsibility to identify and evaluate students who show signs of a possible disability. If you suspect your child needs special education services and hasn't been screened or evaluated, it's important to take action. Write a letter requesting an FIE today. Keep a copy for your records and send it via certified mail with return receipt requested. This provides proof that Irving ISD received your request and ensures the evaluation timeline begins promptly.

ARD Meetings in Irving ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal, and it refers to the committee that makes decisions about your child's special education program. The ARD committee is comprised of you (the parent), school administrators, teachers, special education staff, and other relevant professionals. Together, this committee develops your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP), which outlines their specific educational goals, services, and accommodations. Remember, you are an equal and essential member of this committee, and your input is vital.

Irving ISD is required to provide you with at least five calendar days' written notice before any ARD meeting. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, the date, time, location, and a list of attendees. If you need more time to prepare, remember you can request a 10-day recess at any time during the ARD meeting. This allows you to take a break, gather more information, or consult with an advocate before making any decisions. It's important to understand that you do not have to sign the IEP on the same day as the meeting. You have the right to take the IEP home to review it carefully and seek advice before signing. Never feel pressured to make a decision you're not comfortable with.

As a parent, you have the right to participate fully in the ARD process and advocate for your child's needs. Do not be afraid to ask questions, express your concerns, and offer suggestions. The ARD committee should work collaboratively to develop an IEP that meets your child's unique needs and helps them achieve their full potential within Irving ISD. Before your next ARD meeting in Irving ISD, download our free ARD preparation checklist. It will help you organize your thoughts, gather relevant information, and be prepared to advocate effectively for your child.

Dyslexia Services in Irving ISD

Texas law mandates that Irving ISD screen all students for dyslexia. This screening process aims to identify students who may be at risk for reading difficulties. If your child is identified as having characteristics of dyslexia, Irving ISD is required to provide appropriate interventions and support. The standards and procedures for identifying and supporting students with dyslexia are outlined in the 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook, which Irving ISD must adhere to. This handbook provides guidance on screening, evaluation, and evidence-based interventions.

Students identified with dyslexia in Irving ISD are entitled to structured literacy intervention, which is a systematic and explicit approach to teaching reading and spelling skills. Depending on the severity of their needs, students with dyslexia may qualify for a 504 Plan or an IEP. A 504 Plan provides accommodations and modifications to help students access the general education curriculum, while an IEP provides more intensive special education services. If your child has not been screened for dyslexia and you have concerns about their reading abilities, request a screening in writing from Irving ISD today. Early identification and intervention are crucial for helping students with dyslexia succeed.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Irving ISD

Discipline can be a challenging area for students with disabilities. It's important to know that suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative days in Irving ISD triggers specific legal protections under IDEA. Once that threshold is reached, the school must conduct a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This review determines whether the student's behavior was a manifestation of their disability or a result of the school's failure to properly implement the IEP. This ensures that students are not punished for behaviors that are directly related to their disability.

Furthermore, a suspension exceeding 10 cumulative days constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA, requiring an ARD meeting to discuss the implications of the suspension and determine whether the student's current placement is appropriate. If your child has been suspended, especially if it's approaching or exceeding 10 days, this is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face. You must act within days to protect your child's rights. The school district must provide you with notice of an MDR meeting and ensure you understand your rights and options. If your child has been suspended from Irving ISD, use our Discipline Defense Kit immediately. It will guide you through the process and help you advocate for your child's rights.

Filing a Complaint Against Irving ISD

If you believe Irving ISD has violated your child's special education rights, you have several avenues for recourse. One option is to file a State Complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This is a free process, and the TEA is required to investigate and resolve the complaint within 60 days. Another option is to request Mediation through the TEA. Mediation is a voluntary process where you and Irving ISD representatives meet with a neutral mediator to attempt to resolve the dispute. Mediation is also free and can be a less adversarial way to reach a resolution.

For more serious violations, you can request a Due Process Hearing. This is a more formal legal proceeding where an impartial hearing officer hears evidence and makes a decision about the dispute. Due Process Hearings can be complex and may require legal representation. Finally, you can file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR), which investigates allegations of discrimination based on disability. Remember, these rights exist regardless of what Irving ISD staff may tell you. You have the right to advocate for your child and ensure they receive a free and appropriate public education. Take action now to protect your child's rights.

Frequently Asked Questions About Irving ISD Special Education

The questions every Irving ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Irving ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Irving ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Irving ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Irving ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Irving ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Irving ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Irving ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Irving ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
