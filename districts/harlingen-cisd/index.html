Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Harlingen CISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Harlingen CISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the world of special education can feel overwhelming, but you're not alone. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), both federal and Texas law guarantee that children with disabilities are entitled to a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) designed to meet their unique needs. This means specialized instruction, related services, and support to help your child succeed in school. Harlingen CISD is responsible for providing these services to eligible students residing within its boundaries. The Texas Education Agency (TEA) oversees all school districts in the state, including Harlingen CISD, to ensure compliance with these laws. TEA Region 1 Education Service Center provides additional support and resources to Harlingen CISD.

Remember, as a parent, you have significant and enforceable legal rights throughout the entire special education process. From the initial evaluation to the development and implementation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP), you are a critical member of the team. Your input, concerns, and observations are vital to ensuring your child receives the appropriate support. These rights are protected by law, and it's important to understand them so you can effectively advocate for your child's needs within Harlingen CISD. Don't hesitate to ask questions, seek clarification, and assert your rights whenever necessary.

Knowing your rights and understanding the process are the first steps to ensuring your child receives the education they deserve. You have the power to be a strong advocate and partner with Harlingen CISD to create a successful learning environment for your child.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Harlingen CISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability that is impacting their educational performance, you have the right to request a special education evaluation from Harlingen CISD. This evaluation, known as a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE), is a comprehensive assessment of your child's strengths and needs. To initiate the process, you must submit a written request to the school principal or special education director at Harlingen CISD. It's important to understand that a verbal request is not sufficient to start the evaluation timeline.

Once Harlingen CISD receives your written request and you provide written consent for the evaluation, the district has 45 school days to complete the FIE. This timeframe is strictly enforced by law. Furthermore, Child Find laws mandate that Harlingen CISD proactively identify, locate, and evaluate children within its jurisdiction who may have a disability and require special education services. This means the district has a responsibility to identify students who may need help, even if you haven't specifically requested an evaluation. As your next step, write a letter requesting a Full Individual Evaluation of your child. Keep a copy for your records and send the letter via certified mail to ensure proof of delivery to Harlingen CISD.

ARD Meetings in Harlingen CISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal committee. The ARD committee is responsible for developing, reviewing, and revising your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP). The ARD meeting is where decisions are made about your child's special education services, goals, and placement within Harlingen CISD. You are a crucial member of this committee and your voice matters. It's vital that you understand your rights and actively participate in these meetings.

Harlingen CISD is required to provide you with written notice at least five calendar days before any ARD meeting. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, who will be attending, and your rights as a parent. During the ARD meeting, you have the right to ask questions, express your concerns, and provide input on your child's IEP. If you feel overwhelmed or need more time to consider the information presented, you can request a 10-day recess at any point during the meeting. This gives you time to consult with advocates, therapists, or other professionals and ensures you're making informed decisions. Furthermore, remember that you are not obligated to sign the IEP on the same day as the ARD meeting. Take your time to review the document carefully before signing.

Your participation is essential to creating an IEP that meets your child's unique needs. Understanding your rights and actively engaging in the ARD process will help ensure your child receives the appropriate support within Harlingen CISD. Dyslexia Services in Harlingen CISD

Texas law mandates that Harlingen CISD screen all students for dyslexia. This is a critical step in identifying students who may need specialized support to succeed in reading and writing. The standards for dyslexia screening, identification, and intervention are outlined in the 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook, which Harlingen CISD must adhere to. This handbook provides detailed guidance on evidence-based practices for addressing dyslexia.

If your child is identified as having dyslexia, they are entitled to structured literacy intervention, which is a research-based approach to teaching reading and spelling. Depending on the severity of their needs, students with dyslexia may also qualify for a 504 Plan, which provides accommodations and supports within the general education classroom, or an IEP, which provides more intensive special education services. If your child has not been screened for dyslexia, or if you have concerns about their reading development, request a dyslexia screening in writing from Harlingen CISD today.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Harlingen CISD

When a student with a disability is disciplined, specific procedures must be followed to protect their rights under IDEA. If your child is a special education student and is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year within Harlingen CISD, it triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This review is conducted by the ARD committee to determine if the behavior that led to the suspension was a manifestation of your child's disability. If the committee determines that the behavior was a manifestation of the disability, the district must provide supports and services to address the underlying cause of the behavior.

A suspension of more than 10 days also constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA, requiring an ARD meeting to discuss your child's placement and services. This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a special education student. You must act quickly to ensure your child's rights are protected. If your child has been suspended or is facing disciplinary action, use the Discipline Defense Kit immediately to understand your rights and advocate for your child within Harlingen CISD.

Filing a Complaint Against Harlingen CISD

If you believe that Harlingen CISD has violated your child's rights under IDEA or other special education laws, you have several options for filing a complaint. These escalation paths exist to ensure accountability and provide recourse when disagreements arise. Regardless of what Harlingen CISD staff tell you, you always have the right to pursue these options.

Your options include: (1) filing a State Complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA), which is a free process that must be resolved within 60 days; (2) requesting Mediation through TEA, which is also free and provides a neutral third party to help resolve the dispute; (3) pursuing a Due Process Hearing, which is a more formal legal proceeding; and (4) filing a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR). Understanding these options is essential for effectively advocating for your child's rights within Harlingen CISD. If you feel that Harlingen CISD has violated your child's rights, begin documenting the violations and research the TEA State Complaint process now to prepare your case.

Frequently Asked Questions About Harlingen CISD Special Education

The questions every Harlingen CISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Harlingen CISD? +

Submit a written request to the Harlingen CISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Harlingen CISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Harlingen CISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Harlingen CISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Harlingen CISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Harlingen CISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Harlingen CISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
