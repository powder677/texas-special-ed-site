Dyslexia Services in Harlingen CISD

Dyslexia Services in Harlingen CISD

Texas Dyslexia Handbook 2024

Harlingen Consolidated Independent School District (CISD) is committed to providing comprehensive dyslexia services to students in accordance with the Texas Dyslexia Handbook. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook, published by the Texas Education Agency (TEA), serves as the primary resource for identifying, assessing, and providing appropriate interventions for students with dyslexia and related disorders. This handbook provides guidelines for screening, evaluation, instruction, and accommodations. It emphasizes the importance of early identification and evidence-based, structured literacy instruction.

Parents are encouraged to review the Texas Dyslexia Handbook, which is available for download from the TEA website. Understanding the guidelines outlined in the handbook will help parents advocate effectively for their child's needs.

Mandatory Screening

Harlingen CISD conducts mandatory dyslexia screening for all kindergarten and first-grade students. This universal screening is designed to identify students who may be at risk for dyslexia. The screening process typically involves assessing foundational reading skills such as phonological awareness, letter-sound knowledge, and rapid naming. These screenings are aligned with the guidelines presented in the Texas Dyslexia Handbook and are administered by trained personnel within the district. Students who exhibit characteristics of dyslexia during the screening process will be further evaluated to determine eligibility for dyslexia services.

Structured Literacy Programs

Harlingen CISD implements Structured Literacy programs for students identified with dyslexia. Structured Literacy is a research-based approach to reading instruction that is systematic, explicit, and cumulative. These programs address all critical components of reading, including phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension. Teachers providing Structured Literacy instruction are trained in the principles of Structured Literacy and use evidence-based strategies to support students' reading development. The specific programs used may vary across campuses, but all programs adhere to the guidelines outlined in the Texas Dyslexia Handbook.

504 vs IEP for Dyslexia

Students with dyslexia may receive support through either a 504 plan or an Individualized Education Program (IEP). A 504 plan is designed for students who have a disability that substantially limits one or more major life activities, such as learning. A 504 plan provides accommodations and modifications to the learning environment to ensure equal access to education. An IEP, on the other hand, is for students with a disability that requires specialized instruction and related services. Students with dyslexia who require intensive, individualized interventions and support beyond accommodations may be eligible for an IEP under the category of Specific Learning Disability (SLD).

The determination of whether a student requires a 504 plan or an IEP is made by a team of professionals, including teachers, administrators, and parents. The decision is based on the student's individual needs and the level of support required to ensure their academic success.

Accommodations List

Accommodations are adjustments to the learning environment or instructional practices that help students with dyslexia access the curriculum and demonstrate their knowledge. Common accommodations for students with dyslexia in Harlingen CISD may include:

  • Extended time on tests and assignments
  • Use of assistive technology (e.g., text-to-speech software)
  • Preferential seating
  • Modified assignments
  • Oral testing
  • Access to notes or graphic organizers
  • Multi-sensory teaching methods
  • Reduced workload
  • Breaks during instruction

This is not an exhaustive list, and specific accommodations will be determined based on the individual needs of the student.

How to Request Evaluation

Parents who suspect their child may have dyslexia should contact their child's teacher or the school principal to express their concerns. To formally request an evaluation for dyslexia, parents should submit a written request to the school principal or the school's special education department. The request should include specific information about the student's reading difficulties and any relevant observations or documentation. Once the request is received, the school will initiate the evaluation process, which may include standardized testing, classroom observations, and review of the student's academic history. Parents will be involved throughout the evaluation process and will receive a copy of the evaluation results.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

Is my child entitled to dyslexia testing in Texas? +

Yes. Texas law requires all districts to screen students for dyslexia and related disorders in Kindergarten and First Grade, and at any grade level if a parent or teacher requests it.

What accommodations can a dyslexic child get in Texas? +

Accommodations may include extended time on tests, text-to-speech software, oral administration of tests, reduced-answer-choice formats, and use of a calculator. These can be provided through a 504 Plan or an IEP.

What is the Texas Dyslexia Handbook? +

The Texas Dyslexia Handbook is a state guidance document updated in 2024 that outlines districts' responsibilities for identifying, evaluating, and serving students with dyslexia using structured literacy methods.
