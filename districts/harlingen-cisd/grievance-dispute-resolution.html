Discipline Rights & Grievances in Harlingen CISD: URGENT GUIDE

This guide is designed to help parents navigate the often-confusing disciplinary process within Harlingen Consolidated Independent School District (CISD), especially when dealing with a child who has special needs or is facing serious disciplinary action. TIME IS OF THE ESSENCE. Understand your rights and act quickly.

The 10-Day Suspension Rule

Harlingen CISD, like all Texas school districts, operates under specific guidelines regarding suspensions. Generally, a student can be suspended for up to three school days for a single incident. However, repeatedly being suspended for smaller infractions can add up. Once your child accumulates more than 10 school days of suspension in a single academic year, significant changes and protections kick in, especially if your child has an IEP or 504 plan. This is a critical threshold. Keep meticulous records of every suspension your child receives.

What Is an MDR? (Manifestation Determination Review)

If your child with an IEP (Individualized Education Program) or a 504 plan is facing a suspension exceeding 10 school days (cumulative or consecutive), the school MUST conduct a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This meeting brings together the ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee, including you, the parent, to determine if the behavior leading to the suspension was a manifestation of your child's disability. Was the behavior caused by, or did it have a direct and substantial relationship to, the disability? Was it a direct result of the school's failure to implement the IEP?

If the MDR determines the behavior WAS a manifestation of the disability or due to a failure to implement the IEP, the suspension CANNOT proceed as planned. The school must then take immediate steps to address the issues with the IEP, behavior plan, or services. The child *may* return to their original placement. Demand this meeting IMMEDIATELY if your child is nearing the 10-day suspension mark. Don't let them suspend your child without this crucial review.

Change of Placement

A "Change of Placement" occurs when a student is removed from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days, or when a series of removals creates a pattern that constitutes a change of placement. This includes expulsions, removals to an alternative education program (AEP), or suspensions. For students with IEPs or 504 plans, any Change of Placement triggers heightened procedural safeguards under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA).

If a Change of Placement is being considered, ensure a full ARD meeting is held. Advocate fiercely for your child's needs. Question the appropriateness of the proposed placement. Request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at the district's expense if you disagree with the school's evaluation. The burden is on the school to prove the new placement is appropriate and addresses your child's needs.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe Harlingen CISD has violated your child's rights regarding discipline or special education, you have the right to file a grievance. The first step is to file a Level 1 Grievance with the campus principal. Do this in writing and be SPECIFIC. Clearly state the policy or law you believe was violated, the date of the incident, and the specific actions taken that you are grieving. Include all supporting documentation. Keep a copy of everything you submit. The principal has a limited time (typically 10 school days - confirm with Harlingen CISD policy) to respond. If you are unsatisfied with the principal's response, you can escalate the grievance to Level 2 (the Superintendent) and then to Level 3 (the School Board).

TEA State Complaint

You also have the right to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This is a formal complaint alleging violations of IDEA. This can be filed concurrently with a grievance, or after exhausting the grievance process. You MUST file within one year of the alleged violation. The TEA will investigate your complaint and issue a decision. Visit the TEA website for the complaint form and instructions.

Due Process

IDEA provides parents with the right to request a due process hearing to resolve disputes with the school district regarding the identification, evaluation, or educational placement of a child with a disability. This is a legal proceeding similar to a court trial. You will need legal representation. This is a significant step and should be considered if other methods of dispute resolution have failed. Contact Disability Rights Texas or an education attorney immediately if you believe due process is necessary.

Remember, DOCUMENT EVERYTHING. Time is of the essence. Advocate fiercely for your child's rights. Don't be afraid to ask questions and demand answers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
