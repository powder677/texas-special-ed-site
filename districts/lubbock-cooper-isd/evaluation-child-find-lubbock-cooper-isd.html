A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine whether your child qualifies for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). In LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD, an FIE goes beyond basic screening to examine your child's cognitive abilities, academic performance, social-emotional functioning, and physical development.

Parents in LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD should know that an FIE is different from classroom assessments or benchmark testing. This evaluation is a detailed, multi-faceted process that includes:

  • Psychological and cognitive testing
  • Academic achievement assessments
  • Speech and language evaluations (if needed)
  • Occupational therapy assessments (if needed)
  • Behavioral and social-emotional evaluations
  • Medical and developmental history review
  • Classroom observation and teacher input

The goal of a LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD special education evaluation is to create a complete picture of your child's strengths, challenges, and educational needs.

LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD has a legal obligation under IDEA to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities who may need special education services. This process is called "Child Find." Understanding this responsibility empowers parents in LUBBOCK-COOPER to advocate effectively for their children.

While LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD staff members actively look for signs that a student may need evaluation, you as a parent have the right to request an evaluation at any time. You do not need to wait for school staff to identify concerns. The district cannot refuse your request without proper documentation.

The LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD child find process includes:

  • Universal screening in early grades
  • Teacher referrals based on classroom performance
  • Parent-initiated requests
  • Evaluation of students who struggle academically or behaviorally
  • Assessment of students with suspected disabilities

Step-by-Step: How to Request an Evaluation in LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD

Step 1: Prepare Your Request

Before you formally request a LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD evaluation request, gather documentation of your concerns. This strengthens your case and helps the evaluation team understand your perspective.

Document the following:

  • Specific behaviors or academic struggles you've observed
  • When these concerns began
  • How they impact your child's learning or daily functioning
  • Any interventions you've tried at home
  • Feedback from teachers or previous schools
  • Your child's developmental milestones and medical history

Step 2: Submit Your Written Request

In LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD, you should submit your evaluation request in writing. While a verbal request may initiate discussion, a written request creates an official record and starts the legal timeline.

Send your request to:

  • Your child's campus special education coordinator
  • The Director of Special Education for LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD
  • Your child's principal

Your letter should include:

  • Your child's full name, grade level, and campus
  • Specific areas of concern (academic, behavioral, speech, motor skills, etc.)
  • Examples of struggles or differences you've noticed
  • A clear statement: "I am requesting a Full Individual Evaluation under IDEA"
  • Your contact information
  • Your preferred method of communication

Keep a copy for your records and consider sending the request via email or certified mail so you have proof of delivery.

Step 3: Receive Notification and Consent Form

Once LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD receives your request for evaluation, the district must provide you with written notice explaining the evaluation procedures, your parental rights, and the evaluation plan within a reasonable timeframe. You will also receive a Permission to Evaluate form.

This notice should explain:

  • What will be evaluated and why
  • What assessment tools will be used
  • Your rights as a parent throughout the process
  • The timeline for completing the evaluation
  • How results will be communicated

Step 4: Sign the Permission to Evaluate Form

LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD cannot begin the evaluation without your written consent. This is a critical protection for parents. You have the right to ask questions about the evaluation plan before signing.

Before you sign, make sure you understand:

  • Which specific evaluations will be conducted
  • Who will conduct each assessment
  • How long the entire process will take
  • What information will be gathered and from whom
  • Your right to include independent evaluations

Do not hesitate to request clarification or modifications to the evaluation plan. You can request additional areas of evaluation if you believe they're necessary.

The LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD 45-Day Timeline Explained

Texas Education Code and IDEA require that LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD complete the FIE within 45 calendar days from the date you sign the Permission to Evaluate form. This LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD 45 day timeline is a legal requirement, not a suggestion.

Understanding this timeline helps you track progress and know when to expect results:

  • Days 1-10: Initial assessments and data collection begin; teachers may provide input
  • Days 10-35: Formal testing and evaluations are conducted; observations occur in classroom and other settings
  • Days 35-45: Final assessments are completed; results are analyzed and compiled
  • Day 45 or before: You receive the Evaluation Report and notice of meeting for Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD)

If LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD cannot meet the 45-day deadline due to circumstances beyond the district's control, they must provide written explanation and a specific date when the evaluation will be complete. Extensions are rare and must be documented.

Mark your calendar 45 days from when you sign the permission form. If you haven't heard from LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD by that date, contact the Special Education department immediately.

What to Expect During the Evaluation Process

When your child is undergoing a LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD special education evaluation, you may wonder what actually happens. Here's what the process typically involves:

Assessment Activities

Your child will participate in various tests and activities designed to assess different areas:

  • Cognitive testing: Problem-solving, reasoning, and processing ability
  • Academic assessment: Reading, math, and writing skills
  • Behavior rating scales: Teachers and parents complete questionnaires about behavior
  • Classroom observation: Evaluators watch your child in the classroom setting
  • Interviews: School staff and parents discuss concerns and strengths

Information Gathering

LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD will request information from you about:

  • Your child's birth and developmental history
  • Medical conditions or medications
  • Family history of learning or developmental concerns
  • Behavior at home and in the community
  • Strengths and interests

Provide detailed, honest information. The more the evaluation team knows, the more accurate their assessment will be.

Understanding Your Evaluation Results and ARD Meeting

After LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD completes the FIE, you'll receive the Evaluation Report and notice of an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. This meeting is where the evaluation team presents findings and discusses whether your child qualifies for special education services.

Before the ARD Meeting

Request a copy of the Evaluation Report at least 48 hours before the meeting. This gives you time to:

  • Review the findings thoroughly
  • Ask clarifying questions
  • Identify areas you agree with and areas you disagree with
  • Prepare your input for the meeting

At the ARD Meeting

The team will determine whether your child has a disability that requires special education services. The evaluation must show both:

  • A diagnosed disability or condition
  • A need for specially designed instruction due to the disability

If the team determines your child qualifies, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be developed. If they determine your child does not qualify, you have the right to request an independent evaluation at district expense or pursue mediation or due process.

Federal law and Texas Education Code protect your rights throughout the LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD special education evaluation process. Understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively:

  • Right to Request: You can request an evaluation anytime, for any reason
  • Right to Written Notice: LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD must provide all notices in your home language
  • Right to Consent: No evaluation can begin without your written permission
  • Right to Timely Completion: The evaluation must be completed within 45 days
  • Right to Comprehensive Assessment: Your child must be evaluated in all areas of suspected disability
  • Right to Independent Evaluation: You can request an independent evaluation at district expense if you disagree with LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD results
  • Right to Participation: You must be included in the ARD meeting and all decisions
  • Right to Interpreter Services: If you need an interpreter, LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD must provide one

Common Questions Parents Ask About LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD Evaluations

Can LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD Refuse My Evaluation Request?

No. While LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD can request additional information if your request is vague, they cannot refuse a clear request for evaluation. If they deny your request, ask for the reason in writing and contact the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

What if My Child Attends a Private School in the LUBBOCK-COOPER Area?

LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD still has responsibility for finding children with disabilities in private schools within district boundaries. You can request an evaluation through the district.

Can I Request Specific Evaluations in My LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD Request?

Yes. If you have concerns about speech, occupational therapy, or specific areas, include these in your written request. LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD must evaluate all areas of suspected disability.

What Happens If LUBBOCK-COOPER ISD Misses the 45-Day Deadline?

Document the missed deadline with dates and communications. Contact the Special Education director and request a reason in writing. If the delay was unjustified, you may be entitled to compensatory services. Consider consulting a special education attorney near LUBBOCK-COOPER.
