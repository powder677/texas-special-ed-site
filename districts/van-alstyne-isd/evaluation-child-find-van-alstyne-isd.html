VAN ALSTYNE ISD, serving 2,612 students in the VAN ALSTYNE area, is committed to identifying students who may need special education services. A Full Individual Evaluation is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine whether your child has a disability and whether they qualify for special education services under federal and Texas law.

The evaluation process in VAN ALSTYNE ISD special education is governed by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and the Texas Education Code. These regulations ensure that every student receives a thorough, unbiased assessment conducted by qualified professionals.

What Is a Full Individual Evaluation?

An FIE is a complete assessment that examines your child's cognitive abilities, academic achievement, social-emotional functioning, and behavioral characteristics. For families in VAN ALSTYNE ISD, this evaluation involves multiple specialists and assessment tools to create a clear picture of your child's strengths and needs.

How to Initiate Child Find in VAN ALSTYNE ISD

Parents can trigger VAN ALSTYNE ISD child find through several methods:

  • Written Request: Submit a formal written request for a special education evaluation to the VAN ALSTYNE ISD special education department
  • Verbal Request: Contact your child's campus principal, classroom teacher, or school counselor to express concerns
  • Teacher Referral: Teachers or other school staff may refer your child for evaluation if they observe potential disability indicators
  • Pre-Kindergarten Screening: VAN ALSTYNE ISD offers screening programs that may identify children needing further evaluation

Once you've initiated contact, VAN ALSTYNE ISD must acknowledge your request and begin the process within established timelines.

The VAN ALSTYNE ISD 45 Day Timeline: What You Need to Know

One of the most important aspects of requesting a VAN ALSTYNE ISD evaluation is understanding the legal timeline. By Texas law, VAN ALSTYNE ISD has 45 calendar days from receiving your written request to complete the Full Individual Evaluation and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting.

Breaking Down the 45-Day Timeline

Days 1-5: VAN ALSTYNE ISD receives your written request and notifies you of receipt. The district begins planning what assessments will be conducted.

Days 6-40: VAN ALSTYNE ISD special education specialists conduct various assessments. Your permission is required before testing begins, which extends the timeline if consent is delayed.

Days 41-45: VAN ALSTYNE ISD compiles results and schedules your ARD committee meeting to discuss findings and determine eligibility.

What Can Extend This Timeline?

Several factors can legitimately extend the VAN ALSTYNE ISD 45 day timeline:

  • Parent request for delay or continuance
  • Absence of required parental consent documentation
  • Need for independent evaluations or outside assessments
  • Inability to schedule assessment appointments within the window
  • Medical or behavioral issues affecting test administration

Always ask VAN ALSTYNE ISD for written notification if extensions are necessary and the new completion date.

Step 1: Prepare Your Written Request

While verbal requests to schools can trigger the process, submitting a written request for evaluation to VAN ALSTYNE ISD creates a documented record and formally starts the 45-day clock. Your letter should:

  • State clearly: "I am requesting a Full Individual Evaluation for special education eligibility"
  • Include your child's name, grade, and current school within VAN ALSTYNE ISD
  • Briefly describe your concerns about academic performance, behavior, or development
  • Provide your contact information and preferred communication method
  • Request written acknowledgment of receipt

Step 2: Submit to the Right VAN ALSTYNE ISD Contact

Address your request to the VAN ALSTYNE ISD special education director or coordinator. You can also submit to your campus principal. Send via email (requesting read receipt), certified mail, or hand-deliver with a signature confirming receipt. This ensures VAN ALSTYNE ISD has documented proof of your request date.

Step 3: Provide Informed Consent

VAN ALSTYNE ISD must send you a Notice of Proposed Evaluation and request your written consent before assessments begin. This document explains what will be tested, who will conduct evaluations, and your rights. Do not skip this step—VAN ALSTYNE ISD cannot proceed without your signed consent.

Step 4: Participate in Assessment Planning

VAN ALSTYNE ISD special education staff should discuss what assessments are appropriate for your child. Ask questions about:

  • Specific tests being administered
  • Qualifications of evaluators
  • Timeline for each assessment
  • How results will be communicated to you

Step 5: Gather Supporting Documentation

While VAN ALSTYNE ISD conducts their evaluation, collect relevant records:

  • Previous school records and progress reports
  • Medical records or health concerns
  • Developmental history (milestones achieved)
  • Documentation of intervention attempts at home or school
  • Notes about behavioral or academic struggles

Share these with VAN ALSTYNE ISD evaluators to provide comprehensive context.

Step 6: Prepare for the ARD Meeting

Near the end of the 45-day timeline, VAN ALSTYNE ISD schedules your Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committee meeting. This meeting discusses evaluation results and determines eligibility. Prepare by:

  • Reviewing evaluation results VAN ALSTYNE ISD provides before the meeting
  • Writing down questions or concerns
  • Considering what supports your child needs
  • Bringing your own documentation or records

Your Rights as a VAN ALSTYNE ISD Parent During Evaluation

The Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) guarantees specific rights to parents of students in VAN ALSTYNE ISD:

Right to Independent Evaluation

If you disagree with VAN ALSTYNE ISD's evaluation results, you have the right to request an independent evaluation at public expense. VAN ALSTYNE ISD must either agree or go to due process to prove their evaluation was appropriate.

Right to Prior Written Notice

VAN ALSTYNE ISD must provide written notice before proposing or refusing to initiate any change to your child's identification, evaluation, or special education placement.

Right to Participate

You are a full participant in VAN ALSTYNE ISD's evaluation and ARD process. Your input about your child's strengths, concerns, and needs is essential.

Right to Records Access

You can request to review all assessment results, reports, and records VAN ALSTYNE ISD has compiled about your child.

What Happens After the VAN ALSTYNE ISD Evaluation Is Complete

Receiving the Evaluation Report

VAN ALSTYNE ISD must provide you a comprehensive evaluation report explaining findings, how data relates to your child's suspected disability, and recommendations. The report should be understandable—ask VAN ALSTYNE ISD staff to clarify any terminology or findings you don't understand.

Eligibility Determination

At your ARD meeting, the VAN ALSTYNE ISD team discusses whether your child qualifies for special education under one of thirteen disability categories. Qualifying categories include:

  • Specific Learning Disability
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Speech/Language Impairment
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Visual Impairment
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Deaf-Blindness

Individualized Education Program (IEP) Development

If your child qualifies, VAN ALSTYNE ISD develops an IEP at your ARD meeting. This document outlines your child's annual goals, specific services, modifications, and placement in the least restrictive environment.

Common Questions from VAN ALSTYNE ISD Parents About Evaluations

Can VAN ALSTYNE ISD refuse to evaluate my child?

VAN ALSTYNE ISD cannot refuse an evaluation request without documented evidence that your child does not have a disability. If they refuse, they must provide written explanation. You can request a due process hearing if you disagree with their refusal.

What if my child has already been receiving intervention at VAN ALSTYNE ISD?

Response to intervention (RTI) data is helpful but cannot prevent evaluation. If your child continues struggling despite intervention, VAN ALSTYNE ISD should still evaluate for potential disability.

Will the evaluation cost money?

No. VAN ALSTYNE ISD must provide evaluation at no cost to parents. If you request an independent evaluation after disagreeing with VAN ALSTYNE ISD results, the district covers the cost unless they prove theirs was appropriate.

What if my child is already in private school?

VAN ALSTYNE ISD has child find obligations even for private school students. Contact VAN ALSTYNE ISD special education to request evaluation.
