Understanding the ARD Meeting and IEP in HIDALGO ISD

The HIDALGO ISD ARD meeting is where educators, specialists, and parents come together to determine if a child qualifies for special education services and, if so, what services and supports they'll receive. Every decision made during the HIDALGO ISD IEP process must be based on the child's individual needs, strengths, and abilities. In HIDALGO ISD, the ARD committee has the authority to make critical decisions about placement, services, and educational goals that directly impact your child's success.

Understanding HIDALGO ISD IEP Goals and Services

HIDALGO ISD IEP goals are measurable objectives your child will work toward during the school year. These goals form the heart of your child's Individualized Education Program and guide all instruction in HIDALGO ISD special education settings.

What Makes Strong HIDALGO ISD IEP Goals?

Effective HIDALGO ISD IEP goals are SMART:

  • Specific: Clearly states what skill your child will learn
  • Measurable: Includes a concrete way to measure progress
  • Achievable: Realistic for your child to accomplish in one year
  • Relevant: Addresses your child's actual needs and priorities
  • Time-bound: Includes a specific timeline for achievement

During your HIDALGO ISD ARD meeting, don't accept vague goals. Ask HIDALGO ISD staff to explain exactly how progress will be measured and how often you'll receive updates.
