Understanding ARD Meetings in HARLANDALE ISD

An ARD meeting is the formal process through which HARLANDALE ISD determines whether your child qualifies for special education services and develops an individualized education plan. The term "ARD" stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal—representing the three primary functions of these critical meetings for families throughout HARLANDALE.

HARLANDALE ISD follows Texas Education Code (TEC) §614.34, which requires ARD committees to meet at least once every school year to review your child's progress. These meetings bring together parents, educators, and specialists to make collaborative decisions about your child's educational needs and services.

Who Participates in a HARLANDALE ISD ARD Meeting?

The ARD committee for HARLANDALE ISD families typically includes:

  • Parents or legal guardians (you have the right to bring support persons)
  • Special education director or designee from HARLANDALE ISD
  • General education teacher (if your child attends mainstream classes)
  • Special education teacher or service provider
  • School counselor or administrator
  • Your child (when appropriate, especially for transition planning)
  • Other specialists as needed (speech therapist, occupational therapist, school psychologist, etc.)

Parents in HARLANDALE ISD have the right to bring an advocate, attorney, or family member to support them during the meeting. This is particularly valuable if you want professional guidance through the HARLANDALE ISD ARD process.

Step-by-Step: The HARLANDALE ISD ARD Meeting Process

Step 1: Request or Referral for Evaluation

The first step in the HARLANDALE ISD ARD process begins with either a referral or your request for an evaluation. Teachers, parents, or other school staff can request that HARLANDALE ISD evaluate your child for special education eligibility within 30 school days of the referral.

To initiate this process for your child in HARLANDALE ISD:

  1. Submit a written request to the special education department
  2. Provide specific concerns about your child's learning or development
  3. Request in writing that evaluation begin immediately
  4. Keep copies of all correspondence with HARLANDALE ISD

Step 2: Receive Notice of Evaluation and Consent

HARLANDALE ISD must provide you with written notice before conducting any evaluation. This notice explains the evaluation procedures, types of assessments, and your rights as a parent. You must give written consent before HARLANDALE ISD can begin the evaluation process.

Important timeline: HARLANDALE ISD has 60 calendar days to complete the evaluation after receiving your written consent. Parents in HARLANDALE ISD should mark this deadline and follow up if needed.

Step 3: Comprehensive Evaluation

During this phase, HARLANDALE ISD conducts comprehensive assessments in all areas related to your child's suspected disability. Evaluations may include cognitive testing, academic achievement assessments, speech and language evaluations, occupational therapy assessments, and behavioral observations.

You have the right to independent evaluations outside HARLANDALE ISD if you disagree with school findings. If HARLANDALE ISD refuses to pay for an independent evaluation, you can request a due process hearing.

Step 4: Scheduling the Initial ARD Meeting

Once evaluations are complete, HARLANDALE ISD must schedule an ARD meeting within a reasonable timeframe. The school will provide you with written notice of the meeting date, time, and location at least 10 calendar days in advance. For HARLANDALE ISD families, this notice should include:

  • Meeting purpose and date/time
  • List of required ARD committee members
  • Your right to bring additional participants
  • Information about interpreter services if needed

Step 5: The Initial ARD Meeting

At your HARLANDALE ISD ARD meeting, the committee will review evaluation results and determine whether your child qualifies for special education services. This is the "Admission" component of the ARD process for HARLANDALE ISD families.

What happens during the meeting:

  1. Review and discuss evaluation results
  2. Determine eligibility based on disability categories under IDEA
  3. If eligible, develop the IEP with your input
  4. Discuss placement and service options
  5. Establish goals and objectives
  6. Sign IEP documents (you can take time to review before signing)

Remember: you don't have to agree immediately. Parents in HARLANDALE ISD can request time to review documents, consult with advocates, or schedule a follow-up meeting if needed.

Developing Your Child's IEP in HARLANDALE ISD

Understanding HARLANDALE ISD IEP Goals

HARLANDALE ISD IEP goals are the heart of your child's special education plan. These goals must be specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound (SMART). Each goal should address your child's unique needs identified during evaluation.

For HARLANDALE ISD families, common goal areas include:

  • Academic skills (reading, math, writing)
  • Communication and language development
  • Social-emotional and behavioral skills
  • Fine and gross motor skills
  • Functional living and vocational skills (for older students)
  • Self-advocacy and independence skills

Strong HARLANDALE ISD IEP goals should include baseline data (where your child is currently), measurable objectives, and progress monitoring strategies. Don't hesitate to ask clarifying questions—goals should be clear and understandable to all HARLANDALE ISD ARD committee members and parents.

Services and Accommodations in HARLANDALE ISD

HARLANDALE ISD must provide a free appropriate public education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment (LRE). The IEP document specifies:

  • Related services: Speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, physical therapy, etc.
  • Special education services: Hours and frequency of specialized instruction
  • Accommodations: Modifications to how your child learns (extra time, assistive technology, modified assignments)
  • Modifications: Changes to curriculum content or expectations
  • Placement options: General education classroom, resource room, or specialized program within HARLANDALE ISD

Placement decisions for HARLANDALE ISD students must prioritize inclusion in regular classrooms to the maximum extent appropriate. Your input as a parent is critical in determining the right setting for your child.

Your Rights in the HARLANDALE ISD ARD Process

HARLANDALE ISD ARD Rights Every Parent Should Know

Federal and state laws protect parents' rights throughout the HARLANDALE ISD ARD process. Understanding these rights ensures you can advocate effectively for your child:

  • Right to notice: HARLANDALE ISD must provide written notice before evaluation or changes to services
  • Right to informed consent: You must agree to evaluation and initial IEP placement
  • Right to participate: Meaningful participation in all ARD decisions affecting your child
  • Right to review records: Access to all educational and evaluation records
  • Right to independent evaluation: Obtain evaluations outside HARLANDALE ISD at no cost if you disagree with school assessments
  • Right to due process: File a due process complaint if you disagree with HARLANDALE ISD's decisions
  • Right to interpretation: Meetings conducted in your native language if needed
  • Right to additional participants: Bring advocates, attorneys, or supporters to HARLANDALE ISD ARD meetings

Preparing for Your HARLANDALE ISD ARD Meeting

HARLANDALE ISD ARD Preparation Checklist

Proper preparation for your HARLANDALE ISD ARD meeting significantly impacts outcomes. Use this checklist to get ready:

  1. Request all evaluation reports from HARLANDALE ISD at least one week before the meeting
  2. Review reports thoroughly and highlight areas of concern or disagreement
  3. Gather documentation of your child's strengths, challenges, and needs at home
  4. List your questions and concerns to discuss during the HARLANDALE ISD ARD meeting
  5. Identify specific goals you want addressed in the IEP
  6. Consider bringing a support person—a special education advocate, attorney near HARLANDALE, family member, or trusted friend
  7. Practice assertive communication strategies beforehand
  8. Bring written notes to reference during the meeting
  9. Prepare a list of questions about services, placement, and progress monitoring

Communicating Effectively at Your HARLANDALE ISD ARD Meeting

During your HARLANDALE ISD ARD meeting, focus on collaborative problem-solving. Share observations from home, ask clarifying questions, and express concerns professionally. If HARLANDALE ISD proposes services or goals you disagree with, say so respectfully and ask for alternatives.

Take notes during the meeting and ask for clarification on any terminology. You can request that HARLANDALE ISD make changes to the IEP before you sign. Don't feel pressured to agree immediately—take the documents home to review if needed.

Annual Reviews and Changes to Your IEP in HARLANDALE ISD

Reviewing Progress in HARLANDALE ISD

HARLANDALE ISD must hold an ARD meeting at least annually to review your child's progress toward IEP goals. These "Review" meetings assess whether goals are being met and whether changes to services are needed.

Between annual HARLANDALE ISD ARD meetings, you have the right to request a meeting at any time if you believe changes are necessary. Additionally, HARLANDALE ISD must provide you with progress reports on IEP goals at least as frequently as progress reports for non-disabled students.

Triennial Reevaluations in HARLANDALE ISD

Every three years, HARLANDALE ISD must conduct a comprehensive reevaluation to determine if your child continues to qualify for special education. Reevaluations follow the same process as initial evaluations. You must consent before HARLANDALE ISD begins the reevaluation.

Understanding Dismissal from Special Education in HARLANDALE ISD

The "Dismissal" component of the ARD process addresses when students exit special education services. HARLANDALE ISD can propose dismissal only if your child no longer meets eligibility criteria or if you request it.

Before HARLANDALE ISD dismisses your child from special education, the ARD committee must document that:

  • Your child no longer has a disability requiring special education
  • Your child can succeed in general education with standard accommodations
  • A reevaluation confirms ineligibility
  • Dismissal is documented in the IEP with your agreement

Parents in HARLANDALE ISD have the right to request a due process hearing if they disagree with dismissal recommendations.

If your child hasn't been evaluated by HARLANDALE ISD, schedule a meeting with your child's teacher or the special education department to discuss your concerns. If your child already has an IEP, prepare thoroughly for your next HARLANDALE ISD ARD meeting using the strategies outlined in this guide.

Remember: you are your child's most important advocate. HARLANDALE ISD families are protected by strong federal and state laws ensuring your child receives appropriate special education services. By understanding the HARLANDALE ISD ARD process and your rights, you're equipped to participate meaningfully in your child's educational planning and success.
