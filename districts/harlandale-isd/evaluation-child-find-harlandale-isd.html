HARLANDALE ISD has a legal obligation under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with suspected disabilities. This responsibility is known as "child find." Whether your child attends a public school, charter school, or private school in the HARLANDALE area, the district must respond to your evaluation concerns.

The child find obligation means that HARLANDALE ISD cannot ignore signs of disability or delay evaluations based on your child's age, enrollment status, or academic performance. If you suspect your child needs special education services, HARLANDALE ISD is required by law to take your concerns seriously and move forward with assessment.

This is an important protection for HARLANDALE ISD families. You don't need a formal diagnosis or medical documentation to request an evaluation—your observations and concerns are enough to trigger the district's obligation to evaluate.

Multiple people can initiate an evaluation request in HARLANDALE ISD:

  • Parents or guardians – You have the right to request an evaluation at any time
  • Teachers and school staff – Educators can refer students for evaluation
  • The student – Students aged 18 and older can request their own evaluation
  • Medical professionals – Doctors or therapists can recommend evaluation to the district
  • Other district personnel – Counselors, administrators, or special education coordinators may initiate referrals

As a parent in HARLANDALE ISD, you have equal authority to request evaluation. You don't need permission from teachers or administrators—your request triggers the district's legal obligation to respond.

Special Considerations for HARLANDALE ISD Parents

Some parents worry about requesting evaluation because they fear negative consequences or believe their concerns won't be taken seriously. In HARLANDALE ISD, like all Texas districts, you are protected from retaliation. The district cannot penalize you, your child, or any family member for requesting a HARLANDALE ISD special education evaluation.

Making a formal request ensures HARLANDALE ISD documents your concern and begins the required timeline. Here's how to do it properly:

Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing

While HARLANDALE ISD may accept verbal requests, always submit your evaluation request in writing. This creates an official record and ensures the district can't claim they didn't receive your request. Send a letter or email to your child's school principal and the special education department.

Your written request should include:

  • Your child's full name, date of birth, and current grade level
  • Your name and contact information
  • A clear statement that you're requesting a Full Individual Evaluation
  • Specific areas of concern (academic performance, behavior, speech, social skills, etc.)
  • Examples of what you've observed at home or school
  • The date you're submitting the request

You might write: "I am formally requesting a full individual evaluation for my child, [name], to assess suspected disabilities in the areas of reading and social-emotional development. I have observed [specific examples] that concern me."

Step 2: Send to the Right HARLANDALE ISD Contact

Address your request to:

  • Your child's building principal
  • HARLANDALE ISD Special Education Department
  • The Director of Special Education for HARLANDALE ISD

Send your request via email with read receipt confirmation, hand delivery with a signature, or certified mail. Keep copies for your records. This ensures HARLANDALE ISD cannot claim they didn't receive your formal evaluation request.

Step 3: Document Everything

After submitting your request to HARLANDALE ISD, maintain a timeline of all communications. Note the date you submitted the request, who received it, and any responses from the district. This documentation becomes important if you need to advocate for timely evaluation.

The HARLANDALE ISD 45-Day Evaluation Timeline

Once HARLANDALE ISD receives your evaluation request, the district must complete the assessment and provide you with results within a specific timeframe. Understanding this timeline is crucial for HARLANDALE ISD parents.

Critical Timeline Requirements

Texas Education Code and IDEA require HARLANDALE ISD to complete the evaluation process within 45 calendar days of receiving your written request. This is a hard deadline—the district cannot extend this timeline without your written consent.

The 45-day clock includes:

  • All testing and assessments
  • Review of existing educational records
  • Parent conferences and interviews
  • Report preparation
  • The evaluation results meeting

If HARLANDALE ISD misses this deadline without your permission, it's considered a procedural violation. Document the date HARLANDALE ISD received your request in writing so you can track whether the district meets the 45-day timeline.

What Happens During the 45 Days in HARLANDALE ISD

During your child's evaluation period in HARLANDALE ISD, the district will likely:

  • Conduct academic and cognitive testing
  • Perform behavioral and social-emotional assessments
  • Review medical and educational history
  • Observe your child in classroom and other settings
  • Interview teachers, parents, and other relevant people
  • Assess speech, language, or motor skills if applicable
  • Gather any prior evaluations or assessments

HARLANDALE ISD should keep you informed throughout this period. You have the right to provide input about your observations and concerns about your child's learning and behavior.

Understanding the Results Meeting for HARLANDALE ISD Families

At the conclusion of the evaluation, HARLANDALE ISD must hold an evaluation results meeting with you before the 45-day deadline expires. This is a critical meeting for HARLANDALE ISD parents.

What to Expect at Your Meeting

HARLANDALE ISD will present the evaluation report, explain findings, and discuss whether your child is eligible for special education services. The district must provide you with:

  • Written evaluation report – You should receive this before or at the meeting
  • Clear explanation of results – In language you understand, not jargon-heavy terminology
  • Eligibility determination – Whether HARLANDALE ISD found evidence of disability under IDEA
  • Your rights documentation – Information about Procedural Safeguards
  • Next steps – What happens if your child is found eligible or not eligible

If Your Child is Found Eligible

If HARLANDALE ISD determines your child has a disability and needs special education, the district must develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) within 30 days. This IEP outlines the special education and related services your child will receive.

You have the right to participate fully in developing your child's IEP. HARLANDALE ISD parents often find it helpful to bring an advocate or special education attorney to these important meetings.

If Your Child is Not Found Eligible

Sometimes HARLANDALE ISD determines that a child doesn't meet eligibility criteria despite evaluation. You have important rights in this situation:

  • Request an independent evaluation – The district may be required to pay for an outside evaluation if you disagree
  • Appeal the decision – Request a due process hearing to challenge HARLANDALE ISD's eligibility determination
  • Seek Section 504 Plan – Your child may qualify for accommodations under Section 504 even if not IDEA-eligible
  • Request reevaluation – You can ask for another evaluation after waiting periods

Don't accept an ineligibility decision without careful consideration. If you believe HARLANDALE ISD made an error, consult with a special education attorney near HARLANDALE or a special ed advocate HARLANDALE to understand your options.

Your Rights Throughout the HARLANDALE ISD Evaluation Process

As a parent in HARLANDALE ISD, you have fundamental rights during evaluation:

Right to Participate

You must be invited to participate in meetings about evaluation, testing, and results. HARLANDALE ISD must schedule meetings at times that work for you and provide interpretation services if you speak a language other than English.

Right to Records and Information

HARLANDALE ISD must provide you with all evaluation materials, test results, and educational records. You have the right to inspect and copy everything the district has about your child. This includes raw test scores, classroom observations, and any communications between staff members.

Right to Independent Evaluation

If you disagree with HARLANDALE ISD's evaluation, you can obtain an independent evaluation (IEE) at district expense. The district must explain your right to an IEE in writing and tell you how to request one.

Right to Procedural Safeguards

HARLANDALE ISD must provide you with a document explaining your due process rights, including how to file complaints, request due process hearings, and appeal district decisions.

Common Challenges HARLANDALE ISD Parents Face

Understanding potential obstacles helps you advocate more effectively in HARLANDALE ISD:

Delayed Responses

Some HARLANDALE ISD families report delayed acknowledgment of evaluation requests. Counter this by requesting written confirmation that the district received your request. Document the date and method of submission.

Incomplete Evaluations

HARLANDALE ISD must evaluate all areas of suspected disability. If the district only tests one area when multiple concerns exist, request additional assessment. You can require supplemental testing during the 45-day period.

Pressure to Decline Evaluation

Occasionally, HARLANDALE ISD staff may discourage evaluation with comments like "we want to try interventions first." While classroom support is valuable, you have the right to evaluation regardless of prior interventions. Don't let staff recommendations override your concerns.

Lack of Parent Participation

Some HARLANDALE ISD evaluations proceed with minimal parent input. Insist on being contacted before testing begins, during evaluation, and at the results meeting. Your observations are essential data for the evaluation.

File a Complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA)

You can file a formal complaint with TEA if HARLANDALE ISD violates special education law. TEA investigates complaints and can require corrective action by the district.

Preparing for Success with HARLANDALE ISD Evaluation

To maximize the effectiveness of your HARLANDALE ISD FIE evaluation, take these steps:

  • Document observations – Keep specific examples of academic or behavioral struggles
  • Gather records – Collect prior testing, medical evaluations, and old report cards
  • Write detailed request – Include specific concerns and examples in your initial request to HARLANDALE ISD
  • Track timeline – Mark the 45-day deadline on your calendar
  • Request independent evaluation – If possible, get an outside evaluation before HARLANDALE ISD completes theirs
  • Prepare questions – Write down questions about results before the meeting
  • Bring support – Consider bringing an advocate or trusted person to meetings

Your advocacy makes the difference. HARLANDALE ISD families who are organized, persistent, and informed get better outcomes for their children.

Moving Forward After HARLANDALE ISD Evaluation

Whether your child is found eligible or not, the evaluation is just the beginning. If HARLANDALE ISD determines your child qualifies for special education, you'll participate in developing an IEP. If not eligible, you can pursue alternative accommodations or challenge the decision.

Remember: your role as advocate for your child in HARLANDALE ISD is critical. Trust your instincts, document everything, and don't hesitate to seek professional support from advocates or attorneys when needed.
