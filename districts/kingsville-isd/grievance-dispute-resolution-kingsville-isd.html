Filing a Complaint with KINGSVILLE ISD

Submit your complaint in writing to KINGSVILLE ISD's special education department. Due process is the formal legal procedure available when disputes with KINGSVILLE ISD cannot be resolved through informal means or complaints. This is a more serious process that may result in a hearing before an impartial hearing officer.

When to Consider Due Process for KINGSVILLE ISD Disputes

Due process is appropriate when you and KINGSVILLE ISD fundamentally disagree about your child's eligibility, IEP, placement, or the provision of appropriate services. Examples include disagreement over evaluation results, proposed changes to placement that you believe are harmful, or failure to implement promised services.

Due process requires filing a request within certain timeframes and following specific procedures. Once you file a due process complaint with KINGSVILLE ISD, you enter a formal process that typically includes resolution meetings and potentially a hearing.

The Due Process Timeline with KINGSVILLE ISD

Understanding the timeline helps you plan your advocacy strategy. Key dates in the KINGSVILLE ISD due process process:

  • Day 1: You submit your due process complaint in writing to KINGSVILLE ISD
  • Days 2-5: KINGSVILLE ISD must provide you with a copy of your procedural safeguards if you haven't received one recently
  • Days 1-15: KINGSVILLE ISD must conduct a resolution meeting (unless you and the district agree to waive this)

  • Days 30+: If unresolved, either party may request a hearing to be conducted within 45 school days

These timelines are strict. Missing deadlines can result in loss of rights, so mark your calendar and consult with an advocate or attorney if you're uncertain about timing.

Filing a Complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA)
