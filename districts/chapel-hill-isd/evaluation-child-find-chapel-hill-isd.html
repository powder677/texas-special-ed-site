A Full Individual Evaluation is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine whether your child has a disability and whether they qualify for special education services. In CHAPEL HILL ISD, the FIE process is governed by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §29.001-29.016.

The CHAPEL HILL ISD special education evaluation process is free and must be conducted in your child's native language or mode of communication. This assessment looks at all areas of suspected disability, including cognitive ability, academic performance, social-emotional functioning, and physical development.

Parents in CHAPEL HILL ISD often wonder whether their child might benefit from special education services. If you've noticed your child struggling academically, having difficulty with social interactions, or showing signs of developmental delays, requesting an evaluation is the first critical step.

CHAPEL HILL ISD has a legal obligation known as "child find" to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities within the district. This includes children who attend private schools, are homeschooled, or have not yet enrolled in kindergarten.

How Child Find Works in CHAPEL HILL ISD

The CHAPEL HILL ISD child find process means the district must actively search for children who may need special education services. This includes screening students during the regular school day and responding to parent concerns.

  • District staff monitor student performance through classroom observations
  • Teachers and counselors refer students who show signs of struggle
  • Parents can initiate the process independently
  • Universal screening may identify students who need further evaluation

If your child attends CHAPEL HILL ISD schools or lives in the CHAPEL HILL area, the district must ensure appropriate evaluation opportunities are available.

You have the right to request a CHAPEL HILL ISD evaluation request at any time. You don't need to wait for teacher referrals or permission—your request alone is sufficient to start the process.

Step 1: Submit Your Written Request

The most important step is putting your request in writing. While verbal requests may be acknowledged, a written request creates an official record and starts the CHAPEL HILL ISD 45 day timeline for completion.

Contact the Special Education Department at CHAPEL HILL ISD:

  • Submit your request by email, letter, or hand-delivery
  • Address your request to the Director of Special Education
  • Keep a copy for your records
  • Request written confirmation of receipt

Your CHAPEL HILL ISD special education evaluation request should clearly state your child's name, grade level, and your concerns about their learning or development. You don't need to diagnose or label your child—simply describe what you've observed.

Step 2: Initial Response from CHAPEL HILL ISD

Within a reasonable timeframe, CHAPEL HILL ISD must acknowledge your request and begin the evaluation planning process. The district will contact you to discuss your concerns and explain the evaluation process.

This initial meeting may happen by phone, email, or in person. Be prepared to discuss:

  • Specific areas of concern (academic, behavioral, speech, motor skills)
  • Your child's medical and family history
  • Previous evaluations or interventions attempted
  • Current performance at home and school

Step 3: Evaluation Plan Development

Before beginning your child's CHAPEL HILL ISD special education evaluation, the district must develop a written Evaluation Plan and obtain your written consent. This plan outlines which assessments will be conducted and why.

Your Evaluation Plan should include:

  • Specific tests and assessments to be administered
  • Timelines for each component
  • Staff who will conduct evaluations
  • Notification of your rights during the process
  • Your right to have an independent evaluation if you disagree

As a parent, you must sign the Evaluation Plan before CHAPEL HILL ISD can begin testing. This is your opportunity to ask questions and ensure you understand what will happen.

The CHAPEL HILL ISD 45 Day Timeline

One of the most critical aspects of the CHAPEL HILL ISD FIE evaluation process is the timeline. Under IDEA, CHAPEL HILL ISD has 45 calendar days from receiving your written consent to complete the entire evaluation and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting.

Understanding the 45-Day Clock

The CHAPEL HILL ISD 45 day timeline is strict and legally enforceable. If the district doesn't meet this deadline without valid reasons, you have grounds for complaint.

Important: The 45 days does NOT include:

  • Days when school is not in session
  • Any period you request additional time to review information
  • Excused delays with documented parent agreement

In CHAPEL HILL, parents and district staff should agree on a reasonable timeline that allows for thorough evaluation while respecting this legal requirement.

What Happens During These 45 Days in CHAPEL HILL ISD

During your child's CHAPEL HILL ISD evaluation request process, multiple assessment tools may be used:

  1. Cognitive Assessment: Measures intelligence and reasoning abilities
  2. Academic Testing: Evaluates reading, math, writing, and other academic skills
  3. Speech/Language Evaluation: If communication concerns exist
  4. Physical/Occupational Therapy Assessment: If motor or self-care concerns exist
  5. Behavioral/Social-Emotional Assessment: Evaluates emotional and social functioning
  6. Review of Records: Prior academic records, report cards, attendance

Your Rights as a Parent in CHAPEL HILL ISD Special Education

Throughout the CHAPEL HILL ISD special education evaluation process, you have significant rights under federal law and Texas regulations.

Key Parent Rights During Evaluation

  • Right to Prior Written Notice: You must be informed of all proposed actions in writing
  • Right to Participate: Your input and observations are critical to the evaluation
  • Right to Interpreters: If you speak a language other than English, CHAPEL HILL ISD must provide interpretation
  • Right to Independent Evaluation: You can request an independent FIE at district expense if you disagree with CHAPEL HILL ISD results
  • Right to Confidentiality: Your child's evaluation records are protected
  • Right to an Advocate: You may bring a special education advocate or attorney to meetings

Families in CHAPEL HILL ISD should understand that you are an equal partner in this process, not just a bystander. Your knowledge of your child is invaluable.

The ARD Meeting: Results and Next Steps

Once the CHAPEL HILL ISD FIE evaluation is complete, the district will schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. This meeting must occur within the 45-day timeline and is where your child's eligibility is determined.

What Happens at the ARD Meeting

The ARD committee will include you, special education teachers, general education teachers, and an administrator. Together, you'll review all evaluation results and determine if your child qualifies for special education services.

At the ARD meeting, the team will:

  • Review and discuss all evaluation results
  • Determine eligibility for special education services
  • Identify your child's disability category (if applicable)
  • Discuss strengths and needs
  • If eligible, develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP)

Possible Outcomes at CHAPEL HILL ISD

After your child's CHAPEL HILL ISD evaluation request is completed, there are three possible outcomes:

  1. Eligible for Special Education: Your child qualifies and will receive an IEP and services
  2. Not Eligible: Your child doesn't meet criteria but may receive accommodations under Section 504
  3. Evaluation Inconclusive: Additional assessment or monitoring is needed

In CHAPEL HILL ISD, if you disagree with the eligibility decision, you have the right to request an independent evaluation.

What to Know About Texas Education Code Requirements

CHAPEL HILL ISD follows Texas Education Code §29.004, which requires comprehensive and nondiscriminatory evaluations. The law ensures that assessments are:

  • Administered in your child's native language or mode of communication
  • Conducted by qualified personnel
  • Culturally and linguistically appropriate
  • Comprehensive across all areas of suspected disability
  • Based on multiple sources of information

As a parent in CHAPEL HILL ISD, you can reference these requirements if you believe the evaluation wasn't thorough or appropriate.

Preparing for Success: Tips for CHAPEL HILL ISD Parents

As you move forward with a CHAPEL HILL ISD evaluation request, these strategies will help you get the best possible outcome for your child.

  • Gather any prior evaluations or testing results

During the CHAPEL HILL ISD Special Education Evaluation

  • Provide honest information about your child's history
  • Ask questions about tests and procedures
  • Request clarification of any confusing information
  • Maintain a file of all documents and correspondence

At the ARD Meeting for Your Child's CHAPEL HILL ISD FIE Results

  • Bring a support person or advocate if helpful

  • Ask for written explanations of findings
  • Take notes during the meeting
  • Request copies of all evaluation reports
  • Don't feel pressured to make immediate decisions

Frequently Asked Questions About CHAPEL HILL ISD Evaluations

How long does a CHAPEL HILL ISD evaluation take?

The CHAPEL HILL ISD 45 day timeline applies from your written consent to the ARD meeting. Individual testing sessions may occur over several weeks, but the overall process must be completed within 45 calendar days.

What if CHAPEL HILL ISD says my child doesn't need evaluation?

If CHAPEL HILL ISD refuses your CHAPEL HILL ISD evaluation request, they must provide written explanation. You can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency or pursue due process.

Can I bring someone to meetings at CHAPEL HILL ISD?

Yes. You may bring family members, friends, a special education attorney near CHAPEL HILL, or a special ed advocate CHAPEL HILL to any meeting with the district.

What if I disagree with CHAPEL HILL ISD's evaluation results?

You have the right to request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at CHAPEL HILL ISD expense if you disagree with the district's findings on your child's CHAPEL HILL ISD FIE evaluation.
