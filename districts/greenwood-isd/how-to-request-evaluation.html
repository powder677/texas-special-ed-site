Parents in GREENWOOD ISD can request a special evaluation (FIE - Full Individual Evaluation) when they suspect their child has a disability that affects their educational performance. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, GREENWOOD ISD is obligated to evaluate students who may qualify for special education services.

A formal evaluation in GREENWOOD ISD can identify if your child has dyslexia, attention deficit disorder, emotional problems, physical or developmental disabilities, and other conditions that require specialized services.

Signs your child may need an evaluation

  • Persistent difficulties in reading, writing, or math despite quality instruction
  • Behavioral problems or difficulty following instructions
  • Delays in language development or social skills
  • Concerns about hearing, vision, or motor skills
  • School anxiety or refusal to attend school

As a parent in GREENWOOD ISD, you have fundamental rights under special education law. These rights are protected by both federal and Texas law.

Your specific rights include:

  • Right to request evaluation: You can request a special education evaluation at any time during the school year
  • Right to notification: GREENWOOD ISD must inform you about the evaluation results within established timelines
  • Right to participate: You must be included in all meetings related to your child's special education
  • Right to legal representation: You can bring a special education attorney or advocate to any meeting in GREENWOOD ISD
  • Right to review records: You can access all of your child's educational records maintained by GREENWOOD ISD
  • Right to challenge decisions: If you disagree with GREENWOOD ISD's decisions, you can request a due process hearing

Process for Requesting Evaluation in GREENWOOD ISD

The process for requesting a special education evaluation in GREENWOOD ISD is straightforward and structured. Follow these steps to formally initiate the process.

Step 1: Initial Request

Contact your school principal in GREENWOOD ISD or the special education department. You can make your request verbally, but it is important to do so in writing to create a record. Send a letter or email to the GREENWOOD ISD special education coordinator expressing your concern and formally requesting an evaluation.

Your request should be clear and specific about the areas of concern. For example: "I am requesting a full special education evaluation for my child due to significant difficulties in reading and writing."

Step 2: Notification of Parents' Rights

Within 15 calendar days, GREENWOOD ISD must provide you with a notification of parents' rights in Spanish. This document explains your rights under IDEA and Texas law. Read it carefully and keep a copy for your records.

Step 3: Informed Consent

GREENWOOD ISD must obtain your written consent before proceeding with the evaluation. The consent form should explain what will be evaluated, when the evaluation will begin, and who will conduct the tests. Do not sign this form until you fully understand what it entails.

Step 4: Conducting the Evaluation

Once GREENWOOD ISD has your consent, specialized evaluators will conduct tests in areas such as academic skills, behavior, language development, and social-emotional functioning. The evaluation must be comprehensive and culturally appropriate.

Important Timelines for GREENWOOD ISD

It is critical to understand the timelines established by IDEA for GREENWOOD ISD to comply. These timelines protect students by ensuring they receive services without unnecessary delays.

Comprehensive Evaluation in GREENWOOD ISD: What to Expect

A complete evaluation in GREENWOOD ISD examines multiple areas of your child's development and performance. GREENWOOD ISD must use a variety of instruments and methods to obtain a complete picture.

Areas typically evaluated

  • Academic skills: Reading, writing, math, spelling
  • Language development: Expression, comprehension, pronunciation
  • Cognitive functioning: Thinking skills, memory, reasoning
  • Social-emotional development: Behavior, peer relationships, coping skills
  • Motor development: Gross and fine motor coordination
  • Developmental information: Medical history, family background
  • Classroom observations: How your child functions in their current school environment

GREENWOOD ISD must use certified evaluators and valid test materials. Evaluators may include school psychologists, speech-language pathologists, special education specialists, and other professionals.

The ARD Team Meeting in GREENWOOD ISD

After completing the evaluation, GREENWOOD ISD will schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) team meeting, also called an IEP meeting. This is a critical meeting where results are discussed and a personalized educational plan is developed.

ARD meeting participants

  • You (parent or guardian)
  • Your child (when appropriate)
  • GREENWOOD ISD special education coordinator
  • General and special education teachers
  • Evaluators who conducted the tests
  • Administrative representative from GREENWOOD ISD
  • Your attorney or advocate (if desired)

What happens during the meeting

  1. Evaluation results are presented and discussed
  2. The team determines if your child meets the criteria for special education
  3. If eligible, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) is developed
  4. Specific services your child will receive are determined
  5. You sign consent documents

In GREENWOOD ISD, you have the right to participate fully in this meeting. If you cannot attend in person, GREENWOOD ISD must offer alternatives such as videoconferencing.

The Individualized Education Program (IEP) in GREENWOOD ISD

If your child is declared eligible for special education in GREENWOOD ISD, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be developed. This document is the heart of special education services and is specific to your child's needs.

Essential components of the IEP

  • Statement of your child's current performance level
  • Measurable annual goals related to your child's needs
  • Description of how progress will be measured
  • Specific services and supports your child will receive
  • Necessary accommodations and modifications
  • Information on participation in the general curriculum
  • Age-appropriate transitions

GREENWOOD ISD must review and update the IEP annually, at a minimum. You can request a review meeting at any time if you have concerns.

If GREENWOOD ISD Denies Your Evaluation Request

In some cases, GREENWOOD ISD may deny the evaluation request. However, GREENWOOD ISD must provide a written explanation of why it denies the evaluation and must notify you of your due process rights.

Your options if you disagree

  • Request an independent evaluation: GREENWOOD ISD must inform you how to obtain a publicly funded evaluation
  • File a complaint with TEA: Contact the Texas Education Agency
  • Request a due process hearing: Requires specialized legal representation
  • Seek a special education attorney: An attorney can help you understand if GREENWOOD ISD acted appropriately

Final Steps: Preparing for Success in GREENWOOD ISD

After requesting an evaluation in GREENWOOD ISD, maintaining organized documentation is essential. Create a file that includes:

  • Meeting dates and notes of what was discussed
  • Copies of evaluations and test results
  • The current IEP and any amendments
  • Examples of your child's schoolwork
  • Communications about your child's progress

Stay in regular contact with GREENWOOD ISD teachers and specialists. Active communication is the best way to ensure your child receives the services they need.
