Signs Your Child May Need Evaluation

Parents in HUNTSVILLE ISD should consider requesting an evaluation if they observe:

  • Significant delays in speech, language, or academic skills compared to peers
  • Difficulty with reading, writing, or mathematics that hasn't improved with classroom interventions
  • Behavioral challenges that interfere with learning or social relationships
  • Attention problems or difficulty following directions
  • Concerns about hearing, vision, or motor development
  • Emotional or mental health concerns affecting school performance
  • Suspected autism spectrum disorder or other developmental disability

RTI and Pre-Evaluation Considerations

Some HUNTSVILLE ISD schools may use Response to Intervention (RTI) strategies before conducting a HUNTSVILLE ISD special education evaluation. However, this is not required before you can request an FIE. You can request evaluation at any time if you have concerns about your child's learning.

If your child is currently receiving interventions through RTI, you can still request evaluation simultaneously. HUNTSVILLE ISD cannot delay an evaluation simply because interventions are in progress.

Step 1: Submit Your Request in Writing

To initiate the HUNTSVILLE ISD evaluation request process, you must make a written request. This is crucial for documentation and legal protection. Your written request should:

  • Be addressed to the school principal, campus special education coordinator, or HUNTSVILLE ISD special education department
  • Include your child's full name, grade level, and school
  • Clearly state that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation
  • Briefly describe your concerns about your child's learning or development
  • Include your contact information and signature

Keep a copy for your records. Consider delivering it in person and asking for a dated receipt, or send it via email so you have confirmation of delivery. This creates a documented paper trail that HUNTSVILLE ISD received your request.

Step 2: Understand the HUNTSVILLE ISD 45 Day Timeline

One of the most important aspects of HUNTSVILLE ISD evaluation request procedures is the timeline. Once HUNTSVILLE ISD receives your written request, the district has 45 calendar days to complete the evaluation and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss results.

The HUNTSVILLE ISD 45 day timeline begins on the day the school receives your written request. HUNTSVILLE ISD families should understand:

  • The 45 days includes all assessment activities, testing, observations, and data gathering
  • The ARD meeting to review results must occur within this 45-day window
  • This timeline is required by Texas Education Code and IDEA regulations
  • Extensions are only permitted with your written consent

If HUNTSVILLE ISD needs additional time, they must request your written permission before the 45 days expire. You are not obligated to grant an extension.

Step 3: Provide Consent for Evaluation

Within a few business days of receiving your evaluation request, HUNTSVILLE ISD should contact you with a proposed evaluation plan. This plan outlines:

  • Specific areas to be assessed (academics, speech, behavior, social-emotional, etc.)
  • Types of assessments that will be used
  • Timeline for completion
  • Your rights as a parent

You must provide written consent before testing begins. Do not feel pressured to consent immediately—review the plan carefully. If you have questions about why certain assessments are included or if you believe important areas are missing, discuss these concerns with the evaluation team at HUNTSVILLE ISD.

What Happens During the HUNTSVILLE ISD FIE Evaluation

Comprehensive Assessment Components

A true HUNTSVILLE ISD FIE evaluation should be comprehensive and multidisciplinary. This means it examines multiple areas of functioning:

  • Cognitive/Intellectual Abilities: Problem-solving, reasoning, and learning capacity
  • Academic Achievement: Performance in reading, writing, and mathematics
  • Speech and Language: Expressive and receptive language skills
  • Social-Emotional/Behavioral: Emotional regulation and social skills
  • Motor Skills: Fine and gross motor development
  • Adaptive Functioning: Daily living and self-care skills
  • Medical/Developmental History: Review of records, observations, and interviews

Evaluation Team Composition

HUNTSVILLE ISD is required to use a multidisciplinary team for evaluation. This team typically includes:

  • Special education coordinator or diagnostician
  • General education teacher(s)
  • Speech-language pathologist (if communication is a concern)
  • School psychologist or counselor
  • Other specialists as needed (occupational therapist, physical therapist, etc.)
  • Parent input (you are part of the team)

Your Role During the Evaluation Process

As a parent, you are a critical member of the evaluation team in HUNTSVILLE ISD. You should:

  • Complete detailed developmental history questionnaires honestly and thoroughly
  • Share concerns and observations from home
  • Attend any meetings or conferences regarding the evaluation
  • Ask questions if assessment procedures are unclear
  • Request clarification on preliminary findings if needed

The ARD Meeting: Reviewing HUNTSVILLE ISD FIE Results

What to Expect at Your ARD Meeting

At the end of the HUNTSVILLE ISD 45 day timeline, the evaluation team will hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. This meeting reviews all evaluation results and determines whether your child qualifies for special education services under HUNTSVILLE ISD.

Before the meeting, you should receive:

  • A copy of the complete evaluation report
  • Written notice of the meeting date, time, and location
  • Information about your rights as a parent
  • The names of team members who will attend

Review the evaluation report carefully before the meeting. Take notes on questions, concerns, or disagreements. You have the right to bring additional people to the ARD meeting—consider bringing a trusted family member, advocate, or special education attorney near HUNTSVILLE if you feel you need support.

Can I Request Evaluation During Summer?

What if I Disagree with the Results?
