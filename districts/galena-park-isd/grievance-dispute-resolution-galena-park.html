Filing a Formal Complaint with TEA

If informal resolution and mediation don't work, you can file a formal GALENA PARK ISD TEA complaint with the Texas Education Agency. TEA investigates complaints that GALENA PARK ISD has violated special education laws or failed to comply with IDEA requirements.

A TEA complaint is particularly appropriate if you believe GALENA PARK ISD has systematically failed to provide services, violated your child's rights, or engaged in discriminatory practices. The complaint process is formal but remains free for parents.

Who Can File a TEA Complaint

You, as a parent of a student in GALENA PARK ISD, can file a complaint. You may also authorize a special education advocate or attorney to file on your behalf. Organizations serving special needs children can also file complaints on behalf of families.

Time Limits for Filing TEA Complaints

You must file your GALENA PARK ISD TEA complaint within one year of the date you discovered (or should have discovered) the violation . This deadline is important—complaints filed after one year will be dismissed. However, if GALENA PARK ISD continues to violate your child's rights, each instance creates a new one-year window.

What to Include in Your Complaint

Your formal complaint to TEA about GALENA PARK ISD must include:

  • Your child's name and date of birth
  • GALENA PARK ISD name and address
  • Specific description of the alleged violation
  • Dates when the violation occurred
  • Facts supporting your allegation
  • Reference to specific IDEA or Texas Education Code violations
  • Proposed solutions or compensatory services needed
  • Your signature and contact information

The TEA Complaint Investigation Process

After you file, TEA has 10 calendar days to notify GALENA PARK ISD of your complaint. The district then has 10 days to provide a response to TEA. TEA investigators may interview school staff, review your child's records, and examine GALENA PARK ISD policies.

TEA must issue a written decision within 60 calendar days of receiving your complaint. In exceptional circumstances, this timeline can extend to 45 additional days. The decision explains whether GALENA PARK ISD violated special education law and what corrective actions must occur.

If you believe GALENA PARK ISD has significantly violated your child's rights or failed to provide appropriate services, you may request a GALENA PARK ISD due process hearing. This is a formal legal proceeding before an impartial hearing officer to resolve disputes about your child's identification, evaluation, placement, or services.

Due process hearings are more adversarial than other procedures and may damage relationships with GALENA PARK ISD staff. However, they're necessary when the district refuses to provide needed services or when disagreements are fundamental.

When to Consider Due Process

A due process hearing is appropriate when:

  • GALENA PARK ISD refuses to evaluate your child for special education eligibility
  • Your child's IEP is significantly inadequate or fails to provide FAPE (Free Appropriate Public Education)
  • GALENA PARK ISD wants to change your child's placement against your wishes
  • Your child has been suspended or expelled for disability-related behavior without proper procedures
  • GALENA PARK ISD denies related services your child needs
  • Previous complaints and mediation have failed to resolve serious violations

Filing a Due Process Complaint

You must file your GALENA PARK ISD due process complaint within two years of when you discovered (or should have discovered) the problem. File with GALENA PARK ISD and send a copy to TEA. The complaint should include the same detailed information as a TEA complaint, plus requested hearing outcomes.

GALENA PARK ISD must schedule a resolution meeting within 15 days. This final informal opportunity allows the school to address your concerns before the hearing. If issues remain unresolved after this meeting, the hearing process moves forward.

The Due Process Hearing

The hearing is conducted by an impartial hearing officer with special education law knowledge. Both you and GALENA PARK ISD present evidence, call witnesses, and present arguments. You have the right to an attorney or advocate representing you, though this means additional costs.

The hearing officer issues a written decision within 45 days determining whether GALENA PARK ISD provided FAPE to your child. If you prevail, the district may be ordered to provide compensatory services or reimburse you for private services. Either party may appeal to state-level review.
