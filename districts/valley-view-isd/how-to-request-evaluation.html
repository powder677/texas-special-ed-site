Step 1: Talk to Teachers and the School in VALLEY VIEW ISD

Your first step should be to communicate with your child's teachers and school staff. Teachers in VALLEY VIEW ISD are experienced in recognizing when a student might benefit from a special education evaluation.

Schedule a conference with the teacher or school principal. Explain your specific concerns about your child's academic performance or behavior. Ask if they have noticed similar concerns in class.

School staff in VALLEY VIEW ISD may offer interventions before a formal evaluation, such as additional tutoring or classroom accommodations. However, if you believe your child needs a special education evaluation in VALLEY VIEW ISD, you do not need to wait for the school to suggest this.

Step 2: Submit a Formal Request for Evaluation in VALLEY VIEW ISD

You can request a special education evaluation by submitting a written request to VALLEY VIEW ISD. This request is known as an "Initial Evaluation Request for Eligibility Determination" or "Referral for Evaluation".

How to Submit the Request

Write a formal letter addressed to the school principal or the special education coordinator of VALLEY VIEW ISD. In the letter, you should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • The grade and school they currently attend in VALLEY VIEW ISD
  • A clear description of your academic, behavioral, or developmental concerns
  • Specific examples of how these difficulties affect your child's learning
  • Your contact name, phone number, and email address
  • A clear request for a comprehensive special education evaluation

Keep a copy for your records and hand-deliver the letter if possible, or send it by certified mail to have proof that it reached VALLEY VIEW ISD.

Response Time in VALLEY VIEW ISD

Under Texas law, VALLEY VIEW ISD has 15 school days to respond to your request. The school must:

  • Confirm receipt of your request
  • Explain the evaluation process
  • Request your signed consent to proceed with the evaluation

Once you sign the consent form, VALLEY VIEW ISD has 60 calendar days to complete the evaluation.

Step 3: The Evaluation Process in VALLEY VIEW ISD

A special education evaluation in VALLEY VIEW ISD, also called a "Full Individual Evaluation" (FIE), is a comprehensive and multidimensional assessment of your child.

Areas Evaluated

Evaluators in VALLEY VIEW ISD will examine multiple areas to get a complete picture of your child's functioning:

  • Cognition: Thinking and problem-solving skills
  • Academics: Performance in reading, writing, and math
  • Language: Language expression and comprehension
  • Behavior: Social and emotional behavior
  • Motor skills: Gross and fine motor skills
  • Health and development: Medical and developmental history

Evaluators in VALLEY VIEW ISD

The evaluation team in VALLEY VIEW ISD may include:

  • School psychologist
  • Special education specialist
  • Special education teacher
  • Speech-language pathologist (if necessary)
  • Other specialists as per your child's needs

Step 4: Eligibility Meeting in VALLEY VIEW ISD

After the evaluation is completed, VALLEY VIEW ISD schedules a meeting with you to discuss the results. This is the "Eligibility Determination Meeting."

What Happens at the Meeting

At the meeting in VALLEY VIEW ISD, the evaluation team will present the evaluation findings. They will determine if your child qualifies for special education services under one of the following categories:

  • Intellectual disability
  • Visual impairment
  • Hearing impairment
  • Speech/language impairment
  • Orthopedic impairment
  • Autism
  • Traumatic brain injury
  • Other health impairment
  • Emotional disturbance
  • Specific learning disability
  • Deaf-blindness
  • Multiple disabilities

Your Role at the Meeting

You can bring someone to support you, such as a family member, a special education attorney near VALLEY VIEW, or a special education advocate in VALLEY VIEW.

Step 5: Development of the Individualized Education Program (IEP)

If your child qualifies for special education services in VALLEY VIEW ISD, the next step is to develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

What an IEP Contains

  • Current levels of performance: How your child is doing academically and in other areas

  • Special education services: What support your child will receive
  • Accommodations and modifications: Changes in how your child is taught or assessed
  • Participation in general education: How much time will be spent in regular classes
  • Transition to adult services: For students aged 14 and older

IEP Meetings in VALLEY VIEW ISD

The IEP team in VALLEY VIEW ISD includes parents, teachers, specialists, and administrators. You will have an IEP meeting every year, and you can request additional meetings at any time.
