As a parent of a student in DAYTON ISD, you have rights protected under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act. These rights ensure that your child receives a free and appropriate public education.

Key Parental Rights in DAYTON ISD

  • Right to request evaluation: You can ask DAYTON ISD to evaluate your child at any time if you suspect a disability
  • Participation in decisions: DAYTON ISD parents must be present at all important special education meetings
  • Access to records: You can review all of your child's records in DAYTON ISD within 10 school days
  • Due process: If you disagree with DAYTON ISD's decisions, you can request a due process hearing
  • Spanish interpretation: DAYTON ISD must provide Spanish interpretation during all meetings

Understanding these rights is the first step to effectively advocating for your child in DAYTON ISD. Do not hesitate to contact DAYTON ISD's special education department if you have questions about your rights.

If you observe any of these warning signs, it's time to consider requesting a special education evaluation in DAYTON ISD for your child.

Academic Indicators

  • Significant difficulty learning to read, write, or do math
  • Consistently low grades despite effort
  • Delay in language or communication development
  • Difficulty following instructions or understanding class content

Behavioral and Emotional Indicators

  • Difficulty interacting with peers
  • Anxiety, depression, or drastic mood swings
  • Lack of concentration or extreme hyperactivity

Developmental Indicators

  • Delays in fine or gross motor skills
  • Sensory difficulties (hearing, vision, sensory processing)
  • Coordination problems or clumsiness
  • Delayed social development compared to peers

If you recognize any of these indicators in your child, requesting a special education evaluation in DAYTON ISD is an important step and protected by law.

How to Formally Request an Evaluation in DAYTON ISD

The process for requesting a special education evaluation in DAYTON ISD is straightforward and designed to be accessible to all parents. Here is the procedure you should follow.

Step 1: Contact DAYTON ISD

Your first step is to contact DAYTON ISD's special education department. You can do so in several ways:

  • Call DAYTON ISD's special education office directly
  • Send a written request (it can be by email or letter) addressed to the special education director
  • Speak in person with your child's teacher or school counselor
  • Complete a referral form available at DAYTON ISD schools

Important Tip: Although the law does not require a written request, we recommend sending a letter or email to DAYTON ISD to create a record of your request. This protects your rights and ensures clear documentation of when you requested the evaluation.

Step 2: Submit the Request in Writing

Prepare a letter addressed to DAYTON ISD's special education director that includes:

  • Your child's full name and student ID number
  • Current grade and school in DAYTON ISD
  • Specific description of academic, behavioral, or developmental concerns
  • Concrete examples of the difficulties you have observed
  • Your contact information (phone and email)
  • Explicit request for a special education evaluation

Keep a copy of this letter for your records. DAYTON ISD must acknowledge receipt of your request.

Step 3: DAYTON ISD Must Respond

Under Texas law and IDEA, DAYTON ISD has 15 school days to respond to your evaluation request. The response must include:

  • Confirmation that your request was received
  • Explanation of what evaluations will be conducted
  • Information about your rights as a parent
  • Request for informed consent to proceed with the evaluation

DAYTON ISD must also inform you if it initially determines that the evaluation is not necessary and specifically explain its reasons.

The Evaluation Process in DAYTON ISD

Once DAYTON ISD approves the special education evaluation, the process can take between 30 and 60 days. Here's what to expect.

Types of Evaluations in DAYTON ISD

DAYTON ISD may use various evaluation methods to obtain a complete picture of your child's development:

  • Psychoeducational evaluations: Intelligence tests, academic achievement, and cognitive skills
  • Behavioral evaluations: Classroom observation and behavior questionnaires
  • Language development evaluations: Communication and language processing tests
  • Medical and physical evaluations: Motor skills, vision, and hearing examinations
  • Functional evaluations: Observation of how the child functions in real learning environments

All these procedures in DAYTON ISD must be conducted in your family's preferred language when possible.

Your Role During the DAYTON ISD Evaluation

As a parent, your participation is crucial in the evaluation process in DAYTON ISD. You can:

  • Provide information about your child's developmental history
  • Share observations about behavior at home
  • Ask questions about evaluation procedures
  • Request explanations in Spanish if necessary
  • Obtain copies of all test results

DAYTON ISD must keep you informed throughout the special education evaluation process.

Understanding the Results: The Eligibility Determination Meeting

After completing the evaluation, DAYTON ISD will schedule an eligibility determination meeting to discuss the results. This meeting is a critical moment in DAYTON ISD's special education process.

What to Expect at the DAYTON ISD Eligibility Meeting

During this meeting in DAYTON ISD, the following will be discussed:

  • Detailed findings from all evaluations conducted
  • Whether your child meets the criteria for a disability category under IDEA
  • Your child's specific needs identified during the evaluation
  • Next steps if your child is eligible for services in DAYTON ISD

DAYTON ISD must provide a certified Spanish interpreter during this meeting to ensure you fully understand all information.

Possible Evaluation Outcomes in DAYTON ISD

If your child is found eligible: DAYTON ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) that specifies the special education services your child will receive.

If your child is not found eligible under IDEA: DAYTON ISD might offer protections under Section 504, which may include accommodations to help your child access general education.

If you disagree: You have the right to request an independent evaluation at DAYTON ISD's expense or to proceed with a due process hearing.

Developing the Individualized Education Program (IEP) in DAYTON ISD

If your child is determined eligible for special education in DAYTON ISD, the next step is to develop an IEP (Individualized Education Program). This document is the action plan for your child's education in DAYTON ISD.

Key Components of the DAYTON ISD IEP

  • Present levels of performance: Description of where your child is academically and functionally
  • Annual goals and objectives: What your child will achieve during the school year in DAYTON ISD
  • Special services: Speech therapy, special education, counseling, or other services in DAYTON ISD
  • Time in general education: How much time your child will spend in regular classes versus special education in DAYTON ISD
  • Accommodations and modifications: Changes in how material is taught to your child in DAYTON ISD
  • Progress measures: How DAYTON ISD will evaluate if your child is meeting their goals

Your Participation in the Development of the DAYTON ISD IEP

As a parent, you are an equal member of the DAYTON ISD IEP team. You can:

  • Share your concerns and priorities for your child
  • Ask questions about any proposals from DAYTON ISD
  • Request changes or amendments to the IEP
  • Refuse services proposed by DAYTON ISD
  • Request a Spanish interpreter during all IEP meetings

Do not sign the DAYTON ISD IEP unless you completely agree with the plan. You can request time to review the document or request an additional meeting.
