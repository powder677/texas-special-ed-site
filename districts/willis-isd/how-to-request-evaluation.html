The federal Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas state law guarantee specific WILLIS ISD parent rights. As a parent of a student in WILLIS ISD, you have the right to:

  • Request an evaluation at any time if you suspect your child has a disability
  • Fully participate in all evaluation and planning meetings
  • Receive information in Spanish if it is your primary language
  • Obtain a copy of all evaluation results
  • Request an independent evaluation at district expense if you disagree with WILLIS ISD
  • Seek an advocate for special education if needed

These rights are protected under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act and IDEA. WILLIS ISD is legally obligated to respect and honor these rights.

Step-by-Step Process: How to Request an FIE Evaluation in WILLIS ISD

Step 1: Contact Your Child's School

The first step in the WILLIS ISD FIE Spanish process is to directly contact your child's school. A Full Individual Evaluation (known as "FIE" in English) is what WILLIS ISD conducts to determine if your child qualifies for special education.

Who should you call?

  • Classroom teacher: Start with your child's primary teacher
  • School counselor: They can help coordinate the request
  • WILLIS ISD special education office: Direct contact with the specialized department
  • School principal: If you need escalation or additional assistance

Request to speak with someone who can help you with the initial evaluation. Specifically mention that you wish to request a formal evaluation to determine if your child qualifies for WILLIS ISD special education.

Step 2: Submit Your Request in Writing

Although you can make a verbal request, it is important to get written confirmation. Send an email or a letter addressed to the school principal and WILLIS ISD special education coordinator.

Your request should include:

  • Student's full name and date of birth
  • Current grade and school in WILLIS ISD
  • Specific description of academic or behavioral concerns
  • Relevant history (hearing problems, vision, developmental delays)
  • Clear request: "I am requesting a full special education evaluation"

Example opening: "By means of this, I request a full psychoeducational evaluation for my child [name], who is in [grade] at [school name]. I have concerns about..."

Step 3: Participate in the Initial Consultation Meeting

Within 15 school days, WILLIS ISD must respond to your request. The school will schedule a consultation meeting to discuss your concerns. This is not the evaluation itself, but a conversation about whether the evaluation is appropriate.

In this WILLIS ISD meeting, you should:

  • Explain what you have noticed at home and at school
  • Share any previous evaluations or medical reports
  • Ask questions about the evaluation process
  • Request that any documents be provided to you in Spanish
  • Ask for a clear timeline for the full evaluation

Step 4: Authorize the Formal Evaluation

If WILLIS ISD and you agree to proceed, you will receive an informed consent form to sign. No evaluation can be conducted without your written permission. Read it carefully and ensure you understand what will be evaluated.

WILLIS ISD FIE evaluators typically assess:

  • Cognitive ability (intelligence and reasoning)
  • Academic achievement (reading, writing, math)
  • Language and communication development
  • Social-emotional behavior
  • Classroom observations
  • Background and developmental information

Step 5: Participate in the Evaluation Testing

Once authorized, WILLIS ISD specialists (school psychologists, speech pathologists, special educators) will conduct the evaluations. This process typically takes 4 to 8 weeks.

Your child may be evaluated individually during the school day. These evaluators are trained professionals from WILLIS ISD special education who regularly work with students with disabilities.

Step 6: Results and Eligibility Meeting (IEP)

Within 60 school days of your initial consent, WILLIS ISD must complete the evaluation and convene a meeting. This is the most important meeting: here it will be determined if your child qualifies for special education.

In this WILLIS ISD meeting, you and the special education team will:

  • Review all test results
  • Discuss whether your child has a disability under IDEA
  • Determine if they need special services
  • If they qualify, they will create an Individualized Education Program (IEP)

Possible outcome options:

  • Qualifies: An IEP will be developed with personalized special services
  • Does not qualify for IDEA: But may qualify under Section 504 (accommodation plan)
  • Independent evaluation: If you disagree, you can request WILLIS ISD to pay for an external evaluation

Important Information about WILLIS ISD FIE in Spanish

Spanish-speaking families in WILLIS ISD have specific language rights. The district must provide:

If WILLIS ISD does not provide these language services, explicitly request them. Say: "I need everything to be done in Spanish" or "I need an interpreter at the meeting."

What to Do if WILLIS ISD Denies the Evaluation?

If WILLIS ISD says it will not evaluate your child, you have options. The district must provide written notice explaining why it is denying the request.

If you disagree with WILLIS ISD's decision to deny the evaluation request, you can:

  • Request mediation: A neutral process facilitated by the state of Texas
  • File a formal complaint: With the Texas Education Agency (TEA)
  • Request a due process hearing: Before an independent hearing officer
  • Obtain an independent evaluation: At your own expense (although WILLIS ISD may have to pay for it later)
  • Seek legal advice: Consult a special education attorney near WILLIS

Do not hesitate to resort to these processes if you believe your child needs an evaluation and WILLIS ISD refuses.
