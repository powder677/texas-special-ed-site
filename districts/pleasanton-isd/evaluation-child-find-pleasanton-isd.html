A Full Individual Evaluation in PLEASANTON ISD special education is a comprehensive assessment designed to understand your child's strengths, challenges, and educational needs. The evaluation examines cognitive abilities, academic performance, social-emotional development, and physical or sensory capabilities.

The purpose of a PLEASANTON ISD FIE evaluation is to determine if your child has a disability that requires special education services. This evaluation is protected under federal law and PLEASANTON ISD must follow strict guidelines and timelines when conducting assessments.

Unlike simple screenings or classroom observations, an FIE is in-depth, multidisciplinary, and focuses on all areas of suspected disability. Parents in PLEASANTON ISD should understand that this evaluation is free—your school district pays for it.

Before requesting an evaluation, it's important to know about PLEASANTON ISD child find responsibilities. Under IDEA, PLEASANTON ISD must actively search for children who may need special education services, from birth through age 21.

This means PLEASANTON ISD cannot wait for parents to request evaluations. The district must:

  • Identify children with disabilities through screening and referral processes
  • Evaluate children suspected of having disabilities at no cost to families
  • Locate homeless children and children in foster care who need evaluations
  • Ensure evaluations are non-discriminatory and conducted in the child's native language

If your child attends PLEASANTON ISD schools and teachers have noticed struggles, the district should have already initiated discussion about an evaluation. However, you as a parent have the right to request a PLEASANTON ISD special education evaluation at any time.

How to Request a PLEASANTON ISD Evaluation: Step-by-Step Process

Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing

While you can request a PLEASANTON ISD evaluation request verbally, always submit your request in writing. This creates a paper trail and starts the official timeline. Send a letter or email to your child's school principal and the Director of Special Education for PLEASANTON ISD.

Your written request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current grade level and school in PLEASANTON ISD
  • Specific concerns about your child's learning, behavior, or development
  • Examples of when you noticed these concerns
  • Any evaluations or assessments done outside PLEASANTON ISD (private evaluations, doctor's assessments, etc.)
  • Request for an FIE in specific areas of concern (reading, math, social-emotional, speech, etc.)

Keep a copy for your records and consider sending it via email with a read receipt or certified mail so you have proof PLEASANTON ISD received your request.

Step 2: Understand the PLEASANTON ISD 45 Day Timeline

Once PLEASANTON ISD receives your written evaluation request, the district has 45 calendar days to complete the full evaluation and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. This PLEASANTON ISD 45 day timeline is critical and mandated by Texas Education Code §29.004.

Here's what happens during this period:

  • Days 1-5: PLEASANTON ISD notifies you in writing of their intent to evaluate and requests written consent
  • Days 6-40: The multidisciplinary team conducts evaluations in all suspected disability areas
  • Days 41-45: PLEASANTON ISD holds an ARD meeting to discuss results and determine eligibility

Parents in PLEASANTON ISD should mark these dates on a calendar and follow up if you haven't heard from the district within this window.

Step 3: Provide Written Consent

PLEASANTON ISD must obtain your written consent before beginning any evaluation. The district will send you a Permission to Evaluate form that explains:

  • The areas your child will be evaluated in
  • The types of tests and assessments that will be used
  • Your right to have an independent evaluator present
  • Your right to obtain independent educational evaluations at district expense under certain circumstances

You have the right to ask questions about the evaluation plan before signing. Don't hesitate to request clarification from PLEASANTON ISD special education staff about any aspect of the proposed PLEASANTON ISD special education evaluation.

Areas PLEASANTON ISD May Evaluate

Depending on your child's specific concerns, the PLEASANTON ISD FIE evaluation may include assessments in multiple areas:

  • Cognitive/Intellectual: IQ testing and reasoning abilities
  • Academic: Reading, math, writing, and spelling achievement
  • Speech/Language: Receptive and expressive language skills
  • Social-Emotional/Behavioral: Emotional development and classroom behavior
  • Fine and Gross Motor: Coordination, balance, and physical development
  • Vision and Hearing: Screening for sensory disabilities
  • Adaptive Behavior: Ability to handle daily living tasks independently

Your PLEASANTON ISD evaluation request will specify which areas are relevant to your child's concerns.

What to Expect During the Evaluation Process

Who Will Evaluate Your Child

PLEASANTON ISD will assemble a multidisciplinary team including special education teachers, counselors, speech therapists, or other specialists depending on your child's needs. A school psychologist typically leads the evaluation for a PLEASANTON ISD FIE evaluation.

Where and When Evaluations Happen

Testing usually occurs at your child's school during the school day, though PLEASANTON ISD may conduct some assessments in other locations. Parents typically aren't present during individual assessments, but you should ask about observing portions of the evaluation.

Your Right to Participate

You have the right to:

  • Be present during evaluation meetings and discussions
  • Ask questions about testing procedures and results
  • Provide information about your child's history and behavior at home
  • Request an independent evaluator attend the evaluation
  • Share any outside evaluations with the PLEASANTON ISD team

The ARD Meeting and Eligibility Decision

Within the PLEASANTON ISD 45 day timeline, the school will schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. This meeting is where PLEASANTON ISD shares evaluation results and determines if your child qualifies for special education services.

What Happens at the ARD Meeting

During this critical meeting for your child's education in PLEASANTON ISD:

  • The evaluation team presents findings from all assessments
  • The team determines if your child has a disability under IDEA
  • If eligible, the team develops an Individualized Education Program (IEP)
  • You discuss placement and services your child will receive
  • You review your parent rights under federal and state law

Come prepared with questions and any outside information that helps PLEASANTON ISD understand your child's needs. Bring an advocate or attorney if you feel you need support—this is your right.

Understanding Eligibility Categories

In PLEASANTON ISD and throughout Texas, children may qualify for special education under these disability categories:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Blind/Low Vision
  • Deaf/Hard of Hearing
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Specific Learning Disability
  • Speech/Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury

If PLEASANTON ISD Says Your Child Doesn't Qualify

If the evaluation team determines your child doesn't have a disability requiring special education, PLEASANTON ISD must provide this decision in writing with an explanation. This doesn't mean your child can't receive help—they may qualify for a 504 Plan or interventions through Response to Intervention (RTI) programs.

You have the right to:

  • Request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at PLEASANTON ISD expense
  • File a due process complaint if you disagree with the decision
  • Request mediation services
  • Consult with a special education attorney near PLEASANTON about your options

Working With Special Education Advocates and Attorneys in the PLEASANTON Area

Many parents in PLEASANTON ISD benefit from professional support during the evaluation and IEP process. A special ed advocate PLEASANTON parents can hire can attend meetings, help interpret results, and ensure your child's rights are protected.

Consider consulting a special education attorney near PLEASANTON if:

  • The district is not completing evaluations within the 45-day timeline
  • You believe PLEASANTON ISD is discriminating against your child
  • You want to ensure your child receives appropriate services

These professionals understand PLEASANTON ISD procedures and can advocate strongly for your child's needs.

Parent Rights and Protections in PLEASANTON ISD

Texas Education Code and IDEA protect your rights throughout the PLEASANTON ISD evaluation request process. You have the right to:

  • Request an evaluation at any time during your child's enrollment in PLEASANTON ISD
  • Receive evaluation results and explanations in understandable language
  • Provide information from doctors, outside evaluators, or other professionals to PLEASANTON ISD
  • Request written copies of all evaluation reports and test results
  • Request an independent evaluation if you disagree with PLEASANTON ISD findings
  • Have someone of your choice attend ARD meetings
  • Appeal PLEASANTON ISD decisions through due process

Key Takeaways for PLEASANTON ISD Parents

Requesting a PLEASANTON ISD FIE evaluation is your right as a parent, and understanding the process empowers you to advocate effectively for your child. Remember these essential points:

  • Submit your PLEASANTON ISD evaluation request in writing to start the official timeline
  • The PLEASANTON ISD 45 day timeline is law—hold PLEASANTON ISD accountable
  • You can request evaluations in specific areas related to your concerns
  • Bring documentation from doctors, therapists, or outside evaluators to support your request
  • Ask questions and participate actively in all meetings
  • Know your rights—PLEASANTON ISD must follow federal and state regulations
  • Don't hesitate to seek help from a special ed advocate PLEASANTON area families trust

Your child's education is too important to leave to chance. If you have concerns about your child's learning or development in PLEASANTON ISD, requesting a comprehensive evaluation is an important first step toward getting them the support they need to succeed.
