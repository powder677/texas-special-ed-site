How long does Texas have to complete an evaluation?
Once a written evaluation request is received, the district must complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) and hold an ARD meeting within 45 school days. This timeline is strictly enforced by the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Verbal requests do not start the clock — always submit in writing and keep a copy.

Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in Eanes ISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability that impacts their ability to learn effectively in the general education setting, you have the right to request a special education evaluation from Eanes Independent School District (ISD). This process initiates the formal consideration of your child's eligibility for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas state law.

How to Submit a Written Request

The first and most important step is submitting a written request for a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). This request should be addressed to the school principal or the school's special education director. While a formal form isn't usually required, clarity and thoroughness are key. Your letter should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth.
  • Your child's current grade level and teacher's name.
  • A detailed description of your concerns about your child's academic, behavioral, or developmental progress. Be specific; list specific areas of difficulty, examples of problems encountered, and any interventions already tried.
  • Any relevant medical diagnoses or information from doctors or other professionals. Include copies of reports if available.
  • A clear statement requesting a full and individual evaluation to determine eligibility for special education services.
  • Your contact information, including phone number and email address.
  • The date of the letter.

Keep a copy of your letter for your records. Sending the request via certified mail with return receipt requested provides proof of delivery.

The 45 School Day Timeline

Upon receiving your written request, Eanes ISD has specific timelines mandated by Texas law. The district has 15 school days to decide whether or not to conduct the evaluation. If they decide to evaluate, they must obtain your written consent. Once you provide written consent, the district has 45 school days to complete the FIE. This timeline excludes school holidays and breaks. It is crucial to understand this timeline and to keep track of the deadlines. If the district exceeds the timeline without justification, you have the right to file a complaint.

What the FIE Must Cover

The Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment conducted by qualified professionals to determine if your child has a disability and, if so, what special education and related services they need. Texas law mandates the FIE assess all areas related to the suspected disability. This can include:

  • Academic achievement: Reading, writing, math.
  • Intellectual functioning: Cognitive abilities.
  • Language and communication skills.
  • Motor skills: Fine and gross motor abilities.
  • Social and emotional development.
  • Adaptive behavior: Daily living skills.
  • Sensory processing.

The evaluation must utilize multiple assessment measures, including observations, parent input, teacher input, standardized tests, and informal assessments. The team conducting the FIE must be qualified and certified in the relevant areas of assessment.

Child Find Obligations

Eanes ISD, like all school districts in Texas, has a "Child Find" obligation. This means they have a legal responsibility to actively seek out and identify children within their jurisdiction who may have disabilities and be in need of special education services, regardless of whether those children are enrolled in public school. This applies to children aged 3 through 21. If you believe your child may have a disability and the district hasn't initiated an evaluation, you still have the right to request one. Don't rely solely on the district to identify a potential need; be proactive in advocating for your child.

What If They Refuse

If Eanes ISD refuses to conduct an FIE, they must provide you with written notice explaining their reasons. This notice must include a description of other actions considered and the reasons for rejecting those actions. You have the right to challenge this decision. You can request mediation, file a formal complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA), or request a due process hearing. Due process hearings are formal legal proceedings where you can present evidence and argue your case before an impartial hearing officer. Seek legal advice from a special education attorney to understand your rights and options if the district refuses to evaluate.

After the Evaluation

Once the FIE is complete, the ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee will meet to discuss the results. The ARD committee includes you, your child (when appropriate), teachers, administrators, and evaluation personnel. The ARD committee will determine if your child meets the eligibility criteria for special education services under Texas law. If eligible, the ARD committee will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) tailored to your child's specific needs. The IEP will outline specific goals, accommodations, modifications, and related services designed to help your child succeed. You are an active participant in the ARD process, and your input is critical. Review the IEP carefully and ensure it adequately addresses your child's needs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Texas? +

Submit a written request directly to the school's principal or special education director. The written request starts the mandatory 45 school-day clock. Keep a dated copy for your records.

What is a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in Texas? +

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment the school must complete to determine if your child has a disability and needs special education services. It must cover all areas of suspected disability and be completed within 45 school days of your written consent.

Can a Texas school refuse to evaluate my child? +

Yes, but only if the district provides written notice (Prior Written Notice) explaining why they are refusing and what data they relied on. You have the right to challenge that refusal through mediation or a due process hearing.
