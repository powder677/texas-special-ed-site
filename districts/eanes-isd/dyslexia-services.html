Dyslexia Services in Eanes ISD

Texas Dyslexia Handbook 2024

Eanes Independent School District (ISD) adheres to the guidelines and procedures outlined in the Texas Dyslexia Handbook. The most current version is the Texas Dyslexia Handbook 2024. This handbook provides comprehensive information on dyslexia identification, intervention, and support services in Texas public schools. Parents are strongly encouraged to familiarize themselves with this document, which can be accessed online through the Texas Education Agency (TEA) website. The handbook includes definitions of dyslexia, characteristics of dyslexia, screening procedures, instructional strategies, and legal requirements related to dyslexia services in Texas schools. It's a valuable resource for understanding your rights and the district's responsibilities regarding dyslexia support. Understanding the contents of this handbook is crucial for advocating effectively for your child's needs.

Mandatory Screening

Texas Education Code mandates dyslexia screening for all students in kindergarten and first grade. Eanes ISD implements these screenings according to the guidelines outlined in the Texas Dyslexia Handbook. These screenings are designed to identify students who are at risk for dyslexia early on. In addition to the mandatory screenings, students in other grade levels may be screened if there are concerns raised by teachers, parents, or the student themselves. If a student is identified as at-risk through the screening process, further evaluation and intervention may be recommended. Parents will be notified of the screening results and any subsequent recommendations. Early identification and intervention are crucial for mitigating the impact of dyslexia.

Structured Literacy Programs

Eanes ISD utilizes structured literacy programs for students identified with dyslexia or at risk for dyslexia. Structured literacy is an evidence-based approach to reading instruction that is explicit, systematic, cumulative, and diagnostic. These programs focus on phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension. The specific programs used may vary depending on the campus and the student's individual needs. These programs are designed to provide intensive, individualized support to help students develop the foundational skills necessary for reading success. Regular progress monitoring is conducted to ensure that students are making adequate progress and to adjust instruction as needed. Examples of structured literacy programs often used (though specific implementation may vary) include programs focusing on multisensory phonics instruction.

504 vs IEP for Dyslexia

Students with dyslexia may receive support through a Section 504 plan or an Individualized Education Program (IEP) under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). A 504 plan provides accommodations and modifications to ensure that students with disabilities have equal access to education. This might include accommodations like extended time on tests, assistive technology, or preferential seating. An IEP is a more comprehensive plan that outlines specialized instruction and related services for students with disabilities who require more intensive support to access the general education curriculum. The decision of whether a student requires a 504 plan or an IEP is made by a team of professionals, including parents, based on the student's individual needs and assessment results. If a student's dyslexia significantly impacts their ability to access the curriculum, an IEP may be necessary.

Accommodations List

Common accommodations provided to students with dyslexia in Eanes ISD may include, but are not limited to:

  • Extended time on tests and assignments
  • Preferential seating
  • Use of assistive technology (e.g., text-to-speech software, audiobooks)
  • Modified assignments
  • Oral testing
  • Copies of notes
  • Frequent breaks
  • Quiet testing environment

The specific accommodations provided will be determined based on the student's individual needs and outlined in their 504 plan or IEP. Parents should actively participate in the development of these plans to ensure that their child's needs are adequately addressed.

How to Request Evaluation

If you have concerns about your child's reading development and suspect dyslexia, you should contact your child's teacher, school counselor, or the campus principal. A written request for an evaluation is recommended. The request should outline your specific concerns and provide any relevant information or observations. The school will then initiate a process to determine whether further evaluation is warranted. This process may involve initial screenings, classroom observations, and a review of the student's academic history. If an evaluation is deemed necessary, the school will obtain parental consent and conduct a comprehensive evaluation to determine if the student meets the criteria for dyslexia services. You have the right to receive a copy of the evaluation report and to participate in meetings to discuss the results and any recommendations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

Is my child entitled to dyslexia testing in Texas? +

Yes. Texas law requires all districts to screen students for dyslexia and related disorders in Kindergarten and First Grade, and at any grade level if a parent or teacher requests it.

What accommodations can a dyslexic child get in Texas? +

Accommodations may include extended time on tests, text-to-speech software, oral administration of tests, reduced-answer-choice formats, and use of a calculator. These can be provided through a 504 Plan or an IEP.

What is the Texas Dyslexia Handbook? +

The Texas Dyslexia Handbook is a state guidance document updated in 2024 that outlines districts' responsibilities for identifying, evaluating, and serving students with dyslexia using structured literacy methods.
