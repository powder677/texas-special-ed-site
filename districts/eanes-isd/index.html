What can I do right now if Eanes ISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Eanes ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Eanes ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the world of special education can feel overwhelming, but you are not alone. Special education, as defined by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas law, is designed to provide a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) to children with disabilities, tailored to their individual needs. This means your child is entitled to specialized instruction and related services, provided at no cost to you, to help them succeed academically and developmentally. The goal is to ensure your child has access to the same educational opportunities as their non-disabled peers, to the greatest extent possible.

Eanes ISD, like all school districts in Texas, operates under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA provides guidance, resources, and monitors compliance with state and federal laws regarding special education. In addition, Eanes ISD receives support from TEA Region 13 Education Service Center, which offers professional development, technical assistance, and other services to educators and families in the region. This collaborative framework is designed to ensure that students with disabilities in Eanes ISD receive the support they need to thrive.

It is crucial to remember that as a parent, you have legally enforceable rights throughout the special education process. From the initial evaluation to the development and implementation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP), you are an essential member of the team. Eanes ISD must adhere to specific timelines, procedures, and legal requirements to protect your child's rights. Understanding these rights is the first step in advocating effectively for your child's needs and ensuring they receive the best possible education. Don't hesitate to ask questions and seek clarification at every step. Your active participation is vital.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Eanes ISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability that requires special education services, the first step is to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) from Eanes ISD. It is crucial to understand that this request must be submitted in WRITING. A verbal request to a teacher or administrator, while helpful for initiating the conversation, does not officially start the timeline for the evaluation. Make sure your written request clearly states your concerns about your child's academic, behavioral, or developmental progress and why you believe a special education evaluation is warranted.

Once Eanes ISD receives your written request and you provide written consent for the evaluation to proceed, the district has 45 school days to complete the FIE. This evaluation will involve a comprehensive assessment of your child's strengths and weaknesses, across various domains, to determine if they have a disability and, if so, what specific educational needs they have. It's also important to know about "Child Find." These laws require Eanes ISD to proactively identify, locate, and evaluate all children within their jurisdiction who may have a disability and be in need of special education services, regardless of whether they are enrolled in public school. If you have concerns, voice them! Act now: Write a letter requesting a Full Individual Evaluation for your child. Keep a copy for your records, and send it to the Eanes ISD Special Education Department via certified mail with return receipt requested. This provides you with proof of delivery and ensures the timeline begins officially.

ARD Meetings in Eanes ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal. The ARD committee is the group responsible for making decisions about your child's special education services, including eligibility, IEP goals, and placement. You, as the parent, are a vital member of the ARD committee, and your input is essential. Eanes ISD is required to provide you with at least five calendar days written notice before any ARD meeting. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, the date, time, location, and the names of the individuals who will be attending. Review this notice carefully and contact the school immediately if you have any questions or need to reschedule.

During the ARD meeting, you have the right to participate fully in the discussion and advocate for your child's needs. If you feel overwhelmed or need more time to consider the information presented, you can request a 10-day recess at any time during the ARD meeting. This will pause the meeting and give you time to review documents, consult with experts, or simply process the information. Remember that you are not required to sign the IEP the same day as the ARD meeting. You have the right to take the IEP home, review it carefully, and seek outside advice before signing it. Your signature indicates your agreement with the IEP, so it's crucial to be comfortable with all aspects of the plan before providing your consent. You can also partially consent to the IEP, agreeing to some parts while disagreeing with others.

Furthermore, Eanes ISD is obligated to consider your input and concerns during the ARD process. If you disagree with a decision made by the ARD committee, you have the right to pursue dispute resolution options, such as mediation or a due process hearing. Do not hesitate to voice your concerns and advocate for what you believe is best for your child. Before your next ARD meeting in Eanes ISD, download our free ARD preparation checklist. It will help you organize your thoughts, gather relevant information, and prepare to effectively advocate for your child's needs.

Dyslexia Services in Eanes ISD

Texas law mandates that Eanes ISD screen every student for dyslexia. This screening is typically conducted in kindergarten or first grade, but can occur at any point if concerns arise. The purpose of this screening is to identify students who may be at risk for dyslexia and provide them with appropriate intervention. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook, published by the TEA, sets the standards that Eanes ISD must follow in identifying and serving students with dyslexia. This handbook provides detailed guidance on screening, evaluation, and intervention for dyslexia.

If your child is identified as having dyslexia by Eanes ISD, they are entitled to structured literacy intervention. This evidence-based approach focuses on teaching the foundational skills of reading, such as phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension. Depending on the severity of your child's dyslexia and their individual needs, they may qualify for a 504 Plan or an Individualized Education Program (IEP). A 504 Plan provides accommodations and modifications to help your child access the general education curriculum, while an IEP provides specialized instruction and related services tailored to their specific needs. If your child has not been screened for dyslexia and you have concerns about their reading skills, request a screening in writing from Eanes ISD today. Early identification and intervention are crucial for students with dyslexia.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Eanes ISD

Discipline can be a challenging issue for students with disabilities, and it's important to understand your child's rights in these situations. If a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days in Eanes ISD in a school year, this triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). The MDR is an ARD committee meeting to determine if the student's misconduct was a manifestation of their disability or a result of Eanes ISD's failure to properly implement the IEP. This is a critical safeguard to prevent students with disabilities from being unfairly punished for behaviors related to their disability.

Furthermore, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative days constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA, requiring an ARD meeting to determine if the suspension is appropriate and how to address the student's behavioral needs. This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a special education student, and you must act within days. If your child has been suspended and you believe their behavior is related to their disability, immediately request an ARD meeting to conduct a Manifestation Determination Review. If your child has been suspended from Eanes ISD, use the Discipline Defense Kit. It contains resources to help you understand your rights and advocate for your child during the disciplinary process.

Filing a Complaint Against Eanes ISD

If you feel that Eanes ISD is not fulfilling its legal obligations to your child, there are several avenues you can pursue to address your concerns. It's essential to know that these rights exist regardless of what any staff member may tell you. The first is a TEA State Complaint, a free and relatively straightforward process where you file a formal written complaint with the Texas Education Agency outlining the specific violations you believe have occurred. TEA will investigate your complaint and issue a decision within 60 days. The second is Mediation through TEA, which is also free. This involves a neutral third-party mediator facilitating a discussion between you and Eanes ISD to reach a mutually agreeable resolution.

If these initial steps do not resolve your concerns, you have the right to request a Due Process Hearing, which is a more formal legal proceeding where an impartial hearing officer hears evidence and makes a decision about the dispute. You also have the right to file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR), alleging discrimination based on your child's disability. Each of these options has specific timelines and procedures that must be followed, so it's important to understand your rights and seek legal advice if necessary. These options exist to ensure Eanes ISD meets its obligations to your child. Research these options and choose the path that best suits your needs.

Frequently Asked Questions About Eanes ISD Special Education

The questions every Eanes ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Eanes ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Eanes ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Eanes ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Eanes ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Eanes ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Eanes ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Eanes ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Eanes ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
