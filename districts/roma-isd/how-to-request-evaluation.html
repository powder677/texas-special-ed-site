A special education evaluation in ROMA ISD is the first step to determine if your child qualifies for specialized services. If your student is struggling with learning, behavior problems, or developmental delays, an FIE (Full Individual Evaluation) can identify their specific needs.

Parents in ROMA ISD have rights guaranteed under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act. Knowing these rights empowers you to advocate for your child effectively.

Signs that your child might need an evaluation

  • Difficulty reading, writing, or doing math
  • Frequent behavior problems in class
  • Difficulty following instructions or concentrating
  • Speech or language delay
  • Anxiety or depression affecting learning
  • Social or interaction difficulties with peers
  • Consistent low academic performance despite interventions

Step 1: Contact your child's school

The first step is to contact your child's classroom teacher or school counselor at their school within ROMA ISD. Clearly and specifically express your concerns about your student's academic performance or behavior.

Request a meeting to discuss possible interventions before formally requesting the evaluation. Many schools in ROMA ISD offer support programs such as tutoring, small reading groups, or behavior plans.

Step 2: Submit a formal written request

Although you can verbally request an FIE evaluation in ROMA ISD, it is best to do so in writing. This creates an official record of your request and establishes the legal 15-business-day timeline.

Your letter should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Their current grade and teacher
  • Specific concerns about learning or behavior
  • Concrete examples of observed difficulties
  • Explicit request for a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE)
  • Your name, phone number, and email address

Send this letter to the school principal and the ROMA ISD special education department. Keep a copy for your records.

Step 3: Await the parental rights notification

Within 15 business days after your written request, ROMA ISD must send you a Parental Rights Notification document. This document explains:

  • Your rights as a parent in ROMA ISD
  • The evaluation process
  • The areas that will be evaluated
  • How the results will be used
  • Your right to a due process hearing

Read this document carefully. If you have questions, ask ROMA ISD staff.

Step 4: Participate in the consent for evaluation meeting

ROMA ISD must meet with you before initiating the evaluation. In this meeting, the school team will explain what areas they will evaluate and what tests they will use.

You must sign a consent for evaluation form before testing begins. You have the right to ask questions and request clarifications about the process.

Important: ROMA ISD cannot begin the evaluation without your written consent.

What Do They Evaluate in ROMA ISD?

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in ROMA ISD examines multiple areas of your child's development and learning:

Standard evaluation areas

  • Cognitive skills: Reasoning, memory, problem-solving
  • Academic performance: Reading, writing, math
  • Behavior and emotions: Anxiety, depression, classroom behavior
  • Motor development: Gross and fine motor coordination
  • Language: Language comprehension and expression
  • Social skills: Interaction with peers and adults
  • Health history: Medical or vision/hearing problems

ROMA ISD will use a combination of standardized tests, classroom observations, interviews with parents and teachers, and review of school records.

After the Evaluation: The PPT Meeting in ROMA ISD

After completing the evaluation, ROMA ISD will schedule a Personalized Plan (PPT) meeting to discuss the results with you.

What to expect at the PPT meeting

The ROMA ISD team will present the evaluation findings in language you understand. They will explain if your child qualifies for special education under one of these categories:

  • Autism
  • Cognitive impairment
  • Emotional disturbance
  • Hearing impairment
  • Visual impairment
  • Orthopedic impairment
  • Speech or language impairment
  • Traumatic brain injury
  • Other health impairment
  • Multiple disabilities
  • Specific learning disability

If your child qualifies, the ROMA ISD team will develop an IEP (Individualized Education Program) with specific goals and support services.
