The 10-Day Suspension Rule

Texas law generally allows schools to suspend students for up to three days for each offense. Accumulation of multiple suspensions can significantly disrupt your child's education. Crucially, a student CANNOT be suspended for MORE THAN 10 days cumulatively in a school year unless a Change of Placement (see below) occurs. Pay close attention to the number of suspension days your child has already received. If your child is approaching or has exceeded 10 days, understand the following: Wylie ISD must provide alternative educational services beginning with the 11th day of suspension.

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

If your child is facing a suspension that will result in a cumulative total of more than 10 school days in a school year, and they have an IEP or 504 plan, Wylie ISD MUST conduct a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). The MDR team (including you, the parent) must determine if the conduct in question was a manifestation of your child's disability OR if the conduct was a direct result of the school's failure to implement the IEP or 504 plan. DEMAND an MDR if one isn't immediately scheduled.

If the MDR team determines the behavior WAS a manifestation or a result of IEP/504 failures, your child CANNOT be disciplined in the same manner as a student without a disability. The IEP/504 plan must be reviewed and revised to address the behavior. FIGHT for your child's needs to be met.

Change of Placement

A Change of Placement occurs when disciplinary action removes your child from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days OR constitutes a pattern of removals that total more than 10 school days in a school year because the child's behavior is substantially similar to the child's behavior in previous incidents that resulted in the series of removals. For students with disabilities, any Change of Placement triggers MDR requirements. If the school is attempting to change your child's placement (e.g., sending them to DAEP, expulsion), understand your rights and demand an MDR immediately! This is especially important under IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act).

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe Wylie ISD has violated your child's rights or failed to properly implement their IEP/504 plan regarding disciplinary actions, you can file a Level 1 Grievance. This is the first step in a formal complaint process. The grievance policy is available on the Wylie ISD website and likely in the student handbook. Your Level 1 Grievance should be:

  • Written and detailed, including dates, times, witnesses, and specific violations.
  • Submitted promptly to the appropriate person (usually the principal or campus administrator). Check the policy for deadlines.
  • Keep a copy of everything you submit.

The school is required to respond within a specific timeframe outlined in the grievance policy. If you are unsatisfied with the response, you can appeal to Level 2 (usually the superintendent or designee).

TEA State Complaint

If you believe Wylie ISD has violated state or federal special education laws (IDEA), you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA has specific requirements for complaints, including:

  • The complaint must be in writing.
  • It must allege a violation that occurred no more than one year prior to the date the complaint is submitted.
  • It must include a proposed resolution of the problem to the extent known and available to the parent/complainant at the time the complaint is submitted.

TEA will investigate the complaint and issue a decision. Filing a TEA complaint can be a powerful tool, but it's often a lengthy process. Consider this as an option if other avenues fail.

Due Process

Under IDEA, you have the right to due process if you disagree with decisions made by Wylie ISD regarding your child's special education program or disciplinary actions. Due process is a formal legal proceeding where you can present evidence and arguments to an impartial hearing officer. This is a serious step, and legal representation is strongly recommended. Due process can be costly and time-consuming, but it can be necessary to protect your child's rights.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
