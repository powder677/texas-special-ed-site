A Full Individual Evaluation, or FIE evaluation in GRANBURY ISD, is a complete assessment of your child's academic, social, emotional, and physical abilities. This evaluation determines whether your child has a disability that impacts their education and qualifies them for special education services.

The evaluation process in GRANBURY ISD involves multiple professionals, including special education teachers, psychologists, and other specialists who assess your child using standardized tests, classroom observations, and interviews with parents and educators.

Why this matters: Without a proper evaluation, your child won't have access to the specialized instruction and services they may need to succeed academically. Parents in GRANBURY ISD who suspect their child has a disability should know that requesting an evaluation is a fundamental right protected by federal law.

Before diving into how to request an evaluation, it's important to understand GRANBURY ISD child find responsibilities. Child Find is a federal requirement that obligates GRANBURY ISD to actively identify, locate, and evaluate all children who may have disabilities, regardless of how severe.

This means GRANBURY ISD must:

  • Screen all students for potential disabilities
  • Respond to parent concerns about development or learning
  • Evaluate students in all areas of suspected disability
  • Provide evaluations at no cost to families

If you believe your child isn't receiving attention for potential learning or developmental concerns, you can invoke child find by requesting an evaluation directly from GRANBURY ISD.

Step 1: Document Your Concerns

Before making a formal GRANBURY ISD evaluation request, gather specific examples of your concerns. Write down:

  • Academic struggles (specific subjects or skills)
  • Behavioral or social challenges
  • Speech, hearing, or physical concerns
  • Attendance patterns or school refusal
  • When you first noticed these concerns
  • Any previous testing or evaluations outside school

This documentation strengthens your request and helps GRANBURY ISD understand which areas need evaluation.

Step 2: Request a Meeting with School Personnel

Contact your child's school in GRANBURY ISD and request a meeting with the teacher, counselor, or special education coordinator. Come prepared with your documented concerns and ask what interventions have been tried to address them.

Many schools recommend intervention strategies first, but you don't have to wait for interventions to complete before requesting a special education evaluation in GRANBURY ISD. You have the right to request a formal evaluation at any time.

Step 3: Submit Your Written Evaluation Request

This is the critical step. Send a written request for a GRANBURY ISD FIE evaluation to your child's school and to the GRANBURY ISD special education department. While verbal requests may be noted, a written request creates an official record and starts the legal timeline.

Your request should include:

  • Your child's full name and student ID
  • Specific areas of concern (academic, behavioral, speech, etc.)
  • Why you believe evaluation is needed
  • A request for evaluation in all areas of suspected disability
  • Your contact information

Send this via certified mail or hand-deliver it, requesting a dated receipt. Keep copies for your records.

Step 4: Receive Notice and Provide Consent

Within 15 calendar days of your evaluation request, GRANBURY ISD must provide you with written notice describing:

  • The evaluation procedures they plan to use
  • Areas that will be assessed
  • Your rights and procedural safeguards
  • Information in your native language if needed

You must provide written consent before GRANBURY ISD can begin the evaluation. Do not feel pressured to consent to anything you don't understand. Ask questions about the assessment tools, timelines, and evaluators.

The Critical GRANBURY ISD 45 Day Timeline You Need to Know

Once you consent to the evaluation, GRANBURY ISD has 45 calendar days to complete the GRANBURY ISD 45 day timeline for the full individual evaluation. This is a hard deadline established by Texas Education Code §29.004.

This 45-day period includes:

  • All assessments and testing
  • Classroom observations
  • Interviews with parents and teachers
  • Review of educational records
  • Development of the evaluation report

The GRANBURY ISD 45 day timeline can only be extended if:

  • You request additional time
  • Your child has excessive absences
  • Unforeseen circumstances prevent completion

Mark your calendar 45 days from your consent date. If you don't receive an evaluation report and IEP eligibility meeting invitation by that date, contact GRANBURY ISD special education administration immediately.

What to Expect During the GRANBURY ISD Special Education Evaluation Process

Types of Assessments in GRANBURY ISD Evaluations

Your child's evaluation in GRANBURY ISD may include:

  • Cognitive/Intellectual Testing: Measures reasoning, memory, and problem-solving abilities
  • Academic Achievement Tests: Assesses reading, math, writing, and other academic skills
  • Behavioral/Social-Emotional Assessments: Evaluates behavior, emotions, and social skills
  • Speech and Language Evaluation: If communication is a concern
  • Occupational Therapy Evaluation: If fine motor or sensory issues are suspected
  • Physical Therapy Evaluation: If gross motor concerns exist
  • Classroom Observations: Direct observation of your child in learning environments

The Eligibility Meeting After Your GRANBURY ISD FIE Evaluation

Within the 45-day timeline for GRANBURY ISD evaluation completion, you'll be invited to an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. This is where GRANBURY ISD determines if your child qualifies for special education services.

At this meeting, you'll review the evaluation results, discuss whether your child meets criteria for one of 13 disability categories under IDEA, and if eligible, develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Your Rights as a Parent in GRANBURY ISD

Parents in GRANBURY ISD have significant legal protections during the evaluation process:

  • Right to Free Evaluation: GRANBURY ISD cannot charge you for any evaluation services
  • Right to Participate: You must be involved in planning and reviewing the evaluation
  • Right to Interpreter: If needed, GRANBURY ISD provides interpretation for meetings
  • Right to Prior Written Notice: You receive written notice of all evaluation-related decisions
  • Right to Obtain Independent Evaluation: If you disagree with GRANBURY ISD results, you can request an independent evaluation at district expense
  • Right to Representation: You can bring an advocate or attorney to meetings
  • Right to Records: You can request copies of all evaluation data and educational records

When to Seek Support: Special Education Attorney Near GRANBURY and Local Advocates

While many GRANBURY ISD evaluations proceed smoothly, sometimes parents need additional support. Consider consulting with a special education attorney near GRANBURY or a special ed advocate GRANBURY if:

  • GRANBURY ISD refuses to evaluate your child after a written request
  • The evaluation doesn't adequately assess areas of concern
  • GRANBURY ISD misses the 45-day deadline without justification
  • The eligibility decision seems incorrect
  • You disagree with the evaluation methodology or results
  • Your child is struggling and GRANBURY ISD isn't providing adequate support

A special ed advocate GRANBURY can attend meetings with you, help interpret evaluation results, and ensure GRANBURY ISD follows proper procedures. Many advocates in the GRANBURY area specialize in special education law and have deep knowledge of Texas requirements.

Preparing for Your Child's Evaluation in GRANBURY ISD

After submitting your GRANBURY ISD evaluation request, here's how to prepare:

  • Gather any prior evaluations or medical records
  • Prepare a list of questions about the evaluation process
  • Ask about which evaluators will conduct assessments
  • Request that your child be evaluated in all suspected areas of disability
  • Ensure your child gets adequate sleep before testing
  • Discuss the evaluation with your child in age-appropriate terms
  • Ask for copies of all evaluation reports before the eligibility meeting

After the Evaluation: Next Steps for GRANBURY ISD Families

Once your child's GRANBURY ISD FIE evaluation is complete and you've attended the ARD meeting, your path depends on the eligibility decision.

If your child qualifies for special education: GRANBURY ISD will develop an IEP with specific goals, services, and accommodations. You'll have an ARD meeting annually to review and update the IEP.

If your child doesn't qualify: You have the right to request an independent evaluation at GRANBURY ISD expense, request a due process hearing, or pursue evaluation through your pediatrician or private providers.

Final Thoughts: Advocating for Your Child in GRANBURY ISD

Requesting a GRANBURY ISD FIE evaluation is an act of advocacy for your child. Understanding the process, knowing your rights, and staying organized puts you in the strongest position to get your child the support they deserve.

The GRANBURY ISD 45 day timeline ensures timely evaluation, but your involvement throughout the process is what truly matters. Come to meetings prepared, ask questions, request clarification on anything you don't understand, and don't hesitate to seek professional guidance from a special ed advocate GRANBURY or attorney if needed.

Your child's educational future depends on identifying their needs and ensuring GRANBURY special education services meet those needs appropriately. You are your child's most important advocate.
