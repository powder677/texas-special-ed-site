How to Effectively Contact Carrollton Farmers Branch ISD Special Education Staff

Communicating effectively with the Carrollton Farmers Branch ISD special education team is crucial for your child's success. Here are some tips:

  • Best Times: Generally, mornings (8:00 AM - 11:00 AM) or late afternoons (3:00 PM - 4:30 PM) are good times to call, avoiding lunch hours.
  • Email vs. Phone: Email is often best for initial contact, as it allows staff to review your concerns and respond thoughtfully. Phone calls are better for urgent matters or follow-up conversations.
  • First Message: Clearly state your child's name, campus, teacher, and specific concern. Be concise and polite. For example: "My name is [Your Name], and I am the parent of [Child's Name], a student in Ms. [Teacher's Name]'s class at [School Name]. I am writing to discuss [briefly state your concern]."

Important: After any phone conversation, always follow up with a written email summarizing the discussion and any agreed-upon actions. This creates a clear record.

What to Do If You Can't Reach Anyone

If you're having trouble getting in touch with someone, follow these escalation steps:

  • Step 1: Contact the teacher directly.
  • Step 2: If you don't receive a response within a reasonable timeframe (e.g., 2 business days), contact the campus Special Education Coordinator.
  • Step 3: If you still need assistance, contact the Carrollton Farmers Branch ISD Special Education Director at the district level. Contact information should be available on the CFBISD website.
  • Step 4: As a last resort, you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Refer to Texas Education Code Chapter 29 for information on special education dispute resolution.

Your Right to District Records

You have the right to access your child's educational records and information about Carrollton Farmers Branch ISD staff.

FERPA: Under the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA), you have the right to request and review your child's educational records within 45 days of your written request. Contact the campus principal or Special Education Coordinator to initiate this process.

Public Information Act: The Texas Public Information Act (Texas Government Code, Chapter 552) allows you to request information about Carrollton Farmers Branch ISD, including staff contact information. To submit a request, send a written request (email is acceptable) to the district's designated public information officer. Clearly state what information you are seeking.

Make sure to keep copies of all correspondence and requests for your records.
