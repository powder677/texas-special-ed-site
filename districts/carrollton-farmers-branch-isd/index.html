What can I do right now if Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. Special Education in Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the special education system can feel overwhelming, but you are not alone. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas state law, special education is designed to provide individualized instruction and support to students with disabilities who require it to access their education. This includes everything from specialized teaching methods and therapies to assistive technology and classroom accommodations. The goal is to ensure your child receives a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) in the Least Restrictive Environment (LRE). It's about creating a learning environment where your child can thrive and reach their full potential within Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD.

Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD, like all public school districts in Texas, operates under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA provides guidance and resources to ensure districts are adhering to state and federal special education laws. Additionally, Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD receives support from TEA Region 10 Education Service Center, which offers professional development and technical assistance to educators and administrators in the region. Knowing this framework can help you understand how decisions are made and where to turn for additional support.

Throughout the entire special education process, from initial evaluation to the development and implementation of an Individualized Education Program (IEP), you have legally enforceable rights as a parent. These rights include the right to participate in all meetings concerning your child's special education, the right to access your child's educational records, the right to request independent evaluations, and the right to challenge decisions made by Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD if you disagree with them. Understanding these rights is crucial to advocating effectively for your child.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD

The first step in accessing special education services for your child is requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). It is essential to understand that this request must be made in writing. A verbal request to a teacher or administrator, while potentially helpful in raising awareness, does not formally start the timeline for evaluation. The written request should clearly state that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation to determine if your child has a disability and needs special education services.

Once Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD receives your written request and obtains your written consent to evaluate, they have a maximum of 45 school days to complete the Full Individual Evaluation. This timeline is strictly enforced by Texas law. It's also important to remember the "Child Find" mandate, which requires Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD to proactively identify, locate, and evaluate all children within their jurisdiction who may have a disability and need special education services, regardless of whether they are enrolled in public school. However, as a parent, you don't need to wait for the district to act on its own. Take charge.

Your next step: Draft a letter requesting a Full Individual Evaluation for your child. Clearly state your concerns and reasons for believing your child may need special education services. Keep a copy of the letter for your records and send the original to the Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD special education director via certified mail with return receipt requested. This provides proof that the district received your request and starts the 45-day clock.

ARD Meetings in Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD: Your Rights at the Table

The ARD committee, which stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal, is the cornerstone of the special education process. This committee is responsible for determining your child's eligibility for special education services, developing the Individualized Education Program (IEP), and making decisions about your child's placement. The ARD committee includes you, as the parent, along with school administrators, teachers, and other relevant professionals. It is a collaborative process where everyone works together to create a plan that meets your child's unique needs within Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD.

Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD is required to provide you with written notice at least five calendar days before any ARD meeting. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, the date, time, and location, and a list of the individuals who will be attending. During the ARD meeting, you have the right to participate fully in all discussions and decisions. You also have the right to request a ten-day recess, which is a break in the meeting, at any point if you need more time to consider information or consult with outside experts. Don't feel pressured to make immediate decisions.

It is important to remember that you are not required to sign the IEP on the same day as the ARD meeting. Take your time to review the document carefully and ensure that it accurately reflects your child's needs and your agreement with the proposed services. If you have any concerns or disagreements, you have the right to request another ARD meeting to address them. Your voice is critical in this process, so be prepared and advocate for your child's best interests within Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD.

Before your next ARD meeting in Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD, download our free ARD preparation checklist. This checklist will help you organize your thoughts, gather relevant information, and prepare questions to ask the ARD committee, ensuring you are fully equipped to advocate for your child.

Dyslexia Services in Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD

Texas law mandates that Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD screen all students for dyslexia, typically in kindergarten and first grade. This screening is designed to identify students who may be at risk for reading difficulties. If your child is identified as potentially having dyslexia, further evaluation and intervention are required. The specific methods used to screen for dyslexia are outlined by the state.

The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook sets the standards that Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD must follow in identifying, evaluating, and providing services to students with dyslexia. If your child is identified with dyslexia, they are entitled to structured literacy intervention, which is a research-based approach to teaching reading that focuses on phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and reading comprehension. Depending on the severity of your child's dyslexia and their individual needs, they may qualify for a 504 Plan or an Individualized Education Program (IEP) to provide additional support and accommodations within Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD.

If your child has not been screened for dyslexia, or if you have concerns about their reading development, request a dyslexia screening in writing from Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD immediately. Document your request and keep a copy for your records. Don't wait; early identification and intervention are crucial for students with dyslexia.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD

Disciplinary actions, particularly suspensions, can have a significant impact on students with disabilities. If your child, who receives special education services in Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD, is suspended for more than 10 cumulative school days in a school year, this triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This review is a critical process to determine if your child's behavior was a manifestation of their disability or a result of the school's failure to appropriately implement their IEP. It is crucial that you understand your rights and responsibilities during this process.

Suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative days constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA, requiring an immediate ARD meeting. This meeting will determine whether the behavior leading to the suspension was caused by or directly and substantially related to the child's disability; or, was the direct result of the Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD's failure to implement the IEP. This is one of the most time-sensitive and critical situations you will face as a parent of a special education student. You must act within days. The district is legally obligated to hold this meeting. Do not let them delay. Your child's educational future depends on it.

If your child has been suspended, use our Discipline Defense Kit immediately. This kit provides you with the information and resources you need to advocate for your child's rights and ensure that the MDR process is conducted fairly and appropriately within Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD.

Filing a Complaint Against Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD

If you believe that Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD is not fulfilling its obligations under IDEA or other special education laws, you have several avenues for resolving your concerns. It's important to remember that these rights exist regardless of what school staff may tell you.

The first escalation path is filing a formal State Complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This is a free process, and TEA is required to investigate and resolve the complaint within 60 days. Another option is Mediation through TEA, which is also a free service that brings you and Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD together with a trained mediator to attempt to resolve your disagreements. If mediation is unsuccessful or you prefer a more formal process, you can request a Due Process Hearing, which is similar to a court trial and is presided over by an impartial hearing officer. Finally, you can file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR), alleging discrimination based on disability. Each of these options has its own procedures and timelines, and the best choice for you will depend on the specific nature of your concerns. Remember to document everything and seek legal advice if needed.

Frequently Asked Questions About Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD Special Education

The questions every Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
