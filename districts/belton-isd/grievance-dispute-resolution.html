Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

If your child with an IEP is facing a suspension that will result in a Change of Placement (see below), or any accumulation of suspensions that exceed 10 school days in a school year, the school MUST conduct a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This meeting is CRUCIAL. The purpose of the MDR is to determine if the behavior leading to the disciplinary action was:

  • Caused by or directly related to the child's disability, or
  • The direct result of the school's failure to implement the IEP.

Prepare for the MDR! Gather any evidence that supports your position, including IEP documents, behavior intervention plans (BIPs), medical records, and your own observations. If the MDR team determines the behavior *was* a manifestation of the disability, the school must take immediate steps to address the IEP and BIP. This may include revising the IEP, providing additional support, or implementing alternative disciplinary measures.

Change of Placement

A "Change of Placement" occurs when a student is removed from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days, or when there is a pattern of removals that constitute a change. This could include suspension, expulsion, or placement in an alternative educational setting. Any Change of Placement triggers specific rights under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) for students with IEPs. You have the right to challenge the Change of Placement if you believe it violates your child's rights.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe Belton ISD has violated your child's rights, you can file a Level 1 grievance. This is the first step in the district's formal complaint process. Don't delay! There are strict deadlines for filing grievances. Contact Belton ISD for the specific grievance policy and timeline. Typically, you must submit your grievance in writing to the school principal or designated administrator. Clearly state the specific violation, the facts supporting your claim, and the remedy you are seeking. Keep a copy of your grievance and any documentation.

TEA State Complaint

If you are not satisfied with the outcome of the district's grievance process, or if you believe the district is not properly addressing your concerns, you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA complaints are investigated by the state. There are specific requirements for filing a TEA complaint, including a timeframe. Visit the TEA website for more information and the complaint form. Provide detailed information and supporting documentation.

Due Process

The IDEA guarantees students with disabilities the right to "due process." This means you have the right to participate in decisions regarding your child's education and to challenge those decisions if you disagree. You have the right to access your child's educational records, request evaluations, and participate in IEP meetings. If you are facing a disciplinary action that could result in a Change of Placement, you have the right to request a due process hearing. This is a formal legal proceeding where you can present evidence and argue your case before an impartial hearing officer. Due process hearings can be complex, so seeking legal representation is strongly recommended.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
