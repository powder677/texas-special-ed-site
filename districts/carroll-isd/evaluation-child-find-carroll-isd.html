A Full Individual Evaluation is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine whether a child has a disability that adversely affects their educational performance and whether they qualify for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA).

In CARROLL ISD, an FIE goes beyond a simple academic assessment. It typically includes:

  • Cognitive and intellectual testing to measure thinking and reasoning abilities
  • Academic achievement assessments in reading, math, and written expression
  • Speech and language evaluations if communication concerns exist
  • Social-emotional and behavioral assessments to understand emotional development
  • Occupational or physical therapy evaluations if motor skills are a concern
  • Classroom observations by the evaluation team
  • Parent and teacher interviews about the child's functioning across settings

CARROLL ISD uses these evaluations to build a complete picture of your child's strengths and challenges, which is essential for developing an appropriate Individualized Education Program (IEP) if your child qualifies for services.

Under federal law, school districts like CARROLL ISD have a legal obligation called "child find" to identify, locate, and evaluate all children suspected of having disabilities. This is true whether your child attends a public or private school or is homeschooled in the CARROLL area.

How CARROLL ISD Child Find Works

CARROLL ISD staff members—including teachers, counselors, and administrators—are trained to recognize signs that a student might need an evaluation. If school personnel observe developmental delays, academic struggles, or behavioral concerns, they may initiate the CARROLL ISD child find process.

However, parents have the right to request an evaluation at any time, regardless of whether the school has flagged concerns. You don't need permission from teachers or administrators to ask for a CARROLL ISD evaluation request.

Reasons to Request an FIE in CARROLL ISD

Parents in CARROLL ISD often pursue evaluations when their child experiences:

  • Significant gaps between ability and academic performance
  • Difficulty learning to read despite adequate instruction
  • Speech or language delays
  • Behavioral challenges that impact learning
  • Difficulty with social interactions or emotional regulation
  • Concerns about autism spectrum disorder or ADHD
  • Physical disabilities affecting school participation
  • Developmental delays noted by pediatricians or therapists

Initiating a CARROLL ISD FIE evaluation is straightforward, though the process varies slightly depending on whether you're making a formal written request or responding to school-initiated concerns.

Step 1: Make Your Request in Writing

Send a written request for a CARROLL ISD special education evaluation to your child's campus principal or to the CARROLL ISD Special Education Department. Your request should be clear and specific about your concerns.

A strong request includes:

  • Your child's full name and current grade level
  • Specific areas of concern (academic, behavioral, developmental)
  • Examples of struggles you've observed at home
  • Previous evaluations or professional assessments
  • Any family history of learning or developmental disabilities
  • Your contact information and preferred method of communication

You can hand-deliver your request, mail it, email it, or submit it through the school's website. Keep a copy for your records and consider sending it via email so you have confirmation of receipt.

Step 2: Understand the 15-Day Response Window

Once CARROLL ISD receives your written request for a CARROLL ISD evaluation request, the district has 15 school days to respond. During this time, CARROLL ISD may:

  • Agree to conduct the Full Individual Evaluation
  • Request additional information from you about your concerns
  • Propose a Response to Intervention (RTI) or other support plan instead of an evaluation
  • Decline to evaluate (they must provide written reasons)

If CARROLL ISD disagrees that an evaluation is necessary, they must provide you with written notice of their decision and information about your due process rights.

Step 3: Obtain Written Consent for Evaluation

If CARROLL ISD agrees to conduct the evaluation, they will send you a document requesting your written consent. This is a critical step—CARROLL ISD cannot begin the CARROLL ISD FIE evaluation without your formal permission.

Carefully review what assessments will be conducted and ask questions if anything is unclear. You have the right to request specific evaluations you believe are necessary, such as speech-language pathology or occupational therapy assessments.

Sign and return this consent form as soon as possible to start the evaluation timeline.

The CARROLL ISD 45-Day Evaluation Timeline

Once you provide consent, the CARROLL ISD 45 day timeline begins. This is the critical window during which CARROLL ISD must complete all evaluation components and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss results.

What Happens During the 45-Day Window

The CARROLL ISD 45 day timeline for special education evaluation includes:

  • Days 1-30: Individual assessments are administered, observations are conducted, and teachers/parents are interviewed
  • Days 31-40: Test results are scored and analyzed; a written evaluation report is drafted
  • Day 41-45: ARD meeting is scheduled and held to review findings and determine eligibility

During this period, evaluators from CARROLL ISD (which may include special education teachers, school psychologists, speech pathologists, and other specialists) will assess your child in multiple ways.

Classroom Observations and Testing

Your child will participate in testing sessions during the school day. CARROLL ISD evaluators will also observe your child in the regular classroom setting to see how they function academically and socially compared to peers.

You have the right to be present during testing. Many parents find it helpful to observe to understand what their child is experiencing and to ask questions after the evaluation concludes.

Meeting the CARROLL ISD 45 Day Timeline

CARROLL ISD is legally required to meet this deadline. If circumstances beyond the district's control delay evaluation, CARROLL ISD must notify you in writing and explain the reason for the extension.

If CARROLL ISD exceeds the 45-day timeline without your agreement, you may file a due process complaint. This is an important safeguard for parents in CARROLL ISD families.

Your Rights During the CARROLL ISD FIE Evaluation Process

As a parent in CARROLL ISD, you have significant legal protections under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and the Texas Education Code.

Key Parent Rights in CARROLL ISD Special Education Evaluation

  • Right to request an evaluation: You can ask for a CARROLL ISD evaluation request at any time, for any reason
  • Right to participate: You must be included in meetings and decisions about your child's evaluation
  • Right to review records: You can access all evaluation materials and test results before the ARD meeting
  • Right to an independent evaluation: If you disagree with CARROLL ISD's assessment, you can request an independent evaluation at the district's expense (in certain circumstances)
  • Right to interpreter services: If English is not your primary language, CARROLL ISD must provide interpretation
  • Right to refuse consent: You can decline an evaluation or refuse certain assessment components
  • Right to due process: You can file a formal complaint if you believe CARROLL ISD violated special education law

Getting a Copy of the Evaluation Report

CARROLL ISD must provide you with a copy of the complete evaluation report before the ARD meeting. Review this carefully and note any concerns about:

  • Whether assessments seem to accurately reflect your child's abilities
  • Whether cultural or linguistic factors may have affected results
  • Whether the evaluator's conclusions match the test data
  • Whether your child's strengths are properly documented

Understanding CARROLL ISD Eligibility Categories

If the CARROLL ISD FIE evaluation indicates your child has a disability, the ARD team will determine which eligibility category applies. CARROLL ISD recognizes 13 disability categories under IDEA:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Deaf-Blindness
  • Deafness
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Specific Learning Disability
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Visual Impairment

Your child must meet criteria in one of these categories AND have a documented need for special education services to qualify for an IEP in CARROLL ISD.

What Happens After the CARROLL ISD 45-Day Evaluation

Once the evaluation is complete, CARROLL ISD must hold an ARD meeting to discuss findings and next steps.

The ARD Meeting Process

The Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting brings together:

  • You (the parent or guardian)
  • Your child (when appropriate)
  • CARROLL ISD special education and regular education teachers
  • A school administrator or designee
  • Evaluation team members
  • Other specialists as needed

During this meeting, the team reviews evaluation results and decides whether your child qualifies for special education services under CARROLL ISD policy.

If Your Child Qualifies for Special Education in CARROLL ISD

The ARD team will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) that outlines:

  • Your child's present level of academic and functional performance
  • Annual goals tailored to address identified needs
  • Special education services (type, frequency, duration)
  • Related services (speech therapy, counseling, occupational therapy, etc.)
  • Accommodations and modifications in the regular classroom
  • Standardized testing accommodations
  • Transition planning (for students age 14 and older)

If Your Child Does Not Qualify

If CARROLL ISD determines your child does not have a disability under IDEA, you have the right to:

  • Request an independent evaluation at CARROLL ISD's expense
  • Ask for a meeting to discuss alternative supports or interventions
  • Explore eligibility for Section 504 protections
  • File a due process complaint if you disagree
