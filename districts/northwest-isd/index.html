What can I do right now if Northwest ISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Northwest ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Northwest ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the special education system can feel overwhelming, but understanding your rights and the processes involved is crucial for advocating for your child. Special education, under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas law, is specially designed instruction to meet the unique needs of a child with a disability. This can include accommodations, modifications, and related services like speech therapy, occupational therapy, or counseling.

Northwest ISD operates under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA Region 11 Education Service Center provides support and resources to Northwest ISD, helping the district implement effective special education programs. As a parent, you have legally enforceable rights throughout the entire special education process, from initial evaluation to the development and implementation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP). Understanding these rights is key to ensuring your child receives the appropriate support and services in Northwest ISD.

Remember, you are an equal and vital member of your child's ARD committee and your voice matters. As your child moves through the Northwest ISD system, it's important to keep current on the applicable special education laws.

Action to take now: Bookmark this page for easy access to this important information.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Northwest ISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability that requires special education services, the first step is to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) from Northwest ISD. It's important to know that this request must be in writing. A verbal request does not trigger the timeline for the evaluation. The written request should clearly state your concerns and the reasons why you believe your child may need special education services. You can address this letter to the principal of your child's school or the special education director for Northwest ISD.

Once Northwest ISD receives your written consent to evaluate, the district has 45 school days to complete the FIE. This evaluation will assess your child's strengths and weaknesses in various areas, such as academic skills, communication skills, and social-emotional development. Child Find is a legal requirement that mandates Northwest ISD to proactively identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities who reside within their boundaries, regardless of whether they are enrolled in public school. This means the district should be actively looking for students who may need services, even if you haven't specifically requested an evaluation. If you have any concerns, don't hesitate to reach out.

Action to take now: Draft a written request for a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE), make a copy for your records, and send it to the principal of your child's Northwest ISD school via certified mail with return receipt requested.

ARD Meetings in Northwest ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal, and it refers to the committee responsible for making decisions about your child's special education program. The ARD committee includes you, as the parent, along with school staff such as teachers, administrators, diagnosticians, and related service providers. Northwest ISD is required to provide you with written notice at least five calendar days before any ARD meeting. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, the date, time, location, and the names of the individuals who will be attending.

During the ARD meeting, you have the right to participate fully in all discussions and decisions regarding your child's IEP. If at any point during the ARD meeting you feel overwhelmed or need more time to consider the information being presented, you can request a 10-day recess. This gives you time to review the proposed IEP, consult with experts, and gather your thoughts. You are not obligated to sign the IEP on the same day as the ARD meeting. Take your time to carefully review the document and ensure that it accurately reflects your child's needs and your concerns. Northwest ISD is required to follow the agreed upon IEP to the best of their ability.

The IEP will be reviewed at least annually, but more often if needed. Parents are always welcome to schedule an ARD meeting if they feel their child's IEP is not being implemented correctly or no longer meets their child's individual needs.

Dyslexia Services in Northwest ISD

Texas law mandates that Northwest ISD screen every student for dyslexia. This screening is typically conducted in early elementary grades to identify students who may be at risk for reading difficulties. The Texas Dyslexia Handbook, updated in 2024, sets the standards that Northwest ISD must follow for identifying and providing services to students with dyslexia. This handbook outlines the specific screening procedures, instructional methods, and accommodations that should be used.

If your child is identified as having dyslexia in Northwest ISD, they are entitled to structured literacy intervention. This evidence-based approach focuses on teaching the foundational skills of reading and spelling in a systematic and explicit manner. In addition to structured literacy intervention, students with dyslexia may also qualify for a 504 Plan or an IEP. A 504 Plan provides accommodations to help students access the general education curriculum, while an IEP provides specialized instruction and related services tailored to the student's individual needs.

Action to take now: If your child has not been screened for dyslexia in Northwest ISD, submit a written request for screening to the school principal.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Northwest ISD

Discipline can be a particularly sensitive issue for students with disabilities. If a special education student in Northwest ISD is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year, it triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This review is conducted by the ARD committee to determine if the student's misconduct was caused by their disability or if it was a result of the school's failure to properly implement the IEP. A suspension of that length constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA, requiring an ARD meeting.

This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a special education student. You must act quickly to protect your child's rights. If your child has been suspended or is facing disciplinary action, contact us or another special education advocate immediately. An ARD meeting must be held promptly to determine whether the behavior was a manifestation of their disability and to review the IEP.

Action to take now: If your child has been suspended in Northwest ISD, immediately use the Discipline Defense Kit.

Filing a Complaint Against Northwest ISD

If you believe that Northwest ISD is not complying with special education laws, you have several options for filing a complaint. Understanding these options is crucial, and remember that these rights exist regardless of what any Northwest ISD staff member may tell you. The first, and often simplest, path is to file a TEA State Complaint. This is a free process, and TEA is required to resolve the complaint within 60 days. This process involves submitting a written complaint to TEA outlining the specific violations you believe have occurred.

Another free option is Mediation through TEA, where a neutral third party helps you and Northwest ISD reach a mutually agreeable resolution. If mediation is unsuccessful or you prefer a more formal process, you can request a Due Process Hearing, which is a more adversarial legal proceeding before an independent hearing officer. Finally, you can file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR) if you believe that Northwest ISD has discriminated against your child based on their disability. Filing a complaint with OCR is also a free process. Each of these escalation paths has specific procedures and timelines, so it's important to carefully consider your options and seek legal advice if needed.

Action to take now: Research the TEA State Complaint process to become familiar with the requirements and procedures.

Frequently Asked Questions About Northwest ISD Special Education

The questions every Northwest ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Northwest ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Northwest ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Northwest ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Northwest ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Northwest ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Northwest ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Northwest ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Northwest ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
