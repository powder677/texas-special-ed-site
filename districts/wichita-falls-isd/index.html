About Special Education in Wichita Falls ISD

Wichita Falls ISD serves the Texas area, providing special education services to students with disabilities. The district operates under the oversight of TEA Region unknown, ensuring compliance with state and federal regulations regarding special education. Wichita Falls ISD is committed to providing a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) to all eligible students.

The Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) process in Wichita Falls ISD follows specific timelines. After a referral for special education services, the district has approximately 45 school days to complete a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Parents will receive at least a five-day written notice prior to any ARD committee meeting to allow adequate time to prepare and participate.

Common Questions from Wichita Falls ISD Families

What if I disagree with the results of my child's FIE?

You have the right to disagree with the FIE results. In Wichita Falls ISD, you can request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at public expense if you believe the district's evaluation was not appropriate. The district may choose to either grant the IEE or initiate a due process hearing to defend their evaluation.

My child's IEP goals aren't challenging enough; what can I do?

You have the right to advocate for more challenging goals that will help your child progress. Discuss your concerns with the ARD committee, providing specific examples of areas where you believe your child can achieve more. You can also bring data or work samples to the ARD meeting to support your request for more rigorous goals.

How can I stay informed about changes in special education law affecting my child?

Wichita Falls ISD provides information on special education policies and procedures through its website and parent meetings. Additionally, consider joining local parent groups or advocacy organizations that focus on special education. These groups often share updates on legal changes and provide support and resources to families.

Practical Tip for Parents

Navigating special education in a district like Wichita Falls ISD (approximately 10,000 students) can sometimes feel overwhelming. Because smaller districts often have one special education director handling numerous responsibilities, communicating via email is often more effective than phone calls. Email allows you to create a written record of your communications, which can be helpful if you need to reference past conversations or decisions.
