What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

An MDR, or Manifestation Determination Review, is a critical meeting held when a student with an IEP faces disciplinary action resulting in a suspension of more than 10 school days or a change of placement. The purpose of the MDR is to determine if the student's behavior was caused by, or directly and substantially related to, the student's disability, OR if the behavior was the direct result of the district's failure to implement the IEP.

IF AN MDR IS REQUIRED, YOU HAVE THE RIGHT TO BE PRESENT AND PARTICIPATE! Demand access to all records and documentation related to your child's behavior and IEP BEFORE the meeting. Don't be intimidated. Advocate for your child. If the MDR determines the behavior WAS related to the disability or IEP failure, the district MUST take steps to remedy the situation. They cannot just continue to suspend or expel your child.

CRITICAL: Even if your child doesn't have a formal IEP, if you suspect a disability (ADHD, learning disability, emotional disturbance, etc.), inform the school IN WRITING immediately. This can trigger child find obligations under IDEA and may entitle your child to an MDR if discipline escalates.

Change of Placement

A change of placement occurs when a student is removed from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days, or when a series of removals creates a pattern totaling more than 10 days in a school year and constitutes a significant change in the student's educational program. This can include moving your child to a Disciplinary Alternative Education Program (DAEP).

A change of placement requires procedural safeguards under IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) if your child has an IEP. This includes the right to challenge the decision through due process (see below).

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe Wichita Falls ISD has violated your child's rights, start with a Level 1 Grievance. This is the first step in the formal grievance process. Obtain the grievance form from the school or district website. Be specific and factual. Include dates, names, and a clear description of the incident or violation. Submit the grievance to the designated school administrator (usually the principal). There is a deadline, so act quickly! Typically, you have a limited number of days (often 10-15) to file the grievance from the date you became aware of the issue. Keep a copy of the grievance and proof of submission.

If you are not satisfied with the Level 1 response, you can escalate to Level 2 and then Level 3, following the district's grievance policy. Each level has its own deadlines, so stay organized and meet each deadline.

TEA State Complaint

If you believe Wichita Falls ISD has violated IDEA or other special education laws, you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This is a formal process and requires specific documentation. This is in addition to, not instead of, the grievance process. The TEA will investigate your complaint and can issue findings of non-compliance, requiring the district to take corrective action.

Due Process

Due process is a legal procedure that allows parents of students with disabilities to challenge decisions made by the school district regarding their child's education. You can request due process if you disagree with the MDR outcome, a proposed change of placement, or other special education-related issues. Due process is a formal and potentially lengthy process, but it provides you with the opportunity to present your case before an impartial hearing officer. Consulting with an attorney specializing in special education law is highly recommended if you pursue due process.

REMEMBER: DOCUMENT EVERYTHING! Keep copies of all correspondence, emails, disciplinary referrals, IEPs, and any other relevant documentation. Time is of the essence. Don't delay in taking action to protect your child's rights. Contact Disability Rights Texas or another advocacy organization for assistance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
