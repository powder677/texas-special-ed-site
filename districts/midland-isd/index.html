About Special Education in Midland ISD

Midland ISD serves the West Texas area, providing educational services to a large student population. The district is committed to ensuring that students with disabilities receive a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment. Midland ISD operates under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA) Region 18, adhering to all state and federal regulations regarding special education services.

The Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) process in Midland ISD follows specific timelines to ensure timely evaluations and individualized education program (IEP) development. Following a referral for special education, the district has approximately 45 school days to complete the Full and Individual Evaluation (FIE). Parents will receive a written notice of the ARD meeting at least five school days prior to the scheduled meeting.

Common Questions from Midland ISD Families

What if I disagree with the school's evaluation results?

If you disagree with the results of your child's FIE, you have the right to request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at public expense if certain conditions are met. Talk to your child's ARD committee and the special education administration about the process to request an IEE.

My child attends a magnet school. Where will special education services be provided?

Special education services for students attending magnet schools within Midland ISD are determined on a case-by-case basis by the ARD committee. The committee will consider your child's individual needs and the services available at both the magnet school and potentially at a support campus.

How can I get more involved in my child's special education program?

Parent involvement is crucial! Actively participate in ARD meetings, communicate regularly with your child's teachers and special education staff, and consider joining the Midland ISD Special Education Parent Advisory Committee (PAC) if one exists. The PAC can provide valuable support and a voice for parents.

Practical Tip for Parents

Navigating a large district like Midland ISD (with over 24,000 students) can feel overwhelming. A helpful first step is to connect directly with the dedicated special education campus coordinator at your child's school. They are your primary point of contact and can address most concerns or connect you with the appropriate district-level resources before escalating issues to the central administration office.
