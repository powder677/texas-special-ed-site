ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal—the formal meeting where school teams and parents work together to develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). In WIMBERLEY ISD, the ARD committee determines whether your child qualifies for special education, what services they'll receive, and how their progress will be measured.

Under Texas Education Code Section 29.001, WIMBERLEY ISD is required to hold ARD meetings to ensure every student with a disability receives an appropriate, free public education. Whether this is your first WIMBERLEY ISD ARD meeting or a review meeting, knowing what to expect gives you confidence and prepares you to be an effective partner in your child's education.

Key Players at a WIMBERLEY ISD ARD Meeting

The ARD committee at WIMBERLEY ISD includes:

  • Parents or guardians (that's you—your voice matters)
  • Special education teacher from WIMBERLEY ISD
  • General education teacher representing regular classroom instruction
  • School administrator or designee familiar with special education
  • Diagnostician or counselor who can interpret evaluation results
  • Your child (when appropriate, especially for transition planning)
  • Other specialists (speech therapist, occupational therapist, etc., as needed)

Understanding the WIMBERLEY ISD IEP Process Step-by-Step

The WIMBERLEY ISD IEP process follows specific stages mandated by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas law.

Step 1: Referral and Initial Evaluation

The process begins when someone—a teacher, parent, or healthcare provider—refers your child for evaluation. WIMBERLEY ISD must evaluate your child within 60 calendar days if you consent to testing. This evaluation determines whether your child qualifies for special education services under one of thirteen disability categories recognized by Texas.

Step 2: Initial ARD Meeting for Eligibility

Once evaluations are complete, WIMBERLEY ISD holds an initial ARD meeting to determine eligibility. The committee reviews assessment data and decides whether your child has a disability affecting educational performance. If eligible, an IEP is developed. If ineligible, WIMBERLEY ISD may offer a 504 Plan or other supports.

Step 3: Developing WIMBERLEY ISD IEP Goals

At your WIMBERLEY ISD ARD meeting, the committee develops measurable annual goals addressing your child's needs. WIMBERLEY ISD IEP goals must be specific, observable, and achievable within one school year. They should focus on academic skills, social-emotional development, or functional abilities depending on your child's needs.

Effective goals follow the SMART framework: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. For example, instead of "improve reading," a strong goal for a WIMBERLEY ISD student might be: "By May, [Student] will read grade-level passages with 90% accuracy on weekly assessments."

Step 4: Determining Services and Placement

The ARD committee at WIMBERLEY ISD specifies what services your child needs—special education instruction, speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, or other supports. The committee also determines your child's placement, typically on a continuum from general education with supports to specialized settings. WIMBERLEY ISD is committed to providing services in the least restrictive environment appropriate for your child.

Step 5: Annual Review

WIMBERLEY ISD holds an ARD meeting at least annually to review your child's progress, update goals, and adjust services as needed. You may request a meeting more frequently if you have concerns about your child's progress in the WIMBERLEY ISD IEP process.
