Clint ISD Special Education Resources

⚡ Quick Answer

What can I do right now if Clint ISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Clint ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Clint ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the special education system can feel overwhelming, but you're not alone. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas law, special education provides specialized instruction and related services to eligible students with disabilities. These services are designed to meet your child's unique needs and help them succeed in school. Clint ISD is committed to providing a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) to all eligible students within its boundaries.

Clint ISD, like all school districts in Texas, is overseen by the Texas Education Agency (TEA). To assist districts, TEA provides support through its regional Education Service Centers (ESCs). Clint ISD is located within TEA Region 19 Education Service Center, which offers various training, resources, and technical assistance to the district and its staff. This collaboration helps ensure that Clint ISD implements best practices in special education.

It's essential to remember that you, as a parent, have enforceable legal rights throughout the entire special education process. From initial evaluation to developing and implementing an Individualized Education Program (IEP), you are a vital member of your child's ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee. Your voice matters, and you have the right to participate fully in all decisions concerning your child's education. Clint ISD must respect these rights and provide you with the necessary information and support to make informed choices.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Clint ISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability that impacts their learning, the first step is to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) from Clint ISD. It is critical that this request is made in writing. A verbal request, while it may be well-intentioned, does not officially start the timeline for evaluation under state and federal law. Only a written request triggers the district's obligation to evaluate your child.

Once Clint ISD receives your written consent to evaluate, they have 45 school days to complete the FIE. During this time, qualified professionals will assess your child's academic, cognitive, and developmental skills to determine if they meet the criteria for special education eligibility. Remember, Child Find laws require Clint ISD to proactively identify students who may need special education services. This means that if the district suspects your child may have a disability, they also have a responsibility to initiate the evaluation process. Don't wait; if you have concerns, you have the right to request an evaluation.

Your next step: Write a letter to the Clint ISD special education director, clearly stating that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for your child. Include your child's name, date of birth, school, and grade. Keep a copy of the letter for your records and send it via certified mail with a return receipt requested. This will provide you with proof that Clint ISD received your request.

ARD Meetings in Clint ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal. The ARD committee is the group of individuals responsible for making decisions about your child's special education program. This committee includes you, your child (when appropriate), a special education teacher, a general education teacher, a representative from Clint ISD, and other relevant professionals.

Clint ISD is required to provide you with at least five calendar days of written notice before any ARD meeting. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, the date, time, location, and the names of the individuals who will be attending. It's important to remember that you have the right to request a ten-day recess during an ARD meeting. This means that if you feel overwhelmed or need more time to consider information presented, you can ask the committee to pause the meeting for up to ten calendar days to allow you time to process everything.

Another critical right to remember: You are not required to sign the IEP (Individualized Education Program) on the same day it is presented. You have the right to take the IEP home, review it carefully, and seek advice from outside professionals if needed. Only sign the IEP when you are comfortable and agree with all of its provisions. Clint ISD must respect your decision and schedule another meeting to finalize the IEP if necessary. Dyslexia Services in Clint ISD

Texas law mandates that Clint ISD screen all students for dyslexia. This screening is typically conducted in the early grades to identify students who may be at risk for reading difficulties. If your child's screening indicates a potential risk for dyslexia, Clint ISD is required to provide further evaluation and, if diagnosed, appropriate interventions.

The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook sets the standards that Clint ISD must follow in identifying and supporting students with dyslexia. This handbook outlines the criteria for diagnosis, the types of structured literacy interventions that must be provided, and the accommodations and modifications that may be necessary to ensure your child's success. Students identified with dyslexia are entitled to receive structured literacy intervention, a specific type of reading instruction designed to address the core deficits associated with dyslexia. Depending on the severity of your child's dyslexia and their individual needs, they may also qualify for a 504 Plan or an IEP. If your child has not yet been screened for dyslexia, request screening in writing immediately from Clint ISD.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Clint ISD

Disciplinary actions, particularly suspensions, can have a significant impact on students with disabilities. If your child with an IEP or 504 Plan is suspended for more than 10 cumulative school days in a school year within Clint ISD, this triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). An MDR is a process to determine whether the student's misconduct was a manifestation of their disability or a result of the district's failure to implement the IEP or 504 Plan. This is considered a Change of Placement under IDEA.

This situation requires immediate action. A suspension exceeding 10 days constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA and requires an ARD meeting to be held within a matter of days. This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a special education student. If the ARD committee determines that the behavior was a manifestation of your child's disability, they cannot be suspended or expelled in the same way as a general education student. They must instead review and revise the IEP or 504 Plan to address the underlying behavioral issues. If your child has been suspended, use our Discipline Defense Kit immediately to protect your child's rights.

Filing a Complaint Against Clint ISD

Sometimes, despite your best efforts, disagreements with Clint ISD may arise regarding your child's special education services. In these situations, it's important to know that you have several options for escalating your concerns. There are multiple avenues you can pursue if you disagree with the district's decisions, and you have the right to utilize these regardless of what you might be told by district staff.

The first escalation path is filing a TEA State Complaint, a free process that is typically resolved within 60 days. Mediation through TEA is another free option, providing a neutral third party to help facilitate communication and find a resolution. You also have the right to request a Due Process Hearing, a more formal legal proceeding where an impartial hearing officer will make a binding decision. Finally, you can file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR), which investigates allegations of discrimination based on disability. Remember, these rights exist regardless of what Clint ISD staff may tell you. Familiarize yourself with these options and don't hesitate to pursue them if you believe your child's rights are being violated. Start by reviewing the TEA's Dispute Resolution Handbook to understand the complaint process in detail.

Frequently Asked Questions About Clint ISD Special Education

The questions every Clint ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Clint ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Clint ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Clint ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Clint ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Clint ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Clint ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Clint ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Clint ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
