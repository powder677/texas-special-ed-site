The 10-Day Suspension Rule

Clint ISD, like all Texas schools, has rules about how long your child can be suspended. Generally, a student can be suspended for up to 3 school days for each incident. Crucially, if your child's cumulative suspensions (across different incidents) reach 10 school days in a school year, this triggers important rights, especially if your child has an IEP or 504 plan. Keep meticulous records of every suspension. Don't rely solely on the school to track this; mistakes happen. Double-check their figures. Demand written documentation for every suspension.

What Is an MDR?

MDR stands for Manifestation Determination Review. This is a meeting that must be held if your child with an IEP or 504 plan faces a suspension exceeding 10 cumulative days in a school year, or is being considered for a Change of Placement (see below). The purpose of the MDR is to determine if the student's behavior leading to the disciplinary action was caused by their disability or a failure to implement the IEP/504 plan. Prepare THOROUGHLY for this meeting! Gather all relevant medical records, IEP documentation, behavior intervention plans (BIPs), and any communication with the school related to your child's disability. If the MDR finds that the behavior was a manifestation of the disability or a failure to implement the IEP/504, the school cannot discipline the student in the same way as a non-disabled student. They MUST address the underlying cause of the behavior.

Change of Placement

A "Change of Placement" is any removal of your child from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days, or a series of removals that constitute a pattern (even if each removal is less than 10 days). This can include expulsion, alternative education programs (DAEP or JJAEP), or even certain in-school suspensions if they drastically alter the student's educational experience. If Clint ISD proposes a Change of Placement, it triggers significant rights under IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) if your child has an IEP. You have the right to challenge the Change of Placement, request an MDR, and invoke Due Process (see below). DO NOT agree to anything without understanding the full implications.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe Clint ISD has violated your child's rights or acted unfairly in a disciplinary matter, you have the right to file a grievance. The first step is usually a Level 1 grievance, submitted in writing to the school principal or the administrator directly responsible for the action you are grieving. Be specific and detailed in your grievance. Include dates, names, descriptions of events, and the specific policies or laws you believe were violated. Keep a copy of your grievance and any responses from the school. There are strict timelines for filing grievances; MISSING THE DEADLINE COULD MEAN LOSING YOUR RIGHT TO APPEAL. Consult Clint ISD's policy manual immediately for specific deadlines and procedures.

TEA State Complaint

If you are unsatisfied with the outcome of the grievance process within Clint ISD, or if you believe the district is violating state or federal special education laws, you can file a formal complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA complaints require very specific information and evidence. The TEA investigates the complaint and can order the district to take corrective action. Contact TEA immediately to understand the specific requirements for filing a complaint and to ensure you meet all deadlines. This is a powerful tool, but requires careful preparation.

Due Process

If you disagree with Clint ISD's decisions regarding your child's IEP, disciplinary actions, or Change of Placement, you have the right to request Due Process through the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Due Process is a formal legal procedure that allows you to present your case to an impartial hearing officer. This is a complex process that often requires legal representation. Contact a special education attorney or advocate immediately if you are considering Due Process. This is especially crucial if the district is attempting to expel your child or make a significant Change of Placement. You have very specific timelines for requesting Due Process, and missing those deadlines can be devastating. This is your LAST resort within the school system and you need professional assistance to navigate it effectively.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
