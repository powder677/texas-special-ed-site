Requesting an Evaluation in Lake Travis ISD

⚡ Quick Answer

How long does Texas have to complete an evaluation?
Once a written evaluation request is received, the district must complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) and hold an ARD meeting within 45 school days. This timeline is strictly enforced by the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Verbal requests do not start the clock — always submit in writing and keep a copy.

Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in Lake Travis ISD

This guide outlines the process of requesting a special education evaluation for your child in Lake Travis ISD (LTISD), adhering to Texas state law. If you suspect your child may have a disability affecting their educational performance, you have the right to request a comprehensive evaluation to determine eligibility for special education services.

How to Submit a Written Request

The most crucial step in initiating the special education process is submitting a formal, written request for an evaluation. This request serves as official notification to LTISD of your concerns. The letter should be addressed to the appropriate school official, typically the Principal, Special Education Director, or Section 504 Coordinator at your child's campus. Keeping a copy of the letter, with the date you sent it, is imperative.

Your letter should clearly state the following:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth.
  • Your child's school and grade level.
  • A clear statement requesting a full and individual initial evaluation for special education services. Examples include: "I am formally requesting a full and individual initial evaluation for my child, [Child's Name], to determine eligibility for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA)." or "I am requesting a special education evaluation for [Child's Name] to determine if he/she is eligible to receive special education services and related services."
  • A detailed explanation of your concerns regarding your child's academic, behavioral, or developmental difficulties. Be specific and provide examples. Mention any specific areas of struggle, such as reading, writing, math, attention, or social skills. Reference any previous interventions or accommodations that have been attempted and their outcomes. Include examples of failing grades or test scores if applicable.
  • Your contact information, including your phone number and email address.
  • Clearly state that you are requesting a full and individual evaluation under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA).

Sending your request via certified mail with return receipt requested provides proof of delivery and acceptance. You can also hand-deliver the request and obtain a signed receipt from the school. This documentation is essential in case of any disputes regarding the timeline or the district's response.

The 45 School Day Timeline

Texas law mandates specific timelines for the special education evaluation process. Upon receiving your written request, LTISD has 15 school days to determine if an evaluation is warranted. This is known as the decision-making period. During this time, the school will review your request and gather additional information, such as teacher observations and academic records. If they decide to evaluate, you must be provided with written notice and parental consent forms to sign.

If the school decides to evaluate, they have a total of 45 school days (from the date they receive your consent) to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committee meeting to discuss the results and determine eligibility for special education services. This 45-day timeline excludes school holidays and breaks exceeding five school days.

What the FIE Must Cover

The Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment conducted by qualified professionals. It must cover all areas of suspected disability and assess your child's educational needs. Texas law requires the FIE to include information from various sources, including:

  • Individual testing, including standardized tests of academic achievement, cognitive abilities, and adaptive behavior.
  • Classroom observations.
  • Parent input and observations.
  • Teacher input.
  • Review of academic records and work samples.
  • Assessments in all areas related to the suspected disability, such as speech-language, occupational therapy, physical therapy, or psychological services.

The FIE must be administered in the child's native language or mode of communication, unless it is clearly not feasible to do so. The evaluation report must be written in clear, understandable language and provide specific recommendations for instructional strategies and supports.

Child Find Obligations

LTISD has a legal obligation under federal and state law, often referred to as "Child Find," to actively identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities residing within the district, regardless of whether they are enrolled in public school. This means that LTISD should not wait for parents to request an evaluation. If school personnel suspect that a child may have a disability, they are required to take steps to determine if an evaluation is necessary. This responsibility applies to all children from birth to age 21.

What If They Refuse

If LTISD refuses to conduct an evaluation, they must provide you with written notice explaining the reasons for their refusal. This notice must include information about your right to disagree with the school's decision and the procedural safeguards available to you under IDEA. You have the right to request mediation, file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA), or request a due process hearing to challenge the school's decision.

After the Evaluation

Once the FIE is completed, an ARD committee meeting will be scheduled. The ARD committee includes you, your child (when appropriate), school administrators, teachers, and evaluation personnel. At the ARD meeting, the evaluation results will be discussed, and the committee will determine if your child meets the criteria for special education services. If your child is found eligible, the ARD committee will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) that outlines the specific services and supports your child will receive to address their educational needs. Remember to actively participate in the ARD process and advocate for your child's needs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Texas? +

Submit a written request directly to the school's principal or special education director. The written request starts the mandatory 45 school-day clock. Keep a dated copy for your records.

What is a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in Texas? +

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment the school must complete to determine if your child has a disability and needs special education services. It must cover all areas of suspected disability and be completed within 45 school days of your written consent.

Can a Texas school refuse to evaluate my child? +

Yes, but only if the district provides written notice (Prior Written Notice) explaining why they are refusing and what data they relied on. You have the right to challenge that refusal through mediation or a due process hearing.
