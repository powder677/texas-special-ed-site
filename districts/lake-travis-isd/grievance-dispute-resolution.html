Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

If your child in Lake Travis ISD is facing disciplinary action, especially if they have an IEP or 504 plan, ACT NOW! Time is of the essence. The 10-Day Suspension Rule

Texas law allows schools to suspend students for up to three days for certain violations, and potentially longer (up to 10 days) for other violations. PAY ATTENTION! Accumulating suspensions, even for short periods, can lead to more severe consequences like placement in a Disciplinary Alternative Education Program (DAEP). If your child is approaching 10 days of suspension in a school year, it's CRITICAL to understand your rights, particularly if your child has a disability.

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

If your child with an IEP is facing a suspension that will exceed 10 cumulative school days in a year (or a change of placement - see below), Lake Travis ISD MUST conduct a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This meeting determines whether the student's behavior was caused by, or directly and substantially related to, their disability; OR whether the behavior was a direct result of the school's failure to implement the IEP. This is HUGE.

Demand an MDR immediately if your child is facing disciplinary action that could lead to more than 10 days of suspension. Ensure you, the IEP team (including relevant specialists), and the administration participate. Bring documentation supporting your claim that the behavior is related to your child's disability or a failure in IEP implementation. If the MDR determines the behavior was a manifestation of the disability, the school CANNOT discipline the student in the same way as a student without a disability. The IEP must be reviewed and revised, and a Functional Behavioral Assessment (FBA) and Behavior Intervention Plan (BIP) may be necessary.

Change of Placement

A "Change of Placement" under IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) occurs when a student with an IEP is removed from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days, or when there is a pattern of removals that constitute a change. This includes placement in a DAEP or home suspension. If a Change of Placement is happening or threatened, trigger the MDR process IMMEDIATELY! A Change of Placement significantly impacts your child's education, and you have the right to fight it if it's not appropriate or legally sound.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe Lake Travis ISD has violated your child's rights related to discipline (even outside of special education), you can file a formal grievance. The first step is typically a Level 1 grievance, addressed to the campus principal. Document EVERYTHING! Keep copies of all correspondence, emails, and notes from meetings. Your grievance should clearly state the specific violation, the dates it occurred, and the remedy you seek. Follow Lake Travis ISD's grievance policy precisely (find it on their website). Expect a response within a specific timeframe (check the policy). If you are unsatisfied with the response, you can escalate to Level 2 and potentially Level 3.

TEA State Complaint

If you believe Lake Travis ISD has violated federal or state laws related to special education, you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This is a more formal process and requires specific evidence. TEA has a limited timeframe for investigating complaints, so act quickly. This is a powerful tool, but it requires careful preparation and documentation. Consider consulting with a special education attorney to help you prepare a strong TEA complaint.

Due Process

If you disagree with the outcome of an MDR or other disciplinary decisions, and you believe your child's rights under IDEA have been violated, you have the right to request a Due Process Hearing. This is a formal legal proceeding where you can present evidence and argue your case before an impartial hearing officer. Due Process is a complex and time-consuming process. Seek legal representation immediately if you are considering requesting a Due Process Hearing. The timelines for filing a Due Process complaint are strict, so don't delay.

Remember: You are your child's best advocate. Be informed, be persistent, and seek legal counsel as soon as possible. Don't let Lake Travis ISD violate your child's rights.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
