Dyslexia Services in Lake Travis ISD

Dyslexia Services in Lake Travis ISD

Texas Dyslexia Handbook 2024

Lake Travis ISD (LTISD) is committed to providing comprehensive dyslexia services aligned with the updated Texas Dyslexia Handbook (TDH) 2024. This handbook, published by the Texas Education Agency (TEA), serves as the guiding document for identifying, evaluating, and providing support to students with dyslexia. Parents and educators are encouraged to familiarize themselves with the TDH, which can be accessed on the TEA website. The handbook outlines best practices in dyslexia intervention, including the implementation of structured literacy approaches. Key updates in the 2024 handbook often include refined screening procedures, clarified evaluation criteria, and enhanced guidance on evidence-based interventions. LTISD adheres to all requirements and recommendations outlined in the TDH to ensure that students with dyslexia receive appropriate and effective support.

Mandatory Screening

LTISD implements mandatory dyslexia screening for all kindergarten students, as well as any student exhibiting characteristics of dyslexia at any grade level. These screenings are designed to identify students who may be at risk for dyslexia early in their academic careers. Screening typically involves assessing foundational literacy skills such as phonological awareness, phonemic awareness, letter-sound knowledge, and rapid automatized naming (RAN). The specific screening tools used by LTISD are aligned with the TDH recommendations and are administered by trained personnel. If a student exhibits indicators of dyslexia during the screening process, further evaluation may be recommended to determine the need for intervention services. Parents will be notified promptly if their child is identified as potentially at-risk and will be provided with information about the next steps in the evaluation process.

Structured Literacy Programs

LTISD utilizes structured literacy programs for students identified with dyslexia or at risk of dyslexia. Structured literacy is an evidence-based approach to reading instruction that explicitly and systematically teaches the foundational skills necessary for reading success. These programs emphasize phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension. Key characteristics of structured literacy programs include:

  • Explicit Instruction: Skills are taught directly and clearly.
  • Systematic Instruction: Skills are taught in a logical sequence, building upon previously learned concepts.
  • Cumulative Instruction: New skills are integrated with previously taught skills to ensure retention.
  • Diagnostic Teaching: Instruction is tailored to meet the individual needs of the student based on ongoing assessment.
LTISD employs trained educators who are proficient in delivering structured literacy instruction. The specific programs used by LTISD may vary depending on the campus and the individual needs of the student, but all programs align with the principles of structured literacy and the recommendations of the TDH.

504 vs IEP for Dyslexia

Students with dyslexia may receive support through either a 504 plan or an Individualized Education Program (IEP) depending on the severity of their learning disability and the level of support required. A 504 plan provides accommodations and modifications to the general education environment to ensure that students with disabilities have equal access to learning. Students with dyslexia who have a 504 plan may receive accommodations such as extended time on tests, preferential seating, or assistive technology. An IEP, on the other hand, is a more comprehensive plan for students with disabilities who require specialized instruction and related services. An IEP is developed by a team of professionals, including parents, and outlines specific goals and objectives for the student's education. Students with dyslexia who have an IEP may receive specialized reading intervention, speech-language therapy, or other related services. The determination of whether a student requires a 504 plan or an IEP is made on a case-by-case basis by the school's ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee.

Accommodations List

The following is a non-exhaustive list of accommodations that may be provided to students with dyslexia in LTISD:

  • Extended time on tests and assignments
  • Preferential seating
  • Quiet testing environment
  • Use of assistive technology (e.g., text-to-speech software, audiobooks)
  • Copies of notes
  • Graphic organizers
  • Chunking of assignments
  • Oral administration of tests
  • Modified reading materials
  • Frequent breaks
The specific accommodations provided to each student will be determined based on their individual needs and outlined in their 504 plan or IEP.

How to Request Evaluation

If you suspect that your child may have dyslexia, you can request an evaluation by contacting your child's teacher, school counselor, or campus administrator. The request should be made in writing and should include specific examples of your child's difficulties with reading, writing, or spelling. The school will then initiate a process to determine if a full evaluation is warranted. This process may include classroom observations, review of academic records, and informal assessments. If the school determines that a full evaluation is necessary, they will obtain your written consent and conduct a comprehensive evaluation to assess your child's reading skills and identify any underlying learning disabilities. You have the right to participate in all aspects of the evaluation process and to receive a copy of the evaluation report. LTISD is committed to working collaboratively with parents to ensure that students with dyslexia receive the support they need to succeed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

Is my child entitled to dyslexia testing in Texas? +

Yes. Texas law requires all districts to screen students for dyslexia and related disorders in Kindergarten and First Grade, and at any grade level if a parent or teacher requests it.

What accommodations can a dyslexic child get in Texas? +

Accommodations may include extended time on tests, text-to-speech software, oral administration of tests, reduced-answer-choice formats, and use of a calculator. These can be provided through a 504 Plan or an IEP.

What is the Texas Dyslexia Handbook? +

The Texas Dyslexia Handbook is a state guidance document updated in 2024 that outlines districts' responsibilities for identifying, evaluating, and serving students with dyslexia using structured literacy methods.
