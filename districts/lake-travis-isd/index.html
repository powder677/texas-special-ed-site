About Special Education in Lake Travis ISD

Lake Travis ISD serves the Texas area, providing a range of special education services to students with disabilities. The district is committed to ensuring all students have access to a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment. Lake Travis ISD operates under the oversight of TEA Region unknown, adhering to all state and federal regulations regarding special education.

The ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) process is a crucial part of special education in Lake Travis ISD. After a referral for special education services, the district has approximately 45 school days to complete a Full and Individual Evaluation (FIE). Once the FIE is complete, parents will receive at least a 5-day written notice prior to the ARD meeting. This meeting is where the ARD committee, including parents, teachers, and administrators, will discuss the evaluation results and determine if the student is eligible for special education services. If eligible, the committee will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) tailored to the student's specific needs.

Common Questions from Lake Travis ISD Families

What if I disagree with the ARD committee's decision?

You have the right to disagree with the ARD committee's decisions. You can request another ARD meeting to further discuss your concerns. You can also pursue other options, such as mediation or filing a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

How do I request an evaluation for my child?

If you suspect your child may have a disability that requires special education services, you can request an evaluation in writing from your child's school principal or the special education department. Be sure to include your specific concerns and any supporting documentation you have.

What types of therapies are available in Lake Travis ISD?

Lake Travis ISD offers a variety of therapies as determined necessary by the ARD committee, including speech therapy, occupational therapy, and physical therapy. The availability of specific therapies can vary depending on the student's needs and the district's resources.

Practical Tip for Parents

Lake Travis ISD, while growing, still operates with a relatively streamlined administrative structure. With approximately 10,000 students, it is possible the special education director manages many responsibilities. When communicating with the district, especially regarding important issues, consider using email rather than phone calls. Email provides a written record of your communication, which can be helpful if disagreements or misunderstandings arise later on. Keep copies of all emails related to your child's special education services.
