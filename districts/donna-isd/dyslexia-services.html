Dyslexia Services in Donna ISD

Dyslexia Services in Donna ISD

This guide provides information about dyslexia services offered in Donna Independent School District (ISD), adhering to Texas state law and best practices as outlined in the Texas Dyslexia Handbook.

Texas Dyslexia Handbook 2024

Donna ISD's dyslexia services are aligned with the Texas Dyslexia Handbook (2024). This handbook, published by the Texas Education Agency (TEA), is the primary resource for understanding dyslexia, identification procedures, and appropriate interventions in Texas public schools. Parents and educators are encouraged to familiarize themselves with this document. It outlines the definition of dyslexia, the state's mandated screening procedures, and the components of effective dyslexia intervention programs.

Mandatory Screening

Texas law mandates dyslexia screening for all students in kindergarten and first grade, as well as students in grades 2-12 who show signs of reading difficulties. These screenings are designed to identify students who may be at risk for dyslexia. In Donna ISD, screenings are conducted using TEA-approved screeners, focusing on areas such as:

  • Phonological Awareness: Recognizing and manipulating the sounds of language.
  • Rapid Automatized Naming (RAN): Quickly naming a series of familiar objects or symbols.
  • Letter-Sound Correspondence: Understanding the relationship between letters and sounds.
  • Decoding Skills: Ability to sound out words.

If a student's screening results indicate a potential risk for dyslexia, further evaluation and support will be provided. Parents will be notified of the screening results and any subsequent recommendations.

Structured Literacy Programs

Donna ISD utilizes structured literacy programs for students identified with dyslexia. Structured literacy is an explicit, systematic, cumulative, and diagnostic approach to reading instruction. It focuses on teaching the foundational skills necessary for reading and spelling success. These programs typically include components such as:

  • Phonology: The study of speech sounds.
  • Sound-Symbol Association: Linking sounds to letters.
  • Syllable Instruction: Understanding syllable types and patterns.
  • Morphology: The study of word parts (prefixes, suffixes, roots).
  • Syntax: Understanding sentence structure.
  • Semantics: Understanding the meaning of words and sentences.

Specific program names used in Donna ISD may vary by campus. Contact your child's school or the district's Special Education department for information about the specific programs used.

504 vs. IEP for Dyslexia

Students with dyslexia may receive support through a Section 504 plan or an Individualized Education Program (IEP). The determination of which plan is appropriate depends on the severity of the student's needs.

  • Section 504 Plan: A 504 plan provides accommodations and modifications to the general education environment to ensure a student with a disability has equal access to learning. This is appropriate when a student's dyslexia impacts their ability to access the curriculum but does not require specialized instruction.
  • IEP (Individualized Education Program): An IEP is developed for students who require specialized instruction and related services due to their disability. This is appropriate when a student's dyslexia significantly impacts their academic progress and requires intensive, individualized intervention.

The decision of whether a 504 plan or an IEP is needed is made by a team of professionals, including teachers, administrators, and parents.

Accommodations List

Accommodations are adjustments to the learning environment that help students with dyslexia access the curriculum. Common accommodations provided in Donna ISD include:

  • Extended time on tests and assignments.
  • Preferential seating.
  • Use of assistive technology (e.g., text-to-speech software).
  • Modified assignments.
  • Oral reading of tests.
  • Use of graphic organizers.
  • Provision of notes.
  • Quiet testing environment.

The specific accommodations provided will be determined based on the individual student's needs and outlined in their 504 plan or IEP.

How to Request Evaluation

If you suspect your child may have dyslexia, contact their teacher or the school principal to express your concerns. You can formally request a dyslexia evaluation by submitting a written request to the school principal or the Special Education department. The request should include your child's name, grade, and a description of your concerns. Donna ISD will follow state guidelines for conducting evaluations and determining eligibility for dyslexia services. The district is committed to providing timely and appropriate support to students with dyslexia.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

Is my child entitled to dyslexia testing in Texas? +

Yes. Texas law requires all districts to screen students for dyslexia and related disorders in Kindergarten and First Grade, and at any grade level if a parent or teacher requests it.

What accommodations can a dyslexic child get in Texas? +

Accommodations may include extended time on tests, text-to-speech software, oral administration of tests, reduced-answer-choice formats, and use of a calculator. These can be provided through a 504 Plan or an IEP.

What is the Texas Dyslexia Handbook? +

The Texas Dyslexia Handbook is a state guidance document updated in 2024 that outlines districts' responsibilities for identifying, evaluating, and serving students with dyslexia using structured literacy methods.
