What can I do right now if Donna ISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Donna ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Donna ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Special education, as defined by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas law, is specially designed instruction provided to children with disabilities who need additional support to succeed in school. This might involve adapted teaching methods, specialized curriculum, related services like speech therapy or occupational therapy, and modifications to the classroom environment. The goal is to ensure that every child, regardless of their disability, has access to a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) tailored to their individual needs.

Donna ISD, like all school districts in Texas, operates under the guidance of the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA oversees the implementation of special education laws and provides resources and support to school districts. Additionally, Donna ISD benefits from the support of TEA Region 1 Education Service Center, which offers training, technical assistance, and other services to help the district effectively serve students with disabilities. It is important to remember that as a parent of a child with a disability in Donna ISD, you have enforceable legal rights throughout the entire special education process. These rights are designed to protect your child's interests and ensure that you are an active participant in decisions about their education.

Knowing your rights and understanding the special education process is crucial for advocating effectively for your child within Donna ISD. You are a vital member of the team responsible for creating and implementing your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP). Donna ISD should work collaboratively with you to meet your child's unique needs. Take the time to familiarize yourself with your rights and the resources available to you. Your voice matters.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Donna ISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability that requires special education services in Donna ISD, the first step is to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). This evaluation is designed to determine if your child has a disability and, if so, what types of services they need. It's critical to understand that your request must be made IN WRITING. A verbal request does not start the clock on the evaluation timeline. This written request should be addressed to the school principal, the special education director, or your child's teacher. Be sure to clearly state that you are requesting a special education evaluation for your child.

Once Donna ISD receives your written consent to evaluate, the district has 45 school days to complete the FIE. Child Find laws require Donna ISD to proactively identify students who may need special education services, even if you haven't made a formal request. This means that the district should be actively looking for students who are struggling and may have an undiagnosed disability. However, you don't need to rely solely on Donna ISD's efforts. If you have concerns, you have the right to request an evaluation at any time. Take action today to ensure your child receives the support they deserve. Write a letter requesting a Full Individual Evaluation, keep a copy for your records, and send it to the appropriate person via certified mail with return receipt requested. This provides proof that Donna ISD received your request.

ARD Meetings in Donna ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal. An ARD meeting is a crucial component of the special education process in Donna ISD. It is a meeting where parents, educators, and other relevant professionals come together to discuss your child's educational needs, develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP), and make decisions about their special education services. Remember, you are an equal member of this team, and your input is invaluable.

Donna ISD is required to provide you with at least five calendar days' written notice before any ARD meeting. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, the date, time, and location, and a list of the people who will be attending. During the ARD meeting, you have the right to ask questions, express your concerns, and participate fully in the decision-making process. Furthermore, you have the right to request a 10-day recess at any time during the ARD meeting. This allows you time to review the information presented, consult with experts, or simply take a break to process everything. It's also important to remember that you do not have to sign the IEP on the same day as the ARD meeting. You have the right to take the document home, review it carefully, and sign it later if you are comfortable with the contents.

Understanding your rights at ARD meetings is paramount to advocating for your child. Don't be afraid to speak up, ask questions, and ensure that the IEP truly reflects your child's needs. Take an active role in shaping your child's educational journey within Donna ISD. Before your next ARD meeting in Donna ISD, download our free ARD preparation checklist.

Dyslexia Services in Donna ISD

Texas law mandates that Donna ISD screen every student for dyslexia. This screening is designed to identify students who may be at risk for this learning disability. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook sets the standards that Donna ISD must follow in identifying and supporting students with dyslexia. This handbook provides guidance on screening procedures, instructional methods, and other critical aspects of dyslexia intervention.

If your child is identified as having dyslexia in Donna ISD, they are entitled to receive structured literacy intervention. This type of instruction is specifically designed to address the core deficits associated with dyslexia, such as phonological awareness, decoding, and spelling. Depending on the severity of your child's dyslexia and their individual needs, they may qualify for a 504 Plan or an IEP. A 504 Plan provides accommodations to help students with disabilities access the general education curriculum, while an IEP provides more intensive supports and services. If your child has not been screened for dyslexia and you have concerns, request a screening in writing from Donna ISD immediately.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Donna ISD

Discipline issues can be particularly challenging for students with disabilities. It's crucial to understand that suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative days in Donna ISD triggers certain protections under IDEA. Once this threshold is reached, it constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA, meaning that the school district must hold a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). The purpose of the MDR is to determine whether the student's misconduct was a manifestation of their disability or a result of the school's failure to implement the IEP.

If your child is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days, Donna ISD is required to convene an ARD meeting to conduct the MDR. This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a special education student. You must act within days to protect your child's rights. At the MDR, the ARD committee will review the student's IEP, relevant evaluation data, and any other information related to their behavior. If the committee determines that the misconduct was a manifestation of the disability, the student cannot be suspended and the ARD committee must develop a plan to address the behavior. If your child has been suspended, use the Discipline Defense Kit immediately to understand your rights and take appropriate action.

Filing a Complaint Against Donna ISD

If you believe that Donna ISD is not properly implementing special education laws or is violating your child's rights, you have several avenues for filing a complaint. These escalation paths exist to ensure that your concerns are addressed and that your child receives the services they are entitled to. These rights exist regardless of what any staff member in Donna ISD may tell you.

Your options include: (1) Filing a TEA State Complaint: This is a free process where you submit a written complaint to the Texas Education Agency outlining your concerns. TEA is required to investigate and resolve the complaint within 60 days. (2) Mediation through TEA: This is another free option that involves a neutral third party facilitating a discussion between you and Donna ISD to resolve the dispute. (3) Due Process Hearing: This is a more formal legal process where you present your case to an impartial hearing officer who will make a decision. This can be a more complex and time-consuming process and typically requires legal representation. (4) Filing a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR): This option is available if you believe Donna ISD has discriminated against your child based on their disability. Choose the path that best suits your needs and remember that you have the right to advocate for your child's education. Contact an advocate to learn more about your options and file your complaint as soon as possible.

Frequently Asked Questions About Donna ISD Special Education

The questions every Donna ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Donna ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Donna ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Donna ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Donna ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Donna ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Donna ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Donna ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Donna ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
