Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?

What Is an MDR? (Manifestation Determination Review)

MDR stands for Manifestation Determination Review. This is CRITICAL if your child has an IEP (Individualized Education Program) or a 504 plan. If Donna ISD is considering a suspension longer than 10 school days OR a Change of Placement, they MUST conduct an MDR.

The MDR process determines whether your child's misconduct was caused by or directly related to their disability. Did Donna ISD fail to implement your child's IEP or 504 plan? If so, the behavior is considered a manifestation of the disability, and disciplinary action may need to be modified or eliminated. DEMAND an MDR if your child is facing significant disciplinary action and has an IEP or 504 plan.

Prepare for the MDR meeting! Gather all relevant documentation, including IEP/504 documents, incident reports, and medical records. Bring a knowledgeable advocate or attorney if possible. Be ready to explain how your child's disability impacts their behavior and how Donna ISD may have failed to provide appropriate supports.

Change of Placement

A "Change of Placement" occurs when a student is removed from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days OR when there is a pattern of removals that constitute a change of placement, even if each removal is for 10 days or less. This can include placement in a Disciplinary Alternative Education Program (DAEP) or even expulsion.

Changes of Placement have serious consequences. They can disrupt your child's education, isolate them from peers, and negatively impact their social-emotional well-being. If Donna ISD is considering a Change of Placement, you have the right to challenge it.

If your child has an IEP, IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) provides specific protections regarding Changes of Placement. IMMEDIATELY consult with an attorney specializing in special education law.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe Donna ISD has violated your child's rights, you can file a grievance. Start with a Level 1 grievance, typically submitted to the principal or immediate supervisor.

Document everything! Write a clear and concise grievance letter outlining the specific violation, the dates and times involved, and the relief you are seeking. Include any supporting documentation. Keep a copy of the grievance and any responses you receive. Deadlines are crucial, so familiarize yourself with Donna ISD's grievance policy and adhere to them strictly. If your Level 1 grievance is denied, you may be able to appeal to higher levels within the district.

TEA State Complaint

If you believe Donna ISD has violated federal or state laws related to special education (IDEA), you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA complaints have specific requirements and deadlines. Consult TEA's website for detailed information and complaint forms.

TEA investigations can be lengthy, but a successful complaint can force Donna ISD to correct systemic issues and provide remedies for your child.

Due Process

For students with IEPs, IDEA provides the right to "due process," which allows you to challenge decisions made by Donna ISD regarding your child's education. Due process hearings are formal proceedings with legal representation. This is a complex process; securing legal counsel is ESSENTIAL if you are considering due process.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
