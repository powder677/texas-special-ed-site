A SOMERSET ISD special education evaluation is a thorough assessment designed to examine your child's academic, social, emotional, and behavioral functioning. The evaluation looks at multiple areas of development to determine if your child has a disability that affects their educational performance and requires specialized instruction or services.

Under Texas Education Code Section 26.301, school districts like SOMERSET ISD are required to conduct comprehensive evaluations for students suspected of having disabilities. The SOMERSET ISD 45 day timeline is critical—once you request an evaluation, the district has 45 calendar days to complete all testing and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss results.

Why Your Child Might Need an FIE

Parents in SOMERSET ISD often wonder when an evaluation becomes necessary. Common reasons include:

  • Significant gaps between your child's ability and academic performance
  • Difficulty with reading, writing, or mathematics that doesn't respond to quality instruction
  • Speech and language concerns affecting communication or academics
  • Behavioral challenges that interfere with learning
  • Suspected autism spectrum disorder or developmental delays
  • Attention or executive functioning difficulties
  • Physical or sensory impairments affecting school performance

If you've noticed any of these concerns with your child, requesting an evaluation through SOMERSET ISD is the first step toward getting help.

You don't have to wait for the district to identify your child—you have the right to request a SOMERSET ISD evaluation request at any time. As a parent of a student in SOMERSET ISD, this is one of your most important legal rights.

Step 1: Submit Your Written Request

The most important action you can take is to submit a written request for evaluation. While you can request an evaluation verbally, having written documentation creates an official record and starts the 45-day clock. Contact your child's campus or SOMERSET ISD's Special Education department and request a referral form. You can also write a letter stating that you believe your child has a disability and needs a SOMERSET ISD special education evaluation.

Your request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current school and grade level
  • Specific areas of concern (academic, behavioral, social, developmental)
  • Examples of challenges you've observed at home or school
  • Your contact information and preferred method of communication

Step 2: Understand SOMERSET ISD's Response Timeline

After receiving your written request for a SOMERSET ISD FIE evaluation, the district must respond within a specific timeframe. According to the SOMERSET ISD 45 day timeline, the district has 45 calendar days from the date you request evaluation to complete all assessments and conduct an ARD meeting.

However, the district must provide you with a Notice of Consent for Evaluation before beginning any testing. This document explains:

  • Why an evaluation is being considered
  • What types of assessments will be conducted
  • Your rights as a parent in the evaluation process
  • How results will be used

You must sign and return this notice giving consent before SOMERSET ISD can begin testing.

Step 3: Provide Information About Your Child

SOMERSET ISD evaluation teams gather comprehensive information to understand your child's strengths and needs. You'll likely be asked to complete questionnaires about:

  • Developmental history and milestones
  • Family medical and educational history
  • Behaviors and skills at home
  • Any previous evaluations or testing
  • Current medications or treatments
  • Social and emotional functioning

Providing thorough, honest information helps SOMERSET ISD create an accurate picture of your child's abilities and challenges.

What Happens During a SOMERSET ISD Special Education Evaluation

Once consent is obtained, SOMERSET ISD will conduct a comprehensive SOMERSET ISD special education evaluation. Understanding what to expect helps you prepare and support your child through the process.

Evaluation Components

A complete FIE typically includes assessments in these areas:

  • Cognitive/Intellectual Ability: How your child learns and processes information
  • Academic Achievement: Reading, math, writing, and other academic skills
  • Speech and Language: Communication and language development (if indicated)
  • Social-Emotional Functioning: Behavior, mood, and social skills
  • Motor Skills: Fine and gross motor development (if indicated)
  • Adaptive Behavior: Daily living and independence skills
  • Classroom Observation: How your child functions in the school environment

Different students may need different combinations of assessments depending on areas of concern.

Who Conducts the Evaluation

SOMERSET ISD assembles a multidisciplinary team to conduct your child's evaluation. This team typically includes:

  • Special education teacher or diagnostician
  • School psychologist
  • Speech-language pathologist (if language concerns exist)
  • General education teacher
  • School nurse or healthcare professional (if relevant)
  • Parents/guardians

Your participation as a parent is required, and your observations are considered essential data for the evaluation.

The SOMERSET ISD 45 Day Timeline: Meeting Your Deadlines

The SOMERSET ISD 45 day timeline is a legal requirement that protects your rights. Once SOMERSET ISD receives your written consent to evaluate, the clock starts ticking. Here's what to expect in this timeline:

Days 1-10: Initial Planning and Parent Consent

SOMERSET ISD must notify you of the planned evaluation, provide you with the Notice of Consent, and allow you time to ask questions. Ask the district which specific assessments will be used and why each is necessary.

Days 10-30: Assessment and Data Collection

Your child will participate in various assessments over this period. Some may occur at school during the school day, while others might require outside testing. The district may also collect classroom observation data and academic records.

Days 30-45: Compilation and ARD Meeting

As the 45-day period concludes, SOMERSET ISD compiles all evaluation results and schedules an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. This meeting, held before day 45, determines eligibility and discusses results with you.

What If SOMERSET ISD Misses the Deadline?

If SOMERSET ISD doesn't complete the evaluation within 45 days, you have grounds for a formal complaint. Extensions are possible only with documented parent consent or documented parent unavailability.

The ARD Meeting: Understanding Your Child's Evaluation Results

The ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting is where SOMERSET ISD presents SOMERSET ISD FIE evaluation results and determines if your child qualifies for special education. This meeting is crucial—you'll learn about your child's eligibility status and next steps.

What to Expect at the ARD

During the ARD meeting for your child's SOMERSET ISD special education evaluation:

  • The evaluation team reviews all assessment results in detail
  • Results are explained in parent-friendly language (they should avoid excessive jargon)
  • The team discusses whether your child meets criteria for a disability category
  • If eligible, the team develops an Individualized Education Program (IEP)
  • If not eligible, the team discusses other support options

Disability Categories in SOMERSET ISD

SOMERSET ISD can identify children in these disability categories under IDEA:

  • Specific Learning Disability
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Visual Impairment
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Deaf-Blind
  • Physical Disability
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Multiple Disabilities

Your child must meet both the evaluation criteria for a category AND show that the disability adversely affects their educational performance to qualify for special education.

Your Rights as a SOMERSET ISD Parent During Evaluation

SOMERSET ISD parents have significant legal protections during the evaluation process. Knowing these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Key Parent Rights

  • Right to Request Evaluation: You can request a SOMERSET ISD evaluation at any time, and the district must respond within legal timeframes
  • Right to Informed Consent: The district must explain evaluations and obtain your written permission before testing begins
  • Right to Interpreter/Translation: If English isn't your home language, SOMERSET ISD must provide translated notices and interpretation at meetings
  • Right to Review Records: You can request and review all evaluation materials, test results, and educational records
  • Right to Independent Evaluation: If you disagree with SOMERSET ISD's evaluation, you can request an independent evaluation at district expense
  • Right to Bring Support: You can bring a trusted friend, family member, or advocate to any SOMERSET ISD meeting
  • Right to Dispute Findings: You can request mediation or due process if you disagree with evaluation results or eligibility decisions

Taking the Next Steps: After the FIE Evaluation

Once your child completes a SOMERSET ISD FIE evaluation, what happens depends on the findings.

If Your Child Qualifies for Special Education

If SOMERSET ISD determines your child has a disability and needs special education services, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) is developed. The IEP outlines:

  • Your child's present levels of performance
  • Annual goals in areas affected by the disability
  • Special education and related services
  • Supports and accommodations
  • Placement in the least restrictive environment
  • Progress monitoring methods

SOMERSET ISD reviews and updates IEPs annually, with parents participating fully in all decisions.

If Your Child Doesn't Qualify

If SOMERSET ISD determines your child doesn't meet eligibility criteria, ask about other support options, including:

  • Section 504 Plans (for students who have disabilities affecting major life activities but don't qualify for special education)
  • Tiered support systems and interventions
  • English Language Learner (ELL) services
  • Gifted and talented programs
  • Counseling or behavioral support services

You can also request an independent evaluation or seek another opinion if you disagree with SOMERSET ISD's findings.
