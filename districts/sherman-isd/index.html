Special Education in Sherman ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the world of special education can feel overwhelming, but you are not alone. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), both federal and Texas state law guarantee specific rights and protections for students with disabilities. Special education is designed to provide individualized instruction and support to help your child succeed in school. This can include a range of services, from specialized academic instruction to speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, and more. Remember, the goal is to ensure your child receives the Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) they are entitled to.

Sherman ISD, like all school districts in Texas, operates under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Sherman ISD also receives valuable support and resources from TEA Region 10 Education Service Center, which provides professional development and technical assistance to educators in the region. This collaboration aims to strengthen special education services and outcomes for students. Understanding this framework is crucial as you advocate for your child's needs within Sherman ISD.

Crucially, as a parent, you have enforceable legal rights throughout the entire special education process in Sherman ISD. These rights include the right to participate in all decision-making, the right to access your child's educational records, the right to request evaluations, and the right to challenge decisions you disagree with. Don't let anyone tell you otherwise. Knowledge is power. Right now, familiarize yourself with the Procedural Safeguards provided by Sherman ISD; they outline your rights in detail.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Sherman ISD

If you suspect your child has a disability that is impacting their ability to succeed in school, the first step is to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) from Sherman ISD. This evaluation is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine if your child has a disability and, if so, what specific educational needs they have. It's extremely important to understand that your request must be submitted in writing to the school principal or Special Education Director. A verbal request does NOT start the clock ticking.

Once Sherman ISD receives your written consent to evaluate, they have 45 school days to complete the FIE. This timeline is legally binding, and Sherman ISD must adhere to it. It is also important to remember the principle of Child Find. This legal mandate requires Sherman ISD to proactively identify, locate, and evaluate children with disabilities who may be in need of special education services, even if those children are not yet enrolled in school. You are not solely responsible for this process; Sherman ISD has an obligation to actively seek out students who may need help. Right now, draft a letter requesting a Full Individual Evaluation for your child, clearly stating your concerns and observations. Keep a copy for your records and send the original to the principal via certified mail with return receipt requested so you have proof it was received.

ARD Meetings in Sherman ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal. An ARD committee meeting is where important decisions are made about your child's special education program. This committee, which includes you, teachers, administrators, and other relevant professionals, develops and reviews your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP). The IEP is a legally binding document that outlines your child's specific educational goals, services, and accommodations.

Sherman ISD is required to provide you with at least five calendar days' written notice before any ARD committee meeting. This notice must include the purpose of the meeting, the date, time, location, and a list of who will be attending. You have the right to request a recess of up to 10 calendar days at any point during the ARD meeting if you feel you need more time to consider information or seek outside advice. You also do not have to sign the IEP at the conclusion of the ARD meeting. You have the right to take the document home, review it carefully, and seek additional input before making a decision.

Remember, you are an equal member of the ARD committee, and your input is invaluable. Do not be intimidated by the presence of school staff. You know your child best. It is critical to advocate for their needs and ensure the IEP truly reflects their individual requirements. Before your next ARD meeting in Sherman ISD, download our free ARD preparation checklist, available on the texasspecialed.com website, to help you organize your thoughts, prepare your questions, and feel confident at the table.

Dyslexia Services in Sherman ISD

Texas law mandates that Sherman ISD screen all students for dyslexia. This screening is intended to identify students who may be at risk for reading difficulties and to provide early intervention. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook sets the standards for how Sherman ISD must conduct these screenings and provide services to students identified with dyslexia. This handbook is a critical resource for parents and educators alike, outlining best practices for dyslexia intervention.

Students identified with dyslexia in Sherman ISD are entitled to structured literacy intervention. This approach focuses on explicit and systematic instruction in phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and reading comprehension. Depending on the severity of the dyslexia and the student's individual needs, they may also qualify for a 504 Plan or an IEP, providing additional accommodations and supports. If your child has not been screened for dyslexia, even if they are excelling in other subjects, request a screening in writing from Sherman ISD. Early identification and intervention are key to ensuring your child's reading success.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Sherman ISD

Disciplinary actions, particularly suspensions, can have a significant impact on students with disabilities. If your child with an IEP or 504 plan is suspended for more than 10 cumulative school days in a school year by Sherman ISD, this triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). The MDR is a process to determine if the behavior leading to the suspension was a manifestation of your child's disability. This also constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA and requires an ARD committee meeting to review the relationship between the behavior and the disability.

This situation is one of the most time-sensitive you will face as a parent of a special education student. If your child has been suspended and is approaching or has exceeded 10 days, you must act quickly. Sherman ISD is required to hold an ARD meeting to conduct the MDR within a specific timeframe. It is crucial to attend this meeting prepared to advocate for your child and ensure their rights are protected. Do not delay. If your child has been suspended by Sherman ISD, immediately download and use our Discipline Defense Kit, available on the texasspecialed.com website. It provides essential information and strategies for navigating the disciplinary process and protecting your child's rights.

Filing a Complaint Against Sherman ISD

If you feel that Sherman ISD is not meeting its obligations under IDEA or other special education laws, you have several avenues for filing a complaint. These escalation paths are available to you regardless of what Sherman ISD staff may tell you. The first option is to file a State Complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This is a free process, and TEA is required to investigate and resolve the complaint within 60 days. Another option is Mediation through TEA, also free. This provides a neutral third party to help you and Sherman ISD reach a mutually agreeable resolution.

For more serious disputes, you can pursue a Due Process Hearing, a more formal legal proceeding where evidence is presented, and a hearing officer makes a decision. This process can be more complex and may require legal representation. Finally, you can also file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR) if you believe Sherman ISD has discriminated against your child based on their disability. Remember, you have the right to advocate for your child's needs and hold Sherman ISD accountable for providing a Free Appropriate Public Education. Right now, if you are considering filing a complaint, document all your interactions and concerns in writing, including dates, times, and names of individuals involved. This documentation will be crucial as you pursue your chosen course of action.

Frequently Asked Questions About Sherman ISD Special Education

The questions every Sherman ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Sherman ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Sherman ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Sherman ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Sherman ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Sherman ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Sherman ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Sherman ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Sherman ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
