ANGLETON ISD has a legal obligation under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities within its jurisdiction. This responsibility is called "child find," and it applies to all students in ANGLETON ISD regardless of the severity of their disability.

As a parent in ANGLETON ISD, you don't need to wait for your child's teacher to suggest an evaluation. You have the independent right to request a Full Individual Evaluation at any time. ANGLETON ISD cannot deny your request without conducting the evaluation or providing you with written explanation and procedural safeguards.

Understanding this foundational right empowers you to take action. Many parents in the ANGLETON area don't realize they can initiate the evaluation process themselves, which can accelerate getting their child the support they need.

What is a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in ANGLETON ISD?

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment used to determine whether your child has a disability under IDEA and whether they qualify for special education services. For ANGLETON ISD students, an FIE typically includes multiple types of assessments conducted by qualified professionals.

Components of an ANGLETON ISD FIE

  • Cognitive/Intellectual Assessment: Tests your child's thinking, reasoning, and learning abilities
  • Academic Achievement Testing: Measures your child's performance in reading, math, writing, and other academic areas
  • Behavioral/Social-Emotional Assessment: Evaluates your child's behavior, social skills, and emotional functioning
  • Speech-Language Evaluation: Assesses communication abilities if concerns exist
  • Physical/Motor Assessment: Examines coordination and motor skills if relevant
  • Medical/Developmental History Review: Examines your child's background and health information
  • Teacher Input and Classroom Observations: Gathers information from educators working with your child

The specific assessments included in your child's ANGLETON ISD evaluation request will depend on their individual needs and the concerns you've identified.

Step-by-Step Process for Requesting an Evaluation in ANGLETON ISD

Step 1: Document Your Concerns

Before making a formal request, document the specific concerns you have about your child's development or academic performance. Keep records of:

  • Specific behaviors or academic struggles you've observed
  • When these concerns started
  • How these issues affect your child's learning and daily functioning
  • Any interventions you've already tried at home
  • Communication with teachers about these concerns

This documentation strengthens your case when you submit your ANGLETON ISD evaluation request and helps the evaluation team understand your child's history.

Step 2: Submit Your Written Request

Contact your child's school campus or the ANGLETON ISD Special Education Department and request a Full Individual Evaluation in writing. You can deliver this request by:

  • Hand-delivering it to the campus office or special education department
  • Mailing it certified with return receipt
  • Email (request confirmation of receipt)
  • Completing the district's formal evaluation request form

Important: Use the phrase "Request for Full Individual Evaluation" or "Child Find Referral" clearly in your request to ensure ANGLETON ISD processes it correctly.

Step 3: The 15-Day Response Window

Once ANGLETON ISD receives your written request, the district must respond within 15 calendar days. During this time, ANGLETON ISD will either:

  • Agree to conduct the evaluation and provide you with a Permission to Evaluate form
  • Decline the evaluation and provide a written explanation with your procedural safeguards (Prior Written Notice)

If ANGLETON ISD declines your evaluation request, they must provide specific reasons why they believe an evaluation is not necessary. You can challenge this decision through due process procedures.

Step 4: Provide Written Permission

If ANGLETON ISD agrees to proceed with the special education evaluation, you will receive a "Permission to Evaluate" form. You must sign this form authorizing ANGLETON ISD to conduct the evaluation. This is a critical step—the evaluation cannot legally begin until you provide written consent.

Review the permission form carefully. It should outline the types of assessments ANGLETON ISD plans to use and your rights throughout the process.

Step 5: The 45-Day Timeline for ANGLETON ISD

This is crucial: ANGLETON ISD has 45 calendar days from the date you sign the Permission to Evaluate form to complete the entire ANGLETON ISD 45 day timeline for testing, gathering information, and scheduling an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss results.

The 45-day clock includes:

  • All testing and assessments
  • Gathering information from parents and teachers
  • Compiling and reviewing all evaluation data
  • Scheduling and holding the ARD meeting

If ANGLETON ISD cannot meet this deadline, they must request your written agreement to extend the timeline. You are not required to grant an extension.

Step 6: Participate in the ARD Meeting

At the end of the 45-day ANGLETON ISD evaluation timeline, the district will schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. This meeting brings together:

  • You (the parent)
  • Your child's teacher(s)
  • A school administrator
  • Someone who can explain the evaluation results
  • Other relevant ANGLETON ISD staff

At this meeting, ANGLETON ISD will present all evaluation findings and determine whether your child qualifies for special education services under one of thirteen disability categories recognized under IDEA.

Understanding ANGLETON ISD Disability Categories

If the evaluation results indicate your child has a disability, ANGLETON ISD must identify which category applies. The thirteen IDEA disability categories include:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Deaf-Blindness
  • Deafness
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Specific Learning Disability
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Visual Impairment (including blindness)

A child must meet criteria in both the disability category and show that the disability adversely affects educational performance to qualify for services in ANGLETON ISD.

Your Rights During the ANGLETON ISD Evaluation Process

Prior to the Evaluation

  • Receive a written explanation of your procedural safeguards
  • Provide information about your child's background and development
  • Ask questions about the evaluation process
  • Request specific evaluators or assessment types

During the Evaluation

  • Request progress updates on the ANGLETON ISD evaluation request timeline
  • Participate in classroom observations and teacher interviews
  • Provide input about your child's strengths and challenges
  • Review preliminary findings if you request them

At the ARD Meeting

  • Bring an advocate, attorney, or supportive person
  • Receive copies of all evaluation reports before or at the meeting
  • Ask questions and request clarification
  • Disagree with findings and request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE)
  • Request a due process hearing if you disagree with results

What If You Disagree With ANGLETON ISD's Evaluation Results?

You have the right to challenge any aspect of your child's ANGLETON ISD special education evaluation. If you disagree with the results, you have several options:

Request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE)

You can request an IEE conducted by an evaluator not employed by ANGLETON ISD. ANGLETON ISD must either fund this evaluation or file for due process to defend their evaluation. An IEE provides an independent perspective and can be powerful if you believe ANGLETON ISD's evaluation was incomplete or inaccurate.

Request a Due Process Hearing

If ANGLETON ISD refuses to provide special education services based on the evaluation, you can request a due process hearing through the Texas Education Agency. This legal proceeding allows you to present evidence and arguments before an impartial hearing officer.

Consult With a Special Education Attorney

Parents in ANGLETON ISD dealing with complex cases often benefit from professional guidance. A special education attorney near ANGLETON can review your child's evaluation, advise you on your options, and represent you in hearings or negotiations with ANGLETON ISD.

Working With Advocates in ANGLETON ISD

A special ed advocate in ANGLETON can be invaluable throughout the evaluation process. Advocates help by:

  • Explaining evaluation results in plain language
  • Ensuring ANGLETON ISD follows proper procedures
  • Identifying gaps in the evaluation
  • Advocating at ARD meetings
  • Helping you understand your child's rights
  • Connecting you with resources and support

Many advocates work on a volunteer basis or for minimal cost, making professional support accessible to families throughout ANGLETON ISD and the surrounding area.

Key Timelines and Deadlines for ANGLETON ISD Parents

  • 15 days: ANGLETON ISD must respond to your evaluation request
  • 45 days: ANGLETON ISD must complete the full evaluation and hold an ARD meeting from the date you sign the Permission to Evaluate
  • 30 days: You have 30 days to request an IEE if you disagree with ANGLETON ISD's evaluation
  • 2 years: Results from ANGLETON ISD evaluations are typically valid for two years

Preparing for Success: Tips for ANGLETON ISD Parents

To make the evaluation process as smooth and effective as possible:

  • Be specific: Clearly describe your concerns rather than vague statements
  • Document everything: Keep records of all communication with ANGLETON ISD
  • Provide context: Share information about your child's developmental history, medical background, and family circumstances
  • Bring support: Have someone you trust at the ARD meeting to help you stay focused
  • Ask for copies: Request copies of all reports and assessment results
  • Take notes: Document what was discussed and agreed upon at meetings
  • Follow up in writing: Send emails confirming agreements made verbally with ANGLETON ISD staff

Next Steps After Your ANGLETON ISD Evaluation

If your child qualifies for special education services, ANGLETON ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). If your child does not qualify but has health or behavioral concerns, ANGLETON ISD may recommend a 504 Plan.

If you believe ANGLETON ISD made errors in the evaluation process or disagree with the results, don't hesitate to exercise your rights. Contact a special ed advocate in ANGLETON or a special education attorney near ANGLETON for guidance on protecting your child's educational rights.

Remember: You are your child's most important advocate. By understanding the ANGLETON ISD FIE evaluation process and staying actively involved, you give your child the best chance of receiving the support they need to succeed in ANGLETON ISD schools.
