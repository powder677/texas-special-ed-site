How to Effectively Contact College Station ISD Special Education Staff

Communicating effectively with College Station ISD's special education staff is crucial for your child's success. Knowing the best practices for contact can save you time and frustration.

The best times to contact staff are generally before or after school hours, or during their designated planning periods. Email is often the most efficient way to initiate contact, as it allows staff to manage their time effectively and provide thoughtful responses. When emailing, be clear and concise in your subject line (e.g., "IEP Question - [Child's Name]") and body. Include your child's name, grade, and specific concerns.

When calling, be prepared to leave a detailed message with your name, number, your child's name and school, and a brief summary of your concern. Remember to always follow up any phone conversation with an email summarizing the discussion and any agreed-upon actions. This creates a written record and ensures everyone is on the same page.

What to Do If You Can't Reach Anyone

If you're having difficulty reaching someone, it's important to escalate your concerns systematically. Begin by contacting your child's teacher. If you don't receive a response within a reasonable timeframe (e.g., 2-3 business days), reach out to the campus special education coordinator. If you still need assistance, contact the College Station ISD Director of Special Education.

If you've exhausted all local resources and believe College Station ISD is not meeting your child's needs as outlined in their IEP, you have the right to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Review Texas Education Code Chapter 29 for detailed information regarding special education services and your rights. Document all attempts to resolve the issue locally before filing a complaint.

Your Right to District Records

As a parent, you have the right to access your child's educational records. Under the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA), College Station ISD must provide you with access to your child's records within 45 days of your written request. This includes IEPs, evaluations, and other relevant documents.

You can also request staff contact information through the Texas Public Information Act (Texas Government Code, Chapter 552). This act allows you to request public information from governmental entities, including College Station ISD. While personal information may be redacted, you can often obtain email addresses and phone numbers of district staff.

To submit a records request, send a written request (email or letter) to the College Station ISD Special Education Department. Clearly state the specific records you are requesting and include your child's name, date of birth, and school. Keep a copy of your request for your records.
