Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

The 10-Day Suspension Rule

CSISD can suspend your child for up to 3 days at a time, but the cumulative total of suspensions cannot exceed 10 days in a school year without triggering significant due process rights, especially if your child receives special education services under IDEA or has a 504 plan. Track all suspensions carefully! If your child is approaching or has exceeded 10 days of suspension, it's time to take action. Reaching this threshold can trigger a 'Change of Placement' issue (explained below). Contact the school immediately requesting a meeting to discuss your child's behavior and potential support strategies.

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

If your child has an IEP and faces a suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days, a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) *must* be conducted. This meeting determines if the misconduct was caused by your child's disability OR by the school district's failure to implement their IEP. You, the parent, *must* be invited and participate in this meeting. Don't let the school exclude you. Prior to the meeting, request all relevant documentation, including incident reports, witness statements, and your child's complete IEP. If the MDR team determines the behavior was a manifestation of the disability or a result of IEP implementation failure, your child *cannot* be suspended beyond 10 days. The IEP team must then develop or revise the IEP to address the behavior. Fight for appropriate support!

Change of Placement

A "Change of Placement" occurs when disciplinary action significantly alters your child's educational setting. This can be triggered by multiple short-term suspensions, long-term suspension, expulsion, or placement in a Disciplinary Alternative Education Program (DAEP). Any Change of Placement for a student with an IEP raises significant legal concerns and triggers specific procedural safeguards under IDEA. CSISD *must* follow proper procedures, including conducting an MDR if the change is due to disciplinary reasons exceeding 10 days. If you believe a Change of Placement is occurring illegally, document everything and seek legal advice immediately. Even a placement in DAEP (Disciplinary Alternative Education Program) may be a change of placement.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe CSISD has violated your child's rights, you can file a grievance. Start with a Level 1 Grievance, typically submitted to the principal or relevant administrator. Obtain the district's grievance policy from the CSISD website (search for "CSISD grievance policy"). Your grievance should be clear, concise, and factual. Include dates, names, and specific details of the alleged violation. Attach supporting documentation (emails, incident reports, etc.). Keep a copy of your grievance and proof of submission. The district has a limited time to respond (check the policy for specific deadlines). If you are not satisfied with the Level 1 response, you can escalate the grievance to the next level (Level 2, etc.) as outlined in the policy.

TEA State Complaint

If you believe CSISD has violated state or federal special education laws, you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This is a formal complaint that can trigger a TEA investigation. The TEA complaint process has specific requirements and deadlines. Research the process on the TEA website (search for "TEA Special Education Complaint"). You will need to provide detailed information about the alleged violation and supporting documentation. Filing a TEA complaint can be a powerful tool, but it's crucial to follow the TEA's procedures precisely.

Due Process

Under IDEA, students with disabilities (and their parents) have significant due process rights, including the right to a hearing before an impartial hearing officer if you disagree with the school district's actions regarding your child's education. This is a formal legal proceeding. You can challenge the MDR determination, a Change of Placement, or any other aspect of your child's special education program. Filing for due process starts with a formal request to the TEA. This is a complex process, and you should strongly consider seeking legal representation from an attorney specializing in special education law. Don't delay - strict deadlines apply!

This guide is for informational purposes only and does not constitute legal advice. Time is critical. If you believe your child's rights are being violated, seek immediate legal counsel. Document everything!

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
