What can I do right now if Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the world of special education can feel overwhelming, but you're not alone. Special education, as defined by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas law, ensures that children with disabilities receive a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) tailored to their unique needs. This means your child is entitled to specially designed instruction and related services to help them succeed in school. These supports can range from specialized tutoring and therapies to assistive technology and modified curriculum.

Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD, like all school districts in Texas, operates under the guidance of the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA provides oversight and ensures districts adhere to state and federal special education laws. Additionally, Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD receives support from TEA Region 1 Education Service Center, which offers training and resources to educators and families. Knowing these support systems exist is crucial as you advocate for your child's needs within Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD.

Crucially, remember that as a parent, you have enforceable legal rights throughout the entire special education process. From initial evaluation to the development and implementation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP), your voice matters. Understanding your rights and knowing how to assert them is essential to ensuring your child receives the appropriate services and support they deserve within the Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD system. Take the time to familiarize yourself with your rights under IDEA and Texas law; it's the first step in becoming an effective advocate for your child.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD

The first step in accessing special education services is requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). It's vitally important to understand that this request must be in writing. A verbal request to a teacher or administrator, while helpful for initial conversations, does not officially start the evaluation timeline. A formal, written request ensures your child's case is properly documented and triggers the legal obligation for Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD to act.

Once Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD receives your written consent to evaluate, they have 45 school days to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). This evaluation will assess your child's strengths and weaknesses across various areas, such as academic achievement, cognitive abilities, and social-emotional development. Keep in mind that Child Find laws mandate Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD to proactively identify students who may have disabilities and require special education services, even if you haven't explicitly requested an evaluation. But don't rely solely on the district; be your child's strongest advocate. Write a letter requesting the evaluation today, even if you've already spoken to someone at the school. Keep a copy for your records and send it via certified mail with return receipt requested, so you have proof of delivery.

ARD Meetings in Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal. The ARD committee is a group of individuals, including you, the parent, and school personnel, who collaborate to make decisions about your child's special education program. It's where the IEP is developed, reviewed, and revised. It's critical to understand that Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD is legally required to provide you with at least five calendar days of written notice before any ARD meeting. This allows you adequate time to prepare, gather information, and consider your child's needs. Carefully review the notice and mark the date on your calendar.

As a parent, you have significant rights during the ARD meeting. One of the most important is the right to request a 10-day recess. This means you can ask to pause the meeting for up to 10 calendar days to consider the information presented, seek outside consultation, or simply process the decisions being made. This is your right and you should not hesitate to use it. Another crucial point: you are not obligated to sign the IEP the same day as the ARD meeting. Take your time to review the document thoroughly and ensure it accurately reflects your child's needs and goals. Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD cannot pressure you to sign immediately.

Remember, you are an equal member of the ARD committee, and your input is invaluable. Don't be afraid to ask questions, express your concerns, and advocate for what you believe is best for your child. Preparation is key to a successful ARD meeting. Dyslexia Services in Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD

Texas law mandates that Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD screen all students for dyslexia. This is a crucial step in identifying children who may struggle with reading and spelling due to this specific learning disability. These screenings are typically conducted in the early grades to allow for timely intervention. If your child is experiencing difficulties with reading, even if they haven't been formally screened, it's important to investigate further.

The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook sets the standards for dyslexia identification and intervention that Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD must follow. If your child is identified as having dyslexia, they are entitled to structured literacy intervention, which is a research-based approach that explicitly teaches phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension. Depending on the severity of their dyslexia and their individual needs, your child may also qualify for a 504 Plan or an IEP to provide additional accommodations and support. If your child has not been screened for dyslexia, immediately request a screening in writing from Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD's special education department.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD

Disciplinary actions, especially suspensions, can have a significant impact on students with disabilities. It's crucial to understand your child's rights when it comes to discipline within Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD. If a student with an IEP or 504 plan is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year, this triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). The MDR is an ARD meeting to determine if the behavior leading to the suspension was a manifestation of the child's disability or a result of the school's failure to properly implement the IEP or 504 plan. This constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA and requires an immediate ARD meeting.

This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a child with disabilities. You must act quickly to protect your child's rights. The school district is obligated to conduct the MDR within a specific timeframe. Failure to do so can result in a violation of your child's rights under IDEA. Time is of the essence. If your child has been suspended, use our Discipline Defense Kit immediately to understand your rights and ensure your child receives the protections they are entitled to within Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD.

Filing a Complaint Against Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD

If you believe Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD is not fulfilling its obligations under IDEA or other special education laws, you have several avenues for filing a complaint. Understanding these escalation paths is crucial when your child's rights are being violated. You have the right to pursue these options regardless of what any staff member at Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD tells you.

The first option is to file a TEA State Complaint. This is a free process, and TEA is required to resolve the complaint within 60 days. Another free option is mediation through TEA, where a neutral third party helps you and the district reach a mutually agreeable resolution. If mediation is unsuccessful or you prefer a more formal process, you can request a Due Process Hearing, which is similar to a court case. Finally, you can file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights if you believe Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD has discriminated against your child based on their disability. Choose the option that best suits your situation and remember that you have the right to advocate for your child's educational needs. Research your complaint options and prepare to file your initial complaint today if you believe Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD is not meeting your child's needs.

Frequently Asked Questions About Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD Special Education

The questions every Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Pharr-San Juan-Alamo ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
