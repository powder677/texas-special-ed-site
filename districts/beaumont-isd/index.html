What can I do right now if Beaumont ISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Beaumont ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Beaumont ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the world of special education can feel overwhelming, but you are not alone. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas state law, special education is designed to provide tailored instruction and support to children with disabilities so they can thrive academically and socially. This includes a range of services, from specialized instruction in reading or math to speech therapy, occupational therapy, and counseling. The goal is to ensure your child has access to a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) that meets their unique needs within Beaumont ISD.

Beaumont ISD, like all school districts in Texas, operates under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA provides guidance, resources, and monitors compliance with state and federal special education laws. Beaumont ISD also receives valuable support from TEA Region 5 Education Service Center, which offers professional development, technical assistance, and resources for educators and families. This collaboration aims to ensure that Beaumont ISD provides high-quality special education services to all eligible students.

Remember, as a parent, you are a vital member of your child's educational team, and you have enforceable legal rights throughout the entire special education process within Beaumont ISD. This includes the right to participate in all meetings, access your child's records, disagree with evaluations and decisions, and seek resolution if you believe your child is not receiving the appropriate services. Knowing your rights is the first step to advocating effectively for your child's needs. Understanding these rights will empower you to work collaboratively with Beaumont ISD to create the best possible educational plan for your child. Learn more about your specific rights in the TEA publication, "Parent's Guide to the Admission, Review, and Dismissal Process."

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Beaumont ISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability that impacts their ability to learn, the first crucial step is to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) from Beaumont ISD. This evaluation is a comprehensive assessment of your child's strengths and needs across various areas, such as academic skills, cognitive abilities, communication skills, and social-emotional development. It's important to know that to formally start the special education process, you MUST submit your request in WRITING. A verbal request to a teacher or principal, while a good starting point for conversation, does NOT trigger the legal timelines for evaluation.

Once Beaumont ISD receives your written consent to evaluate, the district has 45 school days to complete the FIE. It is also important to understand that "Child Find" laws require Beaumont ISD to proactively identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities who reside within the district, regardless of whether they are enrolled in public school. This means the district has a responsibility to seek out students who may need special education services, even if you haven't specifically requested an evaluation. If you believe your child needs an evaluation, take action now. Write a letter to the Special Education Director at Beaumont ISD requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for your child. Be sure to date the letter, clearly state your concerns, and include your child's name, date of birth, school, and grade. Keep a copy of the letter for your records and send it via certified mail with return receipt requested to ensure proof of delivery.

ARD Meetings in Beaumont ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal. An ARD meeting is a legally mandated gathering of professionals and parents who come together to make decisions about your child's special education program. This team reviews evaluation data, determines eligibility for special education services, develops the Individualized Education Program (IEP), and makes placement decisions. Your active participation in these meetings is crucial to ensuring your child's needs are met within Beaumont ISD.

Beaumont ISD is required to provide you with written notice at least five calendar days before any ARD meeting. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, who will be attending, and your rights as a parent. You have the right to request a ten-day recess, or break, at any time during the ARD meeting. This gives you time to process information, consult with outside experts, or simply take a break from a potentially stressful situation. It's also important to remember that you are not obligated to sign the IEP on the same day as the ARD meeting. Take the time you need to review the document carefully and ensure you fully understand and agree with its contents. Your signature indicates your participation in the process, not necessarily your complete agreement.

You are an equal partner in the ARD process within Beaumont ISD, and your voice matters. The ARD committee must consider your input and perspectives when making decisions about your child's education. If you disagree with any aspect of the IEP, you have the right to document your concerns in writing and pursue further action, such as requesting mediation or filing a complaint. Dyslexia Services in Beaumont ISD

Texas law mandates that Beaumont ISD screen every student for dyslexia. This screening is typically conducted in early elementary grades to identify students who may be at risk for reading difficulties. If your child's screening indicates potential indicators of dyslexia, Beaumont ISD is required to provide further evaluation and, if diagnosed, appropriate interventions. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook outlines the specific procedures and guidelines that Beaumont ISD must follow regarding dyslexia identification and intervention.

Students identified with dyslexia in Beaumont ISD are entitled to structured literacy intervention, which is a research-based approach that focuses on explicit and systematic instruction in phonological awareness, phonics, reading fluency, vocabulary, and reading comprehension. Depending on the severity of your child's dyslexia and their individual needs, they may qualify for a 504 Plan, which provides accommodations and support within the general education classroom, or they may be eligible for special education services under an IEP. If your child has not been screened for dyslexia, and you have concerns about their reading skills, request a dyslexia screening in writing from Beaumont ISD immediately. Be sure to keep a copy of your request and send it via certified mail.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Beaumont ISD

Discipline and suspensions can be particularly challenging for students with disabilities. Under IDEA, certain protections are in place to ensure that disciplinary actions are fair and appropriate. Specifically, if a student with a disability is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year within Beaumont ISD, this triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). The MDR is an ARD meeting to determine if the student's behavior was a manifestation of their disability or a result of the school's failure to implement the IEP. This also constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA and requires an ARD meeting.

This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you may face as a parent of a special education student in Beaumont ISD. You must act within days to protect your child's rights. If your child has been suspended, immediately request an ARD meeting to conduct a Manifestation Determination Review. Document all communication with the school regarding the suspension and the ARD meeting request. Do not delay. The school district has very specific timelines. Use the Discipline Defense Kit to understand your rights and prepare for the MDR. The timeline for Manifestation Determination Reviews are very short, so do not delay in understanding and defending your child's rights to a free and appropriate public education.

Filing a Complaint Against Beaumont ISD

If you believe that Beaumont ISD is not complying with special education laws or that your child's rights are being violated, you have several avenues for filing a complaint. The first option is to file a TEA State Complaint. This is a free process, and TEA is required to investigate and resolve the complaint within 60 days. The second option is to request mediation through TEA. Mediation is a voluntary process where you and Beaumont ISD representatives meet with a neutral mediator to try and resolve the dispute. This is also a free service offered by TEA.

The third, more formal, option is to request a Due Process Hearing. This is a more adversarial process where a hearing officer hears evidence and makes a decision about the dispute. Due process hearings can be complex and may require legal representation. Finally, you can file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR). OCR investigates complaints of discrimination based on disability. These rights exist regardless of what Beaumont ISD staff tell you. If you are being denied rights or services, it is important to know and exercise your escalation path.

Frequently Asked Questions About Beaumont ISD Special Education

The questions every Beaumont ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Beaumont ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Beaumont ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Beaumont ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Beaumont ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Beaumont ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Beaumont ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Beaumont ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Beaumont ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
