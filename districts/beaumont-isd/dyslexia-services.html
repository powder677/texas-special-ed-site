Dyslexia Services in Beaumont ISD

Dyslexia Services in Beaumont ISD

This guide provides an overview of dyslexia services offered by Beaumont Independent School District (BISD) in accordance with Texas state law and guidelines. It is intended for parents, educators, and students seeking information about identification, intervention, and support for dyslexia.

Texas Dyslexia Handbook 2024

BISD follows the guidelines outlined in the most current version of the Texas Dyslexia Handbook, which is currently the 2024 edition (or its most recent updated version). This handbook, published by the Texas Education Agency (TEA), provides comprehensive information about dyslexia, including its definition, characteristics, identification procedures, and research-based interventions. The handbook can be accessed on the TEA website. BISD staff are trained and utilize these guidelines for screening, evaluation, and instruction of students with dyslexia. Any updates or amendments to the handbook are immediately adopted by BISD to ensure best practices are implemented.

Mandatory Screening

BISD implements mandatory dyslexia screening for all students in kindergarten and first grade. These screenings are typically conducted in the fall and spring. The purpose of the screening is to identify students who may be at risk for dyslexia. The screening measures used are research-based and aligned with the criteria outlined in the Texas Dyslexia Handbook. If a student demonstrates characteristics associated with dyslexia during the initial screening, further evaluation will be conducted to determine if a formal diagnosis is warranted. Parents will be notified if their child is identified as potentially at-risk for dyslexia and will be informed of the next steps in the process.

Structured Literacy Programs

BISD provides Structured Literacy intervention programs for students identified with dyslexia. These programs are research-based, explicit, systematic, and multi-sensory. They address the core components of reading: phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and reading comprehension. Intervention is provided by trained educators who have specialized knowledge in dyslexia and Structured Literacy methodology. The specific program(s) utilized by BISD will vary depending on the student's individual needs, but all programs adhere to the principles of Structured Literacy and are aligned with the Texas Dyslexia Handbook.

504 vs IEP for Dyslexia

Students with dyslexia may receive support through a Section 504 plan or an Individualized Education Program (IEP), depending on their individual needs. A 504 plan provides accommodations to ensure students with disabilities have equal access to education. This might include accommodations such as extended time on tests, preferential seating, or the use of assistive technology. An IEP is a more comprehensive plan that is developed for students with disabilities who require specialized instruction to make progress in the general education curriculum. To qualify for an IEP under the category of Specific Learning Disability (which can include dyslexia), the student's disability must significantly impact their educational performance and require specially designed instruction. The determination of whether a student qualifies for a 504 plan or an IEP is made on a case-by-case basis by a team of professionals, including teachers, administrators, and parents.

Accommodations List

Examples of accommodations that may be provided to students with dyslexia in BISD include, but are not limited to:

  • Extended time on assignments and tests
  • Preferential seating (e.g., near the teacher)
  • Use of assistive technology (e.g., text-to-speech software, audiobooks)
  • Modified assignments or assessments
  • Oral administration of tests
  • Provision of notes or outlines
  • Breaks during long tasks
  • Use of graphic organizers
  • Reading aloud to the student (when appropriate)

The specific accommodations provided will be determined based on the individual needs of the student as documented in their 504 plan or IEP.

How to Request Evaluation

If you suspect your child may have dyslexia, you should contact your child's teacher or the school principal. You can also contact the BISD Special Education Department directly to request an evaluation. A written request is recommended. The school will gather data on the student's academic performance and conduct screenings. If, after the initial screenings and data gathering, there is reason to suspect dyslexia, the school will proceed with a full evaluation. Parents are integral members of the evaluation team and will be involved in all stages of the process. You have the right to review all evaluation reports and participate in the development of your child's 504 plan or IEP.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

Is my child entitled to dyslexia testing in Texas? +

Yes. Texas law requires all districts to screen students for dyslexia and related disorders in Kindergarten and First Grade, and at any grade level if a parent or teacher requests it.

What accommodations can a dyslexic child get in Texas? +

Accommodations may include extended time on tests, text-to-speech software, oral administration of tests, reduced-answer-choice formats, and use of a calculator. These can be provided through a 504 Plan or an IEP.

What is the Texas Dyslexia Handbook? +

The Texas Dyslexia Handbook is a state guidance document updated in 2024 that outlines districts' responsibilities for identifying, evaluating, and serving students with dyslexia using structured literacy methods.
