Dyslexia Services in East Central ISD

Dyslexia Services in East Central ISD

East Central Independent School District (ECISD) is committed to providing comprehensive support for students with dyslexia, ensuring they receive the appropriate interventions and accommodations to thrive academically. This guide outlines the services available in ECISD, aligning with the Texas Dyslexia Handbook.

Texas Dyslexia Handbook 2024

The Texas Dyslexia Handbook 2024, published by the Texas Education Agency (TEA), is the guiding document for dyslexia services in Texas public schools. ECISD adheres to the guidelines and requirements outlined in this handbook, which includes information on identification, evaluation, instruction, and accommodations for students with dyslexia. The handbook provides detailed information on Structured Literacy, progress monitoring, and the rights of students with dyslexia. It's crucial for parents, educators, and administrators to be familiar with this resource.

Mandatory Screening

ECISD conducts mandatory dyslexia screening for all students in kindergarten and first grade. This screening is designed to identify students who may be at risk for dyslexia. The screening process typically involves assessing phonological awareness, rapid automatized naming, and letter-sound knowledge. Further screening or evaluation may be recommended based on the results of the initial screening and teacher observations. Parents will be notified if their child is identified as potentially at risk and will be involved in the decision-making process regarding further evaluation.

Structured Literacy Programs

ECISD utilizes Structured Literacy programs that align with the principles of explicit, systematic, cumulative, and multisensory instruction as recommended in the Texas Dyslexia Handbook. These programs focus on teaching foundational reading skills such as phonics, phonological awareness, reading fluency, vocabulary, and reading comprehension in a structured and sequential manner. Examples of programs that might be used within ECISD (check with the specific campus for currently implemented programs) include, but are not limited to, multi-sensory teaching programs aligned with evidence-based practices. These programs are delivered by trained teachers and specialists who are knowledgeable in dyslexia intervention strategies.

504 vs IEP for Dyslexia

Students with dyslexia may qualify for support under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act or through an Individualized Education Program (IEP) under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). A 504 plan provides accommodations and modifications to the general education environment to ensure equal access to learning. This is appropriate when a student's dyslexia primarily impacts their ability to access the curriculum. An IEP is developed when a student's dyslexia significantly impacts their academic progress and requires specialized instruction and related services. The determination of whether a student requires a 504 plan or an IEP is made by a multidisciplinary team, which includes parents, teachers, and other relevant professionals, based on a comprehensive evaluation of the student's needs.

Accommodations List

Accommodations are adjustments to the learning environment or instructional methods that help students with dyslexia access the curriculum and demonstrate their knowledge. Common accommodations provided to students with dyslexia in ECISD may include, but are not limited to:

  • Extended time on tests and assignments
  • Use of assistive technology (e.g., text-to-speech software, speech-to-text software)
  • Preferential seating
  • Modified assignments
  • Oral reading of tests and assignments
  • Use of graphic organizers
  • Multi-sensory teaching techniques
  • Access to notes or outlines
  • Reduced workload
  • Quiet testing environment

How to Request Evaluation

If you suspect your child may have dyslexia, you should contact your child's teacher, school counselor, or the campus principal. A written request for evaluation is recommended and should be directed to the school principal or Special Education department. The request should include specific concerns and observations related to your child's reading, writing, and spelling abilities. The school will then initiate a process that may include data collection, classroom observations, and formal assessments to determine if a full evaluation is warranted. Parents are an integral part of the evaluation process and will be actively involved in all stages.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

Is my child entitled to dyslexia testing in Texas? +

Yes. Texas law requires all districts to screen students for dyslexia and related disorders in Kindergarten and First Grade, and at any grade level if a parent or teacher requests it.

What accommodations can a dyslexic child get in Texas? +

Accommodations may include extended time on tests, text-to-speech software, oral administration of tests, reduced-answer-choice formats, and use of a calculator. These can be provided through a 504 Plan or an IEP.

What is the Texas Dyslexia Handbook? +

The Texas Dyslexia Handbook is a state guidance document updated in 2024 that outlines districts' responsibilities for identifying, evaluating, and serving students with dyslexia using structured literacy methods.
