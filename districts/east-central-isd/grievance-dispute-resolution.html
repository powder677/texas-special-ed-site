Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

THIS IS CRITICAL if your child has an IEP (Individualized Education Program) or a 504 plan! If the district is considering a suspension that will result in a change of placement (see below), or if your child has accrued more than 10 days of suspension in a school year, a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) MUST be conducted. The MDR team, including you as the parent, determines if the behavior was:

  • Caused by or directly and substantially related to the child's disability, OR
  • The direct result of the school district's failure to implement the IEP.

If EITHER of these is true, the behavior is considered a "manifestation" of the disability. The district cannot suspend your child if the behavior is a manifestation of their disability, unless the behavior involves weapons, drugs, or serious bodily injury. In these cases, they may be placed in an Interim Alternative Educational Setting (IAES) for up to 45 school days.

DEMAND AN MDR MEETING IMMEDIATELY if the 10-day threshold is reached or a change of placement is being considered! Don't let them tell you otherwise. Gather all documentation related to your child's disability and prepare to advocate fiercely.

Change of Placement

A Change of Placement occurs when a student is: (1) removed for more than 10 consecutive school days; or (2) subjected to a series of removals that constitute a pattern because they total more than 10 school days in a school year; the student's behavior is substantially similar to the student's behavior in previous incidents that resulted in the series of removals; and additional factors such as the length of each removal, the total amount of time the student is removed, and the proximity of the removals to one another. This includes suspensions, expulsions, or placement in an alternative educational setting. Any disciplinary action that significantly alters your child's learning environment requires adherence to IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) protections, including the MDR process outlined above.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe East Central ISD violated your child's rights, you can file a formal grievance. The first step is a Level 1 Grievance. This typically involves submitting a written complaint to the principal or appropriate administrator. Be specific and concise. Include dates, times, names of individuals involved, and detailed descriptions of the incidents. State the specific violation of district policy, state law, or federal law that you believe occurred. Keep copies of everything you submit and document all communication with the district.

TEA State Complaint

You can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA) if you believe East Central ISD violated state or federal laws related to special education. This is a more formal process with specific timelines and requirements. Gather all documentation, including IEPs, evaluations, disciplinary records, correspondence with the district, and your Level 1 Grievance (if applicable). TEA investigations can be lengthy, but they can lead to significant corrective action.

Due Process

Due process is your right to a fair and impartial hearing if you disagree with the district's decisions regarding your child's special education services or disciplinary actions. This includes the right to present evidence, cross-examine witnesses, and be represented by an attorney or advocate. Understanding and exercising your due process rights is crucial to protecting your child's educational future. If a MDR results in the district stating the behavior was NOT a manifestation of disability, you have the right to ask for an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at the district's expense, and to initiate due process to challenge the manifestation determination.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
