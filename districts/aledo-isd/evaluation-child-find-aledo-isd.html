Texas law and federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) guarantee that every child in ALEDO ISD has the right to a free, appropriate public education. If you believe your child may have a disability that affects their learning, ALEDO ISD must evaluate them at no cost to your family.

The ALEDO ISD child find system is designed to identify students who may need special education services. This means the district is actively looking for students who might benefit from evaluation and services, whether they're in general education, preschool, or at home.

Key Legal Protections for ALEDO ISD Families

  • You have the right to request an evaluation at any time during your child's school years
  • ALEDO ISD must respond to your request in writing within a reasonable timeframe
  • The evaluation must be conducted in your child's native language or primary mode of communication
  • You have the right to participate in all meetings and decisions about your child's evaluation
  • All evaluations must be conducted by qualified professionals at no cost to your family

As a parent in ALEDO ISD, you know your child better than anyone. Trust your instincts if you notice patterns that concern you. Some common signs that your child may benefit from an ALEDO ISD special education evaluation include significant struggles with reading, writing, math, or social interactions that don't improve with regular classroom instruction.

Consider requesting an evaluation if your child experiences:

  • Difficulty keeping up academically despite effort and support at home
  • Behavioral challenges that interfere with learning or social relationships
  • Speech or language delays compared to same-age peers
  • Physical coordination problems or fine motor skill delays
  • Significant anxiety, mood changes, or emotional regulation difficulties
  • Suspected attention or executive function problems affecting school performance
  • Sensory sensitivities or responses to environmental stimuli

Step-by-Step: How to Request an ALEDO ISD Evaluation

Step 1: Express Your Concern in Writing

The most important thing you can do as a parent in ALEDO ISD is put your request for an evaluation in writing. While you can speak with your child's teacher or school counselor first, sending a written request creates an official record that ALEDO ISD must respond to.

Send your written request to your child's campus principal and the ALEDO ISD Director of Special Education. You can deliver it in person, email it, or send it by certified mail. Keep a copy for your records and document the date you sent it.

Step 2: Clearly State Your Concerns

In your written request, explain specifically why you believe your child needs an ALEDO ISD special education evaluation. Be concrete and specific rather than general. Instead of "my child struggles in school," write "my child has difficulty with reading fluency and comprehension and scores significantly below grade level on classroom assessments."

Include any outside evaluations or assessments you've had conducted, medical diagnoses, or documented concerns from other professionals. This information helps ALEDO ISD understand your child's complete picture.

Step 3: Know the ALEDO ISD 45-Day Timeline

Once ALEDO ISD receives your written request for an ALEDO ISD FIE evaluation, the district has a specific timeline to respond. Under Texas Education Code, ALEDO ISD must complete the evaluation within 45 calendar days from the date you submit your request.

This ALEDO ISD 45 day timeline is critical. Within this period, ALEDO ISD must:

  • Schedule a meeting with you to discuss the evaluation process
  • Explain what areas will be evaluated
  • Obtain your written consent to proceed with testing
  • Conduct all necessary assessments and tests
  • Complete all evaluation reports and findings

The ALEDO ISD Evaluation Process: What to Expect

The Initial Meeting

Shortly after ALEDO ISD receives your evaluation request, the school will schedule a meeting with you. This meeting happens before formal testing begins. The team will explain what they plan to evaluate, what tests they'll use, and how long the process takes.

At this meeting with ALEDO ISD staff, you'll need to sign consent forms before any evaluation can begin. This is your opportunity to ask questions about the ALEDO ISD FIE evaluation process and understand what your child will experience.

Assessment and Testing

The actual ALEDO ISD special education evaluation typically includes assessments in several areas depending on your child's concerns. A comprehensive ALEDO ISD FIE evaluation may include cognitive testing, achievement testing, behavioral observation, and input from teachers and parents.

Different professionals may be involved, including:

  • School psychologists (conduct cognitive and behavioral assessments)
  • Special education teachers (assess academic achievement)
  • Speech-language pathologists (if speech or language concerns exist)
  • Occupational or physical therapists (if fine or gross motor concerns exist)
  • School counselors or social workers (for behavioral or emotional concerns)

The Evaluation Report

Once all testing is complete, ALEDO ISD will compile a comprehensive report of your child's FIE evaluation results. This report explains what was tested, how your child performed, and whether your child qualifies for special education services under IDEA categories recognized in Texas.

Understanding ALEDO ISD FIE Results and Next Steps

The ARD Meeting

After the ALEDO ISD evaluation is complete, the school will schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. This is where you'll meet with the evaluation team, special education staff, general education teachers, and administrators to discuss results.

During the ARD meeting for your child's ALEDO ISD FIE evaluation, the team will:

  • Present all evaluation findings and test results
  • Discuss whether your child meets criteria for special education eligibility
  • If eligible, develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) tailored to your child's needs
  • Determine the level of services and placement your child will receive
  • Establish goals and progress monitoring plans

If Your Child Qualifies for Services

If the ALEDO ISD evaluation determines your child qualifies for special education, you'll work with the team to create an IEP. This legal document outlines exactly what services ALEDO ISD will provide, how progress will be measured, and what supports your child will receive.

In ALEDO ISD, special education services range from consultation and resource services to self-contained classrooms, depending on your child's needs and the least restrictive environment appropriate for them.

If Your Child Does Not Qualify

If the ALEDO ISD FIE evaluation determines your child doesn't meet criteria for special education under IDEA, you have options. You can request a meeting to discuss results, ask for clarification, or request an independent evaluation at ALEDO ISD's expense.

Even if your child doesn't qualify under IDEA, they may still be eligible for supports through a Section 504 plan or Response to Intervention (RTI) services within the general education program.

Preparing for Success: Tips for ALEDO ISD Parents

Gather Documentation Before Your ALEDO ISD Evaluation Request

Before you submit your written ALEDO ISD evaluation request, compile any documentation that supports your concerns. This might include:

  • Report cards and progress reports showing academic struggles
  • Teacher notes or emails expressing concerns
  • Attendance records (if behavioral issues are suspected)
  • Medical records or diagnoses from doctors
  • Results from outside evaluations (tutoring, private testing)
  • Examples of work samples showing your child's performance

Be Prepared for the ALEDO ISD Evaluation Process

Understanding what to expect during the ALEDO ISD special education evaluation helps you support your child. Many parents worry about the testing experience, but trained professionals in ALEDO ISD know how to work with children in age-appropriate ways.

Talk with your child about the upcoming evaluation in positive, reassuring terms. Explain that school staff want to understand how they learn best so they can get help if needed. Encourage rest and healthy meals before testing days.

Attend Meetings and Ask Questions

Your participation is invaluable in the ALEDO ISD FIE evaluation process. Attend all meetings, take notes, and don't hesitate to ask questions. You're the expert on your child, and your input helps ALEDO ISD professionals understand your child's strengths and needs.

Special Considerations for ALEDO ISD Families

If English Is Not Your Primary Language

ALEDO ISD must provide interpreters and materials in your primary language throughout the evaluation request and evaluation process. Inform the school of your language needs in writing to ensure proper accommodations are made.

If You Disagree with ALEDO ISD Evaluation Results

You have the right to disagree with the results of your child's ALEDO ISD FIE evaluation. You can request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at ALEDO ISD's expense if you believe the evaluation was incomplete or inaccurate.

You also have the right to request mediation or due process hearings if you disagree with decisions made in your child's ARD meeting. Many parents in the ALEDO area find it helpful to consult with a special ed advocate ALEDO or special education attorney near ALEDO if disputes arise.

Resources and Support for ALEDO ISD Parents

You don't have to navigate the ALEDO ISD special education evaluation process alone. Several resources are available to parents in ALEDO ISD and the surrounding area:

  • ALEDO ISD Special Education Department: Your first point of contact for questions about the evaluation process and special education services
  • Campus Special Education Coordinator: Available at your child's campus to discuss concerns and next steps
  • Special Education Parent Training and Information Centers: Free resources for parents across Texas
  • Special ed advocate ALEDO: Professional advocates can guide you through the process and represent your interests
  • Special education attorney near ALEDO: If disputes arise, legal counsel can protect your child's rights

Conclusion: Taking Action for Your Child

Requesting an ALEDO ISD FIE evaluation is a positive step toward getting your child the support they need to succeed. While the process may seem overwhelming, understanding your rights and following the steps outlined in this guide empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Remember that ALEDO ISD staff want to help your child. By communicating clearly about your concerns, preparing thoroughly, and staying engaged throughout the ALEDO ISD evaluation process, you're giving your child the best possible chance of receiving appropriate services and support.

If you have questions about starting the ALEDO ISD special education evaluation process, contact your child's campus principal or the ALEDO ISD Special Education Department today. Your written evaluation request is the beginning of your child's journey toward success.
