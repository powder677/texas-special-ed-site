What is a Special Education Evaluation in ALEDO ISD?

A special education evaluation, also known as a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation) in ALEDO ISD, is a comprehensive process that examines your child's development, health, vision, hearing, and academic performance. This evaluation determines if your child qualifies for special education services under federal and Texas state laws.

In ALEDO ISD, special education evaluations are free for all students. The cost can never be passed on to parents. If you believe your child needs a special education evaluation in ALEDO ISD, you have the right to request one.

Why Might an Evaluation Be Needed in ALEDO ISD?

ALEDO ISD parents may request evaluations if they observe:

  • Difficulties with reading, writing, or mathematics
  • Developmental or speech/language delays
  • Behavioral or emotional problems
  • Difficulty paying attention or concentrating
  • Hearing or vision problems
  • Motor or coordination problems

Parents of students in ALEDO ISD have fundamental rights protected by federal law, specifically under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.

Key Rights for ALEDO ISD Parents:

  • Right to request evaluation: You can request a special education evaluation at any time during the school year in ALEDO ISD
  • Timely evaluation: ALEDO ISD must begin the evaluation within 30 calendar days of receiving your written request
  • Complete evaluation: ALEDO ISD must evaluate all relevant developmental and academic areas
  • Due process: You have the right to a due process hearing if you disagree with ALEDO ISD's decisions
  • Participation in meetings: You must participate in all evaluation and planning meetings in ALEDO ISD
  • Language interpretation: ALEDO ISD must provide Spanish interpretation for all communications

Step 1: Prepare Your Written Request for ALEDO ISD

The first step to requesting a special education evaluation in ALEDO ISD is to submit a written request. Although the law does not require a specific format, it is important that your request is clear and documented.

What to Include in Your Request to ALEDO ISD:

  1. Your child's full name and date of birth
  2. Current grade and school in ALEDO ISD
  3. A clear description of your specific concerns
  4. Concrete examples of the difficulties you have observed
  5. Any previous evaluations or diagnoses
  6. Your contact information (phone and email)
  7. Explicit request: "I am requesting a special education evaluation (FIE) to determine if my child is eligible for special education services in ALEDO ISD"

Important tip: Send your request by certified mail or deliver it in person to the ALEDO ISD office. Request written confirmation that it was received. Keep a copy for your records.

Step 2: The ALEDO ISD Evaluation Process

Once ALEDO ISD receives your written request, the special education evaluation process begins. This process is regulated by the Texas Education Code Section 29.004.

Evaluation Timeline in ALEDO ISD:

  • Within 30 days: ALEDO ISD must send information about your parental rights and request signed consent
  • Within 60 days: ALEDO ISD must complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) and schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting
  • At the ARD meeting: Results will be discussed, and eligibility for special education services will be determined

Areas ALEDO ISD Will Evaluate:

ALEDO ISD must comprehensively evaluate all relevant aspects of your child's development:

  • Academic skills (reading, writing, math)
  • Language and communication skills
  • Motor functioning (gross and fine)
  • Hearing and vision
  • Social and emotional functioning
  • Cognitive ability and general development

Step 3: The ALEDO ISD ARD Meeting

The Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting is a critical meeting in ALEDO ISD where evaluation results are discussed and important decisions about your child's services are made.

Who Participates in the ALEDO ISD ARD Meeting?

  • Parents or legal guardians
  • An administrative representative from ALEDO ISD
  • Your child's teacher
  • An evaluation specialist from ALEDO ISD
  • Other professionals as needed (speech therapist, school psychologist, etc.)

What Happens at the ALEDO ISD ARD Meeting:

During the ALEDO ISD ARD meeting, evaluation results will be presented, eligibility for special education will be determined, and an Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be developed if applicable. ALEDO ISD must provide a professional interpreter if necessary.

It is important that you ask questions during this meeting. You have the right to fully understand the results and their implications for your child.

Options If ALEDO ISD Denies the Evaluation

If ALEDO ISD denies your request for a special education evaluation, they must provide you with a written explanation for their reasons. This is not the end of the process.

Your Options in ALEDO ISD:

  • Request a review of the decision: You can meet with ALEDO ISD administrators to discuss the decision
  • Obtain an independent evaluation: At your expense, you can obtain a special education evaluation from an independent professional outside of ALEDO ISD
  • Request a due process hearing: You have the right to dispute ALEDO ISD's decision before a due process judge
  • Contact a special education attorney: An attorney specializing in special education near ALEDO can help you understand your legal options
  • Seek a special education advocate in ALEDO: A special education advocate can guide you at no cost

Important Documentation and Records

Keeping organized records is essential when navigating the special education system in ALEDO ISD. These documents protect you and your child.

Documents You Should Keep:

  • Copy of your written request to ALEDO ISD
  • Confirmation that ALEDO ISD received your request
  • All parent rights notices sent by ALEDO ISD
  • Complete evaluation report (FIE) from ALEDO ISD
  • Notes from the ALEDO ISD ARD meeting
  • Your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP)
  • Correspondence with ALEDO ISD
  • Relevant medical records

Tip: Create a well-organized physical or digital folder with all your ALEDO ISD documents. This will facilitate quick access when you need it.

Frequently Asked Questions about Special Education Evaluation in ALEDO ISD

Can I verbally request a special education evaluation in ALEDO ISD?

While you can make the request verbally, it is recommended to make a written request. A written request creates an official record and initiates the legal 30-day timeline that ALEDO ISD must adhere to.

How much does a special education evaluation cost in ALEDO ISD?

Special education evaluations are completely free in ALEDO ISD. There is no cost to parents. If ALEDO ISD attempts to charge you, that is illegal.

What happens if I disagree with ALEDO ISD's results?

You have the right to dispute any decision made by ALEDO ISD. You can request an independent evaluation, meet with administrators, or request a due process hearing. A special education attorney near ALEDO or a special education advocate can help you.

Can I bring someone with me to the ALEDO ISD ARD meeting?

Yes, absolutely. You can bring a special education advocate, an attorney, a friend, or a family member to any meeting with ALEDO ISD to support you.

Requesting a special education evaluation in ALEDO ISD is an important and positive step to ensure your child receives the academic support they need. ALEDO ISD parents have protected legal rights in this process, and ALEDO ISD is legally bound to follow specific procedures.

Remember: the goal of the special education process in ALEDO ISD is to ensure that every student receives a free appropriate public education (FAPE). If you believe your child needs a special education evaluation in ALEDO ISD, do not hesitate to request one. Your concerns are valid, and you have every right to seek answers and support for your child in ALEDO ISD.
