A Full Individual Evaluation is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine whether your child has a disability that affects their ability to learn and whether they qualify for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). CLEBURNE ISD is legally required to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities in the district, regardless of the severity of their disability.

CLEBURNE ISD's child find system ensures that children with suspected disabilities are identified and evaluated. This requirement applies to all children ages 3-21, including those who are homeschooled or attend private schools within the CLEBURNE area. If you believe your child may have a disability, you have the right to request a CLEBURNE ISD child find evaluation at no cost to your family.

What Types of Disabilities Qualify?

Under Texas law and IDEA, the following disability categories qualify for special education services in CLEBURNE ISD:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
  • Emotional/Behavioral Disorders
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Specific Learning Disability (dyslexia, dyscalculia, dysgraphia)
  • Speech/Language Impairment
  • Hearing Impairment or Deafness
  • Visual Impairment or Blindness
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment (ADHD, etc.)
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Multiple Disabilities

Parents in CLEBURNE ISD can request a CLEBURNE ISD evaluation request in writing through several methods. The most important thing to remember is that your request must be in writing—a verbal conversation with a teacher or administrator is not sufficient to officially start the evaluation timeline.

How to Submit Your Request

To initiate a CLEBURNE ISD FIE evaluation, you can:

  1. Email the Special Education Department – Contact your campus special education coordinator or the CLEBURNE ISD special education office directly
  2. Submit a Written Letter – Mail or hand-deliver a written request to your child's school principal or the Director of Special Education
  3. Complete an Official Referral Form – Ask the school if CLEBURNE ISD has a specific evaluation request form
  4. Request at an ARD/IEP Meeting – If your child already has an IEP, you can request additional evaluations during the annual ARD meeting

Keep a copy of your written request and document when and how you submitted it. This is crucial for protecting your rights under the CLEBURNE ISD 45 day timeline requirement.

What to Include in Your Request

Make your special education evaluation request clear and specific. Include:

  • Your child's full name, date of birth, and current grade level
  • Specific concerns about your child's learning, behavior, or development
  • How long you've noticed these concerns
  • Any interventions or strategies already tried at home or school
  • Request for a Full Individual Evaluation (use this exact language)
  • Your contact information and preferred communication method

Example: "I am requesting a Full Individual Evaluation for my child [Name] to determine whether they have a disability affecting their education. My child has struggled with reading comprehension since first grade and has not made adequate progress despite classroom interventions."

Understanding the 45-Day Timeline for CLEBURNE ISD Evaluations

One of the most important protections for parents in CLEBURNE ISD is the CLEBURNE ISD 45 day timeline. This is a legal requirement that the district must follow carefully. After you submit a written evaluation request, CLEBURNE ISD has exactly 45 calendar days to complete the entire FIE and provide you with written results.

Important Timeline Details

The 45-day clock starts from the date CLEBURNE ISD receives your written evaluation request. This timeline includes:

  • Obtaining parental consent for evaluation
  • Completing all assessments and testing
  • Gathering records and teacher input
  • Holding the ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to discuss results
  • Providing you with a written evaluation report

CLEBURNE ISD cannot extend this timeline without your written permission. If the district needs more time, they must ask for your consent in writing before the 45 days expire. You should not feel pressured to agree to an extension unless you genuinely need more time to discuss results with your family.

What If CLEBURNE ISD Misses the Deadline?

If CLEBURNE ISD fails to complete your child's evaluation within 45 days, this is a violation of your child's rights under federal law. Document the missed deadline and consider consulting with a special education attorney near CLEBURNE or contacting a special ed advocate CLEBURNE families trust for guidance on next steps.

What Assessments Are Included in a CLEBURNE ISD FIE?

A comprehensive CLEBURNE ISD special education evaluation includes multiple types of assessments to get a complete picture of your child's strengths and needs. CLEBURNE ISD must use multiple measures and cannot rely on a single test or observation.

Common Evaluations in CLEBURNE ISD

  • Cognitive/IQ Testing – Assesses overall intellectual ability and processing skills
  • Achievement Testing – Measures academic performance in reading, math, writing, and language
  • Speech/Language Evaluation – Checks communication skills and language development
  • Behavioral/Social-Emotional Assessment – Evaluates behavior, emotions, and social functioning
  • Classroom Observations – Teachers document how your child performs in the school setting
  • Medical/Health Review – CLEBURNE ISD examines health records and development history
  • Parent/Teacher Rating Scales – Standardized questionnaires from adults who know your child well

CLEBURNE ISD will only conduct assessments relevant to your child's suspected disability. You have the right to ask why specific evaluations are recommended and to ask questions about any assessment before it's administered.

Your Rights During the CLEBURNE ISD Evaluation Process

As a parent of a child in CLEBURNE ISD, you have specific legal rights during the evaluation process. Understanding these rights helps you advocate effectively for your child.

Key Parent Rights in CLEBURNE ISD

  • Right to Prior Written Notice – CLEBURNE ISD must inform you in writing about the evaluation plans, what will be tested, and when it will happen
  • Right to Parental Consent – You must give written permission before CLEBURNE ISD begins testing (with limited exceptions)
  • Right to Interpreter Services – If English is not your primary language, CLEBURNE ISD must provide an interpreter at ARD meetings
  • Right to Access Records – You can review all evaluation materials and your child's educational records
  • Right to Independent Evaluation – If you disagree with CLEBURNE ISD's evaluation, you may request an independent evaluation at district expense
  • Right to Advocate or Attorney – You can bring a special ed advocate CLEBURNE or attorney to ARD meetings
  • Right to Refuse Services – You can decline special education services even if your child qualifies

Preparing for the ARD Meeting After Your CLEBURNE ISD Evaluation

The ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting is where CLEBURNE ISD discusses the evaluation results and determines whether your child qualifies for special education services. This meeting typically occurs within the 45-day evaluation timeline.

How to Prepare Effectively

Before the Meeting:

  • Request and review all evaluation reports at least one week before the ARD meeting
  • Write down questions about any results you don't understand
  • Prepare a list of your child's strengths and challenges
  • Consider bringing a special ed advocate CLEBURNE parents recommend for support
  • Gather any outside evaluations or medical records (even from providers outside CLEBURNE)

During the Meeting:

  • Ask CLEBURNE ISD staff to explain test scores in language you understand
  • Share information about your child's functioning at home
  • Don't feel rushed to make decisions immediately
  • Request copies of all documents before leaving
  • Ask for clarification on eligibility determination and next steps

Common Challenges in the CLEBURNE ISD Evaluation Process

Parents in CLEBURNE ISD sometimes encounter obstacles during evaluation. Knowing what to expect helps you respond effectively.

Challenge: CLEBURNE ISD Delays or Denies Your Request

If CLEBURNE ISD doesn't acknowledge your written request within a few days, follow up by email or phone. Document all communication. If the district refuses to evaluate, request the refusal in writing and consider contacting the Texas Education Agency (TEA) or consulting a special education attorney near CLEBURNE.

Challenge: CLEBURNE ISD Refuses to Test for Certain Concerns

CLEBURNE ISD must evaluate all areas of suspected disability. If they refuse to test for speech, behavior, or another specific concern, ask why in writing. If you disagree, you can pursue an independent evaluation.

Challenge: Evaluation Results Seem Inaccurate

If you believe the CLEBURNE ISD special education evaluation doesn't accurately reflect your child's abilities, you have options. You can request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at CLEBURNE ISD's expense, bring outside evaluations to the ARD meeting, or request a second opinion.

Next Steps After Eligibility Determination by CLEBURNE ISD

Once CLEBURNE ISD completes the evaluation, there are two possible outcomes: your child qualifies for special education services, or they do not qualify.

If Your Child Qualifies

CLEBURNE ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) at the ARD meeting. This legal document outlines the special education services, accommodations, modifications, and goals your child will receive. You'll meet annually to review and update the IEP, and CLEBURNE ISD must provide the services detailed in the plan.

If Your Child Does Not Qualify

You have the right to disagree with CLEBURNE ISD's eligibility determination. You can request an independent evaluation, ask for a second ARD meeting to discuss additional concerns, or pursue a due process hearing. Some students who don't qualify under IDEA may still qualify for accommodations under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.
