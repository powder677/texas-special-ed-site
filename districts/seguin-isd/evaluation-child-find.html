Requesting an Evaluation in Seguin ISD

⚡ Quick Answer

How long does Texas have to complete an evaluation?
Once a written evaluation request is received, the district must complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) and hold an ARD meeting within 45 school days. This timeline is strictly enforced by the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Verbal requests do not start the clock — always submit in writing and keep a copy.

Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in Seguin ISD

How to Submit a Written Request

If you suspect your child may have a disability that requires special education services within Seguin ISD, the first step is to submit a written request for a full and individual evaluation (FIE). This request should be clear, concise, and addressed to the appropriate school personnel. This could be the school principal, special education director, or your child's teacher. Make sure to keep a copy of the request for your records. A sample letter might include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth.
  • Your child's grade level and current school.
  • A detailed description of your concerns regarding your child's academic, behavioral, or developmental progress. Be specific about the challenges your child is facing. Provide examples.
  • The reason you believe your child may have a disability requiring special education services.
  • Your contact information, including phone number and email address.
  • A clear statement requesting a full and individual evaluation to determine eligibility for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas state law.

Delivery is also important. Sending the letter via certified mail with a return receipt requested provides proof that the school received your request. You can also hand-deliver the request and ask for a signed and dated copy for your records. This documentation can be crucial if there are any disputes regarding the timeline or the district's response.

The 45 School Day Timeline

Texas law mandates a strict timeline for the special education evaluation process. From the date the school district receives your written request for an FIE, they have 15 school days to determine whether to evaluate your child. If the district decides to evaluate, they must provide you with written notice and obtain your consent for the evaluation. This notice will explain the types of assessments that will be conducted.

Once you provide your written consent for the evaluation, the school district then has 45 school days to complete the evaluation and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committee meeting to discuss the evaluation results and determine your child's eligibility for special education services. It's important to note that these are *school* days, not calendar days. This means weekends, holidays, and school breaks are not included in the 45-day count. The ARD committee includes parents, administrators, teachers, and any other relevant professionals.

What the FIE Must Cover

The Full and Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment designed to identify your child's strengths and weaknesses, and to determine if they have a disability that adversely affects their educational performance. Texas law requires that the FIE cover all areas of suspected disability. This may include academic achievement, cognitive abilities, speech and language skills, motor skills, adaptive behavior, social-emotional development, and physical or medical conditions.

The evaluation must be conducted by qualified professionals, such as educational diagnosticians, licensed psychologists, speech-language pathologists, and occupational therapists. The evaluation must use a variety of assessment tools and techniques, including observations, interviews, and standardized tests. The results of the evaluation should be presented in a clear and understandable manner, and the evaluation report should include specific recommendations for educational interventions and supports.

Child Find Obligations

Seguin ISD, like all school districts in Texas, has a legal obligation under IDEA and Texas law to actively seek out and identify children with disabilities who may be in need of special education services. This is known as "Child Find." This obligation applies to all children residing within the district, regardless of whether they are enrolled in public school, private school, or are homeschooled. If the district has reason to suspect that a child may have a disability, they are required to initiate an evaluation, even if a formal written request has not been submitted by the parents. Parents can always contact the district to express concerns about their child's development, triggering the Child Find obligation.

What If They Refuse

If Seguin ISD refuses your written request for an FIE, they must provide you with a written explanation of their decision, outlining the reasons for the refusal. This written notice must also inform you of your right to dispute the district's decision through mediation or a due process hearing. You have the right to request a meeting with school officials to discuss their decision. If you disagree with the district's decision, you should consider seeking legal advice from an attorney specializing in special education law. The Texas Education Agency (TEA) also provides resources and information for parents of children with disabilities.

After the Evaluation

Following the completion of the FIE, the ARD committee will meet to review the results and determine if your child is eligible for special education services. If your child is found eligible, the ARD committee will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). The IEP is a legally binding document that outlines your child's specific educational needs, goals, and the services and supports they will receive to achieve those goals. You are a crucial member of the ARD committee and have the right to participate fully in the development of your child's IEP. Make sure you understand all aspects of the IEP and that it addresses your child's individual needs. You have the right to disagree with the IEP and to request revisions. Ongoing communication and collaboration between parents and the school are essential for ensuring your child's success in special education.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Texas? +

Submit a written request directly to the school's principal or special education director. The written request starts the mandatory 45 school-day clock. Keep a dated copy for your records.

What is a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in Texas? +

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment the school must complete to determine if your child has a disability and needs special education services. It must cover all areas of suspected disability and be completed within 45 school days of your written consent.

Can a Texas school refuse to evaluate my child? +

Yes, but only if the district provides written notice (Prior Written Notice) explaining why they are refusing and what data they relied on. You have the right to challenge that refusal through mediation or a due process hearing.
