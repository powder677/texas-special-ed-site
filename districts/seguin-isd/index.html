What can I do right now if Seguin ISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Seguin ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Seguin ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the special education system can feel overwhelming, but you're not alone. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas law, your child is entitled to a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) designed to meet their unique needs. Special education encompasses specially designed instruction and related services provided to eligible children with disabilities. These services aim to help your child make progress in the general education curriculum and prepare them for further education, employment, and independent living.

Seguin ISD, like all school districts in Texas, is overseen by the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA provides guidance and resources to ensure districts like Seguin ISD comply with federal and state special education laws. Additionally, Seguin ISD receives support from TEA Region 20 Education Service Center, which offers training, technical assistance, and other resources to educators and families. Understanding this framework is key to advocating effectively for your child.

It's crucial to remember that you have enforceable legal rights throughout the entire special education process. From initial referral to the development and implementation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP), your voice matters. Don't let anyone tell you otherwise. Seguin ISD is obligated to follow specific procedures and timelines, and you have the right to challenge decisions you disagree with. This website is designed to empower you with the information you need to advocate for your child's best interests within the Seguin ISD special education system.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Seguin ISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability that requires special education services, the first step is to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) from Seguin ISD. It is absolutely crucial that this request is submitted in writing. A verbal request does not start the legal timeline. Once Seguin ISD receives your written consent to evaluate, they have 45 school days to complete the FIE and hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to discuss the results.

Remember that Seguin ISD also has a legal responsibility under Child Find laws to proactively identify students who may need special education services. However, it is always best to be proactive. If you are concerned about your child's academic, behavioral, or developmental progress, don't hesitate to request an evaluation. Don't wait. Write a letter to the Special Education Director at Seguin ISD, clearly stating that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation for your child. Be sure to include your child's name, date of birth, school, and grade level, as well as your contact information. Keep a copy of the letter for your records and send the original via certified mail, return receipt requested, so you have proof of delivery.

ARD Meetings in Seguin ISD: Your Rights at the Table

The ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee meeting is where decisions are made about your child's special education program. This meeting is critically important, and you are a vital member of the ARD committee. You have the right to participate fully in the discussion and to offer your input on all aspects of your child's IEP (Individualized Education Program). Seguin ISD must provide you with at least five calendar days' written notice before any ARD meeting, giving you time to prepare and gather any necessary information.

During the ARD meeting, don't feel pressured to make decisions on the spot. You have the right to request a 10-day recess at any time during the meeting if you need more time to consider the information presented or to consult with outside experts. You also do not have to sign the IEP the same day. You can take the document home to review it carefully and sign it later. Remember, the IEP is a legally binding document, so it's essential that you understand and agree with its contents.

Your presence at the ARD table is non-negotiable and your voice matters. You are your child's strongest advocate. Always assert your rights and never be afraid to ask questions or express your concerns. Remember that collaboration between home and school is crucial for your child's success. Before your next ARD meeting in Seguin ISD, download our free ARD preparation checklist to help you stay organized and prepared.

Dyslexia Services in Seguin ISD

Texas law mandates that Seguin ISD screen every student for dyslexia. This screening is designed to identify students who may be at risk for reading difficulties. Early identification is crucial because it allows for timely intervention and support. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook sets the standards that Seguin ISD must follow in identifying and serving students with dyslexia. This handbook outlines best practices for screening, evaluation, and intervention.

If your child is identified as having dyslexia, they are entitled to receive structured literacy intervention, which is a research-based approach that focuses on phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension. Depending on the severity of your child's needs, they may qualify for a 504 Plan or an IEP to provide additional support and accommodations. If your child has not been screened for dyslexia, or you have concerns about their reading progress, request a dyslexia screening in writing from Seguin ISD today.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Seguin ISD

Disciplinary actions, such as suspensions, can have a significant impact on students with disabilities. If your child with an IEP or 504 Plan is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year in Seguin ISD, this triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). An MDR is an ARD meeting to determine if the behavior that led to the suspension was caused by or directly related to your child's disability. This also constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA, meaning the school cannot unilaterally change your child's placement without following specific procedures.

This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a special education student. You must act quickly to protect your child's rights. If your child has been suspended for more than 10 days, contact the school immediately to request an MDR meeting. Do not delay. This situation requires swift action and informed advocacy. If your child has been suspended, use the Discipline Defense Kit to understand your rights and prepare for the MDR meeting.

Filing a Complaint Against Seguin ISD

If you feel that Seguin ISD is not meeting its legal obligations to your child, you have several options for filing a complaint. It's important to remember that you have these rights regardless of what any Seguin ISD staff member might tell you. One option is to file a State Complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This is a free process, and TEA is required to resolve the complaint within 60 days. Another option is to request Mediation through TEA, which is also a free service that brings you and Seguin ISD together with a neutral mediator to try to resolve the issue.

If mediation is unsuccessful, or if you prefer, you can request a Due Process Hearing, which is a more formal legal proceeding. You can also file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR). Each of these options has different procedures and timelines, so it's important to understand the pros and cons of each before making a decision. Regardless of which path you choose, documenting all communication and actions related to your child's special education is essential. If you are considering filing a complaint against Seguin ISD, begin by gathering all relevant documents and information to support your case.

Frequently Asked Questions About Seguin ISD Special Education

The questions every Seguin ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Seguin ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Seguin ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Seguin ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Seguin ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Seguin ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Seguin ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Seguin ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Seguin ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
