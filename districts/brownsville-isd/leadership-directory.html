How to Effectively Contact Brownsville ISD Special Education Staff

Communicating effectively with Brownsville ISD special education staff is crucial for your child's success. Understanding the best practices can help ensure your concerns are addressed promptly and efficiently.

Best Times to Contact: Generally, mornings before 8:00 AM or after school hours (3:30 PM - 4:30 PM) are ideal for reaching teachers. Check the Brownsville ISD website for specific school hours and staff availability.

Email vs. Phone: For detailed questions or concerns, email is preferred, as it allows staff to provide thoughtful responses. For urgent matters, a phone call may be necessary. However, always follow up any phone conversation with an email summarizing the discussion and any agreed-upon actions.

What to Say in Your First Message: Be clear and concise. Include your child's name, grade, school, and a brief summary of your concern. For example: "Dear [Teacher's Name], I am writing to you regarding [Child's Name]'s progress in reading. I have noticed [specific observation] and would like to discuss strategies to support them further."

Important Tip: Always document every communication. Keep copies of emails and notes from phone conversations. This record can be invaluable if further action is needed.

What to Do If You Can't Reach Anyone

If you are having difficulty reaching the relevant special education staff at Brownsville ISD, follow these escalation steps:

  • Step 1: Contact your child's special education teacher.
  • Step 2: If unresolved, contact the campus Special Education Coordinator or Diagnostician.
  • Step 3: If still unresolved, contact the Brownsville ISD Special Education Director. Contact information should be available on the district website.
  • Step 4: As a last resort, you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

Remember that Texas Education Code Chapter 29 outlines the rights and responsibilities related to special education services. Familiarize yourself with these provisions.

Your Right to District Records

As a parent, you have the right to access your child's educational records and information about Brownsville ISD's special education programs.

FERPA Rights: Under the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA), Brownsville ISD has 45 days to respond to a written request for your child's educational records.

Public Information Act: The Texas Public Information Act (Texas Government Code, Chapter 552) grants you the right to request information related to district operations, including staff contact information and policies. This can be useful if you need to contact specific personnel or understand district procedures.

Submitting a Records Request: All records requests should be submitted in writing (email or letter) to the Brownsville ISD's designated records custodian. Clearly state the specific records you are requesting. The Brownsville ISD website should provide information on how to submit these requests and contact information for the records custodian.
