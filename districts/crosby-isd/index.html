What can I do right now if Crosby ISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Crosby ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Crosby ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

If you're navigating the special education system in Crosby ISD, you're likely feeling overwhelmed. You're not alone. Special education, as defined by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas law, is designed to provide your child with a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) tailored to their unique needs. This means specialized instruction and related services are available to eligible students with disabilities at no cost to you. Remember, this is a right, not a privilege.

Crosby ISD operates under the guidance of the Texas Education Agency (TEA), which sets the standards for special education services across the state. Furthermore, Crosby ISD receives support from the TEA Region 4 Education Service Center, providing resources and training to ensure the district is implementing best practices. But ultimately, the responsibility to provide appropriate services falls on Crosby ISD. What's most critical for you to understand is that you have enforceable legal rights throughout the entire special education process, from initial evaluation to the development and implementation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP). Do not hesitate to exercise them.

The staff at Crosby ISD are supposed to be knowledgeable and supportive, but it's crucial to be your child's strongest advocate. This means understanding your rights, asking questions, and documenting everything. The special education journey can be challenging, but with the right information and support, you can ensure your child receives the services they need to thrive in Crosby ISD.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Crosby ISD

The first step in accessing special education services is requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for your child. It's vital to know that this request MUST be in writing. A verbal request to a teacher or administrator, while helpful for initial conversations, does NOT officially start the evaluation timeline. Once Crosby ISD receives your written request and you provide written consent for the evaluation, the district has 45 school days to complete the FIE. This timeline is legally binding, so keep track of the dates.

It's also important to be aware of Child Find laws. These laws mandate that Crosby ISD proactively identify, locate, and evaluate all children within its boundaries who may have a disability and need special education services, regardless of whether they are enrolled in public school. If you suspect your child needs special education services, even if you haven't been contacted by the school, you have the right to request an evaluation. Be proactive. Don't wait for Crosby ISD to come to you. Prepare your written request now, keeping a copy for your records, and send it to the Special Education Director at Crosby ISD via certified mail with return receipt requested. This provides you with proof that the district received your request and starts the 45-day clock.

ARD Meetings in Crosby ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal. These ARD meetings are the cornerstone of the special education process. It's in these meetings that your child's eligibility for special education is determined, their IEP is developed, and progress is reviewed. Crosby ISD is required to provide you with at least five calendar days written notice before any ARD meeting, giving you time to prepare and gather your thoughts. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, the date, time, location, and who will be attending. Review this notice carefully and don't hesitate to contact the school if you have any questions or need clarification.

During the ARD meeting, you are a crucial member of the ARD committee. Your input and perspective are invaluable. You have the right to express your concerns, ask questions, and disagree with any recommendations made by the school staff. Remember, you know your child best. At any point during the ARD meeting, you can request a 10-day recess. This means you can ask for a break in the meeting to gather your thoughts, seek outside consultation, or simply take some time to process the information being presented. This is your right, and you should not hesitate to exercise it. Additionally, you are not required to sign the IEP the same day as the ARD meeting. Take the IEP home, review it carefully, and seek advice if needed before making a decision.

The ARD process in Crosby ISD should be collaborative and focused on your child's best interests. Be prepared to advocate for your child's needs and ensure their IEP is designed to help them achieve their full potential. Before your next ARD meeting in Crosby ISD, download our free ARD preparation checklist. This checklist will help you organize your thoughts, gather relevant information, and ensure you are fully prepared to participate effectively in the ARD meeting.

Dyslexia Services in Crosby ISD

Texas law mandates that Crosby ISD screen all students for dyslexia. This screening process is designed to identify students who may be at risk for reading difficulties. The standards for dyslexia screening and intervention in Crosby ISD are guided by the 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook, which outlines best practices and legal requirements. This handbook serves as the benchmark for appropriate dyslexia services, and Crosby ISD is expected to adhere to its guidelines. If your child is identified as having dyslexia, they are entitled to structured literacy intervention, a research-based approach that focuses on explicit and systematic instruction in phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension.

Depending on the severity of your child's dyslexia and their specific needs, they may qualify for a 504 Plan or an Individualized Education Program (IEP). A 504 Plan provides accommodations and supports within the general education classroom, while an IEP provides more intensive special education services. It's essential to understand the differences between these two options and advocate for the level of support that is most appropriate for your child. If your child has not been screened for dyslexia and you have concerns about their reading skills, request a dyslexia screening in writing from Crosby ISD. Early identification and intervention are crucial for students with dyslexia to achieve reading success.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Crosby ISD

Discipline can be a challenging topic, especially when it involves a student with special needs. If your child with an IEP or 504 Plan is suspended from Crosby ISD for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year, this triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This is a critical process designed to determine whether the behavior that led to the suspension was caused by or directly related to your child's disability. It also constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA, meaning a more formal process is required to determine if your child's placement in Crosby ISD remains appropriate.

The MDR must be conducted in an ARD meeting, where the committee will review your child's IEP, evaluation data, and other relevant information to determine the relationship between their disability and the behavior. This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a special education student. You must act within days to ensure your child's rights are protected. Don't delay. If your child has been suspended and you believe their disability played a role in the behavior, immediately use our Discipline Defense Kit to understand your rights and prepare for the Manifestation Determination Review. Proactive action is essential to safeguard your child's access to education within Crosby ISD.

Filing a Complaint Against Crosby ISD

If you feel that Crosby ISD is not meeting its legal obligations to your child, or if you have exhausted all other avenues for resolving your concerns, you have several options for filing a formal complaint. These rights exist regardless of what staff at Crosby ISD may tell you; they are protected by law. Your first step can be to file a TEA State Complaint. This is a free process where TEA investigates your allegations and issues a written decision within 60 days. You can also pursue mediation through TEA, which is a free, voluntary process where a neutral mediator helps you and the district reach a mutually agreeable resolution.

If mediation is unsuccessful or not appropriate, you can file a Due Process Hearing request with TEA. This is a more formal legal proceeding where an Administrative Law Judge hears evidence and makes a binding decision. Finally, you can file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR), which investigates allegations of discrimination based on disability. Each of these escalation paths has its own specific procedures and timelines, so it's important to research your options carefully and seek legal advice if needed. Remember, you have the right to hold Crosby ISD accountable for providing your child with a Free Appropriate Public Education.

Frequently Asked Questions About Crosby ISD Special Education

The questions every Crosby ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Crosby ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Crosby ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Crosby ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Crosby ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Crosby ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Crosby ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Crosby ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Crosby ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
