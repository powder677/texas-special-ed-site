What can I do right now if Pflugerville ISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Pflugerville ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Pflugerville ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the special education system can feel overwhelming, but it's important to remember you are not alone. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas state law, every child with a disability is entitled to a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) designed to meet their unique needs. This means Pflugerville ISD is legally obligated to provide your child with specially designed instruction and related services to help them succeed in school. Your active participation and informed advocacy are crucial to ensuring your child receives the support they deserve.

Pflugerville ISD, like all school districts in Texas, operates under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA provides guidance and resources to districts, and Pflugerville ISD also benefits from the support of TEA Region 13 Education Service Center, which offers professional development, technical assistance, and other services to help educators effectively serve students with disabilities. This statewide network is designed to help ensure every child in Texas, including those in Pflugerville ISD, receives a quality education.

Throughout the entire special education process, from initial evaluation to the development and implementation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP), you have legally enforceable rights. These rights include the right to participate meaningfully in all meetings concerning your child, the right to access your child's educational records, and the right to challenge decisions made by Pflugerville ISD if you believe they are not in your child's best interest. Understanding these rights is the first step toward becoming an effective advocate for your child. The team at TexasSpecialEd.com is here to help you every step of the way.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Pflugerville ISD

The first step in accessing special education services for your child in Pflugerville ISD is to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). This evaluation is conducted by a team of professionals to determine if your child has a disability that qualifies them for special education and related services. It is absolutely crucial that you submit this request IN WRITING. A verbal request, while you might think it's enough, does not officially start the timeline required by law. Pflugerville ISD is legally obligated to respond to your written request.

Once Pflugerville ISD receives your written consent to evaluate your child, the district has 45 school days to complete the FIE. This is a strict timeline, and it's important to keep track of the dates. Remember that Child Find laws require Pflugerville ISD to proactively identify students who may need special education services, so if you suspect your child has a disability, don't wait for the district to come to you. You know your child best. Write a letter to the Special Education Director at Pflugerville ISD, clearly stating you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation for your child, [Child's Name], date of birth [Date of Birth], and attending [School Name]. Keep a copy of the letter for your records and send it via certified mail, return receipt requested, so you have proof of delivery. This documentation is essential if you ever need to demonstrate that you made a timely request.

ARD Meetings in Pflugerville ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal, and these meetings are the cornerstone of the special education process. An ARD committee meeting is where decisions are made about your child's eligibility for special education services, the goals in the Individualized Education Program (IEP), and the appropriate placement to implement the IEP. Pflugerville ISD is required to provide you with at least five calendar days' written notice before any ARD meeting, giving you time to prepare and gather your thoughts and documentation. This notice must include the purpose of the meeting, who will be attending, and your rights as a parent.

During the ARD meeting, remember that you are an equal member of the team. Your input and perspectives are invaluable. If, at any point during the meeting, you feel overwhelmed or need more time to consider the information presented, you have the right to request a 10-day recess. This gives you the opportunity to consult with outside experts, review documents, or simply take a break to process everything. It is a right provided to you by Texas law. Furthermore, you are never obligated to sign the IEP the same day it is presented. Take the document home, review it carefully, and sign it only when you are confident that it meets your child's needs. Pflugerville ISD personnel may pressure you to sign, but remember that is against the law to coerce parental signature.

You have a right to have all team members in attendance at the ARD table, so if you believe your child needs the expertise of a specific professional who is not invited, be sure to request their attendance ahead of time. Your voice matters, and your participation is essential to ensuring your child receives the best possible education within Pflugerville ISD. Before your next ARD meeting in Pflugerville ISD, download our free ARD preparation checklist from our website to ensure you are fully prepared to advocate for your child.

Dyslexia Services in Pflugerville ISD

Texas law mandates that Pflugerville ISD screen every student for dyslexia. This screening process is designed to identify students who may be at risk for reading difficulties related to dyslexia. If your child's screening indicates a potential risk, Pflugerville ISD is required to conduct further assessments to determine if your child meets the criteria for dyslexia. The determination is made by an evaluation team following the same procedures as any special education referral.

The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook sets the standards that Pflugerville ISD must follow in identifying and serving students with dyslexia. If your child is identified as having dyslexia, they are entitled to structured literacy intervention, which is a research-based approach to teaching reading that focuses on phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension. In some cases, students with dyslexia may also qualify for a 504 Plan or an IEP, depending on the severity of their needs and the impact of dyslexia on their educational performance. If your child has not been screened for dyslexia, or if you have concerns about their reading development, request in writing that Pflugerville ISD conduct a dyslexia screening immediately.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Pflugerville ISD

Discipline can be a particularly challenging area for students with disabilities. If your child has an IEP or 504 plan, they have additional protections under the law when it comes to disciplinary actions. Suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative days in Pflugerville ISD triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This review is conducted by the ARD committee to determine if the behavior that led to the suspension was caused by or directly related to your child's disability. This is a critical step to ensure that students are not being punished for behaviors that are beyond their control.

Suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative days also constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA, requiring an ARD meeting to determine how to proceed. This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a child with special needs. The timelines are often very short, and you must act quickly to protect your child's rights. If your child in Pflugerville ISD has been suspended, contact an advocate or attorney as soon as possible to understand your options and ensure that your child receives the appropriate support and services. If your child has been suspended, immediately use our Discipline Defense Kit to guide your actions.

Filing a Complaint Against Pflugerville ISD

If you believe that Pflugerville ISD is not fulfilling its legal obligations to your child, or if you disagree with decisions made by the district, you have several options for resolving the issue. It's important to understand these options and to choose the one that is most appropriate for your situation. Remember, you have these rights regardless of what any Pflugerville ISD staff member tells you.

The first, and often simplest, path is to file a TEA State Complaint. This is a free process, and TEA is required to investigate your complaint and issue a decision within 60 days. Mediation through TEA is another free option, and it involves a neutral third party facilitating a discussion between you and Pflugerville ISD to try to reach a mutually agreeable resolution. If these options are unsuccessful, you have the right to request a Due Process Hearing, which is a more formal legal proceeding. Finally, you can file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR), which investigates allegations of discrimination based on disability. Do not be afraid to assert your rights. Contact an advocate or attorney to determine which path is best for your situation.

Frequently Asked Questions About Pflugerville ISD Special Education

The questions every Pflugerville ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Pflugerville ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Pflugerville ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Pflugerville ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Pflugerville ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Pflugerville ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Pflugerville ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Pflugerville ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Pflugerville ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
