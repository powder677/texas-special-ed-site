About Special Education in Corpus Christi ISD

Corpus Christi ISD serves the Coastal Bend area, providing a range of special education services to students with disabilities. The district is committed to ensuring that all students have access to a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment. Corpus Christi ISD operates under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA) Region 2, adhering to all state and federal regulations regarding special education.

A key part of the special education process is the Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committee meeting. After a referral for special education services, the district has a timeline of 45 school days to complete a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Once the FIE is complete, parents will receive a written notice at least five school days prior to the scheduled ARD committee meeting. This allows parents time to review the evaluation results and prepare any questions or concerns.

Common Questions from Corpus Christi ISD Families

What if I disagree with the school's evaluation results?

You have the right to obtain an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at public expense if you disagree with the district's evaluation. You must request this IEE in writing. The district is required to either fund the IEE or initiate a due process hearing to demonstrate that their evaluation was appropriate.

My child's IEP isn't being followed. What should I do?

First, communicate directly with your child's teacher and the campus special education coordinator to understand the reason for the deviation. If the issue persists, document the instances where the IEP is not being implemented and contact the district's special education department for assistance in resolving the problem.

How can I get more involved in my child's ARD committee?

Actively participate in all ARD committee meetings by sharing your insights and concerns about your child's needs. Consider bringing documentation, such as outside evaluations or progress reports, to support your input. You are a valuable member of the team and your perspective is crucial.

Tip for Parents

Corpus Christi ISD is a large district with approximately 37,000 students. Navigating a large district can sometimes feel overwhelming. A practical tip is to always start with your child's campus special education coordinator. These individuals are dedicated to supporting special education students at their specific school and are often the best first point of contact before escalating concerns to the district office.
