Requesting an Evaluation in Rockwall ISD

⚡ Quick Answer

How long does Texas have to complete an evaluation?
Once a written evaluation request is received, the district must complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) and hold an ARD meeting within 45 school days. This timeline is strictly enforced by the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Verbal requests do not start the clock — always submit in writing and keep a copy.

Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in Rockwall ISD

How to Submit a Written Request

In Texas, a parent who suspects their child may have a disability is entitled to request a full and individual initial evaluation (FIE) to determine if the child is eligible for special education services. The first step in this process is submitting a written request to Rockwall ISD. This request should be addressed to the school principal or the Special Education Director. While you can verbally request an evaluation, a written request creates a clear record and ensures compliance with legal timelines.

Your written request should clearly state that you are requesting a full and individual initial evaluation for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas state law. Include your child's full name, date of birth, grade level, and the school they attend. Briefly describe your specific concerns about your child's academic, behavioral, or developmental progress. Be specific about what you are seeing at home and what teachers may be observing at school. For example, you might state, "My child is significantly behind in reading comprehension compared to their peers" or "My child has difficulty focusing and completing tasks, leading to frequent disruptions in the classroom." Providing supporting documentation, such as report cards, doctor's notes, or work samples, can strengthen your request.

It is important to retain a copy of your request for your records. Send the request via certified mail, return receipt requested, to ensure you have proof that the school received it. This documentation can be invaluable if disagreements arise later in the process.

The 45 School Day Timeline

Texas law sets a strict 45-school-day timeline for completing the FIE, starting from the date the school receives your written request. Within this timeframe, the school must determine whether to proceed with the evaluation. If they decide to evaluate, they must complete the evaluation, convene an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committee meeting to discuss the evaluation results, and determine if the child is eligible for special education services.

The 45-school-day clock pauses during official school breaks, such as summer vacation or extended holidays. Rockwall ISD is required to provide written notice of any pauses in the timeline. It is crucial to keep track of this timeline and communicate with the school if you notice any delays. If the evaluation is not completed within the allotted time, you have the right to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

What the FIE Must Cover

The FIE must be comprehensive and individual, meaning it must assess all areas related to the suspected disability. The evaluation should include a variety of assessments, such as academic testing, psychological testing, speech and language evaluation (if applicable), and observations of the child in their educational setting. The evaluation team should consist of qualified professionals, including educational diagnosticians, special education teachers, psychologists, and other specialists as needed. The team must consider information from multiple sources, including parent input, teacher observations, and the child's academic records.

The FIE should identify the child's strengths and weaknesses, determine if the child has a disability under IDEA and Texas state law, and identify the specific educational needs of the child. The report should clearly articulate how the disability impacts the child's ability to access the general education curriculum. It must provide recommendations for appropriate special education and related services to address the child's needs. Parents have the right to request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at public expense if they disagree with the school's evaluation. However, the school has the right to show that its evaluation was appropriate.

Child Find Obligations

Texas law mandates that school districts have a "Child Find" obligation. This means that Rockwall ISD has a legal responsibility to actively identify, locate, and evaluate all children within their jurisdiction who may have a disability and be in need of special education services, regardless of whether the child is enrolled in public school. This obligation extends to children who are homeless, wards of the state, or attending private schools.

You do not have to have definitive proof of a disability to request an evaluation. Simply having a reasonable suspicion that your child may have a disability is sufficient to trigger the school's Child Find obligation. If you believe your child may have a disability, do not hesitate to contact Rockwall ISD and request an evaluation. They are legally obligated to respond to your concerns.

What If They Refuse

If Rockwall ISD refuses to conduct an FIE, they must provide you with written notice explaining the reasons for their refusal. This notice must comply with specific legal requirements and explain your rights to challenge the school's decision. You have the right to request mediation or a due process hearing to challenge the school's decision. Mediation is a voluntary process where you and the school attempt to resolve the dispute with the assistance of a neutral third party. A due process hearing is a formal legal proceeding where an impartial hearing officer hears evidence and makes a decision on the matter. You have specific timelines for requesting mediation and due process, so it is crucial to act quickly if you disagree with the school's decision.

After the Evaluation

Following the completion of the FIE, an ARD committee meeting will be convened. This committee includes the parents, school administrators, teachers, evaluators, and other relevant personnel. The purpose of the ARD meeting is to review the evaluation results, determine if the child is eligible for special education services, and, if eligible, develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). The IEP is a legally binding document that outlines the child's specific educational needs, goals, and the special education and related services that will be provided to meet those needs. Parents are equal members of the ARD committee and have the right to actively participate in all decisions regarding their child's education. If you disagree with any aspect of the IEP, you have the right to request changes and appeal the ARD committee's decision.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Texas? +

Submit a written request directly to the school's principal or special education director. The written request starts the mandatory 45 school-day clock. Keep a dated copy for your records.

What is a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in Texas? +

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment the school must complete to determine if your child has a disability and needs special education services. It must cover all areas of suspected disability and be completed within 45 school days of your written consent.

Can a Texas school refuse to evaluate my child? +

Yes, but only if the district provides written notice (Prior Written Notice) explaining why they are refusing and what data they relied on. You have the right to challenge that refusal through mediation or a due process hearing.
