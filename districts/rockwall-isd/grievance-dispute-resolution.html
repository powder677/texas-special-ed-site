The 10-Day Suspension Rule

Rockwall ISD can suspend your child for up to 3 school days for disciplinary reasons. However, they can accumulate up to 10 days of suspension throughout the school year. If your child's suspensions are nearing 10 days, or if a single suspension will push them over that limit, this triggers significant rights, ESPECIALLY IF YOUR CHILD HAS AN IEP OR 504 PLAN. Monitor this closely! Keep a detailed record of every suspension, including dates, reasons, and duration.

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

THIS IS EXTREMELY IMPORTANT IF YOUR CHILD HAS AN IEP OR 504 PLAN. An MDR is a meeting to determine if your child's misconduct was a manifestation of their disability. In other words, did their disability cause them to act the way they did? Or, did the school fail to properly implement the IEP or 504 plan?

An MDR *MUST* be held if your child faces a suspension exceeding 10 cumulative days in a school year, or a proposed Change of Placement (see below). The school is responsible for convening the MDR meeting. Demand it! Immediately request the date and time. Prepare! Gather all relevant documentation: IEP/504 plan, disciplinary records, teacher observations, medical reports. Consult with a special education advocate or attorney immediately. Don't go into this meeting unprepared. If the behavior *was* a manifestation, your child CANNOT be disciplined in the same way as a student without a disability. The IEP/504 team must then develop a functional behavioral assessment (FBA) and/or a behavior intervention plan (BIP) or revise the existing one.

Change of Placement

A Change of Placement occurs when a student is removed from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days, or when a series of removals creates a pattern of removals totaling more than 10 school days in a year. This can include placement in a Disciplinary Alternative Education Program (DAEP) or expulsion. If your child faces a Change of Placement, understand that this is a SERIOUS escalation.

Changes of Placement trigger the MDR process discussed above if your child has an IEP or 504 plan. Fight ANY Change of Placement if it seems unfair or discriminatory. Review the IEP or 504 to ensure the proposed placement meets your child's needs. Consider independent educational evaluations (IEEs) to bolster your case.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe Rockwall ISD has violated your child's rights, file a Level 1 grievance. This is the first step in the formal grievance process. Obtain the grievance form from the Rockwall ISD website or the school principal. Carefully document the specific violation, dates, times, witnesses, and the relief you are seeking (e.g., removal of the suspension, reinstatement to the regular classroom). Submit the completed grievance form to the appropriate campus administrator (usually the principal). Time is of the essence. There are strict deadlines for filing grievances, so act quickly! Keep a copy of everything you submit.

TEA State Complaint

If the Level 1 grievance doesn't resolve the issue, or if you believe Rockwall ISD is violating the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). The TEA complaint process is more complex and requires specific documentation. Consult with a special education advocate or attorney for assistance. This is a powerful tool to hold the district accountable.

Due Process

Under IDEA, you have the right to request a due process hearing if you disagree with the school district's decisions regarding your child's special education services. This is a formal legal proceeding where you can present evidence and challenge the district's actions. Due process hearings are complex and require legal representation. Contact a special education attorney immediately if you are considering this option.

IMPORTANT: Document everything. Consult with legal counsel or a special education advocate immediately if you feel your child's rights are being violated. Don't let the school intimidate you. Your child's education and future are at stake.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
