Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing

Your request for a SPRINGTOWN ISD evaluation request must be submitted in writing to start the formal process. A verbal request to a teacher or administrator is not sufficient under Texas Education Code and IDEA requirements.

What to include in your written request:

  • Your child's full name, date of birth, and grade level
  • Specific concerns about your child's learning, behavior, or development
  • Examples of difficulties you've observed at home
  • Any relevant medical or developmental history
  • Request for a full individual evaluation
  • Your contact information (phone and email)

Send your request via email or certified mail to the SPRINGTOWN ISD special education department. Keep a copy for your records with the date sent. Email provides the best documentation that SPRINGTOWN ISD received your request on a specific date.

Step 2: SPRINGTOWN ISD Reviews Your Request

Once SPRINGTOWN ISD receives your written request, the district has specific timeframes to respond. The special education coordinator in SPRINGTOWN ISD will acknowledge your request and explain the next steps.

SPRINGTOWN ISD may propose conducting Response to Intervention (RTI) or other assessment strategies before a full FIE. However, you have the right to request a full individual evaluation directly. If you explicitly ask for an FIE evaluation in SPRINGTOWN ISD, the district cannot delay by requiring RTI first.

Step 3: Obtain Parent Consent

SPRINGTOWN ISD must obtain your written consent before beginning the evaluation. This consent form explains:

  • What assessments will be conducted
  • Why each assessment is necessary
  • Who will conduct the evaluations
  • Timeline for completing the SPRINGTOWN ISD FIE evaluation
  • Your rights during the process

Important: You can refuse to consent to the evaluation, but SPRINGTOWN ISD can pursue an due process hearing if they believe your child needs evaluation. You can also withdraw consent at any time during the SPRINGTOWN ISD special education evaluation.

Understanding the 45-Day Timeline for SPRINGTOWN ISD FIE Evaluations

The SPRINGTOWN ISD 45 day timeline is a critical deadline established by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). Understanding this timeline helps you track progress and ensure SPRINGTOWN ISD complies with federal requirements.

How the 45-Day Clock Works

The 45-day timeline starts when you provide written consent for the evaluation. SPRINGTOWN ISD must complete all assessments, compile findings, and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting within this SPRINGTOWN ISD 45 day timeline.

What happens during these 45 days in SPRINGTOWN ISD:

  1. Days 1-3: SPRINGTOWN ISD acknowledges your request and sends consent form
  2. Days 4-10: You return signed consent; SPRINGTOWN ISD assigns evaluators
  3. Days 11-40: Assessments occur (cognitive, academic, behavioral, social-emotional, speech/language, occupational therapy if needed)
  4. Days 41-45: SPRINGTOWN ISD compiles report and schedules ARD meeting to discuss results

The ARD meeting must occur before the 45 days expire. This is where SPRINGTOWN ISD presents findings and determines eligibility for special education services.

What If SPRINGTOWN ISD Misses the 45-Day Deadline?

If SPRINGTOWN ISD fails to complete the evaluation within 45 days, this is a procedural violation. Document the delay in writing and contact the district's special education director. You may also file a due process complaint or contact the Texas Education Agency (TEA) to file a state complaint.

What Assessments Are Included in a SPRINGTOWN ISD Special Education Evaluation

A comprehensive SPRINGTOWN ISD special education evaluation includes multiple assessment types to build a complete picture of your child's abilities and needs.

Cognitive Assessment

A school psychologist administers a standardized intelligence test to measure your child's reasoning, problem-solving, and processing abilities. This assessment helps identify intellectual disabilities or giftedness with learning differences.

Academic Achievement Testing

Specialized assessments measure your child's reading, mathematics, writing, and oral language skills. SPRINGTOWN ISD compares results to grade-level expectations to identify specific academic gaps.

Behavioral and Social-Emotional Assessment

Teachers and parents complete rating scales, and a school counselor or psychologist may conduct interviews. These assessments identify emotional disturbance, anxiety, or behavioral disorders that affect learning.

Speech and Language Evaluation

If speech/language concerns are noted, a speech-language pathologist (SLP) evaluates receptive language, expressive language, articulation, and voice. This is crucial for SPRINGTOWN ISD students with communication difficulties.

Occupational and Physical Therapy Assessments

If physical coordination, fine motor skills, or sensory processing concerns exist, SPRINGTOWN ISD may include occupational therapy (OT) or physical therapy (PT) evaluations.

Classroom Observations and Work Samples

SPRINGTOWN ISD evaluators observe your child in classroom settings and review work samples to see how your child functions in academic and social environments.

Your Rights During the SPRINGTOWN ISD Evaluation Process

As a parent in SPRINGTOWN ISD, you have important rights during the special education evaluation process. Understanding these rights ensures you can effectively advocate for your child.

Right to Participate and Ask Questions

You have the right to participate in all ARD meetings discussing your child's evaluation. You can ask questions about assessment methods, results, and recommendations. SPRINGTOWN ISD must provide information in your native language if English is not your primary language.

Right to Obtain Independent Evaluations

If you disagree with SPRINGTOWN ISD's evaluation results, you can obtain an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at SPRINGTOWN ISD's expense if you request it before or after an ARD meeting. SPRINGTOWN ISD may challenge the reasonableness of your IEE request through due process.

Right to Review Records

You can review all evaluation reports, test scores, and documents SPRINGTOWN ISD uses to make eligibility decisions. Request records at least five business days before the ARD meeting so you have time to review before discussing results.

Right to Bring Support

You can bring an advocate, attorney, family member, or trusted friend to ARD meetings in SPRINGTOWN ISD. Having support helps you process information and advocate effectively.

After the SPRINGTOWN ISD FIE Evaluation: What Happens Next

The ARD Meeting

The Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting is where SPRINGTOWN ISD presents evaluation findings and determines eligibility. The ARD committee includes you, the school psychologist, special education teacher, general education teacher, and an administrator.

SPRINGTOWN ISD must determine whether your child:

  • Has a qualifying disability under IDEA or Texas Education Code
  • Needs special education services to benefit from general education
  • Should receive services in the least restrictive environment

If Your Child Qualifies

SPRINGTOWN ISD develops an Individualized Education Program (IEP) outlining services, accommodations, modifications, and goals. Your child begins receiving special education services based on the IEP.

If Your Child Doesn't Qualify

SPRINGTOWN ISD must provide written notice explaining why your child doesn't meet eligibility criteria. You can request an independent evaluation or pursue due process if you disagree. Some students may qualify for accommodations under Section 504 even if they don't qualify for special education under IDEA.
