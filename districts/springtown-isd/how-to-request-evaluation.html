Federal and state law guarantees specific rights to all parents in SPRINGTOWN ISD. Understanding these parental rights in SPRINGTOWN ISD will empower you during the evaluation process.

Right to Request an Evaluation

Any parent in SPRINGTOWN ISD can formally request a special education evaluation at any time. You do not need teacher permission or to wait for the school to suggest it. SPRINGTOWN ISD must respond to your request within two business weeks with an evaluation proposal.

Right to a Comprehensive and Fair Evaluation

SPRINGTOWN ISD must evaluate your child in all areas of concern using multiple methods and instruments. The evaluation must be in your child's native language and must not be discriminatory. This means the evaluation must consider your family's culture and language.

Right to Participate in All Decisions

You have the right to be present at all important meetings in SPRINGTOWN ISD. This includes the special evaluation meeting in SPRINGTOWN ISD where results are discussed. SPRINGTOWN ISD must send you written notices in your language before any evaluation or change in services.

Right to Obtain a Second Opinion

If you disagree with SPRINGTOWN ISD's evaluation results, you may request an independent educational evaluation at SPRINGTOWN ISD's expense under certain circumstances. You may also obtain a private evaluation at your own cost.

How to Request an Evaluation in SPRINGTOWN ISD

The process for requesting an evaluation in SPRINGTOWN ISD is straightforward. Here are the specific steps you should follow:

Step 1: Contact the SPRINGTOWN ISD Special Education Director

Contact the SPRINGTOWN ISD special education office. You can find the phone number and address on the SPRINGTOWN ISD website or by calling your child's school. Ask to speak with someone who can assist you with the evaluation process.

Step 2: Make a Formal Written Request

Although you can make a verbal request, it is best to do so in writing. Send a letter to the SPRINGTOWN ISD director requesting a special education evaluation. Include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • The school they attend in SPRINGTOWN ISD
  • Specific concerns you have about your child's learning or development
  • Your name and contact information
  • Specific examples of difficulties you have observed

Keep a copy of your request and consider delivering the letter in person to receive an acknowledgment of receipt from SPRINGTOWN ISD.

Step 3: Await SPRINGTOWN ISD's Evaluation Proposal

SPRINGTOWN ISD has two business weeks to respond to your request. The school will send you a consent form outlining what will be evaluated and who will conduct the evaluation. Read this document carefully and ask questions if you do not understand anything.

Step 4: Provide Signed Consent

You must sign the consent form before SPRINGTOWN ISD can begin the evaluation. Without your signature, the school cannot legally proceed. If you have questions about the proposed tests, it is entirely appropriate to ask SPRINGTOWN ISD to clarify what will be evaluated.

The SPRINGTOWN ISD Evaluation Process

After you give your consent, SPRINGTOWN ISD will conduct a complete special evaluation in SPRINGTOWN ISD. This process typically includes several components:

Academic Evaluations

SPRINGTOWN ISD specialists will evaluate your child's reading, writing, math, and other academic skills. This may include standardized tests, classroom observations, and analysis of student work.

Psychological Evaluations

A SPRINGTOWN ISD school psychologist may assess your child's cognitive abilities, processing, memory, and reasoning. They may also evaluate emotional and behavioral factors affecting learning.

Speech and Language Evaluations

If there are communication concerns, SPRINGTOWN ISD may include an evaluation by a speech-language pathologist. This assesses expression, comprehension, pronunciation, and other aspects of language.

Medical or Physical Evaluations

Depending on the concerns, SPRINGTOWN ISD may seek medical information or recommend physical evaluations to rule out vision, hearing, or other health issues.

Educational and Developmental History

SPRINGTOWN ISD evaluators will review your child's school records, grades, attendance, and medical history. They may also interview teachers and school staff about your child's performance and behavior.

The entire evaluation process in SPRINGTOWN ISD must be completed within 60 calendar days from the date of your written consent.

The Evaluation Results Meeting in SPRINGTOWN ISD

After completing all tests, SPRINGTOWN ISD will schedule a FIE in SPRINGTOWN ISD (Full Individual Evaluation Eligibility Determination Meeting). FIE stands for "Full Individual Evaluation."

What to Expect at the Meeting

During this critical meeting in SPRINGTOWN ISD, the evaluation team will present the results of all tests. They will explain what they found, how it affects your child's learning, and their recommendations. You have the right to ask questions and express your concerns.

Eligibility Decision

The SPRINGTOWN ISD team will determine if your child qualifies for special education services under one of the disability categories defined under IDEA. In Texas, these categories include autism, visual impairment, deafness, emotional disturbance, intellectual disability, multiple disabilities, traumatic brain injury, orthopedic impairment, and others.

Next Steps

If your child qualifies, SPRINGTOWN ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). If they do not qualify, the SPRINGTOWN ISD team will discuss other support options that may be available.
