Understanding Special Education Services in SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD

SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD special education programs serve students ages 3-21 who have disabilities affecting their educational performance. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code, the district must provide a free, appropriate public education (FAPE) to all eligible students.

Our special education services include:

  • Speech-language pathology services
  • Occupational and physical therapy
  • Specialized classroom instruction (resource, self-contained, and separate day programs)
  • Behavioral support services and counseling
  • Assistive technology services
  • Vision and hearing services
  • Dyslexia screening and intervention
  • Transition planning for students ages 14-21

SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD families can access services at multiple campuses across our service area, with trained special education staff committed to supporting each student's individual needs.

How to Refer Your Child for Special Education Evaluation

If you believe your child needs special education services, the first step is requesting an evaluation through SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD special education. You have the right to request an evaluation at any time, and the district has specific timelines it must follow.

Step-by-Step Referral Process

  1. Submit a written referral to your campus special education coordinator or the district Special Education Department. You can request a referral verbally, but written documentation is important.
  2. District reviews your request within 15 calendar days. SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD will notify you whether the district agrees an evaluation is warranted.
  3. Receive evaluation timeline — the district will complete the evaluation within 60 calendar days and schedule an SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD ARD meeting to discuss results.
  4. Participate in ARD meeting where the team determines eligibility and develops an Individualized Education Program (IEP) if your child qualifies.

Important Evaluation Timelines

Texas law and IDEA require SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD to complete evaluations within 60 calendar days of receiving your written request. The district cannot delay this timeline without your written consent. If your child is suspected of having dyslexia, SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD dyslexia program assessment may be included as part of the comprehensive evaluation.

Understanding ARD Meetings and IEP Development

The SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD ARD meeting is where educational decisions about your child are made. ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal, and this meeting is your opportunity to collaborate with school staff to create an appropriate education plan.

Who Attends the ARD Meeting

Your SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD ARD meeting must include:

  • You (the parent or guardian)
  • Your child (when appropriate)
  • Special education teacher
  • General education teacher
  • District representative who can make service decisions
  • Evaluation specialist or diagnostician
  • Other professionals as needed

What Happens During the Meeting

At your SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD ARD meeting, the team will review evaluation results, discuss your child's strengths and needs, and determine whether your child qualifies for special education. If eligible, you'll develop an SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD IEP services plan that outlines:

  • Your child's present levels of academic and functional performance
  • Annual IEP goals and measurable objectives
  • Specific services your child will receive (frequency and duration)
  • Accommodations and modifications in the general curriculum
  • Participation in state and district assessments
  • Transition planning (for students 14 and older)

You have the right to bring an advocate or attorney to your SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD ARD meeting. Many parents find an advocate helpful in understanding complex educational jargon and ensuring their child's needs are adequately addressed.

Parent Rights and Special Education Due Process

Federal and Texas law provide strong protections for parents in special education. Understanding your SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD parent rights ensures you can advocate effectively for your child.

Your Core Rights

SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD parent rights include:

  • Prior written notice — the district must notify you in writing before proposing or refusing to change your child's identification, evaluation, or placement
  • Informed consent — you must consent to initial evaluation and initial IEP services
  • Access to records — you can review all educational records within 10 school days
  • Independent evaluation — you can request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at district expense if you disagree with the school's evaluation
  • ARD meeting participation — you have equal say in all educational decisions
  • Due process hearing — you can request a due process hearing to resolve disputes about your child's education
  • Mediation — you can request mediation to resolve disagreements without going to a hearing

If You Disagree with the School's Decisions

Parents in SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD have options if they believe the district is not providing appropriate services. Before pursuing formal complaint procedures, consider requesting a meeting with your campus principal or the Special Education Director to discuss your concerns. Many issues can be resolved through collaborative problem-solving.

If informal resolution doesn't work, you can file a due process complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA) or request mediation. A special education attorney near SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO can help you understand your options and represent you in formal proceedings. Additionally, a special ed advocate SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO can provide valuable support in understanding special education law and advocating for your child's needs.

Dyslexia Services and Intervention Programs

Texas law requires all school districts to screen students for dyslexia and provide specialized intervention. SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD dyslexia program offers evidence-based interventions for students who struggle with reading due to dyslexia or other reading-related disabilities.

How Dyslexia Screening Works

SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD dyslexia program includes universal screening in kindergarten and first grade, plus screening for any student who shows signs of reading difficulty. If your child is identified as at-risk for dyslexia, the district will:

  • Notify you within 30 calendar days
  • Provide intervention services through the Response to Intervention (RTI) process
  • Monitor progress with frequent assessments
  • Conduct a comprehensive evaluation if dyslexia is suspected

Students identified with dyslexia through SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD dyslexia program receive specialized instruction from a certified dyslexia therapist or reading specialist. Intervention is intensive, systematic, and based on your child's specific reading profile.

IEP Services and Accommodations for Academic Success

SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD IEP services are tailored to your child's unique needs and may include specialized instruction, related services, and accommodations in the general education setting.

Types of Services

Depending on your child's needs, SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD IEP services may include:

  • Resource instruction — pull-out support in reading, math, or other academic areas
  • Self-contained classroom placement — instruction in a specialized classroom with smaller student-teacher ratios
  • Related services — speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, or other support services
  • Classroom accommodations — extended time on tests, modified assignments, preferential seating, or use of assistive technology
  • Behavioral supports — positive behavior intervention and support (PBIS) strategies tailored to your child

Monitoring Progress

Throughout the school year, SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD teachers monitor your child's progress toward IEP goals. You should receive progress reports at least as often as the district reports progress for non-disabled students. If your child is not making adequate progress, the ARD team can meet to revise the IEP and adjust services.

Transition Planning for Students Ages 14+

For students with IEPs in grades 9-12, SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD special education provides transition planning to prepare for life after high school. Transition planning focuses on post-secondary education, employment, independent living, and community participation.

What's Included in Transition Planning

Starting at age 14, your SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD ARD meeting must address transition services including:

  • Career exploration and vocational assessment
  • Work-based learning experiences and internships
  • Academic coursework aligned with post-secondary goals
  • Life skills training (budgeting, independent living, self-advocacy)
  • Connection to community resources and adult services
  • Assistive technology evaluation for adult use
  • Planning for postsecondary education or employment

SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD families should actively participate in transition planning to ensure the IEP reflects your child's strengths, interests, and post-secondary goals.

Frequently Asked Questions About SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD Special Education

How long does the evaluation process take?

SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD must complete evaluations within 60 calendar days. If you request an evaluation in December or near the end of the school year, timelines may extend into the next school year with your written consent.

Can I request a second opinion?

Yes. If you disagree with SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD special education evaluation results, you can request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at district expense. The district must provide you with information about obtaining an independent evaluation.

What if my child doesn't qualify for special education?

If your child doesn't meet disability criteria but has learning difficulties, SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD may provide support through Response to Intervention (RTI), Section 504 plans, or general education accommodations. Discuss these options with your campus principal or special education coordinator.

Can I attend every ARD meeting?

Absolutely. You have the legal right to attend every SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD ARD meeting and participate in all decisions about your child's special education. The district must schedule meetings at times convenient for you when possible.
