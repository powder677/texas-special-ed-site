How long does Texas have to complete an evaluation?
Once a written evaluation request is received, the district must complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) and hold an ARD meeting within 45 school days. This timeline is strictly enforced by the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Verbal requests do not start the clock — always submit in writing and keep a copy.

Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in San Marcos CISD

This guide provides information for parents or legal guardians who believe their child may have a disability and requires special education services within the San Marcos Consolidated Independent School District (CISD) in Texas. Understanding your rights and the process is crucial to advocating for your child's educational needs.

How to Submit a Written Request

The first step in initiating the special education process is to submit a written request for a Full and Individual Evaluation (FIE). This request should be addressed to the school principal, the special education director, or the ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee chair at your child's school. While verbal requests are sometimes made, a written request creates a documented record and helps ensure the timeline begins properly. Your request should clearly state that you are requesting a special education evaluation for your child due to concerns about their academic or behavioral performance.

In your written request, be specific about your concerns. Include examples of your child's difficulties, such as specific areas of academic struggle (reading, writing, math), behavioral issues impacting their learning environment, or social-emotional challenges. Providing documentation like report cards, teacher communication, or medical records that support your concerns can be helpful. Be sure to include your child's full name, date of birth, grade level, and the school they attend, along with your contact information (phone number and email address). Keep a copy of your request for your records. Consider sending the request via certified mail with return receipt requested for proof of delivery.

The 45 School Day Timeline

Texas law mandates a specific timeline for the special education evaluation process. Once the school receives your written request, they have 15 school days to either provide you with prior written notice of their decision to conduct an evaluation or provide prior written notice of their decision not to conduct an evaluation. If the school decides to evaluate, they must obtain your written consent for the evaluation to proceed.

If you consent to the evaluation, the school then has 45 school days (excluding legal holidays and summer break) to complete the evaluation and hold an ARD committee meeting to discuss the results. This timeline is crucial, and it's important to track the dates to ensure the school district adheres to these legal requirements. If the 45-day timeline is not met, you have the right to request mediation or file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

What the FIE Must Cover

The Full and Individual Evaluation (FIE) must be comprehensive and assess all areas related to the suspected disability. This often includes academic testing, cognitive assessments (IQ testing), speech and language evaluations, occupational therapy evaluations, and psychological evaluations. The specific areas assessed will depend on the nature of your concerns and the school's professional judgment.

The evaluation team must use a variety of assessment tools and strategies, including standardized tests, observations of your child in the classroom, and interviews with you and your child. The evaluation must be conducted in your child's native language or other mode of communication, unless it is clearly not feasible to do so. The results of the FIE will be used to determine if your child has a disability that qualifies them for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas state law.

Child Find Obligations

San Marcos CISD, like all school districts, has a legal obligation under Child Find to actively identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities residing within its boundaries who are in need of special education and related services, regardless of whether they are enrolled in public school, private school, or are homeschooled. If you suspect your child has a disability, do not hesitate to contact the district, even if they are not currently enrolled in a San Marcos CISD school.

What If They Refuse

If the school district refuses to conduct an evaluation, they must provide you with prior written notice explaining the reasons for their refusal. This notice must include a description of other options considered and the basis for their decision. You have the right to disagree with the school's decision and pursue options such as requesting mediation, filing a formal complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA), or requesting a due process hearing. You should document all communication with the school district and consult with an advocate or attorney specializing in special education law.

After the Evaluation

After the FIE is complete, an ARD committee meeting will be scheduled to discuss the results. The ARD committee includes you (the parent), school administrators, teachers, and evaluation personnel. At the ARD meeting, the committee will determine if your child meets the criteria for special education services. If your child is found eligible, the ARD committee will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) tailored to your child's specific needs. The IEP will outline specific goals, accommodations, modifications, and related services (such as speech therapy, occupational therapy, or counseling) designed to help your child succeed in their education. You have the right to participate fully in the ARD process and advocate for your child's needs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Texas? +

Submit a written request directly to the school's principal or special education director. The written request starts the mandatory 45 school-day clock. Keep a dated copy for your records.

What is a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in Texas? +

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment the school must complete to determine if your child has a disability and needs special education services. It must cover all areas of suspected disability and be completed within 45 school days of your written consent.

Can a Texas school refuse to evaluate my child? +

Yes, but only if the district provides written notice (Prior Written Notice) explaining why they are refusing and what data they relied on. You have the right to challenge that refusal through mediation or a due process hearing.
