How to Effectively Contact San Marcos CISD Special Education Staff

Building strong communication with your child's special education team is crucial. For San Marcos CISD, consider these tips:

  • Best Times: Early morning or late afternoon are generally good times to reach teachers, before and after instructional time. Campus administrators are usually available throughout the school day.
  • Email vs. Phone: For detailed questions or to share documents (IEP, evaluations), email is preferable. For quick updates or urgent matters, a phone call might be better.
  • First Contact: In your initial message, clearly state your child's name, school, grade, and the specific topic you wish to discuss. Be polite and concise. For example: "Dear [Teacher's Name], I'm writing to discuss [Child's Name]'s progress in reading and schedule a brief meeting to review strategies we can use at home."

Important Tip: Always follow up any phone conversations with an email summarizing the discussion and any agreed-upon actions. This creates a written record.

What to Do If You Can't Reach Anyone

If you're having trouble getting a response from San Marcos CISD special education staff, follow these steps:

  • Step 1: Contact the teacher directly (email and phone). Allow 2 business days for a response.
  • Step 2: If no response, contact the campus Special Education Coordinator. Their contact information should be available on the school's website or by calling the front office.
  • Step 3: If you still don't receive a response, contact the San Marcos CISD Director of Special Education at the district office.
  • Step 4: As a last resort, you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Refer to Texas Education Code Chapter 29 for information regarding special education laws and your rights.

Your Right to District Records

As a parent, you have legal rights to access your child's educational records and certain district information.

  • FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act): You have the right to request and inspect your child's educational records within 45 days of your written request. This includes IEPs, evaluations, and progress reports.
  • Public Information Act (Texas Government Code, Chapter 552): You can request information about San Marcos CISD operations, including staff contact information. However, personally identifying information may be redacted.

To submit a records request, send a written request (email or letter) to the San Marcos CISD Special Education Department. Clearly state the specific records you are requesting and your relationship to the student. Include your contact information for follow-up.
