What can I do right now if San Marcos CISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. San Marcos CISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. Special Education in San Marcos CISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the world of special education can feel overwhelming, but you're not alone. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas law, your child has the right to a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) designed to meet their unique needs. Special education encompasses specially designed instruction and related services, like speech therapy, occupational therapy, or counseling, provided to eligible students with disabilities. The goal is to help your child access the general education curriculum and make progress in school. San Marcos CISD is committed to fulfilling this obligation for every student within its boundaries.

San Marcos CISD operates under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA provides guidance and resources to ensure districts throughout the state, including San Marcos CISD, comply with federal and state special education laws. In addition to TEA, San Marcos CISD receives support from TEA Region 13 Education Service Center, which offers professional development and technical assistance to educators. This multi-layered support system is designed to help San Marcos CISD provide the best possible services to students with disabilities.

It's crucial to remember that you, as a parent, have enforceable legal rights throughout the entire special education process. From the initial evaluation to the development and implementation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP), you are a vital member of the team. You have the right to participate in all decisions affecting your child's education, to access your child's educational records, and to seek redress if you believe your child's rights have been violated. Understand that San Marcos CISD must respect these rights at every turn.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in San Marcos CISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability and require special education services, the first step is to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) from San Marcos CISD. It's absolutely critical that this request be submitted in writing. A verbal request, while you may be told it's sufficient, does not trigger the legal timelines mandated by IDEA and Texas law. A written request ensures that your concerns are formally documented and that the evaluation process begins promptly.

Once San Marcos CISD receives your written consent to evaluate, they have 45 school days to complete the FIE. This evaluation will assess your child's strengths and weaknesses in various areas, such as academic achievement, cognitive abilities, and social-emotional functioning. Furthermore, remember that "Child Find" laws require San Marcos CISD to proactively identify students who may need special education services, even if you haven't made a formal request. Don't hesitate to express your concerns to your child's teacher or principal, as they may already be aware of potential issues. If you haven't already requested an evaluation and have concerns about your child's learning or development, write a letter to the principal of your child's school requesting a Full Individual Evaluation. Keep a copy for your records and send the letter via certified mail with return receipt requested to confirm it was received by San Marcos CISD. The clock starts ticking from that moment forward.

ARD Meetings in San Marcos CISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal, and an ARD meeting is the cornerstone of the special education process. This is where decisions about your child's eligibility for special education, their IEP, and their educational placement are made. San Marcos CISD is legally obligated to provide you with at least five calendar days' written notice before any ARD meeting, allowing you time to prepare and gather any relevant information. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, the date, time, location, and who will be attending. Don't hesitate to contact the school if you need to reschedule the meeting to a time that better suits your availability. Remember, you are an essential member of the ARD committee.

During the ARD meeting, you have the right to participate fully in all discussions and decisions. If at any point you feel overwhelmed or need more time to consider the information presented, you can request a 10-day recess. This allows you to take a break, consult with an advocate or attorney, and return to the meeting with a clearer understanding of the proposed IEP. Furthermore, you are not required to sign the IEP on the same day as the ARD meeting. Take your time to review the document carefully and ensure that it accurately reflects your child's needs and goals. Don't be pressured into signing something you're not comfortable with. The ARD committee must reconvene if you have edits or changes to the IEP. San Marcos CISD is expected to fully consider your input.

Your voice matters in the ARD process. You know your child best, and your insights are invaluable to the team. Do not be afraid to speak up, ask questions, and advocate for your child's needs. Before your next ARD meeting in San Marcos CISD, download our free ARD preparation checklist. This checklist will help you organize your thoughts, gather relevant information, and ensure that you are fully prepared to participate effectively in the meeting.

Dyslexia Services in San Marcos CISD

Texas law mandates that San Marcos CISD screen every student for dyslexia. This screening is typically conducted in early elementary school, but if you have concerns about your child's reading skills at any grade level, you have the right to request an evaluation. Dyslexia is a specific learning disability that affects reading accuracy, fluency, and comprehension. Early identification and intervention are crucial for students with dyslexia to develop the necessary skills to succeed in reading.

The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook sets the standards that San Marcos CISD must follow in identifying and supporting students with dyslexia. This handbook provides guidance on screening procedures, evidence-based interventions, and accommodations. Students identified with dyslexia are entitled to structured literacy intervention, which is a systematic and explicit approach to teaching reading skills. Depending on the severity of their dyslexia and their individual needs, students may also qualify for a 504 Plan or an IEP, which can provide additional support and accommodations. If your child has not been screened for dyslexia and you have concerns about their reading abilities, request a dyslexia screening in writing from San Marcos CISD today. Put your request in writing; keep a copy for your records, and send it certified mail.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in San Marcos CISD

Disciplinary actions can be particularly complex for students with disabilities. It's essential to understand your child's rights if they face suspension or other disciplinary measures in San Marcos CISD. If a student with a disability is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year, it triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This review is a crucial process to determine whether the student's misconduct was a manifestation of their disability or a result of the school's failure to implement the IEP. A suspension for more than 10 days also constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA, which requires an ARD meeting to discuss the appropriateness of the student's current educational setting and services.

The Manifestation Determination Review is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a special education student. The MDR must be conducted within a specified timeframe after the suspension. This means you need to act quickly to ensure your child's rights are protected. Do not delay! If your child has been suspended, use our Discipline Defense Kit immediately. This kit provides essential information and resources to help you navigate the disciplinary process and advocate for your child's rights within San Marcos CISD.

Filing a Complaint Against San Marcos CISD

While we hope that you can resolve any issues you have with San Marcos CISD through collaboration and communication, it's important to know your options if you feel your child's rights have been violated. There are several escalation paths available to you, regardless of what district personnel may tell you. These are your rights under federal and Texas law. First, you can file a TEA State Complaint, which is a free process that TEA is required to resolve within 60 days. Second, you can request Mediation through TEA, which is also free and provides a neutral third party to help facilitate a resolution. Third, you have the right to request a Due Process Hearing, which is a more formal legal proceeding before an Administrative Law Judge. Finally, you can file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR) if you believe San Marcos CISD has discriminated against your child based on their disability.

Frequently Asked Questions About San Marcos CISD Special Education

The questions every San Marcos CISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in San Marcos CISD? +

Submit a written request to the San Marcos CISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in San Marcos CISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does San Marcos CISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires San Marcos CISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how San Marcos CISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if San Marcos CISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. San Marcos CISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
