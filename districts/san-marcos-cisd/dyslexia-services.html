Dyslexia Services in San Marcos CISD

Texas Dyslexia Handbook 2024

San Marcos CISD (SMCISD) adheres to the guidelines outlined in the latest version of the Texas Dyslexia Handbook. This handbook, published by the Texas Education Agency (TEA), provides comprehensive information about dyslexia identification, intervention, and support services for students in Texas public schools. It is crucial for parents and educators to familiarize themselves with this document. Key updates for 2024 may include revised screening procedures, updated intervention methodologies, and clarified definitions of dyslexia-related terms. Parents are encouraged to access the most current version of the Texas Dyslexia Handbook on the TEA website to stay informed about the district's practices and their child's rights. SMCISD staff receives ongoing professional development to ensure alignment with the latest guidance and best practices.

Mandatory Screening

SMCISD conducts mandatory dyslexia screening for all students in kindergarten and first grade. This screening is designed to identify students who may be at risk for dyslexia. The screening process typically involves assessments of phonological awareness, letter-sound knowledge, rapid naming, and other foundational literacy skills. The specific screening tools used are selected based on their validity and reliability in identifying students with characteristics of dyslexia. If a student's screening results indicate a potential risk for dyslexia, further evaluation and intervention may be recommended. Parents will be notified of the screening results and provided with information about next steps if intervention is suggested. Ongoing monitoring of literacy development continues throughout the elementary grades to identify students who may develop difficulties later on.

Structured Literacy Programs

SMCISD utilizes structured literacy programs to provide targeted intervention for students identified with dyslexia or at risk for dyslexia. Structured literacy is an evidence-based approach to reading instruction that emphasizes systematic and explicit teaching of phonological awareness, phonics, morphology, syntax, and semantics. These programs are implemented by trained teachers and specialists using research-based strategies. Intervention groups are typically small to allow for individualized attention and differentiated instruction. The specific structured literacy program(s) employed by SMCISD may vary depending on the campus and grade level, but all programs align with the principles of structured literacy and the requirements outlined in the Texas Dyslexia Handbook. Progress monitoring is conducted regularly to track student growth and adjust instruction as needed.

504 vs IEP for Dyslexia

Students with dyslexia may be eligible for support services through a 504 plan or an Individualized Education Program (IEP). A 504 plan provides accommodations and modifications to ensure that students with disabilities have equal access to education. An IEP is a more comprehensive plan developed for students with disabilities who require specialized instruction and related services. The determination of whether a student requires a 504 plan or an IEP depends on the severity of their learning difficulties and the level of support needed. If a student's dyslexia significantly impacts their ability to access the general education curriculum and requires specialized instruction beyond accommodations, an IEP may be warranted. The ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee, which includes parents, teachers, and other relevant professionals, makes the final decision regarding eligibility for special education services.

Accommodations List

Common accommodations for students with dyslexia in SMCISD may include:

  • Extended time on tests and assignments
  • Use of assistive technology (e.g., text-to-speech software, speech-to-text software)
  • Audiobooks or recorded materials
  • Preferential seating
  • Modified assignments or reduced workload
  • Access to graphic organizers and other visual aids
  • Multi-sensory teaching strategies
  • Frequent breaks
  • Oral administration of tests
This list is not exhaustive, and accommodations are individualized based on the student's specific needs and documented in their 504 plan or IEP. Parents are encouraged to collaborate with the school team to determine the most appropriate accommodations for their child.

How to Request Evaluation

If you suspect your child may have dyslexia, you should contact your child's teacher or the school principal to express your concerns. You can request a formal evaluation for dyslexia by submitting a written request to the school. The school will then initiate the evaluation process, which may include assessments of reading, spelling, writing, and other relevant skills. Parental consent is required before any evaluation can be conducted. The evaluation results will be reviewed by the school team, and a determination will be made regarding eligibility for dyslexia services. Parents have the right to participate in all stages of the evaluation and intervention process. If you have any questions or concerns, please contact the Special Education Department at San Marcos CISD.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

Is my child entitled to dyslexia testing in Texas? +

Yes. Texas law requires all districts to screen students for dyslexia and related disorders in Kindergarten and First Grade, and at any grade level if a parent or teacher requests it.

What accommodations can a dyslexic child get in Texas? +

Accommodations may include extended time on tests, text-to-speech software, oral administration of tests, reduced-answer-choice formats, and use of a calculator. These can be provided through a 504 Plan or an IEP.

What is the Texas Dyslexia Handbook? +

The Texas Dyslexia Handbook is a state guidance document updated in 2024 that outlines districts' responsibilities for identifying, evaluating, and serving students with dyslexia using structured literacy methods.
