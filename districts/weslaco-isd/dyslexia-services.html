Dyslexia Services in Weslaco ISD

Dyslexia Services in Weslaco ISD

Weslaco ISD is committed to providing comprehensive services to students with dyslexia, ensuring they receive the support necessary to achieve their full potential. This guide outlines the services available and the process for accessing them, aligned with Texas state law.

Texas Dyslexia Handbook 2024

Weslaco ISD adheres to the guidelines outlined in the Texas Dyslexia Handbook (2024 edition), published by the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This handbook serves as the primary resource for understanding dyslexia, identifying students who may have dyslexia, and providing appropriate instruction and interventions. Parents and educators are encouraged to familiarize themselves with this document, available on the TEA website. The handbook provides updated information on assessment, instruction, and best practices for supporting students with dyslexia in Texas schools. Key areas addressed include characteristics of dyslexia, screening procedures, instructional strategies, and legal requirements.

Mandatory Screening

Weslaco ISD conducts mandatory dyslexia screening for all students in kindergarten, first grade, and second grade. This screening is designed to identify students who may be at risk for dyslexia. The screening process typically involves assessments of phonological awareness, rapid automatized naming, and other literacy-related skills. Parents will be notified if their child's screening results indicate a potential risk for dyslexia. In addition to these mandated screenings, teachers are trained to recognize the characteristics of dyslexia in students of all ages and may refer students for further evaluation at any grade level if concerns arise.

Structured Literacy Programs

Weslaco ISD utilizes structured literacy programs that are evidence-based and aligned with the requirements of the Texas Dyslexia Handbook. These programs provide explicit, systematic, and multi-sensory instruction in foundational reading skills. Structured literacy addresses phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and reading comprehension. Instruction is individualized and tailored to meet the specific needs of each student. Examples of structured literacy approaches that may be implemented include Orton-Gillingham based programs or similar programs meeting the Texas requirements.

504 vs IEP for Dyslexia

Students with dyslexia may receive support through a Section 504 plan or an Individualized Education Program (IEP), depending on their individual needs.

  • Section 504 Plan: A 504 plan provides accommodations to students with disabilities to ensure they have equal access to the general education curriculum. It is appropriate for students whose dyslexia primarily impacts their ability to learn in a general education setting.
  • IEP (Individualized Education Program): An IEP is a legally binding document developed for students with disabilities who require specialized instruction and related services. It is appropriate for students whose dyslexia significantly impacts their academic performance and requires more intensive intervention than accommodations alone can provide. The IEP outlines specific goals, objectives, and services designed to address the student's individual needs.
The determination of whether a student requires a 504 plan or an IEP is made on a case-by-case basis by a team of educators and parents.

Accommodations List

Weslaco ISD provides a range of accommodations to support students with dyslexia. Examples of accommodations that may be implemented include, but are not limited to:

  • Extended time on tests and assignments
  • Use of assistive technology, such as text-to-speech software
  • Preferential seating
  • Modified assignments
  • Oral administration of tests
  • Use of graphic organizers
  • Access to notes or outlines
  • Frequent breaks
The specific accommodations provided are determined based on the student's individual needs and documented in their 504 plan or IEP.

How to Request Evaluation

If you have concerns that your child may have dyslexia, you should contact your child's teacher or the school principal. A written request for an evaluation should be submitted to the school principal. The request should outline your concerns and any observations you have made regarding your child's reading and writing skills. Upon receiving the request, the school will initiate the evaluation process, which may include assessments of reading, writing, spelling, and other relevant skills. Parents will be actively involved in the evaluation process and will receive a copy of the evaluation results. The school will then convene a committee to determine if the student is eligible for dyslexia services.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

Is my child entitled to dyslexia testing in Texas? +

Yes. Texas law requires all districts to screen students for dyslexia and related disorders in Kindergarten and First Grade, and at any grade level if a parent or teacher requests it.

What accommodations can a dyslexic child get in Texas? +

Accommodations may include extended time on tests, text-to-speech software, oral administration of tests, reduced-answer-choice formats, and use of a calculator. These can be provided through a 504 Plan or an IEP.

What is the Texas Dyslexia Handbook? +

The Texas Dyslexia Handbook is a state guidance document updated in 2024 that outlines districts' responsibilities for identifying, evaluating, and serving students with dyslexia using structured literacy methods.
