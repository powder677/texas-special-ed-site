Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

Discipline Rights & Grievances in Weslaco ISD - URGENT

If your child is facing disciplinary action in Weslaco ISD, especially if they have special needs, you need to act NOW. This guide provides critical information about your rights and how to protect your child. Time is of the essence. Read carefully and take action.

The 10-Day Suspension Rule

Weslaco ISD, like all Texas school districts, has limits on how long your child can be suspended. Generally, a student cannot be suspended for more than three school days for any single offense unless a mandatory removal applies under the Texas Education Code. Cumulative suspensions accumulating to more than ten school days in a school year trigger additional protections, especially for students with disabilities under IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act). TRACK EVERY SUSPENSION DAY CAREFULLY! If your child's total suspensions are approaching ten days, pay very close attention to the sections below, particularly Change of Placement and the Manifestation Determination Review (MDR).

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

An MDR is a meeting to determine if your child's misbehavior was caused by, or directly and substantially related to, their disability. It also investigates if the misbehavior was a direct result of the school district's failure to implement your child's IEP (Individualized Education Program). IF YOUR CHILD HAS AN IEP AND IS FACING SUSPENSION OF MORE THAN 10 DAYS OR A CHANGE OF PLACEMENT, DEMAND AN MDR IMMEDIATELY! The MDR must be conducted within 10 school days of the decision to change the student's placement due to a violation of the student code of conduct. Insist that all relevant members of the IEP team, including you, are present. Document everything discussed and ask for a copy of the MDR results in writing. A favorable MDR finding means your child CANNOT be disciplined in the same way as a non-disabled student for that behavior. The IEP team must conduct a functional behavioral assessment (FBA) and develop a behavior intervention plan (BIP), unless they are already in place. Review and modify the BIP as needed.

Change of Placement

A "Change of Placement" occurs when a student with a disability is: removed from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days; or subjected to a series of removals that constitute a pattern because they total more than 10 school days in a school year AND the student's behavior is substantially similar to the student's behavior in previous incidents that resulted in the series of removals; AND additional factors such as the length of each removal, the total amount of time the student is removed, and the proximity of the removals to one another. This could mean being sent to a Disciplinary Alternative Education Program (DAEP) or being expelled. ANY CHANGE OF PLACEMENT TRIGGER THE NEED FOR AN MDR. If Weslaco ISD attempts to change your child's placement without an MDR, you MUST immediately file a complaint (see sections below).

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe Weslaco ISD is violating your child's rights, start with a Level 1 grievance. This is the first step in the formal grievance process. Obtain the grievance form from the district (usually available on their website or at the administration building). Clearly and concisely state the facts of the situation, the specific policy or law you believe was violated (cite the Texas Education Code, IDEA, etc.), and the remedy you are seeking (e.g., reinstatement, reversal of suspension, MDR meeting). Submit the grievance WITHIN the timeframe specified by Weslaco ISD policy (usually within 15 days of the incident). Keep a copy of the grievance and all supporting documentation. Level 1 is typically submitted to the campus principal. Expect a written response within the timeframe outlined in the district policy.

TEA State Complaint

If the Level 1 grievance doesn't resolve the issue, or if you believe Weslaco ISD is systemically violating special education laws, you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Go to the TEA website (tea.texas.gov) and search for "Special Education Complaints." The process is outlined there. A TEA complaint is a formal accusation of non-compliance with state and federal law. You must provide specific details, documentation, and evidence of the alleged violations. TEA will investigate and issue a decision. This can lead to corrective action plans for the district. This option should be considered if you have received a negative MDR outcome or the district is refusing to conduct one.

Due Process

For significant disagreements regarding your child's special education needs (including discipline issues related to their disability), you have the right to request Due Process. This is a formal legal process involving a hearing before an administrative law judge. Due Process is a serious undertaking and you should strongly consider seeking legal representation from an attorney specializing in special education law. Information on Due Process is available on the TEA website. Filing for Due Process initiates a timeline with specific deadlines, so act quickly.

DISCLAIMER: This guide provides general information and should not be considered legal advice. Consult with an attorney specializing in special education law for specific guidance regarding your child's situation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
