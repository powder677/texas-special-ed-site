What can I do right now if Weslaco ISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Weslaco ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Weslaco ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the special education system can feel overwhelming, but you're not alone. Special education, as defined by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas law, ensures that children with disabilities receive a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) designed to meet their unique needs. This includes specially designed instruction, related services (like speech therapy or occupational therapy), and any necessary accommodations to help your child succeed in school. Remember that these supports are not privileges, but legally protected rights.

Weslaco ISD, like all school districts in Texas, operates under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA provides guidance, resources, and monitors compliance with special education laws. Weslaco ISD also receives direct support from the Education Service Center Region 1, which offers professional development for teachers and assistance to families. This means that Weslaco ISD is accountable for providing a quality special education program, and TEA and Region 1 are there to support them and ensure they are meeting the needs of students with disabilities.

Crucially, as a parent, you have enforceable legal rights at every stage of the special education process in Weslaco ISD. These rights include the right to participate meaningfully in all decisions about your child's education, the right to access your child's educational records, the right to request evaluations, and the right to challenge decisions you disagree with. Remember, the law is on your side. Begin documenting everything. Right now, create a dedicated file (physical or digital) and start collecting every email, letter, and document related to your child's special education journey in Weslaco ISD.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Weslaco ISD

If you suspect your child has a disability that is impacting their ability to learn, you have the right to request a special education evaluation from Weslaco ISD. This is the first critical step in determining if your child is eligible for special education services. To initiate the process, you must submit a written request to the school principal or the district's special education director. It's important to understand that a verbal request does not start the timeline for the evaluation. The request needs to be in writing to trigger the district's legal obligation to act.

Once Weslaco ISD receives your written request and obtains your written consent for evaluation, the district has 45 school days to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). This evaluation is a comprehensive assessment of your child's strengths and weaknesses, designed to determine if they have a disability and, if so, what specific educational needs they have. Keep in mind also that Child Find laws mandate Weslaco ISD to proactively identify students who may need special education services, even if you haven't made a formal request. However, it's always best to be proactive and advocate for your child. Your voice matters.

Your next step is crucial: Draft a written request for a special education evaluation for your child. Include your child's name, date of birth, school, and a brief description of your concerns. Make a copy of the letter for your records. Then, send the original letter to the school principal via certified mail with a return receipt requested. This ensures you have proof that Weslaco ISD received your request and started the 45-day clock.

ARD Meetings in Weslaco ISD: Your Rights at the Table

The ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee is a group of individuals, including you as the parent, who meet to make decisions about your child's special education program. ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal. This committee develops, reviews, and revises your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP). Weslaco ISD is required to provide you with at least five calendar days' written notice before any ARD meeting. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, the date, time, location, and a list of who will be attending. Review this notice carefully and make sure you understand why the meeting is being held.

During the ARD meeting in Weslaco ISD, you have the right to actively participate and express your opinions and concerns. The ARD committee must consider your input when making decisions about your child's education. If at any point during the meeting you feel overwhelmed or need more time to consider information, you can request a 10-day recess. This gives you time to gather your thoughts, consult with experts, or simply process the information presented. This 10-day recess is a powerful tool that you are entitled to use, so don't hesitate to ask for it.

Finally, remember that you do not have to sign the IEP the same day as the ARD meeting in Weslaco ISD. You have the right to take the IEP home, review it carefully, and seek advice from others before signing. Your signature indicates that you agree with the IEP as written. If you disagree with any part of the IEP, you have the right to refuse to sign it and to pursue other options, such as mediation or a due process hearing. Your agreement to the IEP is important, so take your time to consider the plan for your child's special education services in Weslaco ISD.

Before your next ARD meeting in Weslaco ISD, download our free ARD preparation checklist. This checklist will help you organize your thoughts, gather important information, and ensure that you are fully prepared to advocate for your child's needs.

Dyslexia Services in Weslaco ISD

Texas law mandates that Weslaco ISD screen all students for dyslexia, typically in kindergarten and first grade. This screening is designed to identify students who may be at risk for developing dyslexia and to provide them with early intervention services. It's essential to confirm with your child's teacher or school administration that this screening has been done for your child. If you have concerns about your child's reading abilities, don't wait for the formal screening process. Your insights as a parent are invaluable.

The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook sets the standards that Weslaco ISD must follow in identifying and supporting students with dyslexia. This handbook outlines the criteria for identifying dyslexia, the types of instructional interventions that must be provided, and the qualifications of teachers who provide these interventions. Students identified with dyslexia are entitled to structured literacy intervention, which is a research-based approach that focuses on phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension. Some students with dyslexia may also qualify for a 504 Plan or an IEP, depending on the severity of their needs.

If your child has not been screened for dyslexia and you have concerns about their reading abilities, request a dyslexia screening in writing from Weslaco ISD immediately. Reference the Texas Dyslexia Handbook in your request and emphasize your concerns about your child's reading progress.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Weslaco ISD

When a special education student in Weslaco ISD is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days in a school year, this triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). An MDR is a process to determine if the student's misconduct was a manifestation of their disability. In other words, the ARD committee must determine if the behavior that led to the suspension was caused by or directly related to the child's disability. This also constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA, requiring a formal review of the student's IEP and placement.

This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a special education student in Weslaco ISD. You must act quickly. An ARD meeting must be convened within a matter of days to conduct the Manifestation Determination Review. If the committee determines that the behavior was a manifestation of the disability, the school cannot suspend the student. Instead, the ARD committee must review and revise the IEP to address the underlying causes of the behavior. This situation requires immediate action and a deep understanding of your child's rights. Do not hesitate to contact an advocate or attorney for guidance.

If your child has been suspended from Weslaco ISD, immediately use the Discipline Defense Kit to understand your rights, prepare for the MDR meeting, and advocate for your child's needs.

Filing a Complaint Against Weslaco ISD

If you believe that Weslaco ISD has violated your child's special education rights, you have several options for filing a complaint. It's important to remember that these rights exist regardless of what any Weslaco ISD staff member may tell you. The first option is to file a State Complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This is a free process, and TEA is required to investigate and resolve the complaint within 60 days. Another option is to request Mediation through TEA. Mediation is also free and involves a neutral third party who helps you and Weslaco ISD try to reach an agreement.

If mediation is unsuccessful, or if you prefer, you can request a Due Process Hearing. This is a more formal legal process where an administrative law judge hears evidence and makes a decision about your child's rights. Due process hearings can be costly and time-consuming, so it's often helpful to seek legal representation. Finally, you can file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR) if you believe that Weslaco ISD has discriminated against your child based on their disability. Each of these pathways provides a way to hold Weslaco ISD accountable and ensure that your child receives the special education services they are entitled to.

Review each of these escalation options and determine which is the best first step to take given your circumstances and your desired outcome. Document everything to support your position.

Frequently Asked Questions About Weslaco ISD Special Education

The questions every Weslaco ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Weslaco ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Weslaco ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Weslaco ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Weslaco ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Weslaco ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Weslaco ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Weslaco ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Weslaco ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
