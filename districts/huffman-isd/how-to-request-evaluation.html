Any parent, guardian, or legal custodian can request a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation) for their child in HUFFMAN ISD. You do not need to wait for the school to suggest an evaluation; you have the right to request one at any time.

Teachers, school counselors, and other professionals in HUFFMAN ISD can also refer a student for evaluation. However, parent requests carry equal weight and must be seriously considered by the district.

Parent Rights in HUFFMAN ISD

Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and the Texas Education Code, parents of students in HUFFMAN ISD have specific rights:

  • Right to request a full evaluation at no cost
  • Right to an evaluation within 60 calendar days from the request
  • Right to be part of the evaluation team
  • Right to receive results in your language
  • Right to a second opinion if you disagree

Step 1: Prepare Your Written Request

The most effective way to request an evaluation in HUFFMAN ISD is to make the request in writing. While a verbal request must be considered, a written letter creates an official record of the process.

Your request should include:

  • Student's full name and date of birth
  • Current grade in HUFFMAN ISD
  • Specific description of academic or behavioral concerns
  • Concrete examples of problematic situations
  • Your full name, email, and phone number
  • Date of the request

Step 2: Submit the Request to the Special Education Department

In HUFFMAN ISD, your request should be addressed to the Special Education Department or the Director of Special Education. You can submit the request:

  • In person at the HUFFMAN ISD central office
  • By certified mail
  • By email to the special education department's address
  • Through the online portal if HUFFMAN ISD offers one

Important recommendation: Send your request in a way that allows you to confirm its receipt. Keep a copy for your records.

Step 3: Understand HUFFMAN ISD's Timeline

Once you submit your request in HUFFMAN ISD, the district has 60 calendar days to complete the evaluation. This period begins on the first school day after they receive your request.

Within these 60 days, HUFFMAN ISD must:

  1. Conduct evaluations in all areas of concern
  2. Analyze the collected information
  3. Convene an ARD meeting (evaluation team meeting)
  4. Present you with the results and recommendations

Evaluation Areas in HUFFMAN ISD

HUFFMAN ISD must evaluate in all areas where difficulties are suspected. Special education evaluations typically include:

Academic Evaluation

HUFFMAN ISD specialists will evaluate reading, writing, mathematics, and other academic areas. They may use standardized tests, work portfolios, and classroom observations.

Developmental Evaluation

For younger students in HUFFMAN ISD, motor, language, and social-emotional development may be evaluated.

Language Evaluation

If English is not your child's native language, HUFFMAN ISD must evaluate in both Spanish and English. This is crucial to distinguish between learning difficulties and language limitations.

Psychological Evaluation

A school psychologist from HUFFMAN ISD can conduct tests for intelligence, cognitive processing, and social-emotional functioning.

Medical Evaluation

In some cases, HUFFMAN ISD may recommend hearing, vision, or medical evaluations related to the assessment.

Preparing for the HUFFMAN ISD ARD Meeting

After completing the evaluations in HUFFMAN ISD, an ARD (Admission, Review, Dismissal) meeting will be held. This is your opportunity to understand the results and decide on special education services.

What to Bring to the HUFFMAN ISD ARD Meeting

Prepare for your meeting in HUFFMAN ISD by bringing:

  • Examples of student work
  • Notes or reports from previous evaluations
  • Observations about the child's behavior at home
  • Noted questions you wish to ask
  • An attorney or special education advocate (if desired)

Your Rights During the ARD Meeting in HUFFMAN ISD

In HUFFMAN ISD, during your ARD meeting you have the right to:

  • Fully understand the evaluation results
  • Ask questions and request clarifications
  • Agree or disagree with the results
  • Request additional evaluations
  • Propose changes to the service plan
  • Have someone support you at the meeting

If You Disagree with HUFFMAN ISD's Results

If you disagree with the results of the special education evaluation in HUFFMAN ISD, you have options available.

Request for Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE)

You can request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at HUFFMAN ISD's expense. The district must provide you with information on where to obtain this evaluation and what costs they will cover.

Mediation and Dispute Resolution

HUFFMAN ISD must offer free mediation if there are disagreements about the evaluation or services. This is a confidential process where a neutral mediator helps resolve differences.

Due Process Hearing

If not resolved through mediation, you can request a due process hearing. An impartial official will review your child's case in HUFFMAN ISD.

Frequently Asked Questions about Evaluation in HUFFMAN ISD

How much does the evaluation cost in HUFFMAN ISD?

The evaluation is completely free for parents in HUFFMAN ISD. The district is required by law to pay all evaluation costs.

Can my evaluation request be denied in HUFFMAN ISD?

HUFFMAN ISD cannot deny a parent's evaluation request. If the district refuses, you can request mediation or a due process hearing.

What happens if HUFFMAN ISD does not meet the 60-day deadline?

If HUFFMAN ISD does not complete the evaluation within 60 days, you can file a formal complaint. This is important to ensure your child receives timely evaluation.

Can I bring someone with me to HUFFMAN ISD meetings?

Yes. You can bring a family member, friend, or a professional such as a special education attorney or special education advocate to any meeting related to the evaluation in HUFFMAN ISD.

Next Steps After the Evaluation in HUFFMAN ISD

If the evaluation determines that your child qualifies for special education in HUFFMAN ISD, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be developed. This plan details the specific services, supports, and educational goals for your child.

If your child does not qualify for special education services according to HUFFMAN ISD, but you believe they need support, you can explore other options such as Section 504 or compensatory education services.
