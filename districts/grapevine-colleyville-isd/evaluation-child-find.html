Requesting an Evaluation in Grapevine-Colleyville ISD

⚡ Quick Answer

How long does Texas have to complete an evaluation?
Once a written evaluation request is received, the district must complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) and hold an ARD meeting within 45 school days. This timeline is strictly enforced by the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Verbal requests do not start the clock — always submit in writing and keep a copy.

Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in Grapevine-Colleyville ISD

If you believe your child may have a disability and requires special education services in Grapevine-Colleyville ISD (GCISD), you have the right to request a full and individual evaluation. This guide outlines the process, timeline, and your rights under Texas law, primarily the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code Chapter 29.

How to Submit a Written Request

The first step is to submit a written request for an evaluation to the GCISD. This request should be addressed to the principal of your child's school, the Special Education Director for GCISD, or your child's teacher. While a specific form is not legally required, a written request provides a clear record of your actions and triggers the district's legal obligations. The request should clearly state that you are requesting a full and individual evaluation for special education services. Include the following information:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth.
  • Your child's grade level and current school.
  • A detailed description of your specific concerns about your child's academic, behavioral, or developmental progress. Be specific as possible. Instead of "difficulty in reading," write "struggles to decode words, comprehends very little of what is read aloud, and scores below grade level on reading assessments."
  • Any previous interventions or supports your child has received.
  • Any relevant medical or psychological diagnoses.
  • Your contact information, including your phone number and email address.
  • The date of your request.

Keep a copy of the request for your records. Certified mail with return receipt requested is recommended to ensure proof of delivery.

The 45 School Day Timeline

Texas law mandates a strict timeline for the special education evaluation process. Once GCISD receives your written request, they have 15 school days to provide you with prior written notice regarding whether or not the district intends to conduct an evaluation. If they decide to evaluate, they must provide you with the required notice and consent forms. You must provide written consent before the evaluation can begin.

From the date GCISD receives your signed consent, they have 45 school days to complete the evaluation and ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee meeting. This timeline is defined in Texas Administrative Code (TAC) §89.1011. The 45 school day timeline excludes days between student's last day of spring instruction and the first day of fall instruction.

What the FIE Must Cover

The Full and Individual Evaluation (FIE) must be comprehensive and address all areas of suspected disability. It should include a variety of assessment tools and strategies, including:

  • Observations of your child in their classroom environment (and other relevant settings).
  • Review of your child's academic records and progress reports.
  • Input from you, as the parent, regarding your child's strengths, weaknesses, and needs.
  • Standardized testing in areas such as academic achievement, cognitive abilities, language skills, and social-emotional functioning.
  • Assessments relevant to specific areas of concern, such as speech-language pathology, occupational therapy, or psychological evaluations.

The FIE must be conducted by qualified professionals. Under Texas law, these professionals must hold appropriate certifications and licenses.

Child Find Obligations

GCISD has a legal obligation under IDEA and Texas Education Code to actively seek out and identify children with disabilities within its boundaries, regardless of whether the parents make a referral. This is known as "Child Find." This obligation requires the district to have procedures in place to identify, locate, and evaluate children who may need special education services, including those who are not yet enrolled in public school.

If you suspect your child has a disability, do not hesitate to contact GCISD, even if your child is not currently attending a GCISD school. The Child Find obligation ensures that all children with disabilities have access to appropriate educational services.

What If They Refuse

If GCISD refuses to conduct an evaluation, they must provide you with written notice explaining the reasons for their refusal. This notice must be understandable and explain your procedural safeguards. You have the right to challenge this decision through various dispute resolution options, including:

  • Mediation: A voluntary process where a neutral third party helps you and the school district reach an agreement.
  • Due Process Hearing: A formal legal proceeding before an impartial hearing officer.
  • Filing a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

It is highly recommended that you seek legal counsel or advocacy support if GCISD refuses to evaluate your child. Organizations like Disability Rights Texas can provide valuable assistance.

After the Evaluation

After the evaluation is complete, an ARD committee meeting will be scheduled to discuss the results and determine if your child is eligible for special education services. The ARD committee includes you (as the parent), school administrators, teachers, and evaluation personnel. If your child is deemed eligible, the ARD committee will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) tailored to meet your child's specific needs. You are an integral part of this process and have the right to actively participate in all decisions regarding your child's education. Always remember to advocate for what you believe is best for your child.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Texas? +

Submit a written request directly to the school's principal or special education director. The written request starts the mandatory 45 school-day clock. Keep a dated copy for your records.

What is a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in Texas? +

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment the school must complete to determine if your child has a disability and needs special education services. It must cover all areas of suspected disability and be completed within 45 school days of your written consent.

Can a Texas school refuse to evaluate my child? +

Yes, but only if the district provides written notice (Prior Written Notice) explaining why they are refusing and what data they relied on. You have the right to challenge that refusal through mediation or a due process hearing.
