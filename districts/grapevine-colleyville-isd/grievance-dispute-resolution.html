Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

The 10-Day Suspension Rule

GCISD, like all Texas school districts, can suspend your child for up to 10 school days for disciplinary reasons. It's crucial to track these suspensions. Cumulative suspensions exceeding 10 days in a single school year trigger crucial protections, especially if your child has an IEP (Individualized Education Program) or a Section 504 plan. PAY ATTENTION TO THE CUMULATIVE NUMBER OF SUSPENSION DAYS! Any behavior incident potentially leading to suspension should be carefully documented.

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

An MDR (Manifestation Determination Review) is a critical process under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). If your child with an IEP is facing a Change of Placement (see below) due to disciplinary reasons, the school MUST conduct an MDR. This meeting determines if your child's behavior was a manifestation of their disability or if it was a direct result of the school's failure to implement the IEP.

Crucially, the burden of proof is on the SCHOOL. They must demonstrate the behavior was NOT related to the disability or a failure to implement the IEP. Gather any documentation (medical records, IEP data, teacher communications) that supports a link between the behavior and the disability or IEP shortcomings.

If the behavior IS determined to be a manifestation, the IEP team must conduct a Functional Behavioral Assessment (FBA), implement a Behavior Intervention Plan (BIP), or revise an existing one. Your child typically returns to their current placement unless you and the school agree to a change.

Change of Placement

A Change of Placement occurs when your child is: (1) removed from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days; or (2) subjected to a series of removals that constitute a pattern (e.g., multiple short-term suspensions totaling more than 10 days) because of substantially similar behavior. The school district CANNOT unilaterally change your child's placement beyond 10 days if your child has an IEP without an MDR.

IMMEDIATELY object in writing if you believe the school is attempting to create a de facto change of placement without following the proper procedures, especially if your child has an IEP. Keep copies of everything!

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

GCISD likely has a grievance policy outlined on their website or in the student handbook. A Level 1 Grievance is typically the first step in formally challenging a disciplinary decision. You will likely need to submit a written grievance to the principal or other designated school official. ACT QUICKLY. There are strict deadlines for filing a grievance. Document everything: dates, times, conversations, and the specifics of the disciplinary action you are contesting.

In your grievance, clearly state: what happened, why you believe the disciplinary action was unfair or violated your child's rights, and what outcome you are seeking (e.g., removal of the suspension, modification of the disciplinary action). Provide any supporting documentation (witness statements, medical records, IEP data).

TEA State Complaint

If the school district fails to adequately address your concerns through the grievance process, or if you believe they are violating IDEA or other special education laws, you can file a State Complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA investigates these complaints. The TEA website has the necessary forms and instructions. Again, deadlines apply. Gather all documentation and be prepared to clearly articulate the specific violations you believe have occurred.

Due Process

Under IDEA, you have the right to Due Process if you disagree with the school district's actions regarding your child's special education services or disciplinary actions. This is a more formal legal process that can involve mediation, administrative hearings, and potentially court appeals. Filing for Due Process triggers specific timelines and legal procedures. **Consult with an attorney immediately if you are considering filing for Due Process.**

REMEMBER: Document everything. Request everything in writing. Keep meticulous records. Seek legal advice. Don't let the school district steamroll you. Your child's education and well-being are at stake.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
