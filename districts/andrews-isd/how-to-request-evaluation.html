Children in ANDREWS ISD may face learning challenges for many different reasons. A FIE (Full Individual Evaluation) in ANDREWS ISD can identify if your child has a disability affecting their education and if they qualify for special education services.

A formal evaluation through ANDREWS ISD is the first step to obtaining an Individualized Education Program (IEP) or a 504 Plan. Without this evaluation, your child cannot receive special education services protected under federal law.

Signs Your Child Might Need an Evaluation

  • Consistent difficulties learning to read, write, or do math
  • Behavioral problems that do not improve with regular discipline
  • Difficulty communicating or understanding instructions
  • Developmental delays compared to peers their age
  • Attention or concentration problems in class
  • Low academic performance despite effort and support
  • Social or interaction difficulties with other children

Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, parents in ANDREWS ISD have clear and specific rights. Understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Parent Rights in ANDREWS ISD for Special Education

  • Request an evaluation: You can ask ANDREWS ISD to evaluate your child at no cost
  • Participation in decisions: You must be included in all meetings regarding evaluation and services in ANDREWS ISD
  • Rights information: ANDREWS ISD must provide you with a notice of procedural safeguards in your language
  • Second opinion: You have the right to an independent evaluation paid for by ANDREWS ISD under certain circumstances
  • Interpreter or translator: ANDREWS ISD must provide interpretation at the evaluation meeting if necessary
  • Review records: You can review all of your child's educational records in ANDREWS ISD
  • Legal support: You can bring a special education attorney or advocate to ANDREWS ISD meetings

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in ANDREWS ISD

The process for requesting an evaluation in ANDREWS ISD is relatively straightforward, but it's important to do it correctly. Be sure to document each step to protect your child's rights.

Step 1: Contact Your Child's School

Start by contacting the school your child attends in ANDREWS ISD. You can speak with:

  • Your child's teacher
  • The school counselor
  • The school principal
  • The special education department at ANDREWS ISD

Explain your specific concerns about your child's learning or behavior. Be clear and detailed about what you have observed at home.

Step 2: Make a Formal Written Request

This is the most important part of the process. Although you can verbally request an evaluation, ANDREWS ISD must respond differently to a written request. Send a letter or email to your child's school requesting a formal evaluation.

Your request should include:

  1. Your full name and the student's name
  2. The student's date and grade level in ANDREWS ISD
  3. Specific description of academic or behavioral concerns
  4. The words: "I am requesting an evaluation under IDEA to determine if my child is eligible for special education"
  5. Your phone number and email address
  6. Date of the request

Important: Send this request by certified mail or deliver it in person to ANDREWS ISD, requesting a receipt. Keep a copy for your records.

Step 3: Await ANDREWS ISD's Response

By federal law, ANDREWS ISD has 15 calendar days to respond to your written request. The school must provide you with:

  • Confirmation that they received your request
  • Explanation of the next steps
  • A copy of your procedural safeguards in Spanish
  • Information about available services

Step 4: Informed Consent

ANDREWS ISD must obtain your written consent before conducting any evaluations. This means you must sign a consent form that explains what evaluations will be conducted and why.

Read this form carefully. You can ask questions about any evaluation proposed by ANDREWS ISD. You do not have to sign anything you do not understand or agree with.

The Evaluation Process in ANDREWS ISD

A comprehensive special education evaluation in ANDREWS ISD generally takes 45 calendar days from the time you sign consent. The ANDREWS ISD team will evaluate your child in several areas.

Typical Evaluation Areas

  • Cognitive: Intellectual ability and reasoning
  • Academic: Reading, writing, math
  • Psychosocial: Emotions, behavior, social interactions
  • Motor: Physical skills and coordination
  • Communication: Language, speech, hearing
  • Medical: Relevant health history

During the process, ANDREWS ISD may also:

  • Review previous academic and behavioral records
  • Conduct classroom observations
  • Speak with current and former teachers
  • Administer formal psychoeducational tests
  • Evaluate medications the student may be taking

The Eligibility Determination Meeting in ANDREWS ISD

After completing the evaluations, ANDREWS ISD schedules an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committee meeting. This is where the evaluation results in ANDREWS ISD are discussed.

What to Expect at the ARD Meeting

The ANDREWS ISD meeting will include several participants:

  • You (the parents)
  • An ANDREWS ISD representative with decision-making authority
  • Your child's general education teacher
  • The special education teacher
  • The evaluation specialist who conducted the tests
  • An interpreter if needed

In ANDREWS ISD, the team will review:

  1. The results of each evaluation conducted
  2. Whether your child has a disability under IDEA
  3. Whether the disability negatively affects your child's education
  4. Whether your child needs special education to succeed

If the ANDREWS ISD team determines that your child is eligible, they will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). If not eligible, you have the right to request an independent evaluation at ANDREWS ISD's expense.

Next Steps After the Evaluation in ANDREWS ISD

If your child is eligible for special education in ANDREWS ISD, the next step is the development of the IEP. This document is crucial and protects your child's rights.

Developing the Individualized Education Program (IEP)

The ANDREWS ISD team will work with you to create an IEP that includes:

  • Description of your child's strengths
  • Areas where support is needed
  • Specific and measurable educational goals
  • Services and supports your child will receive
  • Duration and frequency of services
  • How progress will be measured
  • Educational placement (regular class, resource, intensive special education)

You have important rights during the development of the IEP in ANDREWS ISD. You must be an equal participant, not just someone who signs documents.

Resources and Support for Parents in ANDREWS ISD

You are not alone in this process. There are several organizations and services available for parents in ANDREWS ISD and the Texas area.

Special Education Advocate

A special ed advocate near ANDREWS or a special education attorney can help you understand your rights. Many attorneys offer free initial consultations for parents in ANDREWS ISD.

Information and Referral Centers

  • Contact the ANDREWS ISD special education department directly
  • Look for local agencies in the ANDREWS area that support families with disabilities
  • Access online resources from the Texas Education Agency

Frequently Asked Questions about Special Education Evaluation in ANDREWS ISD

How much does an evaluation cost in ANDREWS ISD?

Initial evaluations in ANDREWS ISD are completely free. ANDREWS ISD is obligated to pay for all necessary evaluations.

What happens if ANDREWS ISD denies my request?

If ANDREWS ISD refuses to evaluate your child without a valid reason, you can file a formal complaint with the Texas Education Agency or request a due process hearing.

Can my child be evaluated in our language?

Yes. ANDREWS ISD must provide evaluations in Spanish if it is your family's primary language. They can also use interpreters during all meetings.

Can I bring a special education attorney to ANDREWS ISD meetings?

Absolutely yes. You have the right to bring legal support to all meetings related to special education in ANDREWS ISD.

Conclusion: Advocating for Your Child in ANDREWS ISD

Requesting a special education evaluation in ANDREWS ISD is a courageous and important step to support your child's learning. Although the process may seem complicated, remember that you have clear rights as a parent of a student in ANDREWS ISD.

Follow these steps, maintain careful documentation, and do not hesitate to seek support if you need it. The staff at ANDREWS ISD is there to help your child succeed, and you are your child's most important advocate.

If you have specific questions about the evaluation process in ANDREWS ISD, contact your child's school or the ANDREWS ISD special education department directly. They are available to support you through every step of this important process.
