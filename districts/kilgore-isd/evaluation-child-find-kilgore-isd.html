A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment conducted by KILGORE ISD to determine if your child has a disability that affects their education and whether they qualify for special education services. This evaluation examines your child's academic performance, cognitive abilities, social-emotional functioning, and other factors relevant to learning.

Parents in KILGORE ISD should understand that an FIE evaluation request is your gateway to accessing special education services and support. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code, KILGORE ISD is required to evaluate your child if you have reasonable concerns about a disability.

The evaluation is free of charge to families in KILGORE ISD. You will not pay assessment fees, testing costs, or evaluation expenses. This ensures that all KILGORE ISD families can access the evaluation process regardless of financial circumstances.

KILGORE ISD's child find system is designed to identify students who may have disabilities and need special education services. This process applies to all children in the KILGORE area, including those in preschool, general education classrooms, and private schools within the district's jurisdiction.

How KILGORE ISD Child Find Works

KILGORE ISD uses multiple methods to identify students who may need special education evaluations:

  • Teacher Referrals: Teachers monitor student progress and can refer students for evaluation when academic or behavioral concerns arise
  • Parent Requests: You can request evaluation directly from KILGORE ISD
  • Screening Data: KILGORE ISD reviews standardized test scores, classroom performance, and progress monitoring data
  • Developmental Concerns: Preschool staff in KILGORE ISD can identify developmental delays
  • Speech or Hearing Issues: Health screenings may indicate need for evaluation

If KILGORE ISD suspects your child may need an evaluation, the district will contact you and discuss your concerns. This is part of KILGORE ISD's commitment to early identification.

Your Right to Request a KILGORE ISD Evaluation: Taking the First Step

As a parent, you have the absolute right to request an evaluation of your child in KILGORE ISD, regardless of whether the school has referred your child. You do not need teacher permission, administrator approval, or any other prerequisite to make this request.

How to Submit Your KILGORE ISD Evaluation Request

Put your request in writing. The most important step is documenting your request. While you can discuss concerns verbally with teachers or administrators, submit a written evaluation request to ensure KILGORE ISD has legal notice.

Your written request should go to:

  • Your child's campus principal
  • The KILGORE ISD Special Education Department
  • The Director of Special Education for KILGORE ISD

What to Include in Your Request

Your written request doesn't need to be formal or legalistic. Include the following information:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current school/campus within KILGORE ISD
  • Grade level and current teacher(s)
  • Specific concerns about your child's learning, behavior, or development
  • Examples of how these concerns impact your child at home or school
  • Any prior evaluations or assessments your child has had
  • Your contact information (phone and email)
  • Your signature and date

You can request evaluation via email, hand-delivered letter, or phone (though follow up any phone call with written confirmation). This creates a paper trail that protects your rights in KILGORE ISD.

The 45-Day Timeline: KILGORE ISD's Legal Responsibility

Once KILGORE ISD receives your evaluation request, the district has a strict legal deadline. KILGORE ISD must complete the 45-day timeline from the date they receive your written request.

What the KILGORE ISD 45-Day Timeline Means

The KILGORE ISD 45 day timeline is not negotiable—it's required by IDEA and Texas Education Code. Within 45 days, KILGORE ISD must:

  1. Conduct comprehensive testing in all areas of suspected disability
  2. Review existing evaluation data from your child's school records
  3. Complete all individual assessments and evaluations
  4. Meet with you (as the parent) to discuss results
  5. Determine eligibility for special education services
  6. Provide you with a copy of the evaluation report

Important note: The 45-day timeline can only be extended with your written permission. KILGORE ISD cannot unilaterally extend this deadline. If you agree to additional time, that agreement must be in writing.

What Happens During the KILGORE ISD Evaluation Process

During your child's special education evaluation in KILGORE ISD, the district will typically conduct:

  • Cognitive Assessment: Testing your child's thinking, reasoning, and learning abilities
  • Academic Achievement Testing: Assessing reading, math, writing, and other academic skills
  • Speech and Language Evaluation: If speech concerns exist
  • Occupational or Physical Therapy Evaluation: If motor skills are a concern
  • Behavioral/Social-Emotional Assessment: Reviewing behavior patterns and emotional functioning
  • Classroom Observation: Observing your child in KILGORE ISD classrooms
  • Parent Interview: Discussing your child's developmental history and concerns
  • Review of School Records: Examining grades, attendance, and prior assessments

KILGORE ISD special education staff conduct these evaluations. You have the right to be present during testing and to ask questions about the evaluation process.

Understanding KILGORE ISD Special Education Eligibility Categories

If KILGORE ISD determines your child has a disability, your child must also qualify under one of 13 eligibility categories. These categories are defined by Texas Education Code and IDEA.

Primary Eligibility Categories in KILGORE ISD

The most common categories that KILGORE ISD students qualify under include:

  • Specific Learning Disability (SLD): Difficulty in reading, math, writing, or listening despite adequate instruction
  • Intellectual Disability: Significant limitations in cognitive functioning and adaptive behavior
  • Emotional/Behavioral Disorder: Patterns of behavior or emotional response that negatively impact education
  • Speech/Language Impairment: Difficulties in communication development and function
  • Other Health Impairment (OHI): Conditions like ADHD, asthma, or seizure disorders affecting education
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder: Affecting social interaction, communication, and behavior

KILGORE ISD evaluators will determine which category, if any, best describes your child's needs based on the comprehensive evaluation data.

Your Rights During the KILGORE ISD Evaluation Process

Federal and Texas law provide specific protections for parents during special education evaluations in KILGORE ISD. Understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Key Parent Rights in KILGORE ISD

  • Right to Request Evaluation: You can request evaluation at any time without reason
  • Right to Written Notice: KILGORE ISD must notify you in writing before evaluation begins
  • Right to Prior Consent: You must consent before KILGORE ISD begins evaluation testing
  • Right to Access Records: You can review all evaluation data and school records
  • Right to Interpretation: KILGORE ISD must explain results in language you understand
  • Right to Independent Evaluation: You can request an independent evaluation if you disagree with KILGORE ISD's results
  • Right to Due Process: You can file a due process complaint if KILGORE ISD violates your rights

These protections ensure that the KILGORE ISD evaluation request and subsequent assessment process respects your role as your child's advocate.

What Happens After the 45-Day Timeline: The IEP Meeting

Within the KILGORE ISD 45 day timeline, you'll be invited to an Individualized Education Program (IEP) meeting. This is where KILGORE ISD shares evaluation results and makes eligibility decisions.

The IEP Meeting Process in KILGORE ISD

At this meeting, KILGORE ISD will present:

  • Detailed evaluation results and findings
  • Explanation of what the data means for your child
  • Determination of disability and eligibility
  • If eligible, proposed special education services and supports
  • Educational goals and accommodations

You have the right to bring support to this meeting—a family member, advocate, or special ed advocate familiar with KILGORE ISD special education services can attend and help you understand information and ask questions.

Come prepared with your own observations, concerns, and priorities for your child. This is your opportunity to ensure the IEP reflects your child's actual needs.

If KILGORE ISD Declines to Evaluate: Your Options

Sometimes KILGORE ISD may refuse to evaluate your child or may not complete the KILGORE ISD 45 day timeline appropriately. You have recourse options to protect your child's rights.

Addressing Evaluation Concerns in KILGORE ISD

Request a Second Opinion: If KILGORE ISD evaluates your child but you disagree with the results, you can request an independent evaluation at KILGORE ISD's expense under certain circumstances.

File a Due Process Complaint: If KILGORE ISD violates IDEA procedures, including missing the 45-day timeline, you can file a due process complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

Consult with Special Education Professionals: Speaking with a special education attorney near KILGORE or a special ed advocate can help you understand your options and next steps when KILGORE ISD creates barriers to evaluation.

Building Your Case: Documentation Tips for KILGORE ISD Parents

Before requesting evaluation from KILGORE ISD, prepare documentation that strengthens your case:

  • Keep a Concern Log: Document specific instances where your child struggles—behavioral incidents, missed assignments, academic concerns
  • Gather Prior Testing: Collect any outside evaluations, psychological assessments, or medical diagnoses
  • Save Work Samples: Keep examples of your child's classwork showing patterns of difficulty
  • Document Communications: Save emails and written communication with KILGORE ISD staff about your concerns
  • Record Developmental History: Note delays from infancy/early childhood that may be relevant

This documentation will be valuable when you submit your KILGORE ISD evaluation request and during the evaluation process.

Conclusion: Advocating for Your Child in KILGORE ISD

Requesting an FIE evaluation in KILGORE ISD is a critical step toward getting your child the support and services they deserve. Understanding the process, your rights, the 45-day timeline, and available resources empowers you to navigate the system effectively.

Remember: KILGORE ISD has a legal obligation to evaluate your child if you have reasonable concerns about disability. You don't need permission or teacher agreement—a written request is sufficient to start the process.

If you need additional support, KILGORE ISD families can connect with special ed advocates or consult with a special education attorney near KILGORE to ensure your child receives appropriate evaluation and services. Your advocacy matters, and your child's future is worth the effort.
