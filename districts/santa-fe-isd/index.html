What can I do right now if Santa Fe ISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Santa Fe ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Santa Fe ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the world of special education can feel overwhelming, but you are not alone. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), both federal and Texas law guarantee specific rights and protections for students with disabilities. Special education is specially designed instruction that addresses the unique needs of your child, enabling them to access the general curriculum and make progress in school. This may include specialized teaching methods, assistive technology, related services like speech therapy or occupational therapy, and modifications to the learning environment.

Here in Santa Fe ISD, the school district operates under the guidance of the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA provides oversight and ensures that Santa Fe ISD is adhering to all state and federal regulations regarding special education services. TEA Region 4 Education Service Center is a valuable resource that offers training and support to Santa Fe ISD staff, further helping to ensure that your child receives the best possible education. Remember, as a parent, you have legally enforceable rights at every stage of the special education process. Understanding these rights is the first step in becoming an effective advocate for your child.

Your voice matters, and Santa Fe ISD is required to collaborate with you throughout the entire special education process, from initial evaluation to the development and implementation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP). Don't hesitate to ask questions, express your concerns, and actively participate in all meetings regarding your child's education.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Santa Fe ISD

If you suspect your child may have a disability that is impacting their ability to learn in school, you have the right to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). This evaluation is crucial to determine if your child is eligible for special education services in Santa Fe ISD. It is important to remember that the request must be made in writing. A verbal request to a teacher or administrator, while helpful for raising the initial concern, does not officially start the evaluation timeline required by law. The request should clearly state that you are requesting a special education evaluation for your child and briefly outline the reasons for your concern.

Once Santa Fe ISD receives your written request and obtains your written consent to evaluate, the district has 45 school days to complete the FIE. This timeline is important, so be sure to document the date you submit the request. Also, understand that "Child Find" laws require Santa Fe ISD to proactively identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities who reside within their boundaries, regardless of whether they are enrolled in public school. So, even if you haven't formally requested an evaluation, if Santa Fe ISD suspects your child may have a disability, they have a responsibility to investigate further. Don't wait - if you haven't already done so, write a letter to the school principal formally requesting a special education evaluation for your child. Keep a copy for your records and send the letter via certified mail with return receipt requested to ensure proof of delivery.

ARD Meetings in Santa Fe ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal. This committee is responsible for making decisions about your child's special education program. The ARD committee is comprised of school personnel, including administrators, teachers, and specialists, and most importantly, you, the parent. Santa Fe ISD is legally required to provide you with written notice at least five calendar days before any ARD meeting. This notice must include the purpose of the meeting, the date, time, location, and a list of individuals who will be attending.

Remember, as a parent, you are an equal member of the ARD committee, and your input is essential. Don't be afraid to ask questions, express your concerns, and advocate for your child's needs. If you feel overwhelmed or unprepared during an ARD meeting, you have the right to request a 10-day recess, which is a break in the meeting. This allows you time to gather your thoughts, consult with an advocate, or obtain additional information. You also have the right to request that specific individuals be present at the ARD meeting, such as therapists or outside experts. Furthermore, you are not required to sign the IEP on the same day as the ARD meeting. Take your time to review the document carefully and ensure that you fully understand and agree with all of the provisions. If you disagree with any aspect of the IEP, you have the right to document your dissent.

The ARD meeting is where the team determines eligibility for special education and creates (or updates) the IEP. Remember that you are a critical member of that team. You know your child best. Before your next ARD meeting in Santa Fe ISD, download our free ARD preparation checklist to help you organize your thoughts and ensure that all of your child's needs are addressed. This checklist will walk you through the IEP components, the data that must be collected, and the accommodations and modifications you should ask for.

Dyslexia Services in Santa Fe ISD

Texas law mandates that Santa Fe ISD screen all students for dyslexia. This screening is designed to identify students who may be at risk for reading difficulties. The standards for identifying and serving students with dyslexia are outlined in the 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook, which Santa Fe ISD is required to follow. This handbook provides guidance on screening procedures, instructional methods, and the types of accommodations and support that students with dyslexia should receive.

If your child is identified as having dyslexia in Santa Fe ISD, they are entitled to structured literacy intervention, which is a research-based approach to teaching reading that focuses on phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension. Depending on the severity of your child's dyslexia and their individual needs, they may qualify for a 504 Plan or an IEP, both of which provide accommodations and support to help them succeed in school. If your child has not been screened for dyslexia, and you have concerns about their reading skills, request a dyslexia screening in writing from your child's school principal. Keep a copy of your request for your records.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Santa Fe ISD

Discipline can be a complex issue when it comes to students with disabilities. It is essential to understand your child's rights if they are facing disciplinary action at school. If a student receiving special education services in Santa Fe ISD is suspended for more than 10 cumulative school days in a school year, it triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This review is conducted by the ARD committee to determine if the student's behavior was caused by, or directly related to, their disability, or if it was the result of the school's failure to implement the IEP.

A suspension of more than 10 cumulative days constitutes a change of placement under IDEA, which requires an ARD meeting to be held within a specific timeframe. This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face in special education because failing to act quickly can jeopardize your child's right to a free and appropriate public education (FAPE). You must respond immediately. If your child has been suspended or is facing disciplinary action at Santa Fe ISD, don't wait - use our Discipline Defense Kit to understand your rights and protect your child's access to education. This kit will help you prepare for the MDR and advocate for appropriate supports and interventions.

Filing a Complaint Against Santa Fe ISD

If you have concerns about your child's special education services and you are unable to resolve them through communication with school staff in Santa Fe ISD, you have several options for filing a formal complaint. These options exist regardless of what you are told by Santa Fe ISD personnel. One option is to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This is a free process, and TEA is required to investigate and resolve the complaint within 60 days. Another option is to request mediation through TEA, which is a voluntary process that brings you and Santa Fe ISD representatives together with a trained mediator to try to reach a mutually agreeable resolution. Mediation is also free.

You also have the right to request a Due Process Hearing, which is a more formal legal proceeding where an impartial hearing officer hears evidence and makes a decision about your child's special education services. Due process is the most expensive and adversarial option. Finally, you can file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR), which investigates allegations of discrimination based on disability. Understanding these escalation paths is essential to ensuring your child receives the FAPE they are entitled to. If you feel that Santa Fe ISD is not meeting its obligations, explore these options and choose the path that best suits your needs. Begin by documenting every communication and action taken by Santa Fe ISD regarding your child's special education services.

Frequently Asked Questions About Santa Fe ISD Special Education

The questions every Santa Fe ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Santa Fe ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Santa Fe ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Santa Fe ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Santa Fe ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Santa Fe ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Santa Fe ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Santa Fe ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Santa Fe ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
