Dispute & Discipline Rights in Santa Fe ISD

🚨 Know This Now

Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

Discipline Rights & Grievances in Santa Fe ISD

URGENT GUIDE: Discipline Rights & Grievances in Santa Fe ISD

If your child is facing disciplinary action in Santa Fe ISD, time is of the essence. Understand your rights and act quickly. This guide provides critical information. This is NOT legal advice. Consult with an attorney specializing in education law immediately if you have serious concerns.

The 10-Day Suspension Rule

Santa Fe ISD can suspend your child for up to 10 school days for a single incident. Beyond 10 days, more stringent rules and potentially a Change of Placement are triggered, especially for students with disabilities. Even a short suspension can disrupt your child's education and negatively impact their record. Track every day of suspension carefully.

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

If your child is classified under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and faces a suspension that will result in a Change of Placement (see below), the school MUST conduct a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This review determines if your child's behavior was caused by or directly related to their disability, or if the behavior was a result of the school's failure to implement the IEP (Individualized Education Program).

Crucially, you MUST be invited to the MDR meeting and actively participate. Prepare thoroughly. Gather evidence that demonstrates the connection between your child's disability and the behavior in question. Bring medical documentation, IEP records, and any communication with school staff. Demand that the school prove their IEP was implemented properly. If the MDR finds that the behavior WAS a manifestation of your child's disability or the IEP was not followed, they MUST return your child to their original placement.

Change of Placement

A Change of Placement occurs when a student is removed from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days, or for a pattern of removals that constitutes more than 10 school days in a school year. This can involve suspension, expulsion, or placement in an alternative education setting. For students with IDEA protections, a Change of Placement triggers specific rights, including the MDR process. Watch for cumulative suspensions; the district may try to circumvent the rules by issuing multiple short suspensions. Document every instance.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

Santa Fe ISD has a formal grievance process. The first step, Level 1, involves submitting a written complaint to the principal or immediate supervisor of the person causing the grievance. Act fast. There are strict deadlines for filing grievances. Obtain a copy of the district's grievance policy (usually available on the district website) and follow it precisely. Your grievance should clearly state the facts of the situation, the specific policy violations, and the remedy you are seeking. Keep copies of everything you submit and document all communication with the school district. Even if you feel overwhelmed, it is vital to get something on record immediately.

TEA State Complaint

If you believe Santa Fe ISD is violating state or federal laws related to special education, you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This is a more formal process than a grievance and requires specific documentation and evidence. The TEA will investigate your complaint and determine if the district is in violation. This can be a lengthy process, but it's an important option if other avenues have failed. Search the TEA website for "Special Education Complaint Resolution Process" to find the necessary forms and instructions.

Due Process

For students with disabilities under IDEA, you have the right to Due Process. This means you have the right to challenge the school's decisions regarding your child's education, including disciplinary actions. Filing for due process involves a formal hearing with an impartial hearing officer. This is a complex legal process, and you absolutely MUST seek legal representation from an attorney experienced in special education law. Due process can be a powerful tool to protect your child's rights, but it requires significant preparation and legal expertise. Don't wait until the last minute. Seek counsel immediately.

Remember: Document everything. Keep accurate records of all communication, meetings, and incidents. Time is your enemy. Act quickly and decisively to protect your child.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
