What can I do right now if New Caney ISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. New Caney ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in New Caney ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the special education system can feel overwhelming, but you're not alone. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas law, special education is specifically designed instruction and related services provided to children with disabilities who need it in order to succeed in school. This includes everything from academic support to therapies like speech or occupational therapy, tailored to your child's individual needs. New Caney ISD is committed to providing these services to eligible students residing within its boundaries.

As a public school district, New Caney ISD operates under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA). The TEA ensures that districts like New Caney ISD are following federal and state laws regarding special education. Furthermore, New Caney ISD benefits from the support and resources provided by TEA Region 4 Education Service Center, located in Houston. This regional center offers training, technical assistance, and other support services to help New Caney ISD provide high-quality special education programs. Remember, you play a crucial role in this process.

It's vital to remember that you have enforceable legal rights throughout the entire special education process. From the initial evaluation to the development and implementation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP), you have the right to participate fully and advocate for your child's needs within New Caney ISD. These rights are designed to ensure that your child receives a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) that meets their unique learning needs. Take the time to understand your rights so you can be the most effective advocate for your child.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in New Caney ISD

If you believe your child may need special education services, the first step is to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) from New Caney ISD. It's crucial to understand that this request must be in writing. Verbal requests do not start the clock for evaluation timelines. Once New Caney ISD receives your written consent to evaluate your child, the district has 45 school days to complete the FIE. This evaluation will help determine if your child has a disability that qualifies them for special education services.

Remember that New Caney ISD also has a legal obligation under Child Find laws to proactively identify students who may need special education services, regardless of whether a parent has requested an evaluation. If you have concerns about your child's academic, behavioral, or developmental progress, don't hesitate to reach out to the school. To begin the evaluation process, write a letter to your child's principal or special education director clearly stating your request for a special education evaluation. Keep a copy for your records and send the letter via certified mail, return receipt requested, so you have proof of delivery.

ARD Meetings in New Caney ISD: Your Rights at the Table

ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal. An ARD committee meeting is where important decisions about your child's special education program are made. In New Caney ISD, the ARD committee includes you, your child (when appropriate), teachers, administrators, and other professionals who have knowledge about your child's needs. The ARD committee develops, reviews, and revises your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP).

New Caney ISD must provide you with at least five calendar days' written notice before any ARD meeting. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, the date, time, and location, and who will be attending. During the ARD meeting, you have the right to actively participate, ask questions, and share your concerns. You also have the right to request a 10-day recess (a break) at any point during the ARD meeting if you need more time to consider the information being presented or if you feel overwhelmed.

It is also critical to remember that you do not have to sign the IEP on the same day as the ARD meeting. You have the right to take the IEP home, review it carefully, and seek advice from advocates or attorneys before signing. Your signature indicates your agreement with the IEP, so it's important to make sure you fully understand and are comfortable with the plan. Remember, you are an equal member of the ARD committee and your voice matters.

Dyslexia Services in New Caney ISD

Texas law mandates that New Caney ISD screen every student for dyslexia at certain grade levels. Dyslexia is a learning disorder that affects reading accuracy and fluency. Early identification and intervention are crucial for students with dyslexia to succeed. If your child is identified as having dyslexia, they are entitled to receive structured literacy intervention, which is a specific type of instruction designed to address the core deficits associated with dyslexia.

The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook sets the standards that New Caney ISD must follow in identifying and serving students with dyslexia. Depending on the severity of your child's dyslexia and their individual needs, they may qualify for a 504 Plan or an Individualized Education Program (IEP) to provide them with the necessary accommodations and support. If your child has not been screened for dyslexia and you have concerns, request a dyslexia screening in writing from your child's school.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in New Caney ISD

Disciplinary actions, such as suspensions, can have a significant impact on students with disabilities. If your child with an IEP or 504 plan is suspended for more than 10 cumulative school days in a school year in New Caney ISD, this triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). The MDR is a process where the ARD committee determines if your child's misconduct was caused by or directly related to their disability, or if it was a result of New Caney ISD's failure to implement the IEP. Suspending a special education student for more than 10 days is considered a Change of Placement under IDEA, requiring an ARD meeting to determine next steps.

This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent of a special education student. You must act quickly to protect your child's rights. Failure to respond promptly could result in your child being unfairly disciplined or denied necessary services. If your child has been suspended from New Caney ISD, contact a special education advocate or attorney immediately. Use the Discipline Defense Kit if your child has been suspended.

Filing a Complaint Against New Caney ISD

If you believe that New Caney ISD has violated your child's rights under IDEA or other special education laws, you have several avenues for resolving the issue. It's important to remember that you have the right to pursue these options regardless of what you are told by New Caney ISD staff. The first escalation path is to file a TEA State Complaint, which is a free process managed by the Texas Education Agency. TEA is required to investigate and resolve the complaint within 60 days.

Another option is Mediation through TEA, which is also a free service. Mediation involves a neutral third party who helps you and New Caney ISD reach a mutually agreeable resolution. If mediation is unsuccessful, or if you prefer, you can pursue a Due Process Hearing, which is a more formal legal proceeding where an impartial hearing officer makes a decision. Finally, you can also file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR) if you believe New Caney ISD has discriminated against your child based on their disability.

Frequently Asked Questions About New Caney ISD Special Education

The questions every New Caney ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in New Caney ISD? +

Submit a written request to the New Caney ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in New Caney ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does New Caney ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires New Caney ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how New Caney ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if New Caney ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. New Caney ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
