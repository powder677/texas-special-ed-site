Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

The 10-Day Suspension Rule

New Caney ISD can suspend your child for up to 3 school days without a formal hearing. However, a single suspension or series of suspensions cannot exceed 10 cumulative school days in a school year without triggering certain procedural rights, especially if your child has an IEP (Individualized Education Program) or a 504 plan.

What to do: Track every suspension day. The moment your child approaches or exceeds 10 days (cumulative), demand a meeting to discuss discipline and potential violations of IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) if applicable.

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

If your child with an IEP or 504 plan faces a suspension that constitutes a Change of Placement (see below), or any removal for more than 10 school days (cumulative), the school must conduct a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR). This meeting determines if your child's behavior was a manifestation of their disability.

Key Points about the MDR:

  • Purpose: To determine if the behavior was caused by, or had a direct and substantial relationship to, the child's disability, or if the behavior was a direct result of the school's failure to implement the IEP.
  • Participants: You (the parent), relevant members of the IEP team (including school representatives), and the child (if appropriate).
  • Outcome: If the behavior *is* a manifestation of the disability, the school cannot discipline the child in the same way as a non-disabled student. They must review the IEP and consider modifications to address the behavior. If the behavior is *not* a manifestation, the child can be disciplined in the same manner as a student without a disability, but the school must still provide FAPE (Free Appropriate Public Education).

Demand an MDR immediately if your child with an IEP or 504 plan is facing significant disciplinary action. Don't let the school drag their feet!

Change of Placement

A "Change of Placement" occurs when a disciplinary action significantly alters your child's educational environment. This can happen through suspensions, expulsions, or changes to the educational setting. A Change of Placement triggers additional procedural safeguards, particularly for students with IEPs or 504 plans.

Examples of Change of Placement:

  • Long-term suspension (more than 10 school days cumulatively).
  • Expulsion.
  • Transfer to a different school or program for disciplinary reasons.

If a Change of Placement is occurring, ensure the school is adhering to all IDEA requirements, including holding an MDR (if applicable), providing continued educational services, and adhering to due process requirements.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe the school has violated your child's rights or school policy, you can file a formal grievance. New Caney ISD likely has a multi-level grievance process. The first step is typically a Level 1 grievance, usually filed with the principal or relevant administrator.

Steps for Filing a Level 1 Grievance:

  • Obtain the grievance form: Get this from the school or district website.
  • Document everything: Gather all evidence (emails, witness statements, suspension notices, IEP documents, etc.).
  • Be clear and concise: State the specific policy or right that was violated, and explain how it was violated.
  • State your desired outcome: What remedy are you seeking? (e.g., removal of the suspension, reinstatement to the previous placement, modification of the IEP).
  • Meet deadlines: Grievances usually have strict deadlines. Find out what the deadline is and submit it ON TIME.
  • Keep a copy: For your records.

TEA State Complaint

If the local grievance process fails to resolve the issue, you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). A TEA complaint is a formal allegation that a school district has violated state or federal laws related to special education.

TEA Complaint Considerations:

  • Timeliness: Complaints must typically be filed within one year of the alleged violation.
  • Specificity: The complaint must be specific and detailed, outlining the alleged violation and providing supporting documentation.
  • Exhaustion of Remedies: TEA may require you to exhaust local grievance procedures before filing a state complaint.

Due Process

For students with IEPs, parents have the right to request a due process hearing if they disagree with the school's actions regarding identification, evaluation, educational placement, or the provision of FAPE. This is a formal legal proceeding.

Due Process: A Last Resort. Due process hearings are complex and can be time-consuming and costly. Consider mediation or other alternative dispute resolution methods before pursuing due process. However, if all else fails, and you believe your child's rights have been violated, a due process hearing may be necessary. Consult with an attorney immediately.

Remember: Time is crucial. Act quickly to protect your child's rights. Seek legal advice from a qualified attorney experienced in special education law.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
