Dyslexia Services in New Caney ISD

Dyslexia Services in New Caney ISD

Texas Dyslexia Handbook 2024

New Caney ISD adheres to the guidelines outlined in the most current version of the Texas Dyslexia Handbook, published by the Texas Education Agency (TEA). The 2024 handbook provides comprehensive information regarding the identification, assessment, and provision of services for students with dyslexia and related disorders. This includes legal requirements under state and federal law. Parents and educators are encouraged to familiarize themselves with the handbook, which can be accessed on the TEA website. The handbook details the definition of dyslexia, risk factors, indicators, screening procedures, instructional approaches, and parental rights. It's a crucial resource for understanding the district's commitment to supporting students with dyslexia.

Mandatory Screening

New Caney ISD conducts mandatory dyslexia screening for all kindergarten and first-grade students, as mandated by Texas law. These screenings are designed to identify students who may be at risk for dyslexia. The screening typically involves assessing foundational reading skills such as phonological awareness, phonemic awareness, letter-sound knowledge, rapid automatized naming (RAN), and decoding skills. If a student demonstrates difficulties during the initial screening, further evaluation and intervention may be recommended. Parents will be notified of screening results and any recommended actions. These early screenings are critical for providing timely support and preventing reading difficulties from escalating.

Structured Literacy Programs

New Caney ISD implements structured literacy programs for students identified with dyslexia or at risk for dyslexia. These programs are research-based and utilize explicit, systematic, sequential, and multi-sensory instruction. The focus is on developing foundational reading skills, including phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension. These programs are designed to be intensive and individualized to meet the specific needs of each student. The district selects programs aligned with the principles of structured literacy and provides ongoing professional development for teachers to ensure effective implementation. Examples of program components may include explicit phonics instruction, syllable division strategies, morphology study, and guided reading practice.

504 vs IEP for Dyslexia

Students with dyslexia in New Caney ISD may receive support through a Section 504 plan or an Individualized Education Program (IEP), depending on their individual needs. A 504 plan provides accommodations and modifications to ensure that students with disabilities have equal access to the general education curriculum. This typically includes classroom accommodations like extended time on tests, preferential seating, and assistive technology. An IEP, on the other hand, is a more comprehensive plan for students with disabilities who require specialized instruction and related services to access the curriculum. To qualify for an IEP under the category of Specific Learning Disability (which includes dyslexia), the student must demonstrate a significant discrepancy between their cognitive ability and their academic achievement, despite appropriate instruction. The decision to provide support through a 504 plan or an IEP is made on a case-by-case basis by a team of professionals, including educators, administrators, and parents.

Accommodations List

New Caney ISD offers a variety of accommodations for students with dyslexia, depending on their individual needs. Common accommodations may include:

  • Extended time on tests and assignments
  • Preferential seating
  • Use of assistive technology (e.g., text-to-speech software, audiobooks)
  • Modified assignments
  • Visual aids
  • Multi-sensory instruction
  • Use of graphic organizers
  • Chunking of tasks
  • Dictation software
  • Alternative testing formats
  • Reduced workload
  • Reading passages aloud during assessments
This is not an exhaustive list, and the specific accommodations provided will be determined by the student's ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee for students with IEPs, or the 504 committee.

How to Request Evaluation

Parents who suspect their child may have dyslexia should contact their child's teacher, counselor, or the campus principal to discuss their concerns. To formally request a dyslexia evaluation, parents should submit a written request to the school principal or Special Education Department. The request should include specific information about the student's reading difficulties and any supporting documentation, such as previous assessments or observations. Upon receiving the request, the school will initiate the evaluation process, which may include assessments in reading, writing, and related areas. The evaluation results will be reviewed by a team of professionals, including educators, administrators, and parents, to determine if the student meets the criteria for dyslexia and requires specialized instruction or accommodations. Contact information for the New Caney ISD Special Education Department can be found on the district's website.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

Is my child entitled to dyslexia testing in Texas? +

Yes. Texas law requires all districts to screen students for dyslexia and related disorders in Kindergarten and First Grade, and at any grade level if a parent or teacher requests it.

What accommodations can a dyslexic child get in Texas? +

Accommodations may include extended time on tests, text-to-speech software, oral administration of tests, reduced-answer-choice formats, and use of a calculator. These can be provided through a 504 Plan or an IEP.

What is the Texas Dyslexia Handbook? +

The Texas Dyslexia Handbook is a state guidance document updated in 2024 that outlines districts' responsibilities for identifying, evaluating, and serving students with dyslexia using structured literacy methods.
