What is an ARD Meeting in KERRVILLE ISD?

An ARD meeting is the formal process through which KERRVILLE ISD identifies, evaluates, and serves students with disabilities. The term "Admission, Review, and Dismissal" reflects the three main purposes: admitting a student to special education services, reviewing their progress regularly, and dismissing them from services when appropriate.

In KERRVILLE ISD, ARD meetings are governed by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §89.1010. These meetings are required at least once annually for every student receiving special education services in the district. The KERRVILLE ISD ARD meeting brings together parents, teachers, and school administrators to collaborate on your child's educational plan.

For KERRVILLE ISD families, these meetings represent a critical opportunity to ensure your child's individual education plan reflects their unique needs, strengths, and potential. Understanding your role and your rights in the KERRVILLE ISD IEP process empowers you to be an effective advocate.

Understanding the IEP: Your Child's Roadmap

The Individualized Education Program (IEP) is the heart of special education in KERRVILLE ISD. This legally binding document outlines your child's current academic and functional performance, identifies their special education needs, and establishes measurable annual goals.

Key Components of a KERRVILLE ISD IEP

Every KERRVILLE ISD IEP must include several critical components:

  • Present Levels of Academic and Functional Performance: Describes what your child can currently do academically, socially, emotionally, and functionally
  • Measurable Annual Goals: Specific KERRVILLE ISD IEP goals your child will work toward throughout the school year
  • Special Education Services: Details the type and frequency of services your child will receive
  • Accommodations and Modifications: Supports and changes to the general curriculum or testing procedures
  • LRE Statement: Explanation of how your child will be educated in the Least Restrictive Environment
  • Transition Planning: For students age 14 and older, goals and services related to post-secondary outcomes

KERRVILLE ISD IEP goals must be measurable, meaning they include specific criteria and timelines for determining whether your child has met the goal. Effective goals are neither too easy nor unrealistic—they stretch your child toward growth while remaining achievable.

Preparing for Your KERRVILLE ISD ARD Meeting

Successful KERRVILLE ISD ARD preparation begins weeks before the scheduled meeting. Parents who prepare thoroughly have greater influence over their child's IEP and can ensure their concerns are heard and documented.

Four to Six Weeks Before Your Meeting:

  • Request a copy of your child's current IEP and all recent evaluations
  • Gather progress reports, report cards, and teacher feedback from the past year
  • Document any concerns or observations about your child's performance at home
  • Review your child's attendance and behavior records

Two Weeks Before Your Meeting:

  • Draft proposed KERRVILLE ISD IEP goals based on your child's needs
  • Create a list of questions or concerns to discuss
  • Arrange for a special ed advocate KERRVILLE to attend if desired
  • Request any specific school evaluations or data you haven't yet reviewed

One Week Before Your Meeting:

  • Prepare a one-page summary of your child's strengths, challenges, and goals
  • Organize all documents in a binder with tabs
  • Write down specific examples of concerns or successes to share
  • Confirm the meeting date, time, and location

The Step-by-Step KERRVILLE ISD ARD Meeting Process

Knowing what to expect during your KERRVILLE ISD ARD meeting reduces anxiety and helps you participate more confidently. Here's how the meeting typically unfolds:

Before the Meeting Starts

Arrive 10-15 minutes early to the KERRVILLE ISD ARD meeting. Review the agenda and introduce yourself to anyone you haven't met before. Bring your binder with notes and documents. If you've arranged for a special ed advocate KERRVILLE to attend, ensure they're checked in and ready to participate.

Opening and Introductions

The KERRVILLE ISD ARD facilitator will welcome everyone and explain the meeting's purpose. All participants introduce themselves, including teachers, special education staff, and any outside professionals. At this point, mention if you brought an advocate or if you're requesting to record the meeting.

Review of Current Performance

The team reviews your child's KERRVILLE ISD IEP performance from the past year. This includes academic data, assessment scores, progress toward previous goals, and behavioral or social-emotional information. Teachers and specialists share observations about your child's strengths and areas needing improvement.

Discussion of Assessment Results

If your child was recently evaluated, the evaluator presents findings. For students in KERRVILLE ISD special education, evaluations may include cognitive, academic, speech/language, occupational therapy, or psychological assessments. Ask questions to understand what the results mean for your child's educational needs.

Determining Eligibility or Continued Services

The team determines whether your child meets eligibility criteria for special education services in KERRVILLE ISD. For annual reviews, the team confirms continued eligibility. If your child is being evaluated for the first time, this is when the team decides whether special education services are appropriate.

Developing or Revising KERRVILLE ISD IEP Goals

This is a central part of your KERRVILLE ISD ARD meeting. The team discusses and develops new KERRVILLE ISD IEP goals for the upcoming year. Goals should address your child's most significant needs, be measurable with clear success criteria, and include specific timelines (usually annual). Share your observations and concerns to ensure goals reflect what matters most for your child.

Determining Services and Placement

The team specifies what special education services your child will receive, how often, and where. This includes pull-out services, resource classes, self-contained classrooms, or inclusion in general education with support. For KERRVILLE ISD students, the team must justify any placement outside the general education classroom and ensure the Least Restrictive Environment principle is followed.

Discussing Accommodations and Modifications

The KERRVILLE ISD IEP outlines accommodations (changes in how instruction is delivered without changing content) and modifications (changes to the content itself). Examples include extended time on tests, modified assignments, preferential seating, or simplified materials.

Closing Discussion and Next Steps

The team reviews the complete document and explains next steps. Clarify when services begin, how progress will be monitored and communicated, and when the next KERRVILLE ISD ARD meeting will occur. Request a copy of the finalized IEP before you leave.
