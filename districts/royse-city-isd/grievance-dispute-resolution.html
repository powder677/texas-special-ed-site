The 10-Day Suspension Rule

Royse City ISD can suspend your child for up to 10 school days for disciplinary reasons. BUT, if a child with an IEP (Individualized Education Program) is facing a suspension that will result in a total of more than 10 cumulative school days in a single school year, things get much more complex. This triggers specific rights and protections under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). Track your child's suspension days meticulously! Document everything!

Pay attention to pattern suspensions. Even if each individual suspension is less than 10 days, a pattern of short suspensions for similar behaviors can also trigger a "Change of Placement" and require a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR).

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

An MDR is a meeting held to determine if your child's behavior that led to the suspension was caused by, or directly and substantially related to, your child's disability. It also considers if the behavior was a direct result of the school district's failure to implement the IEP. This is CRUCIAL!

Demand an MDR immediately if the potential suspension will result in more than 10 cumulative days out of school. You, as the parent, are a key member of the MDR team, along with school administrators and relevant IEP team members.

Prepare for the MDR! Gather evidence: IEP documents, behavior intervention plans (BIPs), teacher communications, medical records - anything that supports your argument that the behavior is linked to your child's disability or the district's failure to implement the IEP.

If the MDR team determines the behavior WAS a manifestation of your child's disability, the school district MUST conduct a functional behavioral assessment (FBA), implement a behavior intervention plan (BIP), or revise an existing BIP to address the behavior. Your child also returns to their previous placement (unless you and the district agree otherwise).

If the MDR team determines the behavior WAS NOT a manifestation of your child's disability, the district can discipline your child in the same way it disciplines students without disabilities. However, they MUST continue to provide FAPE (Free Appropriate Public Education) during the suspension.

Change of Placement

A "Change of Placement" occurs when disciplinary actions result in a significant alteration to your child's learning environment. This can include suspensions exceeding 10 days, expulsion, or assignment to an alternative educational setting. Any Change of Placement triggers MDR requirements for students with IEPs.

Don't let the district railroad you into accepting a Change of Placement without a thorough discussion and consideration of all options. Advocate for your child to remain in their current placement or a less restrictive environment whenever possible.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe Royse City ISD has violated your child's rights or misinterpreted policies related to discipline, you have the right to file a grievance. Start with a Level 1 Grievance.

Document EVERYTHING! Gather all relevant documents, emails, and witness statements. Write a clear and concise statement outlining the specific policy violations and the impact on your child.

Submit your Level 1 Grievance in writing to the appropriate campus administrator (usually the principal). Retain a copy for your records. The administrator has a limited time frame (usually 10 school days, but verify with district policy) to respond. If you are not satisfied with the response, you can escalate to Level 2.

TEA State Complaint

If you believe Royse City ISD has violated the IDEA or other special education laws, you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This is a formal process with specific deadlines.

TEA investigations can take time, but a finding in your favor can lead to corrective action by the district and potentially benefit other students. Gather all documentation and follow the TEA's complaint procedures carefully.

Due Process

If you disagree with the MDR outcome or the district's disciplinary actions, and you have exhausted other avenues, you have the right to request a due process hearing. This is a legal proceeding, and you may want to seek legal representation.

A due process hearing involves presenting evidence and testimony before an impartial hearing officer. The hearing officer's decision is binding. Navigating the due process system is complex, so consulting with an attorney is highly recommended.

Important Reminder: This guide provides general information and should not be considered legal advice. Seek legal counsel immediately if you believe your child's rights are being violated. Don't delay - your child's future depends on it!

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
