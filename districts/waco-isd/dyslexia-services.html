Dyslexia Services in Waco ISD

Dyslexia Services in Waco ISD

This guide outlines the dyslexia services provided by Waco Independent School District (WISD), adhering to Texas state guidelines and legal requirements. It provides information on screening, instruction, accommodations, and the process for requesting an evaluation for dyslexia.

Texas Dyslexia Handbook 2024

WISD follows the guidelines outlined in the Texas Dyslexia Handbook (2024), published by the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This handbook serves as the primary resource for educators and parents regarding identification, evaluation, and instruction for students with dyslexia. It provides detailed information on evidence-based practices, legal requirements under state and federal law, and the role of the Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committee or Section 504 committee in addressing the needs of students with dyslexia. The handbook is a living document, and the latest version should always be consulted for the most up-to-date information.

Mandatory Screening

Texas law mandates dyslexia screening for all kindergarten and first-grade students. WISD conducts these screenings using TEA-approved assessments to identify students who exhibit characteristics of dyslexia. Screening typically focuses on phonological awareness, rapid naming, and letter-sound knowledge. If a student shows signs of dyslexia during the initial screening, further assessments may be conducted to determine the need for intervention services. Parents will be notified of the screening results and any subsequent actions taken.

Structured Literacy Programs

WISD utilizes Structured Literacy programs for students identified with dyslexia. Structured Literacy is an evidence-based approach that explicitly teaches phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and reading comprehension. These programs provide systematic and sequential instruction, building a strong foundation in reading skills. Examples of Structured Literacy programs may include (but are not limited to) curricula that focus on explicit phonics instruction and multi-sensory learning techniques. The specific program used will be determined based on the individual needs of the student.

504 vs IEP for Dyslexia

Students with dyslexia may receive services under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act or through an Individualized Education Program (IEP) under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). The determination depends on the severity of the student's needs and the impact of dyslexia on their academic performance.

  • Section 504: A student may qualify for 504 services if dyslexia substantially limits their ability to learn. 504 plans typically provide accommodations and modifications to the learning environment to ensure equal access to education.
  • IEP: An IEP is developed for students with a disability (including dyslexia) that requires specialized instruction and related services to make progress in the general education curriculum. This requires the student to meet specific eligibility criteria according to IDEA.
The ARD committee (for IEPs) or the 504 committee will determine the appropriate plan based on a comprehensive evaluation of the student's needs.

Accommodations List

Accommodations are adjustments to the learning environment or instructional methods that help students with dyslexia access the curriculum. Examples of common accommodations provided in WISD include:

  • Extended time on tests and assignments
  • Preferential seating
  • Oral reading of tests
  • Use of assistive technology (e.g., text-to-speech software)
  • Modified assignments
  • Visual aids
  • Note-taking assistance
This list is not exhaustive, and specific accommodations will be determined based on the individual needs of the student.

How to Request Evaluation

Parents who suspect their child may have dyslexia can request an evaluation from WISD. To initiate the process:

  1. Contact the school: Reach out to your child's teacher, school counselor, or principal to express your concerns.
  2. Submit a written request: A formal written request for an evaluation is recommended. This request should outline your concerns and reasons for suspecting dyslexia. Keep a copy for your records.
  3. Evaluation process: WISD will conduct a comprehensive evaluation to determine if the student meets the criteria for dyslexia. This evaluation may include standardized tests, observations, and a review of the student's academic history.
  4. Committee review: Following the evaluation, the ARD committee (if special education services are considered) or the 504 committee will meet to discuss the results and determine the appropriate course of action.
You have the right to participate in all aspects of the evaluation and planning process. Please communicate openly with school personnel to ensure your child receives the necessary support.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

Is my child entitled to dyslexia testing in Texas? +

Yes. Texas law requires all districts to screen students for dyslexia and related disorders in Kindergarten and First Grade, and at any grade level if a parent or teacher requests it.

What accommodations can a dyslexic child get in Texas? +

Accommodations may include extended time on tests, text-to-speech software, oral administration of tests, reduced-answer-choice formats, and use of a calculator. These can be provided through a 504 Plan or an IEP.

What is the Texas Dyslexia Handbook? +

The Texas Dyslexia Handbook is a state guidance document updated in 2024 that outlines districts' responsibilities for identifying, evaluating, and serving students with dyslexia using structured literacy methods.
