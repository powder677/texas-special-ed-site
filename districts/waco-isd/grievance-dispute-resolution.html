Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

The 10-Day Suspension Rule

Waco ISD can suspend a student for up to 3 school days for any violation of the Student Code of Conduct. They can suspend up to 10 days for more serious offenses. Crucially, if your child is suspended for 10 days or more cumulatively in a school year, it triggers specific rights, especially if your child has an IEP or 504 plan. What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

An MDR (Manifestation Determination Review) is a critical meeting that MUST occur if a student with an IEP faces a Change of Placement (see below) due to disciplinary action. The purpose is to determine if the student's behavior was:

  • Caused by or directly and substantially related to the child's disability; OR
  • The direct result of the school district's failure to implement the IEP.

If the answer to either of these questions is YES, the behavior is considered a "manifestation" of the disability. In this case, the student CANNOT be disciplined in the same way as a student without a disability. The district must then address the underlying causes of the behavior and ensure the IEP is appropriate. Demand an MDR immediately if a Change of Placement is being considered for your IEP child.

Change of Placement

A Change of Placement occurs when a student is:

  • Removed from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days; OR
  • Subjected to a pattern of removals that constitute more than 10 school days in a school year because the behavior is substantially similar to the student's previous misbehavior.

This is EXTREMELY IMPORTANT. Even if each suspension is less than 10 days, the cumulative effect of multiple short suspensions for similar behaviors CAN trigger a Change of Placement, and therefore the right to an MDR for students with IEPs! Watch for this pattern and be proactive.

If your child's placement is being changed, especially through expulsion or assignment to a Disciplinary Alternative Education Program (DAEP), immediately demand an MDR if they have an IEP or 504 plan. Waco ISD MUST convene this meeting. Document your request in writing (email and certified mail are best).

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe Waco ISD has violated your child's rights (e.g., by failing to implement their IEP, improperly suspending them, or denying an MDR), you can file a Level 1 Grievance. The specific process is outlined in Waco ISD's policies (search their website for "student grievance policy"). Generally, this involves submitting a written complaint to the school principal or designated official.

Important: There are strict deadlines! Do not delay! Find the grievance policy immediately and adhere to the timeline. Be clear, concise, and include all relevant details and supporting documentation. Keep copies of everything you submit.

TEA State Complaint

If the Level 1 Grievance doesn't resolve the issue, or if you believe Waco ISD is violating federal or state special education laws (IDEA), you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Information on filing a TEA complaint can be found on the TEA website (search for "TEA special education complaints").

A TEA complaint can be a powerful tool, but again, there are deadlines. Gather all documentation, including IEPs, suspension notices, emails, and any other evidence that supports your claim.

Due Process

The Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) guarantees your child certain due process rights, including the right to challenge the school's decisions regarding their education and discipline. This often involves a formal hearing before an impartial hearing officer.

Due process hearings are complex and require legal expertise. If you are considering pursuing due process, it is ESSENTIAL to consult with a qualified special education attorney immediately. Time is of the essence!

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
