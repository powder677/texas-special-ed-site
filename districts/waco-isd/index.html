What can I do right now if Waco ISD is ignoring my child's needs?

Submit a written evaluation request today — verbal requests do not start the legal clock. Waco ISD has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the evaluation. If your child is being disciplined or suspended, that timeline is even more urgent. Use the guides below to take action now.

Special Education in Waco ISD: What Every Parent Needs to Know

Navigating the special education system can feel overwhelming, but you are not alone. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas law, special education is designed to provide individualized instruction and support to students with disabilities who need it to access the general education curriculum. This means your child is entitled to a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) tailored to their unique needs. This may include modifications to the classroom environment, specialized instruction, assistive technology, and related services like speech therapy or occupational therapy.

Waco ISD, like all school districts in Texas, operates under the oversight of the Texas Education Agency (TEA). TEA provides guidance and resources to ensure that districts are complying with state and federal laws related to special education. Additionally, Waco ISD receives support from TEA Region 12 Education Service Center, which offers professional development, technical assistance, and other services to help improve outcomes for students with disabilities. Region 12 acts as a valuable conduit between the state and the local district, helping to implement best practices in Waco ISD classrooms.

Throughout the entire special education process, from initial evaluation to the development and implementation of an Individualized Education Program (IEP), you, as a parent, have enforceable legal rights. These rights are designed to protect your child's interests and ensure that you are an active and informed participant in all decisions made about their education. Don't hesitate to assert those rights. If you feel your child is not receiving the services they need in Waco ISD, it is crucial to understand your options and advocate for their best interests.

How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in Waco ISD

If you suspect that your child may have a disability that is affecting their ability to learn, you have the right to request a special education evaluation from Waco ISD. It is essential to understand that this request must be made in writing. A verbal request, while important to start the conversation, does not officially initiate the timeline for the evaluation process. The written request should clearly state your concerns about your child's academic, behavioral, or developmental progress and why you believe a special education evaluation is necessary. Include any relevant documentation, such as report cards, medical records, or previous evaluations, that support your request.

Once Waco ISD receives your written request and you provide written consent for the evaluation to proceed, the district has 45 school days to complete a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). This comprehensive evaluation will assess your child's strengths and weaknesses in various areas, such as academic skills, cognitive abilities, communication skills, and social-emotional functioning. Remember that Waco ISD also has a legal obligation under Child Find laws to proactively identify students who may have disabilities and may need special education services, even if a parent hasn't made a direct request. If you think your child may need an evaluation, don't wait for the district to take the lead. Write a letter today. Keep a copy for your records, and send the original to the school principal via certified mail with return receipt requested. This will provide proof that Waco ISD received your request and will help you track the timeline for the evaluation process.

ARD Meetings in Waco ISD: Your Rights at the Table

The ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee meeting is a critical component of the special education process. The ARD committee is responsible for determining whether your child is eligible for special education services and, if so, for developing an Individualized Education Program (IEP) tailored to their specific needs. The ARD committee consists of you, the parent; school administrators; teachers; and other professionals who have expertise in your child's area of need. It is vital that you attend these meetings prepared to advocate for your child's best interests.

Waco ISD is required to provide you with at least five calendar days' written notice before any ARD meeting. This notice should include the purpose of the meeting, the date, time, and location, and the names of the individuals who will be attending. It is essential to review this notice carefully and contact the school if you have any questions or concerns. During the ARD meeting, you have the right to actively participate in all discussions and decisions. If you feel overwhelmed or need more time to consider the proposed IEP, you can request a 10-day recess at any time. This allows you to take a break from the meeting and seek outside advice or support. Remember, you are not required to sign the IEP on the same day as the ARD meeting. You have the right to take the IEP home, review it carefully, and sign it later if you agree with its contents.

Remember that your voice is essential at the ARD table. The school is required to seek out your input and consider it during the ARD process. Don't be afraid to ask questions, express your concerns, and share your insights about your child's learning style, strengths, and challenges. Before your next ARD meeting in Waco ISD, download our free ARD preparation checklist. It will help you organize your thoughts and come prepared to advocate effectively for your child's needs.

Dyslexia Services in Waco ISD

Texas law requires Waco ISD to screen all students for dyslexia at certain grade levels. This screening is intended to identify students who may be at risk for dyslexia and provide them with appropriate interventions. The standards that Waco ISD must follow for dyslexia screening, identification, and intervention are outlined in the updated 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook. This handbook provides detailed guidance on best practices for supporting students with dyslexia, including the use of structured literacy approaches.

If your child is identified as having dyslexia in Waco ISD, they are entitled to receive evidence-based, structured literacy intervention. Depending on the severity of their needs, students with dyslexia may qualify for a 504 Plan, which provides accommodations and modifications to the general education classroom, or an IEP, which provides more intensive, individualized instruction and support. If your child has not been screened for dyslexia, and you have concerns about their reading or spelling skills, request a dyslexia screening in writing. A written request is essential to trigger the school's obligation to respond.

Discipline, Suspensions, and Your Child's Rights in Waco ISD

It is crucial to understand your child's rights when it comes to discipline, especially suspensions, in Waco ISD. If a student with a disability is suspended for more than 10 cumulative school days in a school year, certain protections under IDEA are triggered. Specifically, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative days constitutes a Change of Placement under IDEA and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) be completed to determine if the behavior leading to the suspension was a manifestation of the student's disability.

The Manifestation Determination Review is conducted by the ARD committee. If the ARD committee determines that the behavior was a manifestation of the student's disability or that Waco ISD failed to implement the IEP, the student cannot be suspended for more than 10 days. This is one of the most time-sensitive situations you will face as a parent. You must act quickly to ensure that your child's rights are protected. If your child has been suspended or is facing disciplinary action, immediately use our Discipline Defense Kit. This resource will help you understand your rights, gather documentation, and advocate effectively for your child's needs within the Waco ISD disciplinary process.

Filing a Complaint Against Waco ISD

As a parent of a special education student in Waco ISD, you have the right to file a complaint if you believe the district has violated your child's rights under IDEA or other applicable laws. There are several escalation paths available to you, each with its own procedures and timelines. One option is to file a State Complaint with the TEA. This is a free process, and TEA is required to investigate and resolve the complaint within 60 days. Another option is to request Mediation through TEA, which is also free and provides a neutral third party to help you and Waco ISD reach a mutually agreeable resolution.

If mediation is unsuccessful, or if you prefer a more formal legal process, you can file a Due Process Hearing request. This involves presenting your case to an impartial hearing officer who will make a binding decision. Finally, you can also file a complaint with the Federal Office for Civil Rights (OCR) if you believe Waco ISD has discriminated against your child based on their disability. Remember, these rights exist regardless of what any staff member at Waco ISD may tell you. Don't be intimidated. Start with the TEA State Complaint form today.

Frequently Asked Questions About Waco ISD Special Education

The questions every Waco ISD parent should know the answer to.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Waco ISD? +

Submit a written request to the Waco ISD Special Education Director. The district has 45 school days from your written consent to complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). Always keep a dated copy of everything you send and follow up in writing.

What are my rights at an ARD meeting in Waco ISD? +

You have the right to 5 days written notice before any ARD meeting, the right to bring an advocate or attorney, the right to request a 10-day recess at any time before decisions are finalized, and the right to refuse to sign the IEP the same day. You can also record the meeting with advance notice under Texas law.

Does Waco ISD have to screen my child for dyslexia? +

Yes. Texas law requires Waco ISD to screen all students for dyslexia. If your child has not been screened, submit a written request to the campus principal immediately. The 2024 Texas Dyslexia Handbook governs how Waco ISD must identify and serve students with dyslexia.

What if Waco ISD suspends my special education child for more than 10 days? +

A suspension exceeding 10 cumulative school days triggers a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) and is a Change of Placement under IDEA. Waco ISD must convene an ARD meeting. This is a legal emergency — contact a special education advocate immediately.
