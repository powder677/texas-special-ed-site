Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

Discipline Rights & Grievances in Temple ISD: URGENT Guide

If your child is facing disciplinary action in Temple ISD, especially suspension or potential expulsion, ACT IMMEDIATELY. Time is NOT on your side. This guide is designed to give you crucial information about your rights and how to fight back.

The 10-Day Suspension Rule

Temple ISD, like all Texas school districts, can suspend your child for up to 10 cumulative school days in a year without triggering a "Change of Placement" under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). Pay close attention! Keeping track of the number of suspension days is vital, especially if your child has an IEP (Individualized Education Program) or 504 plan. After 10 days, certain protections kick in.

Important: Even a single day of suspension can have long-term consequences. Document everything, get everything in writing, and keep records of all communication with the school.

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

If your child has an IEP or 504 plan, and the district is considering a suspension longer than 10 cumulative days, or is recommending a "Change of Placement" (explained below), a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) MUST be conducted. This meeting brings together school staff, you (the parent), and relevant members of your child's IEP or 504 team. The purpose is to determine if the behavior that led to the disciplinary action was:

  • Caused by or directly related to your child's disability.
  • A direct result of the school district's failure to implement your child's IEP or 504 plan.

CRITICAL: Prepare for this meeting. Review your child's IEP/504. Gather any relevant documentation (medical records, therapist reports, incident reports, etc.). If the MDR determines the behavior WAS a manifestation of their disability, the school MUST take steps to address the underlying cause of the behavior. This may involve revising the IEP/504, providing additional support, or changing the placement (if appropriate).

WARNING: The school may try to pressure you into agreeing with their assessment. Don't be intimidated. Stand your ground. If you disagree with the MDR outcome, you have the right to challenge it through a due process hearing (see below).

Change of Placement

A "Change of Placement" occurs when a student with an IEP or 504 plan is removed from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days or when a series of removals constitutes a pattern. This could involve suspension, expulsion, or being moved to a different school or program. Even seemingly small changes can constitute a change of placement.

PROTECT YOUR CHILD! A Change of Placement triggers additional IDEA protections, including the right to a MDR and continued educational services, even while suspended or expelled. The school MUST provide services to enable your child to continue to participate in the general education curriculum, although in another setting, and to progress toward meeting the goals set out in the child's IEP.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe Temple ISD has violated your child's rights or is not following proper procedures, you can file a grievance. Start at Level 1, which typically involves submitting a written complaint to the principal or relevant administrator. Be specific! Detail the dates, times, individuals involved, and the specific violation. Include supporting documentation. Keep a copy of your grievance and proof of delivery. The school is required to respond within a specific timeframe (check Temple ISD policy; usually within 10 business days). If you are not satisfied with the response at Level 1, you can escalate to higher levels within the district.

TEA State Complaint

You also have the right to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA) if you believe Temple ISD has violated state or federal special education laws. TEA complaints have specific requirements, including timelines and documentation. Research the TEA complaint process thoroughly and ensure you meet all criteria. This is a powerful tool, but it requires meticulous preparation.

Due Process

If you disagree with the outcome of an MDR, or if you believe Temple ISD has violated your child's special education rights and attempts to resolve the issue through grievances and TEA complaints have failed, you have the right to request a due process hearing. This is a formal legal proceeding where you can present evidence and arguments to an impartial hearing officer. Due process hearings are complex and can be costly. Consider seeking legal representation from a special education attorney.

This is an overwhelming process. Don't face it alone. Seek support from parent advocacy groups, special education attorneys, or disability rights organizations. Your child's future depends on it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
