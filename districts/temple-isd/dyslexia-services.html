What are my child's dyslexia rights in Texas?

If your child is identified, they are entitled to a structured literacy program and — if eligible — a 504 Plan or IEP with accommodations like extended time and audio support.

Dyslexia Services in Temple ISD

Dyslexia Services in Temple ISD

Texas Dyslexia Handbook 2024

Temple ISD adheres to the guidelines outlined in the Texas Dyslexia Handbook. The 2024 update to the Handbook provides the most current information regarding identification, evaluation, and instruction for students with dyslexia in Texas public schools. It emphasizes the use of evidence-based practices and multi-tiered systems of support (MTSS). The Handbook is a vital resource for parents, educators, and administrators to ensure consistent and effective dyslexia services are provided across the district. Key updates in the 2024 handbook often include clarifications on screening procedures, specific criteria for identification, and updated research on effective interventions. Parents are encouraged to review the Texas Dyslexia Handbook, available on the Texas Education Agency (TEA) website. Familiarizing yourself with its contents is essential for understanding your child's rights and the support they are entitled to receive.

Mandatory Screening

Temple ISD implements mandatory dyslexia screening for all students in kindergarten and first grade, as mandated by Texas law. This initial screening aims to identify students who are at risk for developing dyslexia. The screenings typically assess foundational reading skills such as phonological awareness, phonological memory, rapid naming, and letter-sound knowledge. Students who demonstrate characteristics of dyslexia during these screenings will receive targeted interventions and may be referred for further evaluation if necessary. Parents will be notified of their child's screening results and any recommended next steps. In addition to the mandatory screenings, students in other grades may be screened if concerns arise from teachers, parents, or the student themselves. Early identification and intervention are crucial for maximizing a student's reading potential.

Structured Literacy Programs

Temple ISD utilizes Structured Literacy programs to provide explicit, systematic, and multi-sensory instruction for students with dyslexia. Structured Literacy addresses all key components of reading: phonology (speech sounds), orthography (spelling), morphology (word parts), syntax (sentence structure), and semantics (meaning). Instruction is sequential and cumulative, building upon previously learned skills. Common characteristics of Structured Literacy programs used in Temple ISD include: explicit teaching of phonics rules, opportunities for multi-sensory learning (visual, auditory, kinesthetic), and consistent practice with decoding and encoding (reading and spelling). The specific program used may vary depending on the student's individual needs and grade level. Parents are encouraged to communicate with their child's teacher and the campus dyslexia specialist to learn more about the specific Structured Literacy program being used.

504 vs IEP for Dyslexia

Students with dyslexia in Temple ISD may receive support through a 504 plan or an Individualized Education Program (IEP), depending on the severity of their learning needs. A 504 plan provides accommodations to ensure a student with a disability has equal access to the learning environment. This focuses on removing barriers to learning. An IEP, on the other hand, is designed for students with disabilities who require specialized instruction to make progress in the general education curriculum. IEPs are more comprehensive and include measurable goals, specially designed instruction, and related services. The determination of whether a student qualifies for a 504 plan or an IEP is based on a comprehensive evaluation and the recommendations of the ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee. Key differences include the level of intensity of services and the depth of the individualized learning plan.

Accommodations List

Accommodations for students with dyslexia in Temple ISD are designed to provide support without changing the essential content of the curriculum. These accommodations may be provided through a 504 plan or an IEP. Common accommodations include:

  • Extended time on tests and assignments
  • Audiobooks or text-to-speech software
  • Use of assistive technology (e.g., word processors with spell check)
  • Preferential seating
  • Modified assignments (e.g., reduced workload, alternative assessments)
  • Visual aids and graphic organizers
  • Read aloud accommodations (depending on specific subject and skill being assessed)
  • Frequent breaks
  • Copies of notes
  • Access to a quiet testing environment
The specific accommodations provided will be individualized based on the student's needs.

How to Request Evaluation

If you suspect your child may have dyslexia, contact their teacher or the school principal at your child's campus in Temple ISD. You can also contact the district's special education department. A written request for a dyslexia evaluation is recommended. This request should outline your concerns and any observations you have made regarding your child's reading difficulties. The school will then initiate the evaluation process, which may include standardized assessments and observations. Parental consent is required before any evaluation can take place. After the evaluation is completed, the school will convene a meeting to discuss the results and determine if your child meets the criteria for dyslexia. If dyslexia is identified, the team will develop an appropriate plan of support, which may include a 504 plan or an IEP. Timely communication with the school is crucial throughout the evaluation process.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

Is my child entitled to dyslexia testing in Texas? +

Yes. Texas law requires all districts to screen students for dyslexia and related disorders in Kindergarten and First Grade, and at any grade level if a parent or teacher requests it.

What accommodations can a dyslexic child get in Texas? +

Accommodations may include extended time on tests, text-to-speech software, oral administration of tests, reduced-answer-choice formats, and use of a calculator. These can be provided through a 504 Plan or an IEP.

What is the Texas Dyslexia Handbook? +

The Texas Dyslexia Handbook is a state guidance document updated in 2024 that outlines districts' responsibilities for identifying, evaluating, and serving students with dyslexia using structured literacy methods.
