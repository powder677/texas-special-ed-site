How to Effectively Contact Temple ISD Special Education Staff

Building strong communication with your child's Temple ISD special education team is crucial. Here are some tips for effective communication:

  • Best Times to Contact: Generally, mornings before school starts or after school hours are the best times to reach teachers by phone. Email is often a more convenient method for initial contact.
  • Email vs. Phone: For detailed information or complex issues, email allows you to clearly articulate your concerns and provides a written record. For quick questions or urgent matters, a phone call might be more appropriate.
  • What to Say in Your First Message: Introduce yourself, your child's name and grade, and briefly explain the reason for your contact. Be specific about what you need from the staff member. For example, "I'm John Smith, parent of Jane Smith in 3rd grade. I'm contacting you regarding her recent IEP goals."

Important Tip: Always follow up any phone conversations with an email summarizing the discussion and any agreed-upon actions. This creates a clear record and ensures everyone is on the same page.

What to Do If You Can't Reach Anyone

If you're having trouble reaching someone at Temple ISD regarding your child's special education services, follow these steps:

  • Step 1: Contact the teacher directly. Allow 2 business days for a response.
  • Step 2: If you don't hear back, contact the Campus Special Education Coordinator at your child's school.
  • Step 3: If you still need assistance, contact the Temple ISD Special Education Director. Their contact information is listed below.
  • Step 4: As a last resort, you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

Remember your rights under Texas Education Code Chapter 29, which outlines special education services for students with disabilities.

Your Right to District Records

As a parent, you have the right to access your child's educational records.

  • FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act): Temple ISD must provide you with access to your child's educational records within 45 days of your written request.
  • Texas Public Information Act (Texas Government Code, Chapter 552): You can request information about Temple ISD staff, including their contact information.

To submit a records request, send a written request to the Temple ISD Special Education Director. Clearly state the specific records you are requesting and include your child's name and date of birth. Keep a copy of your request for your records.
