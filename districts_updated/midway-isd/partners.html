About Special Education in Midway ISD

Midway ISD serves the Texas area, providing a comprehensive special education program for students with disabilities. The district is committed to ensuring that all students have access to a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment. Midway ISD operates under the oversight of TEA Region unknown, adhering to all state and federal regulations regarding special education services.

The Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) process is a crucial part of special education in Midway ISD. After a referral for special education services, the district has a timeline of 45 school days to complete the Full and Individual Evaluation (FIE). Parents will receive written notice of the ARD meeting at least 5 school days prior to the scheduled meeting, allowing ample time to prepare and participate in the development of their child's Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Common Questions from Midway ISD Families

What types of services are available for my child in Midway ISD?

Midway ISD offers a range of special education services, including specialized instruction, related services such as speech therapy and occupational therapy, and accommodations and modifications in the general education classroom. The specific services your child receives will be determined by the ARD committee based on their individual needs as outlined in their IEP.

Who do I contact if I have concerns about my child's progress in special education?

The first point of contact is usually your child's special education teacher. You can also reach out to the campus principal or the special education department at the district level. Open communication is key to ensuring your child receives the support they need.

How can I be more involved in the ARD process?

Parents are essential members of the ARD committee. Review your child's evaluation reports, prepare questions, and actively participate in the discussion. Don't hesitate to share your insights and advocate for your child's needs. You can also bring an advocate or other support person to the ARD meeting.

Practical Tip for Midway ISD Parents

As a district with approximately 10,000 students, Midway ISD's special education department may have one director handling many responsibilities. To ensure your communication is documented, consider using email to communicate with the special education director and other key personnel. This creates a paper trail of your concerns and requests, which can be helpful if you need to reference past conversations or decisions.
