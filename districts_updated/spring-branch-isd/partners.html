About Special Education in Spring Branch ISD

Spring Branch ISD serves the Texas area and is committed to providing a high-quality special education program for students with disabilities. The district operates under the oversight of TEA Region unknown and adheres to all state and federal regulations regarding special education services. SBISD strives to create inclusive learning environments where all students can reach their full potential.

When a student is suspected of having a disability, Spring Branch ISD follows a specific timeline to ensure a thorough evaluation and individualized support. The district has 45 school days to complete the Full and Individual Evaluation (FIE) after receiving parental consent. Parents will receive at least a 5-day written notice before any Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committee meeting, allowing ample time to prepare and participate in the decision-making process for their child's education.

Common Questions from Spring Branch ISD Families

What if I disagree with the ARD committee's decisions?

You have the right to disagree with the ARD committee's decisions. You can request another ARD meeting to further discuss your concerns, or you can pursue options such as mediation or a complaint with the Texas Education Agency. Be sure to document your concerns in writing.

How do I request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at SBISD expense?

If you disagree with the school's evaluation, you have the right to request an IEE. SBISD may grant your request or initiate a due process hearing to show that its evaluation was appropriate. Be prepared to explain specifically why you believe the SBISD evaluation was insufficient.

What specialized supports are available for my child's specific disability within SBISD?

SBISD offers a range of specialized supports, including specialized instruction, assistive technology, related services (like speech therapy or occupational therapy), and modified curriculum. The specific supports available will depend on your child's individual needs as determined by the ARD committee. You can ask the ARD committee for specific examples of how those supports will be provided.

Tip for Parents in Spring Branch ISD

With approximately 10,000 students, Spring Branch ISD might have a more centralized special education administration. Often, a single special education director oversees many aspects of the program. Given this, it's highly recommended to communicate with the district through email rather than phone calls. This ensures you have a written record of all communication and requests, creating a valuable paper trail for future reference.
