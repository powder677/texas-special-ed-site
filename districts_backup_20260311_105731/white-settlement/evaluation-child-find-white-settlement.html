Child Find is a federal requirement under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) that mandates school districts like WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD identify, locate, and evaluate all children suspected of having disabilities. What Is a Full Individual Evaluation?

An FIE evaluation in WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine whether your child has a disability and whether they qualify for special education services. This evaluation examines cognitive abilities, academic performance, social-emotional functioning, physical health, and communication skills.

The evaluation process for WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD special education evaluation requests is thorough and must use multiple assessment methods to ensure accurate identification of your child's needs.

Step 1: Submit Your Written Request

The first critical action is to submit a written request for evaluation to WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD. While you can make a verbal request, having everything in writing creates an official record and ensures compliance with the WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD 45 day timeline.

Address your request to the Director of Special Education at WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD. Include the following in your letter:

  • Your child's full name, date of birth, and grade level
  • Specific concerns about your child's learning, behavior, or development
  • Examples of difficulties you've observed at home or school
  • Any previous evaluations or assessments your child has received
  • Your contact information and preferred communication method
  • A clear statement requesting a Full Individual Evaluation

Keep a copy for your records and deliver the request to WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD by hand, email, or certified mail. Request written confirmation of receipt from WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD.

Step 2: Understand the WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD 45 Day Timeline

Once WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD receives your written evaluation request, the district has 45 calendar days to complete the assessment and hold an eligibility meeting. This WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD 45 day timeline is a legal requirement, though it can be extended by mutual written agreement between you and the district.

The 45-day clock starts the day after WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD receives your written request. Mark this date on your calendar and track progress carefully.

Step 3: Provide Informed Consent

Within the WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD 45 day timeline, the district must request your informed consent before beginning the evaluation. You will receive a Prior Written Notice (PWN) and a Procedural Safeguards Notice explaining your rights.

The consent form must describe the evaluation in detail, including:

  • Types of tests and assessments that will be administered
  • Who will conduct the evaluation
  • Where evaluations will take place
  • Your right to have an independent evaluation if you disagree with WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD results
  • Information about confidentiality and records

Do not sign consent forms immediately. Take time to review them carefully. You can request clarification from WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD special education staff before proceeding.

What to Expect During the WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD FIE Evaluation

Assessment Components

A comprehensive WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD FIE evaluation typically includes multiple assessment areas:

  • Cognitive Assessment: Testing intellectual ability and reasoning
  • Academic Achievement: Reading, math, writing, and spelling performance
  • Social-Emotional Assessment: Behavior, emotional functioning, and social skills
  • Communication Evaluation: Speech, language, and hearing screening
  • Motor Skills: Fine and gross motor development assessment
  • Vision and Hearing Screening: Identifying sensory issues
  • Classroom Observation: Direct observation of your child in educational settings

WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD must use multiple measures and cannot rely on a single assessment tool to make eligibility decisions.

Who Conducts the Evaluation?

For a WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD special education evaluation, trained personnel conduct each assessment component. This may include:

  • School psychologists
  • Special education teachers
  • Speech-language pathologists
  • Occupational or physical therapists
  • General education teachers

You can request information about the evaluator's credentials and experience.

What Happens at the IEP/ARD Meeting

  • The special education director or representative
  • General education teacher(s)
  • Special education teacher(s)
  • School psychologist or evaluator
  • Other specialists who conducted assessments

During this meeting, WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD staff will present evaluation findings and discuss whether your child meets eligibility criteria under IDEA.

Your Rights and Protections During the Process

Procedural Safeguards

Texas and federal law provide significant protections for parents throughout the WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD evaluation request process:

  • Right to Notice: WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD must provide prior written notice in your native language
  • Right to Participate: You must be included in all meetings regarding your child
  • Right to Records: You can inspect all educational records and evaluation materials
  • Right to Independent Evaluation: If you disagree with WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD FIE evaluation results, you can obtain an independent evaluation at district expense under certain circumstances
  • Right to Due Process: You can file a complaint or request mediation if you disagree with WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD decisions

When WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD May Refuse to Evaluate

While WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD must honor child find requirements, the district can refuse evaluation only in specific circumstances:

  • You refuse to provide consent after being fully informed
  • The suspected disability is purely based on lack of instruction in reading or math
  • The suspected disability is English language learner status without accompanying disability
  • You request evaluation for a child under age 3 (different procedures apply)

If WHITE SETTLEMENT ISD refuses your evaluation request, they must provide written explanation of their reasons.
