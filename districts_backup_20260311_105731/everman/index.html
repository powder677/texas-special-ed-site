Understanding Special Education in EVERMAN ISD

EVERMAN ISD special education services are designed to meet the unique needs of students with disabilities under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act. Our district provides a continuum of services ranging from full inclusion in regular classrooms to specialized resource programs and separate settings when appropriate.

The primary vehicle for special education services in EVERMAN ISD is the Individualized Education Program (IEP), a legally binding document that outlines your child's academic and functional goals, accommodations, modifications, and related services. Every student receiving special education in EVERMAN ISD has an IEP developed by a team that includes parents, teachers, administrators, and specialists.

EVERMAN ISD families benefit from a range of special education categories including:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
  • Emotional/Behavioral Disorders
  • Intellectual Disabilities
  • Specific Learning Disabilities
  • Speech/Language Impairments
  • Visual and Hearing Impairments
  • Orthopedic Disabilities
  • Dyslexia and Related Literacy Disorders
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Other Health Impairments

EVERMAN ISD IEP Services & Development

The EVERMAN ISD IEP services process begins with a comprehensive evaluation of your child's strengths, needs, and educational performance. This evaluation determines eligibility for special education and identifies the specific services your child requires.

The IEP Development Process

Your child's IEP is developed collaboratively during an EVERMAN ISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal meeting). This meeting brings together:

  • Parents/Guardians - you are essential team members with decision-making authority
  • Special Education Teacher - knows your child's performance in special education
  • General Education Teacher - provides information about classroom performance and curriculum
  • School Administrator - ensures resources and implementation support
  • District Representative - coordinates services across EVERMAN ISD
  • Related Service Providers - such as speech therapists, counselors, or occupational therapists

During the EVERMAN ISD ARD meeting, the team reviews evaluation results, discusses your child's current performance, and establishes measurable annual goals. The meeting typically lasts 60-90 minutes and results in a written IEP document that guides instruction and services for one year.

IEP Components & Services

EVERMAN ISD IEP services may include:

  1. Specialized Instruction - delivered in resource rooms or pull-out settings
  2. Speech/Language Therapy - for communication disorders
  3. Occupational Therapy - for fine motor and functional skills
  4. Physical Therapy - for gross motor development and mobility
  5. Behavioral Supports - including positive behavior intervention plans
  6. Counseling Services - for emotional and mental health needs
  7. Assistive Technology - including specialized software and devices
  8. Accommodations & Modifications - adjusting how content is taught or tested

EVERMAN ISD families can request an ARD meeting at any time during the school year if services need adjustment. Annual reviews are required, and evaluations must be redone at least every three years to ensure continued eligibility.

EVERMAN ISD Dyslexia Program

The EVERMAN ISD dyslexia program provides specialized instruction for students with dyslexia and related literacy disorders. Texas Education Code Section 38.003 requires all school districts to identify and serve students with dyslexia using evidence-based interventions.

If you believe your child has dyslexia, EVERMAN ISD can conduct a dyslexia evaluation that looks at reading fluency, decoding, spelling, and other literacy skills. The EVERMAN ISD dyslexia program utilizes research-based reading programs and intensive small-group instruction.

Dyslexia Services in EVERMAN ISD

  • Universal Screening - all students screened for reading difficulties
  • Diagnostic Assessment - comprehensive evaluation for dyslexia characteristics
  • Specialized Instruction - structured literacy and phonics-based programs
  • Progress Monitoring - regular assessment to ensure intervention effectiveness
  • Parent Communication - monthly reports on reading progress
  • Accommodation Plans - extended time on tests, text-to-speech technology, and other supports

Many students in the EVERMAN area benefit from early identification and intervention in dyslexia, often preventing the need for more intensive special education services. If your child struggles with reading despite strong instruction, request a dyslexia evaluation through your child's school.

EVERMAN ISD Parent Rights & Procedural Safeguards

EVERMAN ISD parent rights are protected under IDEA and Texas Education Code. Understanding these rights is essential for advocating effectively for your child.

Core Parent Rights in EVERMAN ISD

EVERMAN ISD parent rights include:

  • Right to Notice - receive written notice before any evaluation or service change
  • Right to Consent - give or withhold permission for initial evaluation and services
  • Right to Participate - attend all ARD meetings and have meaningful input
  • Right to Independent Evaluation - obtain a second opinion at district expense if you disagree with EVERMAN ISD evaluation
  • Right to Records - access your child's educational and medical records
  • Right to Due Process - file a formal complaint if you believe EVERMAN ISD violated your rights
  • Right to an Advocate - bring a special ed advocate EVERMAN or attorney to meetings
  • Right to Dispute Resolution - request mediation or due process hearing

EVERMAN ISD must provide you with a document explaining EVERMAN ISD parent rights in your native language at the beginning of special education services and whenever policies change.

Requesting an Evaluation

If you believe your child has a disability and needs special education services, you can request an evaluation in writing to your child's school principal or the EVERMAN ISD Special Education Department. The district has 15 calendar days to respond and must conduct a full evaluation within 60 calendar days.

You do not need to wait for a teacher referral—you can request an evaluation at any time. Document your concerns in writing, including specific examples of how your child struggles academically or behaviorally compared to peers.

Common Questions Parents Ask About EVERMAN ISD Special Education

What does it cost to access special education in EVERMAN ISD?

Special education services are provided at no cost to parents in EVERMAN ISD. All evaluations, ARD meetings, and services are funded by federal and state special education dollars. You cannot be charged for meetings, evaluations, or IEP-related services.

Can my child attend regular classes while receiving EVERMAN ISD IEP services?

Yes. The principle of Least Restrictive Environment (LRE) requires EVERMAN ISD to educate students with disabilities alongside non-disabled peers to the maximum extent appropriate. Most students receiving EVERMAN ISD IEP services spend significant time in regular classrooms with accommodations and modifications.

What if I disagree with EVERMAN ISD's evaluation or IEP?

You have several options. You can request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at district expense, request a due process hearing, or file a state complaint. Consider consulting with a special ed advocate EVERMAN before pursuing formal disputes.

How often should EVERMAN ISD ARD meetings occur?

An EVERMAN ISD ARD meeting must occur at least annually. However, you can request additional meetings whenever your child's needs change, services are ineffective, or you want to discuss concerns about progress.
