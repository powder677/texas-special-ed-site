Understanding Special Education in FREDERICKSBURG ISD

FREDERICKSBURG ISD special education serves students ages 3-21 who have disabilities that impact their ability to access and benefit from general education. Our mission is to provide each child with a free, appropriate public education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment possible, as required by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and the Texas Education Code.

With an enrollment of 2,876 students across FREDERICKSBURG, we maintain specialized programs and trained staff dedicated to meeting the needs of children with various disabilities. Our special education services include classroom instruction, related services, behavioral support, and transition planning to prepare students for post-secondary success.

Who Qualifies for Special Education Services

Students in FREDERICKSBURG ISD may qualify for special education if they have a disability in one of these categories:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
  • Deaf-Blindness
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Hearing Impairment or Deafness
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment (OHI)
  • Specific Learning Disability (SLD), including dyslexia
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI)
  • Visual Impairment or Blindness

Having a disability alone does not automatically qualify a child for special education. The child must also need special education services to benefit from a general education curriculum. FREDERICKSBURG ISD evaluates each child individually to determine eligibility.

FREDERICKSBURG ISD ARD Meetings and IEP Development

If your child is found eligible for special education, FREDERICKSBURG ISD will schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to develop your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP). The ARD committee at FREDERICKSBURG ISD includes parents, teachers, special education staff, and sometimes the child.

What to Expect at Your FREDERICKSBURG ISD ARD Meeting

The FREDERICKSBURG ISD ARD meeting is your opportunity to collaborate with educators to design a program tailored to your child's needs. During this meeting, the team reviews evaluation results, discusses your child's strengths and challenges, and develops an IEP document.

Your ARD meeting will address:

  • Special education services, placement, and program options
  • Related services (speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, etc.)

  • Behavior support plans if needed
  • Transition planning for students age 14 and older
  • Progress monitoring and evaluation schedules

Parents in FREDERICKSBURG ISD should come to the ARD meeting prepared with questions, concerns, and input about your child's needs. You know your child best, and your perspective is essential to creating an effective IEP.

Your Role in the FREDERICKSBURG ISD ARD Process

As a parent, you are an equal member of the ARD committee for your child in FREDERICKSBURG ISD. This means your voice matters in all decisions about your child's education.
