Understanding BULLARD ISD Special Education Programs

BULLARD ISD special education serves approximately 450+ students with disabilities across our district, from kindergarten through age 22. We provide a full continuum of services designed to help each child succeed academically, socially, and emotionally.

Our special education programs in BULLARD are based on the Individualized Education Program (IEP) model, which ensures every child receives services tailored to their specific needs. We serve students with a wide range of disabilities, including:

  • Specific Learning Disabilities (SLD)
  • Speech and Language Impairments
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
  • Emotional and Behavioral Disorders
  • Intellectual Disabilities
  • Other Health Impairments
  • Visual and Hearing Impairments
  • Orthopedic Impairments
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Traumatic Brain Injury

Parents in BULLARD ISD can feel confident that our special education staff includes certified special education teachers, speech-language pathologists, counselors, and support specialists trained to deliver evidence-based interventions.

Getting Started: The BULLARD ISD Referral and Evaluation Process

If you suspect your child may have a disability, the first step is requesting an evaluation through BULLARD ISD. Parents, teachers, or other professionals can initiate this process.

Steps for Requesting an Evaluation

  1. Submit a Written Request: Contact your child's campus or the BULLARD ISD Special Education Department with a written request for evaluation. You can submit this via email, letter, or in person.
  2. Receive Written Notice: Within 15 calendar days, BULLARD ISD will provide you with written notice of the evaluation proposal and explain your parent rights.
  3. Sign Consent Form: Review and sign the evaluation consent form. BULLARD ISD cannot proceed with evaluation without your written consent.
  4. Comprehensive Evaluation: The evaluation team will assess your child using multiple measures, including classroom observations, assessments, parent and teacher input, and medical records.
  5. Eligibility Meeting: Within 60 calendar days, BULLARD ISD will hold a meeting to review evaluation results and determine eligibility for special education services.

If your child is found eligible for services through BULLARD ISD special education, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be developed at your initial IEP meeting.

Special Considerations for Early Intervention

Parents of children birth to age three should know that BULLARD ISD coordinates with the Texas Early Intervention Program (EIP) for younger children. If your preschool-age child (ages 3-5) shows developmental delays, contact BULLARD ISD's Special Education Department to explore Early Childhood Special Education (ECSE) services.

BULLARD ISD IEP Services and Your Child's Education Plan

The BULLARD ISD IEP is the legal document that outlines all special education services your child will receive. This individualized plan is developed collaboratively between school staff and parents to address your child's unique learning needs.

What's Included in Your IEP

Your child's IEP under BULLARD ISD special education includes:

  • Present Levels of Academic and Functional Performance (PLAFP): Current strengths and needs across academic, social, behavioral, and functional areas
  • Measurable Annual Goals: Specific, realistic goals your child will work toward each school year
  • Special Education Services: The specific services BULLARD ISD will provide, including frequency, duration, and location
  • Least Restrictive Environment (LRE) Placement: Where your child will receive services—general education, resource, self-contained, or alternative settings
  • Accommodations and Modifications: Changes to the general curriculum or testing procedures to support your child's success
  • Transition Services: For students age 16 and older in BULLARD ISD, plans for post-secondary education, employment, and independent living

BULLARD ISD IEP services may include special education instruction, speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, behavioral support, and assistive technology, depending on your child's individual needs.

The BULLARD ISD ARD Meeting Process

The BULLARD ISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal meeting) is where all special education decisions are made. Understanding this process empowers you to be an active participant in your child's education.

When ARD Meetings Occur

BULLARD ISD families should expect ARD meetings:

  • Initial IEP Meeting: After your child is found eligible for special education services
  • Annual Review: Once per school year to review progress and update the IEP
  • IEP Revision Meetings: When changes to services or placement are needed
  • Reevaluation Meetings: Every three years, or sooner if requested by parents or staff
  • Dismissal Meeting: If your child no longer qualifies for special education services in BULLARD ISD

Preparing for Your BULLARD ISD ARD Meeting

To make the most of your BULLARD ISD ARD meeting, come prepared with:

  • Any outside evaluations or medical reports relevant to your child's needs
  • Questions or concerns you want addressed
  • Examples of your child's strengths and challenges at home
  • Goals you'd like included in the IEP
  • A support person (advocate, family member, or friend) if desired

Remember: You are an equal member of the ARD team in BULLARD ISD. Your input is valuable, and decisions should be made collaboratively, not unilaterally by school staff.

BULLARD ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

Federal and Texas law guarantee specific rights to parents of children in special education. Understanding BULLARD ISD parent rights ensures you can advocate effectively for your child.

Key Parent Rights in BULLARD ISD Special Education

  • Right to Notice: BULLARD ISD must provide written notice of evaluation proposals, IEP meetings, and changes to services in your native language
  • Right to Consent: You must give permission for initial evaluation and initial IEP placement. You can revoke consent at any time.
  • Right to Participate: You have the right to attend all ARD meetings and be an equal participant in decision-making for BULLARD ISD special education services
  • Right to Records: You can request and review all educational records and evaluation materials related to your child
  • Right to Independent Evaluation: If you disagree with BULLARD ISD's evaluation, you can obtain an independent evaluation at the district's expense under certain circumstances
  • Right to Dispute Resolution: If you disagree with BULLARD ISD's special education decisions, you can request mediation or due process hearing
  • Right to Prior Written Notice: BULLARD ISD must provide written explanation before proposing or refusing to make changes to your child's education

Texas Education Code Section 29.001 and the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) protect these rights for parents in BULLARD ISD.

Navigating Disagreements

If you disagree with decisions made at a BULLARD ISD ARD meeting or believe your child is not receiving appropriate services, you have options. Consider requesting an IEP revision meeting to discuss your concerns. If informal resolution doesn't work, BULLARD ISD families can pursue mediation or a due process hearing.

BULLARD ISD Dyslexia Program and Reading Support Services

Dyslexia is one of the most common learning disabilities, affecting 15-20% of the population. BULLARD ISD dyslexia program provides specialized instruction for students who struggle with reading.

Identifying Dyslexia in BULLARD ISD

If your child shows signs of dyslexia—such as difficulty with phonics, slow reading speed, letter reversals, or spelling challenges—request a dyslexia evaluation through BULLARD ISD. Teachers and parents can both initiate this process.

Under Texas law (Senate Bill 540), students in BULLARD ISD must be screened for dyslexia by the end of first grade. If your child qualifies for the BULLARD ISD dyslexia program, they'll receive intensive, specialized instruction using evidence-based reading interventions.

Services Available Through BULLARD ISD Dyslexia Program

  • Dyslexia Specialist Instruction: Direct instruction from trained dyslexia specialists in BULLARD ISD
  • Structured Literacy Programs: Research-based curricula focusing on phonology, phonics, fluency, and comprehension
  • Progress Monitoring: Regular assessment to track your child's response to intervention
  • Parent Communication: Regular updates on your child's progress in the BULLARD ISD dyslexia program
  • General Education Support: Accommodations and modifications in regular classrooms to support reading success

If your child qualifies for special education through BULLARD ISD, dyslexia services can be incorporated into their BULLARD ISD IEP to ensure comprehensive support.
