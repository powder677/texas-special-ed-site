Understanding Special Education in ALEDO ISD

Special education in ALEDO ISD is designed to provide free, appropriate public education (FAPE) to students with disabilities ages 3 through 21. Under federal law (IDEA) and Texas Education Code, ALEDO ISD must identify, evaluate, and provide services to students whose disabilities affect their educational performance.

ALEDO special education services are individualized based on your child's unique needs. No two Individualized Education Plans (IEPs) are exactly alike, and that's by design. The special education process exists to ensure your child receives the specific support needed to access the general education curriculum.

Who Qualifies for ALEDO ISD Special Education?

Students may qualify for ALEDO ISD special education services if they have one of these qualifying disabilities:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder – including significant differences in social communication and restricted behaviors
  • Dyslexia and related disorders – including dyscalculia and dysgraphia
  • Emotional disturbance – including anxiety, depression, and behavioral challenges
  • Hearing impairment – including deafness
  • Intellectual disability – including significant cognitive and adaptive functioning differences
  • Orthopedic impairment – including cerebral palsy and physical disabilities
  • Other health impairment – including ADHD, epilepsy, and chronic health conditions
  • Speech or language impairment
  • Traumatic brain injury
  • Visual impairment – including blindness

Simply having a disability diagnosis doesn't automatically qualify a student for ALEDO ISD special education. The disability must negatively impact educational performance, and the student must require special education services to succeed.

The Referral and Evaluation Process

The first step toward ALEDO ISD special education services is referral and evaluation. Parents, teachers, or other school personnel can refer a student for evaluation. If you believe your child needs services through ALEDO ISD, don't wait—request an evaluation in writing to your child's campus.

How to Request an Evaluation

Send a written request to your child's school principal or special education coordinator. Your request doesn't need to be formal, but it should clearly state that you believe your child may need special education services. Keep a copy for your records.

Once ALEDO ISD receives your referral, the district has 15 school days to notify you in writing about the request for evaluation and obtain your written consent. You'll receive detailed information about what will be evaluated and why.

The Evaluation Timeline

After you sign consent for evaluation, ALEDO ISD has 60 calendar days to complete the evaluation and hold an ARD meeting to discuss results. This timeline is required by Texas Education Code and IDEA.

The evaluation typically includes:

  • Academic achievement testing in reading, math, and writing
  • Cognitive ability assessment
  • Social-emotional and behavioral evaluation
  • Speech and language screening (if appropriate)
  • Review of classroom performance and teacher input
  • Parent/guardian interview and history
  • Medical or health history review

ALEDO ISD ARD Meetings and IEP Development

The ARD (Admission, Review, Dismissal) meeting is where parents and educators come together to discuss your child's special education needs and create an Individualized Education Plan. For ALEDO ISD families, the ARD meeting is your opportunity to be a full partner in planning your child's education.

What Happens in an ALEDO ISD ARD Meeting?

Your ALEDO ISD ARD meeting will include your child's parents, the special education teacher, the general education teacher, an administrator, and anyone else with relevant information about your child. If your child is 15 or older, transition planning discussions will also occur.

During the meeting, the ARD committee will:

  1. Review evaluation results and determine if your child qualifies for special education
  2. Discuss your child's strengths, weaknesses, and learning needs
  3. Determine the special education services your child requires
  4. Write measurable annual goals focused on closing achievement gaps
  5. Decide on the least restrictive environment (LRE) for your child
  6. Address any behavior, communication, or sensory needs
  7. Plan for transition services if your child is 15 or older

Your Rights During ALEDO ISD IEP Services Planning

ALEDO ISD special education is built on the principle that parents are equal partners. You have the right to:

  • Receive advance notice of the ARD meeting with at least five school days' notice
  • Bring an advocate or attorney to support you during the meeting
  • Participate in all decisions about your child's placement and services
  • Request independent evaluations if you disagree with ALEDO ISD's assessment
  • Ask questions and request clarification about any assessment results or proposed services
  • Request reconsideration of any placement or service decision
  • Have the IEP reviewed and revised annually, or more often if needed

ALEDO ISD Dyslexia Program and Support

  • Specialized reading instruction focused on decoding and fluency

ALEDO ISD Parent Rights and Due Process

Federal law and Texas Education Code guarantee significant rights to parents of students in special education. Parents in ALEDO ISD have specific protections and procedural safeguards throughout the special education process.

Your ALEDO ISD Parent Rights Include

Right to written notice: ALEDO ISD must provide written notice before evaluating your child, changing their placement, or denying services. The notice must be in your native language if you're not fluent in English.

Right to informed consent: You must provide written permission before ALEDO ISD can conduct an initial evaluation or change your child's placement. You can always withdraw consent in writing.

Right to access records: You can request and review all special education records within 10 school days. ALEDO ISD must provide copies at no cost if the fee would prevent you from accessing records.

Right to confidentiality: All special education information about your child is confidential. ALEDO ISD can only share information with people who have a legitimate educational interest.

Understanding ALEDO ISD Parent Rights Documentation

At your first ARD meeting and annually thereafter, ALEDO ISD must provide you with a complete explanation of your parent rights. This document explains:

  • How to request an evaluation
  • How to appeal ALEDO ISD decisions through due process
  • How to request mediation
  • Your right to an independent educational evaluation (IEE)
  • Disciplinary procedures for students with disabilities

If you have questions about your specific rights in ALEDO ISD, ask your special education coordinator for this document in writing.

When You Disagree with ALEDO ISD Decisions

Parents in ALEDO ISD have multiple options if they disagree with the district's special education decisions. You don't have to accept every recommendation, and you have formal procedures to challenge decisions you believe are wrong.

Resolving Disagreements with ALEDO ISD

Step 1: Informal Problem-Solving – Talk directly with your child's teacher, special education coordinator, or campus administrator. Many disagreements are resolved through simple conversation and additional information.

Step 2: Request a Follow-Up ARD Meeting – Ask ALEDO ISD to reconvene the ARD committee to discuss your concerns. The district must schedule this meeting within 10 school days of your written request.

Step 3: Request Mediation – Texas offers free mediation services to help ALEDO ISD families and the district resolve disputes. A neutral mediator facilitates discussion without taking sides.

Step 4: File a Due Process Complaint – If other options don't resolve the disagreement, you can file a formal due process complaint with the Texas Education Agency. This initiates an investigation and may lead to a hearing.
