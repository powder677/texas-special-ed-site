KINGSVILLE ISD must provide a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) to eligible students with disabilities. The process begins with a referral for a KINGSVILLE ISD special education evaluation. You don't need to wait for a teacher recommendation—you can request an evaluation directly.

Parents in KINGSVILLE ISD often don't realize they can initiate the evaluation process themselves. Whether your child is struggling academically, socially, or behaviorally, you have the legal right to ask for testing to determine if a disability is the underlying cause.

Step 1: Submit a Written Request

Contact your child's campus or the KINGSVILLE ISD Special Education Department directly. While verbal requests can work, submit your request in writing to create an official record. A simple letter or email stating that you're requesting a Full Individual Evaluation for special education is sufficient.

Your written request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current school and grade level
  • Specific concerns about learning, behavior, or development
  • Any relevant medical or developmental history
  • Request for a full individual evaluation
  • Your contact information

Step 2: Understand the KINGSVILLE ISD 45-Day Timeline

Once KINGSVILLE ISD receives your written request for a KINGSVILLE ISD FIE evaluation, the district has a specific timeline to follow. Under Texas Education Code, KINGSVILLE ISD must obtain your written consent and complete the evaluation within 45 calendar days.

This KINGSVILLE ISD 45 day timeline is critical. The clock starts the day the district receives your written request. Mark this date on your calendar and follow up if you don't hear back within a few days.

Step 3: Provide Consent

KINGSVILLE ISD must provide you with a Notice of Proposed Evaluation (NPE) before testing begins. This document explains the evaluation plan in detail. You must sign and return the consent form to authorize the KINGSVILLE ISD special education evaluation.

Take time to review the NPE carefully. Understand what areas will be assessed and which professionals will conduct the testing. Ask questions if anything is unclear.

Step 4: Participate in the Evaluation Process

During the KINGSVILLE ISD 45 day timeline, your child may participate in various assessments including cognitive testing, academic achievement testing, behavioral observation, and social-emotional evaluation. You may be asked to complete rating scales or provide information about your child's development and behavior at home.

As a parent in KINGSVILLE ISD, you have the right to observe evaluations and ask questions about the process. Some parents choose to hire an independent evaluator to provide additional perspectives on their child's needs.

What Happens After the KINGSVILLE ISD FIE Evaluation Is Complete

Eligibility Determination Meeting

Within the KINGSVILLE ISD 45 day timeline, the district must hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. This is where KINGSVILLE ISD presents evaluation results and determines if your child qualifies for special education services under IDEA.

To qualify for services in KINGSVILLE ISD, your child must meet two criteria: have a disability (such as autism, dyslexia, emotional disturbance, other health impairment, etc.) AND require special education services because of that disability.

If Your Child Qualifies

If KINGSVILLE ISD determines that your child is eligible, the ARD committee will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). This legal document outlines the special education and related services your child will receive, goals for the school year, and how progress will be measured.

If Your Child Does Not Qualify

If KINGSVILLE ISD concludes your child does not qualify for special education, you have the right to request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at district expense. You can also request a due process hearing to challenge the determination.

Understanding Disability Categories in KINGSVILLE ISD

KINGSVILLE ISD special education covers 13 disability categories under IDEA. Common categories include:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) – persistent patterns in social communication and restricted behaviors
  • Dyslexia – specific learning disability in reading, eligible under Other Health Impairment in Texas
  • Specific Learning Disability (SLD) – significant discrepancy in academic performance
  • Speech or Language Impairment – difficulty with communication
  • Emotional Disturbance (ED) – behavioral or emotional challenges
  • Other Health Impairment (OHI) – conditions like ADHD, asthma, or seizure disorders
  • Intellectual Disability (ID) – significant limitations in intellectual and adaptive functioning

Understanding which category might apply to your child helps you articulate your concerns when requesting a KINGSVILLE ISD FIE evaluation.
