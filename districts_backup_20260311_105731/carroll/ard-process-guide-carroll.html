What is an ARD Meeting and Why It Matters for CARROLL ISD Families

An ARD meeting is a required conference where a team of educators, specialists, and parents come together to develop, review, or modify your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP). In CARROLL ISD, these meetings are governed by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §89.1010.

The CARROLL ISD ARD meeting serves as the legal foundation for all special education services your child receives. During this meeting, the team evaluates your child's strengths, needs, and educational goals while determining what services and accommodations your child requires to access the general education curriculum.

For parents in CARROLL ISD, understanding the purpose of ARD meetings helps you prepare meaningful contributions that benefit your child's educational outcomes.

  • Parents in CARROLL ISD can request additional ARD meetings if circumstances change or concerns arise

During your CARROLL ISD ARD meeting, the team creates a detailed IEP including present levels of performance, annual goals, special education services, accommodations, and placement decisions. Services begin as soon as the IEP is developed.

Step 6: Annual Review and Updates

CARROLL ISD schedules annual ARD meetings to review progress, update goals, and ensure your child's IEP remains appropriate. Parents can request additional meetings anytime.
