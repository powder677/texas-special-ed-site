Understanding Special Education in LOVEJOY ISD

LOVEJOY ISD special education serves students ages 3-21 who have disabilities that impact their educational performance. Our district is committed to providing free, appropriate public education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment (LRE) for all eligible students, as required by federal law under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA).

LOVEJOY ISD currently serves approximately 3,959 students, and we maintain a dedicated special education department focused on identifying, evaluating, and supporting students with disabilities. Whether your child needs services related to autism, dyslexia, intellectual disabilities, speech and language disorders, or other qualifying conditions, LOVEJOY ISD has trained professionals ready to help.

The special education process in LOVEJOY ISD begins with referral—either through parent request or teacher observation—and continues through comprehensive evaluation, eligibility determination, and ongoing support services tailored to your child's individual needs.

Getting Your Child Evaluated: The Referral Process

How to Request an Evaluation in LOVEJOY ISD

Parents in LOVEJOY ISD can request a formal special education evaluation at any time during the school year. You don't need to wait for a teacher referral—you have the legal right to initiate the process yourself.

To request an evaluation:

  1. Contact your child's campus administrator or the LOVEJOY ISD special education office
  2. Submit a written request for evaluation (email, letter, or in-person)
  3. Specify the areas of concern (academic, behavioral, social-emotional, speech, motor skills, etc.)
  4. Provide background information about your child's developmental history

The Evaluation Timeline

Once you submit a written request for evaluation, LOVEJOY ISD has 60 calendar days to complete a comprehensive evaluation and hold an ARD meeting to discuss results and eligibility. This timeline is crucial—if the district needs additional time, they must request written permission from you.

The evaluation may include:

  • Cognitive/IQ testing
  • Academic achievement assessments
  • Speech and language evaluation
  • Occupational or physical therapy assessment
  • Behavioral rating scales and observations
  • Review of school records and previous evaluations
  • Vision and hearing screening

Understanding Evaluation Results

LOVEJOY ISD will present evaluation results at your LOVEJOY ISD ARD meeting. A qualified examiner will explain what was assessed, what the scores mean, and how the findings relate to your child's educational needs. Don't hesitate to ask questions—understanding the results is essential for making informed decisions about your child's education.

LOVEJOY ISD ARD Meetings and IEP Development

What is an ARD Meeting?

An ARD (Admission, Review, Dismissal) meeting is where parents, teachers, administrators, and specialists discuss your child's educational needs and create a plan to address them. If your child is found eligible for special education, the ARD committee develops an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

LOVEJOY ISD ARD meetings happen at critical points:

  • Initial ARD: After evaluation is complete, to determine eligibility
  • Annual ARD: Once per school year to review progress and update goals
  • Tri-annual review: Every three years (some districts do comprehensive re-evaluation)
  • IEP amendment meetings: When changes are needed between annual meetings

Your Rights During LOVEJOY ISD ARD Meetings

Parents have significant rights throughout the LOVEJOY ISD ARD meeting process. Understanding these rights ensures you can advocate effectively for your child:

  • Right to participate: You are a required member of the ARD committee
  • Right to bring support: You can invite an advocate, attorney, or knowledgeable family member
  • Right to prior written notice: LOVEJOY ISD must notify you of the meeting time, location, and purpose
  • Right to review records: You can examine all educational and evaluation records
  • Right to disagree: You can request an independent evaluation or file a due process complaint
  • Right to interpret services: LOVEJOY ISD must provide interpreters if needed

Come to every meeting prepared with questions, notes about your child's progress at home, and documentation of any concerns. Your perspective as a parent is invaluable.

LOVEJOY ISD recognizes that LOVEJOY ISD dyslexia program services are critical for students with reading disabilities. Under Texas Education Code §38.003, districts must provide dyslexia screening and intervention for all students who need them.
