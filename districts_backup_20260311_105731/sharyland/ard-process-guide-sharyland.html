An ARD meeting is a mandatory conference where a team of educators, specialists, and parents discuss your child's special education eligibility and services. In SHARYLAND ISD, these meetings determine whether your child qualifies for special education and what accommodations or services they'll receive.

The term "ARD" stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal. Each component serves a specific purpose:

  • Admission: Determining if your child qualifies for special education services
  • Review: Evaluating your child's progress and adjusting the IEP (Individualized Education Program) annually
  • Dismissal: Ending special education services when appropriate

For families in SHARYLAND ISD, the ARD meeting is where your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP) is created, reviewed, and adjusted. This legally binding document outlines everything from academic goals to behavioral supports.

SHARYLAND ISD IEP Goals and Services

The Individualized Education Program (IEP) developed during your SHARYLAND ISD ARD meeting is the legal blueprint for your child's special education services. Understanding how to review and advocate for strong SHARYLAND ISD IEP goals is crucial.

What Makes Strong SHARYLAND ISD IEP Goals

Effective SHARYLAND ISD IEP goals are SMART: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. Each goal should clearly state what skill your child will develop, how progress will be measured, and the timeline for achievement.

For example, a strong goal might read: "By March 2025, [Child's name] will read grade-level text with 90% accuracy as measured by quarterly reading assessments."

Types of Services in a SHARYLAND ISD IEP

Depending on your child's needs, SHARYLAND ISD might provide:

  • Special education instruction: Specialized teaching to address learning disabilities
  • Related services: Speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, or physical therapy
  • Accommodations: Changes to how material is presented (extended time, large print, etc.)
  • Modifications: Changes to curriculum content or expectations
  • Behavioral supports: Positive behavior intervention plans (PBIP) or social-emotional services
  • Assistive technology: Devices or software to support learning

Understanding Least Restrictive Environment (LRE)

SHARYLAND ISD must place your child in the Least Restrictive Environment (LRE)—meaning your child should spend as much time as possible in regular education classrooms with non-disabled peers. This might mean your child receives some services in a separate special education classroom and other services in the general classroom.

During your SHARYLAND ISD ARD meeting, ask specifically where services will be provided and how much time your child will spend with non-disabled peers.
