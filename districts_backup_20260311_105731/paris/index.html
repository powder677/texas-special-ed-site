Understanding PARIS ISD Special Education Services

PARIS ISD serves approximately 3,832 students across the district, with specialized programs to support children with disabilities and unique learning needs. Whether your child has been identified with a learning disability, autism spectrum disorder, speech impairment, or other qualifying condition, PARIS ISD special education provides evidence-based interventions and services tailored to individual needs.

The district operates under Texas Education Code requirements and federal IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) guidelines. Every student with an identified disability is entitled to a free, appropriate public education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment (LRE). This means your child receives services designed specifically for them while remaining integrated with non-disabled peers whenever possible.

PARIS ISD employs certified special education teachers, speech-language pathologists, counselors, and support staff dedicated to helping your child succeed academically and socially.

The IEP Process: Your Child's Individualized Education Plan

PARIS ISD IEP services begin with a comprehensive evaluation if you believe your child has a disability affecting education. The Individualized Education Program (IEP) is the legal document that outlines your child's educational goals, accommodations, and specialized instruction.

Initial Evaluation and Referral

You can request an evaluation at any time by contacting your child's campus or the PARIS ISD special education office. The district has 60 calendar days to conduct a comprehensive evaluation once you sign consent forms. This evaluation examines academic performance, cognitive abilities, social-emotional functioning, and physical capabilities relevant to education.

PARIS ISD uses multiple assessment methods to identify disabilities, including standardized testing, classroom observations, work samples, and parent input. You'll be asked to participate in interviews and provide information about your child's developmental history and current functioning.

The ARD Meeting and IEP Development

Once evaluation is complete, PARIS ISD holds an ARD meeting (Admission, Review, Dismissal). This meeting brings together parents, special education teachers, general education teachers, and administrators to discuss your child's evaluation results and develop the IEP.

During the PARIS ISD ARD meeting, the team will:

  • Review evaluation findings and determine eligibility
  • Identify your child's strengths and needs
  • Set measurable annual goals in academic and functional areas
  • Determine special education services, accommodations, and modifications
  • Establish the least restrictive environment for your child's learning
  • Discuss transitions plans (for students ages 14 and older)

Parents have the right to bring an advocate or attorney to the PARIS ISD ARD meeting. You can also request the meeting be held at a convenient time and request an interpreter if needed. The ARD team reviews and revises the IEP annually, though you can request additional meetings if circumstances change.

PARIS ISD Parent Rights and Due Process

Understanding your rights is essential to being an effective advocate for your child. Texas law and federal IDEA guarantee specific protections for parents in PARIS ISD and across the state. PARIS ISD parent rights include:

Key Parent Rights

  • Participation in Decision-Making: You must be included in all meetings about your child's identification, evaluation, IEP development, and placement decisions.
  • Prior Written Notice: PARIS ISD must provide written notice before taking any action regarding special education services. You have the right to request an explanation in your native language.
  • Access to Records: You can review all educational records, evaluation reports, and IEP documents. PARIS ISD must provide copies within 10 business days of your request.
  • Independent Evaluation: If you disagree with PARIS ISD's evaluation, you can request an independent evaluation at district expense. Ask the special education office for procedures.
  • Due Process Hearing: If you dispute PARIS ISD decisions, you can file a due process complaint to resolve disagreements through an impartial hearing.
  • Mediation: Before pursuing due process, parents and PARIS ISD can attempt mediation to resolve disputes collaboratively.

The Texas Education Agency (TEA) provides free parent training and information centers to help you understand your rights under special education law. You can also contact a special education attorney near PARIS if you need legal guidance on your child's case.

PARIS ISD Dyslexia Program and Reading Interventions

Dyslexia is a specific learning disability that affects how the brain processes written language. If you believe your child has dyslexia, PARIS ISD has specialized identification and intervention procedures. The PARIS ISD dyslexia program provides research-based instruction to help students with reading difficulties.

Dyslexia Identification in PARIS ISD

Any student in PARIS ISD can be referred for dyslexia evaluation if they show characteristics like difficulty with phonemic awareness, decoding, fluency, or spelling. Teachers conduct universal screening in kindergarten and first grade, and referrals can happen at any grade level.

The PARIS ISD dyslexia program uses the Texas Dyslexia Handbook criteria for identification. If your child is identified with dyslexia, they receive:

  • Evidence-based interventions delivered by trained dyslexia specialists
  • Structured literacy instruction focusing on phonics and word study
  • Accommodations such as extended time, large print materials, or audio texts
  • Progress monitoring to track reading improvement
  • Parent communication about intervention progress

Ask your child's school about referral procedures if you suspect dyslexia. Early identification and intervention make significant differences in reading outcomes.

Special Education Service Options in PARIS ISD

PARIS ISD offers multiple service delivery models to meet diverse student needs. Your child's IEP determines which services and settings are appropriate based on their disability and educational goals.

Service Delivery Models

  • Resource Services: Your child receives special education instruction in a pull-out setting for specific periods while attending general education classes for other subjects.
  • Self-Contained Classes: Students with more intensive needs receive instruction in specialized classrooms with specially trained teachers and smaller student-teacher ratios.
  • Co-Teaching/Inclusion: Special education and general education teachers collaborate in the regular classroom, with specialized instruction provided during the regular class period.
  • Related Services: Speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, physical therapy, and other services are provided based on IEP needs.
  • Behavioral Support: Students needing behavioral interventions receive support through positive behavior support plans and functional behavior assessments.

PARIS ISD focuses on inclusive practices, meaning students with disabilities are educated alongside non-disabled peers to the maximum extent possible while receiving necessary specialized instruction.

Transition Planning for Secondary Students

Beginning at age 14, PARIS ISD develops transition plans as part of the IEP. Transition planning helps students move from school to post-secondary life, including employment, higher education, and community living.

The transition plan includes:

  • Vocational exploration and career assessment
  • Community-based work experiences
  • Life skills instruction
  • Post-secondary education planning
  • Interagency coordination with adult service providers

PARIS ISD partners with community agencies to help students develop skills for adult independence and success.
