Understanding Special Education in GALVESTON ISD

GALVESTON ISD serves approximately 5,982 students across the district, with specialized programs designed to meet the diverse learning needs of students with disabilities. Special education in GALVESTON ISD is guided by federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas Education Code requirements.

The district provides a continuum of services ranging from inclusion in regular classrooms with support to specialized programs for students with more intensive needs. Whether your child is in elementary, middle, or high school, GALVESTON ISD special education services are designed to provide a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) in the Least Restrictive Environment (LRE).

Who Qualifies for Special Education in GALVESTON ISD?

Students in GALVESTON ISD may qualify for special education if they have one of 13 federally recognized disabilities and require specialized instruction as a result. These disabilities include autism, dyslexia, emotional disturbance, hearing impairment, intellectual disability, multiple disabilities, orthopedic impairment, other health impairment, speech or language impairment, traumatic brain injury, visual impairment, and specific learning disability.

Simply having a diagnosis is not enough—the student must demonstrate that the disability adversely affects educational performance and requires special education services beyond what general education can provide.

The ARD Meeting Process in GALVESTON ISD

The Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting is the cornerstone of special education planning in GALVESTON ISD. This is where your child's educational program is developed, reviewed, and adjusted to meet their specific needs.

What Happens During a GALVESTON ISD ARD Meeting?

During an ARD meeting for GALVESTON ISD students, the ARD committee reviews your child's current performance, discusses assessment results, identifies strengths and needs, and develops or revises the IEP. The meeting must include you (the parent), special education staff, general education teachers, and district representatives.

The ARD committee discusses:

  • Your child's strengths and interests
  • Academic and behavioral performance data
  • Whether the current IEP is meeting goals
  • Any changes needed to services or placement
  • Accommodations and modifications
  • Goals for the upcoming school year
  • Transition planning (for students age 14 and older)

GALVESTON ISD ARD Meeting Timeline and Frequency

GALVESTON ISD is required to hold an ARD meeting at least once per year to review your child's IEP. However, you can request an ARD meeting at any time if you have concerns about your child's progress or services. The district must schedule ARD meetings at a mutually agreeable time that allows all required participants to attend.

For initial evaluations, GALVESTON ISD must complete testing and hold an ARD meeting within 60 calendar days of receiving written consent to evaluate.

GALVESTON ISD IEP Services and Program Options

GALVESTON ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

Understanding your rights is essential to effectively advocate for your child. The GALVESTON ISD parent rights are extensive and protected under both federal and Texas law.

Key Parent Rights in GALVESTON ISD Special Education

As a parent of a student receiving special education in GALVESTON ISD, you have the right to:

  • Notice and Consent: Be notified before the district evaluates, places, or changes services for your child, and provide written consent before initial evaluation
  • Participate in Meetings: Attend all ARD meetings and participate fully in decisions about your child's program
  • Access Records: Review and receive copies of all educational records and assessment results within 10 school days
  • Independent Evaluation: Obtain an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at district expense if you disagree with the district's evaluation
  • Dispute Resolution: Request mediation or due process hearing if you disagree with GALVESTON ISD's decisions
  • Compensatory Services: Seek compensatory education services if GALVESTON ISD failed to provide a FAPE
  • Progress Reports: Receive regular reports on your child's progress toward IEP goals (at least as often as progress is reported to general education students)

Communicating with GALVESTON ISD Special Education Staff

Maintain regular communication with your child's special education team in GALVESTON ISD. Request written summaries of conversations, keep copies of all correspondence, and document concerns in writing. This creates a clear record if disputes arise.

Getting an Evaluation in GALVESTON ISD

The first step toward special education services in GALVESTON ISD is a comprehensive evaluation. If you suspect your child has a disability affecting school performance, you can request evaluation in writing to the special education director.

GALVESTON ISD Evaluation Timeline

Once GALVESTON ISD receives your written request for evaluation, the timeline is:

  1. Days 1-5: District provides notice of intent to evaluate and requests written consent
  2. Days 6-60: District completes all assessments (academic, cognitive, speech/language, behavioral, etc.)
  3. Day 60: ARD meeting held to review results and determine eligibility
  4. If Eligible: IEP developed at the same meeting and services begin

Do not wait for the school to identify your child's needs. If you have concerns, request evaluation immediately—the 60-day clock doesn't start until GALVESTON ISD receives your written request.
