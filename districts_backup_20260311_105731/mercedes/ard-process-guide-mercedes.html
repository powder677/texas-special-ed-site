What is an ARD Committee and Why It Matters for MERCEDES ISD Students

An ARD committee is a team of educators, specialists, and parents who meet to create and review your child's special education plan. For MERCEDES ISD families, this committee ensures your child receives appropriate services and accommodations to support their learning. The ARD process is governed by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §89.1010.

The MERCEDES ISD ARD committee typically includes your child's general education teacher, special education teacher, school administrator, diagnostician or counselor, and you as the parent. Your child may also participate, especially if they're old enough to contribute meaningfully to the discussion about their educational goals.

Understanding your MERCEDES ISD ARD rights empowers you to ensure your child receives a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) tailored to their individual needs.

Understanding the MERCEDES ISD IEP Process: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Referral for Evaluation

The process begins when someone—typically a teacher, parent, or school counselor—refers your child for a special education evaluation. In MERCEDES ISD, you have the right to request an evaluation if you believe your child has a disability. The school must respond to your request within a reasonable timeframe and obtain your written consent before conducting comprehensive assessments.

For MERCEDES ISD families, this initial referral is the gateway to understanding whether your child qualifies for special education services.

Step 2: Comprehensive Evaluation

Once you've consented, MERCEDES ISD specialists will conduct a thorough evaluation using multiple assessment tools. This includes academic testing, psychological evaluation, speech and language assessment, occupational therapy evaluation, or other assessments relevant to your child's suspected disability. The evaluation must be comprehensive and non-discriminatory.

The entire evaluation process must be completed within 60 calendar days from the date you signed the consent form. MERCEDES ISD will provide you with a copy of the evaluation results before the initial ARD meeting.

Step 3: Initial ARD Meeting

Following the evaluation, MERCEDES ISD will schedule your initial MERCEDES ISD ARD meeting to discuss the results and determine if your child qualifies for special education. You'll receive written notice at least 10 days before the meeting. This notice must include the meeting date, time, location, and the participants who will attend.

During this meeting, the team will review evaluation data and decide whether your child has a disability under IDEA and needs special education services.

Step 4: IEP Development

If your child qualifies, the ARD committee will develop an MERCEDES ISD IEP. This legally binding document outlines:

  • Your child's current levels of academic and functional performance
  • Measurable annual goals aligned with grade-level curriculum
  • Special education services and accommodations
  • Transition services (for students age 16 and older)
  • Progress monitoring plans
  • Placement in the least restrictive environment (LRE)

The MERCEDES ISD IEP process is collaborative. Your input as a parent is essential in identifying realistic, meaningful goals for your child.

Step 5: Ongoing ARD Reviews

MERCEDES ISD must review your child's IEP at least annually. You'll receive notice of the annual ARD meeting, and the team will examine progress toward goals, adjust services as needed, and ensure the IEP remains appropriate. Triennial (every three years) comprehensive evaluations are also required unless you and the school mutually agree to waive them.

MERCEDES ISD IEP Goals: What Parents Need to Know

Characteristics of Strong MERCEDES ISD IEP Goals

Effective MERCEDES ISD IEP goals are SMART: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. For example, rather than "improve reading," a strong goal states: "By May 15, [child's name] will read grade-level text with 90% accuracy and answer comprehension questions with 80% accuracy."

Goals should address your child's priority needs and align with grade-level curriculum when possible. In MERCEDES ISD, the team should discuss how goals will help your child access the general education curriculum and prepare for post-secondary outcomes.

Types of Goals in MERCEDES ISD IEPs

  • Academic Goals: Reading, math, writing, science, social studies
  • Behavioral/Social-Emotional Goals: Self-regulation, social skills, communication
  • Motor Goals: Fine or gross motor skill development
  • Functional Goals: Life skills, independence, vocational skills
  • Transition Goals: Post-secondary education, employment, independent living (for older students)

Understanding Your Rights in the MERCEDES ISD ARD Process

Your Primary MERCEDES ISD ARD Rights

Texas law and federal IDEA regulations grant you significant rights throughout the MERCEDES ISD ARD meeting process. Understanding these rights ensures you can advocate effectively for your child.

  • Right to Notice: You must receive written notice of any ARD meeting and its purposes
  • Right to Participation: You are a full member of the ARD committee with equal voice in decisions
  • Right to Records: You can access and obtain copies of all special education records
  • Right to Independent Evaluation: If you disagree with MERCEDES ISD assessments, you can request an independent evaluation at school expense
  • Right to Interpretation: If English is not your primary language, MERCEDES ISD must provide an interpreter
  • Right to Due Process: You can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA) or request due process hearing
  • Right to Prior Written Notice: Before making changes to your child's placement or services

Placement Options in MERCEDES ISD

Federal law requires that students receive services in the least restrictive environment (LRE). For MERCEDES ISD families, this means your child should be educated with non-disabled peers to the greatest extent possible. The ARD committee considers placement options ranging from general education classroom with supports to specialized settings, always seeking the most inclusive appropriate placement.
