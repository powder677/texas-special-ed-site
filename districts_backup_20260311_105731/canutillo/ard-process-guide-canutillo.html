Understanding CANUTILLO ISD IEP Goals and Objectives

The heart of your CANUTILLO ISD IEP process is the development of meaningful goals. CANUTILLO ISD IEP goals should directly address your child's academic, behavioral, and social-emotional needs while building on their strengths.

What Makes Strong CANUTILLO ISD IEP Goals

Effective CANUTILLO ISD IEP goals are SMART: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. Rather than vague statements like "improve reading," strong goals specify exactly what skill your child will develop and how progress will be measured.

Example of a strong goal: "By May 2025, [Student] will read grade-level fiction texts with 80% accuracy and answer comprehension questions with 75% accuracy, as measured by monthly reading assessments and teacher observation."

Types of Goals in CANUTILLO ISD IEPs

  • Academic goals: Reading, math, writing, and subject-specific skills
  • Speech/language goals: Communication and language development
  • Behavioral/social goals: Classroom behavior, social interaction, and self-regulation
  • Functional/life skills goals: Independence, self-care, and community skills
  • Physical/occupational therapy goals: Motor skills and adaptive functioning

During your CANUTILLO ISD ARD meeting, ensure that CANUTILLO ISD proposes goals aligned with your child's needs and your family's priorities. You have the right to input and to challenge goals you believe are inappropriate.
