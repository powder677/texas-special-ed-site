Understanding the IEP Process at BRIDGE CITY ISD

BRIDGE CITY ISD IEP services begin with a comprehensive evaluation and continue through annual review meetings. An IEP (Individualized Education Program) is a legally binding document that outlines your child's goals, services, accommodations, and placement. As a parent in BRIDGE CITY ISD, you have the right to participate meaningfully in every step of this process.

Initial Evaluation and Eligibility

If you suspect your child needs special education support, you can request an evaluation in writing to your child's principal or the special education department. BRIDGE CITY ISD has 60 calendar days from your written request to complete the evaluation and hold an eligibility meeting. The evaluation must be comprehensive and consider information from multiple sources, including classroom performance, parent input, and formal assessments.

At the eligibility meeting for BRIDGE CITY ISD special education, the ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee reviews evaluation results and determines whether your child qualifies. This committee includes you, your child's teachers, a special education representative, and other specialists as needed.

Developing Your Child's IEP

Once your child is found eligible for BRIDGE CITY ISD special education, the ARD committee develops an IEP within 30 days. This document includes:

  • Present Levels of Academic and Functional Performance (PLAAFP) – detailed description of your child's current skills and challenges
  • Measurable Annual Goals – specific, achievable objectives in academic and functional areas
  • Special Education and Related Services – therapy, classroom support, assistive technology, or other services your child receives
  • Supplementary Aids and Services – accommodations like extended time, modified assignments, or preferential seating
  • Placement and LRE Statement – where your child will receive services (general education, resource room, self-contained, etc.)
  • Transition Plans – for students 14 and older, planning for post-secondary education, employment, and independent living
