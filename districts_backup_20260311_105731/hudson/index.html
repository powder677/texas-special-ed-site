Understanding HUDSON ISD Special Education Services

HUDSON ISD special education serves students ages 3-21 who qualify under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). Our district provides a full continuum of services designed to meet each student's unique needs, from general education classroom support to specialized instruction in separate settings.

Every student receiving services through HUDSON ISD special education has an Individualized Education Program (IEP) that outlines their specific goals, services, and accommodations. These services are provided at no cost to families, as required by federal law.

With over 2,777 students enrolled in HUDSON ISD, we maintain a dedicated special education department focused on ensuring every student with a disability receives the support they need to succeed academically and socially.

Who Qualifies for HUDSON ISD Special Education?

Under Texas Education Code §29.002, students may qualify for special education if they have one or more of these disabilities:

  • Specific Learning Disability (SLD) – including dyslexia and dyscalculia
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Hearing Impairment or Deafness
  • Visual Impairment
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Other Health Impairment (OHI) – including ADHD, asthma, diabetes
  • Multiple Disabilities

If you believe your child may qualify, contacting HUDSON ISD special education is the first step toward getting your child evaluated and connected with appropriate services.

The HUDSON ISD IEP Services Process

The IEP is the cornerstone of special education in HUDSON ISD. This legally binding document guides all instruction, accommodations, and services your child receives. Understanding how HUDSON ISD IEP services work helps you advocate effectively for your child.

Step 1: Referral and Evaluation Request

The process begins when a teacher, parent, or school counselor has concerns about a student's learning or development. In HUDSON ISD, you can request an evaluation at any time by contacting the special education department in writing. There's no cost to you—the district covers all evaluation expenses.

Once HUDSON ISD receives your referral, the district has 15 school days to respond and explain evaluation procedures. Parents must provide written consent before evaluation can begin.

Step 2: Comprehensive Evaluation

HUDSON ISD special education uses multiple evaluation methods to get a complete picture of your child's abilities and needs. Evaluations typically include:

  • Academic achievement testing
  • Cognitive assessments
  • Speech and language evaluations (when appropriate)
  • Classroom observations
  • Review of school records and medical history
  • Parent and teacher interviews

This comprehensive approach ensures HUDSON ISD makes accurate decisions about eligibility and services. The entire evaluation process typically takes 45 calendar days.

Step 3: Eligibility Meeting

Within 15 school days of completing evaluations, HUDSON ISD holds an eligibility meeting to discuss results and determine if your child qualifies for special education. This is your first HUDSON ISD ARD meeting—ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal.

If your child is found eligible, the same meeting becomes your IEP development meeting, where the team creates your child's initial IEP.

Step 4: IEP Development and Implementation

Your child's HUDSON ISD IEP services are documented in a detailed plan that includes:

  • Present Levels of Academic and Functional Performance (PLAAFP) – current abilities and challenges
  • Measurable Annual Goals – what your child will work toward achieving
  • Special Education Services – the instruction and support your child receives
  • Related Services – speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, etc.
  • Accommodations and Modifications – changes to make learning accessible
  • Least Restrictive Environment (LRE) – where services are provided
  • Transition Services – for students 16 and older moving toward post-secondary goals

HUDSON ISD implements the IEP immediately after it's developed. You'll receive regular progress reports on your child's goal achievement, typically every progress report period.

HUDSON ISD ARD Meetings: What to Expect

The HUDSON ISD ARD meeting is where crucial decisions about your child's education happen. Understanding what to expect helps you prepare and participate effectively.

When ARD Meetings Occur

HUDSON ISD must hold ARD meetings at least annually to review and update your child's IEP. However, meetings can be scheduled more frequently if:

  • Your child isn't making adequate progress toward goals
  • You or the school request a meeting
  • You notice a significant change in your child's needs
  • Your child is transitioning between schools or programs

HUDSON ISD will provide at least 10 days' notice before an ARD meeting, and the district will schedule it at a time that works for you.

Who Attends HUDSON ISD ARD Meetings

Your HUDSON ISD ARD meeting brings together a team including:

  • You (the parent)
  • Your child (when appropriate)
  • Special education teacher
  • General education teacher
  • School administrator or designee
  • District representative with knowledge of district resources
  • Evaluator or person knowledgeable about evaluation results
  • Other specialists (speech therapist, psychologist, etc.) as needed

You have the right to bring an advocate or attorney to your HUDSON ISD ARD meeting. Many parents find a special ed advocate HUDSON area families trust helpful for understanding district proposals and ensuring their child's needs are met.

Preparing for Your ARD Meeting

Come prepared to discuss your child's progress, ask questions, and advocate for services your child needs. Before your HUDSON ISD ARD meeting, gather:

  • Current academic grades and work samples
  • Progress reports on IEP goals
  • Any outside evaluations or medical information
  • Notes about concerns you've observed at home
  • Questions or goals you want addressed

HUDSON ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

Federal law and Texas Education Code guarantee significant HUDSON ISD parent rights in special education. Understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Your Key HUDSON ISD Parent Rights

Right to Evaluation and Identification: You can request an evaluation at any time. HUDSON ISD must respond within 15 school days and obtain your written consent before testing begins.

Right to Prior Written Notice: HUDSON ISD must notify you in writing before making any significant changes to your child's identification, evaluation, placement, or services. This notice must be in your native language.

Right to Participate in IEP Decisions: You're a full team member in all special education decisions. Your input is required, and HUDSON ISD cannot move forward without your involvement.

Right to Review Records: You can access all of your child's educational records within 10 school days of requesting them from HUDSON ISD. These records are confidential and protected.

Right to Independent Evaluation: If you disagree with HUDSON ISD's evaluation, you can request an independent evaluation at district expense. This is particularly important if you believe HUDSON ISD has missed a disability like dyslexia.

Right to Dispute Resolution: If you disagree with HUDSON ISD special education decisions, you have options including mediation, due process hearings, and filing complaints with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

For a complete explanation of HUDSON ISD parent rights, the district provides a "Parent Rights and Responsibilities in Special Education" notice at the beginning of each school year and upon request.

HUDSON ISD Dyslexia Program and Services

Dyslexia is a specific learning disability affecting reading, spelling, and written expression. HUDSON ISD maintains a dedicated dyslexia program because Texas Education Code §38.003 requires districts to provide dyslexia services to students identified with this condition.

Identifying Dyslexia in HUDSON ISD

The HUDSON ISD dyslexia program begins with screening. All students in grades K-2 are screened for dyslexia risk factors. If your child shows risk indicators, HUDSON ISD will recommend evaluation.

A comprehensive dyslexia evaluation includes assessment of:

  • Phonemic awareness and phonics
  • Fluency and automaticity
  • Reading comprehension
  • Spelling and written expression
  • Cognitive processing abilities

If your child is identified with dyslexia in HUDSON ISD, they qualify for special education services under the "Specific Learning Disability" category.

HUDSON ISD Dyslexia Program Services

Students in the HUDSON ISD dyslexia program receive specialized intervention using evidence-based reading instruction, typically including:

  • Structured Literacy Instruction – systematic, explicit teaching of phonics, word structure, and reading mechanics
  • Multisensory Techniques – engaging multiple sensory pathways to reinforce learning
  • Specialized Teacher Training – instruction from educators trained in dyslexia intervention
  • Progress Monitoring – frequent assessments to ensure intervention effectiveness
  • Accommodations – tools like extended time, text-to-speech, and alternative formats

The HUDSON ISD dyslexia program is typically delivered as part of your child's overall IEP. Services can be provided in the general education classroom, resource room, or combination of settings depending on your child's needs.

If you suspect your child has dyslexia, contact HUDSON ISD special education immediately. Early identification and intervention make a tremendous difference in reading outcomes.
