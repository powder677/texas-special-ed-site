Understanding Special Education in BRAZOSPORT ISD

BRAZOSPORT ISD special education serves students ages 3-22 who qualify under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). The district identifies and evaluates students who may have disabilities affecting their ability to benefit from general education, then provides free, appropriate public education (FAPE) tailored to each child's unique needs.

Special education isn't a place—it's a service. In BRAZOSPORT ISD, eligible students receive services in a variety of settings, from mainstream classrooms with support to specialized resource rooms and self-contained programs, depending on individual needs and the Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Who Qualifies for Special Education?

BRAZOSPORT ISD special education covers 14 disability categories recognized under federal law, including:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment (OHI)
  • Specific Learning Disability (SLD)
  • Speech/Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI)
  • Visual Impairment
  • Deaf-Blindness
  • Noncategorical Early Childhood

Each child is evaluated individually. Having a diagnosis alone doesn't guarantee eligibility—the evaluation must demonstrate that the disability adversely affects educational performance and requires special education services.

The ARD Process: Your Child's IEP Meeting

The BRAZOSPORT ISD ARD meeting is where decisions about your child's education are made collaboratively. ARD stands for "Admission, Review, and Dismissal," and these meetings are required by Texas Education Code §29.005 and federal law under IDEA.

What Happens in a BRAZOSPORT ISD ARD Meeting?

During a BRAZOSPORT ISD ARD meeting, a team of educators, specialists, parents, and (when appropriate) the student reviews assessment results, discusses your child's strengths and needs, and creates or updates the Individualized Education Program.

The ARD team at BRAZOSPORT ISD will:

  1. Review current evaluation data and classroom performance
  2. Determine if your child remains eligible for special education
  3. Set measurable annual goals targeting priority areas
  4. Determine the appropriate placement and service delivery model
  5. Decide on accommodations and modifications
  6. Plan transitions (to high school, post-secondary life, etc.)
  7. Address any behavioral concerns through a Behavior Intervention Plan if needed

Your Rights as a Parent in BRAZOSPORT ISD ARD Meetings

Parents in BRAZOSPORT ISD have significant rights during the ARD process. You are an equal member of the team—your input matters and must be considered in decision-making.

Key parent rights include:

  • Receiving notice of the meeting at least 10 days in advance
  • Bringing an advocate or attorney to the meeting
  • Reviewing all records and evaluation reports before the meeting
  • Requesting a postponement if you need more time to prepare
  • Requesting an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at district expense if you disagree with district evaluations
  • Having the meeting scheduled at a mutually agreeable time and location
  • Requesting interpretive services if English isn't your primary language
  • Receiving a copy of the IEP in writing

BRAZOSPORT ISD IEP Services and Support

BRAZOSPORT ISD IEP services are individualized based on your child's unique needs. The IEP is a legally binding document that specifies exactly what services your child will receive, how often, and in what setting.

Types of Services Available

Depending on your child's IEP, BRAZOSPORT ISD may provide:

  • Related Services: Speech-language pathology, occupational therapy, physical therapy, counseling, and social work services
  • Specialized Instruction: Direct teaching in academic and functional skills
  • Assistive Technology: Devices and software to support communication and learning
  • Behavioral Support: Positive behavior intervention plans and counseling
  • Transition Services: Career preparation, job training, and post-secondary planning for students age 14+
  • Extended School Year (ESY): Summer services to prevent regression for students who qualify

When you attend your next BRAZOSPORT ISD ARD meeting, specifically ask which services your child will receive, the frequency and duration of each service, and the educator responsible for delivering it.

BRAZOSPORT ISD Parent Rights and Procedural Safeguards

Understanding BRAZOSPORT ISD parent rights is essential for advocating effectively for your child. Federal and Texas law provide numerous protections to ensure your child receives a free, appropriate public education and that you're involved in all major decisions.

  • Consent Requirements: Your permission is required before initial evaluation and before providing special education services
  • Access to Records: Right to inspect and review all educational records within 10 school days

BRAZOSPORT ISD Dyslexia Program

Dyslexia is one of the most common specific learning disabilities, and BRAZOSPORT ISD dyslexia program is designed to identify and support students who struggle with reading due to dyslexia and related disorders.

Dyslexia Screening and Evaluation in BRAZOSPORT ISD

Texas Education Code §38.003 requires that all students be screened for dyslexia. In BRAZOSPORT ISD, dyslexia screening typically occurs in kindergarten and first grade, with additional screenings for students who show reading difficulties.

If your child shows signs of dyslexia—such as difficulty rhyming, slow reading speed, trouble with decoding, or reversed letter/word orientation—you can request an evaluation from BRAZOSPORT ISD at any time. Signs of dyslexia may appear as early as preschool but are often most apparent in early elementary grades.

Dyslexia Intervention Services

How to Request an Evaluation in BRAZOSPORT ISD

If you believe your child needs special education services, you can request an evaluation at any time. You don't need to wait for the school to recommend one—parental request is a valid reason to begin the evaluation process.

Steps to Request Evaluation

  1. Submit a Written Request: Contact your school's special education department or administrator with a written request for evaluation. Email works, but keep a copy for your records.
  2. Specify Your Concerns: Clearly describe the academic, behavioral, or developmental concerns you've observed.
  3. Provide Context: Share relevant history (early development, previous schools, medical information).
  4. Expect Timeline: BRAZOSPORT ISD has up to 60 school days to complete the evaluation and hold an eligibility meeting.
  5. Consent Process: The district will send prior written notice and request your consent before beginning the evaluation.

Once BRAZOSPORT ISD completes the evaluation, you'll be invited to an ARD meeting to discuss results and determine eligibility. If your child qualifies, an IEP will be developed. If your child doesn't qualify but has a disability that impacts education (Section 504 plan eligibility), alternative services may be discussed.
