UVALDE CISD is committed to identifying students who may qualify for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). A Full Individual Evaluation is the formal assessment process that determines whether your child has a disability and needs special education support.

Under Texas Education Code and federal law, UVALDE CISD must conduct a comprehensive evaluation that examines your child's cognitive abilities, academic performance, social-emotional functioning, and physical health. This evaluation is free to families in UVALDE CISD and is a critical first step toward accessing special education services.

The evaluation process ensures that decisions about your child's special education placement are based on comprehensive data, not assumptions or single test scores. Parents in UVALDE CISD have significant rights throughout this process, including the right to participate in meetings and access all evaluation results.

You may request a UVALDE CISD special education evaluation if you notice your child is struggling academically, behaviorally, or socially in ways that typical classroom instruction hasn't resolved. Early intervention is important—the sooner concerns are identified, the sooner your child can receive support.

Signs Your Child Might Need an Evaluation

  • Difficulty keeping up academically despite teacher intervention and tutoring
  • Behavioral challenges that interfere with learning or classroom participation
  • Speech, language, or communication difficulties
  • Fine or gross motor skill delays
  • Social difficulties or trouble making friends
  • Emotional or mental health concerns affecting school performance
  • Suspected sensory impairments (hearing or vision problems)
  • Attention or concentration difficulties

You don't need to wait for your child to fail academically before requesting an evaluation. UVALDE CISD child find procedures require the district to identify students with disabilities regardless of whether they're failing, succeeding, or performing at grade level.

Don't Wait for Teacher Referral

While teachers can refer students for evaluation, you don't need teacher permission to request one yourself. As a parent, you have the independent right to request a UVALDE CISD FIE evaluation at any time during your child's school career.

Requesting an evaluation in UVALDE CISD is straightforward, but it's important to do it correctly to ensure your request is documented and processed promptly.

Step 1: Contact UVALDE CISD Special Education Department

Start by reaching out to UVALDE CISD's special education department or your child's campus. You can request a meeting with the special education coordinator or campus administrator. Ask specifically to discuss your concerns about your child's learning or development.

If you're unsure how to reach the special education office, call your child's school directly and ask for the special education director or campus special education contact. UVALDE CISD staff can answer initial questions about the evaluation process.

Step 2: Submit a Written Request for Evaluation

While you can make an initial verbal request, always submit your evaluation request in writing. This creates a paper trail and ensures UVALDE CISD officially documents your request. Your written request should be clear and specific.

Your letter should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current grade level and campus
  • Specific concerns about your child's learning or development
  • Examples of behaviors or academic struggles you've observed
  • A clear statement: "I request that my child receive a Full Individual Evaluation"
  • Your contact information (phone and email)
  • The date of your request

Send this letter to your child's campus principal and the UVALDE CISD special education director via email and certified mail. Keep copies of everything you send.

Step 3: Expect Contact Within 15 Days

UVALDE CISD must respond to your evaluation request within 15 days. A special education representative will contact you to discuss your concerns and explain the evaluation process. This is your opportunity to share detailed information about your child.

During this conversation, UVALDE CISD staff will ask you questions about your child's developmental history, medical background, and any previous evaluations or services. Be prepared to discuss specific examples of your concerns.

Understanding the UVALDE CISD 45 Day Timeline

One of the most important aspects of the UVALDE CISD special education evaluation process is the UVALDE CISD 45 day timeline. This federal requirement protects your family by ensuring evaluations happen promptly.

What Is the 45-Day Rule?

UVALDE CISD has 45 calendar days from the date of your written request to complete all evaluations and hold an initial ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. This timeline ensures your child's case moves forward efficiently.

The clock starts when UVALDE CISD receives your written request. School holidays and breaks count toward this 45-day window unless your family provides written consent to extend the timeline. The 45 days can only be extended if you and UVALDE CISD staff mutually agree in writing.

What Happens During the 45 Days

During this period, UVALDE CISD will conduct various assessments, including:

  • Cognitive or IQ testing
  • Academic achievement testing in reading, math, and writing
  • Speech and language evaluation (if indicated)
  • Occupational or physical therapy evaluation (if needed)
  • Behavioral or social-emotional assessment
  • Vision and hearing screening
  • Review of your child's school records and work samples
  • Parent and teacher interviews

You'll likely be asked to complete questionnaires about your child's behavior, development, and functioning outside of school. Your input is crucial to the evaluation process.

The Initial ARD Meeting

At the end of the UVALDE CISD 45 day timeline, you'll attend an ARD meeting where results are discussed. UVALDE CISD must provide you with a written evaluation report at or before this meeting. You have the right to have an advocate or attorney present at this meeting.

Your Rights During the UVALDE CISD Evaluation Request Process

Parents in UVALDE CISD have significant legal protections throughout the evaluation process. Understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Right to Written Consent

UVALDE CISD must obtain your written consent before beginning any evaluation. You have the right to refuse evaluation, though UVALDE CISD can pursue other due process options if they believe evaluation is necessary. You can also consent to some evaluations while declining others—be specific about which areas you authorize.

Right to Independent Evaluation

If you disagree with UVALDE CISD's evaluation results, you have the right to request an independent evaluation at district expense. This is particularly valuable if you believe UVALDE CISD missed something important or evaluated your child inappropriately. Document your disagreement in writing and request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE).

Right to Interpreter Services

If you're a non-English speaking parent in the UVALDE area, UVALDE CISD must provide an interpreter at no cost for all meetings and written communications. Request this when you submit your evaluation request.

Right to Participation and Records

You have the right to participate in all meetings regarding your child's evaluation and receive copies of all evaluation reports, records, and test results. Ask UVALDE CISD for copies at least a few days before your ARD meeting so you can review the information in advance.

What to Expect in UVALDE CISD's Evaluation Report

UVALDE CISD's evaluation report is a comprehensive document that forms the basis for determining whether your child qualifies for special education services. Understanding this report helps you participate meaningfully in decision-making.

Key Components of the Evaluation Report

Your UVALDE CISD special education evaluation report should include:

  • Background information: Developmental history, medical history, and previous evaluations
  • Assessment results: Scores from cognitive, academic, and behavioral tests with explanations
  • Observations: How your child performed during testing and in classroom settings
  • Summary and conclusions: Whether your child meets the criteria for a disability under IDEA
  • Recommendations: Suggested services, accommodations, or further evaluation needs

Each evaluator (special education teacher, school psychologist, speech therapist, etc.) will include a separate report within the overall evaluation. UVALDE CISD must ensure all evaluations are current, comprehensive, and based on multiple sources of data.

Understanding Test Scores

Test scores can be confusing. Ask UVALDE CISD evaluators to explain what scores mean in plain language. Request that they explain your child's strengths and weaknesses, not just numerical scores. A good UVALDE CISD evaluator will help you understand how your child's performance compares to peers and what it means for their learning.

Next Steps After UVALDE CISD Evaluation

Once your UVALDE CISD special education evaluation is complete, the process moves toward determining eligibility and developing services if your child qualifies.

Eligibility Determination

UVALDE CISD must determine whether your child has a disability that adversely affects educational performance. The disabilities covered under IDEA include autism, intellectual disability, emotional disturbance, specific learning disability, speech-language impairment, orthopedic impairment, hearing impairment, visual impairment, traumatic brain injury, and other health impairment.

Your child must meet criteria for one of these disabilities AND require special education services to benefit from public education. Meeting just one part isn't enough—both conditions must be present for UVALDE CISD to find your child eligible.

Developing an Individualized Education Program (IEP)

If your child is found eligible, UVALDE CISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) specifying the services, accommodations, and modifications your child needs. You're a required participant in IEP development. Your input about your child's needs, strengths, and family priorities must be considered.

If Your Child Doesn't Qualify

If UVALDE CISD determines your child doesn't have a disability, you have options. You can request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at district expense, request a due process hearing, or pursue a 504 Plan evaluation if your child has a condition substantially limiting major life activities.

Common Questions About UVALDE CISD Evaluation Requests

Can I request an evaluation if my child is doing well academically?

Yes. UVALDE CISD's child find obligation applies to all students, regardless of academic performance. Some children with disabilities mask difficulties or have specific learning profiles where they perform well in some areas while struggling in others. If you have concerns about your child's learning, behavior, or development, you can request evaluation.

What if UVALDE CISD refuses my evaluation request?

UVALDE CISD cannot refuse a parent's request for evaluation. If the district denies your request or delays responding, request that they provide written explanation of their refusal. You can file a due process complaint and request an independent evaluation at district expense. Contact a special ed advocate UVALDE or an attorney for guidance.

How long does the evaluation actually take?

The legal timeline is 45 days from your written request to the ARD meeting. However, actual testing may occur over several weeks. The time depends on how busy UVALDE CISD's evaluation team is and how many different evaluations your child needs.

Will evaluation results be shared with teachers?

Yes. Once your child has an evaluation, results become part of their school record. Teachers and staff who work with your child will have access to appropriate information. However, you can request that certain sensitive information be limited to specific staff members.
