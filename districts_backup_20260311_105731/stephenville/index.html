Understanding STEPHENVILLE ISD Special Education Services

STEPHENVILLE ISD special education provides services to eligible students ages 3-21 who have disabilities that impact their ability to access and benefit from general education. Our district follows the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code requirements to ensure every child receives a free, appropriate public education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment.

Special education in STEPHENVILLE ISD isn't a place—it's a service. Your child may receive services while remaining in the regular classroom, attending resource classes, or in more specialized settings depending on their individual needs. The goal is always to help students with disabilities participate meaningfully in school and prepare for life after graduation.

Who Qualifies for Services in STEPHENVILLE ISD

Students may qualify for STEPHENVILLE ISD special education services if they have one of the following disabilities as defined by Texas Education Code §89.1003:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
  • Deaf-Blindness
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Specific Learning Disability (including dyslexia)
  • Speech/Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Visual Impairment

If you suspect your child may need special education services, contact your child's campus principal or the STEPHENVILLE ISD Special Education Department to request an evaluation.

The ARD Meeting Process in STEPHENVILLE ISD

The STEPHENVILLE ISD ARD meeting is where all important decisions about your child's special education services are made. ARD stands for "Admission, Review, and Dismissal," and it's a team meeting that includes you, educators, specialists, and your child when appropriate.

An STEPHENVILLE ISD ARD meeting must be held at least once annually to review your child's progress and update their Individualized Education Program (IEP). Additional ARD meetings may be called anytime you or the school believes changes are needed.

What Happens During an ARD Meeting

During your STEPHENVILLE ISD ARD meeting, the team will:

  1. Review evaluation results and your child's current performance
  2. Discuss your concerns and priorities for your child's education
  3. Determine whether your child remains eligible for services
  4. Set annual goals and measurable objectives
  5. Decide what services and accommodations your child needs
  6. Determine the appropriate educational placement
  7. Discuss assessment participation and state testing accommodations

Preparing for Your STEPHENVILLE ISD ARD Meeting

Come to your STEPHENVILLE ISD ARD meeting prepared to be an active participant. Gather information about your child's strengths, challenges, and needs both at school and at home. Bring any outside evaluations or medical records that might be relevant.

You have the right to bring an advocate or attorney to your STEPHENVILLE ISD ARD meeting. Many parents in STEPHENVILLE ISD find it helpful to have support, especially if they anticipate disagreements about services. You may also request the meeting be held at a time and location convenient for you.

STEPHENVILLE ISD IEP Services and Your Child's Education Plan

Your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP) is the legal document that guides all STEPHENVILLE ISD IEP services. This document describes your child's current performance, annual goals, required services, accommodations, modifications, and placement decisions.

STEPHENVILLE ISD IEP services may include specialized instruction, speech-language pathology, occupational therapy, physical therapy, counseling, behavior support services, and assistive technology. The specific STEPHENVILLE ISD IEP services your child receives depend entirely on their individual needs.

Types of Special Education Services Available in STEPHENVILLE ISD

Families in STEPHENVILLE ISD can access various specialized services including:

  • Resource Room Instruction—Students spend part of the day in a specialized classroom for targeted instruction in academic or functional skills
  • Inclusive Classroom Support—Special educators provide push-in support within the general education classroom
  • Speech and Language Services—Licensed speech pathologists provide therapy for communication disorders
  • Occupational and Physical Therapy—Therapists address fine motor, gross motor, and functional life skills
  • Counseling and Mental Health Support—School counselors and psychologists provide emotional and behavioral support
  • Behavior Intervention Services—Specialists develop positive behavior support plans and teach replacement behaviors
  • Assistive Technology—Tools and devices to help students access curriculum and communicate

Your child's IEP will specify the frequency and duration of each service. Progress toward IEP goals is monitored regularly and reported to parents at least as often as grades are issued.

STEPHENVILLE ISD Dyslexia Program and Reading Support

Dyslexia is a specific learning disability that affects reading, and STEPHENVILLE ISD dyslexia program services are available for eligible students. Texas Education Code §38.003 requires all districts to identify and provide services for students with dyslexia.

If your child struggles with reading despite adequate instruction and opportunity to learn, evaluation for dyslexia may be appropriate. The STEPHENVILLE ISD dyslexia program uses evidence-based, structured literacy instruction to help students develop foundational reading skills.

Accessing STEPHENVILLE ISD Dyslexia Services

The STEPHENVILLE ISD dyslexia program typically includes:

STEPHENVILLE ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

Federal law guarantees specific STEPHENVILLE ISD parent rights to ensure your child receives appropriate services and you're meaningfully involved in decision-making. Understanding these STEPHENVILLE ISD parent rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Your Key Special Education Rights in STEPHENVILLE ISD

As a parent in STEPHENVILLE ISD, you have the right to:

  • Request evaluation—Ask for a comprehensive evaluation to determine if your child has a disability and needs special education
  • Participate in decision-making—Be a full member of the ARD team with equal say in decisions
  • Review records—Access and review all educational records regarding your child
  • Receive prior written notice—Get written notification of proposed changes to identification, evaluation, or placement
  • Obtain independent evaluations—Get evaluations from professionals outside STEPHENVILLE ISD at district expense if you disagree with district evaluations
  • Request mediation or due process—Pursue dispute resolution if you disagree with district decisions
  • Receive services in your native language—Get communications in a language you understand

STEPHENVILLE ISD families also have the right to confidentiality of your child's records and to be informed about any disciplinary actions related to your child's disability.

Understanding Prior Written Notice and Procedural Safeguards

Whenever STEPHENVILLE ISD proposes to change your child's special education services, placement, or identification status, you must receive prior written notice at least 10 days before the change. This notice must explain the proposed action, why it's being proposed, other options considered, and your procedural rights.

At the beginning of each school year and whenever your child is newly identified as needing special education, STEPHENVILLE ISD must provide you with a document explaining your procedural safeguards. Review this document carefully and keep it for reference.
