The ARD Meeting Process in PINE TREE ISD

An ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal meeting) is the foundation of special education in PINE TREE ISD. This is where critical decisions about your child's education are made, and parent participation is not just welcomed—it's required by law.

What Happens During a PINE TREE ISD ARD Meeting?

During a PINE TREE ISD ARD meeting, the team reviews evaluation results, discusses your child's strengths and needs, and develops an Individualized Education Program (IEP). The ARD team in PINE TREE ISD includes you (the parent), special education teachers, general education teachers, a school administrator, and other specialists as needed.

The ARD meeting typically covers:

  1. Current Performance—How your child is performing academically and socially
  2. Goals and Objectives—What your child will work toward during the school year
  3. Services and Supports—What special education services your child will receive
  4. Placement—Where your child will receive instruction (general education, resource room, or specialized setting)
  5. Transition Planning—For students 14 and older, planning for post-secondary success

Your Rights During the ARD Process

PINE TREE ISD parents have specific rights during the ARD meeting process. You have the right to be informed of all decisions before they're implemented, to bring support persons or advocates to meetings, and to request independent evaluations if you disagree with PINE TREE ISD's findings.
