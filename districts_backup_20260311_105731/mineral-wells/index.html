The ARD Process: Your Child's Roadmap to Services

The Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting is the most important meeting you'll attend as a parent in MINERAL WELLS ISD. This is where decisions are made about your child's eligibility, services, goals, and placement.

What Happens During a MINERAL WELLS ISD ARD Meeting

Your MINERAL WELLS ISD ARD meeting will include your child's teachers, school administrators, specialists, and you as the parent. The committee reviews evaluation results, discusses your child's strengths and needs, and creates or updates an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

  • Initial ARD meeting: Occurs within 30 days of completing your child's evaluation
  • Annual ARD meeting: Must occur at least once per school year to review progress
  • Manifestation determination meeting: Required if your child is being disciplined for behavior related to their disability
  • Reconvene meetings: Can be called anytime concerns arise about your child's progress or services

Preparing for Your MINERAL WELLS ISD ARD Meeting

Come prepared with questions, concerns, and documentation about your child. Bring any outside evaluations, medical records, or observations from home that might be relevant. It's perfectly acceptable to bring a support person, an advocate, or an attorney to your MINERAL WELLS ISD ARD meeting.

Ask for the evaluation report and assessment data at least three days before the meeting. Review it carefully and write down questions. Understanding what the test results mean will help you participate more effectively in the decision-making process.

MINERAL WELLS ISD IEP Services: What Your Child Can Receive

An Individualized Education Program (IEP) is a legal document that describes the special education services your child will receive. MINERAL WELLS ISD IEP services are customized based on your child's individual needs and might include:

Common MINERAL WELLS ISD IEP Service Categories

  • Special education instruction: Specialized teaching in a resource room or classroom setting
  • Speech-language pathology: Services for communication disorders
  • Occupational therapy: Support with fine motor skills and daily living activities
  • Physical therapy: Assistance with gross motor skills and mobility
  • Counseling services: Social-emotional support and mental health services
  • Assistive technology: Tools and devices that help your child access education
  • Behavioral interventions: Support for students with behavior challenges
  • Transition services: Planning for post-secondary life after high school

The number of minutes per week, the location of services, and which staff members provide them are all determined during your child's MINERAL WELLS ISD ARD meeting. These specifics must be clearly documented in the IEP.

Progress Monitoring in MINERAL WELLS ISD

MINERAL WELLS ISD is required to monitor your child's progress toward IEP goals at least as often as general education students receive progress reports—typically every four to six weeks. You should receive regular updates about whether your child is meeting the objectives outlined in their IEP.

If your child is not making adequate progress toward their goals, the ARD committee can meet to make adjustments to services, goals, or instructional strategies. Don't wait for the annual ARD meeting if you're concerned about progress.

MINERAL WELLS ISD Parent Rights and Special Education Law

Texas Education Code Section 29.001 and the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) guarantee you specific rights as the parent of a student with disabilities. Understanding your MINERAL WELLS ISD parent rights is essential to advocating effectively for your child.

  • Right to evaluate: Your child receives a comprehensive evaluation before being placed in special education

  • Right to records: You can access all educational records related to your child
  • Right to due process: You can request a hearing if you disagree with MINERAL WELLS ISD's decisions
  • Right to refuse: You can refuse services or request your child be evaluated
