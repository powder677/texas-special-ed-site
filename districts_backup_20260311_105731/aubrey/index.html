Understanding AUBREY ISD Special Education Overview

AUBREY ISD serves approximately 4,480 students across our AUBREY area schools, with a dedicated special education department committed to providing appropriate services for students with disabilities. Our special education program operates under federal law (IDEA), state law (Texas Education Code), and local policies designed to ensure every eligible student receives a free, appropriate public education in the least restrictive environment.

In AUBREY ISD, special education is not a place—it's a service. Students receive services based on their individual needs, whether that means support within the regular classroom, resource room instruction, or separate classroom placement. The AUBREY ISD special education team works collaboratively with families to develop meaningful educational plans that address each student's unique strengths and challenges.

Parents in AUBREY ISD have specific rights and responsibilities outlined in state and federal law. Understanding these rights empowers you to be an effective advocate for your child and to participate fully in all educational decisions affecting your child's placement and services.

AUBREY ISD Referral & Evaluation Process

The journey toward receiving special education services in AUBREY ISD begins with a referral for evaluation. Any parent, teacher, or school staff member can request that your child be evaluated to determine if a disability exists and if special education services are needed.

How to Request an Evaluation

  • Submit a written request to your campus principal or the Special Education Department
  • Specify your concerns about your child's learning, behavior, or development
  • Include relevant background information (medical history, developmental milestones, previous concerns)
  • For AUBREY ISD families, requests should be submitted as soon as concerns arise

AUBREY ISD must respond to evaluation requests within 15 school days. The district will either begin the evaluation process or provide a written explanation if they decline. Parents have the right to disagree with the district's decision not to evaluate.

The Evaluation Timeline

Once AUBREY ISD begins the evaluation process, the district has 60 calendar days to complete all assessments and hold an eligibility meeting. This timeline is strict and important—parents should track dates carefully. The evaluation in AUBREY ISD will assess your child across multiple areas relevant to the suspected disability, which may include:

  • Cognitive and intellectual functioning
  • Academic achievement and performance
  • Social-emotional and behavioral functioning
  • Speech and language abilities (when applicable)
  • Physical abilities and motor skills
  • Adaptive/functional living skills

Parents in AUBREY ISD have the right to request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at district expense if you disagree with AUBREY ISD's evaluation results. This is an important protection for families who believe the district's assessment is incomplete or inaccurate.

AUBREY ISD ARD Meetings & IEP Development

The AUBREY ISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) is the formal meeting where your child's eligibility for special education is determined and where Individualized Education Programs are developed. For AUBREY ISD families, this meeting is your opportunity to collaborate with educators in making decisions about your child's education.

What to Expect in an AUBREY ISD ARD Meeting

The AUBREY ISD ARD meeting brings together parents, teachers, special educators, administrators, and sometimes the student. This team reviews evaluation results, discusses your child's strengths and needs, and makes decisions about:

  • Eligibility for special education services
  • Special education placement and setting
  • Annual IEP goals and objectives
  • Specific services and accommodations
  • Extended school year (ESY) eligibility when needed
  • Transition planning for students age 14 and older

Preparing for Your AUBREY ISD ARD Meeting

Parents in AUBREY ISD should come prepared to these important meetings. Before your AUBREY ISD ARD meeting, gather records of your child's performance at home, any outside evaluations, medical information, and questions or concerns you want addressed. Many families find it helpful to write down specific goals they want included in the IEP.

Remember that you have the right to bring a support person or advocate to your AUBREY ISD ARD meeting. If you feel overwhelmed by the process or want additional perspective on your child's needs, having an extra set of ears can be invaluable. Some families in AUBREY ISD work with education advocates or special education attorneys before meetings to ensure their child's needs are fully understood and documented.

Understanding AUBREY ISD IEP Services

AUBREY ISD IEP services are the specific supports and instruction your child receives to address their educational needs. These services are individualized based on your child's disability category and unique profile. AUBREY ISD IEP services might include:

  • Special education instruction in academics
  • Speech-language pathology services
  • Occupational or physical therapy
  • Counseling or mental health support
  • Behavior intervention and support services
  • Assistive technology and accommodations
  • Related services supporting educational access

The amount, frequency, and duration of AUBREY ISD IEP services must be documented clearly in your child's IEP. These are not suggestions—they are commitments that AUBREY ISD must fulfill. Parents should verify that services are actually being provided as documented.

AUBREY ISD Parent Rights & Advocacy

AUBREY ISD parent rights are guaranteed protections under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code Chapter 29. Understanding these rights is essential for families in AUBREY ISD advocating for their children.

Your Core AUBREY ISD Parent Rights

  • Prior Written Notice: AUBREY ISD must provide written notice before proposing or refusing to change your child's identification, evaluation, placement, or IEP services
  • Parental Consent: You must consent to initial evaluation and initial special education placement
  • Participation: You have the right to participate in all meetings regarding your child's special education
  • Confidentiality: Your child's records are protected and cannot be shared without your consent
  • Independent Evaluation: You can obtain an independent evaluation at district expense under certain circumstances
  • Due Process: If you disagree with AUBREY ISD decisions, you have the right to mediation or formal due process hearing

Parents in AUBREY ISD should request a copy of their complete AUBREY ISD parent rights document at the beginning of the school year. This detailed document explains each right and how to exercise it.

AUBREY ISD Dyslexia Program & Reading Support

The AUBREY ISD dyslexia program provides specialized support for students with dyslexia and related reading difficulties. Dyslexia is a specific learning disability affecting reading, spelling, and written expression that requires specialized instruction approaches.

Understanding the AUBREY ISD Dyslexia Program

Texas law (Senate Bill 1045) requires every school district to identify students with dyslexia and provide appropriate intervention. The AUBREY ISD dyslexia program includes:

  • Screening and identification of students at risk for dyslexia
  • Evidence-based intervention services using structured literacy approaches
  • Specialized dyslexia instruction delivered by trained educators
  • Progress monitoring to ensure intervention effectiveness
  • Accommodations such as extended time, text-to-speech technology, and modified assignments

Students in the AUBREY ISD dyslexia program may receive services through general education intervention, a 504 plan, or through special education services depending on their needs. The AUBREY ISD dyslexia program works to ensure struggling readers receive intensive, specialized instruction as quickly as possible.

Requesting Dyslexia Evaluation

If you suspect your child has dyslexia, request a dyslexia screening through your campus. Parents can request a full evaluation for dyslexia, and AUBREY ISD will provide assessment results and recommendations for intervention. Early identification and intervention provide the best outcomes for students with reading difficulties.

Special Education Support Services in AUBREY ISD

Beyond the IEP process, AUBREY ISD offers various support services and programs to help students with disabilities succeed:

Speech-Language Pathology Services

Students in AUBREY ISD with speech and language disorders receive services from certified speech-language pathologists. These services address articulation, fluency, language development, voice, and swallowing disorders when they impact educational performance.

Occupational & Physical Therapy

AUBREY ISD provides occupational and physical therapy services to students whose disabilities affect their ability to access education. These related services support fine motor skills, gross motor development, sensory processing, and functional independence.

Behavioral & Mental Health Support

Students in AUBREY ISD with emotional or behavioral disabilities receive support including counseling, behavior intervention plans, and crisis support. AUBREY ISD employs school counselors and behavioral specialists to support student mental health and positive behavior.

Assistive Technology in AUBREY ISD

AUBREY ISD recognizes that assistive technology can dramatically improve access to education for students with disabilities. Students may receive assistive technology devices and training, including text-to-speech software, speech-to-text programs, adaptive keyboards, and specialized software supporting learning.
