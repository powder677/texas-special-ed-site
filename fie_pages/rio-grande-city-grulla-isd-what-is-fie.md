# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Rio Grande City Grulla ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Rio Grande City Grulla ISD and you've heard the term "FIE" mentioned by a teacher, counselor, or another parent, you're in the right place. Whether your child is struggling in school, has been recommended for testing, or you simply want to understand your options, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know about the Full Individual Evaluation process. As part of our Rio Grande Valley community, you deserve clear answers and support as you navigate this important step in your child's educational journey.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A FIE, or Full Individual Evaluation, is a comprehensive assessment that Texas school districts use to determine whether a child has a disability and needs special education services. You might also hear it called a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply "special education testing."

Think of a FIE as a detailed picture of your child—their strengths, challenges, learning style, and specific needs. It's not just one test, but a collection of assessments, observations, and information gathered from multiple sources to truly understand how your child learns.

Under federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and the Texas Education Code Chapter 29, every child suspected of having a disability must receive a FIE before they can qualify for special education services. This evaluation is provided at no cost to you and is conducted by trained professionals within Rio Grande City Grulla ISD.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Rio Grande City Grulla ISD?

Rio Grande City Grulla ISD serves approximately 9,480 students across our community. Like all Texas school districts, Rio Grande City Grulla ISD is required by law to identify, locate, and evaluate all children who may have disabilities—a responsibility called "Child Find."

A FIE is important because it:

- **Opens doors to support**: Without a FIE, your child cannot receive special education services or an Individualized Education Program (IEP)
- **Identifies specific needs**: The evaluation pinpoints exactly where your child needs help, whether that's reading, math, speech, behavior, or other areas
- **Guides educational planning**: The results help teachers and specialists create a learning plan tailored to your child
- **Provides legal protections**: Once your child qualifies, they receive important protections under federal and state law

At Rio Grande City Grulla ISD, the special education team works with families throughout this process to ensure your child gets the support they deserve.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE looks at many different areas of your child's development and learning. The specific assessments used will depend on your child's suspected disability, but a complete evaluation typically includes:

**Cognitive Ability (Intelligence)**
- How your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems

**Academic Achievement**
- Reading, writing, and math skills compared to grade-level expectations

**Communication Skills**
- Speech, language understanding, and expression abilities

**Motor Skills**
- Fine motor skills (writing, cutting) and gross motor skills (running, balance)

**Social and Emotional Development**
- How your child interacts with others, manages emotions, and behaves in different settings

**Adaptive Behavior**
- Daily living skills and how independently your child functions

**Health and Medical History**
- Vision, hearing, and any medical conditions that might affect learning

The evaluation team will also review your child's school records, classroom work, and teacher observations. They may observe your child in the classroom and will ask you to complete questionnaires about your child's behavior and development at home.

## How to Request a FIE at Rio Grande City Grulla ISD

You have the right to request a FIE at any time if you suspect your child may have a disability that affects their learning. Here's how to do it:

**Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing**

While you can make a verbal request, we strongly recommend putting it in writing. This creates a record and starts the official timeline. Your letter should include:
- Your child's full name and date of birth
- Your child's school and grade
- A brief statement that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation
- Your specific concerns about your child's learning or development
- Your contact information
- The date

**Step 2: Submit Your Request**

You can submit your written request to:
- Your child's teacher
- The school principal
- The campus counselor
- The Rio Grande City Grulla ISD Special Education Department

**Step 3: Watch for the District's Response**

Once the district receives your request, they must respond within 15 school days. They will either:
- Provide you with a consent form to begin the evaluation, OR
- Send you a written notice explaining why they don't believe an evaluation is needed (along with information about your right to disagree)

**Sample Request Letter:**

*Dear [Principal's Name or Special Education Director],*

*I am the parent of [Child's Name], a [grade] student at [School Name]. I am requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child because I am concerned about [briefly describe concerns, such as "difficulty reading," "trouble focusing in class," or "struggles with social interactions"].*

*Please contact me at [phone number] or [email address] to discuss next steps.*

*Thank you,*
*[Your Name]*
*[Date]*

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law sets clear deadlines to ensure evaluations happen in a timely manner. Here's what you need to know:

**15 School Days**: After receiving your written request, Rio Grande City Grulla ISD must provide you with a Notice of Proposed Evaluation and a consent form within 15 school days.

**45 School Days**: Once you sign and return the consent form, the district has 45 school days to complete the evaluation and hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to discuss the results.

**Important Notes:**
- School days do not include holidays or summer break
- The timeline may be extended if you and the district agree in writing
- If your child is absent for an extended period, this may affect the timeline

Keep copies of all documents and note the dates when you submit paperwork. If you feel the district is not meeting these deadlines, you have the right to file a complaint.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

After the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and filled with technical language, but don't let that intimidate you. Here's what to look for:

**Background Information**: A summary of your child's history, including medical, developmental, and educational background.

**Assessment Results**: Scores from each test administered, often shown as standard scores, percentiles, or age/grade equivalents.

**Observations**: Notes from professionals who observed your child during testing and in the classroom.

**Summary and Conclusions**: An explanation of what the results mean and whether your child meets the criteria for a disability category under IDEA.

**Recommendations**: Suggestions for services, supports, or accommodations that may help your child.

**Tips for Reviewing the Report:**
- Request a copy before the ARD meeting so you have time to read it
- Write down any questions you have
- Ask the evaluator to explain anything you don't understand
- You can bring someone with you to help review the report

You have the right to receive explanations in language you understand, including Spanish or other languages spoken in your home.

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

After the FIE is completed, Rio Grande City Grulla ISD will schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. This is a team meeting where you and school staff come together to:

1. **Review the evaluation results**
2. **Determine eligibility**: Does your child meet the criteria for special education services under one of the 13 disability categories recognized by IDEA?
3. **Develop an IEP**: If eligible, the team will create an Individualized Education Program outlining goals, services, and supports for your child

**Who Attends the ARD Meeting?**
- You (the parent or guardian)
- A general education teacher
- A special education teacher
- A representative from Rio Grande City Grulla ISD who can commit resources
- Someone who can interpret the evaluation results (often the school psychologist)
- Others you choose to invite (a family member, advocate, or friend for support)

**Remember**: You are an equal member of the ARD committee. Your input matters, and you do not have to agree to anything you don't feel is right for your child.
