# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Mission CISD Parents

If you're a parent in Mission CISD and you've heard the term "FIE" from your child's teacher, counselor, or another parent, you're in the right place. Understanding the special education process can feel overwhelming, especially when you're worried about your child's progress in school. Whether your child attends one of Mission's elementary schools, middle schools, or Mission High School, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know about the Full Individual Evaluation—the first major step toward getting your child the support they need to succeed.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A **Full Individual Evaluation (FIE)** is a comprehensive assessment that determines whether your child qualifies for special education services in Texas. Think of it as a detailed snapshot of your child's unique strengths, challenges, and learning needs.

The FIE is sometimes called a "psychoeducational evaluation" because it looks at both psychological factors (like how your child thinks and processes information) and educational factors (like how they're performing academically). This evaluation is required by both federal law (the **Individuals with Disabilities Education Act**, or IDEA) and **Texas Education Code Chapter 29** before any child can receive special education services.

A FIE is not a single test. It's a collection of assessments, observations, and information-gathering conducted by a team of qualified professionals. The goal is to understand your child as a whole person—not just to identify what's difficult, but also to discover their capabilities and potential.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Mission CISD?

Mission CISD serves approximately 14,020 students across the Rio Grande Valley, and like all Texas school districts, it is committed to identifying and supporting students who need specialized instruction. The FIE is important because it:

- **Opens the door to services**: Without a completed FIE, your child cannot be found eligible for special education
- **Provides a roadmap**: The evaluation results help educators understand exactly how to support your child's learning
- **Establishes baseline data**: The FIE creates a starting point to measure your child's progress over time
- **Ensures appropriate placement**: Results guide decisions about the right classroom setting and supports
- **Protects your child's rights**: The formal evaluation process ensures your child receives a fair and thorough assessment

For Mission CISD families, the FIE process connects you with the district's special education department and begins an important partnership between you and your child's school team.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE examines multiple areas of your child's development and learning. The specific assessments depend on your child's suspected disability and individual needs, but typically include:

**Cognitive Ability**
How your child thinks, reasons, solves problems, and processes information. This often involves standardized IQ testing.

**Academic Achievement**
Your child's current skill levels in reading, writing, math, and other academic areas compared to grade-level expectations.

**Communication Skills**
How well your child understands and uses language, including speaking, listening, and social communication.

**Motor Skills**
Fine motor abilities (like writing and cutting) and gross motor abilities (like running and coordination), when relevant.

**Social and Emotional Functioning**
How your child interacts with others, manages emotions, and handles social situations.

**Adaptive Behavior**
Your child's ability to handle everyday activities and life skills appropriate for their age.

**Health and Developmental History**
Information about your child's medical background, developmental milestones, and any relevant family history.

The evaluation team will review existing records, observe your child in different settings, interview you and your child's teachers, and conduct formal assessments. Your input as a parent is a crucial part of this process—you know your child better than anyone.

## How to Request a FIE at Mission CISD

You have the right to request a FIE for your child at any time. Here's how to do it in Mission CISD:

**Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing**

While you can make a verbal request, we strongly recommend submitting a written request to create a clear record. Address your letter to:

- Your child's principal, AND
- The Mission CISD Special Education Department

Your letter should include:
- Your child's full name and date of birth
- Your child's school and grade
- A clear statement that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation
- A brief description of your concerns about your child's learning or development
- Your contact information
- The date

**Step 2: Keep Copies of Everything**

Make a copy of your request letter before sending it. Keep a folder with all documents related to your child's evaluation.

**Step 3: Await the District's Response**

After receiving your written request, Mission CISD must respond within **15 school days**. They will either:
- Agree to evaluate and send you a consent form, OR
- Decline to evaluate and provide written notice explaining why

If the district agrees to evaluate, you'll receive paperwork explaining the evaluation process and asking for your written consent. **The evaluation cannot begin until you sign and return this consent form.**

**Sample Request Letter:**

> Dear [Principal's Name] and Mission CISD Special Education Department,
> 
> I am the parent of [Child's Name], a [grade] student at [School Name]. I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child.
> 
> I have concerns about [briefly describe concerns—for example: my child's reading progress, attention in class, speech development, etc.]. I believe an evaluation would help us understand how to better support my child's learning.
> 
> Please contact me at [phone number] or [email] to discuss next steps.
> 
> Thank you for your attention to this request.
> 
> Sincerely,
> [Your Name]
> Date: [Date]

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law establishes clear timelines to ensure evaluations happen promptly:

| Milestone | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| District response to your written request | Within **15 school days** |
| Completion of the FIE | Within **45 school days** of receiving your signed consent |
| ARD meeting to review results | Within **30 calendar days** of completing the FIE |

**Important notes about the 45-day timeline:**

- The clock starts when the district receives your signed consent form
- Days when school is not in session for 5+ consecutive days (like summer break or winter break) typically do not count
- The district and parents can agree in writing to extend the timeline if needed
- If your child is absent for extended periods, this may affect the timeline

If Mission CISD is not meeting these timelines, you have the right to ask questions and advocate for your child. Document all communications and don't hesitate to reach out to the special education department for updates.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

The FIE report can be lengthy and full of technical language, but you have every right to understand it fully. Here's what to expect:

**Background Information**
A summary of your child's history, including developmental milestones, medical history, educational history, and your concerns.

**Assessment Results**
Detailed findings from each evaluation area, including standardized test scores. You'll often see terms like:
- *Standard scores* (average is typically 100)
- *Percentile ranks* (how your child compares to same-age peers)
- *Age or grade equivalents* (use these cautiously—they can be misleading)

**Observations**
Notes from professionals who watched your child in the classroom or during testing.

**Eligibility Determination**
The team's conclusion about whether your child meets criteria for one of Texas's 13 disability categories under IDEA.

**Recommendations**
Suggestions for supports, services, accommodations, or instructional strategies.

**Tips for reviewing the report:**

- Request a copy of the report **before** the ARD meeting so you have time to review it
- Write down questions as you read
- Ask the school psychologist or diagnostician to explain anything you don't understand
- Focus on the recommendations section—this guides your child's support plan

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

Once the FIE is complete, Mission CISD will schedule an **ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting**. This is Texas's version of what federal law calls the IEP meeting.

- You (and anyone you invite to support you)
- A school administrator
- Your child's teacher(s)
- The professionals who conducted the evaluation
- A special education teacher
- Other specialists as needed
