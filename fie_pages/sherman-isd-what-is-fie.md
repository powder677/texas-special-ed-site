# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Sherman ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Sherman ISD and you've heard the term "FIE" mentioned at your child's school, you're in the right place. Whether your child is struggling academically, facing behavioral challenges, or you simply suspect they may need additional support, understanding the Full Individual Evaluation process is an important first step. Sherman ISD serves approximately 10,650 students across the district, and like all Texas public schools, they are required to identify and evaluate children who may need special education services. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about the FIE process—in plain, parent-friendly language.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A FIE, or Full Individual Evaluation, is a comprehensive assessment that Texas public schools use to determine whether a child has a disability and needs special education services. You might also hear it called a "full and individual evaluation" or a "psychoeducational evaluation."

Think of the FIE as a detailed picture of your child—their strengths, challenges, learning style, and any areas where they might need extra support. It's not just one test; it's a collection of assessments, observations, interviews, and reviews that work together to help the school understand your child's unique needs.

The FIE is required under both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas state law (Texas Education Code Chapter 29) before a child can receive special education services. This evaluation is provided at no cost to families.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Sherman ISD?

For families in Sherman ISD, the FIE serves as the gateway to special education services and supports. Without a completed FIE, your child cannot be found eligible for special education—which means they can't receive an Individualized Education Program (IEP) or the specialized instruction that comes with it.

The FIE matters because it:

- **Identifies your child's specific needs** so that support can be tailored to them
- **Documents strengths** as well as areas of challenge
- **Provides legally required evidence** for eligibility decisions
- **Creates a baseline** to measure your child's progress over time
- **Ensures Sherman ISD provides appropriate services** under state and federal law

The evaluation process also gives you, as a parent, valuable information about how your child learns and what strategies might help them succeed—both at school and at home.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is comprehensive by design. Depending on your child's suspected areas of need, the evaluation may include assessments in some or all of the following areas:

- **Cognitive ability** – How your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems
- **Academic achievement** – Reading, writing, math, and other academic skills
- **Communication skills** – Speech, language, and how your child expresses and understands information
- **Social and emotional development** – How your child interacts with others and manages emotions
- **Adaptive behavior** – Daily living skills and independence appropriate for their age
- **Motor skills** – Fine motor (writing, cutting) and gross motor (running, climbing) abilities
- **Health and developmental history** – Medical information and developmental milestones
- **Behavior** – Patterns of behavior that may affect learning

Sherman ISD must evaluate your child in all areas related to the suspected disability. The evaluation team will use a variety of tools and strategies—not just one test—to get a complete picture.

## How to Request a FIE at Sherman ISD

You have the right to request a FIE for your child at any time. Here's how to do it:

**Step 1: Put your request in writing**

While you can make a verbal request, a written request creates a clear record and starts the legal timeline. Write a simple letter or email to your child's principal or the Sherman ISD Special Education Department.

Your letter might say something like:

*"Dear [Principal's Name], I am requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [Child's Name], who is in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned about [briefly describe your concerns]. Please send me the necessary paperwork to begin the evaluation process. Thank you, [Your Name]"*

**Step 2: Wait for the school's response**

Once Sherman ISD receives your written request, they must respond within 15 school days. They will either:
- Send you a consent form to sign, agreeing to the evaluation, OR
- Send you written notice explaining why they are refusing to evaluate, along with your rights

**Step 3: Sign and return the consent form**

The evaluation cannot begin until you provide written consent. The legal timeline starts once the school receives your signed consent form.

**Step 4: Keep copies of everything**

Make copies of your request letter, the consent form, and any other communication. This documentation can be valuable throughout the process.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law sets clear deadlines to protect families and ensure timely evaluations. Here's what you need to know:

| Milestone | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| School responds to your request | Within **15 school days** of receiving your written request |
| FIE must be completed | Within **45 school days** of receiving signed consent |
| ARD meeting to review results | Within **30 calendar days** of completing the FIE |

**Important notes about the timeline:**

- The 45-school-day deadline does not include days when school is not in session (like summer break or winter break, if they are longer than 5 consecutive days)
- If you provide consent fewer than 35 school days before the end of the school year, the district may have additional time
- If Sherman ISD misses these deadlines, you have the right to file a complaint

## Understanding Your FIE Report

Once the evaluation is complete, you will receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and technical, but here's what to look for:

**Background information** – A summary of your child's history, including any previous evaluations, medical information, and educational background

**Assessment results** – Scores and descriptions from each test or assessment tool used

**Observations** – Notes from evaluators who observed your child in the classroom or during testing

**Summary of findings** – An explanation of what the results mean for your child

**Eligibility determination** – Whether your child meets the criteria for one or more of the 13 disability categories under IDEA

**Recommendations** – Suggested strategies, accommodations, or services that may help your child

Don't hesitate to ask Sherman ISD staff to explain anything in the report that you don't understand. You can also request a meeting before the ARD to review the report with the school psychologist or evaluation specialist.

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

After the FIE is completed, Sherman ISD will schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. This is where the evaluation results are officially reviewed and eligibility is determined.

**Who attends the ARD meeting?**
- You (and anyone you choose to bring for support)
- A district representative who can commit resources
- Your child's teacher(s)
- A special education teacher
- The person who conducted or can interpret the evaluation results
- Your child (when appropriate)

**What happens at the ARD meeting?**

1. The team reviews the FIE results
2. The team determines if your child meets eligibility criteria for special education
3. If eligible, the team works together to develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP)
4. You discuss goals, services, accommodations, and placement

Remember: You are an equal member of the ARD committee. Your input matters, and you have the right to ask questions, express concerns, and request changes.

## Your Rights During the FIE Process at Sherman ISD

As a parent, you have important rights throughout the FIE process. These rights are protected under IDEA and Texas Education Code Chapter 29:

- **Right to request an evaluation** – You can ask for a FIE at any time
- **Right to informed consent** – The school must explain the evaluation and get your written permission before testing
- **Right to receive the FIE report** – You must receive a copy of the evaluation results
- **Right to participate in all meetings** – You are a full member of the ARD committee
- **Right to disagree** – If you disagree with the FIE results, you can request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at the district's expense
- **Right to confidentiality** – Your child's records are protected by federal privacy laws
- **Right to receive notice in your native language** – Sherman ISD must communicate with you in a language you understand
- **Right to bring an advocate** – You can bring anyone you choose to ARD meetings for support

Sherman ISD must provide you with a copy of your procedural safeguards (your rights) at key points in the process. Read this document carefully—it's your roadmap to understanding what you're entitled to.
