# What Is a FIE? A Parent's Guide for Longview ISD Families

If you're a parent in Longview ISD wondering whether your child might need extra support at school, you've come to the right place. Understanding the evaluation process is the first step toward getting your child the help they deserve. Whether your child attends one of Longview's elementary schools, middle schools, or Longview High School, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know about the Full Individual Evaluation—often called a FIE—in clear, straightforward language.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A FIE, or Full Individual Evaluation, is a comprehensive assessment that Texas school districts use to determine whether a child qualifies for special education services. Think of it as a detailed portrait of your child—their strengths, challenges, learning style, and individual needs.

Unlike a simple screening or classroom test, a FIE involves multiple trained professionals who look at your child from different angles. It typically includes assessments in areas like thinking skills, academic achievement, social-emotional development, and behavior. The goal isn't to label your child but to understand how they learn best and what supports might help them succeed.

In Texas, the FIE is required by both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and state law (Texas Education Code Chapter 29) before a child can receive special education services. This evaluation is provided at no cost to you—it's completely free.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Longview ISD?

The FIE serves as the foundation for everything that follows in your child's special education journey at Longview ISD. Here's why it matters:

**It identifies your child's unique needs.** Every child is different, and the FIE helps pinpoint exactly where your child needs support—whether that's in reading, math, speech, behavior, or another area.

**It determines eligibility.** The results help the school team decide if your child qualifies for special education services under one of the 13 disability categories recognized in Texas.

**It guides the development of your child's plan.** If your child qualifies, the FIE results shape their Individualized Education Program (IEP), ensuring the services and supports are tailored specifically to them.

**It protects your child's rights.** A thorough, well-conducted FIE ensures your child isn't incorrectly identified—or overlooked—for services they need.

For Longview ISD families, the FIE process connects you with the district's special education team, who will become important partners in your child's education.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE looks at your whole child, not just one area. Depending on your child's suspected needs, the evaluation may include some or all of the following:

**Cognitive Ability (Intelligence)**
This measures how your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems. It's not about how "smart" your child is—it's about understanding their thinking patterns.

**Academic Achievement**
These tests look at your child's skills in reading, writing, and math compared to other children their age.

**Speech and Language**
This evaluates how well your child understands language, expresses themselves, and uses speech sounds.

**Social-Emotional and Behavioral Functioning**
This area explores your child's emotions, behavior, social skills, and mental health needs.

**Adaptive Behavior**
These assessments measure practical life skills—things like self-care, communication, and getting along with others.

**Motor Skills**
Fine motor (like writing and cutting) and gross motor (like running and jumping) skills may be evaluated if there are concerns.

**Health and Developmental History**
The team will also gather information about your child's medical history, developmental milestones, and any previous evaluations.

The specific areas tested depend on your child's suspected disability and individual concerns. The evaluation team will only assess areas relevant to your child's needs.

## How to Request a FIE at Longview ISD

You have the right to request a FIE for your child at any time. You don't need to wait for the school to suggest it. Here's how to make a request in Longview ISD:

**Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing**

While you can make a verbal request, we strongly recommend putting it in writing. This creates a paper trail and starts the official timeline. You can write a simple letter or email like this:

*"Dear [Principal's Name or Special Education Department],*

*I am the parent of [Child's Name], a student in [Grade] at [School Name]. I am concerned about my child's progress in [specific area—reading, behavior, speech, etc.]. I am requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) to determine if my child qualifies for special education services.*

*Please contact me at [phone number] or [email address] to discuss next steps.*

*Sincerely,*
*[Your Name]"*

**Step 2: Submit to the Right Person**

You can send your written request to:
- Your child's principal
- Your child's teacher
- The Longview ISD Special Education Department
- The district's central office

**Step 3: Keep a Copy**

Always keep a copy of your request and note the date you submitted it. This is important for tracking the timeline.

**Step 4: Wait for the School's Response**

Once Longview ISD receives your request, they must respond within **15 school days**. They will either:
- Provide you with a consent form to begin the evaluation, OR
- Send you a "Prior Written Notice" explaining why they are declining to evaluate

If the district agrees to evaluate, they will send you paperwork explaining what the evaluation will include and asking for your written consent. The evaluation cannot begin until you sign and return this consent form.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law sets clear deadlines for the FIE process. Understanding these timelines helps you know what to expect and ensures the district stays on track.

**15 School Days**
After receiving your written request, Longview ISD has 15 school days to either give you a consent form or provide written notice explaining why they won't evaluate.

**45 School Days**
Once you sign and return the consent form, the district has 45 school days to complete the FIE and hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to review the results.

**Important notes about the timeline:**
- "School days" means days when school is in session—holidays and summer break don't count
- If your child is absent for an extended period, the timeline may be adjusted
- If you submit a request near the end of the school year, the timeline continues into the next school year

If Longview ISD is running behind on these deadlines, you have every right to contact the special education department and ask for an update.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

After the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and technical, but don't let that intimidate you. Here's how to approach it:

**Request a copy before the ARD meeting.** You have the right to receive the FIE report before the meeting where it will be discussed. This gives you time to read it carefully and prepare questions.

**Focus on these key sections:**

- **Background Information** – This summarizes your child's history and the concerns that led to the evaluation
- **Test Results** – This section includes scores from various assessments; ask the evaluator to explain what the numbers mean
- **Observations** – Notes about how your child behaved and responded during testing
- **Conclusions and Recommendations** – The evaluator's interpretation of the results and suggested next steps
- **Eligibility Determination** – Whether your child qualifies for special education under one of the disability categories

**Ask questions.** If something in the report doesn't make sense, ask for clarification. The evaluation team should be willing to explain their findings in plain language.

**Share your perspective.** If something in the report doesn't match what you see at home, speak up. Your input matters.

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

Once the FIE is complete, Longview ISD will schedule an ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal). In other states, this is often called an IEP meeting. At this meeting, you and the school team will:

**Review the FIE results together.** The evaluation team will explain what they found and answer your questions.

**Determine eligibility.** The team will decide whether your child qualifies for special education services under one of Texas's 13 disability categories.

**If eligible, develop an IEP.** If your child qualifies, the team will work together to create an Individualized Education Program (IEP) outlining the goals, services, and supports your child will receive.
