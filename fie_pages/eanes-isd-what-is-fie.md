# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Eanes ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Eanes ISD and suspect your child may need extra support at school, you've likely heard the term "FIE" mentioned by teachers, counselors, or other parents in the Austin area. Understanding what a Full Individual Evaluation is—and how it works within Eanes ISD specifically—is the first step toward getting your child the help they deserve. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know in plain, parent-friendly language.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A FIE, or Full Individual Evaluation, is Texas's comprehensive assessment process used to determine whether a child qualifies for special education services. You might also hear it called a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation."

Think of the FIE as a detailed snapshot of your child's unique strengths and challenges. It's not a single test but rather a collection of assessments, observations, and information-gathering that helps the school understand how your child learns, processes information, and functions in an educational setting.

Under both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and the Texas Education Code Chapter 29, school districts like Eanes ISD must conduct a FIE before a child can receive special education services. This evaluation is provided at no cost to you as a parent.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Eanes ISD?

Eanes ISD serves approximately 7,890 students across its highly regarded schools in the Austin area. With high academic expectations and a commitment to student success, ensuring every child has the support they need is essential.

A FIE is important because it:

- **Opens the door to services**: Without a FIE, your child cannot qualify for special education services or an Individualized Education Program (IEP)
- **Provides a complete picture**: The evaluation looks at your child from multiple angles, not just academics
- **Identifies specific needs**: Rather than guessing what might help, the FIE pinpoints exactly where your child needs support
- **Creates a baseline**: The results help track your child's progress over time
- **Guides educational planning**: Teachers and specialists use FIE results to design effective interventions

For Eanes ISD families, a thorough FIE ensures your child can access the same opportunities as their peers, even if they learn differently.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is comprehensive by design. Depending on your child's suspected areas of need, the evaluation may assess:

**Cognitive Ability**
How your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems. This often includes an IQ assessment.

**Academic Achievement**
Your child's current performance in reading, writing, math, and other academic areas compared to grade-level expectations.

**Communication Skills**
How your child understands and uses language, including both spoken and written communication.

**Social and Emotional Functioning**
Your child's behavior, emotional regulation, social skills, and mental health needs.

**Adaptive Behavior**
Daily living skills and how independently your child functions compared to same-age peers.

**Motor Skills**
Fine motor abilities (like writing and cutting) and gross motor skills (like balance and coordination).

**Sensory Processing**
How your child responds to sensory input like sounds, textures, and visual stimuli.

**Health and Developmental History**
Information about your child's medical background, developmental milestones, and any previous evaluations or diagnoses.

The specific assessments included in your child's FIE will depend on the concerns that prompted the evaluation. The team should evaluate all areas of suspected disability.

## How to Request a FIE at Eanes ISD

Any parent can request a FIE for their child at any time. Here's how to do it in Eanes ISD:

**Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing**

While you can verbally ask for an evaluation, a written request creates documentation and starts the legal timeline. Address your letter to:

- Your child's principal, OR
- The Eanes ISD Special Education Department

Your letter should include:
- Your child's full name and date of birth
- Your child's current school and grade
- A brief description of your concerns
- A clear statement that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation
- The date and your signature

**Step 2: Submit Your Request**

Deliver your request via:
- Hand delivery (ask for a date-stamped copy)
- Email (keep a copy of the sent message)
- Certified mail with return receipt

**Step 3: Wait for the District's Response**

Eanes ISD must respond to your request within 15 school days. They will either:
- Agree to evaluate and send you a consent form, OR
- Decline to evaluate and provide written notice explaining why

**Sample Request Language:**

*"I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [Child's Name], who attends [School Name] in [Grade]. I am concerned about [briefly describe concerns, such as reading difficulties, attention issues, or social challenges]. Please evaluate my child in all areas of suspected disability. Thank you for your attention to this request."*

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law establishes clear timelines to protect families from unnecessary delays:

| Step | Timeline |
|------|----------|
| District response to your request | Within **15 school days** |
| You sign and return consent form | At your discretion (take time to ask questions) |
| District completes the FIE | Within **45 school days** of receiving signed consent |
| ARD meeting to review results | Within **30 calendar days** of FIE completion |

**Important notes about the timeline:**

- The 45-school-day clock doesn't start until you sign and return the consent form
- School days don't include holidays, breaks, or summer vacation
- If your child is absent for an extended period, the timeline may be adjusted
- The district can request a timeline extension in certain circumstances, but they must document the reason

If Eanes ISD misses these deadlines without valid reason, they may be in violation of state law. You have the right to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency if this occurs.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

The FIE report can be lengthy and filled with technical language. Here's how to make sense of it:

**Key Sections to Look For:**

- **Background Information**: Your child's history, including input you provided
- **Assessment Results**: Scores from each evaluation, often with charts and graphs
- **Behavioral Observations**: Notes on how your child performed during testing
- **Summary and Conclusions**: The evaluator's interpretation of all the data
- **Recommendations**: Suggested supports, services, or next steps

**Understanding the Scores:**

Most tests use standard scores with an average of 100. Generally:
- 90-110: Average range
- 80-89: Low average
- 70-79: Borderline
- Below 70: May indicate significant difficulty

Percentile ranks show how your child compares to peers. A percentile of 25 means your child scored higher than 25% of children the same age.

**Tips for Reviewing the Report:**

- Request a copy before the ARD meeting so you have time to read it
- Highlight sections you don't understand and bring questions
- Ask the evaluators to explain findings in plain language
- Consider having an advocate or educational consultant review it with you

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

Once the FIE is complete, Eanes ISD will schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. In Texas, this is what other states call an IEP meeting.

**At the ARD meeting, the team will:**

1. Review the FIE results together
2. Determine if your child qualifies for special education under one of the 13 disability categories
3. If eligible, develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) with goals and services
4. Discuss placement options and any accommodations needed

**Who attends the ARD meeting:**

- You (and anyone you invite for support)
- A general education teacher
- A special education teacher
- An Eanes ISD administrator or representative
- The professionals who conducted the FIE
- Related service providers (if applicable)
- Your child (when appropriate)

**Remember:** You are an equal member of the ARD committee. Your input matters, and you can ask questions, express disagreement, and request time to consider decisions.

## Your Rights During the FIE Process at Eanes ISD

Texas and federal law provide important protections for families during the evaluation process:

**You have the right to:**

- Request a FIE at any time
- Receive prior written notice before the district evaluates (or refuses to evaluate) your child
- Give or withhold informed consent—the evaluation cannot happen without your written permission
- Receive the FIE report at no cost
