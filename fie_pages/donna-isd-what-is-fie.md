# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Donna ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Donna ISD and you've been hearing terms like "FIE" or "Full Individual Evaluation," you're not alone in wondering what it all means. Whether your child is struggling in school, a teacher has expressed concerns, or you simply want to understand your options, this guide is here to help. Donna ISD serves families across the Rio Grande Valley, and understanding the special education evaluation process is an important first step in getting your child the support they deserve.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation—commonly called a FIE—is Texas's comprehensive assessment used to determine whether a child qualifies for special education services. You might also hear it referred to as a "psychoeducational evaluation." This isn't just a single test; it's a thorough, multi-faceted evaluation that looks at your child from many different angles.

Under federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas Education Code Chapter 29, school districts like Donna ISD are required to conduct a FIE before a child can receive special education services. The evaluation must be completed at no cost to you, and it's designed to identify your child's unique strengths and challenges.

Think of the FIE as a detailed picture of how your child learns, processes information, and functions in a school setting. It's the foundation for building an educational plan tailored specifically to your child's needs.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Donna ISD?

For Donna ISD families, the FIE serves several critical purposes:

**It opens doors to support.** Without a FIE, your child cannot qualify for special education services under IDEA. This evaluation is the gateway to individualized instruction, therapies, accommodations, and other supports that can make a real difference in your child's education.

**It provides a complete picture.** The FIE goes beyond grades and behavior reports. It examines cognitive abilities, academic achievement, social-emotional functioning, and more. This comprehensive view helps educators understand *why* your child may be struggling—not just *that* they're struggling.

**It guides decision-making.** The information gathered during a FIE directly informs the development of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP). With approximately 13,240 students in the district, Donna ISD uses FIE results to ensure that each child's educational plan is truly individualized.

**It establishes a baseline.** The FIE creates a documented snapshot of your child's abilities at a specific point in time, which can be valuable for tracking progress and making adjustments to services over the years.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is not a one-size-fits-all test. Texas law requires that the evaluation assess all areas related to your child's suspected disability. Depending on your child's needs, the FIE may include:

- **Cognitive ability (intelligence testing):** How your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems
- **Academic achievement:** Reading, writing, and math skills compared to grade-level expectations
- **Communication skills:** Speech, language comprehension, and expression abilities
- **Social-emotional functioning:** How your child interacts with others and manages emotions
- **Adaptive behavior:** Daily living skills and how your child functions in various environments
- **Motor skills:** Fine motor (writing, cutting) and gross motor (running, jumping) abilities
- **Health and developmental history:** Medical information that may impact learning
- **Behavioral observations:** How your child behaves in different settings
- **Vision and hearing screenings:** To rule out sensory issues affecting learning

The evaluation team will select assessments based on your child's specific concerns. For example, if speech is a concern, a speech-language pathologist will conduct specialized testing. If there are concerns about attention or behavior, those areas will be thoroughly examined.

## How to Request a FIE at Donna ISD

As a parent, you have the right to request a FIE at any time if you suspect your child has a disability that affects their learning. Here's how to do it in Donna ISD:

**Step 1: Put your request in writing.** While you can make a verbal request, a written request creates a clear record and starts the official timeline. Address your letter to your child's principal or the Donna ISD Special Education Department.

**Step 2: Be specific about your concerns.** Explain why you believe your child needs to be evaluated. Include examples of struggles you've observed at home or concerns teachers have shared.

**Step 3: Keep a copy for your records.** Always make a copy of your request and note the date you submitted it. This documentation protects you and ensures accountability.

**Step 4: Wait for the district's response.** Donna ISD must respond to your request within 15 school days. They will either provide consent forms to begin the evaluation or explain in writing why they are declining to evaluate.

**Sample Request Letter:**

*Dear [Principal's Name or Special Education Director],*

*I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [Child's Name], who is in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned about [briefly describe concerns, such as difficulty reading, trouble focusing, social challenges, etc.].*

*Please send me the necessary consent forms to begin this process. Thank you for your attention to this matter.*

*Sincerely,*
*[Your Name]*
*[Date]*
*[Contact Information]*

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas has specific timelines that Donna ISD must follow once you request a FIE:

**15 school days:** After receiving your written request, the district must respond by either providing you with consent forms or issuing a Prior Written Notice explaining why they're declining to evaluate.

**45 school days:** Once you sign and return the consent form, Donna ISD has 45 school days to complete the evaluation and hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to review the results.

**Important notes about the timeline:**

- The 45-day clock starts the day after the district receives your signed consent
- School days don't include holidays, weekends, or summer break (unless your child attends summer school)
- If your child is absent for an extended period, the timeline may be adjusted
- The district cannot delay the evaluation process unnecessarily

If you feel Donna ISD is not meeting these timelines, document your concerns in writing and consider reaching out to the Special Education Department or contacting a parent advocacy organization.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

The FIE report can be lengthy and filled with technical terms. Here's how to approach it:

**Request a copy in advance.** Ask for the FIE report at least a few days before your ARD meeting. This gives you time to read it carefully and prepare questions.

**Look for these key sections:**

- **Background information:** Your child's history, including developmental milestones and medical information
- **Assessment results:** Scores from various tests, often presented as standard scores, percentiles, or age/grade equivalents
- **Observations:** Notes from professionals who observed your child during testing and in the classroom
- **Summary and recommendations:** The evaluation team's conclusions about whether your child qualifies for special education and what services might help

**Understanding the numbers:**

- A standard score of 100 is considered average
- Percentile ranks show how your child compares to same-age peers (50th percentile = average)
- Scores significantly below average in certain areas may indicate a disability

**Ask questions.** If something in the report doesn't make sense, ask the evaluators to explain it in plain language. You have every right to understand every part of your child's evaluation.

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

Once the FIE is complete, Donna ISD will schedule an ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal meeting—Texas's version of an IEP meeting). This is where important decisions are made about your child's education.

**At the ARD meeting, the team will:**

1. **Review the FIE results.** Each professional who participated in the evaluation will explain their findings.

2. **Determine eligibility.** Based on the FIE, the team will decide whether your child qualifies for special education under one or more of the 13 disability categories recognized by IDEA.

3. **Develop the IEP (if eligible).** If your child qualifies, the team will begin creating an Individualized Education Program that outlines goals, services, accommodations, and supports.

**Who attends the ARD meeting?**

- You (and anyone you choose to bring for support)
- A general education teacher
- A special education teacher
- A representative from Donna ISD who can commit resources
- Someone who can interpret the evaluation results
- Related service providers, if applicable (speech therapist, occupational therapist, etc.)
- Your child, when appropriate
