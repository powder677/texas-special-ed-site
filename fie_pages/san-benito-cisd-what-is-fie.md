# What Is a FIE? A Guide for San Benito CISD Parents

If you're a parent in San Benito CISD and someone has mentioned that your child might need a "FIE," you're probably wondering what that means and what to expect. You're not alone—many families in our Rio Grande Valley community have walked this same path. This guide is here to help you understand the Full Individual Evaluation process, know your rights, and feel confident as you navigate special education services for your child in San Benito CISD.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A FIE, or Full Individual Evaluation, is a comprehensive assessment that Texas school districts use to determine whether a child has a disability and needs special education services. You might also hear it called a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation."

Think of a FIE as a detailed snapshot of your child—how they learn, what challenges they face, and what strengths they bring to the classroom. The evaluation looks at many different areas of your child's development and learning to get a complete picture.

Under both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas state law (Texas Education Code Chapter 29), school districts like San Benito CISD must conduct a FIE before determining if a child qualifies for special education services. This isn't just a single test—it's a collection of assessments, observations, and information gathering designed to understand your child's unique needs.

## Why Is a FIE Important at San Benito CISD?

The FIE is the foundation of everything that follows in the special education process at San Benito CISD. Here's why it matters so much:

**It opens doors to support.** Without a FIE, your child cannot receive special education services through an Individualized Education Program (IEP). The evaluation is the first step toward getting your child the help they need.

**It identifies your child's unique profile.** Every child is different. The FIE helps San Benito CISD staff understand exactly how your child learns, where they struggle, and where they shine.

**It guides educational planning.** The results of the FIE directly shape the goals, services, and accommodations that will be written into your child's IEP. A thorough evaluation leads to better, more personalized support.

**It establishes a baseline.** The FIE gives teachers and specialists a starting point to measure your child's progress over time.

For families in San Benito CISD, the FIE process connects you with the district's special education team and begins an important partnership focused on your child's success.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is comprehensive by design. Texas law requires that the evaluation assess all areas related to your child's suspected disability. Depending on your child's needs, the FIE may include:

**Cognitive ability (thinking skills):** This looks at how your child reasons, solves problems, and processes information.

**Academic achievement:** How is your child performing in reading, writing, and math compared to other students their age?

**Communication skills:** This includes both understanding language (receptive) and expressing thoughts (expressive), as well as speech clarity.

**Social and emotional development:** How does your child interact with others? Are there behavioral concerns or emotional challenges affecting learning?

**Adaptive behavior:** Can your child handle age-appropriate daily living skills and function independently?

**Motor skills:** This may include both fine motor skills (like writing and cutting) and gross motor skills (like running and balance).

**Health and developmental history:** The evaluation team will review your child's medical background, developmental milestones, and any relevant family history.

**Observation:** Trained professionals will observe your child in their classroom or other school settings to see how they function in a typical day.

The specific assessments used will depend on your child's suspected disability. For example, a child who may have a learning disability will receive different tests than a child who may be on the autism spectrum.

## How to Request a FIE at San Benito CISD

As a parent, you have the right to request a FIE at any time if you suspect your child has a disability that's affecting their learning. Here's how to do it in San Benito CISD:

**Step 1: Put your request in writing.** While you can make a verbal request, a written request creates a clear record and starts the legal timeline. Address your letter to the principal of your child's school or to San Benito CISD's Special Education Department.

**Step 2: Be specific about your concerns.** In your letter, describe what you're seeing at home and what concerns you have about your child's learning or development. You don't need to know what disability your child might have—that's what the evaluation will determine.

**Step 3: Keep a copy for your records.** Always keep copies of any written communication with the school district.

**Step 4: Wait for the district's response.** San Benito CISD must respond to your request. They will either agree to evaluate your child and send you a consent form, or they will decline and provide you with written notice explaining why.

**Sample language for your request:**

*"Dear [Principal's Name], I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [Child's Name], who is in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned about [briefly describe your concerns, such as difficulty reading, behavior challenges, or speech delays]. I am requesting this evaluation to determine if my child has a disability and needs special education services. Please send me the consent forms to begin this process. Thank you, [Your Name]"*

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas has specific timelines to protect families and ensure evaluations happen promptly. Here's what you need to know:

**Consent to evaluation:** After San Benito CISD receives your written request, they must give you a consent form to sign. You must give written permission before any evaluation can begin.

**45 school days:** Once you sign and return the consent form, San Benito CISD has 45 school days to complete the FIE. This does not include days when school is not in session, such as summer break, winter break, or holidays.

**Important exceptions:** The 45-day timeline may be extended if your child misses school for three or more days during the evaluation period, or if your child enrolls in a different district during the process.

**What if the district says no?** If San Benito CISD declines to evaluate your child, they must give you Prior Written Notice explaining their decision. You have the right to disagree and request mediation or a due process hearing.

Understanding these timelines helps you track the process and know when to follow up if things seem delayed.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

After the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and filled with technical language, but it contains crucial information about your child. Here's how to approach it:

**Request a draft in advance.** Ask San Benito CISD for a copy of the FIE report before your ARD meeting so you have time to read and understand it.

**Look for these key sections:**

- *Background information:* A summary of your child's history and the concerns that led to the evaluation
- *Assessment results:* Scores and descriptions from each test administered
- *Observations:* Notes from professionals who observed your child
- *Summary and conclusions:* An explanation of what the results mean
- *Eligibility determination:* Whether your child meets the criteria for one or more disability categories under Texas law
- *Recommendations:* Suggestions for services, supports, and accommodations

**Ask questions.** If something in the report doesn't make sense, ask the school psychologist or diagnostician to explain it in plain language. You have every right to understand what's in your child's evaluation.

**Compare to what you see at home.** Does the report match your experience with your child? If something seems off, speak up. Your perspective as a parent is valuable.

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

Once the FIE is complete, San Benito CISD will schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. In other states, this is often called an IEP meeting—in Texas, we call it an ARD.

**At this meeting, the team will:**

1. Review the FIE results with you
2. Determine if your child is eligible for special education services
3. If eligible, identify your child's disability category (or categories)
4. Begin developing an Individualized Education Program (IEP) with goals, services, and supports tailored to your child's needs

**You are a vital member of the ARD committee.** Your input matters, and the team cannot make decisions without you. Texas law specifically recognizes parents as equal participants in this process.
