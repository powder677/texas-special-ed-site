# What Is a FIE? A Guide for McAllen ISD Parents

If you're a parent in McAllen ISD and you've been hearing terms like "FIE" or "evaluation" mentioned regarding your child, you're in the right place. Navigating special education can feel overwhelming, especially when you're learning a whole new vocabulary. This guide is designed specifically for families in our McAllen community to help you understand what a Full Individual Evaluation is, why it matters, and how the process works in our district. You are your child's greatest advocate, and understanding the FIE is an important first step.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A **Full Individual Evaluation**, commonly called a FIE, is a comprehensive assessment that Texas school districts use to determine whether a child has a disability and needs special education services. You might also hear it referred to as a "psychoeducational evaluation."

Think of the FIE as a detailed snapshot of your child—how they learn, what challenges they face, and what strengths they bring to the classroom. It's not just a single test but a collection of assessments, observations, and information gathered from multiple sources, including you as the parent.

Under federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and the Texas Education Code Chapter 29, school districts like McAllen ISD are required to conduct a FIE before determining if a child qualifies for special education services. This evaluation is provided at no cost to families.

## Why Is a FIE Important at McAllen ISD?

The FIE is the foundation of your child's special education journey. Here in McAllen ISD, serving over 20,000 students across our community, the district uses the FIE to:

- **Identify whether your child has a disability** that impacts their ability to learn in a general education setting
- **Understand your child's unique learning profile**, including their strengths and areas where they need support
- **Determine eligibility** for special education services under one of the 13 disability categories recognized in Texas
- **Guide the development of an Individualized Education Program (IEP)** if your child qualifies

Without a FIE, your child cannot receive special education services through McAllen ISD. It's the key that opens the door to the support and accommodations your child may need to succeed in school.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE looks at your child from many different angles. The specific assessments included will depend on your child's suspected areas of need, but a comprehensive evaluation typically examines:

- **Cognitive ability** – How your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems
- **Academic achievement** – Your child's skills in reading, writing, and math compared to grade-level expectations
- **Communication skills** – How your child understands and uses language
- **Social and emotional development** – Your child's behavior, emotional regulation, and social interactions
- **Adaptive behavior** – Daily living skills and how your child functions independently
- **Motor skills** – Fine motor (writing, cutting) and gross motor (running, jumping) abilities, if concerns exist
- **Health and developmental history** – Information gathered from you about your child's medical background and developmental milestones

The evaluation team at McAllen ISD will select assessments based on your child's specific concerns. For example, if speech is a concern, a speech-language pathologist will be involved. If there are behavioral concerns, assessments in that area will be included.

## How to Request a FIE at McAllen ISD

As a parent, you have the right to request a FIE for your child at any time. Here's how to do it in McAllen ISD:

### Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing
While you can make a verbal request, we strongly recommend putting it in writing. This creates documentation and starts the official timeline. You can write a simple letter or email stating:

*"I am requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [child's name], because I am concerned about [briefly describe your concerns]. Please send me the necessary consent forms."*

### Step 2: Submit Your Request
Send your written request to:
- Your child's teacher
- The school's principal
- The special education department at your child's campus

You may also contact the **McAllen ISD Special Education Department** directly for guidance on where to submit your request.

### Step 3: Receive and Sign the Consent Form
After receiving your request, the district must provide you with a consent form. **The evaluation cannot begin until you sign and return this form.** Take your time to review it and ask questions if anything is unclear.

### Step 4: Participate in the Process
You'll be asked to complete parent questionnaires and provide information about your child's history. Your input is a critical part of the evaluation—no one knows your child better than you do.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law establishes clear timelines to protect families and ensure evaluations happen promptly:

| Step | Timeline |
|------|----------|
| District must respond to your request | Within a **reasonable timeframe** (typically 15 school days to provide consent forms) |
| After you sign consent | The FIE must be **completed within 45 school days** |
| ARD/IEP meeting | Must be held within **30 calendar days** after the FIE is completed |

**Important:** The 45-school-day timeline does not include days when school is not in session for more than 5 consecutive days (like winter or summer break). If your child transfers from another Texas district, different timelines may apply.

If McAllen ISD needs more time, they must obtain your written agreement. You are not required to agree to an extension.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

Once the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and full of technical language, but here's what to look for:

### Key Sections of the FIE Report:
- **Background information** – Your child's history, including what you shared
- **Assessment results** – Scores from each test administered, often with explanations of what they mean
- **Observations** – Notes from teachers and evaluators about your child's behavior and learning
- **Summary and conclusions** – A synthesis of all the data collected
- **Eligibility determination** – Whether your child meets criteria for a disability category
- **Recommendations** – Suggestions for services, supports, or accommodations

### Tips for Reading the Report:
- Don't hesitate to ask the evaluation team to explain any terms or scores you don't understand
- Request a meeting to review the report before the official ARD meeting if you need more time
- Focus on the "recommendations" section—this will guide what happens next
- Remember that the report should reflect your child, not just numbers and labels

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

After the FIE is completed, McAllen ISD will schedule an **Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting**. In Texas, this is what other states call an IEP meeting. The ARD meeting must occur within 30 calendar days of completing the FIE.

### At the ARD Meeting, the Team Will:
- Review the FIE results together
- Determine if your child qualifies for special education under one of the 13 disability categories
- If eligible, develop an **Individualized Education Program (IEP)** outlining goals, services, and accommodations
- Discuss placement options and related services your child may need

### Who Attends the ARD Meeting?
- You (the parent) – you are a vital member of the team
- A general education teacher
- A special education teacher
- A school administrator (or representative)
- Someone who can interpret evaluation results (often the diagnostician)
- Related service providers (if applicable)
- Your child (when appropriate)

**You can also bring someone for support**—a family member, friend, advocate, or anyone who helps you feel comfortable.

## Your Rights During the FIE Process at McAllen ISD

Texas law and IDEA provide strong protections for families. As a parent in McAllen ISD, you have the right to:

- **Request a FIE at any time** – The school must respond to your request
- **Give or refuse consent** – The evaluation cannot happen without your written permission
- **Receive information in your native language** – McAllen ISD serves many Spanish-speaking families, and you have the right to receive documents and communication in Spanish if that's your preferred language
- **Participate in all meetings** – You are an equal member of the ARD committee
- **Review all records** – You can access your child's educational records at any time
- **Request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE)** – If you disagree with the FIE results, you can request an evaluation by an outside professional at the district's expense
