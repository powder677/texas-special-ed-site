# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Wylie ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Wylie ISD and you've heard the term "FIE" mentioned by teachers, counselors, or other parents, you're not alone in wondering what it means and how it might help your child. Whether your student attends school in Wylie, Sachse, Murphy, or any of the growing communities served by this thriving district, understanding the Full Individual Evaluation process is an important first step toward getting your child the support they deserve. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about FIEs in Wylie ISD—in plain, parent-friendly language.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation, or FIE, is a comprehensive assessment that Texas school districts conduct to determine whether a child has a disability and qualifies for special education services. You might also hear it called a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation."

Think of a FIE as a detailed snapshot of your child's strengths, challenges, and unique learning needs. It's not just one test—it's a collection of assessments, observations, and information gathered from multiple sources to create a complete picture of how your child learns and what support they might need.

The FIE is required under both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas state law (Texas Education Code Chapter 29) before a child can receive special education services. This evaluation is provided at no cost to families—the school district covers all expenses.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Wylie ISD?

Wylie ISD serves approximately 19,530 students across its campuses, and the district is committed to meeting the diverse learning needs of every child. A FIE is important because it:

- **Identifies your child's specific learning needs** in a way that classroom observations alone cannot
- **Determines eligibility** for special education services under one or more of the 13 disability categories recognized in Texas
- **Provides the foundation** for creating an Individualized Education Program (IEP) tailored to your child
- **Protects your child's rights** by ensuring that decisions about their education are based on comprehensive, objective data
- **Opens doors to support** including specialized instruction, related services like speech therapy or counseling, accommodations, and more

In a district as large and diverse as Wylie ISD, a thorough FIE ensures that your child isn't overlooked and receives the individualized attention they need to succeed.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE looks at many different areas of your child's development and functioning. The specific assessments used will depend on your child's suspected disability and individual concerns, but a FIE typically evaluates:

**Cognitive Ability (Intelligence)**
How your child thinks, reasons, and processes information

**Academic Achievement**
Your child's skills in reading, writing, math, and other academic areas

**Communication Skills**
Speech, language, and how your child expresses and understands information

**Social and Emotional Functioning**
Your child's behavior, emotional regulation, and social skills

**Adaptive Behavior**
Daily living skills and how your child functions in everyday situations

**Motor Skills**
Fine motor (writing, cutting) and gross motor (walking, coordination) abilities, if concerns exist

**Health and Developmental History**
Medical information, developmental milestones, and any relevant background

The FIE team will also gather information from you as the parent, your child's teachers, and other professionals who know your child well. Your input is invaluable—no one knows your child better than you do.

## How to Request a FIE at Wylie ISD

Any parent can request a FIE for their child at any time. You don't need to wait for the school to suggest it. Here's how to make your request in Wylie ISD:

**Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing**

While you can make a verbal request, a written request creates documentation and starts the official timeline. Write a simple letter or email to your child's principal or the Wylie ISD Special Education Department stating that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation for your child.

Your letter might say something like:

*"Dear [Principal's Name], I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [Child's Name], who is in [grade] at [school name]. I have concerns about [briefly describe your concerns]. Please send me the necessary consent forms to begin this process. Thank you, [Your Name]"*

**Step 2: Submit Your Request**

You can deliver your written request to:
- Your child's school principal
- Your child's teacher or school counselor
- The Wylie ISD Special Education Department

Keep a copy of your request and note the date you submitted it.

**Step 3: Wait for the District's Response**

Wylie ISD must respond to your request within **15 school days**. The district will either:
- Provide you with a "Notice and Consent for Evaluation" form to sign, OR
- Provide written notice explaining why they are declining to evaluate, along with information about your rights

**Step 4: Give Your Written Consent**

The evaluation cannot begin until you sign and return the consent form. Review it carefully, ask questions if needed, and return it promptly to start the evaluation timeline.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas Education Code and federal law establish clear timelines to ensure evaluations happen promptly. Here's what Wylie ISD must follow:

| Step | Timeline |
|------|----------|
| District responds to your request | Within **15 school days** |
| You receive and return consent form | At your discretion (but delays extend the overall timeline) |
| District completes the FIE | Within **45 school days** of receiving your signed consent |
| ARD meeting to review results | Within **30 calendar days** of completing the FIE |

**Important notes about the timeline:**

- The 45-school-day clock doesn't count days when school isn't in session for 5 or more consecutive days (like summer break or winter break)
- If your child is absent for an extended period, this may affect the timeline
- You and the district can mutually agree to extend deadlines if needed

If you feel Wylie ISD isn't meeting these timelines, document your concerns and don't hesitate to ask for updates or seek guidance from the resources listed at the end of this page.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

Once the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and full of technical terms, but don't be intimidated—you have the right to understand everything in it.

**What the report includes:**

- **Background information** about your child's history and the reasons for the evaluation
- **Assessment results** with scores, percentiles, and descriptions of what was measured
- **Observations** from evaluators and teachers
- **Summary of findings** explaining your child's strengths and areas of need
- **Eligibility determination** stating whether your child qualifies for special education under one of the 13 disability categories
- **Recommendations** for services, supports, or next steps

**Tips for reading the report:**

- Request a copy of the report **before** the ARD meeting so you have time to review it
- Highlight terms or sections you don't understand and write down your questions
- Ask the school psychologist or diagnostician to explain results in plain language
- Remember that scores are just one piece of the puzzle—the whole picture matters most

You can also request a meeting with the evaluator before the ARD to go over the results privately.

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

After the FIE is complete, Wylie ISD will schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting within 30 calendar days. This meeting brings together a team to discuss the evaluation results and determine next steps.

**Who attends the ARD meeting:**

- You (the parent)
- A general education teacher
- A special education teacher
- A Wylie ISD representative who can commit resources
- The person who conducted or can interpret the evaluation
- Others you or the school invite (you can bring a friend, advocate, or outside professional)

**What happens at the ARD:**

1. The team reviews and discusses the FIE results
2. The team determines whether your child is eligible for special education
3. If eligible, the team develops an Individualized Education Program (IEP)
4. Goals, services, accommodations, and placement are discussed and decided

**Remember:** You are an equal member of the ARD committee. Your input matters, and you should never feel pressured to sign documents you don't agree with. You can request more time to think or reconvene the meeting.
