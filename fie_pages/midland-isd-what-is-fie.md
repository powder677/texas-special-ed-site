# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Midland ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Midland ISD and you've been hearing terms like "FIE" or "Full Individual Evaluation," you're not alone—and you've come to the right place. Whether your child is struggling in school, has been identified by a teacher as possibly needing extra support, or you simply want to understand the special education process better, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know about the FIE process right here in Midland. With approximately 28,340 students across the district, Midland ISD serves a diverse community, and understanding how evaluations work is the first step toward getting your child the support they deserve.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation—commonly called a FIE—is Texas's comprehensive assessment process used to determine whether a child qualifies for special education services. You might also hear it referred to as a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation."

Think of a FIE as a detailed snapshot of your child's unique learning profile. It looks at how your child thinks, learns, communicates, behaves, and functions in school. The evaluation is conducted by a team of qualified professionals and is required by both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and state law (Texas Education Code Chapter 29) before any child can receive special education services.

The FIE is not a single test—it's a collection of assessments, observations, interviews, and reviews designed to paint a complete picture of your child's strengths and challenges.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Midland ISD?

For Midland ISD families, the FIE serves several critical purposes:

**It opens the door to support.** Without a completed FIE, your child cannot qualify for special education services in Texas. The evaluation is the essential first step.

**It identifies your child's specific needs.** Every child is different. The FIE helps the school understand exactly where your child needs help—whether that's reading, math, speech, behavior, or something else entirely.

**It guides the creation of an Individualized Education Program (IEP).** The results of the FIE directly inform the goals, accommodations, and services that will be written into your child's IEP.

**It establishes a baseline.** The FIE provides data that Midland ISD can use to measure your child's progress over time.

**It protects your child's rights.** Texas law requires that evaluations be thorough, fair, and conducted in a timely manner. The FIE process ensures your child is evaluated properly before any decisions are made.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is comprehensive by design. Depending on your child's suspected areas of need, the evaluation may include assessments in some or all of the following areas:

- **Cognitive ability (intelligence):** How your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems
- **Academic achievement:** Your child's skills in reading, writing, math, and other academic areas
- **Speech and language:** How your child understands and uses language to communicate
- **Social and emotional development:** Your child's behavior, emotional regulation, and social skills
- **Adaptive behavior:** How your child handles everyday life skills like self-care and independence
- **Motor skills:** Fine motor (writing, cutting) and gross motor (running, jumping) abilities
- **Health and developmental history:** Any medical or developmental factors that may affect learning
- **Vision and hearing:** Screenings to rule out sensory issues that might impact learning

The specific assessments used will depend on your child's individual concerns. For example, if your child struggles primarily with reading, the evaluation will focus more heavily on literacy-related assessments.

## How to Request a FIE at Midland ISD

As a parent, you have the right to request a FIE at any time if you suspect your child may have a disability that affects their learning. Here's how to do it in Midland ISD:

### Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing

While you can make a verbal request, we strongly recommend submitting a written request. This creates documentation and starts the official timeline. You can write a simple letter or email that includes:

- Your child's full name and date of birth
- Your child's school and grade
- A clear statement that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE)
- A brief description of your concerns
- Your contact information
- The date

### Step 2: Submit Your Request

Send your written request to your child's school principal, the campus special education coordinator, or Midland ISD's Special Education Department. Keep a copy for your records and note the date you submitted it.

### Step 3: Wait for the District's Response

Once Midland ISD receives your request, they must respond within 15 school days. The district will either:

- **Agree to evaluate** and send you a "Notice and Consent for Evaluation" form to sign, OR
- **Decline to evaluate** and provide you with written notice explaining why (you have the right to dispute this decision)

### Step 4: Give Your Consent

The evaluation cannot begin until you sign and return the consent form. Once you do, the timeline officially starts.

**Sample Request Letter:**

*Dear [Principal's Name],*

*I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [Child's Name], who is in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned about [briefly describe concerns, such as "difficulty reading at grade level," "struggles with attention and focus," or "challenges with social interactions"].*

*Please send me the necessary consent forms to begin this process. I can be reached at [phone number] or [email address].*

*Thank you for your attention to this request.*

*Sincerely,*
*[Your Name]*
*[Date]*

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law establishes clear timelines to ensure evaluations happen promptly. Here's what you need to know:

| Milestone | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| District response to your request | Within **15 school days** of receiving your written request |
| Completion of the FIE | Within **45 school days** of receiving your signed consent |
| ARD/IEP meeting to review results | Within **30 calendar days** of completing the FIE |

**Important notes about the 45-day timeline:**

- The clock starts when the district receives your signed consent form
- The timeline does not include days when school is not in session for 5 or more consecutive days (such as summer break, winter break, or spring break)
- If your child is absent for an extended period, this may affect the timeline

If Midland ISD does not meet these deadlines, they must provide you with written notice explaining the delay and propose a new completion date.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

Once the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and technical, but here's what to look for:

### Key Sections of a FIE Report

**Background Information:** A summary of your child's history, including developmental milestones, medical history, and educational background.

**Assessment Results:** The scores and findings from each evaluation area. These are often presented as standard scores, percentiles, or grade equivalents.

**Observations:** Notes from evaluators about how your child performed during testing and how they behave in the classroom.

**Summary and Conclusions:** The evaluator's interpretation of what the data means for your child.

**Eligibility Determination:** Whether your child meets the criteria for one or more of the 13 disability categories recognized under IDEA and Texas law.

**Recommendations:** Suggestions for services, supports, accommodations, or further evaluation.

### Tips for Reading the Report

- **Request a copy before the ARD meeting** so you have time to review it
- **Highlight questions** as you read—write them down to ask at the meeting
- **Look up unfamiliar terms** or ask the school to explain them
- **Focus on the recommendations section**—this is where actionable suggestions appear
- **Remember:** The report is one piece of the puzzle, not the whole picture

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

After the FIE is complete, Midland ISD will schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting within 30 calendar days. In Texas, the ARD committee is equivalent to what federal law calls the IEP team.

### What Happens at the ARD Meeting?

At this meeting, you'll join school staff to:

1. **Review the FIE results** together
2. **Determine eligibility** for special education services
3. **Discuss your child's needs** based on the evaluation findings
4. **Develop an IEP** if your child qualifies, including goals, services, and accommodations
