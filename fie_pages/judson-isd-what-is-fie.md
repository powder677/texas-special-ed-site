# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Judson ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Judson ISD and you've been hearing terms like "FIE" or "Full Individual Evaluation," you're not alone—and you're in the right place. Whether your child is struggling in school, a teacher has raised concerns, or you simply want to understand your options, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know about the FIE process in Judson ISD. As one of the larger districts in the San Antonio area serving approximately 25,670 students, Judson ISD has dedicated staff to support families through special education evaluations, and understanding this process is the first step toward getting your child the help they deserve.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A **Full Individual Evaluation (FIE)** is Texas's comprehensive assessment used to determine whether a child qualifies for special education services. You might also hear it called a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation."

Think of the FIE as a detailed portrait of your child—their strengths, challenges, learning style, and needs. It's not a single test but rather a collection of assessments, observations, and information gathered by a team of qualified professionals.

Under both federal law (the **Individuals with Disabilities Education Act**, or IDEA) and **Texas Education Code Chapter 29**, school districts like Judson ISD are required to conduct a FIE before a child can receive special education services. This evaluation is provided at no cost to you as a parent.

The FIE helps answer important questions:
- Does my child have a disability that affects their learning?
- What are my child's specific strengths and areas of need?
- What kind of support would help my child succeed in school?

## Why Is a FIE Important at Judson ISD?

For families in Judson ISD, the FIE serves as the gateway to special education services and support. Without a completed FIE, your child cannot be found eligible for an Individualized Education Program (IEP) or receive specialized instruction.

Here's why the FIE matters for your family:

- **It provides a complete picture**: The FIE looks at your child from multiple angles—academic, cognitive, behavioral, social, and sometimes physical—to understand the whole child.

- **It identifies specific needs**: Rather than guessing what might help, the FIE pinpoints exactly where your child needs support.

- **It's legally required**: Judson ISD must follow specific procedures and timelines set by Texas law, which protects your child's right to a thorough evaluation.

- **It guides the creation of services**: The results of the FIE directly inform what services, accommodations, and goals will be included in your child's IEP.

- **It establishes a baseline**: The FIE documents your child's current levels of performance, which helps track progress over time.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is comprehensive by design. The specific assessments included will depend on your child's suspected areas of need, but evaluations typically examine:

### Cognitive Ability
This measures how your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems. It's often referred to as an "IQ test" but provides much more nuanced information about your child's learning potential.

### Academic Achievement
These assessments look at your child's current performance in reading, writing, and math compared to grade-level expectations.

### Communication Skills
If language concerns exist, a speech-language pathologist may evaluate your child's ability to understand and express language.

### Social and Emotional Functioning
This area examines your child's emotional well-being, behavior, and social skills through rating scales, observations, and sometimes interviews.

### Adaptive Behavior
This looks at how your child handles everyday life skills appropriate for their age.

### Motor Skills
If concerns exist, an occupational therapist or physical therapist may assess fine motor skills (like writing) or gross motor skills (like walking and coordination).

### Health and Vision/Hearing
A review of your child's health history and sensory functioning ensures that vision or hearing problems aren't contributing to learning difficulties.

**Important**: Texas law requires that the FIE assess your child in **all areas related to the suspected disability**. If you believe an area has been overlooked, you have the right to request additional assessments.

## How to Request a FIE at Judson ISD

You have the right to request a FIE for your child at any time. Here's how to do it in Judson ISD:

### Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing

While you can make a verbal request, **always follow up in writing**. This creates a paper trail and starts the official timeline. You can:

- Write a letter or email to your child's principal
- Send your request to the campus special education contact
- Contact Judson ISD's Special Education Department directly

### Step 2: What to Include in Your Request

Keep it simple but specific:

> "I am requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [Child's Name], who is in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned about [briefly describe your concerns—reading difficulties, attention issues, behavior, etc.]. Please send me the necessary consent forms to begin this process. Thank you."

### Step 3: Watch for the Response

Once Judson ISD receives your written request, they must respond within **15 school days** by either:
- Providing you with a consent form to sign (agreeing to the evaluation), OR
- Providing written notice explaining why they are refusing to evaluate, along with your rights to challenge that decision

### Step 4: Give Written Consent

The evaluation cannot begin until you sign and return the consent form. The legal timeline for completing the FIE starts when the district receives your signed consent.

**Pro tip**: Keep copies of everything you send and receive, and note the date you submitted your request.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas has specific timelines to ensure evaluations happen promptly. Here's what you need to know:

| **Action** | **Timeline** |
|------------|--------------|
| District response to your request | Within **15 school days** |
| Completion of the FIE | Within **45 school days** of receiving signed consent |
| ARD meeting to review results | Within **30 calendar days** of completing the FIE |

### Key Points About the 45-Day Timeline:

- The clock starts when Judson ISD receives your **signed consent form**
- Only **school days** count (not weekends, holidays, or summer break)
- The timeline can be extended if your child misses school for an extended period
- If your child is evaluated during the summer, different rules may apply

If you feel Judson ISD is not meeting these timelines, document your concerns in writing and don't hesitate to ask for updates.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

After all assessments are complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and full of technical language, but understanding it is crucial. Here's what to expect:

### Components of a FIE Report:

- **Background Information**: Your child's history, including developmental milestones, medical history, and educational background
- **Assessment Results**: Scores and observations from each evaluation conducted
- **Interpretation**: What the scores mean in plain language
- **Conclusions**: A summary of your child's strengths and areas of need
- **Eligibility Determination**: Whether your child meets criteria for one or more disability categories under IDEA
- **Recommendations**: Suggestions for services, accommodations, and supports

### Tips for Reading the Report:

1. **Request the report before the ARD meeting** so you have time to review it
2. **Highlight areas you don't understand** to ask about at the meeting
3. **Look for your child's strengths**—these are just as important as challenges
4. **Compare the findings to what you see at home**—does the picture match?
5. **Don't be afraid to ask questions**—the evaluators should be willing to explain their findings

You are entitled to receive a copy of the full FIE report at no cost.

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

Once the FIE is complete, Judson ISD will schedule an **ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting** within 30 calendar days. In Texas, the ARD committee serves the same function as what federal law calls the "IEP team."

### At the ARD Meeting, the Team Will:

1. **Review the FIE results** with you in understandable terms
2. **Determine eligibility** for special education services
3. **If eligible**, develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) for your child
4. **Discuss placement** and services that will help your child succeed
