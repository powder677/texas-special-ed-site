# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Copperas Cove ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Copperas Cove ISD and suspect your child may need extra support at school, you're in the right place. Whether your family is connected to Fort Hood (now Fort Cavazos) or you've called this Central Texas community home for generations, understanding the special education evaluation process is an important first step. This guide will walk you through the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE)—what it is, how to request one, and what to expect along the way at Copperas Cove ISD.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation, commonly called a FIE, is a comprehensive assessment that Texas school districts must complete before a child can qualify for special education services. You might also hear it referred to as a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation."

Think of the FIE as a detailed snapshot of your child's unique strengths, challenges, and learning needs. It's not just one test—it's a collection of assessments, observations, and information gathering designed to understand how your child learns and what supports might help them succeed.

The FIE is required by both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas state law (Texas Education Code Chapter 29). This means Copperas Cove ISD must follow specific rules and timelines when conducting evaluations, which helps protect your child's rights.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Copperas Cove ISD?

The FIE serves as the foundation for everything that follows in the special education process at Copperas Cove ISD. Here's why it matters:

**It determines eligibility.** The FIE helps the school team decide whether your child qualifies for special education services under one of Texas's 13 eligibility categories, such as learning disability, autism, speech impairment, or emotional disturbance.

**It identifies your child's unique needs.** Beyond eligibility, the FIE reveals specific areas where your child may need support—whether that's reading comprehension, social skills, attention, or something else entirely.

**It guides the development of services.** If your child qualifies, the FIE results directly inform the creation of their Individualized Education Program (IEP), ensuring services are tailored to their specific needs.

**It establishes a baseline.** The FIE provides important data that Copperas Cove ISD can use to measure your child's progress over time.

For military-connected families at Fort Cavazos, having a thorough FIE can also make transitions to new schools smoother, as the evaluation provides documentation that follows your child.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE looks at your child from multiple angles to create a complete picture. Depending on the concerns, the evaluation may include:

- **Cognitive ability** – How your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems
- **Academic achievement** – Current skill levels in reading, writing, and math
- **Communication skills** – Speech, language understanding, and expression
- **Motor abilities** – Fine motor skills (like writing) and gross motor skills (like coordination)
- **Social and emotional functioning** – How your child interacts with others and manages emotions
- **Adaptive behavior** – Daily living skills and independence
- **Behavior** – Patterns of behavior that may affect learning
- **Health and developmental history** – Medical factors and developmental milestones
- **Observation in the classroom** – How your child performs in their natural learning environment

The specific areas assessed depend on your child's suspected disability and concerns. Copperas Cove ISD must evaluate in all areas related to the suspected disability, and the evaluation must be conducted in your child's native language whenever possible.

## How to Request a FIE at Copperas Cove ISD

Any parent or guardian can request a FIE for their child at any time. Here's how to do it:

### Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing

While you can make a verbal request, a written request creates documentation and officially starts the clock on Texas timelines. You can:

- Write a simple letter or email to your child's principal or the Copperas Cove ISD Special Education Department
- Use the district's referral form if one is available

Your letter doesn't need to be complicated. Here's a simple example:

*"Dear [Principal's Name], I am requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [child's name], who is in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned about [briefly describe concerns—for example, reading difficulties, attention challenges, social skills]. Please contact me to discuss next steps. Thank you, [Your name, phone number, email]."*

### Step 2: Wait for the District's Response

Within 15 school days of receiving your written request, Copperas Cove ISD must either:
- Provide you with a consent form to sign, agreeing to the evaluation, OR
- Provide written notice explaining why they are refusing to evaluate, along with your rights

### Step 3: Give Written Consent

Before any evaluation can begin, you must sign a consent form. The evaluation timeline doesn't start until the district receives your signed consent.

**Keep copies of everything!** Save your request letter, any responses, and the signed consent form.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas has specific timelines to ensure evaluations happen promptly. Here's what you should know:

| Milestone | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| District response to your request | Within **15 school days** |
| Completion of the FIE | Within **45 school days** of receiving signed consent |
| ARD meeting to review results | Within **30 calendar days** of completing the FIE |

**Important notes about the 45-day timeline:**

- The clock starts when Copperas Cove ISD receives your signed consent
- School holidays and breaks lasting 5+ consecutive days don't count toward the 45 days
- Summer vacation doesn't count unless your child is attending summer school
- If your child is absent for 3+ days during the evaluation period, those days may not count

If you signed consent near the end of the school year, the evaluation may extend into the following school year, but the district should communicate this timeline with you clearly.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

Once the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and filled with educational terminology, but you have every right to understand it fully.

### What's Included in the Report

- **Background information** – Your child's history, health information, and previous interventions
- **Assessment results** – Scores from each test administered, often including standard scores and percentile ranks
- **Observations** – Notes from classroom observations and assessment sessions
- **Interpretation** – What the scores and observations mean for your child
- **Summary and recommendations** – Overall findings and suggested next steps

### Tips for Understanding the Report

- **Request a copy before the ARD meeting.** You have the right to receive the FIE report before the meeting where it will be discussed. Ask for it at least a few days in advance so you have time to review it.
- **Write down your questions.** As you read, note anything confusing or concerning.
- **Look at the "big picture."** While individual scores matter, patterns across multiple assessments often tell the most important story.
- **Ask for explanations.** At the ARD meeting, you can ask the evaluator to explain any part of the report in plain language.

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

Within 30 calendar days of completing the FIE, Copperas Cove ISD will hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. In many other states, this is called an IEP meeting—in Texas, we call it an ARD.

### Who Attends the ARD Meeting

- You (the parent or guardian)—you are a vital member of this team
- At least one of your child's regular education teachers
- At least one special education teacher or provider
- A district representative who can commit resources
- Someone who can interpret the evaluation results (often the school psychologist)
- Others as appropriate (related service providers, advocates, etc.)
- Your child (when appropriate)

### What Happens at the Meeting

1. **Review of the FIE** – The team discusses the evaluation results
2. **Eligibility determination** – The team decides if your child qualifies for special education
3. **IEP development** – If eligible, the team works together to create your child's Individualized Education Program, including goals, services, and accommodations

**Remember:** You are an equal member of the ARD committee. Your input, concerns, and insights about your child are essential.
