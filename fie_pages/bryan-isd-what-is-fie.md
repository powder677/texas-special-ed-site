# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Bryan ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Bryan ISD wondering whether your child might need extra support at school, you've likely come across the term "FIE" or Full Individual Evaluation. Whether your child attends Crockett Elementary, Sam Rayburn Middle School, Bryan High School, or any of the district's campuses, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know about the FIE process. Understanding this evaluation is the first step toward getting your child the support they deserve in one of Central Texas's largest school districts.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation—or FIE—is Texas's comprehensive assessment used to determine whether a child qualifies for special education services. You might also hear it called a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation."

Think of the FIE as a detailed portrait of your child's unique strengths and challenges. It looks at how your child learns, processes information, communicates, and functions in the school environment. The evaluation is conducted by a team of qualified professionals and is required under both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas state law (Texas Education Code Chapter 29).

The FIE isn't a single test—it's a collection of assessments, observations, and information-gathering designed to give everyone (including you!) a complete picture of your child's educational needs.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Bryan ISD?

For Bryan ISD families, the FIE serves as the gateway to special education services and supports. Without a completed FIE, the district cannot determine whether your child is eligible for an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Here's why the FIE matters for your family:

- **It identifies specific learning differences** that might not be obvious in the classroom
- **It provides documentation** that Bryan ISD needs to offer appropriate services
- **It helps teachers understand** exactly how your child learns best
- **It establishes a baseline** to measure your child's progress over time
- **It gives you valuable insights** into your child's strengths and areas for growth

Bryan ISD serves a diverse community of approximately 15,530 students, and the district's special education team conducts FIEs regularly. Your child is one of many who may benefit from this important evaluation process.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is comprehensive by design. Depending on your child's suspected areas of need, the evaluation may include some or all of the following:

**Cognitive Ability**
Assessment of your child's overall intellectual functioning, including reasoning, problem-solving, and thinking skills.

**Academic Achievement**
Evaluation of reading, writing, math, and other academic skills compared to grade-level expectations.

**Communication Skills**
Assessment of how your child understands and uses language, including speech and language abilities.

**Social and Emotional Functioning**
Examination of your child's behavior, emotional regulation, social skills, and mental health needs.

**Adaptive Behavior**
Evaluation of daily living skills and how your child functions independently in various settings.

**Motor Skills**
Assessment of fine motor skills (like writing) and gross motor skills (like coordination and movement) when relevant.

**Health and Developmental History**
Review of medical records, developmental milestones, and any health factors that might affect learning.

The specific assessments included in your child's FIE will depend on the areas of concern. Bryan ISD must evaluate your child in all areas related to the suspected disability—no more, no less.

## How to Request a FIE at Bryan ISD

Any parent or guardian can request a FIE at any time. You don't need to wait for the school to suggest it, and you don't need anyone's permission to ask.

**To request a FIE in Bryan ISD:**

1. **Put your request in writing.** While verbal requests are valid, a written request creates clear documentation and starts the clock on important timelines.

2. **Address your letter to the right people.** Send your request to your child's principal and the Bryan ISD Special Education Department. Keep a copy for your records.

3. **Be specific about your concerns.** Briefly describe why you believe your child may need special education services.

4. **Include the date and your contact information.** This helps ensure timely communication.

**Sample language for your request:**

*"I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [Child's Name], who attends [School Name] in [Grade]. I am concerned about [brief description of concerns, such as reading difficulties, attention challenges, or social struggles]. Please send me the consent forms and information about next steps. Thank you."*

Once Bryan ISD receives your written request, the district has **15 school days** to respond by either providing you with consent forms to sign or explaining in writing why they are refusing to evaluate.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law establishes clear timelines to protect your family:

| Step | Timeline |
|------|----------|
| District response to your request | Within **15 school days** |
| You review and sign consent forms | Take the time you need |
| Evaluation completed and report provided | Within **45 school days** of receiving signed consent |
| ARD meeting to review results | Within **30 calendar days** of completing the FIE |

**Important notes about these timelines:**

- The 45-school-day timeline does not include days when school is not in session for more than 5 consecutive days (like summer break or winter break)
- If your child transfers to Bryan ISD during an evaluation, the district must coordinate with the previous district to complete the FIE promptly
- You can agree in writing to extend these timelines if needed, but the district cannot pressure you to do so

## Understanding Your FIE Report

When Bryan ISD completes your child's evaluation, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can feel overwhelming at first, but understanding its key components will help you advocate effectively for your child.

**The FIE report typically includes:**

- **Background information:** Family history, developmental milestones, medical history, and educational history
- **Assessment procedures:** A list of all tests and methods used during the evaluation
- **Test results:** Scores and what they mean, often including standard scores, percentiles, and grade equivalents
- **Observations:** Notes about your child's behavior during testing
- **Summary and conclusions:** The evaluation team's interpretation of results
- **Recommendations:** Suggestions for educational supports and interventions

**Tips for reviewing your FIE report:**

- Request the report at least a few days before the ARD meeting so you have time to read it carefully
- Make a list of questions as you read
- Don't hesitate to ask Bryan ISD staff to explain any terms or scores you don't understand
- Consider having a trusted friend, family member, or advocate review the report with you

Remember: You are a vital member of the evaluation team. Your observations and insights about your child matter just as much as the test scores.

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

After the FIE is complete, Bryan ISD will schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. This is where the evaluation results are discussed and, if your child qualifies, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) is developed.

**What to expect at the ARD meeting:**

- The evaluation team will explain the FIE results
- The group will determine whether your child meets eligibility criteria for special education under one of the 13 disability categories recognized by Texas
- If eligible, the team will begin developing an IEP tailored to your child's needs
- You'll discuss goals, services, accommodations, and placement

**Your role at the ARD meeting:**

- You are an equal member of the ARD committee
- Your input about your child's strengths, challenges, and needs is essential
- You can bring someone with you for support (a family member, friend, or advocate)
- You have the right to ask questions, request clarification, and take time to consider decisions
- You do not have to sign the IEP on the spot if you need more time

## Your Rights During the FIE Process at Bryan ISD

As a parent in Texas, you have important rights throughout the evaluation process:

**The right to give or withhold consent**
Bryan ISD cannot evaluate your child without your written permission. You can also revoke consent for special education services at any time.

**The right to be informed**
The district must explain the evaluation process, your rights, and any decisions in language you can understand. If English is not your primary language, Bryan ISD must communicate with you in your native language.

**The right to participate**
You are a member of the evaluation and ARD teams. Your input is not optional—it's required.

**The right to review records**
You can see all educational records related to your child, including the FIE report.
