# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Burleson ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Burleson ISD and you've heard the term "FIE" mentioned by teachers, counselors, or other parents, you're in the right place. Whether your child is struggling in school, has been identified with possible learning differences, or you simply want to understand the special education process better, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know about the Full Individual Evaluation in Burleson ISD. Serving approximately 12,740 students across the Fort Worth region, Burleson ISD has dedicated special education staff ready to support your child's unique learning needs—and it all starts with understanding the FIE.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation, commonly called a FIE, is Texas's comprehensive assessment process used to determine whether a child qualifies for special education services. You might also hear it referred to as a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation."

Think of the FIE as a detailed portrait of your child as a learner. It goes far beyond a single test or a teacher's observations. Instead, it gathers information from multiple sources—assessments, observations, parent input, teacher feedback, and more—to create a complete picture of your child's strengths, challenges, and educational needs.

The FIE is required by both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas state law (Texas Education Code Chapter 29) before any child can receive special education services. This ensures that every child is evaluated fairly and thoroughly before any decisions are made about their education.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Burleson ISD?

For Burleson ISD families, the FIE serves as the foundation for everything that follows in the special education process. Here's why it matters so much:

**It opens doors to support.** Without a FIE, your child cannot be found eligible for special education services. The evaluation is the first official step toward accessing specialized instruction, therapies, accommodations, and other supports your child may need.

**It identifies your child's unique needs.** Every child learns differently. The FIE helps Burleson ISD staff understand exactly how your child processes information, where they excel, and where they need additional help.

**It guides educational planning.** The results of the FIE directly inform your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP), ensuring that goals, services, and supports are tailored specifically to them.

**It protects your child's rights.** Texas law requires that evaluations be conducted in a nondiscriminatory manner, using multiple measures, and with your informed consent. This process helps ensure your child is treated fairly.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is comprehensive by design. Depending on your child's suspected areas of need, the evaluation may assess:

- **Cognitive ability** – How your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems
- **Academic achievement** – Your child's current skill levels in reading, writing, and math
- **Communication skills** – Speech, language understanding, and expression
- **Social and emotional functioning** – How your child interacts with others and manages emotions
- **Adaptive behavior** – Daily living skills and how your child functions independently
- **Motor skills** – Fine motor (writing, cutting) and gross motor (running, jumping) abilities
- **Health and developmental history** – Medical information that may affect learning
- **Behavior** – Patterns of behavior that may impact learning in the classroom

The specific areas evaluated depend on your child's individual concerns. For example, if there are no speech concerns, a speech-language evaluation may not be included. Burleson ISD's evaluation team will determine which assessments are appropriate based on referral information and your input as a parent.

## How to Request a FIE at Burleson ISD

As a parent, you have the right to request a FIE at any time if you suspect your child may have a disability that affects their learning. Here's how to do it in Burleson ISD:

**Step 1: Put your request in writing.** While you can make a verbal request, submitting a written request creates documentation and starts the official timeline. Address your letter to your child's principal or the Burleson ISD Special Education Department.

**Step 2: Be specific about your concerns.** In your letter, describe the challenges your child is experiencing. Include examples from home and any feedback you've received from teachers.

**Step 3: Keep a copy for your records.** Always maintain copies of any communication with the district.

**Sample language for your request:**

*"Dear [Principal's Name], I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [Child's Name], who is in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned about [briefly describe concerns, such as reading difficulties, attention issues, or social challenges]. I am requesting this evaluation to determine whether my child qualifies for special education services. Please send me the necessary consent forms. Thank you, [Your Name]"*

Once Burleson ISD receives your request, they must respond within **15 school days** by either providing you with a Notice of Proposed Evaluation and consent forms, or a Notice of Refusal explaining why they do not believe an evaluation is warranted.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law establishes clear timelines to ensure evaluations happen promptly:

- **15 school days** – After receiving your written request, Burleson ISD must provide you with a Notice of Proposed Evaluation and consent forms (or a notice of refusal).
- **45 school days** – Once you sign and return the consent form, the district has 45 school days to complete the evaluation and hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to review the results.

It's important to note that the 45-day timeline counts *school days*, not calendar days. Summer breaks, holidays, and other days when school is not in session do not count toward this timeline.

If your child is absent for an extended period or if you and the district agree to an extension in writing, the timeline may be adjusted. However, in most cases, Burleson ISD must complete the FIE within these legal timeframes.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

After the evaluation is complete, you will receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and filled with technical language, but don't let that intimidate you. Here's what to look for:

**Background information** – This section summarizes your child's history, including developmental milestones, medical history, and educational background.

**Assessment results** – Each evaluator will report their findings. Look for standard scores, percentile ranks, and descriptions of what the numbers mean. Don't hesitate to ask the evaluation team to explain any terms you don't understand.

**Observations** – Evaluators often include notes about how your child approached tasks, their attention, behavior, and engagement during testing.

**Summary and recommendations** – This is often the most important section. It brings together all the information to answer key questions: Does your child have a disability? Does that disability affect their educational performance? What supports might help?

**Pro tip:** Request a copy of the FIE report *before* your ARD meeting so you have time to review it and prepare questions. Burleson ISD should provide this to you in advance.

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

Once the FIE is complete, Burleson ISD will schedule an ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal). In Texas, this is our term for the IEP meeting. This meeting brings together a team that includes:

- You, the parent (and anyone you invite for support)
- A school administrator or representative
- Your child's teacher(s)
- The evaluators who conducted the FIE
- A special education teacher
- Other specialists as appropriate

At the ARD meeting, the team will:

1. Review the FIE results together
2. Determine whether your child meets eligibility criteria for special education under one of the 13 disability categories
3. If eligible, begin developing your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP)

Remember, you are an equal member of this team. Your insights about your child are invaluable, and you have the right to ask questions, share concerns, and participate fully in all decisions.

## Your Rights During the FIE Process at Burleson ISD
