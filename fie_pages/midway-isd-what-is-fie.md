# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Midway ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Midway ISD and you've been hearing terms like "FIE" or "evaluation" during conversations about your child's learning or development, you're in the right place. Whether your child attends school in Woodway, Hewitt, or anywhere else within the Midway Independent School District, understanding the FIE process is an essential first step toward getting your child the support they may need. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about Full Individual Evaluations in clear, straightforward language.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A FIE, or Full Individual Evaluation, is a comprehensive assessment that Texas school districts conduct to determine whether a child has a disability and needs special education services. You might also hear it called a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply "the evaluation."

Think of a FIE as a detailed portrait of your child as a learner. It looks at how your child thinks, learns, communicates, and behaves across multiple settings. The evaluation gathers information from many sources—including you as the parent—to create a complete picture of your child's strengths and areas where they may need additional support.

Under federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and the Texas Education Code Chapter 29, school districts like Midway ISD must conduct a FIE before a child can be found eligible for special education services. The evaluation is provided at no cost to families.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Midway ISD?

At Midway ISD, with approximately 8,420 students spread across multiple campuses, educators work hard to meet the diverse needs of every child. However, some students need more specialized support than general education alone can provide.

A FIE serves several crucial purposes:

- **Identifies whether your child has a disability** that affects their ability to learn in a typical classroom setting
- **Determines eligibility** for special education services under one of the 13 disability categories recognized in Texas
- **Provides detailed information** that helps teachers and specialists understand exactly how your child learns best
- **Creates a foundation** for developing an Individualized Education Program (IEP) tailored to your child's unique needs
- **Documents your child's current levels** of performance, which helps track progress over time

Without a FIE, your child cannot receive special education services through Midway ISD. That's why understanding this process is so important for Central Texas families.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is comprehensive by design. Texas law requires that evaluations assess all areas related to your child's suspected disability. Depending on your child's needs, the evaluation may include:

**Cognitive Ability (Thinking Skills)**
This looks at how your child processes information, solves problems, and learns new concepts.

**Academic Achievement**
Assessments measure your child's current skill levels in reading, writing, math, and other academic areas.

**Communication Skills**
Speech and language assessments evaluate how well your child understands and uses language, including articulation, vocabulary, and social communication.

**Motor Skills**
If there are concerns about coordination or physical development, evaluations may assess fine motor skills (like writing) and gross motor skills (like walking or running).

**Social and Emotional Development**
This includes looking at your child's behavior, emotional regulation, social interactions, and mental health.

**Adaptive Behavior**
These assessments examine daily living skills and how independently your child can function in various settings.

**Health and Developmental History**
Your input about your child's medical history, developmental milestones, and family background provides essential context.

**Observation**
Evaluators will observe your child in their natural school environment to see how they function in the classroom.

The specific assessments included in your child's FIE will depend on the concerns that prompted the evaluation. For example, a child suspected of having a learning disability in reading will receive different assessments than a child being evaluated for autism.

## How to Request a FIE at Midway ISD

As a parent, you have the right to request a FIE at any time if you suspect your child may have a disability affecting their education. Here's how to make that request in Midway ISD:

**Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing**

While you can make a verbal request, a written request creates documentation and starts the official timeline. Write a simple letter or email that includes:

- Your child's name and date of birth
- Your child's school and grade
- A clear statement that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation
- A brief description of your concerns
- Your contact information
- The date

**Step 2: Submit Your Request**

Send your written request to your child's principal, the campus special education coordinator, or the Midway ISD Special Education Department. Keep a copy for your records.

**Sample Request Language:**

*"Dear [Principal/Special Education Coordinator], I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [child's name], who is in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned about [briefly describe concerns, such as 'difficulty reading at grade level' or 'challenges with social interactions']. Please contact me to discuss next steps. Thank you, [Your name, date, and contact information]."*

**Step 3: Wait for the District's Response**

Once Midway ISD receives your written request, they must respond within 15 school days. The district will either:

- Agree to evaluate and provide you with consent forms, or
- Decline to evaluate and provide you with written notice explaining why

If the district agrees to evaluate, you'll receive a "Notice and Consent for Evaluation" form. The FIE timeline officially begins when you sign and return this consent form.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas has specific timelines designed to protect families and ensure evaluations happen promptly. Here's what you need to know:

**15 School Days**
After receiving your written request, Midway ISD has 15 school days to provide you with the consent form (or written notice if they decline to evaluate).

**45 School Days**
Once you sign and return the consent form, the district has 45 school days to complete the FIE and hold the ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to review results.

**Important Notes About the Timeline:**

- "School days" means days when school is in session—holidays, weekends, and summer break typically don't count
- If your child is absent for an extended period, or if there's a holiday break of five or more consecutive school days, the timeline may be adjusted
- The 45-day timeline is serious—Midway ISD is required by law to meet this deadline

If you feel the district is not meeting these timelines, don't hesitate to follow up in writing and reference Texas Education Code Chapter 29.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

After the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and technical, but understanding it is important. Here's what to look for:

**Background Information**
This section summarizes your child's history, including information you provided about development, medical history, and educational background.

**Assessment Results**
You'll see scores from various tests and assessments. These are often reported as standard scores, percentile ranks, or age/grade equivalents. Ask the evaluator to explain what these numbers mean for your child.

**Observations**
The report includes observations of your child during testing and in the classroom.

**Summary and Conclusions**
This section pulls everything together and explains whether your child meets the criteria for a disability category under Texas and federal law.

**Recommendations**
The evaluation team will make recommendations about eligibility and may suggest strategies or services to help your child.

**Tips for Reviewing Your FIE Report:**

- Request a copy of the report before the ARD meeting so you have time to read it
- Write down questions as you review it
- Ask for a meeting with the evaluator if you need help understanding the results
- Remember that you know your child best—your perspective matters

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

Once the FIE is complete, Midway ISD will schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. In Texas, this is the equivalent of what other states call an IEP meeting.

**At the ARD meeting, the team will:**

1. **Review the FIE results** together with you
2. **Determine eligibility** for special education services based on the evaluation findings
3. **Identify your child's disability category** (if eligible)
4. **Develop an IEP** (Individualized Education Program) if your child qualifies

**Who Attends the ARD Meeting?**

- You (the parent or guardian)
- A general education teacher
- A special education teacher
- A district representative
- Someone who can interpret the evaluation results (often the school psychologist)
- Related service providers (if applicable
