# What Is a FIE? A Guide for San Angelo ISD Parents

If you're a parent in San Angelo ISD and you've heard the term "FIE" mentioned by teachers or staff, you're not alone in wondering what it means. Whether your child is struggling in class, has been recommended for evaluation, or you simply want to understand the special education process better, this guide is here to help. San Angelo ISD serves over 13,000 students across our West Texas community, and many families each year navigate the FIE process as the first step toward getting their child the support they need to thrive.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation—commonly called a FIE—is a comprehensive assessment that Texas school districts conduct to determine whether a child has a disability and qualifies for special education services. You might also hear it referred to as a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation."

The FIE isn't just a single test. It's a collection of assessments, observations, and information gathering designed to create a complete picture of your child. This evaluation looks at how your child learns, processes information, communicates, and functions in different settings. It's required by both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas state law (Texas Education Code Chapter 29) before any child can receive special education services.

Think of the FIE as the foundation for understanding your child's unique learning profile. It helps answer important questions: Does my child have a disability? What are their strengths? Where do they need support? What kind of services would help them succeed?

## Why Is a FIE Important at San Angelo ISD?

For San Angelo ISD families, the FIE serves as the gateway to special education services and support. Without a completed FIE, the district cannot provide your child with an Individualized Education Program (IEP) or specialized instruction.

Here's why the FIE matters specifically for your family:

**It identifies your child's unique needs.** Every child learns differently, and the FIE helps pinpoint exactly how your child processes information, what challenges they face, and what strengths they bring to the table.

**It ensures appropriate services.** San Angelo ISD uses FIE results to design educational programs tailored to your child—not a one-size-fits-all approach, but support that addresses their specific needs.

**It's legally required.** Texas law mandates that districts like San Angelo ISD conduct a thorough evaluation before determining eligibility. This protects your child's right to a free appropriate public education (FAPE).

**It establishes a baseline.** The FIE creates a snapshot of your child's abilities at a specific point in time, which helps track progress and adjust services as they grow.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

The FIE examines multiple areas of your child's development and learning. While the specific assessments vary based on your child's suspected areas of need, a typical FIE at San Angelo ISD may include:

**Cognitive/Intellectual Ability**
This portion measures how your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems. It helps identify their learning potential and cognitive strengths.

**Academic Achievement**
These assessments evaluate your child's current performance in reading, writing, and math compared to same-age peers.

**Speech and Language Skills**
If there are concerns about communication, the evaluation will assess how your child understands and uses language.

**Adaptive Behavior**
This looks at your child's daily living skills, social skills, and ability to function independently in various settings.

**Social-Emotional and Behavioral Functioning**
The FIE may examine your child's emotional well-being, behavior patterns, and social interactions.

**Motor Skills**
If needed, fine motor (writing, cutting) and gross motor (running, coordination) skills may be assessed.

**Sensory Processing**
For some children, the evaluation includes how they process sensory information like sounds, textures, or visual input.

**Health and Developmental History**
The evaluation team will review your child's medical history, developmental milestones, and any relevant background information.

The evaluation must be comprehensive enough to identify all of your child's special education needs, not just the most obvious ones.

## How to Request a FIE at San Angelo ISD

As a parent, you have the right to request a FIE for your child at any time. You don't have to wait for the school to suggest it. Here's how to make a request in San Angelo ISD:

**Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing**
While you can make a verbal request, we strongly recommend submitting it in writing. A simple letter or email works fine. Include:
- Your child's name and date of birth
- Your child's school and grade
- A clear statement that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation
- A brief description of your concerns
- Your contact information
- The date

**Step 2: Submit to the Right Person**
You can submit your request to your child's teacher, the school principal, or directly to San Angelo ISD's Special Education Department. Keeping a copy for your records is always a good idea.

**Step 3: Receive and Sign Consent**
After receiving your request, San Angelo ISD must provide you with written notice and a consent form. The district cannot proceed with the evaluation until you sign this consent. Take time to read it carefully—you have the right to ask questions before signing.

**Sample Request Letter:**

*Dear [Principal's Name],*

*I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [Child's Name], who is in [grade] at [school name]. I have concerns about [briefly describe concerns—for example, "reading difficulties," "trouble focusing in class," or "social challenges"].*

*Please send me the consent forms and information about the evaluation process. I look forward to working with the evaluation team.*

*Sincerely,*
*[Your Name]*
*[Phone Number]*
*[Email]*
*[Date]*

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law establishes clear timelines that San Angelo ISD must follow:

**45 School Days**
Once you sign the consent form, San Angelo ISD has 45 school days to complete the FIE. This means actual days that school is in session—not calendar days. Summer breaks, holidays, and school closures don't count toward this timeline.

**Exceptions to the Timeline**
The 45-day rule may be extended if:
- Your child misses school for extended periods
- You and the district mutually agree to an extension in writing
- Your child transfers to San Angelo ISD from another district while an evaluation is in progress (though the district must still complete it "as quickly as possible")

**What Happens During Those 45 Days**
During this period, the evaluation team at San Angelo ISD will:
- Review your child's educational records
- Gather input from you and teachers
- Conduct observations in different settings
- Administer various assessments
- Compile findings into a comprehensive report

**Tracking the Timeline**
Mark your calendar when you sign consent. Count school days, not weekends or holidays. If the timeline isn't met, you have the right to ask why and advocate for completion.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

When the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and filled with unfamiliar terms, but understanding it is essential. Here's what to look for:

**Background Information**
This section summarizes your child's history, including developmental milestones, medical information, and educational background.

**Assessment Results**
You'll see scores from each assessment administered. These are often reported as standard scores, percentile ranks, or age/grade equivalents. Don't worry if the numbers seem confusing—you have the right to have everything explained.

**Observations**
The evaluators will describe what they observed during testing and possibly in classroom settings.

**Interpretation and Analysis**
This section explains what the scores mean in practical terms—how your child's results compare to typical peers and what they suggest about learning needs.

**Eligibility Determination**
The report will indicate whether your child meets criteria for one or more disability categories under IDEA, such as:
- Learning Disability
- Autism
- Emotional Disturbance
- Intellectual Disability
- Speech or Language Impairment
- Other Health Impairment
- And others

**Recommendations**
The report may include suggestions for services, accommodations, or strategies to support your child.

**Tips for Reviewing the Report:**
- Request a copy before your ARD meeting so you have time to read it
- Highlight questions or terms you don't understand
- Don't hesitate to ask for a meeting to review the report with the evaluator
- Consider having a trusted friend, advocate, or family member review it with you
