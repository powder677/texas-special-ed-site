# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Forney ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Forney ISD and you've heard the term "FIE" mentioned by a teacher, counselor, or another parent, you're in the right place. Forney is one of the fastest-growing districts in the DFW metroplex, serving approximately 16,840 students across its schools. With that growth comes an increasing number of families navigating the special education process for the first time. Whether your child is struggling in school, has a suspected disability, or you simply want to understand what a FIE means for your family, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know—in plain, parent-friendly language.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A FIE, or Full Individual Evaluation, is a comprehensive assessment that Texas school districts use to determine whether a child has a disability and needs special education services. You might also hear it called a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation."

Think of the FIE as a detailed snapshot of your child's strengths, challenges, and unique learning needs. It's not a single test—it's a collection of assessments, observations, and information gathered from multiple sources to paint a complete picture of how your child learns and functions.

Under both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas state law (Texas Education Code Chapter 29), schools must conduct a FIE before a child can qualify for special education services. This evaluation is provided at no cost to you as a parent.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Forney ISD?

The FIE serves as the foundation for your child's entire special education journey in Forney ISD. Without it, the district cannot legally provide special education services or develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) for your child.

Here's why the FIE matters so much:

- **It identifies your child's specific needs.** Rather than guessing what might help, the evaluation provides data-driven insights into how your child learns best.
- **It determines eligibility.** Texas recognizes 13 disability categories under which a child may qualify for special education. The FIE helps determine if your child meets the criteria for one or more of these categories.
- **It guides programming decisions.** The results inform what services, supports, and accommodations your child will receive at their Forney ISD campus.
- **It establishes a baseline.** The FIE creates a starting point so future progress can be measured accurately.

In a growing district like Forney ISD, ensuring your child receives the right evaluation is the first step toward getting them the support they deserve.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is comprehensive by design. Texas law requires that evaluations assess all areas related to a suspected disability. Depending on your child's needs, the FIE may include:

**Cognitive Ability**
Assessments that measure how your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems. This is often called an "IQ test," though it's much more nuanced than a single number.

**Academic Achievement**
Testing in reading, writing, and math to see how your child performs compared to same-age peers and whether there are significant gaps between ability and achievement.

**Speech and Language Skills**
Evaluation of how your child understands language (receptive) and expresses themselves (expressive), including articulation and social communication.

**Motor Skills**
Assessment of fine motor abilities (like writing and cutting) and gross motor skills (like running and balance) if concerns exist.

**Social-Emotional and Behavioral Functioning**
Questionnaires and observations that look at your child's emotional regulation, behavior patterns, social skills, and mental health needs.

**Adaptive Behavior**
Evaluation of daily living skills and how independently your child can function in various environments.

**Health and Developmental History**
A review of medical records, developmental milestones, and any relevant health conditions.

The specific assessments included in your child's FIE will depend on the concerns identified. Forney ISD's evaluation team will determine which areas need to be assessed based on referral information and your input as a parent.

## How to Request a FIE at Forney ISD

Any parent or guardian can request a FIE for their child at any time. You don't need to wait for the school to suggest it, and you don't need anyone's permission. Here's how to make your request in Forney ISD:

**Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing**

While you can verbally ask for an evaluation, a written request creates documentation and starts the legal timeline. Address your letter to your child's principal or the Forney ISD Special Education Department.

Your letter should include:
- Your child's full name and date of birth
- Your child's school and grade
- A clear statement that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation
- A brief description of your concerns
- Your contact information
- The date

**Step 2: Submit Your Request**

Deliver your written request in one of these ways:
- Hand-deliver it to the school office and ask for a date-stamped copy
- Email it to your child's principal and the campus special education contact
- Send it via certified mail to the Forney ISD Special Education Department

**Step 3: Wait for the District's Response**

Once Forney ISD receives your written request, they have **15 school days** to respond. They will either:
- Provide you with a "Notice and Consent for Evaluation" form to sign, OR
- Provide written notice explaining why they are refusing to evaluate, along with information about your rights

**Step 4: Give Written Consent**

The evaluation cannot begin until you sign the consent form. Review it carefully to understand what assessments will be conducted. Signing consent for evaluation does not mean you're agreeing to special education services—it simply allows the evaluation to take place.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas has specific timelines that protect families and ensure evaluations happen promptly. Here's what you need to know:

| Milestone | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| District response to your written request | Within **15 school days** |
| Completion of FIE after you sign consent | Within **45 school days** |
| ARD meeting to review results | Within **30 calendar days** of FIE completion |

**Important notes about the 45-day timeline:**

- The clock starts the day after you provide written consent
- The timeline does not include days when school is not in session for 5 or more consecutive days (like summer break or winter break)
- If your child is absent for an extended period, the timeline may be adjusted

If you requested an evaluation at the end of the school year, Forney ISD must still respond within 15 school days, even if those days extend into summer. However, the 45-day evaluation timeline typically pauses during extended school breaks.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

Once the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and full of technical language, but understanding it is essential for advocating for your child.

Here's what you'll typically find in a FIE report:

**Background Information**
A summary of your child's history, including developmental milestones, medical history, educational history, and the concerns that led to the evaluation.

**Assessment Results**
Detailed findings from each evaluation area, often including standardized scores. You'll see terms like:
- *Standard scores* (average is typically 100)
- *Percentile ranks* (shows how your child compares to peers)
- *Age/grade equivalents* (use with caution—these can be misleading)

**Observations**
Notes from evaluators who observed your child during testing and possibly in classroom settings.

**Summary and Interpretation**
An explanation of what the scores mean and how different areas connect.

**Eligibility Determination**
A statement about whether your child meets criteria for one or more of the 13 disability categories recognized in Texas.

**Recommendations**
Suggestions for services, supports, and accommodations—though the final decisions are made at the ARD meeting.

**Tips for reviewing the report:**

- Request a copy at least a few days before your ARD meeting so you have time to read it
- Highlight areas you don't understand and prepare questions
- Look for your child's strengths, not just challenges
- If something doesn't match what you know about your child, speak up

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

Once the FIE is complete, Forney ISD will schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. In other states, this is called an IEP meeting, but Texas uses the term ARD.

The ARD meeting must occur within **30 calendar days** of the FIE's completion. At this meeting, a team—including you—will:

1. **Review the FIE results** together
2. **Determine eligibility**
