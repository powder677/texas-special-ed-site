# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Laredo ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Laredo ISD and you've been hearing terms like "FIE" or "evaluation" tossed around in conversations about your child's education, you're in the right place. With approximately 20,592 students across the district, Laredo ISD serves a diverse community of learners—and some of those students may need additional support to thrive in school. Understanding the Full Individual Evaluation process is your first step toward getting your child the help they deserve. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know, in plain language, so you can confidently advocate for your child.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation—commonly called a FIE—is a comprehensive assessment that determines whether your child has a disability and needs special education services. Think of it as a detailed portrait of your child's strengths, challenges, and unique learning needs.

In Texas, school districts are required to conduct a FIE before a child can receive special education services. This isn't just a single test—it's a collection of assessments, observations, and information-gathering that looks at the whole child. You might also hear it called a "psychoeducational evaluation," but in Texas, FIE is the official term used in schools.

The FIE is required under two important laws: the federal Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and the Texas Education Code Chapter 29. These laws exist to protect your child's right to a free and appropriate public education.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Laredo ISD?

For families in Laredo ISD, the FIE serves as the gateway to special education services and support. Without this evaluation, the district cannot determine whether your child qualifies for an Individualized Education Program (IEP) or what specific services they might need.

The FIE matters because it:

- **Identifies your child's specific needs** so teachers and specialists know exactly how to help
- **Establishes eligibility** for special education services under one of Texas's 13 disability categories
- **Creates a baseline** for measuring your child's progress over time
- **Ensures your child receives appropriate support** rather than a one-size-fits-all approach
- **Protects your rights** as a parent under federal and state law

In a district as large as Laredo ISD, the FIE helps ensure that every child who needs additional support is identified and served appropriately.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is thorough by design. It looks at multiple areas of your child's development and learning to create a complete picture. Depending on your child's suspected needs, the evaluation may include:

**Cognitive Ability**
How your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems. This is often measured through standardized intelligence testing.

**Academic Achievement**
Your child's current performance in reading, writing, math, and other school subjects compared to grade-level expectations.

**Communication Skills**
How well your child understands language (receptive) and expresses themselves (expressive), including speech and language assessments.

**Motor Skills**
Fine motor abilities (like writing and cutting) and gross motor skills (like running and balance), often assessed by an occupational or physical therapist.

**Social-Emotional Functioning**
Your child's behavior, emotional regulation, social skills, and mental health needs.

**Adaptive Behavior**
Daily living skills and how independently your child functions in various settings.

**Health and Developmental History**
Information about your child's medical background, developmental milestones, and any previous interventions.

The evaluation team selects assessments based on your child's specific concerns—not every child receives the same battery of tests.

## How to Request a FIE at Laredo ISD

You have the right to request a FIE for your child at any time. Here's how to do it in Laredo ISD:

**Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing**

While you can make a verbal request, we strongly recommend submitting your request in writing. This creates documentation and starts the official timeline. Address your letter to the principal of your child's school or the Laredo ISD Special Education Department.

Your letter should include:
- Your child's full name and date of birth
- Your child's school and grade
- Your specific concerns about your child's learning or development
- A clear statement requesting a Full Individual Evaluation
- Your contact information
- The date

**Step 2: Keep Copies of Everything**

Make a copy of your request letter before submitting it. If you hand-deliver it, ask for a date-stamped copy. If you email it, save the sent email and any responses.

**Step 3: Wait for the District's Response**

Once Laredo ISD receives your written request, they have 15 school days to respond. They will either agree to evaluate your child and send you consent forms, or they will decline and provide written notice explaining why.

**Sample Request Language:**

*"Dear [Principal's Name], I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [Child's Name], who is in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned about [briefly describe your concerns]. Please contact me at [phone/email] to discuss next steps. Thank you."*

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law sets specific deadlines to keep the evaluation process moving. Here's what to expect:

| Milestone | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| District responds to your request | Within 15 school days |
| You receive consent forms | Included with the district's response |
| Evaluation is completed | Within 45 school days after you return signed consent |
| ARD meeting is held | Within 30 calendar days after the FIE is completed |

**Important:** The 45-school-day timeline only begins when Laredo ISD receives your signed consent to evaluate. Don't let those forms sit on your kitchen counter—return them promptly to avoid delays.

Also note that "school days" doesn't count weekends, holidays, or summer break (unless your child attends summer school). This can sometimes extend the calendar time, so stay in communication with the school.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

Once the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can feel overwhelming—it's often lengthy and filled with technical terms and numbers. Here's how to make sense of it:

**Sections You'll Typically Find:**

- **Background Information**: Your child's history, previous interventions, and the reason for the evaluation
- **Assessment Results**: Scores and descriptions from each test administered
- **Observations**: Notes from evaluators who watched your child in class or during testing
- **Summary and Conclusions**: An overview of findings and what they mean
- **Recommendations**: Suggestions for support, services, or further evaluation
- **Eligibility Determination**: Whether your child qualifies for special education

**Tips for Reading the Report:**

- Don't panic about numbers. Ask the evaluator to explain what scores mean in plain language.
- Focus on the "Summary" and "Recommendations" sections first.
- Write down your questions and bring them to the ARD meeting.
- You have the right to request a meeting with evaluators before the ARD to review findings.

Remember, you know your child best. If something in the report doesn't match what you see at home, speak up.

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

After the FIE is completed, Laredo ISD will schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. In other states, this might be called an IEP meeting, but in Texas, we call it an ARD.

During the ARD meeting, a team—including you—will:

1. **Review the FIE results** together
2. **Determine eligibility** for special education services
3. **If eligible, develop an IEP** (Individualized Education Program) outlining your child's goals, services, accommodations, and placement

**Who Attends the ARD Meeting?**

- You (and anyone you invite for support)
- A school administrator
- Your child's teacher(s)
- The professionals who conducted the evaluation
- A special education teacher or specialist
- Your child (when appropriate)

**Your Role Matters**

You are an equal member of this team. Your input about your child's needs, strengths, and challenges is invaluable. Don't hesitate to ask questions, share concerns, or request time to think before signing any documents.
