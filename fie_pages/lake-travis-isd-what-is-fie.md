# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Lake Travis ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Lake Travis ISD wondering whether your child might need special education services, you've likely come across the term "FIE." Whether your child is struggling academically, behaviorally, or socially at one of Lake Travis's schools, understanding the Full Individual Evaluation process is your first step toward getting them the support they deserve. This guide is designed specifically for Lake Travis ISD families to help you navigate the evaluation process with confidence.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation—commonly called a FIE—is a comprehensive assessment that Texas school districts must conduct before a child can qualify for special education services. You might also hear it referred to as a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation."

Think of the FIE as a detailed portrait of your child's unique strengths, challenges, and learning needs. It's not a single test but rather a collection of assessments, observations, and information-gathering that helps paint a complete picture of how your child learns and what support they might need.

Under both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas Education Code Chapter 29, school districts like Lake Travis ISD are required to conduct a FIE at no cost to you before determining whether your child is eligible for special education services.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Lake Travis ISD?

Lake Travis ISD serves approximately 11,640 students across its schools in the Austin area. With a district of this size, ensuring that every child receives individualized attention can be challenging—which is exactly why the FIE process matters so much.

A properly conducted FIE helps Lake Travis ISD:

- **Identify your child's specific learning differences** rather than applying a one-size-fits-all approach
- **Determine eligibility** for special education services under one or more of the 13 disability categories recognized in Texas
- **Develop appropriate goals and services** tailored to your child's unique needs
- **Establish a baseline** for measuring your child's progress over time
- **Ensure resources are directed** to students who need specialized instruction

For your family, a thorough FIE means that decisions about your child's education are based on solid evidence rather than guesswork.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is designed to look at your whole child, not just one area of concern. Depending on your child's suspected needs, the evaluation may include assessments in several areas:

**Cognitive Ability (Intelligence)**
These assessments measure how your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems. They help identify intellectual strengths and weaknesses.

**Academic Achievement**
These tests look at your child's performance in reading, writing, math, and other academic areas compared to grade-level expectations.

**Speech and Language**
If communication is a concern, evaluators will assess your child's ability to understand language, express themselves, and use speech sounds correctly.

**Motor Skills**
Fine motor skills (like writing and cutting) and gross motor skills (like running and jumping) may be evaluated, particularly for younger children.

**Social-Emotional and Behavioral Functioning**
This includes looking at your child's emotional well-being, behavior patterns, social skills, and adaptive functioning (everyday life skills).

**Health and Developmental History**
Your input about your child's medical history, developmental milestones, and family background is an essential part of the evaluation.

**Classroom Observations**
Evaluators will typically observe your child in their natural school environment to see how they function day-to-day.

The specific assessments included in your child's FIE will depend on the areas of suspected disability and concern. Lake Travis ISD must evaluate all areas related to your child's suspected disability.

## How to Request a FIE at Lake Travis ISD

Any parent or guardian can request a FIE for their child at any time. You don't need to wait for the school to suggest it. Here's how to make your request in Lake Travis ISD:

**Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing**

While you can make a verbal request, written requests create a clear record and start the legal timeline. You can:
- Write a letter or email to your child's principal
- Send your request to the Lake Travis ISD Special Education Department
- Hand-deliver a written request to your child's school and ask for a date-stamped copy

**Sample Language:**
*"I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [Child's Name], who is in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned about [briefly describe your concerns—academic struggles, behavior, speech, etc.]. Please consider this my written request for evaluation under IDEA and the Texas Education Code."*

**Step 2: Wait for the District's Response**

Once Lake Travis ISD receives your written request, they have **15 school days** to respond. They must either:
- Provide you with a consent form to sign, agreeing to the evaluation, OR
- Give you written notice explaining why they are refusing to evaluate, along with information about your rights

**Step 3: Give Your Written Consent**

The evaluation cannot begin until you sign and return the consent form. Review it carefully to understand what assessments will be conducted.

**Pro Tip:** Keep copies of all paperwork and note the dates you send and receive documents. This creates a helpful timeline if any questions arise later.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law provides specific timelines to ensure evaluations happen promptly. Here's what you should know:

| Milestone | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| District responds to your written request | Within **15 school days** |
| FIE must be completed after you give consent | Within **45 school days** |
| ARD meeting held to review results | Within **30 calendar days** of FIE completion |

**Important Notes:**

- The 45-school-day timeline does not include days when school is not in session (like summer break, winter break, or spring break)
- If your child is absent for an extended period, this may affect the timeline
- If you requested the evaluation fewer than 35 school days before a break of 5 or more days, the timeline may be adjusted

These timelines exist to protect your family and ensure your child isn't left waiting indefinitely for help.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

After the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and filled with technical language, but it contains crucial information about your child. Here's what to look for:

**Background Information**
This section summarizes your child's history, including developmental milestones, medical history, and previous educational experiences.

**Assessment Results**
You'll see scores from various tests, often presented as standard scores, percentiles, or age/grade equivalents. Don't get too hung up on individual numbers—look for patterns.

**Observations**
Evaluators will describe what they noticed about your child during testing and classroom observations.

**Summary and Conclusions**
This is where evaluators pull everything together to explain what the results mean as a whole.

**Eligibility Determination**
The report will indicate whether your child meets criteria for one or more of the 13 disability categories under IDEA.

**Recommendations**
Evaluators may suggest strategies, accommodations, or services that could help your child succeed.

**Tips for Reading the Report:**
- Request a copy of the report **before** the ARD meeting so you have time to review it
- Write down any questions as you read
- Don't hesitate to ask evaluators to explain anything in plain language
- Consider having an advocate or trusted friend review it with you

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

Once the FIE is complete, Lake Travis ISD will schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 calendar days. In other states, this is often called an IEP meeting. The ARD committee will:

1. **Review the FIE results** as a team
2. **Determine eligibility** for special education services
3. **Develop an IEP** (Individualized Education Program) if your child qualifies

**Who Attends the ARD Meeting?**
- You (the parent or guardian)
- A general education teacher
- A special education teacher
- Someone who can interpret evaluation results (often the school psychologist)
- An administrator who can commit district resources
- Your child (when appropriate)
- Anyone else you invite (advocate, family member, outside therapist)

**Remember:** You are an equal member of the ARD committee. Your insights about your child are invaluable, and you have the right to participate meaningfully in all decisions.

If your child is found eligible, the team will work together to create an IEP with specific goals and services. If your child is not found eligible, you have the right to disagree and pursue options like an Independent Educational Evaluation.
