# What Is a FIE? A Parent's Guide to Full Individual Evaluations in Waller ISD

If you're a parent in Waller ISD and you've been hearing terms like "FIE" or "Full Individual Evaluation," you're in the right place. Whether your child attends school in Waller, Hempstead, Prairie View, or any of the communities served by this growing district northwest of Houston, understanding the FIE process is an important first step in getting your child the support they need. With approximately 9,120 students across the district, Waller ISD serves a diverse community of learners—and that includes children who may need specialized educational services. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about FIEs in clear, straightforward language.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation—commonly called a FIE—is Texas's comprehensive assessment process used to determine whether a child qualifies for special education services. You might also hear it referred to as a "psychoeducational evaluation." Think of it as a detailed look at your whole child: how they learn, how they process information, their strengths, and areas where they might need extra support.

The FIE isn't a single test. Instead, it's a collection of assessments, observations, and information-gathering activities conducted by a team of qualified professionals. This evaluation is required by both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas state law (Texas Education Code Chapter 29) before any child can receive special education services.

The goal of the FIE is to understand your child as a unique learner and to provide the school with the information needed to design appropriate supports if your child qualifies.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Waller ISD?

Every child learns differently, and some children face challenges that require more than what general education can provide. In Waller ISD, like all Texas school districts, a FIE serves as the gateway to special education services.

Here's why the FIE matters for your family:

- **It identifies your child's unique needs.** The evaluation looks at multiple areas of development and learning to create a complete picture.
- **It determines eligibility.** Texas recognizes 13 disability categories for special education, and the FIE helps determine if your child meets the criteria for one or more.
- **It guides educational planning.** The information gathered becomes the foundation for your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP) if they qualify.
- **It protects your child's rights.** The evaluation process ensures decisions are based on data, not assumptions.

For Waller ISD families, having a thorough FIE means your child's teachers and support staff will have the information they need to help your child succeed in their specific school environment.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is designed to look at your child from multiple angles. Texas law requires that evaluations assess all areas related to a suspected disability. Depending on your child's needs, the FIE may include:

- **Cognitive ability (intelligence testing):** How your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems
- **Academic achievement:** Reading, writing, and math skills compared to grade-level expectations
- **Speech and language skills:** How your child understands and uses language to communicate
- **Motor skills:** Fine motor abilities (like writing) and gross motor skills (like coordination)
- **Adaptive behavior:** Daily living skills and how your child functions independently
- **Social and emotional development:** How your child interacts with others and manages emotions
- **Health and developmental history:** Medical factors that might affect learning
- **Observation in the classroom:** How your child performs in their actual school setting
- **Input from parents and teachers:** Your insights about your child at home and the teacher's observations at school

The evaluation team will select specific assessments based on your child's suspected areas of need. No two FIEs look exactly alike because no two children are exactly alike.

## How to Request a FIE at Waller ISD

As a parent, you have the right to request a FIE at any time if you suspect your child may have a disability that affects their learning. Here's how to do it in Waller ISD:

**Step 1: Put your request in writing.**
While you can make a verbal request, a written request creates documentation and starts the legal timeline. Include your child's name, date of birth, school, and your specific concerns about their learning or development.

**Step 2: Address your request to the right person.**
You can submit your written request to your child's principal, teacher, or directly to Waller ISD's Special Education Department. Keep a copy for your records and note the date you submitted it.

**Step 3: Wait for the district's response.**
Once Waller ISD receives your written request, they must respond within 15 school days. The district will either provide you with a consent form to begin the evaluation or provide written notice explaining why they're declining to evaluate, along with information about your rights.

**Sample request language:**
*"Dear [Principal/Special Education Director], I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [child's name], who is in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned about [briefly describe your concerns—for example, difficulty reading, trouble focusing, challenges with social skills]. Please send me the consent form to begin this evaluation. Thank you, [Your name and contact information]."*

Remember: The school cannot evaluate your child without your written consent, and you have the right to ask questions before signing.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law establishes clear deadlines to ensure evaluations happen in a timely manner. Here's what you need to know:

- **15 school days:** After receiving your written request, Waller ISD must respond by either giving you a consent form or providing written notice of their decision not to evaluate.
- **45 school days:** Once you sign and return the consent form, the district has 45 school days to complete the FIE and hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to discuss the results.

**Important notes about the timeline:**

- "School days" means days when school is in session, not calendar days. Summer break, winter break, and other holidays don't count.
- If your child is absent for an extended period, the timeline may be adjusted.
- If you have concerns that the district isn't meeting these deadlines, document everything and consider reaching out for support.

Understanding these timelines helps you advocate effectively for your child and hold the district accountable.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

After the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and filled with technical terms, but it contains crucial information about your child. Here's how to approach it:

**Key sections to look for:**

- **Background information:** Your child's history, including your input as a parent
- **Assessment results:** Scores from various tests, often presented in standard scores, percentiles, and age/grade equivalents
- **Observations:** What evaluators noticed during testing and classroom observations
- **Summary and conclusions:** An overview of findings and how different pieces fit together
- **Eligibility determination:** Whether your child meets criteria for one or more of Texas's 13 disability categories
- **Recommendations:** Suggestions for educational supports and services

**Tips for understanding the report:**

- Don't hesitate to ask for a meeting to have the report explained to you before the ARD meeting.
- Request definitions for any terms or scores you don't understand.
- Remember that you know your child best—if something in the report doesn't match what you see at home, speak up.
- You have the right to receive the report in advance of the ARD meeting so you can review it.

If the report feels overwhelming, that's okay. You can bring someone with you to meetings to help you process the information.

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

Once the FIE is complete, Waller ISD will schedule an ARD meeting. "ARD" stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal—Texas's term for what federal law calls the IEP team meeting.

**At the ARD meeting, the team will:**

1. Review the FIE results together
2. Determine whether your child is eligible for special education services
3. If eligible, discuss your child's specific needs and begin developing an IEP
4. If not eligible, discuss other support options that might be available (like a 504 plan or Response to Intervention services)

**Who attends the ARD meeting:**

- You (and anyone you want to bring for support)
- A school administrator or designee
- Your child's teacher(s)
- The professionals who conducted the evaluation
- A special education teacher (if your child is found eligible)
- Your child (when appropriate, especially for older students)
