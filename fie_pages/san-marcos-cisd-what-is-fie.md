# What Is a FIE? A Guide for San Marcos CISD Parents

If you're a parent in San Marcos CISD and you've been hearing terms like "FIE" or "Full Individual Evaluation" from your child's teachers or school staff, you're in the right place. As part of the Austin-area educational community serving approximately 8,210 students, San Marcos CISD follows Texas state guidelines for identifying and supporting students who may need special education services. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about the FIE process—what it is, why it matters, and how to navigate it for your child.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation, commonly called a FIE, is a comprehensive assessment that Texas school districts use to determine whether a child has a disability and needs special education services. You might also hear it referred to as a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation."

Think of the FIE as a detailed portrait of your child's unique learning profile. It looks at how your child thinks, learns, communicates, and behaves in educational settings. The FIE isn't a single test—it's a collection of assessments, observations, and information gathered from multiple sources to create a complete picture of your child's strengths and challenges.

Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and the Texas Education Code Chapter 29, school districts like San Marcos CISD are required to conduct a FIE before determining that a student is eligible for special education services. This evaluation is provided at no cost to you as a parent.

## Why Is a FIE Important at San Marcos CISD?

The FIE serves several crucial purposes for your child's education within San Marcos CISD:

**It opens doors to support.** Without a completed FIE, your child cannot be found eligible for special education services. The evaluation is the first formal step toward accessing individualized support, accommodations, and specialized instruction.

**It identifies your child's unique needs.** San Marcos CISD serves a diverse student population, and every child learns differently. The FIE helps school staff understand exactly how your child processes information, where they excel, and where they need additional help.

**It guides educational planning.** The results of the FIE don't just determine eligibility—they inform the creation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP), ensuring that supports and goals are tailored specifically to your child.

**It establishes a baseline.** The FIE provides documented information about your child's current abilities, which can be used to measure progress over time and adjust services as needed.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is comprehensive by design. Depending on your child's suspected disability and areas of concern, the evaluation may assess:

- **Cognitive abilities** – How your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems
- **Academic achievement** – Current performance in reading, writing, math, and other academic areas
- **Communication skills** – Speech, language, and the ability to express and understand information
- **Social and emotional development** – How your child interacts with others and manages emotions
- **Adaptive behavior** – Daily living skills and how your child functions independently
- **Motor skills** – Fine motor abilities (like writing) and gross motor skills (like coordination)
- **Health and developmental history** – Medical factors that may affect learning
- **Behavior** – Patterns of behavior in different settings that may impact learning

The specific areas evaluated will depend on your child's individual concerns. San Marcos CISD's evaluation team will determine which assessments are appropriate based on the referral information and your input as a parent.

## How to Request a FIE at San Marcos CISD

Any parent or guardian can request a FIE for their child at any time. Here's how to do it in San Marcos CISD:

### Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing

While you can verbally ask for an evaluation, a written request creates documentation and starts official timelines under Texas law. Write a simple letter or email that includes:

- Your child's full name and date of birth
- Your child's school and grade
- A clear statement that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation
- A brief description of your concerns about your child's learning or development
- Your contact information
- The date

### Step 2: Submit Your Request

Send your written request to:

- Your child's principal
- The campus special education coordinator
- San Marcos CISD's Special Education Department

Keep a copy of everything you submit, and note the date you delivered it.

### Step 3: Wait for the District's Response

Once San Marcos CISD receives your written request, they must respond within **15 school days**. The district will either:

- Agree to evaluate and provide you with a consent form, or
- Decline to evaluate and provide you with written notice explaining why

If the district agrees to evaluate, you'll receive a "Notice and Consent for Full Individual Evaluation" form. The evaluation timeline doesn't begin until you sign and return this consent form.

### Sample Request Letter

> Dear [Principal's Name or Special Education Coordinator],
>
> I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [Child's Name], who is in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned about [briefly describe your concerns—for example, "difficulty reading at grade level," "struggles with attention and focus," or "challenges with social interactions"].
>
> Please contact me to discuss next steps and provide the consent forms needed to begin this process.
>
> Thank you for your attention to this matter.
>
> Sincerely,
> [Your Name]
> [Phone Number]
> [Email Address]
> [Date]

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas has specific legal timelines that San Marcos CISD must follow:

| Milestone | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| District response to your written request | Within **15 school days** |
| Completion of the FIE after you give consent | Within **45 school days** |
| ARD/IEP meeting after FIE completion | Within **30 calendar days** |

**Important notes about the 45-day timeline:**

- The clock starts when the district receives your signed consent form
- The 45 days refer to **school days**, not calendar days
- Holidays and breaks when school is closed for 5+ consecutive days do not count
- Summer days typically do not count unless your child attends summer school

If you signed consent near the end of the school year, ask San Marcos CISD's special education department exactly when you can expect the evaluation to be completed.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

Once the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and technical, but understanding its key parts will help you advocate for your child:

### Key Sections of the FIE Report

**Background information** – This section summarizes your child's history, including developmental milestones, medical history, and previous educational experiences. Review this for accuracy.

**Assessment results** – You'll see scores from various tests administered. Don't focus only on numbers—look at what the evaluator says the scores mean for your child's learning.

**Observations** – Evaluators often observe your child in classroom settings or during testing. This section describes how your child approached tasks, their attention, behavior, and interactions.

**Summary and recommendations** – This is often the most important section. It pulls everything together, explains whether your child meets eligibility criteria for special education, and suggests what kinds of support might help.

### Tips for Reviewing the Report

- **Request a copy before your ARD meeting** – You're entitled to receive the report before the meeting so you have time to review it
- **Write down your questions** – Note anything you don't understand or that doesn't match your experience with your child
- **Look for strengths** – The FIE should identify what your child does well, not just areas of difficulty
- **Check for accuracy** – Ensure names, dates, and background information are correct

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

After San Marcos CISD completes the FIE, an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting will be scheduled within 30 calendar days. This is Texas's term for what federal law calls the IEP meeting.

### At the ARD Meeting, the Team Will:

1. **Review the FIE results** – Evaluators or special education staff will explain the findings
2. **Determine eligibility** – The team (which includes you!) decides if your child qualifies for special education under one of Texas's 13 disability categories
3. **Develop the IEP** – If eligible, the team creates an Individualized Education Program with specific goals and services

### Your Role at the ARD Meeting

You are a full and equal member of the ARD committee. You have the right to:

- Ask questions about anything you don't understand
- Share your own observations
