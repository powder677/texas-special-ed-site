# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Wichita Falls ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Wichita Falls ISD and you've been hearing terms like "FIE" or "evaluation" mentioned by your child's teacher or school staff, you're in the right place. Navigating the special education process can feel overwhelming, but understanding what a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is—and how it works specifically within Wichita Falls ISD—is an important first step in getting your child the support they need to thrive. This guide is designed to walk you through the entire process in clear, straightforward language so you can feel confident advocating for your child.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation, commonly called a FIE, is a comprehensive assessment that Texas school districts must complete before a child can qualify for special education services. You might also hear it referred to as a "psychoeducational evaluation." Think of it as a detailed look at your child's unique strengths, challenges, and learning needs.

The FIE isn't just one test—it's a collection of assessments, observations, and information gathering designed to paint a complete picture of how your child learns and develops. This evaluation is required by both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas state law (Texas Education Code Chapter 29).

The purpose of a FIE is to determine:
- Whether your child has a disability that affects their learning
- How that disability impacts their ability to access education
- What types of support, services, or specialized instruction your child might need

## Why Is a FIE Important at Wichita Falls ISD?

For families in Wichita Falls ISD, the FIE serves as the gateway to special education services and supports. With approximately 12,980 students across the district, Wichita Falls ISD serves a diverse community with children who have a wide range of learning needs.

The FIE matters because:

**It's legally required.** Before your child can receive any special education services through Wichita Falls ISD, the district must complete a FIE. This isn't optional—it's the law.

**It identifies your child's specific needs.** Every child is different. The FIE helps pinpoint exactly where your child needs support, rather than using a one-size-fits-all approach.

**It guides your child's educational plan.** The results of the FIE directly inform the creation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP), ensuring services are tailored to their unique needs.

**It establishes a baseline.** The FIE provides documented information about your child's current abilities, which helps track progress over time.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is comprehensive by design. It looks at multiple areas of your child's development and learning. While the specific assessments used will depend on your child's suspected areas of need, a FIE typically evaluates:

**Cognitive Ability (Intelligence)**
This measures how your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems. It helps identify intellectual strengths and challenges.

**Academic Achievement**
This looks at how your child performs in reading, writing, and math compared to grade-level expectations.

**Communication Skills**
If speech or language concerns exist, the evaluation will assess how well your child understands and uses language.

**Motor Skills**
This may include both fine motor skills (like writing or using scissors) and gross motor skills (like running or balance).

**Social and Emotional Development**
Evaluators look at how your child interacts with others, manages emotions, and behaves in different settings.

**Adaptive Behavior**
This examines your child's daily living skills and independence, such as self-care, following routines, and problem-solving in real-world situations.

**Health and Developmental History**
Information about your child's medical history, developmental milestones, and any previous evaluations is gathered and reviewed.

**Observations and Input**
Teachers, parents, and sometimes the child themselves provide input through interviews, questionnaires, and direct observations in the classroom.

## How to Request a FIE at Wichita Falls ISD

As a parent, you have the right to request a FIE at any time if you suspect your child may have a disability affecting their learning. Here's how to do it in Wichita Falls ISD:

**Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing**

While you can verbally ask for an evaluation, a written request creates a clear record and starts the official timeline. Address your letter to the principal of your child's school or the district's Special Education Department.

Your letter should include:
- Your child's full name and date of birth
- Your child's school and current grade
- A clear statement that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation
- A brief description of your concerns about your child's learning or development
- Your contact information
- The date

**Step 2: Submit Your Request**

Deliver your written request to the school. You can hand-deliver it (and ask for a date-stamped copy), send it by email, or mail it via certified mail so you have proof of delivery.

**Step 3: Wait for the District's Response**

Once Wichita Falls ISD receives your request, they must respond within 15 school days. The district will either:
- Agree to evaluate and send you a consent form, or
- Decline to evaluate and provide you with written notice explaining why

**Step 4: Give Written Consent**

If the district agrees to evaluate, you'll receive paperwork asking for your consent. The evaluation cannot begin until you sign and return this consent form. Take time to read it carefully, and don't hesitate to ask questions.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law sets specific deadlines to ensure evaluations are completed in a timely manner. Here's what you need to know:

**15 School Days**
After receiving your written request, the district has 15 school days to either agree or refuse to evaluate your child.

**45 School Days**
Once you provide written consent, the district has 45 school days to complete the evaluation and hold the meeting to review the results. This meeting is called an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting.

**Important Notes:**
- "School days" means days when school is in session, so summer break, holidays, and other closures don't count toward the timeline
- If your child is absent for an extended period, the timeline may be adjusted
- The 45-day timeline applies only after consent is received, not from the date of your initial request

If Wichita Falls ISD misses these deadlines, document the delay and reach out to the Special Education Department to understand the reason and get the process back on track.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

After the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and may contain unfamiliar terminology, but understanding it is essential.

**Key sections of the FIE report typically include:**

- **Background Information:** Your child's history, previous services, and the reason for the evaluation
- **Assessment Results:** Scores and descriptions from each test administered
- **Observations:** Notes on your child's behavior during testing and in the classroom
- **Interpretation of Findings:** What the results mean in practical terms
- **Conclusions:** Whether your child meets criteria for a disability category under IDEA
- **Recommendations:** Suggestions for supports, services, or further assessment

**Tips for Reading the Report:**

- Request a copy before the ARD meeting so you have time to review it
- Highlight areas you don't understand and write down questions
- Look at both strengths and challenges—the FIE should identify both
- If scores and percentiles are confusing, ask the school psychologist or evaluator to explain them in plain language

Remember, you know your child better than anyone. If something in the report doesn't seem accurate or is missing, speak up.

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

Once the FIE is complete, Wichita Falls ISD will schedule an ARD meeting. ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal—it's Texas's version of the IEP meeting used in other states.

**At this meeting, the team will:**

1. Review and discuss the FIE results
2. Determine if your child qualifies for special education under one of the 13 disability categories in IDEA
3. If eligible, begin developing an Individualized Education Program (IEP) with goals, services, and accommodations

**Who attends the ARD meeting?**

- You (the parent)—you're an essential member of the team
- A general education teacher
- A special education teacher
- A representative from Wichita Falls ISD who can commit resources
- Someone who can interpret the evaluation results (often the school psychologist)
- Your child, when appropriate
- Anyone else you'd like to bring for support (an advocate, family member, etc.)
