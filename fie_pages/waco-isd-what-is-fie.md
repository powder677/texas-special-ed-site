# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Waco ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Waco ISD and you've been hearing terms like "FIE" or "evaluation" mentioned in conversations about your child's learning, you're in the right place. Whether your child is struggling in the classroom, has been identified by a teacher as potentially needing extra support, or you simply want to understand what options are available, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know about the Full Individual Evaluation process in Waco Independent School District.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation—commonly called a FIE—is a comprehensive assessment that determines whether your child qualifies for special education services in Texas public schools. Think of it as a detailed look at your child's unique strengths, challenges, and learning needs.

The FIE is sometimes referred to as a "psychoeducational evaluation" because it examines both psychological and educational factors that affect how your child learns. This evaluation is required by federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas state law (Texas Education Code Chapter 29) before any child can receive special education services.

It's important to understand that a FIE isn't a single test—it's a collection of assessments, observations, and information gathered from multiple sources to create a complete picture of your child. The evaluation is conducted at no cost to you by qualified professionals within the school district.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Waco ISD?

For families in Waco ISD, the FIE serves as the gateway to accessing vital educational support for your child. With approximately 14,240 students across the district, Waco ISD serves a diverse community with varying learning needs. The FIE helps ensure that every child who needs specialized instruction receives an education tailored to their individual needs.

The FIE is important because it:

- **Identifies specific learning differences** that may be affecting your child's academic progress
- **Determines eligibility** for special education services under one or more of the 13 disability categories recognized by IDEA
- **Provides detailed information** that helps teachers and specialists understand how your child learns best
- **Creates a foundation** for developing an Individualized Education Program (IEP) if your child qualifies
- **Documents your child's current levels** of performance across multiple areas
- **Ensures equal access** to a free appropriate public education (FAPE) for every student who needs it

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

The FIE looks at your child from multiple angles to understand their complete learning profile. Depending on your child's suspected areas of need, the evaluation may include assessments in some or all of the following areas:

### Cognitive Ability
This measures how your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems. It helps identify your child's intellectual strengths and any areas where they may need additional support.

### Academic Achievement
These assessments look at your child's skills in reading, writing, math, and other academic areas compared to grade-level expectations.

### Communication Skills
Speech-language pathologists may evaluate your child's ability to understand language (receptive) and express themselves (expressive), as well as articulation and social communication.

### Motor Skills
If there are concerns about your child's fine motor skills (like handwriting) or gross motor skills (like coordination and balance), occupational or physical therapists may conduct evaluations.

### Social-Emotional and Behavioral Functioning
This examines how your child manages emotions, interacts with peers and adults, and handles the social demands of school.

### Adaptive Behavior
These assessments look at your child's practical life skills and how independently they can function in daily activities appropriate for their age.

### Health and Developmental History
Information about your child's medical background, developmental milestones, and any previous interventions is gathered to provide context for current functioning.

The evaluation team at Waco ISD will review existing data and determine which specific assessments are needed based on your child's individual situation. You'll be informed about which areas will be evaluated before testing begins.

## How to Request a FIE at Waco ISD

As a parent, you have the right to request a FIE for your child at any time if you suspect they may have a disability that is affecting their learning. Here's how to make that request in Waco ISD:

### Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing
While you can make a verbal request, we strongly recommend submitting your request in writing. This creates a paper trail and starts the official timeline. You can:

- Write a letter or email to your child's principal
- Send your request to the Waco ISD Special Education Department
- Give a written request directly to your child's teacher

### Step 2: What to Include in Your Request
Your letter doesn't need to be formal or lengthy. Simply include:

- Your child's full name and date of birth
- Your child's school and current grade
- A statement requesting a Full Individual Evaluation
- A brief description of your concerns about your child's learning or development
- Your contact information
- The date

### Sample Request Letter

> Dear [Principal's Name],
>
> I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [Child's Name], who is currently in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned because [briefly describe your concerns—for example: "they are struggling significantly with reading and falling behind their peers" or "they have difficulty focusing and completing work in class"].
>
> I understand that this evaluation will help determine whether my child qualifies for special education services. Please contact me to discuss next steps and provide consent forms.
>
> Thank you for your attention to this request.
>
> Sincerely,
> [Your Name]
> [Phone Number]
> [Email Address]
> [Date]

### Step 3: Wait for the District's Response
Once Waco ISD receives your written request, they must respond within 15 school days. They will either:

- Provide you with a "Notice and Consent for Full Individual Evaluation" form to sign, OR
- Provide you with a "Prior Written Notice" explaining why they are declining to evaluate and what data supports that decision

If the district agrees to evaluate, the FIE timeline officially begins when you sign and return the consent form.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas has specific legal timelines that Waco ISD must follow throughout the FIE process. Understanding these deadlines helps you know what to expect and ensures your child's evaluation moves forward appropriately.

### Key Timeline Requirements

| Milestone | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| District response to your request | Within **15 school days** of receiving your written request |
| Completion of the FIE | Within **45 school days** of receiving signed consent |
| ARD/IEP meeting to review results | Within **30 calendar days** of completing the FIE |

### Important Notes About Timelines

- The 45 school days do **not** include days when school is not in session for more than 5 consecutive days (like summer break or winter break)
- If your child is absent for an extended period or transfers from another district, the timeline may be adjusted
- You and the district can mutually agree in writing to extend the timeline if needed
- These timelines are established under Texas Education Code §29.004 and must be followed

If you feel that Waco ISD is not meeting these legal timelines, you have the right to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

## Understanding Your FIE Report

After all the assessments are complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can feel overwhelming at first glance—it's often lengthy and filled with specialized terminology. Here's how to make sense of it:

### Sections You'll Typically Find in a FIE Report

**Background Information**: This section summarizes your child's history, including developmental milestones, medical information, and educational background.

**Assessment Results**: Each area evaluated will have its own section with:
- The name of the test(s) administered
- Your child's scores (often shown as standard scores, percentiles, or age/grade equivalents)
- What those scores mean in plain language

**Observations**: Evaluators will describe what they noticed about your child during testing—their attention, effort, behavior, and interaction style.

**Summary and Conclusions**: This section pulls everything together and explains what the results suggest about your child's learning profile.

**Eligibility Determination**: The report will indicate whether your child appears to meet criteria for one or more disability categories under IDEA.

**Recommendations**: The evaluators will suggest strategies, accommodations, or services that may help your child succeed.

### Tips for Reading Your FIE Report

- **Ask for a meeting** before the ARD to review the report with the evaluator if you need help understanding the results
- **Look for the "plain language" summaries** that explain what scores mean
- **Focus on the recommendations** section—this often contains the most practical information
- **Make a list of questions**
