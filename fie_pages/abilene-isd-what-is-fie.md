# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Abilene ISD Parents

If you're an Abilene ISD parent wondering whether your child might need extra support at school, you've come to the right place. Navigating special education can feel overwhelming, but understanding the evaluation process is an important first step. Here in Abilene, our district serves nearly 15,000 students across our West Texas community, and many families just like yours are asking the same questions about how to get their child the help they need. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) process at Abilene ISD.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation, commonly called a FIE, is a comprehensive assessment that Texas school districts must conduct before a child can receive special education services. You might also hear it referred to as a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation."

Think of the FIE as a detailed portrait of your child—their strengths, challenges, learning style, and unique needs. It's not just a single test, but rather a collection of assessments, observations, and information gathering designed to give everyone a complete picture of how your child learns and what support they might need to succeed.

The FIE is required under both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas state law (Texas Education Code Chapter 29). This means Abilene ISD must follow specific rules and timelines when conducting these evaluations, and you have important rights throughout the process.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Abilene ISD?

The FIE serves as the foundation for your child's entire special education journey at Abilene ISD. Here's why it matters so much:

**It determines eligibility.** Before your child can receive special education services, the district must establish that they have a qualifying disability that affects their educational performance. The FIE provides the evidence needed to make this determination.

**It identifies your child's unique needs.** Every child is different, and the FIE helps identify exactly where your child needs support—whether that's in reading, math, behavior, speech, social skills, or other areas.

**It guides the development of services.** The information gathered during the FIE directly shapes the Individualized Education Program (IEP) that Abilene ISD will create for your child. A thorough evaluation leads to better, more targeted services.

**It establishes a baseline.** The FIE gives you and the school a starting point to measure your child's progress over time.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE at Abilene ISD looks at multiple areas of your child's development and functioning. The specific assessments used will depend on your child's suspected disability and areas of concern, but may include:

**Cognitive ability** — How your child thinks, reasons, and processes information

**Academic achievement** — Your child's current performance in reading, writing, math, and other academic areas

**Communication skills** — How your child understands and uses language, both spoken and written

**Social and emotional development** — How your child interacts with others, manages emotions, and handles social situations

**Adaptive behavior** — Your child's ability to handle everyday life skills appropriate for their age

**Motor skills** — Fine motor skills (like writing) and gross motor skills (like running or climbing)

**Health and developmental history** — Information about your child's medical background, developmental milestones, and any previous evaluations

**Behavioral observations** — How your child acts in different settings, including the classroom

The evaluation team will gather information through formal testing, observations, parent and teacher interviews, and review of existing records. You'll be asked to complete questionnaires and share your insights about your child—because nobody knows your child better than you do.

## How to Request a FIE at Abilene ISD

Any parent can request a FIE for their child at any time. You don't need to wait for the school to suggest it. Here's how to make your request:

**Step 1: Put your request in writing.** While you can make a verbal request, a written request creates a paper trail and starts the official timeline. Address your letter to your child's principal or the Abilene ISD Special Education Department.

**Step 2: Be specific about your concerns.** Describe what you're seeing at home and what concerns led you to request the evaluation. For example: "My son struggles significantly with reading compared to his classmates" or "My daughter has difficulty staying focused and completing assignments."

**Step 3: Keep a copy for your records.** Always keep copies of everything you send to the district.

**Step 4: Send your request through a trackable method.** Consider hand-delivering it and asking for a dated receipt, sending it via email and requesting confirmation, or using certified mail.

Here's a simple template you can use:

*Dear [Principal's Name or Special Education Director],*

*I am concerned about [briefly describe your concerns]. I believe an evaluation would help us understand my child's needs and determine if special education services are appropriate.*

*Please send me the required consent forms as soon as possible. I understand that Abilene ISD has 15 school days from receiving my signed consent to propose an evaluation plan.*

*Thank you for your attention to this request.*

*Sincerely,*

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law sets clear deadlines for the evaluation process. Here's what you can expect at Abilene ISD:

**Within 15 school days** of receiving your written request, the district must either:
- Provide you with a Notice of Proposed Evaluation and consent forms, OR
- Provide you with a Prior Written Notice explaining why they're refusing to evaluate (you have the right to dispute this decision)

**Within 45 school days** of receiving your signed consent, the district must:
- Complete all evaluation components
- Write the FIE report
- Make the report available to you

**Important:** The 45-day timeline only counts school days when students are in attendance. Summer days, holidays, and breaks don't count toward this deadline.

**Before the ARD meeting**, you have the right to receive a copy of the FIE report. Texas law requires that you receive it at least 48 hours before the meeting where your child's eligibility will be discussed. Don't be afraid to ask for it—you need time to review and understand the findings.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

The FIE report can be lengthy and full of educational and psychological terminology. Don't be intimidated! Here's what you'll typically find and what to look for:

**Background information** — This section summarizes your child's history, including developmental milestones, medical history, and educational background. Check this section for accuracy.

**Assessment results** — You'll see scores from various tests. These often include:
- *Standard scores* — These compare your child to other children the same age. A score of 100 is typically average.
- *Percentile ranks* — These show what percentage of children scored at or below your child's level. For example, the 25th percentile means your child scored as well as or better than 25% of children their age.

**Observations** — This section describes how your child behaved during testing and in classroom observations.

**Conclusions and recommendations** — The evaluators will share their professional opinions about your child's needs and whether they qualify for special education.

**Tips for reviewing your FIE report:**
- Read it more than once—it's a lot to absorb
- Highlight anything you don't understand and bring questions to the ARD meeting
- Compare the report to what you see at home—does it match your experience of your child?
- Look for strengths, not just challenges—a good FIE identifies both
- Request a meeting with the evaluator if you need clarification before the ARD
