# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Seguin ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Seguin ISD and you've been hearing terms like "FIE" or "Full Individual Evaluation," you're not alone—and you're in the right place. Whether your child is struggling in school, a teacher has expressed concerns, or you simply want to understand what support might be available, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know about the FIE process right here in Seguin. As a mid-sized district serving approximately 7,140 students in the San Antonio region, Seguin ISD has dedicated special education staff ready to support your family through this important process.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation—commonly called a FIE—is Texas's comprehensive assessment process used to determine whether a child qualifies for special education services. You might also hear it referred to as a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation."

Think of the FIE as a detailed snapshot of your child's unique learning profile. It looks at how your child thinks, learns, processes information, and functions in school. The evaluation gathers information from multiple sources—testing, observations, teacher input, and your insights as a parent—to create a complete picture of your child's strengths and challenges.

Under both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and the Texas Education Code Chapter 29, school districts must complete a FIE before a child can receive special education services. This isn't just paperwork—it's a legal requirement designed to ensure that every child who needs support gets the right kind of help.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Seguin ISD?

For families in Seguin ISD, the FIE serves as the gateway to special education services and supports. Here's why it matters:

**It identifies your child's specific needs.** Every child learns differently, and a FIE helps pinpoint exactly where your child excels and where they may need additional support.

**It determines eligibility.** Texas recognizes 13 disability categories under which a child may qualify for special education. The FIE provides the data needed to determine if your child meets the criteria for one or more of these categories.

**It guides the development of support plans.** If your child qualifies for special education, the FIE results will directly inform the creation of their Individualized Education Program (IEP), ensuring that goals and services are tailored to their unique needs.

**It protects your child's rights.** The formal evaluation process includes important legal protections for both you and your child, ensuring that decisions are made based on comprehensive, accurate information.

In a district the size of Seguin ISD, your child's evaluation team will likely include professionals who understand the local community and the specific resources available within the district.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is comprehensive by design. Texas law requires that it assess all areas related to your child's suspected disability. Depending on your child's needs, the evaluation may include:

**Cognitive ability (intelligence testing)** – How your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems

**Academic achievement** – Your child's current skill levels in reading, writing, and math

**Speech and language skills** – How your child understands and uses language to communicate

**Motor skills** – Fine motor abilities (like writing) and gross motor skills (like coordination)

**Social and emotional development** – How your child interacts with others, manages emotions, and handles social situations

**Adaptive behavior** – Your child's ability to handle everyday tasks and age-appropriate responsibilities

**Health and developmental history** – Medical information, developmental milestones, and any relevant background

**Behavioral assessments** – Patterns of behavior that may impact learning

The evaluation team at Seguin ISD will determine which specific assessments are needed based on your child's suspected areas of difficulty. You'll be asked to provide input, and the team must consider information from a variety of sources—not just test scores.

## How to Request a FIE at Seguin ISD

As a parent, you have the right to request a FIE at any time if you suspect your child may have a disability that affects their learning. Here's how to make that request in Seguin ISD:

### Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing

While you can make a verbal request, we strongly recommend submitting a written request. This creates a paper trail and starts the official timeline for the evaluation process.

Your letter should include:
- The date
- Your child's full name and date of birth
- Your child's school and grade
- A clear statement that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation for special education
- A brief description of your concerns
- Your contact information

### Step 2: Submit Your Request

You can deliver your written request to:
- Your child's principal
- The school counselor
- The Seguin ISD Special Education Department

Keep a copy for your records and note the date you submitted it.

### Step 3: Wait for the District's Response

Once Seguin ISD receives your written request, they have **15 school days** to respond. The district will either:
- Provide you with a "Notice of Proposed Evaluation" and a consent form, OR
- Provide you with a "Prior Written Notice" explaining why they are declining to evaluate

If the district agrees to evaluate, you'll need to sign the consent form before any testing can begin.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law establishes clear timelines to ensure evaluations happen promptly. Here's what you should know:

| Milestone | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| District response to your written request | Within **15 school days** |
| Completion of the FIE after you give consent | Within **45 school days** |
| ARD/IEP meeting after FIE completion | Within **30 calendar days** |

**Important notes about the 45-school-day timeline:**

- The clock starts when you sign and return the consent form
- School days do not include holidays or breaks when students are not in school
- If your child is absent for an extended period, the timeline may be adjusted
- If you request an evaluation during the summer, the timeline may be affected—but the district cannot use summer break as a reason for unreasonable delays

If Seguin ISD is approaching the deadline and you haven't heard anything, don't hesitate to reach out to the Special Education Department for an update.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

After the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and filled with technical terms, but understanding it is crucial. Here's what to look for:

**Background information** – This section summarizes your child's history, including developmental milestones, medical history, and educational background.

**Assessment results** – You'll see scores from various tests, often presented as standard scores, percentiles, or age/grade equivalents. Don't be intimidated by the numbers—ask the evaluation team to explain what they mean in plain language.

**Observations** – The evaluators will describe how your child behaved and performed during testing, as well as observations from the classroom.

**Summary and conclusions** – This section pulls everything together and provides the evaluation team's professional interpretation of the results.

**Eligibility determination** – The report will indicate whether your child meets the criteria for one or more of Texas's 13 disability categories.

**Recommendations** – Suggestions for how to support your child, which may include special education services, accommodations, or other interventions.

**Tips for reviewing the report:**

- Request a copy of the FIE report **before** the ARD meeting so you have time to review it
- Write down any questions you have
- Look up unfamiliar terms or ask the school to explain them
- Consider sharing the report with your child's doctor or an outside professional for a second perspective
