# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Santa Fe ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Santa Fe ISD and you've been hearing terms like "FIE" or "Full Individual Evaluation," you're not alone in wondering what it all means. Whether your child is struggling in school, a teacher has expressed concerns, or you simply want to understand your options, this guide is here to help. Santa Fe ISD serves approximately 4,230 students across the district, and like all Texas school districts, it has specific processes in place to identify and support students who may need special education services. Understanding the FIE process is your first step toward getting your child the help they deserve.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation, commonly called a FIE, is a comprehensive assessment that Texas school districts use to determine whether a child has a disability and needs special education services. You might also hear it referred to as a "psychoeducational evaluation."

Think of the FIE as a detailed portrait of your child—how they learn, where they excel, and where they might need additional support. It looks at many different areas, from academic skills and cognitive abilities to social-emotional development and behavior.

The FIE is required by both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas state law (Texas Education Code Chapter 29) before any child can receive special education services. This evaluation is provided at no cost to families—the school district pays for it entirely.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Santa Fe ISD?

For Santa Fe ISD families, the FIE serves several crucial purposes:

**It opens doors to support.** Without a FIE, your child cannot qualify for special education services through an Individualized Education Program (IEP). The evaluation is the gateway to accommodations, specialized instruction, and related services your child may need.

**It provides a complete picture.** Teachers and parents often see pieces of the puzzle, but a FIE brings together information from multiple sources—testing, observations, interviews, and records—to create a comprehensive understanding of your child's strengths and challenges.

**It guides educational planning.** The results help Santa Fe ISD staff develop an educational plan tailored specifically to your child's unique needs, rather than using a one-size-fits-all approach.

**It establishes legal protections.** Once your child qualifies for special education through the FIE process, they're entitled to specific rights and protections under federal and Texas law.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is thorough by design. Depending on your child's suspected areas of need, the evaluation may include:

- **Cognitive ability** – How your child thinks, reasons, and processes information
- **Academic achievement** – Reading, writing, math, and other school-related skills
- **Speech and language** – How your child understands and uses language
- **Motor skills** – Fine motor (writing, cutting) and gross motor (running, coordination) abilities
- **Social-emotional functioning** – How your child handles emotions, relationships, and social situations
- **Adaptive behavior** – Daily living skills and independence
- **Health and developmental history** – Medical factors that may affect learning
- **Behavior** – Attention, focus, and behavioral patterns in different settings

The evaluation team selects assessments based on your child's specific concerns. For example, if reading is the primary concern, the FIE will include in-depth reading assessments. If attention and behavior are issues, those areas will be thoroughly evaluated.

## How to Request a FIE at Santa Fe ISD

Any parent or guardian can request a FIE for their child at any time. Here's how to do it in Santa Fe ISD:

**Step 1: Put your request in writing.** While you can make a verbal request, a written request creates documentation and starts the official timeline. Address your letter to the principal of your child's school or the Santa Fe ISD Special Education Department.

**Step 2: Be specific about your concerns.** Briefly explain why you believe your child may need an evaluation. For example: "My daughter struggles significantly with reading despite extra help at home" or "My son has difficulty focusing and completing classwork."

**Step 3: Keep a copy for your records.** Always keep copies of any documents you submit to the district.

**Sample Request Letter:**

*Dear [Principal's Name or Special Education Director],*

*I am the parent of [Child's Name], a [grade] student at [School Name]. I am requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) to determine whether my child qualifies for special education services.*

*My concerns include [briefly list concerns]. I am requesting this evaluation under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code Chapter 29.*

*Please send me the consent forms and any other documents needed to begin this process. Thank you for your attention to this request.*

*Sincerely,*
*[Your Name]*
*[Date]*
*[Contact Information]*

After receiving your request, Santa Fe ISD must respond within 15 school days by either providing you with consent forms and a Notice of Proposed Evaluation or providing written notice that they're declining to evaluate (along with reasons why).

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law sets clear deadlines for the evaluation process. Understanding these timelines helps you know what to expect and ensures the district stays on track:

| Step | Timeline |
|------|----------|
| District response to your request | Within **15 school days** |
| You review and sign consent forms | Take the time you need (but don't delay unnecessarily) |
| District completes the FIE | Within **45 school days** of receiving your signed consent |
| ARD meeting to review results | Held within **30 calendar days** of completing the FIE |

**Important notes about the 45-day timeline:**

- The clock starts when the district receives your signed consent
- The 45 days count school days, not calendar days
- Days don't count when school is closed for holidays lasting 5+ consecutive days
- Summer days typically don't count unless your child attends summer school

If Santa Fe ISD misses these deadlines, you have the right to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

The FIE report can be lengthy and full of technical language. Here's what you'll typically find and how to make sense of it:

**Background information** – Your child's history, including developmental milestones, medical history, and previous educational experiences.

**Assessment results** – Scores from various tests, often presented as standard scores, percentiles, or grade equivalents. Don't get too caught up in the numbers—focus on what they mean for your child.

**Observations** – Notes on how your child behaved during testing and in the classroom.

**Summary and recommendations** – The most important section. This explains what the evaluators found and whether your child meets criteria for a disability under IDEA.

**Tips for reviewing the report:**

- Request the report at least a few days before the ARD meeting so you have time to read it
- Highlight terms or sections you don't understand and ask for explanations
- Focus on the recommendations—what is the team suggesting for next steps?
- Remember that you know your child best. If something doesn't sound right, speak up

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

Once the FIE is complete, Santa Fe ISD will schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. In Texas, what other states call an "IEP meeting" is called an ARD meeting.

At this meeting, you'll join a team that typically includes:

- You (the parent)
- A special education teacher
- A general education teacher
- A district representative
- The person who conducted the evaluation (or someone who can interpret results)
- Others as appropriate (speech therapist, counselor, etc.)

During the ARD meeting, the team will:

1. **Review the FIE results** together
2. **Determine eligibility** – Does your child meet the criteria for one of the 13 disability categories under IDEA?
3. **If eligible, develop an IEP** – This includes goals, services, accommodations, and placement decisions

Remember: You are an equal member of this team. Your input matters, and you don't have to agree to anything you're uncomfortable with.

## Your Rights During the FIE Process at Santa Fe ISD

Texas and federal law give you important protections throughout the evaluation process:

**The right to request an evaluation.** You can request a FIE at any time, and the district must respond.

**The right to informed consent.** The district cannot evaluate your child without your written permission. You must be told what assessments will be used.

**The right to participate.** You should be involved in the process, provide input about your child, and attend all meetings.

**The right to review records.** You can see all educational records and evaluation materials related to your child.
