# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Friendswood ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Friendswood ISD and suspect your child may need additional support at school, you've likely heard the term "FIE" mentioned by teachers, counselors, or other parents. Understanding what a Full Individual Evaluation is—and how it works specifically within Friendswood ISD—can feel overwhelming at first. You're not alone, and this guide is here to walk you through every step of the process in clear, straightforward language. Friendswood ISD serves approximately 5,890 students across its schools, and the district has established processes to evaluate children who may qualify for special education services.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A FIE, or Full Individual Evaluation, is a comprehensive assessment that Texas school districts must complete before a child can receive special education services. You might also hear it called a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation."

Think of the FIE as a detailed snapshot of your child's unique learning profile. It looks at many different areas—academic skills, cognitive abilities, social-emotional development, and more—to determine whether your child has a disability that affects their ability to learn and whether they need specially designed instruction.

The FIE is required under two important laws:
- **IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act)** – the federal law that guarantees students with disabilities the right to a free appropriate public education
- **Texas Education Code Chapter 29** – the state law that outlines how Texas schools must implement special education services

The FIE is not a single test. It's a collection of assessments, observations, interviews, and data gathered by qualified professionals to create a complete picture of your child's strengths and challenges.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Friendswood ISD?

For Friendswood ISD families, the FIE serves as the gateway to special education services. Without a completed FIE, your child cannot be identified as eligible for special education support, and an Individualized Education Program (IEP) cannot be developed.

The FIE matters because it:

- **Identifies your child's specific needs** – Every child learns differently. The FIE helps pinpoint exactly where your child needs support.
- **Determines eligibility** – Texas recognizes 13 disability categories for special education. The FIE helps determine if your child qualifies under one or more of these categories.
- **Guides programming decisions** – The results inform what services, accommodations, and supports will be included in your child's IEP.
- **Establishes a baseline** – The FIE creates a starting point to measure your child's progress over time.
- **Protects your child's rights** – A thorough evaluation ensures your child receives the services they're legally entitled to under federal and state law.

Friendswood ISD, like all Texas public school districts, is required to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities who may need special education services—this is called "Child Find."

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

The FIE is designed to look at your child from multiple angles. Depending on your child's suspected areas of need, the evaluation may include:

**Cognitive/Intellectual Functioning**
Assessments that measure how your child thinks, reasons, solves problems, and processes information.

**Academic Achievement**
Tests that evaluate reading, writing, math, and other academic skills compared to grade-level expectations.

**Communication Skills**
Evaluation of speech, language, and communication abilities, including how your child understands and expresses language.

**Social-Emotional and Behavioral Functioning**
Assessment of emotional well-being, social skills, behavior patterns, and how your child interacts with others.

**Adaptive Behavior**
How your child handles everyday life skills like self-care, following routines, and functioning independently.

**Motor Skills**
Fine motor (writing, cutting) and gross motor (walking, running, coordination) abilities when relevant.

**Health and Developmental History**
Review of medical records, developmental milestones, and any health factors that may affect learning.

**Classroom Observations**
A professional will observe your child in their natural school environment to see how they function in the classroom.

**Parent and Teacher Input**
Your insights as a parent are invaluable. Teachers and other school staff who work with your child also provide information through interviews and rating scales.

Texas law requires that the evaluation be conducted in all areas related to your child's suspected disability. The evaluation team must use multiple sources of information—not just a single test score—to make decisions.

## How to Request a FIE at Friendswood ISD

Any parent can request a FIE for their child at any time. Here's how to do it in Friendswood ISD:

**Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing**

While you can make a verbal request, always follow up in writing. This creates documentation and starts the official timeline. Your written request should include:
- Your child's full name
- Your child's date of birth and grade
- The school your child attends
- A clear statement that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation
- Your concerns about your child's learning, development, or behavior
- The date of your request
- Your contact information

**Step 2: Submit Your Request**

You can submit your written request to:
- Your child's principal
- The school counselor
- The special education department at Friendswood ISD
- Your child's teacher (who should forward it to the appropriate staff)

**Step 3: Wait for the District's Response**

Once Friendswood ISD receives your written request, they have **15 school days** to respond. The district will either:
- Provide you with a "Notice and Consent for Full Individual Evaluation" form to sign, OR
- Provide you with written notice explaining why they are refusing to evaluate, along with information about your rights

**Step 4: Give Written Consent**

If the district agrees to evaluate, you must sign the consent form before any testing can begin. The official evaluation timeline starts the day the district receives your signed consent.

*Tip: Keep copies of everything you submit and receive. A simple folder or binder dedicated to your child's special education paperwork can be incredibly helpful.*

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas has specific timelines that Friendswood ISD must follow:

| Step | Timeline |
|------|----------|
| District response to your request | Within **15 school days** of receiving your written request |
| Completion of the FIE | Within **45 school days** of receiving signed parental consent |
| ARD/IEP meeting to review results | Within **30 calendar days** of completing the FIE |

**Important notes about the 45-school-day timeline:**
- School days do not include days when school is closed (summer break, winter break, spring break, holidays)
- If your child is absent for an extended period, this may affect the timeline
- If you consent to the evaluation within 35 school days before the end of the school year, the district may have additional time, but they must still complete it as quickly as possible

If you believe Friendswood ISD is not meeting these legal timelines, you have the right to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

After the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and filled with technical language, but here's what to look for:

**Background Information**
A summary of your child's history, including developmental milestones, medical history, and educational background.

**Assessment Results**
Detailed results from each test administered, often including standard scores, percentile ranks, and descriptions of what the scores mean.

**Observations**
Notes from classroom observations and how your child behaved during testing.

**Summary and Conclusions**
A synthesis of all the data that explains your child's strengths and challenges.

**Eligibility Determination**
A statement about whether your child meets criteria for one of the 13 disability categories in Texas.

**Recommendations**
Suggestions for educational programming, though final decisions are made at the ARD meeting.

**Tips for reviewing the FIE report:**
- Request the report at least a few days before the ARD meeting so you have time to review it
- Highlight areas you don't understand and bring questions to the meeting
- Focus on the "Summary" and "Recommendations" sections first
- Remember: scores are just one piece of the puzzle

You have the right to have the FIE report explained to you in a way you can understand. Don't hesitate to ask questions.
