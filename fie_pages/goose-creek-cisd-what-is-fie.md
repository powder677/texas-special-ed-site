# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Goose Creek CISD Parents

If you're a parent in Goose Creek CISD and you've been hearing terms like "FIE" or "Full Individual Evaluation," you're not alone—and you've come to the right place. Whether your child is struggling in school, a teacher has expressed concerns, or you simply want to understand your options, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know about the FIE process in your district. Serving the Baytown area and surrounding communities with approximately 23,810 students, Goose Creek CISD follows Texas state guidelines to evaluate children who may need special education support. Let's break it down together.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation—commonly called a FIE—is a comprehensive assessment that determines whether your child has a disability and needs special education services. Think of it as a detailed portrait of your child's strengths, challenges, and unique learning needs.

In Texas, a FIE is sometimes referred to as a "psychoeducational evaluation." It's required by federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas Education Code Chapter 29 before any child can receive special education services.

The FIE isn't just one test—it's a collection of assessments, observations, and information-gathering designed to understand your whole child. Qualified professionals, such as licensed specialists in school psychology (LSSPs), educational diagnosticians, and speech-language pathologists, conduct these evaluations.

The goal? To give your child's school team (and you!) the information needed to provide the right support.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Goose Creek CISD?

Every child learns differently, and some children face challenges that require specialized instruction or support. At Goose Creek CISD, the FIE serves as the gateway to special education services. Without a completed FIE, the district cannot determine eligibility or create an Individualized Education Program (IEP) for your child.

Here's why the FIE matters for families in your community:

- **It identifies your child's specific needs.** Rather than guessing what might help, the FIE provides data-driven insights.
- **It's legally required.** Texas law mandates that districts conduct a FIE before providing special education services.
- **It empowers you as a parent.** The evaluation gives you concrete information to advocate for your child.
- **It ensures appropriate services.** The results guide the creation of a plan tailored to your child—not a one-size-fits-all approach.

For families across the Houston region, including those in Baytown, Highlands, and surrounding Goose Creek CISD neighborhoods, understanding the FIE process is the first step toward getting your child the help they deserve.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE looks at many different areas of your child's development and learning. The specific assessments depend on your child's suspected disability and individual concerns, but typically include:

### Cognitive Ability
How your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems. This is often measured through standardized intelligence tests.

### Academic Achievement
Your child's current performance in reading, writing, math, and other academic areas compared to grade-level expectations.

### Communication Skills
How well your child understands and uses language, including speech, listening, and social communication.

### Social and Emotional Functioning
Your child's behavior, emotional regulation, social skills, and mental health needs.

### Adaptive Behavior
How your child handles everyday life skills like self-care, safety awareness, and independence.

### Motor Skills
Fine motor abilities (like handwriting) and gross motor skills (like balance and coordination), if concerns exist.

### Health and Developmental History
Information about your child's medical background, developmental milestones, and any relevant family history.

The evaluation team will gather information from multiple sources—including you! Parent input is a critical part of the FIE process.

## How to Request a FIE at Goose Creek CISD

As a parent, you have the right to request a FIE for your child at any time. You don't need to wait for the school to suggest it. Here's how to make your request in Goose Creek CISD:

### Step 1: Put It in Writing
Write a letter or email to your child's principal or the district's Special Education Department requesting a Full Individual Evaluation. Be specific about your concerns.

**Sample language:**
*"I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [child's name], who attends [school name]. I am concerned about [briefly describe your concerns—for example, reading difficulties, attention issues, social challenges]. Please consider this my written consent to begin the evaluation process."*

### Step 2: Submit Your Request
You can deliver your request by:
- Email to your child's principal or campus special education contact
- Hand-delivery to the front office (ask for a date-stamped copy)
- Mail to the Goose Creek CISD Special Education Department

### Step 3: Keep Records
Save a copy of your request and note the date you submitted it. This is important because Texas law sets strict timelines once a request is received.

### Step 4: Respond to Consent Forms
After the district receives your request, they will send you paperwork asking for your written consent to evaluate. Review it carefully, sign it, and return it promptly—the timeline doesn't officially begin until the district receives your signed consent.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas takes timelines seriously. Under the Texas Education Code and IDEA, Goose Creek CISD must complete the FIE within specific deadlines:

- **Within 15 school days** of receiving your written request, the district must either provide you with a consent form to sign OR provide written notice explaining why they are refusing to evaluate.
- **Within 45 school days** of receiving your signed consent, the district must complete the FIE and provide you with a copy of the evaluation report.

**Important notes:**
- "School days" means days when school is in session—summer break, holidays, and closures don't count.
- If your child is absent for an extended period, the timeline may be adjusted.
- You can agree in writing to extend the timeline if needed, but the district cannot pressure you to do so.

If Goose Creek CISD misses these deadlines without a valid reason, you have the right to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

## Understanding Your FIE Report

Once the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and filled with technical terms, but don't be intimidated—it's your right to understand it fully.

### What the Report Includes:
- **Background information:** Your child's history, including health, development, and educational experiences.
- **Assessment results:** Scores and descriptions from each test administered.
- **Observations:** Notes from classroom observations and interactions with your child.
- **Summary and interpretation:** What the results mean in plain language.
- **Eligibility determination:** Whether your child meets criteria for one or more disability categories under IDEA.
- **Recommendations:** Suggestions for services, supports, or accommodations.

### Tips for Reading the Report:
- **Ask for a pre-meeting.** You can request to receive the FIE report before the ARD meeting so you have time to review it.
- **Highlight questions.** Write down anything you don't understand to ask the evaluation team.
- **Focus on recommendations.** This section often contains the most actionable information.
- **Request clarification.** You have the right to ask evaluators to explain their findings in parent-friendly language.

Remember, this report belongs to you. You can share it with outside professionals, tutors, or advocates if you choose.

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

After the FIE is complete, Goose Creek CISD will schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. In Texas, this is what other states might call an IEP meeting.

### Purpose of the ARD Meeting:
- Review the FIE results as a team
- Determine if your child qualifies for special education under one of the 13 IDEA disability categories
- If eligible, develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) outlining goals, services, and supports

### Who Attends:
- You (and anyone you invite, such as a family member or advocate)
- A district representative
- Your child's teacher(s)
- The professionals who conducted the evaluation
- Other specialists as needed

### Your Role:
You are an equal member of the ARD committee. Your input matters. You can ask questions, share concerns, and disagree with recommendations. In Texas, ARD decisions should be made by consensus whenever possible.
