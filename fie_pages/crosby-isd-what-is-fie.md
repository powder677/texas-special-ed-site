# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Crosby ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Crosby ISD and you've been hearing terms like "FIE" or "Full Individual Evaluation" at school meetings, you're not alone. Many families in our close-knit community between Houston and Baytown find themselves navigating the special education process for the first time. Whether your child is struggling in the classroom, has been identified with a possible learning difference, or you simply want to understand what resources are available, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know about the FIE process at Crosby ISD.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A FIE, or Full Individual Evaluation, is Texas's comprehensive assessment process used to determine whether a child qualifies for special education services. You might also hear it called a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation."

Think of a FIE as a detailed portrait of your child's unique strengths, challenges, and learning needs. It's not a single test but rather a collection of assessments, observations, and information gathered from multiple sources—including you, the parent.

Under both the federal Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and the Texas Education Code Chapter 29, school districts like Crosby ISD must complete a FIE before a child can receive special education services. This ensures that every child's needs are carefully understood before decisions are made about their education.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Crosby ISD?

At Crosby ISD, the FIE serves as the foundation for all special education decisions. Without this evaluation, the district cannot determine whether your child qualifies for special education services or what supports they might need.

Here's why the FIE matters for your family:

- **It identifies your child's specific needs.** Every child learns differently, and the FIE helps pinpoint exactly where your child needs support.
- **It's required by law.** Texas law mandates that districts conduct a FIE before providing special education services, protecting your child's right to an appropriate evaluation.
- **It guides educational planning.** The results of the FIE directly inform your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP), ensuring that goals and services match their actual needs.
- **It ensures your child isn't overlooked.** With approximately 6,680 students in Crosby ISD, the FIE process helps ensure that every child who needs support receives individualized attention.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE looks at your child from multiple angles to create a complete picture. Depending on your child's suspected needs, the evaluation may include:

**Cognitive/Intellectual Assessment**
This measures how your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems. It helps identify intellectual strengths and areas where your child may need additional support.

**Academic Achievement**
These assessments examine your child's skills in reading, writing, math, and other academic areas compared to grade-level expectations.

**Speech and Language Skills**
If there are concerns about communication, the FIE may include assessments of how your child understands and uses language.

**Social and Emotional Functioning**
This looks at your child's behavior, emotional well-being, social skills, and how they interact with peers and adults.

**Adaptive Behavior**
These assessments examine daily living skills and how independently your child can function in various settings.

**Motor Skills**
If there are concerns about fine motor skills (like writing) or gross motor skills (like running or coordination), these may be evaluated.

**Health and Developmental History**
Your input about your child's medical history, developmental milestones, and home life is a crucial part of the FIE.

The specific assessments included in your child's FIE will depend on the areas of concern. Texas law requires that the evaluation be comprehensive enough to identify all of your child's special education needs.

## How to Request a FIE at Crosby ISD

You have the right to request a FIE for your child at any time. Here's how to do it at Crosby ISD:

**Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing**

While you can make a verbal request, a written request creates documentation and starts the official timeline. Address your letter to the principal of your child's school or the Crosby ISD Special Education Department.

Your letter should include:
- Your child's full name and date of birth
- Your child's school and grade
- A clear statement that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE)
- A brief description of your concerns about your child's learning or development
- Your contact information
- The date

**Step 2: Submit Your Request**

Deliver your request to your child's school or the district's special education office. Keep a copy for your records, and consider sending it by email so you have a timestamp, or requesting a receipt if delivered in person.

**Step 3: Wait for the District's Response**

Within 15 school days of receiving your request, Crosby ISD must either provide you with a "Prior Written Notice" agreeing to evaluate your child along with consent forms, or provide you with a Prior Written Notice explaining why they are declining to evaluate.

**Step 4: Give Written Consent**

If the district agrees to evaluate, you'll receive consent forms. The FIE cannot begin until you sign and return these forms. Take time to review them and ask questions if anything is unclear.

**Sample Request Letter:**

*Dear [Principal's Name or Special Education Director],*

*I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [Child's Name], who is in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned about [briefly describe your concerns—for example, "his difficulty with reading" or "her struggles with attention and completing classwork"].*

*Please contact me to discuss next steps. I can be reached at [phone number] or [email address].*

*Thank you for your attention to this request.*

*Sincerely,*
*[Your Name]*
*[Date]*

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas has specific legal timelines that Crosby ISD must follow:

| Milestone | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| District response to your request | Within **15 school days** |
| You review and sign consent forms | Take the time you need (timeline pauses until consent is given) |
| FIE completion | Within **45 school days** of receiving your signed consent |
| ARD meeting to review results | Within **30 calendar days** of FIE completion |

**Important notes about the timeline:**

- The 45-school-day clock does not include days when school is not in session for more than 5 consecutive days (like summer break or winter break).
- If your child is under 5 years old, the timeline is 45 calendar days instead of school days.
- These timelines are set by the Texas Education Code and cannot be extended without your written agreement.

If Crosby ISD does not meet these deadlines, you have the right to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

After the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and technical, but understanding it is key to advocating for your child.

**What the report includes:**

- **Background information:** Your child's history, including developmental, medical, and educational background
- **Assessment results:** Scores and explanations for each test administered
- **Observations:** Notes from evaluators about your child's behavior during testing
- **Interpretation:** What the scores and observations mean for your child
- **Eligibility determination:** Whether your child meets criteria for one or more disability categories under IDEA
- **Recommendations:** Suggested supports, services, or accommodations

**Tips for understanding the report:**

- **Request a copy before the ARD meeting.** Texas law gives you the right to receive the FIE report before the meeting where it will be discussed.
- **Don't hesitate to ask questions.** The school psychologist or diagnostician who conducted the evaluation should be available to explain results in plain language.
- **Look beyond the numbers.** While test scores matter, the observations and recommendations often provide the most useful guidance.
- **Consider the whole picture.** A single score doesn't define your child. Look at patterns across multiple assessments.

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

Once the FIE is complete, Crosby ISD will schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. In Texas, this is what other states might call an IEP meeting.

**At the ARD meeting, the team will:**

1. **Review the FIE results** together, with explanations from the evaluation team
2. **Determine eligibility** for special education services
3. **Identify your child's needs** based on the evaluation
4. **Develop an IEP** (Individualized Education Program) if your child qualifies
