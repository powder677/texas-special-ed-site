# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Temple ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Temple ISD and someone has mentioned your child might need a "FIE," you're probably wondering what that means and what to expect. You're not alone—many families in our Central Texas community have walked this path before you. Whether your child is struggling in the classroom, showing signs of a learning difference, or facing other challenges at school, understanding the FIE process is an important first step toward getting them the support they deserve. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about the FIE process specifically within Temple Independent School District.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A FIE, or Full Individual Evaluation, is Texas's comprehensive assessment process used to determine whether a child qualifies for special education services. You might also hear it called a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation."

Think of a FIE as a detailed portrait of your child as a learner. It looks at how your child thinks, learns, processes information, and functions in an educational setting. The evaluation examines your child's unique strengths alongside any areas where they may need additional support.

Under both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and the Texas Education Code Chapter 29, school districts like Temple ISD must conduct a FIE before they can provide special education services to any student. This evaluation is provided at no cost to you—the school district covers all expenses associated with the assessment.

A FIE isn't just a single test. It's a collection of assessments, observations, interviews, and reviews conducted by qualified professionals who are trained to understand how children learn and develop.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Temple ISD?

The FIE serves several critical purposes for Temple ISD students and their families:

**It opens the door to services.** Without a completed FIE, your child cannot be found eligible for special education services. The evaluation provides the formal documentation required by law to access specialized instruction, accommodations, and related services.

**It creates a complete picture of your child.** Temple ISD's evaluation team gathers information from multiple sources and perspectives to understand your child as a whole person—not just their challenges, but their gifts and capabilities too.

**It guides educational planning.** The results of the FIE don't just determine eligibility; they also inform the creation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP). The evaluation helps teachers and specialists understand exactly how to support your child's learning.

**It establishes a baseline.** The FIE documents your child's current levels of performance, which helps the team track progress over time and adjust services as needed.

**It ensures appropriate placement.** Texas law requires that students be placed in the least restrictive environment appropriate for their needs. The FIE helps determine what that looks like for your individual child within Temple ISD's schools.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is thorough and individualized, meaning it's tailored to your child's specific concerns. However, most evaluations include several key components:

### Cognitive/Intellectual Assessment
This portion measures how your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems. It looks at areas like verbal comprehension, visual-spatial reasoning, working memory, and processing speed.

### Academic Achievement
These assessments examine your child's current performance in reading, writing, and mathematics compared to grade-level expectations.

### Adaptive Behavior
This component looks at your child's daily living skills and how independently they can function in various settings—at home, at school, and in the community.

### Social-Emotional and Behavioral Assessment
Evaluators may assess your child's emotional well-being, behavior patterns, social skills, and how they interact with peers and adults.

### Speech and Language
If there are concerns about communication, the evaluation may include assessments of how your child understands and uses language.

### Motor Skills
For some children, assessments of fine motor skills (like handwriting) and gross motor skills (like coordination and balance) may be included.

### Health and Developmental History
The evaluation team will review your child's medical history, developmental milestones, and any relevant health information you provide.

### Observations
A member of the evaluation team will observe your child in their natural school environment to see how they function in the classroom.

### Parent and Teacher Input
Your insights as a parent are invaluable. You know your child better than anyone. The team will also gather information from your child's teachers and other school staff who work with them regularly.

The specific assessments included in your child's FIE will depend on the concerns being investigated. For example, a child suspected of having a specific learning disability will have different assessments than a child who may have autism spectrum disorder.

## How to Request a FIE at Temple ISD

Any parent or guardian can request a FIE for their child at any time. You don't need to wait for the school to suggest it, and you don't need anyone's permission. Here's how to make your request in Temple ISD:

### Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing

While you can make a verbal request, we strongly recommend submitting your request in writing. This creates a paper trail and starts the legal timeline. Your written request can be a simple letter or email that includes:

- Your child's full name
- Your child's date of birth and current grade
- Your child's school and teacher(s)
- A brief description of your concerns
- A clear statement that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation
- The date of your request
- Your contact information

### Step 2: Submit Your Request

You can submit your written request to any of the following:

- **Your child's teacher**
- **Your child's school principal**
- **Temple ISD Special Education Department**

Keep a copy of your request for your records, and note the date you submitted it. If you send it by email, save the sent message. If you hand-deliver it, ask for a date-stamped copy.

### Sample Request Language

*"I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [Child's Name], who is currently in [grade] at [school name]. I have concerns about [briefly describe concerns, such as 'reading difficulties' or 'social-emotional challenges']. Please contact me at [phone/email] to discuss next steps. Thank you for your attention to this request."*

### Step 3: Watch for the District's Response

After receiving your request, Temple ISD must respond within 15 school days. They will either:

1. **Agree to evaluate** and send you a "Notice and Consent for Full Individual Evaluation" form to sign, OR
2. **Decline to evaluate** and provide you with written notice explaining their decision (called "Prior Written Notice")

If the district agrees to evaluate, they will also send you a copy of your procedural safeguards—a document explaining your rights throughout the special education process.

If Temple ISD declines your request, you have the right to challenge that decision. The notice they provide must explain how you can do so.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law establishes clear deadlines for the FIE process. Understanding these timelines helps you know what to expect and ensures the district stays on track:

### 15 School Days to Respond
From the date Temple ISD receives your written request, they have 15 school days to provide you with the consent form (or prior written notice if they decline).

### 45 School Days to Complete the Evaluation
Once you sign and return the consent form, Temple ISD has 45 school days to complete the FIE and hold the initial ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to discuss the results.

**Important notes about the 45-day timeline:**

- "School days" means days when school is in session and students are present
- The timeline does not include days when school is closed for holidays or breaks
- Summer days typically do not count toward the 45 days unless summer school is in session
- The clock starts the day after the district receives your signed consent

### Exceptions to the Timeline

There are limited circumstances when the 45-day timeline may be extended:

- If your child is absent for an extended period
- If your child transfers to Temple ISD from another district during the evaluation process (special rules apply)
- If you and the district mutually agree to an extension in writing

If you believe Temple ISD has missed a deadline, you have the right to follow up and, if necessary, file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency.
