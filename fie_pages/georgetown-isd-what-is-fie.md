# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Georgetown ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Georgetown ISD and your child is struggling in school, you may have heard teachers or administrators mention something called a "FIE." Whether your child is having difficulty reading, struggling with behavior, falling behind their peers, or facing other challenges, understanding the FIE process is an important first step toward getting them the support they need. This guide is designed specifically for Georgetown ISD families to help you navigate this process with confidence. With approximately 13,670 students across the district, Georgetown ISD serves a diverse community, and the special education team is here to help identify and support students who may need additional services.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation—commonly called a FIE—is Texas's comprehensive assessment process used to determine whether a child qualifies for special education services. You might also hear it referred to as a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation."

The FIE is not a single test. Instead, it's a collection of assessments, observations, and information-gathering activities designed to paint a complete picture of your child's strengths, challenges, and educational needs. This evaluation looks at many different areas of your child's development and learning to help the school understand how they can best be supported.

Under both the federal Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and the Texas Education Code Chapter 29, a school district must conduct a FIE before a child can receive special education services. This ensures that every child who needs extra support is properly identified and that services are tailored to their individual needs.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Georgetown ISD?

For Georgetown ISD families, the FIE serves several critical purposes:

**It identifies your child's unique needs.** Every child learns differently, and the FIE helps pinpoint exactly where your child may need additional support—whether that's in reading, math, speech, behavior, social skills, or another area.

**It determines eligibility for special education.** Texas recognizes 13 different disability categories under which a child may qualify for special education services. The FIE provides the data needed to determine if your child meets the criteria for one or more of these categories.

**It guides the development of services.** If your child qualifies for special education, the information gathered during the FIE directly informs their Individualized Education Program (IEP). This means the supports and services your child receives will be specifically designed to address their needs.

**It creates a baseline.** The FIE establishes where your child is right now, which helps teachers and specialists track progress over time and adjust supports as needed.

At Georgetown ISD, the special education team uses the FIE process to ensure that students receive appropriate, individualized support within the district's schools and programs.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is comprehensive by design. While the specific assessments used will depend on your child's suspected areas of need, a typical FIE at Georgetown ISD may include evaluation in the following areas:

**Cognitive Ability (Intelligence)**
This assesses how your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems. It helps identify their overall learning potential and any differences between their abilities and academic performance.

**Academic Achievement**
These assessments measure your child's current skills in reading, writing, and math compared to grade-level expectations.

**Speech and Language**
If there are concerns about how your child communicates, understands language, or expresses themselves, speech-language assessments may be included.

**Social and Emotional Functioning**
This looks at your child's emotional well-being, behavior, social skills, and how they interact with peers and adults.

**Adaptive Behavior**
These assessments evaluate daily living skills and how independently your child can function in various settings.

**Motor Skills**
If there are concerns about fine motor skills (like writing or cutting) or gross motor skills (like running or climbing), occupational or physical therapy assessments may be conducted.

**Health and Developmental History**
The evaluation team will gather information about your child's medical history, developmental milestones, and any previous interventions or therapies.

**Observation and Input**
The FIE process also includes classroom observations and input from you as the parent, your child's teachers, and other relevant individuals who know your child well.

The evaluation team will select assessments based on your child's specific areas of concern, so not every FIE looks exactly the same.

## How to Request a FIE at Georgetown ISD

As a parent, you have the right to request a FIE at any time if you suspect your child may have a disability that affects their learning. Here's how to make that request in Georgetown ISD:

**Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing**

While you can make a verbal request, we strongly recommend submitting a written request. This creates documentation and starts the official timeline. Your letter should include:
- Your child's name and date of birth
- Your child's school and grade
- A brief description of your concerns
- Your request for a Full Individual Evaluation
- Your contact information
- The date

**Step 2: Submit Your Request**

You can deliver your written request to:
- Your child's principal
- Your child's teacher
- The campus special education coordinator
- Georgetown ISD's Special Education Department

**Step 3: Wait for the District's Response**

Once Georgetown ISD receives your written request, they have **15 school days** to respond. The district will either:
- Provide you with a "Notice and Consent for Evaluation" form to sign, OR
- Provide you with a "Prior Written Notice" explaining why they are declining to evaluate and what options you have

**Sample Request Letter:**

> Dear [Principal's Name],
>
> I am the parent of [Child's Name], who is in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned about my child's progress in [specific areas of concern, such as reading, behavior, attention, etc.].
>
> I am requesting that Georgetown ISD conduct a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) to determine whether my child is eligible for special education services.
>
> Please contact me at [phone number] or [email address] to discuss next steps.
>
> Thank you for your attention to this matter.
>
> Sincerely,
> [Your Name]
> [Date]

Keep a copy of everything you submit for your records.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law establishes clear timelines to ensure the evaluation process moves forward promptly. Here's what to expect:

**15 School Days**
After Georgetown ISD receives your written request, the district has 15 school days to either provide you with consent forms to sign or give you written notice explaining why they won't evaluate.

**45 School Days**
Once you sign and return the consent form, Georgetown ISD has 45 school days to complete the FIE and hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to review the results. This 45-day clock only counts school days, so summer breaks, holidays, and other non-school days don't count toward the deadline.

**Exceptions to the Timeline**
There are limited circumstances where the timeline may be extended, such as:
- Your child is absent from school for an extended period
- You and the district mutually agree in writing to an extension
- Your child enrolls in a new district during the evaluation process

If you feel Georgetown ISD is not meeting these timelines, you have the right to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

After the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and filled with technical language, but understanding it is important because it will guide your child's educational planning.

Here's what you'll typically find in a FIE report:

**Background Information**
This section summarizes your child's history, including developmental milestones, medical information, and educational background.

**Assessment Results**
Each assessment administered will have its own section with scores and explanations. You'll see terms like "standard scores," "percentiles," and "age equivalents." Don't hesitate to ask the evaluation team to explain what these numbers mean for your child.

**Observations**
The report will include observations of your child during testing and possibly during classroom activities.

**Conclusions and Recommendations**
This section interprets what all the data means and provides recommendations for how to support your child.

**Eligibility Determination**
The report will indicate whether the evaluation team believes your child meets the criteria for one of Texas's 13 disability categories.

**Tips for Reviewing the Report:**
- Request a copy of the FIE report before the ARD meeting so you have time to review it
- Highlight anything you don't understand and prepare questions
- Consider bringing a trusted friend, advocate, or family member to help you process the information
- Remember that you are an expert on your child, and your input matters
