# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Dickinson ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Dickinson ISD and you've been told your child might need a Full Individual Evaluation—or you're wondering if your child should be evaluated for special education services—you're in the right place. Serving approximately 12,340 students across the Dickinson community, Dickinson ISD works with families every day to identify and support students with unique learning needs. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about the FIE process, your rights as a parent, and how to advocate for your child every step of the way.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation, commonly called a FIE (pronounced "fee"), is a comprehensive assessment that Texas school districts use to determine whether a child has a disability and qualifies for special education services. You might also hear it referred to as a "psychoeducational evaluation."

Think of the FIE as a detailed look at your child as a whole person—not just their academic performance, but also how they think, learn, communicate, and interact with others. The evaluation is conducted by a team of qualified professionals, which may include school psychologists, educational diagnosticians, speech-language pathologists, and other specialists depending on your child's suspected needs.

Under both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and the Texas Education Code Chapter 29, school districts like Dickinson ISD are required to conduct a FIE before a child can begin receiving special education services. This ensures that every child's needs are properly identified and that the right supports are put in place.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Dickinson ISD?

The FIE is the foundation for everything that follows in your child's special education journey at Dickinson ISD. Here's why it matters so much:

- **It identifies your child's unique strengths and challenges.** The FIE helps paint a complete picture of how your child learns best and where they may need additional support.

- **It determines eligibility for special education.** Texas law requires a FIE before a student can be found eligible for services under one of the 13 disability categories recognized by IDEA.

- **It guides the creation of your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP).** The results of the FIE directly inform the goals, accommodations, and services that will be included in your child's educational plan.

- **It ensures your child receives appropriate services.** Without a thorough evaluation, it's impossible to know exactly what kind of help your child needs. The FIE makes sure Dickinson ISD can provide targeted, effective support.

For families in the Dickinson community, the FIE is your child's ticket to accessing the specialized instruction and related services they deserve.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE looks at multiple areas of your child's development and functioning. The specific assessments included will depend on your child's suspected disability, but a comprehensive FIE typically evaluates:

- **Cognitive ability (intelligence):** How your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems
- **Academic achievement:** Your child's skills in reading, writing, math, and other academic areas
- **Communication skills:** How well your child understands and uses language
- **Social and emotional development:** Your child's behavior, emotional regulation, and social interactions
- **Adaptive behavior:** Daily living skills and how your child functions independently
- **Motor skills:** Fine motor (writing, cutting) and gross motor (running, jumping) abilities, if concerns exist
- **Health and developmental history:** Medical information that may impact learning
- **Observation:** How your child behaves and learns in the classroom setting
- **Parent and teacher input:** Valuable insights from the people who know your child best

The evaluation team will review existing data, conduct formal assessments, observe your child, and gather information from you and your child's teachers. All of these pieces come together to create a complete picture of your child's needs.

## How to Request a FIE at Dickinson ISD

As a parent, you have the right to request a FIE at any time if you suspect your child may have a disability that affects their learning. Here's how to do it in Dickinson ISD:

**Step 1: Put your request in writing.**

While you can make a verbal request, it's always best to submit your request in writing. This creates a paper trail and starts the clock on the district's legal timeline to respond. You can email or hand-deliver a letter to your child's school principal or the Dickinson ISD Special Education Department.

**Step 2: Be specific about your concerns.**

In your letter, briefly describe why you believe your child may need an evaluation. You might mention struggles with reading, difficulty paying attention, speech delays, social challenges, or any other concerns you've observed at home or that teachers have reported.

**Step 3: Keep a copy for your records.**

Always keep a copy of your written request and note the date you submitted it. This will be important if you need to track the district's timeline later.

**Sample Request Letter:**

> Dear [Principal's Name],
>
> I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [Child's Name], who is in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned about [briefly describe your concerns, such as "difficulty with reading comprehension" or "challenges with social interactions and emotional regulation"].
>
> I understand that Dickinson ISD has 15 school days to respond to this request. Please contact me to discuss next steps.
>
> Thank you for your attention to this matter.
>
> Sincerely,
> [Your Name]
> [Your Contact Information]

Once Dickinson ISD receives your written request, they have **15 school days** to respond by either providing you with a Notice of Proposed Evaluation and consent forms or a written explanation of why they are refusing to evaluate.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law sets clear deadlines for the FIE process to ensure that families don't have to wait too long for answers. Here's what you need to know:

| Step | Timeline |
|------|----------|
| District response to your request | Within **15 school days** of receiving your written request |
| Completion of the FIE | Within **45 school days** of receiving your signed consent |
| ARD meeting to review results | Within **30 calendar days** of completing the FIE |

**Important notes:**

- The 45-school-day timeline does not include days when school is not in session for more than 5 consecutive days (like summer break or winter break).
- If you and Dickinson ISD agree in writing, the timeline can be extended.
- The district cannot begin the evaluation until you have provided written consent. Don't delay returning those consent forms if you want the evaluation to move forward!

## Understanding Your FIE Report

After the evaluation is complete, you will receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and full of technical language, but don't let that intimidate you. Here's a quick guide to understanding what you'll find:

**Sections you'll typically see:**

- **Background information:** Your child's history, including developmental milestones, medical history, and previous educational experiences
- **Assessment results:** Scores from the various tests administered, often presented as standard scores, percentiles, or age/grade equivalents
- **Observations:** Notes from classroom observations and interactions during testing
- **Summary of findings:** A clear explanation of your child's strengths and areas of need
- **Eligibility determination:** The team's recommendation about whether your child meets the criteria for one or more disability categories under IDEA
- **Recommendations:** Suggested next steps, including types of services or supports that may benefit your child

**Tips for reviewing the report:**

- Ask for a copy of the report **before** the ARD meeting so you have time to read it carefully
- Write down any questions or areas of confusion
- Don't hesitate to ask the school psychologist or diagnostician to explain anything you don't understand
- Remember: you know your child best. If something in the report doesn't match what you see at home, speak up!

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

Once the FIE is complete, Dickinson ISD will schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. In Texas, the ARD committee is the team that makes decisions about your child's special education services. You are a vital member of this team!

**At the ARD meeting, the committee will:**

1. **Review the FIE results** and discuss what they mean for your child
2. **Determine eligibility** for special education services under one of the 13 IDEA disability categories
3. **Develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP)** if your child is found eligible, including goals, services, and accommodations
4. **Discuss placement** and where your child will receive services
