# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Grand Prairie ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Grand Prairie ISD and you've been hearing terms like "FIE" or "evaluation" from your child's school, you're in the right place. Whether your child is struggling academically, socially, or behaviorally, understanding the Full Individual Evaluation process is your first step toward getting them the support they need. Grand Prairie ISD serves a diverse community of over 26,000 students across the DFW metroplex, and the district has dedicated staff to help guide you through this process. This page will walk you through everything you need to know about the FIE—what it is, why it matters, and how to navigate it as a Grand Prairie ISD parent.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation, commonly called a FIE, is a comprehensive assessment that determines whether your child has a disability and needs special education services. In Texas, this evaluation is required before any child can receive special education support through the school district.

You might also hear it called a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation." The FIE looks at your whole child—not just academics—to understand their unique strengths, challenges, and needs.

The FIE is mandated by federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas state law (Texas Education Code Chapter 29). This means Grand Prairie ISD must follow specific guidelines and timelines when evaluating your child, and you have important rights throughout the process.

Think of the FIE as a detailed portrait of how your child learns, processes information, and interacts with their environment. It's the foundation for creating an educational plan that truly fits your child.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Grand Prairie ISD?

The FIE serves as the gateway to special education services at Grand Prairie ISD. Without this evaluation, the district cannot determine if your child qualifies for an Individualized Education Program (IEP) and the specialized support that comes with it.

Here's why the FIE matters for your family:

- **It identifies your child's specific needs.** Rather than guessing what might help, the FIE provides data-driven insights into how your child learns best.
- **It's required by law.** Grand Prairie ISD cannot place your child in special education without completing a FIE first.
- **It informs the IEP.** The evaluation results directly shape the goals, accommodations, and services your child will receive.
- **It establishes a baseline.** The FIE documents your child's current abilities, which helps track progress over time.
- **It ensures appropriate placement.** The evaluation helps determine the right educational setting and level of support.

For Grand Prairie ISD families, the FIE process connects you with district specialists who understand local resources and can help ensure your child thrives in their school community.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is truly comprehensive—it examines multiple areas of your child's development and functioning. While the specific assessments vary based on your child's suspected disability and concerns, the evaluation typically covers:

**Cognitive Ability**
This measures how your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems. It's often what people think of as an "IQ test," but it's much more nuanced than a single number.

**Academic Achievement**
These assessments look at your child's skills in reading, writing, and math compared to grade-level expectations.

**Communication Skills**
If there are concerns about speech or language, the FIE will examine how your child understands and uses language.

**Social and Emotional Functioning**
This area explores your child's behavior, emotional regulation, social skills, and mental health.

**Adaptive Behavior**
These assessments examine daily living skills and how independently your child functions in various environments.

**Motor Skills**
If relevant, fine motor skills (like writing and cutting) and gross motor skills (like running and jumping) may be assessed.

**Health and Developmental History**
The evaluation team will review your child's medical background, developmental milestones, and any relevant health factors.

The FIE also includes observations of your child in their classroom and input from you as the parent. Your perspective is invaluable—you know your child better than anyone.

## How to Request a FIE at Grand Prairie ISD

You have the right to request a FIE at any time if you believe your child may have a disability affecting their education. Here's how to do it in Grand Prairie ISD:

**Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing**
While you can make a verbal request, a written request creates documentation and starts the official timeline. Address your letter to your child's principal or the Grand Prairie ISD Special Education Department.

**Step 2: Include Key Information**
Your request should include:
- Your child's full name and date of birth
- Your child's school and grade
- Your specific concerns about your child's learning or development
- Your contact information
- The date of your request

**Step 3: Submit Your Request**
You can deliver your letter by hand (keep a copy for yourself), send it via email, or mail it to the school or district office. If you hand-deliver it, ask for a date-stamped copy as your receipt.

**Step 4: Wait for the District's Response**
Grand Prairie ISD must respond to your request within 15 school days. They will either agree to evaluate your child and send you a consent form, or they will deny your request with a written explanation.

**Sample Request Letter:**

*Dear [Principal's Name],*

*I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [Child's Name], who is in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned about [briefly describe your concerns—examples: difficulty reading at grade level, trouble focusing in class, social challenges with peers].*

*I understand that Grand Prairie ISD has 15 school days to respond to this request. Please contact me at [phone] or [email] to discuss next steps.*

*Thank you for your attention to this matter.*

*Sincerely,*
*[Your Name]*
*[Date]*

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law establishes clear timelines to ensure evaluations happen promptly. Here's what you need to know:

**15 School Days**
After receiving your written request, Grand Prairie ISD has 15 school days to either provide you with a consent form to begin the evaluation or give you written notice explaining why they're declining to evaluate.

**45 School Days**
Once you sign and return the consent form, the district has 45 school days to complete the FIE and hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to review the results.

**Important Notes About the Timeline:**
- The 45-day clock starts when the district receives your signed consent
- School days don't include holidays, weekends, or summer break (unless your child attends summer school)
- If your child is absent for an extended period, it may pause the timeline
- The timeline can only be extended if both you and the district agree in writing

If Grand Prairie ISD misses these deadlines without a valid reason, you have the right to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

## Understanding Your FIE Report

After the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and full of technical language, but understanding it is important. Here's what to look for:

**Background Information**
This section summarizes your child's history, including developmental milestones, medical information, and educational background.

**Assessment Results**
For each area tested, you'll see scores and descriptions. These often include:
- *Standard scores* (average is typically 100)
- *Percentile ranks* (showing how your child compares to same-age peers)
- *Age or grade equivalents*

**Observations**
The evaluators will describe what they noticed about your child during testing and classroom observations.

**Summary and Conclusions**
This section pulls everything together and explains whether your child meets criteria for a disability category under IDEA.

**Recommendations**
The report should include suggestions for supporting your child's learning.

**Tips for Reviewing the Report:**
- Request a copy before the ARD meeting so you have time to read it
- Highlight sections you don't understand and bring questions to the meeting
- Focus on the recommendations—these inform your child's IEP
- Remember that you can request clarification from the evaluation team

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

Once the FIE is complete, Grand Prairie ISD will schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. This is where the evaluation results are reviewed and decisions are made about your child's eligibility and services.

**Who Attends the ARD Meeting?**
- You (and anyone you choose to bring for support)
- A general education teacher
- A special education teacher
- A district representative
- Someone who can interpret evaluation results
- Related service providers
