# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Lubbock ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Lubbock ISD and you've been hearing terms like "FIE" or "Full Individual Evaluation," you're not alone—and you've come to the right place. With nearly 24,329 students across the district, Lubbock ISD serves a diverse community of learners, and many families each year navigate the special education evaluation process for the first time. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about the FIE process, your rights as a parent, and how to advocate effectively for your child in the South Plains region.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A **Full Individual Evaluation (FIE)** is Texas's comprehensive assessment process used to determine whether a child qualifies for special education services. Think of it as a detailed "snapshot" of your child—looking at how they learn, process information, communicate, and function in a school setting.

The FIE is sometimes called a "psychoeducational evaluation" and is required by both federal law (the **Individuals with Disabilities Education Act**, or IDEA) and state law (the **Texas Education Code, Chapter 29**) before any child can receive special education services.

The FIE isn't just a single test. It's a collection of assessments, observations, and information-gathering conducted by a team of qualified professionals. The goal is to understand your child's unique strengths and challenges so the school can provide the right support.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Lubbock ISD?

At Lubbock ISD, a FIE serves as the foundation for your child's entire special education journey. Without a completed FIE, the district cannot determine whether your child is eligible for special education services or develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

The FIE matters because it:

- **Identifies your child's specific learning needs** rather than relying on assumptions
- **Determines eligibility** for one or more of the 13 disability categories recognized under IDEA
- **Provides data** that shapes your child's educational goals and accommodations
- **Creates a baseline** for measuring your child's progress over time
- **Ensures legal protections** for your child under federal and state law

For Lubbock ISD families, having a thorough FIE means your child's teachers, specialists, and support staff will have the information they need to help your child succeed.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

The FIE looks at multiple areas of your child's development and functioning. While the exact assessments vary based on your child's suspected disability, a typical FIE may evaluate:

**Cognitive Ability (Intelligence)**
How your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems.

**Academic Achievement**
Your child's current skill levels in reading, writing, math, and other academic areas.

**Communication Skills**
How your child understands and uses language, both spoken and written.

**Social and Emotional Development**
Your child's behavior, emotional regulation, and social interactions.

**Adaptive Behavior**
Daily living skills and how your child functions independently.

**Motor Skills**
Fine motor skills (like writing) and gross motor skills (like walking and coordination).

**Health and Developmental History**
Medical background, developmental milestones, and any relevant health concerns.

**Observation in Natural Settings**
How your child behaves and learns in the classroom environment.

The evaluation team may include school psychologists, educational diagnosticians, speech-language pathologists, occupational therapists, and other specialists depending on your child's needs.

## How to Request a FIE at Lubbock ISD

As a parent, you have the right to request a FIE at any time if you suspect your child may have a disability that affects their learning. Here's how to make that request in Lubbock ISD:

**Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing**
While you can make a verbal request, a written request creates documentation and starts the legal timeline. Address your letter to your child's principal or the Lubbock ISD Special Education Department.

**Step 2: Be Specific About Your Concerns**
Describe what you're observing at home and what concerns you have about your child's learning or development. You don't need to diagnose—just share what you're seeing.

**Step 3: Submit and Keep a Copy**
Hand-deliver your request and ask for a date-stamped copy, or send it via email so you have a record. You can also send it by certified mail.

**Step 4: Wait for the District's Response**
Lubbock ISD must respond to your request within **15 school days**. They will either provide you with consent forms to begin the evaluation or explain in writing why they are declining to evaluate.

**Sample Request Language:**

*"Dear [Principal's Name], I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [Child's Name], who is in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned about [briefly describe concerns, such as difficulty reading, trouble focusing, social challenges, etc.]. Please send me the necessary consent forms to begin the evaluation process. Thank you, [Your Name, Date]"*

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law establishes clear timelines to ensure evaluations happen promptly. Here's what you need to know:

| Step | Timeline |
|------|----------|
| District response to your request | Within **15 school days** |
| You review and sign consent | Take the time you need to understand what you're signing |
| Evaluation completion | Within **45 school days** of receiving signed consent |
| ARD/IEP meeting | Within **30 calendar days** of completing the FIE |

**Important Notes:**
- The 45-school-day timeline does not include days when school is not in session (like summer break, winter break, or spring break).
- If your child transfers to Lubbock ISD during an evaluation, the district must work with you to establish a new timeline to ensure a prompt completion.
- These timelines are legal requirements, and Lubbock ISD is obligated to follow them.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

After the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and technical, but understanding it is essential for advocating for your child.

**Key Sections of the FIE Report:**

- **Background Information**: Your child's history, including developmental, medical, and educational background
- **Assessment Results**: Scores and interpretations from each evaluation area
- **Observations**: Notes from professionals who observed your child
- **Summary and Conclusions**: An overview of findings and what they mean
- **Eligibility Determination**: Whether your child qualifies for special education services
- **Recommendations**: Suggested supports, services, or accommodations

**Tips for Reviewing the Report:**

1. **Request the report before the ARD meeting** so you have time to review it thoroughly
2. **Highlight questions** or sections you don't understand
3. **Look up unfamiliar terms** or ask the evaluation team to explain them
4. **Compare the findings** to what you observe at home
5. **Bring your questions** to the ARD meeting

Remember, you are an expert on your child. If something in the report doesn't match what you see at home, speak up.

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

Once the FIE is complete, Lubbock ISD will schedule an **Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting**. This meeting must occur within **30 calendar days** of completing the evaluation.

At the ARD meeting, a team—including you—will:

1. **Review the FIE results** together
2. **Determine eligibility** for special education services
3. **Discuss your child's needs** based on the evaluation findings
4. **Develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP)** if your child is found eligible

**Who Attends the ARD Meeting?**

- You (the parent)
- A general education teacher
- A special education teacher
- A school district representative
- Someone who can interpret the evaluation results (often the school psychologist or diagnostician)
- Your child (when appropriate)
- Anyone else you choose to invite (advocate, family member, outside expert)

**Remember:** You are a full and equal member of the ARD committee. Your input matters, and you have the right to participate in every decision about your child's education.

## Your Rights During the FIE Process at Lubbock ISD

Texas law and IDEA provide strong protections for parents throughout the evaluation process. Here are your key rights:

**Before the Evaluation:**
- You must give **written consent** before any evaluation can begin
- You have the right to understand what assessments will be conducted
- You can ask questions about the evaluation process

**During the Evaluation:**
- Evaluations must be conducted in your child's native language when possible
