# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Hays CISD Parents

If you're a parent in Hays CISD and you've been hearing terms like "FIE" or "Full Individual Evaluation" from your child's school, you're not alone in wondering what it all means. Whether your child attends school in Kyle, Buda, or one of the many growing communities throughout Hays County, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know about the FIE process. With approximately 23,450 students across the district, Hays CISD serves a diverse community of learners—and understanding the evaluation process is your first step toward getting your child the support they deserve.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation, commonly called a FIE, is Texas's comprehensive assessment used to determine whether your child qualifies for special education services. You might also hear it referred to as a "psychoeducational evaluation," and it's one of the most important steps in the special education process.

Think of a FIE as a detailed portrait of your child's learning profile. It looks at how your child thinks, learns, processes information, and functions in school. The evaluation is conducted by a team of qualified professionals—including licensed specialists in school psychology (LSSPs), educational diagnosticians, and sometimes speech therapists or other specialists—who work together to understand your child's unique strengths and challenges.

Under both the federal Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and the Texas Education Code Chapter 29, a school district cannot provide special education services to a child without first completing a FIE. This evaluation serves as the foundation for all decisions about your child's educational needs.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Hays CISD?

At Hays CISD, the FIE serves as the gateway to special education services and supports. Here's why this evaluation matters so much for your family:

**It identifies your child's unique needs.** Every child learns differently. The FIE helps pinpoint exactly where your child excels and where they may need additional support, whether that's in reading, math, attention, social skills, or other areas.

**It determines eligibility.** Texas recognizes 13 different disability categories under which students can qualify for special education. The FIE provides the data needed to determine if your child meets the criteria for one or more of these categories.

**It guides educational planning.** The information gathered during a FIE doesn't just sit in a file—it becomes the roadmap for creating your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP). Teachers and specialists at your child's Hays CISD campus will use this information to design instruction and supports tailored specifically to your child.

**It establishes a baseline.** The FIE creates a snapshot of your child's abilities at a specific point in time, which helps the team measure progress in the years ahead.

**It's your child's ticket to services.** Simply put, without a completed FIE, Hays CISD cannot provide special education services to your child—no matter how much everyone agrees they need support.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is comprehensive by design, meaning it looks at many different areas of your child's development and functioning. While the specific assessments used will depend on your child's suspected needs, a FIE typically evaluates:

### Cognitive Ability
This measures how your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems. It's often what people think of as "IQ testing," but it's much more nuanced than a single number.

### Academic Achievement
This looks at your child's current skill levels in reading, writing, and math compared to same-age peers. It helps identify specific areas where your child may be ahead, on track, or behind.

### Processing Skills
This examines how your child takes in, organizes, and uses information. It might include:
- Visual processing (how they interpret what they see)
- Auditory processing (how they interpret what they hear)
- Processing speed (how quickly they can work with information)
- Working memory (how they hold and manipulate information in their mind)

### Adaptive Behavior
This assesses your child's practical, everyday skills—things like self-care, communication, social skills, and independence at home and school.

### Social-Emotional Functioning
This looks at your child's emotional well-being, behavior patterns, and how they interact with others.

### Communication Skills
If there are concerns about speech or language, a speech-language pathologist may evaluate your child's ability to understand and use language.

### Motor Skills
If needed, an occupational therapist or physical therapist may assess fine motor skills (like handwriting) or gross motor skills (like balance and coordination).

### Health and Developmental History
The evaluation team will also gather information about your child's medical history, developmental milestones, and any previous evaluations or diagnoses.

The FIE team selects specific assessments based on the areas of concern identified for your child. Not every child will be evaluated in every area—the evaluation should be tailored to your child's suspected disability and individual needs.

## How to Request a FIE at Hays CISD

As a parent, you have the right to request a FIE at any time if you suspect your child may have a disability that affects their learning. Here's how to make that request in Hays CISD:

### Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing
While you can make a verbal request, we strongly recommend putting your request in writing. This creates a paper trail and starts the official timeline for the district to respond.

Your letter should be simple and direct. Here's an example:

> Dear [Principal's Name or Special Education Coordinator],
>
> I am the parent of [Child's Name], a [grade] student at [School Name]. I am requesting that Hays CISD conduct a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) to determine whether my child qualifies for special education services.
>
> I have concerns about my child's [briefly describe concerns—for example, "reading skills," "ability to focus in class," "social interactions with peers," etc.].
>
> Please send me the necessary consent forms and information about the evaluation process. I look forward to working with the evaluation team.
>
> Sincerely,
> [Your Name]
> [Your Contact Information]
> [Date]

### Step 2: Submit Your Request
You can deliver your written request to:
- Your child's principal
- Your child's teacher
- The campus special education coordinator
- The Hays CISD Special Education Department

Keep a copy of everything you submit, and note the date you delivered it.

### Step 3: Wait for the District's Response
Once Hays CISD receives your written request, they must respond within **15 school days**. They will either:
- Provide you with a "Prior Written Notice" agreeing to evaluate your child, along with consent forms for you to sign, OR
- Provide you with a "Prior Written Notice" explaining why they are declining to evaluate, along with information about your rights to challenge that decision

### Step 4: Give Your Written Consent
Hays CISD cannot begin evaluating your child until you sign and return the consent form. This is your permission for the evaluation to move forward. Take time to read the consent form carefully—it should explain what assessments will be conducted and who will be conducting them.

### Step 5: Stay Engaged
Once you've given consent, the evaluation process begins. You may be asked to:
- Complete questionnaires about your child's behavior and development
- Provide copies of previous evaluations, medical records, or other relevant documents
- Share your observations and concerns about your child

Your input is a valuable and required part of the FIE process.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law establishes clear timelines for the FIE process to ensure your child doesn't wait too long for answers. Here's what you need to know:

### The 15-School-Day Rule
After Hays CISD receives your written request for an evaluation, they have **15 school days** to either give you a consent form to sign or provide written notice explaining why they're declining to evaluate.

### The 45-School-Day Rule
Once you sign and return the consent form, Hays CISD has **45 school days** to:
1. Complete all evaluation activities
2. Write the FIE report
3. Hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to review the results with you

This 45-day clock only counts school days, so it pauses during summer break, winter break, spring break, and other holidays when school is not in session.

### Exceptions to the Timeline
There are limited circumstances where the 45-day timeline may be extended:
- If your child is absent for an extended period
- If your child transfers to Hays CISD from another Texas district mid-evaluation (the districts must coordinate, and you must agree to a new timeline)
- If you and the district mutually agree in writing to extend the timeline
