# What Is a FIE? A Parent's Guide for Bastrop ISD Families

If you're a parent in Bastrop ISD and you've heard the term "FIE" mentioned by your child's teacher, counselor, or another parent, you're in the right place. Whether your child attends school in Bastrop, Elgin, Smithville, or any of the other communities served by this growing district of approximately 12,940 students, understanding the Full Individual Evaluation process is an important step in getting your child the support they need. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about FIEs in clear, straightforward language—because every parent deserves to understand the process that shapes their child's educational journey.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation, commonly called a FIE, is a comprehensive assessment that determines whether your child has a disability and needs special education services. Think of it as a detailed portrait of your child—their strengths, challenges, learning style, and unique needs.

In Texas, a FIE is sometimes referred to as a "psychoeducational evaluation." It's required by both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas state law (Texas Education Code Chapter 29) before any child can receive special education services.

The FIE isn't a single test. It's a collection of assessments, observations, and information-gathering conducted by a team of qualified professionals. This team might include school psychologists, educational diagnosticians, speech-language pathologists, and other specialists depending on your child's suspected areas of need.

Most importantly, a FIE is designed to look at your whole child—not just their struggles, but also their capabilities and potential.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Bastrop ISD?

For families in Bastrop ISD, the FIE serves as the gateway to special education services and supports. Without a completed FIE, the district cannot determine eligibility for special education, and your child cannot receive an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

The FIE matters because it:

- **Identifies whether your child has a qualifying disability** under one of the 13 categories recognized by IDEA (such as autism, specific learning disability, speech impairment, or emotional disturbance)
- **Provides detailed information** about how that disability affects your child's ability to learn and participate in school
- **Guides the development of appropriate services** by pinpointing exactly where your child needs support
- **Establishes a baseline** that can be used to measure your child's progress over time
- **Ensures your child receives a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE)** as required by law

Bastrop ISD, like all Texas school districts, must follow specific procedures when conducting FIEs to ensure that evaluations are thorough, fair, and completed within legal timelines.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is comprehensive by design. The evaluation team will look at multiple areas to get a complete picture of your child. Depending on your child's suspected disability, the FIE may assess:

**Cognitive Ability (Intelligence)**
How your child thinks, reasons, solves problems, and processes information.

**Academic Achievement**
Your child's skills in reading, writing, math, and other academic areas compared to grade-level expectations.

**Communication Skills**
How well your child understands and uses language, including speech, listening, and expressing ideas.

**Social and Emotional Development**
Your child's behavior, emotional regulation, social skills, and how they interact with peers and adults.

**Adaptive Behavior**
Daily living skills and how independently your child can function in various settings.

**Motor Skills**
Fine motor skills (like writing and cutting) and gross motor skills (like running and balance), if concerns exist.

**Health and Developmental History**
Information about your child's medical history, developmental milestones, and any factors that might affect learning.

**Observation and Input**
The team will observe your child in different settings and gather input from you, teachers, and other caregivers who know your child well.

Texas law requires that evaluations be conducted in all areas related to your child's suspected disability—nothing should be left out that might impact their education.

## How to Request a FIE at Bastrop ISD

You have the right to request a FIE at any time if you suspect your child may have a disability that affects their learning. Here's how to make that request in Bastrop ISD:

**Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing**

While you can make a verbal request, we strongly recommend putting it in writing. A written request creates documentation and starts the legal timeline. You can:

- Write a letter or email to your child's principal
- Send your request to the campus special education coordinator
- Contact Bastrop ISD's Special Education Department directly

**Step 2: Include Key Information**

Your request doesn't need to be formal or complicated. Simply include:

- Your child's name, grade, and campus
- Your contact information
- A brief explanation of your concerns (for example, "I'm concerned about my child's reading progress" or "My child seems to struggle with attention and focus")
- A clear statement that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation

**Step 3: Keep Copies**

Save a copy of your written request along with any proof of delivery (such as email confirmations or certified mail receipts).

**Sample Request Letter:**

> Dear [Principal's Name],
>
> I am the parent of [Child's Name], a [grade] student at [School Name]. I am concerned about my child's [describe concerns briefly]. I am formally requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) to determine whether my child has a disability and qualifies for special education services.
>
> Please contact me at [phone/email] to discuss next steps. Thank you for your attention to this request.
>
> Sincerely,
> [Your Name]
> [Date]

Once Bastrop ISD receives your request, they must respond within **15 school days** by either providing you with a consent form to begin the evaluation or a written notice explaining why they are refusing to evaluate.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law establishes clear timelines to ensure evaluations happen promptly. Understanding these deadlines helps you advocate effectively for your child.

**15 School Days**
After receiving your written request, the district has 15 school days to provide you with:
- Prior Written Notice of their decision (to evaluate or not)
- A consent form for you to sign if they agree to evaluate

**45 School Days**
Once you sign and return the consent form, Bastrop ISD has 45 school days to:
- Complete all evaluation activities
- Write the FIE report
- Hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to review the results

There are a few exceptions to the 45-day timeline, such as if your child misses school for an extended period. However, the district should communicate with you if any delays occur.

**Mark Your Calendar**

When you sign the consent form, note the date. Count 45 school days (not calendar days) from that point. School holidays and breaks don't count, but regular school days do.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

After the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and filled with technical language, but it contains crucial information about your child.

**What the Report Includes:**

- **Background information** about your child's history
- **Assessment results** from each evaluation conducted
- **Interpretation** of what those results mean
- **Summary of findings** across all areas assessed
- **Eligibility determination** — whether your child qualifies for special education
- **Recommendations** for educational programming

**Tips for Reading the Report:**

1. **Request the report before the ARD meeting** so you have time to review it carefully
2. **Look up unfamiliar terms** — don't be afraid to ask the evaluation team to explain anything
3. **Pay attention to the "Recommendations" section** — these often guide what goes into the IEP
4. **Note your child's strengths** — a good FIE highlights capabilities, not just challenges
5. **Write down your questions** to bring to the ARD meeting

Remember, you know your child better than anyone. If something in the report doesn't match what you see at home, speak up.
