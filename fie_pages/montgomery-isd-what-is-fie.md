# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Montgomery ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Montgomery ISD and someone has mentioned that your child might need a "FIE," you're probably wondering what that means and what to expect. Whether your child is struggling in school, has been referred by a teacher, or you've noticed developmental concerns at home, understanding the Full Individual Evaluation process is an important first step. This guide is designed specifically for Montgomery ISD families in the Houston area, helping you navigate what can feel like an overwhelming process with confidence and clarity.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A FIE, or Full Individual Evaluation, is Texas's comprehensive assessment process used to determine whether a child qualifies for special education services. You might also hear it called a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation."

Think of the FIE as a detailed snapshot of your child—how they learn, where they struggle, and what their unique strengths are. The evaluation is conducted by a team of qualified professionals, which typically includes a licensed specialist in school psychology (LSSP), educational diagnosticians, and other specialists depending on your child's needs.

Under both the federal Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and the Texas Education Code Chapter 29, school districts like Montgomery ISD must conduct a FIE before a child can receive special education services. This isn't just a formality—it's a legal requirement designed to ensure your child receives appropriate support tailored to their individual needs.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Montgomery ISD?

Montgomery ISD serves approximately 9,870 students across its campuses, and like all Texas school districts, it has a responsibility to identify and support students with disabilities. The FIE is the gateway to special education services, making it one of the most important steps in your child's educational journey.

Here's why the FIE matters for your family:

- **Identification**: It helps determine whether your child has a disability that impacts their learning
- **Individualized support**: Results guide the development of an Individualized Education Program (IEP) tailored specifically to your child
- **Legal protection**: A properly conducted FIE ensures your child's rights under federal and state law are protected
- **Baseline data**: It provides valuable information about your child's current abilities and needs
- **Eligibility determination**: It establishes whether your child qualifies for special education services under one of the 13 disability categories recognized in Texas

For Montgomery ISD families, having a thorough FIE means your child's teachers and support staff will have the information they need to help your child succeed.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is comprehensive by design—it looks at the whole child, not just one area of concern. The specific assessments included will depend on your child's suspected disability and individual needs, but a FIE typically evaluates:

**Cognitive Ability**
This measures how your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems. It's often referred to as an IQ test, but it's much more nuanced than a single number.

**Academic Achievement**
This looks at your child's skills in reading, writing, math, and other academic areas compared to grade-level expectations.

**Communication Skills**
A speech-language pathologist may assess how your child understands and uses language, both spoken and written.

**Social and Emotional Functioning**
This examines your child's behavior, emotional regulation, social skills, and mental health needs.

**Adaptive Behavior**
This evaluates daily living skills and how your child functions independently in various settings.

**Motor Skills**
If concerns exist, an occupational or physical therapist may assess fine and gross motor abilities.

**Health and Developmental History**
The team will review your child's medical history, developmental milestones, and any relevant background information you provide.

As a parent, your input is invaluable during this process. You know your child better than anyone, and the evaluation team will ask you to complete questionnaires and provide information about your child's behavior and abilities at home.

## How to Request a FIE at Montgomery ISD

You have the right to request a FIE at any time if you suspect your child has a disability that affects their learning. Here's how to do it in Montgomery ISD:

**Step 1: Submit a Written Request**
While you can make a verbal request, putting it in writing creates documentation and starts the clock on legal timelines. Address your letter to your child's principal or the Montgomery ISD Special Education Department.

**Sample Request Language:**
*"I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [child's name], who is in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned about [briefly describe your concerns]. Please send me the necessary consent forms and Prior Written Notice as required by law."*

**Step 2: Wait for the District's Response**
Montgomery ISD must respond to your request within 15 school days. They will either:
- Provide you with a Notice of Proposed Evaluation and consent forms, OR
- Provide a Prior Written Notice explaining why they are declining to evaluate

**Step 3: Give Written Consent**
The evaluation cannot begin until you sign and return the consent form. This is your green light—the timeline for completing the FIE starts when the district receives your signed consent.

**Where to Send Your Request:**
Contact your child's campus or reach out to the Montgomery ISD Special Education Department directly. You can find contact information on the Montgomery ISD website under the Special Education or Student Services section.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law establishes clear timelines to ensure evaluations happen promptly. Here's what you need to know:

**15 School Days**
After Montgomery ISD receives your written request for a FIE, they have 15 school days to provide you with the Prior Written Notice and consent forms (or explain why they're declining to evaluate).

**45 School Days**
Once the district receives your signed consent, they have 45 school days to complete the FIE and hold the ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to discuss the results.

**Important Notes:**
- "School days" means days when school is in session—summer break, holidays, and weekends don't count
- If your child is absent for an extended period during the evaluation, this may affect the timeline
- If Montgomery ISD misses these deadlines, you have the right to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency

Mark your calendar when you submit consent and track the 45-day timeline. This helps ensure the process stays on track.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

The FIE report can be lengthy and filled with technical language, but it contains crucial information about your child. Here's how to make sense of it:

**What You'll Find in the Report:**
- **Background information**: Your child's history, previous interventions, and health information
- **Assessment results**: Scores from cognitive, academic, and other evaluations
- **Observations**: Notes from evaluators who observed your child during testing
- **Interpretation**: What the scores and observations mean for your child's learning
- **Eligibility recommendation**: Whether your child meets criteria for a disability category
- **Recommendations**: Suggested supports, services, and accommodations

**Tips for Reviewing Your Report:**
- Request the report before the ARD meeting so you have time to read it carefully
- Highlight terms or sections you don't understand and ask for clarification
- Look at the "recommendations" section—this often contains valuable suggestions
- Remember that scores are just one piece of the puzzle; the narrative sections provide important context

Don't hesitate to ask the school psychologist or diagnostician to explain anything that's unclear. Understanding this report helps you advocate effectively for your child.

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

Once the FIE is complete, Montgomery ISD will schedule an ARD meeting (called an IEP meeting in other states). ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal Committee, and this meeting is where important decisions are made about your child's education.

**At the ARD Meeting:**
- The evaluation team presents and explains the FIE results
- The committee determines whether your child qualifies for special education
- If eligible, the team develops an Individualized Education Program (IEP)
- Goals, services, accommodations, and placement are discussed
- You are a full member of this committee and your input matters

**Who Attends:**
- You (the parent)
- A general education teacher
- A special education teacher
- An LSSP or educational diagnostician
- A district representative who can commit resources
- Related service providers (if applicable)
- Your child (when appropriate)
- Anyone else you or the school invites

**Your Role:**
You are an equal member of the ARD committee. Come prepared to share your insights, ask questions, and advocate for what your child needs. If you feel overwhelmed, you can bring a support person—a friend, family member, or advocate.
