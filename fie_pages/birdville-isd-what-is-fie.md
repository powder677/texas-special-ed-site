# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Birdville ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Birdville ISD and you've been hearing terms like "FIE" or "full evaluation" from your child's school, you're in the right place. Whether your child is struggling in class, showing signs of a learning difference, or has already been flagged for additional support, understanding the Full Individual Evaluation process is an important first step. With approximately 22,180 students across the district, Birdville ISD serves a diverse community of families in the Fort Worth region, and the district has established processes to identify and support students who may need special education services. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about the FIE—what it is, how it works, and what it means for your child's education.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A **Full Individual Evaluation (FIE)** is a comprehensive assessment that Texas school districts, including Birdville ISD, must conduct before a child can qualify for special education services. You might also hear it called a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation."

Think of the FIE as a detailed portrait of your child as a learner. It looks at your child's strengths, challenges, and unique needs across multiple areas—not just academics, but also things like how they process information, communicate, and interact with others.

The FIE is required under two important laws:
- **The Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA)** – the federal law that guarantees children with disabilities the right to a free appropriate public education
- **Texas Education Code Chapter 29** – Texas state law that outlines specific requirements for special education in our state

This isn't a single test—it's a collection of assessments, observations, and information-gathering designed to give a complete picture of your child's educational needs.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Birdville ISD?

The FIE serves as the foundation for everything that comes next in your child's special education journey at Birdville ISD. Without a completed FIE, your child cannot be found eligible for special education services, and the school cannot develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Here's why the FIE matters so much:

- **It identifies whether your child has a disability** that impacts their ability to learn in a typical classroom setting
- **It pinpoints specific areas of need** so services can be tailored to your child
- **It highlights your child's strengths** that can be built upon in their educational program
- **It provides baseline data** that helps track your child's progress over time
- **It ensures appropriate support** rather than a one-size-fits-all approach

For Birdville ISD families specifically, the FIE helps the district's special education team understand how to best serve your child within the programs and resources available across the district's schools.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

The FIE is comprehensive by design. Texas law requires that it assess your child in **all areas related to the suspected disability**. Depending on your child's needs, the evaluation might include:

### Cognitive Ability
How your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems. This is often measured through an IQ or intelligence test.

### Academic Achievement
How your child performs in reading, writing, math, and other school subjects compared to same-age peers.

### Speech and Language Skills
How well your child understands language, expresses themselves, and uses communication in social situations.

### Motor Skills
Your child's fine motor abilities (like writing and cutting) and gross motor skills (like running and balance).

### Social and Emotional Functioning
How your child interacts with others, manages emotions, and copes with daily challenges.

### Adaptive Behavior
Your child's practical life skills and ability to function independently in everyday situations.

### Health and Developmental History
Information about your child's medical background, developmental milestones, and any factors that might affect learning.

### Classroom Observations
How your child behaves and performs in their actual school environment.

The evaluation team at Birdville ISD will select specific assessments based on your child's suspected areas of need. You'll have the opportunity to provide input about your concerns before testing begins.

## How to Request a FIE at Birdville ISD

As a parent, you have the right to request a FIE at any time if you suspect your child may have a disability that affects their learning. Here's how to do it in Birdville ISD:

### Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing
While you can make a verbal request, **written requests create a clear record and start the official timeline**. Write a simple letter or email to your child's principal or the Birdville ISD Special Education Department stating that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation for your child because you have concerns about [describe your concerns briefly].

**Sample language:**
> "I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [child's name], who is in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned about [his/her/their] [describe concerns, such as difficulty reading, trouble focusing, speech delays, etc.]. Please send me the necessary consent forms to begin this process."

### Step 2: Submit to the Right People
Send your written request to:
- Your child's school principal
- The campus special education coordinator
- You may also contact the Birdville ISD Special Education Department directly

Keep a copy of your request and note the date you submitted it.

### Step 3: Wait for the District's Response
Once Birdville ISD receives your request, they must respond. Within **15 school days**, the district must either:
- Provide you with **Prior Written Notice** agreeing to evaluate and consent forms to sign, OR
- Provide you with **Prior Written Notice** explaining why they are refusing to evaluate

### Step 4: Give Written Consent
The evaluation cannot begin until you sign and return the consent form. The official evaluation timeline starts once the school receives your signed consent.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas has specific timelines that Birdville ISD must follow. Understanding these can help you know what to expect:

| Step | Timeline |
|------|----------|
| District responds to your evaluation request | Within **15 school days** of receiving your request |
| FIE must be completed | Within **45 school days** of receiving signed parent consent |
| ARD/IEP meeting held to review results | Within **30 calendar days** of completing the FIE |

**Important notes about the timeline:**

- The **45 school days** does not include days when school is not in session for more than 5 consecutive days (like summer break or winter break)
- If your child is absent for an extended period, this may pause the timeline
- If you and the district agree in writing, the timeline can be extended
- You can request an expedited timeline if your child has been expelled or is in certain disciplinary situations

If Birdville ISD doesn't meet these deadlines, you have the right to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

Once the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and technical, but here's what to look for:

### Key Sections of the FIE Report

**Background Information**
A summary of your child's history, including developmental milestones, medical history, and educational background.

**Assessment Results**
Scores and descriptions from each test administered. Look for both standard scores (which compare your child to same-age peers) and percentile ranks.

**Observations**
Notes from the evaluators about how your child approached tasks, their behavior during testing, and observations from the classroom.

**Summary and Conclusions**
The evaluators' interpretation of what the results mean for your child.

**Recommendations**
Suggestions for educational strategies, services, or supports that might help your child.

### Tips for Reading the Report

- **Don't panic about scores.** Numbers don't tell the whole story. Focus on what the results mean for helping your child.
- **Look for patterns.** Where does your child show strength? Where do they struggle?
- **Write down questions.** Bring them to the ARD meeting.
- **Request a pre-meeting explanation.** You can ask Birdville ISD staff to walk you through the report before the official ARD meeting.

Remember: You should receive a copy of the FIE report **before** the ARD meeting so you have time to review it. Texas law supports your right to meaningful participation, which means having time to understand the evaluation results.
