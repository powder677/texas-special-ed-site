# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Little Elm ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Little Elm ISD and you've heard the term "FIE" mentioned by teachers, counselors, or other parents, you're not alone in wondering what it means and whether your child might need one. Little Elm ISD serves a growing, diverse community in the DFW metroplex, and like all Texas school districts, it follows specific procedures when evaluating students who may need special education services. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about the Full Individual Evaluation process—in plain, parent-friendly language.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A **Full Individual Evaluation (FIE)** is a comprehensive assessment that Texas school districts conduct to determine whether a child has a disability and needs special education services. You might also hear it called a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply "the evaluation."

Think of the FIE as a deep dive into understanding your child as a learner. It's not just one test—it's a collection of assessments, observations, and information-gathering designed to paint a complete picture of your child's strengths, challenges, and educational needs.

Under both the **Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA)** and the **Texas Education Code Chapter 29**, school districts must complete a FIE before they can determine if a child qualifies for special education services. This evaluation is provided at no cost to you as a parent.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Little Elm ISD?

For families in Little Elm ISD, the FIE serves as the gateway to special education support. Without this evaluation, the district cannot legally provide specialized instruction or related services through an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

The FIE helps Little Elm ISD staff understand:

- Whether your child has a disability that affects their learning
- How that disability impacts their ability to access the general education curriculum
- What specific supports, services, or interventions your child needs to succeed
- Your child's unique strengths that can be built upon in the classroom

With approximately 8,920 students in the district, Little Elm ISD works to ensure every child who needs support receives an appropriate and timely evaluation. The FIE is the first critical step in that process.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE looks at your child from multiple angles. Texas law requires that evaluations be comprehensive and assess **all areas of suspected disability**. Depending on your child's needs, the FIE may include:

- **Cognitive ability** – How your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems
- **Academic achievement** – Reading, writing, math, and other academic skills
- **Communication skills** – Speech, language, and how your child expresses and understands information
- **Social and emotional development** – How your child interacts with others and manages emotions
- **Adaptive behavior** – Daily living skills and how your child functions in various settings
- **Motor skills** – Fine motor (writing, cutting) and gross motor (running, jumping) abilities
- **Health and developmental history** – Information about your child's medical background and developmental milestones
- **Behavior** – Patterns of behavior that may affect learning
- **Observation** – How your child performs in their natural classroom environment

The evaluation team will use a variety of tools, including standardized tests, interviews with you and teachers, classroom observations, and a review of your child's educational records.

## How to Request a FIE at Little Elm ISD

As a parent, you have the right to request a FIE at any time if you suspect your child has a disability that's affecting their education. Here's how to make that request in Little Elm ISD:

**Step 1: Put your request in writing**

While you can make a verbal request, a written request creates documentation and starts the official timeline. You can email or hand-deliver a letter to your child's school.

**Step 2: Address your request to the right person**

Send your request to your child's principal, the campus special education coordinator, or the Little Elm ISD Special Education Department. Keep a copy for your records.

**Step 3: Be specific about your concerns**

In your letter, briefly explain what you're observing and why you believe your child may need an evaluation. You don't need to diagnose anything—just share what you're seeing at home and what the school has reported.

**Sample language:**
*"I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [Child's Name], who attends [School Name] in Little Elm ISD. I am concerned about [briefly describe concerns, such as difficulty reading, attention challenges, speech delays, etc.]. I am requesting this evaluation to determine if my child qualifies for special education services."*

**Step 4: Wait for the district's response**

Once Little Elm ISD receives your written request, they must respond within **15 school days** by either providing you with consent forms to sign or issuing a Prior Written Notice explaining why they are declining to evaluate.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas has specific timelines that school districts must follow, and it's helpful to know what to expect:

| Step | Timeline |
|------|----------|
| Parent submits written request | Day 1 |
| District provides consent form or Prior Written Notice | Within **15 school days** |
| Parent signs and returns consent | Clock starts when district receives signed consent |
| District completes FIE | Within **45 school days** of receiving signed consent |
| ARD meeting to review results | Held within **30 calendar days** of FIE completion |

**Important notes:**
- The 45-school-day timeline does not include days when school is not in session for more than 5 consecutive days (like summer break or winter break)
- If your child was referred for evaluation between March 1 and the last day of school, you and the district can agree to a summer timeline to complete the evaluation before the next school year begins

## Understanding Your FIE Report

After the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and filled with technical terms, but here's what to look for:

**Key sections of the FIE report:**

- **Background information** – Your child's history, including input you provided
- **Assessment results** – Scores and descriptions from each evaluation area
- **Observations** – What evaluators noticed during testing and in the classroom
- **Summary and conclusions** – Whether your child meets the criteria for a disability under IDEA
- **Recommendations** – Suggestions for services, supports, and interventions

**Tips for reviewing the report:**

- Request a copy of the FIE report **before** the ARD meeting so you have time to read it
- Make a list of questions about anything you don't understand
- Pay attention to both the scores and the narrative descriptions—numbers don't tell the whole story
- Look for your child's strengths, not just their challenges

Don't hesitate to ask the school psychologist or diagnostician to explain any part of the report in plain language.

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

Once the FIE is complete, Little Elm ISD will invite you to an **ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting**. In other states, this might be called an IEP meeting, but in Texas, we use "ARD."

At the ARD meeting, a team—including you—will:

1. Review the FIE results together
2. Determine whether your child qualifies for special education under one of the 13 disability categories in IDEA
3. If eligible, develop an **Individualized Education Program (IEP)** outlining goals, services, and supports
4. Discuss placement options and how services will be delivered

**You are a vital member of this team.** Your input about your child matters, and you have equal say in the decisions being made. If you disagree with something, you can voice your concerns, request additional time, or decline to sign the IEP until you've had more time to consider it.

## Your Rights During the FIE Process at Little Elm ISD

Texas law and IDEA guarantee you important rights as a parent. Here are some key protections to keep in mind:

- **Right to request an evaluation** – You can request a FIE at any time
- **Right to informed consent** – The district cannot evaluate your child without your written permission
- **Right to receive information in your native language** – If English isn't your primary language, you can request translation services
- **Right to review all records** – You can see all educational records related to your child
