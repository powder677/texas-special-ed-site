# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Texarkana ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Texarkana ISD and you've been hearing terms like "FIE" or "Full Individual Evaluation," you're not alone in wondering what it all means. Whether your child is struggling in school, a teacher has expressed concerns, or you simply want to understand your options, this guide is here to help you navigate the evaluation process with confidence. Serving approximately 7,230 students in the heart of East Texas, Texarkana ISD has teams dedicated to identifying and supporting students who may need special education services—and understanding the FIE is your first step toward getting your child the help they deserve.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation, commonly called a FIE, is a comprehensive assessment that Texas school districts must complete before a child can qualify for special education services. You might also hear it referred to as a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation."

Think of the FIE as a complete picture of your child—how they learn, where they excel, and where they might need additional support. It's not just one test; it's a collection of assessments, observations, and information gathered from multiple sources, including you as the parent.

The FIE is required by both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas state law (Texas Education Code Chapter 29). These laws exist to protect your child's right to a free and appropriate public education, and the FIE is the gateway to accessing specialized support.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Texarkana ISD?

For Texarkana ISD families, the FIE serves as the foundation for everything that follows in your child's special education journey. Here's why it matters:

**It identifies your child's unique needs.** Every child learns differently. The FIE helps pinpoint exactly where your child needs support—whether that's in reading, math, speech, behavior, or other areas.

**It determines eligibility for services.** In Texas, a child must meet specific criteria to qualify for special education. The FIE provides the data needed to make this determination fairly and accurately.

**It guides the creation of your child's educational plan.** If your child qualifies, the FIE results will directly inform the goals and services written into their Individualized Education Program (IEP).

**It establishes a baseline.** The FIE documents where your child is right now, which helps teachers and specialists measure progress over time.

At Texarkana ISD, the evaluation team understands the unique character of your community and works to ensure every child has the opportunity to succeed.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

The FIE is designed to look at the "whole child" and may assess several different areas depending on your child's suspected needs:

- **Cognitive ability (intelligence):** How your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems
- **Academic achievement:** Current skill levels in reading, writing, and math
- **Communication skills:** Speech, language, and how your child expresses and understands ideas
- **Motor skills:** Fine motor (writing, cutting) and gross motor (running, balance) abilities
- **Social and emotional development:** How your child interacts with others and manages emotions
- **Adaptive behavior:** Daily living skills and independence appropriate for their age
- **Health and developmental history:** Medical information, developmental milestones, and any relevant background
- **Behavioral observations:** How your child acts in different settings, including the classroom

Not every child will be evaluated in all areas. The evaluation is tailored to address the specific concerns that led to the referral in the first place. You'll have input into what areas should be assessed.

## How to Request a FIE at Texarkana ISD

As a parent, you have the right to request a FIE at any time if you suspect your child may have a disability that affects their learning. Here's how to do it:

**Step 1: Put your request in writing.** While you can make a verbal request, a written request creates a clear record and starts the official timeline. You can write a simple letter or email to your child's principal or the Texarkana ISD Special Education Department.

**Step 2: Include key information.** In your request, include:
- Your child's full name and date of birth
- Your child's school and grade
- A brief description of your concerns
- Your contact information
- The date of your request

**Step 3: Keep a copy.** Always keep a copy of your written request for your records.

**Sample language you can use:**

*"Dear [Principal's Name], I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [Child's Name], who is currently in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned about [briefly describe concerns, such as difficulty reading, behavioral challenges, speech delays, etc.]. Please contact me to discuss next steps. Thank you, [Your Name, Date]"*

**Step 4: Expect a response.** Once Texarkana ISD receives your request, the district must respond within 15 school days by either agreeing to evaluate (and providing consent forms) or explaining in writing why they are declining to evaluate.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law sets clear deadlines to make sure evaluations happen in a timely manner. Here's what you need to know:

**15 school days:** After receiving your written request, Texarkana ISD must respond within 15 school days. If they agree to evaluate, they'll provide you with a consent form and a Notice of Proposed Evaluation explaining what assessments will be conducted.

**45 school days:** Once you sign and return the consent form, the district has 45 school days to complete the FIE and hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to review the results. This timeline does not include days when school is not in session for more than five consecutive days (like summer break or winter break).

**Your consent is required.** The evaluation cannot begin until you provide written consent. You have the right to ask questions and fully understand what will be evaluated before signing.

If the district misses these deadlines, you have the right to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

## Understanding Your FIE Report

After the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and filled with technical language, but don't be intimidated—you have every right to understand what it says about your child.

**Key sections to look for:**

- **Reason for referral:** Why the evaluation was requested
- **Background information:** Health history, developmental milestones, and educational history
- **Assessment results:** Scores and explanations from each evaluation area
- **Observations:** Notes on how your child performed during testing and in school
- **Summary and recommendations:** The evaluator's conclusions and suggestions for support

**Tips for reviewing the report:**

- Request a copy of the report before the ARD meeting so you have time to read it
- Highlight anything you don't understand and prepare questions
- Ask the school psychologist or diagnostician to explain scores and what they mean for your child
- Remember that you know your child best—if something doesn't seem accurate, speak up

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

Once the FIE is complete, Texarkana ISD will schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. In Texas, this is the meeting where the special education team—including you—comes together to review the evaluation results and make important decisions.

**At the ARD meeting, the team will:**

1. Review the FIE findings together
2. Determine whether your child qualifies for special education under one of the 13 disability categories recognized in Texas
3. If eligible, begin developing an Individualized Education Program (IEP) with specific goals and services
4. Discuss placement options—where and how services will be provided

**You are a vital member of the ARD committee.** Your input matters, and Texas law guarantees your right to participate in every decision. You can bring someone with you for support, ask for clarification, and request additional time if you need it to make informed decisions.

If the team determines your child does not qualify for special education, you'll receive a written explanation. You have the right to disagree and request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at the district's expense if you believe the FIE was not conducted appropriately.
