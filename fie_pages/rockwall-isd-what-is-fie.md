# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Rockwall ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Rockwall ISD and you've been hearing terms like "FIE" or "Full Individual Evaluation" mentioned at school meetings, you're not alone. Whether your child is struggling academically, facing behavioral challenges, or you simply want to understand what support options exist, learning about the FIE process is an important first step. This guide is designed specifically for Rockwall ISD families to help you navigate the special education evaluation process with confidence and clarity.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation, commonly called a FIE, is Texas's comprehensive assessment process used to determine whether a child has a disability and needs special education services. You might also hear it referred to as a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation."

The FIE is not a single test—it's a collection of assessments, observations, and information gathering that creates a complete picture of your child's strengths, challenges, and educational needs. This evaluation is required by both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and the Texas Education Code Chapter 29 before any child can receive special education services.

Think of the FIE as a detailed roadmap. It helps identify where your child excels, where they need support, and what kinds of services might help them succeed in school.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Rockwall ISD?

Rockwall ISD serves approximately 18,650 students across its campuses in the DFW metroplex. With a district this size, there are children with a wide range of learning needs, abilities, and challenges. The FIE process ensures that every child who may need special education services receives a thorough, individualized assessment.

A properly conducted FIE at Rockwall ISD helps:

- **Identify specific learning differences** such as dyslexia, ADHD, autism, speech-language impairments, or emotional disturbances
- **Establish eligibility** for special education services under one of the 13 disability categories recognized by IDEA
- **Pinpoint your child's unique strengths** that can be built upon in their educational program
- **Guide the development of appropriate goals and services** tailored to your child's individual needs
- **Create a baseline** for measuring your child's progress over time

For Rockwall ISD families, the FIE is the foundation upon which all special education decisions are made. It's not just paperwork—it's the key to unlocking the right support for your child.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

The FIE looks at your child from multiple angles to create a comprehensive understanding of their abilities and needs. While the specific assessments will vary based on your child's suspected disability and areas of concern, a typical FIE at Rockwall ISD may include:

### Cognitive Ability (Intelligence Testing)
This measures how your child thinks, reasons, and processes information. It's not about how "smart" your child is—it's about understanding how their brain works.

### Academic Achievement
These assessments look at your child's skills in reading, writing, math, and other academic areas compared to grade-level expectations.

### Adaptive Behavior
This evaluates how well your child handles everyday life skills, social situations, and practical tasks appropriate for their age.

### Communication Skills
Speech-language assessments examine how your child understands and uses language, both spoken and written.

### Social-Emotional and Behavioral Functioning
These assessments look at your child's emotional well-being, behavior patterns, and social skills.

### Motor Skills
If concerns exist, fine motor (writing, cutting) and gross motor (running, coordination) abilities may be assessed.

### Health and Developmental History
Information about your child's medical history, developmental milestones, and any relevant health factors is gathered.

### Observations and Interviews
The evaluation team will observe your child in different settings and talk with you, teachers, and others who know your child well.

**Important:** As a parent, you are a vital part of this process. Your input about your child's history, behaviors at home, and your concerns is essential to a complete evaluation.

## How to Request a FIE at Rockwall ISD

Any parent can request a FIE for their child at any time. You don't need to wait for the school to suggest it, and you don't need anyone's permission to make the request.

Here's how to request a FIE in Rockwall ISD:

### Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing
While you can verbally request an evaluation, **always follow up in writing**. This creates a paper trail and starts the legal timeline. Your written request should include:

- Your child's full name and date of birth
- Your child's school and grade
- A clear statement that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation for special education
- A brief description of your concerns
- Your contact information
- The date

### Step 2: Submit to the Right Person
You can submit your request to:
- Your child's principal
- Your child's teacher
- The campus special education coordinator
- The Rockwall ISD Special Education Department

### Step 3: Keep Copies of Everything
Make a copy of your request before submitting it. If you hand-deliver it, ask for a dated receipt. If you email it, keep the sent message and any replies.

### Sample Request Letter

> Dear [Principal/Special Education Coordinator],
>
> I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [Child's Name], who is currently in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned about [briefly describe your concerns—for example, "their difficulty with reading," "their struggles with focus and attention," or "their challenges with social interactions"].
>
> I understand that Rockwall ISD has 15 school days to respond to this request with a Notice of Proposed Evaluation. Please contact me at [phone number] or [email address] to discuss next steps.
>
> Thank you for your attention to this matter.
>
> Sincerely,
> [Your Name]
> [Date]

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas has specific timelines that Rockwall ISD must follow once you request a FIE. Understanding these deadlines helps you advocate effectively for your child.

### Within 15 School Days
After receiving your written request, Rockwall ISD must either:
- Provide you with a Notice of Proposed Evaluation and consent forms, OR
- Provide written notice explaining why they are refusing to evaluate

### Within 45 School Days
Once you sign and return the consent form, Rockwall ISD has 45 school days to:
- Complete all evaluations
- Write the FIE report
- Hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to review results

**Note:** The 45-day timeline only counts school days when students are in attendance. Summer break, winter break, and other holidays do not count toward this timeline.

### Exceptions to the Timeline
Certain situations may pause or extend the timeline:
- Your child misses school for more than three consecutive days
- You and the district mutually agree to an extension in writing
- The request is made close to the end of the school year (specific rules apply)

**Pro Tip:** Mark your calendar! Keep track of when you submitted consent and count 45 school days forward. This helps you know when to follow up if you haven't heard from the district.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

The FIE report can be lengthy and filled with technical language, but it contains crucial information about your child. Here's what to look for:

### Background Information
This section includes your child's history, health information, and the concerns that led to the evaluation.

### Assessment Results
Each assessment will have its own section explaining what was tested, how your child performed, and what the scores mean. Look for:
- **Standard scores** (average is typically 100)
- **Percentile ranks** (shows how your child compares to same-age peers)
- **Descriptive categories** (such as "average," "below average," or "above average")

### Observations
This describes how your child behaved during testing and in classroom settings.

### Summary and Conclusions
This is where the evaluation team explains whether your child meets criteria for a disability and qualifies for special education services.

### Recommendations
This section suggests what types of services, supports, or accommodations might help your child.

**Don't wait until the ARD meeting to read the report.** Request a copy at least a few days before the meeting so you have time to review it, write down questions, and process the information.
