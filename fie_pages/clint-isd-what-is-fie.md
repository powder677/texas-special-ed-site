# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Clint ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Clint ISD and you've heard the term "FIE" mentioned by a teacher, counselor, or administrator, you're not alone in wondering what it means and why it matters for your child. Whether your family is in Clint, Horizon City, or one of the surrounding communities in eastern El Paso County, understanding the Full Individual Evaluation process is an important step in getting your child the support they need to thrive in school. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about FIEs in Clint ISD—in plain language, with your family's needs in mind.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation, or FIE, is a comprehensive assessment that Texas school districts are required to conduct before a child can receive special education services. You might also hear it called a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "full evaluation."

The FIE isn't just one test—it's a collection of assessments, observations, and information-gathering activities designed to give a complete picture of your child's strengths, challenges, and educational needs. The evaluation looks at how your child learns, processes information, communicates, and functions in a school setting.

Both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas state law (Texas Education Code Chapter 29) require that this evaluation be completed before a school district can determine whether a child qualifies for special education services and, if so, what kind of support they need.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Clint ISD?

For families in Clint ISD, the FIE serves as the foundation for all special education decisions. With approximately 10,340 students across the district, Clint ISD serves a diverse community with varying needs, and the FIE helps ensure that each child is evaluated as an individual.

The FIE is important because it:

- **Identifies whether your child has a disability** that affects their ability to learn in a general education setting
- **Provides detailed information** about your child's specific learning profile
- **Guides the development of services and supports** tailored to your child's unique needs
- **Establishes a baseline** to measure your child's progress over time
- **Ensures decisions are based on data**, not assumptions or guesswork

Without a FIE, your child cannot be found eligible for special education services in Clint ISD or any other Texas school district. That's why understanding this process is so essential.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is designed to be comprehensive, meaning it looks at all areas that might be affecting your child's ability to learn. Depending on your child's specific concerns, the evaluation may assess:

**Cognitive Ability (Intelligence)**
How your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems. This often includes an IQ test but looks at multiple aspects of cognitive functioning.

**Academic Achievement**
Your child's current skill levels in reading, writing, math, and other academic areas compared to grade-level expectations.

**Speech and Language**
How your child understands language (receptive) and expresses themselves (expressive), including vocabulary, grammar, and social communication.

**Motor Skills**
Fine motor skills (like writing and cutting) and gross motor skills (like running and coordination) that may affect school performance.

**Social and Emotional Functioning**
Your child's behavior, emotional regulation, social skills, and mental health needs.

**Adaptive Behavior**
Daily living skills and how independently your child functions compared to same-age peers.

**Health and Developmental History**
Information about your child's medical history, developmental milestones, and any factors that might impact learning.

**Sensory Processing**
How your child responds to sensory input like sounds, textures, or visual information (particularly important for children who may be on the autism spectrum).

The specific areas evaluated will depend on the concerns identified for your child. Before the evaluation begins, you'll be informed about which assessments will be conducted.

## How to Request a FIE at Clint ISD

As a parent, you have the right to request a FIE for your child at any time if you suspect they may have a disability that's affecting their learning. Here's how to make that request in Clint ISD:

### Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing
While you can make a verbal request, a written request creates documentation and starts the official timeline. Write a simple letter or email to your child's principal or the Clint ISD Special Education Department.

**Sample language:**
*"I am writing to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [Child's Name], who is currently in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned about [briefly describe your concerns—for example, reading difficulties, attention issues, social challenges]. Please send me the consent forms and information about the evaluation process. Thank you."*

### Step 2: Date and Keep a Copy
Always date your request and keep a copy for your records. Note when and how you submitted it.

### Step 3: Wait for the District's Response
Once Clint ISD receives your written request, they have **15 school days** to respond. They will either:
- Provide you with a "Notice and Consent for Evaluation" form to sign, OR
- Provide you with written notice explaining why they are refusing to evaluate, along with information about your rights

### Step 4: Give Written Consent
The evaluation cannot begin until you sign and return the consent form. Review it carefully to understand what assessments will be conducted.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law sets clear deadlines to ensure families don't have to wait indefinitely for answers. Here's what you need to know:

| Milestone | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| District response to your written request | Within **15 school days** |
| Completion of the FIE after you give consent | Within **45 school days** |
| ARD meeting to review results | Within **30 calendar days** of FIE completion |

**Important notes about the timeline:**

- The 45-school-day clock starts the day after the district receives your signed consent
- School days don't include holidays, weekends, or days when school is closed for weather or other emergencies
- Summer days typically don't count unless your child attends summer school or the district has agreed to evaluate during summer
- If your child is absent for extended periods, it may affect the timeline

If Clint ISD does not meet these timelines, you have the right to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

## Understanding Your FIE Report

Once the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and technical, but understanding it is important. Here's what to look for:

**Background Information**
This section summarizes your child's history, including medical, developmental, and educational information.

**Assessment Results**
Each assessment area will have its own section with scores, descriptions, and observations. You'll see terms like:
- *Standard scores* (average is typically 100)
- *Percentile ranks* (shows how your child compares to peers)
- *Age or grade equivalents* (use with caution—these can be misleading)

**Evaluation Team's Observations**
The evaluators will describe what they observed during testing, including your child's behavior, attention, effort, and any factors that might have affected results.

**Summary and Conclusions**
This section pulls everything together and should clearly explain:
- Whether your child meets the criteria for a disability under IDEA
- Which disability category (or categories) may apply
- How the disability affects your child's education

**Tips for reading the report:**
- Request the report before the ARD meeting so you have time to review it
- Make a list of questions about anything you don't understand
- Ask the evaluator to explain scores and what they mean for your child
- Don't hesitate to ask for a meeting to go over the report before the formal ARD

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

After the FIE is completed, Clint ISD will schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 calendar days. In other states, this is often called an IEP meeting, but Texas uses the term "ARD."

**At the ARD meeting, the team will:**

1. **Review the FIE results** together as a group
2. **Determine eligibility** – Does your child qualify for special education under one of the 13 disability categories recognized by IDEA?
3. **If eligible, develop an IEP** – The Individualized Education Program outlines the goals, services, accommodations, and supports your child will receive
