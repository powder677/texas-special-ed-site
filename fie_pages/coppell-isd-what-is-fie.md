# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Coppell ISD Parents

If you're a parent in Coppell ISD and you've been hearing terms like "FIE" or "Full Individual Evaluation," you're not alone—and you've come to the right place. Whether your child is struggling in school, has recently been referred for testing, or you simply want to understand your options, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know about the FIE process in Coppell ISD. As a district serving approximately 13,180 students in the heart of the DFW metroplex, Coppell ISD has established procedures to evaluate children who may need special education support, and understanding these procedures is the first step toward getting your child the help they deserve.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A Full Individual Evaluation, commonly called a FIE (pronounced "fee"), is Texas's comprehensive assessment process used to determine whether a child has a disability and needs special education services. You might also hear it referred to as a "psychoeducational evaluation."

Think of a FIE as a detailed snapshot of your child's strengths, challenges, and unique learning needs. It's not just one test—it's a collection of assessments, observations, and information gathering that helps paint a complete picture of how your child learns, processes information, and functions in a school setting.

Under federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and the Texas Education Code Chapter 29, school districts like Coppell ISD are required to conduct a FIE before a child can qualify for special education services. This evaluation is provided at no cost to families and must be completed by qualified professionals.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Coppell ISD?

For Coppell ISD families, the FIE serves as the gateway to special education services and support. Here's why it matters:

**It identifies your child's unique needs.** Every child learns differently. A FIE helps uncover specific learning disabilities, developmental delays, attention challenges, speech and language difficulties, or other conditions that might be affecting your child's success in school.

**It's required for special education eligibility.** Without a completed FIE, your child cannot receive an Individualized Education Program (IEP) or access special education services in Coppell ISD schools.

**It guides educational planning.** The results from a FIE help teachers, specialists, and parents work together to create targeted interventions and accommodations that actually address your child's specific challenges.

**It establishes a baseline.** The FIE provides documented evidence of your child's current abilities, which can be used to measure growth and adjust services over time.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is comprehensive by design. Depending on your child's suspected areas of need, the evaluation may assess:

- **Cognitive ability** – How your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems
- **Academic achievement** – Reading, writing, and math skills compared to grade-level expectations
- **Speech and language skills** – How your child understands and uses language
- **Motor skills** – Fine motor (writing, cutting) and gross motor (coordination, balance) abilities
- **Social-emotional functioning** – Behavior, emotional regulation, and social skills
- **Adaptive behavior** – Daily living skills and independence appropriate for your child's age
- **Health and developmental history** – Medical factors that may affect learning
- **Classroom observations** – How your child performs in their actual learning environment

The specific assessments included in your child's FIE will depend on the concerns that prompted the evaluation. Coppell ISD's evaluation team will select appropriate tools based on your child's suspected disability category.

## How to Request a FIE at Coppell ISD

As a parent, you have the right to request a FIE at any time if you suspect your child has a disability that's affecting their learning. Here's how to do it in Coppell ISD:

**Step 1: Put your request in writing.**
While you can make a verbal request, we strongly recommend submitting a written request. This creates a paper trail and officially starts the timeline. Address your letter to your child's principal or the Coppell ISD Special Education Department.

**Step 2: Be specific about your concerns.**
In your letter, describe what you're observing at home and what concerns you have about your child's learning or development. You don't need to know what disability your child might have—just share what you've noticed.

**Step 3: Keep a copy for your records.**
Always keep copies of everything you submit to the district, along with the date you submitted it.

**Sample language for your request:**
*"I am writing to formally request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) for my child, [child's name], who is currently in [grade] at [school name]. I am concerned about [briefly describe concerns]. I am requesting this evaluation to determine whether my child has a disability and qualifies for special education services. Please contact me to discuss next steps."*

**Step 4: Wait for the district's response.**
Coppell ISD must respond to your request within 15 school days by either providing you with consent forms to begin the evaluation or providing written notice explaining why they are refusing to evaluate.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law establishes specific timelines that Coppell ISD must follow:

| Milestone | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| District response to your request | Within 15 school days |
| Completion of FIE after you give written consent | Within 45 school days |
| ARD meeting to review results | Within 30 calendar days of FIE completion |

**Important notes about the timeline:**

- The 45-school-day clock starts when the district receives your signed consent form—not when you first made the request.
- School days don't include holidays or summer break, so if you submit consent near the end of the school year, the timeline may extend into the next school year.
- If your child is absent for an extended period, this may also affect the timeline.

If Coppell ISD misses these deadlines without a valid reason, they may be in violation of state law. Don't hesitate to follow up in writing if deadlines are approaching.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

Once the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and full of technical language, but here's what to look for:

**Background information** – A summary of your child's history, including input you provided

**Assessment results** – Scores from each test administered, often including standard scores and percentile ranks

**Observations** – Notes from classroom observations and examiner observations during testing

**Summary and conclusions** – The evaluation team's interpretation of what the data shows

**Eligibility determination** – Whether your child meets criteria for one or more disability categories under IDEA

**Recommendations** – Suggested next steps, accommodations, or services

**Tips for reviewing the report:**

- Request a copy of the report at least a few days before your ARD meeting so you have time to review it
- Write down questions about anything you don't understand
- Pay attention to both strengths and weaknesses identified
- Look at the recommendations section carefully—these often inform IEP goals

You have every right to ask the evaluation team to explain results in plain language. If something doesn't match what you see at home, speak up.

## What Happens After the FIE? (The ARD Meeting)

After the FIE is completed, Coppell ISD will schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting. In Texas, this is what other states might call an "IEP meeting."

**Who attends the ARD meeting:**
- You (the parent or guardian)
- A district representative
- Your child's teacher(s)
- The professionals who conducted the evaluation
- Any other specialists relevant to your child's needs
- You may also bring someone to support you, such as an advocate or family member

**What happens at the ARD meeting:**
1. The evaluation team presents the FIE results
2. The committee determines whether your child is eligible for special education
3. If eligible, the team develops an Individualized Education Program (IEP)
4. Goals, services, and accommodations are discussed and agreed upon

**Remember:** You are an equal member of the ARD committee. Your input matters, and you do not have to agree to anything on the spot. You can request to take materials home, ask for another meeting, or request changes to proposed plans.

## Your Rights During the FIE Process at Coppell ISD

Texas law and IDEA provide you with important protections throughout the evaluation process:

**Right to request an evaluation.** You can request a FIE at any time, regardless of whether the school has expressed concerns.

**Right to informed consent.** The district cannot evaluate your child without your written permission. You must understand what assessments will be conducted.
