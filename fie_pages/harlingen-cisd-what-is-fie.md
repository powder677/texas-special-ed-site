# What Is a FIE? A Guide for Harlingen CISD Parents

If you're a parent in Harlingen CISD and you've been hearing terms like "FIE" or "Full Individual Evaluation" from your child's school, you're not alone—and you've come to the right place. Whether your child is struggling in class, showing signs of a learning difference, or you simply want to understand what the evaluation process looks like in our district, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know. Harlingen CISD serves over 17,000 students across our community in the Rio Grande Valley, and the district's special education team works with families every day to identify and support students who may need additional help to succeed.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A FIE, or Full Individual Evaluation, is a comprehensive assessment that Texas school districts must conduct before a child can receive special education services. You might also hear it called a "psychoeducational evaluation" or simply a "special education evaluation."

Think of the FIE as a detailed picture of your child—their strengths, challenges, learning style, and any areas where they might need extra support. The evaluation looks at many different aspects of your child's development and academic performance to determine two things:

1. Whether your child has a disability as defined by federal and state law
2. Whether that disability affects their ability to learn in the general education setting, making them eligible for special education services

The FIE is required under both the federal Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and the Texas Education Code Chapter 29. This means Harlingen CISD must follow specific rules and timelines when conducting evaluations—rules that are designed to protect your child and your family.

## Why Is a FIE Important at Harlingen CISD?

For families in Harlingen CISD, the FIE is the gateway to accessing special education services and supports that can make a real difference in your child's education. Without a completed FIE, your child cannot be found eligible for special education, and the school cannot develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) to address their specific needs.

The FIE is important because it:

- **Identifies your child's unique needs** — Every child learns differently, and the FIE helps uncover exactly where your child might need support
- **Provides a legal foundation** — The evaluation creates documentation that protects your child's right to receive appropriate services
- **Guides educational planning** — The information gathered in the FIE directly shapes the goals and services in your child's IEP
- **Ensures fairness** — The evaluation process includes safeguards to make sure your child is assessed fairly and accurately

In a district as large and diverse as Harlingen CISD, the FIE helps ensure that each child is seen as an individual and receives the personalized attention they deserve.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is called "full" because it looks at your child from multiple angles. Texas law requires that the evaluation assess all areas related to your child's suspected disability. Depending on your child's needs, the FIE may include:

**Cognitive Ability (Intelligence)**
This measures how your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems. It helps evaluators understand your child's learning potential.

**Academic Achievement**
These tests look at how your child is performing in reading, writing, and math compared to other students their age.

**Speech and Language Skills**
If there are concerns about how your child communicates, the FIE may include assessments of their speech, understanding of language, and ability to express themselves.

**Motor Skills**
Fine motor skills (like writing and cutting) and gross motor skills (like running and balance) may be evaluated if there are concerns in these areas.

**Social and Emotional Development**
This looks at how your child relates to others, manages emotions, and behaves in different settings.

**Adaptive Behavior**
These assessments examine your child's daily living skills and ability to function independently in age-appropriate ways.

**Health and Developmental History**
The evaluation team will review your child's medical history, developmental milestones, and any relevant health information.

**Observation**
Someone from the evaluation team will observe your child in their classroom or another natural setting to see how they function in a real-world environment.

**Parent and Teacher Input**
Your perspective as a parent is essential. You'll be asked to complete questionnaires and share your observations about your child. Teachers will also provide valuable input about your child's performance in school.

The specific assessments included in your child's FIE will depend on the concerns that prompted the evaluation. The goal is to gather enough information to make informed decisions about eligibility and services.

## How to Request a FIE at Harlingen CISD

As a parent, you have the right to request a FIE for your child at any time. You don't need to wait for the school to suggest it. If you believe your child may have a disability that is affecting their learning, here's how to request an evaluation in Harlingen CISD:

**Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing**

While you can make a verbal request, putting it in writing creates documentation and starts the clock on the legal timeline. Write a simple letter or email to your child's principal or the Harlingen CISD Special Education Department. Include:

- Your child's full name and date of birth
- Your child's school and grade
- A brief description of your concerns
- A clear statement that you are requesting a Full Individual Evaluation
- Your contact information
- The date

**Step 2: Submit Your Request**

You can deliver your written request to:

- Your child's school principal
- Your child's teacher (who should forward it to the appropriate administrator)
- The Harlingen CISD Special Education Department directly

Keep a copy of your request for your records, and note the date you submitted it.

**Step 3: Wait for the District's Response**

Once Harlingen CISD receives your written request, they have **15 school days** to respond. The district will either:

- Provide you with a "Notice and Consent for Evaluation" form to sign, OR
- Provide you with written notice explaining why they are refusing to evaluate, along with information about your rights

**Step 4: Give Your Consent**

If the district agrees to evaluate, you'll receive paperwork explaining what assessments will be conducted. The evaluation cannot begin until you sign and return the consent form. Take time to read the paperwork carefully, and don't hesitate to ask questions if anything is unclear.

**A Note About Referrals from Teachers**

Sometimes the school will initiate the referral process. A teacher, counselor, or other staff member who has concerns about your child may refer them for evaluation. If this happens, you'll still receive a consent form to sign before any testing can begin. Remember: you are always part of the process, and no evaluation can happen without your permission.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law establishes clear timelines to ensure that evaluations happen promptly. Here's what you need to know:

**Response to Your Request: 15 School Days**

After you submit a written request for evaluation, Harlingen CISD has 15 school days to either provide you with consent forms or give you written notice of their refusal to evaluate.

**Completion of the FIE: 45 School Days**

Once you sign and return the consent form, the district has **45 school days** to complete the evaluation. This timeline does not include days when school is not in session (like summer break, spring break, or holidays).

**ARD Meeting: 30 Calendar Days**

After the FIE is completed, the district must hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within **30 calendar days** to review the results and determine eligibility.

**Important Exceptions**

The 45-school-day timeline can be extended if:

- Your child is absent from school for an extended period
- You and the district mutually agree in writing to an extension

Keep track of these timelines and mark them on your calendar. If you feel the district is not meeting its legal obligations, you have the right to take action to ensure your child's evaluation is completed on time.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

When the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and filled with technical language, but understanding it is crucial for advocating for your child. Here's how to make sense of it:

**Request the Report in Advance**

You have the right to receive a copy of the FIE report before the ARD meeting. Ask the district to provide it at least a few days early so you have time to review it carefully.

**Key Sections to Look For**

- **Background Information** — A summary of your child's history, including developmental milestones, medical history, and educational background
- **Assessment Results** — Detailed findings from each test administered, usually including scores and what they mean
- **Observations** — Notes from classroom observations
- **Summary and Conclusions** — The evaluation team's interpretation
