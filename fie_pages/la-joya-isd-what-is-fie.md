# What Is a FIE? A Guide for La Joya ISD Parents

If you're a parent in La Joya ISD and you've been hearing terms like "FIE" or "Full Individual Evaluation" from your child's school, you're in the right place. Whether your child is struggling in class, has been recommended for testing, or you simply want to understand your options, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know about the FIE process here in La Joya ISD. You're not alone in this journey—thousands of Rio Grande Valley families navigate this process every year, and understanding your rights is the first step toward getting your child the support they deserve.

## What Is a FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)?

A **Full Individual Evaluation (FIE)** is Texas's comprehensive assessment process used to determine whether a child qualifies for special education services. Sometimes called a "psychoeducational evaluation," the FIE looks at all areas of your child's suspected disability to create a complete picture of their strengths, challenges, and educational needs.

Think of the FIE as a detailed roadmap of how your child learns. It's not just one test—it's a collection of assessments, observations, interviews, and reviews conducted by trained specialists. The FIE is required by both federal law (the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, or IDEA) and Texas state law (Texas Education Code Chapter 29) before any child can receive special education services.

The FIE is designed to answer important questions:
- Does my child have a disability that affects their learning?
- What are my child's unique strengths and challenges?
- What kind of support does my child need to succeed in school?

## Why Is a FIE Important at La Joya ISD?

La Joya ISD serves nearly 24,000 students across the Rio Grande Valley, and with a community this size, the district has dedicated staff and resources to support students with diverse learning needs. A FIE is important because it's the gateway to special education services—without it, your child cannot receive an Individualized Education Program (IEP) or access specialized instruction and support.

For La Joya ISD families specifically, the FIE process matters because:

- **It identifies your child's unique needs.** Every child is different, and a thorough evaluation ensures that any services provided are tailored specifically to your child.
- **It protects your child's rights.** Texas law requires that evaluations be conducted fairly, without bias, and in a way that accurately reflects your child's abilities.
- **It opens doors to support.** Once your child qualifies, La Joya ISD must provide appropriate services, accommodations, and modifications at no cost to your family.
- **It creates a baseline.** The FIE establishes where your child is starting from, making it easier to track progress over time.

## What Does a FIE Evaluate?

A FIE is comprehensive by design. Texas law requires that the evaluation assess your child in **all areas related to the suspected disability**. Depending on your child's needs, the FIE may include assessments in the following areas:

- **Cognitive ability (intelligence):** How your child thinks, reasons, and solves problems
- **Academic achievement:** Reading, writing, math, and other school-related skills
- **Communication:** Speech, language, and how your child expresses and understands information
- **Social and emotional development:** How your child interacts with others and manages emotions
- **Adaptive behavior:** Daily living skills and independence
- **Motor skills:** Fine motor (writing, cutting) and gross motor (running, jumping) abilities
- **Health and vision/hearing:** Any physical factors that might affect learning
- **Behavior:** Patterns of behavior that may impact learning

The evaluation team will use a variety of methods, including:
- Standardized tests
- Observations of your child in different settings
- Interviews with you, teachers, and your child
- Review of school records, report cards, and work samples
- Input from any outside professionals (doctors, therapists) if available

For families in La Joya ISD where Spanish is spoken at home, evaluations should be conducted in the language your child is most comfortable with to ensure accurate results.

## How to Request a FIE at La Joya ISD

As a parent, you have the right to request a FIE at any time if you suspect your child may have a disability that affects their learning. Here's how to make that request in La Joya ISD:

### Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing
While you can make a verbal request, we strongly recommend submitting your request **in writing**. This creates a paper trail and starts the legal timeline. You can write a simple letter or email like this:

> *Dear [Principal's Name or Special Education Department],*
>
> *I am the parent of [Child's Name], a student in [grade] at [school name]. I am requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) because I am concerned about [briefly describe your concerns—for example, "my child's difficulty with reading" or "my child's struggles to focus in class"]. Please contact me to discuss next steps.*
>
> *Thank you,*
> *[Your Name]*
> *[Date]*
> *[Phone number and email]*

### Step 2: Submit to the Right Place
You can give your written request to:
- Your child's principal
- The campus special education coordinator
- La Joya ISD's Special Education Department

**La Joya ISD Special Education Contact Information:**
- Phone: (956) 323-2600
- Address: 200 W. Expressway 83, La Joya, TX 78560

### Step 3: Receive and Sign Consent
Once the district receives your request, they must respond. If they agree to evaluate, you'll receive a **Notice of Proposed Evaluation** along with a **Consent for Evaluation** form. Your child cannot be tested until you sign and return this consent form.

If the district refuses to evaluate, they must provide you with written notice explaining why. You have the right to challenge this decision.

## The FIE Timeline: What Texas Law Requires

Texas law sets strict deadlines to protect families and ensure evaluations happen promptly. Here's what you need to know:

| Step | Timeline |
|------|----------|
| District responds to your request | Within a **reasonable time** (typically 15 school days) |
| You receive consent forms | Before evaluation can begin |
| FIE must be completed | Within **45 school days** after the district receives your signed consent |
| ARD/IEP meeting to review results | Within **30 calendar days** after the FIE is completed |

**Important notes about the 45-school-day timeline:**
- School days do not include holidays, weekends, or summer break (unless your child attends summer school)
- If your child is absent for extended periods, this may affect the timeline
- You and the district can mutually agree to extend the timeline in writing

If La Joya ISD misses these deadlines without a valid reason, this is a compliance issue, and you have the right to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency.

## Understanding Your FIE Report

Once the evaluation is complete, you'll receive a written FIE report. This document can be lengthy and technical, but understanding it is crucial for advocating for your child. Here's what to look for:

### Key Sections of the FIE Report

1. **Background Information:** Your child's history, including developmental milestones, medical history, and previous school performance

2. **Assessment Results:** Scores and findings from each evaluation area (cognitive, academic, behavioral, etc.)

3. **Present Levels of Performance:** A description of how your child is currently functioning in school

4. **Eligibility Determination:** Whether your child meets the criteria for one or more of Texas's 13 disability categories under IDEA

5. **Recommendations:** Suggestions for services, accommodations, or supports your child may need

### Tips for Reviewing the FIE

- **Request a copy before the ARD meeting** so you have time to review it
- **Highlight terms you don't understand** and ask for explanations
- **Compare test scores to the "average" ranges** provided in the report
- **Look at the recommendations section carefully**—these often guide the IEP
- **Ask questions!** You have every right to understand what the report says about your child
