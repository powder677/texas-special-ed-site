Complete Guide to Requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in KENNEDALE ISD

If you're a parent in KENNEDALE ISD and suspect your child may have a learning disability, developmental delay, or other condition affecting their ability to learn, you have the right to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE). This comprehensive guide will walk you through the entire process, timelines, and your rights under Texas special education law.

Understanding Your Rights: Child Find in KENNEDALE ISD

KENNEDALE ISD is legally required to identify, locate, and evaluate all children within the district who may have disabilities. This is called "child find," and it's a fundamental principle of special education law. As a parent of a KENNEDALE ISD student, you have the right to initiate this process at any time.

The child find obligation means KENNEDALE ISD cannot wait for a teacher to refer your child or for you to have a formal diagnosis. If you believe your child needs an evaluation, KENNEDALE ISD must respond to your request, even if school staff disagree. This protection ensures no child in KENNEDALE ISD falls through the cracks.

Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §29.004, KENNEDALE ISD families have the right to request a special education evaluation. This is one of the most powerful tools available to parents in KENNEDALE ISD.

How to Request a KENNEDALE ISD FIE Evaluation

Step 1: Submit Your Written Request

The most important thing to know: your request must be in writing. While a verbal conversation with a teacher or counselor might feel productive, KENNEDALE ISD special education law requires a formal, written KENNEDALE ISD evaluation request to start the legal timeline.

Your written request should be addressed to the Director of Special Education at KENNEDALE ISD. Include the following information:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Your child's grade level and current school within KENNEDALE ISD
  • Your contact information (phone and email)
  • A brief description of your concerns about your child's learning or behavior
  • Specific areas where you believe your child is struggling
  • Your request for a complete FIE evaluation under IDEA

Step 2: Deliver Your Request to the Right Department

Contact KENNEDALE ISD's Special Education Department directly. You can deliver your request by:

  • Hand delivery: Walk in to the KENNEDALE ISD administration office or your child's school
  • Certified mail: Send a certified letter to ensure proof of delivery
  • Email: Request the special education department's email and ask for a read receipt confirmation
  • Registered mail: Another formal method that creates a paper trail

For parents in KENNEDALE ISD, we recommend certified mail or email with confirmation. This creates documentation that KENNEDALE ISD received your request, which is crucial if questions arise later about timelines.

Step 3: Keep Detailed Records

From the moment you submit your KENNEDALE ISD special education evaluation request, document everything. Save copies of:

  • Your written evaluation request
  • Proof of delivery (certified mail receipt, email confirmation, hand-delivery witness)
  • All correspondence with KENNEDALE ISD staff
  • Dates of phone calls and who you spoke with
  • Your child's report cards, benchmark test scores, and teacher notes

This documentation becomes invaluable if you need to pursue due process or work with a special ed advocate near KENNEDALE later.

Understanding the KENNEDALE ISD 45-Day Timeline

One of the most critical aspects of a KENNEDALE ISD FIE evaluation is the legal timeline. Once KENNEDALE ISD receives your written request, they have 45 calendar days to complete the full evaluation and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss results.

The KENNEDALE ISD 45-day timeline begins on the date KENNEDALE ISD receives your written request—not when you verbally mention it to a teacher. This is why written documentation is essential.

What Happens During the 45 Days?

Within the KENNEDALE ISD evaluation process, the district must:

  1. Obtain your written consent before testing begins
  2. Conduct comprehensive assessments in all areas of suspected disability
  3. Administer multiple tests and gather information from multiple sources
  4. Review your child's medical history, developmental background, and school performance
  5. Complete a full written evaluation report
  6. Schedule and conduct an ARD meeting with you

For KENNEDALE ISD families, understanding this timeline means you know when to expect results. If KENNEDALE ISD misses the 45-day deadline without your agreement to extend it, they are in violation of federal law.

Possible Extensions to the KENNEDALE ISD Timeline

KENNEDALE ISD can extend the 45-day KENNEDALE ISD evaluation timeline under specific circumstances, but only if both you and the district agree. Common reasons for extensions include:

  • You request additional time
  • Your child is absent frequently during the evaluation period
  • Medical evaluations outside KENNEDALE ISD need to be incorporated
  • Exceptional circumstances prevent timely completion

Important: Any extension must be agreed to in writing. KENNEDALE ISD cannot unilaterally extend the timeline. If they ask for an extension, you can say "no," and they must complete the KENNEDALE ISD FIE evaluation within the original 45 days.

What Will Be Evaluated in KENNEDALE ISD?

A comprehensive KENNEDALE ISD special education evaluation must assess all areas of suspected disability. This is not a quick screening—it's a thorough, multidisciplinary evaluation.

Areas Typically Assessed in KENNEDALE ISD FIE Evaluations

  • Cognitive/Intellectual Ability: Overall thinking and reasoning skills
  • Academic Achievement: Reading, writing, mathematics, and language abilities
  • Speech and Language: Communication skills and language processing
  • Social-Emotional/Behavioral: Emotional regulation, social skills, and behavior patterns
  • Motor Skills: Fine and gross motor development if relevant
  • Hearing and Vision: Sensory screening to rule out hearing or vision problems
  • Adaptive Behavior: How your child functions in daily living situations

Your KENNEDALE ISD evaluation will include input from teachers, specialists, and you as the parent. The goal is a complete picture of your child's strengths and needs.

The ARD Meeting: Discussing KENNEDALE ISD Evaluation Results

Once KENNEDALE ISD completes the evaluation within the 45-day window, you'll attend an ARD meeting. This meeting discusses the evaluation findings and determines whether your child qualifies for special education services in KENNEDALE ISD.

What to Expect at the KENNEDALE ISD ARD Meeting

The ARD team at KENNEDALE ISD will include:

  • You (the parent)
  • Your child (in some cases)
  • A KENNEDALE ISD special education teacher or coordinator
  • General education teacher(s)
  • Evaluators who conducted the KENNEDALE ISD assessments
  • A KENNEDALE ISD administrator
  • Anyone you invite (advocate, attorney, grandparent, etc.)

During this meeting, KENNEDALE ISD staff will review the evaluation results and discuss whether your child has a disability and qualifies for special education. If your child qualifies, KENNEDALE ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Your Rights During the KENNEDALE ISD ARD Meeting

As a parent in KENNEDALE ISD, you have important rights during the ARD meeting:

  • Right to bring support: Bring a special ed advocate near KENNEDALE or another trusted person
  • Right to ask questions: Understand every test result and recommendation
  • Right to disagree: You can request independent evaluations if you disagree with KENNEDALE ISD's findings
  • Right to take time: You don't have to sign documents at the meeting
  • Right to copies: KENNEDALE ISD must provide you with a copy of the evaluation report

If KENNEDALE ISD Says Your Child Doesn't Qualify

Not every child referred for evaluation in KENNEDALE ISD will qualify for special education services. However, if you disagree with KENNEDALE ISD's finding that your child doesn't have a disability, you have options.

Requesting an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE)

If you believe KENNEDALE ISD's evaluation was incomplete or inaccurate, you can request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at public expense. KENNEDALE ISD must provide information about where to obtain an IEE and may be required to pay for it.

Pursuing Due Process or Mediation

Parents in KENNEDALE ISD can file for due process if they disagree with KENNEDALE ISD's evaluation or eligibility determination. Consider consulting with a special education attorney near KENNEDALE who can review your child's case and advise you on next steps.

Mediation is another option where KENNEDALE ISD and you work with a neutral third party to resolve disagreements outside the due process hearing system.

Working with Special Education Advocates and Attorneys Near KENNEDALE

Throughout the KENNEDALE ISD FIE evaluation process, you may benefit from professional support. A special ed advocate near KENNEDALE can attend meetings with you, explain results, and help you understand your rights. If legal issues arise, a special education attorney near KENNEDALE can represent your interests.

Many parents in KENNEDALE ISD find that having an advocate or attorney present makes the process less stressful and helps ensure their child's needs are properly addressed by KENNEDALE ISD.

As you navigate the KENNEDALE ISD special education evaluation process, remember these critical points:

  • Know the timeline: KENNEDALE ISD has 45 days to complete your child's evaluation
