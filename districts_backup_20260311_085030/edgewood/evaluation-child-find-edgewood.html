Complete Guide to Requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in EDGEWOOD ISD

If you're a parent in EDGEWOOD ISD concerned about your child's learning or development, understanding how to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is one of the most important steps you can take. This comprehensive guide walks you through the entire process for obtaining a special education evaluation in EDGEWOOD ISD, including timelines, your rights, and what to expect at every stage.

Understanding the Full Individual Evaluation in EDGEWOOD ISD

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment conducted by EDGEWOOD ISD special education professionals to determine whether your child has a disability and qualifies for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). The evaluation examines your child's academic performance, cognitive abilities, social-emotional functioning, and physical health.

In EDGEWOOD ISD, the FIE process is designed to identify students who may need specialized instruction and related services. This evaluation is the foundation for developing an Individualized Education Program (IEP) if your child qualifies for special education.

The evaluation process in EDGEWOOD ISD must be:

  • Comprehensive – covering all areas of suspected disability
  • Nondiscriminatory – conducted in your child's native language when appropriate
  • Conducted by qualified professionals – certified specialists and trained evaluators
  • Timely – completed within the EDGEWOOD ISD 45 day timeline

Child Find and Early Identification in EDGEWOOD ISD

EDGEWOOD ISD has a responsibility under Texas law and federal IDEA regulations to identify all children who may have disabilities and need special education services. This is called the "child find" obligation. If you believe your child may need special education services, EDGEWOOD ISD must respond to your concerns.

Who Can Request an Evaluation?

In EDGEWOOD ISD, any of the following people can request a special education evaluation:

  • Parent or legal guardian
  • Classroom teacher or other school staff member
  • School counselor or administrator
  • Healthcare provider with written parental consent
  • Juvenile justice or child welfare agency representative

As a parent in EDGEWOOD ISD, you have the right to request an evaluation at any time, regardless of your child's current grades or performance level. EDGEWOOD ISD cannot deny your request without a documented reason related to existing recent evaluations.

Warning Signs That Your Child May Need Evaluation

Consider requesting an EDGEWOOD ISD child find evaluation if your child:

  • Is significantly behind peers academically despite intervention attempts
  • Struggles with reading, math, writing, or language skills
  • Has difficulty concentrating, following directions, or managing behavior
  • Shows signs of developmental delays (preschool-age children)
  • Has been bullied or is socially isolated
  • Exhibits anxiety, depression, or emotional dysregulation
  • Has received special services from early intervention (Part C) programs

Step-by-Step Process for Requesting an EDGEWOOD ISD FIE Evaluation

Step 1: Document Your Concerns

Before formally requesting an evaluation from EDGEWOOD ISD, document specific examples of your concerns:

  • When you first noticed the concern
  • Specific behaviors, skills gaps, or difficulties
  • What strategies you've tried at home
  • Medical or developmental history that's relevant
  • Any evaluations completed outside of EDGEWOOD ISD (pediatric assessments, private evaluations, etc.)

Having detailed documentation strengthens your request and helps EDGEWOOD ISD understand your perspective clearly.

Step 2: Submit a Written Request for Evaluation

While EDGEWOOD ISD may accept verbal requests, submitting a written evaluation request creates an official record. Address your request to:

  • Special Education Director of EDGEWOOD ISD, or
  • Your child's campus principal, or
  • Special Education Coordinator at your child's school

Your written request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current grade level and campus
  • Specific areas of concern
  • Examples of difficulties you've observed
  • Any previous interventions attempted
  • Your contact information and preferred communication method

Consider requesting delivery confirmation or sending your request through certified mail so you have proof EDGEWOOD ISD received it. This is particularly important for the EDGEWOOD ISD 45 day timeline, which begins when the district receives your request.

Step 3: Provide Informed Consent

Once EDGEWOOD ISD receives your evaluation request, the district must provide you with a Notice of Procedural Safeguards and request your informed written consent to conduct the evaluation.

Before signing the consent form for EDGEWOOD ISD special education evaluation, you should:

  • Understand what the evaluation will assess
  • Know which professionals will conduct the evaluation
  • Review any testing materials if available
  • Ask questions about the process and timeline
  • Understand your right to participate in the evaluation conference

Do not sign consent if you feel pressured or unclear about any aspect of the process. You have the right to ask for clarification and to review all materials before consenting.

Step 4: Participate in the Pre-Evaluation Conference

After EDGEWOOD ISD receives your consent, many schools schedule a pre-evaluation conference with you. This meeting allows you to:

  • Share background information about your child
  • Discuss your specific concerns in detail
  • Review the evaluation plan
  • Ask questions about the assessment process
  • Understand what will be tested and why
  • Clarify the EDGEWOOD ISD 45 day timeline for completion

Come prepared with medical records, previous evaluations, developmental history, and any behavioral or academic concerns you want addressed in the EDGEWOOD ISD FIE evaluation.

The EDGEWOOD ISD 45 Day Timeline for Evaluation

This is critical: EDGEWOOD ISD must complete the evaluation within 45 calendar days of receiving your written consent. This timeline is required by Texas Education Code § 29.004 and federal IDEA regulations.

Understanding the 45-Day Clock

  • Day 1: EDGEWOOD ISD receives your written consent to evaluate
  • Days 2-45: All evaluation activities, testing, and assessments must be completed
  • Day 45 or before: EDGEWOOD ISD must schedule the evaluation conference to present findings and determine eligibility

The 45 days can be extended only if:

  • You agree in writing to an extension, or
  • EDGEWOOD ISD cannot schedule the evaluation conference within the timeframe due to documented circumstances (like testing delays)

In EDGEWOOD ISD, if the 45-day timeline passes without completion, you can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

What Happens During the Evaluation

For your EDGEWOOD ISD special education evaluation, expect these components:

  • Cognitive/Intellectual Testing – measures thinking and reasoning skills
  • Academic Achievement Testing – assesses reading, math, writing, and language abilities
  • Social-Emotional Assessment – evaluates behavior and emotional functioning
  • Classroom Observations – direct observation in school settings
  • Teacher and Parent Interviews – gathering information from multiple perspectives
  • Review of School Records – examining grades, attendance, discipline, and previous assessments
  • Vision and Hearing Screening – rule out sensory issues
  • Medical/Developmental History Review – understanding health factors that may impact learning

You may request that specific areas be included in your child's EDGEWOOD ISD child find evaluation based on your concerns.

Your Rights During the EDGEWOOD ISD Evaluation Process

Texas law and IDEA regulations protect your rights as a parent in EDGEWOOD ISD. Understanding these protections empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Key Parent Rights in EDGEWOOD ISD

  • Right to Written Notice – receive notice in your native language explaining the evaluation plan
  • Right to Informed Consent – give permission before evaluation begins
  • Right to Independent Evaluation – request evaluation by outside professional if you disagree with EDGEWOOD ISD's findings
  • Right to Participate – attend all evaluation conferences and meetings
  • Right to Interpret Results – ask questions and request explanations during the evaluation conference
  • Right to Records – access all evaluation results and student records
  • Right to Due Process – file a complaint if you disagree with decisions made by EDGEWOOD ISD
  • Right to Interpreter Services – request interpretation if you speak a language other than English

EDGEWOOD ISD must provide you with a Notice of Procedural Safeguards that fully explains these rights in writing.

The Evaluation Conference: Understanding EDGEWOOD ISD Findings

At the end of the 45-day evaluation period, EDGEWOOD ISD must conduct an evaluation conference to discuss results and determine your child's eligibility for special education services.

What to Expect at the Conference

At this meeting, the EDGEWOOD ISD evaluation team will:

  • Present test results and findings from each evaluator
  • Explain what the scores mean in plain language
  • Discuss strengths and areas of concern
  • Determine if your child meets eligibility criteria for a disability category under IDEA
  • Decide if your child needs special education services
  • Invite you to participate in developing an IEP (if eligible)

You have the right to bring support to this meeting, including a special education advocate or attorney if desired.

Possible Outcomes from EDGEWOOD ISD Evaluation

Outcome 1: Eligible for Special Education

If EDGEWOOD ISD determines your child has a disability and needs special education, an IEP meeting will be scheduled within 30 days to develop a comprehensive education plan.

Outcome 2: Not Eligible / Consider Section 504

If EDGEWOOD ISD finds your child doesn't qualify for IDEA special education, you may still qualify for accommodations under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act. Request this evaluation if not automatically offered.

Outcome 3: Inconclusive / Need Additional Testing

Sometimes EDGEWOOD ISD may determine that additional evaluations are needed. You can request an independent evaluation or seek outside testing to provide more information.

Common Questions About EDGEWOOD ISD Evaluations

Can EDGEWOOD ISD refuse to evaluate my child?

EDGEWOOD ISD can only refuse evaluation if you've already had a recent comprehensive evaluation for the same suspected disability. The district must provide documented reason for refusal. If refused, you can request a due process hearing or file a complaint with TEA.

What if I disagree with EDGEWOOD ISD's evaluation results?

You have the right to request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at public expense if you disagree with EDGEWOOD ISD's findings. This evaluation can be conducted by a qualified professional outside the school district.
