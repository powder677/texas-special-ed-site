Who Qualifies for WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD Special Education?

Students ages 3-21 in WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD may qualify for special education if they have a disability and require specialized instruction. Under Texas Education Code, eligibility categories include:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Deaf-Blindness
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Specific Learning Disability (including dyslexia)
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Visual Impairment

If you believe your child may qualify, contact WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD's special education department to request a comprehensive evaluation.

How to Access WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD IEP Services

An Individualized Education Program (IEP) is a legal document that outlines your child's special education services in WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD. The IEP process is your pathway to accessing specialized support.

The Initial Evaluation Process

The first step toward WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD IEP services begins with evaluation. Here's what to expect:

  1. Request Evaluation: Contact your child's school principal or the special education department in writing, requesting a comprehensive evaluation
  2. Written Notice: WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD will provide written notice of the evaluation plan within a reasonable timeframe
  3. Comprehensive Assessment: Evaluators will assess your child across multiple areas—cognitive, academic, social-emotional, physical, and developmental
  4. ARD Meeting: Within 60 calendar days, WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD holds an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to determine eligibility

Parents in WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD play a critical role in this process. You'll receive all evaluation reports before the ARD meeting and have time to review the findings with professionals if desired.

Understanding the IEP Document

Your child's IEP developed through WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD special education includes:

  • Present level of academic and functional performance
  • Measurable annual goals aligned with grade-level standards
  • Description of specialized services and supports
  • Frequency and duration of services
  • Accommodations and modifications needed
  • Progress monitoring procedures
  • Transition services (for students age 14+)

The IEP is reviewed annually at your WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD ARD meeting, with opportunities to request changes at any time if your child's needs shift.

Your Rights: WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD Parent Rights in Special Education

WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD parent rights are guaranteed under federal law. Parents must receive written notice of their procedural rights in their native language, and the district is required to ensure you understand these rights fully.

Key Parent Rights in WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD Special Education

As a parent in WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD, you have the right to:

  • Participate meaningfully in all ARD meetings and educational decisions
  • Request evaluations and independent educational evaluations (IEE) at no cost
  • Review all records related to your child's evaluation and education
  • Receive notice in writing before any changes to services or placement
  • Request prior written notice in a language you understand
  • Access dispute resolution through due process hearings or mediation if you disagree with WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD's decisions
  • Bring advocates or attorneys to ARD meetings
  • Obtain copies of all IEP documents and evaluation reports

WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD families should understand that these rights exist to ensure your child receives an appropriate, free public education. Don't hesitate to ask questions or request clarification during ARD meetings.

WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD Dyslexia Program and Specialized Services

Dyslexia is one of the most common specific learning disabilities, and WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD recognizes the importance of early intervention and specialized instruction. If your child has been identified with dyslexia or suspected dyslexia, the district provides targeted support.

What to Know About the WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD Dyslexia Program

Texas Education Code requires districts to provide evidence-based dyslexia interventions. The WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD dyslexia program includes:

  • Screening and identification procedures
  • Specialized reading instruction using approved interventions
  • Progress monitoring with frequent assessments
  • Accommodations such as extended time, audio textbooks, or text-to-speech tools
  • Parent communication about intervention effectiveness

If you suspect your child has dyslexia, request evaluation through WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD. The district cannot wait until your child falls significantly behind to provide support—early intervention is the most effective approach.

Preparing for Your WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD ARD Meeting

The WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD ARD meeting is where critical decisions about your child's education happen. Coming prepared ensures your voice is heard and your child's needs are fully addressed.

Steps to Prepare for Your ARD Meeting

  1. Request documents in advance: Ask for all evaluation reports, current IEP (if applicable), progress reports, and assessments 10 days before the meeting
  2. Review with a clear mind: Read all materials carefully and write down questions or concerns
  3. Gather your observations: Document specific strengths, challenges, and behaviors you observe at home
  4. Consider bringing support: Invite your spouse, a trusted family member, a special education advocate, or an attorney if you believe you'll need advocacy support
  5. Prepare your input: Write down what services, accommodations, or modifications you believe your child needs
  6. Understand the timeline: The meeting should last long enough to thoroughly discuss your child's needs—don't let WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD rush the process

Remember: You are the expert on your child. Your perspective is essential to creating an effective IEP through WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD's special education process.

What Happens During the Meeting

WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD ARD meetings follow a structured format. The team will discuss your child's current performance, determine goals for the coming year, identify services and supports needed, and decide on the least restrictive environment where your child can receive services while making meaningful progress.

If you disagree with any decisions made during your WEST ORANGE-COVE CISD ARD meeting, you can ask for the decision in writing and request mediation or a due process hearing to resolve the disagreement.
