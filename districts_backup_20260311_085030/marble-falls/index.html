Understanding Special Education in MARBLE FALLS ISD

Special education in MARBLE FALLS ISD is designed to provide individualized support for students with disabilities under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code. The district is committed to ensuring every child receives a free, appropriate public education in the least restrictive environment.

MARBLE FALLS ISD special education programs serve students with various disabilities including:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Emotional and Behavioral Disorders
  • Intellectual Disabilities
  • Specific Learning Disabilities
  • Speech and Language Impairments
  • Physical and Other Health Impairments
  • Visual and Hearing Impairments
  • Multiple Disabilities

Each student in MARBLE FALLS ISD special education receives an individualized education program (IEP) tailored to their unique needs and strengths.

The ARD Meeting Process in MARBLE FALLS ISD

The Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting is the foundation of special education in MARBLE FALLS ISD. This critical meeting brings together parents, teachers, and specialists to develop or review your child's educational plan.

What Happens During a MARBLE FALLS ISD ARD Meeting?

During a MARBLE FALLS ISD ARD meeting, the team reviews your child's current performance, discusses strengths and areas for improvement, and determines appropriate goals and services. The meeting typically lasts 60-90 minutes and includes discussion of placement, accommodations, and related services.

Key topics covered in MARBLE FALLS ISD ARD meetings include:

  • Present level of academic and functional performance
  • Annual goals aligned with grade-level standards
  • Special education and related services needed
  • Classroom placement and integration opportunities
  • Progress monitoring and evaluation procedures
  • Accommodations and modifications
  • Transition planning (for students ages 14 and up)

Your Rights During the ARD Process

Parents in MARBLE FALLS ISD have significant rights during the ARD meeting process. You have the right to be a full member of the team with equal voice in all decisions. You can bring additional people to the meeting, request interpreters, and ask for extra time to review documents before making decisions.

You also have the right to request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) if you disagree with MARBLE FALLS ISD's evaluation results, and the district must provide you with information about obtaining one at no cost in certain circumstances.

MARBLE FALLS ISD IEP Services and Support

MARBLE FALLS ISD IEP services are individualized based on your child's specific needs. An IEP is a legal document that outlines all the special education and related services your child will receive.

Types of Services Available

MARBLE FALLS ISD provides comprehensive services through its special education program, including:

  • Special Education Instruction: Pull-out or inclusion-based instruction from certified special education teachers
  • Speech and Language Services: Support from speech-language pathologists for communication needs
  • Occupational Therapy: Services addressing fine motor skills and daily living activities
  • Physical Therapy: Support for gross motor development and mobility
  • School Counseling: Mental health and emotional support services
  • Social Skills Groups: Programs developing peer interaction and social awareness
  • Assistive Technology: Tools and devices to support learning and communication
  • Transition Services: Planning and support for post-secondary life for secondary students

The specific combination of services in your child's MARBLE FALLS ISD IEP depends on their individual assessment results and identified needs.

Service Delivery Models

MARBLE FALLS ISD offers flexible service delivery options to meet different student needs. Some students receive instruction primarily in general education classrooms with supplementary supports, while others benefit from a combination of general and special education settings.

The goal for all MARBLE FALLS ISD families is finding the right placement that supports your child's academic and social-emotional growth while maximizing exposure to grade-level content and peer interaction.

MARBLE FALLS ISD Parent Rights and Procedural Safeguards

Federal law and Texas Education Code provide significant protections for parents of students receiving special education. Understanding your rights helps you advocate effectively for your child within MARBLE FALLS ISD.

Key Parent Rights in MARBLE FALLS ISD

As a parent of a student in MARBLE FALLS ISD special education, you have the right to:

  • Notification: Receive written notice before any evaluation or change in placement
  • Prior Written Consent: Approve initial evaluations and IEP implementation before services begin
  • Participation: Be a full member of all IEP and ARD meetings affecting your child
  • Records Access: Review all educational records, testing data, and assessments
  • Confidentiality: Have your child's information protected under FERPA regulations
  • Independent Evaluation: Request an independent educational evaluation at district expense in certain situations
  • Mediation and Dispute Resolution: Access free or low-cost mediation to resolve disagreements
  • Due Process Hearing: Request a hearing if you disagree with the district's decisions

MARBLE FALLS ISD must provide you with a copy of these procedural safeguards in writing, at least annually or when a due process hearing is requested.

Parent Rights in Special Circumstances

Special situations arise in every MARBLE FALLS ISD special education program. Parents have additional specific rights regarding discipline, manifestation determinations, compensatory education services, and residential placement decisions. Understanding these rights ensures you can advocate effectively when challenges occur.

MARBLE FALLS ISD Dyslexia Program and Literacy Support

Texas Education Code mandates that school districts identify students with dyslexia and provide specialized instruction. MARBLE FALLS ISD has a dedicated MARBLE FALLS ISD dyslexia program serving students with identified dyslexia and related reading disabilities.

How Dyslexia is Identified

Students in MARBLE FALLS ISD may be identified as having dyslexia through various screening measures and comprehensive evaluations. Teachers can refer students for evaluation if they show signs of reading difficulty despite quality instruction. Parents can also request evaluation if they suspect dyslexia.

The MARBLE FALLS ISD dyslexia program uses evidence-based interventions that address the core features of dyslexia including phonological awareness, phonemic awareness, decoding, and fluency.

Specialized Instruction Options

Students in MARBLE FALLS ISD with identified dyslexia receive specialized literacy instruction using multisensory, structured language approaches. Many students receive instruction from teachers certified in dyslexia intervention, using programs with strong research support.

Progress is monitored regularly, and instruction is adjusted based on student response to intervention. The goal is equipping students with the skills and strategies needed for reading success beyond MARBLE FALLS ISD.

Getting Started: Next Steps for MARBLE FALLS ISD Parents

If your child is new to special education in MARBLE FALLS ISD, the process can feel overwhelming. Here's a clear action plan to get started:

  1. Request an Evaluation: If your child hasn't been evaluated, contact your school's special education department in writing to request an initial evaluation. MARBLE FALLS ISD has 15 school days to respond and 60 days to complete the evaluation.
  2. Attend the Initial ARD Meeting: Once evaluation is complete, MARBLE FALLS ISD will schedule an ARD meeting to discuss results and develop your child's first IEP.
  3. Understand the IEP: Request clarification on any part of the IEP you don't understand before you sign. You don't have to agree if you have concerns.
  4. Stay Involved: Attend ARD meetings, communicate regularly with teachers, and monitor your child's progress throughout the year.
  5. Know Your Rights: Keep the procedural safeguards document MARBLE FALLS ISD provides and refer to it when questions arise.
  6. Seek Support if Needed: Don't hesitate to connect with advocates or attorneys if you need guidance navigating complex situations within MARBLE FALLS ISD special education.
