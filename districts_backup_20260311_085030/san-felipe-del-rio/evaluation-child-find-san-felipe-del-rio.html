Understanding What an FIE Is and Why It Matters

A Full Individual Evaluation is a comprehensive assessment conducted by SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD to determine whether your child has a disability and qualifies for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). This evaluation goes far beyond a simple screening—it examines your child's academic performance, cognitive abilities, social-emotional functioning, and physical health.

For parents in SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD, an FIE serves as the foundation for all special education decisions. Without a proper evaluation, your child cannot be identified as eligible for services, and you won't have access to the accommodations and support they may desperately need to succeed in school.

The SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD child find system is designed to identify students who may need evaluation, but as a parent, you also have the right to request an evaluation at any time if you believe your child needs services.

Your Legal Rights: Understanding Child Find in SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD

Texas Education Code Section 504.001 requires all school districts, including SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD, to conduct child find activities. This means the district must actively search for and identify all children who may have disabilities and need special education services.

What This Means for Your Family

The SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD child find obligation includes:

  • Actively seeking students who may be unidentified or underidentified
  • Providing referral information to families at the beginning of each school year
  • Training staff to recognize signs of potential disabilities
  • Accepting referrals from parents, teachers, and community members
  • Conducting evaluations in a timely manner once a referral is made

As a parent in SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD, you don't have to wait for the district to identify your child. You have the right to request a SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD evaluation request at any time, and the district must respond to your written request within specific timeframes.

How to Request a Full Individual Evaluation

The process for requesting a SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD FIE evaluation is straightforward, though it requires you to follow specific procedures to ensure your request is documented and acted upon promptly.

Step 1: Submit Your Written Request

You must submit your evaluation request in writing to be protected by the law. While a verbal conversation with a teacher is a good start, a written request triggers the official timeline. Send your letter to:

  • Your child's campus principal
  • The Director of Special Education for SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD
  • Or both (sending to both is recommended)

Include in your letter:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current campus and grade level
  • Specific concerns about your child's learning or development
  • Examples of how these concerns affect school performance
  • A clear request for a Full Individual Evaluation
  • Your contact information and preferred method of communication

Pro tip: Send your request via email or certified mail with return receipt to create documentation. Keep copies for your records.

Step 2: Expect a Written Response

Within 15 calendar days of receiving your written request for a SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD special education evaluation, the district must provide you with written notice describing:

  • Whether they will evaluate your child
  • If they agree: proposed evaluation procedures and timeline
  • If they refuse: the reasons and your right to request a due process hearing

If SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD refuses to evaluate your child, you can appeal. This is a critical protection under Texas special education law—the district cannot simply ignore your request.

The SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD 45-Day Timeline Explained

One of the most important timelines in special education is the SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD 45 day timeline for completing evaluations. This comes from Texas Education Code Section 504.032 and is a hard deadline that protects parents and students.

When Does the 45-Day Clock Start?

The 45-day period begins on the date SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD receives your written consent for evaluation. This is why it's critical to provide written consent promptly—every day counts.

What Must Happen Within the SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD 45-Day Timeline

Within 45 calendar days, the district must:

  • Complete all evaluation activities
  • Compile evaluation results
  • Schedule and conduct the Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting
  • Determine eligibility for special education services
  • If eligible, develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP)

Important: This 45-day deadline applies to most students in SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD. The only exceptions are when the parent cannot be reached for the ARD meeting or there are unusual circumstances that prevent completion. The district must document any exceptions in writing.

What Happens if SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD Misses the Deadline

If the district fails to meet the SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD 45 day timeline, this is considered a procedural violation. You have rights to remedies, including compensatory education services or other forms of relief. Contact a special education attorney near SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO if you believe the district has missed this deadline.

What Evaluations Are Included in an FIE

A comprehensive SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD FIE evaluation includes multiple components to get a complete picture of your child's abilities and needs:

Academic Assessment

Tests measuring reading, mathematics, writing, and other academic skills. These assessments compare your child's performance to grade-level standards.

Cognitive and Intellectual Assessment

Evaluations of reasoning, problem-solving, processing speed, and overall cognitive functioning. These help identify intellectual disabilities or giftedness.

Social, Emotional, and Behavioral Assessment

Evaluations examining emotional functioning, behavior, social skills, and mental health. This is crucial for identifying conditions like autism, emotional disturbance, or other disabilities affecting school performance.

Speech and Language Assessment

If your child has concerns with communication, speech-language pathologists in SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD will evaluate language development, articulation, and voice quality.

Physical and Developmental Assessment

Assessment of motor skills, physical health, and developmental milestones. For some students, occupational or physical therapy evaluations may be included.

Vision and Hearing Screening

Basic screenings to rule out sensory impairments affecting learning.

The exact evaluations included in your child's SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD special education evaluation depend on your child's specific concerns and the district's recommendations.

Your Rights During the SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD Evaluation Process

As a parent in SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD, you have important protections throughout the evaluation process:

Prior Written Notice and Informed Consent

SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD must provide you with written notice of their evaluation plan and obtain your informed consent before beginning any evaluation. You have the right to ask questions and request modifications to the proposed evaluation plan.

Access to Records

You can request and review all evaluation data, testing materials, and progress reports related to your child's SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD special education evaluation. The district must provide these within reasonable timeframes.

Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE)

If you disagree with SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD's evaluation, you can request an Independent Educational Evaluation at district expense under certain circumstances. Consult a special ed advocate in SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO for guidance on this option.

Interpreter and Communication Support

If your family speaks a language other than English, SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD must provide interpreters and communication supports throughout the evaluation and ARD process.

The ARD Meeting: What Happens After the SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD Evaluation

After completing the SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD FIE evaluation, the district must schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss results and make eligibility decisions.

Who Attends the ARD Meeting

The ARD meeting must include:

  • You (the parent or guardian)
  • Your child (when appropriate)
  • Special education teacher(s)
  • General education teacher(s)
  • District representative or administrator
  • Person who can interpret evaluation results
  • Others you or the district request

What the ARD Committee Decides

The ARD committee will:

  • Review all evaluation results from your child's SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD special education evaluation
  • Determine if your child meets eligibility criteria under IDEA
  • If eligible, identify your child's disability category
  • If eligible, develop an IEP with specific goals and services
  • Determine the least restrictive environment for your child's education

This meeting should occur within the SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD 45 day timeline from when you provided consent for evaluation.

Common Questions About SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD Evaluations

Yes. You don't need a teacher to agree. Simply submit your written request for a SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD special education evaluation directly to the principal or Special Education Director. The district must respond within 15 days.

If the ARD committee determines your child is not eligible despite completing the SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD FIE evaluation, you have the right to request a due process hearing or file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency.

How Long Does a SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD Evaluation Take?

The entire process, from your written request to the ARD meeting, should take approximately 45 days under the SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD 45 day timeline. However, the actual assessment activities may occur over several weeks within that window.

Can I Participate in Testing?
