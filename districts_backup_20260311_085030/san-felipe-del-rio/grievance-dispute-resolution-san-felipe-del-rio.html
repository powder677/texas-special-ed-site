SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD Special Education Complaint Process

When informal resolution doesn't work, filing a formal complaint with SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD initiates the district-level resolution process. A SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD special education complaint must be filed in writing and should detail the specific violation of IDEA or state special education regulations. The district has 10 business days to respond to your complaint.

Your complaint should name the district, identify your child, describe the problem, and explain what resolution you seek. Submit complaints to the Special Education Director at SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD. Request written confirmation of receipt, including a date stamp. This formal record starts the clock on district response timelines.

What to Include in Your Complaint

  1. Date the complaint was filed
  2. Child's full name and current grade level
  3. Specific dates when the alleged violation occurred
  4. Detailed description of the problem and violation
  5. Impact on your child's education and well-being
  6. Documentation supporting your claim (emails, IEP documents, medical records, etc.)
  7. Requested resolution or compensatory services
  8. Your contact information and preferred method of communication

SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD Due Process Hearings

When mediation fails or you prefer a more formal process, a SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD due process hearing provides your opportunity to present evidence before an impartial hearing officer. This is a legal proceeding where both you and SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD present arguments, evidence, and witnesses. The hearing officer issues a binding decision addressing the disputed issues.

Due process hearings represent a significant step and typically warrant working with a special education attorney or advocate familiar with Texas special education law. The hearing process requires filing specific documentation, meeting strict deadlines, and presenting evidence persuasively. Understanding these requirements prevents procedural errors that could undermine your case.

Due Process Timeline and Requirements

  • You must file a due process complaint with specific information (name, address, phone, email)
  • The complaint must clearly describe the issue and proposed resolution
  • SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD has 10 days to respond in writing
  • Both parties must exchange evidence at least 5 business days before the hearing
  • The hearing typically occurs within 30 days of your due process filing
  • The hearing officer has 45 days from the hearing date to issue a written decision
  • Either party may appeal to the TEA within 90 days

Complaint to the Texas Education Agency (TEA)

A SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD TEA complaint represents another option for addressing alleged violations of special education law. Unlike due process hearings, TEA complaints focus on whether SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD violated special education regulations, not on your child's specific placement or services. TEA investigations are free and don't require legal representation.

The Texas Education Agency investigates complaints independently of SAN FELIPE-DEL RIO CISD. If TEA finds violations, the agency can require corrective action, staff training, or compensatory education services. This process protects not only your child but potentially other students in the district facing similar issues.

Filing a TEA Complaint

  • File with the Texas Education Agency's Special Education Compliance and Monitoring Unit
  • Complaints must be filed within one year of the alleged violation
  • Describe the specific violation and impact on your child
  • Include relevant documentation and IEP documents
  • TEA has 60 days to investigate and respond
  • You receive a written report detailing findings and corrective actions
  • TEA complaints and due process cases can proceed simultaneously
