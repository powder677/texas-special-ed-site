Understanding SAN ELIZARIO ISD Special Education Services

SAN ELIZARIO ISD special education serves students ages 3-21 with disabilities who require specialized instruction and support. Our district is committed to providing a free, appropriate public education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment (LRE) for all eligible students.

The special education program in SAN ELIZARIO ISD includes services for students with:

  • Autism spectrum disorder
  • Developmental delays
  • Dyslexia and reading disabilities
  • Emotional and behavioral disorders
  • Hearing impairments
  • Intellectual disabilities
  • Orthopedic impairments
  • Other health impairments
  • Speech and language disorders
  • Visual impairments

With an enrollment of 2,755 students, SAN ELIZARIO ISD maintains specialized staff and resources to support each child's unique learning needs. Our commitment extends across all campuses in the SAN ELIZARIO area to ensure equitable access to special education services.

The ARD Process and IEP Development in SAN ELIZARIO ISD

The SAN ELIZARIO ISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) is the cornerstone of special education planning. This meeting brings together parents, teachers, administrators, and specialists to develop your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP).

What to Expect During Your ARD Meeting

During your SAN ELIZARIO ISD ARD meeting, the team will review your child's current performance, discuss eligibility for special education services, and establish annual goals. The meeting typically lasts 1-2 hours and requires parent participation and consent.

Parents in SAN ELIZARIO ISD should come prepared with:

  1. Questions about your child's progress and challenges
  2. Documentation of concerns from home and medical providers
  3. Information about your child's strengths and interests
  4. Goals you want included in the IEP

Timeline for SAN ELIZARIO ISD Special Education Evaluation

Once you request an evaluation in SAN ELIZARIO ISD, the district has 60 calendar days to complete the assessment and hold your ARD meeting. SAN ELIZARIO ISD must notify you in writing of evaluation results before the meeting.

The evaluation process includes assessments in all areas of suspected disability, conducted by qualified professionals. Parents have the right to request independent evaluations if they disagree with SAN ELIZARIO ISD's findings.

SAN ELIZARIO ISD IEP Services and Accommodations

SAN ELIZARIO ISD IEP services are customized to meet each student's individual needs. These services extend beyond classroom instruction to include related services that support your child's ability to benefit from special education.

Types of Services Available Through SAN ELIZARIO ISD

SAN ELIZARIO ISD families may access the following IEP services:

  • Special education instruction – delivered in resource rooms, self-contained classrooms, or general education settings
  • Speech and language therapy – addressing communication and swallowing needs
  • Occupational therapy – developing fine motor and daily living skills
  • Physical therapy – improving gross motor function and mobility
  • Counseling services – supporting emotional and behavioral health
  • Assistive technology – providing tools and devices to support learning
  • Transportation – specialized transportation when required by the IEP
  • Extended school year (ESY) – summer services for students with significant needs

Every service included in your child's IEP must be documented with frequency, duration, and measurable goals. SAN ELIZARIO ISD provides progress monitoring and reports your child's advancement toward IEP objectives regularly.

Accommodations and Modifications in SAN ELIZARIO ISD Classrooms

Your child's IEP will detail specific accommodations (changes in how material is presented) and modifications (changes to what is taught). Examples of accommodations in SAN ELIZARIO ISD classrooms include extended time on tests, preferential seating, or use of assistive technology.

General education teachers in SAN ELIZARIO ISD receive training on implementing IEP accommodations to ensure consistency across all settings. Parents can request accommodations at any time by contacting the special education department.

SAN ELIZARIO ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

SAN ELIZARIO ISD parent rights are protected under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code. Understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Essential Parent Rights in SAN ELIZARIO ISD Special Education

Parents in SAN ELIZARIO ISD have the right to:

  • Participate fully in all ARD meetings and decision-making processes
  • Receive written notice in a language you understand before any changes to services
  • Obtain independent evaluations at district expense if you disagree with SAN ELIZARIO ISD assessments
  • Access your child's records and confidential information within 10 business days
  • Request mediation or due process if you disagree with SAN ELIZARIO ISD decisions
  • Bring advocates or attorneys to IEP meetings (notify district in advance)
  • Refuse services you don't believe are appropriate
  • Request in-home services and other related services as needed

SAN ELIZARIO ISD provides a procedural safeguards notice in writing at least once yearly. This document explains your rights under Texas Education Code §29.005 and IDEA Section 504. Request a copy from the Special Education Department if you haven't received one.

Dyslexia Services and Reading Support at SAN ELIZARIO ISD

The SAN ELIZARIO ISD dyslexia program provides specialized reading intervention for students with dyslexia and related reading disabilities. Under Texas Education Code §38.003, SAN ELIZARIO ISD must identify students with dyslexia and provide evidence-based interventions.

Dyslexia Identification and Assessment in SAN ELIZARIO ISD

Teachers and parents can refer students for dyslexia screening. SAN ELIZARIO ISD uses scientifically-validated assessment tools to identify reading difficulties. If your child is identified with dyslexia, the district develops an individualized reading plan, which may be included in the IEP or handled through a Section 504 plan.

The SAN ELIZARIO ISD dyslexia program emphasizes:

  • Structured literacy instruction using multisensory techniques
  • Phonological awareness and phonics mastery
  • Regular progress monitoring with frequent adjustments
  • Parent involvement and communication about reading progress
  • Teacher training in evidence-based dyslexia intervention

Students may receive dyslexia services through special education, a Section 504 plan, or within general education intervention programs at SAN ELIZARIO ISD. Services continue for as long as the student demonstrates need.

Transition Planning for Secondary Students in SAN ELIZARIO ISD

Beginning at age 14 (or younger if appropriate), SAN ELIZARIO ISD must develop a transition plan as part of your child's IEP. This plan prepares students for life after high school, focusing on post-secondary education, employment, and independent living.

Transition Services and Goals in SAN ELIZARIO ISD

Transition planning in SAN ELIZARIO ISD includes:

  • Vocational assessment and career exploration
  • Work-based learning experiences through internships and on-the-job training
  • Independent living skills instruction such as budgeting and self-care
  • Community participation and social skills development
  • Post-secondary education planning including college and vocational programs

SAN ELIZARIO ISD coordinates with adult service agencies, vocational rehabilitation, and community partners to support successful transitions. Students and families are invited to participate in transition planning meetings starting no later than the IEP to be in effect when the student turns 16.
