Understanding Special Education in LIVINGSTON ISD

LIVINGSTON ISD special education programs provide specialized instruction and related services to students with disabilities ages 3-21. These services are designed to meet each child's unique needs and help them access the general education curriculum alongside their peers.

All students in LIVINGSTON ISD who are identified as having a disability under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) are eligible for free, appropriate public education (FAPE). This means LIVINGSTON ISD must provide services at no cost to families, and these services must meet your child's individual needs.

Who Qualifies for Special Education in LIVINGSTON ISD

LIVINGSTON ISD special education serves students with the following disability categories:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Specific Learning Disability
  • Emotional/Behavioral Disorder
  • Speech/Language Impairment
  • Visual Impairment
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Traumatic Brain Injury

If you suspect your child may have a disability, you can request an evaluation from LIVINGSTON ISD at any time during the school year. There is no cost for this evaluation, and LIVINGSTON ISD will complete it within a specific timeframe required by Texas law.

The ARD Meeting: Your Voice in LIVINGSTON ISD Special Education

The LIVINGSTON ISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) is where critical decisions about your child's special education program are made. ARD stands for "Admission, Review, and Dismissal," and these meetings are required at least once per year for every student receiving special education services in LIVINGSTON ISD.

What Happens at a LIVINGSTON ISD ARD Meeting

During your LIVINGSTON ISD ARD meeting, a team of educators, specialists, and parents work together to:

  • Review your child's current performance and progress
  • Discuss evaluation results and assessment data
  • Determine eligibility for special education services
  • Develop or revise your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP)
  • Set measurable annual goals
  • Identify the services and supports your child needs
  • Decide the placement and amount of time in general education

Preparing for Your LIVINGSTON ISD ARD Meeting

You have the right to bring anyone to your LIVINGSTON ISD ARD meeting who has knowledge of your child—this might include a family advocate, advocate attorney, or outside evaluators. Come prepared with questions, observations from home, and input about your child's strengths and challenges.

Request your child's current evaluation data at least 5 business days before the meeting. This gives you time to review the information LIVINGSTON ISD will discuss. If you disagree with any proposed changes to your child's program at the ARD meeting, remember that you do not have to sign the IEP that day.

LIVINGSTON ISD IEP Services and Support

Your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP) is the foundation of their special education experience in LIVINGSTON ISD. The LIVINGSTON ISD IEP services outlined in this document are legally binding—LIVINGSTON ISD must provide everything written in your child's IEP.

Components of LIVINGSTON ISD IEP Services

An effective IEP includes:

  • Present Levels: A clear description of your child's current academic and functional performance
  • Measurable Annual Goals: Specific, achievable targets for progress in areas of need
  • Special Education Services: The type and amount of specialized instruction your child will receive
  • Related Services: Support services like speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, or physical therapy
  • Accommodations and Modifications: Changes to how your child accesses the curriculum and demonstrates learning
  • Transition Services: For students age 16 and older, planning for post-secondary education, employment, and independent living

LIVINGSTON ISD IEP services are individualized to each student. Your child's program might include resource room instruction, co-teaching in general education classes, specialized services, or a combination of supports designed specifically for their learning profile.

Progress Monitoring in LIVINGSTON ISD

LIVINGSTON ISD staff monitor your child's progress toward IEP goals regularly—at minimum, you will receive a progress report at least as often as general education students receive report cards. Ask your child's teachers specifically how they're measuring progress on each goal and what the data shows.

LIVINGSTON ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

Understanding your LIVINGSTON ISD parent rights is essential to being an effective advocate for your child. Federal and Texas law give you specific protections and decision-making power in your child's special education program.

Your Key LIVINGSTON ISD Parent Rights

Parents in LIVINGSTON ISD have the right to:

  • Request an evaluation: Ask LIVINGSTON ISD to evaluate your child for special education at any time
  • Participate in all decisions: Your involvement in ARD meetings and educational planning is required
  • Review records: Access all of your child's educational and evaluation records within 5 business days
  • Receive notice: Get written notice before LIVINGSTON ISD makes changes to your child's identification, evaluation, or placement
  • Agree or disagree: You can refuse to sign the IEP if you don't agree with the plan LIVINGSTON ISD proposes
  • Request independent evaluation: If you disagree with LIVINGSTON ISD's evaluation, you can request an independent evaluation at LIVINGSTON ISD expense
  • Dispute resolution: Use mediation or due process hearing to resolve disagreements with LIVINGSTON ISD

When to Assert Your LIVINGSTON ISD Parent Rights

Contact LIVINGSTON ISD's special education office if you notice your child isn't making progress on IEP goals, if LIVINGSTON ISD isn't providing services listed in the IEP, or if you have concerns about your child's placement or program. Document communication in writing (email works well) so you have a record of your concerns.

Remember: You are your child's most important advocate. LIVINGSTON ISD wants to work with you as a partner, but you also have the legal right to push for changes if you believe your child's needs aren't being met.

LIVINGSTON ISD Dyslexia Program and Services

Texas law requires every school district, including LIVINGSTON ISD, to provide screening and intervention for students with dyslexia. The LIVINGSTON ISD dyslexia program is designed to identify students with reading difficulties early and provide intensive, specialized instruction.

Dyslexia Screening in LIVINGSTON ISD

LIVINGSTON ISD screens all students for dyslexia, typically in kindergarten and first grade. If your child shows signs of reading difficulty, LIVINGSTON ISD will recommend further evaluation. Dyslexia is a specific learning disability in reading, and it's not related to intelligence or effort—it's a neurological difference in how the brain processes language.

If your child is identified with dyslexia through LIVINGSTON ISD's evaluation process, they qualify for the LIVINGSTON ISD dyslexia program, which includes specialized reading instruction using evidence-based methods like structured literacy.

Getting Dyslexia Support in LIVINGSTON ISD

If you suspect your child has dyslexia, talk with your child's teacher or contact LIVINGSTON ISD's special education office directly. You can also request a dyslexia evaluation—LIVINGSTON ISD cannot charge you for this assessment. Many LIVINGSTON ISD students with dyslexia benefit from the LIVINGSTON ISD dyslexia program combined with accommodations like extended time on tests and use of text-to-speech technology.

When to Consider a Special Education Attorney Near LIVINGSTON

A special education attorney near LIVINGSTON specializes in education law and represents families in disagreements with school districts. You might consult a special education attorney near LIVINGSTON if:

  • LIVINGSTON ISD is not providing services outlined in your child's IEP
  • You disagree with LIVINGSTON ISD's evaluation or eligibility determination
  • Your child has experienced harassment or bullying related to disability
  • You want to file for due process hearing against LIVINGSTON ISD

Many special education attorneys work on contingency for due process cases, meaning they only get paid if you win. Initial consultations are often free, so it's worth reaching out to discuss your specific situation regarding LIVINGSTON ISD.
