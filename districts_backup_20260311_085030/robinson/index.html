The IEP Process at ROBINSON ISD

What Is an IEP?

An Individualized Education Program (IEP) is a legal document that outlines the special education services your child will receive. ROBINSON ISD IEP services are customized based on your child's strengths, challenges, and educational goals.

Your child's IEP includes current performance levels, annual goals, specific services, accommodations, modifications, and progress monitoring plans. This document guides all instruction and support provided to your child throughout the school year in ROBINSON ISD.

Steps to Obtain an IEP in ROBINSON ISD

  1. Request for Evaluation: Contact ROBINSON ISD special education office to request a full evaluation. You can do this in writing, via email, or in person.
  2. Permission to Evaluate: ROBINSON ISD will send a Permission to Evaluate form. Sign and return within 10 days.
  3. Comprehensive Evaluation: Specialists assess your child in academics, speech, motor skills, behavior, and other areas. This typically takes 30-45 days.
  4. ARD/IEP Meeting: ROBINSON ISD convenes an ROBINSON ISD ARD meeting to review evaluation results and develop the IEP.
  5. Implement Services: Once you and ROBINSON ISD agree on the IEP, services begin immediately.

What Happens at an ARD Meeting?

An ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting is where ROBINSON ISD ARD meeting participants review your child's progress and make decisions about services. The ARD committee includes parents, teachers, special education staff, and sometimes your child.

During a ROBINSON ISD ARD meeting, the team will:

  • Review current performance and progress data
  • Discuss your child's strengths and needs
  • Set measurable annual goals
  • Determine placement and services
  • Identify accommodations and modifications
  • Establish a schedule for reviewing progress

You have the right to bring anyone you choose to a ROBINSON ISD ARD meeting, including family members, advocates, or attorneys. These meetings happen at least annually, but ROBINSON ISD parents can request additional meetings anytime.

Parent Rights in ROBINSON ISD Special Education

Your Legal Rights

Texas law and federal IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) guarantee ROBINSON ISD parent rights that protect your child and empower your voice in decision-making. Understanding these rights is essential for parents of children in ROBINSON ISD.

ROBINSON ISD parent rights include:

  • Right to Notice: ROBINSON ISD must inform you in writing before evaluating, placing, or significantly changing services for your child
  • Right to Consent: You must give written permission before initial evaluation and before changing placement
  • Right to Participate: You must be invited to all ARD meetings and have meaningful opportunity to participate in ROBINSON ISD decisions
  • Right to Records: You can access all educational and special education records within 10 school days
  • Right to Independent Evaluation: If you disagree with ROBINSON ISD's evaluation, you can request an independent evaluation at district expense
  • Right to Due Process: You can file a due process complaint if you disagree with ROBINSON ISD's decisions
  • Right to Native Language: Communication from ROBINSON ISD must be in your home language if different from English

Understanding Your Procedural Safeguards

ROBINSON ISD must provide you with a Procedural Safeguards Notice explaining your rights under IDEA. This document is required by Texas Education Code and must be given to all parents when their child is first evaluated or when you request it.

Keep this document safe. It explains how to file complaints, request due process hearings, and resolve disputes with ROBINSON ISD. You can request a copy at any time from the ROBINSON ISD special education office.

Dyslexia Programs and Support at ROBINSON ISD

ROBINSON ISD Dyslexia Services

Dyslexia is a specific learning disability affecting reading. ROBINSON ISD recognizes dyslexia as a significant barrier to academic success and provides specialized intervention through our ROBINSON ISD dyslexia program.

The ROBINSON ISD dyslexia program includes:

  • Screening: All students are screened for dyslexia risk factors
  • Evaluation: Comprehensive evaluation for students who show signs of dyslexia
  • Intervention: Specialized, evidence-based reading instruction using structured literacy approaches
  • Accommodations: Extended time, text-to-speech technology, and other supports
  • Progress Monitoring: Regular assessment of reading gains

When to Consider Dyslexia Support

Talk to your child's teacher or contact ROBINSON ISD special education if your child shows these signs:

  • Difficulty learning letter names and sounds
  • Slow reading speed or frequent errors
  • Trouble with spelling or written expression
  • Difficulty rhyming or manipulating sounds
  • Family history of dyslexia or reading difficulties

Early intervention is critical. Request dyslexia evaluation as soon as you notice reading struggles. The ROBINSON ISD dyslexia program works best when started early.
