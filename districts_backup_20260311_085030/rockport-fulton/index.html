ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD ARD Meetings: What Parents Need to Know

An ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting is where special education decisions are made for your child in ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD. This is your opportunity to collaborate with teachers, specialists, and administrators to develop the best plan for your child's education. ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD ARD meetings must include your child's parent or guardian, the child (when appropriate), general and special education teachers, a school administrator, and any specialists involved in your child's evaluation.

What to Expect During a ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD ARD Meeting

Understanding the ARD process helps you prepare effectively and advocate for your child. During your ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD ARD meeting, the team will:

  1. Review your child's current evaluation results and performance data
  2. Discuss your child's strengths and areas for improvement
  3. Determine eligibility for special education services
  4. Develop or update your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP)
  5. Set measurable annual goals and specific services
  6. Determine the least restrictive environment for your child's learning
  7. Plan transition services (for students age 14+)

Parents have the right to bring an advocate or attorney to any ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD ARD meeting. If you feel uncomfortable navigating the process alone, a special ed advocate ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD families trust can help ensure your voice is heard and your child's needs are properly addressed.

Timeline and Scheduling Requirements

ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD must hold an ARD meeting within 30 calendar days of receiving a request for evaluation. After your child is identified as needing special education, ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD must convene an ARD meeting at least once per year to review your child's IEP. The district must provide notice at least 10 days before any ARD meeting and accommodate your schedule when possible.

ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD IEP Services and Goals

Your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP) is the legal document that guides all special education services in ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD. ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD IEP services are specifically designed to address your child's unique disabilities and support their progress toward meaningful educational outcomes.

Components of an Effective IEP

A strong IEP includes measurable annual goals that directly address your child's areas of need. Goals should be specific, observable, and progress toward them should be tracked regularly. ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD IEP services should include the frequency and duration of services, the setting where services will be delivered, and how progress will be measured.

Your child's IEP in ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD must also address:

  • Academic instruction in reading, math, and other core subjects
  • Social-emotional development and behavioral supports
  • Communication and language skills
  • Motor development (when relevant)
  • Self-care and daily living skills
  • Career and life skills (for secondary students)

Monitoring Progress in ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD

ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD is required to report your child's progress toward IEP goals at least as frequently as progress is reported for non-disabled peers, typically every nine weeks. Request progress reports regularly and ask specific questions about whether your child is on track to meet annual goals. If your child is not progressing, request an ARD meeting to discuss changes to services or supports.

ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD Parent Rights and Procedural Safeguards

Understanding your rights is essential to effectively advocate for your child in ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD. ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD parent rights are protected under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code.

Your Key Rights as a Parent in ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD

As a parent in ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD, you have the right to:

  • Request an evaluation at any time if you believe your child has a disability
  • Be fully informed and give consent before any evaluation occurs
  • Participate as an equal team member in all ARD meetings
  • Review all educational records related to your child
  • Request an independent evaluation at ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD's expense if you disagree with the district's evaluation
  • Receive notices in a language you understand
  • Appeal ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD special education decisions through due process
  • Obtain special ed advocate ROCKPORT-FULTON families recommend for support and representation

If you disagree with ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD special education decisions, you have multiple options. ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD Dyslexia Program

Dyslexia is a specific learning disability that affects how the brain processes written language. ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD offers a comprehensive dyslexia program for students identified with this condition.

Dyslexia Screening and Assessment in ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD

Texas law requires ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD to screen all students in kindergarten and first grade for dyslexia, and to make screening available to older students. If your child shows signs of dyslexia, you can request a comprehensive evaluation. The ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD dyslexia program uses evidence-based assessment tools to determine whether your child has dyslexia and what interventions are needed.

Signs that your child may have dyslexia include:

  • Difficulty learning letter names and sounds
  • Slow reading development compared to peers
  • Difficulty rhyming or breaking words into sounds
  • Trouble spelling, even after instruction
  • Difficulty organizing thoughts in writing
  • Family history of dyslexia or reading difficulty

Intervention and Support Services

Students identified with dyslexia in ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD receive specialized instruction using structured literacy approaches. This instruction focuses on phonemic awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension. The ROCKPORT-FULTON ISD dyslexia program may be delivered through special education services, as part of general education interventions, or through a combination of both approaches.
