Overview of CHAPEL HILL ISD Special Education Services

CHAPEL HILL ISD special education programs serve students ages 3-22 with disabilities across a wide spectrum of needs. Our district maintains a commitment to the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code requirements, ensuring that every eligible student receives a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) in the Least Restrictive Environment (LRE).

CHAPEL HILL ISD offers multiple program options designed to support your child's academic, social, and developmental growth. Whether your child needs resource support within the general education classroom or more intensive specialized services, our special education team works to create individualized solutions tailored to each student's unique strengths and challenges.

Program Categories Available in CHAPEL HILL ISD

  • Speech-Language Services: For students with communication disorders affecting speech, language, or hearing
  • Learning Support: For students with specific learning disabilities in reading, writing, or math
  • Emotional/Behavioral Support: For students requiring support with emotional regulation and behavior management
  • Physical/Occupational Therapy: For students needing motor skill development and sensory support
  • Autism Spectrum Services: Specialized programs for students with autism diagnoses
  • Intellectual Disability Programs: Comprehensive services for students with cognitive disabilities
  • Visual and Hearing Impairment Services: Specialized support for sensory disabilities

Understanding CHAPEL HILL ISD ARD Meetings and IEP Development

The ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee is the heart of special education in CHAPEL HILL ISD. This team—which includes you as the parent, teachers, special education specialists, and your child (when appropriate)—meets to discuss your child's needs, develop educational goals, and determine the services your child will receive.

CHAPEL HILL ISD ARD meeting procedures follow Texas Education Code §89.1010 and ensure that parents have meaningful participation in all educational decisions. These meetings typically occur annually, though CHAPEL HILL ISD parents can request additional meetings whenever significant concerns arise.

What to Expect at Your CHAPEL HILL ISD ARD Meeting

  1. Pre-meeting notification: You'll receive written notice at least 10 school days before the meeting with the date, time, location, and attendees listed
  2. Review of current performance: The team examines assessment data, classroom observations, and progress reports to understand your child's current abilities
  3. Discussion of eligibility: If this is an initial referral, the team determines whether your child qualifies for special education under IDEA categories
  4. IEP development: The team collaborates to write measurable annual goals, determine placement, and specify services your child needs
  5. Parent input and questions: This is your opportunity to share observations, concerns, and priorities for your child's education
  6. Written agreement: You'll receive a copy of the final ARD document with all decisions documented

Parents in CHAPEL HILL ISD have the right to bring an advocate, ask for clarification on any recommendations, and request additional time if needed. Don't hesitate to ask questions during CHAPEL HILL ISD ARD meetings—these meetings are about finding solutions together.

CHAPEL HILL ISD IEP Services: What Your Child May Receive

CHAPEL HILL ISD IEP services are specialized supports written into your child's Individualized Education Program. These services are based on your child's specific disability and educational needs, as determined by the ARD committee. Services must be documented in writing with clear goals, frequency, duration, and measurable progress indicators.

Related Services Often Included in CHAPEL HILL ISD IEPs

Beyond core special education instruction, CHAPEL HILL ISD IEP services may include related services that support your child's access to education:

  • Speech-Language Pathology: Sessions ranging from 1-5 times per week depending on needs
  • Occupational Therapy: Support with fine motor skills, sensory processing, and daily living tasks
  • Physical Therapy: Assistance with gross motor skills, mobility, and access to the school environment
  • School Counseling: Mental health support and coping skills development
  • Assistive Technology: Access to devices or software that enhance communication and learning
  • Behavioral Support Services: Positive behavior intervention plans and functional behavior assessments
  • Transportation Modifications: Specialized transportation if your child's disability affects safe school access

All CHAPEL HILL ISD IEP services must be provided at no cost to families and documented with clear progress-monitoring procedures. Your child's progress toward IEP goals is reviewed regularly and shared with parents through progress reports.

CHAPEL HILL ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

CHAPEL HILL ISD parent rights are protected under IDEA and Texas Education Code. Understanding these rights empowers you to be an effective advocate for your child. Parents in CHAPEL HILL ISD have the right to be informed, involved, and heard throughout the special education process.

Key Rights of Parents in CHAPEL HILL ISD

  • Right to prior written notice: CHAPEL HILL ISD must inform you in writing before proposing or refusing any special education action
  • Right to participate in ARD meetings: You have the right to attend and meaningfully participate in all meetings about your child's education
  • Right to independent evaluation: If you disagree with CHAPEL HILL ISD evaluations, you can request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at district expense
  • Right to access records: You can review all educational records and obtain copies within 10 school days
  • Right to disagreement: You can decline any CHAPEL HILL ISD recommendation and request additional evaluation or a second opinion
  • Right to procedural safeguards: You have protection against unfair or discriminatory practices in special education
  • Right to due process: If disputes arise, you can file a due process complaint with the Texas Education Agency
  • Right to dispute resolution: CHAPEL HILL ISD offers mediation and other conflict resolution options

Parents in CHAPEL HILL ISD should receive a copy of their procedural safeguards at the beginning of each school year. This document outlines all protections under federal and state law. If you haven't received this document, contact the special education department at CHAPEL HILL ISD directly.

CHAPEL HILL ISD Dyslexia Program and Reading Intervention

Texas Education Code requires all school districts, including CHAPEL HILL ISD, to implement programs for students with dyslexia and related reading disabilities. The CHAPEL HILL ISD dyslexia program provides specialized instruction using evidence-based, structured literacy approaches that help students overcome reading difficulties.

How CHAPEL HILL ISD Identifies and Serves Students with Dyslexia

CHAPEL HILL ISD dyslexia program begins with screening in kindergarten and first grade. If your child shows signs of reading difficulty, CHAPEL HILL ISD will conduct comprehensive evaluation to determine if dyslexia or another reading disability exists. Signs include difficulty with phonics, decoding, spelling, and reading fluency despite adequate instruction.

Once identified, students receive specialized dyslexia intervention through the CHAPEL HILL ISD dyslexia program. This intervention typically includes small group or individual instruction in phonemic awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension using multisensory, structured methods. Many students with dyslexia in CHAPEL HILL ISD benefit from these intensive reading programs and develop strong reading skills.

Getting Started: Next Steps for CHAPEL HILL ISD Parents

If your child is currently in CHAPEL HILL ISD and you have concerns about academics, behavior, or development, here are concrete steps to take:

  1. Request a consultation: Contact your child's teacher or the special education director at CHAPEL HILL ISD to discuss your concerns
  2. Request evaluation: In writing, ask CHAPEL HILL ISD to evaluate your child for special education eligibility. CHAPEL HILL ISD has 15 school days to respond
  3. Gather documentation: Collect medical records, prior evaluations, report cards, and documentation of your concerns to share with the team
  4. Prepare for your first ARD meeting: Write down questions, goals, and priorities you want discussed at your CHAPEL HILL ISD ARD meeting
  5. Advocate throughout: Stay actively involved in your child's education and communicate regularly with CHAPEL HILL ISD staff

The special education team at CHAPEL HILL ISD wants to work with you to support your child's success.
