Steps to Request a Special Education Evaluation in BRENHAM ISD

Step 1: Submit Your Request in Writing

The first step is to request an evaluation from BRENHAM ISD in writing. Although the law does not require a specific form, you must submit your request clearly and documented.

Your request should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current school in BRENHAM ISD
  • Specific description of areas of concern
  • Your name, phone, and email
  • Date of the request

Send this request to the director of your school in BRENHAM ISD or to the special education department. It is advisable to send it by certified mail to have proof of delivery.

Step 2: Prior Consent Meeting for Evaluation

Within 15 school days of receiving your request, BRENHAM ISD must schedule a meeting with you. In this BRENHAM ISD meeting, the team will explain:

  • The types of tests that will be used
  • The areas that will be evaluated
  • Your right to bring a special education advocate or attorney
  • The estimated timeline

You must sign a prior written consent form from BRENHAM ISD before evaluations begin. Without your signed consent, BRENHAM ISD cannot proceed with the evaluation.

Step 3: Completion of the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE)

A FIE (Full Individual Evaluation) in BRENHAM ISD is a comprehensive evaluation that examines multiple areas of your child's development. The BRENHAM ISD team will evaluate:

  • Academic and cognitive skills
  • Speech and language development
  • Motor skills (gross and fine)
  • Emotional and behavioral health
  • Vision and hearing
  • Developmental and medical history

BRENHAM ISD has 60 calendar days from consent to complete the evaluation, unless a different written agreement exists.

Step 4: Eligibility Meeting (ARD/IEP)

After completing the evaluation, BRENHAM ISD will schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) eligibility meeting within the following 30 days.

In this crucial BRENHAM ISD meeting:

  • The team will present the evaluation results
  • They will determine if your child qualifies for special education
  • If qualified, they will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP)
  • You will have the opportunity to ask questions and provide information

This ARD meeting in BRENHAM ISD is where your voice matters most. Come prepared with questions and observations about your child.
