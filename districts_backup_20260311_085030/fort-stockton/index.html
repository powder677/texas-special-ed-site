Understanding Special Education in FORT STOCKTON ISD

FORT STOCKTON ISD is committed to providing a free, appropriate public education (FAPE) to all students with disabilities, ages 3-21. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, FORT STOCKTON ISD must identify, evaluate, and serve eligible students regardless of the severity of their disability.

With 13,480 students across FORT STOCKTON and the surrounding areas, FORT STOCKTON ISD maintains dedicated special education staff who work to ensure every child receives individualized support. The district serves students with a wide range of disabilities including autism spectrum disorder, intellectual disabilities, emotional disturbances, speech/language impairments, orthopedic impairments, visual impairments, hearing impairments, and other health impairments.

Key Facts About FORT STOCKTON ISD Special Education

  • Free services: All special education evaluation and services are provided at no cost to families in FORT STOCKTON ISD
  • Age range: FORT STOCKTON ISD serves students from age 3 through age 21
  • Individualized planning: Each student in FORT STOCKTON ISD special education receives a personalized Individualized Education Program (IEP)
  • Parent partnership: Parents are essential members of the FORT STOCKTON ISD special education team
  • Least restrictive environment: FORT STOCKTON ISD is committed to including students with disabilities in general education to the maximum extent appropriate

FORT STOCKTON ISD IEP Services and the IEP Process

An Individualized Education Program (IEP) is the foundation of special education services in FORT STOCKTON ISD. This legal document outlines your child's present levels of performance, annual goals, and the specific services and supports they will receive. Understanding the FORT STOCKTON ISD IEP services process empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

The Evaluation Process for FORT STOCKTON ISD IEP Services

When you suspect your child has a disability, you can request an initial evaluation from FORT STOCKTON ISD. The evaluation process typically includes:

  1. Request submission: Submit your evaluation request in writing to the FORT STOCKTON ISD special education department
  2. Parental consent: FORT STOCKTON ISD must obtain your written consent before evaluating your child
  3. Comprehensive assessment: FORT STOCKTON ISD will conduct assessments in all areas of suspected disability
  4. ARD meeting: Within 30 calendar days, FORT STOCKTON ISD will hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to discuss results
  5. Eligibility determination: The FORT STOCKTON ISD team will determine if your child qualifies for special education services

If FORT STOCKTON ISD determines your child is eligible, they will develop an IEP at the initial ARD meeting. FORT STOCKTON ISD IEP services must address your child's unique needs and include goals in areas affected by their disability.

Annual FORT STOCKTON ISD ARD Meetings

Parents of students receiving special education in FORT STOCKTON ISD must participate in an ARD meeting at least once per year. During these FORT STOCKTON ISD ARD meeting sessions, your team will review your child's progress, discuss any needed changes to services, and update annual goals.

You have the right to request additional FORT STOCKTON ISD ARD meetings whenever you believe changes are needed. The district must respond to your request within a reasonable timeframe.

FORT STOCKTON ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

Federal and Texas law grant parents substantial rights in FORT STOCKTON ISD special education decisions. Understanding your FORT STOCKTON ISD parent rights ensures you can effectively participate in meetings and make informed decisions about your child's education.

Core FORT STOCKTON ISD Parent Rights

  • Prior written notice: FORT STOCKTON ISD must notify you in writing before proposing or refusing any action regarding your child's special education
  • Participation: You have the right to participate in all meetings about your child in FORT STOCKTON ISD
  • Records access: You can request and review all education records FORT STOCKTON ISD maintains about your child
  • Independent evaluation: If you disagree with FORT STOCKTON ISD's evaluation, you have the right to obtain an independent evaluation at the district's expense under certain circumstances
  • Due process protection: If you dispute FORT STOCKTON ISD decisions, you have the right to request mediation or due process hearing
  • Interpreter services: FORT STOCKTON ISD must provide interpreters if you speak a language other than English

FORT STOCKTON ISD Dyslexia Program and Intervention Services

FORT STOCKTON ISD recognizes dyslexia as a specific learning disability affecting reading. Texas Education Code requires FORT STOCKTON ISD to identify and serve students with dyslexia through evidence-based interventions.

Dyslexia Screening and Identification in FORT STOCKTON ISD

FORT STOCKTON ISD screens all kindergarten students for dyslexia indicators. If your child shows signs of dyslexia, FORT STOCKTON ISD will conduct further evaluation. Early identification is crucial because the FORT STOCKTON ISD dyslexia program is most effective when interventions begin in early grades.

Signs your child may have dyslexia include difficulty with phonemic awareness, slow reading speed, difficulty spelling, and trouble with written expression. If you suspect your child has dyslexia, request that FORT STOCKTON ISD evaluate them.

Dyslexia Intervention Pathways in FORT STOCKTON ISD

Students identified with dyslexia in FORT STOCKTON ISD may receive services through:

  • Special education: If dyslexia significantly impacts educational performance, your child may qualify for special education services under the "Other Health Impairment" or "Specific Learning Disability" category
  • Section 504 Plan: Students who don't qualify for special education but have dyslexia may receive accommodations through a 504 plan
  • Intervention programs: FORT STOCKTON ISD offers dyslexia-specific interventions for students struggling with reading
  • Accommodations: The FORT STOCKTON ISD dyslexia program includes assistive technology, extended time on tests, and alternative formats

Preparing for FORT STOCKTON ISD ARD Meetings

Effective participation in FORT STOCKTON ISD ARD meetings requires preparation. Parents who come prepared make better decisions for their children and have greater influence on the services FORT STOCKTON ISD provides.

Steps to Prepare for Your FORT STOCKTON ISD ARD Meeting

  1. Gather information: Collect recent grades, test scores, and documentation of your child's strengths and challenges
  2. Review current IEP: Read your child's existing FORT STOCKTON ISD IEP before the meeting
  3. Write questions: List questions about your child's progress and needed services
  4. Document concerns: Bring written examples of behaviors or academic concerns you've observed at home
  5. Bring support: Consider bringing a trusted friend, family member, or a special ed advocate FORT STOCKTON to the meeting
  6. Request records: Ask FORT STOCKTON ISD for copies of evaluations and progress monitoring data before the meeting

Remember that FORT STOCKTON ISD staff are required to provide you with a copy of your child's current IEP at least five days before the ARD meeting. Request this early so you have time to review it carefully.
