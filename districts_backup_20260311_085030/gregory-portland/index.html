Understanding GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD Special Education Services

GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD special education provides specialized instruction and support services for eligible students from preschool through high school. Our district serves students with disabilities under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, ensuring every child receives a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) in the Least Restrictive Environment (LRE).

Special education in GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD includes a range of service delivery models designed to meet individual student needs. Whether your child requires resource services, self-contained classrooms, or full-time special education programs, GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD special education staff work collaboratively with families to develop appropriate support plans.

Our commitment to excellence in special education means that parents in GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD have access to trained personnel, evidence-based instructional practices, and meaningful involvement in all educational decisions affecting their children.

The ARD Meeting Process in GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD

An GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) is where your child's special education program is planned, developed, and evaluated. This meeting is required at least annually and brings together your family and school professionals to make collaborative decisions about your child's education.

What to Expect at Your ARD Meeting

The GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD ARD meeting typically includes:

  • Parents or guardians of the student
  • A special education teacher
  • A general education teacher (if applicable)
  • A district representative with decision-making authority
  • An individual who can interpret evaluation results
  • Your child (when age-appropriate)
  • Other specialists as needed (speech therapist, counselor, etc.)

During your GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD ARD meeting, the team reviews your child's current performance, discusses evaluation results, and determines whether special education services are necessary. If your child qualifies, the team develops or updates an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Timeline and Scheduling

GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD must schedule your ARD meeting within 30 calendar days of your request. The district will provide written notice of the meeting date, time, location, and participants at least 10 days in advance. If you need an interpreter, have specific scheduling needs, or want additional participants present, notify the district early.

IEP Services and Individualized Education Programs in GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD

GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD IEP services are customized to address your child's unique learning needs and disabilities. Your child's IEP is a legally binding document that outlines measurable annual goals, specialized instruction, related services, and classroom accommodations.

Components of Your Child's IEP

Every GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD IEP services plan includes:

  • Present Levels of Academic and Functional Performance: How your child currently performs academically and socially
  • Measurable Annual Goals: Specific, achievable objectives aligned with grade-level standards
  • Specialized Instruction: The specific services your child will receive and how often
  • Related Services: Speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, or other support services as needed
  • Accommodations and Modifications: Changes to the classroom environment, testing procedures, or curriculum
  • Transition Planning: For students age 16 and older, post-secondary goals and services

Parents in GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD have the right to review their child's IEP before the meeting and request changes. GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD IEP services must be implemented as written, and progress toward goals must be monitored regularly.

Progress Monitoring and Updates

GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD provides progress reports on GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD IEP services at least as often as progress is reported for non-disabled students. You'll receive updates through report cards, progress monitoring reports, and at scheduled ARD meetings. If your child isn't making adequate progress, you can request an ARD meeting to revise the IEP.

Parent Rights and Procedural Safeguards in GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD

GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD parent rights are protected under federal law. Understanding these rights ensures you can advocate effectively for your child and participate meaningfully in all special education decisions.

Your Essential Rights as a GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD Parent

Parents in GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD have the right to:

  • Free evaluation: Request a comprehensive evaluation at no cost if you believe your child has a disability
  • Notice and consent: Receive written notice before the district evaluates your child, changes services, or changes placement
  • Participate in all decisions: Attend all ARD meetings and have meaningful input on your child's education
  • Access records: Review your child's educational and medical records
  • Dispute resolution: File complaints and participate in mediation or due process hearings if you disagree with the district
  • Independent evaluation: Request an independent evaluation at district expense under certain circumstances
  • Interpreters: Have meetings conducted in your native language

Dyslexia Services and Programs in GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD

GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD dyslexia program provides specialized screening, assessment, and intervention for students with dyslexia and related reading difficulties. Texas Education Code §38.003 requires that all students be screened for dyslexia in kindergarten and first grade, with additional screening as needed.

Dyslexia Identification in GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD

If your child shows signs of dyslexia—including difficulty with phonemic awareness, decoding, spelling, or reading fluency—you can request a GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD dyslexia program evaluation. Signs often emerge in early elementary school but can be identified at any age.

The GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD dyslexia program uses evidence-based screening tools and comprehensive evaluations to identify dyslexia. If your child qualifies, the district provides intensive, structured literacy intervention. This intervention is distinct from general reading instruction and focuses on phonological awareness, sound-symbol relationships, and fluency.

Intervention and Support Through GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD Dyslexia Program

Students in the GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD dyslexia program receive:

  • Specialized, research-based reading instruction
  • Small-group or individualized intervention
  • Regular progress monitoring
  • Classroom accommodations and assistive technology
  • Family involvement and home learning support

Many students served through the GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD dyslexia program also qualify for special education services under an IEP, while others receive intervention through Response to Intervention (RTI) or 504 Plans. Parent involvement is essential for reinforcing literacy skills at home.

Step-by-Step: How to Request Evaluation in GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD

If you believe your child may have a disability or learning difference, here's how to request evaluation through GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD special education services:

  1. Contact your campus special education coordinator or the district special education office with a written request for evaluation. Include specific concerns about your child's learning, behavior, or development.
  2. The district will provide a Notice of Evaluation in Writing within a reasonable timeframe. This notice explains the evaluation process, your rights, and what will be assessed.
  3. Provide written consent for the evaluation. GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD cannot proceed without your permission.
  4. The evaluation will be completed within 60 calendar days as required by Texas Education Code. The district will assess your child in all suspected areas of disability.
  5. An ARD meeting will be scheduled to review evaluation results. At this meeting, GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD will determine eligibility for special education.
  6. If eligible, an IEP will be developed at that same meeting. If not eligible, you'll be informed of other support options.

Parents in GREGORY-PORTLAND ISD should keep records of your child's learning challenges, examples of work, and communication with teachers. This documentation strengthens your request and helps the evaluation team understand your concerns.
