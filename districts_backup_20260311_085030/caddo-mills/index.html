Understanding CADDO MILLS ISD Special Education Services

CADDO MILLS ISD special education serves over 3,073 students, with dedicated programs designed to meet individual learning needs. Our district provides evidence-based interventions, specialized instruction, and related services to ensure every student receives a free appropriate public education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment.

Special education in CADDO MILLS ISD covers students ages 3-21 with disabilities including autism spectrum disorder, dyslexia, intellectual disabilities, speech impairments, emotional/behavioral disorders, and physical disabilities.   • Deafness and Hearing Impairment: Severity affecting educational performance
  • Dyslexia: Specific learning disability in reading (CADDO MILLS ISD dyslexia program available)
  • Emotional Disturbance: Behavioral or emotional patterns affecting academic performance
  • Intellectual Disability: Significantly below-average cognitive functioning

  • Orthopedic Impairment: Physical limitations requiring accommodations
  • Other Health Impairment: Medical conditions (ADHD, diabetes, asthma, etc.)
  • Speech or Language Impairment: Communication deficits requiring therapy
  • Traumatic Brain Injury: Documented brain injury with educational impact
  • Visual Impairment: Vision limitations affecting learning

The ARD Process: Your Child's Individualized Education Plan

An ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting is where CADDO MILLS ISD determines your child's eligibility, creates an IEP, and plans ongoing support. Understanding this process empowers you to advocate effectively for your child's needs.

CADDO MILLS ISD ARD Meeting Timeline

  1. Request for Evaluation: Contact your school or CADDO MILLS ISD special education office. Request evaluation in writing for documentation.
  2. Evaluation Window (45 days): CADDO MILLS ISD has 45 days to conduct comprehensive assessments. You'll receive written notice of testing dates and types.
  3. ARD Meeting Scheduled: CADDO MILLS ISD schedules the initial ARD meeting within 30 days of completing evaluation.
  4. IEP Development: The ARD committee develops your child's Individualized Education Program with specific goals and services.
  5. Annual Reviews: CADDO MILLS ISD reviews and updates the IEP every 12 months (or more frequently if needed).
  6. Triennial Reevaluation: Every three years, CADDO MILLS ISD conducts comprehensive reevaluation to determine ongoing eligibility.

For CADDO MILLS ISD families, bringing support to your ARD meeting strengthens your child's case. Consider bringing a trusted family member, advocate, or special education attorney near CADDO MILLS to help navigate the discussion.

What Happens During a CADDO MILLS ISD ARD Meeting

The ARD committee includes you (the parent), special education staff, general education teacher, and your child (when appropriate). CADDO MILLS ISD will discuss evaluation results, your child's strengths and needs, proposed special education services, and measurable annual goals.

You'll review the CADDO MILLS ISD IEP services your child will receive, including minutes of service weekly, classroom setting, and any behavioral support plans. Ask questions—this is your opportunity to ensure the IEP meets your child's needs and your family's priorities.

CADDO MILLS ISD Dyslexia Program

Texas Education Code mandates dyslexia services for struggling readers. CADDO MILLS ISD dyslexia program provides specialized instruction using evidence-based approaches addressing the neurobiological basis of reading difficulties.

If your child shows signs of dyslexia—reversing letters, slow reading speed, spelling difficulties, or strong oral skills despite reading struggles—request dyslexia screening. CADDO MILLS ISD can evaluate and provide interventions even if your child doesn't qualify for special education.

CADDO MILLS ISD Parent Rights Under Texas Law

Federal law (IDEA) and Texas Education Code guarantee significant protections for families. As a parent in CADDO MILLS ISD, you have rights before, during, and after the special education process.

Your CADDO MILLS ISD Parent Rights Include:

  • Prior Written Notice: CADDO MILLS ISD must notify you before proposing or refusing special education evaluations or services
  • Informed Consent: You must consent before initial evaluation and initial IEP implementation
  • Evaluation at No Cost: CADDO MILLS ISD provides assessment at no expense to your family
  • Independent Educational Evaluation: If you disagree with CADDO MILLS ISD's evaluation, request an independent evaluation
  • Participation in IEP Decisions: Your input shapes your child's educational plan
  • Access to Records: Request copies of your child's school records and evaluation results
  • Due Process: File a formal complaint or due process hearing if CADDO MILLS ISD denies appropriate services
  • Mediation: Request neutral mediation to resolve disputes with CADDO MILLS ISD before formal hearing

CADDO MILLS ISD parent rights are detailed in the Notice of Procedural Safeguards—request this document from your school or special education office. Understanding these protections helps you advocate confidently throughout your child's education.

Getting Started: How to Request Evaluation in CADDO MILLS ISD

Beginning the special education process is straightforward. CADDO MILLS ISD must respond to evaluation requests from parents, teachers, or doctors.

Steps to Request Evaluation for CADDO MILLS ISD Special Education:

  1. Contact Your Child's School: Speak with the classroom teacher or school principal about your concerns
  2. Request Evaluation in Writing: Send a letter or email to the CADDO MILLS ISD special education department requesting comprehensive evaluation
  3. Keep Documentation: Save emails, messages, and records of communications with CADDO MILLS ISD
  4. Provide Relevant History: Share medical records, previous evaluations, or developmental concerns to help CADDO MILLS ISD understand your child's needs
  5. Expect Timeline Notification: CADDO MILLS ISD will confirm receipt and provide written notice of evaluation timeline within 15 days

If your child is struggling academically or behaviorally, don't wait. Early intervention through CADDO MILLS ISD special education services produces better long-term outcomes.
