The Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act guarantee important rights to parents in ARGYLE ISD. These ARGYLE ISD parent rights include the right to request an evaluation, participate in all decisions, and access educational records.

Key ARGYLE ISD Parent Rights

  • Right to request an evaluation: You can request a free evaluation in ARGYLE ISD at any time during the school year
  • Right to equal participation: You must be present at all IEP (Individualized Education Program) meetings
  • Right to interpretation: ARGYLE ISD must provide Spanish interpretation at all meetings and for all documents
  • Right to appeal: If you disagree with ARGYLE ISD's decisions, you can request a due process hearing
  • Right to records: You can review and obtain copies of all of your child's educational records

Step 1: Making a Formal Request for Evaluation in ARGYLE ISD

The first step is to submit a formal request to ARGYLE ISD. This request must be in writing so that ARGYLE ISD has an official record of your request. It is not enough to speak with the teacher or school nurse.

How to Submit Your ARGYLE ISD Special Education Request

To request a special education evaluation in ARGYLE ISD, send a written letter to the Director of Special Education Services at ARGYLE ISD. Although you can communicate by phone, the written request creates an official record that protects your rights.

Your request letter for an ARGYLE ISD FIE (Full Individual Evaluation) should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current grade level
  • School attended in ARGYLE ISD
  • Clear description of academic or behavioral concerns
  • Specific examples of difficulties
  • Your contact information (phone and email)
  • Signature and date

Where to Send Your Request to ARGYLE ISD

Send your ARGYLE ISD special education request by certified mail or deliver it in person to the ARGYLE ISD special education office. Ask for a receipt confirming that they received the request.

Step 2: Understanding the ARGYLE ISD Evaluation Process

Once ARGYLE ISD receives your request, the district has strict timelines established by Texas law to complete the evaluation. ARGYLE ISD must respond quickly and professionally.

Important ARGYLE ISD Special Education Timelines

  • Within 15 days: ARGYLE ISD must respond to your request and explain what will happen next
  • Before the evaluation: ARGYLE ISD must obtain your written consent in Spanish
  • Within 60 days: ARGYLE ISD must complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE)
  • Within 30 days after completing the evaluation: ARGYLE ISD must hold an IEP meeting to discuss the results

What the ARGYLE ISD Evaluation Includes

A comprehensive evaluation in ARGYLE ISD is integral and examines multiple areas of your child's development:

  • Cognitive evaluation: Tests of thinking and reasoning skills
  • Academic evaluation: Tests of reading, writing, and math skills
  • Language evaluation: Assessment of speech and language skills
  • Behavioral and emotional evaluation: Observation of behavior in multiple settings
  • Physical evaluation: Assessment of motor skills and physical limitations
  • Parent interview: Your information regarding your child's development and history

Step 3: The Initial Meeting and Consent

Before ARGYLE ISD can evaluate your child, the ARGYLE ISD special education team must meet with you to explain the process. This is your opportunity to ask questions and ensure you understand exactly what will be done.

What Should Happen at ARGYLE ISD Before the Evaluation

The ARGYLE ISD special education team must provide a notice of parent rights in Spanish. This document explains all your rights under IDEA. ARGYLE ISD must also clearly explain why they believe an evaluation is needed.

You must give written consent before ARGYLE ISD begins the evaluation. This consent must be in Spanish and must be clear and specific about what will be evaluated.

Step 4: Preparing for Your ARGYLE ISD IEP Meeting

After ARGYLE ISD completes the evaluation, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) meeting is held. This is the most important meeting, where ARGYLE ISD will present the results and you will jointly decide if your child qualifies for special education services.

How to Prepare for Your ARGYLE ISD Meeting

  • Request results in advance: Ask ARGYLE ISD to send you the evaluation reports at least 5 days before the meeting
  • Bring notes and examples: Bring examples of schoolwork and notes about the difficulties you have observed
  • Bring an advocate: Consider bringing a special education attorney or parent advocate to the ARGYLE ISD meeting
  • Make a list of questions: Prepare with questions about the findings and proposed services
  • Request Spanish interpretation: Confirm with ARGYLE ISD that an interpreter will be present at the meeting

People Who Must Be at the ARGYLE ISD Meeting

By law, the ARGYLE ISD IEP meeting must include:

  • You (parent or guardian)
  • An ARGYLE ISD administration representative
  • The general education teacher (if applicable)
  • The ARGYLE ISD special education teacher
  • A specialist who participated in the evaluation
  • A Spanish interpreter
  • Your attorney or advocate (if desired)

If You Disagree with ARGYLE ISD's Decisions

If ARGYLE ISD determines that your child does not qualify for special education, but you believe they should, you have options. Similarly, if ARGYLE ISD proposes services that you believe are insufficient, you can challenge that decision.

Your Options if You Disagree with ARGYLE ISD

  • Request a due process hearing: You can request a hearing before an independent administrative judge (not part of ARGYLE ISD)
  • Consult with a special education attorney: An attorney can represent you in a hearing against ARGYLE ISD
  • Seek a second opinion: You can request independent evaluations at ARGYLE ISD's expense in certain cases
  • File a formal complaint: You can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA) about ARGYLE ISD

Advocacy Resources for ARGYLE ISD Parents

You are not alone in this process. There are special education attorneys and advocates available to help parents in ARGYLE ISD and the Texas area.

Available Help for ARGYLE ISD Parents

  • Special education attorney near ARGYLE: Attorneys specializing in special education can represent you in disputes with ARGYLE ISD
  • Special ed advocate ARGYLE: Parent advocates can assist you in ARGYLE ISD meetings
  • State advocacy organizations: There are Texas organizations that provide free information and support
  • Interpretation services: ARGYLE ISD must provide interpreters, but you can also hire private services

Frequently Asked Questions about ARGYLE ISD Special Education

How much does an ARGYLE ISD evaluation cost?

Special education evaluations in ARGYLE ISD are completely free. The school district pays all costs. ARGYLE ISD cannot charge for the evaluation, services, or IEP meeting.

Will my child miss class time for the evaluation in ARGYLE ISD?

Possibly. ARGYLE ISD may need to evaluate your child during school time to observe their behavior in the classroom. However, ARGYLE ISD must minimize lost instructional time.

What if ARGYLE ISD refuses to evaluate my child?

If ARGYLE ISD refuses to evaluate after your request, that is a violation of federal law. You can file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency or seek an attorney to sue ARGYLE ISD.

Can I obtain private special education services if ARGYLE ISD does not provide them?

Yes. If you believe that ARGYLE ISD is not providing appropriate services, you can seek private services. In some cases, ARGYLE ISD may be ordered to pay for private services if the district does not provide an appropriate educational program.
