Understanding SOUTHWEST ISD Special Education Services

SOUTHWEST ISD special education is designed to provide eligible students with disabilities the support they need to access the general education curriculum and succeed academically and socially. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §29.001, SOUTHWEST ISD is required to identify, evaluate, and serve students with disabilities.

Our special education programs serve students with various disabilities including:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
  • Intellectual Disabilities
  • Other Health Impairments
  • Specific Learning Disabilities
  • Speech and Language Impairments
  • Visual or Hearing Impairments
  • Emotional and Behavioral Disorders
  • Physical Disabilities and Orthopedic Impairments

Parents in SOUTHWEST ISD have the right to request an evaluation for special education services at any time. The evaluation process is confidential, comprehensive, and at no cost to your family.

The IEP Process: SOUTHWEST ISD IEP Services Explained

An Individualized Education Program (IEP) is the legal document that outlines your child's special education services. Understanding the SOUTHWEST ISD IEP services process is essential for parents advocating for their children.

Step-by-Step IEP Timeline

  1. Referral and Consent (Week 1): You or a teacher can refer your child for evaluation. SOUTHWEST ISD will obtain written consent before conducting assessments.
  2. Comprehensive Evaluation (Weeks 2-8): SOUTHWEST ISD special education specialists will test your child in all areas of suspected disability using multiple assessment tools.
  3. Eligibility Meeting (Week 8-10): The IEP team meets to review evaluation results and determine if your child qualifies for special education under IDEA.
  4. IEP Development (If eligible): The team develops the IEP, which includes present performance levels, measurable annual goals, and services needed.
  5. Annual Reviews: SOUTHWEST ISD IEP services are reviewed and updated annually, with three-year reevaluations required under Texas law.

What to Expect at Your IEP Meeting

The IEP meeting for your SOUTHWEST ISD child will include the special education teacher, general education teacher, school administrator, and parent(s). You have the right to bring an advocate, translator, or family member for support. The team will discuss:

  • Your child's academic and functional strengths
  • Areas where your child needs support
  • Specific, measurable annual goals
  • Special education services (hours and settings)
  • Accommodations and modifications in the general education classroom
  • Testing accommodations if applicable

You will leave the meeting with a completed IEP document outlining all services your SOUTHWEST ISD child will receive.

ARD Meetings: Your Role in SOUTHWEST ISD ARD Meetings

SOUTHWEST ISD ARD meeting is the Texas term for the IEP meeting. ARD stands for "Admission, Review, and Dismissal," and these meetings are your opportunity to collaborate with educators about your child's program.

Types of ARD Meetings in SOUTHWEST ISD

Initial ARD Meetings: Held when a child is first found eligible for special education. This is when SOUTHWEST ISD special education staff present evaluation results and you all work together to create the first IEP.

Annual ARD Meetings: Required once per school year to review progress toward IEP goals and update services. Most SOUTHWEST ISD families attend these in spring to plan for the upcoming year.

Progress Monitoring Reviews: SOUTHWEST ISD teachers provide regular updates on how your child is progressing toward annual goals, typically quarterly or as specified in the IEP.

Reevaluation ARD Meetings: Every three years, SOUTHWEST ISD must conduct comprehensive reevaluations. Some SOUTHWEST ISD ARD meetings will focus on updated assessments and continued eligibility.

Preparing for Your SOUTHWEST ISD ARD Meeting

  • Request meeting materials at least 5 days before the meeting
  • Write down questions and concerns beforehand
  • Bring documentation of your child's progress at home
  • Ask for clarification on any terms or recommendations you don't understand
  • Request a copy of the IEP before you leave the meeting
  • Take notes or request audio recording of the meeting

SOUTHWEST ISD Dyslexia Program and Reading Support

Dyslexia is a specific learning disability that affects reading, and Texas schools have specific mandates for identifying and serving students with dyslexia. SOUTHWEST ISD dyslexia program follows Texas Education Code §38.003 requirements for dyslexia screening and intervention.

Dyslexia Screening and Services

All SOUTHWEST ISD students in kindergarten and first grade are screened for dyslexia using research-based tools. If your child shows signs of dyslexia, SOUTHWEST ISD will:

  • Conduct formal dyslexia evaluations
  • Provide intensive, evidence-based reading interventions
  • Consider special education eligibility if needed
  • Implement classroom accommodations and modifications
  • Train teachers in structured literacy approaches

Parents in SOUTHWEST ISD who suspect their child has dyslexia can request an evaluation at any time. The SOUTHWEST ISD dyslexia program uses interventions like Orton-Gillingham based methods and multisensory reading instruction proven effective for students with reading disabilities.

Your Rights: SOUTHWEST ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

Understanding your legal rights is critical. SOUTHWEST ISD parent rights are protected under federal and Texas law. Every family in SOUTHWEST ISD receives a Parent Rights and Responsibilities notice describing these protections.

Key Parent Rights in SOUTHWEST ISD Special Education

Right to Prior Notice: SOUTHWEST ISD must notify you in writing before proposing or refusing to change your child's special education identification, evaluation, or placement.

Right to Consent: SOUTHWEST ISD cannot evaluate your child or change services without your informed written consent. You have the right to say no and request a due process hearing.

Right to Records Access: You can request your child's special education file at any time. SOUTHWEST ISD must provide copies within 10 days.

Right to an Independent Evaluation: If you disagree with SOUTHWEST ISD's evaluation, you may request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at district expense.

Right to Due Process: If you and SOUTHWEST ISD disagree about your child's special education, you can file a complaint or request mediation or due process hearing.

Right to Interpreter Services: SOUTHWEST ISD will provide interpreters for meetings at no cost to your family, including during ARD meetings.
