As a parent in EAGLE PASS ISD, you have important rights protected by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas state laws. These EAGLE PASS ISD parental rights are fundamental to the special education process.

Your Main Rights

  • Request an evaluation: You can request in writing that EAGLE PASS ISD evaluate your child at any time.
  • Participate in all decisions: You must be present at all meetings related to evaluation and services.
  • Access records: You have the right to review all of your child's educational and evaluation records.
  • Obtain a copy of the evaluation: EAGLE PASS ISD must provide you with copies of all evaluation reports.
  • Seek an independent evaluation: If you disagree with EAGLE PASS ISD's results, you can request an independent evaluation.
  • Communicate in your language: EAGLE PASS ISD must provide interpretation and documents in Spanish.

These rights are guaranteed by Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act and under Texas state legislation. EAGLE PASS ISD families have important legal protections that ensure a fair and equitable process.

Step 1: Recognizing Signs That Your Child Might Need an Evaluation

The first step is to identify if your child could benefit from an EAGLE PASS ISD special evaluation. Signs can vary depending on age and the type of difficulty they face.

Academic Signs

  • Difficulty learning to read, write, or do math
  • Low grades despite effort
  • Difficulty following instructions
  • Concentration and attention problems
  • Delayed language development

Behavioral and Emotional Signs

  • Difficulty controlling emotions or behavior
  • Social problems with peers
  • Anxiety or depression
  • Aggression or destructive behavior
  • Lack of school motivation

Developmental Signs

  • Delays in motor skills (walking, jumping, writing)
  • Coordination problems
  • Difficulties with verbal communication

If you observe any of these signs in your child, it's time to contact EAGLE PASS ISD to discuss your concerns with teachers or special education staff.

Step 2: How to Request an Evaluation in EAGLE PASS ISD

The formal process of EAGLE PASS ISD requesting an evaluation begins with a written request. Although some teachers may refer students internally, as a parent, you have the right to initiate the request directly.

Submitting the Request in Writing

The safest way to request an evaluation is to submit a written request to EAGLE PASS ISD. This request creates an official record of your request and protects your rights.

What to include in your letter:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Current grade and school in EAGLE PASS ISD
  • Your specific concerns about learning or behavior
  • Concrete examples of difficulties you have observed
  • Any previous evaluations or diagnoses (if applicable)
  • Your contact information (phone and email)
  • Date of the letter and your signature

Where to Send Your Request in EAGLE PASS ISD

Send your written request to the EAGLE PASS ISD special education director or directly to your child's school. Consider sending the letter via certified mail to have proof of delivery.

If you submit the request in person, ask for a receipt confirming it was received. This is important to verify that EAGLE PASS ISD received your request on time.

Important Deadlines

Under Texas law, EAGLE PASS ISD has 15 school days to respond to your evaluation request. Within 60 calendar days, the district must complete the evaluation (with exceptions under limited circumstances).

It is crucial to keep records of all communications with EAGLE PASS ISD, including dates, staff names, and content of conversations.

Step 3: The EAGLE PASS ISD Evaluation Process

Once EAGLE PASS ISD receives your request, the multidisciplinary evaluation team will begin the EAGLE PASS ISD special education process. This process may include multiple types of evaluations.

Types of Evaluations That May Be Included

  • Psychoeducational Evaluation: Examines cognitive, academic, and learning abilities
  • Speech and Language Evaluation: Assesses communication and language skills
  • Occupational and Physical Evaluation: Examines motor skills and functioning
  • Vision and Hearing Evaluation: Rules out sensory problems
  • Behavioral Evaluation: Analyzes behavior patterns in different environments
  • Medical Evaluation: If necessary, may include relevant medical information

Your Participation is Essential

As a parent in EAGLE PASS ISD, you must be informed about all evaluations that will be conducted. Before any evaluation, you must give your written consent.

You are expected to provide information about your child's developmental, medical, and academic history. Your perspective as a parent is valuable because you know your child better than anyone.

EAGLE PASS ISD families have the right to be present during evaluations, although this may vary depending on the type of test. Ask EAGLE PASS ISD staff about your participation options.

Step 4: The Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) Committee Meeting

After completing the evaluations, EAGLE PASS ISD will schedule an ARD Committee meeting. This is the most important meeting where the evaluation results will be discussed.

What to Expect at the ARD Meeting

During the ARD meeting in EAGLE PASS ISD, the findings of each evaluation will be discussed. The team will determine if your child qualifies for special education services under one of several disability eligibility categories.

Categories may include: autism, auditory impairment, visual impairment, other health impairments, intellectual disability, orthopedic impairment, traumatic brain injury, emotional disturbance, specific learning disabilities, and speech or language impairments.

Preparing for the EAGLE PASS ISD Meeting

  • Review all evaluation reports before the meeting
  • Write down your questions and concerns
  • Bring relevant medical or educational records
  • Consider bringing someone for support (partner, friend, or advocate)
  • Ask EAGLE PASS ISD to provide documents in Spanish if necessary
  • Request a Spanish interpreter if you need one

Remember that you have rights in this meeting. You can ask questions, request clarifications, and express your concerns about EAGLE PASS ISD's findings.

Step 5: The Individualized Education Program (IEP)

If your child is eligible, EAGLE PASS ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). The IEP is a legal document that describes the special education services they will receive.

IEP Components

  • Description of the child's current level of functioning
  • Measurable annual goals
  • Special education services to be provided
  • Time in general versus special education
  • Individuals responsible for each service
  • How progress will be measured
  • Necessary accommodations and modifications

It is crucial that the IEP developed by EAGLE PASS ISD is appropriate for your child's specific needs. If you have questions or concerns, express your opinion during the meeting.

Next Steps: Ensuring Your Child's Success in EAGLE PASS ISD

Once your child has been evaluated and an educational plan developed, the work continues. It is important to regularly monitor your child's progress in EAGLE PASS ISD.

Maintain open communication with teachers and special education specialists. Ask about progress toward IEP goals regularly. The IEP is reviewed annually, so be prepared for future EAGLE PASS ISD meetings.
