Understanding ARD Meetings in WEATHERFORD ISD

An ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting is the formal process that WEATHERFORD ISD uses to determine whether your child qualifies for special education services and what those services will look like. The ARD committee meets to discuss your child's needs, create educational goals, and establish a plan for success.

WEATHERFORD ISD families should know that ARD meetings are required by federal law under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §89.1001. These meetings ensure that your child receives appropriate accommodations and specialized instruction tailored to their individual needs.

Who Attends WEATHERFORD ISD ARD Meetings?

The ARD committee for your child in WEATHERFORD ISD must include specific members:

  • You (the parent or guardian) – Your voice and input are critical to the process
  • Your child – Required if 14 years old or older; encouraged for younger students when appropriate
  • Special education teacher from WEATHERFORD ISD
  • Regular education teacher – Usually the classroom teacher
  • School administrator or designated representative from WEATHERFORD ISD
  • School counselor, psychologist, or diagnostician – When assessment or evaluation is discussed
  • Your advocate or attorney – You may bring someone to support you at any WEATHERFORD ISD ARD meeting

Step-by-Step ARD Process for WEATHERFORD ISD Parents

Step 1: Request an Evaluation (or Initial Referral)

If you believe your child has a disability that affects learning, you can request a comprehensive evaluation from WEATHERFORD ISD. Submit your request in writing to your campus principal or the Special Education Department.

Under Texas law, WEATHERFORD ISD has 15 calendar days to respond to your request. They must either agree to evaluate your child or provide written notice of refusal (which you can challenge).

Step 2: WEATHERFORD ISD Conducts Comprehensive Evaluation

WEATHERFORD ISD will evaluate your child in all areas of suspected disability. This typically includes:

  • Cognitive and intellectual assessment
  • Academic achievement testing
  • Speech and language evaluation (if applicable)
  • Social-emotional and behavioral assessment
  • Occupational or physical therapy evaluation (if indicated)
  • Parent interview and questionnaires
  • Classroom observations

You have the right to receive a copy of all evaluation results before the ARD meeting. Request this from WEATHERFORD ISD at least 5 business days in advance so you have time to review everything.

Step 3: Schedule the Initial ARD Meeting

Once evaluations are complete, WEATHERFORD ISD must schedule an ARD meeting within 30 calendar days of completing the evaluation. You'll receive written notice at least 10 days before the meeting date.

Families in WEATHERFORD ISD should know that you can request to reschedule if the proposed date doesn't work for you. Communication with your school is key—call the Special Education Department to arrange a better time.

Step 4: Prepare for Your WEATHERFORD ISD ARD Meeting

Preparation is your greatest power in a WEATHERFORD ISD ARD meeting. Come ready with information about your child's strengths, challenges, and needs at home and in the community.

Gather and bring:

  • Medical or developmental history records
  • Prior evaluations or assessments from outside providers
  • Examples of your child's schoolwork or behavior patterns
  • Current report cards and teacher feedback
  • A list of questions or concerns you want to address
  • Notes about what accommodations or interventions have helped your child

Consider bringing an advocate or special education attorney familiar with WEATHERFORD ISD procedures. Having a "special education attorney near WEATHERFORD" or a qualified special ed advocate WEATHERFORD families recommend can significantly strengthen your position during the meeting.

Step 5: Review Evaluation Results at Your ARD Meeting

The WEATHERFORD ISD evaluation team will present findings from the comprehensive assessment. Ask questions if anything is unclear. You have the right to understand every aspect of the evaluation before proceeding.

Pay particular attention to:

  • Whether the evaluation was comprehensive and unbiased
  • If all areas of concern were adequately assessed
  • Whether results fairly represent your child's abilities
  • If any additional testing is needed

Step 6: Determine Eligibility Under WEATHERFORD ISD

The ARD committee will determine if your child meets IDEA eligibility criteria. In Texas, students may qualify under 13 disability categories including autism, emotional disturbance, learning disabilities, intellectual disability, other health impairment, and speech impairment.

In WEATHERFORD ISD, eligibility means your child has a disability that adversely affects educational performance and requires special education services. You'll receive written notice of the eligibility decision.

Step 7: Develop the IEP (If Eligible)

If your child is found eligible, the ARD committee will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). The WEATHERFORD ISD IEP process must include:

  • Present levels of performance – How your child currently functions academically and behaviorally
  • Annual goals – What your child will accomplish in the next year
  • Special education services – Type, frequency, and duration of services provided
  • Supplementary aids and services – Accommodations your child needs
  • Least restrictive environment statement – Confirmation that your child will be educated with non-disabled peers to the maximum extent appropriate
  • Assessment accommodations – Any modifications needed for state testing
  • Transition services – If your child is 16 or older

Understanding WEATHERFORD ISD ARD Rights and Safeguards

Your Rights During WEATHERFORD ISD ARD Meetings

As a parent in WEATHERFORD ISD, you have significant protections under federal and state law:

  • Right to notification – You must receive written notice 10 days before any ARD meeting in WEATHERFORD ISD
  • Right to participate – Your input is required; WEATHERFORD ISD cannot hold an ARD meeting without you or a representative
  • Right to interpreter – WEATHERFORD ISD must provide translation if English is not your primary language
  • Right to records – You can access all evaluation results, IEP documents, and school records
  • Right to disagree – If you don't agree with WEATHERFORD ISD's decisions, you have options including mediation or due process
  • Right to independent evaluation – You can request WEATHERFORD ISD to pay for an outside evaluation if you disagree with theirs
  • Right to bring support – Bring an advocate, attorney, or trusted person to any WEATHERFORD ISD ARD meeting

WEATHERFORD ISD IEP Goals: Setting Your Child Up for Success

What Makes Effective WEATHERFORD ISD IEP Goals

Strong WEATHERFORD ISD IEP goals are specific, measurable, and directly address your child's areas of need. Each goal should be achievable within one year and monitored regularly.

Effective goals in WEATHERFORD ISD include:

  • Academic goals – Reading, math, writing, or other academic skills
  • Behavior and social goals – Communication, social interaction, behavior management
  • Life skills goals – Self-care, independence, vocational skills
  • Speech/language goals – Communication abilities (if applicable)
  • Motor skills goals – Fine or gross motor development (if applicable)

Advocating for Quality WEATHERFORD ISD IEP Goals

During your ARD meeting in WEATHERFORD ISD, don't hesitate to:

  • Ask if goals are measurable and specific enough
  • Request goals that address your child's most critical needs
  • Suggest goals based on your observations of what your child struggles with
  • Request clarification on how progress will be measured
  • Ensure goals are ambitious but achievable

If WEATHERFORD ISD proposes goals you don't think are appropriate, you can request different goals. You don't have to sign the IEP immediately—you can take it home to review and return with suggestions.

Annual Review and Triennial Re-Evaluation in WEATHERFORD ISD

Annual ARD Review Meetings

Once your child has an IEP in WEATHERFORD ISD, you'll meet annually to review progress and make adjustments. The annual review in WEATHERFORD ISD includes:

  • Progress on current IEP goals
  • Changes in your child's needs or abilities
  • Modifications to services or accommodations
  • Updated present levels of performance
  • New goals for the upcoming year

Triennial Re-Evaluation for WEATHERFORD ISD Students

Every three years, WEATHERFORD ISD must conduct a comprehensive re-evaluation to determine if your child still qualifies for special education. This ensures services remain appropriate as your child grows and develops.

You can request a re-evaluation sooner if you believe your child's needs have changed significantly.
