Understanding HARLANDALE ISD Special Education Services

HARLANDALE ISD special education encompasses a range of programs designed to support students with disabilities from preschool through high school. Our services are provided in compliance with the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §29.001-29.016.

The special education department at HARLANDALE ISD identifies, evaluates, and provides services to students with qualifying disabilities including:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Emotional and behavioral disorders
  • Intellectual disabilities
  • Specific learning disabilities (including dyslexia)
  • Speech and language impairments
  • Visual and hearing impairments
  • Orthopedic and health impairments
  • Other health impairments (ADHD, traumatic brain injury)

HARLANDALE ISD special education services are delivered through various program models, from inclusive classroom settings with support services to specialized programs designed for students with more significant support needs.

The HARLANDALE ISD ARD Meeting Process

The Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting is central to special education in HARLANDALE ISD. This is where critical decisions about your child's education are made collaboratively between school personnel and parents.

What Happens in a HARLANDALE ISD ARD Meeting?

During your HARLANDALE ISD ARD meeting, the ARD committee reviews evaluation results, discusses your child's current performance, and develops or updates the Individualized Education Program (IEP). The committee typically includes:

  • Special education coordinator or ARD facilitator
  • Special education teacher
  • General education teacher (if your child is in regular classes)
  • School principal or designee
  • Parent(s) or guardian(s)
  • Student (if age-appropriate)
  • Other specialists as needed (speech therapist, counselor, etc.)

Timeline for HARLANDALE ISD ARD Meetings

Parents in HARLANDALE ISD should expect the following timeline for special education evaluation and ARD meetings:

  1. Referral to evaluation: Within 15 calendar days of written parental consent
  2. Initial evaluation: Completed within 60 calendar days of consent
  3. Initial ARD meeting: Within 30 days of completed evaluation
  4. Annual review: At least once per school year
  5. Triennial evaluation: Every three years (can be waived with parent consent)

If you have concerns about timelines, contact the HARLANDALE ISD special education department immediately. Parents have the right to request expedited evaluations in certain circumstances.

HARLANDALE ISD IEP Services and Supports

HARLANDALE ISD IEP services are individualized based on your child's specific needs and goals. The IEP is a legal document that outlines all services, accommodations, and modifications your child will receive.

Types of Services in HARLANDALE ISD

Depending on your child's needs, HARLANDALE ISD IEP services may include:

  • Special education instruction in academic and functional skills
  • Speech and language therapy for communication disorders
  • Occupational therapy for fine motor and daily living skills
  • Physical therapy for gross motor development
  • Counseling services for emotional and behavioral support
  • Assistive technology and adaptive equipment
  • Behavioral support services through positive behavior intervention plans (PBIS)
  • Transition services for students 14 and older preparing for post-secondary life

Accommodations and Modifications

Within HARLANDALE ISD IEP services, your child may receive accommodations (changes to how material is presented) or modifications (changes to what is taught). Common examples include extended time on tests, preferential seating, alternative assignments, or modified curriculum goals.

All accommodations and modifications must be specifically listed in your child's IEP and documented in all classes and settings where the child receives HARLANDALE ISD special education services.

HARLANDALE ISD Dyslexia Program

The HARLANDALE ISD dyslexia program provides specialized instruction for students identified with dyslexia or related literacy disorders. Texas Education Code §38.003 requires all school districts to adopt screening and intervention procedures for dyslexia.

Dyslexia Screening and Identification in HARLANDALE ISD

HARLANDALE ISD screens students for dyslexia risk factors at kindergarten and first grade. Early screening helps identify students who may benefit from intensive reading interventions through the HARLANDALE ISD dyslexia program.

Students showing indicators of dyslexia receive a comprehensive evaluation to determine eligibility for specialized instruction. The HARLANDALE ISD dyslexia program uses evidence-based, structured literacy approaches approved by the Texas Education Agency.

What to Expect from HARLANDALE ISD Dyslexia Services

Students in the HARLANDALE ISD dyslexia program receive:

  • Explicit, systematic phonics instruction
  • Multi-sensory learning approaches
  • Small group or individual instruction
  • Frequent progress monitoring
  • Parent involvement in strategy implementation

If you believe your child may have dyslexia, request a dyslexia evaluation through the HARLANDALE ISD special education department. Early intervention significantly improves reading outcomes.

HARLANDALE ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

Understanding your HARLANDALE ISD parent rights is essential for advocating effectively for your child. Federal law (IDEA) and Texas Education Code guarantee you specific protections and decision-making authority.

Core HARLANDALE ISD Parent Rights

Parents in HARLANDALE ISD have the right to:

  • Request evaluation: Ask HARLANDALE ISD to assess your child if you suspect a disability
  • Informed consent: Receive clear information and give written permission before evaluation or placement changes
  • Prior written notice: Be notified of all proposed changes to your child's special education services
  • IEP participation: Attend and actively participate in all ARD meetings
  • Independent evaluation: Obtain an independent evaluation at public expense if you disagree with HARLANDALE ISD's evaluation
  • Records access: Review all educational records and obtain copies
  • Due process hearing: Request a hearing to resolve disputes with HARLANDALE ISD
  • Mediation: Use free mediation services to resolve conflicts with HARLANDALE ISD

Communicating Your HARLANDALE ISD Parent Rights

HARLANDALE ISD must provide parents with a written notice of procedural safeguards at least once per year. This document explains all HARLANDALE ISD parent rights under IDEA and Texas law. Request a copy if you don't have one, or view it on the HARLANDALE ISD website.

Keep copies of all communications with HARLANDALE ISD, including emails, meeting notices, and IEP documents. These records are important if disputes arise and help you track your child's progress.
