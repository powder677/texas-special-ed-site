Fundamental MANOR ISD Parental Rights

Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and the Texas Education Code, parents in MANOR ISD have specific rights. These rights protect your child and ensure they receive a free and appropriate public education.

As a parent in MANOR ISD, you have the right to:

  • Request evaluation: You can request a complete evaluation free of charge
  • Participate in decisions: You must be present at all meetings concerning your child's special education
  • Access to records: You can review all of your child's school documents and records
  • Informed consent: The school must obtain your written consent before evaluating
  • Due process: You have the right to a fair process if you disagree with MANOR ISD's decisions
  • Interpretation: MANOR ISD must provide interpreters during all meetings
  • Documentation in your language: All special education documentation must be available in Spanish

Knowing your MANOR ISD parental rights empowers you to be an effective advocate for your child throughout the entire special education process.

Step by Step: How to Request an Evaluation in MANOR ISD

Step 1: Recognize Warning Signs

Before requesting a MANOR ISD evaluation, it is helpful to identify the areas where your child has difficulties. Common signs include difficulties with reading, writing, or understanding instructions; challenging behavior; difficulties socializing; or language development delays.

If your child in MANOR ISD has consistent academic difficulties despite classroom intervention, or if they exhibit behaviors that affect their learning, it is time to consider a formal special education evaluation.

Step 2: Submit the Formal Request

To request a MANOR ISD evaluation, you must send a written request to the MANOR ISD special education department. You can do this in several ways:

  • Deliver a written letter to your school's principal
  • Send an email request to the MANOR ISD special education department
  • Complete an application form provided by MANOR ISD
  • Request a meeting with the teacher or school counselor

Important tip: Keep a copy of your request for your records. If sending by email, request confirmation of receipt. This protects your rights in MANOR ISD.

Step 3: Pre-Evaluation Conference

Within 15 school days of receiving your request, MANOR ISD must schedule a pre-evaluation conference. In this meeting, the MANOR ISD special education team will discuss:

  • Your concerns about your child's learning
  • Academic performance history
  • Previous interventions that have been attempted
  • Which areas need evaluation
  • The timeline for completing the evaluation

This conference is an important opportunity to communicate your concerns and ensure that MANOR ISD fully understands your child's needs.

Step 4: Consent for Evaluation

MANOR ISD must obtain your written consent before conducting any special education evaluation. This document must be in Spanish and clearly explain:

  • Which areas will be evaluated
  • Which tests will be used
  • Who will conduct the evaluation
  • When the evaluation will occur

You can ask questions before signing. Do not sign anything you do not fully understand. If you need clarification on the MANOR ISD special evaluation process, ask for it before consenting.

Step 5: Full Special Education Evaluation

Once you give your consent, MANOR ISD has 60 school days to complete a full special education evaluation. This MANOR ISD evaluation must include multiple areas:

  • Academic evaluation
  • Language development evaluation (if applicable)
  • Behavioral evaluation
  • Motor skills evaluation (if applicable)
  • Psychological evaluation
  • Visual and auditory evaluation

MANOR ISD may use different evaluation methods, including formal tests, classroom observation, interviews with teachers and parents, and review of previous records.

Step 6: Eligibility Determination Meeting

Within 30 days of completing the evaluation, MANOR ISD must schedule an eligibility determination meeting. In this meeting, the multidisciplinary MANOR ISD team will review the results and determine if your child qualifies for special education services under the IDEA category.

During this meeting, you will have the opportunity to ask questions, share your observations, and contribute to decisions about the services your child needs.

Special Education Categories in MANOR ISD

If your child qualifies, MANOR ISD special education may be offered under various categories:

  • Specific Learning Disability: Significant difficulties in reading, writing, or mathematics
  • Speech/Language Impairment: Difficulties in communication
  • Intellectual Disability: Significantly below average cognitive functioning
  • Emotional Disturbance: Behavioral or emotional problems affecting learning
  • Autism: Characteristics of the autism spectrum
  • Multiple Disabilities: Multiple disabilities affecting education
  • Orthopedic Impairment: Physical limitations
  • Deaf-Blindness: Combined visual and auditory impairments

The MANOR ISD team will determine in which category, if any, best fits your child's needs.

The Individualized Education Program (IEP) in MANOR ISD

What is an IEP?

If your child qualifies for special education services, MANOR ISD will create an Individualized Education Program (IEP) within 30 days of the eligibility determination. The IEP is a legal document that specifies the services and supports your child will receive.

Components of the MANOR ISD IEP

The MANOR ISD IEP must include:

  • Description of current academic and functional performance
  • Measurable annual education goals
  • Description of how progress is measured
  • Specific special education services
  • Accommodations and modifications
  • Participation in state assessments
  • Related services (speech therapy, counseling, etc.)
  • Duration and frequency of services

Your participation is essential in creating the IEP. As a parent, your perspective on your child's strengths and needs is valuable to MANOR ISD.

Full Individual Evaluation vs. Initial Evaluation in MANOR ISD

FIE (Full Individual Evaluation)

The MANOR ISD FIE (Full Individual Evaluation) is the complete evaluation conducted when a student is first suspected of having a disability. This MANOR ISD evaluation is comprehensive and examines all areas of development that might be affected.

Reevaluations in MANOR ISD

MANOR ISD must reevaluate your child at least every three years to determine if they continue to be eligible for special education. You can also request a reevaluation at any time if you believe your child's needs have changed.

How much does a special education evaluation cost in MANOR ISD?

The special education evaluation in MANOR ISD is completely free. There can be no charges to parents for evaluation, reevaluation, or special education services.

What happens if I disagree with MANOR ISD's results?

Can I be present during the MANOR ISD evaluation?
