HIDALGO ISD Special Education: Complete Resource Guide for Parents

Navigating special education services can feel overwhelming, but you're not alone. HIDALGO ISD special education programs are designed to support students with disabilities and ensure they receive a free, appropriate public education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment. This comprehensive guide provides everything parents in HIDALGO ISD need to know about accessing services, understanding your rights, and advocating effectively for your child.

What is Special Education in HIDALGO ISD?

Special education in HIDALGO ISD is a specialized instructional program tailored to meet the unique needs of students ages 3-21 who have disabilities. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §29.001, HIDALGO ISD is required to provide services at no cost to families.

With an enrollment of 2,771 students, HIDALGO ISD maintains specialized programs and trained staff to serve children with various disabilities, including:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
  • Intellectual disabilities
  • Speech and language impairments
  • Emotional and behavioral disorders
  • Dyslexia and specific learning disabilities
  • Orthopedic impairments
  • Visual and hearing impairments
  • Other health impairments

If you believe your child may qualify for services in HIDALGO ISD, requesting an evaluation is the critical first step.

Understanding the IEP Process at HIDALGO ISD

An Individualized Education Program (IEP) is the foundation of special education services in HIDALGO ISD. The IEP is a legally binding document that outlines your child's goals, services, accommodations, and placement. HIDALGO ISD IEP services are developed collaboratively with school staff, parents, and the student (when appropriate).

The Five Steps of the IEP Process

  1. Referral and evaluation: Parents or school staff request a comprehensive evaluation. HIDALGO ISD must complete testing within 60 calendar days.
  2. Eligibility determination: A multidisciplinary team reviews evaluation results to determine if your child qualifies for special education.
  3. IEP development: If eligible, HIDALGO ISD convenes an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee to develop the IEP.
  4. Implementation: Services begin immediately after the IEP is signed by parents and HIDALGO ISD administrators.
  5. Annual review: HIDALGO ISD must conduct at least one ARD meeting each school year to review progress and update the IEP.

What Should Be in Your Child's IEP?

Your child's IEP should include present levels of academic and functional performance, measurable annual goals, the specific services your child will receive, how often services will be provided, and the extent to which your child will participate in general education classes. It must also address any accommodations, modifications, assistive technology needs, and behavioral supports needed for your child to access education.

The ARD Meeting: Your Opportunity to Advocate

The HIDALGO ISD ARD meeting is where critical decisions about your child's education are made. ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal, and these meetings occur when evaluating whether a child qualifies for services, developing or reviewing an IEP, and determining appropriate placement and services.

Before Your HIDALGO ISD ARD Meeting

  • Gather documentation: Collect report cards, test scores, outside evaluations, medical records, and examples of your concerns.
  • Write down questions: List specific topics you want to discuss—placement, services, goals, behavior plans, or transitions.
  • Request prior notice: HIDALGO ISD must provide at least 10 days' notice before an ARD meeting, unless you waive this requirement.
  • Bring a support person: Consider inviting a family member, advocate, or attorney to help ensure your voice is heard.
  • Review the agenda: Ask HIDALGO ISD in advance what topics will be covered.

During the ARD Meeting

Speak up about your concerns, your child's strengths, and your expectations. HIDALGO ISD staff must listen and explain their recommendations. If you disagree with proposed services or placement, say so. You have the right to request specific evaluations, services, or placements, and HIDALGO ISD must respond in writing.

Don't sign the IEP on the spot if you need time to think. You have the right to take the document home, consult with others, and request another meeting to discuss modifications. HIDALGO ISD cannot deny services while you're considering the IEP.

HIDALGO ISD Dyslexia Program and Specific Learning Disabilities

The HIDALGO ISD dyslexia program addresses one of the most common learning disabilities. Under Texas Education Code §38.003, HIDALGO ISD must screen all students for dyslexia and provide specialized instruction if a child is identified.

Signs Your Child May Have Dyslexia

  • Difficulty learning letter-sound relationships
  • Slow reading speed or difficulty fluency
  • Trouble spelling, especially phonetically irregular words
  • Reversing letters or numbers (like "b" and "d")
  • Difficulty with rhyming or phoneme awareness
  • Strong verbal skills but weak reading/writing performance

Getting Your Child Evaluated in HIDALGO ISD

Request a dyslexia screening from HIDALGO ISD. If your child scores at risk, the district must provide specialized, systematic phonics instruction through a dyslexia intervention program. If dyslexia persists, your child may qualify for special education services in HIDALGO ISD as having a specific learning disability.

Early intervention is critical. Children identified with dyslexia in early grades typically show the most progress with intensive intervention.

Understanding Your Parent Rights in HIDALGO ISD

HIDALGO ISD parent rights are extensive and legally protected. Under IDEA and Texas Education Code, you have the right to participate meaningfully in all decisions affecting your child's special education services.

Core Parent Rights

  • Right to free evaluation: HIDALGO ISD must evaluate your child at no cost if special education is suspected.
  • Right to prior written notice: You must receive written notice before HIDALGO ISD proposes or refuses to initiate any action regarding your child's identification, evaluation, or placement.
  • Right to consent: You must give written permission before HIDALGO ISD conducts an initial evaluation or places your child in special education.
  • Right to participate: You must be invited to and may participate in all ARD meetings regarding your child.
  • Right to records: You have the right to inspect and receive copies of all your child's education records within 10 school days of your request.
  • Right to due process: If you disagree with HIDALGO ISD's decisions, you can file a due process complaint to resolve disputes.
  • Right to mediation: You can request mediation to resolve conflicts with HIDALGO ISD without formal proceedings.

Section 504 Plans vs. IEPs

Some students don't qualify for special education but still have disabilities affecting learning. In HIDALGO ISD, these students may qualify for a Section 504 Plan, which provides accommodations and protections. Unlike an IEP, a 504 Plan doesn't require special education services—only accommodations in general education.

If your child has ADHD, anxiety, asthma, or other health conditions affecting school performance, a 504 Plan from HIDALGO ISD might be appropriate. This is especially important if your child was referred but didn't qualify for special education.

Transition Planning and Adult Services

By age 14 (or younger if HIDALGO ISD determines it appropriate), transition planning must begin. This process helps your student prepare for post-secondary life—employment, higher education, independent living, and community involvement.

What Transition Planning Includes

  • Assessment of your student's strengths, interests, and needs
  • Vocational training and job exploration opportunities
  • Life skills instruction (budgeting, shopping, social skills, self-care)
  • Connections to adult service agencies
  • College or career pathway planning
  • Supported employment or internship opportunities

HIDALGO ISD works with students ages 14-22 to ensure they're ready for the transition out of special education services. Ask about transition goals at your child's ARD meeting.

Taking Action: Next Steps for HIDALGO ISD Parents

If your child isn't currently receiving services from HIDALGO ISD, request an evaluation in writing. Email or deliver a letter to your school's principal or HIDALGO ISD's special education director. Include your child's name, grade, and specific concerns (academic, behavioral, social, or developmental).

Once you submit a referral, HIDALGO ISD has 60 calendar days to complete evaluation and hold an ARD meeting. If you're preparing for an upcoming ARD meeting in HIDALGO ISD, document your concerns, gather supporting documents, and consider bringing a support person or advocate.

Remember: HIDALGO ISD special education services are a right, not a privilege. Your child deserves access to the support needed to learn and thrive. Trust your instincts, ask questions, and advocate firmly for what your child needs.
