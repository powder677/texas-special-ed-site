Understanding GRANBURY ISD Special Education Services

GRANBURY ISD special education encompasses a wide range of services designed to meet individual student needs. Whether your child qualifies under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) or Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, GRANBURY ISD is committed to providing appropriate support.

Special education in GRANBURY ISD includes:

  • Speech-language pathology services for communication disorders
  • Occupational and physical therapy for motor skill development
  • Autism spectrum disorder (ASD) programs with specialized instruction
  • Emotional and behavioral support for students with EBD eligibility
  • Learning disability services for students with dyslexia and other learning differences
  • Intellectual disability programs with life skills instruction
  • Hearing and vision services for sensory impairments
  • Other health impairment (OHI) services for chronic medical conditions

Each service area in GRANBURY ISD is staffed by certified special education professionals dedicated to student success and family partnership.

GRANBURY ISD ARD Meetings and IEP Development

What Is an ARD Meeting?

An ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting in GRANBURY ISD is where your child's special education program is developed, reviewed, and evaluated. This meeting brings together parents, educators, and specialists to create an Individualized Education Program (IEP) tailored to your child's unique needs.

During a GRANBURY ISD ARD meeting, the team will discuss evaluation results, determine eligibility, set measurable goals, and identify the services and supports your child will receive. Parents are equal members of this team, and your input is essential to the process.

GRANBURY ISD IEP Services and Implementation

The GRANBURY ISD IEP services outlined in your child's Individualized Education Program are legally binding commitments. These services must be delivered as written, in the settings specified, and with the frequency determined during your ARD meeting in GRANBURY ISD.

Common IEP services in GRANBURY ISD include:

  • Special education instruction (resource, self-contained, or combination)
  • Related services (speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling)
  • Assistive technology devices and services
  • Accommodations and modifications to curriculum
  • Behavioral intervention plans and positive behavior support
  • Transition services for students ages 14 and up

Parents in GRANBURY ISD receive a copy of the IEP and should review it carefully before the ARD meeting concludes. If you have questions or concerns about proposed services, speak up during the meeting—this is your opportunity to ensure your child's needs are fully addressed.

GRANBURY ISD Dyslexia Program and Reading Support

Dyslexia is a specific learning disability affecting reading, spelling, and language processing. GRANBURY ISD dyslexia program provides specialized instruction using evidence-based interventions for students identified with dyslexia.

Under Texas Education Code §38.003, school districts must provide dyslexia services to students who meet identification criteria. The GRANBURY ISD dyslexia program includes:

  • Universal screening for reading difficulties in elementary grades
  • Specialized dyslexia instruction by trained educators
  • Progress monitoring and data-driven adjustments
  • Parent education about dyslexia and home support strategies
  • Coordination with special education services when applicable

If you suspect your child has dyslexia, request a reading evaluation through GRANBURY ISD. Early identification and intervention significantly improve long-term reading outcomes. Parents in GRANBURY ISD can request dyslexia evaluation during parent-teacher conferences, through your campus principal, or by contacting the Special Education Department directly.

GRANBURY ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

Federal and state law guarantee specific rights for parents in special education. Understanding GRANBURY ISD parent rights empowers you to be an effective advocate for your child's education.

Your Key Rights in GRANBURY ISD Special Education

GRANBURY ISD parent rights include:

  • Notice and consent: You must receive written notice before GRANBURY ISD evaluates your child, proposes changes to services, or requests any evaluation
  • Participation: You have the right to participate in all ARD meetings in GRANBURY ISD and help develop your child's IEP
  • Records access: You may request and review all educational records, evaluations, and IEP documents maintained by GRANBURY ISD
  • Independent evaluation: If you disagree with GRANBURY ISD's evaluation, you may obtain an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at district expense under certain circumstances
  • Due process: You have the right to file a formal complaint or due process hearing if you believe GRANBURY ISD is not providing a free appropriate public education
  • Procedural safeguards: GRANBURY ISD must provide a detailed explanation of your procedural rights at least once yearly

These protections apply to all students in GRANBURY ISD with disabilities, regardless of their specific disability category.

The ARD Meeting Consent Process

Before initiating an evaluation or providing special education services, GRANBURY ISD must obtain your written consent. You have the right to say no, ask questions, or request more time to consider the proposal. Never feel pressured to consent during a meeting if you need time to think or seek outside perspective.

If you disagree with a decision made during a GRANBURY ISD ARD meeting, you can request additional meetings, seek input from a special education advocate, or pursue formal dispute resolution through Texas Education Agency (TEA).

The Evaluation and Eligibility Process in GRANBURY ISD

Before your child can receive special education services in GRANBURY ISD, a comprehensive evaluation must be completed. This evaluation determines whether your child has a disability and needs special education services.

How to Request Evaluation in GRANBURY ISD

You can request an evaluation for your child by:

  1. Contacting your child's campus principal or counselor in writing
  2. Emailing the GRANBURY ISD Special Education Department
  3. Calling the central administration office
  4. Submitting a written request at an ARD meeting in GRANBURY ISD

GRANBURY ISD has 15 calendar days to respond to your request and obtain consent before beginning evaluation. The evaluation process typically takes 60 calendar days, though this timeline can be extended with proper notice.

What the Evaluation Includes

A comprehensive evaluation for GRANBURY ISD special education includes:

  • Cognitive and academic achievement testing
  • Speech and language evaluation (if speech concerns exist)
  • Social-emotional and behavioral assessment
  • Physical and occupational therapy evaluation (if motor concerns exist)
  • Review of medical history and developmental milestones
  • Classroom observation and teacher input
  • Parent input and developmental history

All evaluations must be culturally and linguistically appropriate and conducted in your home language if you request it. GRANBURY ISD must consider all evaluation data before determining eligibility.

Transition Services and Preparing for Life After Secondary Education

For students in GRANBURY ISD approaching high school completion, transition planning becomes a critical component of the IEP services provided. Starting at age 14, GRANBURY ISD ARD meetings must include transition-focused planning.

Transition services in GRANBURY ISD address:

  • Post-secondary education and training options
  • Employment preparation and job exploration
  • Independent living skills development
  • Community participation and social connections
  • Self-determination and self-advocacy skills

Parents in GRANBURY ISD should actively participate in transition planning discussions, sharing insights about your child's strengths, interests, and post-graduation goals. Effective transition planning significantly improves outcomes for students after they leave GRANBURY ISD.
