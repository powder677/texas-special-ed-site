Understanding CARROLL ISD Special Education Services

CARROLL ISD special education programs provide legally mandated services to eligible students under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act. Our district maintains a commitment to identifying, evaluating, and serving students with disabilities in the least restrictive environment.

CARROLL ISD offers specialized services across multiple disability categories, including learning disabilities, intellectual disabilities, autism spectrum disorder, emotional and behavioral disorders, speech-language impairments, visual and hearing impairments, orthopedic impairments, and other health impairments.

Who Qualifies for CARROLL ISD Special Education?

Your child may qualify for CARROLL ISD special education services if they have a documented disability that adversely affects educational performance. Disabilities recognized under IDEA include:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Deaf-blindness
  • Deafness
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Specific Learning Disability
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Visual Impairment

Additionally, CARROLL ISD recognizes students who may qualify under Section 504 plans even if they don't meet IDEA eligibility criteria.

The CARROLL ISD IEP Process: From Referral to Services

CARROLL ISD IEP services begin with a comprehensive evaluation process. An IEP (Individualized Education Program) is a legally binding document that outlines your child's goals, accommodations, and specialized instruction.

Step 1: Referral for Evaluation

Parents in CARROLL ISD can request a special education evaluation at any time. Teachers, school counselors, or other professionals may also refer your child. Submit your written referral request to your campus principal or the CARROLL ISD special education department.

Timeline: CARROLL ISD must respond to your referral within 15 school days with a notice of your procedural rights and proposed evaluation plan.

Step 2: Comprehensive Evaluation

CARROLL ISD conducts thorough assessments across all areas of suspected disability. This evaluation includes:

  • Cognitive and academic testing
  • Speech and language evaluation (if applicable)
  • Behavioral and social-emotional assessment
  • Medical and developmental history review
  • Classroom observation
  • Input from parents and teachers

Timeline: CARROLL ISD must complete the evaluation within 60 calendar days of receiving parental consent.

Step 3: Eligibility Determination

CARROLL ISD convenes an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee to review evaluation results and determine eligibility. This committee includes parents, special education teachers, general education teachers, a school administrator, and other specialists as needed.

You will receive a detailed evaluation report before your meeting so you can review findings and ask questions.

Step 4: IEP Development

If your child qualifies, the ARD committee develops an Individualized Education Program detailing present levels of performance, annual goals, specific services, accommodations, and placement recommendations. Parents in CARROLL ISD have equal voice in all IEP decisions.

CARROLL ISD ARD Meetings: What to Expect and How to Prepare

CARROLL ISD ARD meeting attendance is a critical component of your child's special education journey. These meetings occur at least annually, but may be called more frequently as needed.

Before Your CARROLL ISD ARD Meeting

Preparation ensures you advocate effectively for your child's needs:

  1. Request meeting materials at least five business days in advance
  2. Review your child's progress on current IEP goals
  3. Gather observations from teachers, therapists, and home
  4. Document concerns you have about services or progress
  5. Bring a support person if desired (family member, advocate, or attorney)
  6. Prepare questions about goals, services, and placement

During Your CARROLL ISD ARD Meeting

The meeting should be collaborative and focused on your child's needs. Come prepared to discuss:

  • Current academic and functional performance
  • Progress toward IEP goals
  • Effectiveness of current services
  • New concerns or changes in your child's needs
  • Goals for the upcoming year
  • Necessary service adjustments

Remember: You have equal decision-making power in CARROLL ISD ARD meetings. You can request changes, ask for additional evaluations, or request the meeting be reconvened if you disagree with decisions.

After Your CARROLL ISD ARD Meeting

You will receive a copy of the IEP document. Review it carefully and contact CARROLL ISD immediately if any information is inaccurate or if services aren't implemented as written.

CARROLL ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

CARROLL ISD parent rights are protected under federal and Texas law. Understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child's education.

Your Key Parent Rights in CARROLL ISD Special Education:

  • Right to notice: CARROLL ISD must inform you of all meetings, evaluations, and decisions in writing
  • Right to consent: CARROLL ISD cannot evaluate or place your child without your written permission
  • Right to participate: You have equal decision-making authority in all CARROLL ISD special education meetings
  • Right to records: You can request and review all educational records within 10 business days
  • Right to independent evaluation: If you disagree with CARROLL ISD evaluation, you can request an independent evaluation at district expense
  • Right to due process: You can file a due process complaint if you disagree with CARROLL ISD decisions
  • Right to compensatory education: If CARROLL ISD fails to provide services, your child may receive compensatory services
  • Right to mediation: Before due process, you can request mediation through Texas Education Agency

CARROLL ISD will provide you with a detailed notice of procedural safeguards at the beginning of special education involvement and upon request. This document outlines all your rights under IDEA and Texas Education Code.

CARROLL ISD Dyslexia Program and Reading Intervention

CARROLL ISD dyslexia program provides specialized instruction for students with dyslexia and related reading disorders. Under Texas Education Code §38.003, every CARROLL ISD student struggling with reading receives screening and intervention.

Dyslexia Screening in CARROLL ISD

CARROLL ISD conducts dyslexia screening for all kindergarten and first-grade students. If your older child shows signs of reading difficulty, you can request screening at any time.

Dyslexia screening includes phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension assessment. CARROLL ISD uses research-based instruments to identify students who may benefit from specialized reading instruction.

Dyslexia Services Within CARROLL ISD

Students identified with dyslexia or reading disorders in CARROLL ISD receive:

  • Intensive, specialized reading instruction using evidence-based methods
  • Small group or individual instruction
  • Progress monitoring and data-based decision making
  • Accommodation and modifications as needed
  • Parent communication about progress and strategies

Dyslexia services may be provided through Response to Intervention (RTI) or through a formal CARROLL ISD IEP. The level of need determines which pathway is appropriate for your child.

Making Progress: Monitoring Your Child's IEP in CARROLL ISD

After your child's IEP is implemented in CARROLL ISD, ongoing monitoring ensures your child receives the promised services and makes progress toward goals.

Progress Monitoring in CARROLL ISD

Your child's teachers should provide regular progress reports toward IEP goals. Request data about:

  • Progress on academic goals
  • Progress on behavioral or functional goals

  • Any concerns or changes in your child's performance

Progress monitoring happens continuously, but formal progress reports typically come quarterly or at report card time. If your child isn't making adequate progress in CARROLL ISD, request an IEP meeting to revise goals or services.
