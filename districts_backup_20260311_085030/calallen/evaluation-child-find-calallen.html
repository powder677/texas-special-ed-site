Complete Guide to Requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in CALALLEN ISD

If you're a parent in CALALLEN ISD concerned about your child's learning or development, understanding how to request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is one of the most important steps you can take. This comprehensive guide walks you through the entire process of requesting a special education evaluation in CALALLEN ISD, explaining your rights, timelines, and what to expect every step of the way.

Understanding Your Rights: Child Find in CALALLEN ISD

CALALLEN ISD has a legal obligation under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities who need special education services. This obligation is called "Child Find," and it applies to all students in CALALLEN ISD, regardless of grade level or current academic performance.

As a parent in CALALLEN ISD, you have the right to request an evaluation at any time if you suspect your child may have a disability affecting their education. CALALLEN ISD cannot deny your request or delay it indefinitely. Your child's teacher, school counselor, or administrator may also refer your child for evaluation, but the most direct path forward is often a parent-initiated request.

Understanding this foundational right empowers CALALLEN ISD parents to advocate effectively for their children. You don't need permission from school staff, and you don't need to wait for a teacher recommendation to move forward with a CALALLEN ISD special education evaluation.

When to Request a Full Individual Evaluation for Your CALALLEN ISD Child

Parents in CALALLEN ISD should consider requesting an FIE if their child is experiencing persistent academic struggles, behavioral challenges, social difficulties, or developmental delays. Some common signs that your child may benefit from a CALALLEN ISD FIE evaluation include:

  • Significant gaps between your child's potential and classroom performance
  • Difficulty with reading, math, writing, or other academic skills
  • Speech or language delays compared to peers
  • Frequent behavioral incidents or difficulty following directions
  • Poor social skills or difficulty making friends
  • Inability to focus or stay organized despite your efforts at home
  • Physical coordination problems or fine motor skill delays
  • Anxiety, mood changes, or emotional regulation difficulties

Trusting your instincts matters. If you've noticed patterns of struggle in your CALALLEN ISD child's school experience, requesting an evaluation is a reasonable and protective step that costs nothing and can open doors to vital support services.

Step-by-Step: How to Request a CALALLEN ISD Evaluation

Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing

While you can request a CALALLEN ISD child find evaluation verbally, putting your request in writing creates an important paper trail. Your written request starts the CALALLEN ISD 45 day timeline for the district to complete the evaluation process.

Your written request should be addressed to the special education director or the campus principal at your child's school. Include your child's name, grade level, and specific concerns about their learning or behavior. You don't need formal language—a clear, straightforward email or letter works perfectly.

Keep a copy for your records and request written confirmation that CALALLEN ISD received your request. This documentation protects your rights under special education law.

Step 2: Understand the CALALLEN ISD 45 Day Timeline

Once CALALLEN ISD receives your written request, the district has 45 calendar days to complete the evaluation and provide you with results. This timeline is mandated by Texas Education Code and federal special education law.

CALALLEN ISD cannot extend this timeline without your written permission. However, the timeline may be paused if your child is absent from school or if you don't respond to district requests for information or consent. Understanding this CALALLEN ISD evaluation timeline helps you track progress and hold the district accountable.

Step 3: Consent to Evaluation

CALALLEN ISD must obtain your written consent before conducting any evaluations. You'll receive a consent form explaining which areas the district plans to assess. These areas might include academics, speech and language, motor skills, cognitive ability, social-emotional functioning, and behavior.

Review this form carefully. You have the right to ask questions about what will be evaluated and why. If you have concerns about specific tests or assessments, discuss them with the evaluation coordinator at CALALLEN ISD before signing.

Step 4: Participate in the Evaluation Process

As a parent, you're a crucial part of the evaluation. School staff at CALALLEN ISD will likely contact you to gather developmental history, discuss your child's strengths and challenges, and ask about behaviors you've observed at home.

Be honest and detailed in these conversations. Share specific examples of struggles or successes. If your child receives services outside CALALLEN ISD schools—such as speech therapy or counseling—provide that information as well. The more complete picture CALALLEN ISD staff have, the more accurate the evaluation will be.

What Your CALALLEN ISD Evaluation Will Include

A comprehensive CALALLEN ISD FIE evaluation typically includes assessments in multiple areas relevant to your child's concerns. The evaluation may cover:

  • Academic achievement – Reading, math, writing, and language arts skills
  • Cognitive ability – Processing speed, reasoning, memory, and problem-solving
  • Speech and language – Expressive and receptive language skills
  • Social-emotional functioning – Behavior, mood, relationships, and coping skills
  • Motor skills – Fine motor control and gross motor coordination
  • Adaptive functioning – Daily living and self-care skills
  • Vision and hearing screening – To rule out sensory issues

CALALLEN ISD will use multiple measures and methods to evaluate your child fairly. This includes standardized tests, classroom observations, review of school records, and information from parents and teachers. No single test determines eligibility for special education—CALALLEN ISD staff must look at the whole picture of your child's abilities and needs.

Understanding CALALLEN ISD Eligibility Categories

If the CALALLEN ISD special education evaluation finds that your child has a disability, they may be eligible for services under one of 13 disability categories recognized by IDEA and Texas law. Common categories include:

  • Specific Learning Disability (SLD) – Significant difficulty in reading, math, writing, or listening
  • Speech or Language Impairment (SLI) – Delays or disorders in communication
  • Other Health Impairment (OHI) – Conditions like ADHD, asthma, or seizure disorders
  • Emotional Disturbance (ED) – Behavioral or emotional difficulties affecting learning
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) – Social communication and behavioral differences
  • Intellectual Disability (ID) – Significant limitations in cognition and adaptive behavior
  • Visual or Hearing Impairment – Sensory disabilities

Not every child with struggles will qualify for special education. CALALLEN ISD must determine that your child has a disability AND that the disability adversely affects educational performance AND that your child needs special education services to access the curriculum.

After the CALALLEN ISD Evaluation: The IEP Meeting

Reviewing Evaluation Results

Within the CALALLEN ISD 45 day timeline, the evaluation team will meet with you to discuss findings. You'll receive a written evaluation report explaining what was tested, the results, and recommendations. Review this report carefully before the meeting so you can ask informed questions.

Bring a trusted support person to the meeting if that helps you feel more confident. Many CALALLEN ISD parents find it valuable to have an advocate, family member, or special education attorney present when discussing evaluation results.

Eligibility Determination

The evaluation team—which includes you as the parent—determines whether your child is eligible for special education services under CALALLEN ISD special education programs. If your child qualifies, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be developed.

If your child doesn't qualify for special education but is struggling, ask about Section 504 plans or interventions through CALALLEN ISD's general education programs. Some children benefit from accommodations and support even if they don't meet special education eligibility criteria.

Your Rights During the CALALLEN ISD Evaluation Process

Federal law and Texas Education Code give parents significant rights in CALALLEN ISD's special education evaluation process. Understanding these rights protects your interests and your child's future.

  • Right to request evaluation – You can request a CALALLEN ISD FIE evaluation at any time without permission from school staff
  • Right to independent evaluation – If you disagree with CALALLEN ISD's evaluation, you can obtain an independent evaluation at district expense under certain circumstances
  • Right to the 45-day timeline – CALALLEN ISD must complete evaluation within 45 days of your written request (with limited exceptions)
  • Right to notice and consent – CALALLEN ISD must provide notice in your native language and obtain your written consent before evaluating
  • Right to participate – You must be invited and encouraged to participate in all meetings regarding your child's CALALLEN ISD evaluation and educational planning
  • Right to confidentiality – Your child's evaluation records are protected by privacy laws
  • Right to due process – If you disagree with CALALLEN ISD's decisions, you can file a complaint or pursue mediation or due process hearing

Moving Forward: Next Steps After CALALLEN ISD Evaluation

Once your CALALLEN ISD evaluation is complete, several paths may be available depending on the results:

If your child qualifies for special education, CALALLEN ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). This legally binding document outlines your child's goals, services, accommodations, and placement in the least restrictive environment.

If your child doesn't qualify for special education but has a disability under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, CALALLEN ISD may develop a 504 Plan providing accommodations and support.

If evaluation results show your child doesn't have a disability but is still struggling, work with CALALLEN ISD to implement Response to Intervention (RTI) or other general education support strategies.

Regardless of the outcome, remember that requesting an evaluation through CALALLEN ISD is an investment in your child's future. You're taking action to understand your child's needs and connect them with appropriate support—and that matters tremendously.
