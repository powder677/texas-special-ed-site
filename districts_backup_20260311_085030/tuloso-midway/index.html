TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD Special Education: Complete Resource Guide for Parents

Understanding Special Education in TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD

TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD special education provides a continuum of services for students ages 3-21 with disabilities. Our district serves approximately 3,566 students, with dedicated special education staff committed to ensuring every child receives a free, appropriate public education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment.

Federal and state laws—including the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code—protect your child's right to special education services. In TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD, we follow these mandates to identify, evaluate, and serve students with disabilities across all grade levels and school locations.

Who Qualifies for Special Education Services?

Your child may qualify for TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD IEP services if they have a disability in one of these categories:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
  • Dyslexia and related disorders
  • Emotional/Behavioral Disorders
  • Hearing Impairments
  • Intellectual Disabilities
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairments
  • Other Health Impairments
  • Speech/Language Impairments
  • Specific Learning Disabilities (including dyslexia)
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Visual Impairments

If you suspect your child has a disability affecting their education, contact your school's special education department to request an initial evaluation.

The Referral and Evaluation Process for TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD Families

The journey toward TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD IEP services begins with a referral. Parents, teachers, counselors, or other professionals can request an evaluation to determine if your child qualifies for special education.

Step-by-Step Referral Process

  1. Submit a Referral: Request an evaluation in writing to your campus principal or special education department. In TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD, we accept verbal requests, but written documentation ensures proper documentation.
  2. Notice of Referral: You'll receive written notice within a reasonable timeframe explaining the proposed evaluation and your TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD parent rights.
  3. Obtain Permission: You must provide written consent before TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD can evaluate your child. This is a critical protection under federal law.
  4. Comprehensive Evaluation: The district conducts assessments in all areas of suspected disability, including cognitive ability, academic achievement, behavioral/social-emotional functioning, and other relevant areas.
  5. Evaluation Report: Within 60 calendar days of receiving consent, TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD provides a written evaluation report detailing findings and recommendations.

The evaluation process ensures your child's needs are thoroughly understood before any educational decisions are made. TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD uses multiple assessment tools and gathers information from parents, teachers, and other relevant sources.

Dyslexia Identification and Services in TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD

Dyslexia is a specific learning disability affecting reading, spelling, and language processing. If you're concerned your child may have dyslexia, TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD has a dedicated TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD dyslexia program to identify and support these students.

What You Need to Know About TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD Dyslexia Services

Texas Education Code §38.003 requires school districts to assess all kindergarten and first-grade students for dyslexia risk. TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD continues screening and evaluation throughout elementary grades to identify students who may benefit from dyslexia intervention.

Key components of TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD dyslexia program:

  • Universal screening in early grades
  • Specialized assessments for dyslexia when risk is identified
  • Evidence-based interventions using structured literacy approaches
  • Accommodations such as extended time on reading assignments
  • Assistive technology and text-to-speech tools
  • Regular progress monitoring and adjustment of services

If your child is identified with dyslexia, TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD ensures they receive specialized instruction designed to address reading difficulties and build confidence in academic settings.

Understanding the ARD Meeting: Your Partnership with TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD

An TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal meeting) is where you and school staff collaborate to develop your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP). This is the most important meeting in your child's special education experience.

Before Your TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD ARD Meeting

Preparation ensures you're ready to advocate effectively for your child. TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD will send you a meeting notice at least 10 calendar days in advance, specifying the date, time, location, and attendees.

  • Review your child's current grades, test scores, and behavioral data
  • Write down questions or concerns about your child's progress
  • Gather any outside evaluations or medical reports
  • Bring notes about your child's strengths and areas needing support
  • Consider bringing an advocate or special education attorney familiar with TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD if you anticipate disagreement

During Your TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD ARD Meeting

Your TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD ARD meeting typically includes you, your child's teacher(s), special education coordinator, school counselor, and potentially other specialists. Together, you'll:

  • Review your child's evaluation results and current performance
  • Discuss your child's strengths, needs, and learning style
  • Identify annual IEP goals addressing academic, behavioral, and social-emotional needs
  • Determine the services and supports your child requires (speech therapy, counseling, resource classes, etc.)
  • Plan how your child will access the general curriculum alongside non-disabled peers
  • Address accommodations and modifications to testing and assignments

You have the right to bring anyone you choose to your TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD ARD meeting, including a relative, advocate, or attorney. Your voice matters—this is a collaborative process, not a unilateral decision by the school.

After Your TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD ARD Meeting

You'll receive a copy of the IEP document approved at your TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD ARD meeting. Review it carefully and contact the special education director if you have questions or disagree with any component. You're entitled to a revised meeting if changes are needed.

Knowing Your TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD Parent Rights

TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD parent rights are grounded in federal law and ensure your meaningful participation in all special education decisions. Understanding these rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Core TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD Parent Rights Include:

  • Right to Notice: Receive written notice of all special education decisions in your preferred language
  • Right to Consent: Approve or deny evaluation, initial placement, and significant changes to services
  • Right to Participate: Meaningfully contribute to all IEP and ARD meetings
  • Right to Records: Access and request copies of your child's educational records within 10 business days
  • Right to Independent Evaluation: Obtain an outside evaluation at district expense if you disagree with TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD findings
  • Right to Due Process: File a complaint or request a due process hearing if you believe TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD is not providing FAPE
  • Right to Dispute Resolution: Request mediation as an alternative to due process litigation

TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD provides a Parent Rights Notice at the beginning of the school year and whenever special education begins. Ask your child's school for a copy if you don't have one.

Special Education Services and Programs Available in TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD

TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD offers a range of special education services tailored to meet individual student needs. Your child's IEP will specify which services are appropriate based on evaluation results and goals.

Service Options in TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD Special Education

  • Resource Room Instruction: Part-time specialized services with a special education teacher in a small group or individual setting
  • Self-Contained Classes: Full-time special education instruction for students requiring more intensive support
  • Inclusion with Support: General education classes with special education support services
  • Speech/Language Therapy: Services for students with communication disorders
  • Occupational and Physical Therapy: Support for fine and gross motor skills and daily living functions
  • Behavioral Support Services: Counseling, behavior intervention plans, and emotional support
  • Related Services: Transportation, adaptive materials, and assistive technology

TULOSO-MIDWAY ISD special education staff work collaboratively to ensure your child receives services in the least restrictive environment, meaning alongside non-disabled peers to the maximum extent appropriate.
