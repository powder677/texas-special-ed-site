Understanding What a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) Means for WHITEHOUSE ISD Families

A Full Individual Evaluation is a comprehensive assessment designed to determine whether your child has a disability and whether they qualify for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code.

For families in WHITEHOUSE ISD, an FIE typically includes:

  • Cognitive and intellectual assessment
  • Academic achievement testing
  • Speech and language evaluation (if applicable)
  • Occupational or physical therapy assessment (if needed)
  • Social, emotional, and behavioral evaluation
  • Vision and hearing screening
  • Review of medical and developmental history

The goal is to create a clear picture of your child's strengths, weaknesses, and specific needs so that WHITEHOUSE ISD can develop an appropriate Individualized Education Program (IEP) if your child qualifies for services.

Early Screening Programs in WHITEHOUSE ISD

WHITEHOUSE ISD conducts universal screening in early grades, looking for signs that a child may struggle academically or developmentally. These screenings help identify students who would benefit from a WHITEHOUSE ISD special education evaluation before significant gaps develop.

Parent Concerns and the Right to Request Evaluation

You don't have to wait for WHITEHOUSE ISD to identify a concern. Parents in WHITEHOUSE ISD have the legal right to request a WHITEHOUSE ISD FIE evaluation at any time if they believe their child may have a disability. Schools cannot delay evaluation based on age, grade, or previous performance.

Step-by-Step Process for Requesting a WHITEHOUSE ISD Evaluation

Step 1: Document Your Concerns

Before making a formal request, gather specific examples of your concerns. Note behaviors, academic struggles, or developmental delays you've observed at home. Include dates, examples, and how these concerns affect your child's learning or daily functioning.

Step 2: Request an Evaluation in Writing

Send a written request for a WHITEHOUSE ISD evaluation request to your child's school principal or the Special Education Director. While schools may accept verbal requests, written requests create documentation and start the timeline clock. Your letter should include:

  • Your child's name, grade, and school
  • Specific areas of concern (academic, behavioral, social, developmental)
  • Examples of how these concerns affect your child
  • A request for a full individual evaluation
  • Your contact information

Pro Tip: Keep a copy for your records and consider sending it via email with a read receipt or certified mail. This creates a paper trail that helps protect your rights.

Step 3: Attend the Initial Conference

Within a specified timeframe, WHITEHOUSE ISD will schedule a meeting to discuss your concerns. This meeting typically includes parents, classroom teachers, and special education staff. During this conference, WHITEHOUSE ISD will listen to your concerns and determine whether evaluation is warranted.

Step 4: Provide Consent for Evaluation

If WHITEHOUSE ISD agrees that evaluation is appropriate, you'll receive a consent form outlining the proposed evaluation. Review this carefully and ask questions about what areas will be assessed and which professionals will conduct the testing. You must sign and return this consent form before WHITEHOUSE ISD can begin testing.

Step 5: The Testing Process Begins

Once you've consented, WHITEHOUSE ISD special education staff will conduct various assessments. Depending on your child's needs, testing may occur over several weeks and may involve multiple professionals.

The WHITEHOUSE ISD 45-Day Timeline You Need to Know

One of the most critical aspects of the WHITEHOUSE ISD 45 day timeline is understanding what it means and how it protects your rights.

What the 45-Day Rule Means

Texas Education Code § 29.004 requires that WHITEHOUSE ISD must complete all assessments and hold an eligibility meeting within 45 calendar days of receiving your signed consent for evaluation. This timeline is not a suggestion—it's a legal requirement.

The 45-day clock starts the day WHITEHOUSE ISD receives your signed consent form, not the day you initially requested evaluation. Understanding this distinction helps parents in WHITEHOUSE ISD track their timeline accurately.

What Happens During These 45 Days

During the WHITEHOUSE ISD 45 day timeline, evaluators will:

  • Administer standardized tests and assessments
  • Conduct classroom observations
  • Interview parents and teachers
  • Review school records and medical history
  • Compile results and write comprehensive evaluation reports
  • Schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting

The Eligibility ARD Meeting

At the end of the WHITEHOUSE ISD 45 day timeline, WHITEHOUSE ISD will hold an ARD meeting to discuss evaluation results. This meeting includes parents, teachers, special education staff, and an administrator. During this meeting, WHITEHOUSE ISD will determine whether your child is eligible for special education services under one or more disability categories.
