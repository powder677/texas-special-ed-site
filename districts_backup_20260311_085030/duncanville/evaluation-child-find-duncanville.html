Complete Guide to Requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in DUNCANVILLE ISD

If you're a parent in DUNCANVILLE ISD and suspect your child may have a disability affecting their education, requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is often the first critical step toward getting the support your child needs. Understanding the FIE and Child Find in DUNCANVILLE ISD

A Full Individual Evaluation is a comprehensive assessment conducted by DUNCANVILLE ISD to determine whether your child has a disability and qualifies for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). This evaluation examines your child's academic performance, cognitive abilities, social-emotional functioning, and physical health.

Child Find is DUNCANVILLE ISD's legal responsibility to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities, regardless of the severity of their disability. For parents in DUNCANVILLE ISD, understanding this obligation means you have the right to request an evaluation at any time during your child's school years.

The DUNCANVILLE ISD special education evaluation process is designed to be thorough, fair, and conducted in your child's native language when necessary. DUNCANVILLE ISD must follow specific procedures outlined in the Texas Education Code and federal IDEA regulations.

When to Request a DUNCANVILLE ISD Evaluation

Signs Your Child May Need an Evaluation

Parents in DUNCANVILLE ISD should consider requesting an evaluation if their child exhibits:

  • Significant delays in speech, language, or communication skills
  • Difficulty learning new academic concepts despite appropriate instruction
  • Persistent behavioral or emotional concerns at school or home
  • Challenges with social interactions or peer relationships
  • Suspected sensory impairments (hearing or vision difficulties)
  • Motor skill delays or coordination problems
  • Significant discrepancy between ability and academic achievement

Early Intervention and Kindergarten Entry

If your child is under age 3, DUNCANVILLE ISD coordinates with early intervention services through the state. For children entering kindergarten in DUNCANVILLE ISD, screening at age 3-4 can identify potential concerns early, allowing for timely evaluation requests.

How to Request a DUNCANVILLE ISD Special Education Evaluation

Step 1: Put Your Request in Writing

Your evaluation request must be submitted in writing to DUNCANVILLE ISD. While informal requests sometimes occur in conversations with teachers or administrators, a written request creates an official record and triggers the district's legal timeline obligations. Address your letter to the Director of Special Education or Special Services department.

Your written request should include:

  • Your child's current grade and school
  • Specific concerns or reasons for requesting the evaluation
  • Any symptoms or behaviors you've observed at home
  • Your contact information and preferred method of communication

DUNCANVILLE ISD Special Education Department

Step 3: Know Your Rights During the Process

As a DUNCANVILLE ISD parent, you have the right to:

  • Request an evaluation at any time during your child's school enrollment
  • Receive communication in your native language
  • Participate fully in all evaluation meetings

  • Request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at district expense if you disagree with DUNCANVILLE ISD's findings

The DUNCANVILLE ISD 45-Day Timeline

Understanding the 45-Day Evaluation Window

Once you submit a written request for a DUNCANVILLE ISD FIE evaluation, DUNCANVILLE ISD has 45 calendar days to complete the comprehensive evaluation and convene an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting to discuss results. This timeline is mandated under Texas Education Code § 29.004 and federal IDEA regulations.

The 45-day clock starts when DUNCANVILLE ISD receives your written request. If you submit your request on a Friday, that Friday counts as day one. Weekends and school holidays are included in this count, meaning DUNCANVILLE ISD must work efficiently throughout the timeline.

What Happens During the 45 Days

During this period, DUNCANVILLE ISD will:

  1. Obtain your written consent for the evaluation
  2. Conduct classroom observations and teacher interviews
  3. Administer standardized academic and cognitive assessments
  4. Review your child's academic history and records
  5. Gather developmental and health information from parents
  6. Conduct any necessary specialized evaluations (speech, occupational therapy, etc.)
  7. Compile and analyze all evaluation data
  8. Schedule and conduct the ARD meeting

Extensions to the 45-Day Timeline

DUNCANVILLE ISD may request an extension beyond the standard 45 days only if:

  • You and the district mutually agree in writing to extend the timeline
  • Your child is absent for extended periods during the evaluation window
  • Exceptional circumstances prevent timely completion

Any extension must be documented in writing. For DUNCANVILLE ISD families, understanding that extensions require your agreement protects your rights and ensures timely evaluation completion.

The Evaluation Components in DUNCANVILLE ISD

What Tests and Assessments Are Included?

A comprehensive DUNCANVILLE ISD special education evaluation typically includes multiple assessment components:

  • Cognitive/Intelligence Testing: Measures reasoning, problem-solving, and intellectual functioning
  • Academic Achievement Testing: Assesses reading, math, writing, and other academic skills
  • Speech and Language Evaluation: If communication concerns exist
  • Social-Emotional Assessment: Reviews behavioral and emotional functioning
  • Developmental History Review: Examines early childhood milestones and medical history
  • Classroom Observation: Trained evaluators observe your child in educational settings
  • Functional Behavioral Assessment: If behavioral concerns are present

Specialized Evaluations

If your child's needs require it, DUNCANVILLE ISD may include specialized evaluations such as occupational therapy assessments, physical therapy evaluations, or vision and hearing screenings within the comprehensive evaluation process.

The ARD Meeting: Reviewing DUNCANVILLE ISD Evaluation Results

What to Expect at Your Child's ARD Meeting

At the conclusion of the DUNCANVILLE ISD 45-day timeline, the district convenes an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. DUNCANVILLE ISD will present all evaluation findings, discuss whether your child qualifies for special education services, and if eligible, develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

The ARD meeting for DUNCANVILLE ISD families typically includes:

  • You (the parent/guardian)
  • A DUNCANVILLE ISD special education teacher
  • A general education teacher
  • School or district administrators
  • The school psychologist or evaluator
  • Other specialists as appropriate (speech therapist, etc.)

Your Rights During the ARD Meeting

DUNCANVILLE ISD parents have significant rights during the ARD meeting:

  • You may bring an advocate or attorney to the meeting
  • You may request a second opinion or independent educational evaluation
  • You have the right to disagree with DUNCANVILLE ISD's evaluation conclusions
  • You must provide written consent before special education services begin
  • You may request the meeting be recorded (with proper notice)
  • You may request copies of all evaluation reports

If DUNCANVILLE ISD Denies Your Evaluation Request

Understanding Denial Situations

In rare cases, DUNCANVILLE ISD may refuse to conduct a full evaluation, claiming insufficient evidence of a disability. However, under the Child Find obligation, DUNCANVILLE ISD cannot arbitrarily deny an evaluation request. If DUNCANVILLE ISD refuses or delays your request unreasonably, you have recourse options.

What You Can Do

If DUNCANVILLE ISD denies your evaluation request:

  1. Request a written explanation of why DUNCANVILLE ISD believes evaluation is not warranted
  2. Ask to meet with the Special Education Director to discuss your concerns
  3. Contact a special ed advocate near DUNCANVILLE or a special education attorney near DUNCANVILLE
  4. File a due process complaint with the Texas Education Agency
  5. Request mediation through the TEA
