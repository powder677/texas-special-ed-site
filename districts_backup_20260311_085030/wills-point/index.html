Understanding Special Education in WILLS POINT ISD

WILLS POINT ISD special education serves students ages 3-21 who have disabilities that impact their educational performance. Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, WILLS POINT ISD is required to identify, evaluate, and provide appropriate services to all eligible students.

Special education is not a place—it's a service. Students receive specialized instruction and support to access the general education curriculum while working toward individualized goals. In WILLS POINT ISD, special education services may be delivered in general education classrooms, resource rooms, or specialized settings depending on each student's needs.

Eligibility Categories in WILLS POINT ISD

WILLS POINT ISD recognizes 13 disability categories under IDEA:

  • Autism
  • Deaf-Blindness
  • Deafness
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Multiple Disabilities
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Specific Learning Disability
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Visual Impairment

To qualify for services through WILLS POINT ISD, a student must have a disability and require special education and related services as a result of that disability. A comprehensive evaluation must be conducted to determine eligibility.

The Evaluation and IEP Process in WILLS POINT ISD

Understanding WILLS POINT ISD IEP services begins with knowing how the evaluation and IEP development process works. An IEP (Individualized Education Program) is a legal document that outlines your child's educational goals, services, and accommodations.

Step 1: Referral and Consent

Any parent, teacher, or school staff member can request an evaluation. If you believe your child may need special education services, submit a written request to the special education director at WILLS POINT ISD. The district must provide notice and obtain your written consent before conducting any evaluations.

Once you consent, WILLS POINT ISD has 60 calendar days to complete the comprehensive evaluation and determine eligibility.

Step 2: Comprehensive Evaluation

The evaluation team at WILLS POINT ISD uses multiple assessment methods to gather information about your child's strengths and needs. This may include:

  • Academic assessments and classroom performance data
  • Cognitive and developmental testing
  • Speech and language evaluations
  • Occupational and physical therapy assessments
  • Social, emotional, and behavioral observations
  • Parent and teacher input

You have the right to participate in every step of the evaluation process and to ask questions about the assessment results.

Step 3: Eligibility Meeting and IEP Development

After the evaluation is complete, WILLS POINT ISD will hold an eligibility meeting with you, the evaluation team, and other relevant staff. At this meeting, the team will discuss whether your child qualifies for special education services.

If eligible, the team will immediately begin developing your child's WILLS POINT ISD IEP services plan. The IEP includes present levels of performance, annual goals, accommodations, modifications, and related services such as speech therapy, occupational therapy, or counseling.

Your Rights as a Parent in WILLS POINT ISD

WILLS POINT ISD parent rights are protected under federal and state law. Understanding your rights empowers you to be an effective advocate for your child. WILLS POINT ISD families have the right to:

Core Parent Rights

  • Participate in all decisions regarding evaluation, eligibility, and services for your child
  • Receive notice in your native language of all meetings and decisions
  • Inspect and review all educational records
  • Request an independent evaluation at district expense if you disagree with WILLS POINT ISD's evaluation
  • Request a due process hearing if you disagree with identification, evaluation, or placement decisions
  • Obtain copies of all evaluations and documents at no cost
  • Bring an advocate or attorney to all meetings

WILLS POINT ISD must provide you with a complete notice of procedural safeguards in writing at the beginning of the year and whenever you request it.

The ARD Meeting: Your Voice in Special Education Decisions

A WILLS POINT ISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal meeting) is where all special education decisions are made for your child. Understanding what happens in these meetings helps you prepare to advocate effectively.

What Happens During a WILLS POINT ISD ARD Meeting

The WILLS POINT ISD ARD meeting brings together a team that includes you, your child's teachers, special education staff, and other specialists. Together, you will:

  1. Review your child's progress toward current IEP goals
  2. Discuss your child's strengths and areas of need
  3. Develop or revise annual goals
  4. Determine what services and supports your child needs
  5. Decide the appropriate placement (general education, resource, or specialized setting)
  6. Discuss accommodations and modifications
  7. Plan for transition services (for students age 16 and older)

Preparing for Your WILLS POINT ISD ARD Meeting

Before attending a WILLS POINT ISD ARD meeting, gather information about your child's progress at home and school. Write down questions you have and goals you want included. Consider bringing your own assessment data or outside evaluations to the meeting.

Parents in WILLS POINT ISD have the right to request the meeting be held at a convenient time and location. You may bring an advocate, family member, or attorney to support you during the meeting.

WILLS POINT ISD Dyslexia Program and Reading Interventions

The WILLS POINT ISD dyslexia program provides specialized instruction for students with dyslexia and related reading disabilities. Dyslexia is a specific learning disability that affects how the brain processes written language, and early intervention is critical.

Identifying Dyslexia in WILLS POINT ISD

Students in WILLS POINT ISD who struggle with reading, spelling, or writing may be screened for dyslexia. The WILLS POINT ISD dyslexia program uses evidence-based assessments to identify students who need specialized reading instruction.

If your child is identified as dyslexic, WILLS POINT ISD will develop a dyslexia intervention plan that includes intensive, structured literacy instruction. Many students with dyslexia who receive appropriate intervention in WILLS POINT ISD make significant progress in reading.

Dyslexia Services and Accommodations

Students in the WILLS POINT ISD dyslexia program may receive:

  • Pull-out or in-class intensive reading instruction
  • Use of assistive technology (text-to-speech, audiobooks)
  • Extra time on reading and writing tasks
  • Alternative assessments
  • Multisensory, phonics-based instruction
  • Collaboration between general education and special education teachers

Parents in WILLS POINT ISD should request regular progress monitoring data to ensure interventions are effective. If your child is not making adequate progress, the team should meet to adjust the plan.

Frequently Asked Questions About WILLS POINT ISD Special Education

How do I request an evaluation for my child in WILLS POINT ISD?

Submit a written request to the Special Education Department at WILLS POINT ISD. You can email, mail, or deliver the request in person. The district must respond with a written notice within a reasonable time and obtain your consent before beginning the evaluation.

What if I disagree with WILLS POINT ISD's evaluation results?

You have the right to request an independent evaluation at district expense. WILLS POINT ISD must provide information about where you can obtain an independent evaluation. You can also bring the independent evaluation to your next ARD meeting for consideration.

Can my child remain in general education with WILLS POINT ISD special education services?

Yes. WILLS POINT ISD is required to provide services in the least restrictive environment, meaning your child should be educated with non-disabled peers to the maximum extent appropriate. Many students receive special education services while spending most or all of their day in general education classrooms.

What happens to my child's IEP when they graduate from WILLS POINT ISD?

Special education services end when your child graduates with a diploma or reaches age 22. WILLS POINT ISD must provide transition planning starting at age 16 to help prepare your child for post-secondary education, employment, and independent living.
