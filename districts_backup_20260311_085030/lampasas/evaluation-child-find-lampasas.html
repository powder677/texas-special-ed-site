Complete Guide to Requesting a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in LAMPASAS ISD

If you're a parent in LAMPASAS ISD concerned about your child's learning or development, understanding how to request a LAMPASAS ISD FIE evaluation is one of the most important steps you can take. A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is the comprehensive assessment that determines whether your child qualifies for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA).

This guide walks parents in LAMPASAS ISD through every step of the evaluation process, including timelines, your rights, and what to expect when you submit your request.

Understanding Child Find and Your Rights in LAMPASAS ISD

LAMPASAS ISD is legally required to identify, locate, and evaluate all children within the district who may have disabilities and need special education services. This responsibility is called "child find." As a parent, you have the right to request a LAMPASAS ISD child find evaluation at any time if you suspect your child has a disability affecting their education.

Under Texas Education Code §29.004, LAMPASAS ISD must conduct a child find process for all students, including those in private schools, homeschooled students, and children not yet in school. If your child attends school in LAMPASAS ISD or lives within the district boundaries, the district is responsible for locating and serving them.

What Qualifies for Evaluation in LAMPASAS ISD

LAMPASAS ISD evaluates children suspected of having disabilities in 13 categories under IDEA, including:

  • Autism spectrum disorder
  • Emotional disturbance
  • Hearing impairment
  • Intellectual disability
  • Specific learning disability
  • Speech or language impairment
  • Visual impairment
  • Orthopedic impairment
  • Traumatic brain injury
  • Other health impairment
  • Attention deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD)
  • Deaf-blindness
  • Multiple disabilities

If you believe your child may have one of these conditions and it's affecting their ability to learn, you can request a LAMPASAS ISD special education evaluation.

How to Request a Full Individual Evaluation from LAMPASAS ISD

The process for requesting a LAMPASAS ISD evaluation request begins with formal notification to the district. Here's exactly how to do it:

Step 1: Contact the Special Education Department

Your first step is to reach out to LAMPASAS ISD's special education department. You can request an evaluation through:

  • Written request (email or letter) to the special education director
  • Verbal request to your child's school principal or teacher
  • Phone call to LAMPASAS ISD special education services

A verbal request made to any school employee must be documented in writing within two business days by LAMPASAS ISD.

Step 2: Submit Your Request in Writing

While verbal requests are legal, sending a written request to LAMPASAS ISD creates a clear record of when you initiated the evaluation process. This is important for timeline compliance. Your letter should include:

  • Your child's name, date of birth, and grade level
  • Your name and contact information
  • A brief description of your concerns (academic, behavioral, developmental, speech, etc.)
  • Examples of specific behaviors or struggles you've observed
  • The date you're submitting the request

Keep a copy for your records and consider sending it via email or certified mail so LAMPASAS ISD cannot claim they never received it.

Step 3: Understand the Response Timeline

This is critical: LAMPASAS ISD must provide you with written notice within 15 calendar days of receiving your request. This notice will inform you whether the district agrees or disagrees with evaluating your child. If LAMPASAS ISD refuses to evaluate your child, they must explain why in writing and provide you with information about your due process rights.

The LAMPASAS ISD 45 Day Timeline Explained

Once LAMPASAS ISD agrees to conduct an evaluation, the district operates under a strict LAMPASAS ISD 45 day timeline to complete the LAMPASAS ISD FIE evaluation. This 45-day deadline is mandated by Texas law and begins the day LAMPASAS ISD receives your written consent for evaluation.

What Happens During the 45-Day Window

Within these 45 calendar days, LAMPASAS ISD must:

  • Conduct comprehensive assessments in all areas of suspected disability
  • Review existing evaluation data
  • Gather information from parents, teachers, and other relevant sources
  • Administer formal and informal tests
  • Complete a written evaluation report
  • Hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to review findings
  • Provide you with a copy of the evaluation report before the ARD meeting

After the 45 Days: The ARD Meeting

At the end of the LAMPASAS ISD 45 day timeline, LAMPASAS ISD will schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. At this meeting, the evaluation team will present findings and determine whether your child qualifies for special education services under one or more disability categories. You have the right to bring an advocate, attorney, or supportive person to this meeting.

If your child qualifies, LAMPASAS ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) at the same meeting. If your child does not qualify, you'll receive written notice explaining the reasons why.

What to Expect During a LAMPASAS ISD Special Education Evaluation

A comprehensive LAMPASAS ISD special education evaluation goes far beyond a single test. The FIE process includes multiple components to get a complete picture of your child's abilities, challenges, and needs.

Components of the FIE

Academic Achievement Testing: LAMPASAS ISD evaluators will assess your child's reading, math, writing, and overall academic performance through standardized tests.

Cognitive/Intellectual Assessment: Trained psychologists administer intelligence tests to understand your child's thinking abilities and processing speed.

Speech and Language Evaluation: If speech concerns exist, a speech-language pathologist will evaluate your child's communication skills.

Social-Emotional Assessment: Behavioral rating scales and observations help identify emotional or behavioral concerns affecting learning.

Developmental History Review: LAMPASAS ISD will collect information about your child's medical, developmental, and educational history.

Classroom Observation: District evaluators observe your child in the classroom setting to see how they function in a typical school environment.

Parent and Teacher Input: You'll complete rating scales and questionnaires, as will your child's teachers. Your input is crucial to the evaluation process.

Your Rights as a Parent During the LAMPASAS ISD Evaluation Process

Parents in LAMPASAS ISD have significant legal protections throughout the special education evaluation process. Knowing your rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

Key Parent Rights

  • Right to Request Evaluation: You can request a LAMPASAS ISD FIE evaluation at any time without waiting for teacher referral or district initiation.
  • Right to Informed Consent: LAMPASAS ISD must explain the evaluation process and obtain your written permission before testing begins.
  • Right to Review Records: You can access all evaluation materials, test results, and reports LAMPASAS ISD develops.
  • Right to Independent Evaluation: If you disagree with LAMPASAS ISD's findings, you can request an independent evaluation at district expense under certain circumstances.
  • Right to Interpretation: LAMPASAS ISD must provide evaluation in your child's native language or communication method.
  • Right to Bring Support: You may bring an advocate, attorney, or support person to meetings without prior notice.
  • Right to Due Process: If you disagree with evaluation findings or LAMPASAS ISD denies evaluation, you can file for mediation or due process hearing.

What If LAMPASAS ISD Denies Your Evaluation Request?

If LAMPASAS ISD refuses to conduct a LAMPASAS ISD special education evaluation, don't assume the process is over. You have options and protections.

Understanding Denial Reasons

LAMPASAS ISD must provide specific, detailed reasons in writing for any denial. Common reasons include:

  • No indication of disability from available evidence
  • Academic struggles due to lack of instruction rather than disability
  • Behavioral concerns unrelated to a disability condition

Even if LAMPASAS ISD initially denies evaluation, you can request an independent evaluation and provide additional documentation supporting your concerns.

Your Next Steps If Denied

If LAMPASAS ISD denies your LAMPASAS ISD evaluation request, you can:

  • Request a written explanation of the denial
  • Request prior written notice, which explains your due process rights
  • File for mediation with the Texas Education Agency (TEA)
  • File for due process hearing with TEA
  • Consult with a special education attorney near LAMPASAS or a special ed advocate LAMPASAS who can review your case

Parents in LAMPASAS ISD can take several steps to make the evaluation process as smooth and effective as possible.

Before the ARD Meeting

  • Consider bringing an advocate or support person to the meeting

Understanding Your Options After LAMPASAS ISD Evaluation Results

Once LAMPASAS ISD completes the LAMPASAS ISD FIE evaluation and holds the ARD meeting, you'll have important decisions to make about your child's future educational services.

If Your Child Qualifies for Special Education

LAMPASAS ISD will develop an IEP outlining:

  • Your child's present levels of academic and functional performance
  • Annual goals and short-term objectives
  • Special education services and related services your child needs
  • Least restrictive environment (LRE) placement decisions
  • How progress will be monitored and reported

You have the right to question any aspect of the IEP that LAMPASAS ISD proposes and request modifications.

If Your Child Does Not Qualify

If LAMPASAS ISD determines your child does not meet criteria for special education, you can:

  • Request an independent evaluation
  • Ask LAMPASAS ISD about 504 Plan eligibility (different from special education)
  • Request a due process hearing if you disagree with the determination
  • Seek consultation with a special ed advocate LAMPASAS to review the findings
