Understanding What an FIE Evaluation Is in DECATUR ISD

A Full Individual Evaluation, or FIE, is a complete assessment of your child's educational, developmental, behavioral, and social needs. In DECATUR ISD, this evaluation determines whether your child qualifies for special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code Section 29.001.

The DECATUR ISD special education evaluation process examines multiple areas of your child's functioning, including:

  • Cognitive and intellectual abilities
  • Academic achievement and performance
  • Speech and language development
  • Motor skills and physical development
  • Social-emotional and behavioral functioning
  • Vision and hearing capabilities
  • Health and developmental history

For DECATUR ISD parents, knowing that your child will receive a thorough, multi-disciplinary evaluation means you're getting a complete picture of your child's strengths and needs—not just a single test result.

DECATUR ISD Child Find: How the District Identifies Students Who Need Evaluation

DECATUR ISD child find procedures ensure that all children who may have a disability are identified and evaluated. Child find is a proactive system designed to locate students who might need special education services before they fall dramatically behind.

Who Initiates Child Find in DECATUR ISD?

Anyone involved with your child can trigger the child find process in DECATUR ISD:

  • Parents who have concerns about development or learning
  • Doctors and medical professionals who work with your child

The key is that DECATUR ISD is legally obligated to evaluate any child suspected of having a disability, regardless of whether the child is currently struggling in the classroom.

What Happens When Child Find Is Initiated in DECATUR ISD?

How to Request an FIE Evaluation in DECATUR ISD: Step-by-Step Process

Parents in DECATUR ISD have the right to request a DECATUR ISD evaluation request at any time. Here's the process you should follow:

Step 1: Submit Your Written Request

Put your request in writing and submit it to DECATUR ISD's Special Education Department. Your request should include:

  • Your child's name and grade level
  • Your contact information
  • Specific concerns you have about your child's learning, behavior, or development
  • Any previous evaluations or assessments your child has received
  • Information about family history of learning disabilities or disabilities

While a phone call to your child's school or the special education office can start the conversation, always follow up with a written request. Email works, and a paper letter sent certified mail is even better for documentation.

Step 2: DECATUR ISD Reviews Your Request

Once DECATUR ISD receives your special education evaluation request, the district has a legal obligation to respond. The district may request additional information from you about your child's history, development, and current functioning. Answer these questions thoroughly—the more information DECATUR ISD has, the more comprehensive the evaluation can be.

Step 3: Permission to Evaluate

DECATUR ISD must obtain your written consent before beginning the full evaluation. You'll receive a Prior Written Notice that explains the evaluation plan, the specific tests and assessments that will be used, and your rights as a parent. You must sign and return this form to authorize the evaluation.

This is a critical protection for parents in DECATUR ISD—you have the right to know exactly what will be tested and how, and you can request changes to the evaluation plan if you believe certain areas are missing.

Step 4: The Evaluation Team Conducts the FIE in DECATUR ISD

DECATUR ISD's evaluation team typically includes a school psychologist, special education teacher, classroom teacher, and other specialists as needed (speech therapist, occupational therapist, etc.). These professionals will:

  • Administer standardized tests and assessments
  • Observe your child in the classroom and other settings
  • Review your child's school records and medical history
  • Gather information from you about your child's development and functioning at home
  • Consider any cultural or linguistic factors that might affect results

The DECATUR ISD 45-Day Timeline: What You Need to Know

The DECATUR ISD 45 day timeline is one of the most important protections you have as a parent. From the date DECATUR ISD receives your written request for evaluation (or your signed consent to evaluate), the district has 45 school days to complete the entire evaluation and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting to discuss results.

Why the 45-Day Timeline Matters

This timeline ensures that your child doesn't wait months to find out whether they need special education services. It also prevents districts from indefinitely delaying evaluations. For DECATUR ISD families, knowing this deadline means you can hold the district accountable and plan accordingly.

Exceptions to the 45-Day Timeline in DECATUR ISD

DECATUR ISD can request extensions if:

  • You don't return requested paperwork or consent forms

Once DECATUR ISD completes the DECATUR ISD special education evaluation, the district schedules an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting. This is where your child's evaluation team, including you, reviews results and makes decisions about eligibility for special education services.
