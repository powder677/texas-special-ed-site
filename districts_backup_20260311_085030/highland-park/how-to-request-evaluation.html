A special education evaluation is a comprehensive process that determines if your child has a disability affecting their learning and if they need special education services. In HIGHLAND PARK ISD, these evaluations include academic testing, behavioral assessments, and classroom observations.

The purpose of a HIGHLAND PARK ISD special evaluation is to identify your child's strengths and needs to develop an individualized education plan, known as an IEP (Individualized Education Program).

Components of a Comprehensive Evaluation

A comprehensive HIGHLAND PARK ISD evaluation should include:

  • Cognitive and intellectual aptitude evaluation
  • Academic achievement tests
  • Behavioral and emotional evaluation

Under Texas law and IDEA, HIGHLAND PARK ISD parent rights include the right to request an evaluation, participate in all educational decisions, and access your child's information. These rights are designed to protect students and ensure they receive an appropriate education.

Key Rights You Should Know

  • Right to request evaluation: You can request an evaluation at any time if you believe your child has a disability
  • Right to participation: You must participate in all meetings and decisions regarding your child's education
  • Right to a fair evaluation: The evaluation must be culturally appropriate and in your language if necessary
  • Right to due process: If you disagree with HIGHLAND PARK ISD's decisions, you can request a hearing
  • Right to confidentiality: All information about your child must be kept confidential
  • Right to obtain a copy of results: You will receive copies of all evaluations and reports

How to Request an Evaluation in HIGHLAND PARK ISD

The process for HIGHLAND PARK ISD requesting an evaluation is straightforward. You can initiate the process by contacting your child's school or directly with the HIGHLAND PARK ISD special education department.

Step 1: Make Your Request in Writing

Send a letter or email to your school principal or the HIGHLAND PARK ISD special education coordinator formally requesting an evaluation. Your request should include your child's name, date of birth, and a brief description of your concerns.

It is important that your request is in writing. This creates an official record and starts the clock for legal timelines. You can request an appointment with the principal or the HIGHLAND PARK ISD special education coordinator to discuss your concerns in person.

Step 2: Await HIGHLAND PARK ISD's Response

Under Texas law, HIGHLAND PARK ISD must respond to your request within 10 school days. During this time, the school will gather information and assess whether a formal evaluation is necessary.

HIGHLAND PARK ISD may determine that a formal evaluation is needed or may propose an initial intervention plan. If the school believes an evaluation is not necessary, they must provide written reasons.

Step 3: Consent for Evaluation

If HIGHLAND PARK ISD determines an evaluation is appropriate, you will receive a document called "Notice of Proposed Evaluation" detailing what will be evaluated and why. You must sign a consent form for HIGHLAND PARK ISD to proceed with the evaluation.

You have the right to ask questions about the proposed evaluation before giving your consent. Make sure you understand what tests will be administered and who will conduct them.

The HIGHLAND PARK ISD FIE Evaluation Process

A HIGHLAND PARK ISD FIE (Full Individual Evaluation) is the complete formal evaluation that determines your child's eligibility for special education services. The HIGHLAND PARK ISD FIE process involves multiple evaluators and can take several weeks.

Evaluation Timeline

HIGHLAND PARK ISD has 60 school days from receiving your consent to complete the evaluation and hold an IEP meeting. This timeline is established by federal law and is important to ensure decisions are made promptly.

  • Days 1-5: Information is gathered and evaluations are planned
  • Days 6-40: Tests and evaluations are administered
  • Days 41-55: Results are analyzed and reports are prepared
  • Day 60: IEP meeting to discuss results

Information HIGHLAND PARK ISD Will Collect

During the HIGHLAND PARK ISD FIE evaluation, evaluators will collect information from multiple sources:

  • Your child's medical and developmental history
  • Previous academic records
  • School discipline information
  • Interviews with teachers and school staff
  • Information from your family
  • Direct observations at school

Understanding the Results and the IEP

After completing the HIGHLAND PARK ISD special evaluation, you will receive a detailed report explaining the findings. If your child is eligible for services, the results will be used to create their Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Eligibility Categories in HIGHLAND PARK ISD

HIGHLAND PARK ISD may qualify your child under one of the following categories if they have a disability affecting their education:

  • Autism
  • Deafness or blindness
  • Intellectual disability
  • Multiple disabilities
  • Orthopedic impairment
  • Other health impairment
  • Emotional disturbance
  • Specific learning disability
  • Speech or language impairment
  • Visual impairment
  • Traumatic brain injury

The Individualized Education Program (IEP)

The IEP is a legal document that describes the special education services HIGHLAND PARK ISD will provide to your child. It includes academic goals, support services, and how your child's progress will be measured.

As a parent in HIGHLAND PARK ISD, you are an important member of the IEP team. Your opinions, concerns, and information about your child at home are valuable in creating a plan that truly helps your child succeed.
