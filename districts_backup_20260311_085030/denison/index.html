Understanding DENISON ISD Special Education Services

DENISON ISD special education serves students ages 3-21 who have disabilities that affect their ability to learn in a general education setting. Our district provides a full continuum of services, from inclusion in regular classrooms with support to specialized programs designed for students with significant learning differences.

DENISON ISD currently serves 4,977 students across our schools. We employ special educators, speech therapists, occupational therapists, counselors, and other specialists dedicated to meeting each child's unique needs. Whether your child has autism spectrum disorder, dyslexia, speech and language delays, emotional disabilities, intellectual disabilities, or other conditions, DENISON ISD has trained professionals ready to help.

Our special education services are provided at no cost to families in DENISON ISD. All services are funded through federal, state, and local education dollars, ensuring that every eligible student receives the support they need regardless of their family's financial situation.

DENISON ISD ARD Meetings and the IEP Process

The heart of special education in DENISON ISD is the DENISON ISD ARD meeting. ARD stands for "Admission, Review, and Dismissal," and it's the meeting where your child's individualized education plan is created and reviewed.

What Happens at a DENISON ISD ARD Meeting?

During your DENISON ISD ARD meeting, a team of professionals and parents come together to discuss your child's strengths, challenges, and educational goals. The ARD committee reviews evaluation results, discusses current performance, and makes decisions about services and placement.

Every DENISON ISD ARD meeting must include:

  • The parent or guardian
  • The student (when appropriate)
  • A general education teacher
  • A special education teacher or specialist
  • A school administrator or designee
  • Someone who can interpret evaluation results
  • Other specialists as needed (speech therapist, counselor, etc.)

Timeline and Frequency of DENISON ISD ARD Meetings

DENISON ISD must hold an ARD meeting within 30 days of receiving a complete referral for evaluation. After your child is found eligible, DENISON ISD ARD meetings are held at least once yearly to review the IEP, though meetings can be scheduled more frequently as needed.

You have the right to request an DENISON ISD ARD meeting at any time if you have concerns about your child's progress. Simply contact your campus special education coordinator or the DENISON ISD special education department to schedule a meeting.

DENISON ISD IEP Services and Support Options

Your child's DENISON ISD IEP services are detailed in an Individualized Education Program document. This document outlines exactly what services your child will receive, where they'll receive them, and who will provide them.

Types of DENISON ISD IEP Services

DENISON ISD IEP services may include:

  • Special education instruction – pull-out or co-taught classes with a certified special educator
  • Related services – speech and language therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, or physical therapy
  • Assistive technology – devices or software that help your child access learning
  • Classroom modifications and accommodations – changes to how material is presented or how your child demonstrates learning
  • Behavioral support services – for students who need help managing behavior in school
  • Transition planning – for students age 14 and older, preparing for post-secondary education and employment

Placement Options in DENISON ISD

DENISON ISD is committed to serving students in the least restrictive environment, meaning your child should be educated with non-disabled peers to the greatest extent possible. Our district offers multiple placement options through DENISON ISD IEP services.

Many students in DENISON ISD spend most of their day in general education classrooms with special education support. Others benefit from a combination of general education classes and specialized small-group instruction. A smaller number of students require a self-contained classroom where all instruction is provided by a special educator.

DENISON ISD Dyslexia Program and Specialized Interventions

DENISON ISD recognizes that dyslexia program services are essential for students who struggle with reading. Our district provides a comprehensive DENISON ISD dyslexia program that includes screening, assessment, and evidence-based intervention.

How the DENISON ISD Dyslexia Program Works

Every student in DENISON ISD is screened for dyslexia during elementary years. If your child shows signs of reading difficulties, our specialists conduct a comprehensive evaluation to determine if your child has dyslexia or another reading disability.

Students identified through the DENISON ISD dyslexia program receive intensive, structured literacy intervention. This specialized instruction is grounded in research and focuses on phonemic awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension.

The DENISON ISD dyslexia program may be provided through an IEP if your child qualifies for special education, or through a 504 Plan if your child has dyslexia but doesn't need special education. Either way, your child receives the evidence-based support needed to become a strong reader.

Parent Involvement in Dyslexia Intervention

As a parent of a child in the DENISON ISD dyslexia program, you'll receive regular updates on your child's progress. Ask your child's reading specialist for specific strategies you can use at home to support literacy development.

Understanding DENISON ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

Federal and Texas law give parents of students in special education significant rights and protections. Understanding your DENISON ISD parent rights is essential to being an effective advocate for your child.

Your Core DENISON ISD Parent Rights

As a parent in DENISON ISD, you have the right to:

  • Be involved in all decisions about your child's evaluation, eligibility, IEP, and placement
  • Request an evaluation at any time if you believe your child may have a disability
  • Review all records related to your child's special education at least 5 school days before any ARD meeting
  • Have an interpreter at all meetings if English is not your primary language
  • Bring support – an advocate, attorney, or family member can attend DENISON ISD ARD meetings with you
  • Disagree with decisions and request independent evaluations or due process hearings
  • Receive written notice of all proposed changes to your child's services
  • Receive a copy of your child's IEP at least 5 days before implementation

Exercising Your DENISON ISD Parent Rights

Your DENISON ISD parent rights are only meaningful if you use them actively. Don't hesitate to ask questions during DENISON ISD ARD meetings. Request clarification about evaluation results, services, and placement decisions. If you disagree with the school's recommendations, say so clearly and ask for time to consider your options.

DENISON ISD provides all parents with a notice of procedural safeguards at the beginning of each school year. This document explains your DENISON ISD parent rights in detail. If you don't have a copy, request one from your campus principal or the DENISON ISD special education department.

Getting Started: How to Request Special Education Evaluation in DENISON ISD

If you believe your child may have a disability affecting school performance, you can request an evaluation. Here's how the process works in DENISON ISD:

Step-by-Step Process for DENISON ISD Evaluation Requests

  1. Submit a written request to your campus principal or the DENISON ISD special education department. Email or hand-delivered letters work – the request just needs to be in writing.
  2. Wait for DENISON ISD's response within 5 school days. The school will explain what happens next and when the evaluation will begin.
  3. Give permission before testing begins. You'll receive a form asking you to consent to evaluation. DENISON ISD cannot proceed without your written consent.
  4. Participate in the evaluation process. Provide information about your child's developmental history, health, and behavior at home. Your observations are valuable information.
  5. Attend the ARD meeting where evaluation results are discussed. This typically happens within 60 days of your initial request.
  6. Receive the decision about whether your child is eligible for special education services in DENISON ISD.

Throughout this process, you have the right to ask questions and request clarification. Don't feel rushed into decisions. Take time to understand the evaluation results and ask the school to explain anything you don't understand.
