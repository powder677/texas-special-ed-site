Understanding FLORESVILLE ISD Special Education Programs

FLORESVILLE ISD special education serves students ages 3-21 with documented disabilities under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). Our programs are designed to provide inclusive, supportive learning environments where every student can thrive academically and socially.

Who Qualifies for Special Education in FLORESVILLE ISD

Students in FLORESVILLE ISD may qualify for special education if they have one of the following documented disabilities:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Intellectual Disability
  • Specific Learning Disability (including dyslexia)
  • Speech or Language Impairment
  • Emotional Disturbance
  • Orthopedic Impairment
  • Visual or Hearing Impairment
  • Other Health Impairment
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Multiple Disabilities

If you suspect your child may need special education services, the first step is requesting a comprehensive evaluation through FLORESVILLE ISD. This evaluation is free and will determine if your child qualifies for services.

Service Options Available in FLORESVILLE ISD

FLORESVILLE ISD special education offers multiple service delivery models to meet individual student needs:

  • General Education Classrooms with Support: Students receive specialized instruction within the regular classroom setting with resource support
  • Resource Programs: Students spend part of the day in specialized classrooms and part in general education
  • Self-Contained Classrooms: For students with more intensive needs requiring specialized instruction throughout the day
  • Specialized Programs: Including autism support, emotional support, and vocational training programs

The ARD Meeting Process in FLORESVILLE ISD

An FLORESVILLE ISD ARD meeting (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) is where critical decisions about your child's special education are made. Understanding this process empowers you to advocate effectively for your child.

What Happens Before Your FLORESVILLE ISD ARD Meeting

Before your FLORESVILLE ISD ARD meeting, you should receive a meeting notice at least 10 days in advance that includes the meeting date, time, location, and the purpose of the meeting. The notice must list all required participants and inform you of your right to bring an advocate or attorney.

Prepare for your meeting by:

  1. Gathering any outside evaluations or medical documentation you have
  2. Writing down specific concerns about your child's progress
  3. Listing questions you want answered about services and placement
  4. Considering whether you want to bring a special education advocate or attorney
  5. Reviewing your child's current grades, assessments, and teacher feedback

Required Participants in FLORESVILLE ISD ARD Meetings

FLORESVILLE ISD law requires specific team members to attend your ARD meeting. These typically include the campus principal or designee, special education director or representative, general education teacher, special education teacher, parent or guardian, and the student (if appropriate). You have the right to request additional participants, such as your child's therapist or physician.

Your Rights During the FLORESVILLE ISD ARD Meeting

As a parent in FLORESVILLE ISD, you have significant rights during the ARD process:

  • Access to all evaluation results and records before the meeting
  • Full participation in placement and IEP decisions
  • Interpretation of any information presented in your native language
  • The right to disagree with the team's recommendations
  • The ability to request an independent evaluation at district expense under certain conditions
  • The right to bring an advocate, attorney, or support person

FLORESVILLE ISD IEP Services and Your Child's Education Plan

Your child's FLORESVILLE ISD IEP services are detailed in an Individualized Education Plan (IEP) document created during the ARD meeting. This legally binding document outlines exactly what services and supports your child will receive.

Key Components of FLORESVILLE ISD IEP Services

A comprehensive FLORESVILLE ISD IEP services plan includes:

  • Present Levels of Performance: Detailed descriptions of your child's current academic and social-emotional functioning
  • Measurable Annual Goals: Specific, achievable objectives for the school year aligned with grade-level standards
  • Special Education Services: The frequency, duration, and location of specialized instruction and support
  • Related Services: Speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, or other support services needed
  • Accommodations and Modifications: Changes to how your child learns and is assessed
  • Transition Planning: For students 14 and older, plans for post-secondary education, employment, and independent living

Progress Monitoring and IEP Updates

FLORESVILLE ISD IEP services are monitored regularly throughout the school year. You'll receive progress reports on your child's IEP goals at least as frequently as report cards are sent to general education students. If your child isn't making adequate progress, request an ARD meeting to revise the plan.

FLORESVILLE ISD Parent Rights in Special Education

FLORESVILLE ISD parent rights are comprehensive and designed to ensure you remain an equal partner in your child's special education. Understanding these rights is essential for effective advocacy.

Your Rights Before Identification and Evaluation

Parents in FLORESVILLE ISD have the right to:

  • Request an evaluation if you believe your child has a disability
  • Receive evaluation at no cost to your family
  • Have your child evaluated by FLORESVILLE ISD within a reasonable timeframe (typically 60 school days)
  • Understand the evaluation process and results in your native language
  • Obtain an independent evaluation if you disagree with the school's assessment

Your Rights Regarding FLORESVILLE ISD Special Education Records

FLORESVILLE ISD parent rights include full access to your child's educational records. You can request to review, obtain copies of, and request amendments to any records related to identification, evaluation, and placement decisions. You also have the right to confidentiality—your child's special education status is protected information.

Dispute Resolution Options for FLORESVILLE ISD Families

If you disagree with FLORESVILLE ISD's decisions regarding your child's special education, you have several options:

  1. Request a Reconsideration ARD Meeting: Ask for another meeting to reconsider the decision
  2. File a Complaint: Submit a written complaint to the Texas Education Agency (TEA) within one year of the alleged violation
  3. Request Mediation: Work with a neutral mediator to resolve disputes with FLORESVILLE ISD
  4. Due Process Hearing: Request a hearing before an impartial hearing officer

FLORESVILLE ISD Dyslexia Program and Reading Support

The FLORESVILLE ISD dyslexia program provides specialized intervention for students with dyslexia and related reading difficulties. Dyslexia is a specific learning disability affecting reading, spelling, and written expression.

Dyslexia Screening and Identification

FLORESVILLE ISD screens all students in early grades for dyslexia risk. If your child shows indicators of dyslexia, the district will conduct a comprehensive evaluation. Texas Education Code requires that students with dyslexia receive evidence-based intervention, often as part of a dyslexia program or specialized reading support within special education.

FLORESVILLE ISD Dyslexia Program Services

Students identified with dyslexia in FLORESVILLE ISD receive:

  • Intensive, specialized reading instruction using evidence-based methods
  • Direct instruction in phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, and comprehension
  • Progress monitoring to ensure intervention effectiveness
  • Accommodations during testing and classroom activities
  • Ongoing parent communication about progress and strategies to support learning at home
