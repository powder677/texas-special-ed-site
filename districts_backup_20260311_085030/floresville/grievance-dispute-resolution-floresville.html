FLORESVILLE ISD Special Education Complaint Process

If informal resolution fails, you can file a formal FLORESVILLE ISD special education complaint through the Texas Education Agency (TEA). This process is distinct from due process hearings and allows you to challenge whether FLORESVILLE ISD has violated special education law or procedural requirements.

Filing a TEA Complaint Against FLORESVILLE ISD

A FLORESVILLE ISD TEA complaint must be filed with the Texas Education Agency, which investigates whether your school district has violated special education regulations. You have two years from the date of the alleged violation to file this complaint. The investigation process is typically faster and less formal than due process hearings.

To file a FLORESVILLE ISD TEA complaint, you must submit a written complaint that includes:

  1. Your name, address, and phone number
  2. Your child's name and current FLORESVILLE ISD school
  3. Description of the problem related to special education
  4. Proposed solution to the problem
  5. Signature and date
  6. Any supporting documentation

Submit your complaint to the Texas Education Agency, Special Education Division. TEA will notify FLORESVILLE ISD and typically conduct an investigation within 30 calendar days. You and FLORESVILLE ISD will have opportunity to provide additional information during this process.

TEA Complaint Outcomes

Following investigation, TEA issues a written decision addressing whether FLORESVILLE ISD violated special education regulations. If violations are found, TEA can require FLORESVILLE ISD to:

  • Correct identified problems and provide compensatory services
  • Implement corrective action plans
  • Provide staff training
  • Monitor compliance for specified periods
  • Reimburse parents for private services if violations caused need for them

Due Process: Formal Dispute Resolution with FLORESVILLE ISD

A FLORESVILLE ISD due process hearing is a formal legal proceeding before an impartial hearing officer when you and FLORESVILLE ISD cannot agree on critical special education decisions. This process addresses disagreements about identification, evaluation, placement, or provision of free appropriate public education (FAPE).

When to Request Due Process with FLORESVILLE ISD

File a FLORESVILLE ISD due process complaint when you and the district fundamentally disagree on:

  • Whether your child needs special education services
  • Evaluation or re-evaluation procedures and results
  • The appropriateness of your child's IEP
  • Placement in the least restrictive environment
  • Related services your child requires

You have two years from the date you knew or should have known about the problem to request a due process hearing with FLORESVILLE ISD. However, certain exceptions may shorten this period, so consult with a special education advocate or attorney near FLORESVILLE immediately if you believe violations occurred.

The Due Process Timeline

Understanding the FLORESVILLE ISD due process timeline helps you prepare effectively. Here's what to expect:

  • Filing complaint: Submit written due process complaint to FLORESVILLE ISD and TEA
  • Within 2 business days: FLORESVILLE ISD must provide you with procedural safeguards notice
  • Within 15 calendar days: FLORESVILLE ISD must provide detailed response to your complaint
  • Resolution meeting: FLORESVILLE ISD convenes meeting to attempt resolution (if you haven't already had one within 30 days before filing)
  • 30 calendar days: Resolution meeting resolution period; many disputes settle here
  • 30-45 days: Hearing officer conducts impartial due process hearing
  • 30 days: Hearing officer issues written decision

FLORESVILLE ISD Mediation: Collaborative Dispute Resolution

FLORESVILLE ISD mediation is a non-adversarial process where a neutral mediator helps you and district representatives reach voluntary agreement on special education disputes. Many parents in FLORESVILLE ISD find mediation less stressful and more collaborative than due process hearings while still addressing serious concerns.

Benefits of FLORESVILLE ISD Mediation

You can request mediation with FLORESVILLE ISD at any time, even before filing a formal complaint. Contact the special education department at FLORESVILLE ISD to express your interest in mediation. If FLORESVILLE ISD mediation results in agreement, both parties sign a legally binding mediation agreement, and FLORESVILLE ISD implements the agreed-upon changes.
