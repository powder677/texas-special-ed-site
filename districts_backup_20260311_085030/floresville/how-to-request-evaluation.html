How to Request a Special Education Evaluation in FLORESVILLE ISD

The process of FLORESVILLE ISD evaluation request is straightforward and designed to be accessible to all families. There are several ways to initiate a formal evaluation request.

Step 1: Contact the School

The first step is to contact your child's FLORESVILLE ISD school. You can speak with your child's teacher, the school counselor, or the principal. Express your specific concerns about your child's academic performance, behavior, or development.

It is advisable to be clear and specific. Instead of saying "my child is not learning well," try saying "my child struggles with reading and has been two years behind in reading skills since second grade."

Step 2: Submit a Formal Written Request

To initiate an official FLORESVILLE ISD special education evaluation, you must submit a FLORESVILLE ISD FIE Spanish (Full Individual Evaluation request). This must be in written form.

Send a formal letter to the Special Education Department of FLORESVILLE ISD requesting an evaluation. You can:

  • Deliver the letter in person to the district office
  • Send it by certified mail
  • Send it by email to the special education specialist

The request must include your child's name, their date of birth, the school they attend, and a clear description of your concerns.

Step 3: FLORESVILLE ISD Responds Within the Deadline

By law, FLORESVILLE ISD has 15 calendar days to respond to your evaluation request. In FLORESVILLE ISD, this means you will receive a written response indicating whether the district agrees to conduct an evaluation or if it believes it is not necessary.

If FLORESVILLE ISD agrees to proceed with the evaluation, they will initiate the evaluation consent process.

The FLORESVILLE ISD Evaluation Process

Once FLORESVILLE ISD agrees to conduct an evaluation, the process follows specific protocols established under Texas special education law.

Evaluation Consent

FLORESVILLE ISD will send you a consent form that you must sign before testing begins. This document explains what areas will be evaluated, what methods will be used, and where the evaluation will take place. Be sure to read it completely.

Typical Evaluation Areas in FLORESVILLE ISD

Depending on the concerns presented, FLORESVILLE ISD evaluators may examine:

  • Academic skills (reading, writing, math)
  • Language and hearing development
  • Gross and fine motor skills
  • Cognitive functioning and intelligence
  • Social and emotional skills
  • Adaptive behavior
  • Vision and hearing

Evaluation Timeline

FLORESVILLE ISD must complete the entire evaluation within 60 calendar days from receiving the signed consent. This timeframe is mandatory under Texas law.

After completing the evaluation, FLORESVILLE ISD will schedule an Admission, Review, Dismissal, and Enrollment (ARD) Committee meeting within a specific timeframe.

The FLORESVILLE ISD ARD Meeting

The ARD meeting is where FLORESVILLE ISD presents the evaluation results and determines if your child qualifies for special education services. This is a crucial meeting where your rights and voice as a parent are protected.

Who Attends the ARD Meeting

In FLORESVILLE ISD, the ARD meeting must include:

  • The student's parents (you)
  • The general education teacher
  • The special education teacher or special education specialist
  • An administrative representative from FLORESVILLE ISD
  • An evaluator who can interpret the results
  • The student (if age-appropriate)

What to Expect at the FLORESVILLE ISD ARD Meeting

During the FLORESVILLE ISD ARD meeting, professionals will present the evaluation findings. They will explain in clear terms your child's strengths and in what areas they need support. You will have the opportunity to ask questions and express your concerns.

If FLORESVILLE ISD determines that your child qualifies for special education, they will create an Individualized Education Program (IEP). If they determine that your child does not qualify, you can request a second opinion or challenge the decision.

After Approval: The IEP Program in FLORESVILLE ISD

If your child qualifies for special education through FLORESVILLE ISD, a personalized Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be developed. This document is legally binding.

Key Elements of the FLORESVILLE ISD IEP

  • Current levels of academic and functional performance
  • Measurable annual goals for the student
  • Specific special education services to be received
  • Necessary accommodations and modifications
  • How progress will be measured
  • Annual review date (FLORESVILLE ISD reviews annually)

The IEP is a living document in FLORESVILLE ISD, meaning you can request changes if you feel your child's needs are not being met.
