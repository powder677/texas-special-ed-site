Understanding Special Education in JARRELL ISD

JARRELL ISD special education services serve students ages 3-21 who qualify under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). Our district serves over 4,157 students, with specialized programs designed to meet diverse learning needs across all campuses in the JARRELL area.

Special education in JARRELL ISD is built on the principle that every child deserves access to quality education in the least restrictive environment. Whether your child needs speech therapy, behavioral support, resource classroom instruction, or specialized academic services, JARRELL ISD has a continuum of services to meet individual needs.

Key Facts About JARRELL ISD Special Education

  • Serves students with disabilities ages 3-21
  • Provides services in general education classrooms and specialized settings
  • Offers early intervention services through JARRELL ISD's preschool programs
  • Maintains compliance with federal and Texas special education laws
  • Employs certified special education teachers and related service providers

JARRELL ISD ARD Meetings: Your Starting Point

The ARD (Admission, Review, Dismissal) meeting is the cornerstone of special education in JARRELL ISD. This is where your child's JARRELL ISD ARD meeting takes place to develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP), make placement decisions, and review your child's progress.

Understanding what to expect at your JARRELL ISD ARD meeting helps you advocate effectively for your child. The ARD committee includes you as the parent, special education staff, general education teachers, and your child (when appropriate).

What Happens at Your JARRELL ISD ARD Meeting

  1. Initial referral review – Discussion of why your child may need special education services
  2. Assessment results presentation – Special education specialists explain evaluation findings
  3. Eligibility determination – Committee decides if your child qualifies under IDEA
  4. IEP development – Goals, services, and accommodations are written for your child
  5. Placement decision – Determination of where services will be provided
  6. Progress monitoring plan – How the school will track your child's advancement

Your JARRELL ISD ARD meeting should occur within 30 days of receiving a referral for evaluation. Parents have the right to bring an advocate or attorney to any meeting in the special education process.

JARRELL ISD IEP Services & Support Options

JARRELL ISD IEP services are customized based on your child's unique strengths, needs, and learning goals. Once your child qualifies for special education, the ARD committee develops a detailed plan outlining what services your child will receive.

Parents throughout JARRELL ISD can expect a comprehensive menu of JARRELL ISD IEP services, including academic instruction, related services, and behavioral supports.

Academic Services in JARRELL ISD Special Education

  • Resource classroom instruction – Specialized academic support in reading, math, and writing
  • Self-contained classrooms – Full-day instruction for students with significant cognitive disabilities
  • Inclusion support – Special education teacher collaboration in general education classes
  • Specialized curriculum programs – Functional academics, life skills, and vocational training
  • Assistive technology services – Tools to support communication and learning access

Related Services Available Through JARRELL ISD

JARRELL ISD IEP services extend beyond classroom instruction. If your child needs support services to benefit from special education, those can be included in your IEP:

  • Speech-language pathology
  • Occupational therapy
  • Physical therapy
  • School psychology services

  • Audiology services
  • Adaptive physical education

JARRELL ISD Dyslexia Program & Academic Support

JARRELL ISD recognizes that dyslexia and reading disabilities require specialized intervention. The JARRELL ISD dyslexia program provides evidence-based reading instruction for students struggling with literacy development.

Under Texas Education Code § 38.003, JARRELL ISD is required to provide dyslexia services to students who need them. If you suspect your child has dyslexia, you can request an evaluation through the special education referral process or ask about JARRELL ISD dyslexia program services directly.

Dyslexia Services in JARRELL ISD

  • Dyslexia screening – Identification of students at risk for reading disabilities
  • Comprehensive dyslexia evaluation – Detailed assessment by certified specialists
  • Specialized reading instruction – Multisensory, structured literacy programs
  • Dyslexia intervention groups – Small group instruction focused on phonemic awareness, phonics, and fluency
  • Assistive technology for reading – Text-to-speech and other reading support tools

Many students in JARRELL ISD benefit from the JARRELL ISD dyslexia program even before qualifying for special education. Ask your campus about Response to Intervention (RTI) services and general education reading support options.

JARRELL ISD Parent Rights & Advocacy Resources

JARRELL ISD parent rights are protected under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code. Understanding your rights empowers you to be an effective advocate for your child's education.

Parents in JARRELL ISD have specific rights throughout the special education process, from initial referral through IEP implementation and dispute resolution.

Your Essential JARRELL ISD Parent Rights

  • Right to notice – Written notification of all special education decisions and proposed actions
  • Right to consent – Permission required before initial evaluation and IEP implementation
  • Right to meaningful participation – Active involvement in all decisions about your child's services
  • Right to an independent evaluation – You can request an outside evaluation at district expense if you disagree with JARRELL ISD assessment results
  • Right to prior written notice – Detailed explanation of any changes in services or placement
  • Right to records access – Review and obtain copies of all special education documents
  • Right to dispute resolution – Mediation and due process hearings through JARRELL ISD

Understanding Your JARRELL ISD Parent Rights Document

JARRELL ISD must provide all families with a detailed "Notice of Procedural Safeguards" that explains JARRELL ISD parent rights in clear, easy-to-understand language. Request this document at your child's school or download it from the JARRELL ISD website.

Parents in JARRELL ISD have the right to bring anyone they choose to ARD meetings, including advocates, attorneys, and family members who know your child well.

How to Access Special Education Services in JARRELL ISD

Beginning the special education process in JARRELL ISD is straightforward. Whether you're a parent concerned about your child's learning or an educator who's noticed developmental delays, here's how the process works.

Step-by-Step Process for JARRELL ISD Special Education Referral

  1. Express concern to your campus – Contact your child's teacher, counselor, or principal at JARRELL ISD
  2. Submit referral request – Request a formal special education evaluation in writing (email, letter, or form)
  3. JARRELL ISD review meeting – School team meets to discuss your concerns and review existing data
  4. Evaluation plan development – If moving forward, JARRELL ISD creates a detailed evaluation plan
  5. Obtain written consent – You receive and sign permission for evaluation
  6. Comprehensive evaluation – JARRELL ISD completes assessments in all areas of concern
  7. ARD meeting for eligibility – Committee reviews results and determines if your child qualifies
  8. IEP development – If eligible, JARRELL ISD ARD meeting develops your child's service plan
  9. Services begin – Your child receives JARRELL ISD IEP services as outlined in the plan

The entire evaluation process must be completed within 60 days from when you submit your referral. JARRELL ISD should keep you informed throughout.
