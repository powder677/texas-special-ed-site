Who Qualifies for VALLEY VIEW ISD Special Education?

Your child may qualify for VALLEY VIEW ISD IEP services if they have a documented disability that impacts their educational performance. Qualifying disabilities under Texas Education Code include:

  • Speech or language impairment
  • Autism spectrum disorder
  • Emotional disturbance
  • Intellectual disability
  • Visual or hearing impairment
  • Orthopedic impairment
  • Other health impairment

If you suspect your child needs special education services, contact the VALLEY VIEW ISD special education department to request a free evaluation.

The VALLEY VIEW ISD ARD Meeting Process

The VALLEY VIEW ISD ARD meeting is where your child's special education plan gets created and reviewed. ARD stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal, and it's your opportunity to collaborate with teachers, specialists, and administrators to develop the best plan for your child.

What Happens During a VALLEY VIEW ISD ARD Meeting?

A VALLEY VIEW ISD ARD meeting brings together your child's teacher, special education staff, and you as the parent to discuss evaluation results, current performance, and goals. The team reviews data and makes decisions about eligibility, services, placement, and accommodations.

During your meeting, expect to discuss:

  • Your child's strengths and areas of concern
  • Evaluation results from VALLEY VIEW ISD assessments
  • Annual goals and measurable objectives
  • Services needed (speech therapy, counseling, resource classes, etc.)
  • Placement in the least restrictive environment
  • Accommodations and modifications
  • Testing accommodations for state assessments

Your Rights in VALLEY VIEW ISD ARD Meetings

As a parent in VALLEY VIEW ISD, you have significant rights during the ARD process. You can bring an advocate, request an independent evaluation, ask for a second opinion, and refuse services you don't believe are appropriate for your child. VALLEY VIEW ISD parent rights are protected under IDEA and Texas Education Code.

You have the right to:

  • Receive notice of the meeting 10 days in advance
  • Bring anyone you choose (advocate, attorney, family member)
  • Request a new meeting if you disagree with decisions
  • Access all records about your child
  • Request an independent educational evaluation at no cost if you disagree with district findings

VALLEY VIEW ISD IEP Services and Support

VALLEY VIEW ISD IEP services are individualized and based on your child's specific needs. An Individualized Education Program (IEP) is a legally binding document that outlines exactly what services your child will receive, how often, and in what setting.

Types of Services Available in VALLEY VIEW ISD

VALLEY VIEW ISD offers diverse support options to meet different needs:

  • Speech-Language Pathology: For students with communication disorders
  • Resource Classes: Small group instruction in core academic areas
  • Self-Contained Classes: For students needing more intensive support
  • Counseling and Mental Health: Emotional and behavioral support
  • Occupational Therapy: For fine motor and adaptive living skills
  • Physical Therapy: For gross motor and mobility needs
  • Specialized Instruction: One-on-one or small group teaching
  • Assistive Technology: Tools and devices to support learning

VALLEY VIEW ISD Dyslexia Program

The VALLEY VIEW ISD dyslexia program provides specialized instruction for students with dyslexia and related reading disorders. Under Texas Education Code, all students with dyslexia must receive intervention using research-based instruction delivered by certified providers.

Our VALLEY VIEW ISD dyslexia program includes:

  • Comprehensive screening for all students in grades K-3
  • Intensive, structured literacy instruction
  • Multisensory intervention techniques
  • Progress monitoring and frequent reassessment
  • Parent education and home support strategies
  • Collaboration with special education services when needed

If you believe your child has dyslexia, request a referral to the VALLEY VIEW ISD dyslexia program evaluation team. Early identification and intervention make a significant difference in reading outcomes.
