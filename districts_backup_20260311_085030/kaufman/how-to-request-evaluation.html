Steps to Request an Evaluation in KAUFMAN ISD

Step 1: Express Your Concern

The first step to requesting an evaluation in KAUFMAN ISD is to communicate your concerns to your child's teacher or school. Often, academic or behavioral difficulties can be addressed with classroom strategies or through an intervention plan before a formal evaluation.

Talk to the teacher, school counselor, or principal about what you have noticed at home or at school. Note specific dates and examples of behaviors or academic difficulties that concern your family in KAUFMAN ISD.

Step 2: Formal Evaluation Request

If the informal process does not resolve your concerns, you can formally request an evaluation in KAUFMAN ISD. You should make this request in writing to the school principal or the special education coordinator of KAUFMAN ISD. An email, letter, or form request is valid—be sure to keep a copy for your records.

In your request, clearly explain why you believe your child might have a disability. Be specific about the behaviors or academic difficulties you have observed. For example: "My child has significant difficulties with reading compared to his classmates" or "He has trouble following instructions and is easily distracted in class."

Step 3: Consent for Evaluation Notice

Once you receive a formal request for a special education evaluation in KAUFMAN ISD, the school must send you a Consent for Evaluation Notice within a specific timeframe. This document explains what will be evaluated, who will conduct the evaluation, and where it will take place.

You should carefully read this notice, ask questions if anything is unclear, and sign it to authorize the evaluation. Parents in KAUFMAN ISD have the right to request an informational meeting before the evaluation if they desire more information.

Step 4: Complete the Comprehensive Evaluation

The FIE in KAUFMAN ISD is conducted by a multidisciplinary team of specialists who may include school psychologists, special education specialists, speech pathologists, and other professionals according to your child's needs.

The evaluation typically includes:

  • Cognitive and processing skills tests
  • Academic evaluations of reading, writing, and mathematics
  • Speech and language evaluations
  • Emotional and behavioral evaluations
  • Physical and motor development evaluations
  • Teacher information on current academic performance
  • Information from you as a parent about the child's development and behavior at home

The complete process in KAUFMAN ISD generally takes between 30 to 60 days from when you sign the consent until you receive the results.

Step 5: Evaluation Conference Meeting

After completing the special education evaluation in KAUFMAN ISD, a meeting will be scheduled to discuss the results. This is the Evaluation Conference, and it is extremely important for parents and teachers in KAUFMAN ISD.

In this meeting, the special education team will explain:

  • The evaluation findings
  • Whether your child qualifies for special education services
  • Recommended services and supports
  • The proposed Individualized Education Program (IEP)

You have the right to bring an advocate, special education attorney, or anyone who can help you understand the information. Do not hesitate to ask questions about any part of the evaluation or the results.

Special Education in KAUFMAN ISD: What Happens Next

If your child is identified as eligible for services under the special education category, KAUFMAN ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). This document details all the services, supports, and accommodations your child will receive at school.

The Individualized Education Program (IEP)

The IEP is a legal plan that is binding for KAUFMAN ISD and for your family. It specifies academic goals, specialized services, and any adjustments in the learning environment. The IEP is reviewed annually, or more frequently if necessary.

Available Services in KAUFMAN ISD

Depending on the needs identified in the special education evaluation in KAUFMAN ISD, your child may receive services such as:

  • Specialized instruction in reading, writing, or mathematics
  • Speech and language therapy
  • Occupational therapy
  • Behavioral support
  • Counseling services
  • Transition supports for adult life (for high school students)
