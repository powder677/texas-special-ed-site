Understanding SHARYLAND ISD Special Education Services

SHARYLAND ISD special education serves students ages 3-21 with disabilities that impact their ability to access grade-level curriculum. Our district provides a continuum of services designed to meet each student's unique needs, from inclusive classroom settings to specialized programs.

The SHARYLAND special education program is built on the foundation of the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and aligns with Texas Education Code §29.001. Parents in SHARYLAND ISD can expect their child to receive a free, appropriate public education (FAPE) in the least restrictive environment (LRE) that meets federal and state requirements.

Special Education Programs Available in SHARYLAND ISD

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) Programs: Specialized instruction and behavioral support for students with autism diagnoses
  • Intellectual Disabilities Services: Functional academics and life skills instruction tailored to student capabilities
  • Learning Disabilities Support: Targeted intervention for students with reading, math, or written expression challenges
  • Emotional/Behavioral Disorders: Mental health support and behavioral intervention planning
  • Physical and Health Disabilities: Accommodations for students with orthopedic, health, or sensory impairments
  • Speech-Language Pathology Services: Evaluation and therapy for communication disorders
  • Dyslexia Intervention: Specialized reading instruction for students with dyslexia

SHARYLAND ISD families benefit from a dedicated special education team committed to identifying and serving all students who qualify for services.

The ARD Process: Your Child's Individualized Education Plan

The SHARYLAND ISD ARD meeting is where critical decisions about your child's education take place. ARD stands for "Admission, Review, and Dismissal," and this meeting is your opportunity to partner with educators in planning your child's special education program.

What Happens in a SHARYLAND ISD ARD Meeting?

During the SHARYLAND ISD ARD meeting, a team that includes you as the parent, special education teachers, general education teachers, administrators, and other specialists will:

  1. Review evaluation results and current performance data
  2. Determine eligibility for special education under IDEA
  3. Discuss your child's strengths, needs, and preferences
  4. Develop or update the Individualized Education Program (IEP)
  5. Identify appropriate special education and related services
  6. Determine the least restrictive environment for instruction
  7. Establish measurable annual goals and short-term objectives
  8. Address any behavioral concerns or service modifications

Your Rights During ARD Meetings

Parents in SHARYLAND ISD have important protections during the ARD process. You have the right to bring an advocate or attorney to your SHARYLAND ISD ARD meeting, request independent evaluations, and ask for meetings to be rescheduled if you need time to prepare. If you disagree with the team's decisions, you may request mediation or due process procedures under Texas Education Code.

SHARYLAND ISD IEP Services and Implementation

Once your child's Individualized Education Program is developed at the ARD meeting, SHARYLAND ISD IEP services are implemented across the school year. These services are documented in the IEP and may include special education instruction, related services, accommodations, and modifications.

Types of Services Included in SHARYLAND ISD IEP Plans

SHARYLAND ISD IEP services are customized based on your child's specific disabilities and educational needs. Common services include:

  • Specialized Instruction: Small group or individual special education classes focused on individualized goals
  • Related Services: Speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling, and other support services
  • Accommodations: Changes to how assignments are presented or how your child responds (extended time, alternative formats)
  • Modifications: Changes to the curriculum content itself to make it appropriate for your child's level
  • Behavior Intervention Plans: Structured approaches to addressing challenging behaviors
  • Transition Services: Planning for post-secondary education, employment, and independent living

SHARYLAND ISD parents receive progress monitoring reports on SHARYLAND ISD IEP services at regular intervals, keeping you informed about your child's advancement toward annual goals.

SHARYLAND ISD Dyslexia Program and Early Reading Intervention

The SHARYLAND ISD dyslexia program provides specialized instruction for students identified with dyslexia or related reading disabilities. Under Texas Education Code §28.006, SHARYLAND ISD is required to provide evidence-based dyslexia intervention using structured literacy approaches.

How the SHARYLAND ISD Dyslexia Program Works

If your child struggles with reading despite quality classroom instruction, the SHARYLAND ISD dyslexia program may be appropriate. The identification process includes:

  • Screening for reading difficulties using validated assessment tools
  • Comprehensive evaluation by a trained dyslexia specialist or reading expert
  • Documentation of the specific reading deficits affecting your child
  • Enrollment in intensive, structured literacy instruction
  • Regular progress monitoring to track intervention effectiveness

The SHARYLAND ISD dyslexia program uses multisensory, systematic, phonics-based instruction specifically designed for students with dyslexia. Parents in SHARYLAND ISD can request dyslexia screening through their child's campus, and the district must respond promptly to evaluation requests under Texas guidelines.

Understanding Your SHARYLAND ISD Parent Rights

SHARYLAND ISD parent rights are grounded in federal special education law (IDEA) and Texas state regulations. Every parent of a child receiving special education services in SHARYLAND ISD receives a copy of these rights at key transition points, but it's important to understand them proactively.

Critical SHARYLAND ISD Parent Rights You Should Know

Your SHARYLAND ISD parent rights include:

  • Right to Evaluation: Request a comprehensive evaluation if you believe your child has a disability affecting education
  • Right to Participate: Meaningful participation in all ARD meetings and decisions about your child's services
  • Right to Records: Access all educational records and student data related to special education
  • Right to Independent Evaluation: Obtain an independent evaluation at district expense under certain circumstances
  • Right to Prior Written Notice: Receive written notice before the district changes your child's special education services
  • Right to Dispute Resolution: Access mediation or due process procedures if you disagree with the school's decisions
  • Right to Least Restrictive Environment: Education in regular classrooms with peers to the maximum extent appropriate
  • Right to Confidentiality: Protection of your child's personally identifiable special education information

SHARYLAND ISD parents should review their complete Parent Rights notice (issued annually) and contact the Special Education Department with any questions about these protections.

Texas Education Code and SHARYLAND ISD Compliance

SHARYLAND ISD special education operates under Texas Education Code §29.001-29.017, which establishes state requirements beyond federal IDEA mandates. Understanding these regulations helps parents advocate effectively within the SHARYLAND ISD system.

Key Timelines and Deadlines for SHARYLAND ISD Parents

The special education process involves multiple important timelines. SHARYLAND ISD families should be aware of these critical dates to ensure services aren't delayed:

  • Initial Evaluation: SHARYLAND ISD must complete evaluations within 60 calendar days of parent consent (unless extended by parent request)
  • IEP Development: An IEP must be in place within 30 days of determining a child is eligible for services
  • ARD Meetings: IEPs are reviewed at least annually, though SHARYLAND ISD families may request meetings more frequently
  • Reevaluation: Comprehensive reevaluations must occur at least every three years (but not more often than annually without parent consent)
  • Prior Written Notice: SHARYLAND ISD must notify parents in writing at least 10 days before implementing any significant change in services

Understanding these timelines helps SHARYLAND ISD parents ensure services are delivered promptly and disputes are addressed within legal timeframes.
