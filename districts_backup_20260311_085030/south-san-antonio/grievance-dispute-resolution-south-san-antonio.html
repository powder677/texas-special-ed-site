Step Two: SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD Special Education Complaint Process

Filing a Complaint with the District

Contact SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD's Special Education Department to request the formal complaint procedure. Your complaint should include:

Step Three: SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD Due Process Hearing

If you're dissatisfied with the district's response or need a faster resolution, you have the right to request a due process hearing. This is a more formal legal process where an impartial hearing officer reviews your case.

Filing for Due Process

To request a due process hearing with SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD, you must submit a written complaint notice to the district's Special Education Department. Your notice must include:

  • Your child's name and address
  • The name of the school where your child attends
  • A description of the nature of the problem
  • Proposed solutions you've already discussed with the district
  • Whether you plan to have an attorney or advocate represent you

SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD has 10 days to acknowledge receipt of your due process request.

Pre-Hearing Requirements

Before the due process hearing, SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD must hold a resolution meeting within 15 days of receiving your request. This meeting gives the district one final opportunity to resolve your concerns without a formal hearing. You can attend this meeting with an attorney or special education advocate from the SOUTH SAN ANTONIO area.

If SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD offers a resolution you accept, you can sign a settlement agreement and avoid the hearing. If you can't reach agreement, you proceed to the formal hearing.

The Due Process Hearing

A hearing officer will listen to evidence from both you (or your representative) and SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD. You can present witnesses, documents, and expert testimony about your child's needs. The hearing officer will make a decision based on the preponderance of evidence.

Decisions from SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD due process hearings can be appealed to the state level within 90 days.

Filing a Complaint with TEA

The TEA's Special Education Dispute Resolution team investigates complaints from parents about special education services. You can file if you believe SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD has violated IDEA or state special education regulations.

To file with TEA:

  1. Submit a written complaint to the TEA (contact information available at tea.texas.gov)
  2. Include the same information required for district complaints
  3. TEA has 30 days to investigate and respond
  4. TEA's decision is final and binding on SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD

One major advantage of filing with TEA is that you don't have to pay for the investigation, unlike due process hearings where costs may apply.

SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD mediation is free to parents and doesn't require hiring an attorney. A trained mediator meets with you and district representatives to discuss the dispute. The mediator helps both parties understand each other's positions and explore possible solutions.

Key benefits of mediation include:

  • Less adversarial atmosphere

  • Opportunity to preserve your relationship with SOUTH SAN ANTONIO ISD staff

If you reach agreement during mediation, both parties sign a binding settlement agreement. Either party can request mediation during any stage of the dispute resolution process.
