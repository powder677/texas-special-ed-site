Under federal law and the state of Texas, parents in LANCASTER ISD have specific rights in special education. Knowing these rights is fundamental to effectively advocate for your child.

Key Parental Rights in LANCASTER ISD

Right to request evaluation: You can submit a written request at any time for LANCASTER ISD to evaluate your child. The school cannot refuse this request without valid, documented reasons.

Right to free evaluation: LANCASTER ISD must provide a comprehensive evaluation at no cost to families. This includes all necessary specialized tests and assessments.

Right to participate in decisions: As a parent, you are an essential member of LANCASTER ISD's Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) Committee. Your opinions and concerns must be considered in all decisions.

Right to receive information in Spanish: LANCASTER ISD must provide all important special education documentation in your preferred language, including notices of rights and procedures.

Right to an independent evaluation: If you disagree with the results of LANCASTER ISD's evaluation, you can request a publicly funded independent educational evaluation.

The Evaluation Request Process in LANCASTER ISD: Step-by-Step

The process of requesting a special education evaluation in LANCASTER ISD is systematic yet accessible. Follow these steps to ensure your request is processed correctly.

Step 1: Contact the School

Communicate with your child's teacher, school counselor, or the principal of the elementary or secondary school your child attends. Express your specific concerns about your child's academic performance or behavior. This initial communication can identify whether the school has also noticed difficulties.

In LANCASTER ISD, many schools first offer general interventions such as tutoring or support groups. If these interventions do not help after 4-6 weeks, the next step is to formally request an evaluation.

Step 2: Make the Formal Written Request

Send a written request for a special education evaluation to your school's principal or LANCASTER ISD's Special Education Department. This request should be clear, dated, and signed by you.

Your letter can be simple. Include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • The school they attend
  • Specific concerns about their learning or behavior
  • A clear request for a special education evaluation
  • Your phone number and email address

Keep a copy for your records. It is advisable to send the request by certified mail or deliver it in person with a delivery receipt.

Step 3: Prior Written Notice and Consent

Within 10 school days of receiving your request, LANCASTER ISD must send you a formal notice. This notice will explain:

  • Special education procedures and rights
  • Proposed tests
  • The request for your informed consent for the evaluation
  • Contact information for the Special Education Department

You should receive this notice in Spanish if that is your preferred language. You must sign to give your consent before the evaluation begins.

Step 4: Complete Evaluation

Once you give consent, LANCASTER ISD will conduct a comprehensive evaluation of your child. This may include:

  • Cognitive aptitude tests
  • Academic achievement assessments
  • Language and communication tests
  • Behavioral and social development evaluations
  • Motor skills assessment if necessary
  • Review of medical and school records
  • Interviews with teachers and parents

LANCASTER ISD must complete the entire evaluation within 60 calendar days from parental consent. The specialists conducting the evaluation must be certified and competent in their area.

Step 5: LANCASTER ISD ARD Committee Meeting

After completing the evaluation, LANCASTER ISD will notify you about a meeting of the Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) Committee. This committee will include:

  • You (parent or guardian)
  • An administrative representative from LANCASTER ISD
  • The general education teacher
  • The special education specialist or special education teacher
  • A professional who will interpret the evaluation results
  • Other specialists as needed

At this meeting, the evaluation findings will be discussed. The committee will determine if your child is eligible for special education services in LANCASTER ISD. If eligible, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be created.

What Does Eligibility Mean in LANCASTER ISD?

If the evaluation determines that your child has a disability that affects their education, they will be declared eligible for services under IDEA. LANCASTER ISD must provide special education and related services free of charge.

Eligibility categories under IDEA include:

  • Intellectual disability
  • Hearing impairment or deafness
  • Visual impairment or blindness
  • Emotional or behavioral disability
  • Specific learning disability (dyslexia, dyscalculia)
  • Autism spectrum disorder
  • Speech or language impairment
  • Physical or orthopedic disability
  • Limited health or medical conditions
  • Traumatic brain injury

LANCASTER ISD also offers services under Section 504 if your child has a disability that significantly limits a major life activity, even if they do not fully qualify under IDEA.

The Individualized Education Program (IEP) of LANCASTER ISD

If your child is declared eligible, LANCASTER ISD will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). This document is crucial because it outlines all the services and supports your child will receive.

What the LANCASTER ISD IEP Must Include

The IEP must include specific, measurable, and achievable academic and behavioral goals. These goals are based on your child's needs identified in the evaluation. The IEP also describes:

  • Special education classes and services
  • The amount of time your child will spend in regular versus special classes
  • Necessary modifications and accommodations
  • Related therapies (speech, occupational, physical therapy)
  • Special transportation services if necessary
  • How your child's progress will be measured

As a parent, your participation in the creation of LANCASTER ISD's IEP is essential. Your observations, concerns, and goals for your child must be included.

Annual Reviews and Reevaluations

LANCASTER ISD will annually review your child's IEP to ensure it remains appropriate and effective. You will be notified and can participate in this review meeting.

Additionally, LANCASTER ISD must reevaluate your child every three years, or more frequently if you request a reevaluation. These reevaluations ensure that services continue to be appropriate for your developing child.

Preparing for the Evaluation: Practical Tips

To ensure the evaluation in LANCASTER ISD is as comprehensive and useful as possible, consider these steps before the evaluation:

Gather Important Documentation

Prepare a folder with:

  • Grades and academic progress reports
  • Previous evaluations or reports from external specialists
  • Relevant medical records
  • Notes about concerns at home
  • Specific examples of difficulties you have observed

Write Down Your Concerns

Before the evaluation, write specific descriptions of your child's difficulties. For example, instead of saying "they have trouble with reading," specify "they confuse similar letters like 'b' and 'd' and read much slower than their peers."

Communicate Important Health Information

Inform the LANCASTER ISD evaluation team about any medical condition, medication, or circumstance that might affect performance during testing.

Steps After Receiving Evaluation Results

Once LANCASTER ISD completes the evaluation and your child is declared eligible, there are several important steps:

  1. Familiarize yourself with the IEP: Carefully read the Individualized Education Program proposed by LANCASTER ISD before the committee meeting.
  2. Ask questions: Do not hesitate to ask anything you do not understand during the LANCASTER ISD ARD committee meeting.
  3. Request copies: Make sure to receive copies of all documents, the complete evaluation, and the IEP in Spanish.
  4. Stay involved: Maintain regular contact with LANCASTER ISD teachers about your child's progress.
