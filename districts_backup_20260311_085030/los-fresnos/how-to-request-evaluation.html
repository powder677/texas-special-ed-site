Federal and state law protect the rights of parents in LOS FRESNOS CISD when it comes to special education. You have the right to request an evaluation, participate in all decisions, and receive clear information about the process.

Your Fundamental Rights

  • Right to request: You can request an evaluation at any time during the school year
  • Right to notification: LOS FRESNOS CISD must notify you in writing about any proposed evaluation
  • Right to consent: You must give your written consent before the evaluation begins
  • Right to privacy: All information about your child is protected by law
  • Right to interpretation: You can receive information in Spanish throughout the process
  • Right to appeal: If you disagree with LOS FRESNOS CISD's decisions, you can formally appeal

These rights are established in the Texas Education Code and the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). LOS FRESNOS CISD is legally obligated to respect these rights for all families.

Steps to Request a Special Education Evaluation in LOS FRESNOS CISD

The process for requesting a FIE evaluation in LOS FRESNOS CISD (Full Individual Evaluation) is clear and structured. Following these steps will help you get a quick and comprehensive response.

Step 1: Contact the School

The first step is to contact your child's teacher or the school principal. You can express your concerns verbally, but it is important to make a written request to create a formal record.

In LOS FRESNOS CISD, each campus has a special education coordinator who is available to answer your initial questions. Do not hesitate to ask to be put in contact with this person.

Step 2: Submit a Formal Written Request

Send a letter to your child's school requesting a special education evaluation. This request must be in writing to comply with LOS FRESNOS CISD's legal requirements. You can use these key phrases:

"I formally request that LOS FRESNOS CISD evaluate [child's name] to determine if they qualify for special education under IDEA. I have concerns about [describe specific difficulties]. I request a written response within the legally required timeframe."

Send this letter by certified mail or deliver it in person to the school. Keep a copy for your records.

Step 3: Await LOS FRESNOS CISD's Response

By law, LOS FRESNOS CISD must respond to your request within 15 school days. During this time, the school will review your request and determine if an evaluation is required.

The school may request to meet with you to discuss your concerns. This is an important opportunity to explain in detail what you have observed regarding your child's difficulties.

Step 4: Written Consent for Evaluation

If LOS FRESNOS CISD determines that an evaluation is appropriate, they will present you with a consent form. This document explains what will be evaluated, who will conduct the evaluation, and when the process will begin.

Do not sign anything you do not fully understand. If the documents are in English and you prefer Spanish, request a translation before signing. LOS FRESNOS CISD is obligated to provide you with documents in Spanish.

Step 5: The Full Evaluation Is Conducted

Once you have given your consent, LOS FRESNOS CISD has 60 days to complete the evaluation. This evaluation may include academic testing, behavioral assessments, speech and language evaluations, and classroom observations.

Different specialists from LOS FRESNOS CISD will be involved in the evaluation. You have the right to ask questions about what is being evaluated and why.

Step 6: Results Meeting (IEP Meeting)

Within 60 days, LOS FRESNOS CISD will conduct a meeting to discuss the evaluation results. At this meeting, the team determines if your child qualifies for special education.

If your child qualifies, they will create an Individualized Education Program (IEP). If they do not qualify, they will explain why and what other support options may be available.

What to Bring to the Evaluation Meeting

When you meet with the LOS FRESNOS CISD team to discuss the evaluation results, come prepared with important documentation:

  • Your child's medical history
  • Records of previous evaluations from other specialists
  • Examples of your child's schoolwork
  • Records of behavior at home
  • Notes on when the problems began
  • A list of questions you want to ask
  • A supporter or advocate (if you wish to have one present)

Bringing this information will help LOS FRESNOS CISD make more informed decisions about your child's evaluation and service plan.

LOS FRESNOS CISD FIE Evaluation in Spanish

An important part of the process is to ensure that the FIE evaluation in LOS FRESNOS CISD is culturally and linguistically appropriate. If your family primarily speaks Spanish at home, this is especially important.

You have the right for the evaluation to include an assessment of your child's Spanish language, especially if they are in bilingual education or are an English as a Second Language student. LOS FRESNOS CISD must evaluate performance in both languages when relevant.

Be sure to inform LOS FRESNOS CISD about the languages spoken in your home. This ensures that the evaluation is accurate and does not misinterpret language difficulties as learning disabilities.

Options If You Disagree with LOS FRESNOS CISD

Sometimes, parents in LOS FRESNOS CISD disagree with evaluation results or decisions made. The law provides you with several resolution options.

Request for Independent Evaluation

If you disagree with LOS FRESNOS CISD's evaluation results, you can request an independent educational evaluation (IEE). This evaluation is conducted by a professional outside of LOS FRESNOS CISD, often at the district's expense.

This is a powerful tool for parents who feel that LOS FRESNOS CISD's testing does not capture their child's true needs.

Mediation and Complaints Process

LOS FRESNOS CISD must offer you mediation if there are disagreements about the evaluation or services. Mediation is a voluntary process where a neutral mediator helps resolve disagreements.

If this does not work, you can file a formal complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA) or request a due process hearing.
