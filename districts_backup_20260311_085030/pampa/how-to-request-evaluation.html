Students in PAMPA ISD who have learning difficulties, behavioral problems, physical disabilities, or developmental delays may be eligible for special education services in PAMPA ISD. A formal evaluation is the first step in determining if your child needs special support.

Many parents in the PAMPA community wait too long before requesting an evaluation. The sooner special needs are identified, the sooner your child can receive the necessary services and supports to achieve academic success.

Signs Your Child Might Need an Evaluation

  • Difficulty following instructions or completing tasks
  • Frequent behavioral problems at school
  • Delays in speech, language, or motor skills
  • Difficulty reading or understanding mathematical concepts
  • Problems with attention or concentration
  • Excessive anxiety or social difficulties
  • Low academic performance despite effort

Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, parents in PAMPA ISD have parent rights in PAMPA ISD protected by federal law. These rights ensure that you have a voice in your child's educational decisions.

Fundamental Rights in the Process

  • Right to request an evaluation: You can request an evaluation in writing at any time during the school year
  • Right to participate: You must be included in all meetings and decisions regarding your child's education
  • Right to information: You have the right to receive written notices in your language
  • Right to due process: You have the right to a fair and equitable process
  • Right to appeal: You can challenge decisions with which you disagree

It is important to understand that these parent rights apply to all students in PAMPA ISD, regardless of their economic situation or immigration status.

Steps to Request a Special Education Evaluation in PAMPA ISD

Step 1: Communicate with Your Child's School

The first step is to speak with your child's teacher or school counselor. Express your specific concerns and request a meeting with the school team. In PAMPA ISD, informal meetings may occur before a formal evaluation request.

Keep a record of all conversations. Note the dates, who was present, and what was discussed. This documentation will be valuable if you need to work with a special education attorney near PAMPA or an advocate later.

Step 2: Submit a Formal Written Request

To request an evaluation in PAMPA ISD, you must submit a written request. This request should be addressed to the Director of Special Education for PAMPA ISD or your child's school principal.

In your written request, include:

  • Student's full name and age
  • Student ID number (if known)
  • School attended
  • Specific description of your concerns
  • Concrete examples of behaviors or academic difficulties
  • Any prior external evaluations or diagnoses
  • Your name, phone, and email
  • Request for a comprehensive evaluation to be conducted

Send this request by certified mail or deliver it in person to the PAMPA ISD office. Request written confirmation that it was received.

Step 3: Understand the Evaluation Timeline

Under Texas law and IDEA, PAMPA ISD has 60 school days to complete the evaluation after receiving your written request. In some cases, it may take up to 90 calendar days.

During this period, PAMPA ISD may conduct:

  • Standardized academic achievement tests
  • Psychological and cognitive evaluations
  • Behavioral evaluations
  • Language and speech evaluations (if necessary)
  • Occupational or physical evaluations (if necessary)
  • Review of school records and medical history

Step 4: Eligibility Determination Meeting

After completing the evaluations, PAMPA ISD must schedule a meeting with you within 30 days. In this meeting, the results of the PAMPA ISD special education evaluation will be discussed, and it will be determined if your child is eligible for services.

Prepare questions for this meeting:

  • What do the evaluation results show?
  • Is my child eligible for special education services?
  • What disability has been identified?
  • What services are recommended?
  • What will the Individualized Education Program (IEP) be?

Understanding the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in PAMPA ISD

A PAMPA ISD FIE in Spanish or Full Individual Initial Evaluation is a comprehensive analysis of your child's strengths and needs. The PAMPA ISD team must evaluate the child in all areas where a disability is suspected.

Areas Typically Evaluated

  • Academic: Reading, math, writing, science, and social studies
  • Cognitive: Intelligence, memory, processing
  • Communication: Receptive and expressive language
  • Motor: Gross and fine coordination
  • Behavioral and emotional: Social and adaptive skills
  • Medical: Information on the student's general health

PAMPA ISD must use multiple measures and not rely on a single test to make eligibility decisions. This ensures that a complete picture of your child's development is captured.

Informed Consent and Parental Participation

Before PAMPA ISD can evaluate your child, they must obtain your written informed consent. This is a fundamental aspect of parent rights in PAMPA ISD.

What Informed Consent Means

  • Explain what evaluations will be conducted and why
  • Describe who will conduct the evaluations
  • Inform you of your rights during the process
  • Provide this information in your language (Spanish)
  • Give you time to ask questions before signing

You have the right to:

  • Refuse consent for the evaluation
  • Request an independent second opinion
  • Have an attorney or advocate present during the evaluations
  • Review all results before the final meeting

After the Evaluation: Next Steps

If Your Child is Eligible

If PAMPA ISD determines that your child has a disability and needs special education, an Individualized Education Program (IEP) will be developed. This plan is a legal document that describes:

  • The current level of academic and functional performance
  • Measurable annual goals
  • Specific services and supports your child will receive
  • Necessary accommodations and modifications
  • Locations where services will be provided
  • Transition to post-school services (for older students)

As a parent in PAMPA ISD, you have the right to fully participate in the development of the IEP. Your perspectives and priorities are essential components of the plan.

If Your Child is Not Eligible

If PAMPA ISD determines that your child is not eligible for services under IDEA, they may still be eligible under Section 504, which is less restrictive but provides important protections and accommodations.

If you disagree with PAMPA ISD's determination, you have appeal rights. You can request a 30-day conflict resolution meeting or a due process hearing.
