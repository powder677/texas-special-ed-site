Requesting an Evaluation in Forney ISD

⚡ Quick Answer

How long does Texas have to complete an evaluation?
Once a written evaluation request is received, the district must complete the Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) and hold an ARD meeting within 45 school days. This timeline is strictly enforced by the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Verbal requests do not start the clock — always submit in writing and keep a copy.

Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in Forney ISD

This guide provides information for parents in Forney ISD who believe their child may need special education services and are considering requesting an evaluation. It outlines the process, timelines, and legal rights afforded under Texas law, specifically the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) as implemented in Texas Education Code Chapter 29 and the Texas Administrative Code (TAC) Title 19, Part 2, Chapter 89.

How to Submit a Written Request

The first step in obtaining a special education evaluation is to submit a written request to Forney ISD. This request should be addressed to the campus principal, the special education director, or the Section 504 coordinator. Clearly state your concerns about your child's academic, behavioral, or developmental progress. Be specific as possible, providing examples of difficulties you have observed at home and/or concerns raised by teachers. Include any relevant documentation, such as previous test results, medical records, or teacher observations. The letter should explicitly request a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) to determine if your child has a disability and is in need of special education services. Keep a copy of the request for your records. Sending the request via certified mail with return receipt requested provides proof of delivery and receipt date.

The 45 School Day Timeline

Upon receiving your written request, Forney ISD has a specific timeline to follow. Under Texas law, the school district must obtain written parental consent to conduct the FIE within 15 school days of receiving the written request. If the district believes an evaluation is warranted, they will send a consent form for you to sign. Once the district receives your signed consent, they have 45 school days to complete the evaluation and hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committee meeting to discuss the findings. This 45-day timeline does NOT include school holidays or breaks longer than 5 school days. It's crucial to track these dates and communicate with the school if you have any concerns about the timeline. If the district fails to meet this timeline, you have the right to file a complaint with the Texas Education Agency (TEA).

What the FIE Must Cover

The Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment of your child's strengths and weaknesses. The FIE must be conducted by qualified professionals and should cover all areas related to the suspected disability. At a minimum, the FIE must include information regarding the child's health, vision, hearing, social and emotional status, general intelligence, academic performance, communication skills, and motor abilities. It also must address whether the child's suspected disability is impacting their educational performance. The FIE should utilize a variety of assessment tools and strategies, including standardized tests, observations, and parent input. The assessment should be culturally and linguistically appropriate for your child. Parents are entitled to request additional testing or assessments if they believe a particular area has not been adequately addressed.

Child Find Obligations

Forney ISD has a legal obligation under "Child Find" to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities residing within the district, regardless of whether they are enrolled in public schools, private schools, or homeschooled. This obligation extends to children suspected of having a disability, even if they are succeeding academically. If you suspect your child has a disability, you have the right to request an evaluation, even if your child is performing at grade level. The school district cannot deny an evaluation solely based on academic performance if there are other indicators of a potential disability.

What If They Refuse

If Forney ISD refuses to conduct an evaluation, they must provide you with written notice within a reasonable timeframe explaining the reasons for their refusal. This notice must include information about your right to dispute the decision and access dispute resolution options, such as mediation or a due process hearing. Under Texas law, you have the right to request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at public expense if you disagree with the district's refusal or the results of the FIE they conducted. An IEE is conducted by a qualified professional who is not employed by the school district. If you request an IEE at public expense, the district must either grant your request or initiate a due process hearing to demonstrate that their evaluation was appropriate. The burden of proof in this hearing rests with the school district.

After the Evaluation

Once the FIE is completed, an ARD committee meeting will be scheduled. This committee includes you, your child (if appropriate), school administrators, teachers, and evaluation specialists. The ARD committee will review the evaluation results and determine if your child meets the eligibility criteria for special education services under Texas law. If your child is found eligible, the ARD committee will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) tailored to your child's unique needs. The IEP will outline specific goals, accommodations, modifications, and related services necessary to support your child's progress in the general education curriculum. You are an equal member of the ARD committee and have the right to participate fully in all decisions regarding your child's education. Remember to advocate for your child's needs and ensure the IEP is appropriate and effective.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

How do I request a special education evaluation in Texas? +

Submit a written request directly to the school's principal or special education director. The written request starts the mandatory 45 school-day clock. Keep a dated copy for your records.

What is a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) in Texas? +

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment the school must complete to determine if your child has a disability and needs special education services. It must cover all areas of suspected disability and be completed within 45 school days of your written consent.

Can a Texas school refuse to evaluate my child? +

Yes, but only if the district provides written notice (Prior Written Notice) explaining why they are refusing and what data they relied on. You have the right to challenge that refusal through mediation or a due process hearing.
