Is a 10-day suspension a Change of Placement?
Yes. Under IDEA, suspending a special education student for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year triggers a Change of Placement and requires a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) within 10 business days. The school must convene an ARD — you have the right to attend.

Discipline Rights & Grievances in Seguin ISD - URGENT GUIDE!

If your child in Seguin ISD is facing disciplinary action, especially suspension or a potential change of placement, ACT IMMEDIATELY! This guide provides crucial information to protect your child's rights. Time is of the essence. Don't delay in seeking legal counsel or advocacy support if needed.

The 10-Day Suspension Rule

Texas law allows schools to suspend students for up to three school days for certain disciplinary infractions, but longer suspensions are possible. The crucial point: If your child accumulates more than 10 days of suspension in a school year, or if the school proposes a suspension that will push them over that limit, it triggers significant protections, particularly if your child has an IEP (Individualized Education Program) or 504 plan.

What to do: Keep detailed records of all suspensions. Know exactly how many days your child has been suspended. Immediately raise concerns with the school if the 10-day threshold is approaching or exceeded.

What Is an MDR (Manifestation Determination Review)?

An MDR (Manifestation Determination Review) is a critical meeting held to determine if a student's behavior that led to a suspension or expulsion is related to their disability. This is ONLY for students with an IEP or 504 plan. If your child has an IEP or 504 and faces suspension exceeding 10 days, or a change of placement, the school MUST conduct an MDR.

Key Questions the MDR Team Must Answer:

  • Was the conduct in question caused by, or did it have a direct and substantial relationship to, the child's disability?
  • Was the conduct in question the direct result of the school district's failure to implement the IEP?

If the answer to either question is YES, the behavior is considered a manifestation of the disability. The school cannot suspend the student for longer than 10 days (in total for the year) or change their placement. They must conduct a functional behavior assessment (FBA) and implement or revise the Behavior Intervention Plan (BIP).

Change of Placement

A Change of Placement occurs when a student is removed from their current educational setting for more than 10 consecutive school days or when a series of removals creates a pattern of exclusions. This can include long-term suspension, expulsion, or placement in an alternative educational setting.

Pay close attention! Even if the suspensions are short, if they become frequent enough to create a pattern, it can be considered a change of placement, triggering MDR rights under the IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) if your child has an IEP or 504.

IMMEDIATELY demand an MDR if:

  • Your child is facing a long-term suspension or expulsion.
  • Your child is being placed in a DAEP (Disciplinary Alternative Education Program)
  • You believe there is a pattern of short suspensions that equate to a change of placement.

Filing a Level 1 Grievance

If you believe the school has violated your child's rights (e.g., improper suspension, failure to provide services outlined in the IEP, denying an MDR), you can file a grievance. Start with a Level 1 Grievance. This is typically filed with the principal or the individual responsible for the decision you are grieving. Obtain the grievance form from the Seguin ISD website or the school. Be precise and detailed in outlining the facts and the specific violations. Include all relevant dates, names, and supporting documentation. File this grievance IMMEDIATELY. Deadlines are strict!

TEA State Complaint

If the grievance process is unsuccessful, or if the violation is serious, you can file a TEA (Texas Education Agency) State Complaint. This involves submitting a formal complaint to the TEA alleging violations of state or federal special education laws. Visit the TEA website for specific instructions and forms. This is a more formal and involved process, so consider seeking legal assistance.

Due Process

Due process is a legal mechanism under IDEA that allows parents to formally challenge the school district's decisions regarding their child's special education services. This is a more adversarial process and generally requires legal representation. Seek immediate legal counsel if you believe a due process hearing is necessary.

Remember: Document everything. Keep copies of all correspondence, disciplinary notices, IEPs, 504 plans, and grievance filings. Advocate fiercely for your child's rights. Don't be afraid to seek outside help from legal professionals, disability rights organizations, or educational advocates.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers every parent in Texas needs to know.

What is a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) in Texas? +

An MDR is a meeting that must occur within 10 business days when a special education student is suspended for more than 10 cumulative days or faces expulsion. The ARD team determines whether the behavior was caused by the child's disability.

How do I file a Level 1 Grievance with a Texas school district? +

A Level 1 Grievance is filed directly with the campus principal within the timeframe stated in your district's grievance policy (often 15 days of the incident). Submit it in writing, describe the specific policy violated, and keep a copy.

What is a Change of Placement for a special education student? +

A Change of Placement occurs when a district removes a special education student for more than 10 consecutive or cumulative school days in a year. It triggers procedural protections including an MDR and requires an ARD meeting.
