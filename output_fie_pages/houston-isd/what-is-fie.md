## What Is an FIE in Houston ISD?

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment that determines whether your child has a disability and needs special education services in Houston ISD. This evaluation examines all areas of suspected disability and serves as the foundation for creating an Individualized Education Program (IEP) if your child qualifies. Understanding your FIE rights empowers you to advocate effectively for your child's educational needs.

## Why HISD Must Conduct a Full Individual Evaluation

Under federal IDEA law and Texas Education Code Chapter 29, Houston ISD has a "Child Find" duty to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with suspected disabilities within the district boundaries. When you request an FIE or when school personnel suspect a disability, HISD must conduct a comprehensive evaluation that examines all areas of suspected disability — not just academic concerns. This multi-area approach ensures no disability goes undetected and that your child receives appropriate support. The district cannot limit the evaluation scope based on budget constraints or staffing issues.

## How to Request an FIE from Houston ISD

Submit your written request to both your child's school principal and Houston ISD's Director of Special Education Services. Use this exact phrase: "I am requesting a Full Individual Evaluation to determine if my child has a disability and needs special education services." Include your child's full name, school, grade, and specific concerns about academic performance, behavior, communication, or other areas. Houston ISD has exactly 15 school days to respond in writing with either consent forms to begin the evaluation or Prior Written Notice explaining any refusal. Keep copies of all correspondence and send requests via email or certified mail for documentation.

## The 45-School-Day Timeline

Houston ISD's evaluation clock begins ticking the day they receive your signed consent forms, not when you initially requested the FIE. The district has 45 school days to complete all assessments and provide you with a written evaluation report. School days exclude weekends, holidays, and any days when school is not in session for students. HISD must schedule and conduct all necessary evaluations within this timeframe, including assessments by school psychologists, diagnosticians, speech therapists, and other specialists. After completing the report, the district must schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting within 30 calendar days to discuss results and determine eligibility.

## What the HISD Evaluation Must Cover

Houston ISD must evaluate all areas of suspected disability, which typically includes academic achievement, intellectual development, speech and language skills, social-emotional functioning, and behavioral concerns. If occupational or physical therapy needs are suspected, those areas must be assessed. When intellectual disability is considered, the evaluation must include adaptive behavior assessment. All testing must be conducted in your child's primary language or mode of communication unless clearly not feasible. The evaluation team must use multiple assessment tools and cannot rely on a single test score. HISD cannot exclude evaluation areas due to cost or evaluator availability.

## Can Houston ISD Refuse to Evaluate My Child?

Yes, Houston ISD can refuse your FIE request, but only through proper legal procedures. The district must provide Prior Written Notice explaining their refusal, including specific reasons and data supporting their decision. Important: The district cannot refuse an evaluation simply because your child is receiving Response to Intervention (RTI) services or general education supports. If HISD refuses, you have several options: request mediation through the Texas Education Agency, file a State Complaint alleging violation of special education law, or pursue due process hearing rights. Many refusals can be resolved through discussion with district administrators.

## What Happens After the FIE?

Houston ISD must provide you with a complete written evaluation report before the ARD meeting, giving you time to review results and prepare questions. The ARD meeting must occur within 30 calendar days of the evaluation's completion. During this meeting, the ARD team reviews all evaluation data to determine if your child has a disability requiring special education services. If eligible, the team develops an IEP outlining goals, services, and placement. If you disagree with the evaluation results, you have the right to request an Independent Educational Evaluation at the district's expense.

## FIE vs. FIIE in Texas: What's the Difference?

FIE is the general term for any comprehensive special education evaluation. FIIE stands for Full Individual and Initial Evaluation, specifically referring to a child's first evaluation in a particular school district. Whether called FIE or FIIE, the same legal requirements, timelines, and parent rights apply. Houston ISD must follow identical procedures for both, including the 45-school-day timeline and comprehensive assessment requirements.

## Your Rights as a Houston ISD Parent

• Receive the complete evaluation report before the ARD meeting
• Bring an advocate, family member, or attorney to all meetings
• Request an Independent Educational Evaluation if you disagree with HISD's evaluation
• Request reconsideration of eligibility decisions with additional data
• File a State Complaint with the Texas Education Agency for procedural violations
• Access all evaluation records and request copies at reasonable cost
• Have evaluation materials explained in understandable language
