## What Is an FIE in Cypress-Fairbanks ISD?

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment that Cypress-Fairbanks ISD conducts to determine if your child has a disability and needs special education services. This evaluation examines multiple areas of your child's development and learning to create a complete picture of their strengths and needs.

## Why CFISD Must Conduct a Full Individual Evaluation

Under federal law (IDEA) and Texas Education Code Chapter 29, Cypress-Fairbanks ISD has a "Child Find" duty to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities in their district. When there's reason to suspect your child may have a disability that affects their educational performance, CFISD must conduct a comprehensive evaluation across all areas of suspected disability. This isn't optional—it's a legal requirement that ensures every child receives the support they need to succeed academically.

The evaluation must be thorough enough to determine not only whether a disability exists, but also what special education and related services your child might need. CFISD cannot limit the evaluation to just one area of concern; they must examine all aspects of your child's functioning that could be affected by a potential disability.

## How to Request an FIE from Cypress-Fairbanks ISD

You can request an FIE by submitting a written request to your child's school principal and copying the CFISD Director of Special Education. Your request should include the specific phrase: "I am requesting a Full Individual Evaluation to determine if my child is eligible for special education services under IDEA."

Once CFISD receives your written request, they have exactly 15 school days to respond with either consent forms to begin the evaluation or Prior Written Notice explaining why they're refusing. Keep a copy of your request and note the date you submitted it, as this starts the legal timeline. Email is perfectly acceptable and creates an automatic timestamp for your records.

## The 45-School-Day Timeline

After you sign consent for the evaluation, Cypress-Fairbanks ISD has 45 school days to complete the FIE and provide you with a written report. This countdown includes only days when school is in session—weekends, holidays, and summer breaks don't count unless CFISD operates summer programs.

The evaluation must be truly comprehensive, examining all areas where your child might have challenges. Once you receive the completed evaluation report, CFISD must schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee meeting within 30 calendar days to discuss the results and determine eligibility for special education services.

## What the CFISD Evaluation Must Cover

The evaluation must assess all areas of suspected disability, which typically includes academic achievement, intellectual development, and functional performance. If there are concerns about communication, CFISD must include speech and language assessments. Social-emotional functioning should be evaluated when behavioral or emotional challenges are suspected.

When fine motor skills, handwriting, or daily living activities are concerns, occupational therapy assessments must be included. If intellectual disability is suspected, the evaluation must examine adaptive behavior—how well your child manages age-appropriate daily activities. Importantly, all assessments must be conducted in your child's primary language or preferred mode of communication to ensure accurate results.

## Can Cypress-Fairbanks ISD Refuse to Evaluate My Child?

Yes, CFISD can refuse your evaluation request, but only through formal Prior Written Notice that explains their reasoning and your rights. The district cannot use Response to Intervention (RTI) or other tiered support systems as a reason to delay or deny an evaluation request—these processes can run parallel to the evaluation.

If CFISD refuses your request, you have several options: request mediation through the Texas Education Agency, file a State Complaint alleging violation of IDEA procedures, or request a due process hearing. Each option has specific timelines and procedures, so consider which approach best fits your situation.

## What Happens After the FIE?

CFISD must provide you with a complete written evaluation report before the ARD meeting, giving you time to review the results and prepare questions. The ARD committee, which includes you as an equal member, will meet within 30 calendar days of the evaluation's completion to determine if your child qualifies for special education services.

If your child is found eligible, the team will develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) outlining specific goals, services, and accommodations. If you disagree with the evaluation results, you have the right to request an Independent Educational Evaluation at the district's expense.

## FIE vs. FIIE in Texas: What's the Difference?

In Texas, you might see both terms used. FIE is the general term for any comprehensive special education evaluation. FIIE stands for "Full Individual and Initial Evaluation," referring specifically to the first evaluation conducted in a particular district. Whether called an FIE or FIIE, the same legal requirements, timelines, and comprehensive assessment standards apply to both.

## Your Rights as a Cypress-Fairbanks ISD Parent

• Receive the evaluation report in advance of the ARD meeting
• Bring an advocate or support person to all meetings
• Request an Independent Educational Evaluation if you disagree with CFISD's results
• Ask for reconsideration of eligibility decisions
• File a TEA State Complaint if you believe CFISD violated proper procedures
• Request all communications and documents in your preferred language
