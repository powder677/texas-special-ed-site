## What Is an FIE in Northside ISD?

A Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) is a comprehensive assessment that Northside ISD conducts to determine if your child has a disability and needs special education services. As a NISD parent, understanding this process helps you advocate effectively for your child's educational needs.

## Why NISD Must Conduct a Full Individual Evaluation

Under federal IDEA law and Texas Education Code Chapter 29, Northside ISD has a "Child Find" duty to identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities within the district. When you suspect your child has a disability that affects their educational performance, NISD must consider conducting an FIE. The evaluation must examine all areas of suspected disability, not just the concerns that initially prompted the referral. This comprehensive approach ensures no disability goes undetected and your child receives all necessary supports.

## How to Request an FIE from Northside ISD

Submit a written request to your child's campus principal and copy the NISD Director of Special Education. Include this specific language: "I am requesting a Full Individual Evaluation to determine if my child is eligible for special education services under IDEA." This exact phrasing triggers NISD's legal obligations under federal law. Once NISD receives your written request, they have exactly 15 school days to respond with either consent forms to begin the evaluation or Prior Written Notice explaining why they're refusing. Keep a dated copy of your request and delivery confirmation for your records.

## The 45-School-Day Timeline

The evaluation timeline begins when NISD receives your signed consent forms, not when you submitted your initial request. Northside ISD has 45 school days from the consent date to complete the evaluation and provide you with a written report. School days include only days when students attend classes, excluding weekends, holidays, and breaks. After delivering the written report, NISD must hold an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committee meeting within 30 calendar days to discuss results and determine eligibility. This 30-day window uses calendar days, not school days.

## What the NISD Evaluation Must Cover

NISD must evaluate all areas of suspected disability, including academic achievement, intellectual development, speech and language abilities, social-emotional functioning, and behavior. If occupational or physical therapy needs are suspected, those areas require assessment too. When intellectual disability is suspected, the evaluation must include adaptive behavior assessment. All testing must occur in your child's primary language or communication mode unless clearly not feasible. NISD cannot limit the evaluation to only the initial area of concern—they must cast a wide net to identify all potential disabilities affecting your child's education.

## Can Northside ISD Refuse to Evaluate My Child?

Yes, but only through a formal Prior Written Notice that explains their reasoning and your rights. NISD cannot refuse an evaluation simply because your child is receiving Response to Intervention (RTI) services or hasn't "failed" RTI. If NISD refuses, you have several options: request an IEP facilitation meeting to discuss concerns, file a State Complaint with the Texas Education Agency, or request a due process hearing. Mediation is also available at no cost through TEA's dispute resolution system.

## What Happens After the FIE?

NISD must provide you with a complete written evaluation report before the ARD meeting. This report includes all assessment results, interpretations, and recommendations. The ARD committee, which includes you as an equal member, meets within 30 calendar days of the report to determine eligibility and develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP) if your child qualifies. If your child is eligible, the IEP must be implemented immediately unless you request a delay. You maintain the right to request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at district expense if you disagree with NISD's evaluation.

## FIE vs. FIIE in Texas: What's the Difference?

FIE is the general term for any comprehensive special education evaluation. FIIE stands for "Full Individual and Initial Evaluation," which specifically refers to the first evaluation a student receives in a particular school district. Whether NISD calls it an FIE or FIIE, the same legal requirements, timelines, and parent rights apply. Both must be comprehensive, timely, and conducted at no cost to families.

## Your Rights as a Northside ISD Parent

• Receive the complete evaluation report before the ARD meeting
• Bring an advocate, family member, or attorney to all meetings
• Request an Independent Educational Evaluation if you disagree with results
• Ask the ARD committee to reconsider their eligibility decision
• File a State Complaint with TEA if procedures weren't followed properly
• Request due process if you disagree with identification or services
• Have all evaluation materials explained in language you understand
