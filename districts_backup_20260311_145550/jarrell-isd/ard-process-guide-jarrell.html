Understanding ARD Meetings in JARRELL ISD

An ARD meeting in JARRELL ISD is a formal gathering where school personnel, parents, and other qualified individuals meet to discuss your child's special education needs. The acronym stands for Admission, Review, and Dismissal, reflecting the three primary purposes of these meetings throughout your child's special education journey.

For JARRELL ISD families, the ARD committee has the authority to determine whether your child qualifies for special education services, what those services should include, and when they might no longer be needed. This committee typically includes teachers, administrators, counselors, and the parent or guardian.

Under Texas Education Code Section 414.101, every student receiving special education services in JARRELL ISD must have an ARD committee. Understanding your rights and responsibilities in this process helps ensure your child receives the support they deserve.

The Initial Referral and Evaluation Process

How Referrals Work in JARRELL ISD

A referral for special education evaluation in JARRELL ISD can come from parents, teachers, counselors, or other school staff who have concerns about a child's learning or development. You have the right to request an evaluation at any time if you believe your child may need special education services.

Once a referral is made within JARRELL ISD, the district must provide you with a Notice of Proposed Evaluation within a specific timeframe. This notice explains the evaluation process, your rights, and how you can contact the school if you have questions.

The Evaluation Timeline

JARRELL ISD must complete the initial evaluation within 60 calendar days of receiving parental consent. This timeline is required under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). During this period, various assessments and observations occur to gather comprehensive information about your child's abilities and needs.

  • Days 8-45: Evaluation team conducts assessments, observations, and reviews records
  • Days 46-60: ARD committee meets to review findings and determine eligibility

If JARRELL ISD needs additional time, they must notify you in writing before the 60-day deadline expires.

Understanding the IEP (Individualized Education Program)

What is an IEP in JARRELL ISD Special Education?

The JARRELL ISD IEP is a legally binding document that outlines your child's special education program. An Individualized Education Program specifically tailored for your child guides all special education services, accommodations, and goals. This document ensures that JARRELL ISD provides appropriate services matched to your child's unique needs.

Your child's JARRELL ISD IEP must be reviewed and updated at least annually. You can request an ARD meeting at any time if you believe the IEP needs adjustment.

Key Components of Your Child's IEP

Every JARRELL ISD IEP must include specific required components:

  • Present Levels of Performance: Describes your child's current academic and functional abilities
  • JARRELL ISD IEP Goals: Specific, measurable objectives addressing identified needs
  • Special Education Services: Types and frequency of services (resource room, speech therapy, etc.)
  • Accommodations and Modifications: Adaptations to help your child access the curriculum
  • Placement: Where services will be delivered (regular classroom, resource room, separate setting)
  • Progress Monitoring: How and when progress toward goals will be measured
  • Transition Services: For students age 16 and older, planning for post-secondary life

Setting Meaningful Goals

JARRELL ISD IEP goals should be SMART—Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. Rather than vague objectives, effective goals specify exactly what skill your child will master, how progress will be measured, and the timeline for achievement.

For example, instead of "improve reading," a strong JARRELL ISD IEP goal states: "By May 2024, the student will read grade-level passages with 85% accuracy as measured by quarterly curriculum-based measurements."
