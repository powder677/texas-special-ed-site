Your Rights During the PORT ARTHUR ISD Evaluation Request Process

Parents in PORT ARTHUR ISD have specific legal rights throughout the PORT ARTHUR ISD evaluation request process:

PORT ARTHUR ISD must provide written notice explaining the evaluation procedures, what tests will be used, and your rights before any evaluation begins. This notice must be in your native language if English is not your primary language.

Right to Informed Consent

You must give written permission before PORT ARTHUR ISD can begin a comprehensive evaluation. The district cannot evaluate your child without your signed consent, except in specific legal circumstances.

You have the right to be part of the evaluation process, ask questions, and provide information about your child. PORT ARTHUR ISD evaluators must consider parent input when making eligibility decisions.

If you disagree with PORT ARTHUR ISD's evaluation results, you have the right to request an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at district expense under certain conditions.

All evaluation information is confidential and protected under FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act). PORT ARTHUR ISD can only share your child's evaluation results with people who have a legitimate educational need to know.
