How to Request an Evaluation in ALICE ISD

The process for requesting a FIE evaluation in ALICE ISD (Full and Individual Evaluation) is straightforward and accessible for all parents. FIE stands for "Full and Individual Evaluation" and is the official Texas term for special education evaluations.

Step 1: Contact Your Child's School

Start by contacting your child's school. You can speak with:

  • Your child's classroom teacher
  • The school counselor
  • The school's special education coordinator
  • The school principal

Request a special education evaluation in writing. While you can make a verbal request, a written request creates an official record and starts the 15-day legal clock for ALICE ISD to respond.

Step 2: Prepare Your Written Request

Your written request does not need to be formal or lengthy. It should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Your school's name in ALICE ISD
  • Specific concerns about your child's learning or behavior
  • Your contact information (phone and email)
  • Your signature and the date

You can send your request by email, regular mail, or in person. Keep a copy for your records and ask for a delivery receipt if delivering in person.

Step 3: ALICE ISD Responds to Your Request

Within 15 school days of receiving your evaluation request, ALICE ISD must respond. During this time, the school may:

  • Contact you to discuss your concerns
  • Offer you a meeting to review your child's history
  • Request written authorization to proceed with the evaluation

In some cases, ALICE ISD may offer regular classroom interventions first. You can accept or decline these interventions and insist on a full evaluation if you wish.

Step 4: Authorization and Consent

Before ALICE ISD begins the evaluation, they must obtain your written consent. You will receive a form that explains:

  • The tests that will be administered
  • Who will administer them
  • Where they will be conducted
  • Your rights as a parent in ALICE ISD

Read this document carefully. You can ask questions before signing. If you wish to bring a special education attorney near ALICE or a special education advocate in ALICE to review the document, you have that right.

The ALICE ISD Evaluation Process

Once you authorize the evaluation, ALICE ISD has 60 school days to complete it and schedule a results meeting. During this time, special education specialists will evaluate your child in various areas.

The Evaluation Results Meeting

Important Special Education Terms in ALICE ISD

The special education process in ALICE ISD uses specific terminology that is important to understand:

IEP (Individualized Education Program)

If your child qualifies for special education services in ALICE ISD, they will receive an IEP. This document describes:

  • Your child's current level of performance
  • Specific annual educational goals
  • Special services they will receive (therapy, special education, etc.)
  • Where they will receive education (regular class vs. special education)
  • How progress will be measured

504 Plan

If your child does not qualify for an IEP but has a disability that affects a major life activity, they may receive a 504 Plan in ALICE ISD. This provides accommodations (changes in how they are taught or tested) but not special services.

Full and Individual Evaluation (FIE)

The FIE is the comprehensive evaluation that ALICE ISD conducts to determine if your child needs special education. It includes tests in multiple areas.
