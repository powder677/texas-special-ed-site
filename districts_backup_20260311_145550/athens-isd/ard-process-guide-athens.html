Understanding ARD Meetings in ATHENS ISD

An ATHENS ISD ARD meeting is a formal gathering where school personnel, parents, and other professionals come together to discuss your child's special education needs and develop an Individualized Education Program (IEP). The ARD committee is responsible for determining eligibility for special education services, establishing appropriate educational goals, and ensuring your child receives the support needed to succeed.

The ARD process is governed by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Texas Education Code §89.1010. In ATHENS ISD, these meetings are required to occur at least annually, though parents can request additional meetings at any time when concerns arise about their child's progress or placement.

Who Participates in an ATHENS ISD ARD Meeting?

  • Special education teacher – Provides insight into the child's performance in special education settings

The ATHENS ISD IEP Process Step-by-Step

The ATHENS ISD IEP process involves several distinct phases. Understanding each phase helps parents navigate the journey from initial referral through ongoing service delivery.

Phase 1: Referral and Initial Evaluation

The ATHENS ISD IEP process begins when a child is referred for evaluation. Referrals can come from parents, teachers, or other school personnel who have concerns about a child's learning or development.

Once a referral is made to ATHENS ISD, the district must conduct a comprehensive evaluation within 60 calendar days. This evaluation should assess all areas of suspected disability and may include psychological testing, educational achievement assessments, speech and language evaluation, occupational therapy assessment, and developmental history review.

Phase 2: Initial ARD Meeting and Eligibility Determination

Within 30 days of completing evaluations, ATHENS ISD must conduct an initial ATHENS ISD ARD meeting to determine eligibility for special education services. The ARD committee reviews all evaluation data and determines whether your child meets criteria under IDEA disability categories.

In Texas, eligible disability categories include autism, blindness, deafness, deaf-blindness, emotional disturbance, hearing impairment, intellectual disability, multiple disabilities, orthopedic impairment, other health impairment, specific learning disability, speech or language impairment, traumatic brain injury, and visual impairment.

Phase 3: IEP Development and ATHENS ISD IEP Goals

If your child is found eligible, the ARD committee develops an Individualized Education Program. The IEP is the legal document that outlines your child's educational plan. This phase is where ATHENS ISD IEP goals are established.

ATHENS ISD IEP goals must be:

  • Specific and measurable – Goals should clearly define what skill will be addressed and how progress will be measured
  • Appropriate to grade level – Goals should align with grade-level curriculum while addressing the child's special needs
  • Based on present levels of academic and functional performance – Goals must connect to your child's current abilities
  • Focused on areas of need – Goals should target areas where your child requires special education services
  • Realistic and achievable – Goals should challenge your child while remaining attainable within one school year

During the goal-development portion of your ATHENS ISD ARD meeting, parents should advocate for goals that address their child's most significant needs and ask clarifying questions about measurement and progress monitoring procedures.

Phase 4: Special Education Services and Placement Decisions

The ATHENS ISD IEP process requires decisions about least restrictive environment (LRE) and special education services. ATHENS ISD must provide services in the most typical educational setting possible, with removal from general education classrooms only when necessary to meet individual needs.

Decisions during this phase include determining the amount of special education versus general education instruction, identifying related services (speech therapy, occupational therapy, counseling), and establishing the educational setting (resource class, self-contained classroom, mainstream classroom with accommodations).

Phase 5: Annual Review and Triennial Reevaluation

Each year, ATHENS ISD families participate in an annual ARD meeting to review progress toward ATHENS ISD IEP goals and make any necessary adjustments. At least every three years, a comprehensive reevaluation must occur to determine if your child continues to be eligible for services and whether goals and placement remain appropriate.

If your child makes significant progress, ATHENS ISD may recommend dismissal from special education services. Parents in ATHENS ISD have the right to request an independent evaluation before accepting a dismissal recommendation.
