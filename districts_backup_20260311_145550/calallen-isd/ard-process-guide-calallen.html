CALALLEN ISD goals should be SMART—Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. The ARD Meeting Process: What to Expect

Knowing what happens during your CALALLEN ISD ARD meeting helps you prepare mentally and strategically. The process follows a specific structure governed by IDEA and Texas Education Code.

Who Attends CALALLEN ISD ARD Meetings

By law, your CALALLEN ISD ARD meeting must include:

  • Parent(s) or guardian
  • Special education teacher
  • General education teacher (if child is in mainstream classes)
  • CALALLEN ISD administrator or designated representative
  • School counselor, psychologist, or diagnostician
  • Your child (when appropriate for age/maturity)

The Meeting Flow

First: Introductions occur, roles are explained, and you receive copies of CALALLEN ISD ARD rights documentation. The facilitator explains the purpose of the meeting.

Second: The team reviews your child's current performance, including strengths and needs. In CALALLEN ISD, recent assessment data and teacher observations are presented.

Third: If it's an initial evaluation meeting, the team discusses whether your child qualifies for special education services under IDEA.

Fourth: The team develops, reviews, or revises CALALLEN ISD IEP goals and determines needed services and accommodations.

Fifth: Placement decisions are made, determining whether your child receives services in a general education classroom, resource room, or specialized setting in CALALLEN ISD.

Finally: You receive written copies of all decisions. You must sign the IEP, though your signature doesn't mean you agree—it simply documents that you attended your CALALLEN ISD ARD meeting.

Common Scenarios for CALALLEN ISD ARD Meetings
