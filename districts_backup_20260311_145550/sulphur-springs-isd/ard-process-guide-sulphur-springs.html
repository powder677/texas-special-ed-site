Understanding Key Components of the SULPHUR SPRINGS ISD IEP

Your child's IEP is a complex document. Understanding each section helps you monitor your child's progress and advocate effectively at future SULPHUR SPRINGS ISD ARD meetings.

Measurable Annual Goals

SULPHUR SPRINGS ISD IEP goals should be specific, measurable, and directly address your child's disability-related needs. Rather than vague goals like "improve reading," effective goals state exactly what your child will accomplish, how progress will be measured, and by when.

For example: "Student will read grade-level text with 90% accuracy on comprehension questions in 8 out of 10 attempts by May 2024." This goal is measurable and allows you to monitor progress throughout the year.

Special Education Services

This section details frequency and duration of services. It should specify how many minutes per week your child receives each service and where services occur. SULPHUR SPRINGS ISD must provide services according to the schedule in the IEP.

Accommodations and Modifications

Accommodations help your child access the same curriculum (extended time on tests, large print, audio books). Modifications change what your child is taught (different grade-level standards, alternative assignments). SULPHUR SPRINGS ISD must document which accommodations and modifications your child receives.

Progress Monitoring

The IEP must explain how SULPHUR SPRINGS ISD will measure progress on SULPHUR SPRINGS ISD IEP goals and how often you'll receive progress reports. You deserve regular updates about your child's achievement.
