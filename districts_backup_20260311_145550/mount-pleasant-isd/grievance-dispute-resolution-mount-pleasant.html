MOUNT PLEASANT ISD Due Process Hearing: Your Legal Right

When disputes cannot be resolved through complaint procedures or mediation, parents have the right to request a due process hearing. This is a formal legal proceeding before an impartial hearing officer to address alleged MOUNT PLEASANT ISD due process violations.

You can request due process when MOUNT PLEASANT ISD proposes or refuses to initiate or change the evaluation, educational placement, or free appropriate public education of your child. Common scenarios include disagreements about IEP content, requested services the district denies, or proposed placements you believe are inappropriate.

The Due Process Request

Submit a written request for a due process hearing to MOUNT PLEASANT ISD's Special Education Director. Your request must include your child's name, address, school name, a description of the problem, proposed solutions, and facts related to the problem. You have two years from the date you knew or should have known about the problem to file , though this timeline can be shortened in some circumstances.

Pre-Hearing Meeting

Within 15 days of your due process request, MOUNT PLEASANT ISD must conduct a meeting with you to discuss the dispute and attempt resolution. You may bring an advocate or attorney. If MOUNT PLEASANT ISD doesn't resolve the matter to your satisfaction within 30 days, the hearing will proceed.

The Hearing Process

A hearing officer will hear evidence from both you and MOUNT PLEASANT ISD, including testimony and documents. You have the right to be represented by an attorney, present witnesses, examine records, and question the district's witnesses. Hearings typically last one to three days but can extend longer in complex cases. The hearing officer issues a written decision within 45 days of the request.
